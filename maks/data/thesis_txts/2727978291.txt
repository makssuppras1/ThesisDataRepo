ABSTRACT  
 
Disturbances in a railway system greatly affect the operation of train and lead to train delays. 
Delays are a common problem that companies face in their daily operations and reduce the 
performance of the network.  
 
Big data techniques provide powerful tools able to handle vast amounts of data. Clustering is 
the classification of objects into different groups or the partitioning of a data set into 
clusters, so that the data in each cluster share common features. This Thesis reviews and 
compares three established clustering techniques: The K-means algorithm, Affinity 
Propagation Algorithmand DBSCAN algorithm. The comparison is based on validity 
measurement of their results and the performance of the proposed algorithm tested against 
complex data.  
 
Timetable table design, delays, and infrastructure configuration play quite significant roles in 
the competitiveness of railway transportation. This is especially true on double-track busy 
lines like Kystbane, where timetable-parameters and run times are severely restricted by 
train meetings. These train crossings can also make the operation of the line more sensitive 
to disturbances and reveal further recurring delay patterns.  
 
The work presented on this report handles the topic of identification of delay patterns by 
using the most suitable clustering technique and evaluate it according to the data 
characteristics. Delay and delay change pattern from the last two years are presented and 
analyzed to contribute to the identification of delay causes. At the last part, the research is 
focusing to investigate how railway performance impact on passenger behavior and 
satisfaction. 
 
All these contributions together could help to enhance future railway operations in the 
country and thus increase the competitiveness of the railway even more.

1. INTRODUCTION  
 
 
Railway operation is a wide research area. Railway timetables are principally deterministic, 
but train operations depend on so many external and internal influencing factors that the 
actual running, arrival and departure times become stochastic. The simplest benchmarking 
approach for railway assessment is to compare a railway to its own past performance.  
 
Railway authorities and railway operators collect both punctuality data and data regarding 
disturbances. The overall target for the railway punctuality of passenger trains in Denmark is 
95%. For several years, there have been efforts across the railway undertakings to improve 
the punctuality especially in the busy part of the railway network. Improved understanding 
of the problems regarding punctuality and its causes, therefore, is essential.  
 
This research is indented to analyze the delay patterns in the north railway line of 
Copenhagen, Denmark. Inspired by previous research [1] where (Ceretto, 2018) used a big 
data technique, K-means, to classify delay patterns on the same railway line in Copenhagen.  
 
The Thesis explicitly shows how different clustering algorithms preform applied on new 
updated datasets. More precisely, three different clustering algorithms applied and 
evaluated with cluster quality measures and demanding, complex datasets. 
Affinity Propagation Algorithmtested and introduced as a new powerful clustering tool. 
 
Delay Handling is an important part of railway operation. Kystabane is a double-track line 
with mixed traffic, and it seems to have great disturbance sensitivity with high risk of delay 
propagation. A special objective is therefore to emphasize this fact and show how delay 
causes can be identified at specific stations. 
 
On the final part, previous studies that have been focused on the effect of disruptions on 
passenger travel behavior discussed.

2. RELATED WORK  
Railway networks are complex technical systems. The already constructed railway 
infrastructures isn’t easily to change and investments are very expensive. Therefore, future 
operation and demand requires extensive knowledge and analysis of the network.  
 
Railway traffic consists of passenger trains and freight trains. Normally passenger trains are 
divided into the following train services: 
 Long distance trains  
 Regional trains 
 Peak hour trains 
 Suburban lines  
 
Railway operation analysis which is a systematic examination of the performance of a railway 
network, can contribute to a better understanding of railway service behavior and ensure a 
robust railway system.  
 
Operational analysis is essential and relies on data collection, statistical methods or 
modeling related to various aspects of the railway system (including infrastructure, 
scheduling, maintenance, weather conditions, and passenger experience/delays). Critical 
aspects of the performance of a railway system are reliability, punctuality, and delays. 
Reliability and punctuality are important elements in the quality of service for both 
passengers and freight transport on railways. Punctual are the trains which adhere to their 
scheduled departure and arrival times while delays refer to any deviation from the 
scheduled timetable. Vital role plays the analysis of punctuality and the factors that 
contribute to delays. This includes identification of important disturbances and examination 
of the root causes of delays.  
 
 (Joborn,2022)[2] defined new concepts supporting root cause analysis for train delays and 
how different disturbances contribute to unpunctuality. This paper also introduced a new 
method that aimed to do the connection between disturbances and delays more explicit and 
give a more complementary view of reasons to unpunctual trains. Further, it highlighted the 
role of minor disruptions, those that are so small that they are very often neglected when 
analyzing causes of punctuality issues and indicated that disturbance late in the trips have a 
larger impact on punctuality than earlier disturbances. A kind of analysis which could be 
useful in timetable planning for increasing punctuality and help operational traffic control. 
 
 Tools for analyzing railway operation are essential and in great need since the modern 
railway systems have high complexity and scale. These systems involve numerous 
interconnected components and for managing such a complex system requires specialized 
tools that can process vast amount of data, identify patterns, and provide valuable insights 
to optimize performance. Highly important are tools that monitor train schedules and real-
time data identifying patterns of delays, punctuality issues and predict potential disruptions. 
Such a tools help railway authorities and operators stay agile, make data driven decisions 
and continuously improve their services to meet the evolving needs of passengers.

3. IDENTIFICATION OF RECURRENT DELAY PATTERNS USING BIG DATA 
TECHNIQUES 
 
By analyzing the delay profile of a train run along with the comparison of several delay 
profiles, it’s possible to identify clear recurrent delay patterns. These patterns can help to 
identify common causes of delay and can give insights to train operators to work towards 
enhancing the reliability of train services.  
 
The delay profile itself, is a powerful and very useful representation of operation and 
provides a detailed view of the train’s performance and any deviations or disruptions from 
the scheduled timetable. It refers to information about the delays experienced by a train at 
various stops along its path.  
 
In the context of operations, deviation refers to a time difference from the planned or 
scheduled route of a train. More specific, in this case deviation is reported as the departure 
from a scheduled event such as departure, arrival or a nonstop timing point and delays has 
be referred to negative deviations  
 
In this project, a delay profile of a train run is defined as the set of recorded deviations 
throughout its route or part of it, on a specific date.  
 
 
3.2 Clustering algorithms  
 
Cluster analysis, also known as clustering, is a data analysis-exploratory technique that might 
reveal naturally occurring groups or clusters within a dataset. It is an unsupervised machine 
learning method that seeks to group similar data objects based on their characteristics and 
doesn’t have to classify them into any predefined group [12]. In this case, clustering is used 
to partition the datasets of delay profiles and identify train systems that might share 
common causes of delays and potentially improve train scheduling.  
 
Selecting the best clustering algorithm for a particular problem can always be a challenging 
task. The choice of the right clustering algorithm depends on several factors including the 
goals of the analysis, the characteristics of the dataset and the specific requirements of the 
problem at hand. 
 
There isn’t a single clustering algorithm that can be universally considered as the best and 
most suitable since the performance of an algorithm depends on the specific problem and 
dataset characteristics. A well fitted clustering algorithm ideally should produce groups with 
non-overlapping distinct boundaries but in practice, a perfect separation cannot typically be 
achieved [13].

3.2.1 K-means Algorithm  
 
The K-means clustering Algorithm is considered one of the most simple and popular data 
mining algorithms in the research community and has been listed among the top 10 
clustering algorithms for data analysis. It’s an iterative clustering algorithm used to partition 
a dataset into k distinct clusters based on the similarity of data points. It’s a supervised 
machine learning algorithm and it aims to minimize the within-cluster variance and 
maximize the between-cluster variance [14]. 
 
However, despite its popularity, the algorithm has fundamental limitations, including 
inability to handle different data types and problems associated with random initialization of 
the centroids in priory which leads to unexpected convergence. [15] 
 
K-means, due to the fact that can be sensitive to the initial random centroid selection, its 
common practice either to run the algorithm multiple times with different random 
initializations and choose the best clustering solution based on domain knowledge or some 
evaluation criteria and techniques.  
 
The algorithm works as follows:  
 
1. Random initialization of k points, called means or cluster centroids.  
2. Assign each item to its closest mean based on a distant metric (typically Euclidian 
distance) and update of the mean’s coordinates which are the averages of the cluster 
so far.  
3. Repeat the process until convergence for a given number of iterations until the 
centroids no longer change significantly. 
4. When convergence is reached, the algorithm outputs the final clusters. [16] 
 
 
 
Overall, it is an efficient and simple algorithm, but it does have limitations.  
It’s worth to be mentioned that there are variations and improvements to the basic K-means 
algorithm such as handling various types of data, more sophisticates distance metrics and 
dealing with outliers. Additionally, there are some techniques that help in determination of 
the optimal value of k.  
 
The most used techniques for choosing the correct number of clusters are the Elbow method 
and the Silhouette analysis.

Elbow criterion  
 
Elbow method is the oldest, empirical method and is a graphical technique used to 
determine the optimal number of clusters in a dataset, in K-means clustering and is an 
unsupervised learning algorithm.  
To determine the k-value, k=1 to k=n is continuously iterated.  
For every value of k, the within-cluster sum of squares (WCSS) value is calculated. Note, that 
WCSS is defined as the sum of square distances between the centroids and each point.[17]  
The method is based on plotting the explained variation as function of the selected number 
of clusters and picking the elbow of the curve as the number of suitable clusters to use. 
 
 
 
Figure 1 Example of Elbow Criterion1 
 
 
Silhouette analysis  
 
The silhouette method is also a technique to find the optimal number of clusters and 
validation and interpretation of consistency within clusters of data. It evaluates the quality 
and strength of the clusters created using various clustering algorithms in terms of how well 
objects are clustered with other objects that are similar to each other. 
 
The silhouette analysis computes silhouette coefficients of each point that measure how 
much a point is similar to its own cluster (cohesion) compared to other clusters (separation). 
After the silhouette coefficient is computed for each object.

3.2.2 Affinity Propagation Algorithm 
 
Affinity Propagation Algorithm, which was published by Frey and Dueck in 2007, belongs to 
the category of exemplar-based clustering methods and is only getting more popular due to 
its performance, simplicity, and general applicability. 
 
The algorithm takes as impute measures of similarity between data points, at the same time 
considers all data points as potential exemplar and has as a goal to find a set of exemplars 
that represent the data points in the dataset. Exemplar is a data point that belongs in the 
dataset that is representative of itself and a group of similar data points. AP determines the 
number of clusters and does not require the number of the clusters to be specified 
beforehand compared to other algorithms. It can also handle high-dimensional data and is 
relatively robust to noise and outliers. [18] 
 
Affinity Propagation Algorithm works by iteratively exchanging messages between data 
points until a high-quality set of exemplars is found.  The message exchanged between data 
points represents the suitability of one data point being an exemplar for another data point. 
These messages are updated based on a similarity measure between data points and 
corresponding clusters gradually emerges. [17] 
 
 
The Affinity Propagation consists of the next main steps:  
 
1. Assign each data point a preference value, which represents the initial belief that the 
data point is an exemplar. Typically, preference values are set to the median of 
similarity values between data points. 
2. Iterate until convergence, where data points exchange two types of messages: 
responsibility and availability.  The responsibility of one data point for another 
measures how well- suited the first data is to be an exemplar for the second data 
point, considering the availability and the preferences of other exemplars. The 
availability of one data point measures how available it is to be chosen as an 
exemplar, considering the responsibilities from other data points. 
3. After convergence, data points with high availability values are chosen as exemplars. 
The remaining data points are assigned to their nearest exemplar.

3.2.3 DBSCAN Algorithm  
 
DBSCAN (Density-Based Spatial Clustering of Applications with Noise) is a density-based 
clustering algorithm commonly used for discovering clusters of arbitrary shapes in spatial 
data. The algorithm works on the assumption that clusters are dense regions in space 
separated by regions of lower density [19]. The algorithm does not require specifying the 
number of clusters in advance and it groups together data points that are densely 
connected, while identifying noise objects as outliers.  
 
DBSCAN algorithm uses two parameters:  
 
 Minimum Points(minPts): The minimum number of points required to clustered 
together for a region to be considered dense. 
 Epsilon(ε): A distance measure that will be used to place the points in the 
neighborhood of any point. [19] 
 
 
DBSCAN defines two key concepts: 
 
1. Density Reachability: A data point q is density-reachable from another point p if 
there is a chain of core points where each core point is a neighbor of the next, and q 
is within e distance of the last core point.  
 
2. Density Connectivity: two data points p and q are density-connected if there exists a 
core point c such that both p and q are density-reachable from c. 
 
 
Algorithm’s main steps  
 
 The algorithm proceeds by arbitrarily picking up a point in the dataset and expands 
the cluster by adding density-reachable point. 
 If there are at least ‘minPoint’ objects within a radius of ‘e’ to the point, then we 
consider all these points to be part of the same cluster.  
 The clusters are then expanded by recursively repeating the neighborhood 
calculation for each neighboring point.  
 Data points that are not assigned to any cluster and are not core points themselves 
are considered noise points or outliers.  
 
 
DBSCAN Algorithm will be presented only at section 4.3.2.2.

3.4 Cluster evaluation  
 
The evaluation of the quality of the generated partitions is one of the most important and 
vital issues in cluster analysis. It’s necessary to find a way to validate the quality of partitions 
otherwise it would be difficult to make use of different clustering results. There are two main 
categories of clustering validation, external clustering validation and internal clustering 
validation. The main difference between the two categories is whether external information 
is used for clustering validation or not.  
 
Indices usually used for measuring the quality of a partition and they are categorized into 
two classes, external and internal. Internal indices are based on information in strict to the 
data and evaluate the quality of a clustering structure without external information. Many 
time the correct partition is not available it’s possible to estimate the goodness of a partition 
measuring how well separated a cluster is from other clusters and how closely each instance 
is related to the cluster. On the other hand, external indices measure similarity between the 
output.   
 
Evaluation indicator 
 
In this research Calinski-Harabasz Index will be used as an internal cluster validation index 
Calinski-Harabasz (CH) Index introduced by Calinski and Harabasz in 1974 and can be used to 
evaluate the model when the ground truth labels are not known. Also know as Variance 
ratio criterion is a measure of how similar an object is to its own cluster (Cohesion) 
compared to other clusters (Separation) [20].

realized times of the trains at every timing point on the Kystbane and the deviation during 
the period from January to March 2022 for first the part of the analysis and the dataset

Helsingør. The records include information about the operation and about the timing points

Freight and material trains were discarded from the dataset and the initial data analysis was 
conducted using Excel and Python.

at each station. Each record shows the absolute delay profile or the delay change of a train

7. CAUSAL EXPLANATION  
 
Rail operations represent a complex stochastic system that is impacted by delays and 
disruptions from diverse factors like planned and unplanned upstream network delay, 
weather events, equipment/technical failure or construction and repair works. There has 
been limited previous work searching factors that contribute to consistent and repetitive 
delays and understanding the occurrence of these events in depth.   
 
Primary and secondary delays  
 
Delays which represent deviations between realized and scheduled times of events as 
mentioned before are usually categorized in two groups, as “primary” delays that originate 
unexpectedly and are the main and direct factor responsible for a specific disruption in the 
schedule and secondary delays are the indirect consequences of primary delays.[22] 
  
Secondary delays due to their nature and because compound the impact of primary delays 
have a more challenging recovery process. The distinguish between primary and secondary 
factors is an important and essential step when analyzing delays and delay patterns. 
Especially timely identification and resolution of primary causes is an important step to 
prevent the escalation of delays and reduce the ripple effects on the entire railway network. 
 
Trains can be delayed by either scheduled or unscheduled incidents as mentioned before. In 
this chapter, unscheduled delays will be discussed and more specific train congestion on 
certain stations in Kystbane.  
 
 From the previous parts of the analysis, it was observed that there are similarities on the 
delay patterns describing the absolute delay in the southbound direction in both datasets of 
2022 and 2023. Trains that are punctual and they’re coming from the north start increasing 
their delays with result to experience major delays entering the central station. Cluster 
number 3 from chapter 4.3.2.2 describe these trains which is also confirmed from cluster 
number 4 from chapter 4.3.2.2 as most of them belong to this cluster as well. Combining the 
graphs, we get:

The assumption could be that in that specific station trains that are sharing the same track 
experience delays which lead to a cascading effect of delays to other trains. Phenomenon 
also known as ‘train bunch-up’, where two or more subsequent trains that are scheduled to 
run at regular intervals end up running closing together, without having space to catch up 
the delay, causing further delays, increased dwell times at stations, and eventually decrease 
train punctuality.  
 
The aim of this chapter is the research of the potential causes, determine the source, and 
the identification of the primary factors that causing delays on these trains. 
 
Generally, in a well- organized railway system trains are scheduled to arrive and depart at 
specific time to ensure smooth operations and avoid congestion. However subsequent trains 
need to wait for another train to pass or clear a specific section of the track before 
proceeding. It’s important to note that some waiting is expected as part of a well-organized 
railway system, but excessive waiting times can cause major delays, reduce other trains 
punctuality, and finally inconvenience to passengers. [23] 
 
There are numerous approaches for causality identification with those who excel to capture 
dependencies of certain train-events (pass through, departure, arrival) which are known as  
event driven. Event driven approaches with a train event dependency structure are often 
based on either an equation system or a graph representation.

of the potential causes. Train diagram often is used to provide all these information. 
 
 
Train diagram  
 
The timetable is a schedule of the trains on a given rail infrastructure or specific line. It 
contains the arrival and departure time of the selected trains at each intermediate point of 
their route. The construction of the actual timetable has a complex nature, and still needs 
mainly a human planning process. Traditionally, planners use some kind of graphs as the 
main tools for constructing a timetable or analyze an already existing. They called time-space 
train diagram and graphically represents different train systems and the train movement that 
take place between the tracks. One axis displays time, and the other axis depicts space 
(stations). Each line in the train diagram depicts a train number which is usually indicated by 
the number next to the line. Time moves on while the location of a train remains unchanged 
and when a train dwells for some time at a specific station, this gives a vertical line at that 
station. The advantage of a diagram like this is that it makes much simpler and more intuitive 
to read a timetable and to detect conflict.[24]

8. IMPACT OF UNSCHEDULED DELAYS ON PASSENGERS’ BEHAVIOUR  
In the previous chapters, a big data analysis was conducted in order to demonstrate which is 
the most suitable algorithm to identify and categorize the different unscheduled delay 
patterns of a rail route. Consequently the Affinity Propagation Algorithm was applied on the 
Kystbane route as the most suitable option for this exercise. 
 
The scope of this chapter is to look further than the time delay patterns and focus on 
summarizing the behavior that passengers themselves tend to have when facing such delays. 
The following presentation is based on studies conducted on relevant papers which aimed to 
identify the major sources of dissatisfaction as perceived by the passengers as presented 
below.

Another piece of work relevant to the not so much into the passenger’s satisfaction when 
facing a train delay, but mostly the way they act on it, is the paper named “Passenger's 
Behavior in Response to an Unplanned Transit Disruption” [27]. The research here ends up in 
a model that aims to predict the reactions of passengers in the Chicago Metropolitan Area, 
in terms of how they decided to eventually travel to their final destination after their train or 
bus was delayed. As stated in the report: 
 
“In this survey, respondents were intercepted at about 100 separate stops/stations 
belonging to all major operators of transit service in the region including PACE, Metra, and 
CTA, and they were given a survey link and unique PIN to access to the questionnaire. In this 
approach each respondent was faced with four different disruption scenarios in which seven 
potential actions were listed including waiting for a back-up shuttle bus, asking for a ride 
from family/friend, picking up his/her own auto (if any), taking a taxi, using ride-sharing 
services, changing the trip destination, and canceling the trip. Each option, if applicable, was 
further described in terms of waiting time, travel time, arrival time, and cost. Accounting for 
heterogeneity across observations as well as panel effects, the random parameter 
multinomial logit (RPMNL) model is utilized to understand transit users’ decision behavior in 
case of an unplanned service disruption.”[27]

In a nutshell, all three papers above showcased the following: 
 
 
 
Train delays causes is a complicated topic with a strong diversity of contributing 
factors, spanning from network maintenance, weather, knock on effects deriving 
from random events, human and technical resources availability, and so on. 
 
Passenger satisfaction is mainly influenced by the actual time delay of a transport 
means, but that is certainly not the only factor. Provided that time delays can 
never  practically come down to zero, it would be worthwhile for the stations and 
train operators to also pay attention to the side factors that might contribute to the 
overall quality of their services.  
 
Similarly to  the first point, there is also a diversity of factors affecting the 
passengers’ follow-up reactions after experiencing a transport means delay, in order 
to arrive at their assumed destination. 
 
In overall, despite the first part of this Thesis is dealing with train time delays only, it is 
evident that despite this might be the most impactful factor on passengers’ satisfaction, it 
should be always considered under the spectrum of real life, which means that it cannot be 
considered as the only factor to be improved in order an all-around quality service to be 
provided by the rail network.

9. DISCUSSION   
 
 
9.1 Application of research  
 
The study conducted in this Thesis can become useful in terms of obtaining a better 
foundation of how the potential time delays in a particular train line can be predicted, 
clustered and form a clearer basis of understanding in order to be reduced or optimized. As 
shown, the Affinity Propagation Algorithm suggests a simpler, widely applicable and more 
accurate solution in order to analyze the expected delay patterns on a train line, compared 
to the other methods analyzed in chapter 3. Therefore, if used in such manner, it can 
become a powerful tool for railway companies such as Banedanmark and DSB, in order to 
increase their awareness of how, when and where the train delays happen and thus 
contribute in eliminating their occurrence. 
 
With regards to analyzing passengers satisfaction and follow up actions after dealing with a 
train delay, this paper provides a diverse basis of research, which touches upon the causes 
of delays, the commuters satisfaction with respect to different delays and side factors and a 
demonstration of a follow-up after-delay actions’ prediction algorithm.  
 
 
9.2 Limitation and future research  
 
 
Despite the analysis conducted in this Thesis might be proven useful, it has to be seen as 
one part of a bigger and more complex analysis to form a thorough foundation of improving 
the provided rail service.  
 
 
Specifically, as regards the Affinity Propagation Algorithm used here: 
 
 
It has to be furtherly proven by being applied in more lines with different 
characteristics, such as number of train passing, number and distances of stations, 
technical and maintenance status of the network, operational automation levels etc. 
 
It has to be analyzed and improved in combination with non-technical factors such as 
weather conditions, cultural background of the passengers, demographics, safety 
culture and so on.

 
If the algorithm is furtherly developed up to a reliable and efficient level, it can 
become really valuable for train operators who aim at performing analyses of this 
kind in order to improve their service’s punctuality, reliability and commuters’ trust. 
 
 
With reference to the delay causes, passengers satisfaction and behavior part of chapter 8,

10. CONCLUSION 
 
This Thesis aims to fill an important gap in delay pattern recognition with clustering 
techniques and how recurrent delays in Kystbane can be analyzed and explained.  
 
This study proposed a new algorithm to contribute to the delay pattern recognition. Affinity 
Propagation Algorithm is a readily extensible and innovative clustering algorithm that 
identifies exemplars successfully.  The analysis showed that it consistently identified more 
clear patterns and finds better solutions that standard exemplar- based algorithm such as k-
means. DBCSAN algorithm failed to create reasonable number of clusters in this case. 
 
Regarding to the delays, after the analysis in this study of selected clusters that represent 
similar patterns the last two consecutive years, was observed that trains running from 
Helsingør or Nivå towards south direction is very likely to experience delays at Østeport 
station waiting for Oresund trains to departure.  
 
Very often there are minor disruptions in the railway network, which are not taken into 
account, and they are often neglected when analyzing causes of punctuality issues. But it’s 
the same delays that are very likely to be causes of much greater ones or even the reason for 
disruptions that as explained can heavily impact passengers’ satisfaction.
