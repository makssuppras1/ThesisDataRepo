Abstract
This project focuses on dealing with Type 2 Diabetes through the use of smartphone
applications and visualisations. It will through a Lean UX approach, try to find the
answer to whether or not the use of photo-based visualisations will be more effective
in changing the lifestyle of T2 diabetics.
An experiment performed will also be explained, and the data derived from it will be
taken into analysis to try and understand whether the experiment can help to find
the answer.

1.1
Background
New reports from Denmark informs us of approximately 80% of all the patients, who
have been diagnosed with diabetes, suffer from type 2 diabetes(T2D)[7]. This means,
that out of the 260.750 people who have some type of diabetes, 208.000 are diagnosed
with T2D, while approximately 300.000 are considered to have the precursor to T2D,
and it is predicted that in the year 2030, 430.000 Danes will be diagnosed with T2D[7].
Even though the illness is not as bad as type 1 diabetes, or chronic for that case, it
still puts people on a setback when it comes to their everyday lifestyle. Traditionally
one would assume that only the elderly would fall victim to the illness, which is why
the Danes call it ”Old mans diabetes”, but due to the improved conditions of living
amongst the people in the west, younger generations also fall victim to the disease[7].
T2D is an illness, which prevents the body from producing energy as it normally would.
When food is digested into the body, the body breaks the food down to glucose. The
glucose is then taken into the blood, which then normally would be transported into
cells in the body. However, T2 diabetics cannot manage to use insulin in their body
as it normally would, and in worst cases the body does not produce enough insulin.
This will result to the glucose staying in the bloodstream and causes the body to have
a high level of blood sugar. Instead of giving the body energy, the patient instead
ends up feeling sick due to the high blood sugar[9]. The causes of T2D is mostly based
on inheritance, where a single parent who has been diagnosed with T2D will increase
the chance for his/her children by 40%[6]. However, an unhealthy lifestyle is also one
of the main causes for people to develop T2D, which is why - as previously mentioned
- the number of diagnoses from younger generations have started to increase. This is
also why 80% of T2D patients are categorised as overweight[6].

1 Introduction
Beyond normal medication, a healthy lifestyle can luckily change the patients con-
dition. This is why doctors and healthcare professionals recommend patients to eat
healthier and exercise as often as they can. Exercise gives the patients the advantage
of[10]:
• Increasing the effectiveness of the insulin
• Lowering the blood sugar
• Increases physical and mental condition
• Strengthening bones and muscles
While a healthy diet can help reducing blood pressure and blood sugar, by eating and
drinking in moderate amounts[12]. For help many healthcare professionals, doctors,
and the danish diabetes association all recommended for diabetics to make use of
certain apps and tools to help them stay healthy. One of the most used tools have
been the diabetes-diary[8], which many use so they can get advice from professionals
when it comes to following a diet. According to the danish diabetes association, others
make use of exercise apps, specifically the one called InterWalk[11], which can help
users performing interval walks when exercising.
1.2
Motivation
The motivation behind the project comes from acquaintances and family members
who suffer from both T1D and T2D. Since the problems revolving around diabetes is
predicted to increase drastically over the next couple of decades, the idea of helping
diabetics, with an easy and modern solution, motivates me. It will not only help
those who need it, but also motivate them towards a more healthy lifestyle.
1.3
Research Question
As previously mentioned, the idea of fighting T2D lies in both healthy meals, as well
as exercising. Patients also have the possibility of taking medication, which can do
exactly the same as the change of lifestyle. The amount of medication however is
based on the individual patients by their doctors.
The idea of the application is to provide value for the user through tracking cer-
tain data, which in this case will be revolving around the idea of a healthy lifestyle.
This will mean that the persons using the application will be tracking their exercises,
walks, or workout plans manually or through certain built in trackers in their smart-
phone.
The goal for the project can therefore be to create a tool, which can help people

who are diagnosed with T2D to log their daily exercises, and keep them motivated
through visualisations. The target group will be T2 diabetics.
The research question which this project will revolve around can best be summed
up thus:
”Will photo-based visualisations be more effective, compared
to visualisations which does not make use of photos, when it
comes to motivating T2D patients towards exercising more

To find the answer for this research question, I will focus on solving the following

”To which degree is it feasible to expect an improvement with the use

”What factors would be most important to illustrate for a patient with

”Will the use of images, improve the amount of exercising done, and

In the pursuit of the answers for these questions I will try to accomplish the following
sub-goals:
1. Search through scientific experiments, which have already been done with focus
on the same area, as well as already existing mobile applications.(Sub question
1)
2. Try to gather knowledge from experts on the matter, to get most out of the
upcoming design.(Sub question 2)
3. Design an application with the intention of motivating people towards a health-

4. Build the designed application with User Experience Engineering practices.(Sub
question 3)
5. Run tests on certain individuals to gather useful data.(Sub question 3)
6. Analyse the data gathered and use it to conclude the study and the chosen
question.(Sub question 3)

1.4
Market Validation
To be sure that there is a value in the solution for the problem, an early market
validation would be a good initiative to do at first. In lean user experience, which is
an approach I will be using through this project, validation of the market is part of
the process. This will also give an insight in the environment of the T2D patients.
To do this a survey was produced through the website SurveyMonkey. This website
allows users to create surveys, with different kinds of questions, and allow different
kinds of answers. Next, participants where needed for answers. I applied for mem-
bership in a group on Facebook known as ”Diabetes 2 - Danmark”, which is a group
for T2 diabetics in Denmark. The members of the group were asked to answer 10
questions in which they hopefully could relate to. 72 members answered the survey.
The questions were as following:
1. When were you diagnosed with T2D?
2. Was it difficult for you to adapt to a new type of lifestyle?
3. If you answered yes to the previous question, what made it difficult for you?
4. How often do you visit your doctor concerning T2D?
5. Has it always been like that after you got the disease?
6. How often did you exercise before you were diagnosed with T2D?
7. Has it changed since you were diagnosed with T2D?
8. If yes, to what extent has it changed?
9. Has you doctor recommended anything specific to make your everyday easier?
10. Do you feel that they have helped you in an effective way?
The first three questions were chosen as an introduction to survey. The participants
explain a bit about their personal life, and the difficulties which they had to face.
The next five questions revolved around the participants lifestyle and what they do
to fight the disease. The last two questions were chosen to confirm whether or not
the participants had any recommended tools to help them being motivated towards
exercising more often.
The results varied and the participants were almost divided into two groups due to
their answers. The introduction showed that approximately 53% of the participants
had a difficult start with the change of their lifestyle.
However going into detail,
most of them would say that they had most trouble adapting to the fact that their
eating-habits had to change, while only a few meant that exercising was a problem.

The next part of the survey would however focus on that specific topic, as well as
their visits to the doctor. Around 80% of the participants visited their doctors every
third or fourth month, but changes were made after some time apparently. When
asked about the amount of exercising done before the diagnose, 65% were exercising
only once a week or not exercising at all, while 25% exercised 2-3 days a week, and
the rest exercised 4-7 days a week.
around 60% of the participants who fell into
the group which did not exercise that much, also admitted that they did not make
a change in their lifestyle, while the rest of them did. When looking into the last
two questions, one half told that they had been recommended to eat healthy, while
the other half were told to exercise. However 50% admitted that they had problems
staying motivated when it came to exercising, but those who actually kept a high
activity level felt a positive change in their life.
It is clear now that there is a difference when it comes to patients diagnosed with
T2D. Since the doctors consult them differently and gives them different advice, there
must be a factor in which an application focuses on the patient and what they have
do to improve. It is also quite clear that a motivational factor is needed, since many
felt that they did not improve even though it was needed.

2.1
T2D solutions
There are many current solutions, recommended by doctors and health experts, which
are used today to help people manage both T1D and T2D as well. Since T1D is very
different compared to T2D, the tools used by T2 diabetics are not as advanced as the
tools used by T1 diabetics. The problem with T1D is that the insulin is not produced
and has to be added, while T2D solutions focus on controlling the intake of food and
exercising.
Balance Card
The balance card[5] is created for people who have recently been diagnosed with
T2D. It helps the patients to get an overview of their blood pressure, cholesterol, and
other important information which the patients needs to be aware of. It also reminds
them of how often they should visit their doctor to get specific numbers checked.

Diabetes diary[5]
The idea of the diabetes diary[8] is to give diabetics an overview of their every day,
when dealing with diabetes. The user is supposed to note things like their eating
habits, the exercise they do, what they drink and how often, and many other factors.
This will help them to get an overview of whether they are living as healthy as they
think, or if there is room for improvement. They decide themselves how often they
want to write down in their diary according to how much they want to improve, and
when visiting their doctor they can discuss where to improve. A draft of the dairy
can be seen in figure 2.3.

2.2
Related research
Throughout the last decade the evolution and the use of smartphones have taken
many turns, and the use of smartphones covers many areas today, especially when it
comes to health. However due to the paper being a research paper, it is important
to know what has been done scientifically in the field, so the research paper covers
something which has not been done before. Therefore this section will be about re-
search done in the field of smartphones and T2D.
Previous study[19] has shown that the use of self care applications have improved
the conditions of both T1 and T2 diabetics. The study aimed to identify popular
==PAGE:23==
2.2 Related research
11
diabetes applications, and at how great an effect they had on the participants. The
study was done over five months, where participants with both T1D and T2D in
different ages did a Summary of Diabetes Self-Care Activities Assessment[16][1] ques-
tionnaire. The questionnaire focuses on general diet, diabetes-specific diet, physical
activity, blood-glucose testing, foot care, and smoking. Through these measurements
in the questionnaire, a self-care score was calculated with the previously mentioned
variables. The most used applications was found through letting the participants list
the applications they made use of, and afterwards a two sample t-test was made to
check whether there was a statistical significant difference between the participants
who used applications and those who did not. The most used applications for both
types was the application ”mySugr”, which is a digital food diary. The participants
liked this application especially due to the ability to log blood glucose levels(BGL).
The results also showed that the participants using applications had a better score
when it came to blood glucose monitoring, general diet, and physical activity. Other
articles seems to back up the claim, when it comes to improving the motivation and
BGL through the use of telecommunication devices[1].
Another study similar to the aim of this project was the development of InterWalk[24].
This application came with the intentions of motivating T2D patients to exercise more
in the form of interval walks or runs. The article itself revolves around the imple-
mentation and idea of the application, and how it will benefit users who have T2D.
However further study has not been done on the application or its effect.

to design an application from which he would test on participants in several iterations.

was therefore not performed. He did however manage to explore the possibilities of
the market for the application he intended to create.
These two studies pave a way of possibilities from which this project aims to work

This chapter will explain to the reader what tests where done and what results where
derived from them.
The initial idea for testing whether the application would have an impact on the
diabetics, would be to let a group of diabetics try out a fitness application for a pe-
riod and then my application for another periods. The first period would be done
to try to get an overview of the effect which the regular fitness applications would
give, while the second period with my application would be done to see if there was
a difference in results. The results would be measured by the exercises done, and
the blood glucose levels. However due to the periods of time needed for registering a
difference, and the remaining time left to do the experiment, another approach had to
be taken instead. The second idea would then be to test whether the use of imagery
in the applications would motivate people towards exercising more.
5.1
Experiment design
The experiment would be set up and be conducted in a period of three weeks. The
reason why the period of time was chosen, was due to the time which was left of
the project, and the fact that test periods may need more time to really show some
results. It can be assumed that, if a user of a self-reflecting applications was to use
an application in real time, the results would not be shown after the first week, but
instead after a period of time. A qualitative test would also need more data in order
==PAGE:64==
52
5 Experiment
for it to show significant results, which means that the experiment might not show
any significant results. Components needed for the experiment would be a simple
smartphone, which would contain a pedometer and the possibility to download the
Samsung Health application.
The experiment would be performed with the use of four participants, who volun-
teered to the experiment. The participants were close friends, where some of them
were already users of self-tracking health applications. Most of them were users of the
Samsung Health app, primarily for step-tracking. The first part of the experiment
would be done for a week. The four participants would be asked to download the
Samsung Health app if it was not installed on their phone already. They would then
be asked to keep their phone on them all the time when being awake, so the phone
could track their steps. They would also be asked to log every exercise, which they
performed into the application. This would mean that if they went for a run, went to
the gym, or worked out at home, they should log the exercises into the application.
When a week would have passed, I would meet up with the participants and retrieve
the data they had logged. After that I would then introduce them to the next part
of the experiment.
The second part of the experiment would be done over the last two weeks of the
experimental period. The participants of the second part of the experiment, would
be the same four participants from the first one.
This would part be initiated a
week after the start of the first part of the experiment. Much like the first week,
the participants would be asked to use the developed prototype and use that for two
weeks. To do this, they would be sent an APK file, which they could use to install
the application. They were then be asked to keep their phone on them at all time,
when being awake. Now they would log all their exercises just like the first part of
the experiment, but it would now be required of them to take pictures for logging
their exercises as well. They would then fill out the rest of the data, but still keep a
step counting application installed and running, due to the step counter not working
properly on the developed application. They would then have an introduction to how
the application worked, so there would not be any problems when using it. When two
weeks would have passed, I would meet up with the participants and retrieve the data
they had logged. After this would be done, I would then send them a questionnaire
about their experience and personal opinions, and then initiate the analysis of the
gathered data.

Students T-Test
The data gathered from the experiments, which was put into the different tables in
the previous section, did not tell us very much and they came in such low quantities
due to the few participants and the time given. The bar charts shown in figure 5.1,
are very similar, both in form and in numbers. However that does not mean that
the probability for them being significant is equal to nothing. Therefore I will use
this section to perform a Student’s T-Test to figure out how significant the data is in
order to derive some conclusions to my research questions.

Figure 5.1: The average steps for each participant shown in a bar chart, with each of

The Student’s T-Test helps users to know how significant the differences between
two groups of data are. This means that the test will tell us if the differences hap-
pened due to chance or not. The type of test I will make use of is a paired test, where
the values gathered from the participants are matched with each other, so the results
from the first participants first week is matched with the results from his second week.
To prove the significance, a calculation of the p-value[27] would be needed to be done.

CHAPTER 6
Discussion
Throughout the last two chapters the prototype implementation and the tests showed
that there was room for improvement in the project. However since the period of the
project was running out, the most important focus points had to be established, and
the tests which could be done would have to be settled with for now.
6.1
Prototype
It is clear that the prototype did not live up to the design ideas which I initially
started out with. The design of the prototype was changed to such a degree, that it
would not be good enough for sending out to the public on application stores.
First point of improvement would be to change the use of databases. At the mo-
ment the SQLite database managed to establish a foundation to save the data for the
users. However since the SQLite databases are locally stored files, it limits the users
to having their data saved on one device. It also makes the idea of accessing the data
for the users complicated if they tend to switch to a newer phone. This would be
have to be changed for the app to be working properly.
Next, the data visualisations did not live up to the design expectations which were
planned to begin with. The data which could be illustrated through the current pro-
totype are not shown in a presentable way, which a user would see in professionally
developed apps. They are there and they give the user a the information that they
need, but this could also be noted in simpler apps. The design is therefore flawed
compared to professional apps, and needs improvement.
Then there is the flaws and missing details, which are not established in the prototype.
The tab containing weekly data was never established, which is just as important as
other data illustrations, according to the participants of the usability testings. The
step counter needs to work optimally and save data at a given timestamp, and reset
once the day is over. The monthly data visualisation needs the tags representing the
steps taken during the days, and there needs to be implemented a sorting function
which finds the exercises on the given day. The visualisation of the BGL progress
could also be improved to work more dynamically, since it does not at the moment
run properly.
Lastly improving the UX as well as the feel of the app to make it flow and look in
a more beautiful way might increase the users motivation towards improving their
==PAGE:76==
64
6 Discussion
lifestyle, and thereby the usage of the app if it was available in public.
If all of these points are implemented in the prototype at some point, it could live up
to finding its way into an app store.
6.2
Testing
As explained in the beginning of the last chapter, the initial idea would have been to
let T2 diabetics make use of the application and the prototype, and then have them
see whether the use of photo-based visualisations would improve their BGL. However,
after it was made clear from the interview that it takes some time to see the changes,
as well as the possibilities of sickness affecting the numbers, I had to make some
changes in my plans. As I ended up spending more time at some point which was
not planned, the time needed to use this approach was not at my hands.
The Student’s T-Test which I applied in the last chapter, proved that it would be
difficult to conclude whether the research question could be answered with a well
documented answer. I did at the beginning fear that the results would not be that
significant due to the low amount of data. A Student’s T-Test which proves a signif-
icance between two groups of data, would have a p-value of 0.05 or less, and since it
was not the case I could not conclude that the difference was not due to chance. The
only case where it was close to being proved was when doing the test with the results
from week 2 and week 3.
Since this was expected from start I had to come up with a plan to nullify the idea
that photo-based visualisations would help people with exercising. That was why the
questionnaire was performed as well. Here the three opinions on the matter came to
my assistance, since their opinion on the matter was that there was an improvement
when it came to exercising. Since three out four participants personally had the opin-
ion that they found the usage of images more motivating, the calculations from the
test could be ”nullified”, which meant that there was still potential in the idea itself.

CHAPTER 7
Conclusion
If we take a look at the research question once again:
”Will photo-based visualisations be more effective, com-
pared to visualisations which does not make use of photos,
when it comes to motivating T2D patients towards exercising
more frequently?”
The research in literature done in the beginning of the project period showed that
many research papers had been done, where the writers had concluded that the use of
applications had made improvement amongst the participants of their tests. The first
sub question could therefore be confirmed. The second sub question was answered
through our validation which was the interview with Christian Kjær, as well as the
last participants of our design tests. Christian told us about the fact that we should
stick to the most important data, and not implement any unnecessary ”trash data”,
which led us to remove all the unnecessary factors which was implemented to begin
with. The participants responded positively towards the design and choices of data
shown in the prototype, which confirmed that the chosen data was sufficient and
useful factors in such an app.
Lastly I tried to gather some results, which could finalise the last sub question. How-
ever, due to the lack of data, the high p-value from the Student’s T-Test, and the
results from the experiment, it was clear that no conclusion could be derived from
that answer. However the questionnaire proved that there was still potential for a
positive outcome in the future, which means that I cannot conclude that photo-based
visualisations would prove to be more effective than non photo-based ones, but I can
conclude that with further research it could prove to be so.

Future Work
8.1
Finishing the prototype
I really believe that if the prototype was finalised it would work more efficiently
amongst its users. A personal opinion I have is that the more negatives I can find
within an application or a software, the less time I want to spend using it. The more
professional it looks and feels, the easier the chance is of continue using it.
As mentioned in chapter 6, the design has be improved towards the initial idea that
was found towards the end of the usability testings. Beyond that the missing the
factors like databases and storing data efficiently, the initial ideas for how to visu-
alise the data, and creating the flow and look of the app, should be implemented in
a better way.
8.2
Finding the answers
Since the research question relied a lot on the results of the tests, the missing factors
of tests would be a good idea to focus on finishing. As the initial idea was to do the
test with T2 diabetics, as previously mentioned, this should be the main focus for
future works. I have dived into a lot of other factors to support some of the first sub
questions of the research question, which will not be that necessary to spend time on
in the future. If the time is given for future works the first part of the experiment
should be initiated already from start, since there is nothing to prevent you from doing
it. The use of the Samsung Health app could have been done from the beginning of
the project period, which could have given me more time towards the end. However,
it would also need an extreme focus on the first parts of the project, which I was
not ready for at the time. The participants needed should also be found in a higher
quantity, to improve the chances when it comes to working on the data.
