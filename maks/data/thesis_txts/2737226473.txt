1. Abstract 
Currently, the marime sector faces the need to reduce the diﬀerent types of emissions derived 
from its operaons. In this context, the Internaonal Marime Organizaon establishes through 
the Internaonal Convenon for the Prevenon of Polluon from Ships a series of maximum 
values for both greenhouse and other harmful emissions for the environment and health, being 
these limits applicable to all types of vessels within the sector. As part of this framework, 
parcular companies in the sector also set their own environmental impact reducon targets 
that go beyond those set by the Internaonal Marime Organisaon. In this work, the 
introducon of a new Wind Turbine Installaon Vessel design as an asset within the marine 
industry and speciﬁcally within the supply service of the oﬀshore wind industry is taken as a case 
study. Therefore, the main objecve of this project is to ensure compliance with the emission 
regulaons in force in the sector and with the private organisaonal objecves of the operator 
of this new vessel, which is speciﬁcally focused on reducon of CO2 emissions, while opmising 
the associated operaonal and installaon costs of the decarbonisaon technologies to be 
installed for this purpose. 
To achieve this objecve, a socio-economic analysis, parcularly a cost-eﬀecveness analysis, of 
the main alternave fuels on the market is carried out to consider the social cost caused by all 
exisng emissions in the menoned regulaon. Through this assessment, it is observed that 
ammonia fuel cells present the best rao of CO2 emission reducon with respect to cost 
[$/tnCO2] if the annual average of turbines to be installed during the lifeme of the vessel is 
higher than 36. If this average is lower, green methanol engines would become the most cost-
eﬀecve alternave. ANer this, a series of mathemacal opmisaon models are developed, 
which separately simulate the hybridisaon of convenonal diesel engines with the two 
decarbonisaon alternaves menoned during a complete wind turbine installaon. As a result 
of these models, opmal workloads are obtained for each of the convenonal engines and 
alternave fuel-based power units in each of the phases of the installaon, as well as the opmal 
sizing of the capacity installed of each type of power generaon unit and the total cost of the 
energy generaon. Furthermore, these mathemacal models include the possibility of adding 
electric baFeries to act as buﬀers for the energy demand. However, it is demonstrated that the 
use of this technology in combinaon with convenonal engines and ammonia fuel cells or green 
methanol engines is not opmal from an economic point of view when it comes to reducing the 
environmental impact of this operaon. 
Finally, the uncertainty derived from the changing weather condions during the installaon of 
oﬀshore wind turbines is also analysed using the example of the locaon of the ﬁrst project that 
this vessel will carry out. This analysis shows the low sensivity of the total cost of energy 
generaon to large increases in the installaon duraon, and its suﬃcient capacity for 
decarbonisaon under these circumstances, due to the fact that its sizing is based on situaons 
of greater power intensity and, therefore, greater carbon intensity. 
With all of the above, this project not only idenﬁes and implements the current best alternave 
fuel for emission reducon in wind farm installaon operaons, but also establishes a 
methodology that can be used in the future as the diﬀerent market technologies evolve.

2. Introducon 
2.1. Background 
The marime industry is a fundamental component of the global economy. Speciﬁcally, marime 
transport accounts for 80-90% of the internaonal trade. Due mainly to this fact, the shipping 
industry is responsible every year for 1.1 Gt of CO2 emissions, 2.3 Mt of SOx emissions and 3.2 
Mt of NOx emissions [1], represenng respecvely 2.8%, 13% and 15% of the global emissions 
of these types [2]. Furthermore, these amounts are predicted to increase due to an esmated 
growth in marime transport of 3% per year during the rest of the 2020s [3]. 
In view of this high environmental impact, the internaonal reference enty in the sector, the 
IMO, has introduced restricve regulaons in recent years concerning energy eﬃciency 
according to the speciﬁc characteriscs of each type of ship in order to limit carbon emissions, 
as well as speciﬁc limits for non-CO2 emissions depending on the characteriscs of the engines 
and fuels used. In addion, the IMO has set the crucial target of reducing carbon emissions from 
shipping by 40% by 2030 compared to the 2008 baseline [4]. 
On the other hand, researchers in this ﬁeld have focused mainly on emission type quanﬁcaon 
and analysis, on the development of new technologies with low emission levels and on the 
analysis of speciﬁc locaons around the world with a high concentraon of emissions [5]. 
Looking at the diﬀerent technologies adopted by companies in the sector, it can be highlighted 
that there are a number of diﬀerent approaches to address this problem. The suitability of one 
technological alternave or another will depend mainly on the characteriscs of the ship and 
the operaon to be carried out, as this will determine the technical and economic viability of the 
soluon in queson. 
2.2. Movaon

companies involved are looking for soluons to reduce the installaon costs of oﬀshore wind 
turbines and thus reduce the development cost of these projects. Recently, a new concept for

3. State of the art 
In recent years, the challenge of decarbonizing the marime industry has led to research into 
possible opons to address it. Currently, various decarbonisaon technologies as well as 
alternave fuels are being developed on a large scale. The world's ﬁrst hydrogen-powered cargo 
ship operated by Heidelberg Cement and Felleskjøpet [8] and the world's ﬁrst green ammonia-
fuelled tanker designed by Wärtsilä and Grieg [9] are two examples of some of the ﬁrst steps 
taken in this direcon. 
However, the variety of technological alternaves to achieve the industry's objecves is 
remarkable, and it is evident that there is no single soluon for all ship types [2]. Therefore, a 
review of the diﬀerent alternaves proposed in the literature is presented in the following in 
order to provide the reader with an insight into the current available opons on the market. 
3.1. Technological soluons on the market 
This subsecon provides a review of the alternaves based on propulsion and on-board energy 
generaon. Soluons based on energy savings and eﬃciency are therefore not analysed, since, 
as regulaon and organizaonal objecves become more restricve in the future, these 
technologies will no longer be suﬃcient to address the problem of decarbonizaon. In this way, 
the most relevant characteriscs of the alternave fuels proposed by other researchers and 
companies in this ﬁeld are presented, as well as their advantages and disadvantages: 
• 
Biodiesel: 
Currently, several large engine manufacturing companies are invesgang the 
implementaon of this type of fuel in their engines. Companies such as Caterpillar, 
Cummins, MAN and Hyundai oﬀer engines on the market that can blend biodiesel and 
tradional marine fuels in certain proporons up to 100% biodiesel [10]. Hence, this 
makes it an interesng alternave, as it can be used in many of the engines currently 
oﬀered by the manufacturers in the industry. In addion, the proporon of biodiesel to 
be used can be selected prior to the operaon in order to reduce a certain percentage 
and type of emissions, especially CO2. One of the most common pracces is to blend it 
in fuel tanks together with marine diesel oil (MDO) or marine gas oil (MGO) [11]. The 
two most common types of biodiesel used on the market are Hydrotreated Vegetable 
Oil (HVO) and FaFy Acid Methyl Ester (FAME). 
• 
LNG: 
Being a liqueﬁed gas, this fuel occupies 1/600th of the volume it would take up in a fully 
gaseous state, which makes its storage on board very eﬃcient. It allows to reduce NOx, 
SOx and PM emissions specially. However, the reducon of CO2 emissions is not as 
signiﬁcant as the previous ones, which is one of its major disadvantages, as this type of 
greenhouse emission is one of the main focuses when it comes to reduce the 
environmental impact of the industry [12]. On the other hand, LNG has a low caloriﬁc 
value of 50 MJ/kg [13], higher than that of convenonal diesel (43 MJ/kg) and biodiesel 
(39-41 MJ/kg) [14], which indicates that a greater amount of energy can be extracted 
from it for the same amount of mass. Moreover, this fuel has a large market presence at 
the moment, with a total of 300 ships of this type already in operaon or on order in 
2019 [15]. 
• 
Methanol:

with convenonal fuels through the use of dual-fuel engines [17]. Methanol combuson

• 
Hydrogen: 
This fuel certainly has the best environmental characteriscs of all the alternaves 
analysed. This is because it is a carbon-free alternave, which only has NOx emissions in 
the case of being implemented in an ICE. Currently, most hydrogen producon is based 
on fossil energy carriers, mostly natural gas. However, there are other types of 
producon based on electrolysis using electrical energy from renewable sources and 
also based on biomass fermentaon and thermomechanical biomass conversion. In

disadvantage is its low volumetric energy density, which means that it requires 
signiﬁcantly more storage volume than convenonal fuels. 
• 
Ammonia: 
As in the case of hydrogen, this fuel can be applied in both ICEs and fuel cells. Moreover, 
it can be stored on board at a signiﬁcantly lower pressure and higher temperature than 
hydrogen and LNG. Combuson within an ICE produces only NOx and CO2 emissions and 
in the case of applicaon in a fuel cell its emissions are close to zero as in the case of 
hydrogen. In case of applicaon in fuel cells, the same set-up would be used as in the 
hydrogen scenario but with an upstream stage to transform ammonia into hydrogen [19] 
[20]. 
• 
Electric ba&eries: 
Technological alternave with zero emissions during operaon and high energy 
eﬃciency. Its main drawback is the high CAPEX and the reduced autonomy of this 
oﬀshore energy storage system. For this reason, it is currently only used for short sailing 
distances. In many cases, electric vessels have convenonal back-up engines, so that the 
vessel only operates in 100% electric mode at certain moments, such as when 
manoeuvring in port or during stand-by operaons. These systems can also be used to

5. Methodology 
In order to answer the research quesons set out in the movaon for this work, the speciﬁc 
methodology to be applied to this end is presented below. 
Observing the research quesons exposed and considering the context in which this project is 
developed, it is easy to idenfy that the two fundamental components to consider are the 
environmental and the economic impact of the operaons under study. In other words, the 
decision must be taken on how to make a private investment but examining the environmental 
impact of the exhaust gases generated by this technology to be selected. The applicaon of a 
socio-economic analysis can allow to have into account the social aspect within the economic 
costs of the process in queson. A socio-economic analysis is a tool used mainly in polical 
decision processes which assesses all the beneﬁts and disadvantages of making an investment 
with the aim of maximising the social welfare [56]. Although mostly used in the public sector, 
this methodology can also be used in the private sector to idenfy the social impacts of these 
investments. It could also be considered to use a private-economic analysis, however, the socio-
economic analysis goes a step further than the private-economic, taking into account not only 
the direct monetary impact of each alternave but also the social consequences of the same. In 
other words, the socio-economic value is somewhat intangible and may include eﬀects diﬃcult 
to measure in monetary terms, unlike private-economic, which only takes into account monetary 
and tangible impacts. [57] 
It is important to note at this point that in the exisng literature the term cost-beneﬁt analysis is 
commonly used as an equivalent to private-economic analysis or socio-economic analysis. 
Furthermore, the term cost-beneﬁt analysis is also used as a common term for the three diﬀerent 
types of a socio-economic analysis: cost-beneﬁt analysis, cost-eﬀecveness analysis and cost-
eﬀect analysis. However, for the sake of clarity in this work, the term cost-beneﬁt analysis will be 
used as a speciﬁc type of socio-economic analysis. [58] 
Within the three types of socio-economic analysis outlined in the previous paragraph, “cost-
beneﬁt analysis and cost-eﬀecveness analysis, as speciﬁc methods of analysis, allow measures 
to be uniformly classiﬁed according to their economic cost-eﬀecveness” [58]. However, as 
pointed out before, economic cost-eﬀecveness is not necessarily the only relevant 
consideraon for decision-makers. The analysis prior to an investment decision should also seek 
to include all consequences of the investment, including non-monetary impacts on society. If 
these impacts fall on third pares, they are called externalies, so the environmental impact of 
the emissions to be consider in this project belong to this category. 
Unlike cost-beneﬁt analysis, the cost-eﬀecveness analysis “involves the ranking of measures on 
the basis of costs, in order to idenfy the measure that will realize a given objecve at the 
minimum cost” [58]. This characterisc makes this method ﬁt with the established objecve 
menoned in this secon of achieving a certain level of emissions reducon by the MSS ﬂeet. In 
this way, the cost-eﬀecveness of the alternaves in reaching the speciﬁc MSS emission target 
will be analysed. 
The other two types of socio-economic analysis are discarded because they are both parally 
based on the beneﬁts derived from the alternaves considered. Since it is not going to be 
considered any beneﬁt derived from the wind turbine installaon process, these two methods 
would not be suitable for the established purpose.

Currently, there are diﬀerent kinds of fuel cells that can be applied to the marime industry. The 
main types include: Proton Exchange membrane fuel cells (PEMFC), Alkaline Fuel Cells (AFC), 
Phosphoric Acid Fuel Cells (PAFC), Molten Carbonate Fuel Cells (MCFC) and Solid Oxide Fuel Cells

literature references only these two types are analysed for the applicaon in shipping [19] [87]

can vary according to demand [19]. This is why the PEMFC typology is adopted, which must 
always have hydrogen as an input. For this reason, in the case of the ammonia (FC) alternave, 
a cracker system must also be installed to transform the input ammonia into hydrogen. This 
choice explains why the same lifeme has been adopted for both the ammonia (FC) alternave 
and the hydrogen (FC) alternave in the calculaons carried out. However, the cracker 
equipment is esmated to have a lifeme of more than 20 years [89]. Therefore, for calculaon

system will be limited by the lifeme of the WTIV and tugs.

As explained in the methodology, the calculaon of me windows through the alpha-factor

gust wind speed data must be used. The gust wind speed is the highest instantaneous wind

the maximum wind speeds over a period of me [105]. Therefore, gust wind speed must be the 
data used when studying the eﬀects of wind on structures, as this will take into account the

10. Future work 
Having answered the research quesons of this project, future developments of the same are 
proposed below. The funcon of these proposals is the analysis of possible future scenarios

• 
Taking into account that the current WTIV design is prepared for the installaon of 12-

mathemacal opmisaon and the uncertainty analysis would be integrated in the same

Development of a study case in the European market. This work has been focused on
