Abstract
This study examines the construction of patient pathways for the emergency departments in Region Sjælland. In
the health sector more patients are hospitalized, but waiting times occurs while waiting for a nurse or physician,
or while next step is discussed between the physicians. A lot of money are invested in the health sector and to
reduce waiting times in the emergency departments is one of the main points. One way to reduce the waiting
time and save the nurses and physicians the next step discussions are by making patient pathways, which
each contains procedures that are pre-ordered if the pathway is chosen based on the symptoms. These patient
pathways is already a concept used in other parts of the country, however those pathways were constructed by
gathering a big group of specialists, which is not the case in this study.
To construct the patient pathways in Region Sjælland a mathematical model has been formulated. This model
constructs the pathways by consider mismatched procedures, between the patients and the pathways, which has
to be minimized. Due to technical problems it was not possible to run the model on a complete data set, only on
a smaller random sampled set. But still due to large solution times column generation and cut and solve were
applied.
The results show that it is possible to construct patient pathways on the smaller sampled set and that it also
is possible to use these pathways for a big set. Though considering the solution times it was not possible to
make any improvements. When the column generation was applied the solution times were only increased,
even though dual stabilization also were applied, and by using cut and solve the solution time was not better
nor worse. To make any improvements in the future other methods has to be applied to solve the model to get
solution times, and be able to solve a complete set for the considered departments. Furthermore identify the
ﬁnal patient pathways, where symptoms and diseases also are indicate is a future goal.

Introduction
The emergency departments are very important departments within the hospitals. It is the one place where
patients are admitted if they have been involved in an accident, having a physical injury or if they require any
kind of care immediately. The reason why the emergency departments are very important is because all the
acute and special functions are gathered in one place, where the routine and quality are some of the best. It is
also in the emergency departments where injured persons can get a treatment from specialized doctor of a high
quality no matter what time in the day it is [1, 2].
This project is made in collaboration with DTU Management and the emergency departments in Region Sjæl-
land. In the year of 2015 around 14.4 million treatments and visits has been made to the emergency department
and 1.9 millions of those has been acute. In the same year in the Region Sjælland there have been 122.349 per-
sons obtaining an urgent ambulant treatment out of 1.931.523 persons who has contacted the hospital regarding
ambulant treatments, corresponding to 6.3%. By an ambulant treatment it is meant by patients which are regis-
tered in a department but does not occupy bed spaces. On the other hand there were 136.057 persons who got
urgent hospitalized out of the 196.965 persons who were hospitalized in 2015, corresponding to 69.1% of the
persons [1]. In Figure 1 it is seen that from 2012 to 2015 the number of ambulant treatments has decreased, as
well has it in the number of urgent hospitalized patients, though in 2015 there have been a decreasing of 7,000
patients in Denmark.

Not only to reduce the waiting time, but for the whole health sector there has been invested around 47 billion

million Danish krones in 2017 is dedicated for health [10].
In Region Sjælland seeks to decrease the patient waiting time. One way to do this is to construct emergency
packages, like in Region Syddanmark, though at the moment there is no knowledge of how these packages
should be deﬁned. Throughout this Master Thesis these emergency packages, refereed to as "Patient Pathways",
will be constructed using integer programming. The patient pathways are constructed based on data from
emergency departments in Region Sjælland, where patients who has been through the similar procedures will
construct a cluster and therefore a pathway. How many pathways that are needed for the whole Region, or each
department, is also a question that is considered.

2.1
Clinical pathways
Making and using patient pathways, or clinical pathways as it also is called, has already been made. But these
pathways haven’t been constructed in a mathematical way.
2.1.1
Construction of clinical pathways
Back in 2003 Panella et al., [12] made an experiment of making clinical pathways for ﬁve different diagno-
sis’s and conditions in different Italian health care organization. To develop these clinical pathways, teams of
all kinds of physicians, nurses, social workers and administrators were gathered. They were gathered as they
are responsible for patients and have the knowledge of how to manage the diseases. This experiment was run
through out a year, as a six months preperiod and six months postperiod of the implementation, and the indi-
cators of how well the clinical pathways was based on the length of the stay and other processes and outcome
indicators. After implementing these clinical pathways the results shows that three of the ﬁve diagnosis’s and
conditions have had an improvement. These improvements are seen as the length of the stay has decreased, the
processes has been improved and there has been a cost reduction. For the last two diagnosis’s and conditions the
use of clinical pathways were interrupted, this was caused that the doctors didn’t want to follow the pathway.
The reason why the doctors refused to use the pathways was because they felt that the pathway was a "cook-
book", and they believe that the patients suffering from that speciﬁc condition or diagnose can not be treated
the same, dependent on the patient. Another reason was also that some doctors had problems with adapting the
clinical pathway, either by refusing to change their routines or not ﬁlling the documentation properly.
Later in 2009 in the Region of Southern Denmark, [7, 8] the concept of using the so called emergency packages,
similar to the clinical pathways, was considered. In 2012 the emergency packages where constructed. These
packages were alike Panella et al. constructed by physicians, nurses and administrators, who constructed 38
different packages. In connection with implementing the emergency packages, the Region of southern Denmark
also constructed the system that for each of the patients which arrives to the emergency department, they will
be inspected within the ﬁrst half an hour and there must be a diagnosis of this patient within the ﬁrst 4 hours.
After all of this was implemented it was observed that the waiting time was decreased and that the patients stay
in the emergency department was decreased as well.
2.1.2
Impact of clinical pathways
Overall it is seen that the emergency department will improve by implementing the patient/clinical pathways,
both in case of waiting times and treatment time. But there must be an awareness about these packages, such
that they don’t get to general and cookbook alike. When constructing the pathways through the mathematical
model, the pathways will contain certain procedures. This will make the pathways be cookbook alike, but it
must be noticed that the pathways are the underlying basic for the decisions and pre-ordering of the procedures,
not a ﬁxed way of treat patients.

2.3
Clustering using mixed integer programming models
As it is noticed many different methods and algorithms exist to perform clustering. Using mixed integer pro-
gramming (MIP) for clustering is also possible. Three different studies are here presented.
Prodel et al., [14] investigated in 2015 if it was possible to construct clinical pathways, and to see if they reveal
any pathways/clusters within the individual diagnosis’s and conditions. The data which were used was based
on patients suffering from Cardiovascular diseases and had implantable deﬁbrillators in France. This data was
sampled over seven years, which includes a before and after period of the implementation. By doing that
made it possible to investigate if any correlation between these periods occurred. With the use of data mining
and process mining techniques they develop a mathematical model to ﬁnd the best process model representing
the processes. Process model is a graphical representation, where nodes, represent event classes, and edges,
represent the transitions, where graph reductions are applied. When preforming the model, clusters will appear
by combining more of the diagnosis, if they are from the same medical specialty, this could be circulation,
respiratory, ect. The result which Prodel et al. reaches are the most important paths of cardiac problems, from
heart failure until device implementations, surgery, adjustments and possible new heart failures are kept, and
the obtained graph gives a description of how the diseases can evolve and obtain new information about them.
Another way of clustering using MIP is within biology. Qiu et al., [15] have studied biological paths of DNA
and genes, such that the pathways of the DNA’s and proteins of different genes will be found using MIP. For
this study the used data are the interactions in a yeast molecular and protein-protein interactions within human
breast cancer. The model which Qiu et al. uses is a graph and network model to identify the interactions as
subgraphs, with a max number of nodes. The problem is a maximum weighted connected graph problem, where
the nodes are the genes and the edges the interactions. By applying this model to the problem it is possible to see
that some pathways are found. As they in this article are comparing simulated annealing with the mathematical
model, it is explored that the mathematical model gives a better result on the yeast molecule as well for the
human breast cancer.
The last study presented is clustering used to ﬁnd the customer segmentation from a Turkish satellite broad-
casting company. Saglam et al., [16] has made this approach to improve the strategies and market shares of
this satellite broadcasting company, Digiturk, in 2006. Digiturk has 800,000 customers and offers ﬁve product
packages, three pay-per-view services, and other channels and events, and wants to examine if it would be
possible to make one-to-one marketing. To examine this the company wants to divide the customers in groups
based on the transactional factors and a mathematical model. This model is a MIP model where the sum of
the euclidean distances, within the clusters, will be minimized and the objective function will minimize the
maximum distance of the cluster. Here the number of clusters are known a priori. It appears that the model is
very hard and demanding, thus a heuristic is made to improve the solving time and one to improve the clusters.
When the model with the heuristic is applied on the data, clusters are appearing with very few mismatches. This
shows that the model is working and has been improved by the heuristics and the customers can be divided into
groups.
These three studies shows different ways of clustering using MIP models, the ﬁrst two by the use of graph
theory and the last just by a MIP model. As the problems differs a lot and it is not considered to use any of
these approaches, since the methods still will be hard to apply. The graph theoretical method is not evident for
this study even though the ﬁrst study also construct clinical pathways and the second study is very suitable for
this approach. The Turkish television model would, on the other hand, be very close to the model considered in

2.4
Difference between the literature and this project
It has been observed that identifying patient pathways can be carried out in many different ways. Not only
patient pathway are carried out in different way but also generally clustering. That could be by gathering many
persons within the hospital sector, as it was done by Panella et al. [12] and Region of Southern Denmark [7, 8]
with the aim of make an improvement of ﬁnding a diagnosis, treatment or the time in the emergency department.
As this study aims to ﬁnd a mathematical model to construct patient pathways, gathering persons to construct
the pathways will not be an option, but the knowledge of patient pathways that can make improvements in the
emergency department will be brought along.
In Xu and Wunsch [13] many other methods are presented as well, some which might be interesting, but in
this study the main focus is to construct a mathematical operational research model to make patient pathways.
Though this will be carried out without the use of graph theory.
Another way of constructing pathways is through graph theory as both Prodel et al. [14] and Qiu et al. [15]
engage them selves with. The main of these are to construct some paths within each of their problems, but their
problems differ a lot from each other. As Prodel et al. desire to map pathways of a single disease/condition to
predict the total course and predict the paths, Qiu et al. wish to image and map interactions of genes in yeast
molecules and human breast cancer, which are found by subgraphs. For these two cases the use of graph theory
is a good idea and is useful, but for the studied problem there will be hard time using it and is not evident.
Yet using just a mathematical model, without any graph theory, to make patient pathways is also possible.

Problem description
When a patient is entering or referred to the emergency department by a physician, this patient will be registered.
The registration is made in the system of the emergency department of the speciﬁc hospital and when the patient
has been registered with their CPR number, an identiﬁcation number is then assigned to the patient. For every
procedure or treatment the patient has been treated with will be registered under the id number, which the
patient has been given. These treatments could be a blood test or a x-ray, based on the symptoms of the patient.
However to get this treatment, the patient has to wait for their turn which can take time.
Prior to deciding which kind of treatment the patient is assigned to, the physicians will have meetings where
it is discussed what the next treatment or step is. This is time-consuming both for the doctors, who probably
are busy, may be occupied with a surgery or a visitation, and for the patient who has to wait for their turn. To
reduce the waiting/decision time a mathematical model will be constructed, with the objective of the number of
mismatches which occurs when the patient pathways has been identiﬁed.
A cluster is deﬁned as "a number of similar things that occurs together" [11]. In this study clusters are rep-
resented of a group of patients who has been through procedures which are similar. To see how similar the
patients are with the cluster, can be measured by similarities. The clusters gives rise to pathways and the bigger
the clusters are the more it will be considered a pathway. If the clusters are small it will represent one or few
patient who has one special kind of treatment, but there are not enough patients in the cluster to create a path-
way. It is not known how many clusters are needed, therefore this will have to be considered while the model is
formulated. The number of big clusters will indicate how many pathways there will be needed. The number of
pathways can differ from hospital to hospital and in total for the whole region. It has to be evaluated whether
an emergency package must be created or not when only one patient would have the use of it or it should be
combined with other pathway.
The data which is being used for this project, has been provided by the Region Sjælland. This data contains
registrations from emergency departments within four considered departments in the Region, containing infor-
mation about procedure, hospitalization times and days and many more attributes. The symptoms of the patients
on the other hand is not registered in the system, and this could make it hard to check whether the pathways
found are reasonable.
3.1
Notation
Just a bit of notation, which is used in the next section, are presented here.
Procedure:
A procedure is a treatment a person will get.
Patient:
A patient is a person who is admittet at the emergency department. Here the patient
undergoes procedures.
Cluster:
A cluster is a group of patiens who undergoes similar procedures. This can be measured in similarities.
Pathway:
A pathway is represented by a combination of procedures a patient undergoes, and
is constructed based on the big clusters.

Model
The whole process of waiting times and construction of pathways in hospitals comes from a multivariate
stochastic process. A stochastic processes is based on probability theory and is a process consisting of a
21
==PAGE:23==
collection of random variables. This collection of random variables describes points in time and the stochastic
process is describing how this system of random variables are changing over time [17].
For this study when the patients are arriving to the emergency department with random inter-arrival times,
which are independent of each other and registered in the system. When the patients are registered, they
will be assigned to a pathway, which contains the treatment requirements. The use of the pathways are also
random variables, as it wont be possible to predict which pathway must be used before the patient has arrived
and registered. Furthermore, the chosen pathway will describe how long time the patient has to stay in the
emergency department to get their treatment.
For this study the problem will be simpliﬁed to focus on the creation of the patient pathways. To create the
pathways a MIP model is formulated. This MIP model will consider mismatches between procedures of a
patient and procedures of a pathway, such if the same procedures don’t occurs there will be a mismatch. It
is in the interest to create the best possible pathways, thus the number of mismatches has to be minimal. By
using this approach the mismatches are an indication of how well the pathways are constructed and make sure
that not too many nor too few procedures are assigned to the pathways. This will also ensure that the patients
are not getting too many unnecessary treatment or not getting the important ones, and this will also save the
hospital for many resources, like manpower and money. Further more the mode will also distribute the patient
to pathways in the best possible way.

5
Construction of pathways
For this study different approaches are used to solve the mathematical problem. The ﬁrst approach is to solve
the MIP model to optimality with CPLEX in GAMS. This may take a long time since there are many patients
for each departments. Not only are there many patients, but also procedures and pathways which varies and
due to the three variables with respectively two, two and three indices, more than 1 million variables appear in
total.
The second method that is used is the decomposition approach, column generation. As this problem is quite
big, using column generation will help to ﬁnd a solution faster as not all of the variables are considered at the
same time. If column generation has problems with making too many iterations, the master problem grows too
large, has ﬂuctuating dual variables and/or has convergence issue, then dual stabilization can be used. This dual
stabilization method that is used in this study, is known as BoxPen stabilization, where the dual variables are
bounded by a box, and will receive a penalty if they exceed it.
The last method which is applied is a method called Cut-And-Solve. The Cut-And-Solve method has been
developed by Climer & Zhang [18], in 2006. The purpose with using this algorithm is to speed up the solving
part of the model, by ﬁrstly solving the relaxed model and then choose some variables to solve the MIP model
to optimality, these two step are repeated until the optimal value is reached. But it might not be the case that the
model is solved faster, even though this problem would be a great model to use the method on, since the model
don’t have too much reduced cost and therefore not add too many variables to the MIP model which makes it
easier to solve to optimality.

Column Generation
Solving the model faster and make it possible to ﬁnd the pathways of a complete set, can be done by applying
column generation. The column generation approach is based on Danzig-Wolfe decomposition, which relaxes
the MIP model and decompose the model in to a master problem and a subproblem. When the master problem
is solved, the dual variables are send to the subproblem which constructs a column with new variables and
send those back to the master problem. By doing this a lot more variables are considered in less number of
constraints, which makes it easier to ﬁnd an optimal solution to the problem.

columns has to be initialized, then the master problem is solved. When the master problem is solved the dual

Generation of columns
Before the column generation algorithm can get started, an initial solution, in the form of initial columns. There
are many options of how to do this, but ﬁrst it was tried to assign all of the patients to one pathway with one
procedure. The choice of the procedure were one procedure that appeared many times. This seemed to take
to long time for the problem to get close to the solution. The algorithm started by assigning each patient to
a pathway and therefore needed to run through all of the patients before really starting to ﬁnd a track of the
solution, long time here is meant by hours.
To save some time the initialization would assign each patient to a pathway with one procedure, the ﬁrst pro-
cedure which the patients goes through. Then the cost of the columns, mismatches, were calculated if more
procedures occurs.

Cut And Solve
Since the column generation with dual stabilization did not solve the problem as desired, the cut and solve
method is used and presented [18].
The principle of this method is to obtain optimal solutions fast by breaking the MIP in to smaller MIPs which
are solved. Search trees are used to keep a track of the solution, when the solution space are divided up in to
smaller parts and solved. One search tree is seen in Figure 23.

The way the algorithm goes is by divide the solution space by adding piercing cuts, this is done in iterations,
it. First the algorithm starts by identifying a cut and the set Sit, which covers the variables that are going to be
cut away, and removes at least one feasible solution from the solution space in each iteration. In Sit the best
solution of the cut is found. If this is the ﬁrst best solution found, this would be the in the root node of the
search tree. If not, the best solution found in Sit must be compared to the best solution in the search tree. If
the best solution from Sit is better than the best solution in the searching tree, the best solution in the searching
tree is updated. Though the ﬁrst best solution might be initialize to a big number.
When this best solution is found in Sit, the cut is added to the model, such that all the solutions from Sit are
removed and the big solution space, Bit, becomes tighter. Afterwards the lower bound, solution of the relaxed
model, is found for the model in solution space Bit. If the reached lower bound is greater or equal to the best
solution, then the algorithm stops and the best solution will be the optimal solution of the model. If the lower
bound is less than the best solution the algorithm will repeat, and each iteration will correspond to a node in
the search tree. A small overview of how the iterations will look like can be seen in Figure 23. Here RMIP
refers to solution of the relaxed model, MIP is the solution of the original model solved to optimality in the
set Sit, and the small number indicates the iteration number. The pseudo code for the algorithm can be seen in
Algorithm 3.
As mentioned before the best solution is initialized to be a big number, because the original model is a mini-
mization model where lower bounds are interesting and each time a better best solution is found, the solution
will have a smaller value. The next question is how to make the piercing cuts and which variables are in Sit
? These cuts are based on the reduced cost of the relaxed solution of the model. When the variables with
non-zero values has a corresponding reduced cost with no cost will be in the basis, if a variable with value of
zero has a corresponding reduced cost with large cost will have a great impact on the objective. Therefore Sit

resources and be a waste of time and money.
6.1.4
Time

Column Generation
Since the optimal model got troubles with solving for many patients and more pathways, the column genera-
tion algorithm is attempted. The column generation algorithm is ran on the smallest data set, the data set of
Department 2, of 50 patients to have a solution within reasonable amount of time which ﬁnishes. In Table 22
the optimal solution from the original model for Department 2 of 50 patients are seen(ori.), as well the solu-
tion obtained by the column generation algorithm(CG). In this table it is observed that the column generation
algorithm uses a lot more time to solve the models, up to approximated 1400 seconds which corresponds to
approximately 20 minuets. Compared to the original optimal problem, which maximum uses 10 seconds to
solve a problem for 50 patients, the column generation algorithm is slow, and has problems with convergence,
as it also was mentioned and showed in Figure 18 in Section 5.2.4 , it also occurs that many of the ﬁnal path-
ways are fractional, which indicates that the solutions are not useful for the original problem. Thus the column
generation algorithm with dual stabilization(DS) is used.
Even though, when column generation is used, it is fulﬁlled that the lower bounds of the column generation has
to lie between the optimal solution of the original problem, and the lower bound of the original problem.
LP original model ≤LP CG ≤OPT original model
By looking at Table 22, containing the optimal solution of the original problem and the lower bound of the
column generation, and Table 23, containing the lower bounds of the original model, it is seen that it is the
case the lower bound of the column generation is within the lower bound and optimal solution of the original
problem. This also indicates that there are some hope for column generation, as the lower bounds are rather
tight, it just has to be modiﬁed to be faster.

Cut and Solve
The last approach which is used to make the original optimal model to be solved faster, is Cut and Solve. Again
data sets which are used are based on β = 50 patients. In Figure 33 three of the searching trees are seen.
It is observed that the for the ﬁrst pathway, that the optimal solution is found in the very ﬁrst iteration. For
the second and the third pathway it is after the second iteration the optimal solution is found, and this is very
common for the rest of the pathways.
Another very common detail is in the very last iteration it is not possible to ﬁnd the lower bound of the solution
space. As the algorithm terminates and has found the optimal solution, the value of the lower bound must be
larger than the best solution. What happens is that the algorithm starts by making a very small cut, where no
best solution is found. This is seen as the node has the value of INF+, which is the same as in the starting
best solution. The second time a cut is made, a solution is found. For the third cut, it happens that the cut is
too big and includes the rest of the solution space, as all the reduced cost has a very small value close to zero.
In that case it is not possible to ﬁnd a new lower bound as no solution space is left and therefore terminates.
It is then investigate if any different tuning of the algorithm is possible which could avoid this problem. Since
the reduced costs are as small as they are, almost zero, for each iteration it is not possible to restrict the small
solutions space which is used to ﬁnd the best solution by setting α. Thus it does not seem possible to tune the
algorithm.

6.4
The ﬁnal proposal
At this time it is possible to construct patient pathways on smaller samples. These pathways mostly contains
only one procedure thus it can be discussed whether it make sense to make these predeﬁned pathways, a waist
of resources. On the other hand it has been examined, and it is possible, to handle more complex data set
where the patients have had several procedures. It will make more sense to predeﬁne these pathways, even
though patients who undergoes only one procedure is most common. Furthermore, based on the observation of
the target pathways and number of mismatched procedures for this study, it will not be necessary to construct
more than 5 to 8 different pathways, as the number of mismatched procedures don’t change too much. The
examination also showed that it would be possible to construct the pathways based on a smaller sample of
patients and still have a similar procedure pr. patient ratio, compared to a complete set.
To construct these more complex pathways is more time consuming, as it also were noticed that big gaps
appeared after only running the model for an hour. Therefore to make it possible to construct the more complex
pathways another approach has to be considered to get better solving times. These approaches could be to
switch the program language or eventually by using a heuristic.

7
Conclusion
The purpose of this study was to create patient pathways for emergency departments in Region Sjælland. These
pathways are created to help the physician and nurses to take better and faster decisions and to schedule re-
sources ahead. To construct these pathways a mathematical MIP model has been formulated, based on data
from four speciﬁc emergency departments in the region from the past two years. These data indicates where
a patient has been hospitalized and which procedures the patient has been through. The model considers the
number of mismatched procedures between the pathway and the procedures the patients has been through, to be
as small as possible. It also has to ensure that each patient is assigned to a pathway and each pathway contains
at least one procedure. The reason why this structure of considering mismatches is used is to ensure that the
patients don’t get too many or too few procedures. It is a simple way of penalize the choice of procedures, path-
ways and the connected patients, therefore is it in the interest to minimize the mismatches. When the model
has been formulated many tests has been set up, to construct the pathways. In these test it was showed that
the MIP model had problems with handling too many patients and the solution time were very big. Thus other
approaches, column generation and cut and solve, were applied to reduce the solution times.
Based on the test and analysis which has been made, it was only possible to create non-fractional pathways
within more reasonable time with the use of the original MIP model solve by CPLEX in GAMS. Even though
memory problem occurred, it was possible to make the set smaller and solve these. The sets ended up with
having a size of 1000 random sampled patients and it was possible to create pathway up to 6 to 12, depended
on the data set. At a certain point when the model was solved for a target of pathways, it was observed that
number of mismatches did not vary much. In the end the patients of a complete data set was assigned to the
constructed pathways based on 1000 patients. This revealed that it will be possible to make the pathways based
on only 1000 patients, as the same procedure pr. patient ratio occurred. Though it could occur that the solution
time were hours or days.
As the data set are quite big, it was a thought that column generation or cut and solve could work better, since
they are more suitable for problems with big data sets. However this was not the case, when cut and solve were
used the solution times were more or less the same as solving the original MIP model. When solving the model
with column generation, no improvement at all was seen as the solution time were around 100 times worse. To
make a small improvement it was tried to add dual stabilization. This made the solution times smaller but not
good enough. But the column generation algorithm had a big potential therefore it might be possible to make
it work by improving the algorithm. But in total to improve the solution time the programming program has to
be changed or a heuristic can be used.
For the ﬁnal proposals of this study is that the pathways can be constructed, though most of them does not con-
tain more than one procedure. It might not make sense for the pathways to only have one procedure compared
to all the expenses and effort there will be to predeﬁne it. Thus it has been tested to make pathways on more
complex data set, where patients has more than one procedure, then the pathways contains several procedures.
These pathways, on the other hand, make more sense and will be more affordable to use though it can take
longer time to create.
7.1
Future work
In this study the solving times and memory issues have caused many limitations. Therefore a lot of the future
work lies in solving the original model for a complete set or for 1000 patients within reasonable time. Column
generation and cut and solve has been used, in hope of improvements, but these failed to do so.
If column generation still is considered, more improvements must be made. These improvements lies in using
another programming program/language with more possibilities, extend the parameter tuning on bigger sets,
as the ﬁnal parameter values might be different for bigger sets, and make the dual stabilization converge even
faster. Beside of this, many of the ﬁnal solutions pathways obtained fractional solution, which was not desired
therefore branching must be taken into account to get non-fractional solution.
Other methods that also would be considered to solve the problem would be by the use of some heuristic, to

In the future identifying the exact number of pathways, for all of the department and for each of the departments,

which procedures they contain. Additionally also to connect symptoms and diseases with these pathways, such
the pathways can be a tool for the nurses and the physicians to used in the emergency departments and improve
the waiting time and to pre-order procedures. At last it would be interesting to see if the patient pathways
