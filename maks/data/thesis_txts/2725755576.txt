Abstract  
5G is the future … Regardless of what technologies of mobile network generation comes after it, they will all 
be built upon the 5G technology. And the 5G positioning system in its part, will be also the leading 
navigation system once the 5G mobile network potentials are fully exploded and accessible every were and 
by every user and device. But unfortunate there is a catch ,5G technology needs first to be cyber secured, 
before its benefits and capabilities can be totally explored and reached. One of the main weaknesses of the 
5G mobile technology is its vulnerability regarding cyber security. 
Almost the majority of cyber security experts agree that the 5G technology is a total cyber security 
nightmare, due to the high risk that it brings regarding cyber hacking and data theft. And this is all due to 
the absence of any access control and data encryption during the communication process between the User 
Equipment devices and the 5G nubile network. 
“IF” , the issue regarding the cyber security weakness of the 5G technology is not dealt with properly , then 
when the 5G technologies reaches its full potential and accessibility ,the  world will be a total cyber security 
disaster and mayhem, due to  the huge amount of UE, IOT, PCs and many other devices  that will connect to 
the network. In order to prevent this disaster and mayhem from all happing, and in order to securely 
benefit from the full potential and capabilities that 5G mobile network bring (including the 5G positioning 
system), there is a huge demand to cyber secure the 5G technologies by decreasing the trust factor 
regarding the access to the 5G mobile network, in order to achieve a cyber secure 5G positioning system 
from start to end.  
The purpose of this thesis is to cyber secure the 5G positioning system. And a secure 5G positioning system 
can be achieved by implementing a zero-trust strategy. The Zero trust strategy that is chosen in this paper is 
the ZTNA (Zero Trust Network Access) and by combining other different cyber security and network solution 
to achieve a zero-trust strategy on the 5G mobile network. Neither zero trust nor network overlay 
virtualization are new or unknown to the 5G technologies, but what this research thesis bring to the  table , 
is the capability of combining different cyber security method (strategies) and network solution together in 
order to secure the 5G positioning system and achieving a zero trust strategy. 
This master thesis paper will mainly consist of providing a solution to secure the 5G positioning system by 
combining the implementation of the ZTNA (Zero Trust Network Access) on top of the 5G mobile network 
(including the 5G positioning system) with the help of the network overlay virtualization. 
The work in this Master thesis will first start with introducing and exploring the cyber security  weakness 
and potentials of the different technologies dressed in this paper which are , the 5G mobile network , the 5g 
positioning system , the Zero trust strategy , the zero trust network access(ZTNA)  , The Overlay network 
Virtualization and then discuss the cyber security solution this  thesis provides to cyber secure the 5G 
positioning system by combing all the above mentioned technology’s in one cyber secure solution.

1 - Chapter 1   : Introduction  
This Chapter will be used to introduces the purpose of this thesis, the problem to address and solve. And an 
introduction to all the technologies that will be used and implemented to gain and achieve a cyber secure 
5G positioning system with the help of a Zero-Trust architecture.  
 
1.1 - Problem to solve:  
The main problem to solve in my master thesis project Is to make the 5G positioning a more secure solution 
by decreasing any trust effect regarding any access by a user or any app and making the whole 5G 
positioning from start to end a secure solution by implementing a zero-trust model/ architecture.  
1.2 - Why is this problem hard to solve:  
The shorts answer is because the 5G technology increases the risk of hacking and data theft. One of the 
main weaknesses of the 5G mobile technology is its weakness or vulnerability regarding cyber security. 
Almost the majority of cyber security experts agree that 5G technology actually increases the risk of hacking 
and by that we could conclude that 5G is a total cyber security nightmare. The reason why 5G technology is 
a cyber security disaster is, due to the absence of any encryption during the communication process 
between devices using the 5G technology network.  
1-3 - Goals of the thesis:  
The main task or Goals of my master thesis is to prove that we can still secure 5G positioning from any cyber 
threats by combining a Zero-trust model solution together with a 5G positioning solution. This is to prove 
that there is a method to make the 5G positioning a secure solution even though 5G technology is a total 
cyber-security disaster.

with new technologies comes new cyber security vulnerabilities and weaknesses. 
The security vulnerability in 5G positioning will be a huge issue and a headache to solve if not deled with in 
a proper  way. With 5G wireless mobile network technology  , the world we know today , will some day be 
totally different .5G comes with huge promising capabilities these capabilities if used correctly will one day 
change the world we know. 5g promise a gigabyte speeds  low latency less than 1 millisecond  and a huge

This capability will provide a huge step forward for 5g positioning because there would be no or almost no 
latency in response time And this is a huge fact when considering navigation. The  5G traffic capacity and 
ability to accommodate  a huge amount of devices,  could reach to millions and even billions in the near 
future , will surly  help in improving and widening the use for 5g positioning system. 
And because of all this new ability and capabilities that 5G offers , there would surly be a security 
vulnerabilities in 5G positioning,. Therefore there is a huge demand and need to ensure the security of data

When using 5G we would have to reconstruct our whole telecommunication mobile network and this will

1.5 - Why use Zero trust model with 5G positioning  
A zero trust frame works in a totally different way than any other cyber security model. All other cyber 
security solution defines or declare that everything inside an organization network can be trusted. This is 
not the case in a Zero trust model (aka. ZT model), it’s actually the opposite. The ZT model starts with 
defining trust as a cyber threat and a vulnerability that should be dealled firmly with. Implementing a ZT 
model on a 5G positioning solution means implementing the principles of a ZT model on the solution by 
making ZT principles more effective regarding performance execution and security throughout the whole 
positioning process from start to end. The best thing in a zero-trust architecture is that there are no 
assumptions made through software nor hardware regarding security. There are no assumptions made 
regarding were ether a certain device or user is a trustworthiness or not. There is no trust in a zero-trust 
model and that’s why it’s also called a perimeter less security model. The only way to gain access through a 
zero-trust model, is that you are who your claim to be. The ZT model decreases the impact of trust and 
continuously validates every single step of any interaction in the network. So, the 5G positioning solution 
will gain security through a ZT model by removing the effect of the trust factor on the network regardless of 
the process, user, positioning, application in used or data the what 5G position solution is trying to access.  
1.6 - How could we secure 5G positioning system from cyber threats  
The effect of a ZT model on the 5G positioning solution is huge. The ZT model will protect and secure critical 
and sensitive data and applications. The ZT model would actually change the way 5G positioning accesses 
users, apps and data by converting the existed solution regarding users’ access, and apps to a more secure, 
reasonable and sustainable solution what would be more compatible with the new cloud solutions, SDN 
environments and other 5G networks belonging to other service providers. This can be done by 
implementing the following activities on the 5G positioning solution:  
1- Implementing or performing a segmentation on the network.

validity checked.  
3- Providing the highest level of layer threat prevention.

strategies and solution/method to gather in order to  authenticate , secure and encrypt  the access and

2.10 - But how is the 5G mmWave signal transmitted and received? 
As any mobile network signal the transmit ion of a 5G mmWave signal start with undoubtedly: 
1-An 5G mmWave transmitter starts with generating a signal by using an RF signal generator. 
2- The 5G mmWave signal is then amplified by using a power amplifier. In order er compensate for loss of 
the strength during the transmission of the signal. 
3- After the signal is amplified, it is then pass through the modulator which its job is to encode the data on 
the signal, the encoding is used to convert the signal from analog to digital signal form. Modulation is the 
process of converting data from digital signals into an analog signals / radio wave and vice versa. The 
Modulation is done by adding information to an electronic signal. Modulation is done by data 
communication devices which are called modems also known as modulators or de modulators.  
4- After the signal is modulated it’s forwarded and sent through a duplexer, which its job is to separate the 
transmit signal and the receive signal. The duplex is a bidirectional communication system which its job is to 
allow communications between the two end towers or nodes to communicate in order to be able to send 
receive data or signals 
5- After the signal is gone through the duplexer, it is then forwarded to the Circulator, which its job is to 
route the signals from the transmitter to the antenna and from the antenna to the receiver and vice versa. 
In other words the Circulators job is to direct the signals to an array of antennas. 
The RF circulator and the RF isolators are two communication devices which have the job to protect 
sensitive parts of the received or sent signal from the outputs of the transmitter and this is done by the 
process of separating the transmitter signals form the received signals, when the signal touches the input or 
tip of the antenna. 
The antenna array that I mentioned previously where the signal is transmitted to is an array made up from 
multi small cell antennas, which are placed together very closely, in order to create a network of small cells 
where the signal can then be moved through. As I also mentioned in a previous section, each small cell 
antenna covers only a small area compering to what the traditional 4G LTE tower cell covers. 
The reason why the 5G mmWave network is designed to have small cell array of antennas is, because 
==PAGE:23==
23 
 
combination of small cells creates a dense network of coverage. These arrays of antennas use a technology 
called beamforming, which helps to focus the signal in a desired direction towards the end user device. and 
by using the method you would be able to increase the signals strength and by doing so the overall user 
experience will then be significantly improved.

On the other end of the communication end, the user device will then capture the sent signal by using an 
cell antenna. The received signal at the input of the cell antenna is then forwarded  through a low-noise 
amplifier (LNA) that will help to amplifies the signal while adding as little noise as possible .The signal is then 
down converted to a lower frequency by using a device called a mixer and a local oscillator. 
The down convert process is a digital signal processing  process that helps the DDC (digital down-convert ) 
to convert a digitized band limited signal to a lower frequency signal  in order to  achieve a simple 
subsequent radio phase. The down converted signal is then passed through a demodulator, which extracts 
the data from the signal, and then sends it to the user's device for processing.  
Overall, the transmission and reception of 5G mmWave signals require a combination of advanced 
technologies, including RF signal generation, power amplification, modulation, beamforming, and signal

2- Calculate positioning by using the Time Difference of arrival (DL-TDOA) 
Another method to determine the position of a UE by using the method Downlink time difference of arrival 
(DL-TDOA), in this method the positioning is calculated by using time difference. The position of the UE from 
the source the signal is coming from. In this method the positioning is calculated by measuring the time it

this solution/method to work, it relies on very good synchronization between base station and the UE. And 
at the same time this is disadvantage of this method. 
 
3- Calculate positioning by using the Relatiev Time of arrival:UL-RDOA 
Another method to determine the position of a UE by using the method uplink time difference of 
arrival (UL-RDOA),In this solution the position is calculated when the network measures the relative 
time of arrival of the UE SRS from different cells ( BS: base station). The solution works when the Base 
station sends an order(command) to the UE to send an SRS, then the BS starts the process of 
measurement and when finished from calculation the position /location the BS will send the location of 
the UE to the location management function in core network. For this solution or method to work, it

4- Multi-Cell Round Trip Time Method (RTT): 
IN order to measure the position in this method the Base station/ node in 5G gNB (Next Generation NodeB) 
and the UE will exchange and preform a RX and TX  process with each other. The time the signal took for the 
RX-TX process is measured from each cell .Once you have these  measurement reported from the gNB and 
the UE and sent to  the location server in order to determine the Round TRIP Time (RTT) of each cell , then 
the server will be able to declare the UE position. RTT was also possible in 4G but I t only worked for one 
cell at a time from the servant cell. Advantage of this solution is that it doesn’t require a very strong 
Network synchronization between nodes. The disadvantage with this method is that it uses the uplink 
function and this may be a problem cause if the uplink is increased it will then result in interference. The 
uplink happens when the BS sends the calculation and measurements to the location function in the 5G 
core network. 
 
5- Angular -Based positioning Methods. 
Before I begin to describe this method I need to define  what the technique Beam steering is in 5G. 
well, Beam steering in 5G is a technique used in the 5G network in order to direct the signals radio 
waves to a specific target for example a UE Device. The reason why 5G uses the Beam steering is 
because this method could provide a  better , reliable and efficient connection between the BS and the 
UE. Back to Angular -Based Methods, This is a new method in 5G , it’s based on BS beam steering and in 
order to calculate the UE position, this method relies on the below two technique / algorithms. 
something to note before we start with the two algorithms is that the Angular-based methos relies on 
the antenna array orientation. 
 
DL-AOD (Downlink Angel-Of-Departure) Algorithm: In this algorithm the UE starts with measuring the 
DL-RSRP(downlink reference signal receive power)  per beam/gNB (per beam tower).i.e. UE measures 
the active beam and the strongest adjacent beam.These measurements are then used to calculate the 
AoD based on the UE beam location for every 5G basestasion (gNB). The AoD is then used by the 
location server to calculate the position of the UE. 
 
UL-AOA (uplink Angel-Of-arrival) Algorithm: In this algorithm the 5G base station (gNB)  Starts to 
measure the angle-of-arrival, and this is  done based on the beam were  the UE was located in. i.e. The 
gNB measures the beams index of the UL-SRS beam. These measurements /report are then forwarded 
to the Location server in core network in order to determine the UE position.

-. The external application Server askes the 5G network for at location of a UE device.  
-. The external application Server then sends the request to the Gateway Mobile location Center 
(GMLC) to try to identify that request, if it is possible to give location of the UE that is in its area or not. 
(The GMLC  which is responsible for the  event processing and reporting subscriber location information to 
the required endpoints). 
-. The request is then send further to AMF (AMF is  responsible for security for access management even 
when it comes to positioning services). 
-. AMF then sends the request regarding the location information to location management function, 
because location management functions has a direct connection with new generation ran(NG-RAN) with 
base stations, via the NAS interface with the help of NAS protocol. 
( NAS (Non-access stratum): is a functional layer in the NR, LTE, UMTS and GSM wireless 
telecom protocols  , which is stacked  between the core network and user equipment device.) 
-. The location management functions is aware about any positioning measurements and calculations in the 
network and may provide that information (if it has it )further to the AMF and then the AMF sends the info 
back to GMLC which in return  then send the location the UE to external application server.

6.3 - How a Zero Trust Security Model works  
In simple English, Zero Trust security is built on the idea of every user or device that is trying to connect to 
the network is a potential threat in order to ensure a strong security for all users, devices, application, 
system and the most important of all Data. Zero trust solution is driven from the concept “least privilege 
access” in other words no user or device insider or outside the network could gains access without verifying 
their Identity  
Zero Trust approached is achieved by performing a two-way authentication (is also known as a Mutual 
authentication process) were the two parties authenticate each other in an authentication protocol. for a 
user to gain access to a network through Mutual authentication, the user would have to have a Username, 
Password and a Public Key Certificate.  
Mutual authentication adds an extra lager of security by requiring the user to identify itself by using 
username, password and a special pin (or authentication app or even by using biometrics). The main reason 
why Zero Trust Security uses a Mutual authentication process is, because of its ability to detect and block 
any adversarial attacks. In other words, experts consider any security model without a Mutual 
authentication a weak security solution. So the main solution behind Zero Trust Security is that it provides 
an authentication access to a user /IoT/Device by continuously verifying and validating the identity and 
authentication of the user or device, through authentication process that checks the identity with the help 
of authentication algorithms.  
in other words, no user is trusted. This means that Zero Trust Security Model does not trust a user based on 
the password of because of its location, but the trust of a user is approved by looking at the attributes of 
the user or device that is trying to connect and by monitoring the behaviour of the user/device in order to 
gain an understanding of who the user is, how are they gaining access, why they want access and what will 
they do with the access. As mentioned before Zero trust is built on the principle of least privilege (AKA: 
PoLP) and it provides a PoLP access (the access they need to perform the tasks) to every user/device that is 
authenticated to access the network. One of the main reasons for at PoLP solution is to reduce the risk of a

6.7 - The main concept or principles behind ZTNA security model:

needs to perform its job and nothing more than that. Implementing Least privilege access is a priority in any 
ZTNA and requires cautiously managing user permissions and access. Least privilege access in a ZTNA is not 
like a Least privilege access in an VPN. ZTNA is not like in VPN the user in A ZTNA does not gain full access to 
the network once the user is authenticated and authorized.  
2 – MFA (Multi Factor Authentication): MFA is the core of a Zero Trust security network. The principle of an 
MFA is that any users or device requires a multi (more than one) authentication certificate in order to 
authorize it and give it the specified access it need to the network ,in order to perform its job and any more 
access than that ,is illegal in a ZTNA architecture.  
3 – Micro segmentation: The ZTNA uses the Micro Segmentation method, in other words ZTNA breaks down 
security perimeters into smaller manageable zones in order to maintain every zones access separately from 
any other part in the network. Because a Data center could have multi zones of files and data. And by using 
Micro Segmentation method the user can only gain access to a zones in the datacenter that the user is 
authenticated for instead of gaining access to all the data center files and data.  
4 -- Stop lateral movement: The concept or the meaning of the sentence “lateral movement” is when the 
attacker moves around the network after gaining access to it. ZTNA is designed so that there is nothing 
called “lateral movement” and the attacker cannot move from one zone to another, because in a ZTNA the

5 – Continuously monitoring devices and users access control: In a ZTNA the network is continuously

8.7 - Authorization Methods and configuration of  access policies 
Configuring the network access policies  is the first step in building the authorization process in an access 
control environment. 
Configuring access policies  involves  the process of defining and determining what rules can be used to  
determine which user or devices are authorized to access the 5G positioning system and under what 
conditions. One thing to note here is that the access policies can be defined at different levels in the 
network .It can either  be defined at the device or  user or at  the application level.  
Defining and deterring what kind of access policy should be used in the network depend on many factors 
,such as the what level of security  is required in the network and the level of risk associated with granting 
access to user and devices to the network.. 
Here below are some detail description  for types of  configured access policies methods that can be used  
authorize access to the 5G positioning system. 
 
1-Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): The RBAC is an access policies or an approach that grant access to only 
authorized users The RBAC  involves  configuring and assigning roles to users and the users access 
permissions is based on which  roles were assigned to the user. this mean it is the role that determine what 
the user has access to the  network . For example, an administrators have some special roles that give them 
access to all parts of the 5G positioning system, while a normal  user might only have access to certain areas 
of the system it all depends on the role that you user has been assigned.. 
2-Time-Based Access Control: This type of access control is Time-based access control. Which means the 
user have access  at a special time , date or period of a day. This type of access control involves defining 
access policies based on the time of day or day of the week. For example, access to the 5G positioning 
system might be restricted outside of business hours or on weekends or on certain days , it all depends on 
the access control policies of the organization. 
3-Risk-Based Access Control: This type of access is based on risk assessments. This type of access provides 
access based on a dynamic risk assessment of the network As mentioned this type of  access control 
method  involves dynamically adjusting access policies based on the level of risk associated with the device. 
An example for this type of access control : a device might be only allowed access to the 5G positioning 
system only if it passes a risk assessment that takes into account factors such as its security posture, 
behavior, and the device access  history. 
4-Location-Based Access Control: This kind of access is granted based on the devoice location or IP address. 
This kind of access involves defining access policies based on the physical location of the device. An example 
==PAGE:66==
66 
 
for this kind of access control could be : access to the 5G positioning system might be restricted if the device 
is outside of a designated geographic area. 
5-Attribute-Based Access Control (ABAC):This kind of access involves defining access policies based on the 
attributes of the device, such as the device type, device location, or the security posture of  the device .An 
example could be : a device might be allowed access to the 5G positioning system only if it is running the 
latest security patches and has not been rooted out before . 
8.8 - Encryption methods 
Encrypting is the process of transforming data form one form to another .so that oit can be in an unclear 
form to unauthorized users.  Encrypting process also include the decrypting process which mean the 
process of converting the transmitted data back to its original so that it can be readable for authorized 
users. Som e thing to note here is that every encryption methods has its own strengths and weaknesses, 
that why selecting one of the below  method depends on the security and performance level required for

data between the devices and the 5G positioning system: 
1- Advanced Encryption Standard (AES): The AES  is an symmetric encryption algorithm . This standard uses 
the same 128, 192, or 256 bit key secret key for both the encryption  and decryption of the  data. The AES is 
one of the most used  encrypting standard , due to its flexibility ,efficiency and is supported by a wide range 
of  systems and devices. 
2- Transport Layer Security (TLS): TLS protocol that is used to encrypts transmitted data in order to ensure 
that no cyber threat such as eavesdroppers and hackers are not able to read the data and the whole 
communication over the internet is secure. TLS is widely used for encryption of  private and sensitive data 
such as passwords, credit card numbers, and personal GDPR information .As in SSL the TLS can also provide 
authentication and integrity checks by and encrypting data by using the symmetric and asymmetric 
encryption systems. 
2- Quantum Key Distribution (QKD): is a secure communication method that implements a cryptographic 
protocol. It uses quantum mechanics for the exchanges process of keys between two parties.one of its 
disadvantages is that its widely deployed 
3- Public Key Infrastructure (PKI): the PKI is called the Public Key Infrastructure because it uses the public 
key cryptography in the process of authenticating  users and encrypting  data. The users get their public 
keys and other identifying information from the digital certificates  that the PKI standard  issues. 
4- Secure Sockets Layer (SSL):IS an older version of encrypting standards . actually it’s the predecessor of 
the TLS protocol. The SSL protocol  is a digital certificate that authenticates identity of websites in order to 
achieve an encrypted connection. The SSL can encrypt data, perform authentication and do an integrity 
check by using combination of both the symmetric and asymmetric encryption methos .

8.9 - Monitoring system, tools  or  methods used in ZTNA and in the overlay network to 
secure the 5G positioning system 
Here below are some few examples of the available Monitoring systems, tools  or  methods or techniques 
that are available and can be used in the ZTNA and in the overlay network in order to secure and detect 
potential cyber security threats or attacks on the 5G positioning system. 
Different combinations of these tools can also be used together in order to provide a better  monitoring and 
analyzing capabilities for the cyber security solution, it all Depends on the system and network design and 
architecture , the cyber security policies and  other specific security requirements. 
1- Intrusion detection systems (IDS) An intrusion detection system is an alerting system that  monitors the 
network traffic for suspicious behavior and when detected it the sounds an alert to  the admittaturs that a  
malicious activity is detected on the network . IDS systems are passive, meaning they only detect alert on 
potential cyber security threats 
2- intrusion prevention systems (IPS):An intrusion prevention system  is an action or prevention system , 
that also monitors the network traffic for suspicious behavior . what it doses is that when there is an 
indication of any malicious activity on the network, the IPS then immediately (at once) shuts down the 
network before any access can be gained by the intruders in order to prevent further movement in the 
network and alert the administrators that the network is shutdown .IPS systems are an active system ,that 
can block malicious traffic if detected. 
3- Security information and event management (SIEM) systems: SIEM systems is a product that can monitor, 
detect and alert when it finds any unusual and suspicious activity or potential threat when analyzing the 
network  data traffic. The SIEM system can also analyze log data from different network resources like PCs 
,servers ,applications and other network devices.  
4- Network traffic analysis (NTA): The NTA is a method that can be used for monitoring network activities 
and analyze network traffic in order to  detect and identify any malicious activities, potential threats, 
malware, other types of  virus ,suspicious traffic patterns, and data breaches. 
5- User and entity behavior analytics (UEBA) : The UEBA is a cybersecurity solution that uses machine 
learning algorithms to identify unusual behavior patterns in the activities of users ,routers devices ,servers , 
PCs and other devices  on the network, These unusual behavior patterns can indicate a security threat or a 
breach on the network. 
6- Packet capture and analysis: Packet capture and analysis is a monitoring forensics technique that uses the 
traceback technique to capture and analyze individual network packets in order to determine were the 
cyberthreat came from. This technique is used by IT teams to detect and identify potential cyber security 
threats, intrusion attempts, including network-based attacks, packet loss and data breaches through the 
process of analyzing network packets. 
7- Endpoint detection and response (EDR) tools: The EDR , is also known as EDTR (endpoint detection and 
threat response) is an continually monitoring tool ,Which is used to monitor endpoints devices such as 
laptops, desktops, and servers for any suspicious activity, including malware infections, ransomware  and 
unauthorized access attempts and then respond to these cyber security attack  and threats.
