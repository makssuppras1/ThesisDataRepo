Abstract
The present report concerns structural topology optimization of composite power pylons.
By 2020 100,000 new power pylons are needed in Europe alone. They must be light weight, easier
to erect, less costly and must appear more aesthetic than the traditional lattice steel girders.
In the PoPyFu (Power Pylons of the Future) project architects from BYSTRUP have proposed
a new design of composite power pylons in яЌbreglass material to fulяЌl the requirements above.
The present project is part of the structural investigation of the power pylons.
The weight and material consumption play important roles in the design of the power pylons and
must be economised on. At the same time the power pylons are subject to several diяЌerent loads,
motions and weather conditions. It is thus a challenging task to яЌnd the best structural design,
which fulяЌl the requirements in the most optimal way. The purpose of the present work is thus
to get closer to the most optimal material distribution by use of topology optimization.
A Matlab program PoPOpt (Power Pylon Optimization) is developed to handle an optimization
of the cantilever arms of the pylons. The program is implemented from theoretical derivations,
veriяЌed and demonstrated.
The density based SIMP approach and the MMA optimization
algorithm have been the starting point of the implementations. Further the joint in the pylon
is optimized by use of the commercial яЌnite element program ABAQUS. The studies have been
simpliяЌed only to handle isotropic materials.
The results of the optimized cantilever arms and the joint illustrated important aspects in the
material distribution. It was clear how the position of the pylon in the grid had a large inяЌuence
on the acting forces, hence on the most optimal material distribution. It was further shown how
dependent the яЌnal structures were on the initial design domains. This led to the conclusion
that it is advisable to initially determine a яЌxed outer domain, where the goal thus become the
distribution of the interior material in the most optimal way. Finally a study of the яЌneness of the
meshes illustrated the importance of this aspect towards mesh independent and manufacturable
results.
Overall the results and process of optimizing respectively the arms and the joint highlighted some
of the important and general aspects in the structural design. The results from the optimizations
of the composite power pylons are considered as preliminary design drafts of the overall structural
concept.

1.2
Problem DeяЌnition and Limitations
When designing a structure like the composite power pylons, the weight and material consump-
tion play important roles. Both factors have a large inяЌuence on the overall expenses, erection
and transport challenges, as well as on the strength and eяЌciency of the pylon. To economize on
both weight and material an optimization tool can be an eяЌcient method in the initial design
phase where ideas for the overall design are found and discussed. Topology optimization is such
an optimization tool and is used throughout the present work to get closer to the optimal design
of the power pylons.
The goal and objective of an optimization process varies, as well as the constraints and boundary
conditions depend on the problem deяЌnition. The power pylons are subject to several diяЌerent
loads, motions and weather conditions. The structural loads from the pylons themselves and
the conductors in diяЌerent directions, including the wind and snow loads, are some of the most
inяЌuential. The optimization of the structural design of the power pylons is thus based on these
loads, with focus on the material consumption and weight. The composite power pylons are
simpliяЌed to isotropic material and static loads. Both dynamic motions and stability of the
pylons are important in the structural design as well, but not brought into focus in the present
work.
A topology optimization program is developed in Matlab based on a яЌnite element beam model
with varying 2D cross sections. The main focus has been on the development of the topology
optimization environment, including theory based implementations, veriяЌcation and demonstra-
tion. The structural layout of the cantilever arms is optimized with the program.
As the joint between the cantilever arms and the pole is one of the most critical parts of the
structure and exposed to the largest stresses, a detailed analysis of this part is performed. With
commercial яЌnite element programs a topology optimization of the joint is carried out.
The results from both optimizations of the composite power pylons are considered as preliminary
design drafts of the overall structural concept. From the optimized designs the important aspects
towards a яЌnal structural design are discussed.

1.3
Optimization of Composite Power Pylons
The overall goal of the present work is an optimization of composite power pylons, more speciяЌ-
cally a topology optimization. Topology optimization is a mathematical approach that optimizes
material layout within a given design space, for a given set of loads and boundary conditions,
so that the resulting layout meets a prescribed set of performance targets.
The method is an excellent way for engineers to utilize materials in the best way and create the
most optimal structures. The task for the power pylons is simply to carry the load from the
conductors to the ground, but between many solutions it can be diяЌcult to identify the best.
The purpose of using topology optimization is to get closer to the best solution. To approach
this solution the present work is divided into two parts, respectively optimization of the power
pylon arms and optimization of the joint between the arms and pole, see Figure 1.4.
Figure 1.4: Draft model of the power pylon with indication of the joint (green area) and arms
(red area) [10].

2.1
Topology Optimization
Topology optimization is a mathematical approach to optimize material layout within a given
design domain, for a given set of loads and boundary conditions, such that the resulting layout

meets a prescribed set of performance targets. The performance targets can vary in a large

often used performance targets, or objectives, are the compliance (inverse of stiяЌness), volume,
stresses or eigenfrequencies.
To identify the special characteristics of topology optimization, the method is considered in

3.1.2
Method of Moving Asymptotes
The optimization algorithm is chosen to be the Method of Moving Asymptotes (MMA) by
Svanberg ([25] and [26]). This algorithm has proved itself to be versatile and well suited for large
scale topology optimization problems [1]. The MMA method is a mathematical programming
algorithm, well suited for topology design among several other optimization problems, particular
structural optimization.
A part of the reason for this is the яЌexible and at the same time
general method. For example it is able to handle element sizes, densities, shape variables and
material orientation angles as design variables, together with several diяЌerent constraint types
as well.
The MMA routine solves non-linear and smooth optimization problems by solving a sequence
of simpler approximate sub-problems. These sub-problems are separable and convex, and based
on the sensitivity information at the current iteration point. The sub-problems are solved for
example by a dual method or by interior point algorithms, and the solution is used as the next
design in the iterative process. The generation of the sub-problems are controlled by the so
called тmoving asymptotesт, which help to stabilize as well as speed up the convergence of the
general process.
The present optimization problems are ordinary nonlinear programming problems on the тstan-

Conclusion
Throughout the report an optimization tool PoPOpt has been development and implemented
based on theoretical expressions and mathematical algorithms and principals.
The PoPOpt
program has been tested, veriяЌed and demonstrated through simple examples. The arms of
the power pylons were optimized under diяЌerent load cases and with two diяЌerent initial outer
domains. Finally the joint was studied and optimized with use of the commercial яЌnite element
program ABAQUS, again with two diяЌerent domains.
Through the veriяЌcation it was ensured that the PoPOpt program was reliable and stable. It was
thus concluded to use a penalty factor of 2 or 3 to generate void/solid results and reduce inter-
mediate densities, and further it was concluded to apply the density яЌlter to avoid checkerboard
formations. During the veriяЌcation tests it was further discovered how the sensitivity яЌlter was
malfunctioning and how optimization problem 2 was too unstable to be used. In the demon-
stration it was shown with simple load cases how the topology optimization tool distributed
the material diяЌerently within the cross sections to obtain each of the speciяЌc loads in the best
possible way. Further it was observed how the variation in combinations of diяЌerent load cases
and the weighting between them had a considerable impact on the яЌnal cross section design. It
is thus an important conclusion to be especially careful when diяЌerent load cases are weighted
against each other, as the яЌnal result can change drastically even for small variations in the
combinations and weightings.
The cantilever arms of the power pylons were optimized to get closer to the most optimal
structural design in respect to static loads. From the investigation several important conclusions
were made. It was clear how the position of the power pylons in the grid had a considerable
inяЌuence on the governing forces acting, and thus also on the optimized structural design. The
governing forces for a pylon within the grid were the horizontal, contrary the vertical forces for
a pylon at the beginning or the end of the grid being the governing. The optimized structures
were thus characterized by inner oval proяЌles in two diяЌerent directions. Through a combined
optimization it was though seen that the shape of the inner proяЌles approached a circular shape.
It was as a general observation in all cases seen that the largest section forces occurred at the
support resulting in massive cross sections in this area. The above observations should be taken
into consideration in the work towards a яЌnal design of the cross sections.
The work with two diяЌerent initial outer domains led to the second important conclusion. The
work showed how dependent the results were on the initial outer domains, which especially
became clear in the comparison of the results from respectively the rectangular and circular
domain. Based on these observations it is advisable in the use of the tool to initially determine
a яЌxed outer domain, where the goal thus become the distribution of the interior material in the
best way.
==PAGE:75==
Finally the studies with reяЌned meshes illustrated the importance of how яЌne meshes should
be. With the яЌner meshes a more reяЌned material distribution was seen, as well as more clear
thinwalled cross sections in the outer beam elements. To generate manufacturable and mesh
independent results the яЌneness of the mesh is thus very important.
From the optimization of the joint several diяЌerent results were seen, highly dependent on the
speciяЌc load case and initial size of the domain. As it was the case for the cantilever arms, it was
further seen that the results were dependent on the initial outer domain. All the results showed
truss like structures, though with large variations in the position and thickness of the members.
The results for pylon 2 and the combined case, both for the large domain, showed in particular
interesting, elegant and voluminous trusses. Common for the two cases with combined loads
from pylon 1 and 2 were more clearly deяЌned trusses, and thus better manufacturability. The
results are in general seen as a very interesting input in the further discussions of the design of
the joint. It is though advisable to include deformation, stress and stability studies as well.
Overall did the results and process of optimizing respectively the arms and the joint of the
power pylons highlighted some of the important and general aspects in the structural design.
It is though still relevant to study several other aspects to the optimization such as dynamics,
stability and anisotropy in further work.
The present results highlighted thus some of the
relevant facets to be aware of in the design process towards the яЌnal optimal design.

Future Work
The contents and results of the present work lead to ideas for further work in several diяЌerent
directions. The future work can be seen in two main categories, respectively further numerical
experiments and continuous development of the Matlab program PoPOpt.
The further numerical experiments cover an investigation of the power pylon pole similar to the
present investigation of the cantilever arms. This investigation will be a simpler task, as the
pole can be handled with symmetry conditions about both centre lines. This double symmetry
condition will most likely result in a simple design approaching a cone shape.
Other numerical experiments of interest will be dynamic investigations and damping optimiza-
tion. The damping properties are an important aspect of the structural properties of the power
pylons, as the conductors are placed directly on the arms and can be subject to large oscillations
and vibrations. A combined optimization of both the static and damping properties will be a
desirable goal as well.
For the joint between the arms and the pole further tests of the optimization in ABAQUS
will be of interest. Other load cases, a damping optimization with respect to eigenvalues and
eigenmodes and test of diяЌerent input parameters will all be relevant for future work.
The developed program PoPOpt makes a great basic environment for further development and
extensions of many sorts. DiяЌerent directions to develop the program are listed below.
- Anisotropy Implementation of anisotropic materials in contrast to the present isotropic
material. This is already a part of the BECAS environment and with an implementation
in PoPOpt it will be possible to model the actual material properties of the яЌbreglass
material from which the Power Pylons are produced. This will further increase the number
of design variables, as the direction of яЌbres in the diяЌerent layers of the яЌbreglass will be
an interesting optimization design variable.
- Normal stresses Development of objective functions with normal stresses as the objective
will move the optimization goals closer to manufacturability and following stress tests of
the model. A comparison of the results of the diяЌerent objective functions will further be
of interest.
- Damping optimization To fulяЌl the above goals of numerical experiments within dy-
namics and with damping properties as objective function, this part also has to be imple-
mented. DiяЌerent damping objectives could include e.g. minimization of eigenfrequencies,
maximization of the distance between critical eigenfrequencies and the size of diяЌerent
eigenmodes.
- Stability Optimization with focus on stability and risk of buckling will be of special
interest, as the power pylons consist of tall and slender parts which are exposed to these
eяЌects in particular.
==PAGE:77==
- Viscoelastic Materials Implementation of diяЌerent materials with varying properties
including viscoelastic materials. The vicsoelastic materials will be of interest to the opti-
mization of the damping properties. How to implement these special material properties
in the optimization routine along with the яЌbreglass materials, will be an interesting task.
The above mentioned ideas for further work are only some of the most apparent and immediate
investigations and implementations. Future work based on the present can thus be turned in
several diяЌerent directions of interest.

Initial Project Description
Title: Structural and Damping Optimization of Composite Power Pylons
The research project Power Pylons of the Future (PoPyFu) aims to design new power pylons in
composite materials with a simpler and cleaner design than the traditional steel girder masts.
The research project is a cooperation between Bystrup Architecture, Tuco Marine, Aalborg
University and DTU Mekanik.
In the design process of the pylons several engineering challenges arise. The present project

Vectorization of Matrices
The global stiяЌness matrices for cross sections with a яЌne mesh, hence many elements, often

is by using sparse matrices. An eяЌcient method to assemble the global sparse matrices is to
vectorize the local stiяЌness matrices, hence store the stiяЌness values in vectors. A code example
of the vectorization process is given below:
