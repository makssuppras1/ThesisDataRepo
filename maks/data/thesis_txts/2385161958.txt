Abstract
The thesis envisages to design a health monitoring algorithm for railway ballast based
on spectral analysis. An early detection of faults on the railway infrastructure can contribute
in providing a timely maintenance and prevent disruptions in the operation of railway
networks. Simulated data that contains the track responses of the wheel-rail interaction
through high-ﬁdelity simulation software was provided for the analysis in the thesis. The
frequency of dynamic responses in the ballast layer ranges from 50-100Hz. The data was
ﬁltered and processed with different techniques for the purpose of focusing the analysis
only on the ballast region. Different spectral analysis using Fast Fourier Transform and
wavelet transform were implemented on the simulated data. A phase shift was noticed from
the continuous wavelet transform analysis on the acceleration signal as the degradation
occurs. A time domain analysis was performed in this thesis and instantaneous phase
shift in the original signal was identiﬁed as the principal element that demonstrate the
degradation phenomenon.
A mathematical tool, Hilbert transform was proposed for the estimation of the instantaneous
phase from the continuous time signal. An efﬁcient detection method was developed by
combining the Hilbert transform and a GLR based sinusoidal detection method. The test
makes detection process possible even for the smallest degradation of the ballast. Also,
isolation of different levels of degradation is accomplished with the developed detection
method.

CHAPTER 1
Introduction
This chapter gives an introduction about the MSc thesis project titled Railway Ballast
Health Monitoring Using Spectral Methods. In section 1.1 the background and motivation
for the project is discussed. The literature review conducted on the available monitoring
methods of railway infrastructure and the project description (objectives and solutions) are
presented in sections 1.2 and 1.3 respectively. In sections 1.4 and 1.5 project outline and
contributions are stated.
1.1
Background and Motivation
This MSc thesis is carried out in the context of an ongoing project called Intelliswitch,
which is a close cooperation between the Rail Net Denmark , Technical University of
Denmark and four European partners with signiﬁcant proﬁciency within this area. The aim
of the project is to improve safety, reliability and operational lifetime of the 3500 switches
and crossings (SCs) in the Danish railway network. The main objective of Intelliswitch
project is to propose a novel approach to make maintenance of the railway infrastructure
based on intelligent fault detection tool. Thus the present regular manned inspections
could be replaced by smart monitoring of the dynamic responses of the infrastructure
and estimating the detection patterns that would deviate from a fully functional state to a
degraded state.
Railway systems across the world rely heavily on the proper functioning of the railway in-
frastructure. To ensure safety and proper rail transport it is necessary to maintain a healthy
infrastructure. The dynamic response of the switches and crossings depends majorly on
the structural health condition of different components in the infrastructure namely the rail,
sleeper, sleeper pads, ballast and sub ballast layers. This MSc thesis focuses on devising
algorithms for health monitoring of the ballast infrastructure based on the analysis of
dynamic responses of infrastructure during the passage of trains.

CHAPTER 1. INTRODUCTION
As time passes, the structural health of the ballast starts deteriorating due to different
load conditions and train speeds. This can affect the overall stability of the track. If left
unnoticed, the deterioration process can have an adverse effect on the track infrastructure
leading to serious derailments that can cause loss of life, severe disruption in the operation
of rail network and loss of conﬁdence in rail transport by the public. To guarantee depend-
ability and safety of the infrastructure, timely inspection and maintenance is important.
Currently the maintenance is based on regular manned inspections on the quality of the
detail that are available at the surface of the infrastructure. This has some clear drawbacks
due to the unevenness of the decisions and sole access to information available only at the
surface. This raises the demand for an efﬁcient health monitoring system for ballast, which
can constantly monitor the health state of the ballast and efﬁciently detect the deterioration
as soon as it begins by identifying parameters that manifest early stages of degradation.
1.2
Literature Review
For a better planned maintenance of railway infrastructure a faster detection of early
indications of degradation is very important. There are many researches that have proposed
different monitoring methods for railway infrastructure. A review of some state-of-art of
general monitoring methods and algorithms based on spectral analysis was conducted and
are stated brieﬂy in this section.
There have been researches focusing on the parameters that should be measured for
monitoring and methods to do so. Various measurement techniques to estimate railway
track stiffness were employed by some researches. Berggren et al. (2014), introduced an
innovative approach for measuring the track deﬂection and stiffness using an additional
measurement system complemented on an ordinary track recording car. By implement-
ing this method not only did they succeed in monitoring different track defects but also
overcame two drawbacks of other available devices - cost and speed of measurement.
Hodge et al. (2015) carried out a survey on Wireless Sensors Network technology for
condition monitoring of railway infrastructure by focusing on types of sensor devices to be
used, different network designs and type of monitoring systems that can be implemented.
Conditional health monitoring based on vibration signatures was proposed by Yen and Lin
(1999).
Some research projects have proposed monitoring of infrastructures using measured data.
Direct and indirect approaches have been implemented for detecting defects in different
components. Ground penetrating radar (GPR) by Kind (2011), visual inspection (Asplund
et al. (2013)) and core penetration test (CPT) by Brough et al. (2003) are some direct
approaches that have been employed to detect faults in railway infrastructures. The indi-

Some of the spectral analysis based monitoring methods were also reviewed in regard with

(USP) on the dynamic response of the train-track interaction using a time-domain model.
The inﬂuence of USP on dynamic train-track interaction was found to be mainly in the
frequency range 0-250Hz. Galvín et al. (2010) performs an analysis on the inﬂuence of
the high speed trains on the dynamic responses of the track-soil-structure.

1.3.1
Objectives
The following objectives were formulated in order to achieve the main goal of the
project:
• Analyze the data received from the high-ﬁdelity multi-body simulations of the
wheel-rail interactions of both healthy and degraded ballast conditions in different
frequency domain methods.
• Time domain analysis of the simulated data of both the healthy and degraded states
of ballast layer.
• Identify signatures that deviates as the ballast changes from its healthy state to
degraded state, and that can be used for monitoring.
• Design algorithms for health monitoring of the ballast layer based on the fre-
quency and time domain analysis made.
• Evaluate the performance of the devised algorithms based on the probability of
detection, probability of false alarms and probability of detecting the smallest
degradation.

fulﬁllment of the project scope:
• Analysis of the simulated data obtained for both healthy and degraded cases of
ballast using the most common spectral method Fast Fourier Transform.
• A Wavelet Transform analysis was proposed and the whole analysis of the data
was carried out using both the Continuous and Discrete Wavelet Transform.
• The time domain analysis was performed and signature element Instantaneous
Phase shift was identiﬁed as the system changes from its fully functional state as
degradation begins.

are even-load distribution from the rail, dynamic load damping, larger lateral resistance
and water drainage and so forth. The ﬁgure 2.1 illustrates the different components that

2.1
Track Superstructure
Track super structure is deﬁned as the part of the rail road or often referred as the
permanent way, that consists of rail, sleepers, sleeper pads and fastenings. The track
superstructure helps in transmitting the load stress received from the rolling stock to
the substructure i.e, the earth bed or other man-made structures such as the bridges or
tunnels. The track superstructure is also composed of switches and dead crossings. Each
component of the track structure helps in reducing the vertical stresses that the underneath
components should resist. Hence, the physical structure and the mechanical properties of
each component form the basis of the structural design of the railway track design. Figure
2.2 demonstrates the force distribution of different components through the superstructure.

Rail
Two parallel rails form the way of the railway track, where the wheels of the train run.
Hence, it is also considered as the most important structure of the track. The two rails are
held at a ﬁxed distance called as the track gauge. In Denmark the track gauge is 1435 mm.
There are different rail proﬁles used for the track structure. The most common rail proﬁles
that are used currently in Denmark are DSB37, DSB45, DSB60 and UIC60, where the

2.1.2
Fastening system
The fastening system is used to hold the rails on to the sleeper to maintain the track
gauge or the horizontal alignment of the rail. They also ensure transferring of the forces

2.1.3
Sleeper and sleeper pad
Sleepers (Figure 2.4(d)) are transverse beams that keeps the rails at the ﬁxed gauge.
Sleepers provide support to the rail and uniform distribution of stresses from the rail to the
substructure layers underneath. Sleepers are manufactured from different materials such
as wood, concrete and steel.
Sleeper pads: Sleeper pads are added to the track structure for the optimization of the
general design. Adding under sleeper pads (Figure 2.4(c)) can reduce the ground structure
vibration and also can protect the ballast from chipping, thus the degradation process can
be prolonged.

2.4
Filtering
Filtering in signal processing is deﬁned as a process used for removing the unwanted
elements or components from a signal using a device called ﬁlter. The various classiﬁcation
of ﬁlter on different bases can be linear or non-linear, time variant or invariant, passive or
active and the like. Based on the frequency response or the frequency band a ﬁlter passes,
they can be classiﬁed into High-pass ﬁlter, Low-pass ﬁlter, band-pass ﬁlter or a band-stop
ﬁlter etc.
The two types of ﬁlter used in this thesis are Butterworth band-stop ﬁlter and band-pass
ﬁlter. A band-stop or a band-reject ﬁlter is a ﬁlter that attenuates or stops the passage of
the frequencies in a certain frequency band while a band-pass passes only the frequencies
that are available in a certain frequency band.

Conclusion
The spectral analysis using Fast Fourier transform was performed for all ﬁve scenarios
(sensor A1 and A4) on the whole signal and also in segmented sections of the acceleration
signal. An inﬂuence of speed is found on the acceleration signal, the amplitude of the

the degraded ones. The frequency response of the healthy and degraded cases of ballast
appear to be the same with minute changes in magnitude regardless of different scenarios.
A classical Fourier Transform rapidly computes the transformation with 0 bandwidth. The
drawback of the Fourier Transform is that during the transformation it loses the information
regarding the temporal spread of the original signal, thus providing computational results
with zero time resolution and high frequency resolution. Therefore, Fourier analysis is not
suitable for analyzing the local behaviour of a signal.
There are window based Fourier Transform developed to overcome this drawback. Short-
Time Fourier Transform (STFT) is a Fourier based transform where it arbitrates the local
phase information and sinusoidal frequency of the original signal by fragmenting a longer
time series into equal segments and computing the FFT on each segments. And the whole
spectra is plotted as a function of time. It adds a time dimension by computing the product
of the sinusoidal and a window length, which is time localized.
From the review of the state-of-the-art of monitoring methods of railway infrastructure,
it was observed that in STFT, it requires several window size analysis to achieve good
time-frequency resolution (BABU et al. (2015)). The STFT uses a ﬁxed time-frequency
window and thus only uniform resolution is provided from the analysis and could be
inaccurate for signals with wide bandwidths that ﬂuctuate rapidly over time. An advanced
computational tool called Wavelet Transform with adaptive frequency resolution is used in
the thesis for further analysis of the signal.

4.1
Wavelet Transform
Wavelet analysis is a signal processing tool that has been used over the last 30 years.
In some cases wavelet analysis is complimentary to traditional signal processing methods
such as the Fourier transform. Fourier transform does not represents abrupt changes
efﬁciently. It provides only a frequency representation of the signal which is not localized
in time or space. Short-time Fourier Transform uses a sliding window to compute the
spectrogram and can provide the information of both frequency and time, but the length of
the window can limit the frequency resolution.
A wavelet transform decomposes the signal into bands of its time and frequency content
and can provide the space-frequency representation of a signal. The wavelet transform
can be considered as an inﬁnite set of several transforms based on the main purpose
of its computation. There are various ways how wavelet transform can be categorized
into different types. Based on the wavelet orthogonality, the wavelet transform can be
divided into two types, continuous wavelet transform and discrete wavelet transform. The
properties of the two transforms are as follows:
• The continuous wavelet transform uses non-orthogonal wavelets for its development.
The output is obtained as an array of one dimension larger than the input data. As
the orthogonal wavelets are used in this, the correlation of data is high, so large
redundancy is seen which makes it possible to see the results in more humane form.
The evolvement of the frequencies of the signal with the duration of the signal can
easily be seen in this.
• The discrete wavelet transform in contrary gives the output with the same length
as its input. It decomposes a signal into a set of wavelets that are orthogonal to its
scaling and translations. No redundancy is obtained in this.

Polynomial Regression
Polynomial regression in statistics is a process in which a relationship is modelled
between an independent variable x and the dependent variable y as an nth polynomial
degree in x. Polynomial regression forms a non-linear relationship by ﬁtting the values of x
with the corresponding conditional mean of the y denoted by E(y|x), using the method of
least squares. Polynomial regression can be used to describe non-linear phenomena such
as rate of growth of tissue or progression of disease epidemic etc. Although the polynomial
regression ﬁts a non-linear relationship to the data, in statistical estimation theory it is
linear, as in theoretically the regression function E(y|x) is linear in the parameters that are
unknown and to be computed from the data.

CHAPTER 7
Conclusion and Future Work
The thesis deals with devising a condition monitoring algorithm for the ballast layer in
railway infrastructure. Simulated data of the wheel-rail interaction from the high-ﬁdelity
simulation software were provided for the analysis in the thesis. Different data processing
and ﬁltering techniques have been implemented to obtain acceleration signal from the
displacement. The acceleration signal was ﬁltered in the ballast frequency range to focus
on the phenomenon in the ballast layer during the degradation process. Different analysis
have been carried out using various spectral methods.
The ﬁrst approach implemented was the analysis using Fast Fourier Transform to study the
frequency characteristics of the signal. Detailed analysis of the signal was achieved using
the Fourier transform by segmenting the signal and computing the FFT on the interested
segments. From the analysis, no discrepancies were observed on the frequency spectrum of
acceleration signal for healthy and different levels of degraded ballast. Also the limitation
in providing the temporal information was considered as one drawback of the method.
The next approach was using wavelet transform which gives a time-frequency represen-
tation of the signal, for the analysis. The further investigation was performed using both
continuous and discrete wavelet transform. In continuous wavelet transform, the analysis
was performed using the mother wavelet complex morlet with the bandwidth parameter
1.5. The discrete wavelet transform analysis was conducted using the mother wavelet db5
from the wavelet family Daubechies. From the continuous wavelet transform, no variations
on the frequency spectrum was obtained, but a phase shift of the signal was observed with
the increase in the degradation. However the discrete wavelet transform was not efﬁcient
in achieving any signiﬁcant results which could be used for monitoring.
A time domain analysis was executed next to observe the phase change of the signal. The
analysis results conﬁrmed the instantaneous phase shift of the signal as it transform from
its functional state to deteriorated stage. This instantaneous phase change was declared to
be the signature element that could be used for monitoring the ballast layer. A tool, Hilbert

test statistics known as Mann-Whitney and Mood. Even though the method was useful for
making a clear differentiation of the healthy and degraded ballast states, the results did
not seem convenient to use as it produced the same differentiation for different healthy
cases as well. The second approach was to use polynomial regression analysis by ﬁtting
the instantaneous phase of the signal with respect to time using ﬁrst degree polynomial
and monitoring the change in the slope of the instantaneous phase of healthy and degraded
case. The test was helpful in bringing a distinction between faulty and healthy states of
ballast. But the distinction between different levels of degradation was not possible with
this method as the conﬁdence interval of some degraded levels fall in the interval of other
degradation as well.
Finally a sinusoidal detection based method was implemented which keeps track of the
instantaneous phase of the signal and detects as a change occurs in the phase due to
the degradation phenomenon. This method efﬁciently detects even the smallest level of
degradation i.e., 5%. Also the classiﬁcation of different levels of degradation is achieved
with this detection method.
7.0.1
Future Works
• Real-time implementation: The detection method devised for deterioration of
ballast was based on analysis made on the simulated data of track responses of the
wheel-rail interaction. The data processing techniques should be implemented on the
real data obtained from the accelerometers. The cut-off frequencies for band-pass
and band-stop ﬁltering should be determined for the real data. The analysis on the
real time data should be executed with the monitoring methods mentioned in the
thesis. The detection method should be validated for the real-life data based on the
analysis.
• Normalization: An inﬂuence of the train speed is observed on the track responses
of the simulated data. Analysis should be performed on the real data to study the
impact of train speed and load on the real data. If found any, methods should be
implemented to remove the effect of speed and loading condition on the signal, thus
the alterations on the signal will be exclusively due to degradation.
• Improvement in detection method: The sinusoidal detection method can be further
improved by taking into account of unknown amplitude and frequency. Presently
==PAGE:93==
71
the method is solely based on the instantaneous phase shift of the signal, as the
amplitude in the time domain signal does not show signiﬁcant variation nevertheless
of degradation. The test could be further improvised to detect changes in amplitude
and frequency by considering it as unknown.
