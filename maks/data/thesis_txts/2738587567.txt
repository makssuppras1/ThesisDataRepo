Abstract
Natural Language Processing (NLP) is now a crucial component of Artificial Intelligence
(AI) and Computational Linguistics that analyzes and interprets unstructured data. This
research thesis explores the use of NLP methods in processing and comprehending elabo-
rate unstructured data from educational environments and social surveys. Specifically, it
focuses on converting raw text-based data into actionable insights, employing advanced
models such as BERT and RoBERTa. Consequently, this study seeks to deal with the
problem of interpreting massive amounts of unstructured feedback like student comments
or open-ended survey responses for better representation of sentiments or themes in texts.
The data source is taken from [1], which includes different types of interviews. I used
this data and I followed various methods for preprocessing, in order to remove unwanted
features from text. Then the text is analyzed by using sentiment analysis lexicon approach
and transformer models. These models are trained and tested, and the results are
compared with the human labeling to make sure they give accurate results. Visualization
techniques, including word clouds, sentiment distribution graphs and co-occurrence
networks are employed to present the findings in an accessible and interpretable manner.
The results show the applicability of NLP in sentiment and topics extraction from the
text revealing results that can be useful in the identification of efficient teaching practices
and the development of social policies. The analysis reveals the importance of experiential
learning, the effectiveness of workplace training, and the need for technological integration
in education. It also defines the issues related to data availability in relevant research,
language ambiguity, and applying NLP techniques to real-life solutions.
In conclusion, this research shows that with the help of NLP, the approach to the research
of unstructured information in educational and social environments can be radically
changed. This means that through complex models and visualisation methods, the
stakeholders are able to perform an enhanced analysis of the feedback, ultimately leading
to improve the decision making process and policy making. Future research directions
include the development of more sophisticated NLP models, the integration of multimodal
data, and the implementation of real-time analysis systems, all while addressing ethical
considerations and data privacy concerns.

Chapter 1
Introduction
1.1
Contextualize My Topic
Natural Language Processing (NLP), a growing subject in artificial intelligence (AI) and
computational linguistics, is critical for analyzing and interpreting unstructured data. In
the context of educational and social surveys, NLP provides unique tools and approaches
for extracting relevant insights from various types of textual input. This chapter of my
thesis is dedicated to explain the use of natural language processing (NLP) to process
and understand the complex nature of unstructured data, namely data collected from
educational settings and social survey challenges.
Recently, the importance of natural language processing in educational and social research
has grown significantly due to an increased volume of unstructured feedback, which
includes student comments, open-ended survey questions, and informal communication
channels. Traditional data analysis approaches, bound by the restrictions of structured
datasets, fail to successfully decode the rich and often complex information embedded
in such unstructured data. Herein lies the significance of NLP, which can be helpful to
transform raw, text-based data into actionable insights, thereby revolutionizing the way
educational feedback and social survey responses are analyzed and interpreted.
1.2
Initial Motivation
My thesis, ”On Summarization for Non-Structured Data with Applications to Teaching-
Learning and Social Surveys,” is focused on the possibility of transforming student
feedback in the field of education. Currently, the student feedback from traditional
methodologies is often very complex and unstructured. The complexity is not limited to
educational contexts but extends to social surveys, where interpreting large volumes of
unstructured data remains a challenge, as emphasized by Mellon et al. (2023) [2]. The
goal of the thesis is to apply advanced Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques,

Chapter 2
Review of Existing Literature
This literature review aims to explore the current methods and models used in sentiment
analysis, focusing on educational and social survey data. The review is structured to
address the research questions related to the application of NLP techniques, key themes
and sentiments in unstructured data, and the impact of these findings on teaching
methodologies and social policies.

Aim & Categorisation
2.1.1
Sentiment Prediction
This section is structured based on the works of Dhanalakshmi et al. (2016) [9] and
Edalati et al. (2021) [10], which provide a foundational understanding of traditional
and advanced sentiment analysis models. Additional readings are suggested for further
exploration of these topics at the end of the section.
Sentiment Analysis Using Supervised Learning Algorithms
Traditional Supervised Learning Algorithms:
• Support Vector Machines(SVM), which is used to categorize student feed-
back’s sentiment. The advantage of this model is the fact that it can handle high
dimensional data for classification tasks in textual data. SVM model is able to
extract the feature from the text, to classify the polarity and to identify the key
words related to the learning and teaching.
• Naive Bayes Classifier, which is a probabilistic classifier, which is used to classify
the text into positive and negative sentiments. It is efficient in large dataset, since
it assumes independence between the features.

• K-nearest neighbor(KNN), which is a non- parametric based model, which uses
distance as similarity metrics to classify the sentiment. In this research, KNN was
applied to determine the sentiment of a student feedback in terms of how similar it
to has been classified as before;
• Neural Network Classifiers, which is used to classify student feedback, since it
has flexibility to find patterns. The study used a neural network to more accurately
predict the sentiment of text by capturing complex relationships in the text.
• Gradient Boosting, which works by sequentially fitting weak learners, usually
decision trees, to correct the errors of previous models. Each new model aims
to reduce the residual errors of the combined model, enhancing its performance
iteratively. This method effectively minimizes various loss functions, making it
highly versatile and robust for classification tasks [11].
• Ensemble Models, which combine multiple machine learning algorithms to
produce a single robust predictive model. By aggregating the strengths of various
classifiers such as Neural Networks, Random Forests, SVMs, and Gradient Boosting,
ensemble methods often achieve higher accuracy and generalization compared to
individual models. This approach leverages the diversity of model predictions,
thereby reducing overfitting and improving overall performance [12].
In my research, I followed the approach outlined by Dhanalakshmi et al. (2016) [9] by
employing traditional supervised learning algorithms like SVM, Naive Bayes, KNN, and
Neural Networks to classify student feedback sentiments. Additionally, I utilized Gradient
Boosting [11] and Ensemble models[12] to further enhance prediction accuracy. These
models were applied to the datasets, in order to establish a baseline for sentiment predic-
tion, which helped in identifying key areas for improvement in teaching methodologies
based on detailed sentiment analysis.
Advanced Supervised Learning Algorithms: Deep Learning and Transform-
ers:
Apart from these approaches, there are more sophisticated models that have been
developed and proved to enhance the performance in the sentiment analysis experiments.
They are explained in details below.
1. BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers)
Introduction:
BERT, developed by Google in 2018, it is a significant break-
through in natural language processing (NLP). Unlike other models, which scan
through texts in one order (left to right, or right to left), BERT is bidirectional.
This means that BERT can consider the forward pass and the backward pass of all
the words which make it possible for it to learn the context of the word using its
context. This bidirectional nature of BERT is pivotal in capturing the full meaning
of a sentence, especially in tasks requiring nuanced understanding like sentiment
analysis [10].

==PAGE:19==

Application:
BERT’s architecture is particularly well-suited for sentiment analy-
sis in educational contexts, such as analyzing student feedback. Thus, educational
feedback has delicate hints and contextual conditions that should be detected in
order to determine the sentiment accurately. For example, a statement such as ‘The
course was difficult but beneficial’ has a positive connotation of learning despite
the difficulty that may be lost on basic models BERT excels in such functions due
to its capability to infer such meanings and context. In a study about student
feedback to MOOCs (Massive Open Online Courses)[10], BERT has been used to
evaluate multiple dimensions of teaching effectiveness and student satisfaction. The
fact that the model pays much attention to context makes the identification of
sentiments accurate and provides insights that can be used to enhance the teaching
strategies and course contents.
Performance:
This study [10], for instance reveals that BERT yields better
results than conventional models in sentiment classification. The paper titled “The
Potential of Machine Learning Algorithms for Sentiment Classification of Students’
Feedback on MOOC” [10] utilised bidirectional, deep learning and the BERT ap-
proach to improve on the accuracy of classifying the students’ feedback. This was
evident where the study showed that BERT provides much higher accuracy than
the earlier conventional models like SVM and Naive Bayes in dealing with textual
data. There were significant enhancements in performance that BERT offered, as
phases, captured the precise mood and context-specific connotations to the student
feedback than previous methods.
2. RoBERTa (Robustly Optimized BERT Approach)
Introduction:
RoBERTa is a model based on BERT which was created by the
Facebook AI in 2019, that is basically an optimized BERT approach. RoBERTa
is thus an improvement of BERT as it uses more comprehensive data as well as
fine-tune the hyperparameters. Some changes made in such cases consist of longer
training time, the use of larger batches, and the elimination of the ‘next sentence
prediction’ objective employed in BERT. These optimisations make RoBERTa
better and enhance its capability for the range of functions such as the sentiment
analysis.
Application:
In sentiment analysis specifically, RoBERTa’s highly refined train-
ing methodology provides it with a better sense of contextual cues in language
when compared with BERT. This is particularly helpful to elaborate feedback
especially in an educational environment where feedback information may be rich
in information nuances. In particular, compared with simple automated analysis of
rubrics, RoBERTa can better judge the sentiment of student feedback to course
such that it can more clearly distinguish between when the word choice implies
dissatisfaction or praise, allowing for better sentiment classification. Due to its

capability of analyzing complicated languor patterns, it is particularly helpful for
accurate contextual analysis, which can be useful in multi-phased assessments of the

Performance:
Studies have shown that RoBERTa is usually better than BERT
in aspects of performance especially in those situations that involve complex
and subtle sentiment. As highlighted in the study conducted by Edalati et al.
(2023)[10], RoBERTa has been found to perform better on most of those tasks
related to sentiment classification since its training process is more exhaustive.
In the same paper entitled ‘The Potential of Machine Learning Algorithms for
Sentiment Classification of Students’ Feedback on MOOC’ made available at [10],
RoBERTa model was again revealed to have enhanced features in delivering a
significantly high accuracy in handling the complex aspects of students’ feedback
as opposed to BERT. Which makes RoBERTa more viable for most precise and
sentiment analysis applications that require a better understanding of sentiment.
Summary:
Since the advent of deep learning models such as BERT in specific and
RoBERTa in general, the overall approach to sentiment analysis has transitioned from
conventional supervised learning method to deep learning based models. The models
that have been developed in the past few years have laid new standards in this area of
study especially in the context of text analysis. This makes analyses of the sentiments
important in tasks such as student feedback analyses of the sentiment as a virtue of
BERT’s bidirectional context comprehension and RoBERTa’s fine-tuned training process.
When using these models as the components of the sentiment analysis approaches, the
researchers and practitioners will operate at a higher level of accuracy and will obtain
more valuable insights from the textual information [10].
In my work, I used advanced models such as BERT and RoBERTa to improve the
accuracy and depth of sentiment analysis, following Edalati et al. (2021)[10]. These
models were fine-tuned on the feedback data to capture more nuanced sentiments and
context, enhancing the understanding of student emotions and experiences. This approach
ensures that my analysis captures the complexity of student feedback, providing more
actionable insights.

Feature Extraction For model training:
The feedback was analysed to gain infor-
mation about the examination, teaching and other aspects of the learning experience.
These features used as the base for training the predictive models. While traditional
methods focus on word frequency and importance, modern techniques such as BERT
embeddings capture the deep contextual meaning of text, leading to more accurate
sentiment predictions.
To address class imbalance in the dataset, techniques such as SMOTE (Synthetic Minority
Over-sampling Technique) were used. SMOTE helps in balancing the labeled dataset by
creating synthetic samples for the minority class, thereby improving the performance
and robustness of the predictive models [13].
• Traditional Features: There are many forms of features transformation, one

more frequently in positive comments are identified as positive, while negative input is
labeled as negative. The polarity is determined using a statistical process, which ensures
that the sentiment score for each word is correct.
Polarity Determination:
Each word is polarity assigned positive or negative based
on the frequency that appears in this type of feedback. If a word appear equaly in both
categories, it is assigned as positive by default.

4.4
Discussion
The analysis conducted on the unstructured data collected from student feedback and
social surveys has provided valuable insights into the sentiments and themes prevalent
in the feedbacks. By applying various NLP techniques, we were able to identify key
patterns and extract meaningful information that can be utilized to improve teaching
methodologies and inform social policies.
• Sentiment Patterns: The sentiment analysis revealed a predominance of neutral
sentiments in the feedback, with positive sentiments being more frequent than
negative ones. The use of advanced models like RoBERTa demonstrated high
accuracy and reliability in classifying sentiments, aligning closely with human-
labeled data.
• Key Themes: Thematic analysis identified several recurring topics, such as the
importance of experiential learning, the effectiveness of workplace training, and
the need for technological integration in education. These themes highlight areas
where improvements can be made to enhance the learning experience and address
the concerns of students and survey participants.
• Model Performance: Advanced NLP models like BERT and RoBERTa outper-
formed traditional models in sentiment classification, capturing the small details
and context-dependent sentiments expressed in the feedback. The comparison
between human and automated labeling underscored the importance of integrating
human insights with machine learning techniques to achieve more accurate results.
• Visualization: Effective visualization techniques, including word clouds, sentiment
distribution graphs, and co-occurrence networks, played a crucial role in interpreting
the data. These visualizations helped to identify key themes and patterns, making
the insights more accessible and actionable for stakeholders.
Implications for Education and Social Policy:
The findings from this research
have several implications for education and social policy:
• Improving Teaching Methodologies: The insights gained from student feedback
can be used to refine curriculum development, enhance instructor performance, and
increase student engagement. By addressing the common themes and sentiments
expressed in the feedback, educators can tailor their teaching strategies to better
meet the needs of their students.
• Informing Social Policies: The analysis of social surveys provides valuable
information for policymakers. By identifying the key issues and concerns raised by
the public, policies can be formulated to address these needs more effectively. The
integration of NLP techniques with traditional policy-making processes can lead to

• Enhancing Feedback Mechanisms: The use of NLP and visualization techniques
can improve the effectiveness of feedback mechanisms in educational institutions and

their stakeholders.
Challenges and Limitations:
Despite the advancements and benefits of NLP tech-
niques, there are several challenges and limitations to consider:
• Data Availability and Quality: Access to high-quality and comprehensive
datasets remains a significant challenge. Data confidentiality and privacy concerns
can limit the availability of relevant data for analysis.
• Nuances in Language: Capturing the subtleties and context-dependent meanings
in unstructured text remains a complex task. While advanced models like BERT
and RoBERTa show promise, there is still room for improvement in accurately
interpreting nuanced sentiments and idiomatic expressions.
• Human Labeling: The process of manually labeling data is time-consuming and
resource-intensive. Balancing the need for accurate human annotations with the
efficiency of automated methods is a continual challenge.
• Integration and Application: Implementing the findings from NLP analysis into
practical applications, such as curriculum design or policy formulation, requires
careful consideration and collaboration among stakeholders. Ensuring that the
insights are effectively applied to achieve the desired outcomes is crucial.
Future Research Directions:
Future research can build on the findings of this study
by exploring the following areas:
• Enhanced NLP Models: Continued development and refinement of NLP models
to better capture the nuances and context of unstructured text will improve the
accuracy and reliability of sentiment analysis.
• Multimodal Data Integration: Integrating textual data with other data types,
such as audio and video, can provide a more comprehensive understanding of
feedback. Exploring multimodal analysis techniques can enhance the richness of
the insights gained.
• Real-time Analysis: Developing systems for real-time sentiment analysis and
feedback interpretation can help institutions respond more promptly to emerging
issues and trends.
• Ethical Considerations: Addressing ethical concerns related to data privacy
and the responsible use of NLP technologies is essential. Research should focus on
developing frameworks and guidelines to ensure ethical practices in data collection
and analysis.
