Project plan for Dea’s Master Thesis 
 
Below is a description of the project plan, divided into 5 work-packages. 
WP1 
Literature research 
WP1.1 
Research literature for concepts relevant for the thesis such as genetics, polygenic risk scores 
and machine learning.
WP1.2 
Research literature for which disorders are already known to be associated with pregnancy loss. 
WP1.3 
Identify relevant methods for calculation of polygenic risk scores in the COPL WGS data. 
WP2 
Dataset preparation 
WP2.1 
Use a pre-existing model to calculate polygenic risk scores for the COPL participants with WGS 
data.
WP2.2 
Collect the polygenic risk scores as well as other factors such as biomarkers into one dataset. 
WP2.3 
Prepare the dataset for machine learning, this could include handling of missing values, 
dimensionality reduction, partition of the dataset into training and test sets and PCA analyses. 
WP3 
Modelling 
WP3.1 
Test out relevant machine learning algorithms for prediction of factors associated with 
pregnancy loss, such as ploidy-status.
WP3.2 
Apply explainable AI methods such as a SHAP-analysis to find out which factors the model 
found to be important. 
WP4 
Comparison of model to literature 
WP4.1 
Compare the findings of the explainability of the model to existing knowledge of disorders 
associated with pregnancy loss. 
WP5 
Write the report 
WP5.1 
Write introduction 
WP5.2 
Write methods 
WP5.3 
Write results, discussion and abstract
