Abstract 
Due to recent progresses in web technology, nowadays, there are many platforms in which to 
advertise a job and search and hunt the best candidate. The prevailing view is that access to 
diverse talent is a dilemma for the current businesses. However, diverse talent is out there, e.g., 
Denmark performed above the European averages in terms of female doctoral graduates. 
Statistical data reveals that 49% of doctoral graduates in Denmark are women. Therefore, the 
real challenge is to capture that talent through removing pipeline problems, mainly at 
recruitment phase by reducing unconscious biases. DivERS, Diverse Employee Recruitment 
System, is an integrated computer system that allows to find the most suitable applicant for the 
position, through a series of questions and reducing possible biases as gender, age, etc. DivERS 
has been awarded as the best digital startup in Venture Cup Denmark, even it started with a 
prototype system. The prototype so it had been made without taking User Experience and User 
Interface (UX/UI) into account. This master project aims to redesign DivERS system, applying the 
values and principles of the Design Thinking (DT) methodology, in order to guarantee an 
improved UX/UI Design. 
This project not only focuses on the application of DT in UX/UI design, but also investigates and 
experiments an optimal experience for users. Investigating users’ opinion, a direct, constant, 
and iterative design must be maintained, since DT is a cyclical process based on the observation 
of human behavior to solve problems. DT applied to UX/UI Design allows to understand the 
needs and make life easier for users through techniques that humanize human-centric 
technology development. 
This thesis follows a process with iterative designs and the results will be demonstrated in the 
system due to the constant developments of the system.  The redesign is tested by users and 
leads to improvement of the system. 
Finally, we evaluate the results and propose the future development of DivERS. The references 
consulted for the preparation of this thesis and the annexes made are included. 
 
Keywords: User Experience (UX), User Interface (UI), Design Thinking (DT), Human-centric, 
Design, Software Development

Preface 
This thesis is submitted to the Department of Mechanical Engineering at the Technical University 
of Denmark, in fulfilment of the requirements to obtain a Master's Degree in Design Engineering 
from Universidad Politécnica de Valencia, UPV. The project was conducted in collaboration with

Chapter 1 Introduction 
In a decade of great IT expansion, the design and development of web pages are constantly 
evolving over time. Design can be applied through different processes, methodologies and work 
techniques. However, the advantages that can be found in software development when the 
design focuses on the user experience are indisputable. This thesis is focused on using different 
tools to improve and to redesign a current software system for getting an improved user 
experience (UX). 
“User experience (UX) is everything that touches upon 
 your experience with the product or service” 
- 
Norman Nielsen 
 
With the help of design methodologies, as Design Thinking (DT), and taking User Experience 
Design into account, we approach our users in a more empathetic way in order to understand 
their real needs and the details that allow us to provide an appropriate solution. Design Thinking 
(DT) is a human-centered process for creative problem solving that large companies are using to 
speed up and promote innovation in an efficient and successful way (Liedtka, 2014). It is closely 
linked to what is known as UX and UI Design, two important concepts to increase user 
satisfaction, and therefore increase the number of users who use the service.  
It is increasingly important that the service offered to the user be as optimal and successful as 
possible. To generate the interface and user experience of our case study, which is software as 
a service company, a process and application will be developed where the users’ needs and 
problems will be understood. The Design Thinking phases will be used in corresponding to user 
experience design phase, focusing on the first phase of them to understand the context in which 
we find ourselves and to focus on the users’ final experience. 
User experience can be measured by how easy, simple, or intuitive it is to operate or navigate 
the product, system, or service. In our case, user experience will be measured when users use 
DivERS. The users will confirm if the UX is favorable, pleasant and comfortable. Usability and UX 
are two terms that go together to achieve good usability it is essential to guarantee a positive 
user experience.  
The aim of DivERS is to reduce unconscious biases during the shortlisting phase. It can be used 
in two different contexts: one of them is takes when companies are hiring for the best and 
qualified applicant, so that they formulate the specific questions for the job, being able to 
compare between them; the other one is when the applicants are using the system by answering 
the questionnaire. In both of the cases, it is important that the system provides users with a 
good experience and functionality. Thus, it will be tested with real users and their experiences 
will be evaluated in the different iterations of the system development. 
The development and result of this project will allow to offer an improved system in accordance 
with the DT methodology and under the UX and UI criteria.

1.1 Objectives and methodology 
1.1.1 Main objectives 
Although this project has different objectives, we can summarize that the main objective is the 
following: 
- 
Improve UX/UI to redesign a web-based software system, based on Design Thinking 
principals. 
We will apply the DT to develop and redesign the interface of the DivERS software, 
guaranteeing an improved experience for all its users. 
 
1.2.1 Specific objectives 
Accordingly, the thesis has some specific objectives which are: 
- 
To provide an improved user experience for a software. 
- 
To produce and offer an improved system, through UX/UI Design, covering various type 
of customers from different industries (business to business, B2B). 
- 
To produce a completed design of user interface (UI) so that applicants can operate and 
interact with the system properly. 
- 
To redesign the system interface by testing and to guarantee a unique experience for 
the users.  
In this report, the process to reach the final design of the system will be present. In fact, to 
achieve our objectives and to prove that the service is intuitive and correct to be used, the 
system will be testing by potential users. 
1.3.1 Case company 
DivERS, empowered by advanced technology, enables companies to increase diversity and 
inclusion at the very early stages of recruitment which is shortlisting. It ensures transparent and 
unbiased recruitment through standardization of a fast, blind and efficient process. The 
integrability of DivERS in the available recruitment platforms is crucial because it can accelerate 
their implementation and remove the extensive training of a whole new system. The prototype 
and first version of DivERS have been implemented using state-of-the-art web technologies 
(Videos: https://bit.ly/2Z4cS0Q). The aim is not to disrupt the established recruitment tools 
(Applicant Tracking Systems) in the organizations; rather, DivERS can be integrated as an add-
on to ATSs. Moreover, DivERS solution is a digitized scoreboard approach. Compared to 
conventional scoreboard approaches that are extremely time-consuming and prone to human 
biases, our approach is completely blind and the whole process of forming the scoreboard, 
obtaining the applicant’s information, and generating the scoreboard outcome is performed 
automatically.  With a focus on customer needs, we employ the Design Thinking approach to 
further develop our solution. 
DivERS, is a startup that began being tested and validated by the DTU. Although multiple 
potential customers have shown interest in it, the first version of the software is made up of 
basic features.

1.4.1 Research questions 
As shown throughout this report, the research aims to offer an experience for the user of DivERS 
software interface through the application of the Design Thinking methodology. This topic has 
been formulated from an existing prototype system, to be improved for users. It aims to be used 
constantly for the recruitment process at the companies. The companies will use this system for 
the shortlisting to generate objective questions for the applicant. Besides, the applicant will use 
this system only to answer the required questions when applying. The following main research 
questions have been formulated: 
Q1: How to integrate the DT principals involved with UX/UI Design in a software development? 
Q2: Does DivERS platform provide users with a desired and user-friendly solution? 
1.2 Chronology of activities

period of five months and in order to develop this project. It has begun with a stage of 
exploration and reading of the literature related to the topics of the thesis. Then, the system is 
designed through the application of the DT and principles of the UX / UI Design. After the 
development of this redesign, a series of interviews were conducted with potential users of the 
platform, and finally, the analysis and results are presented.

the literature, we began to observe the clients and analyze our target audience, using various 
techniques offered by design thinking. Meanwhile, we were able to obtain a collection of data 
to redesign the prototype of the system. Meanwhile, the interviews were conducted in parallel 
to obtain a previous opinion before to the final redesign. The possible ideas for improvement 
and the final opinions will be observed in the last stage of the project during February, which 
will be the guidelines for changes in development and future decisions of DivERS.

2.1 User Experience  
This section defines the UX design, also known as User Experience, and UI design and its 
difference with UX design. In addition, the commercial value of UX design in industrial 
companies is analyzed. Moreover, the most common methodologies are introduced. 
2.1.1 Definition of UX Design 
First, to define the term UX design, several definitions of user experience experts have been 
discussed. Don Norman and Jakob Nielsen, creators of the Nielsen Norman Group, define User 
Experience (UX) as: “User experience encompasses all aspects of the end-user's interaction with 
the company, its services, and its products”(Norman Nielsen, 2015).  
On the other hand, Laura Klein defines the concept as (Klein, 2013a): “The UX is what the user 
is going to do and how he/she is going to do it, as well as what the product is going to look like”. 
Moreover, Marc Hassenzahl y Noam Tractinsky point it out as: "The user experience is the 
consequence of the internal state of the user, the characteristics of the designed system and the 
context in which the interaction occurs” (Hassenzahl and Tractinsky, 2006). 
Even though the above definitions are not the same, they all share a series of similar words or 
concepts: user experience, product, service, interaction, and process. Therefore, we highlight 
that UX design includes the design processes in which people interact with objects, products or 
services, including what, how, where, why and how they use them. 
Besides, designers must know that they do not design for themselves, but for a user. 
Accordingly, it is important to know the users in order to correctly satisfy their needs and, not 
only offer solutions for their problems, but also present user’s preferred and designs to key 
stakeholders (Roto et al., 2021). 
The UI design, also called User Interface, is another concept which should be differentiate from 
UX design. UI design is the process of designing the visual and interactive properties of a 
product's digital interface so that it is clear and easy for the user to navigate; turn it aesthetically 
pleasing. UI normally covers visual design, UX design and graphic design (IDF, 2018). 
Ken Norton, Partner at Google Ventures, Ex-Product Manager at Google, differentiates them as 
follows: “UX is focused on the user’s journey to solve a problem; UI is focused on how a product’s 
surfaces look and function” (Stevens, 2019). This is why UI designers work closely with user 
experience (UX) designers during the digital design process. While the UX designer is in charge 
of the acquisition and integration process of a product including the brand, design, usability and 
function aspects, the UI designer will transform the information into a digital design consisting 
of a palette of colors, typography and shapes, among others (IDF, 2018). 
The importance of the value of UX Design in a company is remarkable, since not only design 
factors have to be taken into account such as: usability, accessibility, graphic design, usefulness 
or content quality, but also extend the design to the emotional and sentimental to the 
constitution and diffusion of the brand (Curralo and Paiva, 2017). 
==PAGE:20==
                                                        
                                  
 
 
20 
 
In short, the value of UX design is present in companies since it focuses on meeting the 
satisfaction and needs of users resulted in positive experiences that maintain customer loyalty 
to the brand or products. 
2.1.2 UX Design Methodology  
This section will focus on the most common methodologies: "Double Diamond", "Design 
Thinking" and "Design Sprint". The Design Thinking (DT) model is the framework to implement 
the project, so in the subsequent sections, Double Diamond and Design spring are discussed, 
and the five main phases of DT process will be analyzed in detail: Empathize, Define, Ideate, 
Prototype and Test. 
2.1.2.1 The Double Diamond 
The Double Diamond model is a methodology created by the British Design Council in 2005 (KU, 
2012) and tested by large companies to understand their design and innovation process. It 
consists of four phases: Discover, Define, Develop and Deliver; which are represented by two 
diamonds. The first is divided into two phases in which the problem is defined based on 
hypotheses, use cases and design principles; The second diamond is also made up of two phases, 
in which the points of the first are defined and with which one works to create a final solution 
(British Design Council, 2007). 
 
 
Figure 3: Double Diamond Methodology Schem (Chu, 2014) 
It should be noted that Double Diamond is not a linear process as the first part of diamonds is 
related to divergent thinking, as the most creative part and which works with unconventional 
associations. The second uses convergent thinking that focuses on precision to solve problems. 
Both have to come together to get good results. 
2.1.2.2 Design Thinking 
The Design Thinking (DT) model, unlike the Double Diamond, is made up of six stages, which are 
explained in the next section. It focuses particularly on the user and seeks to solve problems 
from the design. DT is a non-linear iterative process employed which is used not only by 
==PAGE:21==
                                                        
                                  
 
 
21 
 
designers, but also by great innovators in literature such as art, music, science, engineering and 
business. 
 
Figure 4: Design Thinking methodology scheme  (Sari and Tedjasaputra, 2018a) 
 
2.1.2.3 Design Sprint 
The last model to highlight is the Design Sprint methodology. It is a method created by Google 
Ventures based on DT, but focused on the time that the different stages carry out. It is 
considered that the process has to be completed in five days or in one week, so that the 
company can work in the most agile, efficient and creative way. 
 
 
Figure 5: Design Sprint methodology scheme (Mendonça de Sá Araújo et al., 2019) 
It is a process that accelerates decision-making by reducing the project risk, since it allows 
simplifying the design process of a product by conducting co-creation workshops and obtaining 
concrete and measurable results. 
Despite the existence of different UX Design methodologies, this project will focus on Design 
Thinking, which is analyzed in detail below. The main reason for selecting Design thinking is the 
ultimate goal of designing the IT solution for customers and based on their preferences and 
requirements.

2.1.4 The fundamental factors to get a successful UX Design 
Despite the amount of information on how to successfully meet customer needs, it is impossible 
to know which is the best solution. However, there will be four aspects that we reclaim to be 
fulfilled in redesign of our system. These are as follows: value, usability, adoptability, and 
desirability (Dang, 2020). 
2.1.4.1 Value 
The value is the common characteristic by which the offered products are crossed with the 
services required by the user. 
The company must guarantee the best value for the user so that it becomes a response to 
proceed with the interaction and encourage their loyalty. Customer traffic clicks in the system 
are related to demographics, customer sources, and conversion rate data. This data helps to 
know the advantages and disadvantages of the system in order to provide the correct solutions. 
The best value for users is the one that offers convenience, comfort and security to perform the 
action they need which is the DivERS platform in our case. Therefore, enough information must 
be provided to be fluent in user interaction. 
2.1.4.2 Usability 
Another very important factor is the usability of a website or system design, as it guarantees 
greater loyalty and satisfaction on the part of the user, since users will normally consider a better 
design that provides comfort and security. 
A system or website that presents a poorer design is prone to suffer a decrease in the percentage 
of customer retention, which means the loss of customers.  
2.1.4.3 Adoptability 
Adoptability corresponds to the ability to access between clients and the company's website or 
system lack of adaptability includes the factors that the user cannot access the system, or that 
the loading time of the system is long. These possible problems can create a very negative 
experience from customer's perspective, inciting a loss of interest on their part. 
2.1.4.4 Desirability 
This factor is very important to attract and inspire the user since they will be more attracted to 
striking images or videos that are related to the product or system. A good design where the 
striking elements are observed for the user, will generate a positive and desirable experience

the identity of the brand and promotes, in a sustainable way, the growth based on the value 
that users offer to the system.  
2.2 Design Thinking 
2.2.1 Definition of DT 
The method chosen to carry out this work is Design Thinking, defined by Tim Brown (executive 
president of IDEO design) as: “A human-centered approach to innovation that relies on the 
designer's set of tools to integrate the people's needs, technology capabilities and the 
requirements for business success” (IDEO, 2020). Hence, DT methodology has been increasingly 
used in recent years in the software industries such as Microsoft. 
DT is user-centered and action-oriented, seeking to provide solutions to the problems in a given 
workspace. Divided into a series of iterative stages, DT stands out for its advantages related to 
advancing and quickly testing hypotheses or prototypes. 
2.2.2 DT process methodologies 
Design Thinking is a user approach, compressed into 6 different steps (Sari and Tedjasaputra, 
2018b). They can be grouped into three large blocks (Tilmann Lindberg, Christoph Meinel, 2011) 
depending on the fluidity of the creative framework: 
- 
Exploring the problem space: it covers the understanding of the client's problem / need. 
Use cases and scenarios [not completely verbalized] are observed, instead of generating 
general hypotheses and theories looking for the problem and synthesizing that 
knowledge for the different stakeholders and their points of view. As a first approach, 
the persona and empathy map are made; persona focuses in illustrate at quick draft 
with some general data about our user; meanwhile, empathy map is created by 
observing, listening to user, as well as build up empathy (Michael Lewrick; Patrick Link; 
Larry Leifer, 2018a). 
 
• 
Stage 1: Empathize - Investigate the needs of users by having an empathic 
understanding of the problem you are trying to solve. 
• 
Stage 2: Define - Starting from the previous stage, it is about accumulating the 
information generated through analysis and observation to define the central 
problems that have been identified. 
 
- 
Exploring the solution space: DT is applied at this stage in order to find a large number 
of alternative ideas, creating a real and tactile representation through sketching and 
prototyping. 
 
• 
Stage 3: Devise - Generate ideas and challenge assumptions. Brainstorming is a 
helpful tool. 
• 
Stage 4: Prototype - This is an experimental phase whose aim is to find the best 
solution and identify possible problems. 
==PAGE:24==
                                                        
                                  
 
 
24 
 
 
- 
Iterative alignment of both spaces: The representations of the ideas are not only tested 
by the design team, but also by users, clients and experts. The DT enables to stay in 
touch with the relevant problem environment and can use this information to refine and 
revise your chosen solution path (s). 
 
• 
Stage 5: Testing - The design thinking is iterative; the prototypes are constantly 
evaluated. So, that the results can be used to refine them by going back to 
previous stages. 
• 
Stage 6: Implement - This is about putting the vision into effect. 
 
Figure 6: Problem and solution space in DT (Tilmann Lindberg, Christoph Meinel, 2011a) 
 
2.2.3 Roles 
During the DT process, the participants are distributed and organized into four types of roles 
(Vetterli et al., 2013). 
Design Thinking Teams  
It is a multidisciplinary team made up of 3-6 people from different areas of specialization 
depending on the development of the software. These members have different skills, roles, and 
curiosities so that they will be able to offer multiple perspectives on problem solving. Thanks to 
this, the chances of failure are minimized and the avoidance of overlooking an important aspect 
is enhanced (Stauffer et al., 2021). 
The people from this team are final super users and stakeholder, UI designers, developers, 
among others. At least one member participating in the DT process must be present in the 
development phase since they have knowledge of the phases of the process and can understand 
why certain aspects of the functional prototype are integrated. 
The multidisciplinary resources in the team plan and execute the DT sprints.  
==PAGE:25==
                                                        
                                  
 
 
25 
 
 
(Potential) Users 
During the first stages of the process, interviews/surveys are carried out and the different types 
of users are observed to facilitate the identification of problems and consequently the critical 
points. In the investigation phases, there are multiple user groups, but as the process progresses, 
a target user or user group is decided (Häger et al., 2015). 
Users must be observable in their environment or in the space of the problem to be dealt with 
and provide the information in order to be able to provide feedback on the prototypes of ideas 
and correctly direct the process (Michael Lewrick; Patrick Link; Larry Leifer, 2018b). 
The target group must be constantly receiving and giving feedback in the iterative 
development and testing cycles (Vetterli et al., 2013). 
Design Thinking Coach 
The DT coaches are the support of the teams in the process, their function is to ensure that the 
team concentrates on its work and in case of discussion, to be responsible for establishing a 
moderation to the possible problems of the team dynamics (Vetterli et al., 2013). 
The presence of a coach is necessary especially in the development phase to be responsible for 
all the tasks to be carried out. However, they can acquire a different presence in the process: 
- 
Available to the team at all times and will be called by the team, if necessary. 
- 
Present at certain times during the process. 
- 
The coach can be one more in the work team, but not in interviews or tests. 
Coach’s role is very important as it helps other people to understand and apply DT to solve many 
different problems. Therefore, it’s required to have experience in Design Thinking application in 
different settings and a good base in two or more disciplines. 
Corporate Liaison / Project Sponsor 
Corporate Liaison is responsible for defining the initial statement of the problem, with a view 
to the project and being the person who has the main contact with the Project Sponsor, with 
the design and development team of the project. The Project Sponsor chooses the problem 
statement according to the strategic decisions of the company (Vetterli et al., 2013). 
The manager or the customer representative must understand the DT's methodology, support 
and motivate the team, provide feedback on ideas and company prototypes, provide interview 
partners, and so forth. (Häger et al., 2015).

2.2.4.2 Lean Start-up 
According to Enric Ries, the father of the Lean Start-up method, a start-up is: “a human 
institution designed to create new products and services in conditions of extreme uncertainty” 
(Ries, 2011a). Entrepreneurship is associated with risk, about “90% of startups fail in the first 5 
years” as perceived in the US context (Forbes, 2015). 
In the early seventies, Lean principles were developed in Japan, in order to optimize production 
processes and reduce any waste in the process (Mueller, 2012). This strategy was a revolution 
in the automotive industry as it involves a reduction in human or material resources, as well as 
the elimination of unnecessary activities and expenses. 
Nowadays, making use of Lean principles have gained attentions in IT development. That is why 
Ries proposes Lean Start-up as a complementary method to Design Thinking and agile software 
(Dobrigkeit and de Paula, 2017), composed of "a set of practices that help entrepreneurs to 
increase the odds of achieving a successful start-up" (Ries, 2011a). 
This method is not only a process that helps product development but also supports start-ups 
that need customer-focused development in a way that is understood and adapted to their 
needs. The objective of Lean Start-up is to create a continuous feedback loop with customers 
during product development (Mueller, 2012). 
The Lean Startup methodology focuses on a three-step circuit that must be complete in the 
shortest possible time and with the minimum investment. It begins by creating a product, then 
the results are measured, and then it will be ended by learning. 
==PAGE:29==
                                                        
                                  
 
 
29 
 
 
Figure 8: Lean Start-up process model  (Ries, 2011b) 
- 
Build. The lean learning cycle begins without being clear from the beginning to the 
ending when a Start-up is launched since there is not enough data to create a product 
that adapts to what the customer wants or needs. Therefore, a minimum viable product 
(MVP) is created, which “is that version of the product that enables a full turn of the 
build-measure-learn loop with minimum amount of effort” (Ries, 2011b). MVP is a 
product with minimal functionalities that can collect the maximum amount of learning 
through customer validation. 
- 
Measure. The objective of this phase is to measure how consumers respond and make 
pertinent decisions from the data obtained. 
- 
Learn. This circuit allows us to know if the business is going to be viable, continue with 
it or pivot, which would mean a substantial readjustment of those ideas that do not 
work. The goal of the build-measure-learn process is to learn from a build built from a 
problem or solution hypothesis (Mueller and Thoring, 2012b). 
What Lean Start-up proposes through this circuit, once the hypotheses are made, is to use the 
MVP to know if the idea will be accepted in the market (using little investment) (Ries, 2011b). If 
so, the functionalities will be increased and if the MVP does not work in the market, it will pivot 
and a new approach to the business will be sought. This method is aimed at Start-ups to be able 
to learn in a shorter time while few resources are invested to implement innovative ideas, but 
without actually creating a company.  
2.2.4.3 Lean Start-up and Design Thinking 
The objectives of Lean Start-up and Design Thinking have similarities since both seek to identify 
the needs of the client to create the correct solution. Both methodologies aim at innovation 
through rapid iteration and a prototype made and tested. However, DT focuses on user-
centered (final users) methods, user research, and observations to solve wicked problems; Lean 
Start-up puts the focus on a customer-oriented (such as recommenders and decision makers 
who pays) to solve an unclear problem. In addition, an important difference between the two is 
in the testing part, since the DT seeks to fail as soon as possible to be successful later; In Lean, 
the term “pivot” is used, that is, fail fast so that when a hypothesis is incorrect, then it can be 
==PAGE:30==
                                                        
                                  
 
 
30 
 
updated and modified in the shortest possible time and tested again (Mueller and Thoring, 
2012b). 
Due to the characteristics of Lean and Design Thinking, different methods have emerged that 
combine them so that they are used in different phases of the development of a product or 
service.

2.4 DivERS 
2.4.1 DivERS company 
DivERS, Diverse Employee Recruitment System, is a Start-up created in 2019 by Sara Shafiee and 
Alireza Taghizadeh, with the aim of reducing the biases that are unconsciously generated when 
the employment preselection phase is carried out. Today there are problems challenges in this 
process, for example, a male candidate has a 30% greater chance of being hired by a company 
rather than a female candidate. DivERS aims to eliminate discrimination and therefore offers an 
integrated IT platform, which allows the recruiter to find the most diverse and qualified 
applicants for the position. DivERS will offer a database of unbiased questionnaires and the 
recruiter will be able to customize them according to her/his advertised career. 
The first step, the recruiter prepares a series of questions using the readymade templates, based 
on her/his work criteria. Second, applicants answer these questions while applying. Finally, 
DivERS will rank the applicants based on their qualifications. The recruiter will receive a ranked 
but totally blind list for shortlisting, as well as information on who is the best and most qualified 
applicant based on hard skills. 
==PAGE:34==
                                                        
                                  
 
 
34 
 
DivERS has been implemented using state-of-the-art web technologies and is waiting to be 
validated by the DTU in order to be purchased by potential customers who have already shown 
their interest. 
2.4.2 Product and service 
In the hiring process, three main steps are carried out: job advertisement, pre-selection 
(shortlisting) and interview. Although done unconsciously, the recruiter makes a subjective 
preselection of the large number of applications from applicants that it may receive. Some 
organizations seek for an automatic blinded shortlisting process, for this reason they have used 
technologies based on artificial intelligence to select CVs. However, Artificial Intelligent (AI) and 
Machine Learning, systems will present certain human biases. Therefore, the product and 
service offered by DivERS (Fig.8) solves this challenge guaranteeing to select the most qualified 
applicants under the required diversity objectives. 
DivERS offers an automated solution for making blind shortlisting in a fully controlled manner. 
A questionnaire is made based on impartial questions, blind to any human bias that may arise, 
be their gender, age, ethnicity, etc. The recruiter only has to enter her/his work criteria and can 
easily modify and test the set of rules and criteria required. 
The value of DivERS is trying to increase diversity and decrease exclusion by treating all 
participants equally during the preselection process. Thanks to this, innovation can be increased 
by up to 20% and performance by 64%. By standardizing this process, employers can evaluate 
applicants and obtain the most qualified ones, through transparent and impartial analysis. 
2.4.3 Impact 
DivERS seeks equal opportunity for job applicants and eliminates any personal preferences. In 
addition, DivERS supports organizations to provide an equal chance for all applicants by 
eliminating personal preferences. Hence, DivERS empower female applicants for job 
recruitment and promotion, which is aligned with the Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 5. 
Besides, DivERS promotes SDG 10 by including all groups irrespective of age, sex, disability, race, 
ethnicity, origin, religion, etc. DivERS will also promote inclusive and sustainable economic 
growth through diversification based on SDG 8. 
2.4.4 Implementation plan 
The DivERS implementation program focuses on the years 2019-2022. The first potential 
customer for DivERS in the next six months is Technical University of Denmark (DTU), that has 
been DivERS development partner since 2019. Currently, they are working on their second 
prototype, which is based on the latest generation web technologies in comparison of the first 
(Figure 11), based on a more basic development. In early March 2022, Umbraco will begin testing 
DivERS and integration with multiple contracting systems (Oracle) is expected to begin. In 
addition, the potential DTU Discovery grant will allow quick progress of the DivERS software.

Chapter 3 Application of UX and DT 
In this section, the application of User Experience (UX) and Design Thinking (DT) will be applied 
on our case study, DivERS. Therefore, we try to clarify and understand the problem from the 
beginning in order to give it a final solution in an iterative procedure. 
Focusing on the human being, the user experience generation process is subjective since the 
designer has to understand why and how the final user does things, as well as what their physical 
and emotional needs are. Therefore, the tools of the Design Thinking technique will be taken, 
applying each of its steps to its corresponding user experience design phase, focusing on the

Chapter 5 Discussions 
5.1 Result Interviews 
Although each of the questions has different answers, we can conclude that the feedback 
obtained from users is very positive. These users come from different professions and have 
different ages and work situations. 
We can classify the answers according to the type of question. Starting with the general 
characteristics of the system, eight questions were raised to find out how the user interacts with 
the interface. Well, we can confirm that practically all of them is satisfactory based their 
browsing experience, which confirms that the user didn’t have any major problems in fulfilling 
their purposes. In addition, only two people claim to have spent the browsing time as expected, 
the others conclude that they have spent less time than expected to carry out the actions. This 
confirms that they have been able to find the elements easily to complete the actions quickly 
and safely. 
On the other hand, there are two factors that have been rated by our users very high and they 
are: usefulness and functionality. Both are two crucial elements to offer a successful user 
experience. Therefore, we did support the users with functionality and usefulness that meets 
the objective of the project. 
As for possible problems with the system or the need for a help desk, five of them confirm that 
they haven’t had any difficulties. However, two of them have presented problems when 
interacting due to the non-response of the system. This is because the system has been 
redesigned at the same time and due to technical problems, some of the actions didn’t respond. 
This aspect is being improved. Another person has recommended a help or clarification option 
in case of doubt to perform the actions. This aspect will be considered for possible 
improvements. This is common in a system that is under development and they already have 
been informed about it in the introductory email. 
Five of them confirm that they use the system often while the other three would sometimes use 
the system. We can translate these answers as the users are happy and satisfied and therefore 
their response to the use of the system is positive in general. Another aspect that has received 
great effective responses is that both the actions and the search options by functions and filters 
are very practical and functional. Therefore, we can conclude that the user has been provided 
with a more practical search adapted to the needs of the client. 
To conclude with the answers to these types of questions, all of them confirm that the system 
has supported their expectations, which is a positive response since we seek to make their 
experience as pleasant as possible and configured to their needs. 
From the questions about the features, we have obtained the answers that are explained below. 
Firstly, the most used or relevant element of the system is the dashboard, followed by applicant, 
messages and job and the least users and admins. We hoped that dashboard would be the most 
important elements from which the user could add the job, modify or delete it. Therefore, it has 
met our expectations. Another element on which we have spent time studying how to present 
it in the system is the applicant option, since the user can obtain all the information, they need 
from them and even compare. It has been the second most relevant, therefore we consider that 
==PAGE:99==
                                                        
                                  
 
 
99 
 
we have approached the needs that the user requires. The rest of the functions are more 
secondary and therefore have had a lower response. 
The interviewees have considered that, in the job creation section, it is easy to create, modify 
or delete questions, as well as to create the questionnaire, which is important since it is one of 
the main functions to be achieved with the system and confirms that usability is optimal. 
Regarding the features modifications, six people think that we should do it basing on 
importance. Three and two people would redesign it based on positioning and legibility and one 
person based on clarify and coherence. Therefore, for future changes, we will redesign the 
elements of the system prioritizing their importance. 
Finally, the two questions with open answers have helped us to know specifically the technical 
failures of the system. On the one hand, the color range has to be improved or modified when 
the interface is converted to dark mode since it isn’t easy to read the information. In addition, 
the need to present information on job status must be considered, since one of the interviewees 
considers that it isn’t very relevant. It could be an option to eliminate it; if the user wants to 
know the status of the job, it can be notifications or information provided in the description. On 
the other hand, the sizes of the icons must be studied according to the size of the text for a 
better balance between them. Another comment that we can discuss is the one found in table 
53 (question 14), one of the interviewees recommends a connection between the system and 
public information of the candidate. However, the purpose of DivERS is the opposite of that as 
all unblinded information is still kept, we can’t add objective factors. 
5.2 UX/UI Design factors 
Based on Chapter 2 about what factors influence to guarantee the existence of the user 
experience, we can affirm that our redesign has fulfilled the proposed objectives to ensure a 
good UX and UI. 
5.2.1 Value 
This feature is present when the service meets customer requirements, ensuring customer 
loyalty. We can conclude that thanks to question 1: “How would you describe your overall 
experience with the system?” and the question 5: “How often would you like to use this system 
(in the future)?”, among others, the users are safe and satisfied with the system, assuring that 
they would use it often and therefore they would be current users of the platform. 
5.2.2 Usability 
Based on usability questions, our interviewees confirmed that they have found easy to navigate 
the system to a great extent, and that the options offered are convenient to find and use. So, 
we can say that the DivERS system provides comfort and safety to the user. 
5.2.3 Adoptability 
In general, the system presents some technical issues when the user has interacted with it, due 
to the fact that it has been modified at the same time as it was being redesigned. However, the 
system adapts to what the user requires to access the functions. We have gotten feedback that 
some of the actions haven’t worked, but in general they have been able to access and interact 
with it properly. 
==PAGE:100==
                                                        
                                  
 
 
100 
 
5.2.4 Desirability 
Although the user has found the elements functional and useful, none of the interviewees has 
highlighted that the system is characterized by aesthetics. In any case, there are certain 
repetitive elements to create certain actions, such as when the user wants to ‘add a job’ or apply 
or the filter options, which favor the attraction and ease of understanding by users and increases 
the confidence and loyalty. 
5.3 Design Thinking progress 
After the knowledge acquired previously about the different stages of the process, these have 
been completed according to the established chronology. Firstly, the DivERS CTOs provided 
information about the possible users of the system and the first stage of mapping, began to be 
carried out. For this reason and due to accessibility, people from our network were contacted 
to get to know them more closely and find out what their possible needs were. Thanks to this 
information, methods such as the persona and empathy map were carried out. At the same 
time, and in reference to DivERS, hook canvas, 5whys, among others, were made to also know 
what the company offers. 
On the one hand, the hook canvas model has made it possible to discover what is behind the 
user's automatic behavior so that they use the different applications that DivERS has. This has 
allowed us to know the strong and weak points (Dafo) of the system and what makes the user 
use it recurring. From the 5whys technique, we extract 5 answers to questions about the system 
to get closer to the main objective and clarify the problem we were facing. 
As for the user, the persona served us for a first contact with and the empathy map to find out 
what their concerns are and getting deeper into their feelings. From them we extracted three 
profiles, and we obtained information about their pains and gains so that we can comply with 
them after the redesign of the system, since we seek that the user experience meets their 
expectations. The customer journey has helped us to know where the user begins to interact 
with the system and which are the most repeated actions. When we redesigned, we started it 
where the user begins to navigate. In addition, we knew which are the most repeated actions 
(such as: the actions of going ‘back’ and ‘next’) so that there would be a certain similarity in all 
of them due to the number of repetitions by the user. 
Then, we started to log into the system and see all the available options. We began to redesign 
based on importance: applicant questionnaire, dashboard and jobs, and finally the applicants, 
users and notifications. 
When we finished the part of the jobs, two people were interviewed to find out their first 
opinions about the platform. The style and the way in which the redesigned elements were 
presented at this point would mark the rest of the system's functions. Therefore, it was 
considered to interview two industrial design engineers, currently employed, to find out what 
deficiencies and what were the strengths of the redesigned so far. 
Once these answers were obtained, some of the comments were applied to improve the system 
and the rest of the parts were finalized. 
When we got to the final result, the last six people, from different professions and ages, were 
interviewed. These results have been analyzed in order to be considered in the future design of 
DivERS and to confirm that we have met our objective.

Chapter 6 Conclusions 
During these five months of this thesis, we have been able to redesign the DiVERS software by 
applying the DT methodology in UX/UI Design. Therefore, the main research objective has been 
achieved, as well as the specific objectives proposed. Personally, I feel grateful for the help of 
my supervisors to acquire the knowledge used in the result of this thesis. 
 
Due to the exploration and reading that is reflected in Chapter 2, we have acquired great 
knowledge to be able to apply different steps of the methodology and to be able to complete 
each required stage to improve the UX/UI Design of the system. Since the system was a 
prototype, it was easy to know how to start with the redesign process in such a limited time, 
since most of the sections needed to be studied and improved. The platform had been created 
to reach the system functionality. 
 
We have conducted formal and informal interviews from the first moment we began to redesign 
the system, since we mainly need to know who we are targeting and who are the user and who 
will use the service. For this reason, we interviewed and applied the first step of the DT through 
the persona and empathy map techniques, among others. In addition, we apply some facilities 
to learn more about DivERS, both their strong and weak points and what is the need to cover. 
So that, during this stage, we were able to get a more personal view of user needs and possible 
gaps or requirements that we need to apply to the redesign. 
 
Moreover, considering the aspects that influence a better User Experience (UX), the interface 
was redesigned. The considered aspects are: value, usability, adoptability and desirability. 
During this stage and as redesign progressed, we received the first opinions from the users as 
an iterative process. We started by improving two of the most important parts of the system: 
the questionnaire for the applicant and the option to ‘add a job’. This redesign would conduct 
the design and style that was consistent for the rest of the other sections. We began to define 
more and more the components that are repeated in the system to achieve consistency across 
the platform. Our ultimate goal was that the users to navigate through the system and achieve 
their goal in the most optimal way. 
 
After a couple of months of improvements and changes, we finally defined the final result. We 
compiled all the information that the final interviews offered us both for a possible improvement 
of the future system and to know the degree in which we had come closer to our objective. 
Hence, we prepared a video to introduce the system, the link, and the username for the 
interviewees to access the system. For this, an interview of 15 questions related to UX Design 
was carried out, which have given us information about what are the improvements that will be 
maintained in the system and which ones should be improved since it is constantly redesigned. 
 
Therefore, we have met our objectives defined at the initiation stage of this project, and the 
thesis has managed to achieve and expand the conceptual and technical knowledge regarding 
UX/UI design, the methodologies that can be used to improve it, and specifically how apply the 
Design Thinking methodology in the redesign or design a product or service.
