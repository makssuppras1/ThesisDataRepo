This project proposes that DolphinEyes  ApS develops a haircut mask based on a reconceptualisation of the current 
DolphinEyesยฎ  bathing mask. It has been found that a haircut mask addresses the need of the hairdressers and this 
segment has been found to be the best choice based on economic feasibility and solving a need apparent in the user 
group. 
A practice analysis and observation of the hairdressers and customers (Children, parents, and adults) determined 
that the children are having a hard time sitting still and get hair in and around their eyes. This makes it hard for the 
hairdresser to cut the children properly.
Through user research, it has been proven that the haircut mask solves the problem with hair clippings disturbing the 
children during the haircut. Existing haircut masks does not fulfil the needs for the children and a new haircut mask 
has been reconceptualised based on the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask. Through research at hairdressers and data from 
measuring childrenโs heads, the reconceptualized mask has been designed to fulfil the needs of both hairdressers 
and children.
Two different sizes for the DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask has been developed, a Small and a Medium. The small version 
has been tested at hairdressers and found to be suitable, while the medium size has been designed based on the 
tested smaller version and then scaled to fit larger children.
To determine the economic viability of the idea, a business case has been developed, illustrating, and recapping the 
competitors, market, and the economics of developing the DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask.

Project brief
The DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask protects the eyes of children ages 3-8 against water and soap. With the transparent 
mask, the user can see during showers, which allows the user to keep their existing balance which increases the comยญ
fort and security during the shower. 
The project is done in collaboration with DolphinEyesยฎ. The scope of the project is to create a reconceptualisation 
of the existing mask and at the same time to create a feasible business case by reconceptualising the DolphinEyesยฎ 
bathing mask for the segment, that have the most potential, both in need and in economic feasibility. Research on 
the selected target group, competitors and different adhesives will generate new concepts that can be detailed to 
be as close to a market launch as possible. A business case will be developed for the concept and a selected target 
group.

DolphinEyes ApS 
DolphinEyesยฎ is a bathing mask, but it is also the name of a Company. The Company is owned by inventor and the 
founder, Helle Kayerรธd. Helle founded the company in 2011 based on her invention of the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing 
mask. The company is based in Nรฆrum and is 100% Danish owned.
In 2006 Helle experienced that her 2 children cried every time they had to have their hair washed. They cried becauยญ
se they didnโt like water and soap in or around their eyes.  She tried many solutions, among others she tried to cover 
their eyes with washcloths, swimming googles, but nothing helped and some of these solutions even meant that the 
childrenโs hair was not washed properly. Neither parent nor children thought the hair wash situation was enjoyable 
and it resulted in a lot of frustration and tears. This started Helleโs invention of the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask.
As a trained engineer, with a MBA and a HD, Helle thought she could do something about this dilemma. With 
her background in the medico sector Helle began searching for a solution and that was how the bathing mask 
DolphinEyesยฎ was created. 
Helle describes the development as:
โI quickly found out that there was no fast and easy solution. The shampoo mask needs to protect the eyes and yet 
not touch the hair so the hair can be properly washed. The easiest solution was to find a way to get the shampoo 
mask to stay attached to the face. 
I have worked with skin-friendly adhesives for many years, so I knew very well that I was embarking on a long 
journey, with lots of testing of adhesives, to find a solution that could both adhere to the face and resist soap and 
water, be easy to place on the childโs face, not hurt at all to remove, be skin-friendly and allergy free. It took 5 
yearsโฆ but then it was ready!" - Helle Kayerรธd (Dolphineyes, 2016)
Most people, children, and adults, that does not like to get their hair washed is afraid of getting water and soap into 
their eyes. The natural reaction is to close your eyes, but this results in a diminished balance and creates insecurity. 
DolphinEyesยฎ  counteracts this, as it allows the user to have open eyes during the bath, without getting soap and waยญ
ter in the eyes. (Dolphineyes, 2016)
The mask is made in Poly Propylene. The print on the mask is sealed in by a Poly Propylene laminate so that it will neยญ
ver be in contact with the skin. The mask is patented in USA, Japan and Europe and is CE marked (DolphinEyes, 2016)

Initial research on different RSGs
Approach
To get a better understanding of the mechanisms working in and around DolphinEyesยฎ  the project group has used a 
redefined version of the Social construction of technology theory or simply SCOT (Bijker, 1995) to frame the reconยญ
ceptualization and decide who the  design is for and how the mask should be used?
Pinch and Bijker define Relevant social groups (RSG) as a group of individuals who share the same interpretation of 
an artefact (in this case the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask), they are not necessarily a part of the same institution or 
organization, but they can be. Actors can be part of more than one RSG and each RSG has its own interpretation of 
the artefact. (Trevor J. Pinch and Wiebe E. Bijker, 1984) 
This means that there can be a lot of different RSGs for the same artefact and you could say that there are a high inยญ
terpretive flexibility. (Trevor J. Pinch and Wiebe E. Bijker, 1984) Interpretive flexibility implies that there are numerous 
different ways to perceive and interpret an artefact and not only one is correct but all are correct for the corresponยญ
ding RSG. 
The two concepts of closure and stabilization are closely linked. When the interpretive flexibility diminishes, or ceaยญ
se to exist and consensus is made among the RSGโs, closure has been reached. Closure removes the different RSGโs 
attributions of meaning towards the artefact and only leaves one remaining. When reaching closure a high degree of 
stabilization for the artefact will occur in one RSG and possibly more. (Bijker, 1995)
Degree of stabilization describes the way an artefact is developed and accepted within a RSG. This usually happens 
in degrees and over time. The more homogenous the attributed meaning towards the artefact is within the RSG, the 
higher is the degree of the stabilization  (Bijker, 1993). The degree of stabilization is different from RSG to RSG. It can 
grow and diminish over time (Trevor J. Pinch and Wiebe E. Bijker, 1984).
The term technological frame is also relevant for SCOT. Technological frames are not individual but located between 
individuals, in the interactions in the different RSGs. It consists of tacit knowledge, know-how, test protocols, scienยญ
tific theories, goals and cultural values. Technological frames cannot be perceived as a fixed frame, but are built up 
within the RSG as a part of the stabilization process of an artefact. The technological frame is sustained and fuelled 
by interactions and thereby ever changing. The frame provides the individuals in the RSG with goals, thoughts and 
the tools for action (Bijker, 1993).
RSGs can be identified in two different ways by Rolling the snowball or following the actors. In this report only Rolยญ
ling the snowball. When using Rolling the snowball, relevant actors are asked at the end of their interview who else 
should be interviewed to uncover the whole story (Bijker, 1995).
At the moment, there have been established a closure for the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask, with low interpretive flexiยญ
bility among the different RSGs. Everybody accept the mask as a bathing mask used for children during a bath.
During the project, the project group wants to increase the interpretive flexibility and make a problem-redefinition 
to create new attributions of meanings and new closures. To do this the SCOT theory had to be turned upside down. 
Normally one would start either with the RSGs and find the attribution of meaning or start with the attribution of 
meaning and find the RSGs.
For this project, it is not possible to find RSGs or the attribution of meaning for the reconceptualised mask, because 
it simply does not exist yet. Therefore, the project group has looked at plausible situations where a reconceptualised 
mask could be used in. Then RSGs and plausible attribution of meaning have been found. This means that instead 
of the attribution of meaning towards an artefact defining the RSG or vice versa, the project group has defined the 
boundaries for the RSGs and the plausible attribution of meaning for the RSGs. 
To qualify the assumptions, the project group has talked with actors that were thought to be members of different 
RSGs and asked about what attribution of meaning they would have towards the mask, rolled the snowball and found 
new possible actors. The project group has then corrected the assumptions and chosen the most interesting RSGs to 
research further

The different RSGs 
The project group has conducted several interviews, by looking at plausible situations in which a redefined mask can 
be used. The following are why and how the project group has considered the different RSGs. 
Adult
In this RSG, the project group got two men at 55 years and 65 years to test the mask, to see how it works on adults.   
The first person to test the mask is Per aged 55, who has dandruff. Per is using a shampoo, which will hurt his eyes 
if he gets any in his eyes. The initial thought was, that the mask could protect his eyes and make it easier for him to 
wash his hair. Per also asked his wife to try the mask. He described their experience with the mask: โIt was easy to 
open the packaging, easy to remove the strips and mount it on the forehead. However, we both had problems with 
the adhesive strip opening and allowing water to leak in.โ Another issue was blurred vision due to the transparency 
of the material. Per and his wife tried the mask in the size medium and did not think it was wide enough to get the 
adhesive strip to go to the temple as it is shown on the packaging (Worksheet 5 โ Per tries the mask). 
The second person to try the mask was Flemming who has cancer and is 65 years old. Flemming is treated with antiยญ
bodies, which gives him big open wounds and at the time he tried the mask, the open wounds were quite notable in 
the face-area. The wounds cannot be exposed to water, therefore Flemming puts water resistant tape over them beยญ
fore taking a shower. He describes his experience as surprising how the mask stuck and he did not get any water and 
soap in the eyes. However, he believed that it was not suitable for him, because he prefers the water running over his 
face while showering (Worksheet 6 โ Flemming tries the mask). 
For some reason, there is a difference in how the mask works/adheres on the skin of the adult test persons. This is 
probably due to some differences in the condition in their skin (wrinkles, texture etc.). Flemming for example, has 
extremely dry skin due to the antibodies. For Per and his wife, the mask in its current design was not a good solution. 
If the problems described above can be fixed they will be able to use the mask as intended and a shampooing mask 
will possibly be able to help them to avoid that any harmful shampoo get into their eyes. When considering how 
many people has the same needs as Flemming or Per, the numbers are estimated to be relatively low.

The Practice of cutting hair
Approach 
To better understand the hairdressers and childrenโs need for a mask and why both actors behave the way they do, 
the project group has used practice theory and studied the practice of cutting hair and the practice of getting your 
hair cut. By doing this, the problematic situations have been documented first hand and the project group got a betยญ
ter understanding of the process and steps involved in the cutting of hair from the perspective of both the customer 
and the hairdresser. At the same time the door has been opened for the opportunity of co-creation to be used later 
in the development of the new mask. It is important when using the results of the research later in the process, that 
the results show findings as close to reality as possible. Therefore, practice theory has been used.
When studying practices, it is important to understand the definition behind. Reckwitz defines a practice as:
โโฆ a routinized type of behaviour which consists of several elements, interconnected to one other: forms of bodily 
activities, forms of mental activities, โthingsโ and their use, a background knowledge in the form of understanยญ
ding, know-how, states of emotion and motivational knowledge. A practice โ a way of cooking, of consuming, of 
working, of investigating, of taking care of oneself or of others, etc. โ forms so to speak a โblockโ whose existence 
necessarily depends on the existence and specific interconnectedness of these elements, and which cannot be reduยญ
ced to any one of these single elements.โ(Reckwitz, 2002)
This means that almost everything we do, every day, all day can be categorized as practices. To eat, to sleep, to work 
etc. this is practices. This helps us as designers and researchers, because the project group now has a way of looking 
at these doings.  It is known that they are routinized and thereby they can be studied. 
When utilizing practice theory and observing the practices, the project group will automatically gain an intrinsic 
knowledge about future behaviour in similar situations. This also means that the project group can look at different 
hairdressers' and customersโ practices and understand their needs. Bourdieu introduces the concept of field, habitus, 
and capital, as being at the core of the practice itself.
Social Field: The context of the practice. Different social fields can be seen as the arena of the practice. The social 
field is a microcosm where field-specific rules applies to agents and institutions. These rules are not specific but tacit 
in nature and allow agents to forestall future tendencies and opportunities. Examples of social fields: art, careers, 
literature etc.
==PAGE:29==
29
Capital: To enter the social field, agents must possess a unique amount and structure of resources that they can put 
forward to be allowed to enter the field, this is capital. The capital also determines where in the social field the agent 
is positioned. Bourdieu names four different capitals: economic, cultural, social, and symbolic. The different capitals 
can be converted. Ex. When buying a book, economic capital is used in exchange for cultural capital.
โข	
Economic capital: a personโs wealth and incomes. Can be converted directly to money and property. More 
easy to transform into other capitals. This form of capital can more easily be transformed to other types of 
capital than the other way around.
โข	
Cultural capital: transferred by family and education. Primary cause for status in a social field. It exists in 
three forms: 
โข	 incorporated state: Intellectual qualifications, inherited or human capital.
โข	 objectivized form: Material objects, e.g. books, paintings etc. that are transferable in physical state. 
โข	 institutionalized form: cultural competence, e.g. a formalized academic qualification.
โข	
Social capital: a personโs entirety of social relations. Oneโs network and resources that can be legitimized by 
the family, group, or class membership and that allow access to material and immaterial resources, informaยญ
tion, and knowledge 
โข	
Symbolic capital: Honour and recognition.  Cannot stand by itself but consist of acknowledgment of the entiยญ
rety of oneโs capital on a given social field. E.g. other capitals are converted. 
Habitus: It is a complex concept of a personโs perception and how they think, perceives, feels, evaluates, and speยญ
aks. The habitus is formed partly during childhood, primary habitus, from the family by learning the parents' ways of 
thinking, feeling and behaving. The habitus is also partly formed by oneโs education at school and experiences in life, 
secondary habitus.
The primary habitus is imbedded in the person and never loses its impact, it dominates and influences the secondary 
habitus. The habitus is an ever-evolving concept that changes due to life experiences. The habitus is the concept that 
allows a person to cope with the unforeseen. It unconsciously guides the person in performing a practice. Habitus 
also ensure that the agent acts and recognizes according to the specific social field and it is rules. The habitus structuยญ
res the practices and reproduces social fields. (Walther, 2014)
According to Watson, Pantzar and Shove: โpractices are defined by interdependent relations between material, comยญ
petences and meanings.โ (Watson, Pantzar and Shove, 2012) From this a framework is proposed to analyse the pracยญ
tice.  This model describes what competences, materials and images are needed to perform the practice and thereby 
what is a part of the practice itself. 
Competencies/skill: Is the competencies/know-how necessary to perform the practice. 
Images/meaning: describes how the practices itself is perceived, by the individual performing it.
Material: is the artefacts or services that are used during the practice.
Using the CIM analysis and looking at the informant images, material and competencies as well as identifying the 
social field, capitals and habitus or the lacking of these will shed light on the elements of the practice itself and which 
elements are needed to perform the practice (Hauge, 2015). The project group has developed an interview guide, 
and observed hairdressers, while cutting hair. The project group has tried to take both the customer (children, adult 
and parent) into consideration, as well as the hairdresser during the observation. The CIM analysis will be made for 
all actorโs present and will show the different elements of the haircut practice and the observation will shed light on 
any problematic situations in the practice.

All the observations of childrenโs haircut performed by the project group, contained the same elements: an interactiยญ

a cape on, in most cases mounted the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask, sprayed hair with water to make it easier to cut 
the hair and then the hairdresser started cutting the child's hair. During the haircut, the hairdresser talked with either 
the child or small talked with the parent. The parent either observed what the hairdresser did or sat in the next room 
waiting. 
The hairdressers never had the same approach on how to get the child to use/try the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask. At 
Vivi Hair Design, the hairdresser Anja persuaded the child Lรฆrke aged 3, saying if she took the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing 
mask on she would look cool and the small hairs would not tickle her (worksheet 31 - Lรฆrke gets a haircut).
 At Peter Adamsen Hairdesign, it was not all children that would try the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask (Worksheet 28 -

mask, because they are in a โnoโ-phase. The children aged 4-10 wanted to try the DolphinEyesยฎ mask. It was obserยญ

said yes to trying on the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask, when asked before the haircut, both commented that it was

The project group observed that a 3-year-old child took the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask off before the hairdresser

When visiting Salon de Coiffure, their customers, which where +30 years, would not try the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing

All the hairdressers who have tried the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask on their customers suggest that the size of the

success and that it was easier to perform the haircut with the mask than without.

Key points from the Competitive analysis
From the competitive analysis of the market it can be concluded that 2 variants exist on the market. Handheld face 
shields and forehead mounted Face masks. The handheld shields are a substitute product for a haircut masks and 
the customers must hold the face shield up to the face during a haircut/styling, which can be irritating and frustraยญ
ting.  For the hairdresser, it can be hard to avoid hitting the face shield with the scissor and comb because of the 
thickness of the face shield material.  The project group observed, during the research, that the children have a 
hard time sitting still and it is therefore believed that the children will not be able to use the handheld face shield, 
because they will not be able to hold it in front of their face for longer periods of time. On the plus side the face 
shields can be used many times and are very robust.
It seems to be the facemasks from Facepro and Ecoheads that are most similar to the proposed DolphinEyesยฎ 
haircut mask . All three products have similar form and function. 
The project group does not find the haircut masks sold on Aliexpress and eBay to be as big competitors as the 
others. The general opinion of these versions (or most things sold on these platform) is that the consumer cannot 
always be sure the information on the sites are correct. There is no information on which adhesive is used on these 
types of mask and therefore it could cause a serious allergic reaction to the skin. Another incorrect information is 
that the Starmember face cap is using the material called FET plastic, which does not exist.

Design Brief
Concept
A haircut mask that will prevent children from getting hair in their eyes, and on their nose and face during a haircut. 
The idea with: 
The haircut mask creates a comfortable hairdressing experience, both for the children and for the parents, and makes 
the haircut easier for the hairdresser. A haircut for children without the mask is usually accompanied with a lot of 
movements from the children, which affects a hairdresserโs work. 
The idea in: 
A haircut mask based on the current DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask, thus creating a reconceptualised mask tailored to 
use during childrenโs haircut.
Market
Segment
Users: Children age 3-10 who are getting a haircut and the hairdressers 
whom perform the haircut. 
Buyer: The owner of the salons, who oversees buying goods and proยญ
ducts for the salons. 
The need
From research, it is seen that the children are getting hairs in and 
around the eyes and the face. This makes them move their head and it 
makes it hard for the hairdressers to cut the children and affects their 
ability to create a good haircut.
Image 
The image of DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask should be in line with the image of the DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask - fun and 
exciting. The colour palette needs to be similar to the existing DolphinEyesยฎ colours โ variation of blue, green, white, 
yellow and pink. 
Competitors
From the competitor analysis, it is found that Facepro is the only direct competitor in the Danish market. The Faceยญ
pro mask has the same function as the proposed DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask, but is only targeting adults. Based on 
research it is estimated that the potential market is relatively big and the proposed DolphinEyesยฎ  haircut mask is the 
only product known to target children.

Product and service 
Customer Pain
When parents decide that it is time for their children to get a haircut, it is always with some fear in the back of their 
head. Will my child be able to sit still? Will she be scared? Can the hairdresser cut her hair properly? And a lot of 
other thoughts cross the mind of the parents.
When children go to the hairdresser to get a haircut they are having a hard time sitting still and keeping their eyes 
closed. Children are naturally curious and would like to see what happens during a haircut. The children move their 
heads either to see what the hairdresser is doing or in some cases to avoid the scissor because they are uncomfortaยญ
ble or afraid of getting hair in their eyes.  
For the hairdressers, this is a problem. It means that they are challenged both in ensuring that the children do not get 
hair in their eyes and in performing the haircut to the best of their abilities.
Now the hairdresser tries her very best and sometimes it works out fine and the children can sit still and close their 
eyes, at other times the hairdressers end up having to stop the haircut halfway through or missing sections of the 
hair because the child refuses to do what it is told. 
There is no available solution, stopping the children getting hair in their eyes and at the same time allowing them 
to see what the hairdresser is doing. The solutions available on the market today are haircut masks which are all aiยญ
med at adults. They are too big for most adults and therefore also for the children. The solutions today extend out in 
the hairline which makes it very hard for the hairdresser to cut the hair properly, because the comb and scissor get 
caught in the mask.

Business idea
DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask is a haircut aid for hairdresser. The DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask is a new and innovative

the childโs forehead directly above the eyebrows. Here it will sit as a protective shield and direct any hair clippings

hair in or around the eyes and will ensure that the child always can see what the hairdresser is doing, which will miniยญ

The DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask is the first of its kind targeting children. The DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask will prevent

the child look fun. These factors added up will create a more enjoyable and fun haircut for the children.
The DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask will, since the children are enjoying their haircut more, make it easier for the hairยญ

The DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask will allow DolphinEyes ApS to penetrate a new and existing market. Now, no haircut

Further work
To finish the DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask and make it ready for production, further work is needed. Firstly, the final 
materials need to be decided upon. To do this, several different material samples from different suppliers need to 
be acquired. Based upon tests, the project group recommends using PET, but other materials with similar qualities 
should not be neglected and should be tested. The most important qualities are that it is transparent and see thยญ
rough and do not collapse under the weight of the wet hair and water spray. The material needs to be tested on how 
it feels when using it for a mask as well as transparency and price.
At the same time, an investigation should be launched considering if the adhesives can be bought cheaper at another 
distributor. If another adhesive is found which can perform in the same way and is cheaper this should not be overยญ
looked.
The reconceptualized mask and the final materials should be tested in multiples different salons as well as on chilยญ
dren of different age to ensure that all aspect of the mask are thought through and no problems arise during use. 
At the same time the project group recommends testing the size M mask on adults. This will give valuable informaยญ
tion about whether the mask can be used in the adult segments as well, or if it needs to be redesigned. The project 
group thinks that the size M DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask will be suitable to use for adults, and if so it, can be made in 
an unprinted version or a version with a smaller print targeted adults.
Afterwards, the print should be developed and designed to more specifically target hairdressers. Maybe other aniยญ
mals can be chosen that are not aquatic animals. The brand identity of the mask should be kept. Here the project 
group recommends using childrenโs focus groups to qualify the print and the colours, as well as spread the message 
among parents and children.
To find the most suitable producer of the packaging, further research should be conducted. As mentioned the project 
group recommend a similar solution to the self-locking post-box. It is important that a solution is found and produced 
which suits the mask in height and width and can contain 50 pcs of the masks. Also, the project group recommends 
that the print is printed directly on the box to give it the best feeling of quality.
When the DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask is in production and have been launched using the available outlet that 
DolphinEyes ApS possess, the possibility of making agreements with hairdresser wholesale companies (companies 
that sells product to hairdresser) should be considered. This will open up for more hairdressers gaining knowledge of 
the DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask.
When the DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask is launched and successful it is worth to consider if more products can be adยญ
ded to the product portfolio. Based on the RSG selection, earlier in this report, the project group recommend doing 
more research into elderly people and teenagers. Both have benefits. Elderly people have a need that can be solved 
by the mask. Even though the elderly who tried the mask were negative, they might be willing to accept another 
version. The teenagers were in general positive and teenagers are known to buy products based on recommendation 
from peers, design and novelty. If a DolphinEyesยฎ mask can penetrate the market of teenagers the number of possiยญ
ble buyers is large.

Discussion 
As shown in the Business case the project group think that the project is a Go. Throughout the project the project 
group has in general been good at reflecting and base all conclusions on the research and business opportunities. 
The project group researched different RSGโs and found a business opportunity -Hairdressers which was worth to 
research further. The project group could have done more research on the other (not selected) RSGโs but given the 
time and the answers received, they were deemed to be less likely to generate revenue. Especially the elderly is still 
thought by the project group to be interesting to work with. The project group should have found a group of elderly 
people where it would have been possible to observe a field test of the mask.
The project group has used practice theory to research the practice of cutting hair and has from this gained an intrinยญ
sic knowledge about the hairdressers Practices, habits and how they deal with problematic situations. This knowledยญ
ge has been valuable for the project group later in the project, the same is applicable for the Personas and user jourยญ
neys. From the research the project group has also identified that the need is real and that a haircut mask is usable 
for the hairdressers and children.
The reconceptualising of the DolphinEyesยฎ haircut mask has been limited due to the patent making constraint on 
how it can be developed. The project group could have chosen not take the patent into consideration and create a 
reconceptualization from scratch, rejecting the claims of the patent stating that for example it should have an adheยญ
sive strip etc. This would have opened op for a different solution space, but would also create a mask that was not 
covered by the patent thus making it harder to keep IP properties, and ensure to keep competitors from copying such 
a mask. This would be an economically risky decision and therefore the project group choose not to go this way.
The project group has had a hard time finding suppliers for both the adhesive and for the mask itself. The small numยญ
ber of suppliers that the project group has been able to establish contact with has not all been very helpful. In Hindยญ
sight, the project group thinks that contacts to suppliers should have been done much earlier and maybe this would 
have helped the project group to do a final material selection and have a bigger selection of adhesives. This late start 
has also hindered the finalizing of the writing process, and optimization of the final details.
The project group should also have taken the time to contact hairdresser wholesale companies to hear their opinion 
and the qualify the product even further. This could have opened for strategic partnerships and would have created 
an initial contact that could benefit DolphinEyesยฎ.

Conclusion

how the project group have solved the different learning goals:
โข	
Based on creative analysis and modelling, develop relevant models, systems and processes for how to solยญ

The project group have developed a morphology chart to help find the best solution to the identified user-needs. 
Furthermore, a usability test and matrix have been used to ensure that the reconceptualised DolphinEyesยฎ haircut 
mask satisfies the user need better than the competitors.
โข	
Delineate a complex, open problem, by seeing this in a broader professional and social context, and based 
on desk-research, field research and literature studies to establish Strategic Development options. 
The project group has based on the existing DolphinEyesยฎ bathing mask identified a number of different possibly 
paths for the reconceptualized mask. By analysing big data on possible target groups the field was narrowed down 
to a few concepts for which a market existed. Literature studies was conducted to further ensure the viability of the 
possibly concepts. Finally, field tests were used to tie together the idea and the practical real world application. All 
three methods provided valuable information and the triangulation of research methods ensured that a viable develยญ
opment option was agreed upon.

Research different user groups, identify meaning of attribution and create Relevant social groups, and seleยญ
ct a target user group and specification based on economic feasibility. 
In the process of selecting the most profitable user group, different methods for RSG selection have been applied. 
SCOT Theory have been applied to ensure that the selection of the RSG was based upon a thorough research to inยญ
crease the validity.
โข	
Create several concepts for the reconceptualization of the DolphinEyesยฎ eye protecting mask using diffeยญ

The project group have not used a decision matrix method. During the reconceptualizing of the new mask the projeยญ

that the decision matrix method did not feel right during the development, whereas the morphology was deemed to

Analyse and develop interaction between DolphinEyesยฎ eye protecting mask and the user groups. 
Throughout the development process, the end user has been a main focus of the group. It has been clear that all acยญ

mask should be able to deliver. Therefore, extensive user testing has been a focus, and the group has tried to incorยญ

so that the final concept was able to deliver as much value as possibly, given the constraints of the project

In writing, visually and verbally communicate analysis results, concept proposals and design solutions. 
The project group has focused on mixing different techniques of conveying complex idea generation in the form of a 
report and incorporating visualisations as much as possible.

Create a well-documented business case, based on user studies and economic feasibility 
A solid business case is the foundation of every business idea, therefore the project group has tried to encompass all 
the found inputs in order for the business case to deliver a true picture of the business side of the idea and allow the 
project group to recomen a Go/No Go

Create mock-ups of different solutions, based on ideation results.
