Summary (English)
This thesis concerns interior-point methods for non-symmetric conic optimiza-
tion. Similar to the symmetric case a scaling matrix is used for linearization of
the central path. In order to determine the scaling matrix that in theory gives
the fastest guaranteed convergence a semidenite programming problem must
be solved at each iteration. In the thesis it is shown that in dimension 3 this
problem can be solved by a simple bisection algorithm. The example considered
throughout the thesis is the exponential cone, and it is shown that every self-
concordant barrier for this cone has parameter at least 3. Finally, it is shown
that a sucient criteria for polynomial convergence of a class of primal-dual
methods for non-symmetric cones holds in general. Until now this has been an
open problem.

Chapter 1
Introduction
Conic programming is a special case of convex programming, but many opti-
mization problems arising in practice can be formulated in conic form. Promi-
nent among these are linear programming and semidenite programming prob-
lems. The conic formulations of these are within the realm of symmetric cones,
and hence the majority of the research so far has been focused on algorithms
for symmetric cones.
Recently the company MOSEK has released a new version of their large-scale
conic optimization software. Among other new features, the new version con-
tains an algorithm for exponential cone optimization. Many optimization prob-
lems arising in statistics and machine learning can be formulated using expo-
nential cones. Logistic regression is one example, minimizing Kullback-Leibler
divergence is another.
The exponential cone is non-symmetric and generalizations of symmetric meth-
ods are needed to deal with it. Conic optimization problems are solved using
iterative interior-point methods.
A vital ingredient of the algorithms is the
choice of socalled scaling matrix, which is used to linearize the set of equations
that determine the search direction for the next iterate. For symmetric cones
there are natural ways to choose this scaling matrix.
Tunçel has proposed a general method for choosing scaling matrices for non-
==PAGE:12==

symmetric cones. Dierent scaling matrices yield dierent guarantees with re-
spect to converge speed. The scaling matrix that leads to the fastest guaranteed
convergence is called optimal, although in practice it might not result in the
fastest convergence. To calculate this optimal scaling matrix one has to solve a
semidenite programming problem at each iterate. Alternatively one can use a
simple rank 4-update of an initial "guess" of scaling matrix to obtain a valid, but
not necessarily optimal, scaling. MOSEKs algorithm is based on the latter. By
numerical experiments MOSEK has demonstrated the eciency of their algo-
rithm. But a complete theoretical proof of the algorithms worst-case complexity
is still outstanding. On the other hand, Tunçel has proved that a certain class
of algorithms will have polynomial complexity under additional assumptions on
the optimal scaling matrices.
The aim of this thesis project is to investigate whether the choice and calculation
of scaling matrix for non-symmetric cones can be improved.
In addition we
will address several theoretical questions, where the proof, that the "additional
assumptions" hold in generel for all cones, gures most prominently.
The thesis is structered as follows: In the rst part we set the scene by briey
going through the theory of interior-point methods for convex and conic pro-
gramming. We then describe relevant parts of the MOSEKs algorithm for the
exponential cone. In the second part of the thesis we present the results of our
work and investigations.
Below is a list of the contributions of this thesis, in order of appearance and
importance:
1. By a simple argument we derive a lower bound on the barrier parameter
for the exponential cone. This shows that the barrier used by MOSEK is
optimal with respect to the barrier parameter.
2. A bisection algorithm for the determination of the optimal scaling ma-
trix for 3-dimensional cones. This result makes it possible to eciently
compare the performance of MOSEKs choice of scaling matrix with the
optimal one. The bisection algorithm is also used to analyze if the addi-
tional assumptions needed for polynomial complexity of algorithms using
Tunçel scaling are fullled for the exponential cone.
3. We prove that the additional assumptions mentioned before hold for any
cone in any dimension, as long as the barrier parameter is at least 2. This
shows that Tunçel scaling leads to polynomial time primal-dual algorithms
for both symmetric and non-symmetric cones.
To the best of our knowledge all these results are new.

Conic programming
Conic programming is a special case of convex optimization. It is a rigid envi-
ronment (in a positive sense) with many laws and symmetries, which makes it
possible to build ecient algorithms that exploit these symmetries.
In this section we x a lot of notation.
We will consider both the primal and the dual cones to be embedded in the same
space Rd and will treat the standard inner product as vector multiplication.
