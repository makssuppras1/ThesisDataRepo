Abstract
In today’s digital landscape, reading is vital for learning and staying informed, particularly
for students who spend extended reading periods on screens. The continuous use of
digital reading causes eye­related fatigue and reduces processing speed of information.
This study is part of the ”Reading the Reader” project that explores how different text
spacing formats(standard spacing, letter spacing, line spacing, and their combination)
affect reading performance, cognitive effort, and visual behavior. The main goal was to
identify whether these formation of passages could support more comfortable and efficient
reading experiences.
The research involved seventeen participants who varied by their age range from 20 to 62
along with different native languages while practising different professions and belonging
to various genders with normal or corrected­to­normal vision. The tobii eye tracker was
utilized to collect eye movement data included raw gaze data, pupil diameter, time stamps
and other relevant information. This data was subsequently processed into fixation du­
ration, fixation rate, saccade amplitude, pupil size, and regression ratio using the I2MC
algorithm. Participants read randomized passages using different spacing formats on a
site designed specifically for the research, and comprehension was measured through
multiple­choice questions.
Primary analysis had done with the eye tracking metrics which disclosed that fixation dura­
tion showed greater stability under the combined(sd=32.82) and letter spacing(sd=27.53)
condition compared to standard (sd=46.48) and line spacing(sd=41.2) condition, suggest­
ing more uniform cognitive processing when letter spacing only and combined spacing
were applied. Similarly, fixation rate expressed lower variability in the combined spacing
(sd=0.43) and letter spacing(sd=0.51) condition compared to standard (sd=0.58) and line
spacing (sd=0.9) condition reflecting more smoother and more consistent eye movement
patterns. Other metrics provided differences similarly for the spacing conditions. The sta­
tistical analysis for seeking deep insights based on t­tests, Linear Mixed Models (LMMs),
and ANOVA revealed that letter spacing and combined spacing significantly improved
reading efficiency compared to standard and line spacing by addressing p­value(p<0.05).
Tukey’s post hoc analysis was also conducted for more conservative pairwise compar­
isons.
The smaller standard deviation and p­value confirms that letter and combined spacing
formats simultaneously maximize reading efficiency along with reader performance con­
sistency.
Finally, qualitative user feedback was collected, displaying that participants
preferences of comfortable reading did not always match with their actual reading per­
formance, highlighting that perceived ease does not necessarily lead to a better reading
outcome.

Introduction
In an increasingly digital world, the development of reading established one of the main
approaches for people to access information. A majority of scientific research into reading
behavior throughout decades has focused on particular groups either through studies
involving children or students or people with visual impairments while also conducting
research using artificial controlled conditions. Real­world readers consist of individuals
who possess various language skills and professional backgrounds while having different
ages and vision capabilities as they use text digitally in various natural settings. This
thesis aims to address the gap by examining how text spacing, an important yet frequently
overlooked aspect of digital typography, influences reading behavior among a wide range
of individuals with normal vision.
The present work is part of the “Reading the Reader” project, a collaboration between
Technical University of Denmark (DTU) and Royal Danish Academy of Architecture. The
”Reading the Reader”1 project explores how architectural principles of space, layout, and
form influence cognitive processes such as reading. By integrating design insights with
cognitive science methods, the project aims to redefine reading experiences in modern
digital and architectural contexts. Within this broader framework, the present study eval­
uates how typographic spacing modifications across letter and line spacing affect visual
processing, fluency and comprehension using eye­tracking measures.
The present study departs from traditional research which follows specific demographic
groups such as children and second­language learners and visually impaired readers
by studying a diverse real­world sample. The research gathers participants who belong
to diverse demographic groups representing different ages and genders while holding
different professional experience levels and language abilities and having either normal
vision or corrected vision. The approach plays an essential role in enabling researchers to
extend their results to diverse groups and contribute to building digital reading platforms
accessible to everyone.
The ultimate goal of this research is to examine how basic typographic modifications be­
tween letter and line spacing affect reading patterns while reducing cognitive strain to
improve inclusive digital text formats. Research investigates real­time reading actions in
a digital environment with naturalistic elements to create practical knowledge for designing
reading content that reaches diverse user base.
To achieve this, the thesis executes its evaluation through eye­tracking technology which
measures gaze behavior alongside comprehension testing to determine cognitive im­
pacts. The research findings will advance cognitive science and design methods through
enhanced comprehension of how minimal text layout modifications boost digital reading
quality across all users.
1.1
Motivation
People now spend more time reading on digital screens rather than printable materials,
which can lead to eye fatigue, decreased comprehension, and reduced reading efficiency.
This is challenging, especially for professionals, researchers, students, or anyone who
encounters significant difficulties when relying on digital study material.

This project exists within the ”Reading the Reader” project that merges reading behav­
ior research with modern eye­tracking technology improvement. Real­time eye­tracking
technology serves as the central purpose of the project to solve problems encountered
by readers in academic and practical situations. The field of readability research faces
an ongoing challenge because it needs real­time behavioral measurements which verify
how type modifications boost reading speed.
Previous studies analyzed targeted populations including children and elderly people
along with visually impaired readers but little research exists about text formatting effects
on typical audiences with normal vision during reading. Text spacing stands out as a basic
yet effective strategy to enhance reading quality which people commonly overlook. The
core belief of this research project shows that small variations in text formation includ­
ing modifying letter or line spacing affect the way we read and understand information
content. By investigating real­time eye­tracking data among diverse readers, this study
contributes to the broader goal of designing more readable, user­friendly digital content
that supports comfort, fluency, and comprehension.
Reading interfaces designed for digital environments need to support different abilities of
readers since digital use is expanding. Educational materials, e­books, online articles,
and assistive reading tools require better scientific formatting standards to develop digital
reading practices which provide comfort and accessibility to all users. This thesis expands
academic knowledge in cognitive psychology as well as human­computer interaction while
providing practical value for educational development and accessibility programs and UX
design applications.
1.1.1
Problem Statement
The research focuses on studying how inter­letter spacing and inter­line spacing affect
reading behavior when using eye­tracking techniques. The analysis centers on how dif­
ferent inter­letter gaps impact fixation time and speeding up saccade movements and
reducing regressions, which are significant indicators of reading speed. The study inves­
tigates inter­line spacing effects on reading fluency and comprehension while determining
if greater spacing improves comprehension and smooths eye movement activities. This
study examines the merged impact of inter­letter spacing with inter­line spacing on read­
ing performance evaluation. A 4x4 Latin Square ANOVA design was used to present text
conditions systematically and minimize participant effects as well as presentation order
effects. The research employs real­time eye­tracking data to offer hands­on recommen­
dations regarding typography optimization in digital reading spaces improving reading
accessibility across digital platforms.
Research Questions
The research study will address the following research questions to meet its objectives.
RQ1: Does space really matter for efficient reading on normal visioned people?
RQ2: How does inter letter, inter line spacing and their combined effect influence eye
tracking matrices associated with cognitive processing efficiency during reading?
RQ3: What impact does letter and line spacing have on reading speed and comprehen­
sion accuracy?

Eye tracking technology is widely used in research to investigate and determine the diffi­
culty encountered by individuals with dyslexia or visual impairments. The purpose of this
technology is to improve reading comprehension for dyslexic and visually impaired indi­
viduals through typography controls and text style modification and specific intervention
applications that optimize content accessibility and simplicity. The scope of the thesis is

2.1
Theoretical Background
Reading is a complex process that engages both our visual perception and cognitive abil­
ities. Interestingly, even the most subtle features of text layout, such as the spacing be­
tween letters and lines, can have a profound effect on how we interpret and understand
the written content, influencing our overall reading experience. The objective of this lit­
erature review is to investigate the established knowledge regarding the influence of text
spacing on reading, particularly from visual and cognitive perspectives. This research
specifically focuses on prior studies employing eye tracking, as it provides quantifiable
insights into readers’ physical interactions with various text layouts.
Investigation the influence of different type of text spacing is crucial for several reasons.
Firstly, where and how people read such as in a classroom or on a screen, can impact
on how well they understand and enjoy while reading. For instance, traditional and digital
platforms offer different functionalities for literacy engagement, highlighting the impor­
tance of understanding how they interact with reading practices[1]. Secondly, the design
of a text, layout, format can make a reading easier or harder. Altering the arrangement
of text can help people enjoy reading more, which close associated with motivation [2].
Moreover, the concept of multimodel literacy acknowledges that students incorporates
various linguistic and semiotic resources from their personal environment into learning
context. This necessitates a framework that acknowledges the impact of these elements
on the learning process [1].
2.2
Eye­Tracking in Reading Research
Eye tracking is a powerful method for investigating the reading process, providing unique
insights into visual engagement of individuals with text. This technique uses special de­
vice that track and record eye movements, generating data that clarifies cognitive process
behind reading behavior. Comparing key metrics of eye tracking such as fixation and sac­
cade reveal fundamental aspect of cognitive engagement such as how people pay atten­
tion and process those information [3]. Studies has shown that longer fixation suggests
the reader is encountering difficulties, whereas certain patterns of eye movement how an
individual; trying to comprehend the information[4].
Sung et al. focused on the online reading behavior of fifth­graders, demonstrate that eye
movement data correlate between comprehension and spatial text features. By analyzing
fixation location and duration in relation in cognitive process during reading, they establish
that the spatial characteristics of texts such as layout, spacing, formatting are essential
fro understanding how young readers engage with reading materials [5]. Eye tracker
uses fixation, saccade, regression to measure how long a person is looking at a word
which tells a lot about the reading behavior and thought process. Fixation is the amount
of time a reader spend time in one word, longer fixation depicts the difficulty of reading.
People with dyslexia tend to have longer fixation duration compared to the non­dyslexic

2.3
Text Formatting and Readability
While researching for RQ1, the author identified a detailed analysis concerning the effect
of different type pf spacing on comprehension. Manipulating space in text have been es­
tablished in early research, which demonstrate the alignment of typographical features
with the natural and cognitive structure of language. In particular, studies by Jandreau
and Bever found that increasing the space between phrases of sentence significantly
enhances reading comprehension, especially those who with average or below­average
reading skill. Their research finding highlights that spacing is not merely an aesthetic con­
sideration but serves as a syntactic signal that aids reader’s comprehension by reducing
cognitive load during sentence processing. James R. Booth found no space in between
words reduce reading speed compared to spaced words about 14% and 53% accordingly
to the best and worst reader [9].
2.3.1
Different type of Spacing on Reading Comprehension
Moreover, different types of spacing differences create various reading difficulties during
character perception while impacting visual clutter management and efficient eye tracking
abilities. The study by Young et al. (Young et al., 1992) showed that warning identification
speeds improved when researchers adjusted character spacing which proves adequate
spacing makes text easier to read by minimizing letter interference [10]. Word spacing
creates an essential influence on how readers interact with text.Another research had
been done on chiniese children to test three spacing conditions—unspaced, semi­spaced,
and fully­spaced could be effective eon reading performance. The spacing conditions
were applied to texts of varying difficulty and orientation (horizontal vs. vertical) and they
found that reading texts written in horizontal format enabled quicker comprehension rates
than reading texts written in vertical format. The speed of reading difficult texts increased
with spaced formats and space helps young readers manage text segmentation although
it might reduce their performance in deep understanding tasks [11]. Cui[12] investigated
through research the reading patterns of Chinese­as­a­second­language (CSL) students
along with native readers who read both spaceless and spaced Chinese texts. Word­
spacing led to enhanced reading efficiency for CSL learners through shorter fixations
combined with fewer regressions in their reading patterns but non­word and pinyin (a
Romanization system for Chinese characters which teaches pronunciation to beginners)
spacing disrupted the reading flow due to unfamiliar character combinations.
The standard typography method uses distinct word spaces that remain narrow to main­
tain horizontal word continuity. Reynolds and Walker Reynolds & Walker (2004) demon­
strated how restricting word spacing maintains letter­word segmentation during reading
which results in improved reading performance [13]. According to Mirault et al. (2019)[14]
the absence of word spacing obstructs reading fluency and causes difficulties in perceiv­
ing word boundaries.

2.3.2
Influence of Letter and Line Spacing in general
Research for RQ2, it is found that letter and line spacing influence reading comprehension
and cognitive analysis on eye tracking field. Chiu and Drieghe conducted an eye tracking
experiment to investigate the influence of spaces in inter letter and inter line on reading
through the concept of visual crowding using an EyeLink 1000 eye­tracker sampling at
1,000 Hz. They found that wider letter spacing shorten the fixation duration whereas line
spacing had subtle improvements [15]. Research indicates that a subtle adjustments in
letter spacing between words enhance word encoding by enlarging the observable letter
group thus reducing eye movement times, fixation duration and making reading operations
more efficient [16][17]. However, reduced crowding produces shorter fixations according
to Paterson et al. [18] and Boer et al. [19] but excessive spacing between letters leads to
reading delays even with these shorter fixation periods as Korinth et al. [20] reported. The
trade­offs indicate that letter spacing must reach an optimal level to minimize crowding
effects while preventing reading speed reduction from additional saccadic planning costs
[risko].
In parallel, inter line spacing regulates both the eye fixation positioning and their arrange­
ment vertically. According to Li et al. [li2019] waving the spacing between lines alters both
the duration of each fixation and the total reading duration through lessened line interfer­
ence. Studies by Hou et al. proved wider line spacing improves readability combined with
decreased visual strain among specific visual populations including older adults accord­
ing to Hou et al. [21]. The space increase between lines promotes optimal text tracking
efficiency and requires fewer additional fixations since eye­tracking metrics measure this
phenomenon well.

2.3.3
Spacing on individuals with visual impairments
Academic research reveals that modifying spacing produces different effects on reading
abilities between dyslexic readers and people with central vision loss. Increased letter
spacing provides dyslexic children and adults with better visual crowding so they can rec­
ognize letters more effectively[25][26]. Early research demonstrates that dyslexic children
experienced better oral reading results with enlarged letter spacing yet unimpaired read­
ers showed no benefit and sometimes worsened their reading speed [27]. Wider spacing
facilitates letter identification for dyslexic readers but simultaneously reduces text pro­
cessing performance for rapid readers thus requiring individual adaptions when spacing
font elements.
Researchers consistently demonstrate benefits from increased spacing for readers with
visual impairments. Systematic adjustments to the size of letter spaces resulted in bet­
ter reading speeds together with smaller critical print sizes according to McLeish [28]
among low­vision readers. Beier et al. evaluated how different letter format parame­
ters like space, width and boldness impact reading ability of individuals with age­related
macular degeneration (AMD). Three variations of Times New Roman font were tested by
researchers through increasing space (TNR Space), expanding letter width (TNR Width)
and boldness adjustment (TNR Boldness) before standard font comparison. The authors

2.4
Eye Tracking Metrics and Computational Approaches
Research tools primarily measure fixation duration since it indicates the amount of time
eyes stay still over specific text areas or words. Fixation duration represents cognitive pro­
cessing load because the duration of fixed eye positions typically indicates the difficulty
level of visual and lexical word properties (Rayner et al., 2003). Eye­tracking metrics
require several steps to analyze raw gaze data for calculation purposes. Data cleaning
begins by eliminating tracking failures as well as blinks from the dataset. The filtered data
goes through algorithms which detect precise eye movements referred to as fixations,
saccades and regressions based on established time and position criteria. Different al­
gorithm that have been used in research are­
2.4.1
Dispersion­Threshold (I­DT)
The Identification by Dispersion­Threshold (IDT) algorithm functions as one of the most
adopted algorithms for detection of fixation points in eye tracking research because it de­
tects eye fixation durations when gaze remains steady within a specific boundary area.
The data processing begins by removing blink artifacts before the algorithm implements a
sliding window method to judge spatial dispersion (maximum point distance) and temporal
duration. Observational research utilizing spherical stimuli sets its dispersion threshold at
3 degrees of angular distance with a minimum presentation duration of 80 milliseconds
[30] and static image applications tend to use spatial thresholds up to 60 pixels for min­
imum displays of 100 milliseconds [31]. In other cases the segment is shifted. Motions
between fixation endpoints are identified as saccades. The simplicity of the IDT algo­
rithm earns praise but its accuracy requires threshold calibration that needs to consider
both experimental circumstances and device noises and distinctive eye patterns of indi­
vidual users. Research demonstrates minimal changes in threshold values affect fixation
measurements so research interpretations about cognitive processes become impacted
[30][31].
2.4.2
Identification by Velocity Threshold(I­VT)
The I­VT algorithm represents one of the standard methods organizations use to cate­
gorize eye movements. The I­VT algorithm uses gaze coordinate samples to calculate
velocity then verifies this velocity against its specified threshold. When a gaze sample
produces a velocity result under the threshold value it gets identified as fixational while
velocities above the threshold indicate saccadic movements. Numerous reading studies
that need quick data processing utilize this method because it is easy to understand as
well as efficient to compute [32]. The I­VT technique remains sensitive to data noise since
improper threshold calibration leads to incorrect classifications in particular recording set­
tings.

Kalman Filter(I­KF)

2.5
Statistical Techniques for Analyzing Eye Tracking Data
In reading research using eye­tracking data statistical analysis requires various methods
because the data has inherent complex hierarchical characteristics. Statistical proce­
dures used by researchers for analyzing aggregated eye­tracking data primarily depend
on t­tests and ANOVAs and MANOVAs to contrast metrics among experimental groups.
The research field now prefers linear mixed­effects models (LMMs) and linear regres­
sion for data analysis because eye movement data shows marked variation throughout
participants.
Before LMMs became dominant in the field researchers typically applied repeated mea­
sures ANOVA and paired t­tests for studying how metrics such as fixation duration and
saccade amplitude and regression count changed between high frequency and low fre­
quency word conditions [36]. The methods simplify between­group analysis however,
analysis limitations exist due to data aggregation that could conceal trial­level or individ­
ual data variability. One of the researcher Paterson et al. used ANOVA on fixation and
word recognition metrics and found significant effect on fixation duration and recognition
accuracy [18].
Research now utilizes linear mixed­effects models in eye­tracking investigations because
they provide multiple benefits for this method. LMMs enable researchers to analyze both
permanent effects from fixed variables along with the impacts of random variables like
participant differences and text item variability simultaneously [37]. Statistical power in­
creases due to this hierarchical modeling since it manages within­subject and between­
item variability exactly for eye­tracking data which has precise measurement character­
istics. Researchers analyze both continuous outcomes (first fixation duration and total
reading time) and categorical outcomes (regression occurrence) in their studies using
mixed­effects regression models and generalized versions of them [38].
When multiple dependent variables need simultaneous analysis MANOVA offers a suit­
able technique to evaluate their combined effects on cognitive processing during reading
[39]. The application of logarithmic transformations along with other transformation tech­
niques before analysis helps researchers fulfill assumptions in statistical tests especially
when the data display heavy skewness [39]. Logistic regression presents itself as a sta­
tistical tool in research when studying binary outcomes like fixation success and failure
according to Hui [36].

3.3.3
I2MC Algorithm for Eye Movement Classification
The Identification by Two­Means Clustering (I2MC) algorithm represents a noise­resistant
strategy for eye movement fixation detection introduced by Hessels et al. [33]. The I2MC
algorithm operates optimally when signal noise and tracking loss along with variable data
quality exist because it was specifically designed for naturalistic tasks explored through
consumer­grade eye trackers like the Tobii 4C.
Principle and Purpose:
The basis of I2MC depends on the different velocity patterns in eye movement data during
fixations and saccades. Fixations result in slower and more stationary gaze movements
which differ from the rapid and moving saccadic movements. Eye movement data enter
two distinctive classes by a two­means clustering technique that I2MC uses instead of
strict velocity criteria or dispersion indicators. The specified methodology demonstrates
better adaptability and resilience when dealing with noise or individual data fluctuations.
Two means clustering
Two­means clustering functions as the fundamental technique within I2MC which repre­
sents a simplified k­means clustering algorithm with k = 2. Eye movement data separation
takes place through this method which identifies fixations and non fixations(such as sac­
cades, blinks, or noise) among behavioral states.
The algorithm performs clustering operations through instantaneous velocity assessment
of eye movement data samples. Eye movement scientists can determine velocity by
measuring gaze position changed during time intervals and implement this metric to group
selected data since fixations exhibit low stable velocity rates compared to saccades which
cause rapid fast­paced movements. The clustering algorithm divides velocity data points
into separate groups through a procedure that both minimizes the internal variation within
groups and enlarges their inter­group distances.

The algorithm identifies two centroids representing data values before and after velocity
change while the data points must belong to the nearest calculated centroid. The data­
driven method works without supervision thus enabling classification adjustments that
match specific dataset conditions especially when dealing with variation among partici­

6
Discussion
This study aimed to explore whether spacing affects efficient reading in individuals with
normal vision or corrected eyesight, examining how letter spacing, line spacing, and their
combined effects influence eye movement behavior, cognitive processing, and overall
reading performance. Through a detailed analysis of multiple eye­tracking metrics and
comprehension outcomes across carefully designed text spacing conditions, the findings
provide strong evidence that letter spacing is crucial for enhancing reading efficiency.
6.1
Achievement
The primary objective of this study was to examine how real­time eye­tracking behavior is
influenced by different text spacing formats, and whether these formatting changes affect
reading efficiency and comfort. To achieve this, an experimental platform was developed
that recorded eye movements as participants read passages with varied spacing con­
ditions. After reading each passage, participants also provided subjective feedback on
the perceived difficulty of the text, offering an additional layer of insight into their reading
experience and preferences.
Once the data were collected, a comprehensive analysis was conducted to determine
whether spacing conditions produced meaningful differences in reading behavior. Initial
findings revealed significant variations across spacing types, suggesting that formatting
does influence how people read. To explore these differences in more depth, a series of
statistical tests were carried out.
A pairwise t­test was first used to identify significant differences between individual spac­
ing pairs. Building on these results, Linear Mixed Models (LMMs) were employed to ac­
count for individual variability across participants, revealing that letter spacing and com­
bined spacing had stronger effects on reading behavior than line spacing alone. To further
validate and clarify these patterns, a one­way ANOVA was conducted, which confirmed
significant overall differences among spacing conditions. However, Tukey’s post hoc test,
used for detailed pairwise comparisons, did not reveal significant differences between
spacing combinations, highlighting the nuanced nature of the results.
Together, these layered statistical methods allowed for a robust examination of how spac­
ing affects both objective reading behavior and subjective reading experience. By ad­
dressing the research questions through a combination of behavioral data and reading
pattern, the study provides valuable insight into which formatting choices most effectively
support efficient and comfortable reading.
RQ2: How do inter letter, inter line spacing and their combined effect influence eye
tracking matrices associated with cognitive processing efficiency during reading?
To address Research Question 2, a controlled experiment was carefully designed and
conducted to collect real­time eye­tracking data across various text spacing conditions.
The collected data were then subjected to a stepwise analysis, as detailed in Chapter 4,
focusing on key eye movement metrics associated with cognitive processing. The out­
comes of this analysis, presented in Chapter 5, revealed meaningful differences in reading
behavior under different formatting styles, offering valuable insights into how inter­letter,
inter­line, and combined spacing influence the efficiency of visual­cognitive processing
during reading. The effects were most evident in letter spacing and combined spacing
conditions. Specifically:
46

• Fixation durations were shorter, suggesting that text was processed more quickly.
• Fixation rates were higher, reflecting smoother and more fluent eye movement.
• Saccade amplitudes were longer, indicating that readers were able to move their
eyes more efficiently across words.
• Regression ratios were lower, pointing to fewer rereading attempts and improved
reading flow.
In contrast, line spacing as a single factor leading to no significant performance improve­

plays a larger role than line spacing in improving cognitive ease when reading text.
RQ3: What impact does spacing have on reading speed and comprehension accuracy?
The findings for the research question 3 indicate that reading speed was generally higher
in the line and combined spacing conditions, with participants exhibiting faster reading
when the text was visually more spaced. Among all spacing styles, combined spacing
achieved the highest average reading speed, closely followed by line spacing, while stan­
dard and letter spacing conditions resulted in slower reading performance. This trend was
validated by the Linear Mixed Model (LMM) analysis, which demonstrated a statistically
significant improvement in reading speed upon the introduction of combined spacing.
However, when assessing comprehension accuracy, the results were more nuanced.
Comprehension scores were calculated based on participants’ answers to multiple­choice
questions presented after each passage, organized by spacing condition. This analysis
revealed minimal statistically significant differences in comprehension accuracy across
the various spacing types. One possible explanation is that comprehension was evalu­
ated by formatting style rather than by passage. Since passages were randomly assigned
to users and formats, variability in passage difficulty may have diluted the effect of spac­
ing. Evaluating comprehension by passage rather than by spacing condition might have
revealed more pronounced differences.
Nonetheless, this study aimed to understand how formatting, rather than content, affects
comprehension. Response data collection generated insights related to visual presen­
tation even though the passage content and order were randomized thus analyzing the
impact of visual elements instead of semantic complexity. While research data showed
no statistical variations in comprehension but indicated letter and combined spacing main­
tained similar reading comprehension levels or showed slight improvement at higher read­
ing speeds.
RQ1: Does spacing really matter for efficient reading in people with normal vision?
Several studies have shown that spacing improves reading efficiency for people with
dyslexia and central vision loss. By analyzing research question 2 and 3 the findings
of this study demonstrate conclusive and constant proof that text spacing significantly af­
fects reading efficiency in people with normal vision. Among the various spacing formats
tested, both letter spacing alone and combined spacing consistently facilitated more ef­
ficient reading behaviors in comparison to standard spacing. These two conditions led
to shorter fixation durations, higher fixation rates, larger saccade amplitudes, and lower
regression ratios, all of which are reliable indicators of fluent and less effortful reading.
In contrast, line spacing alone did not generate substantial improvements, a conclusion
supported by multiple studies [35] [48]. Its effects were similar to those of the standard

condition, indicating that simply increasing the space between lines, without adjusting let­

the conclusion that letter spacing is the key factor for enhancing improvements, while
combined spacing does not provide meaningful advantages beyond those offered by let­

• Pairwise t­tests showed letter spacing and combined spacing produced meaningful

• Linear Mixed Models (LMMs) demonstrated that letter or combined spacing en­

• ANOVA tests validated the overall presence of spacing effects because at least one

the effects were subtly present and the test controls for multiple comparisons to
reduce false detections.
The study answers the first research question through triangulation of results from varied
statistical approaches and behavioral measures. Results from eye movement efficiency
and reading speed evaluation show that letter and combined spacing consistently improve
reading comprehension of written text, even when vision is unimpaired.
6.2
Limitations
While this study provides valuable insights into the influence of text spacing on reading
behavior, it is necessary to acknowledge certain limitations.
First, the real­time collection of eye­tracking data was compromised by using predefined
and randomized content that might affect reading difficulty. Due to the variability in pas­
sage complexity and the fact that each participant read different passages in various spac­
ing formats, it is possible that some effects were influenced by the text content rather
than formatting alone. Additionally, analysis of comprehension accuracy was performed
by spacing condition instead of individual passage evaluation likely concealed subtle for­
matting influences because of content variability.
Another limitation of this study arises from the controlled laboratory environment in which
the experiment was conducted, as the setup was designed to closely mimic natural read­
ing conditions with realistic text, lighting, and screen formats although it cannot replicate
the complexity and variability of real world reading experience. People typically perform
various reading actions including skimming and scanning and repeated reading of particu­
lar text sections when dealing with comprehension tasks in everyday situations. However,
participants faced restrictions within the experimental platform structure that prevented
them from revisiting the text after studying it which affected their ability to refer back to
the text while answering questions. This restriction may have influenced comprehension
accuracy and does not adequately reflect how readers engage with text in more flexible,
self­paced environments. Although this design ensured consistency across participants,
it may have compromised ecological validity in the assessment of comprehension.

6.3
Future Works
For future work, research could explore how different spacing formats affect the read­
ing performance of non­native speakers compared to native speakers, as their reading
strategies and processing speeds may vary. Furthermore, conducting the experiment in
a more naturalistic environment, such as a long article on a website with images integrated
between the text, would provide valuable insights into how spacing changes function in
everyday reading contexts.
From a technical perspective, the development of adaptive reading platforms should be
studied for future research as these platforms would adjust spacing based on user behav­
ior utilizing real­time eye tracking feedback. A personalized reader system which adjusts
its formatting would substantially enhance reading effectiveness for users with different
requirements as well as mental workload variations. Moreover, future research should
strengthen the understanding between text layout and reading understanding through
better comprehension evaluation procedures that support readers to revisit content and
match actual reading practices.
Finally, this research directly influences the field of UX interface design. UX designers
working on digital platforms like e­readers and educational applications should utilize re­
search findings to develop reader­oriented content options specifically focused on ad­
justable letter spacing. The results show how minor text layout changes produce mea­
surable benefits for reading speed alongside improved cognitive efficiency which provides
efficient guidance to enhance user experience and inclusive text presentation and reading
accessibility.
6.4
Compare The Findings
Studies like those by Chiu & Drieghe[15] and Perea et al.[22] showed that increased letter
spacing tends to reduce fixation durations and improve comprehension efficiency, similar
to the results in this thesis where both letter and combined spacing led to shorter fixation
durations and higher fixation rates. Similarly, Paterson & Jordan [18] and Tai et al.[23]
found that moderate letter spacing improves reading but excessive spacing could be coun­
terproductive, a pattern also echoed in the current findings where line spacing alone had
minimal benefits compared to letter or combined spacing. Yu et al.[17] emphasized that
reading performance peaks at optimal spacing, reinforcing this thesis’s conclusion that
combined spacing doesn’t always surpass letter spacing alone.

7
Conclusion
The research set out to examine the effects of different text arrangement formats that in­
cludes inter letter spacing inter line spacing and combination of inter letter and inter line
spacing on reading activities as well as reading performance among people with normal
vision or corrected­to­normal vision. Unlike many previous studies that focused on spe­
cific populations such as students, children, or visually impaired individuals, this research
included participants across a wide age range(20 to 62 years), representing various native
languages, professions, and genders that offer a broader insight into real­world reading
behavior.
The findings demonstrate that spacing matters. Among the tested spacing styles, both
letter spacing and combined spacing led to more efficient and stressless reading behav­
ior, indicated by shorter fixation durations, higher fixation rates, longer saccade ampli­
tudes, and fewer regressions, higher reading speed(WPM). Interestingly, line spacing
alone could not show to meaningful improvements and had effects similar to standard
spacing. Comprehension scores, although not significantly different across spacing con­
ditions, remained stable, suggesting that increased spacing did not compromise under­
standing.
These results contribute to the broader goals of the “Reading the Reader” project by sup­
porting the development of more effective strategies aimed at enhancing the reading ex­
perience for a wide range of users. In practical terms, this study can help UX designers
and digital content creators and educators to enhance readability through adjustable or
expanded letter spacing which avoids modifying the source content.
