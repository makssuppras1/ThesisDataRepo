Abstract
Aerial tools is a company based in Copenhagen that specialises in Aerial surveillance using
their self developed VTOL UAV technology. A case is designed for the VTOL UAV based on
the speciﬁcations. The project begins with analysis on the customers needs and requirements,
translating these into user persona. This is used to deﬁne the product requirements. Product
design tools are used to facilitate the deign process. The VTOL UAV designs is explored in
details for shapes, dimensions and weight of the various components. Simple box diagrams
are made to convert the complex geometries into smaller simple shapes for ease of packing
visualisations. This gives the direction towards brainstorming into various ideas for the case,
from complex to simple and ﬁnding a bridge between expectations and reality in realising the
case. Selections are made considering pros and cons of various ideas, and a common ground
is found where the beneﬁts of two or more ideas can be combined to form a useful product.
Having ﬁnalised an idea that contains more than one case for the components, the connections
are explored that might be needed at some locations of the product. Further leap is taken into
the material selection based on these ideas. Various material selection strategies are explored
with each having its own beneﬁts. Based on this material selection and selected design, CAD
dimensions are calculated and a cost and weight analysis is done. This insight paves way to
further redesign the case to optimise the cost and the weight even further. A new direction is
taken to make one single case that shall have all the components together placed securely. The
components are visualised in 3D space, translated, rotated multiple times, time and again, to
ﬁnd the best possible packing. At a ﬁnal stage, a conﬁguration is chosen that packs the com-
ponents in a simple and eﬀective way. The foam for the case is selected, designed in CAD. It
has a simple design, is innovative, is easy to manufacture and assemble and has less moving
parts. Extra shock absorbing protection is given outside the foam. The outer case body is de-
signed, with hinges, locks, handles, and wheels. Aluminium channels are provided along the
case edges for support and strength. These are connected with strong rivet joints. The cost
and weight analysis is performed for the ﬁnal case design. The ﬁnal case design is simple, in-
novative, cost eﬃcient, sustainable, and has a low weight.

Project goals
The main project goals are as listed below.
1. Creative idea generation and brainstorming for potential designs for the case
2. User persona, user centered design study and translating these into product require-
ments.
3. Optimisation of the case design and ﬁnalising on one case design.
4. Modelling of the case designs in CAD software, Solidworks.
5. Selection of potential Materials for manufacturing of the case interior parts and exterior
(case body).
6. Design of production methods for the case outer body and the interior parts of the case.
7. An attempt to ﬁnd sustainability in the design, and the materials for the case.
8. An attempt to make the case cost eﬀective and light in weight.
1.2
Project description
Keywords: Sustainable design, ergonomic design, creative, innovation, light weight, user design,
user interface, usability design, interaction design, smart manufacturing, VTOL, UAV, techno-
logy, product innovation, design feasibility
Project description: In the scope of this masters thesis, an innovative, sustainable, light weight,
ergonomic case will be designed for the UAV VTOL developed by Aerial tools. The case is

should be robust and easy to transport . Also, it is an advantage if it can be customised and

transport and overall assembly before ﬂight campaigns. There are several aspects and stages in
the project work that are aimed for and involve the following.

Chapter 3
Design Ideas
In this chapter on design ideas, various tools are explored for the design of the case. Product
requirements are speciﬁed that shall serve to assist in the design process.
3.1
Product requirements
The case is a fundamental part that contains the various components of the VTOL UAV. A
good case design will not only keep the components in place, but shall also serve to protect the
components from damage or external forces. Some of the crucial requirements of the case are
as given below.
3.1.1
General requirements for the case
1. The case should have a customised design so that the components should ﬁt exactly in
the various compartments of the case.
2. The case should be light in weight so that two or three people can easily carry it.
3. The case design should be ergonomic. It should be possible for the user that the case can
be carried without any hindrance or obstruction from its design.
4. The case should be easy to transport (for eg. wheels, cushions, attachments, handles
etc.). The case should have these features.
5. The components should be easy to be assembled in the case and to be removed from it.
6. There should be enough space for the components to be arranged over the case.
7. The case should have a waterproof body or provision to attach waterproof protection
over it while travelling.
20
==PAGE:21==
8. The case should have a simple design with less complexity of using it.
3.1.2
Case shell requirements
1. The case shell should have high strength to withstand external forces.
2. The case should be having a high impact resistance so that it is not damaged if it falls
from a short height while it is being carried (2-3 feet)
3. The case should have a good stiﬀness so that due to external forces the shell body should
not be deformed.
4. The case shell should be light in weight.
3.1.3
Interior requirements
1. The case interior should be compact and soft and sturdy. The components should be
safely kept in the case and should not move during travel with the case.
2. The compartments should be customised for each and every component.
3. The interior has to be light in weight so that if some parts have to be taken out to reveal
the components it should be able to be done easily.
4. Provision for adding manuals and or small necessary documents inside the case.
5. The case interior should not have any sharp edges or design features that can damage
the components or the user while handling the case.
6. The weight distribution should be balanced in the case, so that no part of the case feels
more heavier than the other.
3.1.4
Non-functional requirements
Non functional requirements are not necessarily involved in the functioning or working of the
product. These requirements can also be called as product features. Some of these require-
ments are listed below.
1. Provision for name and company logo with detailed information for contact if the case is
lost. This information might be best if its engrossed or embedded in the case shell.
2. Radium markers or glowing markers. This can be useful while travelling with the case in
the night time. Also, can be useful in ﬁnding if lost.
3. The product should be visually appealing. The product besides satisfying the functional
requirements should have an aesthetic and a nice appeal to it. This can be achieved by
unique colour, nice shape and designs.
21
==PAGE:22==
4. If the components are to be remove or placed in a speciﬁc way, the case should have a
manual for it.
5. The case should have a low perceived workload. This refers to the number of attempts
the user has to perform for a particular task, in this particular case, the removal and
placement back of the components. The lower the workload the more better the product
experience.
6. The case should be secure. There can be TSA locks attached to the case.

3.2.2
SCAMPER
SCAMPER is a design tool that is used in product development process. It involves creative
brainstorming and this forms the initial phase of any product design and development [9]. It
provides the framework that can simulate questions and triggers that are useful to tackle the
challenge, work, project or product in a unique way. The acronym SCAMPER stands for Sub-
stitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Eliminate, Reduce [9] Applying the technique to the existing
case in hand we have the following points.
1. Substitute
(a) Can biodegradable material substitute the traditional product for packaging (Plastics,
Metal, etc.) and give the appropriate strength and long term quality.
Solution: Wood and some wood products can be used to replace some part of the
case. Fibers and wool can be used as materials for the inner part of the component
case (cushioning).
23
==PAGE:24==
Challenges: A major challenge can be weight and durability. In addition, wood/
cotton/ natural ﬁbers are susceptible to catch ﬁre. Wood and natural ﬁbers includ-
ing wool or cotton can absorb moisture, and an get damaged due to external envir-
onmental conditions. This shall lead to loss of structural strength of the case as well
as damage of the components in contact with it.
(b) If the case is made in plastic, can 3D printing substitute the well known plastic pro-
cessing techniques like injection molding, rotomolding, blow-molding, thermoforming
etc.
Solutions: Since the whole panel for the case is large, additive manufacturing in-
cluding 3D printing can be a challenge.
Challenges: The major challenge is of cost and productivity which are one of the
most crucial parameters in the ﬁnal case design.
(c) Can the hard case be substituted by a soft case.
Solutions: Soft case can substitute the hard case, if the components have to be car-
ried in it.
Challenges: The major challenge is about safety of the components. The compon-
ents are used for aerospace, any minor changes in dimensions can seriously aﬀect
the performance and the ﬂight conditions of the VTOL UAV. A soft case even if
made, shall be made in a large size and having enough cushioning to protect the
component. Although, if it falls from a height, or if a force is applied on it, then the
VTOL UAV components shall be damaged. A soft case scenario can only be applied
as an alternative case, if the components have to be carried to a nearby location
and in hand. For travel and long distances, a hard case is more promising.
(d) If the design requires connection techniques, can snap ﬁt replace screw and nut con-
nections.
Solutions: Snap ﬁt is a good technique for mechanical connection as it is cheap, can
be replaced easily and it usually lasts long. It is one of the techniques used in large
numbers in industry especially plastic industries. It can be used in small connec-
tions in the case, if required.
Challenges: The size of the case is large and this might require more stronger con-
nections like nuts and bolts or screws (in case of panels).
(e) Can interference ﬁt/ press ﬁt substitute snap ﬁt (if required for small connections).
Solutions: In some places where the connection strength required is large, press ﬁts
can be a good choice. In places where reliable locking with small forces is required,
snap ﬁts are a good choice.
Challenges: Interference ﬁt or press ﬁt loses its power in due course of time.
(f) Since the components are large and many, it is natural to distribute them in mul-
24
==PAGE:25==
tiple cases for convenience. Can one case substitute multiple cases.
Solutions: This shall be possible and it depends on the strategy and designing to
pack the parts in the most compact way.
Challenges: This is a challenging task considering the fact that the components
have large dimensions, some parts around 1.6 meters in length. There can be one
case that is very large or multiple smaller cases. In case of multiple smaller cases,
more material shall be required and more users to carry it as well. But if a very
large case is made, then the challenge is of storage, transport, movement and er-
gonomics.
(g) Aluminium is a traditional material that is used a lot in the manufacture of luggage
and trolleys. Can it be replaced by other lighter and better performing materials.
Solutions: This is possible and composites can replace aluminum in terms of better
performance.
Challenges: The replacement with lighter materials shall come at increased mater-
ials and manufacturing costs. Composite panels are high in cost compared to alu-
minium.
2. Combine
(a) If there are two or more cases that are made, can they be combined to make a big-
ger compact case. This can be useful in stacking up the cases during transportation
or carrying.
Solutions: Two or more cases can be stacked over each other, this is possible. One
of the inspirations in this regards is LEGO®bricks. These are stacked over each
other by press ﬁts.
Challenges: The major challenge is that the overall weight shall increase too much if
they are stacked and this shall be a diﬃcult to move the case. In addition the press
ﬁt used in LEGO®bricks works because it is small in size. It is diﬃcult to image
large cases being stacked in this way.
(b) Can a combination be made that two cases are attached to form a kind of platform
or table for arranging the parts together.
Solutions: Yes, two or more cases can be arranged and ﬁxed together with connec-
tions (snap/press ﬁt) to make a platform for arranging and assembly of the VTOL
UAV.
Challenges: The major challenge is that the connection has to be strong and it is
diﬃcult to be realised with a few snap ﬁts or press ﬁts. Stable and stronger connec-
tions like nut and bolts shall be needed that would add more weight to the existing
product and make the process of assembly cumbersome.
(c) Can recycled plastic be combined with the existing case material. This can add
25
==PAGE:26==
more sustainability to the product. Can this combination trade oﬀwith the desired
product performance and product quality?
Solutions: Yes, recycled plastic can be incorporated in the case in certain locations
of the case that does not demand high strength performance.
Challenges: Recycled plastic might have impurities that might severely aﬀect the
performance of the case, especially aﬀecting its strength and durability.
3. Adapt
(a) Can the product adapt to changes in the dimensions of the VTOL UAV.
Solutions: If the interior of the case is made in foam, the foam can be changed to
accommodate changes in the VTOL UAV dimensions or its design.
Challenges: The major challenge is that if the entire design is changed and the com-
ponents are larger in dimensions than the previous ones, then it would be hard to
accommodate this change. This can be realised only when the new drone design is
smaller than the previous one.
(b) Can the case adapt to diﬀerent climate and weather conditions.
Solutions: This depends on the design of the case and the material selection. It is
possible to make the case dust proof, water proof and to withstand climatic condi-
tions.
Challenges: It depends on how good the design it, the materials that are selected
for the case construction.
(c) Can the case adapt to diﬀerent altitudes and areas of low pressures for example dur-
ing travelling via air.
Solutions: This is a crucial point as the case might need to be carried to diﬀerent
locations and might need to be carried via air travel. Due to pressure diﬀerences
there might be chances that the case lid is sealed and its diﬃcult to open the case
lid. This is particularly important if the case is made of plastic and has an o-ring as
a sealing. The o-ring might seal the case and make it pressure tight. The answer to
this is the design of pressure release valves, that release the excess pressure and help
open the case safely.
Challenges: This needs a good selection of material that can accommodate pressure
build ups. A strategic location of the pressure release valves is necessary for safe
working.
4. Modify
(a) Can the case be modiﬁed to a table.
Solutions: This depends on the design of the case and the material selection. With
a suitable design, the case can be modiﬁed into a table.
Challenges: The main challenges are that there would be need of extra features like
26
==PAGE:27==
legs for the table. Also, this modiﬁcation might come at extra cost and extra added
weight to the existing design.
5. Put to Another Use
(a) Can the case be used as a table instead of having a need for another table.
Solutions: Yes this is possible if the dimensions of the case are large, then it can be
surely used for the purpose of assembly of the components together.
Challenges: There are few challenges to this. Major being that the case has to be
horizontally resting and vertical designs of the case might not solve this speciﬁc pur-
pose.
(b) Can the case be used to transport diﬀerent products if the case no longer has the
components in it. For example, if components are taken out and placed in some
other case.
Solutions: Yes this is possible. If the case has essentially foam, then it is possible
that the foam can be removed and replaced with foam with diﬀerent internal geo-
metry.
Challenges: There are no major challenges involved in this.
6. Eliminate
(a) Can material be eliminated (or saved) to increase strength and with less overall
weight of the product. This would facilitate easy transport, make the product con-
venient, save material.
Solutions: Yes this can be possible with a simple eﬃcient design of the case.
Challenges: The main challenge is that of material selection and costs.
(b) Can foam be eliminated and replaced with rubber since rubber has very good shock
absorbing properties.
Solutions: This is hard to realise since rubber has a higher density than foams.
Challenges: The main challenge is the dimensions of the case. Since the case dimen-
sions are large, hence there would be a huge amount of usage of rubber that would

6.1
Material selection process
The various steps involved in the process of material selection are given below[21][20].
1. Translation: Conversion of design requirements into a prescription.
(a) Function: The crucial functions the component is supposed to perform.
(b) Constraints: The conditions that are to be met and should be non-negotiable.
(c) Objective: The parameters that need to be maximized or minimized.
(d) Free variables: The parameters of the study that the designer is free to change.
2. Screening: Elimination of materials that cannot meet the constraints.
3. Ranking: Ordering of the screened materials, by their ability to meet the criteria of ex-
cellence, for example minimizing costs.
4. Exploring the most promising material candidates, in depth, examining the potential of
present use, and how best to do the design process using them, identifying and under-
standing the case history of failures.
5. Final documentation.

Material selection for Packaging
Packaging is something that is seen around in the daily lives be it grocery, parcels, automobiles
or appliances. The major essence of the protection packaging is the capability of converting
kinetic energy into some other form, and at the same time, keeping the peak force on the ob-

impact is unpredictable, the packaging should oﬀer protection on omni-direction. Since the
package has to carry the components with the packaging, light weight is highly essential. Also,
as the packaging might be discarded or thrown away, during or after some time of use, thus it
should be cheap in cost. Foams are materials that are essentially good at this function. The
capacity of a foam for energy absorption is comparable to the solid that it is made of [18].

Chapter 8
Production Strategy
8.1
Case shell - Aluminium 2024 T4
The process for manufacturing of aluminium cases is given below, involves the following steps
in order [4].
1. Detailed CAD designing prior to manufacturing.
2. Flat sheet of aluminium is taken and it is processed through the rolling process. Creation
of grooves and proﬁle for the case. This sheet can be corrugated to provide more stiﬀness
in the case design. Corrugation increases bending stiﬀness, impart more buckling load
capacity, increases bearing capacity, imparts high performance in energy absorption with
a low weight.
3. Further shaping can be done by pressing techniques to give the shape to the case.
4. A pre designed frame is joined to the shaped shell to give extra strength and to encapsu-
late it. Rivets are used in this process. These rivets are the mechanical fasteners and give
a very good connection. These can have strengths as upto 27000 pounds/ square inches,
mostly used in the aviation industries.
5. Further the corners are cleaned, and the edges and corners are given a protection by at-
tachment of a protection covers. This ensures damping of external forces, as well as pre-
vents any injury to the user or others. A hard polymeric material can be the protective
covering for it.
6. Further, attachments like wheels are attached in the same way by use of rivets. The
wheels can have double ball bearings that can give more ease of movements. Slider handles
(telescopic handles) are attached next. Hinges are used to fasten the two halves of the
case/ or the case and the lid.
80
==PAGE:81==
7. Mechanical testing done on the various parts of the case.
8. Later, the foams is put in and a thin protective fabric is later laid over the foam for pro-
tection. This fabric shall protect the foam from damage due to repeated use.
9. Interior testing and validation.
The ﬁg. 11.2 shows the various processing techniques, and the ﬁg. 11.3 shows the shapes that
can be formed for Al 2024 T4 alloy. Following point are crucial if the case has to be made in
Al 2024 T4 alloy.
1. The stiﬀness of the shell can be increased by using corrugated sheets. Sizing and location
of grooves for cutting needs is crucial.
2. Strategic location of strong stiﬀframes to provide structural stability to the case.
3. Using dense foams to provide more structural stability to the case and more security to
the parts involved.
4. If there is one big case, then preferably a low height and wide base.

8.2
Case shell - PP impact copolymer, UV stabilized
The process for manufacturing of PP cases is given below[3].
1. Detailed CAD designing prior to manufacturing.
2. The process starts with the use of pellets of the desired polymer blend (PP impact co-
polymer, UV stabilized). These are stored in large sized silos where they are kept dry
and dust proof and in the highest quality.
3. High pressure vacuum is used to move these pellets into the mixer. In the mixer, a liquid
blowing agent is added. These plastic pellets are then heated to the required temperat-
ure (400-500 degrees Fahrenheit, for polypropylene) for liquefying. This process is the
plasticization of the polymer to the liquid form. This process take around 30-45 seconds
to plasticize the pellets to the liquid form.
Polypropylene and PC+PBT (impact modiﬁed) has an excellent polymer injection mold-
ing properties.
4. Molds are pre designed for the shaping of the case. These molds are made of high quality
alloys so that they can be repeatedly used for a large scale production.
5. Liquid plastic from step 1 is injected in the pressure mold for forming the body of the
case and the lid. The pressures are around 1800 tons.
81
==PAGE:82==
6. After the mold is set and the polymer case is formed to shape, a robotic arm carries the
part, trims away an excess of the polymeric material, and places the case and the lid on
a conveyor belt for the assembly operation.
7. In the assembly process, the following operations take place: installing handles, latches,
hinge pins and the foam setting and the company logo.
8. Handles, latches and the pressure valves are produced in a similar way by injection mold-
ing process.
9. Since the case is to be carried overseas in ﬁghts and might be also used at higher alti-
tudes, the case might experience pressure diﬀerences. This shall prevent the opening and
closing of the case, a situation called ’airtight’ that leads to suction and that prevents
the case to be opened up. To solve this problem, a pressure valve is used.
Similar to releasing a suction cup, the valve equalises the pressure allowing the case to be
opened easily in any pressure conditions or altitudes.
10. Wheels, trolleys and the handles are attached at an assembly station.
11. Handles have a hard and a soft rubber. Soft rubber location is where the handle has a
contact touch with a hand.
12. Once the lower case is assembled, the lid is assembled in the next assembly station. Pad-
locks, latches, pins for latches are arranged and placed in proper locations of the case.
13. Then the protective foam is inserted for the equipments/ components.
14. In the ﬁnal stage, at the last assembly station, the o-rings are installed that make the
case air tight and help it sealing against dust and water as well. The case clasps together
at the location of the o-ring that deforms and seals as the top lid is closed on the lower
part of the case.
The ﬁg. 11.4 shows the various processing techniques and shapes for PP impact copolymer
UV stabilized. Injection molding is the preferred choice if the case is made in PP. The various
aspects for designing the parts in injection molding is listed below [24].
1. Uniform wall thickness: The wall thickness should be kept uniform along the plastic
shell. This is good in terms of cost and product quality. Thick sections take longer to
cool than thin sections, and after ejection, if there is variations in the wall thickness,
there are higher temperatures near the thicker sections, and lower temperatures near the
thinner sections. This may lead to a prominent geometric distortion. Best design prac-
tice is to use a thin wall thickness with vertical ribs in areas requiring higher stiﬀness.
2. Rib design: Ribs can be useful for providing more stiﬀness to the case shell. The rib
thickness is selected as 70% of the shell wall thickness. Height of the ribs is 4 × the shell
82
==PAGE:83==
thickness. The distance between two ribs is 10 times the wall thickness. A detailed ana-
lysis reveals that the practise of using ribs and with the speciﬁcations mentioned, matches
the stiﬀness of the part that is thicker by 30% and which does not have ribs. Had the
ribs not been used, the thicker part would have consumed 15% more of material, and
would have utilised 70 % more of the cycle time in the injection molding process, com-
pared to the thinner part with ribs. Addition of ribs provides a signiﬁcant advantage in
terms of both performance (stiﬀness) and cost (optimised)
3. Corner design: Sharp corners are avoided, due to reasons of product performance, mold
design and the injection molding process. Chamfers are given in the design to help break
the sharp corners, and to provide a single beveled surface.
4. Draft: A draft is provided for ease of plastic melt ﬂow, and to easily remove the part
after it has molded.
5. Undercuts: Undercuts are avoided for ease of plastic molding process.

8.4
Selection of Production Technique
1. Injection molding process is beneﬁcial for large volumes of production. This process is
faster, and can manufacture products in less cycle time, and with less cost per product
piece. However, the capital investment in making molds, toolings, dies, and the layout
of the production is large and high costs are involved in the maintenance of the molds.
Capital is required in assembly line operations. The scale of manufacturing can be very
medium to very high.
2. Making the case out of Aluminium alloy requires investment in cutting, fabrication, pro-
curement, corrugation, assembly operations etc. Large amount of human investment as
working force is required. The scale of manufacturing can be medium to very high.
3. Making the case out of wood/composites requires investment in cutting, fabrication, ad-
hesive bonding and assembly operations. The scale of manufacturing can be small scale
to medium and high. A considerable amount of human investment as working force is
required. This can be more realistic for this project work considering the fact that the
volume of product is not very large in the initial years.

a thin extra foam protection that goes below it, this ensures that the wing does not sink
inside the foam. The same approach is applied for the vertical tail.

with CNC machines much easier. Various parts of the foam can be processed separately
(if needed) and then joined together by strong adhesives. Some areas of the foam have
smooth curved features, for example the foams covering the fuselage. This is done to
ensure that the fuselage is placed in a compact way. These features can be shaped by
carving or sculpting the foam body.
7. There are less moving parts in the foams. The top foam has two symmetric cavities in
it. This can be useful to lift the top foam out of the case. These are designed accordingly
that a hand of a normal person can go into it.
8. The rotors are well protected in the case. They have foam around them to support.
9. The entire case design is ﬂexible in terms that the components can be placed inside with
little restrictions of pre-shaped cavities. Also, the design is easier to be understood by
anyone with little guidance on the locations where the components should be placed.
