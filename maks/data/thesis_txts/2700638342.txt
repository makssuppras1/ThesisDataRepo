ABSTRACT 
 
This project is conducted to investigate how the activity of Candida antarctica 
lipase A (CalA) is affected by different water activities. The reaction of butyric acid 
and ethanol was performed in methyl tert-butyl ether . The catalyzed esterification 
reaction in organic media is assessed by kinetic characterization and molecular 
dynamics simulations.  
In the kinetics study, the experimental design was changed to find the right 
conditions for CalA in order to form ethyl butyrate. The kinetic studies showed that 
90 uL CalA in 3 mL of a reaction media can catalyze an esterification reaction, 
which resulted in the formation of ethyl butyrate after interacting for 30 minutes. 
The specific activity (SA) of CalA were determined, and it was found that the 
highest rate of esterification was observed at a water activity (aw) of 0.53. MD 
simulations were performed to gain molecular insight into the effect of water activity 
on the CalA- catalyzed reaction. The result showed a highest value of the root-mean-
squared-deviation (RMSD), and the root-mean-square-fluctuations (RMSF) of CalA 
at 1.14, the difference was approximately 1.0 Å and 0.4 Å. 
RMSD and RMSF also showed the lowest values at the water activity with the 
highest SA of CalA. Furthermore, the water cluster on CalA’s surface increased with 
increased water activity. The amount of water molecules in water clusters can affect 
specific activity of CalA in an esterification reaction 
.

Dette projekt blev udført for at undersøge hvordan aktiviteten af Candida antarctica 
lipase A (CalA) bliver påvirket af forskellige vandaktiviteter. Reaktionen mellem 
butansyre og ethanol blev udført i methyl tert-butyl ether. Den katalyserede 
esterficeringsreaktion i et organisk medie blev blev bestem ved kinetisk 
karakterisering molekylær dynamiske simuleringer.  
I den kinetiske undersøgelse blev det eksperimentelle design ændret for at finde de 
rette betingelser så CalA kan danne ethylbutyrat. Resultaten viste at 90 uL CalA i 3 
mL af et reaktionsmedium kan katalysere esterficerings-reaktionen, hvilket 
resulterede i dannelsen af ethylbutyrate efter at have interageret i 30 minutter. Den 
specifikke aktivitet (SA) af CalA blev bestemt. Her blev det bestemt, at den højeste 
specifik-aktivitet forekom ved en vandaktivitet (aw) på 0.53. MD-simuleringer blev 
udført for at opnå molekylær indsigt i effekten af vandaktiviteten på den CalA-
katalyserede reaktion. Resultatet viste en højeste værdi af rod-middel-kvadrat-
afvigelsen (RMSD) og rod-middel-kvadrat-udsving (RMSF) af CalA ved 1,14, 
forskellen var cirka 1,0 Å og 0,4 Å. RMSD og RMSF viste, at de laveste værdier 
var ved den vandaktivitet med den højeste SA og CalA. Endvidere steg vand cluster 
på overfladen af CalA ved en øget vandaktivitet. Mængden af vandmolekylerne i 
vandcluster kan påvirke specifik aktivitet af CalA i en esterficeringsreaktion.

1. INTRODUCTION 
 
1.1 Enzymes 
Enzymes are biomolecules that act as biocatalysts. They increase the chemical reaction rate 
without being consumed during the reaction. The enzymes act on molecules called the 
substrate. A specific substrate acts on the active site on the enzyme, to produce a substrate-
enzyme complex that  forms a specific product. The main factor in this reaction is the 
activation energy. Enzymes convert substrates into products by decreasing the activation 
energy for the reaction, so that the substrate needs less energy to undergo a reaction[1]. The 
reaction  mechanism of enzyme activity is shown in figure 1: 
 
Figure 1:The Mechanism of enzyme activity. The figure is made in biorender [2]. 
The catalytic reaction begins when the substrate binds to the active site [3]. 
Enzymes can be extracted from plant and animal cells, microorganisms (cultivation), or 
filamentous fungi[4]. Enzymes have been used in various industries such as pharmaceutical-
, food-, chemical-, and agricultural industries. Lipases have attracted the most scientific 
attention of all known enzymes due to their properties [5]. 
1.2 Lipase 
Lipases are a class of water-soluble enzymes that can be obtained from plants and animals[8]. 
Lipases can also be obtained from microorganisms (bacterial and fungal origin) [9].  The 
function of lipases is to catalyze the hydrolysis of triglycerides into glycerol and fatty acids. 
They can also catalyze the esterification and transesterification reactions in organic media[8]. 
==PAGE:9==
8 
 
Due to their ability to catalyze a wide variety of reactions, lipases are the most used 
hydrolyses enzyme in the industrial scale. They are used in  fine chemicals-, pharmaceutical-
, additives in household detergents, oleochemical-, food- and leather industry [5]. 
Candida antartica (C.antarctica) was in the middle of the 1960s, isolated from sediment 
from the bottom of the ice-covered lake named Vanda located in Antarctica. The yeast was 
named in 1988 Candida antarctica. Candida antarctica lipases are a cold-tolerant enzyme 
that can grow in temperature range 5-32ᵒC. Furthermore, it is very thermostable [9]. 
1.2.1  Candida antarctica Lipase B 
Candida antarctica Lipase A (CalA) and Candida Antarctica Lipase B (CalB) are produced 
by the filamentous fungus Candida Antarctica. CalB is the most used enzyme for hydrolysis 
in organic synthesis and biotransformation[8]. CalB can form and hydrolyze a variety of 
esters stereo specifically  [7]. In 1994, the structure of CalB was solved. CalB is a monomer 
that belongs to the α / β-hydrolase family. It consists of 317 amino acids and having seven 
central β-sheets and ten α-helices. Furthermore, its catalytic triad active site is presented in 
Ser105, His224, and Asp187, and the molecular weight is 33 kDa [11]. 
 
1.2.2 Candida antarctica lipase A  
The x-ray structure of CalA was for the first time reported in 2007 [8]. CalA belongs like 
CalB also to the α / β-hydrolase family. CalA is a monomer and can accept tertiary alcohols 
[8]. The use of CalA is limited due to unknown stability patterns of CalA in various organic 
medias that are commonly used in different biocatalytic systems [12].  
 
1.2.3 Esterification  
Esterification is an equilibrium reaction where alcohol and acid react to form an ester and 
water. In a hydrolysis reaction, the product (ethyl butyrate) reacts with water to break the 
ester bond. Enzymes are added to increase the rate of the reaction. [13] 
Lipases are powerful enzymes that can catalyze the esterification of organic esters. It can also 
catalyze the hydrolysis of fatty acids[10]. Figure 2 shows an example of an esterification 
reaction where the CalA is used as acids catalysts.

1.3 Water activity  
Water activity is used to calculate the amount of water in food and drug. It has an important 
influence on the production and quality of the product. It affects various rate processes such 
as browning, oxidation, degradation of nutrients, enzyme reaction, and especially the growth 
rate of microorganisms [18]. 
Nonaqueous enzymology is an active area and has been investigated in many studies in the 
last two decades. The organic media is used in enzymatic reaction because it has many well-
documented advantages, such as increasing the enzyme's thermostability, reducing microbial 
contamination, and shifting the thermodynamic equilibrium in favour of synthesis[10]. 
Furthermore, the water content in an organic solvent significantly influences enzyme activity, 
as well the appropriate amount of water increases enzyme flexibility. The water molecules 
==PAGE:14==
13 
 
act as a lubricant for the protein to promote flexibility. A very high amount of water in the 
organic solvent will decrease the enzymatic activity and lead to a hydrolysis reaction[19]. 
The water in the organic media binds to the active site of the enzyme. The water activity in 
the organic solvent is exerted by the amount of water molecule bound to the enzyme and not 
by the percentage of pure water in the organic solvent. The water to the enzyme will increase 
the activation up to a certain point and decrease again when the amount of bound water is 
maximized [20]. At high water activity, the water molecules cover the surface of the protein 
and create a hydrophilic microenvironment that prevents the diffusion of the substrate to the 
active site. Therefore, it is important to have an equilibrium between having enough water, 
so the enzyme has the flexibility of catalyzing the reaction in the right direction of the 
equilibrium  [21]. 
Water activity is a thermodynamic measure of water expressed as the vapor pressure of water 
in a sample. The water activity in organic media is defined by the following equation:[18]

1.4 Molecular dynamics simulations with AMBER 
Molecular dynamics simulations are a computer technique used to investigate the structures 
and dynamics of protein, nucleic acids, and carbohydrates under various conditions. It 
provides details concerning the motions of individual particles like atoms as a function of 
time [22]. 
AMBER is the collective name for a group of programs that allows users to carry out and 
analyze MD simulations [23]. AMBER stand for the abbreviation of Assisted Model Building 
and Energy Refinement, that refers to two things which are molecular dynamics simulations 
programs and to a set of molecular mechanical force field that describes the potential energy 
function and parameters of the interactions of biomolecules[23]. The main steps in an MD 
==PAGE:15==
14 
 
simulation are system preparation, simulation, and trajectory analysis, described in the 
method section.  
MD simulation has five ingredients, which are boundary conditions, initial condition, force 
calculation integrator and property calculation, explained below[22]. 
There are two types of boundary conditions, which are isolated boundary conditions (IBC) 
(IBC is used for the study of the cluster and the molecule) and periodic boundary conditions 
(PBC) (is used for the analysis of bulk liquid and solid)  
A large system approximated by PBCs consists of an infinite number of unit cells with several 
copies. As showed figure 8, when a particle leaves the original simulation box, an identical 
image particle enters the original box.  That means the particle can interact with particles in 
the adjacent image box and particles in the same box. The number of particles in the 
simulation box is constant. A minimum image convention is a common form of PBCs 
particles, as each atom interacts with the nearest atom [24]. 
  
Figure 8. The periodic boundary condition. The particle leaves the simulation box (marked with green), and an image 
particle moves to replace it. R is the cutoff distance of the interatomic interaction. The PCB was made in BioRender [2]. 
MD simulations generate a system's atomic trajectory by numeric integration of Newton 
equations of motion for a specific interatomic potential, with certain initial and boundary 
conditions. MD simulations are based on Newton’s second law, which is expressed in the 
following equation: [25]

A force field is a dataset, which can be used to describe the potential energy between atoms. 
We can estimate the potential energy in a system using a force field. When we have the

The trajectory only contains coordinates, given by integration of the equations of motion,

2.1 Molecular dynamics simulations 
 
2.1.1  The setup system   
Preparation of the solvent box with MTBE was the first step in the simulation of CalA in 
MTBE. The solvent box was set to be 88Åx88Åx88Å and not 90Åx90Åx90Å (2Å difference 
in each dimension) because of the periodic boundary conditions.  Based on the calculation in 
appendix 1, a solvent box with a volume: 681470Å3 can hold 3445 MTBE molecules. A 
presentation of the solvent box is shown in figure 10. 
 
Figure 10.The solvent box with 3445 solvent molecules.  The solvent box was made in PyMOL 
Packmol software, which is implemented in AMBER, were used to build the solvent box.  
Then the system was minimized for 5000 minimization steps and heated to 298.15K for 1 ns 
and in the last step, equilibrated for 1 ns. The density of the equilibrated system was 
0.7565g/cm3, which was close to the experimental density: 0.7405 g/cm3 of MTBE[26].  
 
2.1.2 Molecular dynamics simulations of CalA in an organic solvent 
The last frame from the simulation of the solvent box was extracted using cpptraj and saved 
as a pdb-file. The crystal structure (PDB-ID: 2VEO) of CalA was loaded in PyMOL. The 
lipase contains two chains (chain A and chain B) that are close to identical. However, CalA 
is a monomer, and therefore, chain B with its water molecules was deleted. The crystal of 
chain A was cleaned by removing IUM-, GOL-, and PGN ions and the C-terminal's missing

proline (P) was added. The desired number of water molecules were obtained by deleting the 
water molecules with the highest B-factor. Chain A was protonated by using H++ 
software[27]. The internal dielectric was set to 10, external dielectric was set to 4.5 for 
MTBE, pH to 7.0, and the salinity was set to 0.0. The protonated structure is shown in figure 
11. 
A hydrogen atom was added to Asp95 to get a neutral charge, and epsilon-hydrogen in 
His366 was deleted to get the active site conformation presented by Ericsson et al.

the protein-water system was 0 in tleap. The water molecules will have the same position to 
the structure as before protonating. 
The CalA-structure with water molecules was merged with the solvent box in PyMOL, where 
the CalA with water molecules was placed in the middle of the solvent box. In the solvent

2.1.3 Energy Minimization 
Energy minimization on the system was performed to remove bad contacts before molecular 
dynamics simulations were initiated. The energy minimization was performed with maxcyc 
= 5000 (total minimization cycles) as further explained below. The input for minimization 
for run 1, run 2, and run 3 are shown in figure 13.   
==PAGE:21==
20 
 
Sander employs two different minimization algorithms, which are the steepest descent and 
conjugate gradient. The steepest descent was used to remove the largest strain in the system, 
but it converges slowly when close to minima. Here the conjugate gradient method is more 
efficient because it is slower than the steepest descent [28]. Nycyc flag was the steps of 
descent methods, and maxcyc flag was the steps of conjugated descent. When ncyc < maxcyc, 
the steepest descent is used for the first ncyc before switching to the conjugated gradient for 
the remaining conjugated gradient(maxcyc-ncyc) steps. In the three runs, the ncyc  was set to 
1000, in run 2 was set to 1500 and in run 3 was set to 1800 [23]. 
The imin value was set to 1, to get a short minimization and move from local maxima.  The 
ntpr was set to 50 and printed energy information in a mdout file every 50 steps. The value 
for the non-bonded cut was set to 12Å (cut=12).  Constant volume periodic boundary 
conditions were applied, and Particle Mesh Ewald (PME) method was used to limit the 
infinite electrostatics Restraintmask flag was used to specify the restrained atoms. 
Restraint_wt was set to 10, the weight (in kcal/mol -Å2) for the positional restraint[23].

2.1.4 Heating  
The system was heated to 298.15K for 1 ns. The script of heating of the system is shown in figure 14. 
 
Figure 14: The parameters was used to heat the system to 298.15K. 
  
The imin was set to 0 (no minimization).  The ntx was set to 1 to read initial coordinates from 
the inpcrd file. The ntpr was set to 50 and printed energy information in a mdout file every 
50 steps. The nstlim flag was set to 500000 MD steps to run, which is equal to 1ns.  The dt= 
0.002 picoseconds (psec) timesteps are the time unit of MD simulation. The Langevin 
thermostat dynamics (ntt=3) was used, and ig was set to -1;  therefore, the random seed was 
based on the current time and date, so it was different for each run. The gamma_ln = 5.0 flag 
was the collision frequency γ. The ntc=2 flag was used for SHAKE to perform bond length 
constraints on bonds involving hydrogen atoms.  Also, ntf was set to 2 to evaluate force and 
bond interactions, where bonds that included hydrogen atoms were omitted.   The cutoff was 
set to 12Å to truncate the non-bonded interaction and using force shifting. The ntb flag was 
set to 1 meaning that a constant volume periodic boundary condition was used. The iwrap=1 
was used to wrap the coordinates written to the restart and trajectory files. The ioutfm was 
set to 1 to select binary Network Common Data Form (NetCDF) trajectories format of the 
coordinated files.  The nmropt was set to 1 to choose nmr type analysis, that include distance 
and angle restraint.  Temp0 type describes the changing temperature (unit kelvin (K)) that 
has been changed.  ISTEP1 =0 and ISTEP2= 150000, where the change of steps was applied 
and run at the value of VALUE1=10.0 and VALUE2. =289.15. In other words, VALUE1 
and VALUE2 were the changes corresponding to STEP1 and STEP2[28].

2.3.5 Gas Chromatography  
A Gas Chromatograph (Perkin Elmer Clarus 600 GC-FID) was used to analyze the eight vials 
with the sample (ethyl butyrate, MTBE, HCl, 1-hexanol and butyric acid). Helium was used 
as a carrier gas at a flow rate of 0.75mL/min.  The sample is injected through the septum into 
an injection port (GC inlet). The inlet is directly connected to the Nucol (Sigma-Aldrich) 
column (lenght:15mm,0.31 mm i.d., 0.25µm) with a temperature at 200ᵒ   with a split ratio 
of 1:150. The carrier gas transports the sample from the injector through the column and to 
the detector. The GC column is placed in the oven where the temperature was set to 50ᵒC for 
==PAGE:31==
30 
 
the first 5 min. The temperature increases from 50 ᵒC to 100ᵒC at the rate of 25ᵒC/min, and it 
maintains 100ᵒC for 2 min. Then, the temperature increases from 100ᵒC to 175ᵒC to at a rate 
of 25ᵒC/min and maintain the 175ᵒC for 2 min. The sample evaporates at high temperature 
and separate inside the column and is detected by the detector. The detector temperature was 
set to 260ᵒC, and the sample was evaporated, separated, and detected by the detector. The 
detector makes an electrical signal based on time. The electrical signal measured to quantitate 
the amount of each component present in computer as peaks [30]. Figure 22 illustrates a GC 
instrument.

compressed gas supply, and the flow is controlled by pressure and flow regulators. GC is

control. Gas quality is essential to check before use because impurities in the gas can destroy

4.  DISCUSSION 
4.1 Experimental kinetics study of CalA 
The experimental kinetic study was made to investigate the ability of CalA to form ethyl 
butyrate in the esterification reaction between butyric acid and ethanol. The reaction was 
performed in MTBE at five different theoretical water activities to investigate whether 
CalA’s specific activity depends on the water activity.  Due to the lack of  SA studies of 
CalA, this study was compared with the kinetics study of CalB made by Banik et.al [34].  
The experimental result obtained by Banik et.al is shown in figure 47; it is seen that the 
specific activity of CalB was dependent on the water activity because the SA of CalB 
decreases gradually with an increasing initial water activity of the organic solvent. The 
product of the esterification reaction is ethyl butyrate and water. When water activity exceeds 
the required amount for protein flexibility, the equilibrium of the reaction shift in the 
direction of the hydrolysis and the amount of ethyl butyrate decreases. In contrast, this study 
shows that the specific activity of CalA  in figure 48 start to increase with increasing water 
activity but decrease at water activity by 0.97.  That may be due to the water content of the 
medium influence the enzyme flexibility. Therefore, it is crucial to have the right water 
content in an organic medium. The highest activity of CalB and CALA is at 0.12 and 0.53, 
respectively. High water activity in organic solvent decreases the enzyme activity since the 
water will cover the enzyme's active site [34].  
 
Figure 47 show the average specific activity for esterification reaction catalyzed by CalA in MTBE as a function of average 
water activity of two set independent experiments.

The difference in SA for CalA and CalB can be because the enzymes are different and have 
different structures. However,  the interactions between the protein and the organic solvent 
and water activities level are difficult to understand in the experimental studies. Therefore, 
MD simulations study of CalA was performed and described in the section below.

using various water activities. The structural analysis was made to investigate whether 
CalA’s structure undergoes conformational change. The RMSD, RMSF, SASA and Rg were 
used to quantify the structural changes. 
RMSD  was calculated for each simulation and showed how different amount of water 
molecules influence the backbone atoms.

ns for run 3. The backbone for run 1 and run show a stable structure after 25 ns. In figure 26, 
the simulation with 400 water molecules shows that the backbone structure for run 2 differs 
with  at 25 ns. Figure 29, the simulation with 500, shows that the backbone structures for run

4.3 Comparison of  experiential kinetic study  with computation study of CalA 
The correlations between the computational study and CalA's kinetic analysis CalA show 
that the SA of CalA decreased by water activity 0.97. At the same, the RMSD and the RMSF 
of CalA at 1.14 water activity showed a change of approx.  1 Å and 0.4Å. RMSD and RMSF 
also showed a low value at the water activity with highest SA of CalA.  In addition, at 1.14 
water of activity, the cluster was larger than the cluster at lower water activity, and it 
contained more water molecules.  Furthermore, RMSD and RMSF was lowest  at water 
activity  with highest SA of CalA. 
Even though CalA is here compared to  CalB, the two lipases have different significantly 
different structure and reactions pattern. CalB catalyze at different rates, depending on 
reaction medium, time substrate and water activity. It is highly likely that CalA’s enzymatic 
ability also depend on the solvent and water activity; therefore, more studies with CalA in 
different solvents are required. 
 
5. CONCLUSION  
The purpose of this study was to investigate whether CalA catalyzes the esterification 
reaction in organic medias (MTBE) with different water activities. The catalyzed 
esterification reaction in organic media was assessed by an experimental kinetic study and 
computational molecular dynamics simulations.  
The experimental result showed that the SA of CalA were higher when increasing the water 
activity. The SA decreased at a water activity of 0.97.  
The MD-simulation of CalA showed water clustering on CalA’s surface. The water clusters 
increased when increasing the water activity. Furthermore, the different amounts of water 
molecules should be equal to the theoretical water activities. This was almost obtained in this 
study.   
The RMSD and the RMSF analysis of CalA at water activity of 1.14 differed from the other 
water activities, as it increased by 1Å and 0.4 Å. The SASA of CalA increased with increased 
water activity.  And the Rg of CalA increased with increasing water activity.
