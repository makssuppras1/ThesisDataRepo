ABSTRACT  
 
ALPF have successfully been used since 1950 to protect crops from environmental risks. 
They exhibit high resistance against the environmental oxidative conditions, mainly sunlight, 
causing material degradation and quality deterioration. ALPF includes a numerus of 
chemical additives incorporated into plastic resins during processing to provide the desirable 
properties of ALPF need to have. As products with outdoor service life exposed to sunlight, 
they include UV stabilizers to protect the material against the oxidative UV radiation of 
sunlight. Additionally, antioxidants provide the necessary protection against the degradation 
from high temperatures and mechanical shear during the life stage of plastic processing.  
The benefit of both stabilizers is indisputable until to be physically lost. Considering, the 
transition to more circular systems, the loss is necessary to be quantified and disclosed 
since a new output of virgin additive should be added to the system to restore the loss. The 
need for disclosure is even more necessary when the material quality is strongly 
compromised from stabilizer depletion.  
This research study aims to quantify the loss of concentration of two widespread stabilizers 
of ALPF, the UV absorber Chimassorb 81 and the processing antioxidant Irganox 1076 
during the processing and service life stage. Only the service life was successfully 
simulated, where three different types of stabilized ALPF were exposed under accelerated 
artificial env. degradation for 1000 hr equal to 1 year of natural exposure.  
Finally, a gradual loss of UV absorber was quantified through HPLC-UV analysis equals to 
39.7% and 25.7% for ALPF stabilized solely with UV absorber and ALPF stabilized with a 
blend of both stabilizers accordingly. In parallel, the material degradation was monitored by 
FITR analysis throughout the exposure exhibit the success of stabilization to limit the 
material degradation to early stages. The correlation of stabilizer depletion with material 
degradation through the carbonyl index was examined, and a strong correlation between 
them was proved. The quantification of Antioxidant concentration could not be identified due 
to implications during compounding, but the physical loss due to the formation of 
degradation byproducts was discussed.

1 INTRODUCTION 
 
The role of Stabilizers on Agricultural Low-density polyethylene films 
Agricultural plastic films (APF) are extensively used to protect crops against environmental 
risks and have contribute to the shorten of cultivation period since 1950 (Parlato et al., 2020). 
The 60% of the polymer resins for APF is low-density polyethylene (LDPE), mainly used to 
produce monolayer films for greenhouse covers, mulchs, and tunnels (Briassoulis et al., 
2013; Cascone et al., 2020). Such products are durable and flexible and can resist the 
extensive UV radiation of sunlight, rainfalls, and wind forces until 2-3 years (Simpson, 2003). 
The increasing of crops requiring protection (Parlato et al., 2020), combined with the 
success of APF as a protection product, has resulted in high demand. The EU is steadily 
increasing its demand for APF, reaching 536,000 kt out of 1.7 MT, which is the total demand 
for plastics in agriculture (Eunomia, 2020; PlasticsEurope, 2020) 
Nevertheless, LDPE resin cannot be used per se to produce APF sufficient for their 
application. Its property is very poor. Hence, LDPE includes a combination of additive 
compounds in various concentration ratios, and each of them serve a different role (Ambrogi 
et al., 2017). In particular, LDPE includes additives to increase the resistance against severe 
conditions that caused material degradation. Such additives include light stabilizers and 
antioxidants (Andrady and Rajapakse, 2016). The incorporation of these additives into 
LDPE resin is called stabilization (Tolinski, 2015; Wypych, 2015). For simplicity, these 
compounds can be called stabilizers. 
Agricultural low density polyethylene films (ALPF), as products with an outdoor service life 
application they are exposed to UV radiation of sunlight. The impact of UV irradiation on 
LDPE films and on APF has extensively arisen the scientific interest of authors such as 
Briassoulis and Andrady for more than 30 decades. UV radiation alters the material surface 
inducing gradual loss of mechanical properties. As a result, the material can become brittle 
and fragmented (Andrady, 2017; Briassoulis et al., 2004). UV absorber stabilizers provide 
the necessary UV protection to material to protect the crops for 2-3 years. Therefore, the 
benefit of light stabilizers on both the economy and the environment is indisputable. They 
form a product that can last longer without multiple replacements; hence, they lessen the 
==PAGE:14==
2 
 
financial investment of farmers. In addition, they prevent the generation of more APF waste 
and the associated environmental impacts of their End-of-Life (EoL) treatment. 
However, ALPF are exposed to more than one severe condition inducing resin degradation 
throughout lifecycle stages (Vilaplana and Karlsson, 2008). During the plastic processing or 
mechanical recycling, the high temperatures and the mechanical shear of equipment can 
initiate thermomechanical degradation. The stabilization with Antioxidants, is more than a 
necessary step to protect the resin and to increase the recycling rates (Ragaert et al., 2017; 
Wypych, 2015).  
Considering the role of stabilizers against all types of degradation with a lifecycle 
perspective, their benefit on both economy and environment is indisputable. They increase 
the service life, production, and recycling rates. Thus, the environmental impacts associated 
with polymer production, plastic pollution on the terrestrial and marine ecosystem and 
landfilling are mitigated.  
However, their action, so their benefit, stops once they be depleted. Degradation is the 
pathway that induces the more considerable losses of stabilizers because they are 
consumed, migrated, or degraded and transformed to new compounds (Bai et al., 2012; 
Rabek, 1990). Therefore, the material quality is compromised by additive depletion resulting 
to an input of virgin additive to maintain material quality. 
Circular Economy on ALPF 
EU, through the action plan on Circular Economy (CE) on plastic products, highlighted the 
urgent need for transition towards CE. The action plan states to maintain all the consumed 
resources throughout a plastic product life stage in the product system loop. Thus, to 
eliminate the depletion of natural resources and the environmental impacts of their 
extraction (European Commission, 2018). The action plan highlight that the more significant 
material losses take place during the end-of-life (EoL) stage due to the high rate of post-
consumer products on landfills. Therefore, the first step is to transform the economy from 
linear to circular to reclaim all the resources consumed to produce ALPF in the economy 
loop. The most efficient way to close the material loop is through mechanical recycling 
generating secondary raw materials (SMR) to fabricate new plastic products.

Nevertheless, a plastic product is not only the polymer resin itself. In this case, stabilizers 
comprise the chemical content of ALPF. As the degradation inducing the more extensive 
loss of the initial content of stabilizers, resources consumed to produce these stabilizers are 
lost too. Hence more resources should be consumed when virgin stabilizes should be re-
added to restore the previous loss.

Traditionally, little consideration is given to include the chemical content of products as 
consumed resources on actions for CE. Most of the actions from the EU related to chemicals 
additives on plastic are focusing on hazardous additives. Namely, the new action plan on 
CE (European Commission, 2020a) along with recent published chemical strategy 
(European Commission, 2020b) establish actions to eliminate the hazardous additives for 
plastic products respecting the legacy requirements for a toxic-free SMR though recycling.  
The tendency to avoid including additives as input and output resources on product 
inventory is reflecting on life cycle assessment (LCA) studies as well. LCA is the ultimate 
standardized method to quantify all consumed resources and the relevant environmental 
impacts (JRC-IES, 2010). However, many LCA studies or footprint assessments do not 
include stabilizers in their life cycle stage inventory. Fact, that can alter the outcome. This 
may be attributed to the scare studies on quantification of losses during plastic lifecycle 
stages. The lack of data is the biggest challenge for evaluating product systems (Samani

and van der Meer, 2020), resulting in significant assumptions on inputs and outputs 
(Horodytska et al., 2020). 
Despite all the established knowledge on stabilization, the majority of the studies identify the 
loss of stabilizers in relation to loss of mechanical properties, such as elongation at break 
(among the others,La Mantia and Gardette, 2002; Santos et al., 2002; Tzankova Dintcheva 
et al., 2002). Few studies quantify the rate of loss of stabilizers, mostly of Antioxidants 
(Blázquez-Blázquez et al., 2020; Scoponi et al., 2000; Yang et al., 2016). 
Therefore, since the material quality is strongly related to stabilizers depletion, it should be 
shed more light on quantification of stabilizer losses throughout the product life stages and 
disclose them to product data for CE. The outcome is more than necessary to determine the 
circularity and sustainability of ALPF as a complete system, including both material and 
chemical content.

2 SCOPE 
 
2.1 RESEARCH QUESTION 
CE action plan focuses on maintaining all the resources consumed to produce ALPF during 
all the lifecycle stages on the loop as long as possible. Hence to fulfill the aim, it should be 
identified how much resources are lost in each lifecycle stage. Namely during the processing 
and service life stages. Since the material losses are known, the main question arisen is: 
How much are the losses of the chemical content, in other words of additives, during the 
service life stage and processing stage of ALPF lifecycle? When do the more significant 
losses take place during the service life of ALPF? Which type of stabilizer has the more 
significant lost rate? 
2.2 AIM 
The aim of this study is to quantify the losses of two widespread stabilizers on ALPF induced 
by degradation during the processing life stage and service life stage. The target compounds 
are Irganox 1076, a phenolic Antioxidant, and Chimassorb 81, a UV absorber.  
This is more a preliminary idea of losses of these specific additives and cannot reflect the 
behavior of all the different compounds included to protective additives against degradation. 
Even though the limited number of target additives and the simplicity of prepared LDPE 
films, the outcome can initiate actions and frameworks on disclosure of additives on product 
data required for the CE. In addition, the outcome could provide more data on resources 
outputs and facilitate the LCA practitioners to conduct a more complete environmental 
assessment.  Therefore, identifying hot spots of the system where most resources are lost, 
or which lifecycle stage induces the higher environmental impacts can be used to develop 
frameworks on product circularity and sustainability.

BACKGROUND THEORY 
 
The chapter provides the necessary background theory related mainly on the experimental 
part. 
3.1 PLASTIC COMPOUDING AND PROCESSING 
Plastic resins exhibit inferior characteristics when they used in a virgin form. Hence, to create 
a product sufficient for its application, plastic resins in masterbatch pellets or powder are 
mixed with chemical compounds called additives. These compounds, according to their 
function, can provide the desirable characteristics of fire or UV or heat resistance, flexibility, 
durability. The incorporation of additives in plastics takes place during the plastic 
compounding stage. Plastic pellets or powder can be a dry blend or melt blend with 
additives. To achieve a better distribution and incorporation, it is preferred the plastic to be 
melted. By this, the additive is incorporated inside the polymer matric, achieving an even 
dispersion (Murphy, 2001; Pospíšil et al., 1995). The stabilization, meaning the incorporation 
of stabilizers additives, can be performed during plastic processing. Plastic processing is 
the step of the life stage where the plastic product is created. 
During this study, the plastic processing method used was extrusion. Traditionally, the 
method is selected to produce a range of thermoplastic products such as films, containers 
and pipers or frames. The shape is given by changing some parts/elements during extrusion. 
But the common procedure for all the products is the melt extrusion. All the extruders, 
regardless of size, include a long heating barrel, one or more hoppers, a nozzle, and a die. 
Plastic resins are introduced inside the in-heating barrel through the hopper. There the 
plastic is getting melted due to high temperatures (above the melting point of each type of 
plastic) where one or two screws are mixing the melt plastic while they move it to the nozzle. 
In the end, the extruder is coming out from the die. The die can have extra accessories when 
the extruder can take the form of a fiber or a sheet. Some extruders also have extra hopper 
where the additive can be introduced to the melt plastic to achieve the desirable dispersion   
3.2 ADDITIVES FOR AGRICULTURE FILMS  
Plastic products include additive types which provide the desired properties.  Every additive 
type and concentration can influence the quality and appearance of plastics. The aim is to 
have effects with a rate that is sufficient for their applications. For instance, stabilizers and 
==PAGE:20==
8 
 
antioxidants protect plastics against degradation caused by heat or UV light, pigments and 
colorants improve the appearance, fillers and plasticizers enhance the mechanical 
properties and increase the material’s flexibility, and flame retardants act against fire 
propagation (Ambrogi et al., 2017; Andrady and Rajapakse, 2016) 
APFs as a product with outdoor application mainly include stabilizers and antioxidants. 
Generally, they are used in low dosage below 1% (w/w). They do not exhibit toxic of high 
concern characteristics compared with other types of additives, such as brominated flame 
retardants, heavy metals, and phthalates plasticizers.  
Antioxidants 
They are additives that provide a protection against the thermo oxidative degradation 
occurred during (re)processing. They divided into two main classes according to their action 
mechanism against degradation: chain-terminating primary antioxidants and hydroperoxide-
decomposing secondary antioxidants. The most common representatives of these groups 
are hindered phenols and phospites accordingly.  
Hindered phenolics protects against degradation at high processing temperatures or long 
oxidative thermal degradation and are highly efficient, low in volatility. The mechanism 
against oxidation based on the interruption of primary oxidation cycle by removing the  
radicals. Hindered phenols are called like donors antioxidants because they donate a 
hydrogen to radicals such as alkoxy, peroxy, and hydroxy radicals. (Tolinski, 2015; Wypych, 
2015). While they are degraded, they break down into new groups. Hence as the 
concentration of the initial antioxidant is decreasing, the concentration of new degradation 
product included to aldehydes and phospite are increasing.  
UV stabilizers 
UV stabilizers includes additive groups of UV absorber and hindered amine light stabilizers. 
UV absorbers, where this study is focusing, protect against UV degradation absorbing UV 
radiation. Hence, prevent the plastic from absorb the UV irradiation itself. UV absorbers do 
not degraded so fast compared with Antioxidants but the performance is decreasing due to 
limitations of the absorption of UV (Murphy, 2001)

3.3 ENVIROMENTAL DEGRADATION 
The sunlight, rainfalls, the action of winds, the oxygen reach environmental along with the 
moderate temperature, environmental conditions which can initiate the plastic material to 
degrade. For the commercial known plastic types, mainly polyolefins, polystyrene, and 
polyethylene terephthalate are types prone for degradation. The dominant degradation 
mechanism is the photodegradation or UV degradation caused by UV radiation. LDPE 
cannot itself to absorb the damaging UV irradiation but it contains some impurities during its 
processing or compounding, called chromophore impurities which absorb the UV photons 
triggering the formation of free radicals (Andrady, 2017). Once the free radical is formed 
reacts with the oxygen forming the peroxy radicals triggering further chain reactions. New 
oxidation products form plastic degradation is formed after the termination of reaction. The 
most known are ketones formed mainly to the earlier stage of degradation, esters lactones 
and aldehydes and carboxyl acids. During the later stages of degradation vinyls and 
vinylenes are being formed(Figure 2.) (Gewert et al., 2015).  
In macroscopic level, the UV degradation can cause gradual loses material properties. The 
increase of the deterioration of material mechanical lead to the formation of surface cracks 
and brittleness. With the action of mechanical stress by wind or wave action a brittle plastic 
starts to be fragmentized (Andrady, 2017). 
UV degradation is the main factor of environmental degradation, but also other factors can 
accelerate the impact of degradation of exposed plastic. The rain fall can carry on some 
chromophore impurities and contaminate the films with them. Hence the degradation will be 
accelerated. Also, during the UV degradation, some involved additives can migrate from the 
polymer matrix and to the surface. The exposed additives can be washed-out form the 
rainfall so their concentration into plastic is dropped along with the properties provided form 
these additives. Furthermore, macroscopic the action or winds facilitate the fragmentation 
of highly degraded and brittle plastic to the surrounding environment. Microscopic, air 
pollutants can accelerate the oxidative degradation because the can react the involved 
additives. As a result, the additives will be being depleted and they cannot anymore provide 
the desirable characteristics to the material. Therefore the material will have a higher 
tendency to be degraded (Andrady, 2015; Briassoulis et al., 2004).

3.4 PROCESSING DEGRADATION 
The high temperatures above the melting point of each polymer in correlation with the 
mechanical shear during the (re)processing stages can induce degradation of plastic. The 
type of thermal degradation is related with the presence of oxygen. Most of the cases, there 
is absence of oxygen during processing or compounding. In these cases, the type of 
degradation is called thermomechanical degradation. Free radicals are formed initiate 
chemical reactions similar to UV degradation. If the oxygen is involved, the degradation is 
called thermo-oxidative degradation and similar chemical groups with UV degradation will 
be generated

4 MATERIALS AND METHODS 
 
4.1 CHAPTER OVERVIEW  
The ultimate aim of this study is to quantify the loss of content of two widespread different 
type of stabilizers used on APLF, during their service life and processing life stage. To 
achieve the aim, two experimental set-ups of each stage were conducted. The quantification 
of loss of content, i.e of concentration of each stabilizer was performed by solvent extraction 
method followed by high performance liquid chromatography in combination with UV 
detection (HPLC-UV) analysis. Samples on triplicates were obtained in various steps of the 
set-ups for analysis. By this, the pathway of concentration loss can be mapped during the 
life cycles. 
The chapter is divided into the materials and methods followed on carrying out the two 
experimental set-ups. The first set-up includes the compounding on stabilized ALPF and the 
simulation of 1 year exposure to environmental degradative conditions during the service 
life. The simulation was carried out with the use of accelerated weathering chamber. The 
second includes the stabilization of LPDE pellets and the following processing though 
multiple extrusions simulating the processing stage of APF. Nevertheless, due to many 
challenges during extrusion, the simulation could not be achieved. An overview of 
experimental set-ups is illustrated on Figure 3. including all the steps and samplings 
conducted for analysis.

4.2 SERVICE LIFE SIMULATION EXPERIMENTAL SET-UP 
4.2.1 MATERIALS 
LPDE 3020D masterbatch pellets supplied from Lupolen Basel with MW of 300 kg/mol and 
melting point of 114 oC were used for the purpose of compounding stabilized films. Two 
different type of stabilizer compounds were chosen for the stabilization of ALPF based on: 
the widespread used on APF, the compatibility with LDPE, and ability to be identified by the 
available analytical method of HPLC-UV. Based on these characteristics, the chosen 
compounds were the primary Antioxidant, Irganox 1076 and the UV benzophenone 
absorber Chimassorb 81. They are known to be applied on APF (Briassoulis et al., 2017; 
Wypych, 2015) and have been studied for their stabilization effects on various experimental 
studies (among the others, Gugumus, 2002; Koriem et al., 2021). Generally, most plastic 
additives are stabilized with Antioxidants since they protect against oxidative degradation 
mainly in high temperatures (Peña et al., 2001). Such as the temperatures take place during 
processing. In the other side, light stabilizer like UV benzophenones are typically used on 
APF to provide protection against UV radiation caused degradation (Simpson, 2003). Both 
are applied in low dosage concentration below 1% w/w. Traditionally, they are considered

4.2.4 FTIR ANALYSIS 
Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR) is the most common method in studies to 
investigate the stabilization and the effect of UV degradation on the polymer’s surface. IR 
can monitor the rates of surface oxidation by identifying the formed non-volatile oxidation 
products. The determination of the oxidation product groups means to identify and monitor 
the changes of the formed distinctive peaks on the wave number region correspond to each 
oxidation product. The main oxidation products monitored of studies on environmental 
degradation for LPDE  formed easily distinguished peaks are ketones (1713-1718 cm-1), 
esters (1735 cm-1), carbon-oxygen (1000-1200 cm-1) (with 1180 cm-1 as the central 
wavenumber of the band) and vinyl group (909 cm-1) (Brandon et al., 2016; Grause et al., 
2020). Hence, for this study, the focus was given to monitor the development of these peaks 
as an indicator of env. degradation. 
The 20 APLF along with LDPE masterbatch pellets, the formulations before the 
compression, and used stabilizers per se were analyzed using a Nicolet iS50 attenuated 
total reflection Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy (ATR-FTIR) spectrometer from 
Thermo Scientific. APLF were placed on the ATR crystal and infrared (IR) spectrum was 
recorded in reflection mode. The analysis was performed with 32 scans per replicate in the 
range of 4000 to 400 cm-1 at a resolution of 4cm-1 using the OMNIC software.  IR spectra 
record a point of the film surface. Considering the nonuniform surface of the compressed 
films it was chosen that IR spectra record 6 to 8 random points of the surface of each side 
of the films. In addition, the high number of replicates spectra of each sample enabled 
statistical analysis. FTIR spectra can indicate which stabilizer formulation provides a better 
performance against environmental degradation by the increase of oxidation products.

However, to draw a solid conclusion, the formation of dominant oxidation product groups, 
such as carbonyls, should be measured following by statistical analysis.  
For this study, it was chosen to measure the degradation rate and compare the stabilizer 
formulation performance by calculating: the carbonyl index (CI) indicating the first step of 
degradation. It was calculated for each replica spectra. The CI is calculated as ratio of the

the CI suggested from (Albertsson et al., 1987) was the most suitable as the carbonyl group 
peak centered at the 1715 cm-1 corresponding to ketones (equation (a)). The denominator 
is the internal constant band was the absorbance of methyl group at 1465 cm-1.

4.2.5 STATISTICAL ANALYSIS 
The calculation of CI values from replica FTIR spectra generated a population of CI values 
for each of the 20 ALPF. The statistical analysis of CI populations allowed to obtain a solid 
conclusion of which stabilizer formulation had a better performance against environmental 
degradation. This information was necessary to be obtained since the loss of stabilizers is 
connected with their consumption against environmental degradation as a result of their 
performance (Bai et al., 2012; Rabek, 1990).  
One way Analysis of Variances (ANOVA) was performed to determine if the CI populations 
statistically differ from each other by comparing the population means of each population. It 
uses F-test for the statistical significance comparing more than 3 populations simultaneously 
(McHugh, 2011). The categorial independent variable is the time of exposure to 
environmental degradation conditions of the chamber and the quantitative dependent 
variable is the CI values. Namely, the comparison was performed every time for the 4 CI 
populations (one for each stabilizer formulation) for each sampling. By this, ANOVA 
compared populations exposed at the same times to environmental degradation conditions. 
Hence, 4 ANOVA tests were performed in total. The test was performed in R studio and the 
first step was to test if the populations met the ANOVA requirements of (a) normal 
==PAGE:32==
20 
 
distribution and (b) homogeneity of variance by performing a bartlett test. The (c) 
requirements for the independence of observations have been already met since there are 
no relationships between the obtained spectra from each stabilizer formulation. 
ANOVA is a test based on the Null Hypothesis (H0) which in this case is defined as the 
statement there is not any significant statistical difference between the populations. The 
alternative hypothesis (Ha) states that there is at least one population that differs significantly 
from others. The null hypothesis is rejected if the calculated p-value is below 0.05 chosen 
to be the level of significance.  
Afterward, Turkey´s Honestly-Significant Difference (TurkeyHSD) post-hoc test was 
performed to identify which is the population that differs. To achieve this, the test performed 
pairwise comparisons (6 in this case) between the populations. Among the calculated 
factors, is the adjusted p-value of each comparison.  The comparison is based on Null 
hypothesis defined that the population is significant the same. The alternative hypothesis Ha 
states that there is a significant difference between the two populations. If the adjusted p-
value is below 0.05 the H0 is rejected, so there is a significant difference between the 
populations.   
The visualization of the CI populations was conducted by plotting boxplots. Observations of 
the shape of the distribution, the median value (mid-point of the data), and the variance can 
be made. The box plots included a “box” with two whiskers for each population. The box is 
divided into 3 sections: the lower quartile (Q1), the median value (Q2), and the upper quartile 
(Q3).   
The concentration (mg/g) of stabilizers derived from HPLC-UV analysis were treated in a 
similar way. The aim was to identify if the concentration of triplicates of each sampling differs 
significantly.  
The CR has chosen to be illustrated in barplots due to the relatively high variance of 
population values which could not allow the performance of ANOVA. Furthermore, the 
Standard Error (SE), Standard Deviation (SD), and the 95% confidence interval (Ci) were 
calculated for both CI and CR populations. SD is used to measure the variability of the 
population regardless of the distribution. SE indicates the variation of sample means from 
SD. Namely, it describes the uncertainty on estimation of means considering the size of the 
==PAGE:33==
21 
 
population. In this case, the populations did not have the same size. Ci indicates the range 
of values believed to include the actual values of population means with a confidence level 
of 95% (Andrade, 2020).  
The calculated mean values of CI and concentrations (mg/g) were plotted to the time of 
exposure including the ci as an error bar. Finally, the concentration was a plot against the 
corresponding CI to visualize/ investigate a correlation between them.

4.4.1 MICROWAVE ASSISTED EXTRACTION 
The procedure was based on ASTM D7210-13 standard on the extraction of Additives in 
Polyolefin Plastics including MAE as a suggested method. The extraction solvent was 
chosen to be cyclohexane which is a suitable solvent for extract antioxidants from 
polyolefins. Firstly, the extraction was done in triplicates of each APLF sample. The APLF 
were cut in pieces to prepare 0.25 grams for each replica sample.  
Afterward, the replicas samples were placed on 100 ML extraction Teflon vessels along with 
a metallic heating element. The extractions were carried out with an Anton Paar Multiwave 
3000 microwave having a carousel rotor of 16 positions. To control the temperature 
throughout the extraction, one blank vessel (without sample) needs to be placed during each 
extraction.  
==PAGE:35==
23 
 
20 ml of cyclohexane was add to every vessel and the blank ones. The vessels were flashed 
with Argon to eliminate the presence of oxygen and after were closed with pressure leads. 
The carousel was placed on the microwave oven and the program “Antiox Plastics PE” was 
chosen. During the extraction, the temperature was held at 100 oC and 45 min. Then, the 
temperature decreased to 0oC to cool the vessels for 15 min. After the finish of the whole 
procedure, the solved place to glass vials for the HPLC-UV analysis. 
Before starting the extraction, several trials to select the suitable amount of the samples. 
The protocol suggests using 1.5 grams of plastic samples for 20 mL of the selected solvent. 
However, due to previous MAEs using the recommended amount, it was known that the 
plastic samples swelled. The swelling of plastic samples, is a phenomenon that can be 
normally occurred during solvent extraction (Ügdüler et al., 2020). After some trials, the most 
suitable amount to do the MAE was 0.25 grams. However, also this amount results in 
swelling, but the solvent could be removed and analyzed. However, it was necessary to 
centrifugate all extractions after MAE for 3 min. Afterward, the solvents were separated and 
placed in new vessels for HPLC-UV analysis

6 DISCUSSION 
 
The section includes a discussion on results of the experimental setup divided into changes 
on APFL surfaces by the generation of oxidation product groups (section 6.1) and on 
concentration loss of APFL (section 6.2) which is the main objective of the study. In addition, 
a correlation between the material changes and concentration losses is discussed too. 
Afterward, the implication of the generation of antioxidant degradation products is discussed 
as well without being quantified during the experimental setup. The discussion is based on 
findings derived from a literature review  
The discussion focuses mainly on a critical analysis of the results when applied to the field 
of Environmental Engineering. A discussion on arisen challenges related to polymer 
processing are briefly discussed. Finally, recommendations on monitor the stabilizer 
concentration losses induced mainly from degradation on APF are provided. Along with 
recommendation on elimination on stabilizer losses. 
 
6.1 ENV. DEGRADATION OF ALPF: THE ROLE OF STABILIZERS 
Traditionally, Chimassorb 81 UV absorber is not considered as the most efficient UV 
stabilizer can be used alone compared to other light stabilizer (e.g hindered amine 
stabilizer). Most of the times is used in combinations with other light stabilizer (Simpson, 
2003; Tolinski, 2015). However, the protection which it provided to ALPF during 1000 hr of 
exposure under env. degradative conditions is indisputable.  
The generation of LDPE degradation products of carbonyl, carbon-oxygen and vinyl groups 
(figure 8., figure 10.) are successfully moderated for both type of APLF, UV and AUV which 
were stabilized with UV absorber compared with the unstabilized APLF. Moreover, only 
carbonyl peaks of UV and AUV films were clearly formed during the exposure and after 510 
hr the vinyl peak started to be formed. Considering that the formation of vinyl groups take 
place during the last step of oxidation (Babaghayou et al., 2016; Gardette et al., 2013), the 
degradation of AUV and UV ALPF are mostly on early stages.  
To draw a deeper understanding on development of carbonyls which can depict the 
degradation rate of APFL and the role of UV stabilizer, the CI was calculated. The figure 12. 
==PAGE:50==
38 
 
clearly reveals the protection of UV absorber mainly until 510 hr of exposure where the CI 
increase is almost marginal. This is one more finding proving the statement that the 
stabilized films in general exhibits a low increase of carbonyls during the first stages of 
degradation (Briassoulis et al., 2004). The comparison is even bigger after 1000 hr of 
artificial accelerated exposure equals to 1 year of exposure under natural environmental 
conditions. CI for unstabilized APLF tenfold increased compared with AUV and UV types of 
APFL where the CI only increased by 4 and 2 times accordingly. On top of that, the results 
of TurkeyHSD test confirmed that the observed differences on figure 12 are statistically 
significant (p<0.05). 
Nevertheless, in real environmental conditions the results for the prepared ALPF may differs. 
The rate of UV degradation which is the main factor of environmental degradation is highly 
depended on a possible increase of temperature. During the exposure of ALPF to artificial 
accelerated env. degradation the temperature kept constant at 60 oC during UV radiation.    
The rate of UV degradation doubles for every 10oC increase of temperature (Rabek, 1990).  
6.2 QUANITIFICATION OF CONCENTRATION LOSS  
The main objective of this study is the quantification of loss of stabilizers during the 
environmental degradation. Since ALPF are products with outdoor applications, most of their 
service life are exposed to environmental degradation conditions. Hence, it can be said that 
there was quantifiable concentration loss of stabilizers service life of this product.  
However, the HPLC-UV analysis could quantify the concentration only for UV absorber, 
Chimmasrob 81 on UV and AUV type of APFL before and during exposure to env. 
degradation.  
6.2.1 IMPLICATIONS ON STABILIZATION WITH ANTIOXIDANT 
The IR spectra of figures (figure 8., figure 10) the relative high CI of fugure 12. along with 
the high brittleness of ALPF stabilized with Antioxidant, Irganox 1076 rose a ground of 
concern about the stabilization. It was expected from a similar study from Blázquez-
Blázquez et al., 2020 on Irganox 1076 performance and other studies on other antioxidant 
stabilizers performance (e.g, Nomura and Terwilliger, 2019; Yang et al., 2016), that the 
antioxidant performance against UV degradation is poor and their concentration rapidly 
decreases. Generally, Irganox 1076 belongs to hindered phenol antioxidants which exhibit 
poor UV stability under UV irradiation so a low performance (Pospíšil, 1988). However, as 
==PAGE:51==
39 
 
the results exhibited in section 5.1 and 5.1.1, in this study the resistance of stabilized with 
Irganox 1076 films was so poor as the non-stabilized films. The HPLC-UV could not quantify 
the antioxidant in all samples, except from one replica of unexposed films, which is 0.015 
mg/g or 0.0015 % (w/w). And this is not attributed to problem of MAE or HPLC-UV method 
since the solvent and the extraction method was chosen based on ASTM D7210-13 
standard.  
The amount added during compounding was 0.4% (w/w). Hence, it's clear that it could not 
be incorporated successfully. The most possible reason is that antioxidants can be 
successfully introduced into polymer compounds when the polymer melts. For example, 
during the polymer extrusion (Murphy, 2001; Rabek, 1990). The compression molding is a 
process with high temperatures hence it  can cause a loss of antioxidant (Chakraborty and 
Scott, 1977). But considering the low residence time of 10 min in hot press, the concentration 
cannot fall in such extent.  
Due to the implication on antioxidant stabilization, it was chosen to exclude any discussion 
about films stabilized only with Antioxidant. But the discussion included films stabilized with 
UV absorber and antioxidant (AUV type). 
6.2.2 QUANTIFICATION OF UV-ABSORBER LOSS 
The statement that env. degradation can cause loss of stabilizers (Bai et al., 2012; Rabek, 
1990; Scoponi et al., 2000), is proved on this study. Indeed, there is a decrease on the mean 
value of UV concentration with exposure time for AUV and UV films (figure 16). However, 
the result of TukeryHSD test proved that the decrease is not statistically significant when 
only least 250 hr passed between each sampling.  
The overall loss UV absorber content after 1000 hr is 39.7% on UV type and 25.7% on AUV 
films. Similar study on accelerated UV degradation on LPDE films stabilized with a blend of 
two HALS with 0.6% (w/w) addition dosage, exhibited 44% loss after 1500 hr (Scoponi et 
al., 2000). Another study investigating a loss rate of another HALS, Tinuvin 770 on 
polypropylene films exposed under natural aging indicated 50% of loss (Blázquez-Blázquez 
et al., 2020). The results cannot be compared due to the different action mechanism of HALS 
along with the different thickness and type of polymer on last case. But the published studies 
along with this study on Chimassorb 81 can provide a general trend on loss of light stabilizers 
of polyolefin films under UV irradiation.

6.3 INVESTIGATION OF LOSS MECHANISM 
The rate of UV-absorber concentration loss on UV and AUV type of ALPF is not the same. 
After 510 hr started it started to be obvious that UV absorber on UV films started to decrease 
faster. The difference is even more distinguished of the table 5.  where the loss is almost 3 
times and 1.5 times higher on UV films after 770 and 1000 hr accordingly. In parallel, for the 
same time exposure of 770 and 1000 hr the CI of AUV films proved to be statistically 
significant lower compared to UV films. Generally, the two target stabilizers of the study, 
Chimassorb 81 and Irganox 1076 exhibit a synergism on the mechanism  against oxidative 
degradation when they blend together (Chakraborty and Scott, 1977; Wypych, 2015). Even 
though the amount of antioxidant is below the quantification limit of 1 mg/l, it was enough to 
influence and increase the performance of AUV films against env. degradation. However, 
the synergism is indicated to effective after 770 hr of exposure, so on later stages of env. 
degradation (table 3).  
Moreover, the AUV film carrying out small amounts of antioxidant can depict more the 
stabilization condition of a commercial ALPF. It is almost impossible a film to not include a 
necessary amount of processing antioxidant, as Irganox 1076 is, to protect against 
degradative conditions of processing (Vilaplana and Karlsson, 2008).  
The physical loss of stabilizer can be due to consumption while acting against UV 
degradation, to migration, or to volatilization when it reach the polymer surface (Rabek, 
1990; Scoponi et al., 2000). The mentioned studie on stabilizer losses have not investigated 
or determined the factor caused loss. In this study, it is concluded (section 5.3) that there is 
strong linear correlation between concentration decrease and CI for each ALPF type. In 
other words, since CI indicates degradation rate through carbonyl group formation, the loss 
of concentration on each ALPF type is significantly related with degradation. Furthermore, 
this correlation can indicate a presence of synergism since due to the similar pattern of 
fluctuation of figure 12 and figure 13 after 770 hr of exposure.

6.4.1 THE BENEFITS OF STABILIZERS ON CE 
Considering the fundamental aim of CE action plan to maintain all the resources in the loop 
as long as possible, and prevent the environmental burden, additives such as stabilizers 
successfully serve this aim. The stabilization with light stabilizer, as Chimassorb 81 is more 
==PAGE:53==

than necessary. is visual and on figures photo of films. The ubstanibilized films started to 
exhibit visual changes after 510 of exposure and after 770 hr become brittle and having an 
obvious scratch on it. Without a carefully handling a unstabilized films can be exhibit 
fractures. The high brittleness is a well-known result of high degradation caused from 
increase of crystallinity (Brandon et al., 2016; Kalogerakis et al., 2017). Hence an ustabilized 
APF fails to serve its role on protection of crops.  
Even though the CI of APFL stabilized with UV absorber increased, they do not exhibit any 
visual change or any visual indication of brittleness. In addition, a similar study on 
Chimassorb 81 performance, but on HDPE films, during accelerated UV degradation, 
concluded the compound helped the film to maintain the initial mechanical properties 
(elongation at break, tensile strength). 
Additionally, the prolong of service life of ALPF due to the action of light stabilizers benefits 
the agriculture investors since they prevent the extra cost of multiple replacements. 
Consequently, the demand on agriculture plastic will not further increase as well as the 
depletion of raw materials and energy resources and the associated environmental impacts. 
With a political point of view, stabilizers at general can help EU to rely on its own virgin 
material production by decreasing the need of imports (in terms of oil, pellets masterbatch, 
resin, of APF).  
Considering the first aim of CE on closing the loop between the EoL and production through 
mechanical recycling, application of both processing and light stabilizers is advantageous 
again. As it discussed on 6.1, they prevent the high degradation linked with  the deterioration 
of mechanical properties which impede a successful recycling resulting to a SRM which 
cannot meet the material requirements (Francis, 2016; Ragaert et al., 2017). 
Furthermore, the rate of recycling is influenced by the presence of high concern and legacy 
additives (Delva et al., 2019; Hahladakis and Iacovidou, 2018). Their presence can hinder 
all the waste stream resulting to a SMR which cannot fulfill the regulatory requirements for 
a toxic free content. Traditionally, antioxidants and light stabilizers are not considered as 
hazardous or high concern compounds compared to other additives like flame retardants or 
phthalate plasticizers (excluding the heavy metal stabilizers) (Andrady and Rajapakse, 
2016). Most of them are not included on REACH regulation lists of Restriction or 
Authorization or on published candidate list of substances of very high concern (SVHC) for

Authorization. Also, antioxidants like Irganox 1078 and Chimassorb 81 are not even included 
on CLP regulation, so they are not classified into any hazard category. They only be included 
on EU Plastic Food Contact Material (FCM) directive as authorized substances with a 
specific substances migration limit (SML) (table 1). Therefore, they do not pose any 
regulatory limitation ensuring the uptake of SRM on the market. They can be even used for 
food plastic packaging.  
Even though the multiple benefits of stabilizers on increasing the APF circularity, the 
influence of environmental degradation on them induce negative impacts on both the quality

impacts on CE. This study investigates the quantification of stabilizer losses induced by

discussion is based on literature review conducted with key words like: NIAS, degradation 
products, restabilization of plastic films and reprocessing.   
6.4.2 ENVIROMENTAL IMPACTS OF STABILIZER DEGRADATION 
Even though the phenolic antioxidant, Irganox 1076 could not successfully be incorporated 
in ALPF, its degradation can be discussed. Stabilizers are degraded/depleted because they 
are consumed while counteracting against oxidative degradation (Hahladakis et al., 2018; 
Rabek, 1990). During their performance against oxidation they and transformed to other 
compounds, mostly to aldehydes and hydroxyphenyl (Bai et al., 2012; Vera et al., 2018). 
Consequently, degradation products are formed and accumulated as the intact stabilizers 
are consumed (Reingruber and Buchberger, 2010). These degradation products are 
included on a groups of compounds called non intentionally added substances (NIAS) and 
they are trapped inside the plastic (Horodytska et al., 2020; Vera et al., 2018). They have 
lower molecular weight (MW) increasing their water solubility and volatility. The degradation 
products of Antioxidants, specifically Irganox 1076 1010 or Irgafos 168, are well studied and 
identified since they are also used in packaging plastics which compared with APF falls 
under numerus regulations and directives to ensure for the safety of consumers (Groh et al., 
2019).  
==PAGE:55==
43 
 
The migration is the phenomenon where a non-bound to polymer compound is moving from 
the polymer matrix to the surface. When the surface is in contact with another medium, like 
water or food, the compound can migrate from the polymer to the other medium. The risk of 
migration is increasing with the concentration of the compound and is decreasing with higher 
thickness. Also compounds with low MW have more tendency migrate and to be soluble to 
water as they are they are more hydrophilic (Hansen et al., 2014).  
Considering the conditions of migration, degradation products can migrate form polyolefin 
films compared to their high MW “mother” compounds which their concentration are rapidly 
decreasing with the exposure time (Blázquez-Blázquez et al., 2020). While the concentration 
of degradation products is increasing and can be easily migrate (Yang et al., 2016). 
Additionally, the generation of fragment from env. degradation trigger the release of added 
compounds since the available surface area is increasing (Sørensen et al., 2021) 
APF are exposed during their service life under environmental conditions like rain falls or 
can be surrounded by water or soil bodies. Therefore, the degradation products which are 
moved to the surface of APF can migrate and contaminate surrounding water bodies or be 
washed with the rainfalls or even can be exposed to air since are volatile (VOC) or semi-
volatile (SVOC) compounds. Additionally, they exhibit a high mobility to soil bodies as well. 
Indeed, a study on farmlands pollution reveals that degradation products of Antioxidants are 
in abundance in soil bodies around mulch films (Gong et al., 2021). Their behavior in 
presence of agrochemicals like herbicides is not investigated yet. 
When these compounds are released to surrounding environmental media, they can 
potentially be more hazardous than remain on APF. Researchers are still investigating the 
impact to agroecosystems that such release can cause. Also, there is a gap on ecotoxicity 
assessment test for plant toxicity for released compounds from APF (Pereira et al., 2021). 
Even though the degradation products of Irganox 1076 such as are not included on any 
REACH list or CLP regulation, they are considered as possible hazardous. Specifically, the 
table. 6 includes two studied degradation products of Irganox 1076. The first is notified to 
possible pose chronic toxicity to aquatic organisms and the second one may exhibit CMR 
characteristics. Another degradation product which should be mentioned, is 2,4-di-tert-
butylphenol derived from Irgafos 168. Currently it is under endocrine disrupting (ED) 
assessment and it is assed for CMR concerns by community rolling action plan (CoRAP).

pass to aquifers posing a human health risk.

6.4.3 IMPLICATIONS OF SMR QUALITY AND MEHCANICAL RECYCLING   
After the service of a plastic film, the post-consumer products can end up having a relatively 
high amount of NIAS trapped and accumulated inside the material. The presence and 
analysis of them have been a subject of a high number of research studies involving complex 
analytical techniques (among the other (among the others, Cecon et al., 2021; Peters et al., 
2020; Vera et al., 2018). The concentration, and hazard characteristics or even their 
presence on post-consumer plastic is not known to the waste handlers and recyclers. 
Hence, they can be found SRM through mechanical recycling since most of the times cannot 
be removed by purification steps (Horodytska et al., 2020; Pfaendner, 2006). If the 
degradation products are of high concern or a substance of very high concern, the SRM 
cannot used for the fabrication of a new material since they will not meet the regulatory 
requirement for a safe plastic article (De Tandt et al., 2021). Therefore, high-concern NIAS 
==PAGE:57==
45 
 
cause a negative impact on CE since they impede the closing of loop between the end of 
life and production.  
Moreover, NIAS pose one more challenge to quality of SMR make them more sensitive to 
degradation. Antioxidants break down products can trigger and accelerate the processing 
degradation occurred during reprocessing stage of mech. recycling and env. degradation of 
the second service life even they are present on SMR in trace amounts (Yousif and Haddad, 
2013).  
Furthermore, once the oxidation groups of carbonyl (ketones, esters) and vinyl form 
degradation monitored in this study as well (section 5.1) formed, they catalyze any oxidative 
degradation can take place during life stages. Oxidation groups cannot be removed, or the 
material cannot fully restored form previous env degradation though restabilization (La 
Mantia and Gardette, 2002; Vilaplana and Karlsson, 2008)  
Therefore, a SMR derived from a degrade product and regardless any restabilization or 
purification during reprocessing, will fabricate a product which will exhibits higher rates of 
degradation compared to a product from new polymer resins.  
6.5 APPLICATION OF EXPERIMENTAL RESULTS  
Figure. 16 exhibits the significant correlation between the loss of UV stabilizer content and 
material degradation during the service life stage. Since material degradation related to 
deterioration mechanical properties, it can be said that the material quality is strongly 
compromised from the stabilizer depletion. But also, the type of ALPF stabilization (section 
6.3) concluded to influence the loss rate of stabilizer. In this case study, UV and AUV type 
of ALPF gradually lost after 1 year of service life 39.7% and 25.7% of UV stabilizer 
Therefore, there is strong need to disclosure the type of stabilization in polymer data for 
circular economy. In addition, in the disclosure requirements is more than necessary to 
include the loss on stabilizer taking place at any of life cycle stage when there is need to 
transition to circular systems. The loss of almost 26% in the best scenario of this study after 
1 year is a number that cannot be avoided. The reason is that the loss is output of the system 
so there should be a new input again to ensure the quality which is highly related with the 
stabilizer concentration. The loss of a UV stabilizers during production stage, which the 
==PAGE:58==
46 
 
study couldn’t provide, is of equal importance to be including on the chemical inventory on 
production life stage. 
The disclosure data of a chemical inventory are necessary to facilitate LCA which is ultimate 
standardized method quantify the sustainability and circularity of a product and systems. 
LCA studies on plastic products do not choose to include in their inventory data on added 
stabilizers. A possible reason may be that stabilizers added in concentration below 1% (w/w) 
the disclosure requirements thresholds. However, since stabilizers strongly related with 
material quality, their exclusion of an inventory can alter the LCA outcome.  
A LCA outcome having all the necessary data on its inventory can suggest frameworks to 
increase the circularity and sustainability of product design considering all the life cycles 
stages. Moreover, it can identity the parts of the product system inducing most of 
environmental impacts and loss of resources which are necessary findings to consider when 
develop a requirements of a sustainable design. 
 Hence it can pave the way for the transition to more circular systems keeping as much 
possible resource sin the loop. 
Furthermore, results on loss ratio with time and the fact that are strongly related with 
degradation can provide another one valuable information. As it is an output of the system 
and need to be re-add again, the way that the loss is restored in circular system, is through 
the restabilization step on mechanical recycling. The amount which should be re added 
would relate on the remaining amount (Attwood et al., 2006). Hence, knowing the loss per 
exposure time for ALPF, the input of the same stabilizer could be optimized without to re 
add more that it needs. For example, if the films in this case reach the EoL stage after one 
year and collected to be recycled to be a new product with the same outdoor application, it 
is not necessary to re add again the same initial amount of concentration dosage. Knowing 
that around 26% is lost in the best case of this study, even less amount is required to re add 
during restabilization. However, these scenario of optimizing the restabilization would be 
more realistic knowing how is the amount of stabilizer got lost during stabilization during 
processing.    
The chemcial inventory could even more enrich wit the data on loss during stabilization on

6.6 RECOMMENDATION ON MONITORING THE LOSSES 
APF almost never include only 2 compounds similar to this study. They can include a high 
number of stabilizers and other functional additives with different combination and ratios to 
achieve the desirable properties. As it is proven during this study, stabilizers are 
degraded/depleted due to environmental degradation. But the loss ratio of each stabilizer 
can depend on presence of mechanism like synergism (section 6.3) and on various 
combinations between the stabilizers. Additionally, every stabilizer will exhibit a different loss 
ratio due several factors such as: their different structure, action against env. degradation, 
compatibility with every polymer type (Rabek, 1990). 
Therefore, it is almost impossible to obtain an overview information on ratio losses of all 
included additive on APF. Considering the challenges of this study on extrusion, 
stabilization, and extraction of additives for analysis, it is overall very changeling to monitor 
the losses. Considering as well that it is a matter of analytical studies which most of the 
times are complex, limited for complex additives, time consuming and requirements high 
financial investments.  
Hence the solution can be given from a product design and regulation on chemicals and 
products perspectives: 
1. Set mandatory requirements on number and complexity of additives providing similar 
performance. The use of a quite simply UV stabilizers, such as Chemisorb 81 
concluded to be enough to provide the sufficient the necessary protection. The use 
more complex stabilization systems maybe can proved a better results (Tolinski, 
2015), but the benefits for only one additive can overcome the benefit of the 
performance. 
2. Establishment mandatory regulatory requirements to importers/manufactures of 
chemical additives or plastic products to conduct UV degradation test for and 
inclusion this information on registration dossier. Also, this action will provide data on 
degradation products of each stabilizer. This recommendation can be feasible in 
combination with the decrease of number and complexity of additive in plastics.    
3. Transparency and good communication of the data on plastic additives from 
responsible organizations (e.g ECHA). Nowadays, most of the data, number and 
specific characteristics  on additives are offered by published journals or technical 
==PAGE:60==
48 
 
reports (Aurisano et al., 2021; Hansen et al., 2014; Wiesinger et al., 2021), which 
cannot can be easily access from all the stakeholders  
The recommendations require the cooperation and good communication of all the involved 
stakeholders to be established. The first step should be the establishment of a framework 
for an obligatory cooperation of stakeholder towards a data transparency and sustainable 
design. 
6.7 LIMITATIONS AND SOURCE OF UNCERTAINTY  
A limitation was the number or replica samples for concentration quantification which was 
limited to 3 replicas. Sole replicas had quite high sd and ci of the mean values. More replicas 
can provide a clearer picture of concentration loss. 
The biggest limitation maybe is the fact that all the analytical part was done one time, in the 
end of the experiment. So, it was unknown if and how well the target was incorporated in 
the films. Indeed, the antioxidant could be incorporated well. If analysis was done before the 
weathering experiment, the method of incorporation would be changed. Since the 
weathering experiment was finished 3 weeks before the deadline and lasting around 48 days 
(including the problems with the chambers), it was impossible to be repeated. 
A source of uncertainty can be found in the fact that every film was compounded separately, 
and a new formulation was prepared for each one. The analysis was done from one film 
form each formulation (UV, AUV, A) which were not exposed to env. degradation. Hence 
there is chance not all the films to have the same compositions before being placed to 
weathering. 
Also, the statistical analysis of spectra maybe has errors from the absorbance baseline 
 
6.8 RECOMMENDATIONS OF FEATURE WORK AND IMPROVEMENTS 
Firstly, the experimental procedure should be repeated having the same experimental set 
up and material with one main difference. The ALPF should be prepared with the larger 
scale extruder preparing stabilized masterbatches which can be used to prepare films with 
compression molding. High importance is to carefully clean the extruder from residual of 
previous extrusions. It is strongly recommended to analyze the film or the stabilized 
masterbatches before proceeding to experiment of service life or processing life stage 
==PAGE:61==
49 
 
simulation. Also, not destructive methods should be preferred for the analysis of additives 
like UV spectroscopy and FTIR with the necessary calibration, as they was found applicable 
for identify the concentration in study of Scoponi et al., 2000.  
However, a complete study off all the parameters influences the loss should be done to 
obtain a solid understanding. For instance, If APFL were exposed under real env. conditions, 
a target chemical analysis of surrounding soil and surface water could give answer if an 
exposure of UV stabilizer take place 
Instead of HPLC-UV, GC-MS analysis should be preferred to enable the identification of 
degradation products of the antioxidant. This would be a very interesting finding which is of 
equals importance for CE, as it is a new imput of the system. Also it will provide a more 
complete picture of the behavior of stabilizer throughout all the life stages of the plastic 
product. Also, the rate of generation of degradation products can be concluded for such a 
study      
If the same experiment be counted again, the last step should be to perform a LCA of a cost 
benefit analysis included the data on losses.  
The recommendation for another feature work can be the investigation and determination of 
mechanism on how the UV degradation exactly influence the migration or release of 
additives. Mainly how the material changes influence the migration. Many recent studies 
investigated and concluded that the degradation induce the leach of additives like 
plasticizers, and heavy metals on plastic bags, weathered MPs and microfibers (Simon et 
al., 2021; Sørensen et al., 2021; Xu et al., 2020).

7 CONCLUSION 
 
During this research study the experimental set ups simulating service life stages of ALPF 
was successfully conducted with the aim to quantify the concentration loss of both 
stabilizers. 
The beneficial role of stabilizers generally on CE and on protection the ALPF environmental 
degradation is indisputable. They limited the impact of UV degradation on material mostly 
on early stages making a product sufficient to provide protection to crops. However, benefits 
stop once stabilizers are lost or to be transformed to degradation byproducts. Indeed, env. 
degradation caused a gradually loss of UV absorber exhibiting a different loss rate on each 
APLF type after 500 hr. Fact, that can indicate the synergism between UV stabilizer and 
Antioxidant, even if the last one is on very low amount. 
In this case, it was concluded that the loss of UV absorber is strongly related with 
degradation of material saying that the main factor of loss is the UV irradiation. This 
relationship highlight that the material quality is strongly compromised with the stabilizer 
depletion emphasizing the high importance to disclosure data on stabilization in polymer 
data for circular economy. Equals importance discussed to be the need to include the 
degradation products formed during env. degradation of antioxidant as impede CE 
May the finding of loss of almost 26% after 1000 hr in the best-case scenario of the study 
be only a preliminary idea on stabilizer loss. The number cannot exactly reflect the reality 
since a ALPF can have numerus of stabilizer combination. However, the study outcomes 
are important to initiate frameworks to accelerate the mandatory disclosure of stabilizers on 
product inventory of life stages. The reason is that while considering circular product 
systems any loss is an output of the system and since it highly affects the material, the loss 
should be restored again with virgin additive input to restore the quality. 
To monitor the stabilizer loss throughout the life stages proved to be challenging and matter 
of complex experimental set ups and analysis task. Hence, the establishment of regulatory 
actions and design requirements can give the solution to monitor the loss and behavior of 
stabilizers throughout the life stages on the plastic product, such as APLF. However, these 
actions requiring the obligatory communication and cooperation of all the stakeholder 
involved throughout the life stages.
