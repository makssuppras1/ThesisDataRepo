Abstract
In this thesis an ecient method for evaluating nonlinear Froude-Krylov and hyd-
rostatic forces in the time domain is developed in MATLAB.
The program is developed as an extension to the already established DTUMo-
tionSimulator, which solves the equations of motion for a oating body in the
time domain using Impulse Response Functions, derived from frequency-domain
outputs generated by the commercial program WAMIT.
The nonlinear Froude-Krylov force and hydrostatic forces are solved and eva-
luated at each time step and used as an external force in the linear calculations.
This gives the weakly non-linear response of a oating body.
The developed functions are rst evaluated using simple bodies, where analytical
solutions exist. Then, the weakly nonlinear motions and forces on a sphere and a
large barge-like wave energy device are compared to the linear results.
The nonlinear Froude-Krylov force is used to calculate the weakly nonlinear mids-
hip bending moment on a barge-like wave energy device. It is seen that the non-
linear moments have a larger magnitude than the linear results, and that the
dierence between them grows with wave steepness. These moments can be used
to design the structure to avoid damage in survival mode, or to evaluate suitable
locations for an existing device design.
Work on the calibration of a load cell to be used for the measurement of midship
bending moment is also presented.
The experimental work will take place in
Portaferry, Northern Ireland in 2019, on a scale model of the KNSwing barge-like
wave energy device.

Resumé
I dette master projekt bliver en eektiv metode til at evaluate ikke-lineære Froude-
Krylov og hydrostatiske krafter i tidsdomænet udviklet og implementeret i MAT-
LAB.
Programmet er udviklet som en udvidelse af det allerede eksisterende DTUMotio-
Simulator - Der løser bevegelsæsligningerne for et ydende legeme i tidsdomænet.
Ud fra Impuls Respons Funktioner der er fundet i frekvens-domæne resultater fra
det kommercielle program WAMIT.
De ikke-lineære Froude-Krylov og hydrostatiske krafter bliver løst og evalueret
i hvert eneste tidsskridt, disse bliver indsat som en ekstern kraft i de lineæere ud-
regninger.
Dette giver det svagt ikke-lineære respons af et ydende legeme.
De udviklede funtkioner er først blevet brugt til at løse volumen og Froude-Krylov
krafter på simple legemer hvor analytiske løsninger eksisterer.
Det svagt ikke-lineære respons og de ikke-lineære Froude-Krylov krafter bliver
udregnet på en sfære og et pram-lignende bølgenergianlæg.
Disse bliver sam-
menlignet med det lineære respons fra WAMIT koecienterne. De ikke-lineære
Froude-Krylov krafter, bliver brugt til at evaluere det svagt ikke-lineære bøjnings-
moment på midten af prammen. Det ses at det ikke-lineære bøjningsmoment er
større end det der ndes ud fra de lineære resultater, og at forskellen mellem de
to bliver større når bølge hældningen stiger. Disse momenter kan bruges til at
designe bølgenergianlæg så der undgås skade i overlevelses tilstand, eller til at
evaluere egnede lokationer for et eksisterende design.
En belastningscelle er blevet kalibreret, denne skal bruges til at måle midtskibs
bøjningsmomentet på en model af KNSwing bølgenergi anlægget. Disse forsøg skal
udføres i Portaferry, Nord Irland i løbet af 2019.

Introduction
1
Introduction
The main idea of this project is to create an extension of the 'DTUMotionSimula-
tor' program, Bingham (2018), to include weakly nonlinear forces in a time domain
analysis of a body subjected to waves. The linear coecients and forces are solved
in the frequency domain using 'WAMIT'. These are transferred to the time domain
using Fourier transforms.
The nonlinear Froude-Krylov and hydrostatic forces are solved at each times-
tep and added as an external force in the linear DTUMotionSimulator. As the
linear forces from the added mass, damping and scattering are still used, this gives
a "weakly-nonlinear" solution in the time domain.
The theory behind the nonlinear forces and the implementation of the program is
presented in Section 4 and 5.
The Froude-Krylov force and volume calculations are veried using simple geome-
tries where exact solutions exist, this can be seen in Section 6.
A comparison between the linear and weakly nonlinear results from WAMIT is
performed for a sphere and a large barge, in Section 8.
The developed and veried weakly nonlinear program is then used to evaluate
the forces on a barge-like oscillating water column (OWC) wave energy device,
the KNSwing, in the time domain, and to calculate the midship bending moment
for dierent wave steepness' using a strip theory approach with 3D coecients,
Jensen (2001). This can be seen in Section 9.
This is an extension of the midship bending moment measurement campaign
from Hansen (2018), and a precursor to an experimental measurement campaign
that will be carried out in Northern Ireland in 2019 by Kim Nielsen to measure the
midship bending moment. The linear midship bending moment results from WA-
MIT are compared to the weakly nonlinear results from the developed extension
to the DTUMotionSimulator program.
1.1
KNSwing Wave Energy Generator
An oscillating water column wave energy device is a simple and well established
method for generating wave energy. An air chamber with a generator on top is
used to generate wave power. As waves pass by the device the surface elevation
inside the chamber will rise. This will push air out of the chamber and through
the turbine generator at the top and generate power.
The KNSwing OWC wave energy device is designed as a reinforced concrete
structure consisting of 40 chambers. With a total length of 150m in full-scale, and
a width of 18.75m, the expected draft is 9.375m. A concept drawing of the device
can be seen in Figure 1.
It is proposed that in survival mode, where the turbines are closed and no energy
is generated, the device can be modelled as a simple barge, so ignoring the internal
wave chambers for the modeling.
The midship bending moment of the device in survival mode is of great interest
because this represents the maximum forcing the device should be designed to
handle without structural damage. These are often also the steepest and most
nonlinear wave cases that the structure will experience, Penalba et al. (2017).

DTU Motion Simulator
DTUMotionSimulator is a MATLAB package for solving the linear response of a
body subjected to waves, Bingham (2018). It was developed by Harry B. Bing-
ham at the Department of Mechanical Engineering at DTU, the current release is
version 1.2 from 2018.
DTUMotionSimulator solves the equations of motions in the time-domain using
response amplitude functions to compute the linear forces at the excitation fre-
quency. Frequency response functions (FRF), inertia and hydrostatic coecients
for the body are found for a range of frequencies using 'WAMIT'. These are then
interpolated using the incident wave frequencies and amplitudes.
The equations of motion in the time domain given by (4.52) are time stepped using
an explicit Runge-Kutta fourth-order scheme.

4.10
Importance of the nonlinear terms
The importance of including the nonlinear terms in the calculations of the forces
on a body in waves generally depends on the seastate/the wave steepness, the
geometry of the body and the motions of the body, Penalba et al. (2017).
If the waves are steep the linearization assumption used for the formulation of li-
near waves will no longer hold and higher order terms can no longer be neglected.
For bodies where the cross sectional area changes, for example a sphere or a py-
ramid. The change in the surface elevation across the body can greatly change the
Froude-Krylov forces and hydrodynamic forces in time.
If the body motions are large, the assumption of small movements relative to the
body will no longer hold. This can lead to signicant higher order eects.

5
Implementation
The nonlinear equations for the Froude-Krylov force and the hydrostatic force are
implemented in MATLAB as an additional function that can be used together with
'DTUMotionSimulator' to model the weakly nonlinear body motions from a wave.
The fully nonlinear Froude-Krylov and hydrostatic forces are solved and added to
the Linear calculation as an additional external force. The linear radiation forces,
added mass and damping and scattering forces calculated from WAMIT are still
used to solve the equation of motion in the time domain.
The implemented MATLAB function can be seen in Appendix D.
5.1
Body discretization
The body is discretized using a quadrilateral mesh. This discretization could be
done using any kind of mesh, for example a structured or unstructured triangular
or polyhedron mesh.
The program uses the area of each cell, the center coordinates of the cell and
the unit normal vector pointing into the body. As long as these are supplied the
nonlinear forces can be calculated for any type of meshed closed body surface.

10.1
Calibration of Load Cell
A strain gauge type load sensor from VETEK, model 247W-D8 is used for the
calibration. The sensor measures strain when compressed and has a maximum
calibrated load capacity of approximately 980N. The load cell is connected to a
Novatech SGA Amplier, the maximum voltage output is 5V at 980N, and 0V
should correspond to no load.
Following the methodology from Hansen (2018), the force from loading a simply
supported unistrut beam is measured, and a relation between the voltage output
and expected force is found.
This is a very simple calibration method which
obviously gives rise to some errors. The errors for each measurement is estimated
and added using the fractional error estimation method.
The beam is allowed to rotate freely at one end using a hinge while the other end
is over hanging. The load cell is placed at a distance, Lc from the supported end
of the beam. Weights are mounted on a string and moved along the length of the
beam to give dierent reaction forces.

Further Work
11
Further Work
To further extend the accuracy of the developed model it could be useful to use
a more exact description of the body, for example B-splines or some other higher-
order method.
Another option is to use a mesh renement algorithm or another program to gene-
rate a non-uniform mesh, the developed program only takes the cell coordinates,
area and unit normal vector as inputs. By using a non-uniform mesh the most
important parts of the mesh around the free surface of the body can be rened to
both increase the accuracy and decrease the total run time.
The usage of other mesh types should be tested or veried, but there should be
nothing stopping the use of other types of mesh.
Currently the points on the internal free surface are found using the 'inhull'
MatLab command which rst creates a convex hull of the entire body and then
nds the points on the free surface discretization that are inside this convex hull.
It is seen that this is a very heavy function that takes a long time to evaluate. In
the current program it is evaluated in each Runge-Kutta step so four times per
timestep. It should be possible to either develop another function for generating
the internal free surface to the body, or nd a more ecient solution to the pro-
blem. The current developed program has not been optimized for solution speed,
so for ne meshes the run times can be fairly long.
Additional verication of the implemented method should be done against a fully
nonlinear motion solver or using more experimental data where the motions and
pressure/excitation forces are measured.

Conclusion
12
Conclusion
A weakly nonlinear extension to the MATLAB program DTUMotionSimulator
has been developed. The implementation method was thoroughly described and
the functions were rst veried to ensure that the surface integrals gave the exact
volumes for a cuboid and a cylinder. It was as expected seen that the implemented
quadrilateral mesh had rst-order convergence. The nonlinear Froude-Krylov force
was calculated for a cube in rst- and second-order linear waves and compared to
the exact results, and hereafter it was assumed that the developed solution method
was veried, and the implementation was used to test dierent cases.
Using this method, the nonlinear Froude-Krylov pressure and the Froude-
Krylov force were calculated on an FPSO and compared to experimental data.
It was seen that the time variation of the measured pressure was captured very
well. And generally the nonlinear pressure was slightly lower than the measured
pressure at the wave gauges, whereas the linear method often overestimated pres-
sure. Neither the nonlinear nor linear implementation of the pressure included the
additional pressure from the wave scattering, which should also slightly increase
the pressure at the measurement points. The nonlinear and linear Froude-Krylov
force pressure on the FPSO were also compared, it was seen that the nonlinear
pressure had higher peak values in both surge and heave.
The developed program was implemented in a function for use in DTUMotion-
Simulator to give en external force to the linear time series calculations.
The nonlinear implementation of DTUMotionSimulator was run for a sphere and
a large barge to compare the motions and forces with the linear results using only
WAMIT inputs. It was seen that the weakly nonlinear implementation generally
gave slightly larger movements in the tested cases. The Froude-Krylov force was
also compared and it was seen that the WAMIT and nonlinear forces were in phase
with each other but the nonlinear forces in surge had second order eects around
zero for the sphere and the moving barge.
Furthermore the nonlinear forces were for most cases slightly larger than the linear
WAMIT results. So it is seen that there can be some accuracy gained by using
the weakly nonlinear implementation, especially for steeper wave cases where the
linearization assumption will no longer hold. But also for geometries with curves
or a changing cross sectional area or for bodies where the motion amplitude can
no longer be assumed small compared to the body.
A comparison of the dierence between the linear and nonlinear midship ben-
ding moment is made, the moments were calculated using strip theory using the
linear WAMIT coecients and the nonlinear Froude-Krylov force. The moments
were evaluated in the time domain and it is seen that the nonlinear implementation
gives larger midship bending moments compared to the linear results. It was seen
that the dierence between the linear and nonlinear moments increased with the
wave steepness. So for steeper waves it is as expected more important to include
the nonlinear terms to get accurate results.
Preparatory work for experiments in Northern Ireland on the KNSwing test
model was also done. Relations for the voltage and measured force for the load
