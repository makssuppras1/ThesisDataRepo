Abstract
Background: Nowadays, People are using various technologies to empower their knowledge re­
sources. Social learning through online platforms is one of these technologies which helps people
to incorporate together virtually, learn faster, and gain the most trending and recent knowledge
from each other. This approach includes streamlined methods between two individual learners to
create new knowledge, which is valuable and useful for each learner. FullBrain is a startup estab­
lished in 2021 by three Computer Science graduates from the Technical University of Denmark
and two of them have the join degree from the University of Padua. It provides social learning
services through a social learning platform.
Objective: This study was performed in collaboration with FullBrain organization for identifying
suitable approaches to facilitate its user’s requirements. Specifically, it investigates the factors
influencing integrating a social bookmarking service into a social learning platform like (e.g. Full­
Brain) and explores which bookmarking services have the most possibility to acquire academic
users’ needs. In addition, this study focuses to evaluate bookmarking features to create a new
bookmarking engine for FullBrain based on its specific user’s requirements.
Methodology: This study is concocted based upon a design thinking methodology where a set
of different methods were established. By conducting an empathy meeting with FullBrain staff,
I obtained detailed information about organizational structure, targeted users, and the challenges
which users face while getting used of their interface. To have a better understanding of their
challenges, I used the interface to have a hand­in experience to the environment like a normal user
which helped me to understand the interface from a user interaction perspective. Besides that, I
have investigated various social bookmarking tools and applications in order to analyze and deter­
mine current solutions which can fulfill the expectations of FullBrain users while sharing online
resources through this platform. Afterward, I started brainstorming on possible approaches which
resulted in a series of solutions. Another analysis has been established to select the one which
could acquire most users’ needs. Lastly, based on the selected solution, a plug­in prototype has
been created in Adobe XD and validation test has been performed. Moreover, a google chrome
extension was developed and will be patched into FullBrain platform.
Results: The main practical implication derived from the findings of this study is a plug­in imple­
mentation for the FullBrain platform where able the users to bookmark online resources and share
them among other students. Moreover, results of research on integrable bookmarking services
indicate that there are some social bookmarking services that have the potential to be integrated
into a social learning platform like (e.g. Fullbrain). Despite this fact, the integration process is
costly for the user and FullBrain. This resulted in the development of a minimum viable plug­in
for FullBrain with the possibility of patching additional features for further development.
Key Words: bookmarking, social learning platform, FullBrain, tagging, social bookmarking inte­
gration, resource sharing.

Include all types of users who interact with the FullBrain interface.

Users who study in one of the intentional integration fields of science, technology, engineering,

Is a social learning platform where helps STEM students to come together in a virtual platform

Social Bookmarking:
Tag in the form of a link to a resource (e.g. web page) which can be stored, and possibly shared

A descriptive keyword entered by a human individual or generated by a system with the objective
to describe a resource (e.g. a web page)

Introduction
Nowadays human beings inherently tend to learn in a social environment (in a group) because they
learn much better that way. Making group­work environment is also established in the universi­
ties. For most European university students; courses are scheduled based on ECTS (European
credit transfer system) points wherein on average each ECTS is equal to 25­30 working hours that
includes hours spent at the university and outside the university[1]. Fact that students have a time
limitation for each course, they need to put in some extra effort and use techniques to promote
their skills to gain the required knowledge.
Advancement in technology, enabled the possibility of using social learning platforms that can
help students from universities to come together virtually and meet in the FullBrain course spaces.
These course spaces help them to achieve the required knowledge through asking questions, writ­
ing formulas, and sharing ideas. Moreover, people are not able to remember all the valuable
knowledge that has been observed once. This brings up the idea to store them somewhere for later
use. This collected information needs a special place where they are both saved and shared with
others. Internet is performed based on web pages and these web pages can be simply stored by col­
lecting their references that act as bookmarks pointing to that specific URL of the page. Through
this idea, Social bookmarking is explained as a form of shareable bookmarking that allows web­
sites to be bookmarked on the Web using a service instead of using the browser’s bookmarking
feature[2].
Currently, there are plenty of shared bookmarking applications like Pocket[3], Mix[4], and Scoop.it
[5] which concentrate on bookmarking useful resources and sharing daily trending events. There is
also another type of bookmarking application that targets common users and provides services that
people can save what do they love and are concerned about on the web. Twitter services provide
the most famous general­purpose social bookmarking platform where user can post links, images,
and their daily matters that can be saved and seen by other users at a later time[2]. Pinterest is
another example of a common application where has more than 70 million users and these users
get consults from promoted pages that have been advertised on the Pinterest platform[3].
Based on the above facts, this thesis focused on investigating a middle layer social bookmarking
service which can be integrable into a social learning platform helping students to store links
(URLs) and share them at a later time. Based on analysis and investigation through this thesis,
I find out that patching a middle layer bookmarking service is costly for the users and FullBrain
platform. Besides that, relying on external services will have a negative impact on the FullBrain
performance if a patched third­party software stop working or changed the feature functionalities.
interpretation on the middle layer integrable approaches resulted in exploring the concept of an
enhanced plug­in service which is developed for a certain social learning platform.

1.1
Setting the scene
One of the biggest challenges that people who use large information spaces are to remember these
data and their URLs. Conducting this information through their connected network is also another
factor that is challenging for them[6]. this means, there is a gap in archiving visited academic
URLs, annotating, and sharing them based on their needs for a later time. According to FullBrain
deliverable services; this platform is struggling to provide features that allow its users to bookmark
online resources and share them with each other.
Nowadays, the digital means of communication and academic content sharing has been widely
integrated and students are developing their level of technology literacy involve using the tech­
nologies to accomplish specific learning objectives[7]. moreover, the Covid­19 pandemic created
a crisis in education systems that forced schools and universities[8] to make the difficult decision
of closing their doors and conducting distance learning, reflecting greater involvement in the use
of online learning platforms.
FullBrain[9] is a social learning platform where helps STEM students to come together in a virtual
platform and meet in the course spaces. This platform helps them to ask questions, write math for­
mulas in a more reliable way and share online resources. This platform was founded in early 2021
and all of its services are free for the users. FullBrains social learning platform enables the users
to create unique spaces for each university course and helps students to connect with companies
based on their learning impacts.
The core feature of FullBrain is based on sources which are shared by users in the FullBrain plat­
form. When users find online resources to read or plan to share later, they don’t have the time to
provide metadata that could be relevant to their colleagues.
A middle­layer service which can provide features to store users’ searched URLs, resources, and
metadata for later use; which can solve the challenges a user is faced in the FullBrain platform.
This means, integrating a social bookmarking service or developing a plug­in which act as a mid­
dle layer between FullBrain social learning platform and Social bookmarking service could be a
potential approach to satisfy users’ requirements.
1.2
Scope of the thesis
In this thesis, a plug­in will be developed to be patched with the FullBrain social learning platform
in order to facilitate the process of bookmarking and sharing online resources in the FullBrain
platform. Moreover, I will examine various social bookmarking services and investigate their
potential to interact as a middle layer between a Social learning platform like (e.g. FullBrain) and
a social bookmarking service. The focus of this article will be on features, integration facilities
of social bookmarking services, and approaches that help students to tackle storing and sharing
resources. This paper will analyze the type of services that these social bookmarking applications
provide and their targeted users which help define a robust solution to the raised challenges. This

Research questions
This study attempts to answer the following research questions (RQs):
• RQ1: How might we integrate an existing social bookmarking platform in a social learning

• RQ2: How might we design and test a social bookmarking platform for improving learning
experience?
I aim to answer the research questions by exploring the concept of social bookmarking services and
providing a prototype to demonstrate the approaches for bookmarking and sharing online content
through a social learning platform.
1.4
Delimitation
This study will mainly be intended based on the trending social bookmarking platforms. This
means, among many active social bookmarking services, I picked a limited number of Bookmark­
ing services for further investigation. This study will be based on a qualitative approach since
the target group is restricted to students from universities who use the FullBrain social learning
platform to perform course­based tasks within their network and share their knowledge with each
other. Due to time limitations as a final result, a plug­in will be developed for the Fullbrain social
learning platform to streamline the process of storing and sharing resources among students.

Structure of the thesis

research question in section 1.3 and analyze, identify the problem that is mentioned in section 1.1.
In Chapter 4 I have included the result of this report and shows the approaches to the research
questions by analyzing the outcomes from the social bookmarking investigation. Moreover, I will
discuss the scope and future work.

2.1
Social bookmarking
In order to better understand the issue of integrating social bookmarking services into a social
learning platform, this section will focus on previous researches that have been done on social
bookmarking services. Upon investigating the definition of social/user bookmarking (or Tagging,
Collaborative tagging, Social tagging/bookmarking), Ivanjoko defined that ” it is a process of
organizing electronic content, which is carried out by several users in a way that these contents
are indexed/categorized ad hoc to assigned keywords.[10]. The topic, ”Bookmarking” has been
known for a long time and multiple features have been added to empower this technology. One
of the schemes is to use collaboration in order to solve the ”resource discovery”, ”recall” and
”maintenance” problem[11]. There is also a number of systems that have been developed for use
with collaboration, such as CoWing ”Collaborative web indexing”[12], a distributed collaborative
bookmark system that uses additional software (software agent and proxies) to help users manage
their online content.
The social network hierarchy in Figure 2.1 help us to have a better understanding of social services
and provide an illustration on what are their specific indicators. This figure indicates that services
like Facebook and Twitter are social media websites that provide virtual interaction between the
users to post, share, comment and tag their thoughts and daily activities. The social bookmarking
services are derived from social media services and aim at bookmarking users’ favorite content
and highlighting those content. These services have also an important role in spreading the content
which increasing the number of spreads will effect highlighting that specific content among users.
Social News sites which counted under social media networks are deviated into two popular News
websites services and popular websites to submit design­related articles. These services focus on
broadcasting the daily news and releasing the feeds to promote and make it popular. As the sub­
categories for social media include a massive amount of applications and services; for simplicity

Social bookmarking may also be a tool that will help students in their future academic preferences.
A study in 2018[14] has been established among the school students. In the study[14], a social
bookmarking service has been implemented that allowed users to store links to web resources and
these links can be shared among other groups. They introduced two layers of bookmarking ser­
vices. On the first layer, students were asked to define bookmarks, comments, and classify their
bookmarks in a hierarchy of folders. On the second layer, an observer application is used to access
these hierarchies of contents provided by students. Then the bookmarked contents are combined
with information of the relationship between each student and their bookmarks. These data are
further processed to provide recommendations for each particular student.
On the basis of usage of social media among the students, a research has been established in 2019
which explores the relationship between social media and student academic performance[15]. The
research indicates that social media counts as a source of information and communication among
students[16] and this technology become an integral part of individuals every day’s lives[17].
Nowadays, many educational institutions relying on e­learning data usage[18] and using tech­

In order to define the problems based on what has been identified in the first stage, I used the point
of view (POV) Madlib method which has been explained by d.School[39] to shape the user’s
problems into meaningful statements.
This method was formed into practical problem expressions that led us to ideate solutions in the
right direction, namely, in a goal­oriented manner. The POV method provides a brief sight into
user need that can be used throughout the design thinking process. In order to create a problem
identification statement, POV consist of three key elements. The first element is the User which in
our case is the targeted users who interact with the platform. The second component is the user’s
need which has been identified throughout the first stage. The last component is Surprising
insights which are consist of uncovered insights that lead us to find innovative solutions. These
three elements which are used in POV Madlib combined in a structured was as ”[USER] needs
to [USER’S NEED] because [INSIGHT]”. The problem statement is classified based on the user
interaction steps in the POV statements which helped me to follow the processes based on each
path that a targeted user has the interaction to indicate their needs and expected insight.

FullBrain is an organization which focus on the integration of educational technologies that facili­
tate virtual learning for students. This organization provides a platform which enables students to
come together in course spaces to ask questions and share resources with each other. The users in
this platform interact by sharing sources and academic content. When users find online resources
and consider sharing among their FullBrain virtual network, need to post that resource by captur­
ing all metadata from that specific content and pasting it in the FullBrain platform. Through this
procedure, they need to switch multiple times between resource content and the FullBrain plat­
form which is complex and time­consuming. In addition, sometimes they want to share content
at another time. To establish this task they need to use a third­party bookmarking service which
enables them to bookmark and save the content in that specific bookmarking service. This pro­
cedure navigates back to simultaneous switching between a third­party application and FullBrain
platform to fill required metadata for sharing a resource. Through the meeting with FullBrain
board members (subsection 4.1.1) and observing the user interactions (subsection 4.1.2), I found
out that bookmarking online content and sharing a resource are critical challenges which a user
faces while using the FullBrain platform.
5.2
Why is this study important?
Online learning, has influenced the way we communicate and create a mindset which let people
work and learn in a virtual social environment. in this study, I have investigated social learning
platform and social bookmarking services. Combining these two technologies will provide an
efficient approach which let the users organize resources and make sure those resources that are
important to you, are always at your fingertips for sharing among your network at a later time.
Therefore, the manuscripts in this study interpret on development of a plug­in engine which pro­
vide an opportunity to extend the academic resource sharing capabilities and form a collected li­
brary of high­quality academic assets. This research helps us to identify social learning challenges,

frame those challenges in existing literature, and point out the deficiencies. In addition, The ex­
plained technologies offer insights into knowledge sharing solutions through a social learning plat­
form for interactions and discussions among students. These tools enable users to collaboratively
bookmark, create notes, and share online academic content in a social learning environment, in ad­
dition to providing a mechanism to get feedback on the shared content and empower their specific
knowledge capabilities.
5.3
Proposed solution
In this research, I started by asking How Might We questions based on the problem statements
which has been identified using the Point of view method. Afterward, I performed brainstorming
for possible solutions. Through using the cheatstorm method, I elaborated on those solutions which
were able to cover either one or more of the proposed how might we questions. From these ideas,
I selected the three most reasoned concepts and I have elaborated them to determine one possible
solution for further development.
Throughout the design thinking process, I gathered detailed information about challenges and re­
quired features which facilitate the process of resource sharing in the FullBrain platform. The
initial finding navigated me to focus on existing social bookmarking services which have the po­
tential to be integrated with FullBrain in order to allow users to bookmark online content and
share resources for a later time. By performing further investigation I find out that integrating
a third­party bookmarking service with FullBrain social learning platform will cost FullBrain to
have a premium subscription to access the features provided with that specific bookmarking ser­
vice. Some other bookmarking services which provided free services have included advertising
their product on the FullBrain platform or their targeted users that affected FullBrain’s further pro­
gression. Moreover, FullBrain needed to rely on an external third­party service to satisfy its user
needs. Integrating a third­party application would also have a negative impact in case of downtime
or changing the characteristics of bookmarking features.
The developed bookmarking plug­in provides a facility for FullBrain to rely on its own resources
and allow them to patch additional features based on its users’ requirements. This plug­in allows
the users to bookmark their interesting online content easily and access their bookmarked content
within the FullBrain interface. Bookmarking the content will provide the users to create a library
list of their favorite resources and share them whenever they want. Finally, this plug­in would
facilitate the process of sharing a source in the course spaces by auto­filling of the required text
boxes which decreases the amount of time consumed during resource sharing.

I modified the activities which will be performed by users while bookmarking and sharing online
content. In addition, a wireframe is illustrated which helped to test the clickable prototype by
FullBrain users. Based on the tested prototype, I started coding and created an implementation of
the actual plug­in for the FullBrain social learning platform.

5.5
Limitation
Based on the content of this study, I have faced some limitations. This thesis was written under
the influence of the covid­19 pandemic in Denmark. This led to performing virtual meetings
with FullBrain staff and FullBrain platform users. Also, due to the Covid­19 pandemic rules and
regulations for maintaining distance, I could not find a significant number of users to test the
prototype and observe their physical interaction when using the prototype. The FullBrain platform
is launched recently. Therefore, during this project, I have faced some new changes on the platform
which pushed me to think of various solutions which affected it more time­consuming to define
the exact possible approach. To sum up, the developed plug­in is not tested by Fullbrain users due
to time limitations for the project, but I have tested the prototype and verified the solution.

shared content. This means if a user likes content or posts a comment on shared content
will be notified by a message which includes a brief description content of that resource.
