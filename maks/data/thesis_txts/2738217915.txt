Abstract
The battery energy storage system (BESS) has been gradually adopted in modern in-
dustrial use especially with an increasing implementation in power networks. BESS features
various advantages not only for its lifespan, high energy density, but also significance in
balancing the supply and demand with sufficient stability. For the integration of renewable
energy, it plays an important role in storage of excess electricity and release in peak demand,
which greatly reduces the reliance on the real time supply of traditional power plant. Among
all the renewable generation, electric vehicle (EV) is one of the most beneficiary from BESS.
One critical technology in battery for EV, parallel battery system, which increases the ca-
pacity with a constant voltage level, has provided higher energy storage and boost travel
distance. However, for parallel battery pack, a good and robust supplying source with high
converting ratio is needed, thereby bringing out the challenge for high efficiency DC-DC
converter design.
The objective of this thesis is to design a two-stage Buck-LLC converter which converts
768 V to 25 V with a rating power of 1000 W. The whole design involves the analysis of
circuit topology, selection of devices, magnetic design and also PCB prototyping.
Chapter 2 and 3 give detailed analysis of circuit topology applying GaN device, which
features fast speed and low loss. This process is presented focusing on the design of soft-
switching achievement, minimizing the switching loss and conducting loss, pushing to a
higher level of efficiency.
Magnetic design is also a determining factor in loss minimization.
Chapter 4 illus-
trates the design process of magnetic components with planar magnetic technology. For
transformer design, one traditional single core and three multiple core proposals are com-
pared. The trade-off between multiple core design and winding layout is investigated. In this
chapter, a multiple core design based on half-turn structure with high capability of limiting
proximity effect has been selected and verified through simulation.
Finally, a two-stage Buck-LLC converter prototype implemented with proposed planar
transformer (PT) has been constructed. Through experiment, a peak efficiency of 95.6% has
been achieved.
Keywords: Battery energy storage system (BESS); LLC converter; soft-switching;
interleaving; planar transformer (PT); magnetomotive force (MMF); winding loss; matrix
transformer; two-stage architecture

1
Introduction
1.1
Background
Newly emerged battery energy storage system (BESS) technology is increasingly imple-
mented in a wide range of fields. Their critical advantages are brought to modern power
system, electric vehicles and distributed energy systems. BESS features ultrafast charging
and storage capability, which at the same time remains high robustness of balancing energy.
In renewable energy generation, BESS are especially crucial for wind and solar system, whose
supplying demand is unpredictable and to a great extent influenced by environment. It is
both an essential and challenging point to balance storage and supply, and nowadays BESS
is developing in a very fast pace in maintaining the stability of modern power systems.
Among storage systems configurations, parallel battery pack plays an significant role
in BESS. Parallel battery pack realizes increasing the capacity of storage system while re-
maining the same voltage level. Besides, it improved the current distribution and boost
the reliability. For most of applications, parallel battery pack can still perform high fault
tolerance if encountering partial failure. However, there are also many challenges for this
technology. Except from monitoring and balancing the state of charge for each single unit,
a stable and robust power supply and power converting system is needed.
To solve the existing problem and to provided good conditions for the application of
storage system, power converter is a critical point in steady power supply and distribution.
Converter design is essential for power compatibility and efficiency. Today’s power electronics
technology is mature and an indispensable part of storage system. For high conversion ratio
converter, stage-based design and isolation are widely used. Therefore, circuit topology and
magnetic components both decide the efficiency and power density. Loss breakdown analysis
and optimization with magnetic components are vital for efficiency of converter.
Figure 1: BESS power architecture
Since 1960s, investigation on planar magnetic components has brought its popularity
to modern converter design as printed circuit board (PCB) is widely used. Planar magnetic
technology has many advantages including higher power density, better thermal characteris-
tic as well as its convenience for manufacturability for lower cost [9]. With the possibility of
naturally integration with PCB, planar magnetic technology provided much inspiration for
industrial design.
With the widespread use of planar magnetic technology, transformer and inductor de-
sign is no more limited to traditional single core structure. Newly emerged concept such as

matrix transformer which is defined as an array of element cores has been proved useful in
high power and high current application. It can split the current between secondary wind-
ings of element cores thus reduces the power loss [10]. Also, matrix transformer structure
is capable to reduce leakage inductance and magnetomotive force (MMF). However, matrix
transformer requires more magnetic cores than traditional design which leads to hard process
of balancing power density or integration. This project investigates the feasibility of imple-
mentation multiple cores for this converter design specification, and select the optimized one
for prototyping.
This project involves designing a converter including circuit and magnetic component
with planar magnetic technology.
Comparison between multiple core proposals and tra-
ditional single core is made, and optimal case will be applied with current state-of-the-art
technology of power electronics to the converter which offers technical support and optimized
operation for storage system.

2
Topology Selection
According to the project requirement, this DC-DC converter is aimed to transfer a rel-
atively large amount of power of 1 kW. The voltage from input is through high conversion
ratio which leads to high current in the output side.
For an one-stage topology, such large conversion ratio will not be suitable for non-
isolated circuit due to security consideration. Besides, for one-stage topology, at one hand,
the large conversion ratio makes duty cycle difficult to regulate. On the other hand, it in-
creases the complexity of magnetic design, especially turns ratio.
Therefore in this project, a combined converter with isolated and non-isolated two-stage
converter is proposed. Buck converter is widely used with high efficiency, simplicity and ex-
cellent load regulation. For a two-stage topology, buck converter is not only suitable for it
regulation but also its simple structure for high power density. The first stage is selected to
be non-isolated buck converter (Figure 2) which is mainly responsible for signal regulation of
input voltage, being able to supply a stable output for next stage of conversion. To maintain
the balance of operating time between high voltage class switch and the diode, the output
voltage for first stage buck converter is set to be 400 V.
Figure 2: Buck converter schematic
The second stage converter is expected to be of high efficiency to conduct high current
for reducing switching loss and conduction loss. Meanwhile, the transformer in this converter
should convert the 400 V voltage to 25 V, with a turns ratio of 16:1.

4
Magnetic Component Design
Besides power switches, the loss from magnetic components, such as inductor and trans-
former, are also significant to analyse when designing. Due to the existence of magnetic core,
the total loss for both inductor and transformer can be divided to core loss and winding loss.
Core loss includes hysteresis loss and eddy current loss. Hysteresis loss is mainly caused
by repeated magnetization and demagnetization of magnetic material and eddy current loss
is mainly caused by the induction of current within a conductor when exposed to a changing
magnetic field.
For winding loss, skin effect and proximity effect are inevitable especially when oper-
ating in a high switching frequency. Under this case, the current tends to concentrate near
the surface of conductor instead uniformly distributed on the cross-section which causes the
effective area of conductor to reduces. As a result, the resistive loss will be higher with a
dependency of frequency. Proximity effect happens in adjacent conductors that conducting
alternating current. The magnetic field generated by each conductor will interact with each
other [14]. The current will concentrate on the boundary which is closer to the adjacent one
and thus lead to uneven distribution of current density and loss increase.
Due to the loss illustrated above, this chapter gives the design process and parameter
calculation of the inductor and transformer.

4.2
Transformer Design
For LLC converter, a high efficiency transformer is essential due to the high current at
output side under large converting ratio. For transformer, the structure of winding signifi-
cantly affect the magnetomotive force (MMF) and leakage inductance, hence closely influ-
encing the winding loss. In this section, a structure of half-turn design with multiple planar
cores is proposed and corresponding simulation model is built for verification.
Many structure of transformer winding has achieved its maturity of development such as
‘Sandwich’ structure and interleave structure (Figure 36). Interleave structure is commonly
used as industrial product in many fields including power converter and high frequency trans-
former design. Interleave structure can greatly minimize the area that the magnetic field
can penetrate through and hence reduce the MMF between windings.

Different from traditional core structure, planar transformer integrated the winding into
PCB manufacturing which greatly saves the power density and also advances the multiple
core application. For high current cases, paralleling power switches is a common practice

conduction loss.
However, by simply paralleling and increasing the number of switches,
unexpected termination loss and unbalanced current distribution might happen. Therefore,

of element cores which is beneficial to split the large current and reduces the leakage by
splitting turns between each element core. In this section, three multiple-core transformer
structure are proposed and one traditional interleave structure is simulated and compared.

for LLC, the optimized structure will be selected.

6.3
Experiment Result
Under a loading of 0.8 Ω, this prototype can work up to 340V. The main constraint of
limitation of higher power is due to lack of EMI testing and noise interference. Instead of
temperature limit, when the voltage reaches to 350 V, some high frequency oscillation occurs
and both current and voltage have been oscillating and distorted with buzzing sound in PCB.
Though analysis, it could be some oscillation between stray capacitance and inductance as
voltage dependent parameter. From the perspective of security and preventing PCB from
being damaged, the working state power has been controlled in a relatively low level.
Besides, as the voltage increases above 250 V, there were some unexpected noise and

Conclusion & Future Work
7.1
Conclusion
This project investigates the benefit of implementing multiple cores design with half-
turn structure under high voltage and high converting ratio. To improve the loss practice,
winding loss model and circuit model were built and detailed design methodology is devel-
oped. Through the comparison of three winding structure, the most suitable one is applied
to Buck-LLC two stage converter. The proposed winding layout is superior in winding loss
than traditional single core design which can lead to a reduction of 26%, performing a better
limit for proximity effect.
In high output current application, multiple cores design can be a good trade-off between
core loss and winding loss with a suitable winding layout structure. Together with the soft-
switching design from circuit topology and GaN device, the proposed multiple core design
demonstrated its impact in loss reduction and efficiency. Finally, a 200 kHz (Buck) and
500 kHz (LLC) 600 V/25 V 1000 W two-stage converter is demonstrated. The prototype
achieves a peak efficiency of 95.6%.
7.2
Future Work
Although the experiment waveform shows that most of the soft-switching has been
achieved, the working power is temporarily not able to go higher with the increase of voltage
and the efficiency hasn’t meet the expectation as analysis. Based on this situation, some
future work can be carried on after this project which can be concluded as:
• Modification on current PCB
• Integration of multiple core design
• Investigation on optimal switching frequency
• Investigation on proposal 1
• Investigation on implementation of Sigma converter
First, it is of significance to make modification on the current PCB design. Due to
the grounding issue, the noise from power ground is not well isolated with signal output.
That’s also the main reason that the operating voltage is relatively low because of some
unpredictable high frequency resonant happened during testing.
By now, in order to save the cost of customization, matrix transformer design in this
project hasn’t been integrated to united core. Some future work can be carried on the in-
vestigation of that which can be greatly beneficial on reducing the core volume and improve
power density. Also, the proposal 1 has shown huge potential in limiting the proximity ef-
fect and obtaining a low leakage, hence it is worth taking some further research on how the
