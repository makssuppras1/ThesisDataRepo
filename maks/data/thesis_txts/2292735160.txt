RESUMÉ 
___________________________________________________________________ 
 
Dette speciale omhandler optimering af vagtplanlægning for børnelæger på Hvidovre 
Hospital. Den nuværende planlægningsproces er manuel og tidskrævende. Personalets 
ønsker for fridage, feriedage og kursusdage inkluderes i planlægningen. Udfordringer er at 
få tilpasset den eksisterende basisplan til personalets ønsker samtidig med at afdelingens 
behov opfyldes.  
 
Først gennemgås general vagtplanlægning. Derefter beskrivelse forholdene for 
børnelægernes vagtplanlægning. En matematisk model formuleres for situationen på 
baggrund af en general matematisk model. Modellen implementeres for januar 2015 og 
løses som et mixed-integer-programming (MIP) problem. På baggrund af den nuværende 
vagtplanlæggers kommentarer udvides modellen til at inkludere flere præferencer. Den 
udvidet model implementeres og testes.  
 
På baggrund af traditionelle planlægningsproblemer formuleres en matematisk model for 
udvidet ferieplanlægning for sommeren 2015. Denne model implementeres og løses som et 
MIP problem. På baggrund af den oprindelige løsning simplificeres modellen så den kun 
følger de regler som vagtplanlæggeren benytter. Den simplificeret model implementeres og 
testes.

SUMMARY 
_____________________________________________________________________ 
 
This thesis regards optimizing the work schedule planning for pediatricians at Hvidovre 
Hospital. The current planning process is manual and time-consuming. The personnel’s 
requests for days off, vacation and course days are included in the planning. The challenge 
is to adapt the existing basis schedule to personnel’s request while still satisfying the 
department’s demands.  
 
First an introduction to general work scheduling is given. The work scheduling conditions 
for the pediatricians are then described. A mathematical model is formulated for the 
situation based on a general mathematical model. The model is implemented for January 
2015 and solved as a Mixed Integer Programming (MIP) problem. Based on the current 
work schedule planner’s comments the model is extended to include more preferences. 
The extended model is implemented and tested.  
 
A mathematical model for extended vacation planning is formulated based on traditional 
planning problems. The model is implemented and solved as a MIP problem. The model is 
simplified to only include the rules applied by the work schedule planner. The simplified 
model is implemented and tested.

CHAPTER 1 
 
1 INTRODUCTION 
______________________________________________________________________ 
 
Work scheduling often applies to the basic situation but real life does not follow a basis 
plan.  
 
The challenge for the work schedule planner for pediatricians at Hvidovre Hospital is to 
transform the existing basis schedule into a usable work schedule. The problem occurs 
when the doctors request time off for things such as courses or vacation. In these cases the 
basis work schedule has to be reorganized in order to ensure that the ward is still fully 
manned. Special needs and rules also exist for some of the personnel. Currently the work 
schedule planner creates the schedules manually in a spreadsheet document. She first fills in 
the personnel’s request and then juggles with the remaining shifts which must be assigned 
in order to meet demand. Keeping both management and employees happy is often 
impossible. So even though a basis schedule exist the work scheduling is a complex 
planning problem and a time-consuming process. 
 
There are three different categories of pediatricians who each have different shift functions. 
The most experienced and field specialized doctors have the backshift (da: bagvagt), the 
experienced doctors have the middleshift (da: mellemvagt) and the least experienced doctors, 
who often are under education have the frontshift (da: forvagt). The backshift pediatricians 
differ most from the other categories since they only have to be present during the day 
time. The work schedule for the backshift pediatricians will not be considered in this 
report. References to the personnel will therefore not include the backshift employees. The 
middleshift and the frontshift personnel ensure that the pediatrician ward is manned 24 
hours a day.  
 
This thesis differs from found literature in the respect that it regards doctors. Research 
regarding work scheduling for nurses and residents will be used as references as the work 
scheduling conditions for nurses and residents are comparable with the situation for the 
pediatricians. The planning situations all regard complex planning problems for scheduling 
hospital wards full 24 hour demand. Different shift types exist, and often many restrictions 
apply on demand.  
 
Another main difference for this thesis is the inclusion of personnel’s requests as soft 
constraints. The only examples found on adding requests are done by S. Topaloglu [5] and 
H. Beaulieu et al. [3]. In contrast to this thesis, both cases implements requests as hard 
==PAGE:14==
____________________________________________________________________6   
 
constraints which force the models to create a schedule that fulfill the request. The soft 
constraint approach used in this thesis makes it possible to deny requests in order to better 
fulfill demand.  
 
The goal of this thesis is to solve the problem of work scheduling with request using 
mathematical modeling. Before making a mathematical model an introduction to general 
work scheduling will made and the specific rules for the pediatricians will be explained. 
After describing the mathematical model, the model will be tested and the results will be 
discussed. In addition, a model for extended vacation planning will also be described, 
implemented and tested.

CHAPTER 2 
 
2 WORK SCHEDULING 
___________________________________________________________________ 
 
Work scheduling is a traditional planning problem. Work schedules are often very case 
specific and must be specially created and adjusted. There are therefore many different 
ways to approach a work scheduling problem. The approach depends on which qualities 
are prioritized for the work schedule.  
 
 
2.1 THE QUALITY OF A WORK SCHEDULE 
 
In many planning problems the objective is often to minimize cost. An optimal solution 
within work scheduling is often the plan that fulfills demand with minimum cost. As an 
example Butches et al. [7] describes how Air New Zealand saves NZ$15,655,000 per year 
after the crew schedules have been optimized. The big savings can be explained by the high 
complexity of the problem caused by the geographical dimension. 
 
The quality of a work schedule often depends on more than just the economic aspect. The 
quality can also depend on equality and fairness. Equal distributions of shifts and working 
hours lead to a perception of fairness and justice among the employees. The distribution 
can also affect the fairness of a work schedule. In some workplaces the performance of the 
employees can have crucial consequences. A.M. Trinkoff et al. [13] describe a study based 
on the connection between nurses work schedules and patient mortality. The study is based 
on 633 nurses on 71 hospitals in North Carolina and Illinois. The study showed that the 
work schedule was related significantly to patient mortality. The work schedule 
characteristics with most influence was especially long work hours, working while sick and 
weekly burdens.  
 
In Denmark it is normal for all fulltime employees to have five weeks of paid vacation. 
Some companies have periods where they close down. The vacation of the employees is 
thereby held in these periods. Not all companies are able to, or choose to close down. In 
these cases the employees must thereby be given time off for vacation while there is still a 
demand to fulfill. The employees’ knowledge and influence regarding future work 
schedules depends on the work schedule structure and the scheduling process.  Including 
the personnel’s request can increase the level of satisfaction among the employees. M. W.

____________________________________________________________________8   
 
Strevell [10] implies this in his article with the following statement; “If you give people a 
hand in their own destiny they’re happier”.  
 
 
2.2 GENERAL WORK SCHEDULING 
 
The purpose of a work schedule is to have a plan that fulfills demand. Demand within 
work scheduling often refers to the number of employees needed for specific tasks or 
shifts. Work schedules can be divided into two main categories; cyclic and non-cyclic 
schedules.  
 
2.2.1 CYCLIC SCHEDULES 
A cyclic work schedule is, as the name indicates, a schedule which can be reused over and 
over again. Cyclic work schedule often run over a long term period where the cycle is 
repeated. The long term horizon gives the employees great knowledge regarding their 
future working hours.  
Planning over a long term horizon often increases the complexity of the planning problem. 
The advantage with the cyclic schedule is that the solution to the complex planning 
problem is sustainable.  
In some cases individual work schedules can be rotated between the staff. Rotating the 
schedules among all the employees creates a high level of fairness since all employees will 
have been assigned exactly the same amount of shift types or task in the end of the cycle.  
Cyclic scheduling is for example used by J. D. Megeath [6]. who creates a work schedule 
for nurses at the pediatrics ward at Colorado General Hospital.  
 
2.2.2 NON-CYCLIC SCHEDULES 
A non-cyclic work schedule cannot be reused and individual schedules cannot be rotated 
between the staff. Non-cyclic work schedules are unique for each period. This makes it 
possible to include variation of demand, personnel size and personnel types. Individual 
needs and requests can also be included such as vacation, days off or courses. 
 
The planning horizon is often a short term period. Including variations and requests tends 
to increase the complexity. Only covering short-term periods can make the problem easier 
to solve, and this come naturally since future variations or requests are not know for a long 
term horizon. The work schedule problem will need to be solved for each work period. 
 
The individual footprint on the work schedule makes it more difficult to assign the same 
amount of shift types or tasks to all personnel. It can therefore be difficult to achieve the 
same level of fairness as in the cyclic work schedules.

2.4 SOLUTION METHODS 
 
There are different ways to solve work scheduling problems. It is in all cases essential to 
know if a problem is solvable. In some cases as the one described by J. D. Megeath [6] the 
feasibility can easily by found before starting solving. In this example the total demand for 
evening shifts is 147. When the possible working days was multiplied with the employees 
who can be assigned evening shifts a result of only 145 was found. A feasible solution does 
thereby not exist since supply is less than demand. There are often many restrictions in 
work scheduling problem which can make it complex to determine the problem’s feasibility 
before starting to solve the problem. 
 
2.4.1 MANUALLY 
Many work scheduling problems are solved manually. Depending on the situation either a 
standard work schedule is available which must be adjusted, or the work schedule must be 
planned from scratch. Besides fulfilling demand some work schedules also includes 
preferences and requests from the employees which increases the complexity of the model. 
Solving work scheduling problems manually can thereby be a complicated task. 
 
The work schedule problem described by J. D. Megeath [6] was originally solved manually. 
A spreadsheet document was used to keep track of the different rules and restrictions. The 
work schedule planner violated some of the rules and restrictions in order to fulfill 
demand. After the work scheduling problem was reviewed it was concluded, as described 
earlier, that a feasible solution did not exist. The personnel size was then adjusted and an 
improved algorithm for manually solving the problem was implemented.  
 
2.4.2 COMPUTERIZED 
As shown in section 2.3 work scheduling problems can be formulated mathematically. The 
problem can thereby be solved by inserting the mathematical formulation in a 
mathematical solver. The general model described in section 2.3 can be solved as a general 
Mixed Integer Programming (MIP) problem. H. Beaulieu et al. [3] and J. Jlassi [4] also solve 
work scheduling problems by using this method. 
Work scheduling problems often consist of many rules and restriction which leads to a 
long list of constraints. Size matters when these problems must be solved. Some models 
are so extensive that it will take a very long time to solve them, and you cannot always 
afford to use the time or the memory to solve the complete model. A way to handle this is 
to apply the lazy constraints method. The idea of this method is to only add the constraints 
that will be violated. This means that some of the constraints from the complete model will 
be removed and categorized as lazy. If some of the removed constraints are violated the 
==PAGE:23==
____________________________________________________________________15   
 
constraints will be added back to the model. P. Rubin [14] describes lazy constraints in his 
post. He first explains that the candidates for lazy constraints are the ones you already 
know are redundant or very unlikely to be violated. Using lazy constraints will simplified 
the model and can thereby make it easier to solve. He states that the method can improve 
the computation time and possibly the memory footprint.  
 
The work scheduling problem can also be solved using other methods. H. T. Poulsen [1] 
and C. A. White et al. [12] solves work scheduling problems by using tabu search. Tabu 
search is a meta-heuristic method. The heuristic tries to help a local search, typically 
steepest decent, out of the local minima by allowing non-improving solutions. The idea is 
that the use of non-improving solutions will force the search over to other solution areas. 
Allowing non-improving solutions can make the search jump between the same solutions. 
To avoid this, certain solutions will be made illegal. A list, called a tabu list, is created in 
order to keep track of the illegal solutions.

When creating a work schedule it is important to understand the structure and needs for 
the workplace. In this section the rules and needs for the pediatrician ward at Hvidovre 
Hospital will be described. The information has been given by the current work schedule 
planner, Marie Cecilie Lawaetz. 
 
The pediatrics ward must be staffed at all times and different shift types are created in 
order to cover the needs. It will be specified if the rules or needs concern the middleshift, 
frontshift or all personnel.

employees. In this transition period the middleshift employees starts being assigned 
introduction shifts Bintro and regular shift types, bA and DE, for backshift personnel. 
 
If necessary, it is possible to combine two of the basic shifts, kD and LA, into an extended 
shift from 08.00-23.00. This is generally avoided and this shift type has so far only been 
assigned to the work schedule planner herself and for personnel on partly sick leave. This 
double shift can only be used on Fridays.

3.2 DEMAND

3.3 STAFF RULES 
 
The rules in this section concerns the employees. These rules concerns working hours and 
resting time. The rules are created to ensure a good working environment. The year is 
divided into periods of 18 weeks, called the norm period, and some of the rules apply to 
the average in a norm period.  
 
3.3.1 WORKING HOURS 
It is normal in Denmark to work 37 hours a week. Due to the variation of shifts the 
pediatricians are only restricted to work 37 hours per week in average, over the norm 
period. This means that in the end of the norm period each employee must have worked 
666 hours, since          . The employees are not allowed to work less and overtime is 
avoided. If some employees have overtime the employees will be allowed to either take 
time off to counterbalance overtime or get the overtime paid out. 
 
3.3.2 SHIFT RESTRICTIONS 
The night and the weekend shifts are often the most unattractive shifts. There are set upper 
limits on the amount of these shifts each employee can have. An employee is maximally 
aloud to have a night shift every sixth day, in average in the norm period. Employees on 
partly sick leave are not allowed to have any night shifts at all.  
==PAGE:28==
____________________________________________________________________20   
 
An employee must also in average maximally work every other weekend. The weekend is 
determined to start Saturday morning at 08.00 and end Monday morning at 08.00. In terms 
of shift types this means that the weekend starts from the night shift on Friday and ends 
with the night shift on Sunday. All shifts on Saturday and Sunday are considered weekend 
shifts. If an employee works both Saturday and the following Sunday this is only 
considered as having worked in one weekend. Therefore in order to respect the rule the 
weekend shifts are often placed together such that as few employees as possible work in 
the same weekend.  
It is not allowed to have more than 12 working days in a row. This rule is a general rules 
which must always apply. This rule does not, unlike the other rules, only apply to the 
average work week with the norm period.

requirements which makes it unlikely for the model to assign more employees to the shifts 
than the actual demand. The preferences also help to ensure the actual demand. The 
solutions will be checked in order to see if more employees are assigned the shift than the

5.5 USING LAZY CONSTRAINTS 
 
Not all of the constraints described in the mathematical model are implemented. The 
method regarding lazy constraints will be applied to constraint (4). This constraint ensures 
that each employee will not work more than 12 days in a row. The constraint is not 
implemented because it is not expected to be violated. Implementing constraint (4) will also 
require adding many extra subsets and extra constraints since the original constraint has to 
be divided into a constraint for each subset of days. Since there are 28 days in the work 
schedule this would result in adding          different subsets. A constraint for each 
sequence of 13 following works days would also need to added, meaning 15 constraints.  
In order to be assigned 12 following working days without violating the other constraints in 
the model the following must apply on a 13 day period 
 
 The employee must not be assigned any night shifts, since this trigger the next day 
off 
 
 No Sunday shifts in the first 5 days since this trigger a day off in the following week 
 
 No fulfilled requests for getting the entire day off or vacation in this period 
 
Constraint (4) is not expected to be violated based on the fact that both night shifts and 
weekend shift are mainly equally distributed among the employees, and that the amount of 
request for getting days off and vacation is 103.

7.5 DISCUSSION 
 
The results show that it is possible to achieve feasible work schedules. The solutions found 
differentiate from the original solution created by the work schedule planner. The 
difference can be explained by the difference approaches. The complexity of the planning 
problem increases the difficulties in solving it manually. Using a higher amount of D shifts 
comes natural for the program since it helps minimizing the variation from the norm time 
which was a main difference.  There are no strict rules regarding the demand for shift type 
D which can explain why assigning this shift type is not prioritized in the original solution. 
The resulting work schedule depends on the assignments of weight. The work schedule 
planner does not assign weights in the same way but prioritization is still applied. The 
content of the final work schedules depends on evaluator’s prioritization. 
It is prioritized that all request gets fulfilled, sine this is the priority in the original solution. 
The request constraints could therefore have been changed into hard constraints as done 
by H. Beaulieu et al. [3]. The problem has a higher risk of becoming unsolvable when 
requests are implemented as hard constraints, since the solution space increases 
significantly.  
Currently all requests are prioritized equally but individual weights could be assigned to the 
requests. Requests for courses and vacation could for example be prioritized higher than 
request for getting the day off.  
The mathematical model is only implemented for the month of January 2015, but can with 
simple adjustments it can easily be applied be other months. Depending on the situation 
different changes must be made. The basics regarding number of personnel, number of 
days, individual working hour requirements, references to the basis schedule must match. 
The personnel specific constraints might need to be updated and new request must be 
implemented.  
The work schedule planner must create a new plan each period. The work planner gets 
assigned one office shift to create the work schedule. The average running time for solving 
the MIP problem to create a work schedule for the general situation is 42,65 seconds. 
When using the extensions described in this chapter the average running time is 1683,92 
seconds. Despite the significantly higher running time for the extended situation a normal 
work schedule can still easily be made within the timeframe of the office shifts independent 
on the situation. Please note, that it is possible to decrease the running time if lower values 
for the optimality gap is accepted.  
The work planner has informed that she often spends more time than what she gets paid 
for in order to plan the work schedule. The time especially increases in the periods where 
the amount of requests peaks, such as December and the summer period.

8.4.4 DISCUSSION 
Depending on the chosen solution some employees will get requests fulfilled with higher 
priority than others. The model is created such that no employee’s requests are weighted 
higher than others. The result is thereby unbiased and without any use of favoritism. The 
==PAGE:89==
____________________________________________________________________81   
 
personnel who gets request fulfilled with the lowest priority are simply unlucky. In the 
current situation the decisions regarding who gets which requests fulfilled are decided by 
the work schedule planner. Employees getting less prioritized requests than others might 
blame the work schedule planner and suspect favoritism.  
An alternative method to vacation planning is described by M. W: Strevell et al. [10]. The 
problem regards vacation planning for flight crews but the method can easily be applied to 
other fields of profession. The crew gets assigned two weeks of vacation during the year. 
The flight crew submits three choices for vacation for each of the two leave periods, which 
is very similar to how the pediatricians submit their requests for summer vacation. Based 
on these request the work schedule planner makes the schedule. The article describes three 
problems with this method. The first problem is that there was no predetermined method 
for determining which employee gets the period if multiple employees request the same 
period. The employees can, as previous described, blame the work schedule planner if they 
do not get their highest priorities fulfilled. The second problem is that the employees do 
not know what the other employees request. The article states that in an ideal system the 
demand should be made apparent such that the employees can select lower demand 
periods. In the current vacation planning process the employees email their wishes to the 
work schedule planner. It is not known how much the employees discuss vacation requests 
with each other, but it is unlikely that all frontshift and middleshift personnel talk together 
on this matter. The work schedule planner is thereby the only one with the full overview. 
The third and last problem is that there is no motivation for requesting less desirable 
periods, even if the demand for requests was known.  
To solve all these problems a new process for assigning vacation is developed. A point 
system is established making it possible for the employees to weight their requests. Each 
employee gets assigned 20 point which can be used for bidding for the two periods they 
want. The points can be divided as they please. The vacation periods are then assigned to 
the highest bidder. In case of a tie the winner is determined by a random drawing.  
The planning process is divided into two phases. In phase one all employees chose the 
periods they want on a posted calendar but do not assign any weights. In the period of 
phase one the employees can move around on their requests in order to avoid conflicts. At 
the end of phase one the juggling process is closed down. The employees are shown how 
many they compete against before phase two starts. In phase two the employees submit 
their points via a closed bid.  
The conclusion starts with the following statement “People are more willing to accept an 
assignment method if they know that they have a hand in their own destiny”. In this 
bidding approach the employees can only blame themselves if they are not satisfied with 
the result. This takes the burden away from the work schedule planner. Using this open 
calendar system in phase one allows the employees to move around and thereby makes it 
possible to avoid more conflicts than in the previous system.  
 
==PAGE:90==
____________________________________________________________________82   
 
The pediatricians are entitled five weeks of vacation during the year. In the summer it is for 
many employees, as seen in Appendix 9, preferred to have several connected weeks of 
vacation in the summer. The employees also have the possibility to request for time off 
without using a vacation day. As seen for the January work schedule there was 83 requests 
for getting the entire day off.  
The amount of vacation weeks, the preferences for having some of them connected plus 
the possibility for regular days off makes the vacation planning process for the pediatricians 
more complex than the flight crew. Applying the bidding method for the pediatricians will 
therefore be demanding. This would also mean, if the same method is to be applied, that 
the vacation planning has to be done one year ahead. As described earlier the personnel 
force changes a lot during time. As shown in table8.2 for the period from week 25-36 there 
are 3 employees starting, 2 stopping and 3 gets back from leave of absence. This variation 
of personnel size makes it impossible to apply the binding method directly. Elements of 
the method can be implemented. For example instead of only having three priorities the 
requests could be weighted. A calendar including the request could also be published and 
movements could be allowed within a certain period.

9 CONCLUSION 
______________________________________________________________________ 
 
In this thesis the challenge of work scheduling is analyzed and described. The work is 
based on existing literature regarding work scheduling, which is often applied on nurses. A 
mathematical model for creating monthly work schedules for the pediatricians is 
constructed based on a generalized model. The objective of the model is to minimize 
variation from the basis schedule, variation of the norm for working hours and violation of 
preferences along with maximizing the amount of fulfilled request. The constraints ensure 
the fulfillment of restrictions and demand.  
 
The mathematical model is implemented for January 2015. The first chosen solution 
outperforms the original solution on variations from the norm working time, changes from 
the basis and violated preferences. The original solution has a more equal and fair 
distribution of shifts. Extensions for handling equality preferences are implemented. The 
chosen solution for the extended model has a more equal distribution of the different shift 
types than the original solution. As it was also the case for the earlier found solution, this 
solution outperforms the original solution on variations from the norm working time, 
changes from the basis and violated preferences. The results thereby show that a higher 
fulfillment rate of the requirements is achieved when using the implemented models.  
 
One of the current work schedule planner’s main frustrations was that the planning process 
often was very time-consuming. This aspect has also been optimized. The work schedule 
planner can, within the current timeframe for planning, compare different possible 
solutions by weighting the importance of the criteria differently when testing the model. In 
the current process the work scheduling stopped once a feasible solution was achieved. 
This new method can thereby increase the quality.  
 
Another advantage with using the implemented mathematical model is the great likelihood 
of minimization of human mistakes. Mistakes can occur in the implementation of the 
model and when data is updated. The method suggested in this thesis is thereby not 
bulletproof but mistakes are easier to detect since data and rules can be viewed separately. 
A greater acceptance of the work schedule among the employees can also be achieved, 
since the model does not favor any single individuals. Lacking a human touch is also a 
disadvantage. The work schedule planner knows from own experience how it feels to be 
given different shift combination and also knows the employees. She will avoid assigning 
three night shifts within a week to an employee since this is too stressful. The implemented 
==PAGE:92==
____________________________________________________________________84   
 
model simple follows the rules blindly. This disadvantages is minimal since the work 
schedule planner still can apply her own personal touch in some extend when testing 
possible solutions. In the end it is up to her to decide which solution is the most optimal.  
 
Based on the results multiple changes can be applied to the mathematical model. The 
implementation of request can be changes to hard constraints since only solutions with 
zero denied requests are used. The disadvantage of this method is that this can lead to an 
unsolvable problem. This is avoided in the current approach. Additionally the punishment 
connected to variations from the basis schedule can be deleted. The comparison with the 
basis schedule shows a high level of differences, especially in the original model. Similarities 
with the basis schedule are thereby not prioritized as much as first expected. Due to the 
many variations the basis schedule is only relevant for the planner not the employees.  
 
The mathematical modeling is extended to a model for extended vacation planning. The 
original solution shows that the stated rules are not followed very strictly. The model is 
therefore simplified and similar results to the original solution are achieved.  This again 
clearly shows that the work schedule planner prioritizes people’s requests.  
 
The two mathematical models can, besides being implemented to other time periods for 
the pediatricians, also easily be implemented to other departments. It is informed that not 
all departments include requests to the extent that this department does. In some 
departments getting time off is generally the problem of the individual employees. These 
departments thereby follow the basis schedule more precisely and the planning problems 
are thus not as complex but can still be solved by using mathematical modeling.  
 
Future work for optimizing the work schedule for pediatricians can be to apply re-
rostering. The method of re-rostering nurses is described by H. E. S. Lilleby et al. [9] and 
can be used to avoid conflicts. The idea is to use employees from other departments to 
help fulfill demand in highly loaded periods. In order to use this method scheduling has to 
be planned simultaneously for multiple departments. This method also assumes that 
employees can be moved between department in some extend. Besides optimizing further 
within work scheduling, other areas can also considered. As example, D. Pham et al [8] 
applies mathematical modeling to surgical case planning. The suggestions for future work 
are based on the improved results that can be achieved by applying mathematical modeling 
to complex planning problem, as clearly shown in this thesis.
