Abstract
Vaccination is a great contributor to public health.
Vaccine needs to be safe, effective, stable for storage and distribution and be able to
be manufactured consistently. This thesis investigates steps towards a platform for orally
administered sub-unit vaccines. The reason for exploring oral vaccines is that it will provide
faster vaccines for a bigger population, better patient compliance, no infection at injection
site, no need for healthcare personal, less waste from needles, syringes, and sterilization.
The building blocks for this platform are the CAF04 system, along with pectin conjugated
to peptides and TLRa coating the CAF04 liposome with known adjuvant activity.
A
protocol for the conjugation procedure is constructed followed by an analysis investigating
the feasibility of the conjugation. The data provides evidence of the conjugation to different
extents of conjugation of the pectin carboxylates, and of the polymer coating the liposome.
The polymer of pectin and TLRa are coated on the CAF04 system by microfluidics and
evaluated by DLS. This show that the polymers are coating the liposome. Giving a simi-
lar particles distribution of particle size within each parameter. Zeta potential decreasing
demonstrating that the polymers are coating the liposome and zeta potential can be con-
trolled by the extend of conjugation.
Cell experiments confirm that pectin conjugated with various extent of TLR7/8 agonist
elicits TLR7/8 activation. This show that pectin can be fictionalized to tailor properties
onto the CAF adjuvant system.

Introduction
Vaccines is one of the twentieth centuries most important invention to improve health and
reduce death of the world population. It protects people from infectious diseases and it
is estimated that it saves 2-3 mill. lives pr. year worldwide (Delany et al., 2014). While
vaccination is a well-established procedure - especially in the developed countries - there
is still an unmet need in this area.
It could be very beneficial to substitute a parenteral vaccine with an oral vaccine. Par-
enteral techniques accumulate a lot of waste, it demands a well-functioning infrastructure
(distribution, transport, cold chain demand, etc.), trained healthcare personnel and car-
ries the risk of infections at the injection site as well as cross infection between healthcare
personal Davoud Jazayeri et al. (2021). Between 1997 and 2000 in the US 17% of the
healthcare personals injuries was because of injection needles (Mann et al., 2009). From
2020 to 2021 an increase in reported incidents of needle and cut accidents was reported
in Region Hovedstaden - Denmark. 24% was reported from the Covid-19 vaccination sites
(Hovedstaden, 2021). The cost of trained healthcare personal can be up to 25% of the cost
of the vaccine (Vela Ramirez et al., 2017). All these obstacles can be overcome by orally
administration, which additionally has higher patient compliance as well.
Orally administration faces other challenges. It needs to pass through the Gastrointestinal
tract without getting degraded, and at the same time being recognized by the immune
system when presented to the Peyer’s patches and induce the desired immunity. Further-
more, it needs to induce inflammatory responses but not show toxicity. For this purpose
an adjuvant with a high safety profile is needed.
To induce immunity by vaccination an antigen from the pathogen needs to be presented
to the immune system. Presentation of antigen is done by antigen presenting cells, such as
dendritic cell, this presentation will activate the naïve lymphocytes to make antibodies and
store this knowledge in memory cells. When a person gets exposed to the specific pathogen
the immune system is already familiar with it, and can quickly mount an immune response
to limit infection.
This project investigates the possibility of designing orally administered vaccines based
on macro-molecular constructs consisting of pectinated cationic liposomes. This requires
knowledge of the immune system, the GI tract and its subcompartments, and the challenges
they present. This will be elucidated, along with an exposition of the vaccine design, in
relation to the expected challenges it is designed to overcome.
The hypothesize to be investigated is if it is possible to engineer a novel platform to deliver
a vaccine with a liposome displaying adjuvant properties.

Pectin will be provided by Staten serums institute (SSI). Pectin are currently used for
several application in the food industry, this means that it have already proven to be
stable and non-toxic for the human metabolism. The pectin will be covalently conjugated
to a peptide. Tyramine will be used as a model nucleophile to establish the methodology.
This conjugation can then be applied to other polymer e.g. M-cell homing peptide CKS9.
The pectin will also be conjugated to Toll-like 7/8 receptor agonist (4-Amino-2-(ethoxymet-
hyl)-1H-imidazo[4,5-c]quinoline-1-propanamine). This specific TLRa is believed to enhance
adjuvant activities by interaction with PRR’s. When the polymer have been constructed it
will coat the cationic liposome CAF04 by electrostatic interactions. CAF04 is an adjuvant
invented by SSI and have already been confirmed to activate the immune system.
The techniques used to characterize the different polymers designed are multi angle light
scattering, nuclear magnetic resonance and UV-spectroscopy. For the fully formed and
coated liposome DLS will be used. Furthermore, RAW-Blue™cells will be exposed to the
liposomes and pectin:TLRa polymers to investigate immune activation. Caco-2 cells will
be employed to investigate adhesion of pectin conjugated with TLR7/8 agonist to epithelial
cell layer.

Aim
The purpose of this project is to design an orally administered vaccine platform. First
step is to characterize and establish a conjugation reaction between pectin and peptide by
EDC/NHS cobbling. For this reaction tyramine is used as model peptide due to its amine
groups that can be conjugated to the carboxylate groups on the pectin.
To reach this goal of designing an orally administered sub-unit vaccine platform we need to
evaluate every aspect of the platform, as well as the conjugate together with the CAF04.
If developed, the platform for orally administered sub-unit vaccines will be accessible for
more populations and meet the 10th UN sustainability goal about reducing inequalities and
contribute to the third goal good health. Since the oral vaccine will accumulate less waste
from needles, syringes and disinfection tools and need less purification it will also meet
the 12th regarding responsible consumption and production. Since these items will not
need to be produced, the CO2 emission will be reduced, the cost for purchasing support
equipment for injection as well as the healthcare personal that is needed to handle the
intramuscular (IM) or subcutaneous (SC) distributed vaccines will significantly decrease.
The method might be applied to numerous sub-unit vaccines. Additional knowledge for
parenteral vaccines can be obtained as well.
Society will benefit if a larger group of the population obtained immunity since they will

Background
The invention of the vaccine is one of the most beneficial for the world population together
with understanding the importance of hygiene.
To design a vaccine, a deep understanding of the mechanisms in the human body are
necessary. Since this thesis will focus on oral delivery it is important to know what different
environment and challenges the vaccine will meet in the gastrointestinal tract. Especially in
the small intestine. It is in the small intestine most absorption of nutrients take place. M-
cells are specialised cell in the small intestine which are part of the Peyer’s patches, where
they facilitate transcellular transport of pathogens and antigens across the epithelium, to be
presented to the immune cells. From here, an adaptive immune response can be mounted
against pathogens.
This thesis will provide an overview of the main events happening
when the body is exposed to a pathogen. For an oral vaccine, the mucosal immune system
needs to be activated. For this activation knowledge about the gut-associated lymphoid
tissue is needed. The gut-associated lymphoid tissue is where the M-cells reside. Many
investigations have been done in the past of how to target these cells.
The above mentioned knowledge will be use full for vaccine design. Every investigation de-
pend on previous discoveries. The concept of vaccines have been explored since 1796, when
Edward Jenner made the first vaccine against smallpox. Different approaches have been
taken when developing vaccines, among these are live-attenuated, inactivated, sub-unit,
mRNA- and DNA- vaccines. Some approaches to immunisation by vaccination needs an
adjuvant to elicit a sufficient immune response. The concept of adjuvants have been studied
since 1924 where the veterinarian Gaston Ramon categorised the meaning in 1924 as "Sub-
stances used in combination with specific antigen to enhance immunological responses".
Still today it is an area of research.
Stability and toxicity are important parameters when investigating the possibility for an
oral vaccine. Most common administration are parenteral, into the muscle or under the
skin. Very few orally administered vaccines are on the market, but many new concept are
being investigated.
The subject of this thesis is an oral sub-unit vaccine platform consisting of a few build-
ing blocks. The carrier will be the cationic liposome with adjuvant properties - CAF04
- provided by SSI. Additional adjuvant evaluated will be the Toll-like-receptor agonist

7.1
Gastrointestinal Tract (GIT)
The gastrointestinal tract (GIT) comprise of the oral cavity, esophagus stomach and in-
testine. The intestinal system consists of small intestine, appendix, large intestine and the
rectum. The small intestine is then again divided into duodenum, jejunum and ileum. The
main purpose of the GIT is to digest and process the nutrients that enters the oral cavity.
The pathway of the nutrients can be followed in figure 1

The digestion begins in the oral cavity by mechanical and chemical processing. Enzymes
e.g. α-amylase will begin the hydrolysis of saccharides. Mucus will be added as lubricant in
the esophagus. Then the nutrients reach the stomach where hydrochloric acid, pepsin and
mucus are present. The acid denatures proteins, allowing pepsin to degrade proteins and
mucus provides a protective layer in the stomach. The acidic environment in the stomach
has a pH of between 1-2.5. The stomach also acts as storage for the food until it can proceed
to the intestine. In the duodenum, bile secreted by the liver is added, and the pH increases
due to bicarbonate. From here, the mix enters the small intestine, the environment here
is at a pH between 6.0-7.5 and the passage is 3-4 m long. In this pathway, more digestion
takes place and most of the nutrients are absorbed. More salts and mucus are added to
ensure transport, and 90% of water is absorbed in the small intestine. When the mix
reaches the large intestine, most of the nutrients have been absorbed and only water and
salt is left for absorption. The remaining food particles and waste matter is stored until
excreted as faeces through the rectum (Warberg and Kjær, 2016). The GIT surface is
covered in mucus the thickness of this layer various along the intestine. This mucus layer
is acting as a mechanical and physical barrier and here the gut microbiota resides. It is
known that the population of the gut microbiota ca be up to hundreds of trillion (Ohno,
2016), however their exact activities and interactions are not fully understood (Mann et al.,
2009).

lumen consists of mucus-secreting epithelial cells that is formed like a top and a crypt
which makes the large surface area possible. This structure is shown in figure 2 B is villi,
this villi is then again covered with micro villi shown in figure 2 C.

The mucosal epithelial cells are not homogeneous throughout the intestine it has large
variation e.g. it does not have the same permeability along the crypt of the villi.
7.2
Immune system
The immune system is a giant orchestra with numerous players that all play a significant
role. The immune system consists of an innate and an adaptive system. The innate sys-
tem is the first to go into play when the body is invaded by a pathogen. Response will
happen in minutes and can last for days. The adaptive system is more specialised and
respond to an invading pathogen within hours, being active for days or weeks and develop
memory for later attacks. The two different systems are highly dependent on each other.
Immunological memory can last from weeks to a lifetime. The connection between the two
systems is the antigen presenting cell (APC), the DC. All immune cells, or leukocytes, arise
from the bone marrow where they begin as pluripotent stem cells. In the bone marrow
they will differentiate into common lymphoid progenitor or common myeloid progenitor.
A common myeloid progenitor will either become an immature DC or differentiate to gran-
ulocyte/macrophage progenitor. A Granulocyte/macrophage progenitor will develop into
a neutrophil, eosinophil, basophil, monocyte or precursor of mast cell this differentiation
will happen in the blood. The common myeloid progenitor can also differentiate into a
megakaryocyte/erythrocyte progenitor that will either become a megakaryocyte that will
become platelets or an erythroblast that will become erythrocyte this is also happening in
the blood. Some of these will again develop in the tissue. The immature DC will become
mature, an unknown precursor mast cell will become a mast cell and monocyte will de-
velop into a macrophage. These cells represent some of the cells activated by the innate
immune system. Common lymphoid progenitors reside in the bone marrow. As they enter
the blood stream they either develop into B cell, T cell, NK cell, innate lymphoid cells

If the body’s first line of defence is compromised and a pathogen has gained access via

membrane which activate T-cells. T-cell mature in the thymus and get their specificity in
the lymph node by the DC or other APC cell. Neutrophils are the most abundant cells
of the innate immune system. It will engulf pathogenic microorganisms by phagocytoses
thus efficiently destroying them (Otagiri et al., 2017).

As mentioned previously DC is the link between the innate and the adaptive immune

becomes the antigen. The DC travels by the lymphatic vessels to the lymph node. In
the lymph node it presents the antigen to the naïve T-cell causing development into a

7.5
Vaccines
The way a vaccine function is activating immune responds and induce immunity. Prophy-
lactic vaccines are used to prevent illness hence induce immunity before getting exposed
to the pathogen. Vaccines can also be therapeutic and used after the patient have been
exposed to a pathogen.
The first vaccine used was the live attenuated vaccine. A live pathogen is used but its
virulence factors is inactivated. This vaccine strategy induces strong immune responses
but is also the strategy that can give side effects such as inflammation and uncontrolled
replication in rare cases the pathogen has reverted back to its pathogenic form expressing
its virulence factors and causing disease (Vela Ramirez et al., 2017).
The strategy to
weaken or alter the pathogen to be less virulent can be done by passing the virus through
a series of in vitro cultures. At each passage the pathogen becomes better at replicating
and infecting but looses its capability to infect the original human host (Vetter et al.,
2017).
Another approach where intact pathogen is used is the inactivated vaccine. For this strat-
egy radiation, heat or chemicals is used to kill the pathogen. This will still induce immune
responds but not as strong as the live attenuated and often a booster dose is needed. The
benefit is that it can not replicate and additionally it is very stable .
Subunit vaccines uses a part of the pathogen to present antigen to the immune system. It
can be a protein, toxoid, polysaccharide etc. This approach does not induce a very strong
immune responds and will not give long term immune protection. The way to overcome
this challenge is by using adjuvants terminology will be discussed in section 7.5.1. The
subunit vaccines are preferable due to its high safety profile(Vela Ramirez et al., 2017).
Viral vectors will imitate a live infection and by expressing antigens activate immune
responses.
Viral vector facilitate a very strong T-cell response including CD8+ T-cell
(Delany et al., 2014) but there will also be the risk that the vector will integrate in the
host genome.

7.5.1
Adjuvants
The word adjuvant comes from latin and means "aid to help". The veterinarian Gaston
Ramon categorised the meaning in 1924 as "Substances used in combination with specific
antigen to enhance immunological responses".
He observed a higher specific antibody
titer when horses developed abscesses at the injection site. At the same time Glenny et.
al discovered that aluminium salt had the same effect. It was not until the 1980’s and
forward additional adjuvant was licensed for humans. Since Freund’s adjuvant licensed in
the 80’s showed side effects it is now licensed for animals only.
Adjuvants can be classified in two main classes - delivery systems or immune potentiators.
Delivery system will deliver the antigen and at the same time create local proinflamma-
tory responses that will activate the innate immune system. Immunostimulators will also
activate innate responses but they will do it by activating the PRRs (explained in sec-
tion 7.2). At the present adjuvant specific for mucosal immunisation being investigated
are toxins extracted from Escheria coli and Vibrio cholerae, TLR agonists like flaggelin,
poly(I:C), CpG ODNs and small novel molecules such as α-galactosylceramide, chitosan
etc. (Apostólico et al., 2016).
Different compounds have been investigated for adjuvant effects including mineral salts, mi-
crobial products, emulsions, saponins, cytokines, polymers, microparticles, and liposomes.
The purpose of an adjuvant is to elicit immune responses such as: sustained release at the
injection site, up-regulation of chemokines and cytokines, cellular recruitment, increase
antigen uptake and presentation for APC, activation and maturation of APC’s (explained
in section 7.2) and/or activation of inflammation (Awate et al., 2013).

7.5.2
Nanoparticles-Liposomes
The challenge when developing a vaccine is to engineer a formulation that will not be
released and degraded by the acidic environment or enzymes in the gut before the antigens
reach their target. An option is a nanoparticle (NP) such as a liposome. NP’s have in
various studies shown efficacy, safety and the ability to induce both mucosal and systemic
immunity (Jazayeri and Poh, 2019). The size of NP’s range from 1 nm to 1µm. The use
of NP’s as an adjuvant is increasingly due to its biocompatibility and flexibility in shape,
size, and physio chemical properties. It can be a challenge to load the NP’s with antigen
(Jazayeri and Poh, 2019).
Liposomes consist of a membrane of phospholipids with a hydrophobic tail and hydrophilic
head that can self-assemble by hydrophobic interactions. The membrane resembles bio-
membranes and results in a high safety profile.
Currently, liposomes are used as carriers in injectable formulations, but it has properties
that makes them interesting for mucosal delivery.
These properties include biodegrad-
ability, biocompatibility, surface charge variability, and membrane fluidity. Liposomes are
cationic but their surface charge can be changed by various polymers integrated to the
membrane of the liposome (Davoud Jazayeri et al., 2021).
Surface charge can also be
changed according to the chosen lipid for the membrane. Lipid can be chemically modi-
fied to promote specific functions such as temperature stability, pH sensitivity and light
sensitivity. A particles surface charge is called zeta potential and is a measurement of the
magnitude of repulsion and attraction between particles surrounding it. Zeta potential of
a liposome will help predict stability and how it will react in vivo. A liposome with a zeta
potential greater than 30mV negative or positive show better stability in solution but will
clear more rapidly from the system following intravenous administration (Uchegbu et al.,

The choice of liposome depends on the properties of the drug it has to carry. Unilameller
vesicles have a large aqueous core and a small hydrophobic volume making it best suitable

barrier will provide faster drug release, unilamellar vesicles can be made smaller in size, the
release of the drug is very simple. Multilaminar liposomes have a small aqueous core but
large hydrophobic volumes giving great loading of hydrophobic drugs. It will give multiple
barriers hence, slower release, they are larger in size with more complex release.
When loading drug to the liposome two techniques are used. Passive drug loading and
remote (active) loading. Passive loading is a method where the drugs are loaded when
the lipids are hydrated the drug is present in the hydration buffer when the lipids are self
assembling. The drug will be trapped in the liposome core if it is lipophillic and in the
membrane if hydrophobic. Passive loading is an option for all four liposomes. Remote
loading uses pH gradient to load the drug after liposome assembling. The liposome are
prepared with a specific pH in the core. The external buffer is then replaced with a new
buffer with different pH. The drug will be driven by the gradient and be trapped inside
the liposome this technique provides high drug loading.

7.6.1
Oral administration
Oral administration covers tablets, solutions, capsules, nasal spray etc.
An oral vaccine needs to activate the mucosal immune system outlined in section 7.3 on
the way the vaccine will meet degrading enzymes, very low pH between 1-2, bile salts and
the risk of being chemically deactivated. The main function of the bile salt is to degrade
lipids into smaller fractions into triglycerid, monoglyceride and lastly free fatty acids. This
contributes to the risk that the drug will be released to early if the carrier is constructed by
lipids. If the antigen is released to early the liver and small intestine will try to metabolise
the vaccine. The long route makes it difficult to anticipate the bioavailibility of the drug
that will reach the intended site of action and higher dose of antigenic protein or peptides
are needed. Bioavailabillity is a measurement of the active drug that will reach indented
site this can be compromised by poor solubility and permeability. (Grdic et al., 2022) have
shown how important the dose of vaccine is it can mean the difference between tolerance and
immunity. If the body recognise the vaccine as non-pathogenic and the immune responses
are not induced it can result in tolerance instead of protection (Vela Ramirez et al., 2017).
In some cases tolerance is the aim. The body can reacts undesirable with hypersensitivity
to a particle that supposedly does not cause any damage like, plant pores, fungal spores,
insect venom, animal dander, house dust mites, some foods and certain chemical from
latex, jewelry, detergents or cosmetics (Wilson et al., 2019). When this allergic reaction is
the case it can be beneficial to obtain tolerance.
A variety of approaches have been made to design oral vaccines.
To name a few; λ-
bacteriaphages, edible vaccines and microfabricated devices. The challenges with an orally
administered vaccine is that it needs to be soluble, it must have the properties that allows
it to permeate the intestinal membrane and penetrate the mucosal barrier and also be
stable enough that it does not degrade before reaching the intended site. Additionally
there is the possibility that the drug will irritate the digestive tract.
All new inventions build on others discoveries hence, when designing a new vaccine previous
studies must be consulted.

7.8.1
Pectin
Pectin is a linear polysaccharide mainly made up of D-galactturonic acid (GalA) units
that can be wieved in figure 12. These unit are linked in chains by (1-4) glycosidic linkage.
Along the approximatly 1000 saccharide backbone methoxy groups will be distributed and
to a less extend also carboxyl groups and free OH groups. If the methylation is low the
pectin can be amidated this can be done by treating the pectin with ammonia. Xylose,
galactose and arabinose are common sidechains that can be present. The average weight
of a pectin is 50’000-150’000 daltons.
Figure 12: The backbone of pectin structure
GalA.
Pectin is naturally occurring in plants it will
mainly be found in the cells middle lamella of
the cell wall. Here it will provide stability by
cross linking cellulose and hemocellulose fibers.
This natural occurrence is why it is a good
choice for development of oral delivery since it
is already in our diet.
For commercial use it
is derived from citrus and apple peel which is
a bi-product from juice manufacturing. Alter-
natively it can be derived from sugarbeet, sun-
flower heads or mango waste (Allwyn Sundar Raj A and V, 2012; Thakur et al., 1997). It
is used in different food manufacturing processes as a gelling agent, thickener, texturizer,
emulsifier and stabilizer. Pectin has applications in various fields of the pharmaceutical
industry as well(Thakur et al., 1997).
Pectins are soluble in water though the solubility decrease as methyl group increase. The
degree of solubility can be influenced by degree of polymerization and distribution of methyl
groups. Parameters such as pH, temperature and concentration will influence the solubility
as well. Food industry uses pectins ability to form gels this gelation is affected by both
the structure of the molecule but also the impact from the environment surrounding it.
Gelation will occur in the presence of Ca+ ions or sugar and acid. Pectin have a negative
charge the charge density is higher at a higher pH and lower methylation. There is a
distinction between pectins with low degree of methylation (LM) and with high degree
of methylation ( HM). Ca+ ions will provide a physical crosslink between the carboxylic
groups on LM. LM will also be more stable than HM this is due to HM’s tendency to
deesterify under humid conditions.
Amidated LM pectins show increased gel forming
abilities with increased strength. This is reported to be due to hydrogen binding between
the amidated groups (Thakur et al., 1997). HM is less water soluble due to the hydrophobic
methyl groups it has been shown that adding of dimethyl sulfoxide will promote gelation
of HM.

7.8.2
Peptide
A peptide is a short sequence of amino acids covalently linked together by an amide bond,
called a peptide bond. A peptide is usually made up of less than 40 amino acids. Peptides
can have different properties depending on which amino acids they are build of.
The
peptide can have many different structures large and bulky or more small and packed
(Miesfeld and McFoy, 2017).
In the pharmaceutical industry many peptides are investigated for interactions with specific
surface receptors. This property can be used in the investigation of drug delivery systems.
The interest in using peptides is due to its small size it is easy to synthesize, and typically
non-immunogenic (Xu et al., 2020).
Different peptide sequence’s have been discovered that interacts with ligands on the M-
cells. Other sequences show enhanced transport of nano particles for drug delivery.
Peptide CKS9 with the sequence CKSTHPLSC was identified by (Yoo et al., 2010) as a
M-cell homing peptide. Conjugated to chitosan particle by a EDC/NHS cobbling they
constructed an oral vaccine carrier that targeted Peyer’s patches.
In 2022 (Wang et al., 2022) used the knowledge from (Yoo et al., 2010) using CKS9 but
with another approach. Starch was derived from corn and conjugated to CKS9. Micelles
was made with the conjugate and Trp-2 peptide. Micelles correspond to liposomes but
have one phospholipid layer only with head directed outward and tails directed inward. In
vitro experiments showed enhanced selectivity to the micelles by M-cells. This study also
investigated the binding to α5β1 receptor on the surface of the M-cell enhancing uptake
of antigens attached to these micellar delivery systems. Wang et. al. concluded to have
a oral vaccine delivery system with "excellent loading capacity, outstanding storage and
dilution stability, enhanced M-cell targeting release behavior".

7.9.1
Conjugation
When conjugating CKS9 peptide to pectin the conjugation will be between the carboxylate
group of the pectin and the amine on CKS9. This can be done by a carbodiimide (EDC)
cobbling carbodiimedes will enable the formation of amide bonds between carboxylic acid
groups and amines. EDC reacts with the carboxylic group on the pectin that will form a
intermediate that is not very stable in aqueous solution. The peptide that holds a primary
amine group will bond to the pectin. N-substituted urea will be released. This reaction
have optimum condition in a MES buffer at pH 4.5. A buffer is used to keep a stable pH
throughout the reaction.
In 1986 Staros et. al. showed that the unstable intermidiate could be overcome with adding
N-hydroxysulfosuccinimide (Sulfo-NHS) to the reaction (Staros et al., 1986). This reaction
can be followed in figure 14.
Additional benefit with this reaction is no accumulation
of released waste product. MES buffer will continue to be the preferred buffer with the
additional reaction higher pH is possible (ThermoFisher Scientific, 2019). Higher pH can
be beneficial if the reactants thrive in a more neutral environment instead of acidic. For
the relevant conjugation to take place the ligand needs to be uncharged hence, buffer is
important.

7.10.1
Dialysis
Dialysis is a purification process that take advantage of diffusion driven by concentration
gradient. The diffusion will happen over a semi permeable often made of cellulose. This
membrane will retain the molecule of interest by size exclusion since the smaller molecule
will travel through the membrane that can have different pore size. Diffusion is a solution
moving from higher concentration to lower concentration without consumption of energy.
The method takes advantage of osmosis. Osmosis is the diffusion of solvent across a mem-

to a region of higher solute concentration. Dialysis can be done in a variety of sample sizes
and molecular sizes.
7.10.2
Freeze drying
Freeze drying is a process used to purify a product-basically it is removal of water. The
sample is frozen by e.g lowering the sample into nitrogen. Then it is placed in a freeze
dryer that will freeze in vacuum. Resulting in sublimation of the water molecules in the
sample. This way of purifying the product is beneficial for sensitive products e.g. product
that will degrade by dry heating such as protein, DNA etc. The challenge with freeze
drying is that it is highly energy consuming.

Nuclear Magnetic Resonance Spectroscopy (NMR)
NMR is a method used to determine the conformation of a organic molecule which atom
does it contain and where are the functional groups placed.
A powerful magnetic fields is applied and the molecules are arranges according to the
magnetic field. To analyse the molecule the difference in energy of the α and β spin of the
proton in the nucleus of the hydrogen atoms is measured by radio frequency waves.
The environment of every proton is measured.
The information obtained from the NMR spectra is chemical shift, integration and spin-
spin cobbling. The chemical shift will tell the position of the functional groups
NMR is a non destructive method and small samples are needed.
The result will be
displayed in a graph where the x-axe is points per minut (ppm) and the y-axe is arbitrary.
Evaluating big molecules such as polymers will not give an exact molecule as is possible
with small molecules.

Results and Discussion
In the pursuit to design an oral administered sub-unit vaccine polymers with different
intended functions have been produced. Common to all of them are that they are based
on pectin. The benefits of using pectin is that it is natural occurring in fruit and hence,
not likely toxic. It has anionic charges, making it a perfect complexation polymer to the
cationic CAF04 SYSTEM. The conjugation was conducted at a pH of 5, this pH will cause
the pectin to partially hydrolyze, and possible not stay in chains too long or folded. The
challenges with pectin are that it can be difficult to dissolve, and often heat is needed,
though too much heat accelerates pectin hydrolysis. Since it is purified from fruitpeel,
there is the possibility of impurities. One pectin provided was more yellow than the other
indicating impurities in both, but more in the most yellow.
To evaluate if the conjugation proceeded as anticipated, the pectin starting material was
also investigated. Processing of H1NMR data was done with the software MestreNova.
When the pectin was dissolved in D2O one of the distinction between high-methylation
(HM) and low methylation (LM) was observed. It could be confirmed that the HM was
harder to dissolve than LM.

Insulin as a model protein
The last building block for a oral administered vaccine construct is the ability to conjugate
a protein to the pectin or the liposome membrane. Insulin was used for a model protein.
The results from the attempt to conjugate protein (insulin) to the polymer was inconclusive.
The polymers conjugated to the insulin aggregated making the results from the GPC-MALS
invalid. Insulin is known to be a fragile protein to process it might be the insulin that was
causing the aggregation.
The sample containing the liposomes conjugated to insulin got milky white, suggesting
that the liposome had disassembled. The liposomes was not stable enough to undergo the
chemical process that was needed for the insulin to get conjugated. Another source of error
could be that the optimal buffer for the liposome are TRIS buffer. When the liposomes

liposome for insulin conjugation it was centrifuged and stored in milliQ water. This might

containing TRIS/ACETATE buffer and the surrounding environment being water.
If the possibility of loading the antigen on the membrane should be investigated each
individual antigen must be evaluated for stability against the GIT separately. Even when
the carrier is sufficiently stable the antigen can still be metabolised on its way to the small

Transport through epithelial layer
The color labeled polymers was used to investigate if the polymers would be recognised by
epithelial cells and be transported either by transcytosis or between the cell through the
tight junctions.
Figure 34: Pictures from the cell experiment top row showing no addition of polymers and bottom row
showing HM2297:TLRa10% polymer addition
The assay showed no binding to the epithelial surface not the pectin alone or the polymers
figure 34. This means that the cell did not have any receptors recognising the pectin or the
TLRa and no transport across the epithelial cell layer with this polymer alone is possible.
Though it is still possible to see some transport if CKS9 was conjugated. Furthermore,
it is possible that M-cells would recognise the polymer which is the main target for this
carrier.

Further perspectives
Experiments with M-cells must be done to investigate uptake of the entire macro-molecule.
All component separately and in different combinations, pectin:TLRa, pectin:CKS9,
pectin:TLRa:CKS9. This study have evaluated insulin as a model protein with no valid
results. Ovalbumin would be a good choice to test for conjugation, additionally FITC
labeled ovalbumin can be used to investigate transport of the carrier Liau et al. (2015) .
This must illuminates if uptake by the M-cells are possible and if uptake will take place
elsewhere like the epithelial can be done on caco-2 cells. Result of this study have shown
that the TLRa will not elicit epithelial uptake but it is still possible some of the other
component will have this ability. If this will give the results anticipated a natural next
step will be to set up an in vivo study on rodents. Stability depend of the individual
antigen but it would be interesting and a logical next step to evaluate if the carrier will
succeed in all the above mentioned evaluations
It must be tested more specific which TLRa pathways will activate and if it is better than
the ones already investigated. Toxic side effects must also be investigated for this particular
TLRa. It would be interesting to compare this TLRa to other TLRa’s beginning with the
immunostimulators in the CAF-family such as Poly-IC. The scenario that is desired would
be maximum TLR7/8 activation minimum toxicity. A study made by (Kaushik and Kaur,
2021) revealed that alkyl chain between 6 and 16 gave up to 2-3 fold higher activity than
the parent compound, for further investigations this could be a a path to increase activity.
One of the challenges with oral administration is to anticipate the dose that will reach
the lymphatic system. As mentioned previous it needs to be a sufficient dose to induce
immunity and not tolerance. Parenteral sub-unit vaccines already in use demands a booster
shot this does not need to apply to the oral administered sun-unit vaccine but must be
evaluated.
The CAF04 liposome showed instability when exposed to chemical treatment. This insta-

constructed. Another option would be to use a nanoparticle like in the study where CKS9

If the evaluation of this oral administered sub-unit vaccine carrier does not meet the ex-

When working with the peptin and TLRa gelation was often observed this can be explored

Conclusion
In this study, the conjugation of functional entities to pectin was performed and evaluated.
Tyramine as a model peptide and TLR7/8 agonist was conjugated. The purpose was pectin
modification of the CAF adjuvant system exploring the cationic properties of the CAF04
liposome and its abilities to attach the polymers by electrostatic forces.
Tyramine was conjugated to pectin with the extent of 10%, 20% and 50%. The same was
done with TLR7/8 agonist with 2.5%, 5.0% and 10% extent of conjugation. The results
show clear evidence that various extent of conjugation to pectin with tyramine and TLR7/8
agonist are possible.
CAF04 liposome was coated with the constructed polymers changing the liposomes zeta
potential from +31.2mV to -27.4mV for the HM2297 pectin conjugated with TLR7/8 ag-
onist at 10% extent. This knowledge are important since the zeta potential is one of the
properties crucial for cell uptake. This study reveals that the zeta potential can be changed
by the polymers shielding it. A correlation between TLR7/8 agonist conjugation and zeta
potential has been demonstrated as a higher extent of TLRa conjugation the lower the
zeta potential will be. The same construct changed the particle size from 204d.nm with no
polymers to 337d.nm for coating with pectin with 10% extent of conjugation. Validating
the procedure to construct the macro-molecule was the low polydispersity index ranging
from 0.185 to 0.326 states that the particles are highly similar in size.
In the broader context, when designing a drug carrier, three parameters are essential:
choice of carrier, choice of ligand and identifying a target.
Page 53
==PAGE:62==
Master thesis
July 24, 2022
While choosing a carrier the important properties to consider are surface, shape, and size.
These have all been evaluated for the intermediate particle in this report. The difference
between the particle evaluated, and the designed particle is the CKS9 peptide. The design
evaluated are within the scope of an efficient vaccine platform, but it is unknown if CKS9
conjugation changes these properties.
CKS9 was the desired targeting ligand, as literature shows CKS9 to be an efficient M-
cell homing peptide. The experiments in this thesis have confirmed a protocol for the
conjugation of pectin and amines, and it is likely it will translate for peptide conjugation.
Conjugation of TLRa to pectin have also been proven in various extents. Based on the
results from experiment with RAW-Blue™cells the function of the TLRa have been con-
firmed. The experiment showed various extent of TLR activation according to extend of
conjugation. It is found that the TLRa is still active after conjugation to pectin. RAW-
Blue™cell study has confirmed conjugation to pectin have not compromised the site of
action on the TLRa. The carboxylate on the pectin have conjugated to the primary amine
on the TLR7/8 agonist. Leaving the active site exposed for interactions.
TLRa exposure to CACO-2 cell did not show any transport or interactions, knowing that
the TLRa was active it is concluded that the pectin TLRa polymers will not interact
with epithelial layer in the gut lumen. This could be the case with the CKS9 conjugated
construct, since CKS9 could interact with the epithelial layer, though it should have speci-
ficity towards M-cells. Since CKS9 peptide did not reach the laboratory CKS9 needs to
be investigated in an additional study.
The results confirm that the platform of CAF adjuvants, coated with TLRa conjugated
pectin will provide an immune stimulator and immune potentiator adjuvant.
It can be concluded that benefits of using pectin as a carrier of homing peptides and/or
TLRa could be substantial. This study proves the potential of designing an orally admin-
istered vaccine that has the prospective to reach a significant larger part of the population.
