Abstract
Wind turbines are highly dynamic structures which are subjected to large external excitaยญ
tions due to both varying wind speeds and rotation of the large rotor mass.
Accurate and reliable information of the states are essential for ensuring an optimal conยญ
trol solution, that increases operational stability and reduces structural fatigue.
This thesis focuses on the development of an algorithm for accurately and reliably estiยญ
mating acceleration of the tower top states for a real wind turbine system, which utilizes
the existing sensor systems of the specific wind turbine.
For this purpose, a nonlinear mathematical model describing relevant structural and aeroยญ
dynamic dynamics for the foreยญaft and sideยญside movements of the turbine tower top poยญ
sition is developed. Model parameters are estimated from real measurement data using a
modified Recursive Least Squares algorithm.
Estimation of tower top states are achieved by implementation of an Extended Kalman
Filter using measurement data from four 3ยญaxis accelerometers, located inside the nacelle
structure.
The main result of the thesis is an online model based estimation algorithm with good
noise rejecting properties, and the capability of estimating wind turbine tower top accelยญ
eration in the foreยญaft and sideยญside directions.

Introduction
1.1
Motivation
As wind turbine systems continues to become an increasingly preferable choice for reยญ
newable energy production, they simultaneously continues to grow in size to increase
efficiency and to accommodate the growing power consumption.
These larger wind turbine structures are susceptible to great load influences and external
excitations, which increases the risk of material fatigue due to excessive vibrations and
movements over the lifespan of a turbine.
1.2
Scope
To increase the lifespan and efficiency of wind turbine systems, different advanced closedยญ
loop control strategies are employed, which through a multiple of sensors systems attempts
to achieve optimal performance with regards to power production, while simultaneously
attempting to reduce/eliminate movements of the wind turbine structure.
For the control strategies to work as intended, sufficient system information from sensor
measurements are required, which has to be both accurate and reliable for the control stratยญ
egy to work efficiently.
1.3
Main goal
As the importance of reliable sensor measurement data has been introduced. The topic of
this thesis will be the development of an algorithm that produces accurate and reliable senยญ
sor measurement data for the larger wind turbine control system, such that it can perform
the necessary adjustments for optimized and steady operation of the wind turbine based
on as accurate system information as possible.

2.1
Wind turbine systems
Wind turbines are structures that interacts with the wind, by capturing part of the kinetic
energy of the wind and converting it into mechanical energy, which can then be turned
into electrical energy.
There exist different types of wind turbines, with the type most used nowadays being the
Horizontal Axis Wind Turbine, HAWT, with a threeยญbladed rotor configuration. An illusยญ
tration of a general threeยญbladed HAWT is shown in Figure 2.1.
The rotor of a HAWT is located at the top of a tower structure, such that it is placed high
above the ground where the wind have more energy and are less turbulent [1]. The rotor
is a collected term describing the blades and the hub of a wind turbine, or the visible parts
of a wind turbine that interacts with the wind and rotates in a clockwise direction.
The blades are aerodynamic structures that are designed to interact with the wind and opยญ
timize the transformation of kinetic wind energy into useful mechanical energy, know as
lift, while reducing undesirable effects such as drag and wake.
Most modern HAWT are variableยญspeed turbines, which means they have the ability to
operate with high efficiency over a wide range of wind speeds. Varying the speed of the
rotor is achieved through Individual Pitch Control, IPC, of the blades. By changing the
pitch angle, ฮฒ, of each individual blade, the aerodynamics of the individual blades are also
changed, which can be used to make the HAWT more efficient at a specific wind speed.
IPC can also be used to counter aerodynamics asymmetries of the rotor, due to environยญ
mental factors such as water and ice, and thus reduce structural fatigue, or as aerodynamic
breaks.
Through, a limit exist for how much kinetic energy a wind turbine can extract from the
wind, this limit is know as the Betz limit, and limits wind turbines to a maximum effiยญ
ciency of 59.3% [2].
The hub is the center point of the rotor which the blades are attached to. Additionally the
hub connects the rotor assembly to the nacelle/tower structure through a shaft/drivetrain
that consist of a gearbox and a generator, as illustrated in Figure 2.1a.
The shaft directly connected to the rotor/the left side of the gearbox in Figure 2.1a is refยญ
ereed to as the lowยญspeed shaft, while the shaft connected to the generator/the right side
of the gearbox in Figure 2.1a is refereed to as the highยญspeed shaft.

The structure of a HAWT, a heavy mass which rotates while placed at the top of a slender

The most present sources of excitation in a wind turbine system are caused by rotation of

Where rotation of the rotor is related to the tower movement in the Xยญdirection, refereed

the natural frequency of the tower should be designed such that it does not coincide with

2.3.2
The Wind
The wind can be separated into a deterministic and a stochastic component as shown by
the two distinct peaks of the van der Hoven spectrum, one at low frequencies and one at
high frequencies [2] The wind speed can therefore be expressed as
V = Vm + v
(2.2)
where Vm is the mean wind speed representing the deterministic/low frequency compoยญ
nents of the wind,and v is the atmospheric turbulence representing the stochastic/high
frequency components of the wind.
The factors that contribute to the deterministic component are primarily wind shear, tower
shadow and the presence of geographic obstacles or other wind turbines in the surroundยญ
ings. The stochastic component describes the random turbulent fluctuation speed/frequenยญ
cies of local winds, which excites turbine vibrations if a blade is swept through an area of
the rotor plane with a higher or lower wind speeds due to turbulence.
The randomness of turbulence makes it very hard to predict/describe accurately.
On the other hand, the effects of the deterministic component: wind shear and tower
shadow are predictable and can easily be described [10].
โข Wind shear describes the increase of Vm as height above the ground increases, the
decrease in Vm close to the ground is caused by terrain roughness.
This change in Vm as a function of height results in an uneven wind load being exยญ
erted on the blades depending on their azimuth position, as the wind load is lower
for a blade in the lower half of the rotor plane, than when it is in the upper half of
the rotor plane.
The effect of wind shear is part of the aerodynamic imbalance that causes excitation
at the 1P frequency. For HAWT the effect of wind shear is partly mitigated by the
tower, and by placing the turbines at flat open terrains.
โข Tower shadow describes the decrease of Vm directly in front of the tower, due to
the tower of a HAWT being an obstacle for incoming airflow.
The sudden decrease in Vm due to tower shadow, results in a decreased wind load
being exerted on each blade when passing in front of the tower, this excites blade
vibration and is the main cause for the second excitation frequency, known as the 3P
frequency (for a three bladed HAWT, as the blades passes the tower three times per
rotation), which is equal to three times the rotational frequency of the turbine/the
1P frequency as seen in Figure 2.5 [2].

While the RLS algorithm, described in Chapter 3, update the estimate of a static or slowly

4.1
The Kalman filter
A Kalman filter is a linear optimal estimation algorithm, that infers/produces estimations
of the state parameters by combining predictions from a model of the system dynamics
and measurements from the system itself, both of which are influenced by statistical noise
and other uncertainties, to provide the best possible state estimations for the system.
The base theory behind the Kalman filter is based on the assumption of a linear timeยญ
invariant (LTI) system, influenced by independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) Gausยญ
sian noise, in both the process model and measurement observations.
A LTI system in discreteยญtime is described by the stateยญspace equations:

If the assumptions of linearity and Gaussian noise are fulfilled, the Kalman filter will

parameters.

noise influencing the system is known, the Kalman filter is still the best linear estimator

6
Conclusion
In this thesis an estimation algorithm was developed for estimating the tower top states of
a wind turbine system.
As part of the estimation algorithm a simple nonlinear mathematical model was developed
for describing the structural and aerodynamic dynamics of the wind turbine tower when
affected by external forces. In order to linearize the nonlinear model, a 3rd order polynoยญ
mial regression method had to be used for linearization of specific model parameters.
A modified Recursive Least Squares algorithm was used to estimate unknown model paยญ
rameters kt, bt and mt from real measurement data. The modified Recursive Least Squares
algorithm provided consistent parameters estimation, however the results might have sufยญ
fered from low persistent excitation of the regressor signals.
The developed EKF estimation algorithm provided good estimates for the acceleration of
the tower top states in foreยญaft and sideยญside directions, which were consistent with Vestas
own acceleration of the tower top states.
However, the reliability of the algorithm could be improved, as the algorithm is suscepยญ
tible to large crossยญsensor measurement spikes/faults, which can be carried through the
EKF estimation algorithm.
It is suggested that the estimation algorithm should be implemented alongside a faultยญ
tolerant strategy, to protect the algorithm against faulty measurements, which could be a
topic for future work.
The derivation of the wind turbine tower model and its related parameters was an exยญ
tended process, where some assumptions had to be made. The modelling process could
be attempted improved by utilizing a black box modelling strategy, for example by fitting
an adaptive ARMAX model to the input/output data of the wind turbine system.
In conclusion, the thesis has described the development of an online model based estiยญ
mation algorithm, which utilizes the existing sensor systems of the thesis specific wind
turbine, to estimate the acceleration of the wind turbine tower top states in the foreยญaft and
sideยญside directions with good accuracy and noise rejecting.
