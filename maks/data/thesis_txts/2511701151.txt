Abstract 
Flat concrete slabs (traditional beam less structures) have been practiced since the beginning 
of the twenty century in order to fulfill functional requirements like a reasonable free height 
above the slab, flexibility in room lay out, simple form work and reinforcement, savings in 
overall building height, reduction of overall cost and weight of the building and much more. 
Through times, their design and erection methods have been developed, and they are found 
to be more economical and beneficial than in situ cast slabs carried by beams, especially in 
warehouses, car parks, offices, and industrial buildings. 
However, the introduction of precast concrete constructions like precast slabs and beams 
challenged the in-situ cast deck. This is because they can be fabricated at a factory and placed 
on site after they have gained their full strength, so that the time for construction at site is 
reduced dramatically. Although flats slabs fulfill more of the functional requirements listed 
above, than precast slabs carried by beams, they take more time to build and more formwork 
to make during construction. As a result, flat slabs become less competitive. 
Therefore, in this thesis a brand-new beamless structural system is analyzed and designed 
that combines all the advantages of the flat slabs and the precast technology. 
This new beamless system includes four elements. They are the SL-deck (super light deck), 
MD (massive concrete deck), MPD (massive concrete plate deck) and BC (bolted columns). 
The SL-decks are used because of their many structural advantages to span along the longest 
side of the system. They rest at the MD’s by means of a blade connection. The MD’s then also 
transfer their load to the MPD’s with blade connections. Finally, the MPD decks are placed on 
the bolted columns. 
One of the main reasons for the sustainability of the system is that it about 80 % of it consists 
of the newly patented most sustainable, eco-friendly and economical SL decks. 
Each element is designed in the project for static- and live office load. In the process, several 
trial and error calculations are carried out in order to determine the minimum possible cross 
sections and the maximum possible span lengths. Finally, an illustrative office plan and design 
with this new concept of structural system is included.

1.Introduction 
Reinforced concrete beam is a unidimensional structural element, basically used to transfer 
the loads coming from reinforced concrete slabs, and any horizontal structural member to 
the vertical structural element which is mainly called column. This pattern of structural 
system has been used by mankind for a long time. As a result of the higher demand for vast 
area rooms, clear stores height and to ease the installation of building service materials, the 
existence of the beams in structural system has become an hinderance in many larger 
buildings like residential, commercial and public buildings. To acquire the former listed 
demands, beam with higher depth must be designed to ensure a safe transfer of loads to the 
columns. As the span length increases so does the depth of the beam which makes them not 
favorable to look at.  It will also result in difficulties in fixing the building service elements such 
as electric cables, water pipes, ventilation systems.  
To put this building service elements either hole is made on the beams which dramatically 
affect its strength, or it will bend to pass by the bottom side of the beams which result in an 
increase of length of the building service elements. This will cause an increased depth of the 
suspension ceiling, which decreases the clear story height.  
To tackle this problem a beam less structural system is developed in the early 1900. Beam less 
structural system which are commonly called “flat slabs” are concrete slabs reinforced in two 
or more directions, generally without beams or girders to transfer the loads to the supporting 
columns. Flat slab may be of constant thickness throughout in the area or it may be thickened 
as a drop panel. The column may also be of constant section or it may be flared to form a 
column head or capital. The drop panels are effective in reducing the shear stresses where 
the column is liable to punch through the slab and provide an increased moment of resistance 
where the negative moments are greatest. The historical development of this system is 
written in the section below. 
Historic development  
The first use of reinforced-concrete slabs supported solely on columns started from the early 
twentieth century. This structural system had been developing in two different continents, 
America and Europe. It has been developed quite independently from one another. In the US, 
engineers George M. Hill and C.A. P. Turner are mentioned as being the most deserving for 
such development. Thus, first structures with slabs supported on columns came to reality 
based on the design made by Hill in the period from 1899 to 1901. Figure below show the first 
building with flat slabs. 
 
Figure 1 Flat slab designed by Hill (1899-1901) 
In the period from 1905 to 1909, Turner used this system on many structures thus, proving 
its safety, and paving the way toward a wider use of this system in the future.

Although some difference existed in the quantity and distribution of tensile reinforcement,

"mushroom slabs". This name was given because of the shape of the column with capital that 
is a column head, but also because of the speed of construction which was symbolically 
compared to the fast growth of mushrooms. Some of the main   advantages of flat slabs 
(which is traditional beam less structural system) are explained on the section given below. 
2. Advantages of beam less structural system 
In this section the advantages of in situ concrete flat slabs over in situ slabs carried by beams 
will be discussed. Both the beams and slabs are casted on site monolithically in case of in situ 
slabs carried by beams. They both use in situ concrete and framework during construction. 
Today this system is mainly used in undeveloped and developing countries. Sometimes, in situ 
concrete construction are also practiced in different civil structures in developed countries. 
For instance, core of high-rise building is constructed with in situ concrete to acquire the 
better stiffness of the core.  
2.1 Simple formwork and reinforcement 
One of the main advantages of flat slabs  is the simplicity of work with the form work .The 
form work for the flat slabs and columns are made in short periods of time with lesser amount 
of the materials used as form work in comparison to the  traditional structural system(in situ 
==PAGE:13==
                                                                                                                                       Beam less structural system 
3 
slabs carried by beams). This is mainly due to the beams which are casted in between the 
slabs. Thus, if the beam is also casted in site monolithically with the slabs the framework gets 
complicated and needs much more labor hours. It will also require more concrete and 
reinforcement than would be required for flat plates with the same loads and span. 
2.2 No beams simplifying under-floor services outside the drops 
In beam less structural system all the ventilation ducts and electrical ducts are distributed 
without the interference of beams which will ultimate decrease their length used over all the 
building. By this the overall cost of the building decreases and the clear height of the rooms 
increases. 
2.3 Flexibility in room layout 
Flat slabs allow architects to introduce partition walls anywhere required, this allows owner 
to change the size of room layout. Use of flat slab allows choice of omitting false ceiling and 
finish soffit of slab with skim coating. In case the client plans change in the interior and wants 
to use the accommodation to suit the need, flat slab construction is the perfect choice as it 
offers that flexibility to the owner. This flexibility is possible due to the use of square lattice 
and absence of beam that makes channeling of services and allocation of partitions difficult. 
2.4 Saving in building height 
Usually in beam supported slabs; false ceiling is used to house the ducting service and to 
made-up smooth roofing finish for the occupants. This is not only saving the cost of roof 
cladding but also provide comparatively more effective and functional height of the 
building.  Thus, we can say that more floor-to-floor height is needed in case of beam 
supported slab which is not the case with the flat slab. Reducing the height, thickness and 
absence of beams reduces the foundation load ultimately making flat slab as an economical 
solution for roofing system.  An illustrative figure is given below. 
 
 
Figure 4 Saving in building height with flat slabs 
2.5 Shorter construction time 
The absence of beams make reinforcement fixing a simple and easy process and, in some 
areas, prefabricated steel reinforcement mesh available in various sizes can also be used 
==PAGE:14==
                                                                                                                                       Beam less structural system

saving labor cost and saving considerable time.  The ease in fixing of formwork also helps in 
reducing the cost and time.  
2.6 Ease of installation of mechanical an electrical service 
All mechanical and electrical services can be mounted directly on the underside of the slab 
instead of bending them to avoid the beams. Also, during maintenance, if any problem occurs 
in the building service, it easy to figure out and fix them. 
2.7 Buildable score 
This allows standardized structural members and prefabricated sections to be integrated into 
the design for ease of construction. This process makes the structure more buildable, reduce 
the number of site workers and increase the productivity at site, thus providing more 
tendency to achieve a higher Buildable score. 
2.8 Economical 
They are particularly economical for warehouses, parking and industrial buildings, and similar 
structures. 
2.9 Fire resistance capacity 
Hence the exposed surface is larger in beam less structural system, the slabs have more 
structural fire resistance capacity as compared with slabs carried by the beams. For the slabs 
carried by the beams, the failure occurs early with beams since, the beams are exposed to 
fire with three sides and secondly because they are loaded by the slabs in addition to their 
own self-weights. 
3. Development of design of different kind of beam less structures 
3.1 Type of concrete flat slabs. 
Reinforced in situ concrete flat slabs are mainly classified in four categories based on the 
design carried out over the connections between the flat slabs and the columns. The flat slabs 
are generally having the same thickness throughout the area, but sometimes it is designed to 
be thicker over the columns. This is called drop panel which mainly helps to avoid possible 
punching effect of the columns. Columns are also designed to have the same cross section 
area on most cases, but it also made to increases its cross-section area at the top, which is 
called column head.  The four type of flat slabs are illustrated in figure 5 shown below.

3.2 Reinforcement in flat slabs. 
When slack reinforcement is used in flat slabs mainly the reinforcement is placed on position 
and tied properly before the pouring of the concrete. Sometimes the slack reinforcement can 
be welded to each other according to the design in the factor and are easily placed on 
position. Use of prefabricated welded mesh minimizes the installation time of flat slabs. 
Welded mesh is available in standard size and provides better quality control in construction 
of flat slab. Today the post-tensioning method is increasing widely, due to its application and 
it is widely practiced in developed countries. By using post-tensioning method one can design 
the most economic and the safe design. Figure below shows the development of type of 
method of reinforcement which have been used in flat slabs. 
 
Figure 6 Possible reinforcement type in flat slabs 
Even though flat slabs have shown much development since the early 20th century, still it has 
some disadvantages as compared to the modern method of construction i.e. pre casted 
concrete construction which is the most common method of concrete construction in 
developed countries. Precast concrete, a highly efficient, practical method of concrete 
construction makes beautiful buildings possible at a cost that rivals even the most utilitarian 
industrial building. Understanding some of the benefits of precast concrete will assist 
designers in evaluating the impact of precast concrete on the environment and the building 
operation. But so far pre casted are used in structural system where the slabs are carried by 
beams. Therefore, flat slabs keep some of its advantages when compared to the pre casted 
slabs carried by beams, such as flexibility in room layout, ease of installation of mechanical 
and electrical service and saving building height. They are still especially economical when 
building warehouses, parking and industrial structures. 
The major disadvantages of the flat sabs as compared to pre casted concrete is that, it takes 
more time for construction, it is not possible without using of framework, higher cost of 
construction and shorter span. So, the aim of this thesis is to solve the short comings, 
mentioned above, by developing pre casted elements which will form a beam less structural 
system.  
3.3 Pattern of the structural elements in the new beam less structural system. 
Unlike the traditional type of flat slabs which can only be constructed in site, this new system 
will introduce the possibility of flats slabs with fabricated structural elements. In this modern 
time where the pre casting concrete construction is becoming dominant, the ordinary flat slab 
concept is not much in demand. To eliminate this shortcoming, concrete elements will be 
casted in industry which later will be transported and placed in their position to perform this 
new beam less structure. In figure 7 I show the top plan of possible pattern of the elements

4. Detail design of the horizontal structural members. 
Both MD and MPD elements are made of massive concrete C55 (with 55 MPa compressive 
strength) and 25 kN/m^3unit weight. The SL deck is made of both the massive concrete and 
light concrete with a density of 700 kg/m^3. Two type of reinforcements, prestressing and 
slack are used. The steel used for prestressing fpk0,1   of   1860 MPa and the slack reinforcement 
has characteristic yield strength fy,k 0.2 of 550 MPa.  
The analysis and design of each element of this structural system is done for an office load. 
The SL deck elements and the MD elements are analyzed as a one-way slab. This is because 
their length L is greater than twice their width W which makes the load to span along their 
longest side. MPD elements, hence they are square shaped and are loaded from both axis 
they are analyzed as two-way slab. Flexural capacity of all elements is calculated according to 
the linear naiver stress distribution at middle point of their span. Shear stress capacity of each 
elements is calculated for both cases. That is shear capacity of the elements without shear 
reinforcement (when Ved <VRdc) according EN 1991-1-1 (6.2(3)) and shear capacity with shear 
reinforcement (when Ved >VRdc) according EN 1991-1-1 (6.2.3). Finally, their deflection is 
calculated according to Jenson 2012. Each element must fulfill the capacity- and deflection 
control. The capacity control is given in the table below. The capacity control basically 
concerns about the limit state design concept. The cross-section dimensions and their span 
length of each element presented on the sections below, are the ones fulfilling the capacities 
criteria. Hence, all the elements are going to be fabricated in a factory the effect of shrinkage 
is little enough to  be excluded in the calculation of the capacities. But the creep effect of each 
element is included in its deflection control calculation.

4.6 Shear capacity of the SL MD and MPD decks without shear reinforcement 
It is not usual for a slab to contain shear reinforcement, therefore it is only necessary to 
ensure that the concrete shear stress capacity without shear reinforcement (vRd,c=Vud ) is more 
than applied shear stress  at the ends (Ved ),where the shear stress is maximum. In prestressed 
single span members without shear reinforcement, the shear resistance of the regions 
uncracked in bending (where the flexural tensile stress is smaller than fctk,0,05/ γc), the shear 
resistance should be limited by the tensile strength of the concrete. In these regions the shear 
resistance is given by the expression below (according (EN 1991-1-1 section 6.2.2(2)(6.4)). 
VRd,c=(I*bW)/S*√((fctd)^2+α1*σcp*fctd) 
Where 
I    is the second moment of inertia 
bw is the width of the cross-section at the centroidal axis 
S is the first moment of area above and about the centroidal axis 
α1=1 for prestressing cables 
σcp= is the concrete compressive stress at the centroidal axis due to axial loading 
and/or prestressing (in this case prestressing loading) 
As it can be recognized on  the expression above  ,the tension stress of the prestressing cables 
have played  a great role in increasing the  shear capacity  of the decks .The shear capacity of 
SL deck (vRd,c )sl  ,MD (vRd,c )MD,MPD ((vRd,c )MPD  without shear reinforcement is 943kN, 1084 kN 
and 1005 kN respectively. detail calculation is enclosed in Appendix A.

4.8 Deflection control of SL, MD and MPD decks 
Hence the slab respects the inequalities above about the capacity control, it is possible to go 
on with the deflection control. The deflection corresponding to the maximum element 
capacity must be all negative: According Jenson 2012, the description of deflection for 
prestressed simply supported elements is given as follows. 
u0=2/3*(Δup + Δug)<=0 
ul= Δup + Δug+ Δul<=0 
umax= ul + Δuk <=0 
Where: 
u0 = Expected element deflection at delivery 
ul = Resulting long term deflection 
umax=Resulting maximum deflection 
The long-term effect, creep of prestressing cables, is included in deflection control. The 
stiffness reduction related to dead load and prestressing EIk/(1+Фp), this value is 
approximated as EIk/3. And stiffness reduction related to quasi permanent load is also 
approximated as EIk/2,3. 
Δup=1/8*-3*Mbal*L^2/EIk 
Δug=1/10*3*Mg*L^2/EIk 
Δul=1/10*2.3*(Ml-Mg) *L^2/EIk 
Δuk=1/10*1*(Mk-Ml) *L^2/ EIk 
Where L is the longest span of each element and EIk is characteristic flexural stiffness.  
The impact from the total long-term loads should not lead to visible deflection, that is: 
uq=Δup+Δug+Δuq<=0 
The impact from short term loads should respect: 
Δuk<=L/400 
Moreover, in the case in which there is a screed layer above the slab, it should 
also respect the camber rise Cr: 
Cr=-2/3*(Δup+Δug) +50% or2/3*(Δup+Δug)-50%< screed layer thickness (i.e. 0.03 m)

4.9 Design of hinge connections among the elements. 
The blade connections are designed as hinge connections; therefore, they can resist both 
vertical and horizontal reaction force, but it does resist any moment. In this new beam less 
system there are two kinds of blade connections. This classification is mainly based of the 
magnitude of the force transferred through the blades, but the method of the design is the 
same. The design is carried out according to EN-1991-1-1(6,2,3), based on a truss model 
(Figure 6.5) and their development length for enough bondage strength is designed with EN 
1991-1-1(8.42(8.4)) for a straight bar.  
4.9.1 Blades of the H 270 SL deck along MD and MPD decks.

MPD elements. The SL deck contains 8 light concrete blocks alongside its 3 m width and 25

is 600 mm in total. Hence this block is made of the light concrete, to get a better blade design 
the two rows of the blocks along the width are removed and are replaced with strong

tolerance of 20 mm only are designed. All the columns are 2.5 m high and their position is

6.Sustainability and advantages of this new structural system 
Minimum 18 SL deck can be used in this system like the office building above, which accounts 
about 76.9 % of the beam less structural system.  The remaining percentage accounts for the 
MD and MPD. The dominance of the SL deck in the system makes the system more sustainable 
than the traditional flat slabs and pre casted strong concrete construction.  
SL deck which is invented by professor KHZ is one of the most sustainable concrete structural 
elements in our century.  Based on his research: Some of the main advantages, of this new 
beam less structure, due to the inclusion of SL deck is given below. These advantages are close 
to meet UNSDG agreements, concerning the climate issues and the economical use of 
construction materials. 
6.1 Material savings by 50 %. 
The basic idea of super-light structures is to build a skeleton of a strong material interacting 
with a stabilizing layer of a light material. The strong material is made of high-strength 
concrete and the light material is made of light-weight concrete. Light material such as light 
aggregate concrete of density 700 kg/m3 or aerated concrete contains mostly air and a few 
quantities of cheap clay and cement. Therefore, considerable material savings are a 
consequence of the replacement of strong and heavy materials with light ones for a major 
part of the structure. The light material is usually much cheaper than the strong. It fills out 
the shape of the structure and applies the load on the skeleton, which is often curved to be 
optimized. It protects the strong material of the skeleton from impacts and fire, and it 
prevents it from buckling, which typically increases the loadbearing capacity of the skeleton. 
So that with smaller cross section as compared with the traditional concrete technology the 
required resistance capacity can be obtained.by this the material used in construction can be 
reduced by about 50 %. 
6.2 Energy and CO2 savings of 50 %. 
Using the new technology material (SL deck), the energy consumption can be more than 
halved and consequently CO2 emissions from production, transport, and erection can also be 
halved, this makes the SL deck as one of the best ecofriendly construction materials 
==PAGE:48==
                                                                                                                                       Beam less structural system 
38 
6.3 Half price or less. 
By means of super-light structures, engineers can place compressive forces in optimal paths 
and the strong material is fully used. The remaining part of the structure is filled with a cheap 
light material. This is the reason why super-light structures can be made much cheaper than 
traditional structures, where the strong and relatively expensive materials are not confined 
to a minimum.   
6.4 Improved durability. 
Light aggregate concrete does not have the same problems with cracks and frost damages 
etc. as more dense concrete. Modern investigations and more than 2000 years experiences 
with many walls, floors, arches, vaults, cupolas, water basins, quays, bridges, and tunnels 
from Aswan to Scotland document that the material is extremely durable for the weather, 
water, mechanical impact, earthquake, and fire. 
6.5 Extreme thermal insulation. 
SL deck has insulating capacity are almost like wood without problems of rot and fire. This is 
because of it contains large amount of Light-aggregate concrete with a density of only 700 
kg/m3 or less. This light concrete has more thermal insulation than massive concrete. 
Insulation capacity can be further improved by embedding mineral wool or ultra-light blocks 
of aerated concrete of densities 100-150 kg/m3.  
6.6 Better indoor climate. 
Application of light aggregate- or aerated concrete in super-light structures introduces a 
moisture buffer. This absorbs moisture when the air is humid and releases moisture when it 
is dry leading to an improved in-door climate compared to what you experience in traditional 
concrete buildings. 
6.7 User friendly operation and maintenance. 
The SL deck in this new structural system is acting as a ceiling of light-weight concrete, as 
you will find in many super-light buildings, are much easier to drill holes and drive nails in 
than in traditional concrete buildings. Grooves can easily be cut and covered again, and 
electric wires, pipes, and ducts can be inserted and made invisible with a minimum of effort. 
This offers good possibilities for changes, repair and maintenance 
6.8 Increased safety for fire, earthquake, and explosions. 
Light-weight concrete of a super-light structure can be made very fire resistant and can 
protect the strong skeleton. If the skeleton is made of high-strength concrete, it is enough to 
apply 60 mm light-aggregate concrete protection of density 700 kg/m3 in order to protect it 
from most fires. 
Light-weight concrete is capable of absorbing energy from mechanical impact and 
deformations. This is the reason why the materials have proved to be beneficial for crash 
barriers. Super-light structures are therefore capable of damping the effect of impact and 
shakings from explosions and earthquakes increasing the safety for people in buildings made 
from it. 
This advantage of the SL decks makes the new beam less structural system more economical, 
sustainable and safe.

6.9 Its advantage over traditional beam less structures 
The new brand beam less structural system keeps all the advantage of the traditional beam 
less structural system over in situ slabs carried by beam, which are listed in section (2) above. 
In addition to that, this new system will dramatically reduce the time of the construction 
because they are casted in the factory ,it has better control during fabrication ,so that the 
error due to workmanship at the site decreases which increase the safety of the system. Also 
due to the inclusion of the SL deck, as it is explained above, in the system the fire resistance 
capacity increase, amount of construction material used decrease which makes the building 
more economical. 
6.10 Its advantage over pre casted slabs carried by beams. 
In addition to some advantages listed in section (2) like simplification under floor service, 
flexibility in room lay out and saving building height  ,this new beam less structural system 
has  higher fire resistance capacity as compared with pre casted slabs carried by beams  ,one 
is due to the fact that flat slabs has higher fire resistance capacity, secondly due to the 
inclusion of high fire resistant element, SL decks ,in the system . Thirdly with smaller cross 
section as compared with the traditional concrete technology the required resistance capacity 
can be obtained by SL decks. By this the material used in construction can be reduced by half 
percentage. Thus, the system is more economical than pre casted slabs carried by pre casted 
beams especially for offices, parking, warehouse and industrial buildings. 
7.Conclusion 
With this new concept of beam less structures systems, I believe that, some short comings of the old 
beam less structural system(which is mostly called flat slabs) ,Like the use of framework during 
construction which will result higher cost  and time of construction , will be avoided. So that flat 
slabs will be more competitive than the pre casted slabs carried by beams for all type of building. 
Especially for warehouses, offices, parking and other similar buildings. 
8.Future work 
So far, the static office load has been taken into considerations. In the future to ensure its 
dynamic stability, the analysis and design must be revised for both wind and seismic loadings. 
Especially the bade connections must checked with their dimensions concerning seismic 
loading. The wind load can be distributed to the columns. This can be obtained by transferring 
the wind load from the façade surface to the slabs with the help of designed connections 
which then can divided to each column equally. Furthermore, to include the 3D effect of all 
loadings Finite elements program can be used to optimize the dimensions and design of each 
elements. 
Also, the fire load effect will be taken into consideration according the demands from the 
authority. For better distribution of electrical and mechanical service in the building system, 
U shaped columns can a be designed .
