Summary (max. 2000 characters): 
In the process of characterization of materials, strain gauges are the most 
utilized measurement devices. These measurements are generally accepted 
as highly reliable. 
Strain gauges measure the strain through the change in electrical resistance 
of an approximately 5 µm thick grid of Constatan printed on a 45 µm 
Polyamide foil. The 180 GPa elastic modulus of the Constatan alloy has an 
elastic behaviour that is different from the tested material, and for this reason 
the gauges are commercialized with a correction factor calibrated on a steel 
specimen. 
When applying a strain gauge on a more compliant material, with elastic 
modulus in the range 4-75 GPa, the measured strains are found to be lower 
compared to other measurement techniques. Therefore, this phenomenon is 
so called reinforcement effect. 
In this thesis, the impact of geometrical parameters on reinforcement effect is 
studied. Moreover, the behaviour of an improved design is simulated. 
Through usage of FEM simulation the factors examined are the end-loops 
geometry, the grid length, the width and the thickness of the wires. The 
thickness of the specimen is also considered, and the presence of superficial 
coatings is modelled. 
The results provide a complete understanding of the impact of each 
parameter, and show a reduction of the error from 5% - 10% of the existing 
models, in the 4-20 GPa range, to less than 1% when using the proposed 
design in the 4-200 GPa range. This demonstrates that it is possible to 
achieve better performances on materials with relatively low elastic modulus. 
Furthermore, the models were validate with experiments and agreements 
between the FEM models and the experimental data were found.

DTU Wind Energy is a department of the Technical University of Denmark with a unique integration of research, education, innovation and 
public/private sector consulting in the field of wind energy. Our activities develop new opportunities and technology for the global and Danish 
exploitation of wind energy. Research focuses on key technical-scientific fields, which are central for the development, innovation and use of wind 
energy and provides the basis for advanced education at the education. 
We have more than 240 staff members of which approximately 60 are PhD students. Research is conducted within nine research programmes 
organized into three main topics: Wind energy systems, Wind turbine technology and Basics for wind energy.
