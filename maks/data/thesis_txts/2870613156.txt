Abstract
Mental health professionals face significant time constraints due to extensive documen­
tation requirements, often spending up to 30% [1] [2] of their time on session documen­
tation rather than patient care. This thesis presents an innovative AI­powered solution
that automates and streamlines the therapy documentation process. The system uti­
lizes advanced speech recognition and natural language processing to transcribe therapy
sessions and automatically generate structured clinical documentation while maintaining
therapeutic integrity and professional standards. The project takes into account both tech­
nical implementation and entrepreneurial development. On the technical side, it demon­
strates a functional MVP built using modern web technologies (SvelteKit, FastAPI) and
AI components for transcription (Whisper) and journal generation (Large Language Mod­
els (DeepSeek)). The entrepreneurial aspect includes comprehensive market research,
competitive analysis, and the development of a sustainable business model targeting the
mental healthcare sector. The thesis evaluates the system’s effectiveness in reducing
the documentation workload while maintaining high­quality clinical records through qual­
itative and quantitative feedback from mental health professionals and potential users.
The research also addresses important considerations regarding privacy, ethics, and the
integration of AI in mental healthcare settings. The results indicate that the solution can
significantly reduce documentation time while maintaining or improving documentation
quality and consistency, potentially allowing therapists to increase their patient care ca­
pacity. The thesis concludes by discussing scalability prospects and future development
opportunities within the mental health technology sector.

Introduction
1.1
Problem Statement
Mental health professionals today face a critical challenge in balancing their core thera­
peutic responsibilities with increasingly demanding documentation requirements. Current
research indicates that therapists spend up to 30% of their working hours on administra­
tive documentation, significantly reducing the time available for patient care [3] [4] [5].
This administrative burden creates several interconnected problems:
• Reduced therapeutic time, leading to decreased patient access to care
• Increased risk of documentation errors due to time pressure
• Rising burnout rates among mental health professionals
• Potential degradation of care quality due to delayed or rushed documentation
The emergence of advanced AI technologies, particularly in natural language processing
and speech recognition, presents an opportunity to address these challenges. However,
implementing AI solutions in healthcare requires careful consideration of technical feasi­
bility, market viability, and business sustainability while ensuring compliance with stringent
healthcare regulations and privacy requirements.
1.2
Project Context: Healper Platform
This thesis is developed in collaboration with Healper, a Danish mental healthcare plat­
form that provides comprehensive practice management solutions for mental health pro­
fessionals. Healper’s current platform offers:
• Practice Management System: Tools for scheduling, client management, and ad­
ministrative tasks
• Client Portal: Secure interface for client communication and resource sharing
• Documentation System: Basic journaling and record­keeping capabilities
• Billing Integration: Automated billing and insurance processing
• GDPR­Compliant Infrastructure: Secure data handling meeting European health­
care standards
While Healper successfully addresses many administrative aspects of mental health prac­
tice, the platform identifies documentation efficiency as a key area for enhancement. The
current documentation system, while functional, requires manual input from therapists,
contributing to the administrative burden discussed in the problem statement. This collab­
oration aims to develop an AI­powered documentation solution that integrates seamlessly
with Healper’s existing platform, enhancing their service offering while maintaining their
high standards for security and user experience.
1.3
Research Question
This thesis investigates the central question: “How can AI technology be effectively im­
plemented to improve mental health documentation while maintaining a viable business
model?” This overarching question encompasses several key sub­questions:

1.4
Methodology
This research employs a mixed­methods approach to comprehensively assess the feasi­
bility of an AI­powered mental health documentation solution. The methodology is struc­
tured around three primary areas of investigation: market analysis, business analysis,
and technical development.
1.4.1
Market Analysis
The market analysis phase begins with a comprehensive literature review examining cur­
rent trends in mental health technology and documentation practices. This is comple­
mented by a detailed competitive analysis of existing solutions in the market, with partic­
ular attention to their strengths, weaknesses, and market positioning. Primary research
through practitioner and client surveys provides direct insights into user needs, prefer­
ences and willingness to use such a product, while a thorough analysis of regulatory and
compliance requirements ensures the solution’s viability within the healthcare sector.
1.4.2
Business Analysis
The business analysis component employs the Business Model Canvas framework to
systematically evaluate and develop the solution’s commercial viability. This includes
detailed financial modelling and projections for potential revenue streams and cost struc­
tures. Risk assessment and mitigation planning are conducted to identify and address
potential challenges, while a comprehensive go­to­market strategy is developed to guide
market entry and expansion.
1.4.3
Technical Development
The technical development methodology follows an iterative approach, beginning with de­
tailed requirements gathering and system design. This feeds into the development of a
Minimum Viable Product (MVP), incorporating continuous testing and refinement. Per­
formance evaluation is conducted throughout the development process, with particular
emphasis on security and compliance implementation to meet healthcare industry stan­
dards.

1.5
Scope and Limitations
This thesis focuses on developing and evaluating a minimum viable product (MVP), de­
signed so it can then be integrated into the Healper platform. This strategic partnership
provides both opportunities and constraints for the project, as the solution must align with
Healper’s existing infrastructure, user base, and quality standards.
1.5.1
Technical Scope
The technical implementation encompasses the development of a modern web applica­
tion architecture. A front­end application is created to showcase the use case of the
service, a back­end service is implemented to host the generative capabilities of the ap­
plication, and a database is hosted to persist data. While development was done locally,
initial efforts were completed to containerize each application, paving the way for future
cloud deployment.
1.5.2
Business Scope
The business scope focuses primarily on the European mental health sector, with par­
ticular emphasis on markets where Healper has established a presence. The business
model development leverages Healper’s existing user base and distribution channels,
while the integration strategy ensures optimal compatibility with their current service of­
ferings. Special attention is paid to regulatory compliance, building upon Healper’s ex­
isting GDPR­compliant infrastructure. The scope includes development of a sustainable
revenue sharing and partnership model that aligns incentives between all stakeholders.
1.5.3
Limitations
The project operates within several defined constraints. The initial implementation is lim­
ited to English­language therapy sessions, though the architecture is designed to accom­
modate future language expansion. The system is currently optimized for standard ther­
apy session formats, which may not encompass all specialized therapeutic approaches.
The main technical limitation is the project’s reliance on the accuracy of open­sources
AI services for core functionality. Another limitation is the hardware used; an RTX 3070
with 8 GBs of VRAM is used for generation, while all models fit into its capabilities, LLM
outputs can be limited due to memory constraints, especially when loaded alongside the
transcription model. These limitations, while significant, do not impede the project’s ability
to demonstrate proof of concept and provide valuable insights for future development.
This research aims to provide a comprehensive understanding of the market, business,
and technical requirements for successfully implementing AI technology in mental health
documentation while maintaining a focus on practical implementation and real­world via­
bility.

2.1
Market Overview and Size
The mental health technology sector is undergoing a significant transformation, driven by
the integration of artificial intelligence and increasing demand for digital health solutions.
The global online therapy market is projected to grow by $64.1 billion by 2029, with AI­
powered documentation solutions representing a crucial segment of this expansion [6].
This growth is primarily driven by:
2.1.1
Market Drivers
The increasing adoption of AI technologies in healthcare has created favorable conditions
for innovative documentation solutions. Key market drivers include:
• Growing administrative burden on mental health professionals
• Increased acceptance of AI technologies in healthcare settings
• Rising demand for efficient documentation solutions
• Push for improved work­life balance among healthcare providers
• Regulatory pressure for comprehensive documentation
2.1.2
Current Market Trends
Analysis of current market trends reveals several key developments relevant to the pro­
posed solution:
AI Integration in Healthcare
The integration of AI in mental healthcare has accelerated, with particular emphasis on
documentation and administrative tasks. Advanced features including emotional analysis,
mood tracking, and integration with physiological monitoring have become increasingly
common [7] [8].
Hybrid Service Models
A significant trend is the emergence of hybrid therapy models that combine AI­enhanced
documentation with traditional therapeutic practices. This approach has shown promise
in improving information sharing and treatment personalization [7] [9].
Security and Compliance Focus
The market demonstrates strong preference for solutions with robust security measures
and regulatory compliance, particularly regarding HIPAA and GDPR standards [7] [10].
2.2
Market Need Analysis
2.2.1
Current Market Gaps
Analysis of existing solutions reveals several unfulfilled market needs:
Integration Limitations
Current solutions often operate as standalone products, lacking integration with broader
practice management systems. Platforms like Mentalyc [11] and AutoNotes [12] offer
automated documentation but function independently of other practice management tools.
Feature Completeness
Existing solutions typically excel in specific areas but lack comprehensive functionality.
For example, Reflectly focuses on mood tracking but lacks therapeutic integration, while
BetterHelp’s system is limited to their proprietary platform.

2.5
Market Risks and Mitigation
The implementation of AI­powered documentation solutions in therapeutic practice presents
several significant market risks that require consideration and strategic mitigation ap­
proaches.
2.5.1
Adoption Barriers cite
A significant market risk lies in potential resistance from practitioners regarding the adop­
tion of AI technology in their clinical practice. This resistance typically stems from con­
cerns about technology reliability, impact on therapeutic relationships, and disruption to
established workflows [17] [18]. Research in healthcare technology adoption indicates
that practitioners often exhibit cautionary attitudes toward AI integration in clinical settings,
particularly in mental health services where the therapeutic relationship is paramount.

To address these adoption challenges, a comprehensive practitioner onboarding strategy
should be implemented. This approach includes developing training programs that show­

introduction trial periods allow users to experience the system’s benefits while allowing

Market Competition
The potential entry of large technology companies into the therapeutic documentation

strategic positioning and specialized feature development.

with the existing Healper platform. This integration provides a significant competitive bar­
rier through established relationships with practitioners and an intimate understanding of
therapeutic workflows. Furthermore, we will continue developing specialized features that
address specific needs within the therapeutic community, creating a level of customization
that would be difficult for larger, more generalized competitors to replicate effectively.
2.5.3
Regulatory Changes
The evolving landscape of healthcare data protection regulations presents an ongoing
risk to market operations. Healthcare technology solutions must navigate complex and
sometimes conflicting regulatory requirements across different jurisdictions, particularly
concerning patient data privacy and AI implementation in healthcare settings.
To address these regulatory challenges, we have designed our solution with a flexible ar­
chitecture that can adapt to changing compliance requirements. This approach includes
regular compliance audits and updates to ensure alignment with evolving regulations. Our
system architecture allows for modular updates to security protocols and data handling
procedures, enabling rapid response to regulatory changes while maintaining service con­
tinuity.

Primary research conducted with potential users validates the market need for improved

Current State Analysis
4.1.1
Documentation Technology Landscape
The current therapeutic documentation landscape encompasses various technological
approaches, from traditional manual methods to emerging AI­assisted solutions. Elec­
tronic Health Records (EHR) systems have established the foundation for digital docu­
mentation in healthcare, while voice recognition technology has begun transforming data
entry processes. However, existing solutions often lack integration capabilities with prac­
tice management platforms, creating inefficiencies in workflow management.
4.1.2
AI Technology Assessment
Recent and continuous advancements in AI, particularly in speech recognition and Large
Language Models(LLMs), have created new possibilities for automation in healthcare doc­
umentation. Current speech recognition systems achieve accuracy rates above 90% in
long­form contexts, while LLMs have enabled structured data extraction from unstruc­
tured conversations. Many open­source solutions exist for both transcription and LLM
tasks, this allows us to compare these models and find the right fit when taking into ac­
count accuracy and costs.
However, the challenge lies in maintaining this accuracy while handling the nuanced na­
ture of therapeutic interactions.
4.2
Technical Stack Evaluation
4.2.1
Frontend Framework Analysis
The evaluation of frontend technologies focused on frameworks compatible with Healper’s
existing infrastructure while maintaining development efficiency and performance. While
Healper is currently using Sveltekit as its primary front­end framework, other frameworks
should still be considered.
A comparative analysis of current market­leading frameworks—React, Angular, Vue, and
Svelte—reveals distinct strengths tailored to different use cases. React and Angular dom­
inate large enterprise­grade projects due to their extensive ecosystems, comprehensive
features, and ability to support large teams, albeit with a substantial learning curve. On
the other hand, Vue and Svelte excel in small to medium­sized applications, offering ease
of use, developer­friendly features, and suitability for smaller teams. While React boasts
a larger ecosystem and Angular provides robust enterprise capabilities, SvelteKit’s align­
ment with Healper’s architecture and its development speed characteristics make it the
most suitable choice for this specific implementation.

was selected over alternatives based on several key factors:
• Python compatibility, enabling potential future migration to Django (Healper’s pri­
mary framework)
• Built­in async support crucial for real­time audio processing

4.6.1
Primary Technical Risks
The implementation framework faces four primary technical challenges that require con­

cerns, as the architecture demonstrates reduced performance when processing edge
cases that were not accounted for in the design. This is the main concern and should

performance degradation is particularly evident when processing non­standard speech
patterns, environmental noise, or specialized terminology which wasn’t included in train­

as empirical evidence suggests that concurrent processing of high­volume audio submis­
sions during business hours (10:00­14:00 local time) may exceed allocated computational
resources, particularly given the requirement to process multiple 45­60 minute audio files
per user daily.
Lastly, maintaining data security while ensuring continuous regulatory compliance re­
quires organization level planning as processing such sensitive data is the core value
of the solution.

5.4.1
Data Models
PocketBase is configured with custom collections as the foundation of the application’s

The core authentication collection, Users, stores credentials and basic profile information,

To facilitate front­end queries, the schema includes a UI Journal Table, which is a view
rather than a physical collection, aggregating data from multiple collections into a simpli­
fied structure, which is used by the dashboard page.
The schema implements a comprehensive set of relationships that maintain data integrity
throughout the application. Users can be associated with either therapist or patient pro­
files, or potentially both. Therapists maintain relationships with their assigned patients,
while sessions connect patients with their therapists at specific time points. Clinical data
in the form of journals and transcriptions are attached to specific sessions, creating a
complete record of therapeutic interactions.
This relational structure enables efficient querying of patient histories, therapist caseloads,
and session­specific clinical information while maintaining appropriate access controls
through PocketBase’s rule system.
