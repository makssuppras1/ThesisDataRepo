Summary 
Sea transportation contributes significantly to Chinese air pollution. The environment 
friendly dimethyl ether (DME) fuel is produced today from coal. Alternative feedstock 
biomass and municipal solid waste can be used to substitute coal as there are technologies 
available to treat these feedstocks. The selection of gasifier is mostly dependent on 
feedstock accessibility in the province. In order to allocate suitable provinces to place 
dimethyl ether (DME)  plants, different maps of cropland, marginal land, forestry, 
municipal solid waste, and coal reserves are studied in combination with a map over wind 
power availability and installation. The results are five scenarios of provinces based on 
feedstock of (i) coal, (ii) coal replaced by biomass, (iii) coal replaced by municipal solid 
waste, (iv) biomass, and (v) municipal solid waste. Thus, best ranked province candidates 
are Inner Mongolia, Shandong, Shanxi, Zhejiang, and Guangdong. A case study of a coal 
retrofitted DME plant located in Shanxi province using three types of feedstock and 
different technologies will generate six feedstock scenarios, (1) coal, (2) coal with 
Electrolysis Plant, (3) dry bamboo with Bamboo Charcoal Plant, (4) dry bamboo with 
Electrolysis Plant and Bamboo Charcoal Plant, (5) bamboo charcoal and, (6) bamboo 
charcoal with Electrolysis plant. It turns out scenario 1, 2 and , 3 has the lowest cost and 
the DME price obtained will also be lowest for these scenarios. Electrolysis electricity cost 
dominates amongst cost parameters such as input material cost, input material transport 
cost, DME transport cost, Electrolysis plant investment, Electrolysis Plant O&M cost, 
Bamboo Charcoal plant investment and, Bamboo Charcoal plant Operations and 
Management (O&M) cost, in scenario 2, 4 and, 6. Scenario 1, 3 and 5 have lower DME price 
compared to their pair scenarios and it is possible for scenario 2, 4, and 6 to achieve same 
low DME price by adjusting the electrolysis electricity price.

1. Introduction 
Nowadays, air pollution is a concern amongst industrialized and undeveloped countries. 
Amidst 10 of the world’s biggest container ports, 7 of them are located in China, which is 
responsible for a quarter of world containers in 20151. For context, in 2015, 3,75 trillion 
ton kilometers of freight passed through Chinese waters, 10 721 of coastal vessels and 
152 500 of river vessels, not including the large fleet of 694 900 motorized fishing vessels. 
These marine engines operate on diesel but also bunker fuel, which emits carbon dioxide 
(CO2), nitrogen oxides (NOX), sulfur oxides (SOX) and particulate matter (PM). In 2017 
China consumed roughly 174 million tons diesel2 which is 7,9 EJ by using heating value of 
45,6 megajoule per kilogram (MJ/kg)3. Bunker fuel has a relatively small domestic marine 
market in China with production around 20 million tons annually4,5. By using the heating 
value of 45,9 MJ/kg, the energy generated from annual bunker fuel is 0,92 exajoule (EJ).  
 
In 2013, according to China’s Ministry of Environmental Protection (MEP), approximately 
11,3% nitrogen oxides (NOX) and 8,4% of sulfur oxides (SOX) were emitted from marine 
engines. In 2014, emission attributed to navigation corresponded to 33% nitrogen oxides 
(NOX) and 44% of sulfur oxides (SOX) in Hongkong. River and coastal vessels at the port 
of Shanghai contributed to nearly 55% nitrogen oxides (NOX) and 15% of sulfur oxides 
(SOX). New standards to improve the air quality, where registered marine vessels 
operating in Chinese territorial waters are subjected to the emission standards: China I 
standard by 2018 and China II standard by 2021, are introduced in Appendix A. Another 
initiative by China is to expand the Domestic Emission Control Areas (DECA) beyond the 
previous three regions of Pearl River Delta region, Yangtze River Delta region and Bohai 
Rim region, to the coastal waters as shown in Appendix B. 
 
Legislations regarding vessel and engine emission are ways to improve the air quality of 
China. The background of China’s current air situation can be related to its energy 
resource. Compared to the western countries, after the discovery of petroleum, the usage 
of coal declined and was replaced. However, China is rich in coal reserved, scarce in 
petroleum and lean for natural gas. Coal became a trend during the rapid economic 
growth over the past three decades, making China the top coal producer and consumer6. 
Coal is often associated with black and dirty since it is rich in carbon. To put coal 
consumption into perspective, while generating the same amount of energy, it will emit 
more carbon dioxide (CO2) than petroleum and natural gas. Other applications besides 
fuel for power plants are liquid fuel, domestic and industrial heating, synthesis of 
ammonia, chemicals derived from coal tars, calcium carbide and coke oven gases.  
 
A promising alternative clean and green fuel is dimethyl ether (DME) in the shipping 
industry. Heavy fuel oil (HFO) used in marine diesel vessels are now more restricted by 
the legislations due to pollutant emission. Alternative fuels that have been considered as 
candidates are natural gas (LNG), propane (LPG), ethane (LEG) and methane. Dimethyl 
ether (DME) possesses better quality than diesel from an environmental perspective by 
emitting less pollutants7.  
 
Gasification today mainly uses coal as a feedstock8. The syngas of carbon monoxide (CO) 
and hydrogen (H2) is only an intermediate which will be utilized to synthesis dimethyl 
ether (DME) through two methods: direct (one step) and indirect (two step) method. 
Current Chinese facilities are mainly based on the two step method where coal first 
produces methanol and is followed by dimethyl ether (DME) production through

against coal, as the more significant advantage is infinite availability. Furthermore, 
biomass has lower cost compared to other energy resources. However, biomass is faced 
with particular challenges in order to be introduced on a commercial scale due to its low 
energy density and tar formation12. As a result of China’s economic advancement: rising

be taken into consideration during the production of biofuel13. The same is applied for 
considering municipal solid waste (MSW) as an alternative feedstock solution. Enerkem 
has introduced a new thermochemical technology using municipal solid waste as 
feedstock to produce methanol14. 
 
1.1 Energy in China 
China became the largest energy consumer in 2011 with a fast growing economy which 
has led to growing energy demand, especially for petroleum and other fuels. The Energy 
Information Administration (EIA)15 informed that China has surpassed the United States 
by the end of 2013 as the world’s largest net importer of petroleum and other liquids. In 
2014 China accounted for 43% of the world’s oil consumption growth. As a new 
leadership emerged in China in 2013, when Xi Jinping became president and Li Keqiang 
premiership, initiatives were taken by the new administration during the major policy 
meeting held every five years. China will shift away from economy driven export and 
invest in economy characterized by domestic consumption by slowly implementing 
policies and reforms. Concerning the energy sector, the government has decided to base 
the energy pricing schemes on the market, and put more effort into energy efficiency and 
pollution control actions by investing more in renewable energy projects.  
 
The energy consumption in China increased from 1% in 2016 to 3,1% in 2017. Biofuel 
production in 2017 in thousand ton oil equivalent has grown with 18,8%16. The Chinese 
government aims to cap the coal consumption from 66% in 2012 to 62% by 2020 by 
diversifying energy sources and achieve reduced emission of greenhouse gases (GHG) and 
other air pollutants. Another set target was to increase non-fossil fuel energy 
consumption to 15% by 202015. Judging by Figure 1 a), coal contributed to 61% of China’s 
total energy consumption, while renewable energy is next to last with 3%. It means that 
China has achieved the goal of reducing coal consumption ahead of schedule rather than 
by 2020. Oil consumption of 19% has the highest percentage second coal, followed by 
hydroelectricity and natural gas of respectively 8% and 7% in 2018. Nuclear energy had 
the lowest consumption percentage of 2%16.

2. DME production technology 
Dimethyl ether (DME) production consists of two main configurations and are namely (1) 
gasification and (2) dimethyl ether (DME) synthesis, and are commercially available in 
China. Gasification can be divided into five distinct thermal processes of drying, pyrolysis, 
cracking, combustion, and reduction under varying temperatures. Reduction is the most 
relevant chemical reaction, which will reduce carbon dioxide (CO2) and steam (H2O) into 
syngas (CO and H2). In addition, the equilibrium of water-gas shift reaction also takes 
place simultaneously, which will control the gas phase inside the gasifier. The five 
gasification processes are always occurring inside a gasifier, separated into zones in the 
fixed/moving bed gasifier and combined inside the gasification vessel in the fluidized and 
entrained bed gasifier. These gasifiers are each suitable for different sizes of feedstock 
and are each commercially available and utilized in China. However, it should be 
mentioned that study recommends the fixed and fluidized bed gasifier more due to the 
pretreatment required for entrained bed gasifier.  
 
There are two methods to synthesis dimethyl ether (DME): the direct process would be 
direct synthesis of dimethyl ether (DME)  through using a combined catalyst fit for both 
methanol synthesis and methanol dehydration. No requirement is needed for isolation or 
purification of methanol, while the indirect process will separate methanol synthesis and 
dehydration into two different steps. On the contrary to common belief, the indirect 
process is more commercialized as it is easy to start and less expensive.

2.1 Gasification process in five steps 
Chocked combustion or incomplete combustion are alternative names of gasification17. It 
is defined as reaction between solid fuel with air, carbon dioxide (CO2), oxygen, steam, or 
a mixture of these gases in a reduced environment where the air: oxygen ration is strictly 
controlled at a temperature exceeding 800˚C. A disadvantage of air is the low product 
quality of gas since it will be diluted with nitrogen. On the contrary, oxygen rich air will 
produce high quality syngas while the consequence is heavy power consumption in the 
generator.  
 
The final gaseous products are of two types: (1) output gas which can be used as an energy 
source since combustion potential remains, or (2) production of chemicals, liquid fuels, 
or other gaseous fuels where the unburned gas will act as raw material (syngas). The 
definition of syngas is the primary mixture consisting of carbon monoxide and hydrogen 
(CO and H2)8. A noteworthy remark about the product difference between combustion 
and gasification is listed in Table 2. The products of gasification are primarily carbon 
monoxide (CO) and hydrogen (H2) with traces of hydrogen cyanide (HCN), ammonia (NH3) 
or nitrogen (N2) and hydrogen sulfide (H2S) or carbonyl sulfide (COS), in comparison to 
carbon dioxide (CO2) and water (H20) with traces of nitrogen oxide (NOx) and sulfur oxide 
(SOX) derived from combustion.

In addition, most of the solid fuel’s chemical energy still remains with the syngas, whereas 
during combustion, all feedstock energy is converted to heat energy. This indicates that 
the heat derived from the gasification process is low, and in the case of energy transport 
is not limited, the temperature to self-sustain the gasification will drop below the 
threshold temperature18. Thus, the gasification process is much dependent on the heat 
and pressure to break the chemical bonds within the molecular structure but also the coal 
type and configuration of the gasifier8.  
 
Gasification can be described at staged combustion, where a series of specific thermal 
events are aligned in order to convert carbonaceous material, i.e., coal into gaseous 
products. For understanding gasification better, the five distinct thermal processes, 
according to Figure 3. are the following: Drying, Pyrolysis, Combustion, Cracking, and 
Reduction, whereas, during gasification, it is possible to isolate each process to interrupt 
the “flame”17.

The drying process removes moisture physically bound to untreated coal where the heat 
supply can be hot exhaust gaseous mixtures (i.e., raw syngas)19 before entering the 
gasifier in industrial production20. Furthermore, coal with excessive moisture content 
may have an adverse effect on producing clean gas17. The pyrolysis process occurs in the 
absence of air, where the volatiles are removed for production of tars (liquid and gases), 
and what remains is namely charcoal. At a temperature between 200-300 ˚C,  mild 
pyrolysis, which is also named torrefraction is highly beneficial for handling of biomass, 
as it alters the properties of biomass by increasing energy density, improved 
grindability/pulverization, better palletization behavior and higher resistance to 
biodegradation and spontaneous heating. Thus, it is possible to achieve similar properties 
to coal21.  Current pellet plant in China has the capacity of around 13000 ton/a22, biomass 
briquetting plant has capacity between 1000 – 3000 ton/a 23,24 in which the numbers are 
from line production. The other method to substitute coal with biomass is to design a 
gasifier dedicated to biomass.

gas that is compatible with internal combustion engines as tar gas can condense and foul 
the engine valves17. Combustion is an exothermic process that can provide heat for the

2.2.2 Fluidized bed gasifier 
The fluidized bed gasifier is an assembly of cylindrical and vertical vessels with a cyclone

filled with sand that is fluidized by the gasification media injected at the bottom, according

enter the vessel and circulate the fluidized bed (sand), which is then captured in the

a counter-current gasifier, and the outputs are carbon monoxide (CO), hydrogen (H2),

2.4 Synthesis of DME  
Dimethyl ether (DME) is an organic compound30 with the chemical formula of CH3OCH3 
(C2H6O)31 and is a colorless gas at ambient conditions with a faint ethereal odor. It has a 
vapor pressure around approximately 0,6 MPa at 25˚C32 which means dimethyl ether 
(DME) can easily be shipped as a liquified gas under light pressure. Once inhaled, the 
dimethyl ether (DME)  can asphyxiate by displacement of air, and any skin contact with 
the liquid can cause frostbite. It is an extremely flammable gas31.  
 
Dimethyl ether (DME) can be produced from various feedstocks beside coal, biomass, 
municipal solid waste (MSW), and they are natural gas, methane, carbon dioxide, and 
crude oil33. There is a multi-use market for this as an aerosol propellant, gas turbines, 
cooking, and heating and engine fuel. Dimethyl ether (DME) can substitute Liquefied 
Natural Gas (LNG) as they have similar emission and operation parameters to natural gas 
but 1,6-2,8% higher efficiency. At the same time, dimethyl ether (DME) also has similar 
properties to Liquified Petroleum Gas (LPG), and hence 25-30% liquified petroleum gas 
(LPG) can be substituted by dimethyl ether (DME) without any modification as a cooking 
heater. Dimethyl ether (DME)  can be transported using technologies developed for 
liquified petroleum gas (LPG) storage and transportation with minor modifications to 
gaskets, seals, and pumps. Diesel engine (CI engine - Compression Ignition) operated on 
dimethyl ether (DME) emits no smoke, little particles from lubricant oil, nitrogen oxide 
(NOx) reduction (84%) and no sulfur oxide (SO2) emission. Dimethyl ether (DME) is easier 
to ignite when injected into the cylinder due to its high cetane number, which makes it fit 
==PAGE:23==
 
13 
 
for CI engines. In comparison, CI engine has about 30% better fuel economy than SI (Spark 
Ignition) engine34. Another significant advantage is that after a couple of hours, dimethyl 
ether (DME) will decompose in the troposphere and will not contribute to ozone 
depletion32.  
 
Even though dimethyl ether (DME)  has many advantages, it is still not as well-known as 
other fuels. Perhaps it is due to one of the following characteristics34: 
 
• Dimethyl ether (DME) is aggressive towards rubber and plastic 
 
• Increased wear in fuel system due to low lubricity of dimethyl ether (DME)

Depending on the temperature and pressure, hydrogen may occur in various states, which 
can be seen from Figure 14. Hydrogen appears as a gas at high temperature and will turn 
into solid at low temperatures. The range of liquid hydrogen is illustrated by the narrow 
zone between the Critical point and the Triple point where the density is 70,8 kg/m3 at -
253 ˚C. Storage of hydrogen usually refers to reduction of the enormous volume of 
hydrogen gas43.

An interesting solution to large scale storage is the underground hydrogen storage, which 
requires a specific geological structure: aquifer, depleted deposits of natural gas and oil, 
salt caverns. This solution may become an economically feasible solution using a surplus 
of electric energy generated from renewable resources. However, this is not close enough 
to justify planning and to build underground hydrogen storage. Besides, geological 
conditions, electric energy should reach amounts of tens of terra watt-hours (tWh) from 
intermittent suppliers of renewable sources, have a variable price of electrical energy 
during a year43.  
 
The two main types of storing hydrogen are: in porous rocks and in artificial underground 
spaces (caverns). Gas storage underground is a facility artificially created at several 
hundred meters or more underground. Inside the facility roams the working gas (injected 
and withdrawn hydrogen) and cushion gas, which prevents the inflow of water by 
exercising minimum pressure. There are several advantages of underground gas storage 
facilities: (i) safety of storage, (ii) space management, and (iii) cheaper compared to 
surface facilities. Throughout history, the successfully stored hydrogen, they are located 
at Teesside in the UK (since 1972), storing 1 million m3 hydrogen and at Clemons salt 
dome in Texas (since the 1980s) storing 30 million m3 hydrogen. The volume of a single 
salt cavern can vary between 150 000 – 800 000 m3. 
 
3.3.1 Storage in aquifer 
When a formation is of permeable and porous rocks, whereas the pores are occupied by 
fresh or saline water, they are also named aquifer. Aquifer exist more commonly in all 
sedimentary basins compared to the availability of depleted hydrocarbon or salt caverns. 
Often, they are located close to large cities and urban agglomerations43. 
 
The condition of the aquifer is similar to that in depleted oil and gas deposition. Firstly, 
the reservoir must be covered by an impermeable trap of rocks that will prevent gas from 
migrating, and secondly, the rocks selected to store hydrogen must be permeable. Water-
filled pores will be displaced by gas downwards and sideways. How much storage is 
available depends on the porosity, permeability, pressure(which changes during injection 
and withdrawal of the gas), and temperature.  
 
The disadvantages of hydrogen in the aquifer are amongst others: leakage along 
undetected faults, biochemical reaction, or reaction between hydrogen and the minerals 
in the reservoir rock. On the contrary, leakage to the surface is minimal, and no ignition is 
==PAGE:29==
 
19 
 
possible underground due to lack of oxygen. Aquifers are economically more costly 
compared to depleted oil and gas deposits, as it has never been explored before and will 
require drilling of exploration wells to determine the formation. 
 
3.3.2 Storage in depleted oil and gas deposits 
Oil and gas deposits in reservoirs, in the pores or i.e., pores of sandstone and chalk. It is 
sealed above and has an aquifer below. The rocks that seals the trap are of low 
permeability, which will prevent any kind of migration43.  
 
Nowadays, gas deposits are typically used as storage sites since the deposit is usually 
equipped with necessary surface and subsurface installations and can be adapted to 
underground hydrogen storage. In this case, the cost can be reduced.  
During an evaluation of the feasibility of the storage site, several criteria have to be met, 
which included the evaluation of the borehole to surface installations, etc. The main 
criteria the safety of the storage.  
 
In comparison, oil deposit sites are not often converted to an underground hydrogen 
storage facility as the residual oil may enter into chemical reaction with hydrogen and 
form i.e., methane dissolved in oil, which is irreversible. 
 
3.3.3 Storage in salt caverns 
Artificial chambers created in salt deposits are also named salt caverns,-bed or -dome. It 
is perfectly equipped for storing gases under high pressure. The low permeability and 
chemical reactions are most favorable for the storage of hydrogen. When selecting a salt 
deposit, the following should be considered: form, depth, thickness composition, and 
distribution(will affect permeability) of rock in the reservoir43. 
 
3.4 Storage of oxygen (O2) 
The storage of oxygen can happen through various methods, and the two most mentioned 
ones are high pressure and cryogenic44. At high pressure of 200 bars, the oxygen can be 
stored in compressed tanks of various volume capacities for any application45. In a 
cryogenic tank, the temperature is extremely low, thus enabling the gas to remain in liquid 
form46. In order to supply the production of dimethyl ether (DME), the requirement of the 
size of the storage of oxygen is higher compared to the size of a tank. A possible viable 
solution would be to store oxygen in a dome structure. At the NASA’s Kennedy space 
center in Florida, a 3400m3 is being filled with liquified oxygen twice a week to chill down 
the oxygen to -148 ˚C slowly47. 
 
3.5 Hydrogen in China 
Unknown to many, China is the world’s largest hydrogen producer, and the annual 
production in 2016 was 21 million tons. Usually, hydrogen is produced from locally 
available energy sources. Thus the local hydrogen economics will be determined by the 
local strengths. Most hydrogen produced in 2016 were derived from fossil sources. There 
are close to 1000 operating coal gasifiers in China and coal used in gasification accounts 
for 5% of total coal consumption in China. However, the demand for hydrogen around the 
Yangtze River Delta and Pearl River Delta is far from the coal rich sites of Shanxi, Shaanxi 
and Inner Mongolia. Proper pipeline infrastructure has yet to be constructed, and this 
poses a challenge to the transportation and storage of hydrogen48.

4.1 Biomass  
In history, biomass pre-dates coal, oil, hydroelectricity, solar energy, and others. It has 
been in use for thousands of years, ever since humanity understood that rubbing two 
sticks together could provide heat59. In recent years, conventional fuel consisting of 
primary hydrogen (H) and carbon (C), also named hydrocarbon fuel, has grown more 
popular. The general formula is CnHm, and some of them are examples of hydrocarbon 
fuels are coal, petrol, and natural gases60.  
 
All petroleum-based energy sources contribute to the emission of air pollutants such as 
carbon monoxide (CO), carbon dioxide (CO2), nitrogen oxide (NOx), and greenhouse gases 
(GHG) when used in combustion engines for transportation purpose61. Thus, the shift in 
the transportation industry from commercial petroleum-based conventional fuel to 
cleaner alternative fuels such as gas, alcohols, ethanol, and methanol, are more 
environmentally friendly options. As the state of China is aware of the environmental 
issues caused by the consumption of coal has brought, different policies and incentives 
were deployed to improve the situation.  
 
As biomass in an infinite resource which that depends on photosynthesis, this renewable 
source of energy has infinite potential and is a much researched and discussed topic

4.1.1 Characterization of biofuels 
An unlimited supply of renewable energy is the natural resource biomass60.  By chemical 
or biological processes of biomass, biofuel is produced. Biomass is any organic matter 
whose growth is dependent on the sun, and generally refers to photosynthesis. During 
photosynthesis, the plant spends the energy gathered from the sun to convert absorbed 
carbon dioxide and water into oxygen and carbohydrates (sugar)63. Biomass includes 
agriculture crops, forest, wood, mill residues, wastes, animal waste, livestock operation 
residues, aquatic plants, municipal waste, and industrial waste. Another fuel that can also 
be categorized as biomass is fossil fuels since they are remains of plants that has through 
time been fossilized. The broad spectrum of biomass is either residual deposit from 
animal and human activities or the quantity of all living matters originating from the five 
kingdoms in biology: plants, animals, fungi, bacteria, and algae. All life in the five kingdoms 
is renewable since they rely on photosynthesis to survive60.  
 
Biomass produced liquid and gaseous fuels can be used in machinery to produce heat and 
electrical power, which requires biomass conversion processes to convert biomass in 
solid form into more useful forms. There are numerous examples of conversions as

4.1.2 Supply Chain of Biomass 
The supply chain of biomass starts from harvesting and ends at the end user. How biomass 
is supplied depends very much on bulk density and form since it enables effective 
transportation. The most cost efficient transportation methods are in the order of namely: 
truck and rail depending on the distance. On top of transportation costs, biomass cost also 
differs in the different provinces and cannot be generalized. 
 
Biomass from crop residues, forestry, and other resources are geographically widely 
allocated. To enable the production of biomass energy or fuel, two crucial factors to 
consider are65: bulk density and form since it will permit more mass per unit distance to 
be transported; truck transportation mode is usually the more convenient and cheaper 
depending on the distance to bio-refinery, for longer distance rail is the cheaper option. In 
most cases, the truck is always involved at the interface of train, barge, and pipeline 
transport at land or on shores. A rule of thumb is that transportation distance beyond a 
25-50 km radius depending on infrastructure is uneconomical. This will, in the end, affect 
the frequency biomass is supplied to the bio-refinery. A complete supply chain system is 
depicted in Figure 18, which describes how the biomass in the fields arrive to the end users 
as usable fuel66. Biomass feedstock is collected from the distributed farm fields in the form 
of bales, as shown in Figure 19, and are transported by truck to the closest centralized 
storage and preprocessing unit (CSP). Preprocessing refers to density reduction and 
densification through technologies as grinding, briquetting, and pelletizing, where the aim 
is to improve the biomass density relative to bales. Biomass is inert, of low density, and 
energy. The CSP is planned to store and provide raw biomass throughout the 
preprocessing and the finished product pellet and briquette are shown in Figure 20. 
Afterward, either by truck or rail, the feedstock can be transported to the bio-refinery 
plants and will reach the end users.

4.1.3.3 Provisioning of biomass from other means 
Apart from agriculture crop residues, which are widely distributed over China, there are

farms has the potential of 80 billion cubic meter yield of biogas76, which can be converted 
to 3,2 EJ by using the specific of 40 MJ/m3 16. 
 
Forest residue and wood waste originate from three sources in China: urban wood waste, 
forest harvesting and forest metabolism, and timber processing, where roughly 35-40% 
of total forest and wood waste are not collected and used. In total, around 900 million tons 
(16,2 EJ) of forest and wood waste are generated from forest harvesting and forest 
metabolism77.  
 
Collecting cooking oil waste in the urban area is more efficient since the cities are densely 
populated. Usually, the cooking oil waste is correlated to the consumption of edible oils 
and animal fats with a ratio of 20%77. By using different data of Chinese population growth, 
per capita consumption of edible oil, per capita meat and poultry, it is then possible to 
project China’s cooking oil waste. The amount of waste will reach 5,09 million metric tons 
and can in return be refined into 4,58 million metric tons of biodiesel.

6. Result and Discussion 
Dimethyl ether (DME) production technology is mature and ready for the Chinese market. 
The idea behind green dimethyl ether (DME) fuel is to empower the entire production line 
to become a sustainable process. Instead of using a non-renewable resource like coal, 
alternative feedstock such as biomass and municipal solid waste which are in abundance 
in China are considered and is the focus of this study.  
 
6.1 Dimethyl ether (DME) production technology 
The gasification process of drying, pyrolysis, combustion & cracking, and reduction are 
essential and occur more or less in every gasifier. Depending on the gasifier, the feedstock 
needs to be pre-treated before entering the gasifier. However, what determines the choice 
of the gasifier is noted to be due to available feedstock in the province. Just as low grade 
coal is of abundance in South Africa, China is a huge country with vast coal resources. High 
quality coal of energy value 6 245 kcal/kg in Northern China while low grade coal of 3 446 
kcal/kg exists in the south. Each gasifier also has its advantages. Fixed bed gasifier can 
work with feed size 5-75 mm, which avoids grinding and saves cost. On the contrary, fixed 
bed gasifier is sensitive to particle size distribution, which will affect the permeability. 
Low permeability will consequently lead to unstable gas temperature and composition. 
In comparison, fluidized bed and entrained bed gasifiers require additional pre-treatment 
to the feed. From an environmental perspective, entrained bed gasifier would be more 
favored due to the absence of tar formation, as tar is highly toxic and can damage the 
environment and ecosystem if not appropriately handled.  
 
In order to boost dimethyl ether (DME) production, necessary compositions are hydrogen 
(H2) and oxygen (O2) to adjust the H2/CO ratio, methanol synthesis, and utilized during 
combustion. If hydrogen was produced from methane reforming followed by water-gas 
shift reaction, the processes itself is not clean. Methane is a finite resource and carbon 
dioxide is a pollutant and this solution does not contribute to the green production of 
hydrogen. On the contrary, hydrogen produced from water electrolysis in connection to 
wind power can be considered a solution as renewable electricity is utilized and does not 
emit any pollution. In China, there are various technologies for hydrogen production, and 
one of the most common technologies is Steam-Methane Reforming. However, as this 
technology is 20% more expensive than traditional hydrogen produced from coal 
gasification, it is not difficult to figure out which one is the more competitive technology. 
Not to mention that coal is the cheapest and most available resource in China. Water 
electrolysis has yet to be commercially recognized. However, China is investing more in 
the wind power industry, and more offshore wind farms are built since they are closer to 
the harbor and big cities.  
 
6.2 Discovering potential location for DME production 
Dimethyl ether (DME) production requires syngas, which is produced in China today by 
using non-renewable resource, coal.  In order to create green dimethyl ether (DME) fuel, 
the entire production line should be based on clean resources and energy. What are 
considered to be promising candidates are biomass and municipal solid waste. These two 
candidates are in abundance and can be made more available if the government is there 
to support with legislation and incentive as what has been done before which is shown in 
Appendix . Biomass residue and municipal solid waste exists as a consequence of human 
populations residency on earth and cannot be avoided. Instead of allowing them to go to 
==PAGE:56==
 
46 
 
waste, in the case of municipal solid waste, to be incinerated or landfilled, it would be 
much more useful as a resource to produce green dimethyl ether (DME). Furthermore, by 
introducing wind energy, which can boost hydrogen and consequently syngas production 
from the water-gas shift reaction, the entire production line will be one step closer to 
green production. These three main factors noted should be considered in order to 
conduct a complete analysis of discovering potential locations for DME plants.  
The analysis will be based on the maps of (i) existing wind farms and potential regions 
with high wind mean power density, (ii) regions with abundance in coal resources, 
(iii)cropland, marginal land and forest and (iv) cities generating large amount of waste, in 
combination with each other.  
 
The entire dimethyl ether (DME) production facilities do not necessarily have to be 
concentrated to one location, as shown in Figure 2. As water electrolysis is highly reliant 
on wind energy, this would make up one facility. Meanwhile, hydrogen can easily be 
transported long distance and stored elsewhere. Nevertheless, in order to minimize any 
pollution, long distance transportation should be kept to the minimum. The same 
principle is applied during feedstock province ranking. Minimal distance is kept between 
hydrogen production, gasification, and dimethyl ether (DME) synthesis, all facilities 
should be placed within the same province to avoid excess pollutant emission from 
transportation.  
 
Hydrogen, gasification, and dimethyl ether (DME) synthesis facilities do not need to be 
located in the same province. If China is determined to invest in dimethyl ether (DME) 
production, then it is entirely possible to build a pipeline between the facilities. However, 
as the wind power grid has yet been improved in Gansu province, it is a question of how 
invested the Chinese government is regarding this pipeline expansion.   
 
6.2.1 Coal and wind power 
Little information has been found regarding the location of the existing dimethyl ether 
(DME) plant. The location shall be determined in this section by using Figure 33 regarding 
ensured coal reserves from 2011. The province selected should have coal in abundance, 
and with this sole criterion, the provinces that stand out are in the order of Shanxi, Inner 
Mongolia, Shaanxi, Xinjiang, and Guizhou provinces. Anhui, Henan, Shandong, and Yunnan 
province are all of similar coal reserve sizes, and together this makes up nine feasible coal 
based DME provinces.  
 
Coal production is a process utterly reliant on regional water availability and pollutes 1,07 
m3 of groundwater per ton extracted coal. Green dimethyl ether (DME) production sets a 
higher standard for coal production compared to before. Even though China will not 
immediately close down coal production, as the existing coal mines were built before 
water resource became an issue and it is not possible to stop coal mining entirely due to 
water scarcity as the major coal producing provinces remains in 2018: Inner Mongolia, 
Shanxi and Shaanxi province. One can argue that for the sake of green dimethyl ether 
(DME), provinces with stressed water resources will be placed last in the ranking, which 
is shown in Table 13. The provinces are separated into colors of light blue, dark blue and 
red, in order of water scarcity.

6.3 Outlook 
Air pollution in China is an acknowledged issue by the Chinese government, and a part of 
the issue is thanks to China’s big shipping industry. In order to overcome the 
environmental challenges provided by diesel and bunker fuel, China could explore other 
options. The grey highlighted fuels in Table 18 illustrate the fuel by consumption as energy 
in 2017. The yellow highlighted fuels describe the production energy. None of the 
production fuels by energy can sustain China’s demand for coal, natural gas, or oil.

can only cover for 30-35% of the needed amount. If ethanol were substituted by dimethyl 
ether (DME), then the national production would cover the demand. Bunker fuel

Plant electricity cost, Electrolysis Plant O&M cost, Bamboo Charcoal plant investment and, 
Bamboo Charcoal plant O&M cost. It is clear that cost related to Electrolysis Plant

Table 23 of Total cost is if the pay-back time is 1 year.

price. The transportation cost of input material is estimated from how may tips is required 
by dividing the total amount of material with truck capacity. This is multiplied with a one 
trip price. One trip price is the distance multiplied with transportation cost. DME 
transport cost is estimated in the same way. 
 
The Electrolysis Plant investment cost is estimated from the mass flow of hydrogen 
requiring electrolysis production in hours, multiplied with heating value of hydrogen 
which converts to power, Watt. This amount of power is then multiplied with the

efficiency of the Electrolysis plant of 63,6 %. The cost of electricity is total input power 
multiplied with electricity price. The O&M cost is estimated from number of electrolysis

6.4.3.2 Electricity parameter 
The Electrolysis electricity cost and Electrolysis Plant dominates the total cost and will in 
turn influence the DME price as shown in scenario 2, 4 and, 6 judging by Table 23. It is 
assumed that investment cannot be adjusted due to technology maturity. In order to 
lower the DME price, the electricity parameter can be adjusted for the scenarios using low 
Total cost replacing scenarios with high Total cost, for same input feedstock scenarios. 
This means scenario 2 assumes the Total cost of scenario 1, scenario 4 assumed for 
scenario 3, and scenario 6 assumes for scenario 5.   
 
The cost without electricity is obtained from New Total cost subtracted with seven cost 
parameters, beside electricity cost. Electricity price is obtained from the cost without 
electricity subtracted by total electricity required. A few costs, without electricity in 
scenario 2 and 4 will be of negative value. This means no matter how much the electricity 
price is adjusted; it will not be enough. Long pay-back time improves the cost without 
electricity due to investment is spread out over longer period of time. Hence artificial 
electricity price is only viable from year six in scenario 2, year nine in scenario 4 and, year 
one in scenario 6.

without investment by adjusting the electricity price. Now the scenario with lowest 
electricity price in Table 24 is scenario 4, at 0,000147 EURO/kWh in year nine and, this 
scenario is more sensitive to electricity price change.

C.1 First generation biofuel 
First generation biofuel  in commercial use are biodiesel, bioethanol and biogas. Oily 
plants, for instance: palm oil, coconut, rapeseed, peanut, sunflower and soybean are used 
to produce biodiesel through transesterification where biodiesel can substitute diesel 
through minor modification in diesel fueled engines. Triglycerides will react chemically 
with alcohol (e.g. bioethanol) in the presence of an enzyme or catalyst and will generate 
biodiesel with glycerol136. Bioethanol is produced through fermentation while using the 
part of the plant that contain sugar and starch. The top three crops in use are sugarcane, 
sugar beet and corn. Brazil is the country producing most ethanol from sugarcane which 
is available all year around and since their growth flourishes in tropical climate. 73,8 tons 
grows on one hectare of land and yields 9000 liters of ethanol. The return of energy 
investment is 1:8 ration which means 8 unit of energy yields from 1 unit of investment, 
energy production efficiency of 87,5%. From sugar beet and corn yields 2700 liters 
ethanol per hectare, 47,3% energy efficiency and 1350 liters ethanol per hectare, 20% 
energy efficiency respectively and is shown in Table C 164. Biogas is a mixture of carbon 
dioxide and methane, produced through anaerobic digestion of organic materials136.

C.2 Second generation biofuel 
Second generation biofuel is derived from lignocellulosic biomass, nonfood crop 
feedstock, agriculture and forest residues, and industrial waste. The main processes to 
produce second generation biofuel are usually through a pretreatment followed by 
utilization of thermochemical or biochemical technologies. As biomass have different 
sizes, moisture content, density and etc., pretreatment is an important step to prepare the 
biomass for future conversion process. Pretreatment includes briquetting and pelletizing 
with the aim to shape compact, high density solid blocks. The thermochemical process 
refers to the gasification process which includes pyrolysis, combustion and reduction in 
order to extract syngas of carbon monoxide and hydrogen. Gasification of biomass occurs 
by of biomass at high temperature in a gasification medium such as air, oxygen or steam. 
The produced syngas can then participate in syntheses of biofuel such as DME which is 
explained more in chapter 4136. 
 
Biochemical process of second generation biofuel include fracturing cellulose to smaller 
compounds which will then be fermented to alcohol and ethanol. The primary challenge 
of second generation biofuel is the conversion of cellulose. Although, enzymes have 90% 
conversion efficiency of converting sugar to ethanol, the conversion is only 40% from 
cellulose to ethanol. Production of ethanol requires larger amount of feedstock to be 
processed for the same amount of first generation biofuel. From the most efficient 
conversion yields 180 liters of ethanol from 1 ton of feedstock64.

C.3 Third generation biofuel 
In the third generation biofuel is produced by algsae under controlled environment where 
carbon dioxide and key nutrients are supplemented. It is reported to yield 98400 liters 
biodiesel per hectare algae. While the theoretical yield is impressive, it requires tropical 
and subtropical closed incubator like systems which enables higher growth efficiency 
compared to open pound. Here, microalgae are raised in plastic tubes and fed with carbon 
dioxide and various nutrients while the produced oxygen is released regularly64. Third 
generation biofuel is considered a feasible alternative which has yet to be commercially 
accepted136.

Appendix D 
D.1 Renewable Energy Law of People’s Republic of China 
In 2005, China issued the first law regarding energy named Renewable Energy Law of 
People’s Republic of China and was effective from January 1st 2006. This new law was a 
indication of Chinese government’s position regarding renewable energy including 
biomass energy amongst modern energy. In the first chapter of this law, the State 
encourages and supports use of biomass energy; in chapter four “Promotion and 
Application” highlights once again that the State encourages development of energy crops, 
efficient development and use of biomass fuels. The state revised the Renewable energy 
law at the end of 2009 and was implemented on April 1st 2010.  
 
D.2 Medium-and Long Term Development Plan for Renewable Energy 
The Medium-and Long Term Development Plan for Renewable Energy was issued in 2007 
where heavy weight was put on development of biomass power generation, biology liquid 
fuel, densified biofuel and biogas. By 2020, biomass power generation will have the total 
capacity of 30 000 000 kW, annual utilization rate of densified biofuel up to 50 000 000 
tons (1,05 EJ by using the mean heating value of 21 MJ/kg137), biogas to 44 000 000 000 
cubic meters (1,74 EJ138), non-grain ethanol fuel to 10 000 000 tons (89,1 TJ3)  and 
biodiesel to 2 000 000 tons (24,12 MJ3). 
 
D.3 National five year plan  
The national five year plan system has included biomass energy since 1995. In 2001 to 
2005, during the 10th five year plan the planning of the development of agricultural 
biomass energy industry was introduced. Thus, from the 11th five year plan, each five year 
plan contains the special planning for biomass energy industry. In the 12th five year plan 
issued for the period of 2011 to 2015, emphasizes on additional development and 
utilization of crop straw output in China, the plan is to accomplish over 80% 
comprehensive utilization rate and straw energy utilization rate of 13% by 2015. Current 
13th five year plan released in March 2016 focused little to nothing on biofuel 
development as no specific targets were set70. Existing policies regarding biofuel are 
mainly targeted on ethanol productions whereas 99% Chinese produced ethanol is 
derived from wheat and corn (starch-based) feedstock90

to saline-alkali soil and other different land types. The base subsidy is 30 RMB/are for 
forest raw material89.

3. Low interest loan 
If the energy development and utilization project is listed in the National Renewable 
energy industry development guidance catalogue and meets the credit conditions, under 
the assumption that bank loans paid interest are in place; discount interest funds may be 
arranged. The amount of discount interest fund is based on the actual loan in place, 
contract rate interest and the actual paid interest. Maximum yearly discount rate cannot 
exceed 3% and the discount period is between 1 to 3 years. 
 
4. Tax relief 
Tax authorities has established measures for refund on collected VAT for agricultural and 
forestry products: i.e. (logging residues, bucking residues, processing residues), small 
firewood, crop straw and bagasse. The discount of specific portion of tax are 100% and 
80% in 2009 and 2010 respectively. 
 
The 90% tax of the income can be reduced for enterprises which produces products not 
forbidden or restricted by the state and obeys the national and industry standards and 
the main raw material resources can be found in the Catalogue of Resources for 
Comprehensive Utilization Entitling Enterprises to Income Tax Preferences. 
 
D.5 Back end incentives 
1. Quota system 
The quota system was introduced with the purpose of controlling the production and 
consumption of biomass energy/fuel. With the quota system implemented, it is 
mandatory that power and oil companies should make a portion of their produced energy 
from biomass energy89. Thus, the companies will become entire reliant upon the 
government for financial support according to the front-end incentives and also rely on 
the government to create an environment for large scale expansion of biomass energy. An 
active quota policy is the E10 mandate which is a measure to promote usage of more 
ethanol in gasoline.
