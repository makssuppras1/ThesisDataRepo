Abstract 
 
 Microgrid is an emerging form of power generation, distribution and storing system 
which is integrated with different combination of power generation sources like 
renewable energy sources, combined heat and power, and distributed energy resources. 
The advantages of microgrid configuration are low transmission and distribution cost, 
and potentially high reliability, high efficiency and low environmental impact. 
Microgrids are designed to operate in one of two modes, grid connected and islanded. 
Short circuit response of inverter connected distributed generators are very low than that 
of synchronous or induction generators. For these rotating generators the short circuit 
current level is 3-4 times higher than their nominal current. On the other hand, power 
electronics inverter has been designed for short circuit currents only up to 1.1-1.25 
because of thermal limitation of power electronics devices.  
Faults behaviors are different and hereby first we need to study about it for 
implementation of protection system in microgrids. Due to very low impedance in the 
cables between distributed generators to point of common coupling, the locating of fault 
is a very challenging task when connected to the grid. 
In this thesis project, a new approach is developed which uses symmetrical components 
for steeply increasing selectivity of relay are achieved.

1.1 Motivation 
 
Power system currently undergoes extensive change in operating requirements mainly 
because of deregulation and increasing penetration of distributed energy resources 
(DER). Having DER renewable energy resources near to load has benefit of reducing 
losses and avoiding network congestion. In the case of severe system disturbance, 
distribution generation (DG) and storage system can supply the power to end 
consumers. This is known as a microgrid [1, 4 and 5] and depicted in figure 1-1. A 
microgrid poses various benefits to end consumers, utilities and societies are: 
 Enhanced service quality and reliability 
 Enhanced energy efficiency 
 Cost efficient electricity infrastructure replacement 
 Reduced greenhouse gases and pollutant emissions 
Implementations of microgrids are attracted by many governmental organizations in 
US, Japan and EU [6-8]. 
One major effect of microgrid will be on the behavior of protective relays, especially 
over current relays. Over current relays measure current to determine fault. Therefore, 
current contribution during fault plays significant role in proper operation of over 
current relays. A synchronous generator can provide 3-4 times more current than its

DGs are mostly connected to the grid through power electronics interfaces. To ensure 
safety of power electronic components, converters are designed to generate maximum 
current which is 1.1-1.25 times its rated current. This denotes that even during fault, 
converter connected DGs can generate short circuit current which is far less than that

not have enough current for its accurate operation. This is a warning posed due to 
increase in converter connected DGs penetration in microgrid. 
 
Alternatively, existence of power electronic converter interface DGs offers flexibility in 
control of short circuit current. As short circuit current purely depends on control action 
of converter, converter parameters can be adjusted as per desired short circuit response. 
This gives a major advantage for power electronic interface DG. Converter controls can 
be set for fast or slow reactive current response, delayed response, balanced response in 
case of unbalanced faults etc. However, short circuit current remains low in system 
dominated by converter controlled DGs. 
On the other hand, when microgrid is connected to grid connected mode, short circuit is 
supplied by the grid. In such conditions have sufficient magnitude of short circuit 
currents to identify fault by relay. But, in each relay inserted into in each DG have same 
magnitude of fault current and all relay send trip signal to the breaker. All consumer 
suffered by tripping the breaker. Our objective is to supply electricity to the all 
consumer except faulty feeder. Therefore, relay should know where and what type of 
fault is and send trip signal from that feeder’s relay only. 
Therefore, selectivity of relay is most important and main issues for this project.  This 
provides motivation for this study where performance of relay is observed by applying 
different types of fault.

Chapter Two 
2. State-of-the-Art 
 
 
This chapter describes existing microgrid technologies and its protection schemes. Here only 
focus on microgrid technologies, existing converter control methods, grid synchronization 
techniques. At last, different types of protection schemes employed for protection of microgrids 
are described. 
 
 
Four classes of microgrid architectures are reported [13]. They are single facility 
microgrid, multiple facility microgrid, feeder microgrid and substation microgrid.   
In designing a protection system of microgrid, the protection requirements that must be 
considered are reliability, speed, selectivity and cost [14]. Microgrid protection system 
should follow these requirements. 
 
Jenkins et al. [15] presented overcurrent relay protection and Residual Current Devices 
(RCD) is used to protect microgrid from faults. However, the conventional overcurrent 
protection scheme gives rise to problems such as less sensitivity and mal operation [16].  
Oudalov and Fidigatti [17] suggesting the use of directional over current relay with 
communication. This is best choice in terms of technology but require relatively high 
investment cost. 
 
The protection scheme based on DG output voltage measurement was used to detect and 
clear faults [18]. The output voltages were measured and transformed to dq reference 
frame. Any disturbance at DG will be reflected to in dq values. However, this 
technology is used only for islanded and not considering high impedance fault. 
Nikkhajoei and Lasseter [19] proposed a method based on symmetrical and differential 
current components against SLG and LL faults. The differential current components are

2.2 Grid Synchronization 
 
Phase Locked Loop (PLL) is used to synchronize converter with grid by using 
Synchronous Reference Frame (SRF). To precise control of converter, accurate 
information of grid voltage phase and frequency is required. SRF-PLL is commonly 
used for synchronization purpose [22]. Although SRF-PLL works well under balanced 
conditions, under unbalanced condition its performance is not sufficient. This has led to 
development of advanced PLL structures like, Dual Second Order Generalized 
Integrator - PLL (DSOGI-PLL) [23-25]. 
 
DSOGI-PLL is a frequency adaptive PLL structure which uses dual second order 
generalized integrator based Quadrature Signal Generator (QSG) to obtain ﬁltered and 
90 degree shifted component of αβ voltage components. These components are used to

The PI controller is most common control algorithm which is used for compensation of 
error. It calculates an error value as the difference between measured converter output 
current and a desired injected current into the grid. Therefore, controller attempts to 
minimize the error between them.

constant Kp and integral constant Ki. The proportional term is formed by multiplying 
the error signal by a gain Kp. This reduces overall error with time. However, the effect 
of proportional term will not reduce the error to zero and always there are some steady 
state errors. The integral term integrates the error and multiplies with Ki constant value

voltages and currents have the same amplitude and are phase shifted by 120° with 
respect to each other. If either or both conditions are not met, the system is called 
unbalanced or asymmetrical [40-42]. 
 
The ratios 𝑈𝑈 (voltage) and 𝑈𝐼  (current) between the magnitudes of negative and 
positive sequence components of voltage and current respectively are a measure of the

6. Conclusion and Future Work 
 
6.1 Conclusion 
 
 
This chapter summarizes and concludes this study. Recommendations and future work 
are also suggested. 
This thesis performs the study of different faults behavior and develops a common 
protection system in microgrid having different connection length from PCC. 
 
The main conclusions of this thesis are as following: 
 Fault current at branch of DGs is very small and same in each DG’s fault point. 
Therefore, traditional over current protection system does not work. 
 
 Voltage symmetrical components do not have any properties to detect fault 
location. In each branch, they are same in magnitude. 
 
 Current symmetrical components (CSC) have properties to detect fault location 
in microgrid side. Among them, positive sequence component does not locate 
the fault because all positive sequences are in same magnitude in all branches at 
fault points. Negative sequence and positive sequence symmetrical components 
have best properties to locate fault, so that faulted branch can only isolate by 
feeding relay energizing quantity. 
 
 
 In SLG fault, zero and negative current symmetrical components | I0| ≥0.36 pu 
and | In| ≥0.36 pu  threshold values are found to feed trip signal to the relay. 
 
 In LL fault, negative current symmetrical components  | In| ≥0.54 pu
