Summary (English)
Nowadays the importance of new technologies and - through this - the im-
provement of energy generation methods is getting more and more impor-
tant.
The dynamically growing consumption and the nite stock of fossil
energy sources justify the development of applications utilising renewable en-
ergy sources. Based on these ideas, the goal of this thesis is to investigate the
feasibility of the Kalina cycle tailored for low temperature solar applications.
The examined power plant model has relatively small electric output, making
it suitable for o-grid operation and consumer energy generation. The poten-
tial of solar energy and its exploitation are also drawn up, together with the
characterisation of the sun rays. Dierent solar thermal technologies and the
method of a at-plate collector based solar model is presented as well. The
thesis contains the introduction of Kalina cycle with special attention to the
thermodynamic losses and its dissimilarities from Rankine and Organic Rank-
ine cycles. A design- and part-load model of a low temperature Kalina cycle
is introduced with validation, optimisation, sensitivity analysis and discussion
about dierent o-design control strategies. After the optimisation procedure
of the solar model's parameters, the annual analysis and nal evaluation of the
application is presented.

Chapter 1
Introduction
1.1
Goals
Nowadays, the topic to keep the energy generation sustainable is of paramount
importance in developed countries. A possible way to achieve this goal is the
introduction of new solutions. As it will be explained in this thesis, the Kalina
cycle is a relatively new technology which is suitable for exploiting low and
medium temperature heat sources. This is why it is a reasonable choice for
renewable energy sources and heat recovery systems. Based on these ideas it
is worth to analyse it from dierent aspects. I intended to examine the annual
production of a solar energy based small power plant through investigating
dierent parameters.
To do so, creating a design- and part-load model is
essential. The design specication is going to be chosen after an optimisation
process and the best part-load control strategy will also be put under the scope.

1.2
Structure of the thesis
Based on the aforementioned aims of the thesis, a brief overview on the en-
ergy situation of the European Union will be made at rst in order to prove
the justiability and importance of new technologies. It will be shown that
the stock of fossil fuels justies the usage of renewable energy sources and the
improvement of existing energy generation processes.
Since the heat source of the examined technology is solar power, it is reasonable
to investigate the solar energy in dierent countries: Denmark will be com-
pared with one of the most famously solar energy using countries (Spain), and
the target location of the power plant (Hungary). Two most common methods
to utilise low temperature solar energy (at-plate collectors and concentrating
solar collectors) are also briey described. In order to choose the most ap-
propriate method, a basic comparison of the two technologies is needed. This
section will contain the solar model as well. The assumptions and the control
strategy of the solar eld will be discussed.
It is also important to introduce the Kalina cycle in the document in order to
be familiar with its basic operation and advantages compared to the Rankine
and Organic Rankine cycles.
To properly investigate the annual generation, the rst step is the creation of
a design-load model. In this section the basic assumptions will be described.
The method of building the design- and part-load models will also be drawn
up, just like the design parameters of the power plant after the introduction
of the optimisation process. Since validating a model is very important, the
validation of the design-load model and the validation process of the part-
load model will be investigated based on the received data from a German
geothermal power plant. Taking into consideration the fact that the design
parameters of the power plant can highly eect on the nal results, it is rea-
sonable to perform a sensitivity analysis. The last chapter will contain the
feasibility and annual analysis of the power plant after the investigation of the
climate conditions in the target area. Then the conclusion and the description
of the further research paths will take place.

equipment and - as a consequence - a higher level of need for energy appeared
gradually. After a long period of time, common renewable energy sources could
not provide enough energy density for new inventions thus they had to utilise
fossil energy sources too. The biggest step in this process was the rst Indus-

intensive growth and the energy consumption grew to a whole new level. In
those decades the eciency of machines was very low so the use of this energy
source increased exponentially parallel with new investments. The result of
this process was a very contaminated environment. After a short period of
time, people realised that this progress cannot continue.

stitution of the rst organisation is a very good proof. The establishment of

The Kalina cycle
The Kalina cycle (KC) is a modied Rankine cycle (RC). It can be said, that
the whole idea of this layout is very new because it can be traced back only
to the eighties. The need for this new solution appeared with the intention to
use lower temperatures than before. It occurs mostly in case of renewable en-
ergy sources and improvement of existing technologies. The common Rankine
cycle is not the best solution for these applications.
Therefore, the imple-
mentation of Organic Rankine Cycle (ORC) came into view. It uses organic
working uid instead of water, allowing evaporation at lower temperatures as
well. Kalina came out with his invention in the early eighties and suggested to
use ammonia-water mixture in the power generation cycles. This idea is very
new and considered to be the most signicant improvement in thermal power
plant design since the Rankine cycle in the 19th century. It also appears as an
ambitious competitor against ORC. The topic of my thesis is the performance
analysis of a power generation cycle like this, for low temperatures [46].
