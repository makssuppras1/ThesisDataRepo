Abstract

Abstract 
 
The structural glass became a very popular material in recent years. The time when the glass 
was used only as envelope of the building became history. Now the glass is used also as load-
bearing building element, which due to its transparency, high compressive strength and 
environmental resistance became one of the most suitable material for structural use. On the 
other hand using glass as structural material is very complicated because of the brittle 
behaviour glass exhibits. Also because it is very sensitive to the defects of the surface, it 
presents a low tensile resistance. However, the strength of the glass can be improved by the 
process of tempering, which generates a considerable increase in tension strength. 
The common usage of tempered glass is as building façades. In order to assemble the glass 
panels all the holes for joints need to be done before the tempering process. However, it is 
often happening that due to low tolerance, inaccurate placements of the hole and lack of 
flexibility on site, new holes are required. A solution for this, are new type of joints based on 
partial post tempering drilling. Because this hole is drilled after the tempering, they will 
present lower strength, governed by the defects induced by drilling process. 
The report deals with improving of the surface quality at the borehole in order to increase the 
resistance of the surface. The method studied in this report is named “Etching”, it is 
characterized by local removal of the surface defect using strong acids. The main objectives 
of this report were determination of the correct etching procedure and testing of the borehole 
strength variation in order to find the minimum required time of etching. 
An experimental study was made in order to determine the correct procedure of etching for 
avoiding uneven etching, formation of post reaction crystals and unwanted etching of the 
surface around borehole. For study, different times of etching application were considered. It 
was observed a variations in geometry characterised by volumetric increase of the hole and 
rounding effect at the borehole edge. Volume of etched surface characteristic for different 
times of etching was measured and based on it, etching rate curve was determined. Variation 
of the borehole geometry was presented with assumption of possible borehole shapes after 
longer time of treatment. 
For stresses investigation, models of the characteristic geometries for different times of 
etching were created and implemented in the commercial Finite Element Software 
ABAQUS/CAE v.6.14. In order to simulate residual stresses in the glass a parabolic 
temperature state field was used. The models were used to study the residual stress behaviour, 
governed by rounding effect at borehole.  
A test setup for double ring testing was designed in order to test the glass surface at uniform 
tension of the surface around borehole. The results from testing were very prominent, 
presenting a significant increase in strength after a minimum period of etching for removal of 
surface defects was applied. A representation of increasing in strength in function of etching 
time was reproduced and a minimum necessary time of etching was determined. Maximal 
stress in time of failure was determined using finite element modelling and compared with 
results from previous works. 
Keywords: Etching, tempered glass, surface enhancement, double ring testing.

Introduction 
 
3.1 
Structural use of glass 
 
 
Figure 1: MyZeil Shopping Centre in Frankfurt glass roof and facade 
  In the recent years the popularity of glass as a structural material increased significantly. Due 
to its decorative aspects and transparency, the glass was always a wondered material for 
building façades and roofs. Also the researches made in last decades improved the glass 
properties as a material, and with some limitations elements from glass can be used now as 
load bearing one such as facades, beams and fins. A remarkable example of curved façade can 
be seen in Figure 1, where entire building envelope is made of glass panels. 
The biggest challenge in working with glass is the brittle behaviour of this material, which 
means that it have no ductility before breakage. This means that glass cannot redistribute the 
stresses like other material used in construction, which means that: residual stress state, stress 
concentration, loads and ambient conditions need to be considered when the design is made. 
However, besides its brittleness, the glass possesses a significant compressive strength 
equivalent to a high grade steel. The last innovation in treatment of the glass, such, as 
laminating and tempering, pushed the use of glass on a new level and made it a very popular 
material for building’s façades. The strength of the glass is mostly determined by the state of 
the surface, where small flows and cracks make the tensile strength very small in comparison 
to its compressive properties. A good solution for this problem is etching of the glass surface 
with acids. Due to the fact that a part from the glass surface is removed, an effect of rounding 
and smoothening of the surface defect is created, which considerably increase the glass

strength. However, this process reduces the glass transparency, which in many cases is the 
main attribute for the glass elements. 
Because of its brittle behaviour, designing of the joints in glass is a big challenge. Most of the 
times the joint connection is the determining factor in load carrying capacity of the element. 
In order to determine the real strength of the joints, detailed experiments and numerical 
analysis are required to be done. Considering this, it is very important to develop and 
implement adequate design tools, which can give relevant information about real behaviour of 
the joints in time of loading. 
In case of tempered glass, it is impossible to make a hole-through the glass after the tempering 
process. However, in last years a new solution was developed. This new method consists of 
drilling a hole in the tempered glass up to the tension zone its avoiding breakage. This method 
allows creating a connection joint after the tempering process and giving relative good results. 
But due to the fact that the drilling in glass results in creation of numerous defects at the 
borehole surface, the strength of this type of joint are not fully developed. And in order to 
increase the surface quality of the borehole, a series of surface treatments and alternative 
solution can be implemented. This thesis has a focus on determining the influence of the 
borehole surface etching and developing of the adequate etching process in order to get 
highest joint strength. 
Although the glass is a widely used material in construction, there is always a place for new 
improvements and product application.  Further exploring of this material will lead to new 
level of structural design and to bigger challenges in future.

3.2 
 Chemical and physical properties of glass

process goes in to a rigid condition without crystallization. There is a big variety of different 
types of glass but only two type are mostly used in construction. The most common type of 
glass used in construction is soda lime silicate glass SLSG. In case of some special 
applications when high resistance for temperature changes is required (e.g. heat resistant 
glazing, fire protection glazing) borosilicate glass BSG is used. For the purpose of this thesis 
only one type of glass is considered, more precisely soda lime silicate glass SLSG. Any 
further mentioning of glass will be related to this specific type. Chemical composition of the 
glass has an important effect on the general properties of the glass, like melting temperature, 
viscosity and expansion coefficient. A typical chemical composition of the soda lime silicate

A remarkable characteristic of the glass, is its chemical resistance to many different

Using present production processes, it is almost impossible to manufacture glass panes with 
ideal surface. This lead to a conclusion that in order to improve the tension strength of a glass 
pane, different method of improving the surface quality need to be applied. The purpose of the 
thesis, is studying the effect of surface etching which by rounding and smoothening of the 
surface defects on glass can increase the strength. In order to perform this, treatment with 
strong acids of the borehole will be done. As result of this treatment, a part of the glass 
surface will interact with acid and as result of reaction a layer of surface will be removed. By 
process of etching is generating smoothening and rounding of small cracks resulted from 
drilling. 
 
3.3 
Glass production 
 
The production of glass was discovered thousands of years ago and through the time many 
different methods of production were developed. In present the most used method for 
production of the glass is float process.  About 90% of the glass is produced using this method 
patented by the Pilkington Brothers in 1959. The main advantages of this process are its low 
cost, its wide availability, superior optical quality of the glass and also the possibility of 
production of large panes. 
The production process consists of several steps presented schematically in Figure 3. The row 
materials are melted in a furnace at a temperature of about 1550 °C. Then the melted glass is 
poured continuously at approximatively 1000 °C on to a pool of melt tin. The melted glass 
floats on the surface of the tin and it spreads forming a smooth uniform layer with a certain 
thickness, which is gradually cooled down on to rollers. The glass thickness can be controlled 
within a range of 2 to 25 mm by adjusting the speed of rollers. Reducing the speed increase 
the thickness and vice versa. In order to prevent the residual stresses to build up in to the 
glass, the annealing lehr slowly cools the glass. After annealing, the glass is inspected on the 
presence of obvious visual defects and imperfections, which are removed during cutting.  The 
glass is cut to a typical size of 3.21m x 6.00m before being stored.

As a consequence of floating process, the two faces of glass sheets are not identical. Because

reduce the effect of these small defects, the glass can be tempered. 
 
3.4 
Tempering of glass 
 
3.4.1 
Tempering principles 
 
After the glass is manufactured, it is used for different purposes. In order to improve or give 
new property to the glass, it can be processed further in order to produce different glass 
products with specific properties.  For the purpose of this thesis a relevant process is heat 
treatment of the glass, used to get such products like: heat strengthened or fully tempered 
glass. The purpose for tempering the glass is increasing its strength by creating a residual 
stress field in interior of the glass. Tempered glass is characterised by containing a tensile 
stress at the middle of the glass and a compressive stress at the surface.      
                       
 
                            Figure 4: Residual stresses from thermal tempering, far from edges (Nielsen, 2013) 
In Figure 4, is represented the general distribution of the residual stresses of a thermal 
tempered glass, far from the edge.  The stresses inside the glass have a parabolic distribution, 
where compression part at the surface of the glass, represents 20 % of the total thickness of 
the glass specimen on each side. Because the surface of the glass is subjected to a 
compression residual stress, the apparent tensile strength of the glass increases. In some 
certain limits this can be compared to a pre-stressed concrete beam. And due to the fact that 
the interior part of the glass is almost without flows, it has a good resistance to tensile

3.4.2 
Tempering process 
 
The process of tempering, when the characteristic residual stress are induced in to the glass 
structure, is very complex and involve a large number of mechanisms. In this section only the 
main principles are mentioned and for a more in depth information the following papers need 
to be studied (Nielsen J. H., 2010a) or (Shelby, 2005). For a better understanding of the 
process of tempering a schematic illustration of this can be seen in Figure 6. 
 
Figure 6: Production process for tempering the glass panes. (Nielsen, 2010) 
Before the glass is tempered, all necessary pre-treatments need to be done, including cleaning 
of the glass, drilling holes, cutting the necessary geometry and chamfering the edges. This 
should be done before the process of tempering, due to the residual stress state in tempered 
glass, which will make this impossible after. In order to fully temper the glass, it should be

In the first seconds of cooling process, tensile stress is generated on the surface of the glass 
and compression in the middle. At this phase, tensile stress can relax rapidly within viscous 
state of the glass. However, if the temperature is to low, the relaxation is not possible and the 
tensile stress at the surface of the glass can cause the glass to shatter. As soon as the transition 
temperature of the glass is reached (~570°C), the glass solidifies and relaxation stops. The 
glass cools down and the characteristic parabolic distribution across the cross section appears.

3.5 
Glass etching 
  
3.5.1 
Etching principle 
 
Etching process, is known more as a process of using strong acids in order to cut in to the 
unprotected parts of a metal surface or glass surfaces, in order to create a design or a relief 
with decorative purposes. However, it has long been known that etching is a useful method 
for strengthening the glass. By etching surface flows are first reduced in length, rounded, and 
then completely removed. After this, the strength of the glass depends only on defects inside 
the structure of the glass or from damage produced by the reaction products.  
For etching of the glass, usually is used hydrofluoric acid (HF) and other acids based on the 
flour. Usually in order to dissolve water insoluble fluoride compounds, solution which 
contains other strong acids than hydrofluoric acids are made like: H2SO4 and HCl (sulphuric 
acid and hydrochloric acid) in different concentrations. The essence of etching is in removing 
of a surface layer from the glass due to reaction of this with hydrofluoric acid. Which in fact 
by chemical reaction remove atoms from the surface of the glass. Like it was mentioned 
before, glass tension strength directly depends on the surface quality of the glass. Presence of 
flows and micro-cracks reduce the glass strength and by action of the acid this irregularities 
are rounded or even totally removed from glass surface. However, due to the fact that all

3.6 
Residual stresses  
 
 As a result of tempering process, a residual compressive stress is induced at the surface of the 
glass. This creates an effect of closing the surface flows and in the same time increasing the 
strength of the glass. The interior of the glass is usually without flows, which means that it is 
capable to withstand the internal tension stress. Residual stresses in the glass directly depend 
on tempering process conditions like: rate of cooling, geometry of the specimen and in some 
cases even the position of the predrilled holes. 
For the purpose of this thesis, it is very important to measure the residual stresses, because the 
distribution of this reflects the quality of glass product. Also it give an important information 
in relation to strength of the glass specimen.

3.6.1 
Measuring of the residual stresses  
 
Due to the fact that the purpose of this thesis is to study the strength of a borehole after 
treating it with acid, it is very important to know the residual stress at the point where each 
hole is going to be drilled. Also because more specimens of glass are used, it is crucial to 
know the stress distribution of this, for an adequate future comparison of results.  
 
3.6.2 
Measuring technique 
 
There are plenty of techniques for measuring residual stresses, much of which include the 
destruction of the specimen. Taking into consideration that our interest is preserving the 
integrity of the glass, it was chosen a non-destructive method. Most of the non-destructive 
technics are able to measure residual stress distribution only at the surface of the glass. In 
order to find the stress along the thickness of the glass, a method known as SCAttered Light 
Polariscope or (SCALP) was used. This is possible because glass is a photo elastic material, 
which has the ability to affect the light when it is subjected to a stress. 
In order to measure the residual stresses in the glass specimens used for the purpose of this 
thesis, a scattered light polariscope developed by the GlassStress Ltd. was used. It was first 
time introduced in (Anton, 2006) The SCALP operates by the following principle: the 
instrument emits a polarized laser beam, which passes through the glass panel; the light will 
be speeded perpendicular to the laser beam direction.  Due to lattice effect (C. Aben & and H. 
Guillement, 1993) there will be a delay of the reflection, which depends on the stress state of 
the glass. In order to use this effect, the laser beam is angled at 45° relative to the glass 
surface and the reflected light is measured with the camera. This method was verified in 
(Nielsen J. H., 2009), where the stress distribution was measured with SCALP on a high 
stressed panel and after compared with analytical solution. Considering photoelastic 
properties of the SCALP, the glass is made birefringent due to residual stresses.  This means 
that the glass will have a refractive index which depends on the photoelastic constant, C, and 
stress distribution. According to different sources photoelastic constant for float glass and 
tempered one can have slight different values. However, for the purpose of this report values 
given in (Ankergren U. & Bak-Jensen A., 2008)was used. In Table 3 are presented values for 
photoelastic constant C found in different sources.

3.7 
Drilling in the glass 
 
 
  There are two kinds of drill bits which are usually used for drilling in the glass, the diamond 
coated bit and the tungsten carbide bit. For the purpose of this thesis diamond coated bit is 
used, due to resultant shape of the borehole after using it. This type of drill has a flat surface 
at the end and the final hole has a cylindrical shape, with constant diameter until the 
maximum depth.  In Figure 10 a typical diamond coated drill is presented. The coating at the 
end of the drill is glued to the metal surface of the drill. This means that for achieving an 
effective drilling and avoids unnecessary abrasion it is necessary to use the bit at the 
recommended revolution and avoid overheating of this.  In order to avoid overheating of the 
bit, it is recommended cooling it down with special lubricant. But according to suggestions of 
some producers of diamond beats, the best and cheapest lubricant is water. For the purpose of 
this research a cooling water pool around borehole was admitted. In order to inshore enough 
flow of cool water in time of drilling, the layer of water should be at least one centimetre 
thick. For more details about the process of drilling and depth measurement check Appendix 
B and chapter 4.1.1.

4.1 
Drilling experimental plan 
 
When speaking about drilling in tempered glass, the biggest challenge is choosing of the 
correct drilling process and adequate geometry of the holes which will be further investigated.  
As the purpose of this thesis was enhance of the borehole quality using treatment with strong 
acids, the main accent was putted on how the surface will change after treatment and to which 
geometrical changes this treatment can lead to. Knowing this the best solution was drilling 
holes with a regulated shape, for which geometrical changes can be easily seen afterwards. 
During all experiments performed with the glass specimens two types of holes were used: 
1. Regulated cylindrical hole with diameter of 1.8 mm and depth of 1.8mm 
2. Regulated cylindrical hole with diameter of 2.0 mm and depth of 1.8mm 
First type of hole was used in preliminary experiments where correct etching technique was 
developed. Second type was used afterwards for glass specimens which were tested in double 
ring test. The reason why the diameter was changed was in order to see if the rate of diameter 
increase during etching will increase with diameter change. Also because the same geometry 
of holes was used by Jonathan R. Olesen another member of Glass cluster 2015. Due to the 
fact that both of us used double ring test as testing setup this leaded to an opportunity of 
exchange of data. 
In the same time the reason why a depth of 1.8 mm was chosen, was to inshore that during the 
etching, the depth of borehole will not increase up to the limit when the glass will shatter. And 
in the same time to have a relative deep hole to see the effect of etching. All the specimens 
which were further tested using double ring setup, had 2 mm in diameter and 1.8 mm in 
depth. 
4.1.1 
Drilling technique  
 
The machine used for drilling during the experiments was the same as one used before in 
(Catuna, 2014)and (Thorup, 2014) it has the option to vary the drilling speed by controlling 
the rotation per minute (rpm) of machine. For the purpose of this report maximum speed of 
8500 rpm was used, because it was noticed that for the diamond drills higher speed give the 
best results. Oher speeds were also tested, but when diamond drills are used, low speed leads 
to creation of more micro-cracks and chippings on the surface. In order to get the best result 
and minimize the errors, it is necessary to have both high rpm and performant drill. On the 
other hand, high rpm leads to fast increasing in temperatures in the glass close to drilling area.  
Fast increasing in the temperature of the glass can lead to the changes in residual stresses 
around the borehole. This can happen because the stresses in the glass have the same nature of 
provenience, heating and fast cooling. Therefore, repeating this process has an effect on 
present residual stresses. In the same time a too high increase in the temperature of the glass 
can lead to shattering of the glass. By analysing the suggestions of diamond bit producers, a 
solution was founded, by creation of a pool of cold water with at least one centimetre layer of

position, like this following the plate under it. This movement was registered using a data-
logger, and by making the difference between initial position and current position the drilling 
depth variation was determined. 
The data-logger transforms movements of LVDT in to readable variations in voltage. The 
data-logger make cyclic measurements at a specific time interval, which consist in sending a 
constant voltage to LVDT, and measuring received voltage. Each movement of the LVDT tip, 
is recorder by data-logger as changing in voltage, and by calibrating of the device, variations 
in voltage can be transformed in variation in height. 
However, by using only LVDT and data-logger is hard to register the variation in height in 
real time. Considering this, the method was upgraded by adding of a mechanical dial indicator 
next to LVDT. This dial indicator have almost the same principle of work as LVDT, the 
difference is that in order to show the variation in distance it use a mechanical dial. The

4.2.1 
Double ring test 
 
Double ring test is a testing method used for testing the glass.  It is used for determining the 
comparative bending strength of the glass, excluding the effect of the edges. The principle of 
this setup and the designing method of the setup used for the purposes of this thesis will be 
presented further. 
A general idea about how this testing setup works would be that: a square or a circular 
specimen with a certain side length L or radius r3, resting on a supporting ring with radius r2 
shall be loaded by means of a loading ring with radius r1 arranged concentrically relative to 
the supporting ring as in Figure 12. 
 
 
Figure 12: Test arrangement indicated for a circular specimen (DSEN1288-5) 
Using this setup is very convenient for the purpose of this thesis, because the radial tangential 
stresses in the specimen decrease towards the edges of the glass, reducing the risk of failure in 
that point. Moreover, under certain loading F, depending on the setup and specimen 
geometry, in the central part of the convexly bent specimen surface, a tensile field is created 
which extends in all directions and is uniformly distributed. The area subjected to this field is 
bounded by the loading ring. And when the load F increase, the stress in the middle of the 
specimen also increase at a constant rate until the failure occurs. The specimen will fail in the 
most severely stressed surface region, but in the case of the glass specimen with a hole the 
most probable place of failure will be at the edge of this.

4.3 
Etching experimental plan 
 
As the main objective of this project was enhancement of the borehole surface quality by post 
drilling treatment, the chosen method was treatment of the surface with acid. As a reagent 
solution for etching was chosen Ammonium Hydrogen Difluoride solution of 15% 
concentration. It is well known that etching of the glass surface increase its strength of this, 
the effect of etching was studied before in [(B.Proctor, February 1962); (Kollli M., 
Hamidouche M., & Bouaouadja N., 2009) etc.] and the results were very prominent. The acid 
used for the purpose of this thesis, is a descendent of Hydrofluoric acid, which was previously 
used for etching of the glass. It have a high reactivity with the glass, and in fact by reacting 
with glass atoms, a part of them are removed from glass surface, generating by this 
smoothening and rounding of flows from glass surface.  
Because etching of the borehole was not done before, a new method was necessary to be 
created. Therefore, the experimental part was divided in to two groups of experiments: 
1. Finding the correct etching technique 
2. Determination of the minimum etching time for maximal result. 
In the first group of experiments, by a series of trials and analysing of the different methods of 
etching of the borehole, a new method was created. In this report, will be presented only the 
developed method of etching. Explanation of steps used in final method are presented shortly 
in Appendix C, and more detailed versions can be found in Annexes 1. 
The second group of experiments was divided into 3 steps. In each step different etching time 
were applied to the same borehole size, and afterwards tested with double ring testing setup. 
For each time of etching a group of 3 specimens was tested, considered to be the minimum 
necessary amount of specimens, in order to get relevant testing results. The aim of all this 
experiments was to determine the shape of Failure Load – Etching Time curve.  The steps 
which were done are: 
1. In first step were tested specimens with 5 and 16 hours of etching. This first two 
points, were chosen guiding on previously done experiment, where etching effect per

4.3.1 
Etching technique 
 
In previously studied works related to etching of the glass, the glass specimens were fully 
immersed in a bath with acid. For the purpose of this thesis a local etching was necessary; a 
new method was created in order to fulfil this condition. The idea with taping the borehole 
with tape was borrowed from the people who use etching for printing of certain drawings on 
the glass. This was done for avoiding etching of the surface around the borehole. The 
procedure used for etching, consists from several steps which are presented in Figure 15. 
 
Figure 15: Etching procedure steps 
 
17 
Technical University of Denmark – Department of Civil Engineering 
==PAGE:28==
Theory and method for experimental investigation

In the first step of the etching, the glass specimen is washed from different impurities resulted 
from drilling process using a jet of water, and dried using a jet of compressed air. After the 
specimen is dry, the borehole is taped with a piece of tape, in which previously was made a 
round hole, with should have the same diameter as the borehole. The hole in the tape should 
coincide with edges of borehole. After this, using a pipet the borehole is filled with acid and 
covered to prevent evaporation. Experience showed that water from acid still tends to 
evaporate, and in order to have a continuous etching, the acid should be added at least each 15 
minutes. 
The second step is performed after 30 minutes of etching. The acid is removed, and the tape 
around the borehole is changed because it tends to unglue in time. After a new peace of tape is 
placed around the borehole, using a pipet the hole is filled with fresh acid. The acid should be 
mixed afterwards using a plastic rod, this is done in order to dissolve formed crystals resulted 
from the reaction. The cover is placed above and if evaporation is observed, extra acid is 
admitted. 
The third step need to be done after each hour of etching, otherwise agglomerations of 
crystals will form, which will lead to defects in borehole surface and uneven etching. First of 
all, the acid is removed from the borehole and the tape is unglued from the surface. 
Afterwards the specimen is placed in an empty plastic bath, washed with a jet of water and 
afterwards dried using compressed air. The procedure of washing and drying need to be 
performed twice, to be shore that all the formed crystals are removed from the surface. All the 
wastes from washing and changing of acid are stored in a special waste container.  Whole 
procedure is repeated, in function of how many hours of etching are admitted for each 
specimen. 
A more detailed explanation of the purpose of each step of etching can be found in Appendix

solution for glass etching. 
For the purpose of this thesis, a solution of AHD with a concentration of 15 % is used. In 
order to obtain a solution with a certain concentration, it is necessary to dissolve a certain 
quantity of the AHD crystals in water, in dependence how much of final solution is needed.

performing all the steps of experiment.

consists 6.02xE5 mg/L according to (Lide D.R., 2005-2006), and this mean a concentration 
up to ~ 28%.  In order to obtain a solution with a 15% concentration, a simple mathematical

5.1 
Axis-symmetric model description 
 
Since measuring of the residual stresses at the edge of borehole is impossible, and because 
double ring test setup cannot output the stress at the borehole in time of failure. A Finite 
Element Method software ABAQUS/CAE 6.14 was used to determine this values. The 
distribution of stresses around the borehole became of interest, when deformations of the 
borehole form initial shape were observed. Due to changes induced in the surface of the 
borehole after etching, increasing in strength of this in comparison to surrounding surface was 
stated. In order to understand the behaviour of this phenomenon and find the distribution of 
stresses at the borehole, Finite Element characteristic to each new geometry for all periods of 
etching was created. 
For each geometry of the borehole, a parametric study of three types of stresses was studied. 
The distribution of the residual stresses resulted from tempering, stresses induced by double 
ring testing and combination of this. The flows and irregularities on the surface were 
disregarded due to impossibility to reproduce them and for simplifying the analysis. The 
geometry parameters used for modelling, were determined by visual measuring of the copy 
taken from the borehole using a calliper, thus they represent a close approximation of the real 
borehole shape. For better result 3D scanner can be used. 
5.2 
Numerical model parameters 
 
For investigation, a large plate with a drilling hole in the centre was considered. For 
modelling an axis-symmetric model was used, only half of the length with the whole 
thickness of the glass was considered.  The symmetry axis was considered in the middle of the 
hole. The model presents a reproduction of double ring testing setup static scheme.  The 
supporting system is considered the same as in double ring test, with supporting ring replaced 
by a support which blocks movement on vertical direction. The loading is assumed on a 
surface due to the fact that rubber ring are used and they tend to deform. The geometry which 
was used in models is presented in Figure 16.

glass surface. In this paragraph a study of the borehole change in function of etching time will 
be evaluated.

increase, with increasing of the drilling depth and diameter of the borehole. Considering this, 
can be assumed that effect of rounding have a bigger impact on the distribution of stresses at 
the edge, than increasing in depth and diameter. This happen because the etching at the 
surface of the borehole is more prominent than at the bottom. 
With increasing of the etching period, appears the risk that the depth of the borehole will 
increase until it will reach tension zone of tempered glass, this could lead to shattering of the 
entire glass. The compression zone consists 20% of the glass thickness on each side, this 
means that maximal depth until which etching could be done for 10 mm glass would be 2 
mm.  But knowing from (Catuna, 2014)and (Thorup, 2014)that while drilling in the glass the 
neutral axis tends to shift its position, the etching depth could go till 2.3 mm. In order to 
inshore that this happen also in case of etched boreholes, the shifting of neutral axis was 
measured in models for 16, 20 and 24 hours. The measurement showed that neutral axis 
shifted with approximatively 2.5 mm from initial position, this proves that it is valid also for 
this kind of geometry.

6.3.1 
Testing results 
 
In order to determine the effect of etching at borehole in a glass specimen, all the specimens 
were tested using double ring test setup. While glass specimen is tested using double ring 
setup, the side with the borehole is placed down and cantered, in order to face the supporting 
ring plate and get the borehole exact in the middle of the circle. The Figure 29 presents the 
positioning of glass specimen in the double ring test setup. By positioning glass like this, a 
continuous tension stress field will be created around the borehole surface, thus ensuring that 
the failure will happen at the borehole edge. However, in case the failure is generated outside 
the edge of the borehole, this means that the surface of the borehole has a higher strength then 
the rest of the glass surface, so the goal of this experiment is achieved.  The main aim of this 
thesis, was finding the minimum time when this phenomena will happen and correct 
procedure to achieve this.

6.4 
Possible errors 
 
While writing this thesis a series of experiments on glass specimens were done. While 
performing experimental work some things went wrong, this probably had an influence on the 
precision of the results which were obtained and the quality of the work which was done. 
This chapter will present the main sources of errors which were observed during experimental 
work and solutions for avoiding them. 
6.4.1 
Errors related to drilling process 
 
While drilling in the glass was performed, three main possible errors were observed which 
could lead to uneven shape of the borehole, sever chipping of the surface and errors in drilling 
depth. This information can be treated as precaution in case of repeating the process. 
Uneven shape of borehole -First thing which should be done before drilling is started fixing 
the glass panel to the table surface, tape or other fixing tools can be used. The reason why this 
need to be done is that even a slight movement of the glass panel in time of drilling will lead 
to uneven shape of the borehole and creation of sever chipping at the edge. 
Chipping at the edge of borehole – It is important to know is that the drilling need to be 
started slowly by very thin touches of the glass surface, until at least half of millimetre is 
drilled. From the experience obtained while working on this thesis, by doing like this chipping 
at the surface will be reduced to minimum. The avoiding of sever chippings of the edge is 
very important because as bigger defects are on the glass surface as longer time of etching in 
order to remove them is needed. By avoiding chipping the necessary time of etching can be 
decreased from 7 hours up to 5 hours in order to get a good result. This assumption was made

Errors in measuring drilling depth - Due to the fact that from LVDT, the drilling depth cannot

drilling depth. The problem is that while setting starting limit on the dial of measuring tool, it

indicator. In order to avoid this or at least minimize the error this difference in measurements 
need to be considered and compensated.

Errors related to etching process 
 
During etching of the glass specimen many changes in procedure of working were made. The 
first part of the experiments was related to determination of a correct and efficient method for 
etching. However, not all the problems related to procedure were possible to fix. The possible 
sources of errors which were not fixed while etching of the glass are presented further. The 
main reason why they were not fixed is impossibility to get necessary equipment in the time 
when experiments were done. 
Infiltration of acid under tape around borehole – Because of the late period when etching of 
the boreholes was performed, ordering of the special tape resistant to acid was impossible due

Conclusion 
 
 The work within this thesis presents early stage research of the effect induced by the etching 
with acid of the borehole surface on the overall strength of this. The main investigation tools 
used within this thesis were observation of the visible changing under microscope and testing 
of the specimens using double ring test setup for determination of strength increase. 
Numerical analysis was used as supporting tool for determining of the magnitude and 
distribution of stresses within the glass after etching. 
 A new method for etching of the borehole was created by a series of fails and trials basing on 
different already known methods. Main focus was putted on obtaining of a regulated smooth 
surface without defects. It was determined that applying of etching for a longer time, will 
induce high deformation in the borehole shape. The changings in geometry characteristic to 
different periods of etching were reproduced based on measurements made on copies taken 
from real specimens. The characteristic increase in volume of the borehole for each period of 
etching was computed by making 3D models in AutoCAD. Using determined volumes of 
etched glass, a variation of etched volume in function of etching time was plotted in to a 
graph. The graph presented approximated shape of the etching rate curve, based on which real 
distribution was assumed. 
For investigation of the residual stress redistribution in function of new geometry of borehole, 
Finite Element Modelling was used. By plotting the distribution of the residual stresses for 
each geometry, a decreasing in the maximal tangential stress at the edge of borehole was 
observed. This was generated by rounding effect of the edges induced by etching. In the same 
time due to increasing in depth, phenomena of neutral axis shifting towards the tensile zone 
was observed. Recorded shifting was with approximatively 2.5 %, meaning that etching can 
continue up to 22.5 mm without shattering of the glass. 
It was considered that while double ring testing, the surface around borehole is subjected to 
uniform tension, comparable to biaxial tension. Thus, the failure will happen at the weakest 
point in the glass surface. The testing was done on specimens with different time of applied 
etching. From testing results was found that after removing of all surface defects at the 
borehole, the resistance of the borehole surface increase. Due to failure in the surface around 
borehole it can be assumed that resistance of borehole surface is higher than resistance of 
surface around it. Complete removal of defects is made in between 6 and 7 hours of etching or 
until all the chippings from the edge are removed. After removal of all defects, failure load of 
the glass panels should be above the limit of 35 kN. This value represents the lowest 
registered load when failure started outside the borehole. It was registered a high variation in 
loads at which specimens fail , but this variation does not depend any more on the level of 
etching and is totally related to presence of the defects in the rest of the glass. While 
investigation of the tension stresses at the borehole in time of failure, a maximal values 
312.7MPa was recorded. This value is situated at the edge of the borehole and represents only 
the stress in moment of failure. This is not the actual resistance of the borehole surface, due to 
the fact that specimen failed in the surface around borehole. The actual resistance of the 
borehole surface cannot be measured in these conditions. But according to some previously 
done investigation the maximal mechanical resistance recorder from etching of glass reached

7.1 
Future work 
 
For the future works related to the topic of this thesis, the following things could be done in 
order to improve already determined results. 
Determination of more advanced and safe method for etching. 
The method of etching used for the purpose of this thesis had some limitations and issues 
which was impossible to fix due to lack of equipment. In order to avoid unwanted etching of 
the surface around the borehole, to get a borehole with a more uniform geometry, make 
etching safe and most important possible to be performed on site. A method which would 
inshore continuous flow of acid at the borehole and keeping all the process closed into one 
equipment should be done. My purpose of how it should be done can be found in Appendix 
H. 
Full scale modelling of etching process. 
 Due to impossibility to use 3D scanner while performing this thesis, it was impossible to 
reproduce exact geometry of the borehole changes after etching. But if a scanning of the 
surface would be made, this will give a 3D model of the surface with all the flows on it. By 
importing of these geometries in finite element programs, step by step process of etching 
would be reproduced in details. And by analysing of the changes induced in the glass surface 
an exact understanding of how the process of etching increase the strength of the glass can be 
found. Moreover, this would give an understanding of nature of stresses which are created in 
the glass while changings in surface are performed. 
Testing the etched boreholes at pull out and shear. 
It would be interesting to compare the results of the pull-out test and shear connection test 
with the one obtained from double ring testing, made with boreholes which were etched until 
they get free of defects. By testing the etched borehole at shear using a pin with an insert a 
better understanding of surface resistance increase of the borehole surface will be found. But 
for this a method of etching which would result in more uniform shapes of boreholes need to 
be found. Probably this can be made by the machine for etching purposed further.

 Calibration for small distance measurement  
For measuring the drilling depth a special measuring tool LVDT and data logger was used. 
The data-logger is a device which sends at specified interval of time a relative constant output 
to other electronic devices ( in this case LVDT) and reads back the voltage given by this 
device. The time interval used for measuring was 0.1 seconds, this time interval was chosen 
for a better precision of measurements. 
The voltage emitted by the data-logger has a tendency to fluctuate in time, even if the device 
is not connected to any measuring devices. This happens because the voltage from the supply 
is not always constant and it tends to fluctuate in time. In order to reduce the fluctuation, a 
longer warming up of the device can be applied or reducing the length of the cable which 
connect the data-logger and LVDT. 
The data-logger is able to measure only changes in voltage given by measuring device. In 
order to transform the voltage in to distance calibration of those measurements need to be 
done. 
The transformation procedure for measuring the distances is presented further. For the 
calibration a distance measuring an electronic calliper (see Figure 43) was used capable to 
measure distances with a precision up to 0.01mm. The previous mentioned LVDT was fixed 
to the calliper and connected to data-logger. The principle of transforming voltage in 
millimetres consists in: 
- 
Measuring the voltage outputted by the data-logger when there is not connected the 
LVDT through an empty connection. Corresponding to fluctuations in electricity  
- 
 Opening the jaws of calliper with a certain distance and making a record of voltage 
change corresponding to each variation in distance. In total 20 measurements were 
done, with each time increasing the distance. 
- 
All the values were after inserted in a excel file, plotted for making a linear 
approximation and determining the line equation. Using this equation, the difference 
in voltage recorded by data-logger can be transformed into length. 
Figure 42 represents the linear approximation of the values determined from the 
calibration procedure and from the equation obtained a constant is obtained. By 
multiplying the constant (12.093) with the voltage variation the corresponding distance

Etching procedure – main issues and their solution 
One of the main aims of this thesis was finding of the correct way of etching for boreholes in 
tempered glass. It cannot be stated that this is the best method in order to have the best results. 
However, the method described in this thesis was one developed through a series of steps, 
which were developed from general knowledge taken from experience of others. The method 
was developed by analysing different methods and by solving different problems observed 
during experiments. For analysing of the results an optical microscope was used, and pictures 
presented in this work are made using it. 
Due to high number of pictures and data received obtained while performing this experiments, 
the full description of all steps with detailed photos can be found in Annex 1 on the DVD 
handed in with this report. Here will be presented only the explanation of procedure steps 
presented before. 
Why the borehole need to be covered? 
While performing the experiments in order to find the correct method, it was observed that 
acid is evaporating very fast while working under fume cupboard. A solution for this was 
putting a plastic cover on the top like in Figure 44. 
 
Figure 44: Cover type used for etching 
Why the acid net to be added each 15 minutes and why it should be renewed each 30 min? 
The acid is added every 15 minutes, because even if it is covered the acid still tends to 
evaporate and while reacting with glass it is consumed. The acid is changed each 30 minutes 
to remove already reacted substance and increase the rate of etching. Also mixing of acid with 
the rod helps to dissolve formed crystals from the walls and increase the rate of etching. 
Why is necessary to put tape around borehole? 
This is done with two purposes, by using a piece of tape around the borehole is avoided 
etching of the surface around and the shape of the etched edges of hole remains round. It is 
very important to have a round hole in the tape. If the tape have a hole with different shape,

was used. 
 
Figure 45: Borehole Surface edge shape change by using tape with rhombic hole 
 
Why the specimen is dried using a jet compressed air and washed using a jet of water?  
From the first experiment was determined, that applying of compressed air on the surface of 
the borehole, dry fast the crystals formed on the surface of the borehole, and transform them 
in to powder, which is simply blown away from the surface. Therefore, if the removal of the 
crystals is done only by compressed air, there remain regions where crystals are not removed 
completely and with each hour of etching they tend to agglomerate like is presented in Figure 
46.  
 
Figure 46: Crystal agglomerations at the bottom of the borehole 
This crystals were after removed by washing them with a jet of water, until they completely 
disappeared. It was observed that under them the etching stopped, and this leaded to 
formation of deformation of borehole surface like in Figure 47.

This phenomenon could lead to apparition of unnecessary stresses in the surface, due to 
creation of irregularities. Thus a decision that the borehole should be washed using a jet of 
water and dried using a jet of compressed air after each hour, until all the crystals are 
completely removed was adopted. This will inshore a uniform etching of the borehole and 
will help to avoid apparition of defects. By analysing of the surface under microscope was 
determined, that application of compressed air and of water jet for removal the crystals, do not 
affect somehow the surface of the glass.

Appendix H 
Etching machine – Purposed design 
 
In the time when the method for etching was studied, an idea about how to improve the 
procedure and get boreholes with nice regulated shape and make etching process 100% safe 
was developed. This method would give possibility to perform etching faster, more accurate, 
safe and principal possible to be made on site.  
The principle of this method consists in creation of a special etching machine. The schematic 
drawings of the main parts of the machine are presented in Figure 56 and Figure 57. The 
principle of work is explained further. 
 
Figure 56: General Scheme of Etching Machine 
 
Figure 57: Detail of the part connected to glass 
70 
Technical University of Denmark – Department of Civil Engineering 
==PAGE:81==
Appendices 
                                                                                   Appendix H 
 
The principle is: the tank is filled with acid used for etching, preferable hydrofluoric acid in 
combination with sulphuric acid and hydrochloric acid in order to dissolve insoluble fluoride 
reaction products and inshore a better result.  
After the tank is filled on about 70 %, the etching tool presented in detail A is putted around 
the borehole and pressed on the glass. In this time the pump for acid is switched on and 30 
seconds are waited until all the pipes are free of air. After all the system is free of air second 
pump for creation of negative pressure in the system is turned on. Due to lover pressure in the 
system the equipment from detail A will stick to the glass surface, like this inshore that the 
system is closed. Due to higher pressure at inflow pipe acid will get in to borehole and will 
etch the surface uniformly. All the impurities and reaction resulted materials will pass back in 
to acid tank through outflow pipe. Here all the crystals being heavier will go to the bottom of 
the tank and the cycle repeats. 
When the etching is finished the acid pump is switched off as well as negative pressure pump. 
When the inflow pipe is taken out from the etching tool from detail A, the air will start to get 
in because of negative pressure in the system, like this ensuring removal of acid from 
borehole and drying of it. The borehole is afterwards washed with water and checked for 
presence of defects.
