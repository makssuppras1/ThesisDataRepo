Abstract
During the last years, the constantly growing demand for high speed internet has been driv-
ing optical communication systems to exploit newer dimensions to increase the transmission.
The analytical approaches based on the inverse scattering Transform (IST) method that enabled
charaterization of light scattering with rectangular shaped potential barrier are discussed. It
was observed that the approach has the potential to address the inverse scattering problem nu-
merically. The limitation can be overcome by employing a radically different appproach that
is compatible with the nature of the nonlinear optical channel. We therefore propose a model
to use the eigenvalues, from the Inverse Scattering Transform (IST) associated with non-linear
Schrodinger equation which desribes signal propogation. The implementation of model, based
on a generalised NLS equation taking into account linear and nonlinear intraction between the
propogating modes like multi-mode ﬁber(MMF). Inverse scattering problem for two compo-
nent model was presented to evaluate the impact of such interactions on the propogating of
modulated signals. Model is implemented in MATLAB and validated agianst simulation and ex-
perimental workdone is reported. We investigate presented model by imposing condition such
as initial value problem, ﬁxed boundary value problem and periodic boundary values problem.
These are all implemented in Matlab and their related results are presented.

Introduction
The behaviour of the most of nonlinear evolution equations exhibits asymptotic solutions. The
most of solitary waves do not interact mutually but some how in special cases there exist a class
of equations, called integrable systems, in which these wave solutions interact by one another
and passing through each other destructively, these solutions are called Solitons [1]. One of the
best example of these integrable equation is the Korteweg-de Vries equation and the other is the
nonlinear schrodinger equation (NLSE). The class of these nonlinear evolution equations also
include other systems of partial differential equations (PDEs).
The nonlinear Schrödinger equation (NLSE) noted in the literature as the dispersive nonlinear
partial differential equation that plays a unique role in the modeling and understanding of the
wave phenomena relevant to many branches of nonlinear physics. The speciﬁc question of the
formation of waves has been recently extensively studied in this context. The determination of
exact analytic solutions of the focusing one dimensional nonlinear Schrödinger equation (1D-
NLSE) prototyping solutions is now considered as the problem of central importance. Here we
address this question from the perspective of the inverse scattering transform (IST) method that
relies on the integrable nature of the wave equation.
In next chapter we introduce the setup for electro-magnetic tube which is full of positive and
negative charges (ions). We analysis the behaviour of these charges in semi-permeable mem-
brane barrier. Then we discuss two component scattering problem for this setup producing
waves for two components and apply the AKNS scheme on solitons solutions and investigate
these solutions numerically, and will brieﬂy discuss the behaviour of the solution of the two

1.2
Literature and some Deﬁnitions
AKNS method
A method introduced by Ablowitz, Kaup, Newell, and Segur in 1973 that identiﬁes the nonlinear
partial differential equation (NPDE) associated with a given ﬁrst-order system of linear ordi-
nary differential equations (LODEs) so that the initial value problem (IVP) for that NPDE can be
solved by the inverse scattering transform (IST) method.[5]
Direct scattering problem
The problem of determining the scattering data corresponding to a given potential in a differ-
ential equation. [5]
Integrability
A NPDE is said to be integrable if its IVP can be solved via IST.[5]
Inverse scattering problem
The problem of determining the potential that corresponds to a given set of scattering data in a
differential equation.[5]
Inverse scattering transform
A method introduced in 1967 by Gardner, Greene, Kruskal, and Miura that yields a solution to
the IVP for a NPDE with the help of the solutions to the direct and inverse scattering problems
for an associated Linear scattering problem.[5]
Scattering Data
The scattering data associated with a linear scattering problem usually consists of a reﬂection
coefﬁcient which is a function of some spectral parameter say (ζ), a ﬁnite number of constants
ζj that correspond to the poles of the transmission coefﬁcient in the upper half complex plane,
and the bound-state norming constants whose number for each bound-state pole ζj is the same
as the order of that pole. It is desirable that the potential in the linear scattering problem is
uniquely determined by the corresponding scattering data and vice versa.[5]
Soliton
The part of a solution to an integrable NPDE due to a pole of the transmission coefﬁcient in the
upper half complex plane. The term soliton was introduced by Zabusky and Kruskal in 1965
to denote a solitary wave pulse with a particle-like behavior in the solution to the Korteweg-de

Inverse Scattering Transform
The main idea behind the IST method is that each Integrable nonlinear partial differential equa-
tion is associated with a linear scattering problem containing a parameter (Spectral parameter)
and solution to the NPDE appears as the Potential in the correspondin linear scattering prob-
lem. NPDEs are classiﬁed as integrable in the sense that their corresponding IVPs can be solved
with the help of IST [3]. The simplest form of normalised NLS equation can be given by
iut +uxx +|u|2u = 0
(2.1)
In the absence of the nonlinear term in NLS equation, the basic mathematical model to ana-
lyze any kind of pulse propogation in linear media in the standard fourier transform method.
By decomposing the input pulse u(t=0,x) into a set of linear fourier modes, we ﬁrst reduce the
problem of wave propogation to atrivial oscillatory evolution of each Fourier component and
then construct the pulse shape at any propogation distance x by combining all frequency com-
ponents. In the case of a homogenous linear equaion, the only possible Fourier modes are the
plane waves of the the form eiωx where ω denotes the Fourier component and called as modal
eigenvalue [3]. We focus on this equation (2.1) because it cab be solved exactly using the in-
verse scattering method. Since all values of ω are possible in a linear homogeneous medium,
the eigenvalue spectrum is continuous, and the solution for negligible nonlinear effects is com-
peletly discussed in next section ( In AKNS Scheme)

5.2
Conclusion
This work clearly depicts how transmission in a single rectangular potential barrier can be stud-
ied in terms of barrier height and width. Then we extended the proposed model from single to
multiple piece-wise potential barrier using the example of q0 = sech(x). Our work shows how
numerically these rectangle shape pulses transmit throughout the system. We extracted the
eigenvalues by imposing periodic boundary conditions. The advantage of this work is the sim-
plicity in approach and deriving the expressions to obtain important properties of the rectangu-
lar potential barriers. Such results may be used in modeling barriers in devices where constant
transmission is to be achieved by varying the barrier depending on the height and the barrier
width appropriately for transmission. The present work is in continuation of a previous study of
quantum rectangular potential barriers.
