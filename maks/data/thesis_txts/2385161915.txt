Abstract 
The purpose of this work is to take a step toward clarifying what 5G really mean, expanding 
on some of the use case scenarios that 5G could be enable in terms of network speed, 
capacity, latency and Machin type communication based on requirements. This work 
generally explains the previous technology such as 1G, 2G, 3G, 4G in order to understand 5G 
better, including its requirements and challenges and each challenge reflected by scenario. 
Based on the requirements. I have used mmWave frequencies ranges and MIMO Techniques 
and also covering the 5G new interface with the purpose of lowering the interference and 
latency. 
In the work, I also take base on the work of 3 teams NGMN, METIS and 3GPP with different 
scenarios but with the common goal of highest capacity for the user at lowest cost and 
minimized latency. Based on the 3 teams I’ve also made my own scenario such a UDN and 
rural urban which are used in areas with a high user density, trying to provide a higher 
capacity with a fewer number of sites in the system, higher spectral efficiency and wider 
bandwidth. 
The integration of these new radio concepts, such as Ultra Dense networks, massive MIMO, 
ultra-reliable and device to device communications will allow 5G to support the expected 
increase in mobile data volume while broadening the range of application domains that 
mobile system can support beyond 2020.

1.1 Introduction 
5G stands for fifth generation of mobile system. The future of mobile telecommunication is 
5G, it evolution because of fast growth demand by user and data traffic has rapidly increased 
in mobile communication .5G have huge capability with low latency about 1ms.5G will be 
deployed as expectation at 2020 and have very big role in digitalization of world by 
connection billions user with internet at same time. 
5G will come after 4G technology the main differentiator between 4G and 5G is capacity and 
latency, One reason for developing 5G technology over 4G it is speed of delivery could be 
between 10Gbps and 100Gbps with latency about 1ms , It will provide fast broad band speeds 
with enough capacity it does not matter how many people are connected at the same time 
without drop in speed or connection ,at present 4G is capable of between 40ms and 60ms 
which is low latency but not enough to provide real time services. 
5G are broadening their spectrum to higher frequency bands above 6GHz to support a higher 
data rate up to multigigabit per second, which means  5G uses higher frequency signal will be 
much less congested and will be vital for use with internet of thing .In order to enlarge the 
frequency band to accommodate the emerging data traffic a new frequency band should 
define in 5 G mobile communication system is  millimetre-wave band in the range 30-300 
GHz range is considered as a candidate band frequency which is counted as one option to 
increasing capacity. The two main contributions to higher frequency communication first one 
summarizes the candidate frequency band including licensed (classical type of spectrum) and 
unlicensed frequency bands. Second one new network architecture for higher frequency 
communication is proposed. 
One good feature used in 5G as already used in 4G technology is MIMO (multiple input 
multiple output) which is defined as the heart of improving spectral efficiency per links in 
network. The 5G cellular network consisted of 4G base stations, mmWave base station and 
mobile devices.

2.1 First Generation (1G) 
The first generation of mobile network technology is based on analog cellar system, it 
designed for audio transmission appearing in the early 1980, The system was deployed 
entirely for voice traffic with a speed of up to 2.4kpbs, used analogue systems with single 
side band (SSB) Modulation.  
One major problem with 1G was security within the systems the analog signals didn’t allow 
encryption method to be used, the someone not just hear the conversation between user but 
also can steal user information because of lack security interference was common leading to a 
decrease in sound quality, the first-generation mobile system drawbacks are: had lack 
capacity (limit capacity), low speeds and poor voice quality, poor battery life, phone size big 
and Frequent call drop. 
This system based on a technology known as Advance Mobile Phone Services (AMPS) was 
called 1G that time (1983). The AMPS were frequency division multiple access.  
 
2.2 Second Generation(2G) 
With introduction of 2G in the early 1990 we moved away from analog and jumped into the 
digital world. The second generation of mobile communication is GSM Global System for 
Mobile is digital system and designed just for voice services and short message services 
(SMS) over circuit switch with TDMA and FDMA as access methods, it uses 200KHZ 
channels with 8 timeslots each. Voice rates of 13kpbs in full rate while data limited to 
14.4kbps. 
GSM system improve many aspects of analog system such as security by introducing 
encryption and ciphering to avoid eavesdropping, also mobility management services and the 
handover and location area update concepts are more developed furthermore authentication 
methods are employed to verify that a user can register in the network. 
The main entities of a GSM network are the Base Station Subsystem (BSS), Network 
Switching Subsystem(NSS) and Operation Subsystem (OSS). 
2G drawbacks: need to improve transmission quality, unable to support complex data such as 
video, lack system capacity, Cell towers has limited coverage area.

2.3 Third Generation(3G) 
3G standard is UMTS which is proposed by 3GPP standard which define integrating circuit 
switching and packet switching in network. The idea of 3G is mobile broadband the network 
more optimizes towards data rather than with speeds up to 2Mbps, UMTS enable data rate 
384kbps. 3G introduced broadband service for first time. 
Universal Mobile Telecommunication System(UMTS) supports internet protocol 
(IP)multimedia services. UMTS is W- CDMA based in uplink as well as down link. The 
duplexing method is FDD uplink and downlink operate in separate frequency bands 5MHz 
each. Use circuited switch for voice and packet data for data transmission. Instead of TDMA 
and FDMA like in the 2G systems used.  
UMTS employs CDMA as new radio access method, which allows all users to transmit 
simultaneously, each cell in UMTS has a bandwidth of 5MHz, although more capacity can be 
added by allocating more frequency 
The UMTS network component architecture which is main three elements are: 
1.UE domain (mobile user): Which contain mobile equipment with SIM card 
2.UTRAN: Stands for UMTS Terrestrial Radio Access Network and UMTS(UTRAN), 
which includes the UMTS base stations known as nodeB. 
3.Core domain: 
In UMTS technology enhance mobility management also security and encryption improved 
methods. Changing QPSK modulation of UMTS to 16QAM can potentially double bit rate 
thus require better SNR.

2.4 Fourth Generation(4G) 
The new generation come after 3G of networks includes Long Term Evolution(LTE) and 
LTE -Advanced. 
LTE is one of the fastest growing mobile broad band communication technology in world 
now a day and will reach nearly 1 billion connections in 2015 and 3.6 billion in 2020 
The first defined by 3GPP release 8, 4G is appeared because of higher bit rate exceeds 100 
Mbps, higher capacity and low latency, spectral efficiency two to four times than previous 
technology. One more LTE features is flexibility which packet switch optimized and 
bandwidth flexibility with variable allocation from 1.5 MHz to 20 MHz. LTE multiple access 
schemes are Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiple Access(OFDMA) in the downlink 
which is theoretical peak bit rates of 300Mbps, while in the uplink use Single Carrier-
FDMA(SC-FDMA) which is theoretical peak bit rates 75 Mbps 
Bandwidth scalable from 1.5 MHz to 20 MHz, this bandwidth is suitable for operator to 
allocate different services based on spectrum. LTE improve spectral efficiency in 3G of 
wireless system. The important concept used in LTE is MIMO refer to possibility to use more 
than one antenna both in transmitter and receiver side to increase bit rate, in order to work 
need high SNR the OFDM nature high SNR because of utilizing frequency and time division 
multiplexing by this way data rate increase.[8].

2.6 The need for 5G 
The broad band challenges requires massive traffic capacity, higher downlink and uplink 
speeds, better spectrum efficiency, and the flexibility to use new spectrum [2 ].In addition the 
IoT will need to support billions of connections with reduced signalling and low energy 
consumption, and it is mission -critical services need very low latency, high reliability, high 
availability, and very secure connections addressing all these needs will unleash new 
powerful innovative applications and very creative use case. 
5G will have to address the complexity of advanced communication modules and different 
antenna types with different beam-forming capabilities. Examples of this multi antenna 
schemes with large antenna arrays, massive MIMO and clustering of millimetre-wave access 
points addressing the coverage and mobility needs by using beam steered antenna patterns. 
Most depend on use case and deployment scenario it is needed to support different antenna 
types, for example omni-directional antenna patterns, low/high gain beam forming antenna 
pattern, flexible/fixed beam forming pattern and analog/digital/hybrid beam forming is 
required depend on the use case and deployment scenario. The new features distinguish 5G 
from other networks system is native and efficient support of communication schemes like 
multi connectivity, Multi connectivity is a key technology to fulfil 5G requirements related to 
data rate, latency, reliability, and availability. Also, 5G will support novel schemes like the 
network-controlled device to device (D2D) communication including point to point, multi-
cast, broadcast communication.

2.7 Coverage and Capacity 
The two main concepts of a mobile network are coverage and capacity, counts as the two 
main drivers of mobile systems. The coverage and capacity (Traffic handling) are two 
important network parameters for any mobile network operator because related to the quality 
of services which offered by mobile operator to users. 
2.6.1 Coverage 
The one of the important objective due deploying a network is coverage, coverage represent a service 
area of mobile network is covered by installing a sufficient number of base station. A base station 
given coverage and capacity to the overall network and both of them play an important role with 
deploying every generations deployment. The coverage directed related to the propagation properties 
and link budget, and direct relate with spectrum usage and the cell size, the coverage of a cell is 
determined by the capability to provide a required power at the receiver, and this power related to the 
link budget, the link budget direct related to the frequency band as used in the deployment system. In 
the scenarios part will explain the link budget and propagation models according the frequencies 
bands usage. about the indoor coverage some special types of cells are deployed inside the building 
such as femto and Pico cells as the two types of small cells will be widely deploy in 5G technology. 
The indoor coverage suffers from the penetration loss through windows specially in office 
building[9].

6.1 Description 
The purpose of defending the new air interface in 5G system network is how to implement 
the amount of network capacity with fewer a number of sites by using newer wave form and 
use higher modulation level in order improving spectral efficiency of overall system, 
reaching the peak data rate is a target as well. 5G technology require the flexible and 
adaptable new air interface to support different and diverse application to meet the 5G 
requirements as well. New air interface consists of building blocks and configuration 
mechanisms such as adaptive wave waveform, adaptive frame structure, adaptive coding and 
modulation family and adaptive multiple access schemes with these mechanisms the air 
interface able to support the future wide variety of users services spectrum band and traffic 
level [17]. 
The key technology components include new wave form technology Filter Orthogonal 
Frequency Division Multiplexing (Filter - OFDM), a new multiple access technology Spare 
Code Multiple Access (SCMA), the full duplex mode and Massive MIMO technology, the 
purpose of design the new air interface, is to improve connectivity, reduce the latency, and 
improve the spectral efficiency

7 Millimetre Wave Wireless System 
Millimetre wave is one of the key potential of 5G technology which is expected to provide 
hundreds of megahertz bandwidth for wireless transmissions, the mobile data rates can be 
expanded to multigigabit per second range. The short wavelength of the mmWave band 
allows the integration of more antennas into the same physical dimension ,thus enabling the 
usage of MIMO technique [27]. It can be considering the mmWave band in the range 30-300 
GHz range is candidate for new radio bands for 5G mobile communication system, the 
wavelength is between 1mm to 10mm, which is a large number of spectrums that are 
potentially available. In the past or previous technologies millimetre wave were accounted as 
unsuitable for using in radio technology because of the high propagation losses such a high 
frequencies and their poor propagation through obstacles environment [28]. Because of the 
atmosphere and rain mmWave are mainly used in indoor environment and in small cell 
access  The purpose of using mmWave in mobile radio access is to improve the capacity, 
mmWave supports high capacity in particular areas and go hand to hand with other 5G 
technology like advance small cell network, advance antenna system, adaptive coding and 
modulation and multi radio access technology ,by the way integrating the ultra-dense network 
and Heterogenous network increase  network capacity 1000 times without increasing power 
consumption and infrastructure deployment cost by 2020. [28] 
In millimetre wave band a large region of spectrum are an available, it can be consider the 
28GHz and 38 GHz are ideal range for mmWave because of lower propagation loss .mm 
wave bands can support massive peak data rates delivering these data rate for end to end 
service [29]

8.4.1    METIS Scenarios 
METIS (Mobile and wireless communication Enablers for Twenty – twenty Information Society),it 
has consortium of 29 partners, spanning from network operator and telecom manufacturers the 
METIS project aims at developing a system concept that delivers the necessary efficiency and 
scalability, investigating key technology components to support the system  
MEITS 2020 team introduces five scenarios and reflect one specific challenge each that the 
5G system will address 1000x capacity. The scenario as described below: 
1. Amazingly fast: Focuses on high data rate. Amazing fast providing very high data rates 
for future mobile broadband user experience instantaneous connectivity without delay. 
2. Super real time and reliable connection: focuses on new applications and use cases with 
very strict requirements on latency and reliability. 
3. Best experience follows you: focuses on end users on the move with high levels of 
experience. Mobility will be a key factor in future deployments it require reliable user 
experience.  
4. Great service in a crowed: Focus on mobile broadband experience even in the very 
crowded area and conditions such as shopping mall stadiums and concerts. 
5. Ubiquitous things communicating :Focuses on efficient handling of a very large number 
of devices including Machin type communication[37] . 
The purpose of METIS scenarios is to be defined the problem space and with focus on its 
requirements they are mostly end user centric, but aspects relevant for other important actors 
such as society and mobile network operators, the METIS aimed to explain each challenge is 
illustrated by scenario. It is described from end user perspective and it illustrates a 
fundamental challenge. [37].

𝐿= 𝐿𝐹𝑆𝐿 + 𝐴𝑀𝑈− 𝐻𝑀𝐺−𝐻𝐵𝐺− 𝑆𝑢𝑚 𝐾𝐶𝑜𝑟𝑟𝑒𝑐𝑡𝑖𝑜𝑛

  𝐿 : The medium path loss (dB)

𝐻𝑀𝐺: Mobile Station antenna height gain factor

𝐾𝐶𝑜𝑟𝑟𝑒𝑐𝑡𝑖𝑜𝑛: Correction factor gain such as type of environment, water surface.

Due to poor propagation characteristics of the mmWave band it is difficult to secure and 
strong good channel condition and sufficient system coverage in the central system network 
as mentioned earlier the poor propagation might be cause the diffraction, scattering, 
refraction and penetration or free space path loss in both line of sight and non -line of sight 
condition then to achieve high performance of the network and compensate the path loss it is 
important to deploy the massive antenna system(MAS) This is because of beam patterns 
according to the MAS closely correlate with the SINR level the mobile device experiences, 
the level of the SNIR determines the system coverage of the network in the central network 
the coverage defined that the SINR level predicted to over -0.8 Db [42] 
  
8.7.2 System Capacity of the central network: 
The Massive Antenna Structure (MAS) is a key factor for achieving the capacity of the 
central network because the MAS directly affect transmitted signal power obtained by 
antenna gain and reduce the interference between neighboring beams. The MAS and beam 
forming are adopted in the network radio resources are spatially reused and system capacity 
can increase as the number of mobile user increase [42]. Beamforming technology can be 
considered to improve the spectral efficiency of mobile communication network.

9. Conclusion 
5G is the next step in the evolution of mobile communication and will be a key component of 
network society. The capability of 5G wireless access must extend far beyond those of 
previous generations of mobile communications network. The capability of 5G include very 
high data rate, machine to machine communication, very low latency, ultra-high reliability 
and usage of new spectrum range.5G wireless access needs to support massive increase of 
traffic because of high demanding by users.5G wireless access will be realized by the 
evolution of LTE technology integrated with new radio access. The key successful 
technology component of 5G wireless access include access and backhaul integration, 
flexible use spectrum, multi antenna transmission and flexible duplex. 
5G enable a wide range connectivity many applications with new challenges, each challenge 
reflected by scenarios some of the proposed by NGMN team, METIS Team and our proposal 
scenario as in rural and Ultra Dense environments  
The number of users increase for ultra-dense network as used the HetNet type of cells until 
2020 then by using the mmWave spectrum range that provides wider bandwidth can provide 
1000 times the capacity based on 5G requirments but these range of frequency have

to improve the spectral efficency and capacity, on the other side there are disadvantages such

other techniques either using the MIMO beam forming or use higher modulation scheme. Or 
using some mmWave model to componsate the loss as we get due propagation.

10. Future Work 
In the above section, we have been focusing on two different scenarios, rural environment 
and ultra-dense deployment (UDN). The ultra-dense network matches with areas that has a 
high traffic volume such as big cities, stadiums and shopping malls. Then on the other hand, 
rural environment reflects areas with a lower traffic volume such as small villages with low 
population.  
As mentioned earlier, teams such as NGMN, METIS and 3GPP all workout different 
scenarios but trying to reach a common goal, we are aiming for the same goal with our 
scenarios by trying to reflect on one specific challenges and requirements. 
We have reached the conclusion for the future of mobile network that HetNets cell 
deployment are the best use for 5G. HetNets cells increase the network capacity as well as 
decrease the interference between cells with the usage of beamforming MIMO technique.  
The proposed solutions would a combination of both scenarios, in crowded areas small cells 
are deployed and using of mmWave frequency range which offers a wider bandwidth, higher 
data rate and higher capacity. In crowded areas, massive MIMO technique are used for 
increased spectral efficiency. 
The lower dense areas, previously called rural environment, are covered by micro cells which 
covers a big range, around 5 kilometres, with a frequency of 6 GHz. For higher spectral 
efficiency, along with increasing bandwidth we need higher order of modulation. Some level 
of MIMO technique is required in the edge between the cells to be able to provide around 100 
Mbps. 
We have reached the conclusion that the use of HetNets, dynamic and flexible air interface, 
higher modulation schemes and different working frequencies above 6 GHz allows us to give 
the user a higher capacity. 
Spectral efficiency: Spectral efficiency which measured in bits per hertz, measure the 
efficiency of spectrum use. With this value can easily calculate the amount of data bandwidth 
available in a given amount of spectrum. Peak spectral efficiency is determined by the 
highest throughput a technology can deliver in amount of spectrum, occurring with the 
highest-order modulation scheme available and the least amount of coding [18].Higher order 
modulation produces a good quality of signal the radio link can employ higher order 
modulation thus increasing the throughput such as 64 (QAM) Quadrate Amplitude 
Modulation handle conveys six bits per radio symbol compare to 16 (QAM) Quadrate 
Amplitude Modulation conveys 4 bits.[18]. 
The modulation speed is not only factor that influence the overall spectral efficiency of the 
system because with higher order modulation will increase the interference and values of the 
Signal To Noise Ratio (SNR) [19]
