Abstract
The design of building constructions using prefabricated concrete elements is usu-
ally based on a deck-beam-column composition with deck-elements resting on beams
supported by columns. The structural system results in thick deck structures that
is undesirable as it either increases the storey height or reduces the ﬂoor-to-ceiling
height with reduced comfort and daylight as a consequence.
In this project the concept of an integrated beam in SL-deck elements is investigated.
To ease the design process it is investigated whether the calculation methods stated in
Eurocode 2 can be applied. The section forces of the structure are obtained through
basic static considerations where the external forces are transferred to the supports
via the integrated beam.
The applied design theory is veriﬁed by full-scale experiments of the concept. The
production of the specimens are observed and described. The load capacity, deﬂection
behaviour and fracture mechanics are investigated in the experiments.
The experiments are performed for specimens comprised of three SL27-deck elements
with integrated beams joined by post-tensioning. The test specimen is installed can-
tilevered on two column supports and ﬁxed with tension connections.
Forces are
applied as point loads to the cantilevered deck on the center element in order to
maximize the section forces in the element joints. The forces are applied using dis-
placement controlled hydraulic pistons.
A ﬁnite element method model (FEM) is produced to be able to investigate the be-
haviour of the integrated beam concept on other structures. The model is based on
the investigated test specimen. To verify the model the response is compared to the
deﬂection behaviour obtained in the experiments. The model is also used to evaluate
the locations of critical stress concentrations in both the concrete and reinforcement
of the specimen.
The results of the experiments show that the calculation methods applied are con-
servative and can be used to design structures with the integrated beam concept
incorporated. The fracture mechanism of the specimens is predominantly a torsional
failure in the joints with rotation of the center element followed by the expected
v

bending failure in the integrated beam. The failure mechanism is considered ductile
since large deﬂections with visible cracks are obtained for the maximum load. The
deﬂections of the test specimen shows good compliance with both the theory of simply
supported beams and the deﬂection behaviour of the FEM-model. This result can be
used to estimate the deﬂection behaviour of the integrated beam in the serviceability
limit state.

Resum´e
Projektering af bygningskonstruktioner med præfabrikerede betonelementer opbygges
oftest som en dæk-bjælke-søjle konstruktion, hvor dæk-elementer hviler p˚a bjælker,
der understøttes af søjler. Dette konstruktionssystem giver tykke dækkonstruktioner,
hvilket er uhensigtsmæssigt, da det enten øger etagehøjden eller reducerer loftshøjden
hvilket forringer komforten og dagslysindfaldet.
I dette projekt undersøges konceptet med en integreret bjælke i SL-dæk elementer. For
at lette designprocessen undersøges det, om beregningsmetoderne angivet i Eurocode
2 kan anvendes. Snitkræfterne bestemmes ved anvendelse af grundlæggende statiske
principper, hvor de ydre kræfter overføres til søjlerne via den integrerede bjælke.
Den anvendte beregningsteori er veriﬁceret ved hjælp af fuldskala forsøg. Der er ført
tilsyn med produktionen af test elementerne, hvilket beskrives i rapporten. De bereg-
nede bæreevner, udbøjninger og brudmekanismer undersøges i forsøgene.
Forsøgene udføres p˚a testelementer best˚aende af tre SL-dæk med en integreret bjæl-
ke, der er samlet med efterspænding. Testelementet monteres udkraget p˚a to søjler
og fastspændes med trækstænger. Lasterne p˚aføres som punktlaster p˚a det udkragede
centerelement for at maksimere de snitkræfter, der forekommer i samlingerne mellem
elementerne. Lasterne p˚aføres ved hjælp af ﬂytningsstyrede hydrauliske stempler.
En ﬁnite element metode model (FEM) fremstilles for at kunne undersøge opførslen af
konceptet med en integreret bjælke i andre konstruktioner. Modellen opbygges base-
ret p˚a de undersøgte testelementer. For at veriﬁcere modellen sammenlignes opførslen
med udbøjningerne i eksperimenterne. Modellen bruges ogs˚a til at undersøge, hvor
der opst˚ar kritiske spændinger i beton og armering.
Resultaterne af forsøgene viser, at de anvendte beregningsmetoder er konservative og
kan bruges til at projektere konstruktioner, der indkorporerer konceptet med en inte-
greret bjælke. Brudmekanismen i forsøgene er primært et torsionsbrud i samlingerne
med rotation af centerelementet efterfulgt af bøjningsbrud i den integrerede bjælke.
Brudmekanismen er et sejt brud, da der opn˚as store udbøjninger og synlige revner i te-
stelementet for brudlasten. Udbøjningerne i testelementerne er sammenlignelige med
b˚ade teorien for simpelt understøttede bjælker og udbøjningerne fra FEM-modellen.

Chapter 1
Project description
The present project is based on the use of the Super-Light Deck (SL-deck) combined
with the pearl chain principle to obtain an integrated beam in pre-cast deck elements.
Generally pre-cast decks are supported by underlying pre-cast concrete-, steel- or
composite beams mounted on columns. The drawbacks of these solutions are thicker
ﬂoor structure and/or expensive elements. To avoid these drawbacks a solution with
an integrated beam in the SL-deck is investigated and presented in the master thesis
Edge Beam for SL-decks [1]. The principle of the solution is to replace the light-
weight concrete blocks in the end zones with reinforced strong concrete forming an
integrated beam. The elements are installed on temporary supports. Once an entire
ﬂoor is positioned, the elements are post-tensioned in the integrated beam using the
pearl chain principle. Afterwards the deck is ﬁxed to the columns and the temporary
supports can be removed.
The scope of this project is to verify the calculation principles of the suggested solu-
tion for a SL27 deck construction [2]. The method is tested in full-scale experiments
in order to include the practical aspects of construction and to obtain a realistic
load capacity and failure mechanism. Furthermore a numerical ﬁnite element method
(FEM) model is produced for comparison and to be able to investigate the behaviour
of the integrated beam concept on other structures.

3.2 Design Procedure
Design of Test Specimen
A specimen size of 7.2 × 2.3 m with a column distance of L1 = 6 m (Figure 2.2) is

supports are built in steel since it is not of interest for the current project. This choice

3.2
Design Procedure
Design of the specimen is performed iteratively in order to obtain the prescribed fail-
ure calculation-wise. The procedure is split in two parts dependent on each other:
applied loads and specimen capacities. The applied loads should correspond to what
is expected in a design situation with oﬃce load as described in Section 2.1. In this
project it is prioritized to verify the calculation method rather than the capacities of
the exact conﬁguration. This means that the specimen is designed such that failure
by bending moment is obtained at a load multiple times smaller than the capacity of
the pistons. This requires a reduced number of post-tension cables (four instead of
eight).
The design process is based on the design concepts stated in the previous master
thesis [1]. Two reinforcement cages are used to absorb the forces inside the element
and post-tensioning cables are used to achieve the beam-like behaviour through the
joint. First the experimental load distribution is determined statically. Secondly the
capacities are calculated. Thirdly the reinforcement is changed such that bending
moment in the joint is the critical utilization. Lastly the piston load corresponding
to a full bending utilization in the joint is obtained.

joints the bending moment is resisted by the post-tension reinforcement while the

Chapter 5
Numerical Modelling
The use of numerical modelling has numerous advantages. In relation to the present
project a FEM-model can be used to study the eﬀects of diﬀerent parameters or to
assess the design concept in other conﬁgurations or with diﬀerent loads.
A FEM model of the test specimen is produced using the commercial ﬁnite element
software Abaqus CAE. The outer dimensions of the model are 3600 × 2300 × 270 mm.
The present chapter describes the model ant its behaviour.
In Chapter 10 the results of the model is compared with the results of the experiment
and the credibility of the of the FEM-model behaviour is evaluated.
5.1
Elements
Two materials are combined in the model to obtain a realistic behaviour comparable
to the test specimen. 3D solid continuum elements are used for the concrete material.
For the steel reinforcement 1D truss elements are used. The 1D truss elements can
only transfer axial forces . Truss elements are used since the cross section of the
reinforcement is small relative to the dimensions of the test specimen. The 1D truss
elements are cheap from a calculation perspective compared to the 3D continuum
elements.
First and 2nd order elements are considered for the model. The 2nd order elements
are more eﬀective in bending dominated problems since they can handle non-linear
stress distribution within the elements [7]. The large number of nodes in the 2nd order
elements result in a more extensive computation compared to the 1st order elements.
The 1st order elements can only handle linear stress distributions. The fewer number
of nodes means that a ﬁner mesh can be used without extending the computation time.
C3D20 elements are used for the 3D concrete structure and T3D3 elements for the 1D
steel reinforcement for the model using 2nd order elements. C3D8 elements are used
for the 3D concrete structure and T3D2 elements for the 1D steel reinforcement for
the model using 1st order elements. Reduced integration is avoided to obtain greater

5.7
Evaluation Step Method
Abaqus has two diﬀerent methods for calculating static structural responses. The
Static general step uses the Newton-Raphson method that in principle cannot consider
zero or negative stiﬀness material models (such as perfect plastic materials). The
model is displacement controlled, why the stresses are obtained based on the deﬂection
response. The Static general method is therefore applicable.
Static Riks step uses the Riks Arch Length method that is able to determine the
response of the structure when the material stiﬀness is zero or decreasing (softening).
The model is evaluated using both the Riks method and the Newton-Raphson method.
NLgeom is a sub-option that takes geometrical non-linearity eﬀects into consideration.
In the model NLgeom is enabled.

5.8
Convergence
Convergence must be achieved to obtain trustworthy results of the FEM-model. The
behaviour of the test specimen is evaluated in greater detail by reﬁning the mesh.
Local mesh reﬁnement is a mean to obtain convergence in areas of interest. Further-
more it must be considered that the elements will loose precision and credibility if
their shape is distorted signiﬁcantly. This also includes large diﬀerences in initial side
length proportions. The test specimen is a complex 3-dimensional structure with a
complex substructure of reinforcement why Local mesh reﬁnement is not performed.
Convergence of the model is investigated by evaluating diﬀerent mesh reﬁnements and
element types and comparing the Force-deﬂection histories.

7.4 Procedure
Test Set-up and Procedure
7.4
Procedure
In this section the test procedure is described. The procedure is divided in two parts:
the preparation procedure and the test procedure. The preparation procedure is only
performed once and describes everything done before the specimen is installed. The
test procedure includes the steps performed for each specimen.
Preparation procedure
• The load cells are calibrated using the force transducer calibration cell.
• The actuator displacements are calibrated using the distance laser with a pre-
cision of 1 mm.
• The actuator PID controls are calibrated on the basis of a ramp movement of
pistons of approximately 1 mm per minute.
• The displacement gauges are calibrated in MTS TestSuite using a 2-point cali-
bration. The gauges output 0 to 5 V, linearised from zero to max. displacement.
Note that the upper 5-10 mm of the gauges are out of the range.
Test procedure
• The specimen is lifted in place and fastened with nuts and washers while the
crane is still holding it. The crane is removed once fastening is complete.
• The hydraulics is turned on and the displacement gauges and pistons are placed.
• The geometry is measured including positions of the gauges and pistons.
• The piston hydraulics are turned oﬀ. Gravity makes the pistons settle till it is
supported by the specimen. The position is noted.
• The load procedure is generated using MTS TestSuite Multi Purpose Elite
(MPE). The procedure consists of three steps. A screenshot of the procedure in
MPE is shown in Appendix V.
1. The pistons are moved to the previously noted position.
2. The pistons move towards the end position using a ramp speed of approxi-
mately 1 mm per min. The end position is deﬁned as the maximum possible
stroke both pistons can move. In this step data logging and cameras are
started simultaneously.
3. The test is killed upon reaching the end stroke of the pistons or the max-
imum capacity of the specimen is reached. The data is exported to .csv
and .txt ﬁles.

Chapter 11
Future works
This chapter address aspects and problems of the project, that is not investigated due
to the limited time frame but may be investigated in future projects. The issues are
divided into integrated beam concept investigations and FEM-model investigations.
Integrated beam concept investigations
Construction methods for practical constructions
The specimen is constructed using only three SL-deck elements why they are assem-
bled before being installed on the column supports. In construction of a large building,
a large number of elements are used. The SL-deck elements must be installed on tem-
porary supports before they are post-tensioned. The construction method and the
type and use of temporary supports must be investigated before the integrated beam
concept can be used for building construction.
Pure bending in the integrated beam
It may be interesting to investigate the pure bending capacity of the post-tensioned
beam concept. The post-tensioned beam may be constructed without deck parts and
subjected to four-point bending in order to obtain the behaviour. The deﬂection and
capacity can be compared to the results obtained with combined section forces. The
investigation may be performed on a scale model to decrease the cost and production
time.
Column, connection and reactions
The connection and anchoring of the specimen to concrete columns are not investi-
gated in this project. Bending of the deck and integrated beams lead to signiﬁcant
tensile reactions in the columns. The anchoring of the tensile reaction may be inves-
tigated in a future project. Furthermore the behaviour and design of the column may
be investigated since the column is subjected to moments.
89
==PAGE:108==
Future works
Behaviour with edge or joint reinforcement
The transverse pressure causing crack openings in the deck joints is assessed to con-
tribute to the torsional fractures obtained in the experiments. The transverse pressure
may be absorbed by joint- or edge reinforcement in structures to prevent the ”pop-
out” torsional failure. A conﬁguration with this reinforcement may be produced and
tested to verify the bending moment failure.
Integrated beam behaviour during ﬁre
The SL-decks have the ﬁre classiﬁcation REI240 [2]. This is attributed to the isola-
tion obtained by the light-weight concrete blocks. The integrated beam is not isolated
why it may be vulnerable during ﬁre compared to the SL-decks. The temperature
distribution of an integrated beam may therefore be investigated in order to clarify
this behaviour.
Economical analyses
The economic competitiveness of the integrated beam concept may be investigated in
order to compare the solution to existing construction methods.
FEM-model investigations
The FEM-model may be developed in several ways to obtain a more realistic be-
haviour or investigate other constructions using the integrated beam concept. The
potential FEM-model investigations include:
Discrete cracks using XFEM.
Extensive Finite Element modeling (XFEM) may be used to implement discrete cracks
in the concrete specimen to obtain a more realistic behaviour of the model. The im-
plementing of cracks will cause the stresses in the reinforcement to increase resulting
eventually in yielding.
Section modelling
The computational cost of the model can be reduced by studying the joint section
seperately. The joint can be exposed to diﬀerent expected stress states.
Shell modelling.
The computational power required to evaluate the 3D model is extensive, since many
elements are used to obtain convergence. The computational cost can be reduced by
carrying out axial and bending stiﬀness analyses on a cross section of the integrated
beam and deck. The obtained stiﬀnesses can be used to model most of the specimen
as 2D shell elements while the joint is modelled with 3D elements.
90
Department of Civil Engineering - Technical University of Denmark
==PAGE:109==
Future works
Force application using contact mechanics.
Large stress concentrations occur in the model at the force application surface. These
stresses occur because Abaqus displaces the entire surface leading to local translations
in the specimen that does not occur otherwise. Contact mechanics can be applied to
avoid these unrealistic stress concentrations. A piston-head may be modelled with
contact surfaces related to the specimen. The displacement is applied in a single point
to allow for rotation of the piston-head. This approach could reduce the unrealistic
stress concentrations at the force application surface.
Force application using coupled constraints.
A coupled constraint condition to a reference point could also be used to reduce the
unrealistic stress concentrations at the force application surface. The displacement
is applied to the reference point. This method allows for rotation of the constrained
surface and thus reducing the stress concentrations.
Dead-load
Dead-load is not applied in the model to be able to compare the response with the
experiments. Dead-load may be applied in a separate step before applying live-load.
Complete structures
Complete structure models may be developed in order to investigate the behaviour of
the integrated beam concept for varying geometry, material parameters or reinforce-
ment conﬁgurations.

Chapter 12
Conclusion
The concept of an integrated beam in SL-deck elements is investigated in the present
project. The calculation methods for the structural capacities stated in Eurocode 2
is applied for the integrated beam. The section forces are obtained through basic
static considerations where the external forces are transferred to the supports via the
integrated beam. The theory is applied for the design of a full-scale test. The test
is performed for a specimen comprised of three SL27-deck elements with integrated
beam zones joined by post-tensioning. The test specimen is mounted on two column
supports and ﬁxed with tension connections. Forces are applied as point loads to
the cantilevered deck on the center element in order to maximize the section forces
in the element joints. The forces are applied using displacement controlled hydraulic
actuators. Another aspect of the project is to investigate the production methods for
the integrated beam concept and evaluate whether the concept is usable in practise.
Furthermore a numerical ﬁnite element method model is produced, and the behaviour
is compared to the experiments.
The path of force absorption in the specimen is complicated but optimal compared to
the static assumptions. The calculation methods based on Eurocode 2 are considered
conservative since the ultimate loads obtained in the experiments are greater than the
calculated ultimate load. This means that the methods can be used safely to design
an integrated beam. It is furthermore concluded that bending moment is the limiting
calculated capacity of the specimen.
The production and post-tensioning of integrated beams in SL-deck elements are pos-
sible, yet can be optimized. The geometrical composition of the reinforcement cages
may be optimized with respect to the production process such that the prestressing
cables in the deck may be installed without extensive manual labour.
The assumptions used to build the FEM-model of the test specimen yields acceptable
results. The investigated deﬂection/resistance behaviour of the model is similar to
the behaviour observed in the experiments. The model may also be used to obtain the
locations of the critical stresses in the materials. The model in its current form can-
93
==PAGE:112==
Conclusion
not be used to estimate the load capacity of the specimen since inﬁnite deformation
capacity is assumed in the material models, and crack development is not considered.
The fracture mechanism is predominantly a torsional failure in the specimen joint.
The torsional moment is resisted by a shear force couple that produces a transverse
pressure in the joint. The failure develops through crack openings in the deck joint
produced by the transverse pressure. The center element ”pops” out when the cracks
are suﬃciently opened since the shear forces are no longer resisted by the deck joint.
From this point all forces are transferred through the integrated beam that subse-
quently fails in bending by crushing of the concrete in the compression zone. A ductile
failure mechanism is obtained since signiﬁcant visible deformations occurs during fail-
ure. The ﬁrst failure mechanism is torsional failure followed by the expected bending
failure.
The deﬂection of the integrated beam is comparable to the theoretical static deﬂec-
tion before cracking moment is obtained. The bending stiﬀness cannot be precisely
determined between cracking- and ultimate moment. As a consequence the deﬂection
behaviour is diﬃcult to estimate. The deﬂection behaviour may easily be determined
in the uncracked state where the bending stiﬀness is constant. For greater moments
the deﬂection is within the theoretical limits for an uncracked and a fully cracked
beam. Furthermore the deﬂection predominantly occur in the direction of the in-
tegrated beam. The support conditions in the perpendicular direction acts ﬁxed as
intended.
The integrated beam solution has a signiﬁcant rotational stiﬀness for an uncracked
cross section.
The stiﬀness decreases signiﬁcantly once a considerable amount of
cracks are developed in the cross section resulting in exponential rotation develop-
ments. Most cracks develop in the deck joints due to lack of reinforcement. This
means that the center element rotates relative to the edge elements. The rotation
develops suddenly in the so called ”pop-out”.
As a general remark the use of the integrated beam concept in SL-deck constructions is
possible. The design of the integrated beams can be performed using static principles
and the existing calculation methods presented in Eurocode 2. Further investigations
are advised in order to evaluate the practical and economical aspects of the integrated
beam concept.

VÆSENTLIGE FARER FRA ARBEJDSPROCESSEN 
fx laser, vakuum, støvpåvirkning, udstyr 
Test elementet kan falde ned fra opstillingen. Al ophold under testelementet er derfor 
forbudt under forsøget. Ydermere placeres der intet udstyr under elementet under 
forsøget. 
Test elementet er forspændt med store kræfter. Dette er imødekommet ved at sikre at de 
forspændte kabler er fastholdt i kanalerne vha. udstøbning med cementpasta. 
Trykbrud i elementet kan udsende brudstykker. Dette imødekommes ved at placere 
sikkerhedsskærm foran elementet hvor trykbruddet indtræffer. Ydermere holdes 
sikkerhedsafstand til elementet på 1 meter under forsøget, og personlige værnemidler, 
herunder sikkerhedsbriller, sikkerhedssko og hjelm bæres. 
Flytning af elementet med kran. Flytning af elementet med kran foretages af uddannet 
personale i forsøgshallen og personlige værnemidler, herunder sikkerhedssko og hjelm 
bæres under hele flytningen. Ydermere er ophold under elementet ved flytning forbudt. 
 
RISIKO FOR PÅVIRKNING

er det under hele arbejdsprocessen eller kun i en enkelt delproces.

under selve forsøget.

Beskrivelse af projektet: 
 
 
 
 
 
Projektet omhandler design og test af sammenspændte SL-betondæk 
indspændt i søjler ved mekanisk påvirkning. Under forsøget er risiciene 
begrænset. De tilstedeværende risici er følgende:  
Test elementet kan falde ned fra opstillingen. Al ophold under testelementet 
er derfor forbudt under forsøget. Ydermere placeres der intet udstyr under 
elementet under forsøget. 
Test elementet er forspændt med store kræfter. Dette er imødekommet ved 
at sikre at de forspændte kabler er fastholdt i kanalerne vha. udstøbning med 
cementpasta. 
Trykbrud i elementet kan udsende brudstykker. Dette imødekommes ved at 
placere sikkerhedsskærm foran elementet hvor trykbruddet indtræffer. 
Ydermere holdes sikkerhedsafstand til elementet på 1 meter under forsøget, 
og personlige værnemidler, herunder sikkerhedsbriller, sikkerhedssko og hjelm 
bæres. 
Flytning af elementet med kran. Flytning af elementet med kran foretages af 
uddannet personale i forsøgshallen og personlige værnemidler, herunder 
sikkerhedssko og hjelm bæres under hele flytningen. Ydermere er ophold 
under elementet ved flytning forbudt.

FLOWCABLE 
Adding Value to Concrete 
Mai 2011  
TILSETNINGSSTOFF TIL SEMENT FOR Å LAGE BOLTEMØRTEL. 
 
Tekniske data: 
Konsistens:                                       Pulver.
 
Farge:                                                     Grå.
 
Densitet:                                               2,19 
pH-verdi:                       >12 blandet m/vann.
 
Kloridinnhold:                                  < 0,1%.
 
Produktbeskrivelse. 
 
FLOWCABLE er i pulverform og blandes 
med vann og sement for å framstille en 
lettflytende, godt pumpbar, 
svinnkompensert og tiksotrop boltemørtel 
med høy trykkfasthet og god vedheft. 
 
Egenskaper.  
 
FLOWCABLE gir  
• 
Høy flytbarhet med veldig lite eller 
ingen bleeding. 
 
• 
Pumpbar opp til 2 timer ved +20 °C. 
 
• 
Lett ekspanderende, 0,5 – 1 %. 
 
• 
Typisk fasthet v/c 0,25, +20 °C 
• 6 timer   
• 8 timer  
• 10 timer 
• 25 timer 
• 28 dager 
 
• 
God vedheft til stål. Etter 7 døgn, ca. 
15 Mpa. 
 
Takket være sin høye flytbarhet og sine 
tiksotropiske egenskaper fylles alle hulrom  
godt. Dette gir god korrosjonsbeskyttelse. 
Ettersom den høye flytbarheten oppnås 
selv med lavt v/c-tall, får man en kompakt, 
vanntett gysemasse med høy trykkfasthet. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Andre anbefalte anvendelsesområder. 
 
• 
Injisering av spennarmeringskabler. 
 
• 
Tilsetning i mørtel for understøping 
 
Dosering. 
 
Anbefalt doseringsmengde ca. 5% av 
sementvekten. 
 
Bruksanvisning. 
 
Blandingsforholdet skal være ca. 25-30 liter 
vann til 100 kg sement. Start mikseren og 
tilsett ca. 5 % FLOWCABLE, deretter 
sementen. Blande i ca. 3 minutter til 
massen er blitt homogen og plastisk. 
Tilsett ca. 5 liter vann. Fortsett og blande i 
ca. 2 minutter til massen er flytende og 
klumpfri. Blandingen er pumpbar i minst 2 
timer. Hold massen i bevegelse. Så snart 
omrøringen opphører blir massen 
tiksotropisk. 
For et optimalt resultat skal  
temperaturen på kontaktflaten være mellom 
+5 °C til +40 °C. 
 
Forpakning. 
 
FLOWCABLE leveres i 15 kg sekker. 
 
Lagring. 
 
FLOWCABLE skal lagres tørt. Åpnede 
sekker skal anvendes innen en måned.
