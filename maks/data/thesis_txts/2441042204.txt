ABSTRACT 
Analysis of a generally known building structure involves determination of loads coming from the structure’s 
self-weigh and imposed load from among other things people and environmental and accidental load actions 
such as wind load, snow load, and fire. Etc. based on these loads and thus determination of the load 
accumulation, the design of the assumed most critical elements can be decides based on load-bearing 
capacity demonstration and deflection check described in the Danish and European norms and standards. 
The elements and thus the connections must be able to sustain the loads and actions with an acceptable 
level of safety. A building must be stable for all existing combinations of vertical and horizontal loads. This 
means that the structural system should be able to carry all the loads acting on the building and transmits 
these to the foundation. In addition, a building should also be able to resists seismic load, vibration force and 
dynamic effects. This report covers the design of one of three buildings called Kanalvejshusene, building 1, 
which is in Kongens Lyngby in Denmark. The constructions are residential property buildings. The design is 
made from an engineering aspects, whereof relevant assumptions and calculations performed on selected 
static systems in the building. The performed static systems are used for the main structural analysis, stability 
analysis and robustness investigation.  
Structural design of the most critical components in the building including slabs, beams walls and columns is 
obtained by following methods. The methods that have been used are manual hand calculations, finite 
element modelling software “Strusoft FEM design 17” and programmed EXCEL sheets for load-bearing 
capacity check in cold and fire condition from BEF (Betonelementforeningen). The hand calculations are 
primarily made in ECXEL. These methods are mixed together for the analysis of the main structures. Most of 
the load specification and load accumulations are devised by manual hand calculations and these values are 
used for the static system of e.g. beams, slabs and columns that are drawn in FEM-design where analysis 
outputs as reactions, deflections and internal forces as bending, shear and buckling are obtained from the 
program. The stability analysis of the cores in the building are analyzed in FEM design. The program takes all 
the opening in the cores into account, since the openings cause a different inertia of the walls. Any output 
obtained in FEM design is compared with the hand calculations to check if the program analyzing results are 
accurate and also to check the variation in both methods. If the design of the systems is not observed in 
accordance with the standards and limits, it leads to that the building as a whole is not structurally safe and 
stable and hereby another reasonable and relevant solutions have to be used. This situation occurred in this 
project and alternative solutions are described in this report.  
Structural elements including hollow core slab and traditional concrete console beams where Spæncom is 
the producer and composite steel beams, so-called deltabeams are from Peikko groups. These have been 
used in this project as sustainable and flexible solutions. The components in the building are prefabricated, 
which among other things leads to reduced delays (due to weather) and construction time and recycling. 
New solution of beam-column connection has been applied in this project to ensure a safety and stable 
system and this system are called the critical zones or areas in the building.

 INTRODUCTION    
The project, Kanalvejhusene, consist of 3 house buildings. The apartments are very modern, tasteful with 
among other things light in the house from several sides, and well-intentioned layout of kitchen and 
bathrooms from Invita. These housing is located on Kanalvej in the middle of Kongens Lyngby, which are 
unique, because of the proximity to shops, cafes, and green surroundings and cultural offers.    
The project is started in February 2017 and the first apartments are ready for occupancy in February 2018. It 
is a 40,000-square meter residential and commercial building on 16,350 square meter plot housed a new 
Danish headquarters for Microsoft, exclusive shops, grocery stores, fitness, loungers, housing for students, 
etc. Most of the apartments have large balconies, and all residents have access to the large shared atrium 
garden on the first floor. There are parking places in the basement below the homes.  
In thesis project one of the residential buildings will be chosen, which is called building 1. It consists of two 
story underground car parking and 4 floors above terrain, where 2-5 room apartments are available. The 
height of the building above terrain is 15.8 meters.

 DESIGN BASIS AND REQUIREMENTS 
 BASIC REQUIREMENTS 
The construction, Kanalvejshusene, building 1, must be dimensioned and carried out with a sufficient degree 
of certainty that can withstand the loads it is expected to be exposed to and must with it gets a satisfactory 
robustness and durability. Safety is assessed based on the overall construction as a whole, considering 
different types of failure and hereof the consequences. The requirement is demonstrated by ensuring that 
the individual design elements has sufficient safety as well as the overall design has satisfactory safety. The 
safety must cover the basic design assumptions and unintended defects and effects that may occur.

Safety  
Building 1 is a multi-story building, where it is 15.8 meters from the top floor to the terrain deck. According 
to [1], since the building is higher than 12 meters above terrain thus the building is in a high consequence 
class, CC3, where a possible failure involves a high risk of personal injury or will cause major societal 
consequences. 
According to [3], the safety class associated with CC3 is RC3 with a 𝐾𝐹𝐼 =  1.1, that will be applied on 
unfavorable actions for safety differentiation 
In addition, the building must comply with the design standards, i.e. it should be able to absorb both 
vertical and horizontal forces. According to [14] the building must be robust, on which the following 
requirements must apply before a construction is considered robust: 
 
• 
The safety-critical parts of the construction are only slightly sensitive to unintended impacts and 
defects, or   
• 
There is no major failure of the design if a limited part of the structure fails.

Robustness 
Robustness is also described in [6], paragraph 1.5.14: 
“The ability of a structure to withstand events like fire, explosions, impact or the consequences of human 
error, without being damaged to an extent disproportionate to the original cause.” 
According to DS 409, most of constructions are subjected to errors, but usually these errors are within the 
limits of acceptable inaccuracies that are covered by the collaterals in the partial coefficient-system. 
A case of fatal unintentional and unforeseen circumstances, the partial coefficient-system does not consider 
the big serious mistakes of human error. A possibly damage can therefore be limited by building-in 
robustness in the structure.

 DESIGN METHODOLOGY 
The project is solved and designed primarily with classical methods for static calculations. Structural design 
of the main constructions will be carried out by using manual hand calculations and modelling program 
according to applicable relevant statutory requirements for the static assessment, as well as building physics 
functional requirements as specified in BR10. 
Some presentations from relevant courses on DTU, which are also based on the European norms and 
standards will also be used. Computational tools as EXCEL will be used for the calculations. Technical books 
will also be using for designing the construction. These books are also based on DS/EN. 
The Finite element modelling software Strusoft FEM Design will be used for the staircase and elevator shaft, 
which is the core in the building to investigate the overall stability in the building. This will be analyzed and 
compared with manual calculation for more accurate design solution for the core construction. There are 4 
elevator shafts in the building. They are almost similar, therefore one shaft will be analyzed and designed 
with the assumption that investigation of the stability of one of the cores is the same for the rest. 
Additionally, FEM-design will also be used to designing relevant static systems to get outputs such as internal 
forces, reactions and deflections. These outputs will be compared with hand calculation to ensure that the 
program is giving correct outputs. Loads acting on the systems will be determined as hand calculation, and 
thus inserting the value in Fem-design. Fem design can take the self-weight of the element or elements into 
account, where this will be chosen.

The structural concept of the building  
In the tender project, the main structure consists of innovative composite beams and traditional 
prefabricated concrete columns, beams, slabs and walls. These prefabricated elements have potential for 
reuse due to their high durability. For following description reference is made to figures containing element 
layout arrangement including span direction of slab elements in Figure 22 and 23 (Page 17). 
In the apartment’s floorplans, the mostly used internal elements are concrete load-bearing walls that 
separate the apartments apart and carry the floor elements. Composite intermediated supported beams are 
also located in many places in these floorplans to support the floor elements inside the apartments as shown 
in Figure 21. The concrete columns (red indicated) are carrying these beams (blue indication).  
In the ground floor there are almost none internal load-bearing walls, and the concrete slabs are carried by 
beams and columns and some places the external walls (façade). The function of the beams in this floorplan 
is to carry the slab and to carry and transverse the loads from overlying bearing walls (including load-bearing 
or none load-bearing walls) to the columns that proceed the vertical loads to the foundation. Two composite 
intermediate supported deltabeams are very hard loaded. These beams are supporting all overlying elements 
such as load-bearing walls that support the slab elements in each floorplan. This can be a problem regarding 
overloading and static analysis, which will be described and investigated later. These two areas are so-called 
the critical zones or areas in the building, which will be described in the next chapter.   
In all the floors, the external walls (façade walls) are supporting slab-elements only at the corners and the 
concrete façade towards the atrium is just self-supporting. The façades are made of load-bearing concrete 
sandwich elements.

 DESCRIPTION OF STATIC SYSTEM   
The main components of the structure of the building consists of slabs, beams, columns, walls and the 
foundation (Foundation out of part in this thesis). The different types of structural elements are described in 
the following. 
 
Main structural elements in the building 
The components in the building are prefabricated, which among other things leads to reduced delays (due 
to weather) and construction time and recycling. 
Deck component 
The prefabricated slabs are constructed of prestressed hollow core slabs (HCS) from Spæncom, called 
Xtrumax (EX). The type of the floor elements on all floors are Xtrumax. The thickness is different for the two 
divided sections. The HCS in the ground floor is 400 mm and 270 mm in the additional floors. According to 
Spæncom, with these thicknesses it is produced for passive and moderate environmental class. It is possible 
to cut Xtrumax in narrower design than the standard widths. However, no less than: 350 mm for EX 27 and 
EX 40 (element types), which may be required by the elevator shaft. There are some advantages of using HCS 
regarding to recycling and mounting time. HCS element can be e.g. reused in a similar construction. 
In this project it is assumed that HCS elements will be used in whole the building, although it may be 
necessary to use inset-cast concrete in some places. The voids in HCS reduce the amount of concrete needed 
for the floors. This reduction in concrete is also reducing the weight of the slabs hereby lightening the entire 
structure thus beams, columns and bracings that are supporting the floors which can lead to reducing in the 
size of foundations. 
In building 1, the slab elements will be supported by walls and beams.

Beams 
As mentioned, the two type of beams that are used in the building are 
prefabricated prestressed concrete beams from Spæncom and composite 
deltabeams from Peikko with different dimension. Deltabeams are made by 
steel, which after installation on the site it casts with concrete thus the 
composite effect between steel and concrete is achieved. The holes on the 
web flanges and as well as the flanges are slanted cause the placement of 
joint reinforcement across and along the beam to be simplified. 
  
Deltabeams floor structure is a flexible solution. It enables slim and light 
structural solution. The composite effect between steel and concrete means 
that the span of deltabeams between supporting columns can be longer, e.g. more than 10 meters. According 
to Peikko, since the composite beams are often used for Hollow core slabs the span of it can be extended to 
more than 14 meters. It leads to, that open areas can be constructed and less columns needed. Building 1 in 
this project is different as there are many number of apartments in one 
floor plan thus load-bearing concrete walls needed to separate the 
apartments and carry the floor slabs. For example, in domiciles, this 
innovative beam is a good choice because it reduces the total number of 
elements, which helps to reduce foundation costs, a lighter structure leads 
to a lighter foundation.  Each deltabeam connects perfectly to the structure 
through a variety of pre-engineered standard connections.

Vertical load accumulation 
The vertical loads are transmitted down to the foundation through the bearing system. The vertical load on 
the roof and the others floor structures are consisting of snow load, imposed load/payload and dead load 
from the self-weight of the elements. Afterwards by shear, these loads will be taken through the walls and 
beams that transmit the loads to either the column or walls that are supporting them. From here the loads 
will be transmitted to the foundation. Figure 33 shows the load transmission in an excerpt of a structural 
system in the building. The slab is supported by a deltabeam and an external wall. The flanges of the 
deltabeam carries a slab structure from both sides. The beam occupies half of the load coming from the floor 
and the wall occupies the other half as a line load. The line load acting on the deltabeam is divided by the 
two elements that are supporting it by both ends, here the column (600 x 600 mm) and the core wall (are 
not included in this figure). These are transmitted the loads to the foundation.

Mass load 
The horizontal mass load, 𝐴𝑑, is also called seismic impact, which is important to take it into account to 
ensure the strength and stability of structures against unintentional horizontal impacts and small 
earthquakes. The mass load occurs as results of skewness in structures and in smaller earth shakes. For 
example, it may be shakes from nearby traffic or from earthquakes. The mass load acts in the center of gravity 
of the construction and can act in any direction.  
Any vertical load is considered to give rise to horizontal mass loads and it is considered to be able to occur 
simultaneously with the corresponding vertical load.

In the following section the load combinations are described and defined for ultimate limit state (ULS) and 
serviceability limit state (SLS) in accordance to DS/EN 1990: Basis of structural design 200 ([3]). 
Load combinations 
The requirements for a construction’s safety are formulated in relation to limit states. In the norms the limit 
states are divided into ultimate limit state and serviceability limit state. 
The load combination is a combination of loads on a construction, which together give the design load 
impact, e.g., consisting of dead load, live load, environmental load, accidental actions etc. All structures are 
required to be checked for one or more load combinations, includes ultimate limit state, serviceability limit 
state, accident state (e.g. fire)

Documentation of robustness for buildings of high consequence classes 
There should be making demands for documentation of robustness for buildings of high consequence classes. 
A building must be robust, i.e. that a construction must not fails if a failure of a part-construction occurs and 
the main components should be slightly sensitive to unintended influences and defects. 
A way to ensure robustness is to create an inner connection in the construction. The connection can be an 
insertion of reinforcement in the joints between the concrete elements. These reinforcements must ensure 
tension connection on case of local failure.

be secured. This will be investigated in accordance with Hansen, Svend Ole og Jensen, Chr. Bjarne.2010:

 CONCLUSION 
The structural analysis of the building is performed by hand calculations and modelling of the selected and 
assumed most critical components and structure system in Strusoft FEM design. The vertical and horizontal 
load transmission in the building to the foundation are made by hand calculations which are used and 
inserted in Strusoft FEM-design in which the deflections, internal forces, reactions and stresses are obtained. 
Analysis outputs in FEM design are always checked with overall hand calculations to ensure that the program 
is working correctly. The static check of the model outputs was in accordance with the hand calculation 
results with some acceptable deviations. This concludes that the hand calculated static inputs that are 
inserted to the static system models that was drawn in FEM-Design are correct, since their corresponding 
outputs are documented as accurate and reliable. The analytical outputs from FEM design are used for the 
demonstration of the load-bearing capacity check of the individual most critical components in the building, 
stability analysis and robustness investigation. The analytical outputs are used instead of the hand-calculated 
results, because the program analyzes more accurate as many aspects is considering as e.g. finite elements 
mesh. The more mesh divisions the more accurate results are obtained. All the vertical load-bearing capacity 
check and the deflection control was observed according to the norms and standards.  
The original two areas that was called in this report, the critical zones in the building, was not stable as the 
static principle about the deltabeam, D32-400, that supported all overlying elements as walls and slabs could 
not hold on the vertical loads. This concludes that solution 2 was applying to relieve each floor by inserting 
the same type of detabeam in each floor. These must be intermediate supported as the one in the celling 
plan of the ground floor, to observe the load-bearing capacity check. The intermediate supporting columns 
in the additional plans are designed with the same thickness as the inner walls to hide them in the 
apartments. Some columns needed to support the inserted beams at the ends in the additional floorplans. 
These columns are designed to be T-formed in order to preserve a part of the original plan. Hereby, the 
external wall to the atrium that is short because of the intermediate placed windows and the small part of 
the inner wall are assumed to work as one column, including the T-column. The connection between the 
deltabeam and the T-column is satisfied as the determinates contract compression stress is sufficient. 
The overall stability of the building has been investigated and is observed according to the vertical and 
horizontal loads. The normal stresses in the cores do not exceed he concrete design strength, the shear 
stresses are also acceptable, and the cores are stable and sustained against both overturning and sliding. This 
means, there is no need of reinforcement and steel fitting in the core walls. Furthermore, the robustness of 
the construction is demonstrated. The reinforcement in the deck as needed is 5Y14, which is determined 
based on the calculated tensile force.
