ABSTRACT	  
	  
Introduction:	   The	   survival	   rate	   for	   cancer	   patients	   has	   increased	   as	   a	   result	   of	   more	  
advanced	  and	  aggressive	  radiation	  therapy	  and	  chemotherapeutic	  agents.	  Serious	  side	  effects	  
of	   these	   cancer	   treatments	   are	   the	   ovarian	   gonadotoxic	   effects.	   Prior	   to	   these	   treatments,	  
women	  are	  offered	  cryopreservation	  of	  ovarian	  tissue	  as	  an	  option	  for	  fertility	  preservation.	  
However,	   there	   is	   a	   risk	   of	   reintroducing	   cancer	   cells	   after	   autotransplantation.	  
Decellularization	  is	  a	  novel	  strategy	  for	  organ	  replacement,	  where	  the	  cells	  of	  an	  organ	  are	  
removed,	  leaving	  an	  extracellular	  matrix	  (ECM)	  for	  recellularization.	  	  
Aim:	  The	  study	  aims	  to	  produce	  a	  novel	  decellularization	  and	  recellularization	  method	  for	  
human	  ovarian	  tissue.	  	  
Materials	   and	   methods:	   Periodic	   acid-­‐schiff	   staining,	   DAPI	   staining	   and	   quantitative	  
methods	  were	  applied	  to	  confirm	  if	  the	  decellularization	  procedure	  effectively	  removes	  all	  the	  
cells	  and	  preserves	  the	  ECM	  in	  human	  ovarian	  tissue.	  In	  addition,	  PAS	  staining	  were	  used,	  to	  
confirm	   the	   recellularization	   of	   ECM	   with	   ovarian	   cells	   including	   mice	   follicles	   and	   human	  
granulosa	  cells	  (GCs).	  An	  evaluation	  of	  the	  estradiol	  levels,	  secreted	  by	  the	  ovarian	  GCs	  seeded	  
in	  the	  ECM,	  was	  performed	  by	  enzyme-­‐linked	  immunosorbent	  assay	  (ELISA).	  	  
	  Results:	  This	  study	  shows	  a	  decellularization	  procedure	  that	  effectively	  removes	  all	  cells	  and	  
preserves	   the	   ECM,	   without	   any	   visual	   damage	   in	   human	   ovarian	   tissue.	   Furthermore,	   we	  
were	  able	  to	  recellularize	  human	  GCs	  and	  murine	  follicles	  to	  the	  ECM	  of	  human	  ovarian	  tissue,	  
both	   in	   vitro	   and	   in	   vivo	   in	   a	   mouse	   model.	   However,	   the	   results	   showed	   significantly	  
improved	  migration	  and	  survival	  rate	  of	  follicles	  in	  the	  ECM	  when	  grown	  in	  vivo.	  
Conclusion:	  
This	   study	   demonstrates	   a	   new	   method	   for	   decellularization	   and	  
recellularization	  of	  human	  ovarian	  tissue,	  which	  could	  be	  a	  potential	  strategy	  to	  eliminate	  the	  
risk	   of	   reintroducing	   malignant	   cells	   to	   the	   women	   receiving	   autotransplantation	   of	  
cryopreserved	  ovarian	  tissue.	  However,	  optimization	  and	  further	  studies	  are	  needed	  in	  order	  
to	  confirm	  the	  applicability	  of	  this	  new	  and	  promising	  method.

1.	  Introduction	  
1.1	  The	  Ovary	  and	  Folliculogenesis	  
	  
	  
	  
The	  ovaries	  are	  the	  reproductive	  organs,	  whose	  primary	  role	  is	  to	  produce	  oocytes	  for	  
fertilization	  and	  secrete	  reproductive	  hormones,	  that	  will	  make	  the	  reproductive	  tract	  ready	  
for	  fertilization	  (1,2).	  At	  birth,	  approximately	  1-­‐2	  million	  oocytes	  are	  present	  in	  follicles	  (2,3).	  
However	  due	  to	  atresia	  only	  around	  400.000	  follicles	  are	  left	  at	  the	  start	  of	  puberty,	  each	  with	  
the	  potential	  to	  reach	  ovulation.	  Though	  during	  life	  only	  400-­‐500	  oocytes	  reach	  ovulation.	  The	  
oocyte	  reserve	  will	  eventually	  be	  depleted	  at	  menopause	  (4).	  Oocytes	  are	  immature	  ovum	  
that	  develops	  to	  maturity	  within	  a	  follicle.	  A	  follicle	  contains	  an	  oocyte,	  which	  is	  surrounded	  
by	  granulosa	  cells	  (GCs),	  theca	  cells	  and	  a	  basement	  membrane.	  Granulosa	  cells	  and	  theca	  
cells	  interact	  with	  each	  other	  to	  produce	  hormones	  and	  growth	  factors	  (3).	  	  
Folliculogenesis	  last	  for	  375	  days	  and	  is	  the	  differentiation	  of	  resting	  primordial	  follicles	  into	  
preovulatory	   follicles.	   An	   ovarian	   follicle	   will	   go	   through	   following	   stages:	   primordial,	  
primary,	  secondary,	  tertiary,	  antral	  and	  preovulatory.	  Generally	  only	  one	  of	  follicles	  reaches	  
the	   preovulatory	   stage.	   Primordial	   follicles	   are	   characterized	   by	   having	   one	   layer	   of	   flat	  
immature	  GCs	  surrounding	  the	  oocyte	  (3,5).	  Proliferation	  of	  flat	  GCs	  to	  cuboidal	  GCs	  leads	  to	  
the	   transformation	   of	   primordial	   follicles	   to	   primary	   follicles.	   The	   primary	   follicle	   will	   be	  
transformed	  to	  secondary	  follicle	  due	  to	  proliferation	  of	  the	  GCs,	  resulting	  in	  multi-­‐layers	  of	  
GCs,	   a	   zona	   pelucida	   and	   arterioles	   connecting	   the	   follicle	   with	   circulation	   and	   thereby	  
explosion	  to	  endocrine	  factors	  (3,5).	  Additionally,	  two	  layers	  termed	  theca	  externa	  and	  theca	  
interna	  will	  be	  transformed	  from	  the	  stromal	  tissue	  surrounding	  the	  follicle	  (3,6).	  	  
At	  the	  antral	  follicle	  stage	  an	  antrum	  will	  be	  formed	  due	  to	  formation	  of	  fluid-­‐filled	  cavity.	  
Antral	   follicles	   can	   be	   up	   to	   20	   mm	   and	   are	   at	   this	   stage	   gonadotropin	   dependent,	   unlike	  
preantral	   follicles	   (Fig.	   1).	   The	   multilayer	   GCs	   of	   the	   antral	   follicle	   differentiates	   into	   two	  
different	   GCs:	   cumulus	   cell,	   which	   surround	   the	   oocyte	   and	   mural	   granulosa	   cells	   which	  
surround	   the	   antrum	   (6,7).	   The	   follicles	   entering	   the	   antral	   stage	   are	   denoted	   as	   selected,	  
considering	  they	  have	  the	  opportunity	  to	  reach	  ovulation.	  However,	  only	  one	  antral	  follicle	  
will	  reach	  ovulation,	  while	  the	  other	  will	  undergo	  atresia	  (8,9).

The	  Menstrual	  Cycle	  
The	   menstrual	   cycle	   is	   controlled	   by	   hormones	   and	   is	   the	   changes	   the	   female	   body	   goes	  
through	   to	   prepare	   for	   pregnancy.	   Ovulation	   takes	   place	   every	   month	   due	   to	   hormonal	  
changes,	  which	  indeed	  prepare	  the	  uterus	  for	  pregnancy	  and	  last	  for	  approximately	  28	  days	  
(8,11).	  The	  menstrual	  cycle	  consist	  of	  the	  follicular	  phase	  and	  the	  luteal	  phase.	  The	  follicular	  
phase	  starts	  at	  the	  first	  day	  of	  menstrual	  bleeding	  and	  terminates	  about	  14	  days	  later.	  The	  
follicular	  phase	  is	  the	  progression	  of	  primordial	  follicles	  to	  preovulatory	  follicle.	  At	  day	  14	  the	  
luteal	  phase	  begins,	  in	  which	  the	  corpus	  luteum	  is	  generated.	  The	  luteal	  phase	  lasts	  for	  about	  
14	  days	  and	  its	  primary	  role	  is	  to	  produce	  progesterone,	  which	  play	  an	  important	  role	  for	  
preparing	  the	  uterus	  for	  pregnancy	  (11).	  
	  
Hypothalamic-­‐pituitary	   ovarian	   axis	   regulates	   steroid	   hormones	   by	   gonadotropin-­‐releasing	  
hormone	  (GnRH).	  GnRH	  stimulates	  the	  production	  of	  Luteinizing	  Hormone	  (LH)	  and	  Follicle	  
Stimulating	  Hormone	  (FSH),	  which	  induce	  the	  production	  of	  steroid	  hormones	  and	  growth	  of	  
follicles	  (12).	  The	  sex	  hormones	  produced	  will	  inhibit	  the	  production	  of	  FSH	  and	  GnRH.	  In	  the	  
late	  follicular	  phase	  a	  rise	  in	  progesterone	  (P4)	  will	  lead	  to	  a	  small	  increase	  of	  FSH	  and	  a	  high	  
increase	   of	   LH,	   which	   leads	   to	   ovulation	   and	   the	   transformation	   of	   the	   follicle	   into	   corpus	  
luteum.	  The	  GCs	  and	  theca	  cell	  of	  the	  corpus	  luteum	  produce	  a	  high	  amount	  of	  progesterone	  
and	  inhibin	  A,	  which	  will	  inhibit	  the	  production	  of	  GnRH,	  FSH	  and	  LH	  (Fig.	  2).	  If	  no	  pregnancy	  
occurs,	  the	  corpus	  luteum	  degenerates	  resulting	  in	  decreasing	  production	  of	  sex	  hormones,	  
initiation	  of	  bleeding	  and	  a	  new	  cycle	  (12,13).

Ovarian	  Steroidogenesis	  
Both	  FSH	  and	  LH	  produced	  in	  the	  pituitary	  gland	  are	  required	  for	  steroidogenesis,	  because	  
estrogen	  production	  occurs	  in	  GCs,	  which	  are	  controlled	  by	  the	  gonadotropins	  as	  part	  of	  the	  
Two-­‐cell,	  Two-­‐gonadotropin	  system.	  GCs	  cells	  express	  the	  enzyme	  CYP19a1,	  which	  converts	  
androgens	   to	   E2.	   Theca	   cells	   express	   other	   aromatases,	   which	   makes	   them	   able	   produce	  
androgens,	  thus	  it	  is	  important	  that	  GCs	  and	  Theca	  cells	  interact	  with	  each	  other	  (Fig.	  3).

CYP17a1,	  which	  are	  expressed	  mainly	  in	  the	  Theca	  cells,	  converts	  pregnenolone	  and	  P4	  to	  
dehydroepiandrosterone	   (DHEA)	   and	   androstenedione.	   FSH-­‐receptors	   and	   aromatase	  
(CYP19a1),	  which	  are	  expressed	  primarily	  in	  GCs,	  converts	  the	  androgens	  to	  estrogens	  (Fig.

Figure	   4.	   Steroidogenesis.	   Androgens	   are	   produced	   by	   theca	   cells	   and	   transported	   through	   the

1.2	  Fertility	  preservation	  
	  
The	  improved	  survival	  rate	  in	  cancer	  patients	  has	  increased	  because	  of	  more	  advanced	  and	  
aggressive	  radiation	  therapy	  and	  chemotherapeutic	  agents.	  These	  cancer	  treatments	  can	  be	  
gonadotoxic	   and	   cause	   depletion	   of	   the	   follicles	   in	   the	   ovary,	   which	   may	   lead	   to	   sterility.	  
Various	  chemotherapeutic	  agents	  exist	  and	  they	  can	  have	  different	  toxicity.	  However	  the	  most	  
serious	  gonadotoxic	  chemotherapeutic	  agents	  are	  alkylating	  agents,	  which	  is	  predominantly	  
used	   in	   treating	   childhood	   cancer	   (15,16).	   Radiotherapy	   on	   the	   ovaries	   or	   the	   abdominal	  
region	   has	   shown	   to	   induce	   primary	   ovarian	   failure	   (16).	   Various	   fertility	   preservation	  
treatments	   exist	   for	   female	   cancer	   patients,	   however	   a	   part	   of	   them	   are	   not	   applicable	   for	  
children.	   Women	   are	   offered	   fertility	   preservation	   treatments,	   such	   as	   in	  vitro	   fertilisation	  
(IVF)	  and	  in	  vitro	  maturation	  (IVM)	  before	  cancer	  treatment	  (15).	  	  
However,	  the	  drawbacks	  with	  these	  treatments	  are	  the	  low	  amount	  of	  embryos	  and	  oocytes	  
achieved	  and	  the	  treatments	  are	  not	  able	  to	  reinitiate	  the	  ovaries.	  Furthermore,	  some	  cancer	  
patients	   require	   urgent	   treatment,	   which	   in	   some	   situations	   makes	   the	   treatments	  
inapplicable,	  since	  these	  treatments	  require	  approximately	  14	  days.	  These	  treatments	  need	  
vaginal	   access	   to	   the	   ovaries,	   which	   are	   not	   applicable	   for	   Children.	   Currently,	   the	   most	  
suitable	   fertility	   preservation	   treatment	   in	   young	   cancer	   patients	   and	   cancer	   patients	   who	  
need	  urgent	  treatment	  is	  cryopreservation	  of	  ovarian	  tissue	  prior	  to	  gonadotoxic	  treatment	  
(Fig.	  5)	  (16,17).	  	  
	  
==PAGE:13==
13	    
	  
Figure	  5.	  A	  flow	  diagram	  of	  the	  opportunities	  for	  fertility	  preservation	  (18).	  	  
	  
Cryopreservation	  of	  Ovarian	  Tissue	  
Only	   the	   ovarian	   cortical	   tissue	   with	   its	   primordial	   follicles	   can	   used	   for	   cryopreservation,	  
whereas	  ovarian	  medulla	  tissue	  is	  either	  discarded	  our	  used	  for	  research.	  The	  ovarian	  cortex	  
is	  cut	  into	  fragments.	  	  
Each	  fragment	  are	  prepared	  with	  cryoprotectants	  (CPAs)	  and	  freezing	  medium,	  which	  protect	  
the	  cells	  from	  freezing	  damage	  by	  slow-­‐freezing	  down	  to	  -­‐196	  °C	  in	  liquid	  nitrogen	  (Fig.	  6)	  
(16,19).	  	  	  
	  
==PAGE:14==
14	    
	  
Figure	  6.	  Cryopreservation	  processing.	  Upon	  arrival	  the	  cortex	  tissue	  are	  isolated	  from	  the	  ovary	  and	  
cut	  in	  pieces.	  Each	  piece	  of	  tissue	  are	  treated	  with	  cryoprotectants	  and	  slow-­‐frozen	  down	  to	  -­‐196	  °C.	  If	  
patient	  recover	  from	  cancer,	  the	  cryopreserved	  ovarian	  cortex	  can	  be	  transplanted	  into	  the	  patient.	  
The	  pictures	  are	  from	  Laboratory	  of	  Reproductive	  Biology	  (LRB).	  
	  
	  
	  
The	  CPAs	  eliminates	  formation	  of	  ice	  crystals	  in	  the	  tissue,	  which	  reduce	  mechanical	  damage	  
of	  the	  cells	  and	  tissue.	  It	  is	  suggested	  that	  CPAs	  prevents	  ice	  formation	  by	  forming	  hydrogen	  
bonds	  to	  water.	  Penetrating	  CPAs	  are	  primarily	  used	  in	  cryopreservation	  and	  include	  glycerol	  
(GLY),	  dimethylsulfoxide	  (DMSO),	  propylene	  glycol	  (PG	  1,2-­‐propanediol)	  and	  ethylene	  glycol	  
(EG).	  Penetrating	  CPAs	  are	  small	  with	  the	  molecular	  weight	  less	  than	  100	  daltons,	  resulting	  in	  
easier	  penetration	  of	  the	  cell	  membrane	  (Fig.	  7).	  The	  ability	  to	  penetrate	  the	  cell	  membranes	  
prevents	  dehydration	  of	  cells	  during	  freezing	  (20).	  	  	  
	  
	  
	  
Figure	  7.	  The	  structure	  of	  penetrating	  CPAs,	  such	  as	  ethylene	  glycol,	  propylene	  glycol,	  glycerol	  and	  
DMSO	  (21).	  	  
	  
==PAGE:15==
15	    
However,	  many	  CPAs	  are	  toxic	  especially	  in	  high	  concentrations.	  It	  has	  been	  suggested	  that	  
CPAs	  with	  high	  lipophilicity	  correlates	  with	  toxicity,	  since	  it	  makes	  it	  possible	  to	  partition	  into	  
the	   cell	   membrane	   and	   thereby	   destabilizing	   it.	   Strong	   hydrogen	   bonds	   have	   also	   been	  
correlated	   with	   toxicity,	   probably	   due	   to	   disruption	   of	   the	   hydration	   shell	   around	  
macromolecules.	   These	   suggestions	   indicate	   that	   CPAs	   with	   polar	   groups	   with	   weak	  
interactions	  to	  water	  are	  the	  most	  suitable	  CPAs.	  Additionally,	  freezing	  medium	  is	  also	  added	  
to	  the	  sample,	  supporting	  the	  cells	  at	  near	  freezing	  temperature.	  It	  typically	  contains	  salts,	  pH	  
buffers	   and	   sometimes	   nutrition.	   The	   components	   are	   typically	   present	   at	   near	   isotonic	  
concentration,	  so	  the	  cells	  want	  shrink	  or	  swell	  (20).	  
If	   the	   patients	   recover	   from	   cancer	   it	   is	   possible	   to	   thaw	   the	   cortex	   tissue	   using	   thawing	  
medium	  and	  transplant	  fragments	  into	  the	  patient.	  Two	  main	  transplantation	  techniques	  are	  
used	  orthotopic	  and	  heterotopic	  transplantation	  The	  heterotopic	  transplantation	  will	  be	  able	  
to	  initiate	  the	  endocrine	  support	  and	  the	  orthotropic	  transplantation	  of	  cortex	  fragment	  will	  
have	  the	  opportunity	  to	  induce	  ovulation	  of	  the	  oocytes	  and	  restoration	  of	  endocrine	  support.	  	  
Worldwide	   39	   children	   have	   been	   born	   as	   a	   result	   from	   cryopreservation	   of	   cortex	   tissue.	  
Furthermore	   prepubertal	   girls	   have	   induced	   puberty	   after	   autotransplantation	   with	  
cryopreserved	  cortex	  tissue	  (16,17,22).	  	  
A	   concern	   regarding	   autotransplantation	   of	   cryopreserved	   cortex	   tissue	   is	   the	   risk	   of	  
reintroducing	  the	  cancer	  to	  the	  patient	  once	  the	  patient	  has	  been	  cured.	  This	  is	  especially	  a	  
high	  risk	  in	  leukaemia	  patients.	  Therefore	  a	  new	  or	  improved	  treatment	  will	  be	  favourable.	  To	  
solve	   this	   problem,	   researchers	   have	   used	   decellularization,	   where	   cellular	   material	   is	  
removed,	  while	  preserving	  the	  organ	  skeleton	  of	  the	  extracellular	  matrix	  (ECM).	  Next	  would	  
be	  to	  recellularized	  the	  ECM	  with	  healthy	  ovarian	  cells,	  resulting	  in	  reestablishment	  of	  the	  
ovarian	  endocrine	  system	  and	  providing	  a	  niche	  for	  the	  ovarian	  cells	  (23).	  	  
	  
1.3	  Engineered	  Ovary	  	  
There	   is	   a	   high	   demand	   for	   ensuring	   that	   reintroduction	   of	   cancer	   cells	   after	  
autotransplantation	   of	   cryopreserved	   cortex	   tissue	   does	   not	   occur.	   Furthermore,	   the	  
transplants	  should	  not	  have	  the	  risk	  of	  being	  rejected	  by	  the	  immune	  system.	  Several	  studies	  
have	   experimented	   with	   engineering	   artificial	   organs,	   such	   as	   heart,	   lungs,	   kidney	   and	  
pancreas	  (Fig.	  8)(24).	  
==PAGE:16==
16	    
Decellularization	  
	  
	  
	  
	  
The	  idea	  behind	  decellularization	  is	  to	  preserve	  the	  composition	  and	  biological	  activity	  of	  the	  
ECM	  after	  sufficiently	  removing	  all	  cellular	  material.	  Any	  chemical	  used	  to	  decellularize	  the	  
tissue,	  will	  change	  the	  structure	  of	  the	  ECM.	  Hence	  it	  is	  difficult	  to	  optimize	  decellularization	  
experiments	   that	   preserve	   the	   native	   ECM	   and	   remove	   the	   cellular	   material	   efficiently	  
(25,26).	  	  
	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	              
Figure	  8.	  Decellularization	  of	  different	  organs	  including	  heart,	  kidney,	  liver	  and	  lung	  (27).	  	  
The	  ECM	  functions	  as	  an	  environment	  and	  niche	  for	  cells	  and	  include	  components	  that	  affect	  
cell	  adhesion,	  migration	  and	  proliferation.	  For	  that	  reason	  preservation	  of	  ECM	  structure	  and	  
composition	   during	   decellularization	   is	   highly	   advantageous.	   Detergents	   and	   enzymatic	  
treatments	  are	  used	  to	  eliminate	  cellular	  content	  from	  the	  tissue.	  Remaining	  cellular	  material	  
can	  lead	  to	  unfavourable	  host	  responses	  upon	  reintroducing	  the	  ECM	  to	  the	  recipient	  (25,26).	  	  
Several	  compounds	  are	  used	  to	  decellularization,	  such	  as	  enzymes	  and	  detergents.	  However	  
studies	  have	  shown	  that	  detergents	  are	  the	  most	  effective	  to	  remove	  the	  cells	  from	  the	  tissue.	  
Detergents	  ease	  cellular	  lysis	  and	  solubilize	  hydrophobic	  proteins.	  A	  study	  where	  different	  
detergents	   were	   compared	   showed	   that	   sodium	   dodecyl	   sulphate	   (SDS)	   was	   the	   most	  
effective	  to	  remove	  nuclear	  material	  and	  preserve	  the	  tissue	  (28).	  	  
Cell	  membranes	  contain	  phospholipids,	  which	  consist	  of	  a	  hydrophobic	  tails	  and	  a	  hydrophilic	  
head.	  	  
==PAGE:17==
17	    
Phospholipids	  form	  a	  lipid	  bilayer,	  where	  the	  hydrophilic	  part	  is	  directed	  against	  the	  aqueous	  
milieu	  and	  form	  hydrogen	  bonds	  with	  H2O,	  whereas	  the	  hydrophobic	  part	  is	  directed	  inwards	  
between	   the	   hydrophilic	   heads.	   SDS	   is	   an	   ionic	   and	   amphipathic	   molecule	   composed	   of	   a	  
hydrophilic	  head	  and	  hydrophobic	  tails	  (Fig.	  9A).	  Ionic	  detergents	  disrupt	  protein-­‐protein-­‐,	  
lipid-­‐protein	  –	  and	  lipid-­‐lipid	  interactions.	  The	  structure	  allows	  SDS	  to	  aggregate	  into	  micelles	  
(Fig.	  9B).	  At	  low	  concentrations,	  SDS	  remain	  in	  a	  monomeric	  form	  and	  aggregates	  into	  the	  
lipid	  bilayer.	  Though	  at	  high	  concentrations	  SDS	  disrupt	  and	  lyse	  the	  lipid	  bilayer,	  producing	  
micelles.	   The	   disadvantageous	   with	   using	   SDS,	   is	   that	   it	   has	   small	   chance	   of	   denaturising	  
proteins	  (29,30).	  	  
	  
Figure	  9.	  A)	  The	  structure	  of	  SDS,	  showing	  the	  hydrophilic	  and	  hydrophobic	  regions.	  B)	  Illustration	  of	  
SDS	  micelle	  (31).	  
	  
To	   evaluate	   the	   effectiveness	   of	   the	   decellularization	   regarding	   the	   amount	   of	   cellular	  
material	  left	  in	  the	  tissue,	  various	  experiments	  can	  be	  performed	  such	  as:	  histological	  staining,	  
immunoflourescent	   methods	   and	   DNA	   quantification.	   Evaluation	   of	   the	   effectiveness	   of	   the	  
decellularization	  considering	  the	  preservation	  of	  the	  ECM	  can	  be	  performed	  by	  methods	  such	  
as:	   Quantification	   of	   glycosaminoglycan	   (GAG)	   and	   ECM	   proteins	   (collagen	   type	   I,	   collagen	  
type	  IV,	  fibronectin	  (FN),	  and	  laminin-­‐β1),	  histological	  staining,	  immunofluorescence	  methods	  
to	  identify	  the	  presence	  of	  ECM	  proteins	  and	  GAG	  and	  Scanning	  Electron	  microscopy	  (SEM)	  
(25).	  
	  
	  
	  
==PAGE:18==
18	    
Extracellular	  Matrix	  
	  
	  
	  
	  
The	   ECM	   consists	   of	   proteins,	   water	   and	   polysaccharides,	   however	   each	   tissue	   type	   has	   a	  
different	  composition	  (32,33).	  	  
The	   ECM	   consists	   primarily	   of	   fibrous	   proteins	   and	   proteoglycans.	   The	   essential	   fibrous	  
proteins	  are	  elastin,	  collagen,	  FN	  and	  laminin,	  which	  are	  primarily	  produced	  by	  fibroblasts.	  
However,	   Collagen	   is	   the	   main	   fibrious	   protein,	   which	   is	   involved	   in	   the	   regulation	   of	   cell	  
adhesion,	   migration	   and	   chemotaxis.	   Collagen	   is	   produced	   and	   arranged	   in	   sheets	   by	  
fibroblasts.	  The	  predominant	  collagens	  in	  the	  ovary	  are	  collagen	  I,	  III,	  and	  IV.	  Collagen	  I	  is	  
present	  in	  larger	  amount	  in	  the	  outer	  layer	  and	  collagen	  III	  in	  the	  inner	  layers	  of	  the	  ovary.	  	  
Collagen	  I	  is	  predominantly	  present	  in	  theca	  externa	  and	  collagen	  III	  in	  all	  layers.	  In	  the	  basal	  
lamina	  Collagen	  IV	  is	  present	  separating	  granulosa	  and	  theca	  cells	  (33,34).	  Collagen	  is	  joined	  
to	  elastin,	  which	  contribute	  to	  recoil	  tissue	  that	  had	  been	  stretched.	  	  
Another	  fibrous	  ECM	  protein	  is	  FN	  that	  is	  involved	  in	  mediating	  cell	  attachment	  and	  function.

behaviour	   and	   is	   crucial	   for	   migration	   of	   cells.	   Laminin	   form	   sheets	   and	   are	   able	   to	   bind

Another	  fibrous	  protein	  is	  tenascin,	  which	  are	  associated	  with	  healing	  of	  wounds	  in	  the	  tissue.

5.	  Discussion	  
This	  is	  the	  first	  study	  to	  decellularize	  human	  ovarian	  tissue	  and	  successfully	  recellularize	  it	  
with	  human	  granulosa	  cells.	  Furthermore,	  murine	  preantral	  follicles	  were	  able	  to	  engraft	  and	  
survive	  in	  decellularized	  human	  ovarian	  tissue	  after	  transplantation	  to	  immunodeficient	  mice	  
for	  2	  weeks.	  In	  addition,	  an	  optimized	  decellularization	  protocol	  was	  developed	  for	  human	  
ovarian	  tissue.	  The	  first	  decellularized	  ovarian	  tissue	  was	  performed	  by	  researchers	  in	  2015	  
with	   0.1%	   SDS	   for	   24	   hrs.	   (23).	   However	   the	   optimization	   results	   of	   the	   decellularization	  
procedure	  were	  not	  presented.	  In	  addition,	  no	  chemical	  agent	  was	  used	  after	  reaction	  with	  
SDS	  to	  remove	  residual	  nuclear	  material	  from	  the	  DCT	  (23).	  In	  this	  study,	  we	  have	  managed	  to	  
describe	   a	   new	   approach	   towards	   this	   aim	   by	   studying	   and	   identifying	   techniques	   that	  
effectively	   decellularize	   human	   ovarian	   tissue.	   We	   demonstrated	   that	   treatment	   with	   0.1%	  
SDS	  and	  DNase	  I	  for	  6-­‐24	  hrs.	  remove	  cells	  and	  residual	  nuclear	  material	  from	  the	  ovarian	  
cortex	   tissue,	   with	   no	   visible	   damage.	   However,	   according	   to	   the	   results,	   decellularization	  
with	   0.1%	   SDS	   for	   6	   hrs.	   is	   sufficient	   to	   remove	   the	   cells	   and	   DNA	   from	   the	   cortex	   tissue.	  
Additionally,	   the	   color	   of	   the	   PAS-­‐stained	   decellularized	   cortex	   appeared	   more	   light	   pink	  
compared	  to	  the	  non-­‐DNase	  treated	  DCT	  cortex,	  which	  presumably	  is	  due	  to	  the	  elimination	  
of	  residual	  nuclear	  material.	  The	  results	  from	  the	  DAPI-­‐staining	  on	  DCT	  cortex	  treated	  with	  
only	  0.1%	  SDS,	  appeared	  to	  have	  DNA	  all	  over	  the	  tissue,	  which	  were	  effectively	  removed	  by	  
DNase	  I.	  The	  criteria	  for	  successful	  DCT	  were	  met	  for	  tissue	  fragments	  treated	  with	  0.1%	  SDS	  
and	  DNase	  I	  for	  6-­‐24	  hrs:	  
1) No	  nuclear/cell	  material	  visible	  by	  histological	  and	  DAPI	  staining	  
2) DNA	  content	  below	  50	  ng/mg	  DCT	  (25)	  	  
	  
	  
However,	  cortex	  tissue	  treated	  with	  only	  0.1%	  SDS	  did	  not	  meet	  these	  criteria’s.	  
The	  increasing	  focus	  on	  the	  content	  of	  DNA	  is	  due	  the	  residual	  DNA’s	  ability	  to	  initiate	  the	  
innate	  immune	  response,	  causing	  anemia	  and	  chronic	  arthritis.	  Additionally,	  residual	  cells	  in	  
the	  tissue	  can	  induce	  the	  immune	  system,	  leading	  to	  systemic	  lupus	  erythematosus.	  Therefore	  
researchers	  have	  set	  up	  some	  criteria's	  to	  ensure	  that	  the	  DCT	  does	  not	  induce	  severe	  host	  
responses(41).	  	  	  	  	  	  
Whether	  the	  ovarian	  DCT	  meets	  the	  criteria	  for	  effective	  DCT	  is	  not	  mentioned	  in	  the	  study	  
made	  by	  Laronda	  and	  colleagues	  (23).	  	  
==PAGE:41==
41	    
	  
Furthermore,	   in	   present	   study	   the	   microarchitecture	   and	   composition	   of	   the	   DCT	   weren’t	  
executed	   thoroughly,	   which	   is	   necessary	   to	   assure	   that	   the	   achieved	   DCT	   include	   the	  
important	  factors	  for	  cell	  growth,	  proliferation,	  adherence	  etc.	  Several	  researchers	  including	  
Loranda	  and	  colleagues	  have	  extensively	  investigated	  these	  issues	  (23,42).	  Their	  investigation	  
illustrated	  that	  the	  composition	  and	  microarchitecture	  of	  the	  DCT	  was	  nearly	  the	  same	  as	  the	  
native	  tissue.	  It	  is	  important	  to	  have	  a	  sufficient	  amount	  of	  GAG	  in	  the	  DCT,	  since	  the	  molecule	  
is	   closely	   associated	   with	   bioactive	   factors	   e.g.	   a	   component	   of	   the	   GAG	   named	   heparin	  
sulphate	  is	  the	  binding	  site	  for	  different	  growth	  factors	  (36).	  Other	  detergents	  have	  been	  used	  
to	  decellularize	  organ	  tissue,	  such	  as	  Triton	  X-­‐100.	  However,	  research	  has	  been	  shown	  that	  
Triton	   X-­‐100	   is	   not	   effective	   enough	   to	   decellularize	   and	   preserve	   the	   composition	   and	  
architecture	  of	  the	  DCT	  compared	  to	  SDS	  (43).	  However,	  Wen-­‐Yue	  liu	  and	  colleagues	  used	  
Triton	   X-­‐100	   prior	   to	   treatment	   with	   SDS,	   which	   led	   to	   better	   penetration	   of	   the	   ovarian	  
tissue	  by	  SDS	  (42).	  	  	  	  	  
An	   investigation	   on	   whether	   ovarian	   DCT	   could	   be	   stored	   by	   freezing	   was	   performed.	  
Cryopreservation	  of	  the	  DCT	  was	  executed	  with	  different	  CPAs	  including	  DMSO,	  Glycerol	  and	  
dH2O.	   The	   results	   showed	   that	   the	   DCT	   frozen	   with	   DMSO	   caused	   no	   freezing	   damage	  
compared	  to	  the	  tissue	  cryopreserved	  with	  Glycerol	  and	  dH2O.	  This	  indicates	  that	  CPAs	  are	  
essential	  for	  protecting	  the	  DCT	  from	  freezing	  damage.	  At	  LRB	  ethylenglycol	  and	  sucrose	  are	  
used	   to	   cryopreserve	   cortex	   ovarian	   tissue	   by	   slow-­‐freezing	   (17,44).	   This	   procedure	   takes	  
approximately	   3	   hrs.	   whereas	   the	   cryopreservation	   of	   DCT	   with	   DMSO	   takes	   about	   5	   min.	  
Furthermore	  the	  use	  of	  DMSO	  as	  CPA	  for	  cryopreservation	  of	  DCT	  have	  already	  been	  patented	  
(45).	  These	  findings	  suggest	  that	  cryopreservation	  of	  DCT	  with	  DMSO	  is	  effective	  and	  time	  
consuming.	  
Up	  to	  this	  date,	  recellularization	  has	  only	  been	  performed	  in	  porcine	  and	  bovine	  DTC	  seeded	  
with	  rodent	  ovarian	  cells	  (23,42).	  However,	  in	  this	  study	  we	  were	  for	  the	  first	  time	  able	  to	  
recellularize	  human	  ovarian	  DCT	  with	  different	  ovarian	  cell	  types,	  including	  human	  mature	  
and	  immature	  GCs	  and	  murine	  preantral	  follicles.	  Furthermore,	  the	  murine	  preantral	  follicles	  
were	   recellularized	   onto	   both	   human	   medulla	   and	   cortex	   DCT.	   The	   results	   showed,	   that	  
human	  immature	  and	  mature	  GCs	  were	  able	  to	  migrate	  through	  the	  tissue,	  when	  seeded	  and	  
cultured	  on	  the	  human	  cortex	  DCT.	  	  
==PAGE:42==
42	    
	  
These	  recellularized	  cortex	  tissue	  were	  cultured	  at	  different	  time	  points.	  	  At	  day	  4	  and	  6,	  cells	  
began	  to	  migrate	  to	  the	  middle	  of	  the	  DCT,	  though	  more	  cells	  had	  migrated	  at	  day	  10	  and	  12.	  
Indicating	  that	  seeding	  with	  GCs	  for	  10-­‐12	  days	  is	  the	  optimal	  incubation	  time.	  However,	  the	  
cortex	   tissue	   weren’t	   filled	   with	   cells,	   which	   can	   be	   due	   the	   low	   number	   of	   cells,	   short	  
incubation	  time	  or	  that	  the	  cells	  were	  ending	  up	  in	  the	  bottom	  of	  the	  well.	  
The	  histological	  results	  for	  the	  human	  DCT	  seeded	  with	  enzymatic	  isolated	  murine	  preantral	  
follicles	  for	  3	  days	  indicate,	  that	  the	  follicles	  have	  been	  damaged	  by	  the	  enzymes	  used,	  since	  it	  
appears	   that	   only	   the	   GCs	   migrated	   into	   the	   DCT.	   Although	   many	   small	   follicles	   may	   be	  
obtained	   by	   enzymatic	   isolation,	   the	   enzymes	   damage	   the	   theca	   cell	   layer	   and	   basement	  
membrane.	  The	  theca	  cells	  and	  the	  basement	  membrane	  are	  crucial	  for	  structural	  support	  to	  
the	  follicle	  (46).	  Mechanical	  isolation	  of	  follicles	  is	  therefore	  preferred	  and	  used	  in	  the	  seeding	  
experiments	  in	  vitro	  and	  in	  vivo	  in	  this	  study.	  Other	  explanations	  for	  the	  lack	  of	  migration	  of	  
follicles	  can	  be	  due	  to	  the	  short	  incubation	  time,	  residual	  SDS	  in	  the	  DCT	  and	  tissue	  damage	  by	  
SDS,	  resulting	  in	  the	  cells	  not	  having	  their	  optimal	  conditions.	  In	  addition,	  insufficient	  seeding	  
can	   also	   be	   due	   to	   the	   lack	   of	   cell	   types	   that	   normally	   reside	   in	   the	   tissue	   e.g.	   due	   to	   the	  
dependency	  on	  factors	  they	  secrete.	  We	  further	  evaluated	  the	  E2	  levels	  of	  medium	  aspirated	  
from	  both	  human	  medulla	  and	  cortex	  DCT	  seeded	  and	  cultured	  with	  murine	  preantral	  follicles	  
and	   control	   DCT	   in	   vitro.	   At	   day	   2	   and	   5	   the	   concentrations	   of	   E2	   for	   the	   recellularized	  
medulla	  and	  cortex	  tissue	  were	  significantly	  higher	  than	  control.	  In	  addition,	  the	  increase	  of	  
E2	   was	   higher	   for	   recellularized	   cortex	   tissue	   from	   day	   2	   to	   5,	   compared	   to	   recellularized	  
medulla.	  These	  observations	  indicates	  that	  cortex	  tissue	  provide	  a	  better	  niche	  for	  growth,	  
seeding	   and	   differentiation	   of	   follicles.	   In	   addition,	   a	   low	   production	   level	   of	   E2	   in	   control	  
medulla	  and	  cortex	  DCT	  were	  observed,	  possibly	  due	  to	  contamination,	  since	  no	  cells	  were	  
observed	   in	   the	   histology	   analysis	   of	   the	   tissue.	   Researchers	   have	   been	   able	   to	   restore	   E2	  
production	   in	   ovarieoctimized	   mice,	   which	   is	   important,	   to	   ensure	   that	   the	   recellularized	  
ovarian	  tissue	  can	  provide	  endocrine	  support	  in	  vivo	  (23).	  However,	  the	  histological	  results	  
showed	  almost	  no	  migration	  of	  follicles	  into	  the	  DCT,	  which	  suggest	  that	  the	  follicles	  probably	  
ended	  up	  in	  the	  bottom	  of	  the	  wells.	  
	  
==PAGE:43==
43	    
Furthermore,	   we	   demonstrated	   that	   follicles	   seed	   and	   grow	   in	   human	   medulla	   and	   cortex	  
DCT,	   when	   cultured	   in	   vitro	   and	   in	   vivo	   in	   mice.	   Several	   cells	   were	   present	   in	   the	   DCT	  
xenografted	  in	  the	  mouse,	  likely	  originating	  from	  the	  mouse,	  since	  no	  cells	  were	  present	  in	  the	  
non-­‐xenografted	   DCT.	   In	   addition,	   most	   preantral	   follicles	   had	   seeded	   the	   cortex	   tissue	  
compared	  to	  medulla	  tissue.	  However,	  for	  the	  first	  time	  the	  preantral	  follicles	  supported	  the	  
growth	  and	  development	  to	  antral	  follicles	  in	  medulla	  tissue.	  These	  observations	  indicate	  that	  
survival	  and	  growth	  of	  preantral	  follicles	  is	  significantly	  improved	  when	  seeded	  and	  cultured	  
in	   vivo	   in	   mice.	   These	   examinations	   signify	   the	   difficulty	   of	   mimicking	   the	   physiological	  
environment	  that	  is	  present	  in	  a	  host	  organism.	  	  
Numerous	   studies	   have	   attempted	   to	   produce	   artificial	   organs.	   Recently,	   researchers	   have	  
been	  able	  to	  create	  a	  liver	  recellularized	  with	  progenitor	  cells,	  which	  expressed	  some	  hepatic	  
characteristics	  (47).	  Additionally,	  porcine	  pancreas	  has	  been	  decellularized	  and	  seeded	  with	  
pancreatic	  islets,	  which	  secreted	  insulin	  (24,48).	  Another	  reason	  for	  insufficient	  migration	  of	  
follicles	  compared	  to	  e.g.	  pancreatic	  islets	  cells	  can	  be	  due	  to	  follicles	  having	  a	  bigger	  size.	  To	  
solve	  this	  issue,	  researchers	  have	  made	  wounds	  in	  the	  ovarian	  tissue	  for	  better	  migration	  of	  
cells	  (42).	  In	  addition,	  scientists	  have	  tried	  to	  develop	  synthetic	  artificial	  ovarian	  tissue	  made	  
from	   e.g.	   alginate.	   In	   the	   study	   artificial	   alginate	   were	   recellularized	   with	   follicles	   and	  
transplanted	  into	  mice,	  however	  the	  majority	  of	  the	  follicles/cells	  didn’t	  survive,	  due	  to	  the	  
lack	   of	   their	   natural	   environment	   and	   niche	   in	   the	   ovarian	   tissue	   (37).	   This	   is	   another	  
indication	  of	  the	  importance	  of	  using	  biological	  scaffolds,	  which	  provide	  the	  environment	  and	  
niche	   in	   the	   tissue.	   Perhaps	   in	   the	   future	   3D	   synthetic	   artificial	   scaffold	   will	   be	   provided,	  
however	   additional	   research	   has	   to	   be	   performed	   to	   ensure	   that	   the	   scaffold	   contain	   the	  
architecture	  and	  component	  of	  the	  ovarian	  tissue,	  to	  ensure	  the	  right	  niche	  and	  milieu	  for	  the	  
cells.

6.	  Future	  perspectives	  
In	  this	  study	  we	  used	  ovarian	  cells	  to	  evaluate	  the	  seeding	  of	  DCT,	  which	  can	  have	  various	  
challenges	  if	  established,	  due	  to	  the	  limited	  resources	  of	  ovaries	  and	  follicles/cells.	  However,	  
this	  may	  be	  solved	  by	  using	  safe	  artificial	  ovaries	  based	  on	  induced	  pluripotent	  stem	  (iPS)	  
cells	   from	   the	   recipient	   seeded	   on	   donor	   ovarian	   DCT	   (Fig.	   28).	   This	   would	   lead	   to	  
personalized	  ovarian	  transplant	  without	  any	  risk	  of	  introducing	  cancer	  to	  the	  recipient.	  This	  
has	  been	  tested	  in	  other	  tissues	  e.g.	  when	  rat	  lung	  where	  recellularized	  with	  iPS	  cells,	  alveolar	  
cell	   markers	   were	   expressed	   (23).	   In	   addition,	   using	   synthetic	   artificial	   ovaries	   can	   solve	  
limited	  resource	  of	  ovaries.	  However,	  additional	  research	  needs	  to	  be	  performed	  to	  apply	  iPS	  
cells	  and	  synthetic	  artificial	  ovaries	  (37).	  
	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  	  
	  
Figure	  28.	  A	  modified	  figure	  of	  the	  generation	  of	  oocytes	  from	  iPS	  cells	  seeded	  on	  the	  DCT	  (23,49).	  	  	  
In	  the	  future	  the	  microarchitecture	  and	  composition	  of	  the	  DCT	  needs	  to	  be	  evaluated,	  due	  to	  
its	   importance	   for	   cell	   growth,	   proliferation,	   adherence	   etc.	   Studies	   have	   shown	   that	  
hepatocytes	   seeded	   onto	   DCT	   of	   rat	   liver	   partly	   gained	   their	   metabolic	   function	   (47).	   To	  
evaluate	   the	   composition	   of	   the	   DCT	   several	   methods	   can	   be	   performed,	   such	   as	  
immunofluorescence	   and	   quantitative	   measurements	   on	   components	   of	   the	   DCT,	   such	   as	  
collagens,	  fibronectin	  etc.	  Furthermore,	  SEM	  could	  perform	  an	  evaluation	  of	  the	  architecture	  
of	  the	  DCT.	  To	  assure	  that	  no	  residual	  detergent	  are	  left	  in	  the	  DCT	  a	  cytotoxicity	  test,	  such	  as	  
CCK-­‐8	  need	  to	  be	  performed.	  In	  the	  future	  additional	  in	  vivo	  recellularization	  studies	  needs	  to	  
performed	   with	   follicles	   to	   optimize	   the	   number	   of	   follicles	   migrating	   into	   the	   tissue	   and	  
restore	   the	   endocrine	   support.	   The	   optimization	   can	   include	   change	   of	   culture	   medium,	  
follicle	   isolation	   method,	   grafting	   procedure	   etc.	   Furthermore	   recellularization	   of	   human	  
ovarian	  tissue	  with	  human	  follicles	  will	  also	  lead	  the	  research	  closer	  to	  decellularizing	  whole	  
ovaries	  and	  recellularization	  with	  human	  follicles.	  	  
==PAGE:45==
45	    
7.	  Conclusion	  
	  
This	  study	  demonstrates	  a	  new	  method	  for	  decellularization	  and	  recellularization	  of	  human	  
ovarian	   tissue,	   which	   could	   be	   a	   potential	   strategy	   to	   eliminate	   the	   risk	   of	   reintroducing	  
malignant	  cells	  to	  the	  women	  receiving	  autotransplantation	  of	  cryopreserved	  ovarian	  tissue.	  
However,	  optimization	  and	  further	  studies	  are	  needed	  in	  order	  to	  confirm	  the	  applicability	  of	  
this	  new	  and	  promising	  method.
