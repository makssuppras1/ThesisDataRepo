Abstract
This project aims to develop a small prototype of a self-stabilized platform for stretchers.
The project includes the election and description of components used for the prototype,
consisting of DC linear actuators to move the platform, L298N motor controller, MPU-
6050 inertia measurement sensor, and Arduino as a microcontroller to process the data.
It explains the kinematics of the platform to know the desired position of the motor rel-
ative to the platform base. The electronic circuit and communication between modules
are also explained as well as the control for the motor position.

CHAPTER 1
Introduction
Every year more and more people are going out of their homes to enjoy the mountains
and countryside and sometimes accidents happen. When rescue teams have to bring an
injured from the mountains it usually takes a long time, a large group of rescuers and
resources.
The equipment that rescuers use has been the same for years and with the increasing
number of injured people, rescuers are having problems facing this huge demand.
For this reason, I brought up a startup in which we are developing an assistive device that
can be placed under any stretcher and it helps bring injured people down the mountain
much faster. With an electric power propulsion system and rubber tracks, the vehicle
can go through any kind of terrain, and it allows rescues in mountains and countryside
where no roads are available.
Figure 1.1: Rubber track vehicle under a stretcher.
Nowadays, electronics, robotics, and electric vehicles are becoming more popular in
all kinds of fields where before were fully mechanical without any technology. The moun-
tain rescue sector has been always using the same material with small improvements but
now that technology is more accessible than ever we decided to incorporate it into this
field. So far we have developed the first vehicle after going through various prototypes.

1 Introduction
This project tries to solve another issue that rescue teams have which is maintaining
the comfort and stability of the injured person on top of the stretcher. For this reason,
a prototype solving this problem is going to be developed.
1.1
Objectives
This project aims to design and create a prototype of a self-stabilized platform that
can be placed between the assistive device and the stretcher that allowing a stable ride.
The device can be also placed on other surfaces like all-terrain ambulances, boats, and
helicopters.
In this project, a prototype on a reduced scale will be done using the same controller
and electronics that would be used on the real product. To achieve this goal, the work
it will be done on the following topics:
1. The stabilized platform needs to be able to be placed on top of the assistive device.
2. It has to be able to carry the weight of a person on top.
3. The design of the system needs to be done by choosing the most convenient motors
by their shape, weight, and strength.
4. The stretcher needs to be able to stabilize up to 30 degrees.
5. Being able to read the inertial measurements from and Inertial Measurement Unit
(IMU) (3.2.1) which will allow knowing the orientation and inclination of the
stretcher.
6. A microcontroller to be able to work with the information received from the IMU
and move the motors proportionally.

2.2
Stabilization speed
To achieve the main objective of stabilizing the stretcher, it is necessary to know the
speed that the platform needs to be stabilized. Knowing that it needs to be mounted
on top of the assistive device, it is necessary to make some tests to know how the
inclination changes over time. For this reason, a gyroscope (3.2.1) has been mounted on
top to obtain a graph of the inclination in Roll and Pitch angles. The test has been done
on a grass terrain at a speed of 2 Km/h. In case of increasing the speed, the stabilized
platform should move faster.
In the figure (2.1) the blue line of the graph represents the Roll angle and the red
line the Pitch angle. The angle is measured from 0 to 9,8 which is equivalent to 0 to 90
degrees. In this case, our maximum of 30 degrees will be equal to 3,26. When looking at
the Roll angle, it can be seen that the vehicle changes its inclination at a maximum of ap-
proximately 30 degrees in 1 second. This is the reference that will be used on this project.

2.3
Motors
The first thing that needs to be chosen for the design of the self-stabilized stretcher is
the motors. If the motors need to be lightweight it means that it is not possible to use
large motors that are fast and can provide a large amount of torque. Then, the only
solution is to use smaller motors that are less powerful. In order to increase the torque
of the motor, gears can be used to increase the torque but the speed will decrease.
For this reason, the motors that suit the best for this application are the linear actuators.
A linear actuator is a device that converts rotational motion into linear motion. Since
it is able to provide both push and pull movements, it can be used on a large variety
of devices [Dic18]. It is possible to have an accurate motion control which makes them
very easy to operate. They have almost no maintenance and they are energy eﬀicient.
In the following figure (2.2) it can be seen all the components inside a standard linear
actuator. First, it uses an electric motor that provides mechanical energy from electrical
energy. Direct current motors tend to have a high speed and low torque, for this reason
after the motor, linear actuators usually have a gearbox that reduces the speed and
increases torque. Attached to the gearbox, a lead screw allows a piston where a screw
nut is placed to move up and down allowing it to generate the linear motion. Some
linear actuators incorporate two small switches attached to the piston that ensures that

Figure 2.2: Linear actuator [22b].

weight of 5Kg. Since the motor uses a threaded rod inside the linear actuator, it is not

The motor could be powered with a simple DC motor controller of a simple H-bridge

2.4
Design
The self-stabilized platform needs to be mounted on top of the small vehicle used for
bringing down injured people from the mountains. This vehicle has a rectangular surface
on top with a measurement of 440mm x 166mm. It is a relatively small surface compared
to the size of a regular stretcher which is 715mm x 2461mm. This means that the motors
should be placed on top of this surface. In order to do that, four motors are going to
be used for stabilizing the stretcher on top. The four motors will be placed one on each
corner of the vehicle. That will allow us to bring up and down each one of the sides as it
can be seen in the figure (2.4). Another possible configuration that has been taken into
account is using 3 linear actuators instead of 4. In this particular case, since the person
is laying down, the side of the stretcher with only one motor it would need to do two
times more strength than the other side which would mean that a bigger motor would
be needed. By using 4 motors with the same specifications, the weight is balanced and
all motors can be controlled with the same kind of motor controller.
Figure 2.4: Prototype.
Knowing that the stretcher needs to carry the weight of one person on top, and the
average weight is from 80 to 120Kg plus the weight of the stretcher which could be
around 20 Kg, then the stabilizer should hold a maximum of 140 Kg. Knowing that
each linear actuator has a push torque of 1000N which is equivalent to 102Kg, it will be
capable of stabilizing the person on top without any weight issues.
Now we want to see if the stretcher would be able to stabilize fast enough. In this
application, there is no need for the stabilizer to have a fast speed since the inclination
will be gradually and it is not made to have any instantaneous stabilization. The linear
actuators have a speed of 120mm/s and if the distance between motors is 300 and
164 long and wide respectively, it is possible to know which stroke the linear motors
should have. The maximum inclination that the stretcher should work is 30 degrees. By
applying some trigonometry, it is possible to find that the length of the stroke should be
173mm and 95mm for the roll and pitch angles (2.1), (2.2). So a 200mm stroke should
be the best fit for this application. If the speed of the motors is 120mm/s when turning

3.2.1
IMU
The measurement of the platform inclination needs to be done by an electronic gyro-
scope to be able to process the data and move the platform to the desired position.
The sensor is placed on the top platform of the stretcher [20a]. In this case, an inertial
measurement unit (IMU) is used. An IMU is an electronic sensor that measures the
orientation thanks to a combination of accelerometers, gyroscopes, and sometimes mag-
netometers. It is a low-power, low cost and high-performance tracking device suitable
for smartphones, tablets and all kinds of small electrical devices [20a].
The IMU that is going to be used for this project is the MPU-6050 [22k] since it is
the most commonly used for IoT and automated projects.
Figure 3.2: MPU-6050.
The MPU-6050 is a device with 6 degrees of freedom (DoF) that combines a 3-axis
accelerometer with a 3-axis gyroscope, and a Digital Motion Processor (DMP). The
DMP allows for reducing the computational load on the microcontroller by doing com-
==PAGE:21==
3.2 Components
13
plex calculations with the sensor’s information inside the chip.
The accelerometer measures the acceleration of an object in an instantaneous rest
frame. The accelerometer is based on the gravitational acceleration measuring the force
that is applied to each axis when movement is applied. The units of the acceleration
are measured in . It uses a triple-axis micro-electro-mechanical system (MEMS) that
consists of a micro-machined structure placed on the silicon wafer [22c]. This structure
deflects when an acceleration is applied to any of the angles changing the capacitance.
This capacitance is proportional to the acceleration and is converted into an analog out-
put voltage.
The gyroscope measures the angular velocity of the object. The three types of an-
gular measurements depending on the direction are roll, pitch, and yaw. Roll is the
horizontal rotation when seeing the object from the front, the pitch is the vertical ro-
tation seen from the front, and yaw is the rotation seen from above on a flat surface.
It has higher accuracy than the accelerometer but since it is independent of gravity, it
has some drifting errors over time. For more robust and accurate motion sensing, the
gyroscope and accelerometer are usually combined.
Figure 3.3: Roll, Pitch, Yaw angles [21].

The module can be powered with the 5V logic pin of a microcontroller since it has an

which makes it suitable for battery-driven devices.
It uses the Inter-Integrated Circuit (I2C) communication with the microcontroller
which is a bi-directional connection that consists of two signals: serial data (SDA), and
serial clock (SCL). The SDA signal is where the information is exchanged, and the SCL

3.2.3
Motor controller
Microcontrollers usually can only output a 40mA peak in each one of the pins, and this
means that it has not enough current to move electric motors. For this reason, a motor
controller that uses the digital information from the microcontroller and the power from
a stable power supply needs to be used. There are some possibilities to control a motor
without a motor controller but some extra hardware needs to be added.
Using the Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) output from the microcontroller and an
external power supply, it is possible to change the speed of the motor depending on the
duty cycle. The duty cycle is the amount of time that the pulse is ON versus the amount
of time that is OFF. In the figure, 3.5 can be seen two different duty cycles of a PWM
signal with the average voltage.
==PAGE:23==
3.2 Components
15
Figure 3.5: PWM Dutty cycle.
It is possible to connect the PWM signal to the base of a transistor or the gate of
a MOSFET and be able to control the speed. By connecting the drain to the negative
terminal of the motor and the source to the negative terminal of the power supply, the
transistor will close and open very fast and the pulse will result almost in a constant
voltage that the motor will use for turning (3.6). This method can only be used with a
small motor since the current that can go through the transistor is very small and using
a bigger current would heat up the transistor creating power losses and even damaging
the transistor. Another disadvantage of this method is that the motor can only turn in
one direction.
Figure 3.6: Speed controller with a transistor.
To turn the motor in both directions, a H-Bridge electronic circuit can be used.
==PAGE:24==

DC-motors can reverse the spinning direction by applying the inverse voltage in the
terminals of the motor. The H-Bridge configuration like the figure (3.7) uses four tran-
sistors as switching elements. When all switches are open, the motor will stop. When
only switch 1 and 4 are closed, the voltage will flow as indicated in the color blue and
the motor will turn clockwise. When switches 2 and 3 are closed, the motor will turn
counterclockwise [22e]. Switches are replaced by transistors that make the same func-
tionality but can be controlled straight from a microcontroller.
Figure 3.7: H-Bridge reverse circuit.
A solution to simplify the two previous electronic circuits is by using a motor con-
troller. In this case, the most convenient one for this kind of project is the L298N
motor controller . The L298N is a dual H-Bridge motor controller that allows con-
trolling the direction and the speed of two DC motors at the same time. The voltage of
the motors can be between 5V and 32V with a peak current of 2 amps [22i].
The L298N motor controller from the figure (3.8) uses outputs 1 and 2 to connect to one
motor and outputs 3 and 4 to connect to the second one. For powering the motor, a 5
to 32V power source needs to be placed, and in the case of using 12V, the internal 5V
regulator can be used for powering the internal IC, and the 5V can be used as an output
to power small microcontrollers. Signals IN1 and IN2 are used to control the internal
H-Bridge of the L298N allowing to change the turning direction of motor 1. ENA is the
enable pin of motor 1, in case the jumper is used, this signal will be always high and
the motor will run at the maximum input voltage. If the speed needs to be adjusted, a
PWM signal needs to be connected to it and it will work as explained at the beginning
of section 2.2.4. For controlling the second motor, the pins that are going to be used
will be IN3 and IN4 for controlling the direction and ENB to control the speed.

3.2.4
Microcontroller
A microcontroller is a small computer on an integrated circuit, it can contain one or
more processor cores, memory, and input/output peripherals [Lut19]. Microcontrollers
are the brain of any embedded system and can be found in vehicles, robots, home appli-
ances, smart systems, and other devices.
For this project, Arduino is used. An Arduino is an open-source platform that is
commonly used in many robotic projects with sensors, motors and other devices [22a].
It consists of a microcontroller placed on a printed circuit board with some voltage reg-
ulators, LEDs, resistors, and other components. The board includes some peripheral
pins both digital and analog with input/output functions, PWM pins, an I2C communi-
cation port, a serial port, and more. There are different Arduino boards with different
characteristics to be chosen depending on the type of use. For this project, the Arduino
Uno (3.9) is used since it has the necessary pins to connect the motor controllers and
the IMU sensor through I2C communication.

4.2
The control
The control system controls how the system operates to achieve a certain goal. It uses
the calculations used previously and the parameters and configurations of the different
==PAGE:37==
4.2 The control
29
sensors, motors, and motor controllers.
The system contains an input which is the
gyroscope sensor information, a process that contains all the algorithms, and an output
which is the movement of the motors. There are two types of control systems, open-
loop, and closed-loop. The open-loop control system is when the output does not have
feedback from the input to correct variations. The closed-loop control system [22f],
that is the one used in this project, is the one in which the output affects the input
when maintaining the desired output value. To maintain or achieve the desired value, a
controller is implemented, which modifies the error parameter to have a faster and more
precise response.
The controller that maintains the stability of the platform, a special control has
been developed to achieve maximum accuracy and robustness. The sampling frequency
is 100 Hz which is imposed by the IMU sensor rate. The following control has been
developed to control the stabilization of the platform in the following sequence:
1. First, the information from the gyroscope is used to know the inclination of the
sensor. Note that the inclination from 0 to 90 degrees, is received with a value
from 0 to 9,8 which corresponds to the amount of gravity that is applied to the
certain axis. The two axis useful for this application is the roll and pitch. Since
it exists some drift on the angles, an offset is applied to correct the inclination.
Arduino provides a library that makes easier the reading of these values.
2. The gyroscope sensor is very sensitive and any small vibration is detected creating
wrong lectures. To solve this problem, a Kalman filter is added which estimates
a joint probability distribution over the variables for each time frame. Thanks to
this filter, all the high peak values are also smoothed. A library is used to reduce
the amount of code.
3. Then, the PID is computed for both angles and makes a continuous calculation of
the error which is the difference between the desired and the measured value. PID
controller which stands for Proportional Integral Derivative is an automatically
optimized control system used to regulate all kinds of different motors, sensors,
and actuators to the desired value [20b]. Thanks to the closed-loop feedback it
can apply an accurate and responsive correction to a control function.
The following block diagram 4.4 shows how the three control terms of proportional
derivative and integral are applied. The input of the diagram r(t) is the desired
value and the output y(t) is the measured value. The error, e(t) = r(t) −y(t) is
the difference between the desired value and the measured one. The PID control
will try to minimize the error by adjusting the control variable u(t).

6.2 Future works
39
As it can be seen on the picture, when the bottom part is tilted, the top center of
mass is not placed on the middle of the vehicle. This makes that the linear actuators
are not longer working on the direction they are supposed, and have forces that tries to
bend them. That could be solved with a different configuration of the motors.
Since there is no feedback from the motors, it is possible that the platform, instead of
staying in the middle on the stroke it stays higher or lower. If this happens, it is possible
that when the stretcher is tilted, the motors will try to pull further than they are sup-
posed to. In the prototype that means that the nuts get out of the threaded screw. In
real linear actuators, that would not be an issue since they have a switch at the top and
bottom of the stroke that prevents moving further than their length. In case one of the
actuators arrives to the limit, the other will continue moving on the opposite direction
and the stretcher will stay stabilized. The only issue that will happen is that the speed
of the actuator will decrease.
The power consumption of the linear actuators have been tested by adding weight on
top of the motors. The power consumption increases lineal when adding weight on top
of the stretcher. When the motors are stop, the power consumption can be negligible
since it does not have to use energy to stop the motors since the gears create enough
friction.

6.2
Future works
Some possible improvements that could be implemented on the project would be able
make the system more robust are described here:
• If the assistive device goes faster than 2 Km/h, the speed of the stabilized platform
should be increased. Since the motors used have a lot of push force, it could be
possible to put the four motors closer.
By doing this, the speed will increase.
Another option should be to find linear actuators that moves faster, but linear
actuators are not made to move fast, but instead to have a lot of strength.
• Since there is no feedback on the movement of the linear actuators, one of the
problems is that it is not possible to know the high of the stretcher. For this
reason, a solution proposed, it is to place a distance sensor between the stretcher
and the platform that allows to place the stretcher always at the same high. Thanks
to this sensor and the help of the IMU it could be possible to absorb bumps by
reading the gravity on the Z axis.

6 Discussion
• When using real linear actuators that have more precision and less free play, the
measurements on the sensor will be more accurate and a better stabilization will
be possible to be done.
• All the information of the position of the stretcher is relying on a single sensor. If
two sensors were used, it could be possible to do the mean between both sensors
to have more accuracy. Additionally some code could be done to prevent errors
on the values of the inclination.
• As mentioned on the previous chapter, when the platform on the top is tilted, the
center of mass does not stays on the center of the device. This makes that the
linear actuators have side forces that creates bending forces. To avoid this problem,
a better position of the linear motor should be done like a pyramid shape.
