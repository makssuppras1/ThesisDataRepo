Abstract
With the current trend of global urbanization, the Copenhagen Nordhavn area plans to expand with
40,000 new residents and 40,000 new jobs. At the same time, in order to achieve the ambitious goal of 100%
usage of renewable energy, PV penetration in Nordhavn area will increase in the near future. As a result, with
the new loads integrated, the current capacity of distribution line will be challenged. Moreover, during the
N-1 contingency, the severe voltage drop may inﬂuence the reliable operation of the Nordhavn distribution
grid. The Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) is an effective approach for peak load shaving and voltage
support, thus, a 0.5MWh BESS is planned for the ancillary services in Nordhavn area. Therefore, this
thesis deals with the study of the BESS peak load shaving and voltage support services in order to defer the
grid upgrade and improve the operation reliability, from the perspective of Distribution System Operator
(DSO).
In this thesis, the relevant feeders are modelled in Matlab/Simulink. The active power control for peak
load shaving and reactive power control for voltage support are implemented in S functions and control
parameters are deﬁned through user deﬁned interface. The BESS is also evaluated on their annual lifetime
degradation and energy storage. Sensitivity analyses are conducted to evaluate the inﬂuence of different
converter sizes, PV capacities and orientations.
The simulation results show that, for commercial load, with an optimal BESS converter size of 264kW
and the South oriented PV with a capacity of 1MW, the maximum peak load power in the future is
2.711MW, which is 7.2 times larger than the current one. On the other hand, for residential load, with an
optimal BESS converter size of 261kW, the future maximum peak load power is 2.49MW, which is 3.15
times larger. Besides, with the BESS, the voltage drop can be reduced by 2.52% maximally during the N-1
contingency.
The study shows that the peak load shaving service enables the increase of the current load demands.
With a correlation coefﬁcient study between the PV output and load power during the peak load period,
it can be predicted whether the PV penetration can help the peak load shaving or not. With the voltage
support service by the BESS, the voltage drop can be limited within 6%, therefore a reliable operation can
be achieved.

CHAPTER 1
Introduction
In this chapter, the motivation of this master thesis is ﬁrstly explained in the background. Then, the
state of the art of the BESS applications in power systems and a stressed distribution grid are presented.
Subsequently, a literature review concerning the Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) technology is
conducted. Additionally, as part of the EnergyLab Nordhavn project, an overview is brieﬂy introduced.
Furthermore, the objectives of the thesis are proposed and the approach of study is outlined.
1.1
Background
With the increasing of load demands, the power ﬂow of the power system will exceed the capacity limit
of the transmission line, which will result in a stressed grid and inﬂuence the operation reliability of the
grid. In Denmark, the Transmission System Operator (TSO) and Distribution System Operator (DSO) are
responsible for a secure operation of the power system and for carrying out the reinforcement of the grid [1],
therefore, an investment of the grid upgrade is necessary to increase the capacity limit of the existing power
system. Moreover, the increase of load demands can lead to the voltage drop problem, therefore a stressed
grid, which may trip a generator and challenge the secure operation of the grid. The BESS, which can charge
during the off peak hours and discharge during the peak hours rapidly, can provide the peak load shaving
service from the utility’s perspective [2]. Typically, the peak load only occurs a few times a year, therefore,
the BESS can delay or remove the investment of grid upgrade. Besides, the BESS can also provide voltage
support to maintain the voltage magnitudes within the required limit with both active and reactive power
control [3].
Recently, the integration of wind power, solar power and Electric Vehicles (EVs) has a signiﬁcant
development in order to reduce the global greenhouse gas emission. Denmark aims to realize an independence
of fossil fuels and to serve electricity, heating, industry and transport sectors with only renewable energy
by the year of 2050 [4]. However, the characteristic of uncertainty of the renewable energy may lead to
the ﬂuctuation in the power system. For the purpose of peak load shaving, the correlation between the PV
output and load demand is studied in order to investigate how the uncertain generation of PV can help the
peak load shaving service of the BESS.
In this thesis, the BESS peak load shaving and voltage support services are closely related to Copenhagen
EnergyLab Nordhavn project, where, a 0.5MWh BESS is planned to be implemented in line NGT 65 in
order to provide the ancillary services for the 10kV grid. Since there are many commercial and residential
buildings under construction, the motivation of the thesis is to investigate both the maximum load demand
in the future Nordhavn NGT 65 feeder and the optimal converter size of the BESS for peak load shaving
service. Besides, the thesis will analyse the amount of the voltage drop that is reduced by the BESS with

1.2
The State of the Art of BESS Applications in Power System
For the purpose of reducing the global warming emissions, the renewable energy penetration is increasing
to replace the one from the conventional power plants, e.g. coal-ﬁred plants. Denmark is in a leading position
in battling the climate change and has ambitious energy goals. In a short term, compared to the fuel
consumption in 2009, Denmark plans to decrease the fossil fuel consumption by 33% before 2020. At the
same time, Denmark will improve the energy efﬁciency in order to fulﬁl the goal of 33% renewable energy
penetration by 2020 [5]. In a long term, by the year of 2050, Denmark aims to realize the energy penetration
free of fossil fuels and to serve electricity, heating, industry and transport sectors with only renewable energy
[4].
The BESS plays an important role in increasing the power system reliability, penetration of the renewable
energy and the reduction of the cost of the grid [3], and many signiﬁcant projects have been carried out
worldwide [6]. As shown in Fig. 1.1, the BESS can provide thirteen services which are categorized by
three stakeholder groups, customers, utilities which are equivalent to Danish TSOs/ DSOs, and Independent
System Operators/Regional Transmission Organizations (ISO/RTOs) which are equivalent to Danish TSOs.
The services are categorized by the groups which beneﬁt from the services the most, though they may
create values for more than one group. Alternatively, the services are divided into three locations where the
BESS can deliver them, namely, transmission level, distribution level and behind the meter. In the following
sections, ﬁve main services of the BESS will be interpreted in detail, and in this thesis, the services provided
by the BESS will focus on peak load shaving and voltage control.

1.2.1
Frequency Regulation
The imbalance between the active power generation and consumption may lead to a deviation of the
power system frequency which is measured in the unit of Hz. Denmark is part of the Continental Europe
(CE) Synchronous Area, wherein the frequency shall be kept close to the nominal value of 50Hz. According
to the European Network of Transmission System Operators for Electricity (ENTSOE), for different response
time, frequency regulation can be classiﬁed into primary control, secondary control and tertiary control,
while the BESS can be used in all the control strategies [7]. In Denmark, the TSO is responsible for
maintaining the frequency stability [8].
Spinning reserve, used for primary control, is often achieved by keeping conventional power plants
(natural gas or diesel plants) spinning. Compared to them, the BESS can provide a quicker and more
accurate spinning reserve, therefore, it results in a less response time for both positive and negative frequency
regulation. Particularly, when the renewable energy penetration is high, the ﬂuctuation of the power
generation is high, leading to a quicker need of frequency regulation. Besides, the BESS can contribute to
a cost saving from the greenhouse gas emission and maintenance cost caused by the conventional power
plants operation within a certain response time requirement [9].
1.2.2
Peak Load Shaving
Peak load shaving, also known as peak load shifting or load levelling, is to decrease the load demand
during peak hours and to adjust the load demand during off peak hours as shown in Fig. 1.2 [10]. This service
could beneﬁt the customers by avoiding the installation of generation units during peak load time, saving
the bills by reducing loads during peak load time. It can also beneﬁt the DSO/TSO with distribution and
transmission grid upgrade deferral, and reduce the greenhouse gas emissions from conventional generation
units, etc. Therefore, peak load shaving with the BESS has received a great attention and many pilot projects
worldwide has been implemented [11–13].

1.2.3
Voltage Support
Voltage support is another ancillary service that the BESS can provide, especially in distribution grid.
Voltage problems, e.g. voltage drop, may arise when there is a large demand or fault occurring on the
distribution network. The lower voltage will result in a higher current to preserve the active power, therefore,
it will encounter the problems of overheating and higher power losses. Additionally, voltage drop may
also lead to a smaller torque of a generator to yield a tripping of the generator. On the other hand, the
overvoltage problem may also exist with a high penetration of renewable energy. In Denmark, it is DSO
or TSO’s responsibility to keep the voltage magnitude within a change of ±10%. Voltage support can be
achieved by adjusting the tap changer of the transformer, and regulating the reactive power through Static
Var Compensator (SVC) and synchronous generators [19]. The BESS is also capable of regulating the
voltage by injecting or absorbing the reactive power through the control of converter.
In [20], both active power control and reactive power control of the BESS are proposed to regulate the
voltage level in a low-voltage distribution grid with a high PV penetration. Besides, a comparison of two
different BESS planning options, namely, a centralized storage and distributed storage, is carried out. The
paper also indicates that, a distributed storage requires smaller power and energy to provide voltage support
and the best location of the centralized storage is the end of the feeder to minimize the power supply.
In [21], two modes of control strategy of the BESS are developed to implement the voltage support in a
distribution system, namely, Q mode by controlling the converter with reactive power only and PQ mode
by controlling both P and Q and their ratio. Results show that with only Q injection, the BESS size can be
minimized and the BESS can provide the best results for the maximal voltage support.
In [22], the BESS with the Static Synchronous Compensator (StatCom) is modeled in order to regulate
the voltage level in a transmission level system. A hardware experiment is conducted to verify the simulation,
and the results show that the StatCom system could provide a reactive power control independent of the
active power control, which is more rapid and ﬂexible.
1.2.4
Renewable Generation Smoothing
Recently, the integration of wind power and solar power has been increased signiﬁcantly in order to
reduce the global greenhouse gas emission. Compared with year 2014, the worldwide installation capacity of
solar and wind power has been increased by 28% and 17%, respectively [23]. However, the characteristics
of intermittency and uncertainty of the renewable energy lead to the ﬂuctuations in the power system. The
BESS installed beside the wind and solar sites can smooth the power output by storing the excess energy

for the usage later. Nevertheless, due to the movement of the cloud and the changes of the wind speed,
the output of the solar and wind power may encounter a sudden increase or decrease, which will cause the
problem of the system’s instability. An approach to overcome this problem is the power smoothing by the
BESS, which can contribute to the power system reliability with a rapid and quick charging and discharging

For PV power generation system, there is no inertial components leading to a ﬂuctuating output with

strategy, which takes the State Of Charge (SOC), charging and discharging rate and lifetime of the BESS
into account, is presented. In [27], the adaptive control and a novel real-time BESS-based power allocation
strategy are developed in order to smooth the power generation in a wind/PV/BESS hybrid power system.
1.2.5
Energy Arbitrage
The Nord Pool market is an electricity market wherein power contracts of a minimum of one-hour
duration are traded for delivery for the following day among Denmark, Norway, Finland and Sweden [28].
In Denmark, Energint.dk is responsible for the market development and the spot price is available one-day
ahead [29]. The spot price motivates the energy arbitrage service provided by charging the BESS when
the electricity price is low while discharging when the price is high. Another electricity market, regulation
market is used for TSO to balance the power generation and consumption when system operates in real time.
The BESS has the ability of fast charging and discharging, which can beneﬁt from the regulation market. In
[30], an optimal joint bidding strategy is developed for the BESS to join in the day-head energy, reserve, and
regulation markets, considering the battery life estimation and price uncertainty. In [31], with the purpose
of maximizing proﬁts, an optimal operation strategy in the real-time electricity market is developed from the
customer side.
1.3
BESS Technology
The BESS mainly consists of batteries, control system and converters, and a circuit protection part [3].
Control system and converters, also known as control and power conditioning system (C-PCS), is the key
issue of the BESS, which will be discussed separately below.
1.3.1
Batteries
A battery is a device that is composed of modules and cells, which is used for converting chemical energy
into electrical energy and vice versa. It can be charged when there is extra power and discharged later with
a high efﬁciency for both short-term and long-term applications. Moreover, a battery can be installed and
deployed in both distributed and centralized power systems [32]. Some battery types commonly used in
power systems are classiﬁed below.
Lead Acid Battery: It is one of the oldest and most normal batteries, which includes electrodes of
lead dioxide and lead in an acid electrolyte. The advantage lies in the low cost and proven technology of
manufacture, on the other hand, a major drawback is the shorter life time. It can be integrated in the power
system with short/medium-term application [33].

Lithium-ion Battery: This type of battery has the advantages of high efﬁciency, high energy density
and environmental friendly, thus, it has been widely applied in portable electronic devices, such as laptops

1.3.2
C-PCS
C-PCS is the key part of the BESS, which costs more than 25% of the whole BESS [3]. It is an
intermediary between the battery and the grid by maximizing the battery power output, converting DC
to AC power and adjusting the AC power to grid requirements through a set of power electronic devices
[35]. The controller inside also adjusts the charging and discharging activities to ensure a safe operation
of the BESS. With the rapid development of power electronic technologies, the cost of C-PCS has been
decreased, therefore, the application of the BESS will be of high potential in power system reliability and
quality improvement [3].
1.4
Stressed Distribution Grid
In this section, the stressed distribution grid is discussed. In Denmark, the distribution grid includes both
cables and overhead lines with a high voltage level of 6 −20kV and low voltage level of 0.4kV [36]. In
this thesis, the study focuses on the 10kV level Danish distribution grid in Copenhagen Nordhavn area.
The distribution grid normally has a radial conﬁguration while transmission grid has a meshed topology.
The distribution grid has a lower of X/R ratio typically and is assigned for transmitting and distributing the
power from substations to the end users. The characteristics of a stressed distribution grid can be described
in the following sections:
1.4.1
Peak Load
With an increasing power demand, the heavy power ﬂow may have a stress on the distribution lines. Peak
load deﬁnes as that the power demand exceeds the power limit of the distribution lines. Therefore, from
the perspective of DSO/TSO, a capital investment of the new generation units, larger capacity distribution
lines and transformers are needed in order to increase the peak load power [37]. From the perspective of
customers, the higher operation cost during peak hours increases the spot price in return. Henceforth, peak
load shaving service can beneﬁt the DSO with distribution upgrade deferral and customers with the spot
price difference between the peak and valley time [38].
In this thesis, the peak load shaving service from the BESS is mainly investigated from the perspective
of DSO, namely, shaving the peak demand in the future Nordhavn distribution grid with the BESS. The peak
load in this thesis is categorized by two different load proﬁles, namely, commercial load which is represented
by new ofﬁce buildings, and residential load which is represented by new apartments. Generally, the future
Nordhavn area will be extended by 100 hectares within the next 20 years including new commercial buildings
like ’Copenhagen Port company House’and new residential buildings like ’Marmorbyen’ [39].
The peak load demand will not only challenge the distribution limit but also cause a voltage drop.
Therefore, the BESS peak load shaving service will be evaluated from the DSO’s perspective, namely, the
control of the power ﬂow within the distribution limit and the voltage drop limit.

1.4.2
N-1 Contingency
N-1 Contingency in a distribution system can include a sudden outage of a generation unit, a distribution
line or a power transformer. From the existing Nordhavn distribution topology, the interloop design, the
outage of a certain feeder may trip the relay and connect it to the adjacent feeder. The sudden outage will
not only cause the voltage collapse of the feeder but also add a sudden stress to the adjacent one with a
rapid demand rise, and resulting in a rapid voltage decrease. In this project, the BESS service by regulating
the active and reactive power during N-1 contingency will be investigated, respectively. Additionally, the
voltage support performance with different BESS positions and line lengths will also be discussed.
1.4.3
Voltage Rise and Drop
Some major factors that could inﬂuence the voltage magnitude in the distribution grid are:
1. Length and R
X ratio of the distribution lines
2. Power consumption and power factor of the loads
3. Power generation and power factor of generations
From power ﬂow studies, the voltage level is determined by active power, reactive power, line length and
R/X of the distribution line. The voltage rise, for example, in Nordhavn distribution grid, may be caused by
the high integration of PV generation. On the other hand, a voltage drop can be induced from high power
consumption, a long distribution line or a sudden N-1 contingency. In distribution power system, due to a
lower ratio of X/R, the active power plays a more important role of voltage regulation than reactive power
in a way [40]. In this thesis, both the active power and the reactive power regulation on the voltage support
provided by the BESS in the distribution grid are investigated.
1.5
EnergyLab Nordhavn
EnergyLab Nordhavn - New Urban Energy Infrastructure is an ongoing research and demonstration
project aiming to transform the current Nordhavn area into a future smart-energy system with multiple
renewable energy integrated [41]. Copenhagen Nordhavn is one of the largest developing metropolitan in
Europe and there will be around 40,000 new residents and new jobs during the next 50 years [42]. EnergyLab
Nordhavn project is funded by the Energy Technology Development and Demonstration Programme (EUDP)
with a total budget of 143 million DKK and lasts from year of 2015 to 2019 [41].
An overview of the EnergyLab Nordhavn project is shown in Fig.1.3. It is a living laboratory which
demonstrates the integrated energy technologies of heat storage, low-temperature district heating and heat
exchange, smart energy buildings, heat pumps, electric radiators, EVs and the BESS with intelligent control
strategies [43]. Of the project, the integration of the electrical and heat power production, and ﬂexible power
consumption provides an optimal solution to the future optimal energy system. Additionally, the power
generation from wind, solar and biomass energy leads to the phase out of the conventional power plants.
The new energy infrastructure contributes to the ambitious goal of achieving the world’s ﬁrst carbon neutral
capital by 2025 [44].

1.7
Approach of Study
In order to apply the ancillary services of the BESS, the software environment Matlab/Simulink, Simscape
power systems library, is used for modeling. It is part of the Physical Modelling libraries and embedded in
the Simulink environment [45]. With the software, phasor mode is chosen to simulate the system since this
mode is fast and only the magnitudes of voltages and currents rather than the frequency are the main focus
of the thesis. Additionally, the S Function in Simulink is adopted in order to implement the control strategies
of the BESS.
Fig. 1.4 illustrates the overall approach of the thesis. As shown in the ﬁgure, the work ﬂow mainly
includes ﬁve parts, namely, input data processing, Nordhavn distribution grid modeling, BESS control
system developing, control parameters setting and ﬁnally, the outcome. The input data is ﬁrstly collected
from DONG Energy, which includes the annual load proﬁle, annual solar insolation and line parameters,
subsequently, the load type is then identiﬁed. Afterwards, the Nordhavn distribution grid is modeled
including the BESS and PV subsystems. Meanwhile, the BESS control system is proposed by getting power
ﬂow, bus voltage and the BESS SOC from the grid model, as well as control parameters like reference
voltage magnitude, peak load indicator and converter size iterations. Then the output of the control system is
to provide the grid with peak load shaving and voltage support by the active power and the reactive power

2.4
BESS Lifetime Estimation
The BESS lifetime estimation in the thesis refers to the BESS lifetime degradation during the operation.
The rainﬂow counting algorithm is introduced here for the calculation of the number of cycles for the BESS
cyclic aging.
The BESS lifetime degradation is inﬂuenced by many factors, in which, some factors are dependent on
each other. The main factors that inﬂuence the BESS lifetime degradation are [48]:
1. Temperature, it inﬂuences the rate of the chemical reaction inside the battery.
2. Cyclic aging, it refers to the capacity degradation during the BESS charging and discharging perfor-
mance.
3. Calendar aging, it indicates the lifetime degradation during the BESS idle condition.
In order to simplify the BESS lifetime degradation model, only the cyclic aging is considered in this
thesis and two methods are studied in [49]. In this thesis, the rainﬂow counting algorithm is adopted, where
the number of cycles of each Depth Of Discharge (DOD) are calculated.
Before introducing the rainﬂow algorithm, the following terms are interpreted [50].
1. Cycle, according to [50], a half cycle refers to a single charging or discharging performance, while a
full cycle means two symmetrical half cycle behaviour with both charging and discharging perfor-
mance.
2. SOC, it refers to the percentage of energy left in the BESS. The calculation of SOC will be described
in more detail in next chapter together with the modelling.
3. DOD, it represents the absolute value of the SOC difference before and after a certain cycle, which is
bounded by [0, 1].
The rainﬂow counting algorithm is an approach of calculating the cyclic aging by counting the number
of cycles for each DOD [51]. The introduced method counts the number of cycles according to the SOC
curve, by rotating it 90 degree clockwise and imaging it as a roof. With the rainﬂow counting algorithm, the
number of cycles of DOD i during operation is represented as ni. While the lifetime characteristic of the
BESS has the number of cycles Ni under each DOD i. Therefore, the total BESS lifetime degradation can
be calculated as

Modeling Power System
In this chapter, the modeling process of the relevant Nordhavn distribution grid and electrical components
will be introduced and described, wherein the Simulink 2016a software environment is adopted and the
simulation components in ’Simscape’ library are used to model the three-phase power system.
The Matlab/Simulink environment has the advantages of,
1. Visualized modelling, it enables a beginner to have an overview of the components inside the power
system.
2. User deﬁned mask, it enables users to set their parameters from user interface, which is very convenient.
3. Easy to realize some events through electrical components in the library, e.g. circuit breakers during the
N-1 contingency.
4. Quick learning from demos, there are many demos concerning the renewables, PV and the BESS.
Though, it has some disadvantages like, computation time will increase when many S functions are
integrated. It is also a little hard to call complex functions during the model operation. Nevertheless, for
modelling the power system and implementing the control strategy in this thesis, the disadvantages do not
cause big problems.
Other software tools, like Matpower, a tool for solving power ﬂow with a package of Matlab M ﬁles [52],
can also be implemented. It is more efﬁcient to call functions in order to implement complex control
strategies. Additionally, there is no need to concern the connection between functions and physical ports like
in Simulink. But in our thesis, the S function is enough to implement the control strategies and the visualized
electrical components are convenient to implement events, like N-1 contingency, and a good overview of the
positions of the components, like, PV and the BESS. Therefore, Simulink is chosen for the studies.

Discussion
With the development of both new commercial and residential buildings in Nordhavn NGT 65 area,
the BESS peak load shaving will have a good performance for the upcoming peak load and therefore a
distribution grid upgrade deferral, from the aspect of DONG Energy. As a DSO, it is of great importance to
have a secure supply to the grid. Therefore, the future maximum load demand under each converter size with
the 0.5MWh BESS is discussed in detailed. The BESS evaluation from two expects, the annual lifetime
degradation and annual energy storage are also considered in order to balance the cost for BESS operation
and the value of peak load service from DSO’s perspective.
In conclusion, with the study of the BESS peak load shaving in NGT 65, the optimal converter size is
260kW for the maximum peak load shaving capability. Under this condition, the annual maximum load
power for two types of load that can be shaved down to the power limit of Plim = 2.25MW is 2.49MW.
Subsequently, the peak load demand is 6.63 times and 3.15 times of the current load pattern of commercial
and residential load, respectively.
Additionally, the PV penetration will help the peak load shaving for the commercial load because the
peak load hour is always within the PV generation period, namely, a large correlation between the PV
generation and peak load power. Typically, with a PV capacity of 1MW, the optimal converter size is
264kW while with 300 −700kW PVs, the optimal converter size is 300kW. The maximum peak load
demand is 7.2 times of current commercial load pattern. While for residential load, since the correlation of
PV output power and load ﬂow is quite small, the PV generation will give little help to the BESS peak load
shaving. Moreover, the South oriented PV panel has a better support for the BESS peak load shaving than
the East-West oriented one for a larger correlation coefﬁcient.
Since the commercial load has fewer peaks than the residential one, with the same maximum power
ﬂow, the annual BESS lifetime degradation and energy storage for residential load is always larger, therefore
maybe a larger operation cost.

CHAPTER 5
Discussion
From the case study above, it is found that the 0.5MWh BESS which is planned to be placed in node
55259, NGT 65, can provide the peak load shaving and voltage support services. The cases selected in the
project aims to simulate the real-life peak load periods and the voltage drop, therefore the real measurement
data of the commercial load and residential load in Nordhavn area is used. By simulating the stressed grid in
case 1, the load demand is implemented by scaling up the current load pattern in Nordhavn area. Typically,
for the commercial load, the peak load period is from the hour 10 to 13, while for the residential load, the
peak load period lasts from 18 to 20. Although the peak load is over the power limit of Plim = 2.25MW,
there is no voltage problem, e.g. voltage drop more than 5%. Therefore, in case 2, according to the interloop
design in Nordhavn area, the stressed grid with severe voltage drop problem is formulated during the N-1
contingency for two different feeders.
According to the results of case 1, it is shown that with the 200kW BESS converter size, for both
commercial and residential load types, the maximum peak load in the future is the same, namely, 2.434MW.
This is due to that, for the load power during the peak load period, the shapes of the power ﬂow of the two
types of load are quite similar, namely, a sharp peak load during the peak load period. Consequently, the
maximum load that the BESS can shave is determined by its converter size. On the other hand, with the
sensitivity analysis, by changing the converter size, conclusion can be drawn that, with the optimal converter
size of 260kW, the future load demand in NGT 65 can be increased the most, where the energy of the BESS
is fully used during the peak hour period, therefore, the peak load is determined by both the converter size
and the BESS capacity in this condition. The reason for the future peak load demand that cannot be simply
calculated as the summation of the power limit and the converter size, namely, 2.45MW, is that the power
loss on the distribution line should be taken into account.
For other load types, for example, a ﬂat load power during the peak load period, the peak load may be
solely determined by the BESS capacity, namely, the maximum peak load which will be much smaller than
2.434MW with the converter size of 200kW. The reason lies in that, the maximum load power of this kind
of ’Energy peak’ that can be shaved down to the capacity limit is determined by the energy that exceeds the
power limit, therefore, compared to the converter size, the BESS capacity is a main factor to inﬂuence the
maximum load power which can be shaved.
Additionally, the PV penetration in the future Nordhavn NGT 65 is also investigated in case 1. It is
noted that, with a possible capacity up to 1MW PV integration, there is no overvoltage problem during
the summer time when the load demand is lower than winter, and due to the measurement data of the solar
insolation is hourly, therefore, no obvious ﬂuctuation of the PV output occurs here. Henceforth, the focus is
the function of the PV generation for the peak load shaving during the peak load period together with the
BESS. As illustrated in the case study, for different load types, there is a different correlation coefﬁcients

between the PV output power and the load demand during the peak load period. When the coefﬁcient is
closer to 1, there is a stronger relation between them, and therefore, the PV penetration can help the BESS
peak load shaving during the peak load period. For commercial load, since the peak load period lies within
the period of the South-oriented PV generation period with the correlation coefﬁcient of 0.84, the 1MW
PV can increase the maximum peak load power up to 2.711MW. On the contrary, the 1MW East-West

Furthermore, the BESS annual lifetime estimation and annual energy storage are also studied. With an
increasing energy storage, the BESS lifetime degradation increases for a more frequent use during the peak
load shaving service. This observation is valuable for a later study of the DSO, wherein the BESS peak load
shaving service can be paid for maintaining a reliable operation of the grid while the lifetime degradation
will be part of the operation and maintenance cost. Therefore, a cost effective operation can be later studied
based on the amount of energy storage during the BESS peak load shaving and the lifetime degradation
during the service.
In case 2, the voltage support with the BESS is analyzed. Results show that a maximum voltage rise of
2.52% can be achieved by the Q-U droop control of the BESS with a maximum reactive power of 1MVar.
From the comparison of the voltage support for the N-1 contingency at NGT 08 and NGT 65, conclusion
can also be drawn that if the end of the new feeder is closer to the BESS position, it will beneﬁt more from
the voltage support. Besides, the impact of the active power and reactive power on the voltage support is
discussed. The purpose is to investigate the possibility of voltage support service with active power regulation
and compare the results with the reactive power regulation. It is found that with a longer feeder, the active
power will have a more dominant impact on the voltage support. Therefore, for the future Nordhavn grid, if
there is a possibility to extend the distribution line for expanding the Nordhavn distribution network, the
voltage support with active power control will also be effective.

CHAPTER 6
Conclusion
With the current trend of global urbanization, the Copenhagen Nordhavn area plans to expand with
40,000 new residents and 40,000 new jobs. With the new commercial and residential loads integrated, the
current capacity of 10kV distribution line will be challenged. Therefore, an investment into the grid upgrade
will be necessary, which will be quite costly since the construction of the electric infrastructure is in urban
area. Moreover, the interloop design in Nordhavn area enables the connection of two adjacent feeders, which
may result in a large voltage drop at the end of the new feeder, leading to a tripping of the components.
Henceforth, from the perspective of DSO, this thesis investigates the ancillary services of peak load shaving
and voltage support by the 0.5MWh BESS in order to defer the investment of the grid upgrade and improve
the reliability of the system.
From the power ﬂow study of the current NGT 65 feeder, it is observed that there is no peak load or
voltage drop problem. Whereas, it is crucial to predict the maximum peak load demand that can be reached
in the future NGT 65 feeder with the BESS peak load shaving. Moreover, with the integration of PV power
in future NGT 65, it is also of importance to investigate if the PV penetration can help the BESS peak load
shaving to some extent.
With active power control by the BESS with a capacity of 0.5MWh and a converter size of 200kW
suggested by DONG Energy, the results indicate that the maximum load demand will be 6.46 times larger
than the present commercial load demand or 3.08 times larger than the current residential one, where the
maximum peak load power will reach 2.434MW. Moreover, through the sensitivity analysis, the optimal
converter size is 260kW, which can shave the most energy of the peak load with the determined BESS
capacity of 0.5MWh.
Furthermore, the results of PV generation in case 1 show that the PV penetration can contribute to the
BESS peak load shaving for the commercial load. Particularly, with an optimal converter size of 264kW
and 1MW PV, the future load demand can reach up to 7.2 times of the current value, wherein the peak load
demand is 2.711MW. In comparison with the residential load, there is slight impact of the PV penetration
on the BESS peak load shaving, subsequently, the correlation coefﬁcient between PV generation with
different orientations and load demand of two types during the peak load period is discussed. With a value
closest to 1, it is found that the South oriented PV panel can maximally contribute to the BESS peak load
shaving service for the commercial load. On the contrary, with a value closest to 0, the East-West oriented
PV panel can support the least to the BESS peak load shaving for the residential load.
The BESS annual lifetime degradation estimation and energy storage are also studied. From the
perspective of DSO, the study is helpful to determine the BESS converter size from the perspective of
economical operation in the future.
Although there is no voltage drop problem during the peak load period, the voltage support service is

conducted during the N-1 contingency. Results show that the voltage drop will be reduced by 2.35% at
maximal with the BESS voltage support with the N-1 contingency occurring in NGT 08 while 2.52% when
the N-1 contingency occurs in NGT 65. It also indicates that, with voltage support after the N-1 contingency,
the end of the new feeder can have a voltage drop around 5%, which is enough for a reliable operation of
the distribution grid. Subsequently, the end of the new feeder beneﬁts more from the voltage support if its
position is closer to the node where the BESS is integrated since the power loss on the line is less.
Then, the voltage support by active power and reactive power injection from the BESS is discussed.
Comparison indicates that in this 10kV distribution grid, with a line length more than 10km, the active
power will have a dominant impact on the voltage change than the reactive power.
Finally, it is concluded, the peak load shaving service by the 0.5MWh BESS can help the distribution
grid upgrade deferral to some extent and the voltage support service can provide the distribution grid with a
more reliable operation.
6.1
Future Work
On the basis of the analysis of the BESS impact on the stressed distribution grid, the following future
work are suggested:
• EV charging in the feeder NGT 65. The EV charging pattern is worthy to study since there will be
EV integration in EnergyLab Nordhavn project. Consequently, it is an interesting topic to study the
implementation of the BESS peak load shaving service due to the stochastic characteristic of the EV
charging.
• PV output ﬂuctuation. The uncertainty characteristic of the PV generation will also result in a stressed
distribution grid. The BESS service of the renewable generation smoothing is also worthy to study.
• Economical operation of the BESS. By providing the grid with multiple services, there will be
economic beneﬁt from it. Meanwhile, the operation and maintenance cost during the ancillary services
should also be considered. Therefore, an economical operation of the BESS is of great interest.
