Abstract  
Today,  there  are  different  options  available  for  customizing  bookcases  to 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
ﬁt  speciﬁc  shapes,  sizes  or  aesthetic  preferences.   
One  can  either  choose  to  combine  modules  of  mass  produced  bookcases, 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
to  hire  a  carpenter,  to  customize  a  bookcase  in  an  online  3D  design  tool  or 
 
   
 
 
   
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
choose  to  build  one  out  of  materials.   
Custom  bookcases  are  often  seen  designed  to  ﬁt  many  different  contexts 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
of  use  to  for  example  ﬁt  over  a  door,  around  a  window  frame,  over  a  sofa 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
   
 
 
   
 
or  to  ﬁt  a  television  within  a  bookcase.  The  research  of  this  thesis  project 
 
 
   
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
shows  that  the  online  mass  customization  solutions  available  on  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
market  do  not  satisfy  the  complex  contexts  of  use.  
Therefore  the  concept  Shelfer  is  proposed  as  a  company  with  a  new 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
   
 
approach  to  make  customized  bookcases  with  a  performance  optimized 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
online  3D  design  tool  and  with  an  automated  production  process  utilizing 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
CNC  routing.  
The  outcome  of  the  thesis  is  an  extensive  iterative  process  of  clariﬁcation, 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
ideation,  development  and  implementation  concerning  Shelfer  as  well  as 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
validations  and  invalidations  of  assumptions  of  Shelfer  as  a  company 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
during  the  process.   
I  believe  that  there  is  a  strong  case  for  Shelfer  viability  on  the  customized 
 
 
 
     
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
bookcase  market  based  on  the  results  gained  and  the  validations  of 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Shelfers  business  case,  user  segment  and  user  experience.

Chapter  1   
Introduction  
This  report  concerns  the  development,  launch  and  implementation  of  a 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
new  company  Shelfer.  Shelfer  provides  customers  with  customizable 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
furniture  in  a  new  approach  compared  to  existing  customizable  solutions.  
Shelfer  as  an  idea  was  initiated  6  months  prior  to  the  beginning  of  this 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
thesis  project.  The  story  of  how  Shelfer  was  created  began  as  many  other 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
good  ideas  with  a  feeling  of  frustration.  My  brother,  Alexander,  and  his 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
girlfriend  were  expecting  a  child,  and  they  decided  to  acquire  a  custom 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
bookcase  to  optimize  their  storage  at  home.  The  professional  carpenter 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
solutions  they  found  were  too  expensive  for  their  budget  and  Alexander 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
decided  to  build  one  of  his  own.  He  built  a  bookcase  but  ran  into  several 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
problems  in  the  building  process.   
With  a  frustrating  feeling  of  thinking  that  there  should  be  a  smarter 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
solution  available,  Alexander  pitched  the  experience  and  thought  with  me, 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
and  from  there  on  we  started  conceptualizing  Shelfer  as  a  smarter 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
approach  to  customizable  furniture.   
The  foundation  of  Shelfer  was  initiated  with  two  basic  ideas:  
●
With  my  background  in  prototyping  at  the  Technical  University  of 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Denmark,  I  suggested  that  we  produce  the  custom  bookcases  by 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
utilizing  a  CNC  router,  due  to  its  ﬂexibility.  
●
Alexander,  with  his  background  in  software  development, 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
suggested  that  we  create  a  web-based  design  tool  where  we  could 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
get  speciﬁcations  of  the  custom  bookcase  designs.  
With  that  foundation  we  had  created  the  outline  for  a  mass  customization 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
concept,  which  leads  us  to  the  initiation  point  of  this  thesis.  Before  diving 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
more  into  Shelfer,  relevant  theory  and  market  research  will  be  explored.

●
The  physical  product

with  launching  a  startup.

Mass  customization  
It  is  difﬁcult  to  fathom  how  far  retail  products  and  services  have  developed 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
in  the  last  century  and  the  amount  of  personalization  which  is  available  for 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
consumers  today.  
The  ﬁrst  mass  produced  product,  the  Ford  Model  T,  rolled  off  the  ﬁrst 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
assembly  lines  in  1908  [3].At  that  time,  the  Model  T  was  only  available  in  a 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
     
single  color  variant  -  black  -  because  it  was  cheaper  and  longer  lasting

Deﬁning  mass  customization  
Mass  customization  (MC)  is  a  means  for  an  organization  to  produce 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
custom  outputs  in  a  mass  production  environment.  The  aim  of  MC  is  to 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
obtain  a  high  production  volume  at  the  low  costs  associated  with  mass 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
production  (MP)  with  the  ﬂexibility  of  a  wide  different  options  for  the 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
customer  to  choose  from.  
MP  typically  focuses  on  economy  of  scale  with  a  “one  size  ﬁts  all” 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
approach.  MP  is  seen  as  a  production  method  with  high  ﬁxed  costs  and 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
low  variable  costs  with  expensive  manufacturing  equipment  being  able  to 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
produce  products  of  consistent  quality  in  high  volumes.  A  common 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
example  of  a  mass  production  product  is  the  LEGO  brick.  LEGO  have 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
perfected  the  production  of  the  LEGO  brick  so  bricks  produced  today  still 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
ﬁt  the  bricks  produced  back  in  1958.  LEGO  utilizes  very  precise  casting 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
moulds,  robots  to  transport  bricks  between  production  machines  and 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
automated  warehouses  to  automatically  store  the  LEGO  kits.  
 
 
Customization,  contrary  to  MP,  focuses  on  satisfying  the  individual 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
customer  need  without  prioritizing  the  lowest  possible  production  costs.  A 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
common  example  of  a  customization  product  can  be  a  tailor,  measuring 
 
 
   
 
 
 
   
 
 
and  tailoring  a  shirt  based  on  the  customer’s  needs  and  measurements.  
The  preﬁx  “mass”  in  mass  customization  implies  combining  features  from 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
both  mass  production  and  customization  in  one  method.  The  purpose  of 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
mass  customization  is  to  produce  at  a  high  volume  yet  still  offering 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
customization  of  a  product  to  satisfy  different  customer  needs.  Where 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
mass  production  follows  a  “one  size  ﬁts  all”  approach,  MC  follows  a 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
“multiple  sizes  ﬁts  more”  approach.  Such  a  strategy  is  then  “a  synthesis  of 
 
 
 
 
 
   
   
 
 
 
 
two  long  competing  systems  of  management:  the  mass  production  of 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
individually  customized  goods  and  services”  [6].  
MC  leverages  technological  development  with  knowledge  about  customer 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
purchasing  behavior  and  strategies  for  the  manufacturing  method.  [7]

Three  main  elements  of  mass  customization  
John  Zipkin  describes  mass  customization  with  three  capabilities:

obtaining  customer-speciﬁc  information),  process  ﬂexibility  (production 
 
 
 
 
 
 
technology  using  the  information)  and  logistics  (subsequent  processing 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
and  distribution)  [8]  For  a  mass  customization  system  to  work,  all  three 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
elements  must  work  together  as  a  whole.

Elicitation  
The  purpose  of  elicitation  is  to  gather  customer  needs  in  order  to 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
customize  the  product  or  service  accordingly.  Elicitation  can  vary  a  lot 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
depending  on  the  type  of  product  being  customized.  Zipkin  also  states 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
that  elicitation  is  a  challenge  because  customers  have  difﬁculties  deciding 
 
     
 
 
 
 
 
 
their  needs  and  then  communicating  or  acting  on  their  decisions. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Customers  can  easily  be  overwhelmed  with  too  many  selections.  “In  sum, 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
elicitation  is  both  essential  and  difﬁcult.  To  give  customers  exactly  what 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
they  want,  you  ﬁrst  have  to  learn  what  that  is.  It  sounds  simple,  but  it's 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
not.”  [8]  
Process  ﬂexibility  
Manufacturing  processes  need  to  be  ﬂexible  in  order  to  provide 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
customized  products  based  on  the  speciﬁed  preferences.  The  ﬂexibility 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
can  range  from  simple  one-dimensional  processes  to  advanced 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
three-dimensional  processes  such  as  CAD  CAM  processes.  
Logistics  
After  the  product  is  fabricated  according  to  the  customers  speciﬁcations, 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
additional  processing  and  transportation  tasks  are  required.  A  logistic 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
system  such  as  an  automated  customer  details  system  helps  bring  mass 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
customization  systems  to  fruition.  
Mass  customization  challenges  
In  the  book  Mass  Customization  and  Design  Democratization  [9],  Branko 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Kolrevic  and  José  Pinto  Duarte  examine  state  of  the  art  MC  initiatives.  This 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
includes  MC  initiatives  such  as  co-design  customization  tools  for  making 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
lamps  of  complex  shapes  to  co-designing  entire  houses.  Kolrevic  and 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Duarte  reﬂect  on  the  challenges  to  mass  customization  on  three  levels;  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
contextual  level,  the  system  level  and  the  application  level.  
At  the  contextual  level,  it  is  not  enough  to  generate  random  product 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
designs.  The  products  should  stem  from  careful  interpretation  of  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
design  context  including  physical,  technological,  cultural  and  social 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
contexts  as  well  as  the  individual  contexts  of  the  user.  The  aim  of  careful 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
context  interpretation  is  to  achieve  products  with  increased  functional 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
performance  and  high  customer  satisfaction.[9]  
At  the  system  level,  an  appropriate  level  of  automation  must  be  identiﬁed 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
to  materialize  the  customized  design.  The  cost  involved  with  fully 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
automated  machinery,  technological  limitations  or  ethical  issues  might  be 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
contributing  factors  to  identifying  the  appropriate  automation  level.[9]  
At  the  application  level,  there  are  challenges  optimizing  the  functional 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
performance.  Applications  can  be  very  graphically  intensive  when  using 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
3D  modeling  software  and  should  be  optimized  for  running  on  generic 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
computing  hardware.  Also  the  application  should  not  overwhelm  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
customer  with  a  burden  of  choice  when  designing  an  application  with  a 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
high  degree  of  freedom.  Finding  an  appropriate  balance  between  what  is 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
deﬁned  by  the  designer  and  what  is  left  for  the  customer  to  decide  is  key. 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
An  optimal  application  should  assess  and  compare  performance  of  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
different  solutions.  Furthermore  there  should  be  a  balance  in  the  user 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
friendliness  of  the  interface  in  order  for  the  customer  to  promptly  be  able 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
to  understand  and  make  decisions.  Another  solution  could  be  “…  to 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
conﬁgure  the  system  to  work  as  a  digital  designer,  with  the  ability  to 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
engage  in  a  dialogue  with  the  users,  helping  them  to  understand  the 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
needs,  providing  advice,  and  offering  solutions.”  [9]

Product  analysis  of  bookcases  
This  section  will  examine  different  types  of  bookcases,  different  use  cases

What  is  a  bookcase?  
Bookcases  are  one  of  the  most  common  furniture  types  all  over  the  world. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
For  example  Ikea’s  famous  Billy  bookcases  sell  over  60  million  modules 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
every  year.  IKEA’s  most  popular  product  of  all  time  is  the  Billy  bookcase. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
Bookcases  are  generally  used  to  store  or  showcase  objects.  [10]  
The  word  bookcase  might  seem  to  simply  imply  that  the  product  is  a  case 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
     
 
to  store  books,  but  bookcases  are  used  to  store  and  showcase  many 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
different  kinds  of  objects  such  as  decorative  items,  boxes,  pictures, 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
televisions,  speakers,  goods  for  sale,  ofﬁce  articles,  plants  and  the  list 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
goes  on.  
 
 
 
Bookcases  can  be  either  open  bookcases  constructed  of  only  walls  and 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
shelves  or  contain  accessories  such  as  doors,  drawers,  magazine  racks,  ﬂip 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
down  tables  and  in  some  cases  accessories  such  as  pillows  to  make  a  small 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
sitting  area  within  the  bookcase.  
The  size  of  bookcases  ranges  from  small  bookcase  modules  to  tall 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
bookcases  that  reach  from  ﬂoor  to  ceiling  and  wall  to  wall  and  can  either 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
be  free-standing  or  wall  hanged  if  so  desired.  
The  geometric  shape  of  bookcases  is  mostly  rectangular  but  in  some  cases 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
   
 
 
angled  to  ﬁt  uneven  spaces  such  as  slanted  ceilings.  Bookcases  can  be 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
built  into  a  wall  or  used  as  a  stand-alone  module.  
Contexts  of  use  
Bookcases  come  in  many  different  shapes  and  sizes  and  can  be  seen  in 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
many  different  contexts  of  use.  Overall  the  contexts  of  use  can  be  divided 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
into  two  categories;  private  contexts  or  communal  and  commercial 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
contexts.  
For  the  private  contexts  of  use,  bookcases  can  be  seen  in  living  rooms, 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
bedrooms,  kitchens,  home  ofﬁces,  wine  cellars  and  so  on.  
For  the  communal  and  commercial  contexts  of  use,  bookcases  can  be 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
seen  in  libraries,  warehouses,  ofﬁces,  stores,  restaurants,  bars,  cafés  etc.  
The  contexts  of  use  are  countless.  Seen  in  the  light  of  domestication 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
theory  this  means  a  highly  domesticated  product  in  many  different  social 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
contexts.  [11]

Judd,  a  former  architect.  His  untitled  installation  piece  can  be  seen  at  The 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Museum  of  Modern  Art  in  New  York  City.  [12]

a  red  back  panel.  Donald  Judd’s  piece  expresses  the  elegant  aesthetic 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
qualities  of  space  and  symmetrical  shapes.  Similar  to  Donald  Judd's  art 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
piece  a  bookcase  can  by  its  symmetrical  shapes  be  a  form  of  decorative

Customized  bookcases  
Customized  bookcases  made  to  ﬁt  exact  measurements  are  popular  types 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
of  bookcases.  By  ﬁtting  a  bookcase  to  measurements,  the  living  space  can 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
be  optimized,  enabling  use  of  otherwise  unused  storage  space.  The 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
bookcase  can  also  have  a  distinct  look  of  being  purposefully  built  to  the 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
space  it  occupies  in  some  cases  blending  in  with  the  surroundings.  
Acquisition  
There  are  three  main  ways  of  acquiring  a  bookcase  to  ﬁt  speciﬁc 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
measurements:  
1.
Purchasing  and  combining  mass  produced  modules  
2.
Purchasing  a  custom  bookcase  through  a  service  
3.
Purchasing  materials  and  building  a  bookcase  
Purchasing  and  combining  mass  produced  modules  
For  the  option  of  combining  mass  produced  modules  in  standard 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
measurements  the  are  modules  are  combined  to  ﬁt  an  approximate 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
measurement.  The  modules  are  then  assembled  by  the  end  user.  These 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
bookcases  are  more  efﬁcient  to  manufacture  and  require  substantially  less 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
man  hours  from  raw  material  to  ﬁnished  bookcase  than  a  customized 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
bookcase.  The  drawbacks  of  combining  mass  produced  units  are  that  the

Establishing  a  production  process  
With  a  manually  drawn  prototype  as  proof  of  concept 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
that  CNC  routing  and  the  materials  we  had  chosen

We  talked  to  Morten  from  ChipChop,  also  a  member  of  Betafactory.  He

ChipChop  have  successfully  implemented  a  grasshopper  program  for

Double  sided  routing  issue  
With  this  understanding  of  how  the  production 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
system  should  work,  we  were  ready  to  develop  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
system  further.  In  order  to  make  bookcases  with  a 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
minimalistic  aesthetic  design,  we  decided  that  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
surfaces  should  be  without  pin  holes  for  the  shelves. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
To  obtain  such  a  design,  it  is  necessary  to  route  on 
 
 
   
   
 
 
 
 
 
both  sides  of  the  components  to  avoid  having  holes  protruding  on  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
sides.  In  order  to  route  on  both  sides  of  the  components  it  was  necessary 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
to  ﬁx  the  components  and  know  exactly  where  the  already  machined  part 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
is  positioned.  
We  researched  that  this  could  be  a  feature  on  CNC  routers  and  found  that 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
physical  stops  where  the  plywood  sheets  could  be  placed,  and  when 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
ﬂipped  and  put  up  against  the  stops,  one  would  know  exactly  where  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
machined  parts  were  on  the  opposite  side.  The  issue  here  is  that  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Excitech  E2  CNC  router  at  Betafactory  does  not  have  a  stop  feature,  so  we 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
were  at  a  loss.  To  solve  this  issue  two  solutions  were  proposed  
Routing  into  spoilboard  using  dowels  to  ﬁx  the  plywood  sheet  
This  solution  is  the  least  intrusive  and  least  time  consuming  method.  This 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
solution  requires  routing  into  the  MDF  spoilboard  underneath  the  piece  of 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
plywood  in  order  to  get  ﬁxation  points.  
 
 
 
 
Wooden  dowel  stop  system  
Step 1.  Two  holes  are  routed  through  the  plywood  sheet  and  into  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
spoilboard  underneath  
Step 2.  The  components  holes  on  one  side  is  routed  on  one  side  of 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
the  plywood  sheet  
Step 3.  Dowels  are  placed  into  the  holes  on  the  plywood  sheet  
Step 4.  The  plywood  sheet  is  ﬂipped  and  align  the  dowels  
Step 5.  The  other  side  of  the  components  are  routed  
Dovetail  track  sliding  stops  
This  is  the  more  intrusive  method  of  adding  a  metal  slide  to  ﬁt  into  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
dovetail  tracks  on  the  side  of  the  E2.   
Step 1.  Fixated  stops  are  designed  to  be  physically  mounted  to  the  E2  
Step 2.  The  edges  of  the  plywood  sheet  is  routed  in  order  to  have  a 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
   
perfectly  rectangular  plywood  sheet  
Step 3.  The  corners  of  the  plywood  sheet  is  placed  up  to  the  stops  
Step 4.  The  stops  are  taken  off  to  prevent  a  collision  with  the  cnc 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
router    
Step 5.  The  components  holes  on  one  side  is  routed  on  one  side  of 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
the  plywood  sheet  
Step 6.  The  plywood  sheet  is  ﬂipped  and  placed  up  to  the  stops  
Step 7.  The  other  side  of  the  components  are  routed  
Since  we  could  not  ﬁnd  ﬁtting  ﬁxtures  for  the  dovetail  track  online  and  we 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
wanted  a  solution  to  be  implemented  fast,  the  wooden  dowel  stop  system 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
was  chosen.

New  CNC  router  at  Betafactory  
Betafactory  had  installed  a  new  CNC  router  Excitech  E3 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
with  more  capabilities  than  the  E2  version.   
 
New  capabilities  
We  were  very  excited  to  use  the  E3,  as  it  would  automate  and  improve  a 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
   
lot  of  the  processes  that  we  had  issues  with  and  increase  the  overall 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
effectiveness  of  our  production.  
Built-in  stops  
The  E3  has  a  built-in  pneumatic  stop  system  where  pistons  can  ﬁxate  the 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
sheets  in  a  certain  position.   
Stronger  vakuum  
The  spillboard  was  of  a  higher  quality  MDF  with  a  system  where  the  users 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
can  route  far  down  into  the  spillboard.  This  meant  that  the  vacuum  was 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
more  evenly  distributed.  
Automated  cleaning  
The  E3  has  a  cleaning  arm  that  sucks  up  dust  over  the  entire  board  after 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
routing.  This  was  done  manually  before.   
Automated  tool  change  
Instead  of  manual  tool  mounting,  the  E3  CNC  had  a  pneumatic  system 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
that  can  change  automatically  between  10  different  tools.  
Automated  zeroing  of  tools  
The  E3  CNC  can  automatically  zero  out  the  tools,  meaning  that  it  can  zero 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
the  distance  from  the  tip  of  the  tool  to  the  work  surface.  This  was  a  manual 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
step  on  the  smaller  CNC.  
Higher  speed  
The  larger  E3  allows  for  at  faster  speed  when  routing  and  in  between  cuts.  
We  calibrated  the  origin  (coordinate  system)  to  be  within  0.05  mm 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
tolerance  and  made  a  test  for  a  larger  bookcase  with  doors.

Materials  
Unfortunately  Krydsﬁnér-Handelen  did  not  end  up 
 
 
 
 
 
 
offering  melamine  plywood  as  a  standard  material  due  to 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
inconsistency  in  quality.  As  an  alternative  we  decided  to 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
test  the  next  batch  with  multiwall  sheet  material.  Multiwall 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
is  a  PVC  coated  birch  plywood  that  is  a  very  resistant  material.   
Test  with  multiwall  
As  an  alternative  to  melamine  coated  birch  veneer  a  batch  of  white 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
Multiwall  sheets  was  ordered  to  be  tested.  Unfortunately  the  multiwall 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
sheets  warped  a  lot,  causing  large  gaps  up  to  7  millimeters  to  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
spillboard  on  the  concave  side  which  the  vacuum  was  not  strong  enough 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
to  keep  ﬂat.  It  was  ﬁne  on  the  convex  side,  as  the  vacuum  was  able  to 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
ﬂatten  the  sheet.  The  sheet  was  cut  in  half  to  correct  the  bending  but  this 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
unfortunately  was  also  unsuccessful.  The  remaining  sheets  were  returned 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
to  Krydsﬁnér-Handelen.  At  this  point  we  were  at  a  loss.  We  were  not 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
satisﬁed  with  any  precoated  materials  available  since  they  were  either  too 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
expensive  or  too  unreliable.  Ultimately  the  decision  became  to  sand  and 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
paint  veneer  sheets  ourselves,  even  if  it  was  a  time  consuming  process, 
 
 
 
 
     
   
 
 
 
since  the  alternative  of  using  laminated  coated  birch  veneer  was  too 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
expensive.  
Test  with  painted  veneer  
TThe  veneer  sheets  to  be  used  for  painting  were  ordered  in  bulk  of  52 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
pieces  of  sheet  with  a  lower  quality  surface  that  can  have  plugs  and 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
scratches.  To  get  assistance  for  the  painting  process  we  went  to  Duryp 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Professional 
 in 
 Sydhavn. 
 The 
 employee 
 at 
 Dyrup 
 in 
 Sydhavn 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
recommended  that  we  used  a  fast  drying  primer  and  a  paint  with  a  strong 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
   
 
   
 
slightly  elastic  opaque  surface.  The  painting  process  consisted  of  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
following  steps:  
1.
Sand  the  veneer  sheet  on  both  sides  
2.
Stack  the  sheets  in  the  painting  area  
3.
Paint  a  side  with  primer  
4.
Flip  the  sheets  
5.
Paint  the  other  sider  with  primer  
6.
Flip  the  sheets  
7.
Sand  the  sheets  
8.
Paint  with  white  paint  
9.
Flip  the  sheets  
10. Sand  the  sheets  
11. Paint  the  other  side  with  white  paint  
A  test  was  made  by  painting  7  sheets  to  be  used  for  the  next  orders.  The 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
surface  of  the  painted  veneer  sheets  looked  very  good,  with  minor 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
inconsistencies,  such  as  plugs  showing  through  the  paint  ,  dust  particles 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
visible  in  the  paint  and  cavities  in  the  wood  grain.  Though  this  was  a  time 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
consuming  process  and  there  were  small  imperfections  in  the  surface,  this 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
was  a  reliable  and  inexpensive  material  choice  for  now.

Over  the  course  of  this  thesis  project  20  bookcases  were  made.  Of  those 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
11  bookcases  were  sold  to  paying  customers  and  7  were  prototypes.   
The  total  revenue  of  Shelfer  over  the  period  of  this  thesis  is  185.000  DKK 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
after  producing  4  upcoming  orders.  Our  total  expenses  have  been 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
235.000  DKK,  but  with  a  substantial  material  stock  of  65.000  DKK.  and 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
costs  associated  with  the  development  of  Shelfer  and  tools.  
The  social  media  campaign  has  reached  93.056  persons  and  appeared  on 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
screens  255.000  times.  
We  attended  two  exhibitions  and  handed  out  500  business  cards  and  800 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
ﬂyers.  
Shelfer  has  478  followers  on  Facebook,  308  followers  on  Instagram  and 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
245  subscribers  to  our  newsletter.  
Next  steps  
Though  we  have  not  been  able  to  pay  ourselves  yet,  we  plan  on  it  within 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
the  next  months  if  the  tracking  continues  at  the  same  pace.  We  plan  on 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
working  full  time  on  Shelfer  as  soon  as  possible.  
 
 
 
Increasing  Automation  
We  want  to  develop  and  test  a  scalable  bookcase  product  that  can  be 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
assembled  by  our  customers  themselves,  relieving  us  for  a  lot  of  time 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
spent  on  assembly.  This  increases  the  automation  and  ultimately  adds  to 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
the  mass  customization  elements,  and  could  even  lower  the  price  of  the 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
bookcases  even  more.  This  would  need  to  be  tested  because 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
measurement  has  been  a  part  of  our  customer  relationship  and  goes 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
against  the  comments  concerning  assembly  as  an  offering  we  encountered 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
at  the  exhibitions.   
Painting  is  the  process  that  demands  most  manual  work  at  the  moment. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
After  researching  laminated  veneer  sheets  further,  Riga,  a  sweedish 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
provider  of  veneer,  was  found  as  a  provider  of  mass  produced  Formica 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
laminated  birch  veneer.  The  prices  were  comparable  to  what  we  pay  for 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
painted  birch  veneer,  and  we  have  already  ordered  a  batch.   
New  features  
Looking  at  the  overview  of  the  unaddressed  needs  at  the  exhibition  as 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
shown  in  the  afﬁnity  diagram.  We  have  gotten  requests  since  the  launch 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
where  most  of  the  requests  have  been  to  implement  various  features.  The 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
next  features  we  will  be  to  implement  are  drawers  and  to  design  for 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
slanted  ceilings,  since  that 
 is  the  most  requested  and  realistic  to 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
implement.  
Website  2.0  
After  collaborating  with  a  digital  bureau  a  new  revamp  of  our  homepage  is 
 
 
   
 
   
 
 
 
 
   
almost  ﬁnished.  This  website  will  include  a  feature  to  see  the  other 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
customers  bookcases  and  the  pricing  thereof.  We  are  also  working  on 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
setting  up  tracking  to  track  the  users  interaction  on  the  site  in  order  to  see 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
where  users  get  stuck,  what  works  and  what  does  not  work.

Conclusion  
This  thesis  report  presents  the  process  and  the  results  of  developing  and 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
launching  Shelfer  as  a  company  with  a  new  and  improved  approach  to  the 
 
 
   
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
customized  bookcase  market.  
A  gap  was  identiﬁed  in  the  customized  bookcase  market  and  a  problem 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
statement  was  created  by  coupling  a  product  analysis  and  a  market 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
analysis  with  concepts  of  mass  customization.  Shelfer  proved  to  be  a 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
viable  solution  to  the  problem  statement.  
Shelfer  was  ideated,  developed,  launched  and  operated  as  a  digital  mass 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
customization  bookcase  solution  and  proved  successfully  by  having  a 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
competitive  edge  over  the  competition  through  a  performance  optimized 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
3D  design  tool  with  a  high  degree  of  design  freedom  and  a  cost  effective 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
highly  automated  CNC  based  production.  
This  competitive  edge  was  validated  by  positive  feedback  at  exhibitions 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
and  from  buying  customers,  indicating  that  Shelfer  increases  functional 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
performance  and  customer  satisfaction  compared  to  the  current  solutions 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
on  the  customized  bookcase  market.  
By  using  Personas  and  Customer  Journey  Mapping  as  user-centered 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
design  methods,  the  assumptions  of  a  potential  customer  segment  was 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
placed  in  a  framework.  This  framework  served  as  a  foundation  for  later 
 
   
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
validation  or  pivots  based  on  these  assumptions.  
A  strategy  canvas,  a  business  model  and  a  minimum  viable  product 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
exercise  was  conducted  to  convey  how  Shelfer  strategically  performed 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
compared  to  the  competition,  how  Shelfer  would  function  as  a  business 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
and  what  needed  to  be  implemented  to  reach  that  point.  
Through  many  extensive  iterative  problem-solving  phases  Shelfer  was 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
clariﬁed,  ideated,  developed  and  implemented  over  design  iterations 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
improving  upon  the  concept  with  each  iteration.  
By  attending  exhibitions,  executing  social  media  advertising  campaigns, 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
conducting  user  experience  tests  and  by  rigorous  prototyping  to  establish 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
the  right  production  method,  materials  and  right  components,  Shelfer  was 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
launched  and  received  by  customers  as  a  viable  business.   
Reﬂection  
The  experience  of  launching  and  operating  Shelfer  has  been  an  exciting 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
journey.  Shelfer  is  still  in  its  infancy  and  I  look  forward  to  seeing  how  far  it 
 
   
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
   
can  evolve  if  the  momentum  during  this  thesis  is  sustained.  
This  has  not  been  a  regular  project  for  me  as  a  Design  and  Innovation 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
student  at  DTU.  A  regular  project  would  have  been  more  detailed  and 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
thorough  where  this  project  focused  on  prioritizing  results,  feedback  and 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
improvement  over  a  thorough  design  process.  
A  more  thorough  design  process  could  have  been  achieved  by  narrowing 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
the  scope  of  a  project  to  a  more  manageable  level.  The  scope  of  this 
 
 
   
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
thesis  has  been  very  broad.  A  broad  scope  can  have  both  positive  and 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
negative  implications  for  a  design  process.  
In  terms  of  CPS  as  a  design  process,  a  broad  scope  meant  that  we  could 
 
 
 
 
   
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
focus  on  designing  many  aspects  simultaneously  without  dwelling  on 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
detailing  each  aspect.

At  the  other  hand,  making  quick  decisions,  evaluating  and  learning  what 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
to  improve  along  the  way,  might  lead  the  creator  to  miss  the  broader 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
picture  of  a  narrower  scope.  With  a  narrow  scope,  a  designer  can  broaden 
 
   
 
 
   
 
   
 
 
 
out  the  idea  and  solution  space  and  possibly  ﬁnd  a  not  otherwise  intuitive 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
solution  that  potentially  is  more  impactful  than  the  more  obvious.  
As  an  example,  early  considerations  about  the  path  to  establish  a  means 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
of  production  could  have  been  of  beneﬁt  to  Shelfer.  Before  identifying 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Betafactory  as  a  potential  means  of  production,  we  had  spent  several 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
months  identifying  and  contacting  various  production  partners  to  source 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
the  production  -  without  any  success.  
We  could  have  used  the  Function  Means  Tree  model  to  identify  several 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
different  means  before  spending  time  ﬁnding  a  better  solution  to  a 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
non-impactful  mean.  Similarly,  we  could  potentially  have  found  another 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
more  effective  and  impactful  launch  of  Shelfer  if  we  had  broadened  out 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
the  idea  and  solution  space  with  more  design  models.  
 
All  in  all,  a  full-blown  design  process  could  very  well  have  extended  the 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
ﬁrst  stages  in  the  startup  process  for  Shelfer.  The  choice  of  method  and 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
process  depends  on  the  objectives  and  resources  of  the  project.  Shelfer's 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
goal  was  a  relatively  quick  start  with  a  modest  investment  and  a  relatively 
 
   
 
 
 
   
 
 
   
 
quick  payback.  This  is  the  reality  for  many  personal  startups,  where  capital 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
is  modest  and  personal  earnings  are  needed  relatively  early  in  the  process 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
due  to  the  private  ﬁnances  of  the  participants.  On  the  other  hand,  a  larger 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
company  with  a  solid  development  budget  and  a  goal  of  conquering  a 
 
   
 
 
 
   
 
 
   
strategic  area  on  a  slightly  longer  term,  could  afford  a  more  thorough 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
analytical  approach  in  the  initial  stages.  
  
An  examination  of  the  design  and  development  process  and  its 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
methodological  choices  in  relation  to  the  company's  resources  and 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
short-term  and  long-term  goals  could  be  of  great  importance  -  not  least 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
for  small  start-ups.  
  
The  design  and  analysis  methods  used  have  obviously  been  of  great 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
beneﬁt  to  Shelfer,  and  without  these  methods  it  is  far  from  certain  that 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
Shelfer  had  become  a  successful  project  which  is  likely  to  grow 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
signiﬁcantly  with  a  potential  for  giving  the  two  founders  a  reasonable 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
income  along  the  way.  
Therefore,  positioning  the  methods  and  tools  and  designing  a  smaller  set 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
of  template  processes  for  smaller  startups  can  be  of  great  importance  to  a 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
large  number  of  startup  companies  and  thus  have  a  signiﬁcant  societal 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
impact.  
  
Looking  back,  we  have  seen  very  good  results.  The  chosen  approach 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
actually  worked,  and  I  am  elated  with  the  results.
