Chapter 1
Abstract
In Enhanced Geothermal Systems (EGS), hydraulic fracturing is used extensively
in low permeable reservoirs. Although, the energy exploitation is enhanced with
fracturing, the reservoir suﬀers from short-circuiting between the injector and the
producer wells after some years of production.
This study investigates the preferential ﬂow path of water in a multiple fractured
model with parallel fractures. The innovative steps of this study are that it implies
the ﬂow rate at the top of the injection well and accounts for the viscosity varia-
tion due to thermal changes. The model simulates a realistic reservoir considering
the thermal stresses through the injection well. The preference of the ﬂow into the
top fracture, creates short - circuiting. The study optimizes the energy production,
comprehends the limitations of the system, and avoids short - circuiting.
A coupled THM (thermo - hydro - mechanical) system is utilized, which was vali-
dated successfully against numerical and analytical methods (12),(23) . The ﬁnite
method uses linear triangle and tetrahedral elements for computational eﬃciency,
whereas previous studies used quadratic elements (12). The model was reproduced
for the equal distributed ﬂow applied on two fractures (12) and compared with the
novel study. The viscosity variation due to thermal changes inside the injection well
aﬀects the energy production and gives a better approximation than the previous
studies, where viscosity was kept constant.
The study design was set up with moderate iterations. The mechanical interactions
of the multiple fractured system is demonstrated with the variant fracture spacing,
porosity, and stiﬀness of the rock. The geothermal gradient and ﬂow rate eﬀect were
investigated intensively. In addition, the seasonal temperature changes, which vary
up to 30 oC per semester, formulated the following studies, the seasonal temperature

injection and seasonal ﬂow rate study. Lastly, the horizontal well conﬁguration was

The preferential ﬂow path on the top fracture between the injection and production

2.3
Study Purpose
The innovation step in this research is the creation of preferential ﬂow path of the
ﬂuid on one of the multiple parallel fractures. The preferential ﬂow path aﬀects the
size of the aperture. The matrix contracts when cold ﬂuid is passing through the
fracture, as a result to increase the initial aperture and loose energy.
In previous studies the ﬂow was applied at the level of the fracture, indicating that
the ﬂow rate was equally distributed between the fractures, (12). In the current
study was implemented a well - reservoir THM coupled model which applies the
total ﬂow rate at the top of the injection well and monitors a preferential ﬂow path
on the top fracture.
From the cooling of the matrix, the contraction is bigger and so the aperture on the
top fracture has a bigger size rather than the top fracture aperture.
In order to ﬁnd how to prolong the lifetime of the geothermal system and study the
preferential ﬂow path, several model variations were utilized.
A base model scenario with the injection rate at the top of the well was simulated
at ﬁrst and the initial parameters used in (12). And later scenarios with diﬀerent
spacing between the two horizontal fractures, rock properties (Young modulus and
porosity) and geothermal gradient were simulated and compared to the base case
model.
A series of diﬀerent injectivity rates were tested against each other and with the
base case model as well. The main goal was to enhance the production temperature
and consequently the lifetime of the reservoir.

Methodology and Validation
A fully coupled three-dimensional thermo - hydro - mechanical model is utilised to
investigate the interaction between fractures. Fractures are modelled as surface dis-
continuities within a three-dimensional matrix. A contact model is used to resolve
the traction between the opposing fracture surfaces. The matrix is assumed imper-
meable and thus there is no matrix ﬂow, which means that there is no mass transfer.
The coupled equations of poroelasticity and thermoelasticity, (36) are solved numer-
ically using using Galerkin ﬁnite element method. The complex system platform
utilised for this research is named as CSMP ++, (43).
The ensuing set of linear algebraic equations SX = F is solved at each timestep using
the algebraic multigrid method systems, SAMG (46). The iteration coupling adds
to the computational eﬃciency for THM Monte Carlo analysis.
3.1
Coupled THM Model
In this study a fully coupled THM model is used that was developed based on the
general balance equations for ﬂuid mass, momentum, and thermal energy as well
as constitutive equations for variable ﬂuid properties, thermoporoelastic deforma-
tion, and a phenomenological porosity–permeability relationship for crystalline rock,
taking into account hydraulic stimulation eﬀects. The thermo- hydro- mechanical,
ﬁnite model has extensively been presented (12), (14), (15), (19). On the basis of
the THM model, we present an analysis of thermal, hydraulic, and mechanical pa-
rameters on long-term geothermal reservoir evolution. A brief presentation with the
sub-models and the governing equations is following.
For an impermeable matrix rock, the model is subdivided in four sub-models:

the minus sign in cubic law (Eq. 10, Vik et al. (12)).

Chapter 6
Conclusions
In the conclusion section, the reader is able to ﬁnd a logical organization of the
study conducted and the ﬁnal and most general results.
The current model was validated against previous studies (23), (12). The mesh val-
idation provided an accurate computational method to work with. The innovative
model is cheaper and faster compared to Vik, (12), because of the linear element
instead of the quadratic mesh discretization.
The novel study considered two improvements, the ﬂuid viscosity increase and the
injection on the top of the well.
The preferential ﬂow path was intended to be researched explicitly in order to avoid
short - circuiting and enhancing the heat production. The results showed that in a
parallel two fractured system, there is preferential ﬂow on the top fracture.
The top fracture accommodates more than 60m3/hr water out of a total injection of
90m3/hr. More precisely, 67% of the total ﬂow rate goes on the upper fracture and
the rest, 33% on the bottom fracture.
The viscosity that was encountered constant and equal to 1.42x10−4 Pas in the pre-
vious studies referred above. In 30 years of production, viscosity had increased 57%
on the top of production well.
Viscosity is a non linear property and the amount of its increase or decrease de-
pends on the temperature window, which allows speculation. In the present study,
the viscosity increased up to 2.2x10−4 Pas and that caused a temperature drop of
60oC on the top of the production well.

Conclusions
Master Thesis
The coeﬃcient of performance for the geothermal doublet is satisfyingly ranging
from 30 to 20 for a 30 year study of heat production.
The fracture spacing moderation, due to the ﬂow preference was capable to simu-
late the reservoir by altering the injectivity rates. For bigger distances between the
fractures, the mass ﬂow rate was not enough to travel through the bottom fracture
and reach the production well. Therefore, the ﬂow rate was risen from 0.025m3/s to
0.035m3/s.
For shorter fracture spacing, the preferential ﬂow path on the top fracture aggra-
vates, while for fracture spacing more than 500m, even though there is vigorous ﬂow
path on the upper fracture, the aperture growth is smoothed around the injection
well.
The rock properties study of how stiﬀand porous a reservoir is, agrees well with
Vik, (12), for the bottom fracture. Nevertheless, the new study provides updated
data about the top fracture evolution. The most stiﬀand impermeable a rock is,
the severest the preferential ﬂow path gets on the upper fracture.
The geothermal gradient utilized for the comparison of high - temperature geother-
mal production, 40oC/km, and a moderate geothermal gradient, 30oC/km, did not
show major diﬀerences in the production temperature.
Further research could make use of higher geothermal gradients with precaution for
the fracture not to enter the tensile mode regime, where the model is no more valid.
Due to the preferential ﬂow path, the fracture is more likely to exert the compression
mode and enter the tensile regime. For the moderate geothermal gradient in various
fracture spacing, a higher rate (0.035m3/s) was employed.
The ﬂow rate study proved that, the most easy and eﬃcient way to enhance the
heat production in no time, is to modify the injection rates. The lower the ﬂow
mass, the less chances to create shortcuts from injection to production well and the
higher the production temperature.
