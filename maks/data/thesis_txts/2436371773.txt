Executive Summary
The present paper reports the environmental and economical assessment between two speciﬁc
Bottom Ashes (BA) treatment employed at RMB S.p.A. (RMB S.p.A. (2018)). This Italian com-
pany is a European leader in the industry of metals recovery and special waste disposal, having
a particular focus on sustainability and environmental aspects in relation to waste handling.
Firstly, a comparative Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) was performed with the aim to identify the
environmentally friendlier treatment among available alternatives and, if no substantial diﬀerence
is found, propose potential changes to the speciﬁc processes. This is done by identifying the
hot-spots, else referred as the highest contributors to the environmental impacts, and presenting
more sustainable alternatives in terms of environmental impacts.
Secondly, an economical analysis is made in order to evaluate the feasibility of the new production
process within the boundaries of the company. This is implemented by the comparison of the
costs and the revenues related to mentioned processes in order to present which system process
is the more proﬁtable for the company.
The results of this comparative study will be used as a decision making incentive regarding the
company’s business and hence will not be generally disclosed to the general public.
The LCA was performed using EASETECH, a waste-LCA software developed at the Tecnhical
University of Denmark (DTU), which allows calculating the environmental impact assessment of
waste management systems and technologies (DTU (2018)). The employed impact assessment
method ILCD recommended - 2013 Prosuit Global NR DTU resource w/o LT, for characterised
and normalised results. The Functional Unit (FU) expressing the object of assessment is “Ex-
traction and further exploitation of inert material obtained from the treatment of 1000 tonne of
BA” (DTU (2017a)). An inventory analysis is performed identifying ﬁrst and foremost all the
speciﬁc processes necessary to the required BA treatments; secondly, transportation and energy
consumption are added to the inventory; lastly, the extra consumables such as the additives are
included in the model.
After carrying out the impact assessment and interpretation of results, the following conclusions
are drawn:
• When comparing the diﬀerent processes, a better performance is seen in the mixed cement
material production in all the impact categories. A single score obtained by normalisation
also proves a preference towards the cement process in terms of environmental impact.
• On the other hand, the foam glass system is the preferable one on an economical basis.
The foam glass production is a costly process, but it is also more proﬁtable. This is partly
due to the fact that the cement material is treated as waste, thereby having disposal costs
and not providing proﬁts.

Technical Summary
The executive summary aimed at summarizing the report in such a way that readers can rapidly
become acquainted with the content without having to read the entire document. It contains
a brief statement of the proposal, some background information, a concise analysis and main
conclusions lacking in the speciﬁc description on how the analysis is performed. The technical
summary instead provides an overview on the methodologies employed during the assessment,
presenting ﬁnally the main recommendation of the study, in order to aid the readers in the ﬁnal
decision making.
BA treatment and recovery is part of RMB S.p.A. business. They are currently handled to
produce mixed cement material, which is a conﬁrmed product within the company portfolio,
with its own production plant and its share of the market. The foam glass production instead is
a potentially new product, whose business is being explored by RMB S.p.A.
The objective of this study was to assess via LCA the environmental impact of the two diﬀerent
BA treatment processes employed at the company. This ISO certiﬁed method consists of four
stages of assessment.
The ﬁrst phase is the goal and scope deﬁnition in which the frame to follow in the whole LCA
is set. In the goal deﬁnition, the purpose of the study is deﬁned together with limitations and
general working parameters. As a matter of fact, this assessment only compares two scenarios
and it thereby cannot provide a full assessment on the sustainability of the treatments system
processes. In addition, some aspects such as the social consequences are neglected while the
data representativeness in temporal, geographical and technological scale are set to the company
constraints.
From the scope deﬁnition, the deﬁned FU states that the object of the assessment is the ”Extrac-
tion and further exploitation of inert material obtained from the treatment of 1000 tonne of BA”.
The overall ﬂow diagram of the studied system depicted in ﬁgure 4 graphically shows the system
boundaries. Representative data is most crucial in a geographic sense and less in the other two.
The Life Cycle Inventory (LCI) is the phase in LCA for the data collection to build the model
with all processes. Two iterations are applied to increase data detail. The LCI is carried out using
EASETECH, a waste-LCA software developed at DTU, which allows calculating the environmental
impact assessment of waste management systems and technologies (DTU (2018)). The data
for all processes during the supply chain are collected, including BA chemical composition,
speciﬁc treatments, transport, and energy consumption. The results of the LCI model are then
implemented in the next phase.
The Life Cycle Impact Assessment (LCIA) is a vital phase of the LCA, during which the ele-
mentary ﬂows are translated into potential environmental impacts. This analysis is performed
employing the impact assessment method ILCD recommended - 2013 Prosuit Global NR DTU
resource w/o LT (DTU (2017a)).
Interpretation is the last phase of the study which explains the reasons for the outcomes of
the assessment by describing the comparison of the diﬀerent BA treatments.
The cement
process is generally the system with the best performance. However, the foam glass process

has a signiﬁcant crediting value given from the avoided production of the glass raw material.
Therefore, since the data employed for the process simulation are assumed on a scientiﬁc litera-
ture research and could not be optimal, it is worth to better analyze the discussed product system.
In addition to the LCA study, an economical analysis regarding the new business is made. Firstly,
a description of the methodology employed is made. Next, an introduction about the ﬁnal
product is presented. It consists in a brief investigation on the foam glass raw material market
in Europe, in a description about foam glass features together with its competitive advantages
and in a presentation on the technological evolution of the product under discussion. The single
processes are then evaluated singularly with a costs and revenues analysis. The representativeness
of the employed data is fundamental which is the reason why they are supported by scientiﬁc and
web researches. The cement system has low manufacturing cost, but it lacks of revenues, since it
is considered as waste material from the Italian regulation and it therefore implies disposal cost.
Instead, the foam glass production is a high cost process which can however bring considerable
revenues.
In conclusion, the two systems are compared in order to show which is the most proﬁtable
process. This is a crucial step of the analysis because, although the foam glass environmental
assessment is in conﬂict with the economical one, its proﬁtability is an incentive for this business
implementation which has to be developed with an environmental improvement perspective.
The data used in the assessment are mainly from RMB S.p.A. (RMB S.p.A. (2018)), with some
additions from the scientiﬁc literature and other internet sources. Due to the lack of some data,
assumptions and estimations were also required.
Overall, the results of the treatment comparison show the mixed cement material process to be
the preferable one in terms of environmental impact.
As general LCA recommendations valid for both the treatments, the employment of new sources of
alternative energy is fundamental for the environmental improvements of the processes. Moreover,
speciﬁc recommendations for the foam glass system consists in employing recycled material as
additives for the foam glass mixture and trying an industrial production test in order to obtain
better consumption data. Similar recommendation arose also for the business analysis. As a
matter of fact, an industrial test could also improve the eﬃciency of the system due to the
economy of scale reducing the unit costs related to the manufacturing process. Moreover, the
employment of recycled materials could contribute more to save some expenses related to the
additive purchase.
As the recommendations show, the results of this comparative study can be used as a decision
making incentive.

Introduction
Incineration is a widespread waste treatment technology (Allegrini (2014)). BA are the most
abundant solid residue generated by waste thermal treatment, accounting for 10–30% of the
input waste mass, depending on the speciﬁc type of technology and operating conditions applied
(Di Gianﬁlippo (2015)). However, increasing discussions about possible losses of resources through
the incineration process followed by new waste policies are discrediting waste incineration with
respect to source segregation and recycling. Nevertheless, recovery of resources - especially metals
in the scrap form - can be performed after the incineration process by treating the output streams
of the process (Allegrini (2014)). The performances related to the recovery of these metals are
crucial in order to determine their environmental impact. Hence, there’s increasing interest for
tools which could provide a classiﬁcation of the sustainability of industrial processes, products
and services.
One of these tools, which looks at the object of interest from the early life cycle stage of raw
material extraction and follows through until the end-of-life, is the LCA methodology. For
all of the life cycle stages, processes are deﬁned with inputs/outputs and related emissions,
thus enabling the establishment of mass balances of potentially harmful substances within the
system and so called elementary ﬂows leaving the system. Then, by employing a predeﬁned
method that uses speciﬁc relation factors, the emissions can be related to environmental impact
categories, whereupon an assessment of the system’s environmental performance becomes possible.
The aim of this study is to perform a comparative assessment of two diﬀerent options for exploiting
the inert material coming from the treatment of BA at RMB S.p.A.(RMB S.p.A. (2018)), which
is an Italian company that has been for 35 years a European leading in the industry of metals
recovery and disposal of special waste, always keeping a particular focus on the environmental
sustainability.

The actual business of the company, regarding the mentioned inert material, consists in utilizing
it in civil engineering works. However, the company decided to approach the exploitation of this
material in the production of foam glass. The assessment will be achieved by applying the LCA
methodology as proposed by ISO 14040 (2006) and ISO 14044 (2018) and utilizing EASETECH
(DTU (2017a)), the waste-LCA model developed by the DTU. EASETECH is a mass balanced
software which uses transfer coeﬃcients (TCs) to estimate transfer of materials and emissions in
the system being analysed.
In addition to the environmental assessment, an economical analysis is implemented. It consists
in a market research and business analysis about the foam glass process, with a ﬁnal comparison
of the two mentioned system processes employed at the company. The analysis is made on a
hybrid methodology between a Plan-Driven approach and a Change-Driven approach, which
relate a rigorous application of rules, principles and processes together with an implementation

2.2.3
LCI Modelling Framework
This section delivers explanations of where multifunctionalities arise in the life cycle and how
they are treated in the system.
Secondary Functions and Multifunctional Processes
During the treatment studied, some secondary functions arise making the product system a
multifunctional process. The object of the study is the exploitation of the inert material but
together with it there’s the production of recovered metal which actually is the core business of
the company. More speciﬁcally, the secondary functions derived from the product system are the
production of Ferrous recovered metal, Non Ferrous recovered metal and other Inert Materials
destined to further treatment processes.
The ISO 14044 Hierarchy to Solving Multifunctionality
The hierarchy of solution introduced in ISO 14044 (2018) is applied in order to solve the
multifunctionality issue.
Within the product system, neither subdivision of the multifunctional processes nor a system
expansion is possible, as the coproducts cannot be obtained in another way. Actually, ferrous
and non ferrous metals can also be obtained from the extraction of virgin material from land
which have however, diﬀerent characteristics from the metal obtained from the treatment process.
This is the reason why all the coproducts are considered directly dependent from the extraction
of inert material. Moreover, the two diﬀerent processes which are object of the study don’t aﬀect
the quality or quantity of the recovered metal from the original bottom ash. Hence, allocation has
to be applied. A meaningful way to do this is a physical allocation based on the mass amounts
of each product and by-product, in order to follow the ISO 14044 (2018) Hierarchy framework.
Attributional and Consequential LCA
The two main LCI modelling frameworks are the attributional and consequential ones. The
attributional model has the overall aim to look at a product system/service without taking the
surrounding technological environment into consideration directly. This way the full economy is
neglected and the issue of ”what environmental impact can be addressed to product X” stands
in the foreground. Average processes are then the focus of modelling the background system of
the object assessment.
6
==PAGE:18==
Lorenzo Soncina
Master Thesis Project
June 2018
On the other hand, consequential modelling attempts to include the technosphere and the overall
economy in the assessment. The question answered in this case is ”what are the environmental
consequences of consuming X”. Changes in the market demand depending on the FU would be
included as far as possible (Hauschild et al. (2017)).
For this case study, the whole technosphere it’s not taken into account and the focus is kept
solely on the product system. Thus, from this perspective, an attributional approach is applied
with average processes for the background systems.

Uncertainty Analysis
The most important uncertainty in this case is parameter uncertainty. The ﬁrst uncertainty is
from the lack or non-representativeness of the real consumables and consumption data. The
assumption of the consumption data is necessary since the results obtained from the laboratory
test did not supply all the essential information such as the energy input. Actually, the test
provided consumption data which are totally disproportionate compared to an industrial test
which is the reason why the assumed data were sought within scientiﬁc literature.
In addition, the further parameter uncertainty is given from the foam glass additives itself because
they are employed as pristine material while the optimized process could exploit other materials
with a lower environmental impact.
Finally, another (small) source of uncertainty included in the system is given by the assumptions
made ragarding transportation. Firstly, the ”market” nature of the Ecoinvent (Ecoinvent (2018))
system processes just provide an average consumption data which could not be representative.
Moreover, the transport distance of the inert material employed for the foam glass production
could vary based on the location of the hypothetical industrial plant that will be settled in the
company. Thus, the estimation of transport data cause the uncertainty.

6.1
The business analysis methodology
A Business Analysis Methodology refers to the method (rules, principles and processes) that is
applied to implement the business analysis activities within the project. The term methodology
is about describing the underlying principles or rules to follow when applying process, techniques
and activities. The most common methodologies are the Plan - Driven approach and the Change
- Driven approach which are inherently diﬀerent in their approach to delivering project outcomes
and which are therefore placed into two distinct camps (BAE (2015)). They are described below
and ﬁgure 34 presents a comparison of the approaches:
• Plan - Driven approach: the plan driven approach is primarily focused on planning most
of the activities and deliverables is done in the initial stage of the project. The goal is to
try and remove any uncertainty of what will be delivered and aim to plan and execute the
plan in a sequential way. The main disadvantage with the plan driven approach is that by
the time an outcome is delivered the requirements may have changed.
• Change - Driven approach: the change driven approach is, on the other hand, not concerned
with a lot of upfront planning and thrives on delivering on an iterative and incremental
basis. Change is continuously introduced as a result of each iteration and although each
iteration is carefully managed and controlled, change is welcomed and part of delivering
a relevant and focus result. The type of methodology is sometimes confused with being
uncontrolled and completely open and undeﬁned before work is started. Although a lot
of ﬂexibility is allowed for this methodology it requires signiﬁcant planning, designing,
building and testing on an iterative and continuous basis.
The business analysis implemented in this test is an hybrid of the two methodologies presented.
Firstly, the foam glass test was planned in all its activities and the expected results were
explained. Speciﬁcally, all the processes required have been settled as described in section 3.4.
They follow the strict rules based on the principles necessary in order to manufacture the
foam glass (section 3.2). Moreover, a speciﬁc market research together with a cost and proﬁt
assessment is implemented in view of speciﬁc data (section 3.3) obtained from the company and
from scientiﬁc literature.
However, the signiﬁcant variability of the factors related to the test such as the speciﬁc chemical
composition, the amount of additives and the temperatures of the processes involved a strong
grade of uncertainty. For this reason, a lot of change occurred during the testing which, after a
critical remark, have been exploited in order to introduce new iterations with the aim to provide
a precise and focus result desired.
The features described are typical of both the methodologies presented above which can be
however well mixed all together. As a matter of fact, now days most project or organizations
don’t have a speciﬁc methodology of a business plan implementations but rather they follow the
best concept which ﬁt to the desired result (BAE (2015)).

7
Conclusion
In the conclusion, the global results of the environmental and economic assessment are summarized.
Firstly, the systems comparison is described, then the overall limitations of the whole analysis
are exposed and ﬁnally the global recommendations are outlined.
7.1
LCA
7.1.1
Treatment comparison.
The characterised results using ILCD recommended - 2013 Prosuit Global NR DTU resource w/o
LT DTU (2017a) displayed in table 4 present a better performance for the mixed cement process,
suggesting that the foam glass system is worse oﬀthan the mixed cement one. This conclusion is
reinforced by investigating the normalised results and their magnitude for all impact categories,
ﬁnding the foam glass system process to have a high environmental impact contribution in all the
impact categories instead of the mixed cement one which provide crediting values. However, the
foam glass system has better crediting values looking speciﬁcally to the substitution processes of
the two systems. It means that the avoided production of the glass raw material is worthy of
attention giving rise to some recommendations described in section 7.3
7.1.2
Limitations
Most importantly, it has to be stated that the results of this study do not apply to a general or
global comparison between diﬀerent BA treatments. They are highly dependent on the system,
which in this case consists in the diﬀerent exploitation of the inert material obtained from the
BA recovery process in a speciﬁc company. The geographical representativeness is considered
to be good as most system processes employed could be traced back to the country of origin of
the study. However, it also means that the results may not be taken as valid if the same BA
treatments are analyzed in another country than Italy.
Moreover, as mentioned in section 3.2 and then presented in section 6, important limitation are
settled by the uncertainties of the experimental test. As a matter of fact, it is important to state
that the energy consumption data used in the LCA software simulation could not be optimal or
representative even if they have been chosen as precise as possible. Only with an industrial test
the correct data could be obtained and the assessment will be then more realistic.
7.2
Business analysis
7.2.1
Markets comparison.
The results presented, show the substantial diﬀerence between the two treatments. The cement
production cost is low but no proﬁts are provided from the system. As a matter of fact there is
no sales related to the product since it is a waste material for Italian regulations and it involves
disposal costs. However, this is the process which involves the lowest impact scores in terms of

environmental impact and it has to be thereby kept within the core business of the company. On
the other hand, the foam glass product system is a high cost process which involves however
signiﬁcant revenues. Therefore, it is obvious on how the foam glass system is preferred on the
economical perspective. In conclusion, the new business has to be deﬁnitely taken into account
from RMB S.p.A. although a special care is necessary in order to improve the consumables
and electricity consumption. As a matter of fact, the system still has some limitations which
are described in the following section 7.2.2 which could be fronted with the ﬁnal recommendations.
7.2.2
Limitations.
It is important to state how the fundamental limitations are settled from the small scale level
of the foam glass test which is made on a laboratory level. Indeed, as described in section 3,
the consumption related to the foam glass production are deﬁnitely not representative for the
real outcome of the product system. This is intended both on the environmental and economic
perspectives. The elevated energy consumption together with the high damaging grade linked
to the consumables production provide a huge environmental impact. It makes the LCA look
completely negative which is a distorted result since the positive silica sand crediting process
is a positive aspect which has to be taken into account as an incentive for the system process
improvement. The economic aspect is strictly related to the environmental one. The complexity
of the manufacturing processes together with the disproportional consumption of energy and
consumables bring also high costs. Even in this case, the analysis could seem negative which
is proved to be wrong from the economical assessment made with reasonable data (section 6)
obtained from the conservative technical research. In addition, an important limitation described
in section 6 which do not allow a real investment proﬁtability assessment consists in the lack of
empirical data related to an industrial investment. As a matter of fact, the economic analysis
is made only on a variable costs assessment missing in a ﬁxed cost evaluation which could
completely change the analysis outcome. As proven from the limitations described, the system
can be enhanced and some recommendations are therefore presented.
7.3
Final recommendations
Generally, the mixed cement production is recommended for RMB S.p.A from an environmental
point of view but the foam glass system process has meaningful values which must be taken into
account.
The signiﬁcance of this data is evaluated by the LCA practitioner in order to present appropriate
recommendations.
Firstly, the mixed cement production is a process which must to be valorised. Indeed, it is a green
system process which fully contributes to recover the environment. The only recommendation for
this process is thereby to improve its energy consumption employing new sources of alternative
energy.
On the other hand, the foam glass system presents a crucial trade oﬀcondition and deserves of

some considerations because it is the process with the highest potential and margin. The strong
environmental impact of the foam glass process is in contrast with its proﬁtability. However,
the negative impact score can be reduced following some recommendation in order to make the
process favourable in all its aspects. The ﬁnal recommendations for RMB S.p.A. (RMB S.p.A.

• The company has to implement the foam glass test on an industrial scale exploiting therefore

product making the process more eﬃcient. The representative data about the consumption
of electricity and consumables will be therefore obtained together with the realistic outcome
of the assessment.
• The company has to apply his environmental focus also on the new business. In other words,
a new source of additives has to be sought in order to remodel the system environmental
impact. The solution could be implemented by employing recycled materials such as used
glass as a new consumable source. As a result, the crediting score of the system can be
increased together with a reduction of the overall environmental impact.
Last, as already mentioned for the cement system, the employment of new sources of alternative
energy could increase the environmental performance of the product systems, pursuing therefore
the eﬃciency required for the improvement of the production processes.
