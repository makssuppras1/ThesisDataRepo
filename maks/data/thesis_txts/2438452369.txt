Algorithm 4:TRL assessment
➢ Objective: to find the technology readiness level associated with the resource recovery technologyi.
 Define TRL range(1)(Rybicka et al.,2016)Do a literature search and: 
1. Assign TRL range I if the technology has been only used in the laboratory (2) 
2. Assign TRL range II if the technology has both lab and pilot scale (3) applications 
3. Assign TRL range III if the technology has lab, pilot and plant scale applications (4) ii.
 Estimate TRL valueby looking at the following factors
a. 
Number of occurrence found in literature for the technology applications 
This factor would be considered high if there are more than five full scale applications 
recorded in literature, medium for three to five applications and low when less than 
two applications are recorded 
b. General complexity of the technology 
This factor would be considered high if the process technology used is based on well-
established sub technologies (such as traditional membranes or composting). It would 
be considered medium, if the basis of the sub-technologies used are recent 
developments but has some pilot/full scale applications or low if the basis of the 
process technology is novel and untested 
c. 
Fundamental understanding of the process and mechanisms 
This factor is considered high if the reaction/separation mechanisms are completely 
understood and demonstrated in a lab setting, a medium score if the reaction 
mechanisms are somewhat known and a low score if the reaction mechanisms are 
completely unknown   iii.
