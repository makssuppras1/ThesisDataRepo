Abstract 
 
G! proteinGcoupled! receptors! (GPCRs)! constitute! the! largest! superfamily! of! membrane!
bound!proteins!that!are!responsible!for!signal!recognition!and!communication!through!the!cell!
membrane.!Their!involvement!in!several!diseases!has!put!them!in!the!center!of!attention!in!the!
pharmaceutical!industry!with!more!than!40%!of!all!marketed!drugs!targeting!GPCRs.!The!aim!of!
this!project!was!to!find!new!small!molecule!ligands!that!demonstrate!an!inhibitory!effect!on!the!
human! cytomegalovirus! (HCMV)Gencoded! chemokine! receptor! US28.! US28! is! homologous! to!
human!chemokine!receptors!known!to!act!as!coGreceptors!for!HIVGtype!1!entry!into!cells.!So!far,!
no! highly! potent! ligands! have! been! identified! to! inhibit! the! CMVGrelated! action! of! US28,!
rendering!this!receptor!an!interesting!drug!target.!
!
The!work!in!this!report!describes!a!structure!based!virtual!screening!protocol!combined!with!
experimental! testing.! The! virtual! screening! was! based! on! molecular! modeling! of! the! US28!
receptor!in!complex!with!a!low!potent!inverse!agonist!VUF2274.!The!receptorGligand!complex!of!
US28!with!VUF2274!was!generated!using!homology!modeling!and!atomic!property!fields!(APF)!
superimposition! based! on! the! closely! related! CCR5! chemokine! receptor! in! complex! with! the!
small!molecule!inverse!agonist!Maraviroc!(PDB!4MBS).!The!threeGdimensional!conformation!of!
VUF2274! in! the! US28! binding! site! was! used! as! a! query! molecule! for! the! prospective! virtual!
ligand! screening.! Databases! of! stockGavailable! chemical! compounds! containing! more! than! 10!
million!compounds!were!screened,!using!pharmacophore!multiplets,!APF!and!filtering!steps!to!
ensure! chemical! diversity! and! attractive! chemical! scaffolds! with! good! drugGlike! properties.! A!
focused! mini! library! of! 98! compounds! was! acquired! and! subsequently! tested! for! functional!
activity!and!binding!in!primary!and!secondary!screens,!using!IP3!accumulation!and!radioGligand!
displacement! assays! respectively.! Seven! hits! were! identified,! interestingly! representing! three!
pharmacological!classes,!two!binders,!two!agonists!and!three!inverse!agonists.!The!best!inverse!
agonist,!compound!29!(EC50=!0.52!μΜ)!represents!a!novel!chemical!scaffold,!which!has!a!nineG
fold!better!potency!than!the!query!molecule!VUF2274.!
!
The!devised!protocol!is!highly!suitable!in!an!academic!setting,!for!the!discovery!of!ligands!to!
biological! targets! which! are! not! powered! by! high! throughput! screening! (HTS)! or! chemical!
synthesis.! The! ligands! identified! are! directly! related! to! the! further! optimization! and!
development!of!SARGrich!chemical!series!and!suitable!pharmacological!attractive!small!molecule!
tool!compounds.!

1. Introduction 
%
Proteins,! due! to! their! diversity! and! involvement! in! various! biological! processes,! have!
been!by!far!the!most!prominent!drug!targets!in!the!pharmaceutical!industry.!Enzymes,!transport!
proteins,!ion!channels!and!receptors!are!implicated!in!a!series!of!cellular!procedures,!regulating!
the! biosynthesis! and! degradation! of! macromolecules,! migration! of! cells,! the! passage! of! ions!
across!the!membrane,!and!many!more.!Exploration!of!their!structureGfunction!relationship!has!
always!been!crucial!for!drug!discovery!and!development,!as!it!provides!the!knowledge!necessary!
for!the!design!of!better!and!innovative!medicine!(Patrick!2009).!
!
1.1. G Protein-Coupled Receptors 
!
The!G!proteinGcoupled!receptors!(GPCRs)!constitute!the!largest!superfamily!of!membrane!
bound!proteins!that!are!responsible!for!signal!recognition!and!the!communication!through!the!
cell! membrane.! A! large! range! of! endogenous! ligands! binds! to! GPCRs,! such! as! nucleotides,!
peptides,!lipids,!glycoproteins!or!small!chemical!molecules!and!even!ions!(Jensen!et!al.!2012).!
GPCRs! interact! with! neurotransmitters! such! as! serotonin,! noradrenaline,! dopamine! and!
histamine.!As!they!are!major!regulators!of!a!variety!of!biological!mechanisms,!their dysfunction!
leads! to! diseases.! Being! associated! with! neurological,! cardiovascular,! immunological! diseases,!
even! cancer,! GPCRs! have! been! the! center! of! attention! within! the! pharmaceutical! industry,!
representing!more!than!40%!of!all!marketed!drugs!(Katritch!et!al.!2012;!Soroceanu!et!al.!2011;!
Patrick!2009).!
!
GPCRs! are! also! known! as! seven! transmembrane! receptors! (7TM),! since! the! protein! chain!
consists!of!seven!αGhelices.!The!helices!are!usually!annotated!after!roman!numbers!(I,!II,!and!so!
on),! and! they! are! connected! with! each! other! by! three! extracellular! loops! (ECL)! and! three!
intracellular!loops!(ICL).!The!recent!availability!of!GPCR!xGray!crystal!structures!allowed!further!
understanding!the!functional!roles!of!the!extracellular!intracellular!regions!(Katritch!et!al.!2012).!
In!general,!the!extracellular!side!(EC)!of!these!receptors!is!dedicated!to!ligand!recognition!and!
binding,! whereas! the! intracellular! (IC)! is! devoted! to! signaling,! see! Figure! 1.1! (Katritch! et! al.!
2012;! Patrick! 2009).! Despite! the! chemical! diversity! of! the! ligands! that! bind! to! GPCRs,! their!
overall! structure! is! surprisingly! similar.! Their! sequences! though! are! considerably! different!
(Jensen!et!al.!2012).!

1.1.1. GPCR signaling and constitutive activity 
!
G!proteins!are!signaling!proteins!that!are!regulated!by!GPCRs,!and!subsequently!induce!or!
inhibit!the!function!of!membrane!bound!or!intracellular!enzymes.!They!are!constituted!by!three!
components,!the!αG,!βG,!and!γGsubunit,!with!the!two!last!being!very!tightly!bound!to!each!other.!
The! αGsubunit! has! a! binding! site! for! guanyl! nucleotides,! hence! it! binds! guanosine! phosphate!
molecules!(GDP).!G!proteins!are!inactive!while!GDP!is!bound.!When!a!ligand!binds!to!a!GPCR,!it!
triggers! a! conformational! change! of! the! transmembrane! domain! that! results! in! opening! an!
intracellular! binding! site! that! is! recognized! by! G! proteins,! see! Figure! 1.3.! Once! the! G! protein!
binds!to!this,!it!changes!shape!and!the!GDP!molecule!is!released.!The!binding!pocket!of!the!αG
subunit! subsequently! binds! guanosine! triphosphate! molecules! (GTP)! and! the! interactions!
keeping!the!trimeric!complex!together!are!thereby!weakened!leading!to!complex!dissociation!

1.3. Chemokine Receptors 
!
Chemokine! receptors! and! their! endogenous! ligands,! chemokines! (abbreviated! from!
chemotactic! cytokines),! play! a! key! role! in! the! modulation! of! the! immune! system! and! cell!
trafficking,! for! example,! in! the! migration! of! leukocytes! to! sites! of! infection! (Rosenkilde! et! al.!
2001).!Moreover,!their!function!has!been!associated!with!processes!such!as!cancer!development,!
embryogenesis,!angiogenesis,!and!HIVGinfections!(Rosenkilde!&!Schwartz!2004;!Viola!&!Luster!
2008).!
!
The!chemokine!receptor!family!belongs!to!the!class!A!subfamily!of!GPCRs,!with!approximately!
20! different! receptors! that! interact! with! 45! chemokines! (including! their! viral! equivalents)!
(Thiele!&!Rosenkilde!2014).!The!chemokine!receptors!are!divided!into!four!distinct!subfamilies:!
CC,! CXC,! CX3C! and! XC! based! on! the! structure! of! their! cognate! chemokines.! The! chemokine!
classification! is! based! on! the! organization! of! the! two! first! of! usually! four! highly! conserved!
cysteine!residues,!present!in!most!chemokines!(Vassilatis!et!al.!2003;!Murphy!et!al.!2000).!Like!
most!other!GPCRs,!the!majority!of!chemokine!receptors!display!promiscuity!in!their!specificity!
for!chemokines!with!binding!of!more!than!one!type!(Thiele!&!Rosenkilde!2014).!Similarly,!most!
chemokines!bind!to!more!than!one!type!of!chemokine!receptors.!Thus!promiscuity!is!abundant!
in! the! chemokine! system,! and! considered! necessary! to! ensure! proper! control! of! the! immune!
system!under!homeostatic!conditions!as!well!as!during!challenges!of!the!immune!system,!like!for!
instance!during!inflammation.!
!
There!are!several!common!structural!features!among!the!chemokine!receptors.!Previous!studies!
have! shown! that! the! acidic! NGterminus! and! extracellular! regions! of! the! chemokine! receptors!
have!a!central!role!in!regards!to!chemokine!binding!and!receptor!activation!(Hemmerich!et!al.!
1999;! Katancik! et! al.! 1997).! Additionally,! as! in! all! GPCRs,! one! of! the! most! highly! conserved!
features!is!the!disulfide!bridge!between!a!cysteine!residue!in!the!top!of!TMGIII!and!a!cysteine!in!
ECL2!(Thiele!&!Rosenkilde!2014).!Furthermore,!chemokine!receptors!have!two!extra!cysteine!
residues!in!the!EC!regions!that!have!been!considered!to!form!a!disulfide!bridge!between!the!NG
terminal! and! ECL3/top! of! TMGVII.! It! has! been! suggested,! that! both! disulfide! bridges! are!
significant!in!regards!to!chemokine!binding!(Blanpain!et!al.!1999;!Zhou!&!Tai!2000)and!receptor!
signaling!(Ai!&!Liao!2002;!Rummel!et!al.!2013)!

2.3. Experimental methodology 
!
In! order! to! experimentally! validate! the! compounds! for! their! function! and! binding! to!

further!diluted!to!get!an!idea!about!their!activity.!Dilution!rows!of!the!10!mM!stock!solutions!

2.3.2. SPA-bead Inositol tri-phosphate (IP3) accumulation assay  
!
In! order! to! measure! signaling! through! a! receptor! as! a! function! of! Gαq! signaling,! the!
Inositol! triGphosphate! (IP3)! accumulation! assay! is! employed.! The! advantage! of! the! IP3!assay!
compared!to!other!known!assays!that!are!based!on!measuring!effectors!further!downstream!in!
the!signaling!pathway,!is!its!close!proximity!to!receptor!activation.!
!
As!described!in!Section!1.1.1,!GαqGproteins!activate!the!membrane!bound!enzyme!phospholipase!
CGβ! (PLCGβ),! leading! to! hydrolysis! of! phosphatidylinositol! diphosphate! (PIP2)! and! the!
subsequent! formation! of! the! secondary! messengers! 1,4,5Gtrisphosphate! (IP3)! and! 1,2G
diacylglycerol! (DAG).! Over! a! series! of! reactions! catalyzed! by! inositol! phosphatases,! IP3! is!
dephosphorylated!three!times!to!inositol.!Inositol!is!recombined!with!DAG!to!regenerate!PIP2!
and!complete!the!IP3!turnover!cycle.!In!order!to!accumulate!inositol!phosphate!(IP)!in!the!cell,!
the! action! of! the! monophosphatase! enzyme! responsible! for! the! dephosphorylation! of! IP! to!
inositol! is! inhibited! by! the! presence! of! lithium! ions! (Li+),! see! Figure! 2.5! (Leech! et! al.! 1993;!
Patrick!2009).!

4. Conclusions 
!
US28! is! the! best! characterized! viral! chemokine! receptor! encoded! by! the! human!
cytomegalovirus! (HCMV).! US28! is! closely! related! to! its! human! counterparts,! and! has! been!
associated! with! HIVG1! entry,! like! the! human! receptors! CCR5! and! CXCR4.! The! viral! origin,!
expression!at!the!cell!surface!of!virusGinfected!cells!and!high!constitutive!activity!of!US28,!that!
has!been!associated!with!virus!replication!and!survival!(Farrell!et!al.!2013),!indicate!that!US28!
could!be!a!suitable!drug!target!for!specific!targeting!of!CMVGinfected!cells!as!an!efficient!antiviral!
approach! (Spiess! et! al.! 2015).! In! addition,! several! studies! have! suggested! that! US28! is! also!
implicated!in!atherosclerosis,!angiogenesis,!embryogenesis!and!development!of!glioblastomas!in!
