Abstract
The aim of this thesis is to apply relevant theories in order to make a business plan for a new
Icelandic dried fish snack product, where additional value is created for consumers.
Market analyzation for the Icelandic and international marketplaces was conducted in order to
create value proposition that fits the needs of a new customer segment. Health, convenience and
price were found to be key factors in the value proposition.
Manufacturing and packaging ways were explored to develop a product that fulfills the value
proposition.
Financial analysis was conducted using sales forecast and cost analysis. Results of the analysis found
that break even happens in year 3.

Introduction
1.1
Background
Kristinn Olafsson and Thordis Wathne are business partners that had an idea in 2014 to make a new
Icelandic dried fish product in a start-up company. As 90% of start-up fail (Patel N. 2015) they
needed a business plan for the idea in order to be a foundation for preventing failure, and welcomed
that the author of this thesis would make a business plan in order to analyze the feasibility of the
project. The business plan should be valuable for their decision making when it comes to the future
of the product.
The global snack industry is a multi billion business (nielsen, 2014). The trend in the gobal snack
industry is that cosumers want snacks with natural basic ingredients (nielsen, 2014).
Traditional Icelandic dried fish snack is a snack product type made from natural basic ingredients
and has been manufactured (Matís ohf, 2014). The exporting of fish is one of Iceland‘s economy
main building blocks (Íslandsstofa, 2015). Though, the total export of Icelandic dried fish is only a
small fraction of the total export of fish from Iceland, where approximately 50.000 kg of dried fish
is exported from Iceland yearly (Arason S., personal communication, 2016).
The aim of this project is to create additional value to the Icelandic dried fish, by designing and
developing new Icelandic dried fish products, that better meets consumer needs on the local and
international marketplaces. The aim is that the product will find a position in the global snack
industry.
Relevant theories are applied in this project in order to make a business plan for new Icelandic dried
fish products.
1.2
The Research Question
Formulation of a research question is the start of the investigation and is the focal point of the
thesis. The study investigates theories and models in order to construct a well supported business
plan. The research question in this thesis is the following:
How can a start-up create additional value to the traditional Icelandic dried fish in a new product,
in order to better meet consumer needs on local and international marketplaces?

Methodology
The main methodology of the thesis is the following:
1. Relevant research methods are chosen
2. A research is made in order to identify possibilities
3. The possibilities are compared in order to get an optimal solution
4. Optimal solution is presented
It was estimated that a mix of research methods would give a solid overview to base decision
making on. The research methods are five and are the following:

Interviews

Surveys

Taste tests for prototypes (followed with questions)

Available information in form of reports and relevant readings and data

Field trips
Interviews with key people within the industry are made in order to gain knowledge on the relevant
subjects. Interviews with consumers are made to analyze their needs. Interviews gave the
opportunity of getting precise answers. The pros are that a precise feedback where a lot of extra
information is gained. The cons are that it is time-consuming and therefore often as diverse
feedback as desired is not gained.
Surveys with consumers are made to analyze their needs. Surveys has the pros of being able to
attract a lot of answers but the precise can be inadequate as some questions can be misunderstood
and valuable extra information is not gained. It takes short time to make a survey compared to
conducting interviews. The focus on the questions is important in order to get as precise answers
as possible and it is also important to analyze the answers precisely.
Taste tests for prototypes, followed with questions, are made on consumers to get feedback on the
features of the product. That feedback is used to develop the product and tests are run again in
order to measure improvements. It is therefore an iterative process.
Available information in form of reports and relevant readings and data are made in order to gain
knowledge on the relevant subjects.
Field trips are made in order to gain knowledge on the relevant subjects, such as competitors’
products in retailers and their placement, packaging, price, ingredients, nutrients etc.

The Product
Dried fish is a ready to eat food product. In short, fresh fish is dried over a period of time and salt
and in rare cases spice is added. Dried fish has been sold through the years in the form of dried fish
flakes or bits.
The aim of this project is to design a new dried fish product that meets consumers’ needs. The
product that is developed, and has the working title Breki, are very thin dried fish chips (with a
Pringles like shape), with salt or spice. The product should be sold in 30 gr packages.

4.1
Introduction
The aim of this chapter analyze consumers need and industry in order to create a new value
proposition in a new dried fish product for a new customer segment. Based on the analysis a sales
forecast for the product is made. A marketing plan is also created in order to deliver the value

4.2
Market Analysis
In order for a product to be successful you need customers to buy the product. Therefore, it is
important to offer the right product at the right price, in the right place and at the right time. In
order to do so a good knowledge about the market, customers' needs, and the size of the industry
and competitors is crucial. To gain this knowledge a market research is done where each of these
aspects are researched. Market analysis researches the demand for the product and it's aim is to
determine if there's a need and a business foundation for the product in the marketplace.
Surveys, interviews, available readings and field trips were used as research methods, as well as
taste tests on the prototypes were used later in the analysis in order to test the findings in reality.
The main results of the consumer analysis demonstrate the majority of answers by the participants:

Most of the participants eat dried fish.

80% protein in a product is positive.

The fish smell from dried fish is a barrier for buying. Participants would not open and eat
dried fish in open space with other people because of the smell.

The participants feel that it is hard to chew Icelandic dried fish products and that the shape
is inconvenient.

Protein bars, protein drinks, eggs, dried fish, nuts, almonds and nut mixes are used by the
participants as a protein snack.

Participants feel that ready to eat protein snack, other than dried fish, is not healthy enough
as it contains undesirable added ingredients.

4.2.1
Value Proposition
Value proposition is a bundle of benefits in a product or service that a company offer to a specific
customer segment. The value proposition aims at solving the customer segment pain or satisfy its
needs, and is the reason for why the customers should choose the product or service over another.
Therefore, the value proposition is crucial to make a differentiation and stand out in competition.
A market research mainly in form of surveys and interviews were made in order to identify the pain
in the market. The analysis of the research show that the pain in the market that consumer feel is
that there is a lack of being able to have healthy and convenient protein snack. There is a need for
a protein snack product that is both healthy and convenient
Traditional dried fish products are estimated healthy but inconvenient by the customer segment,
due to three main reasons; high fish smell, inconvenience to chew, as well as most dried fish types
are estimated to have bad shape. These three factors, as well as the high price, are a barrier for the
customer segment to buy the product.
Other protein snack products, are different to traditional dried fish, as they are seen as convenient
but unhealthy due to one main reason; they include undesirable unnatural ingredients. Also, the
protein rate is not estimated high enough for many of the other protein snack products.
==PAGE:22==
21
A value proposition has been created. The value proposition aims to solve the abovementioned pain
and create value for the customer segment. The suggested value proposition has three main parts;

Health

All natural ingredients

Convenient

The health part of the value proposition is supposed to differentiate from protein snack products,
other than dried fish products, while the convenient part and the fair price is supposed to
differentiate from dried fish products.
The value proposition is unique and other protein snack products does not have the same value
proposition.
The manufacturing of the product should aim at making a product that fulfills the value proposition
(See chapter 6.2 Marketing).
The value proposition should be delivered by marketing (See chapter 4.3 Marketing Plan).
4.2.2
Customer Segment
Customer segment is a group of customers that share similar needs or characteristics, both related
to demographics and psychographics. Demographics factors can be e.g. age, gender, race and
monthly income, while psychographics factors can be e.g. social class, lifestyle and personality
characteristics. A company can have one large or several smaller customer segments for a product
or service. Grouping consumers into segments are a key in deciding which consumers to serve and
which to ignore, this distinguish can be crucial for a business to be successful.
==PAGE:23==
22
In order to identify the customer segment/s for Breki a market research was made which consisted
mainly of surveys and interviews. The market research aimed at identifying why people would buy
the product and who would buy the product. That indicates what segment are in need of the values
of the product.
In the start the product should be put on the Icelandic marketplace. Icelanders are approximately
332.500, where approximately. 210.000 live in the capital area. There's been a large increase in
tourists in Iceland in the past years and it is expected to continue. Tourists in Iceland were more
than 1.000.000 in 2015 (Ferðamálastofa, n.d). In order to identify the customer segment both
Icelanders and tourists were included in a market research.
Analysis show that health and taste are the main factors for why consumers buy dried fish and would
buy Breki dried fish chips. Luxury was e.g. not a reason to buy. That implies that the product fits
better to healthy minded people than e.g. people that like to treat themselves with luxury snack.
Therefore, it was estimated that sport persons might be in need for the value of the product. That
group of people were analyzed deeper as a possible customer segment. This segment is estimated
to be the segment that is the most healthy minded and is best suited to the value proposition of the
product. Further analysis on sport persons supported their needs for Breki value proposition. Taste
tests, followed with interviews show that this customer segment is interested in buying the product.
Tourists are another segment that have been analyzed. Following is an analysis on sport persons
and tourists.
Sport persons
Sport persons are estimated the most suitable customer segment in Iceland. The product is made
in order to solve the pain for sport persons, of not being able to have a healthy and convenient
protein snack. The values of the product (see Chapter 4.2.2 Value Proposition) fits very well to sport
persons need. The 80% protein, no undesirable ingredients and the convenience of the product fits
to their active lifestyle. The product will therefore first be marketed to sport persons.
When looking at psychographic factors the customer is a person with active lifestyle that do any
kind of sports regularly, 3+ a week. He needs a healthy protein snack regularly, mainly as a mid-day
snack, evening snack or during their sport activity.  When looking at the demographic factors the
age of the customer is 20-45, it fits both gender and the customer doesn’t need to have a specific
income.

The effort should be on sport persons. Later on, deeper analyze on the tourist customer segment
might be made in order to estimate the opportunity of focusing also on that segment, when the
product has a stability by sport persons and is established on he Icelandic marketplace.
As the product fits a wider range of segments, sales are not only dependent on the sport persons
customer segment,  but are expected to come from outside of that segment also.
In chapter 4.3 Marketing plan the marketing plan for the customer segment, sport persons, is

4.2.3
Industry & Competitors
Competitors are companies in the same industry who offer a similar product or service. Making a
differentiation from the competitors can make a customer choose a product or service over
competitor products.
A rough estimate on the size of the dried fish industry is the following:
Approximately 700.000-800.000 kg of quality dried fish is manufactured yearly in Iceland (Arason,
S. personal communication, 2016).  Quality dried fish includes dried fish fillets and dried fish bits.
==PAGE:26==
25
Also, other dried fish, crawled and fish heads are manufactured, which is not included in these
numbers. Approximately 400.000 kg is sold in retailers in Iceland. Approximately 300.000 kg is then
sold in direct sales for “Þorrablót” (Traditional Icelandic festival once a year) and by direct sale (with
a sale to employees in companies, fundraising etc.) Approximately 50.000 kg of the quality dried
fish is then exported, mainly to Norway, and in small quantity to Faroe Islands and Greenland
(Arason S., personal communication, 2016).
Breki is estimated to be within the snack industry, namely the protein snack industry, where the
product will be sold through retailers. Many competitors exist in the market, some of them very
strong. Breki’s main competitors are estimated to be snack products with high protein content.
When analyzing who the main competitors are it’s important to analyze which products fulfill similar

main competitors as those products solve the customer segment need for a ready-to-eat protein
snack. The people that buy these four product groups are estimated to be most likely to buy Breki.
Therefore, brands from these four product groups needs to be analyzed to find what would make
people buy Breki instead of them. Other competitors, that are not estimated as main competitors
but competitors to be aware of are e.g. Beef jerky which has a small position on the Icelandic
marketplace so it is not estimated as part of the main competitors for the Icelandic marketplace.
Eggs solves the need of a healthy protein product but is not estimated as main competitors as it is

solves different need when it comes to thirst vs hunger and is therefore not estimated as the main

The main competitors have been analyzed in order to identify their strengths and weaknesses.
Dried fish
All the dried fish in Iceland is Icelandic. The dried fish that is on the market now comes in two main
versions, fish flakes and fish bites. Mostly dried fish flakes are on the market, while the past years
fish bits have increased on the Icelandic market. These bits are quite thick, most of them have an
irregular form, along with one type in a circular form. One type comes with three different spices.
All these dried fish products contain a high fish smell. No dried fish product on the Icelandic market
is shaped like very thin chips or have a low fish smell.
Most of the packages for dried fish fillets are normally 70-150 gr. A few also comes in 1000 gr
packages. The sizes of packages for dried fish bits are often smaller than for dried fish flakes. Normal
sizes for dried fish bits are 40-150 gr.

5.1
Introduction
Born-global perspective requires a startup to address the needs of a global market from start. As
Breki dried fish chips are supposed to reach external markeplaces in year 3, it is important to analyze
external markeplaces from the start. It is important to have in mind that exporting a product can

another marketplace. It is very important to understand this and not fall into the known trap to

6.2.2
Process
The manufacturing process are, along with the ingredients and recipe a key in the features of the
final product. The manufacturing process turns the ingredients into a final product.
The purpose of dried fish manufacturing is to turn fresh fish in to ready to eat dried fish. In order to
do so the core aim of the manufacturing is to remove water from the fresh fish, in an efficient way,
regarding both time and cost. The main input of the manufacturing, the fresh fish, is approximately
five times heavier than the output, the dried fish (Arason S. personal communication, 2016). That
fact is a key reason for the high cost of dried fish products.
Dried fish can be manufactured indoor or outdoor. Today, most dried fish is manufactured indoor.
Two main factors make the indoor manufacturing more attractive options than outdoor
manufacturing; First, the drying time is few days instead of approximately two weeks and second,
the quality is better as weather conditions don’t effect the drying process (Matís ohf, 2014).

Prevent damage
It is important to prevent damage in the fish, as damage can have bad health effects, bad effects on
taste and appearance and therefore have bad effect on the brand image. Mainly microorganisms
can damage the fish (Matís ohf, 2014). Microorganisms needs water to grow, like any other
organisms. The growth of microorganisms in the fish is though not only depending on the water
quantity in the fish, but so-called unbound water, where bound water does not help the
microorganisms to grow. Water activity (Aw) is a measurement for the unbound water. Water free
food have Aw=0, while clean water have Aw=1. If the water activity is less than 0.6, microorganisms
can’t grow. In order to lower the water activity in a food product salt or sugar can be used, as these
products bounds water and therefore lower the water activity. Freezing can be used as the ice
crystals bounds water and drying, where water is abducted. When making dried fish drying is
==PAGE:53==
52
obviously crucial in order to lower the water activity. As fresh fish have Aw=0.96-0.99, it is important
that right steps are made in a right way in order to prevent damaging the fish until it is a finished
dried fish. As the dried fish needs to be under Aw=0,6, the growth of microorganisms is not possible
in that situation, but the condition afterwards is also important, as many microorganisms are in
resting state and can start to grow if the humidity increases (Matís ohf, 2014).
Microorganisms measurements are done in order to test if the manufacturing process fulfills
requirements, that is to make sure microorganisms won’t live in the dried fish product. In order to
measure it, the dried fish is placed in to airtight container and when in balance the relative humidity
(%RH) in it is measured with hygrometer or water activity meter. A correlation is between the
relative humidity around food products and the water activity (Aw) in it. This is done in order to
make sure that Aw<0,6. Water activity measurement needs to be made for the dried fish chips in
order to guarantee that it is not damaged (Matís ohf, 2014).
Manufacturing process
A manufacturing process has been created in order to manufacture the dried fish chips in context
with the value proposition and gain other important features. It includes ten main steps, which are
the following;
1. Fresh Haddock fillets and haddock mince are mixed together.
2. Fish cylinders are made out of the fresh haddock fillets and fresh haddock mince mix with
the hot-dog machine.
3. The fish cylinders are placed on the drying frame.
4. The fish cylinders (on the drying frame) are put into a freezer.
5. The fish cylinders are taken out of the freezer in order for the fish cylinders to recover.
6. The fish cylinders are cut in to chips with the meat knife.
7. The fish chips are put on drying mechanisms and into the freezer again.
8. The frozen fish chips are taken out of the freezer and put into drying room, on the drying
frame. The temperature & air speed is high.
9. The temperature of the drying room is lowered and the air speed is lowered.

10. The dried fish recovers. After that it is ready to be packed.

Low smell
==PAGE:56==
55
Ingredients and the manufacturing process have an effect on the smell of the dried fish. Using new
fish and spice lower the bad smell that’s distinctive with dried fish. In the manufacturing process,
temperature, air speed and time are all important.
When it comes to ingredients it is important to use new quality fish. Smell gets worse as the fish
gets older. Therefore, keeping the time from fishing to dried fish manufacturing at minimum is key
for low fish smell. Using spice can significantly lower the smell.
In order to lower the smell with the manufacturing process, first by shaping the dried fish as chips,
more water can be removed from the fish than by shaping it like flakes or bits. Second, by drying as
much possible when the fish is fully frozen, with high temperature (30-32°C) and high air speed (4-
5 m/s) in the drying room, for 2-4 hours. By doing so the water reduction is maximized and smell is
lowered, as the fish smell is partly bound in the water in the fish (Arason S., personal
communication, 2016). The freezer and computational drying room, as well as the drying frame, are

By using new fresh fish and by using the abovementioned manufacturing process method, a dried
fish product with much lower smell than any other dried fish product on the market can be
manufactured. Product manufactured in this way is suggested as the Breki dried fish chips for the
local marketplace.
In order to lower the smell even more a spice should be added for the international marketplace.

6.3.2
Materials
Food packages can be divided in four main types; glass, metal, plastic, along with paper and
paperboard. These materials can then be divided into various under-types. The material that is used
in dried fish packages in Iceland is transparent plastic. Following is a short description on the four
main types of food packaging materials:
Plastic
Plastic can be divided in to two main types; thermoplastics and thermosets. Thermoplastics are used
in food packaging, while thermosets are not used, as it is very strong and durable and mainly used
e.g. in automobiles and construction application.
Thermoplastics are ideal for food package, as it can be shaped and molded easily into various
products, such as plastic films, jugs and bottles. Also, most of thermoplastics are recyclable.
Thermoplastic can be divided more down, as more than 30 types of it exist, with different features.
Unwanted material can transform from plastic packaging to a food product. That mostly happens
when it is heated in microwave. (IFT, 2007)
Paper & paperboards
Paper & paperboards, along with paper laminates is used for food packages.
==PAGE:63==
62
Plain paper is not used for food packages, unless for a very short time, as it has a weak barrier
properties. When it is used as a primary material, nearest to the food, it is usually combined with
another material in order to improve the protective and functional properties.
Paperboard is thicker and weights more than paper and is usually made in multiple layers. It is
mainly used for e.g. boxes and trays. It can be an option when choosing a transport packaging for
Breki.
Paper laminate is a coated or uncoated paper. It is made of kraft and sulfite pulp. It is often
laminated with plastic or aluminum to improve various properties. It is used for dried products, but
the cost of it is very high (IFT, 2007).
Metal
Metal is the most versatile of all the packages form. It is a very good when it comes to protection,
barrier properties, formability and recyclability. As well, it is positive regarding consumer
presumption. Aluminum and steel is mostly used in food packages out of metal forms. Aluminum
has high cost compared to other metal and is unable to be welded, which makes it only useful to
make seamless packaging (IFT, 2007).
Glass
Glass has many advantages as a food packages. This option is though costly. Glass has high cost
regarding the material, transport cost and storage (IFT, 2007).

11 Conclusion
In order for a start-up to create additional value to the traditional Icelandic dried fish in a new
product, that better meets consumer needs on local and international marketplaces various aspects
were analyzed. Analyzes on consumers’ needs and the industry on the local and international
marketplaces were made in order to identify opportunities for additional value and make a value
proposition. Analyzing manufacturing and packaging ways were made in order to identify
opportunities to create a product that is in context with the value proposition. Finance analysis were
made in order to estimated if the project was feasible. Organizational structure was suggested. The
risk was estimated, by analyzing risk factors and a risk management strategy provided.
Market analysis showed there is a need for a healthy & convenient dried fish chips. The value
proposition for the new dried fish product should include healthy, convenient and fair price. 80%
protein and all natural ingredients should be aspects of the healthy part of the value proposition
and low smell, easy to chew and nice shape should be aspects of the convenient part of the value
proposition. The value proposition is unique and supposed to differentiate from competitor
products. The customer segment should be sport persons, at the age of 20-45. Various competitors
exist in the protein snack industry, many of them with a strong position, the value proposition is
supposed to differentiate from competitors and gain market share. The product should have a  very
thin shape, crunchy dried fish chips, made out of mix of haddock fillets and haddock mince, with
salt for the local marketplace and spice for international marketplaces. The average price should be
27 DKK and it should be mainly sold through supermarkets and gas stations. A marketing plan consist
of various low-cost activities in order to deliver the value proposition to the customer segment.
The dried fish chips with spice are supposed to reach international marketplaces in year three.
Denmark should be the first international marketplace. Norway and Sweden should follow in year
four and five. Breki should export the product itself, where an employee with competence in
international business is needed.
The ingredients & recipe include natural ingredients, fresh fish and natural salt and spice in order
for the product to be in context with the customer segment needs of a healthy protein snack
product. Using a mix of haddock fillet and haddock mince both fulfill quality, where it fulfills the
value factors of the convenient part of the value proposition and is within cost-limit. The value for
the key factors and equipment are a key in the manufacturing process, which makes it able to
manufacture the dried fish product in context with the value proposition. In order to guarantee the
quality of the dried fish product it is estimated that Breki does not fulfill the competencies needed
in order to manufacture the dried fish product. By outsourcing the manufacturing to experienced
company in dried fish manufacturing, the quality can be fulfilled and the start-up cost is kept low.
Prototypes have been made in context with the market analysis and manufacturing analysis. The
==PAGE:90==
89
prototypes were tested with consumers, in order to get feedback on the desired features of the
product, such as the value proposition factors; low smell, easy to chew and nice shape. This was an
iterative process in order to develop the product and make it better meet consumers’ needs each
time.
Packaging features and materials were analyzed in order to design a packaging solution for the dried
fish product, which both include protection features and marketing features. Three layers of plastic;
Polyethylene (PE) as the innermost layer and Polyamide Nylon as the middle and outermost layer is
estimated the best option for both features, where quality of the features is gained and cost is
acceptable. The packaging solution differentiates from dried fish packaging as it does not let fish
smell through the packaging, it can and should have a zip-lock, it is not transparent except a small
window, the aesthetics is fulfilled and the size of the package should be 30 gr.
The initial capital need is 376.000 DKK and consist of a business plan, hot-dog machine and office
