Abstract 
In this study, six metal porphyrins containing transition metals from the first row were 
investigated for their activities towards Oxygen Reduction Reaction (ORR) and 
Oxygen Evolution Reaction (OER). Furthermore, a comparison was made between 
their gas phase and adsorbed state on metallic substrates (Ag, Au) as well as their 
variation between two functional peripheral groups (substituents), namely Fluorine 
and Hydrogen.  
The calculations were done using VASP coding and BEEF-vdW functional. It 
appeared to depict the behavior of the ring atoms adequately as cited in the literature. 
In particularly the distortion of the planar structure of the molecule coming from the 
steric effects of the adsorbates as well as quadrupole moments. Fluorine substituents 
seem to moderate the effect of deformation and reverse the quadrupole moments. 
Similarly, for the adsorbed porphyrins the central metal is elevated when the 
ORR/OER intermediates are bonded while a sinking of the transition metals was 
observed in regard to the pyrrole rings when porphyrins are adsorbed on the metallic 
substrate. The competing axial ligation cause a weakening of the adsorption of the 
intermediates and alter the scaling relationships of adsorption energies. Furthermore, 
porphyrin adsorption calculations were made also for Au (111) that shown a stronger 
adsorption than Ag (111) surface. 
On the other hand, problematic adsorption of OOH on Cr and Cu (which happened in 
the gas phase) improved significantly on the metal. Moreover, the activities of Cr, Mn 
and Fe are profoundly increased and Iron becomes the most suitable for ORR while 
Cobalt remained the best candidate for OER.  
Time barriers were a very important feature of this study that occasionally limited the 
objectives. Computational cost increased significantly from the substrate added in the 
calculations and varied depending on the system, OOH adsorption was generally the 
less consuming on contrary to O and OH adsorption.

1.Introduction 
1.1 Sustainability issues of fossil fuels 
Contemporary energy technologies are thought to be insufficient in terms of 
sustainability by two ways. Firstly, fossil fuel technologies retain a limited time of 
existence due to non-everlasting resources.  While dramatic climate changes are 
mainly attributed to increased CO2 concentration in the atmosphere coming from the 
exhaust of fossil fuel combustion. Consequently, the scientific concerns on the 
environment have been the driving force to a CO2 mitigation by investigating and 
developing technologies that adsorb the incoming power from the sun or take 
advantage of environmental phenomena such as wind, waves etc. However, these 
technologies could be unreliable especially for mobile applications. On the other 
hand, storing renewable energy through Hydrogen, production and storage, which is 
considered a good energy carrier could be a long term solution of renewables 
reliability. Hydrogen, abundantly existing in nature within various chemical 
compounds, more commonly in water, can be obtained easily and could set the basis 
for a total exemption from fossil fuels. 
1.2 Oxygen Reduction Reaction (ORR) & electrocatalysts in Fuel Cells 
and Electrolysers 
Hydrogen technologies have been explicitly researched the past 50 years and the most 
promising have admittedly been found to be the Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel 
Cell (PEMFC). Unfortunately, massive applicability of PEMFC is hindered due to 
high cost and reliability/durability issues. Another parameter that restrict PEMFC use 
is the energy barrier of Oxygen Reduction Reaction (ORR) due to high overpotential, 
whereas diatomic oxygen is protonated to give water and electricity in the cathodic 
reaction. The reaction mechanism studied on Pt electrocatalyst include several 
individual reactions. Mainly these reactions are recognized as the four electron 
pathway and the two electron pathway with intermediate production of Hydrogen 
peroxide (H2O2). Ideally the reaction mechanism is dominated by the four electron 
pathway for a higher efficiency [1].  
1.3 Alternatives in electrocatalysis and Density Functional Theory 
(DFT) 
However as Pt is a rare and expensive metal, inducing the high cost of Fuel Cell 
operation, research have turned to non-noble metals and organometallic complexes as 
implied by the various books and papers published on Fuel Cell advancements with Pt 
still remaining the most efficient [1] [2] [3] . The latter group of compounds exceeds 
the interest due to its flexibility of accepting any transition metal in the central metal 
atom and are explicitly presented by Asraf et al. [4]. Initially, the interest of the 
catalytic activity of porphyrins was drawn when Janhke et al. [5] in 1976 have shown 
that N4 compounds are active towards ORR while Alt et al. [6] in 1973 had already 
proven that N4 activates the oxygen molecule. Several projects since then tried to 
explain the chemistry of these molecules and the influence of their ligands and 
peripheral substituents as presented in the review papers [7][8]. In regard to the 
variations of the porphyrin molecule and the attributing properties of the central 
==PAGE:8==
 
7 
 
transition metal atom, the possible systems under study, could increase significantly 
which prohibit the experimental screening. 
1.4 Theoretical catalytic studies and porphyrins 
Nonetheless, the evolution of reliable theoretical methods for the study of surface 
processes have worked supplementary to experimental research and helped in sorting 
out candidate materials. Such methods like DFT, treat molecules and atoms in a 
quantum mechanical way. Indicative of the advance of theoretical methods is the 
increase in publications using DFT. Scientific fields like surface science and catalysis 
have been in the forefront of DFT advances as various density functionals were 
developed to cover accurately a large spectrum of processes within these fields.  
1.5 Case introduction 
Recent advances in ORR electrocatalysts as spotted by Li et al. [3] on a review refer 
to Pt based electrocatalysts as the forefront materials of ORR investigation, like Pt/C 
or Pt alloys. In the same study are mentioned efforts of developing an electrocatalyst 
with nonprecious metals, mainly carbon based. Despite their good activity and low 
cost these materials suffer from inadequate durability. Porphyrins on the other hand 
have shown stability in acidic environment. The role of their substituents and central 
transition metal in the catalytic activity towards ORR have been explained by Calle-
Vallejo et al. [9], while their adsorption on silver surface by Hieringer et al. [10] on 
top position.  
In this study was intended to investigate, with the use of DFT calculations, the 
ORR/OER activity of porphyrins adsorbed on three different surfaces, Ag (111) Au 
(111) and Cu (111), on three different positions, ontop bridge and hollow, for 
different orientations of the porphyrin molecule (0, 15, 35 and 45 degrees angle) for 
two different peripheral substituents (H, F). However, limited computational 
resources and the strict time schedule of the master thesis have prohibited this 
extended study. Finally, the metal surfaces were confined to Ag (111) and Au (111) 
for top position, 0 degrees and H atoms as peripheral substituents.  Moreover, 
adsorption energies of the intermediates on porphyrins on vacuum were calculated for 
porphyrins with fluorine peripheral substituents as well.  Finally, a full energetic 
analysis was performed for the gas phase porphyrins and adsorbed porphyrins on Ag 
(111).

2.Theory 
2.1 Density Functional Theory (DFT) 
A system that consist of some molecules and atoms is properly described by quantum 
mechanical laws. Hence the theoretical methods that were developed solve the 
electronic structure problem, as presented in the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation.  
𝐻𝛷(𝑅, 𝑟) = 𝐸𝛷(𝑅, 𝑟) (1.1) 
All the information needed are contained within the Hamiltonian which is the critical 
quantity that is needed to be specified in a form that would make the equation 
manageable to solve: 
𝐻= 𝑇𝑛𝑢𝑐𝑙+ 𝑇𝑒𝑙+ 𝑉𝑛𝑢𝑐𝑙−𝑛𝑢𝑐𝑙+ 𝑉𝑛𝑢𝑐𝑙−𝑒𝑙+ 𝑉𝑒𝑙−𝑒𝑙 (1.2) 
where  𝑇𝑛𝑢𝑐𝑙 and 𝑇𝑒𝑙 are the kinetic energy of the nuclei and electrons respectively, 
while 𝑉𝑥 term represents the electrostatic interactions between nuclei and electrons. 
Unfortunately, Schrödinger equation in closed form is not solvable. Thus several steps 
of approximations are necessary in the lowest expense for the accuracy of the results. 
2.1.1 Born-Oppenheimer approximation 
The kinetic energy of electrons and nuclei are represented distinguished by two 
factors as shown in equation (1.2). However as highlighted by the Born-Oppenheimer 
[11] or adiabatic approximation nuclei masses are bigger than the electron’s in the 
order of 104 to 105 and hence the kinetic energy of electrons are higher in the order of 
102 to 103. Practically the electronic Hamiltonian is defined for fixed nuclei 
coordinates and the kinetic operator of nuclei can be completely removed [12].  
2.1.2 Hohenberg-Kohn theorem  
Eventually Hohenberg-Kohn theorem is the stepping stone for the foundation of DFT. 
In this theorem is declared that in presence of external potential the ground state of a 
system of interacting electrons is uniquely defined. Thus electronic density is 
uniquely associated to the external potential and the identification of the true ground 
state energy is coming through the minimization of the Energy functional. 
Eigensolvers work in a self-consistent way, starting with an initial guess. Within this 
functional all quantum mechanical effects are involved by Exchange-Correlation 
functional (An extra term appeared in equation 2 when introducing quantum statistics 
such as Pauli principle).  
2.1.3 Kohn-Sham equations and Local Density Approximation (LDA) 
Now the density is defined as the sum over single-particle states and Kohn-Sham 
(KS) equations are obtained [13] from the minimization of energy functional under 
normalization constraints. XC functional is considered to be an extra term in regard to 
Hartree-Fock approach that gives improved results, hence KS equations could be 
considered as an expansion of Hartree approach. XC energy cannot be defined since 
XC functional cannot be defined. However, XC energy can be calculated for 
homogeneous electron gas, as for constant electron density which has led to the Local 
Density Approximation (LDA) at the same time with DFT foundation. 
==PAGE:10==
 
9 
 
LDA provided exceptionally good results for a wide range of bulks, but failed to 
describe adequately surface processes and gas phase reactions which hindered the 
expansion of DFT to theoretical chemist. The acceptance of DFT became broader 
with the advent of XC functionals to Generalized Gradient Approximation (GGA) 
which is a step closer to chemical accuracy. However, there are drawbacks that still 
affect the reliability of theoretical results. A special case is the van der Waals forces 
that are not accurately defined and are involved in many adsorption processes.  
2.2 Bayesian error estimation functional-van der Waals (Beef-vdW) 
2.2.1 Van der Waals interactions in theoretical catalytic studies 
The accuracy of the electronic and nuclei interactions described by DFT are 
dependent on the exchange correlation functional in relation to the actual system. 
Porphyrins consist of 37 atoms and interact with a metallic substrate and adsorbates 
(O-, OH-, OOH-), thus chemisorptive as well as physisorptive contributions are 
expected within these interactions. The importance of van der Waals (vdW) 
interactions for porphyrin systems was highlighted by Hieringer et.al [10] that 
measured the bonds and adsorption energies of Iron and Cobalt porphyrins on silver 
surface using PBE XC functional with and without vdW correction and found 
energies 5 times higher in the former case. Thus it is vital to describe as accurately as 
possible the interactions within our system so the choice of a suitable Exchange 
Correlation Functional (XC Functional) is very significant [14]. 
2.2.2 Exchange Correlation (XC) functionals cited for catalytic studies  
Allegedly, Jacob Ladder contains the five most popular density functional 
approximations in order of complexity. Gradient generalized approximation (GGA) 
and local density approximation which are the simplest and cost effective methods do 
not take into account van der Waals interactions. More specifically LDA can describe 
adequately the adsorption of planar carbon based molecules on metal surfaces [15] but 
is incompetent in describing the adsorption of =O, -OH and –OOH on porphyrins 
[16], while RPBE performance is good at the adsorption of the intermediates [16] and 
inadequate for porphyrins on metal surfaces [17]. On the other hand, random phase 
approximation (RPA) could describe van der Waals dispersion, but with a much 
higher computational cost.  Wellendorf et al. [18] developed the Bayesian error 
estimation functional with Van der Waals correlation (BEEF-vdw) by using data sets 
that are adequately applicable in various systems including vdW dominated 
interactions which allows the estimation of the computational error with a slight 
burden on the cost in relation to LDA and RPBE.  
2.2.3 BEEF-vdW citation on catalytic studies 
As pronounced in the study of Wellendorf et al. [18] BEEF-vdW was found to have 
relatively small deviations with Data Sets regarding Molecular reaction energies, 
Chemisorption on solid surfaces, Molecular reaction barriers and Solid state 
properties. Furthermore, in BEEF-vdW was tested in two certain applications. Firstly, 
was applied to the graphene adsorption on Ni (111) surface. Generally, graphene 
develops bonds of varying strength. The results shown a good accordance with 
experimentally validated values at the pshysisorbed state, but enhanced large error 
bars at the local minimum where graphene seems to be chemisorbed. Eventually, a

relatively good agreement was found between BEEF-vdW and experimental values of 
CO adsorption on Rh (111). 
2.3 DFT implementation 
Any improvements in DFT are in expense of computational time. However, since 
valence electrons are mainly responsible for the properties we are interested in, core 
electrons could be substituted by pseudopotentials which saves a lot of time. In DFT 
wave functions are represented by plane waves whose number depends on the 
softness of pseudopotentials. The softer the pseudopotential the smaller the number of 
plane waves needed in the expansion of the wave function. Practically the size of the 
plane wave basis set is defined by the parameter of cut off energy. In spite of 
pseudopotentials been applied to save time there are cases where all-electron 
calculations are crucial. This problem was solved with the development of Projector 
Augmented Wave method [19] which combined all-electron calculations and the 
efficiency of ultra-soft pseudopotentials [12]. 
2.4 Oxygen Reduction Reaction (ORR) or Oxygen Evolution Reaction 
(OER) 
Besides the important role of ORR and OER in life processes, their importance is 
expanded to energy technologies such as Fuel Cells and electrolyzer cells since they 
occur at the cathode and the anode respectively. The reaction can follow the four 
electron pathway shown in equation (1.3), which is the more efficient, or the two 
electron pathway, with the intermediate production of hydrogen peroxide, H2O2, 
which could form –OH and –OOH radicals that may damage the electrolyte: 
𝑂2 + 4𝐻+ + 4𝑒−↔𝐻2𝑂, 
𝑉= 1.229 𝑉 (1.3) 
The sluggish ORR reaction is the main loss of energy conversion efficiency in Fuel 
Cells. An effective electrocatalyst is more needed in order to increase the kinetics and 
make this technology viable. So far Pt is considered to be the more efficient. 
However, PT is a scarce metal that could only be found at very high prices. Thus 
Scientists have sought an alternative to other noble or non-noble metals and transition 
metal macrocyclic complexes.

2.4.1 ORR on Macrocyclic Transition metal 
Porphyrin is a square planar molecule (tetrapyrrolic macrocycle) with high symmetry 
(D4h), met also in nature where it takes part in vital biological processes such as 
photosynthesis, oxygen transport and oxygen activation [20]. Metalloporphyrins are 
identical to the parent porphyrin with an addition of a metal atom to the center and are 
==PAGE:12==
 
11 
 
studied for field responsive application due to their crystalline structure and chemo 
responsive applications due to its reactivity (chemo responsive) as for example ORR. 
R. Jasinski [21] was the first to provide a valuable insight in the catalytic value of 
pthalocyanines, molecules which resemble heme group structurally. While Jahnke et 
al. [5] have shown the significance of four Nitrogen atoms ring and their interaction 
with the central metal atom in catalyzing ORR. Furthermore Alt et al. [6] in his 
studies revealed the advantages of porphyrins based on their conjugated π-electron 
system, which is known for the activation of the oxygen molecule [22]. 
More recent studies conducted on iron-porphyrins and iron or cocalt-substituted 
phthalocyanine by Shi and Zhang were done with the help of DFT. The result of these 
studies elucidated the two-electron pathway of ORR, which is dependent on the 
dioxygen formed with Cobalt systems. In general, it was illustrated that ORR activity 
is related to ionization potential (IP) as well as the di-oxygen binding ability which 
are clearly influenced by the metal atom. The catalytic activity is favored by the IP 
while is decreased with the di-oxygen binding ability [23].

3.1 Porphyrin relaxation 
A very important feature examined in porphyrin relaxation was the magnetic 
moments of the Transition metals. For these calculations we used 6 transition metals 
from the first row of the periodic table. More specifically Chromium (Cr 24), 
Manganese (Mn 25), Iron (Fe 26), Cobalt (Co 27), Nickel (Ni 28) and Copper (Cu 29) 
where the number in each parenthesis stands for the atomic number of each element. 
However, transition metals are studied in chemical compounds where are in oxidized 
state and not in neutral state. Dealing with three different oxidation states –in clean 
porphyrins +2, binding with OH and OOH +3 and with O +4- have complicated the 
identification of magnetic moments of the transition metals. Thus the electronic 
configuration of these elements is identical until 3d orbital: 1s2 2s2 2p6 3s2 3p6 3dx 
since 4s electrons are detached in the oxidized state. 
 
Figure 3-2: Possible spin state analysis for Copper in three different oxidation states  
Electron configuration in orbitals follow Hund’s rule which predict individual 
occupation with electrons of parallel spin for degenerate systems. However, d orbitals 
that are occupied first seem to obtain a lower energetic level in regard to the other d 
orbitals as shown in figure 3-2 and different configurations are possible. Thus a 
variation from Hund’s rule might be observed in transition metals. In this part of the 
study the stability of the transition metals in regard to different magnetic moments is 
tested. 
Initially the calculations were meant to be performed with the GPAW coding [28]. 
However various convergence problems have led to dismiss of GPAW and 
replacement by VASP [29]. Finally, porphyrins were relaxed with BEEF-vdW XC 
functional with periodic boundary conditions and in a 22 Å x 22 Å x 5 Å cell. The 
break condition for the self-consistency loop of the relaxation for the electronic 
degrees of freedom was set at 10-6 eV with maximum of 1000 steps. The cutoff 
energy for the plane waves was 400 eV, Gamma-point of Brillouin zone sampling and 
real space projection with normal precision which is recommended by VASP manual 
for more than 20 Atoms simulations. Moreover, Gaussian smearing (ismear=0) with 
sigma= 0.001 width was used for the partial occupancies for each wave function. 
==PAGE:14==
 
13 
 
Pulay mixer was used while a conjugate gradient algorithm was adopted to relax the 
ions into their instantaneous ground state. The applied break condition for the ionic 
relaxation loop was -0.01 eV forces/atom with 480 maximum steps and prohibited 
cell shape and volume relaxation.

5.Discussion 
5.1 Gas phase porphyrins 
5.1.1 Fluorine effect 
DiMagno et al. [7] in their review on fluorinated porphyrins presented the effects of 
Fluorine on the structure, electronic properties and electrocatalytic potential of 
porphyrins. As a matter of fact, Fluorine is the smallest atom that can be used as a 
substituent instead of Hydrogen while is the most electronegative which results in 
moderating the steric effects caused from the ORR/OER intermediates adsorption.  
Accordingly, fluorination is used to change the electronic properties and solubility of 
the tetrapyrrole ring and restrain oxidative decomposition. Furthermore, Fluorination 
of porphyrins results in electron density removal from σ system and electron density 
donation to π system. Overall, Fluorine is removing electrons from the macrocycle 
and contains a negative charge without imposing deformation. In fact, for oxygen 
adsorption on Chromium and Nickel hydroxyl, adsorption on Iron and peroxide 
adsorption on Chromium (closer to oxygen adsorption), on Iron, on Cobalt and 
Copper the resulting deformation was restrained. However, in the rest cases Hydrogen 
and Fluorine behaved similarly.  
Another point of their work was that Fluorine limits the basicity of Nitrogen and the 
strength of the ring’s field that lets the central metal to higher spin states which was 
verified by various studies on Cobalt porphyrins [31] [32], however this did not occur 
in the present study. Actually among the 48 different calculations for the gas phase 
porphyrins there were only two major variations of the magnetic moments. 
Manganese in the case of oxygen adsorption switched from 1 to 3 for Hydrogen 
peripheral substituents while Iron for hydroxyl adsorption have changed from 3 to 5. 
Furthermore, it is mentioned that Fluorine substituents result in lower overpotential 
for oxygen reduction reaction as reported from [33], [34] and [35]. However, no such 
thing can be confirmed in this study. Metalloporphyrins with Fluorine and Hydrogen 
seem to behave similarly in regard to their thermodynamics towards ORR/OER. An 
individual study of fluorinated porphyrins and porphyrins with Hydrogen substituents 
the former exhibited a slightly higher overpotential, by 0.02 eV which could be 
considered practically the equal while the catalytic activities of Mn, Fe and Cr was 
improved for their fluorinated complexes.  
5.1.2 Deformation of tetrapyrrolic ring 
Through this study is made obvious that the adsorption of the ORR/OER 
intermediates is significantly altering the planar structure of the porphyrin. The 
pyrroles of y axis behave similarly, as the pyrroles on the x axis. This could be 
attributed to strong quadrupole moments that may occur during the adsorption. 
Generally, the adsorbates have a negative charge as the ring, thus the quarters with a 
more negative charge should be repulsed further and occupy a lower position in 
regard to the quarters with a more positive charge. Finally, it was observed that for 
Fluorine peripheral substituents y axis was usually occupying a lower position while 
for Hydrogen we had the contrary effect. DiMagno in his review among the other

reversed [36].

analysis that should be taken into account. Firstly, for Chromium the oxygen 
adsorption is so strong that OOH is decomposed to adsorbed Oxygen and OH which 
could result in poisoning of the active site. However, with Fluorine substituents there 
is a slight improvement. Generally, metals coming from groups that have similar OH 
and O adsorption should be considered outcasts due to the instability caused to the 
OOH adsorbate. On the other hand, metals that loosely bind with oxygen may result 
in a loosely binding of OOH, as shown in figure 4-4c with Copper that has very weak 
Oxygen adsorption energy along with low hydroxyl adsorption energy. Impressively 
enough fluorinated Copper porphyrins adsorbed OOH in a better way despite the fact 
that their adsorption energies were similar. Generally, a balanced strength of metal 
and oxygen binding along with O-O bonding is the most preferred as in the case of 
Nickel, Iron, Cobalt and Manganese which happen to be the most active. Finally, it 
can be said that Fluorine could lead to vast modification of the electronic properties of 
the porphyrins. Combining the information of the activities and the quadrupole 
moments occurred it is observed that porphyrins that do not show this behavior tend 
to enhance better activities. 
5.2 Adsorbed porphyrins 
5.2.1 Adsorption of metalporphyrins on Ag (111) and Au (111)  
Porphyrin molecules are adsorbed in parallel to the substrate and a covalent bond is 
engaged among the central metal and the metal atom below. Furthermore 
chemisorptive or physisorptive contribution of the carbon and nitrogen atoms 
contribute further to the strength of adsorption which was merely verified by the 
Nitrogen atoms behavior. In particularly nitrogen atoms on the x axis appear to 
occupy an on top nearly bridge position on atoms 2 and 5 while Nitrogen atoms on y 
axis seem to occupy a 3-coordinated position between atoms 1, 2 and 4 and 1, 7 and 6 
respectively, figure 4-9. Thus an interaction between the ring and the substrate 
appears when taking into account that the favored position of adsorbates is usually the 
3-coordinated and the difference in z positions between them. The deformation of the 
porphyrin molecule provide further indications of Van der Waals interaction between 
the substrate and the ring that cannot be captured by standard DFT, thus BEEF with 
van der Waals correction have been efficiently depicting structural deformations[8]. 
However, the strength of adsorption is basically related to the central metal’s 
electronic structure. The 3 d orbital splitting was shown in figure 3-2, the two orbitals 
(xy, x2-y2) are within the plane of the molecule (square planar), while the other three 
are extended out of this plane. Metals that have these three orbitals partially occupied 
==PAGE:33==
 
32 
 
or unoccupied tend to make stronger bonds with the metallic substrate as mentioned 
by Gotfried. This affects mainly Nickel (II) d8 and Copper (II) d9 that bind weakly 
while Cobalt (II) d7, Iron (II) d6, Manganese (II) d5 and Chromium (II) d4 seem to 
create more covalent bond. Spin state analysis have shown a variation from the initial 
guesses which can be attributed to a charge transfer from the substrate to the transition 
metal [8]. 
Finally, when comparing adsorption energies on Ag (111) and Au (111) there is a 
constant distinct difference between the two for the same transition metals. In fact, 
gold have a higher work function which give the ability to bind stronger than silver.  
In other theoretical studies Hieringer [10] measured the binding energy as well as the 
bond length of Cobalt and Iron porphyrins on top of Ag (111) substrate with and 
without van der Walls interactions using the PBE functional.  The findings have 
shown that Van der Waals interaction play an important role in the total binding 
energy. Moreover, the bond lengths when taking into account vdW correction were 
found to be 2.83 Å for Cobalt and 2.88 Å for Iron while the Ag atom elevation was 
found to be 0.3 Å for both cases. Hieringer et.al classified this length according to his 
table as covalent. In our study Fe-Ag length is 2.78 Å and Co-Ag is 2.76 Å while the 
Ag atom elevation is 0.08 Å and 0.12 Å respectively. Supposing on electron transfer 
the measured binding energies from their study correspond to 2.529 eV for Iron and 
2.85 eV for Cobalt. Generally, the bond lengths and the elevation of Ag atom vary 
significantly while the adsorption energies are incomparable due to computational 
alterations of the systems.  
Similar theoretical calculations can be found for molecules with similar structures like 
Pthalocyanines but are neglected since they are out of the scope of this study. If there 
would be a broader investigation of alternative structures of porphyrins, then it could 
be beneficial. However, the main focus in this study is on the effects of fluorination of 
the ring and the effect of adsorption of porphyrins on a metallic substrate and how 
their activities are influenced.

5.3 Computational overview 
Van der Waals correction addition and BEEF functional seem to depict the behavior 
of porphyrins quite adequately according to literature citations, in regard to the 
deformation of the molecular square planar conformation like the reversed quadrupole 
moment coming from over fluorination. However, the variation of adsorption energies 
with the values in [9] [30], intrigue the question whether BEEF or RPBE is better in 
describing these sytems. RPBE is acknowledged for the good description of the 
adsorbates on the central metal [17], on contrary to BEEF-vdW that was not cited for 
a relevant study. In general it appears to give similar reaction energies [17] to RPBE 
and slightly overestimated. Finally taking into account the computational resources 
used, it could be said that the calculations with a silver substrate were quite expensive, 
with *OH being the most expensive. Calculations with a gold substrate increased the 
computational cost significantly and were not ready on time which was the reason that 
were not presented in the study.

6.Conclusions 
The purpose of this study was to investigate the catalytic activities of several 
metalporphyrins containing transition metals from the first row with 3d electronic 
structure. The scaling relationships of gas phase with hydrogen and Fluorine 
substituents and adsorbed porphyrins were found as well as the intrinsic overpotential 
that limits the development of these novel electrocatalysts. Furthermore, a volcano 
plot was created in each case in order to evaluate the activities of the porphyrins 
towards ORR/OER. For this purpose, the VASP coding was used with the BEEF-
vdW functional to calculate the total DFT energies of the systems and the zero 
phonon energies as well as the entropic contributions were taken from a similar study 
of Vallejo et.al [30]. 
The morphology of the molecules was studied in terms of the atoms’ z positions. 
Seemingly the planar structure of the molecules was distorted when adsorbates were 
placed on top of the metal which occupied a higher position than the ring. On the 
other hand, Fluorine substituents appeared to reduce steric effects coming from the 
adsorption of intermediates while quadrupole moments that exist in the porphyrin ring 
had been reversed with the presence of over fluorination. Adsorbed porphyrins on Ag 
(111) have shown weaker adsorption than Au (111) and behaved similarly with the 
presence of the adsorbates while the trans ligation weakened the adsorption strength 
of the ORR/OER intermediate as expected. Finally, a sinking of the transition metal 
was observed when the porphyrins were clean while the interaction of the ring with 
the substrate was strongly evidenced when Nitrogen atoms in 3-coordinated sites 
occupied lower z positions than Nitrogen atoms that were on top nearly bridge 
positions.   
Furthermore, adsorption energies of gas phase porphyrins are in disagreement with 
literature values causing intriguing discussion whether BEEF or RPBE could be more 
accurate. Generally adsorbed porphyrins on Ag (111) enhanced an increased 
overpotential. Furthermore, a vast increase of OH adsorption has caused an alteration 
of the limiting steps of ORR and OER. However, adsorption energies of adsorbed 
porphyrins fit well with the linear regression of the gas phase porphyrins except for 
Chromium and Manganese that deviate a lot. Overall catalytic activities for ORR have 
shown vast increase for Cr, Mn and Fe while there has been a significant decrease for 
Co. Moreover, Spin state analysis have shown negative charge transfer from the 
substrate to the transition metals that might be correlated with the weaker adsorption 
energies of the intermediates.  
To sum up, the energy analysis of adsorbed porphyrins has shown interesting 
variations from the gas porphyrins. Firstly, an energy gap in adsorption energies that 
classified the elements into two groups, 6-9 and 10-11 have changed to 6-8 and 9-11. 
As a result, Cobalt’s activity has significantly fallen while Iron and Manganese took 
up instead. Scaling relations seem to give a good overview of the catalytic trends for a 
series of elements with similar electronic structure.

7.Future work 
The lack of citations on ORR/OER activities of adsorbed porphyrins on metallic 
substrates have led to the conclusion that more work is needed to be done in two 
directions: 
1. Extended research of substituents and their position: Vallejo et.al have 
reported on their recent the influence of various substituents of the porphyrin 
ring like H, F, OH, CH3, NH2 and BH2 showing occasionally a breaking of the 
scaling relations which would be beneficial for lowering the overpotential. 
However, the substrate-porphyrin-adsorbate systems were not examined. 
Moreover, over-fluorinated porphyrin rings exhibited sporadic reversed 
quadrupole moments. Thus, limited fluorination in specific sites could be 
examined like meso-substituted (on the carbons that link the pyrrole rings).    
2. Further investigation on adsorption sites of porphyrins and metallic 
substrates: Iniatilly the scope of this study included an adsorption of 
porphyrins and analysis of ORR/OER on Au (111) and Cu (111) apart from 
Ag (111) surface. Furthermore, it was intended to test porphyrin adsorption on 
three different sites, ontop bridge and hollow position, and with 4 different 
angles, 0°, 15°, 30° and 45°. Imperatively the restriction of the objectives to a 
more realistic extension have been made as a necessity for a complete study 
on at least one surface, one site and one angle. However, a broader view on 
these three features could enlighten some aspects on the transition metal 
behavior.
