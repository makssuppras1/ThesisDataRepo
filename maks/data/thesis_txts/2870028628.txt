for conventional piezoceramics e.g. lead zirconate titanate (PZT) as an electromechanical
actuator. Because of its strong electrostriction with a longitudinal electrostriction coeffi-
cient of |M33| > 10âˆ’16, Furthermore, being lead-free and compatible with polymers. The

sis, the experiments were constructed in the finite element methods (FEM) tool COMSOL
and simulated. Through these simulations the transverse electrostriction coeffictient of
CGO were also found to have a value of |M31| â‰¤6.6 أ— 10âˆ’21 5 times lower than of |M33|.

of YSZ, is 2 times smaller than CGO and |M31| for 5CaDC is 50 times larger than that of
CGO.
The modeling in FEM of the electrostriction material through the stress tensor has

Chapter 1
Introduction
1.1
Micropumps for insulin delivery systems
The motivation in this research field of micropumps for insulin delivery systems is to
help people suffering from diabetes. Diabetes is a chronic disease that affects the bodyâ€™s
ability to regulate blood sugar levels. It occurs when the body does not produce enough
insulin or does not use the insulin effectively. Without sufficient insulin or with insulin
resistance, glucose accumulates in the blood, which can lead to high blood sugar levels
(hyperglycemia). If diabetes is not properly treated or controlled, it can cause serious com-
plications such as heart disease, kidney failure, nerve problems, vision loss, and foot prob-
lems. Treatment of diabetes such as insulin pen, insulin pumps, and inhalers is effective
and works, but it is still being worked on to make insulin infusion more convenient.[1, 2]
A recently developed type of insulin pump is the artificial pancreas that automatically
regulates blood sugar levels using a combination of a glucose meter (monitoring blood
glucose continuously).[3] It helps improve blood sugar control and reduces the need for
manual insulin adjustments. However, irritation associated with wearing an insulin pump
is not insignificant, and in a 2020 survey 53% of type 1 Danish patients with diabetes still
reported taking injections.[4] This raised the question of how small a pump capable of de-
livering insulin could be. In this regards we need to understand the materials, responsible
for making the pump expand and retract.
1.2
CGO â€‌giantâ€‌ electrostrictor
Piezoelectricity is the most common electromechanical coupling of materials, because of
its built-in polarization, but only a few materials are piezoelectric. In contrast all mate-
rials have electrostriction, but the electrostrictive effect is usually too weak to be useful.
Piezoelectricity mechanical response (strain or stress) tendency in materials is linear in-
duced by an electric field or polarization, electrostriction mechanical response is quadratic
in electric field or polarization.
However, in 2012, gadolinium-doped ceria (CGO), GdxCe1âˆ’xO2âˆ’y, was discovered
to have an â€‌exceptionally largeâ€‌ electrostriction coefficient, an effect known as â€‌giantâ€‌

There are several beneficial reasons why

In this chapter, we present the theoretical framework for electrostriction essential to finite-
element simulations. This approach builds on our previous work on piezoelectric actuation
in microfluidic systems, formulated as a second-order perturbation theory of continuum
fields.

3.2
Validation of results
The numerical results obtained in this MSc thesis are substantiated by comparison between
different methods. The material properties of CGO are calculated through the simulated
displacement fields validated against experiments and analytic solutions. However, when
conducting numerical modeling, it is essential to verify whether the computations pro-
duce reliable results or merely numerical noise. If a physical system depends on precise
values and gradients of u within very small regions, the numerical discretization must be
sufficiently fine to ensure an accurate representation of these regions. A fundamental veri-
fication step in numerical modeling is performing a mesh convergence study. This involves
systematically refining the mesh several times. The simulation is then repeated for each
refinement level to determine whether further mesh refinement leads to significant changes
in the results, thereby confirming that the chosen mesh resolution is sufficient for accurate
modeling. Due to the extensive manual optimization involved in designing these meshes,
they are predominantly user-defined rather than automatically generated. As a result, im-
plementing an automated refinement process becomes very complex. Therefore, we opted
for making a mesh scaling, e.g. two meshes, one roughly twice more resolution than the
other. As an example, we take the simplest geometry (2D in-plane cantilever) made in
this thesis Fig. 3.2. In Fig. 3.2 is illustrated two meshes, one roughly twice resolution
of the other. In the simulation, the course mesh solved for 338 042 degrees of freedom,
while the refined mesh solved for 1 053 992 degrees of freedom. The resulting mechanical
displacement response at the end of the cantilever, for the refined and course meshes are,
respectively, 0.155753 nm and 0.155760 nm. From this, it is seen that the refinement of
the mesh has a minimal impact on the simulated response.

Summary and discussion
The electrostriction coefficients M31 and M33, at low frequencies, f < 10 was found
to be (nearly) constant values. For 10GdDC M31 = âˆ’6.6 أ— 10âˆ’21 m2 Vâˆ’2 and M33 =
âˆ’5.3 أ— 10âˆ’16 m2 Vâˆ’2, where M31 is 5 orders of magnitude smaller than M33, whereas
for 5CaDC M31 = آ±2.3 أ— 10âˆ’19 m2 Vâˆ’2, a value 50 times larger than that in 10GdDC.
The value of M31 for 5CaDC was so large that it dominated over the contribution from
M33 which therefore cannot be determined here (Fig. 5.6).
This notable difference in
the response for the two cantilevers is due to the fact that M31 acts over the unclamped
electrode length Lelec, whereas M33, only acts via the fringe fields over the length Htf
given by the film thickness[10]. This behavior is also seen in the stress distribution shown
in Fig. 5.10, which illustrates the in-plane stress. The stress is approximately 105 times
higher at the fringe fields over Htf, where the M33 present and dominates, compared to
the surface of the electrode, where the M31 is present.
The relative strengths of the transverse and longitudinal response, respectively, at the
interfaces of the upper electrode-electrostriction layer and the edges (see Fig. 5.10) can be
given by the ratio (M31Lelec)/(M33Htf), which for 10GdDC is 0.76 and for 5CaDC is about
30, assuming the typical value M33 = âˆ’3 أ— 10âˆ’16 m2 Vâˆ’2. Consequently, for 10GdDC,
the overall response results from the elastic, the electrostrictive M31-M33 stress, and the
Maxwell stress, but for 5CaDC it results mainly from the elastic, the electrostrictive stress
M31 and the Maxwell stress. The Maxwell stress tensor, often overlooked, plays a critical
role in the electromechanical behavior, particularly when electrode separation is on the
nanoscale, as in the out-of-plane configuration[30]. Moreover, the results reveal that for
giant electrostrictors based on ceria, the coefficient M31 is several orders of magnitude
smaller than M33, often making it negligible depending on the geometry, a behavior that
contrasts with traditional piezoceramic actuators actuators[31].
In the simulations for 10GdDC, it is shown that the Maxwell stress dominates over

Chapter 7
Conclusion and outlook
In this thesis, we shown the importance at low frequency to include the purely electric
stress in the form of Maxwellâ€™s stress tensor, when simulating electrostriction. We exam-
ined three electrostriction experiments that involve cantilevers. Several interesting results
were obtained with respect to the properties of CGO, providing valuable insights into its
behavior. Next, we delved into theoretical plates analysis of the cantilever experiments,
setting up theoretical models, in which the properties of CGO, longitudinal and transverse
electrostriction tensor coefficients, could be found without numerical computation.
7.1
CGO and other electrostriction
The simulated out-of-plane cantilever indicates for 10GdDC, that the transverse and lon-
gitudinal response both play a role in the overall response result of the cantilever, due to
the geometry, hence the length scale acting over the respective film thickness Htf and the
unclamped electrode length Lelec, even though M31 is 5 orders of magnitude smaller than
M33 around âˆ¼10âˆ’16 magnitude. However, for 5CaDC, where M31 is 50 times larger than
in 10GdDC, thus dominating the contribution of M33, making it unmeasurable. The over-
all response of the 10GdDC is the results from the elastic, the electrostrictive M31-M33,
and Maxwell stress, which dominates over electrostriction stress at low requencies (f < 10
Hz) and captures several key features of the measured deflection spectrum. The overall
response of 5CaDC is mainly from elastic, electrostrictive stress M31 and Maxwell stress.
The Maxwell stress tensor, often overlooked, plays a critical role in electromechanical be-
havior, especially when electrode separation is on the nanoscale, as in the out-of-plane
configuration.
The simulated in-plane cantilever configuration indicate, that the overall response re-
sults from the elastic, the electrostrictive M33 stress. The Maxwell stress and electrostric-
tive M31 response is so small that they are unmeasurable and negligible, compared to
the response from M33. For 10GdDC, M33 was found around the same order magnitude
âˆ¼10âˆ’16 as for the out-plane cantilever, given that 10GdDC produces the same deflection
profile as YSZ. Whereas for YSZ, M33 is twice as small as for 10GdDC. The in-plane can-
tilever configuration compared to the out-of-plane also has a low overall response around
