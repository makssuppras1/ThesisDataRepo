Abstract
The problem of controllability and observability of linear systems of ordinary differential equations (ODE) are intro-
duced. Equivalence between Kalman conditions, controllability of linear systems of ODE and observability of linear
systems of ODE is proven. Through this proof a constructive method for п¬Ғnding a solution to the problem of control-
lability on linear system of ODE is made and an upper bound on this control is derived. The problem of controllability
of the linear wave equation is accounted for and existence and uniqueness to the linear wave equation problem is
established. Three different types controllability are introduced and, in particular, null- and exact-controllability are
analyzed by application of Hilbert Uniqueness Method (HUM). The special case of the 1-d linear wave equation prob-
lem is considered and a method of п¬Ғnding controls solving this problem is realized. Different aspects of the solution
determined through this method are investigated numerically.
The results from numerical investigation is consistent with the derived theory, with the exception that there is an
indication that one can require less regular I.C. than п¬Ғrst expected.

Introduction
This project concerns the controllability and the observability of systems based of linear partial differential equations
(PDE), and in particular of systems involving the linear wave equation. The study and investigation are principally
inspired by the developments in [1] and [2]. Both these works, while highly useful, omit certain details in their
derivations, and this project expends a lot of effort towards a careful derivation of the theory. In addition, this work
proceeds with new theory:
вҖў In connection with the proof of Theorem 2.3, a detailed expression of the constant CвҖІ involved in the control
bound (27) is derived, section 2.2
вҖў An extension to Theorem 0.1 in [3] is introduced through Theorem 4.1 with weaker regularity requirement of
the I.C., section 4
вҖў In connection with proof of Theorem 4.1 an upper bound on the discontinuity of the solution is derived, section
4
Take notice that in this project all spaces are assumed real.
The problem of controllability of PDE revolves in whether a system can be driven to a certain state by only controlling
the system over a boundary or over subdomain in which the dynamics evolve. That is, given a time interval 0 < t < T,
initial and п¬Ғnal state, then the goal is to drive the system from the initial state to the п¬Ғnal state at time T.
Section 2 is a preliminary on п¬Ғnite-dimensional systems. PDE can be considered as inп¬Ғnite-dimensional linear systems
of ODE, and ODE construct п¬Ғnite-dimensional dynamical system whereas PDE generate the inп¬Ғnite-dimensional
ones. Given the fact that PDE corresponds to inп¬Ғnite-dimensional version of п¬Ғnite-dimensional ODE can be useful in
different contexts. In the case of mechanics, PDE are often used in relation to continuum mechanics, whereas ODE
arise in classical mechanics. In numerical analysis, numerical approximation schemes for PDE and those that are
semi-discrete, i.e., discrete in space and continuous in time, yield п¬Ғnite-dimensional systems of ODE. This is relevant
in relation to control theory, where, when passing from п¬Ғnite to inп¬Ғnite dimensions unexpected and unwanted artifacts
appear [1]. Therefore it is convenient to п¬Ғrst consider problems in the п¬Ғnite-dimensional context. This will be useful
when dealing with singular limits from п¬Ғnite to inп¬Ғnite-dimensional systems, in particular, addressing the issues
regarding numerical approximation. Furthermore, it will also be useful to understand the problems and techniques
which will be used in context of PDE [1]. In section 2.1 the problem of controllability of linear systems of ODE is
introduced, and controllability of linear systems of ODE and the Kalman conditions are shown to be equivalent. In
section 2.2 the problem of observability of linear systems of ODE is analyzed, and it is shown that controllability
and observability are equivalent. Furthermore, a constructive method for п¬Ғnding a control that solves the problem of
controllability and an upper bound of this control is introduced.

Section 3 revolves around the problem of boundary controllability of the linear wave equation. In section 3.1, the prob-
lem of boundary controllability of the linear wave equation is introduced and involves theorems securing existence and
uniqueness of the linear wave equation problem with Dirichlet boundary controls. The different types of controllabil-
ity are introduced: exact controllability, approximate controllability and null controllability, and it is shown that for
the linear wave equation, exact and null controllability are equivalent. In section 3.2, the problem of exact boundary
controllability will be analyzed by application of the HUM.
In section 4, the problem of boundary controllability of the 1-d linear wave equation will be analyzed. Furthermore, a
method for п¬Ғnding controls that drive 1-d linear wave equation problems to rest is introduced. The method is consistent
with theory derived in section 3. In the case of discontinuous solutions of the 1-d linear wave equation with controls
determined with the method mentioned above an upper bound of the discontinuity will be derived.
Section 5 contains a numerical analysis of the method that was derived section 4. The numerical analysis consists of
multiple test problems illustrating different aspects of the solution, and includes both observations and explanations.
Section 6 is a discussion of the most interesting observations and section 6.1 highlights points worthy of future work.

Discussion
I agreed with the thesis supervisor for this to be a mainly theoretical project. In other words, the goal was to have
a precise description and treatment of the theory. This resulted in thorough derivations and proofs throughout the
thesis. Initially it was intended to touch upon controllability and observability of the linear wave equation as well as
other PDE - in particular, non time-reversible PDE such as the heat equation. Though, it was clear in the early part
of the project that certain topics, such as equivalence between controllability and observability, and HUM, required
more work. With the amount of effort that was put into details of the theory it was decided that the project should be
limited to only analyze the problem of controllability and observability of linear wave equation in context of boundary
controls.
In the following the most interesting observations from both the theoretical and numerical analysis are outlined. The
different observations will be explained and discussed. In section 6.1 there will be suggested future work that could
expand the project.
In case of the п¬Ғnite dimensional settings, it was shown that controllability of linear systems of ODE and Kalman con-
ditions are equivalent, i.e., one has to only investigate the Kalman conditions to know if (1) is controllable. Likewise,
it was proven, through the observability property (18), that controllability and observability of linear systems of ODE
are equivalent. Within this proof a weakly lower semicontinuous functional J was constructed, that, in the case of
(1) being controllable, was radially unbounded. Hence J has a critical point ЛҶПҲ1. By the deп¬Ғnition of J, the critical
point and п¬Ғnal conditions for (15), ЛҶПҲ1, could be used to construct a control, u, that would drive (1) to x1. The control,
u, determined this way, is written in terms of ЛҶПҲ1 and Btr. Since ЛҶПҲ1 is the critical point of J, and J is deп¬Ғned by
ОҰ, x0, x1 and B, then the control u depends on the state space matrices, initial conditions and п¬Ғnal conditions. The
observability property (18) gave rise to an equivalent property (25) that could be used to deп¬Ғne an upper bound of the
control, u, (27). The upper bound (27) depends on the dynamics of the system, ОҰ, the initial conditions, x0, and the
п¬Ғnal condition, x1.
Continuing to the problem of controllability of the linear wave equation, in section 3 existence and uniqueness of
solution to linear wave equation problem, (28), was secured through Theorem 3.1 and 3.2. Both Theorem 3.1 and 3.2
indicated that the regularity of the I.C. and B.C. have an inп¬Ӯuence on the regularity of the solution and, in particular,
the regularity on the solutionвҖҷs outer normal derivative. This observation is important in connection with how the ПҶ-
and ПҲ-system are deп¬Ғned such that there is a close relation between the ПҲ-system and (28). By Theorem 3.3 it was
deduced that given any set of I.C. (x0, Лңx0) вҲҲHвҲ’1(в„Ұ) Г— L2(в„Ұ), it is possible п¬Ғnd a control u вҲҲL2(ОЈ0) that drives
(28) to rest at п¬Ғnal time T вүҘ2R(z0), i.e., the (28) is null controllable at time 2R(z0). The linear wave equation was
shown to be time-reversible which was used to show the equivalence between null and exact controllability of (28) at
2R(z0).

7
Conclusion
It was shown that the problem of controllability of linear systems of ODE, the Kalman conditions and the problem of
observability of linear systems of ODE are equivalent. The equivalence between the problem of controllability and
observability of linear systems of ODE was shown through the observability property and was then used to construct
a solution to the problem of controllability and to п¬Ғnd an upper bound on this control. A new detailed expression of
the constant involved in this upper bound was derived.
The existence and uniqueness of solution of the linear wave equation problem was established and the problem of
controllability of the linear wave with boundary controls was introduced. The different types of controllability: вҖқap-
proximate controllabilityвҖқ, вҖқexact controllabilityвҖқ and вҖқnull controllabilityвҖқ were accounted for and the equivalence of
exact and null controllability of the linear wave equation was shown. By the application of HUM the exact and null
controllability of the linear wave equation were analyzed.
In case of 1-d setting, a method of п¬Ғnding controls to solve the problem of controllability of the linear wave equation
was found and new theory was established that would require weaker regularity on the I.C.. In case of non-continuous
solutions, an upper bound of the discontinuity was derived. Numerical tests of the solutions with boundary controls
found with the above mentioned method were conducted. The results of most of the numerical experiments were
consistent with the derived theory, with an exception of the requirements of the initial conditionsвҖҷ regularity.
