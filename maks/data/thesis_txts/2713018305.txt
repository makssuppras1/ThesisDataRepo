Abstract
ENGLISH
There are millions of people suffering from diabetes around the world. People who are
self-aware about their health use various methods to maintain their blood glucose. Among
these methods, a mobile application can be very useful as a mobile phone is present with
Persons with Diabetes(PwD) most of the time. This study aims to determine how an appli-
cation can help a person with diabetes to track the amounts of carbohydrates in a given meal.
Specifically, this thesis focuses on newly diagnosed PwD with type-2 diabetes and provides
them with carbohydrates present on each ingredient.
This thesis study is carried out in collaboration with the Denmark Diabetes Association,
which provided the project with insights to make the application better. An agile framework
of various iterations was used to develop an application for five months. The design of the
application was carried out in the online tool Figma and development was done using Flutter.
The feedback from test users was taken in each iteration and changes were made accordingly
to make a more user-focused application. The result showed that the application solves the
problem of calculating carbohydrates, and a PwD could use it to track the carbohydrates level
in a meal.
The result suggests that a person with diabetes can use the application to get the carbo-
hydrates in a meal and learn from it in the future. On this basis, the application can be one
tool for a person with diabetes to manage their blood glucose and to learn from previously
logged meals.

Introduction
According to International Diabetes Federation (IDF), approximately 537 million people
are living with diabetes and it is projected to rise to 637 million by 2030.[1] The American
Diabetes Association (ADA) defines diabetes as a set of disorders characterized by high
blood glucose levels. Diabetes is caused by the body’s inability to make or utilize insulin
appropriately[2]. Insulin is a hormone that functions as a key to help glucose to enter cells
from the diet. Glucose accumulates in the bloodstream if there is not enough insulin. Too
much sugar in the blood has the consequence of affecting various organs in the body over
time, including the kidney, eye, nerves, and cardiovascular system. There are two types of
diabetes, which are:
1. Type 1 diabetes: Type 1 diabetes is an auto-immune disorder in which the pancreas’
beta cells, which usually release insulin, are destroyed. As a result, a person with type
1 diabetes must inject insulin numerous times per day. Type 1 diabetes accounts for
5-10% of people with diabetes.
2. Type 2 diabetes: Type 2 diabetes is the most frequent type of diabetes. It is responsible
for 90-95% of all diabetes cases. When the body does not produce enough insulin or
does not utilize it properly, type 2 diabetes develops. The cornerstone of type 2 diabetes
management is a healthy diet, increased physical activity, and maintaining a healthy
body weight.[3]
In comparison to type 1 diabetes, type 2 diabetes is more prevalent. Type 1 diabetes
is inherited, whereas type 2 diabetes is caused due to poor eating habits, being overweight,
lack of exercising, etc. In contrast to type 1 diabetes, which can only be controlled by taking
insulin, type 2 diabetes can be controlled in more ways than type 1. It includes a balanced
diet, physical activity, and medicine[4]. However, in some circumstances, type 2 diabetics
person may also need to take insulin. This thesis will focus on type 2 diabetes as type 2 is
more common and there are more techniques to manage it.

1.1
Background
1.1
Background
As of January 1, 2018, at least 280,000 people in Denmark had been diagnosed with
diabetes. This corresponds to 4.9 percent of the total population.[5] Type 2 Diabetes Mel-
litus(T2DM) is a progressive disease condition in the which function of beta cells(cells that
regulate insulin) declines as the disease progresses, leading to increased circulation of blood
glucose levels. If T2DM is not managed properly, it can lead to a variety of health prob-
lems, such as cardiovascular disease, diabetic neuropathy, diabetic retinopathy, and diabetic
nephropathy.[6] T2DM onset can be prevented and/or slowed in certain individuals with
proper management, and remission can be achieved.
T2DM has no known origin, how-
ever, it is thought to be connected to lifestyle factors such as obesity, lack of exercise, and
heredity(abnormal genes) that prohibit cells from functioning properly.
Most of the time persons with T2DM are left on their own, with little or no contact
with general practitioners. It is due to limited time and high clinic volumes that limit the
attention that can be given to people with T2DM by general practitioners.[7] As a result of
this, people with T2DM have to rely on self-management of diabetes to reduce or stop the
progression of T2DM.
Diabetes self-management(DSM) is a key to prevent the progression of the disease. Self-
management is the act of actively engaging in self-care activities in order to improve one’s
behavior and well-being. Self-management refers to behaviors such as following a diet plan,
avoiding high-fat foods, increasing exercise, self-glucose monitoring, and foot care.[8] T2DM
self-management is difficult and requires direct patient involvement, including blood glucose
monitoring and focusing and changing lifestyle patterns. Therefore, many individuals with
T2DM struggle to consistently perform diabetes self-care activities, because they have to
manage everything on their own. It is mostly due to the fact that doctors and nurses are not
always available to answer inquiries and handle concerns that people with T2DM face. As
people with T2DM visit their general practitioners every three months. Diabetes self-care
demands dietary and lifestyle modifications, which are required to make decisions to lead a
successful behavior change, which may be possible with a patient-tailored mobile application.
Mobile apps carry the ability to fill in gaps in self-care and enable patients to better
control and manage their chronic illnesses. There are applications available that provide
features like blood sugar tracking, medications (types and dosages), meals, etc. Mobile apps
provide a solution to DSM that is highly scalable and has low cost. But there is still an
issue related to DSM that has not been touched yet. In all the applications available in app
stores, users have to manually calculate and enter the amount of carbohydrates present in
the meal which is time consuming. In this paper, we are trying to research and experiment
to develop an application that calculates the amount of carbohydrates in the food by just
clicking a picture of a meal.

1.2
Problem Statement
1.2
Problem Statement
Maintaining a healthy diet is one of the biggest challenges for person with diabetes
(PwD). PwD needs to severely monitor carbohydrates present in their diet. It is very difficult
to determine what amount of carbohydrate is good for their diabetes management and
which is excessive. At the same time, the PwD have a difficult time figuring out how much
carbohydrates are present in the food that they are taking. To calculate the carbohydrates
they either look at packaging labels or on the internet to figure out how much carbohydrates
are in a particular item, which takes time and requires efforts from PwD.
Moreover, most of the PwD do not know the appropriate method to calculate glucose
or carbohydrates present in their diet. One of the reasons could be that some people are
newly diagnosed with diabetes and have little awareness of the disease at first. They rely on
their general practitioners for diabetes-related information. But PwD have a limited time
with GP’s and it is difficult to gather all the required information in that limited time. As a
result of this, PwD experience difficulties in making an estimate about the amount of glucose
present in the food.
Additionally, it is challenging for PwD to monitor their blood glucose levels.
As it
takes a lot of time and effort to record each readings throughout the day in a diary. It is
difficult for PwD to always carry a notebook with him/her to document their meals and
blood glucose levels. It is also difficult to recall what an individual ate yesterday or the day
before yesterday. Moreover, everyone has a hectic schedule and do not have time to keep
track of every food item they consume[9]. Looking at the work done by Baumel we can see
that making a small intervention using a digital system can actually help users behaviour.
This behaviour help users toward the self management of health in a hectic schedule.
This thesis will consider above problems and tries to solve them by providing a quick
way to calculate the glucose or sugar level present in the meal. And also help them to keep
records of the meal.

Application Review
The research for applications started with a search of several diabetes-related smart-
phone applications. The study’s focus is on diabetes and nutrition management mobile apps
that are already available. The study attempts to assess all nutritional apps on the market
based on their user interface compatibility, user ratings, the quality of nutritional infor-
mation incorporated in the app, tracking choices, cost, and unique diabetes management
capabilities.
We began by identifying 30 apps that could be considered for inclusion in the project.
Furthermore, after employing the inclusion and exclusion criteria, 15 apps were chosen for
additional testing in order to create a comprehensive type 2 diabetes self-management based
on rating given by at least 100 users. Each included app has an android operating system

By examining the 15 apps that were included for further study, we found that the app
’MyFitnessPal’ has mainly focus on fitness and overall health. As a result, we left this app
out and focused on remaining 14 apps. In the app review, 6 out of the 14 apps include the
ability to log meals, blood glucose levels, and other information. 4 out of these 6 apps include
the ability to link to a glucose monitoring device. Depending on the app, each of these 4
apps can connect to a various devices. Furthermore, 1 app offers the ability to share data
with a doctor, 1 app has the ability to export and share reports, however this is a premium
function, and 1 app has the ability to export and import data. The detailed features of each
of these 6 apps has been listed in table:2 below.
The two apps ’Hedia-personal’ diabetes app and ’Diabetes og kulhydratetlling’ have a
database of various food items. The user may look up sugar, carbs, and other nutritional
information for a specific food item. It is simple for diabetic patients to obtain information
on macronutrients such as carbohydrates, sugar, and so on, but it is difficult to find each
and every food item that a user consumes.
The three apps ’Freedom from diabetes’, ’100 Diabetes superfoods’ and ’Beat Diabetic
Diet’ educates PwD about nutrition’s in foods. It informs PwD about which foods are suit-
able for diabetics and which are not. These apps are a learning aid for people with disabilities,
but they do not provide information regarding the amount of glucose in a specific dish. The
remaining three apps were eliminated because they provided no valuable information. They
don’t offer any logging choices.
Overall, no app exists that offers information on the quantity of glucose present in a
given dish that a PwD is eating. There is no app that tells PwD about how a given dish
will effect their blood glucose level after they consume it. As a result, we are attempting to
resolve this issue.

Updated Problem Statement
After conducting research on various apps and literature review, we have discovered that
there are some applications that provide the option to log blood glucose levels in an app.
And users can access an overview of logged blood glucose levels at any time. Moreover, there
are two applications available that calculates carbohydrates for users. But the limitation of

to see if there will be any possibility to create a reliable and user intuitive application for

Is there a possibility to use any type of sensors present on the patient’s mobile to get the
ingredients from the meal?
• How can one create a user centered application for PwD with focus on user experience?
• How can one make PwD learn which meal will help them to minimize the risk of
diabetes?
• How can a PwD use their mobile device to perform a basic calculation of quantity and
carbohydrates without much of a hassle?

4.2
User Story Mapping
The main issues that people with T2DM faces are connected to calculating the amount of
carbohydrates in the food that they are eating. It’s primarily due to the fact that calculating
the carbohydrates in a meal takes time. Furthermore, keeping track of blood glucose levels
and foods consumed over time is tough because keeping a record of everything that PwD
consumes is monotonous.
The solution to these above mentioned problem can be an application that calculates
the amount of carbohydrates present in the food for user. The user just needs to upload
an image of a meal and application will automatically provide the carbohydrates present in
the meal depending upon the quantity of ingredients. It should also allow the user to log a
meal along with their carbohydrates. At any moment, the user can get an overview of food
consumed. Furthermore, the user can obtain a summary of the meal consumed.
The main customer target for an application is people that are newly diagnosed with
type 2 diabetes. It is due to the fact that newly diagnosed people don’t have sufficient
information about food to eat. It is difficult for them to calculate the amount of glucose
present in the meal, because they are not aware of the ways to calculate the carbohydrates.
Further, we tried to narrow down our customer segment to a particular age group. Moreover,
Unique value proposition of Glucose Predictor is that it save user’s time.
User just need to click a picture and application will automatically provide the amount
of carbohydrates present in the meal. It make user’s life easier, because user do not need
to have any hassles of looking at the packaging of items or on internet to find the glucose
content present in the food item. Moreover, this application reduces the risk associated with
sudden increase or decrease in the blood glucose level of a PwD, because it make user aware
about how the food will affect the blood glucose level in advance. Therefore, user can take
decisions accordingly and can avoid upcoming risks. It also reduces anxiety among PwD as
they no longer have to take stress about their blood glucose level.

User Story Mapping
User story mapping encompasses all of the tasks that a user would typically do from the
start to the finish of their journey. It allows teams to obtain useful insights into products
and features mainly software, from the perspective of customers or end-users. User stories,
or activities that end-users can conduct in the context of their journeys, are specifically
defined by the story mapping methodologies.
In simple words, user story mapping is a
different way of looking at product backlogs from the perspective of customers.
It is a
strong prioritizing and planning technique that gives stakeholders a bird’s-eye view of user
journeys and forthcoming product releases, allowing them to prioritize product features more
efficiently[21].
User story mapping basically consists of four things persona, goals, activity and tasks.
Personas are fictitious depictions of people who will use the product or carry out the tasks
outlined in user stories. The user personas describe who the users are and how they might
interact with the product. The user’s desired outcome from using this app is defined by
goals. The things that the user does in order to achieve goals are referred to as activities.
Tasks are the actions that the user takes to complete the tasks.

Feedback from DDA
We had a meeting with Denmark Diabetes Association (DDA) after we finished design-
ing the application. The meeting was held to present the design of our application to get
feedback from experts on diabetes. In the design we focused on finding the glucose present
in the ingredients of a meal. The feedback that we received from DDA was that the glucose
is not the thing that the PwD has to focus on. They need to focus on calculating the car-
bohydrates present in the meal to maintain their blood glucose level. Therefore, after this
meeting we focused on calculating carbohydrates instead of glucose present in the meal.
Moreover, we had some questions that we wanted to ask them. The main question was
"What is the method/formula for calculating the blood glucose level from carbohydrates and
the current glucose level of a PwD?". The answer was, there is no formula or procedure for
estimating blood glucose levels from carbohydrates and current glucose levels of a PwD. The
reason for this is that the way carbohydrates impact people differs from person to person. In
addition to this, blood glucose levels are also affected by other factors such as exercise and
stress. Following this meeting, we attempted to conduct additional research into the above-
mentioned topic. We came across a few publications that attempted to determine blood
glucose levels based on the ingredients in the meal. They were attempting to use machine
learning methods to develop various models and train them using massive data sets [25]. To
build this model, the team needs to do more research and require more time. Therefore, this
is outside the scope of this thesis, and we would be limited to calculating the carbohydrates
from a meal.

6.4.2
Firebase
We have opted to use the Firebase database to store images of items and the ingredi-
ents contained. The key reason for this decision was to store and optimise large collections
of small documents. We used Firestore storage to store the images of food and Firestore
database to store the list of ingredients, food name, URL of image stored in Firebase storage
etc.
Cloud Firestore is Firebase’s fully managed, fast, and server-less cloud-native No-SQL docu-
ment database. It makes data syncing, storage, and querying for mobile, web, and IoT apps
easier. It also provides excellent scalability and supports both live and offline synchroniza-
tion [29].
Firestore is a document-oriented database based on NoSQL. This database contains no ta-
bles or rows. Instead, data is stored in documents that are organized into collections. Each
document contains a set of key-value pairs.
In order to use firestore, it needs to be initialized as seen in the listing:5 below.

Test/Experiments
We used many methods of testing to evaluate the design and implementation of our
application. The first step was Comparative testing we created two distinct prototype designs
and used comparative testing to select the best features from both. Comparative testing
was used primarily to incorporate the best elements of two alternative designs based on
user feedback. This comparative testing was carried out following the initial design of the
first Sprint. The second version of the design was built based on the results of comparative
testing. A usability test was conducted to test the second Sprint, in which users were asked to
navigate through the application and provide feedback. Afterwards third sprint was created
based on the input received in Sprint two. Again the usability test was conducted after
the design of Sprint three. Users were asked to think aloud while navigating around the
application during the usability test, and notes were taken.
When the Sprint three of design was completed, we began to implement the prototype.
The implementation of the design was conducted in three sprints. We used a questionnaire
to conduct usability testing after the completion of first sprint. Users were invited to navi-
gate through the application and respond to questions on a questionnaire. In second sprint,
adjustments were made to the design based on input from testing, and some more function-
alities were introduced. A usability test was undertaken to test implementation of design
after completing the second sprint. Third sprint was created based on input received from
testing and other remaining requirements. The final version of the application was created
by combining all the sprints. The usability test was undertaken to evaluate the application’s
final version, which is detailed in section 8.2.

Usability testing is a method of verifying that an application can perform the stated
tasks effectively. It generally determines the easiness of performing a task by users. We have
used four steps usability approach for testing the usability of our application which can be
seen in figure: 32. The usability testing starts with the methods. Methods are the means
through which usability testing is carried out. There are several ways to conduct a test in
usability testing, including in-person moderated, remote moderated, in-person unmoderated,
and remote unmoderated. The selection of testing methodologies is followed by the crucial
stage of participant recruiting. Participant recruiting in usability testing is the process of
choosing users to test the application or design. It is a crucial phase in the usability testing
process. After selecting the participants for testing, there is a need to define procedures for
testing. Procedures are defined as a list of tasks that user needs to perform during usability
testing. After defining the tasks, we executed the defined methods and tasks through testing.
Then the last step was to analyse the results and feedback received from the testing.

9.1
Functional Requirements
The result of this project is the application “Glucose Predictor”. This application is
successful in achieving most of the functional requirements outlined at the beginning of this
project. The following functional requirements are achieved by the application.
1. Goal: Find the amount of glucose/carbohydrates present in the food.
This goal is accomplished by utilizing a smartphone’s camera, an API called "Log-
Meal", and a database "Frida Food Data". The camera allows users to click an image
or select an image from the gallery which is then uploaded to the API. The API provides
the ingredients present in the meal. The carbohydrates are calculated by comparing
the ingredients of the API with the ingredients from the "Frida Food Data". During
usability testing, we attempted to evaluate this objective by asking users to use our
application to determine the quantity of glucose present in the meal. Users readily
attained this goal by completing actions like uploading and verifying an image. These
tasks require minimum effort from the user as the user needs to press only few buttons
to achieve the required goal.
2. Goal: To assist users in learning about the food and its quantity so that the users can
develop healthy eating habits.
The application can assist users by offering details on food and quantity. This is accom-
plished by using Firebase Firestore. The app gives the total number of carbohydrates
in a meal as well as the amount of each element. This information is stored in Firebase
and all this information can be viewed by users in an Overview Screen of the applica-
tion. Overview Screen contain a list of items where each item corresponds to a logged
meal. Users can click on any item and can see the ingredients present, quantity and
associated carbohydrates. The users can determine from this information how much
quantity of a specific ingredient is safe for them to consume. Additionally, during test-
ing of the application, two users said that using this application the users will learn
about the food and its quantity that is good for them to eat.

3. Goal: To find a quick approach to log meal information for people with disabilities

This application offers a simple method for tracking meals. A mobile phone camera,

solved. It is resolved using the Frida Food Dataset. Currently, we are using a small
directory that stores only a few ingredients and their associated carbohydrates. The
ingredients received from an API are compared with this directory and obtained car-

the ingredients as we are only using a small food dataset. Utilizing the entire Frida
Food Dataset will allow the application to offer the carbs found in almost all of the
ingredients, which will alleviate this problem.

Discussion
The current results from the test with small user group shows that the application can
be useful for recently diagnosed T2DM person. The mobile applications that are available
so far for PwD enable users to register their meals with pictures. They do not offer the
option to save the ingredients and carbohydrates. But if they do so, then users would have
to manually enter each and every ingredient, which is tedious and takes a lot of time.
Additionally, there is just one mobile application that aids PwD in figuring out how
much carbohydrates are present in a certain dish. It contains an ingredient database that
enables users to look up specific substances and provides the corresponding carbohydrates.
However, it takes a lot of time because users must search each ingredient individually if there

10.5
Future Work
Although most of the research question has been answered, there is some future work
that can be done in the application. Some of the future work or development that can be
done on the application are as follows.
Improvement on the API
The log-meal API works well, but sometimes it cannot figure out the meal or gives
strange ingredients. So, an artificially intelligent model found during the literature review
by Jing-sheng Hu et al.[17] can be used on top of the Log meal API. In that way, we can
train our model to be more robust and get more preferred values. As we develop the model,
the application can ask the PwD users to give consent to use their logged meal data and
based on that we can train our model. As this topic is out of scope from the project that
we are doing, this can be a different project on itself.
Bigger dataset of carbohydrates
In the project currently a small set of ingredients from the food Institute has been used
in order to optimize the performance of the application. But in the future, the whole dataset
can be used, which will give the PwD even more information on the carbohydrates. As the
dataset is updated by the food Institute regularly there will be more and more recipes data
along with their carbohydrates. In order to store these data we could use Firebase database
and to compare the ingredients Google Cloud Functions can be used.
Using Cloud Functions
By using a large dataset for carbohydrates, the application can be slow, which can be
prevented by using a google cloud function to calculate the carbs, and updating its value in
the database. The carbohydrates directory can be stored as its own collection in Firebase
database and with the help of Google Cloud function, the application can call the function in
cloud. The function can then compare the recipe present in the image with the recipe present
in the carbohydrates collection to give an accurate result. Using the Cloud function and
storing the carbohydrate directory in the Firebase would free some storage of the application
as well. As the project is already using google Firebase as its database, implementing the
Google Cloud function in the application would not be much difficult. Some research was
done during the project, but due to time constraints, the in-app directory to calculate the
carbs was implemented.
Moreover, during a review meeting with our stakeholders it was pointed out, that the
carbohydrates provided by the application are very accurate numbers which is not applicable
in the real world. This could be improved, in the future by providing range of number of
carbs present in the food. Or by rounding off the number after the calculation with quantity.

Conclusion
At the beginning of the thesis project, there were some ideas or concept provided to us
by the stakeholders that were used to create problem statement. This thesis has defined the
research solving the problem statement and testing these research solution by implementing
a mobile application. The application was developed in three iterations each for design and
implementation respectively, and one iteration lasted for about three weeks. At the end of
these iteration or sprints testing was done with seven to ten test users. These feedback from
test users were analysed and used to make future iteration better.
The thesis at beginning was mostly focus on the design using Figma, but as we did
the research and based on the technology present, we went on to implement these design
using flutter. As per the software development we have used various analysis tools like Lean
Canvas, User story Mapping which provided us broad idea about what problem we were
trying to solve. And using these tools we defined the user stories or requirement for our
thesis and elaborated more on these requirements. Thus we created Use Case Diagram, Use
Case Description, Activity Diagram and Domain Model to make a concrete user stories.
After doing all these analysis to solve the problem statement we started with designing of
the system. During the design of the system we had various question on the analysis for
which we went back and made correction on them. At the end of second iteration for design,
we started with implementation of the system. During the implementation as well, there
was always going back to design and changing it based on feedback from the test users.
This project has answered the research questions that were defined at the beginning of
the project.
1. The first question was "Is there is a possibility to use any kind of sensors present on
the patients mobile phone to get the ingredients from a meal"? It is possible to get the
ingredients of the meal by using the image sensor present in the camera of the mobile
phone. It means that it is possible to get the ingredients of a meal from an image. In
this application, we are using an API LogMeal that provides the information about
ingredients present in a meal from a picture. They are using Artificial Intelligence and
Deep Learning for food recognition. They are using the biggest food dataset in the
world.
2. The second question was "how one can create a user centred application for PwD with
a focus on user experience?" User-eccentric applications put the user and their needs
first during each stage of the creation process using an iterative design process. To
produce mobile application that are incredibly usable and accessible for users, design
teams employ several research and design strategies in user-eccentric apps to involve
users throughout the design process[33]. The application "Glucose Predictor" is a user
centred application. User centred application for PwD is created by involving the users
throughout the design process and development process, to create a highly usable and
accessible application for PwD.It is very important to take user feedback at every step
of design and development and incorporate that feedback into the design. Users were
involved by using different UX testing methods like usability testing and comparative
testing and the feedback provided by sers were taken into account.

3. The third research question for this thesis was How can one make PwD learn which
meal will help them to minimize the risk of diabetes?
PwD has to consume a limited amount of carbohydrates every day in order to maintain
their blood glucose.This application provides carbohydrates present in a meal. This
information can help PwD to make wise decisions like if the carbohydrates present
in the meal is higher than the number of carbohydrates they can consume so they
can decrease the quantity or eliminate the ingredients that have more carbohydrates
from a meal. This will help PwD to minimize the risk of diabetes because by using
this application they will consume only the required amount of carbohydrates and
ultimately maintain their blood glucose levels and minimize the risks of diabetes.
4. The fourth research question was How can a PwD use their mobile device to perform
a basic calculation of quantity and carbohydrates without much of a hassle?
The calculation of carbohydrates present in a meal can be calculated easily using
our application "Glucose Predictor". To calculate the carbohydrates present in the
meal we used a small subset of ’Frida Food data’[31]. We created a small directory
that contains 35 ingredients along with the carbohydrates contained per gram. We
compared the ingredients that are stored in the Firebase with this directory.
The
obtained carbohydrates are multiplied with the quantity of each ingredient and the
users can see the carbohydrates associated with particular quantity of each ingredient.
Overall the thesis has covered all the sectors by answering the research question and
experiments with the technology. The team has gained good knowledge and experience from
the technology used to develop the application. From the project thesis, it can be concluded
that a person who has been diagnosed with T2DM can learn which meal is best to eat by
using the application. And after using the application for a month or two, they can gain
knowledge of various good and bad meals for them.
