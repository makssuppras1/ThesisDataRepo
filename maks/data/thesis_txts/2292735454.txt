INTRODUCTION)
Bacteria!and!other!microbes!possess!an!innate!ability!of!adapting!to!changing!environments!to!ensure!
their!survival.!The!development!of!antibiotic!resistance!genes!(ARG)!in!bacterial!populations!exposed!to!
antibiotics! is! thus! a! natural! and! ancient! phenomenon! (D’Costa! et# al.,! 2011).! Over! the! past! decades,!
however,!the!rapid!dissemination!of!antibiotic!resistance!caused!by!the!exchange!of!resistance!genes!
among!bacteria!has!made!treatment!of!bacterial!infections!increasingly!challenging!and!costly.!Today,!
antibiotic!resistance!is!considered!a!top!threat!for!public!health!and!a!major!financial!burden!for!society!
(World!Health!Organization,!2014).!!
1.)Antibiotic)resistance)–)causes)and)effects)
Antibiotics!are!microbially!produced!antimicrobial!agents!commonly!used!in!the!treatment!of!bacterial!
infections.!However,!anthropogenic!use!and!potential!overuse!of!antibiotics!represent!an!undeniable!
cause! to! the! extensive! spread! of! antibiotic! resistance.! The! majority! of! antibiotics! produced! have!
different!purposes!than!the!evident!therapeutic!use!in!humans.!Prophylactic!use!and!use!as!growthN
enhancing!agents!have!become!common!practice!in!various!industries!including!agriculture,!livestock!
production! and! aquaculture,! but! is! highly! problematic! because! it! creates! selective! conditions! for!
antibiotic! resistant! strains.! Ultimately,! anthropogenic! sources! of! antibiotics! and! antibiotic! resistance!
genes!are!released!in!the!environment,!thus!becoming!available!to!a!wide!variety!of!microorganisms!
(Figure!1)!(Davies!&!Davies,!2010).!
!
Figure) 1) Sources) and) pathways) of) antibiotic) resistance) genes.) Anthropogenic! activities! including! livestock!
production,!agriculture,!aquaculture!and!healthcare!represent!primary!sources!to!antibiotics!and!resistance!genes,!
which!are!passed!onto!a!number!of!secondary!activities!like!wastewater!treatment!or!are!spread!directly!into!the!
environment.!Through!various!mechanisms,!ARG!are!able!to!disperse!widely!among!bacteria.!All!these!activities,!
processes! and! resistance! pathways! create! a! highly! interconnected! and! complex! network,! escalating! the!
dissemination!of!ARG!(Davies!&!Davies,!2010).)

These! activities! and! processes! foster! bacteria’s! capabilities! to! develop! various! resistance! strategies,!
which! in! the! long! term! enable! bacteria! to! achieve! high! levels! of! resistance! to! potentially! multiple!
antibiotics!(Davies!&!Davies,!2010).!Such!microbes!gain!greater!fitness!to!withstand!standard!treatments!
and! are! naturally! selected! for! over! susceptible! microorganisms.! At! present! time,! suppressing! this!
dramatic!development!requires!constant!identification!and!development!of!new!effective!antibiotics,!
while!limiting!use!to!the!strict!necessary!(Walsh,!2000).!Although!regulations!and!surveillance!initiatives!
have! shown! to! limit! the! spread! of! antibiotic! resistance! genes! (Levy,! 2014),! little! is! known! of! their!
development! in! the! environment.! Understanding! the! underlying! mechanisms! and! influential! factors!
shaping! the! evolution! of! resistant! bacteria! and! transfer! of! their! resistance! genes! is! thus! critical! to!
determine!the!extent!of!the!problem!and!tackle!future!challenges!(Rankin!et#al.,!2010).!!
2.)Horizontal)gene)transfer)–)the)main)cause)to)ARG)spread)
Resistance! mechanisms! in! bacteria! may! take! place! spontaneously,! via! genetic! mutations! conferring!
resistance!to!a!particular!antibiotic;!or!through!horizontal!gene!transfer!(HGT),!where!mobile!genetic!
elements!(MGEs)!carrying!antibiotic!resistance!genes!are!exchanged!between!prokaryotes!(Madigan!et#
al.,!2009).!The!acquired!resistance!genes!are!then!passed!on!to!progeny,!a!process!referred!to!as!vertical!
gene!transfer!(VGT)!(Grohmann!et#al.,!2003;!Walsh,!2000).!!
HGT!is!considered!the!principal!contributor!to!the!dissemination!of!antibiotic!resistance!and!the!main!
cause! to! the! emergence! of! multidrug! resistant! pathogens! (Grohmann! et# al.,! 2003;! Walsh,! 2000).! As!
illustrated!in!Figure!2,!HGT!comprises!three!mechanisms:!Transformation,!transduction!and!conjugation!
(Madigan!et#al.,!2009).!!
!
Figure)2)The)three)mechanisms)of)horizontal)gene)transfer:)(1)!Conjugation!involves!the!transmission!of!genetic!
material! through! direct! cellNtoNcell! contact;! (2)! Transformation! consists! in! the! uptake! of! free! DNA! from! the!
surroundings;! (3)! Transduction! involves! the! transfer! of! genetic! material! by! a! bacteriophage! vector! (Klümper,!
2015).)
2.1)Transformation)and)transduction)
Transformation!refers!to!the!uptake!of!naked!exogenous!DNA!from!the!surroundings.!When!prokaryotes!
undergo!cell!lysis,!their!genetic!material!is!broken!into!fragments!and!released!into!the!environment!
(Madigan!et#al.,!2009).!Cells!that!are!able!to!take!up!and!transform!these!DNA!fragments!are!said!to!be!
in!a!state!of!competence!(Madsen!et#al.,!2012;!Madigan!et#al.,!2009).!!

In!transduction,!genetic!material!is!transferred!by!a!bacteriophage,!acting!as!a!vector.!When!infecting!a!
cell,! the! bacterial! virus! causes! the! host! DNA! to! break! down! into! fragments.! While! undergoing!
replication,!a!marginal!fraction!of!phage!offspring!integrates!some!of!the!prior!host’s!DNA!fragments!
into!its!own!genome.!The!transducing!phage!then!leaves!the!cell!and!moves!on!to!the!next!bacterial!cell!
to!infect.!The!phage!binds!to!the!recipient!cell!and!inserts!its!content.!The!donor!cell’s!genes!are!then!
integrated! in! combination! with! the! phage! genes! into! the! recipient! cell’s! chromosome! through!
recombination!(Griffiths!et#al.,!2000;!Madigan!et#al.,!2009).!!
2.2)Conjugation)
Conjugation!is!the!transfer!of!genetic!material!through!cellNtoNcell!contact,!also!referred!to!as!mating.!
The! process! enables! transfer! of! chromosomal! DNA! but! most! commonly! involves! transmission! of!
exogenous!plasmids!residing!in!donor!cells!to!encountered!recipients!(Madigan!et#al.,!2009).!!
The! conjugation! process! involves! some! distinctions,! depending! of! the! type! of! plasmid! transferring.!
Plasmids!are!categorised!into!conjugative,!mobilisable!and!nonNmobilisable!elements.!NonNmobilisable!
plasmids!are!unable!to!transfer!while!mobilisable!plasmids!lack!the!genes!to!transfer!by!themselves!but!
can!be!mobilised!into!a!recipient!if!the!latter!carries!a!conjugative!plasmid!initiating!conjugation!(Smillie!
et#al.,!2010).!Conjugative,!or!conjugal,!plasmids!are!selfNtransmissible!and!carry!all!the!necessary!genes!
to! carry! out! the! transfer! process! independently! of! both! donor! and! recipient! genomes.! Horizontal!
transfer! of! conjugative! plasmids! is! therefore! considered! the! most! efficient! mechanism! for! genetic!
exchange! among! bacteria! and,! as! a! result,! represents! the! primary! cause! to! the! development! of!
antibiotic! resistant! pathogens! (Grohmann! et# al.,! 2003).! Transmission! of! conjugative! plasmids! is!
therefore!in!focus!in!the!present!thesis.!A!more!detailed!description!of!conjugative!plasmids!follows!in!
the!next!section.!
The! conjugation! process! consists! of! four! main! steps! (Figure! 3).! Using! pili! located! on! the! outer! cell!
membrane,!the!bacterial!donor!attaches!to!the!recipient!cell.!A!mating!pore!then!forms!and!the!plasmid!
unwinds! into! two! single! strands,! one! of! which! is! transmitted! to! the! recipient! cell,! while! the! other!
remains!in!the!donor!cell.!Simultaneously,!the!two!single!strands!replicate!by!rolling!circle!into!their!
original! doubleNstranded! circular! form! (Madigan! et# al.,! 2009;! Griffiths! et# al.,! 2000;! Sen! et# al.,! 2012).!
Recipients!having!taken!up!a!plasmid!are!called!transconjugants!and!represent!new!potential!donors.!
Plasmids!encode!for!mechanisms!ensuring!their!vertical!transfer!in!the!new!host!and,!post!conjugation,!
the!plasmid!is!thus!maintained!in!future!generations!(Madsen!et#al.,!2012).!The!host!range!of!a!plasmid!
defines!the!phylogenetic!variety!of!hosts!to!which!it!can!spread!(Klümper!et#al.,!2014).!Plasmids!are!
classified!into!narrow!and!broad!host!ranges!(BHR)!(Jain!&!Srivastava,!2013).!A!plasmid’s!host!range!is!in!
great!extent!determined!by!the!plasmid’s!intrinsic!abilities!to!transfer,!replicate!and!be!maintained!in!
the!new!host,!but!is!also!influenced!by!the!recipient’s!permissiveness,!or!aptitude!to!take!up!the!plasmid!
(Klümper! et# al.,! 2014).! BHR! plasmids! are! characterised! by! their! ability! to! transfer,! replicate! and! be!
subsequently!maintained!across!different!phylogenetic!groups.!BHR!conjugative!plasmids!belonging!to!
incompatibility!group!IncPN1!beta!including!pB10!and!pKJK5!are!particularly!well!characterised,!as!they!
have!been!found!in!numerous!and!diverse!environments!including!soil,!wastewater!and!clinics!(Schlüter!
et#al.,!2007).!These!are!highly!promiscuous,!and!can!practically!transmit!and!replicate!in!all!αN,!βN!and!γN
proteobacteria!(Jain!&!Srivastava,!2013).!Due!to!their!outstanding!features,!BHR!plasmids!are!heavily!
implicated!in!the!spread!of!antibiotic!resistance!genes!(Jain!&!Srivastava,!2013;!Sen!et#al.,!2012;!Klümper!
et#al.,!2015).!

3.)Plasmids))
Plasmids!are!typically!found!in!prokaryotes.!These!MGEs!are!small!doubleNstranded!and!typically!circular!
DNA!elements!residing!freely!in!the!host!cell!and!ranging!in!size!from!1!kbp!to!over!1!Mbp.!Plasmids!are!
selfish! elements! and! are! a! metabolic! burden! to! their! host.! However,! unlike! chromosomes,! plasmids!
carry! exclusively! noncore! but! potentially! useful! genes,! providing! advantageous! characteristics! to! the!
host! (Madigan! et# al.,! 2009).! These! include! various! metabolic! functions,! antibiotic! production! and!
resistance,!conjugation!and!virulence!factors!(Rankin!et#al.,!2010;!Madigan!et#al.,!2009).!!
Figure!4!illustrates!the!genetic!composition!of!a!typical!conjugal!plasmid.!Besides!the!accessory!regions!
conferring! potentially! beneficial! adaptive! traits! to! the! host,! the! genetic! backbone! of! IncPN1! plasmids!
comprises!various!genes!and!modules!responsible!for!their!replication,!maintenance!and!mobility!(Sen!
et# al.,! 2012).! The! replication! module! enables! plasmids! to! replicate! autonomously! of! their! host.! The!
maintenance!region!encodes!for!mechanisms!ensuring!plasmids’!vertical!transmission!to!daughter!cells!
and!stability!within!a!particular!population!(Madigan!et#al.,!2009).!The!mobility!module!consists!of!genes!
that!are!essential!in!the!transmission!of!plasmids!between!bacterial!cells!by!conjugation.!The!trb!region!
consists!of!genes!inducing!the!formation!of!the!mating!pore,!while!the!tra!region!is!involved!in!DNA!
processing! under! transfer.! The! origin! of! replication! (oriV)! determines! the! location! and! direction! for!
replication! of! the! plasmid,! while! the! origin! of! transfer! (oriT)! decides! where! transfer! initiates! and!
terminates.! The! plasmid! is! maintained! by! the! central! control! module! (ctl),! which! comprises! genes!
sustaining!its!stability!in!the!host!(Sen!et#al.,!2012).!

biofilm,!either!returning!to!a!freeNfloating!state!or!attach!to!a!new!surface!and!possibly!colonise!other!

5.1)EPS)production)as)a)result)of)water)stress)
Water!is!an!essential!resource!to!all!forms!of!life!and!water!deprivation!is!one!environmental!stress!
against!which!biofilms!afford!protection.!The!total!water!potential!(Ψ)!is!typically!expressed!in!negative!
megapascal!(NMPa)!and!is!impacted!by!two!main!waterNlimiting!parameters,!namely!osmotic!and!matric!
stress!(Storey!&!Storey,!2000).!Both!mediate!the!water!availability!in!a!given!environment,!but!differ!in!
terms!of!cause!and!effect.!!
In! aqueous! or! hydrated! environments,! the! osmotic! potential! is! the! predominant! factor! for! water!
limitation!and!is!determined!by!the!presence!of!solutes!affecting!the!water!balance!between!microbial!
cells! and! the! environment! (Storey! &! Storey,! 2000).! Increasing! concentrations! of! solutes! lowers! the!
osmotic! component! of! the! water! potential,! resulting! in! higher! stress! (Chang! et# al.,! 2009).! Bacteria!
typically! respond! to! osmotic! stress! by! accumulating! solutes! intracellularly! to! the! point! of! matching!
solute! concentration! in! the! environment! (Csonka,! 1989).! In! dry! habitats,! the! water! available! is!
decreased!due!to!the!adsorption!of!water!molecules!onto!surrounding!solid!particles,!leading!to!matric!
stress!(Chang!et#al.,!2009).!Matric!stress!is!hence!a!result!of!the!physical!removal!of!water!from!the!
surroundings,!literally!causing!the!desiccation!of!cells!(Chang!et#al.,!2007).!As!mentioned!previously,!the!
EPS! matrix! in! biofilms! provides! a! hydrated! and! stable! environment! in! which! embedded! bacteria! are!
protected! (Stewart! &! Costerton,! 2001;! Nadell! et# al.,! 2015).! In! addition,! the! biofilm! matrix! enables!
storage!of!nutrients!and!metabolic!products,!increasing!bacteria’s!chance!of!survival!under!stress!(Chang!
et# al.,! 2009).! Bacterial! populations! exposed! to! matric! stress! therefore! respond! to! matric! stress! by!
increasing!EPS!production!(Chang!et#al.,!2007).!!
The! effects! of! water! limitation! on! biofilm! development! have! been! investigated! extensively,! most! of!
which!use!sodium!chloride!(NaCl)!and!polyethylene!glycol!(PEG)!to!simulate!osmotic!and!matric!stress!
respectively!(Nilsson!et#al.,!2011;!Chang!et#al.,!2007;!Chang!et#al.,!2009;!Gulez!et#al.,!2014).!As!opposed!
to!permeating!NaCl,!PEG!is!a!nonNpermeating!solute!with!molecules!too!large!to!penetrate!the!cell!wall!
of!bacteria!(Chang!et#al.,!2009).!Pseudomonas#putida!is!commonly!used!as!model!organism!for!studying!
the! response! of! environmental! gramNnegative! bacteria! to! water! limitation! because! it! lives! in! diverse!
habitats!that!are!subject!to!variation!in!humidity.!Thus,!P.#putida!will!also!be!used!as!model!organism!in!
the!present!study.!

6.)Plasmids)in)biofilms))
The! correlation! between! plasmids,! conjugation! and! biofilm! development! has! been! recognised.!

The! significance! of! E.# coli# biofilm! structure! has! been! confirmed! in! the! laboratory! and! invasion! of!
exogenous!plasmids!has!been!primarily!observed!in!the!upper!layer!of!biofilms!(Król!et!al.,!2011;!Licht!et#
al.,! 1999;! Hausner! &! Wuertz,! 1999).! Transfer! has! been! shown! to! take! place! rapidly! and! at! highest!
frequencies! instantly! after! introduction! of! donors! (Licht! et# al.,! 1999;! Hausner! &! Wuertz,! 1999).!
However,!the!effect!of!contact!time!on!transfer!frequencies!has!shown!contradictory!results.!On!one!

but!dropped!to!null!thereafter!(Licht!et#al.,!1999).!On!the!other!hand,!extended!mating!time!exhibited!

transconjugants!to!develop!the!ability!to!transfer!on!their!own.!This!explains!why!transconjugants!have!

AIM)AND)OBJECTIVES)
The! previous! sections! provide! insight! within! the! known! factors! and! dynamics! involved! in! plasmid!
invasion!of!biofilm!populations!and!constitute!the!framework!for!the!present!thesis.!The!implication!of!
various!environmental!conditions!as!well!as!plasmidN!and!hostNdependent!parameters!affecting!plasmid!
transfer!has!been!demonstrated!in!several!studies,!confirming!the!complex!nature!of!biofilm!systems.!
The! present! study! strives! to! shed! more! light! on! the! invasion! of! bacterial! populations! established! in!
biofilms!by!plasmids!residing!in!exogenous!donor!strains.!More!specifically,!the!aim!is!to!evaluate!the!
role!of!EPS!as!a!physical!barrier!to!plasmid!uptake!in!established!biofilms.!!
Increasing! understanding! of! these! systems! can! only! be! done! piece! by! piece! and! thus! requires! the!
investigation!of!one!or!few!influential!factors!at!a!time!in!order!to!determine!their!relative!influence.!
Doing!so!requires!the!design!of!controlled!and!simplified!systems!that!reflect!as!accurately!as!possible!
natural! environments! and! conditions! but! at! the! same! time! are! independent! of! all! other! potential!
variables!than!the!focal!ones.!The!experimental!framework!comprises!the!development!of!a!method!for!
visualising!and!quantifying!the!invasion!of!confluent!biofilm!colonies!after!the!introduction!of!a!model!
donor!strain!carrying!BHR!conjugative!plasmid!pKJK5.!Transconjugants!are!detected!using!fluorescence!
reporter! genes! encoded! on! our! model! donor! strains! and! plasmid,! and! transfer! frequencies! are!
quantified!with!microscopy!and!image!analysis.!
The!MSc!research!entails!three!main!objectives:!
1. To!develop!and!optimise!an!assay!for!analysing!plasmid!invasion!into!P.#putida!recipient!biofilm!
colonies!under!different!water!potentials!!
2. To!quantify!and!compare!plasmid!invasion!into!recipient!colonies!of!P.#putida!wild!type!and!
isogenic!mutants!with!differing!abilities!to!produce!EPS!
3. To!compare!plasmid!transfer!frequencies!observed!in!recipients!residing!in!established!biofilms!
versus!in!absence!of!preNestablished!biofilm!!

METHODS)
The! objectives! for! the! present! thesis! comprise! the! design! and! optimisation! of! a! mating! assay! for!
quantifying!exogenous!plasmid!invasion!in!recipient!biofilm!microcolonies!under!conditions!of!varying!
water!stress.!The!following!sections!outline!the!methods!and!materials!applied.!First,!a!description!is!
given!of!the!strains!and!media!used.!Next,!how!the!mating!experiments!were!carried!out!is!explained,!
encompassing!the!standardisation!process!and!final!design!of!the!assay.!Finally,!microscopic!methods!
and!software!used!for!quantification!are!described.!
1.)Strains)
The!recipient!and!donor!strains!used!in!the!present!study!were!constructed!at!the!Panum!Institute!and!
Department!of!Biology!at!Copenhagen!University!and!are!presented!in!Table!1!and!!
Table!2!respectively.!In!order!to!investigate!the!potential!role!of!EPS!as!a!physical!barrier!to!exogenous!
plasmid! invasion,! the! wild! type! strain! Pseudomonas# putida! K2440! and! an! isogenic! mutant! having! a!
reduced!capability!to!form!EPS!were!tested!as!recipients!in!mating!experiments.!Genomic!sequencing!of!
recipient!1,!P.#putida!K2440!wild!type,!revealed!the!presence!of!four!exopolysaccharide!gene!clusters:!
alg! and! bcs,! inducing! alginate! and! bacterial! cellulose! synthesis! respectively,! as! well! as! pea! and! peb,!
which!play!a!key!role!in!biofilm!stability!(Nilsson!et#al.,!2011).!Recipient!2,!P.#putida!K2440!quadruple!
mutant!is!devoid!of!all!four!gene!clusters!and!has!been!shown!to!produce!similar!amounts!of!biofilm!to!
the!wild!type!but!with!significantly!reduced!EPS!content,!leading!to!highly!sensitive!and!unstable!biofilm.!
This! is! explained! by! the! presence! of! components! other! than! exopolysaccharides,! which! induce!
formation!of!a!similar!structural!matrix!but!fail!to!exhibit!the!same!stability!properties!(Nilsson!et#al.,!
2011).!!
Recipient!strains!1!and!2!were!used!in!mating!experiments.!Recipient!strains!3!and!4!are!gfp!mutants!of!
the! same! strains! and! were! used! during! the! assay! design! phase! in! order! to! visualise! them! in! the!
microscopes.!

1.1)Verification)of)strain)behaviour)
Prior!to!performing!experiments,!the!behaviour!of!strains!was!verified!in!terms!of!growth,!fluorescence!
and!antibiotic!resistance!using!selective!media.!!
Strains’! fluorescence! was! verified! in! the! microscope.! As! expected,! donors! emitted! red! signal,! gfpN
marked!recipients!green!signal!and!recipients!free!of!reporter!genes!were!colourless.!!
20µM!isopropyl!βNDNthiogalactoside!(IPTG)!was!used!to!verify!carriage!of!plasmid!pKJK5::gfp!in!donor!
strains.!By!binding!to,!and!thus!inhibiting,!repressor!gene!lacIq,!IPTG!induces!expression!of!gfp!marker!
gene!on!the!plasmid!and!thus!can!be!observed!in!the!microscope.!!!
All! strains! grew! on! standard! Luria! broth! (LB)! agar.! Growing! the! bacteria! on! citrate! media! with! and!
without! antibiotics! enabled! to! separately! select! for! the! two! donor! strains,! P.# putida! recipients! and!
transconjugants!carrying!pKJK5::gfp.!In!matings!with!E.#coli!donors,!LB!TMP30!was!used!to!select!for!E.#
coli!donors!and!transconjugants,!both!encoding!for!TMP30!resistance!on!the!plasmid.!Citrate!represents!
a!carbon!source!that!P.#putida!only!has!the!ability!to!use,!and!was!thus!used!to!counter!select!against!E.#
coli!donors.!Finally,!citrate!combined!with!TMP30!enabled!to!select!for!transconjugants!only.!In!matings!
with! P.# putida! donors,! LB! Tet20! was! used! to! select! for! P.# putida! donors! and! transconjugants,! both!
encoding! for! Tet20! resistance! on! the! plasmid.! Since! all!bacteria! are!P.# putida,! all! could! grow! on! the!
citrate!medium.!!
2.)Culture)conditions)and)media))
All!strains!were!initially!regrown!on!solid!Luria!Broth!(LB)!medium!from!stocks!kept!at!N80°C,!and!stored!
at!4°C!prior!to!use.!All!experimental!work!was!carried!out!in!a!sterile!laminar!flow!bench.!!
2.1)Growth)of)strains))
For!the!mating!experiments,!strains!were!grown!in!20!mL!liquid!LB!for!4N5!hours!at!37°C!in!an!orbital!
shaker! at! 120! rpm.! Approximately! 1.5! mL! of! the! planktonic! culture! was! pipetted! into! an! Eppendorf!
tubes!and!bacterial!cells!were!centrifuged!down!to!a!pellet!at!13,400!rpm!for!4!minutes.!The!LB!medium!
was!then!discarded!and!the!pellet!resuspended!in!1!mL!0.9%!NaCl!solution.!Using!a!spectrophotometer,!
recipient!suspensions!were!normalised!to!OD600!=!0.25,!corresponding!to!3.14!x!107!CFUs/mL!based!on!
quadruplicate! drop! plating! of! dilutions.! Donor! suspensions! were! normalised! to! OD600! =! 0.1! with!
subsequent!tenfold!dilution,!corresponding!to!1.25!x!106!CFUs/mL,!again!based!on!drop!counting.!
2.2)Mating)media))
The!series!of!mating!media!simulating!varying!matric!and!osmotic!stress!conditions!was!prepared!by!
altering!the!water!potential!of!the!basal!medium!with!PEG!8000!and!NaCl!by!N0.25,!N0.5,!N0.75,!N1!and!N
1.5MPa.! Two! solutions! of! equal! volumes! were! prepared! –! a! basal! medium! supplying! bacteria! with!
nutrients! and! a! solution! altering! the! water! potential! of! the! basal! medium.! The! ingredients! and!
corresponding!concentrations!for!both!solutions!are!presented!in!Table!3!(Halverson!&!Firestone,!2000).!
Each!medium!was!prepared!in!200!mL!batches.!The!basal!medium!was!prepared!by!dissolving!0.32!g!
R2B,!0.2!g!MgSO4!7H2O!and!2!g!Gelrite!in!100!mL!deionised!water.!In!the!case!of!matric!potentials!N0.75!
to!N1.5MPa,!MgSO4!7H2O!was!omitted.!Gelrite!was!here!used!as!solidifying!agent!in!place!of!the!usual!
agar!because!of!the!incompatibility!of!agar!with!PEG.!The!solution!was!subsequently!autoclaved!and!
stored!at!75°C!until!needed.!

3.)Assay)design)
The!mating!assay!involved!growing!confluent!colonyNlike!recipient!biofilms,!ideally!having!the!same!size!
and!thickness,!under!conditions!of!varying!water!stress!and!subsequently!introduce!a!known!number!of!
donor!cells!on!top!of!the!colony!while!causing!as!little!disturbance!to!the!colony!as!possible.!The!goal!
was!to!develop!a!reproducible!method,!which!allowed!the!assessment!of!EPS!as!single!variable,!and!to!
obtain!quantifiable!data!based!on!image!analysis.!During!this!design!phase,!the!gfp!mutant!recipient!
strains!were!used!to!be!able!to!visualise!them!in!the!microscope!and!illustrate!the!progress!in!the!scope!
of!achieving!colonies!that!were!confluent!and!did!not!disrupt!under!the!inoculation!of!donors.!The!assay!
was! optimised! through! an! iterative! testing! of! various! parameters,! enabling! the! development! of! a!
standardised! method.! The! parameters! tested! included! culture! media,! incubation! time,! filter! type,!
recipient!and!donor!cell!number,!drop!volume!and!different!approaches!to!the!inoculation!of!donors.!
The!parameters!selected!for!conducting!experiments!are!explained!in!more!detail!in!Results!section!1.!

4.)Filter)matings)
Two!separate!mating!experiments!were!conducted!onto!filters!to!immobilise!bacterial!cells!onto!a!solid!

recipients!in!absence!of!preNestablished!biofilm!under!maximised!donorNtoNrecipient!contact!conditions!
(Musovic!et#al.,!2014;!Klümper!et#al.,!2014).!!

4.2)Matings)without)preNestablished)biofilm)
Matings!were!performed!with!completely!mixed!suspensions!of!donors!and!recipients!to!compare!the!
extent!of!plasmid!transfer!with!and!without!preNestablished!biofilms.!The!method!applied!was!adapted!
from!Klümper!et#al.,!2014!and!was!performed!in!triplicates,!using!a!filtering!device!(Sartorius!filtering!
unit!from!Merck!Millipore).!!
Before!use,!the!filtering!device!was!thoroughly!cleaned!with!ethanol.!White!GF/C!glass!microfibre!filters!
(25! mm! diameter! from! Whatman)! were! then! placed! in! each! well! and! the! lid! closed! tightly.! A! small!
volume!of!0.9%!NaCl!solution!was!then!sucked!through!at!200!mbar!with!a!vacuum!pump!to!verify!that!
the!apparatus!functioned!correctly.!A!sterile!black!polycarbonate!circle!filter!(25!mm!diameter!and!0.2!
μm!pore!size!from!Maine!Manufacturing!LLC)!was!then!placed!on!top!of!each!white!filter!and!suction!
was!repeated.!In!each!well,!42.7!μL!of!recipient!bacterial!suspension!OD600!=!0.1,!corresponding!to!app.!
7!x!105!cells,!was!then!mixed!with!80!μL!donor!suspension!OD600!=!0.001,!corresponding!to!app.!1.32!x!
104!donors.!Suction!was!then!applied!to!immobilise!the!cells!onto!the!filters.!The!excess!of!recipients!
ensured!maximised!cellNtoNcell!contact,!meaning!that!each!donor!cell!gets!in!contact!with!at!least!one!
recipient.!The!black!filters!were!transferred!onto!the!prepared!PEG!amended!plates!and!were!then!dried!
in!the!fume!hood!for!about!10!min!until!completely!dry.!The!plates!were!stacked,!incubated!overnight!
and!the!following!morning!stored!at!4°C!until!needed.!Drop!counting!was!again!carried!out!as!explained!
previously,!to!know!the!number!of!CFUs!inoculated.!
5.)Microscopy)and)quantification))
Transfer!events!were!monitored!and!quantified!using!microscopy!and!subsequent!image!analysis.!The!
stereomicroscope! (SM)! was! used! for! rapid! single! plane! imaging! and! the! confocal! laser! scanning!
microscope! (CLSM)! was! used! for! generating! zNstack! observation! of! transconjugants! throughout! the!
biofilm!colonies.!!
SM! of! transfer! events! was! performed! using! a! Leica! MZ16! FA! microscope.! A! 1x! plan! apochromatic!
objective!with!magnification!zoom!ranging!40N70x!was!used!to!ensure!a!complete!view!of!the!biofilm!
and! clearest! signal.! Conditions! for! detection! of! gfp! and! mCherry! fluorescence! were! 480/20! nm! with!
emission!at!525/40!nm!and!580/25!nm!with!emission!at!650/60!nm!respectively.!Images!were!acquired!
using!a!Leica!DFC300!fluorescence!camera.!Triplicate!filters!were!analysed!under!each!stress!condition.!
For!the!experiments!involving!total!coverage!of!the!filter!with!bacteria,!ten!images!were!taken!randomly!
per!filter.!!
CLSM!is!an!advanced!method!for!studying!microbial!biofilms!in#situ.!The!microscope!has!the!ability!to!
scan!a!sample!at!various!depths!while!the!confocality!enables!to!reject!noise!signals!from!other!planes!

entire!biofilm,!which!can!be!used!to!visualise!the!distribution!of!fluorescent!microbes!within!the!threeN
dimensional!structure!of!the!colony.!Confocal!microscopy!was!performed!using!a!Leica!SP5!with!a!10x!
magnification! objective.! Sequential! imaging! of! the! colonies! was! performed! using! two! fluorescence!
channels.!Argon!and!HeliumNNeon!lasers!set!at!30%!intensity!providing!excitation!wavelengths!of!488!
nm!and!568!nm!were!used!for!detection!of!gfp!fluorescent!transconjugants!and!mCherry!fluorescent!
donors!respectively.!Images!were!acquired!in!1024x1024!pixel!resolution!with!20!frames,!a!line!average!
of!2!and!step!size!ranging!app.!3N7!μm,!depending!on!the!thickness!of!the!biofilm.!!
Images!were!quantified!for!transfer!events!by!automated!or!manual!image!analysis!in!ImageNPro!Plus!

1.)Assay)optimisation)
The! mating! assay! was! designed! and! optimised! to! achieve! the! growth! of! confluent! recipient! biofilm!
microcolonies!with!the!same!number!of!cells!and,!ideally,!of!similar!size!and!thickness.!The!goal!was!to!
develop!a!simple!system!that!enables!to!investigate!the!role!of!EPS!as!sole!variable!in!the!assessment!of!
exogenous!plasmid!invasion!of!biofilms.!In!order!to!reach!this!goal,!the!method!was!developed!to!be!
reproducible!and!to!provide!quantifiable!data.!The!present!section!explains!the!rationale!behind!the!
selection!of!key!parameters!involved!in!the!design!of!the!mating!assay.!!

Inoculation)of)donors)
The!introduction!of!donors!was!optimised!in!terms!of!CFUs,!drop!volume!and!inoculation!methods!to!
minimise! disturbance! of! the! recipient! colonies! and! to! make! sure! plasmid! invasion! was! quantifiable.!
Inoculations! of! 10,000! and! 1,000! donor! cells! resulted! in! overlapping! and! saturated! signals,! which!

conditions.!

DISCUSSION)
As!previous!studies!have!shown,!many!parameters!influence!exogenous!plasmid!invasion!of!biofilms.!
These! include! donor! strains,! plasmid! type,! mating! time! and! donor! adhesion,! nutrient! levels,! CFU!
number!etc.!Our!method!standardises!all!variables!other!than!EPS!under!water!stress,!enabling!us!to!
focus!on!this!single!parameter!only.!The!design!phase!of!the!assay!consisted!in!an!iterative!testing!of!
various! parameters,! which! in! turn! enabled! to! fineNtune! a! quantifiable! and! reproducible! method! for!
investigating! exogenous! plasmid! invasion! of! established! biofilm! colonies.! Triplicate! filters! under!
reference!and!stress!conditions!showed!consistent!results,!confirming!that!the!method!is!reproducible!
and!should!be!adaptable!to!other!systems!comprising!other!strains!and!plasmids.!!
1.)Cell)viability)and)growth)rate)on)PEG)media)
E.#coli!donors!were!not!growing!at!PEG!N0.75MPa!and!is!coherent!with!McAneney!et#al.!(1982),!who!
found!that!water!potential!PEG!N0.8MPa!caused!complete!extinction!of!E.#coli.!Conversely,!P.#putida!was!
able!to!grow!under!all!of!the!applied!stress!conditions.!This!observation!demonstrates!that!bacteria!may!
respond!very!differently!to!water!stress!depending!on!their!genus!and!the!environment!in!which!they!
reside.!E.#coli,!being!an!enteric!bacterium,!is!expected!to!be!more!sensitive!than!P.#putida.!The!latter!is!
an! organism! commonly! found! in! soil! and! other! environments! where! conditions! change! frequently,!
which! explains! their! greater! ability! to! adapt! to! fluctuating! humidity.! PEG! stress! does! not! have! a!
detrimental!effect!on!P.#putida!viability.!CFU!numbers!of!P.#putida!do!not!decrease!under!matric!stress!
and!it!can!thus!be!concluded!that!any!effects!observed!in!mating!experiments!are!not!a!consequence!of!
fewer!viable!recipients!caused!by!PEG.!
The! expansion! rate! based! on! recipient! colony! radius! was! clearly! affected! by! matric! stress,! and!
particularly!reduced!under!the!lowest!water!potential!N1.5MPa!PEG.!Colonies!grown!on!control!medium!
showed!significantly!higher!expansion!rates!than!the!ones!grown!on!PEG.!Matric!stress!induces!a!rather!
upward!growth,!leading!to!the!formation!of!biofilms!that!are!smaller!in!surface!area!but!thicker!(Chang!
et!al.,!2007).!However,!in!our!case,!the!same!conclusion!cannot!be!drawn!when!assessing!the!colonies’!
relative!intensities!and!comparison!of!colony!expansion!rates!and!fluorescence!intensity!rates!shows!
that! water! stress! rather! decreased! than! increased! the! thickness! of! biofilms.! The! relative! intensities!
reflect! expectations! that! recipients! have! more! ease! to! grow! on! controls! whereas! increasing! matric!
stress! affects! cells’! growth! potential! and! activity.! Under! extreme! matric! stress,! P.# putida! wild! type!
exhibits!stronger!fluorescence!intensity,!which!could!be!due!to!its!greater!fitness!under!water!stress!
conditions.!The!difference!in!biofilm!thickness!of!P.#putida!wild!type!and!mutant!was!expected.!Because!
of! its! impaired! ability! to! produce! EPS,! the! mutant! suffers! more! from! desiccation! than! the! wild! type!
strain,!resulting!in!delayed!or!hindered!growth!activity.!
When!comparing!the!two!strains!more!closely,!a!first!observation!is!the!stronger!fluorescence!intensity!
and! sharper! appearance! of! the! mutant! colonies! under! stressNfree! conditions,! whereas! the! wild! type!
colonies!are!slightly!less!intense!and!blurrier.!This!again!could!be!due!to!the!wild!type’s!secretion!of!EPS,!
forming!a!matrix!that!surrounds!the!cells!and!acts!as!a!filter!to!their!fluorescence.!Another!possibility!is!
variation!in!the!two!strains’!metabolic!activity,!inducing!a!difference!in!fluorescence!expression.!At!PEG!N
1.5MPa,!however,!the!wild!type!exhibits!stronger!fluorescence!intensity.!

has! two! possible! explanations.! First,! the! larger! transfer! events! could! be! the! result! of! cell! division! of!
transconjugants.!Indeed,!monitoring!of!recipient!colony!expansion!over!time!revealed!that!conditions!of!

3.)Comparison)of)transfer)frequencies)
The!resulting!transfer!frequencies!from!the!present!study!are!here!compared!to!each!other!and!to!other!
studies.!Plasmid!transfer!frequencies!are!first!compared!between!the!two!isogenic!recipient!strains!P.#
putida#wild!type!and!EPSN!mutant.!The!plasmid!uptake!into!established!biofilm!recipient!colonies!is!then!
compared! to! the! transfer! frequencies! from! the! matings! carried! out! with! the! same! strains,! but! in!
absence!preNestablished!biofilms.!Next,!the!effect!of!matric!stress!on!transfer!frequencies!in!matings!
without!preNestablished!biofilm!under!is!compared!to!a!similar!study!assessing!the!effect!of!metal!stress!
on! community! permissiveness.! Finally,! plasmid! invasion! of! established! biofilm! recipient! colonies! is!
compared!to!two!studies,!investigating!the!invasion!of!E.#coli!biofilms!by!conjugal!plasmids!other!than!
pKJK5.!
Plasmid)transfer)in)P.#putida#wild)type)versus)EPSN)mutant)
In!order!to!investigate!the!role!of!EPS!as!a!barrier!to!exogenous!plasmid!invasion,!two!isogenic!recipient!
strains!differing!in!their!ability!to!secrete!EPS!were!subjected!to!water!stress.!P.#putida#wild!type!carries!
four! exopolysaccharide! gene! clusters,! alg,! bcs,! pea! and! peb# (Nilsson! et# al.,! 2011).! The! isogenic! EPSN!
mutant!is!devoid!of!all!four!gene!clusters,!i.e.!hindering!its!capacity!to!produce!EPS!and!resulting!in!a!
highly!sensitive!and!unstable!biofilm,!which!would!enable!exogenous!donors!to!penetrate!the!biofilm!
more!easily!(Nilsson!et#al.,!2011).!Bacteria!adapt!to!their!environment!to!ensure!their!survival!and!they!
naturally!respond!to!desiccation!by!producing!more!EPS.!The!wild!type!is!therefore!expected!to!secrete!
more! EPS! under! matric! stress,! while! the! mutant! is! expected! to! produce! similar! low! amounts! of! EPS!
independent! of! changes! in! water! potential.! If! EPS! does! function! as! a! barrier! to! exogenous! plasmid!
invasion,!we!expect!plasmid!invasion!in!the!wild!type!biofilm!colonies!to!be!negatively!correlated!with!
increasing!matric!stress!but!expect!to!observe!similar!transfer!frequencies!in!mutant!biofilms!under!all!
conditions.!
Transfer!frequencies!to!both!strains!are!extremely!high!both!in!biofilms!and!in!matings!without!preN
established! biofilm.! Our! study! demonstrates! that! matric! stress! has! no! significant! effect! on! P.# putida!

CONCLUSION)
The! mating! assay! developed! in! this! thesis! provides! an! innovative! approach! to! the! study! and!
quantification! of! exogenous! plasmid! invasion! in! bacterial! populations! residing! in! biofilms.! Due! to! its!
simplicity! and! reproducibility,! the! method! has! great! potential! and! can! easily! be! adapted! to! more!
complex!systems!involving!other!types!of!plasmids!and!bacterial!strains.!
Transfer!frequencies!were!very!high!both!in!P.#putida!biofilms!as!well!as!in!absence!of!preNestablished!
biofilm,!attaining!T/D!ratios!of!0.86!and!0.18!respectively.!BHR!conjugative!plasmid!pKJK5!is!thus!highly!
efficient!in!transferring!to!P.#putida!recipients.!Surprisingly,!transfer!frequencies!were!much!higher!in!
biofilms!than!without!preNestablished!biofilm,!possibly!because!the!presence!of!an!established!biofilm!
creates!a!protective!environment!for!the!donor.!!
It! is! concluded! that! EPS! can! constitute! a! physical! barrier! to! exogenous! plasmid! invasion! of! biofilms!
based!on!three!key!observations.!Transfer!frequencies!in!P.#putida!wild!type!biofilm!colonies!decreased!
dramatically!under!high!matric!stress!of!PEG!N0.75MPa!and!below,!while!this!strong!decrease!was!not!
observed!for!biofilms!formed!by!P.#putida!EPSN!mutant,!unable!to!secrete!EPS.!Thirdly,!pKJK5!invasion!of!
recipient! biofilm! colonies! under! osmotic! stress,! which! do! not! promote! EPS! production,! resulted! in!
transfer! frequencies! similar! to! reference! conditions,! confirming! that! the! observed! drop! in! transfer!
frequencies!is!not!a!result!of!physiological!effects!of!water!stress.!
The!present!study!has!provided!valuable!insight!into!the!complex!dynamics!involved!in!the!horizontal!
transfer!of!resistance!plasmids!in!biofilm!systems!and!demonstrates!that!plasmid!spread!to!biofilms!with!
copious!EPS!is!limited.!!In!the!scope!of!mediating!ARG!spread!in!the!environment,!our!method!provides!
a!solid!foundation!for!investigating!further!the!extent!and!implications!of!plasmid!transfer!in!biofilms.!!
