Abstract
Corynebacterium glutamicum, carries a great potential as a host organism for the
secretory production of heterologous proteins, such as important enzymes and
pharmaceutical proteins, due to its various advantages. In this study, a novel geВӯ
netic switch was characterized which controls the IPTGВӯinducible secreted protein
production in C. glutamicum. This genetic switch works as inverted switch, that
means in the presence of inducer, the expression of the target protein would be
switched OFF. That allows the addition of the IPTG inducer in preculture and the
dilution of the inducer is assumed to trigger the production of the target protein.
The induction of heterologous protein expression in bioprocesses results in high
costs while scaling up the production, if the process is not optimized and follows
the standard procedures that use high amount of IPTG. Therefore, the optimal
IPTG concentration to toggle the switch ON/OFF, was also investigated. CutiВӯ
nase protein was used as model product.
C. glutamicum WT (pEKEx2_BetI_mCherry_PBetI_GFPuv) strain was cultivated
in CGXII medium induced with different IPTG concentrations, in order to charВӯ
acterize the inverted switch behavior by measuring the fluorescence of reporter
protein GFPuv and mCherry. BetI/PBetI works as an ON/OFF switch, for the OFF
switch ,it was referred to GFPuv expression as ON and BetI/mCherry expression
as OFF. Furthermore, the behavior during the cultivation in the course of time
was determined. Following that, the inverted switch behavior was characterized
in the presence of cutinase gene. An enzymatic assay was developed to measure
cutinase activity in the culture supernatants.
The results showed that the inverted switch was working as anticipated. The
addition of the cutinase gene did not have a negative influence on the switch
behavior. The optimal IPTG concentration was reached at 100 ОјM for toggling
the switch ON to OFF. The highest cutinase activity was reached after 6 hours of
cultivation.

Introduction
1.1
Background
Corynebacterium glutamicum is widely known as a natural producer of glutamic
acid and has been developed for industrial production of various amino acids.
Moreover, it has been discovered that C. glutamicum has great potential as a
host organism for efficient secretory production of heterologous proteins [1]. ExВӯ
tracellular protein production is more feasible since it simplifies the downstream
processing and production cost is significantly reduced by it. Efficient production
of the target protein in the culture supernatant can be achieved by optimizing the
bioprocess parameters. These parameters are varied by the individual protein
and should be tailored for the target protein. Therefore, the number of conditions
need to be tested in order to achieve a successful and cost effective production of
the target protein. The extensive knowledge for the bioprocess of C. glutamicum
and availability of tools for its genetic manipulation make this organism even more
interesting.
In most expression systems for C. glutamicum, an E. Coli promoter such as PВӯ
lac,PВӯtac or PВӯtrc, and a lac operator/repressor system has been used to tightly
regulate the recombinant protein expression by IPTG induction [2]. The addition of
the inducer to the main culture starts production, and constant/repeated addition of
the inducer is required during the continuous fermentation process or in repeated
fedВӯbatch fermentation for optimal production. Using synthetic biology genetic
switch for the control of expression in C. glutamicum has been created, which
inverts the use of the inducer molecule. In this case, dilution of the inducer is
assumed to trigger the production of the target protein. Therefore, if the required
IPTG concentrations can be reduced, the production cost could be decreased.
In this study, a genetic OFF switch system was characterized for the control of
secreted protein production in C. glutamicum, which should allow to minimize the
use of the inducer IPTG in a fedВӯbatch process.

==PAGE:15==
1.2
Corynebacterium glutamicum as host
organism for protein expression
Corynebacterium glutamicum is a GramВӯpositive, facultative anaerobic, rodВӯshaped,
nonВӯsporeВӯforming, nonВӯmotile bacterium that is generally recognized as safe (GRAS),
which makes it available for usage also in food industry [3]. The first industrial ferВӯ
mentation process by C. glutamicum was described by Kinoshita in 1957 in order
to produce glutamic acid from glucose [4]. Since then, C. glutamicum became an
important industrial microorganism for producing wide range of products including
secreted heterologous proteins. It is often considered as an alternative bacterial
expression system to E. Coli.
C. glutamicum, as a host organism, has some advantages such as being nonВӯ
pathogenic, having low protease activity in the culture supernatant, not producing
endotoxins and most importantly, being able to secrete properly folded and funcВӯ
tional proteins to the culture [5]. These features make this organism to be chosen
for large scale production. However, compared to E. Coli, C. glutamicum has
some drawbacks, as much lower transformation efficiency and only a few expresВӯ
sion vectors are being available. To resolve these issues, genetic modification of
C. glutamicum strains has been studied over the years. To this date, genetically
modified C. glutamicum strains were obtained, which show more rapid growth, enВӯ
hanced protein synthesis and efficient protein secretion of heterologous proteins
are achieved [6], [7],[8]. There is still a strong demand for more depth investigation
into this organism in the areas such as optimization of promoters to enhance exВӯ
pression efficiency, construction of plasmid vectors for various types of proteins,
host strain mutation towards building up efficient protein secretion pathways, and
a quality by design (QbD) Вӯ driven process optimization of C. glutamicum cultivaВӯ
tion within the bioreactors [5].

1.3
Secretory protein expression in C. glutamicum
Secretory protein expression in C. glutamicum into the culture medium allows
downstream purification to be easier and there is no need for disruptions of the
cells. The oxidizing environment of the extracellular medium results in formation
of disulfide bonds, promoting correct folding of the protein, which often leads to
an active protein form [9]. C. glutamicum can secrete proteins into the medium
via the Sec and Tat pathways. The Sec pathway transports unfolded proteins,
whereas the Tat pathway transports folded proteins [10].
1.3.1
Sec pathway
The general secretory (Sec) pathway transports proteins in an unfolded state and
folding takes places on the trans side of the membrane after the actual transport
event [11].
SecВӯdependent protein secretion systems have a coВӯtranslational targeting sysВӯ
tem and a postВӯtranslational targeting system. In the coВӯtranslational targeting
system, the signal recognition particle (SRP) binds to the nascent peptide and
leads to formation of a complex to a membrane protein FtsY along with ribosomesНҫ
then, this SPR subsequently leads the nascent peptide to the channel complex
(SecYEG). In the postВӯtranslational targeting system, the translationВӯfinished pepВӯ
tide binds to SecB and SecA to reach the SecYEG channel. Protein translocation
starts with the binding of ATP to SecA, and the signal peptide of the secretory proВӯ
tein NВӯterminal region inserts into the proteinВӯconducting channel. Then, SecA hyВӯ
drolyzes ATP and drives the protein translocation, which releases the preprotein.
As a result, polypeptides of the secretory protein enters into the proteinВӯconducting
channel. Next, SecA binds to the inserted preprotein again and hydrolyzes ATP to
drive the translocation process. Multiple cycles of SecA binding and ATP hydrolВӯ
ysis complete the protein translocation, and SecA dissociates from the proteinВӯ
conducting channel. The signal peptide is the first sequence to be inserted into
the proteinВӯconducting channel, followed by the insertion of the NВӯterminal chain
of the mature part of the preprotein, and the signal peptide is cleaved by the Type
I signal peptidase to allow for the release of the protein from the membrane [6].

==PAGE:17==
One major difference between C. glutamicum and other GramВӯpositive bacteria
is that for C. glutamicum, both copies of two secA genes(secA1 and secA2) are
essential for survival [5]. The Sec pathway translocation process in C. glutamicum
is shown in Figure 1.1.
Figure 1.1: Schematic overview of protein transport across the membrane through
the Sec pathway in C. glutamicum. The Sec pathway transports unfolded proВӯ
teins. During coВӯtranslational targeting to Sec, the signal sequence is recognised
at the translating ribosome by ribosomeВӯbound signal recognition particle (SRP)
and the nascent chain is guided via the SRP receptor to the Sec translocon. In
the postВӯtranslational pathway, the substrate is maintained in an unfolded conforВӯ
mation and guided to the Sec translocon by the ATPase, SecA. ATP hydrolysis
by SecA provides the driving force for SecВӯdependent postВӯtranslational protein
export [5],[12].
1.3.2
Tat pathway
In contrast to the Sec pathway, proteins gain their mature conformation in the cytoВӯ
plasm, before being translocated by the twinВӯarginine translocation (Tat) pathway.
Therefore it is an alternative for the secretory production of proteins that cannot
be produced in a properly folded functional form via the Sec pathway [13].
The corynebacterial Tat pathway consists of four components: TatA, TatB, TatC,
and TatE. Most GramВӯpositive organisms lack the tatE gene. However, only TatA
4
==PAGE:18==
and TatC are necessary in C. glutamicum. TatB is not an essential protein for
Tat pathway, however it helps to improve secretory efficiency [14]. TatE provides
same functions as TatA [15]. The TatВӯpathway contains twinВӯarginine motif (RR)
in the signal peptide. The basic structure of the Tat system has two complexes
as a docking complex and a pore complex. The docking complex (TatB and TatC)
recognizes the RR motif of the Tat signal peptide in the folded protein. Then,
the folded protein is translocated across the active pore complex (TatA), with a
structural change of the docking complex [6]. TatA alone cannot complete the
translocation, but it can function with Tat(B)C. While the cargo protein is being

1.4
The lac operon and its induction with
isopropyl ОІВӯDВӯ1Вӯ thiogalactopyranoside (IPTG)
The lac operon is a set of genes in a region of the genome that is involved in lacВӯ
tose metabolism and control of lac gene expression [16]. Lac operon consists of
three structural genes, which are lacZ, lacY and lacA. lacZ encodes the enzyme
ОІВӯgalactosidase, which cleaves lactose into glucose and galactose and it is reВӯ
sponsible for the production of the natural inducer of the lac operon, allolactose.
lacY encodes the lactose permease that is responsible for lactose uptake. lacA
encodes a lactose transacetylase.
Transcription from the lac promoter is regulated by the lacI gene, which encodes
the lac repressor protein LacI. In the absence of inducer like lactose or IPTG, the
lac repressor inhibits the transcription from the lac promoter by binding to the opВӯ
erator region of lac operon and prevents the proper binding of RNA polymerase to
the promoter region. This interrupts the transcription of the lac genes [17]. HowВӯ
ever, as a result of chemical equilibrium between bound and unbound repressor
molecules, the operator site is not continuously occupied by the lac repressor and
thus there is generally a transcription of the lac genes at a low level.
Figure 1.3: Simplified diagram of lac operon. The lac repressor gene (lacI) is
located upstream of the lac operon and has its own promoter. In the absence of
lactose, the lac repressor blocks the expression of the lac operon by binding to the
DNA at a site, called the operator. In the presence of lactose, the lac repressor is
released from the operator and transcription proceeds.
Protein expression can take place with lacВӯderived promoters, which can be inВӯ
duced with the natural inducer lactose or its synthetic analog isopropyl ОІВӯdВӯ1Вӯ
thiogalactopyranoside (IPTG) [18]. Induction of these promoters leads to an inВӯ
6
==PAGE:20==
crease of 1000 folds for the transcription of the lac gene [19]. The lowВӯcost and
nonВӯtoxicity make lactose feasible for engineering products. However, in comВӯ
parison to IPTG, the parameters of lactose inducing different protein expression
highly vary and establishing its optimal working conditions usually requires a large
number of laboratory experiments [20]. Also, since lactose is a naturally occurring
sugar, it is metabolized by a number of prokaryotic and eukaryotic organisms.
Isopropyl ОІВӯDВӯ1Вӯthiogalactopyranoside (IPTG) mimicks allolactose, which binds to
the lac repressor and releases the tetrameric repressor from the lac operon, thereВӯ
fore allows to induce gene expression. However, unlike allolactose, the sulfur (S)
atom creates a chemical bond which is nonВӯhydrolyzable by the cell, preventing
the cell from metabolizing or degrading the inducer. Thus the concentration of
IPTG remains constant and the expression of LacI controlled genes does not beВӯ
come inhibited during the experiments [21]. IPTG inducible expression systems
allow the precise and adjustable separation of growth and production phases by
the variation of inducer concentration and induction time [22].
1.5
The concept of OFF switch
In order to construct a C. glutamicum strain, plasmid with a gene coding of antibiВӯ
otic resistance for kanamycin, the lacI gene (LacI) and the gene for the product
of interest was inserted downstream of the promoter, lac operator and ribosome
binding site sequence. Reporter proteins mCherry and GFPuv signals are used
to demonstrate the state of the switch. For the OFF switch, it is referred to GFPuv
expression as ON and BetI/mCherry expression as OFF. BetI/PBetI works as an
ON/OFF switch.
Betaine inhibitor (BetI), acts as a repressor and betI promoters are negatively
regulated by BetI [23]. BetI is an unusual repressor since it remains bound to
DNA during induction. Also, the position of this repressor gene within the operon
is located at the beginning of the operon while the most regulatory genes are
positioned at the end of the operon [24]. The PBetI promoter was used to, repress
BetI promoter. BetI Вӯ PbetI, repressor Вӯ promoter pair works as an ON/OFF switch.

1.6
Cutinase
Cutinase (E.C. 3.1.1.74) enzyme catalyzes the hydrolysis reactions of cutin and
was originally discovered in phytopathogenic fungi that uses cutin as carbon source
[25]. It is serine esterase belonging to the Оұ/ОІ hydrolase superfamily and containВӯ
ing the classical Ser, His, Asp triad [26]. Cutinase shows several characteristics
belonging to both lipase and esterase enzymes. Cutinase is able to hydrolyse lipid
substrates, unlike the majority of esterases [27]. Unlike typical lipases, cutinase
activity is not activated by interfacial effects, it efficiently hydrolyzes hydrophoВӯ
bic substrates in solution or emulsions [28]. It is able to hydrolyze a wide variety
of substrates such as soluble low molecular weight esters, short and long chain
triacylglycerols, natural and synthetic polyesters and waxes. It also catalyses esВӯ
terification and transesterification reactions [29].
Biocatalysts as cutinase have been used as an alternative to chemical catalysts
in the industry due to their enzymatic characteristics such as high reaction rate,
catalytic activity in both directions, high selectivity against the type of reaction,
high substrate specificity and high activity at room temperature. In addition, the
use of biocatalysts against chemical catalysts reduces the environmental impact
and consumes less energy, which makes them cost effective. However, biocatВӯ
alysts have some disadvantages in the industry as, the enzymes are not stable
at the high temperatures, they are not reusable and it is difficult to maintain conВӯ
trol of the reaction. There are several methods to improve the characteristics of
enzymes, either by modifying the enzyme itself or by techniques that improve its
performance at the industrial level [29].
Cutinase hydrolyzes a broad range of substrates such as pВӯnitrophenyl esters of
fatty acids and triglycerides [30]. It has various properties for applications in indusВӯ
trial products and processes. Cutinase can be used in the dairy industry for the
hydrolysis of milk fat, or in household detergents for grease or stain removal proВӯ
cesses [25]. Recently, the usage of cutinase on degradation of plastics gained a
lot of interest and it can be a sustainable solution for the environment [31],[32],[33].

Results
In this study, a novel inducible expression system for C. glutamicum was tested for
its use to control production of cutinase enzyme. The construct of the strain works
as an inverted control of gene expression. In presence of inducer IPTG, IPTG
binds to the LacI protein and prevents the blockage of Ptac by LacI. Then, Ptac
promoter expresses regulator betI gene and, BetI and mCherry are produced. BetI
blocks PBetI and represses GFPuv production. In the absence of IPTG inducer,
GFPuv reporter is expressed, since LacI binds to Ptac promoter and prevents it
to be activated.
The performance of inducible constructs was evaluated, which avoid addition of
inducers to the main culture. The high concentrations of inducer that have been
used to induce the lac promoter, do not necessarily lead to a higher expression
of the target protein, since it can cause metabolic burden to the cells due to overВӯ
expression of the genes to produce protein production and secretion during the
bioprocess [55], [17], [56]. Therefore, in this study, the turnover point of inducВӯ
tion was investigated using C. glutamicum WT (pEKEx2 _BetI _mCherry _PBetI
_GFPuv) strain against C. glutamicum WT (pEKEx2) strain. An experiment was
set up in 96Вӯwell microplate using different precultures with and without IPTG and
main culture conditions with a gradient of IPTG concentration. Normalized fluoresВӯ
cence intensity over different IPTG concentrations was observed to characterize
the OFF switch behaviour. The addition of IPTG should trigger BetI production that
represses the PBetI hence the GFPuv production. Following that, a growth exВӯ
periment has been conducted to investigate OFF switch behavior in the course of
time. An enzymatic assay was developed in order to determine the cutinase activВӯ
ity in culture supernatants of C. glutamicum. Determined conditions were tested
on C. glutamicum CR099 (pEKEX2_Epr_Cutinase) and C. glutamicum CR099
(pEKEx2_BetI_mCherry_PBetI_Epr_cutinase_GFPuv) strains.

Discussion
The IPTG concentration that is used for induction, should be optimized for indiВӯ
vidual protein production since the unit price of IPTG reagent is expensive and
yields a higher cost in scale up production. Current expression systems for C.
glutamicum, require the addition of the inducer to the main culture for the secreВӯ
tory production of heterologous proteins. This results in continuous addition of
inducer during the continuous fermentation process, in order to maintain growth
after induction in the case of metabolic burden to the cells which would reduce
the yields of production [57]. Also, the constant addition of the inducer is needed
in repeated fedВӯbatch fermentation, in order to achieve high cell density cultures
and improved protein yields. One of the most widely used methods to control gene
expression is molecular switch systems [58]. With the help of different synthetic
biology approaches, a switch system has been constructed in C. glutamicum that
allows to invert the use of the inducer molecule (Jing Shen, unpublished). Using
this switch system, it was assumed that the inducer (in this case IPTG) will be
added to the preculture and dilution of the inducer can trigger the production durВӯ
ing main cultivation. This approach can reduce the amount of IPTG used while
the transcription level is preserved, therefore, the production cost can be further
decreased. Also, the washing steps could be avoided for removing the IPTG from
the preculture as it can cause loss of the cells and make the process even harder.
The focus of this study was to first, characterize the previously constructed genetic
inverted switch and determine the optimal IPTG concentration for induction. Later,
the same switch system was characterized with the addition of cutinase gene and
observed the performance of secreted cutinase protein production. The desired
protein product was repressed(switched OFF) in preculture by addition of IPTG,
because this could affect the cell growth. Later in the batch, in the absence of
IPTG, the protein synthesis was switched ON.

The OFF switch was characterized by using C. glutamicum WT (pEKEx2_BetI
_mCherry_PBetI_GFPuv) strain in different preculture and main culture condiВӯ

sence of IPTG. Main cultivation was performed with a gradient of IPTG concenВӯ

mCherry over different IPTG concentrations were monitored to characterize the
inverted switch behaviour. As expected, in the presence of IPTG, IPTG would

Therefore, the fluorescence intensity of mCherry was increased and GFPuv inВӯ

switched OFF fully at 50 ОјM IPTG concentration. For the following increased conВӯ

after reaching the full repression and from figure 3.2 it can be seen that the GFВӯ

5
Future Work
In future studies, the IPTG range could be further looked into. The data from
the microplate cultivation was not entirely reliable since some fluctuations in the
results happened during inducing with 0.2 mM IPTG. By investigating more differВӯ
ent concentrations in the range of 0.05Вӯ0.4 mM IPTG, more reliable data could be
achieved to determine the optimal concentration of IPTG for induction.
Even though the addition of cutinase gene to the OFF switch system did not afВӯ
fect the switch behavior, the cutinase activity was reached to a lower value comВӯ
pared to conventional switch strain. Therefore, further optimization needed to be
conducted in order to improve the cutinase secretion. Also, the conditions were
determined from the experiments in this study, could be tested in larger volumes
of fermenter in order to evaluate and optimize the scale up production.
Other improvements could be achieved, such as looking into the choice of signal
peptide and secretion pathway which might help in the process of increasing the
protein secretion in strains. While the activity of cutinase by secretion through the
Sec pathway tends to be higher than the Tat pathway, studies have also shown
that recombinant proteins can have a tendency to get stuck in the Sec translocase
complex [2]. Furthermore, the correct folding of the proteins, before secretion
mediated by the Tat pathway, can enhance the stability of the secreted proteins
[14].
The choice of bioprocess conditions differs when are applied in scale up producВӯ
tion and this can influence the performance of the strain. For going from shake
flasks to bioreactor for the cultivation, further characterization is required for scale
up productions. The next step was to determine optimal conditions for a bigger
scale production and test if the OFF switch was efficient to use. However, due
to time limitations, this could not be further looked at. Knowing the optimal IPTG
concentration was required to fully repress the cutinase production, the amount
of dilutions needed to inoculate the main culture from preculture can be deterВӯ

==PAGE:59==
mined. FedВӯbatch fermentation can be chosen for cutinase production since, it
was shown in previous published works that, fedВӯbatch control strategies improve
the formation of the target protein [61], [62], [63]. FedВӯbatch bioprocesses allow to
overcome oxygen limitation at higher cell densities and in large scale bioreactors.
Also, the specific growth rate (Ој) of the culture can be controlled in order to reduce
the undesired byВӯproduct formation and maximize the product formation rate [60].

6
Conclusions
Secreted protein production by microorganisms is an appealing strategy since the
downstream process is simple, and the excretion of recombinant proteins out of
cells can reduce the physiological stress that could accumulate from overexpresВӯ
sion of a protein in the cytoplasm [64]. The induction conditions are one of the
key parameters in the production of the recombinant proteins and they need to be
optimized to increase the yield of the desired product.
In this study, the OFF switch in C. glutamicum which provides inverted use of
the inducer molecule, was characterized. Availability of this OFF switch can help
reduce the use of IPTG. The addition of IPTG repressed the secreted cutinase
protein and the GFPuv protein production while expressing mCherry production.
The IPTG concentration that is needed to fully repress cutinase production was
found as, 100 ОјM IPTG. It is 10 times lower than the amount of conventional
induction concentration which was 1 mM [59]. The decreased IPTG usage is
an advantage since the unit price of IPTG is 664.00 DKK per gram [65] and on
bigger scale, the production cost could be quite high if an excessive amount of
IPTG concentration was used. The inducer concentrations that range between
0.05Вӯ0.2 mM IPTG, can be studied to determine the lowest possible concentration
for heterologous protein expression process. Furthermore, it was demonstrated
that induction with IPTG did not have a negative effect on cell growth. During the
experiments, an enzymatic assay was also developed and optimized in order to
measure cutinase activity in the culture supernatants.
The addition of cutinase gene to the plasmid did not affect the inverted switch beВӯ
havior and showed good results in terms of repression/expression of the genes.
Even though the mCherry and GFPuv productions were more efficient than the
switch without cutinase, the cutinase activity was reached to a lower value comВӯ
pared to conventional switch strain. Therefore, further optimization needed to be
conducted in order to improve the cutinase secretion.
47
==PAGE:61==
In conclusion, the production of secreted proteins in C. glutamicum can be conВӯ
trolled by the inducible expression system that works with an inverted genetic
switch. The inverted switch also provides an opportunity to reduce the IPTG use,
up to 10Вӯ20 times less compared to the recommended amounts of IPTG concenВӯ
trations and allows reducing the production cost of the secreted proteins.
