Abstract
Due to the continuously changing market and increasing client requirements, companies are fac-
ing new challenges every day. To stay competitive, improvement in strategy, product quality,
and time-to-market delivery is required. Nowadays, agile project management is perceived as a
reasonable solution for these struggles, and a great number of enterprises decided to implement
a new way of working. However, the transformation process, because of its complexity, often
requires external consultants’ support with experience in change management.
This dissertation investigates how consulting companies conduct the implementation of agile
project management and highlights the similarities and diﬀerences in the process. The research
is based on the qualitative method. The interviews were conducted at two consulting companies.

1
Introduction
Due to rapidly progressing globalization current market is changing at high speed. This is a
constant race between companies to become competitive, and it drives the signiﬁcant increment
of project complexity, stakeholders involvement, huge investments, and several other aspects that
daily associate business. This all leads to the conclusion that the traditional model of running
projects became insuﬃcient and required to be changed with the aim of executing projects
successfully (9). Many companies ﬁnd Agile Project Management as a reasonable alternative
to a traditional approach. Transformation into Agile might be challenging, however, thanks to
its ﬂexibility, projects are more likely to meet expectations and, in the ﬁnal account, brings
measurable beneﬁts.
1.1
Background
A project is a sequence of unrepeatable, complex, and related tasks with a common goal that has
to be reached in a deﬁned time, does not exceed budget, and meets requirements (10). Because
of the diﬀerent industries companies operate in, there is no universal approach to running the
projects. That is why choosing the most suitable methodology, and tools are crucial to project
success. The traditional project management model is the most common approach to manage
the projects and is broadly described in PMBOK (Project Management Body of Knowledge)
step by step. The book provides manner and tools that could be implemented in the process in
order to ﬁnalize projects with satisfactory product or service. However, projects have become
much more dynamic over the years and require more commitment from the execution team.
This factor brings many challenges for the companies because they cannot react to unexpected
situations smoothly; hence, the project’s ﬁnal result is not that proﬁtable as it was planned in
the initial stage. One example might be a COVID-19 outbreak, which put many ﬁrms from
various industries in serious trouble.
1.2
Purpose and Research Questions
This dissertation aims to present diﬀerent approaches to implementing agile project management
by using two consulting companies’ examples. During the research, the diﬀerences and similar-
ities will be investigated in both examples. Moreover, hence the implementation process brings
many challenges, and a number of factors have to be taken care of this thesis will answer the
question of how to deal with the human factor.
Problem formulation is the following:
What are the diﬀerences and similarities in the implementation of agile project
management process between consulting companies?

2.1
Traditional Project Management
Traditional project management presumes that the client has clearly deﬁned needs, work re-
alization deadline, and budget that is eager to commit for project execution. This approach
works very well when the goal and technique to achieve it is clear and formulated transparently.
However, also the important aspect is the low probability of scope change during project (11).

Traditional project management is conducted by following appropriately deﬁned project man-
agement processes. There are various ways to group those processes however, the PMBOK Guide
elaborate ﬁve process categories and named them Process Groups:
Initiating Process Group
In this step, speciﬁc processes are performed to deﬁne a new phase in ongoing projects or a new
project to obtain approval to start.
Planning Process Group
In this step, there is a deﬁned scope of the project, clarifying the project’s objectives and deter-
mining the path to follow to meet the project’s objectives.
Executing Process Group
In this step are performed the necessary actions to obtain deﬁned in the planning step objectives
in order to fulﬁll projects requirements.
Monitoring and Controlling Process Group
In this step, project progress and activities are tracked, reviewed, and regulated, identifying areas
in the plan that might require modiﬁcation and implementation.
Closing Process Group
In this step all necessary actions that are required to conduct formal closing of the project are
taken (1).
Advantages of traditional approach to project management are:
−Prepare detailed project plan
−Lack of high qualiﬁcation requirements for project team members
−Clearly deﬁned necessary resources to carry out the project
Disadvantages of traditional approach to project management are:
−Limited possibility of making changes during project execution
−Creation of very detailed project plans
−Risk of high project costs
−Risk of exceeding project realization time
−Putting aside client’s real needs (12)

2.2
PRINCE2
PRINCE2 methodology is an international project management standard descended from Great
Britain. The name comes from the ﬁrst letters of "PRoject IN Controlled Environments". The
methodology is owned by the British Crown, on behalf of which the Oﬃce of Government Com-
merce manages the method. Methodology has been created in 70’ when Simpact System LTD
formulated the methodology PROMPT (Project Resource Organization Management Planning
Technique). Initially, methodology was used to execute projects from the IT industry. In 1989,
PROMPT changed its name to PRINCE and maintained until 1996 when the updated version
PRINCE2 was created. In 2009 another standards update took place, introducing signiﬁcant
changes in structure and shape. In 2017 based on many experts from management area experi-
ences, PRINCE2 methodology has been updated (13).
Figure 2: Project Management certiﬁcation popularity (2)
There are many diﬀerent project management methodologies used worldwide. Their popular-
ity, among the others, depends on and geographical location. As illustrated in ﬁgure 2 in Europe,
the most popular used traditional project management methodology is PRINCE2. Whereas, for
comparison in the USA most frequently used methodology is PMP. Choosing the right certi-
ﬁcation in the company might help encourage potential employees to apply for a position in
certain companies and make their onboarding smoother. Frequently, seen practice is that the
customer can decide which speciﬁc project management methodology should be used to run the
project. Hence, the project manager is required to be certiﬁcated in a particular methodology.
A good example is a public sector where for instance, in Poland, this area is monopolized by the
PRINCE2, and the government contractors are enforced to have the exact number of workers
with proven qualiﬁcation by the certiﬁcate (2).

PRINCE2 Structure

Methodology PRINCE2 consists of following elements:
−Principles are the core elements of the methodology - universal principles with recognized
value, typical for every project executed in compliance with methodology regardless of the
size, industry, complexity level, and geographical location. Principles deﬁning the project
as PRINCE2 and make it possible to distinguish it from project carry out according to
diﬀerent methodologies.
The Source of these principles are bad and good experiences
collected by authors (14). These are seven principles:
1. Continued Business Justiﬁcation - a project must make sense from the business per-
spective. Investment return must be clear and use of the resources has to be justiﬁed.
2. Learn from Experience - project team should be accommodated in knowledge from
projects carried out in the past and update the knowledge from lessons learned during
the latest projects.
3. Deﬁne Roles and Responsibilities - Every team member should be aware of responsi-
bilities area of other team members and who is main decision maker.
4. Manage by Stages - In order to cope with diﬃcult tasks it is advisable to divide them
into smaller parts or stages.
5. Manage by Exception - Project that follows the plan and do not come across any delays
or other problems do not requires managers intervention. Higher level manager are

only informed when the problem occurred or is anticipated to occur.
6. Focus on Product - All involved members should be aware in advance of ﬁnal exception
regarding the product. This makes easier to plan working activities.
7. Tailor to the Environment - Methodology might be scaled and tailored to the project
to succeed (15).
−Themes - in PRINCE2 version from 2009 existing components aﬃliated name of themes.
Themes are categories that the project must stick to all-time during the project life cycle.
These seven themes are:
1. Business case - this theme forces management to consider what is the main goal of the
project, what are the costs and beneﬁts. Based on this information theme provides
an answer for the question about project achievability and whether it is worthwhile
to carry out or not.
2. Organization - as a projects require cooperation between experts from diﬀerent ares
this theme deﬁne everyone’s role and responsibility and make sure all activities are
going to work well together.
3. Quality - this theme provides deﬁned quality requirements about ﬁnal product. It is
very important to have this information at the beginning of the projects because that
let project team to keep on track and avoid unnecessary costs.
4. Plans - this theme provides and instruction how previously deﬁned goal will be
achieved.
Planning strategy factors like cost, timescale, product, quality etc.
are
taken into account.
5. Risk - this theme aims to identify, assess and when possible anticipate potential risk
that may occur during project. It is desirable that unexpected events do not happened
however, in case they do the contingency plan is ready to mitigate risk.
6. Change - the situation that happen very often during project life cycle is change. The
clue of this theme is to smoothly deal with new situations that were not deﬁned at
the beginning of the project.
7. Progress - to be sure that the project is progressing according to the plan it is necessary
to measure achievements. It might be done e.g. by reporting after given time or by
setting certain milestones and assess factor like cost, time, quality (14).
−Processes - creates core of the methodology. Because of them project is carried out from
the controlled start until the controlled end. According to the PRINCE2 project man-
agement is done by seven processes. In the previous version there were eight processes
but certain processes has been merged. Each process consists of a few steps. However,
while processes are mandatory, the way of their execution depends on project manager and
management board to which belongs decision about tailoring methodology to the current
project environment (13).

2.4
Agile Project Management
Agile Project Management appeared to fulﬁll the need for realization projects in an environment
of increased uncertainty.
Methodology and presuppositions have been developed and imple-
mented as a solution to improve operating in software design. However, nowadays, methodology,
because of its comprehensive nature, is also used in many diﬀerent projects. Some authors em-
phasize the meaning of simplifying what makes Agile similar to lean thinking, where a core idea
is a ﬂexibility and simplicity. As a result, the project focuses on the suitability of individual
elements and on setting priorities during project execution (10).
In the Agile approach, a project is broken down into small units that are easier to manage.
The outcome of a ﬁnalized unit should be a working solution. Break down the project into
smaller units allows set activities and goals that prioritize the execution process. Project into
smaller units improves immediate modiﬁcation, adding or redeﬁning when needed.
Advantages of Agile Project Management:
−possibility of launching the project without lack of knowledge of overall plan to achieve
project’s goal
−systematizing work over the project in the proper way even though the overall plan is
unknown
−the ﬂow of added business value for the customer is always on point of concentration of
project management as well as each team member
Disadvantages of Agile Project Management :
−lack of advantage over traditional project management in traditional projects

2.5
Agile Manifesto
In 2001 in Utah 17 software developers gathered to discuss how to improve software development.
Their main idea was to "by doing it and helping others do it". As a result of this work something
called the Agile Manifesto has been created that is based on the 4 core values:
Team vision and discipline over individuals and interactions
In the year of 2001 software development witnessed big expansion and it was discovered that
interaction between peopled is much more important than following strictly outlined process.
Hence, the founders stated in the Manifesto that "individual and interactions" mattered more
than "processes and tools". After a years of experience Kent Beck redeﬁned a function of the
individuals in a team. More beneﬁcial for the project was when team member think how "we"
can do the job instead of "he" or "she". Individuals interacting may optimize their own work.
Beck concept says that team vision that goes beyond each member optimization is a crucial to
achieve greater progress (16).

Validated learning over working software
Beck also noticed that paying too much focus on making detailed documentation was not suf-
ﬁcient to release full potential. Very often encountered problem was how to ﬁnd a customer
willing to buy your product. Well working software only partly solved this issue so in a result
Beck decided that validated learning should be put on the top of comprehensive documentation
and working software (16).
Customer discovery over customer collaboration
2001 was the year when was realized that rapidly changing world required new approach in soft-
ware development. Since plans discussed in the beginning of the project very often were outdated
the customer collaboration was in the ﬁrst place. Exception was a startup environment because
the new brand does not have customers and has to discover how to attract them (16).
Responding to change over following a plan
Traditional approach believed that the best practise to develop software was plan in the begin-
ning and follow. As in other aspects rapidly changing world forced to drop this idea and became
more ﬂexible. In this moment leadership plays crucial role. Leader is in charge of anticipated
incoming risk and suggests to begin the change (16).

Structure of Scrum as listed below provides crucial artifacts, roles and activities that needs
to be followed to success with Scrum process:
Scrum artifacts:
Product Backlog: The list of requested items by customer. Constantly being updated and prior-
itized. This is an overview that scrum team has to complete (20).
User Stories: The items listed in the Backlog coming from user stories. It is a user perspec-
tive stories that include parts of functionality or values that team works on. Written in a simple
way and describing the ideas in nontechnical way (20).
Backlog Sizing: Estimation of product Backlog size.
Sprint Backlog: Product Backlog items that have to be done by development team during Sprint.
It is forecast by the development team and decided what work is needed to be done to deliver
the expected functionality of the product. (21).
Burndown Chart: Graphical presentation of remaining tasks of the spring are progressing.
Acceptance Criteria: Provides the step that has to be taken before the story is considered as
done.
Scrum Roles:
Scrum Master: This role is for the person responsible for keeping the team up to date with any
changes and makes sure all activities follow Scrum practices. This person is an intermediary
between a customer who describes the product and requirements and the development team. It
happens that a person from the development team is a Scrum master (22).
Product Owner: A person in this position is responsible for setting product backlog and make
sure that the development team’s eﬀort meet customer’s requirements (20).
Customer: The person or organization who order the product.
Development Team: Group of 5 to 9 members of professionals from diﬀerent areas that work
on the product and is responsible for delivering it within certain number of sprints (23).
Other Stakeholders: Individuals that are not actively taking part in the Scrum process. Other
stakeholders might be directors, project managers or customers (21).

Scrum Activities:
Sprint Planning: In this crucial meeting are included employees assigned to a diﬀerent Scrum
role. There is no deﬁned how long this meeting should take due to diﬀerent organization’s pref-
erences. This meeting’s major task is to deﬁne which stories should be executed in the upcoming
sprint and which can be postponed to another. Required presents on these meetings are for a
development team, Scrum master, and product owner.
Daily Stand-up: Daily ﬁfteen-minute meeting between development team and Scrum master.
Goal of the meeting is to share status with others members and deﬁne future activities. Most
teams take up stand-ups in the morning and discuss what has been done previous day, what
is to be done today and what problems individuals faced that might have an impact on daily
activities (22).
Sprint Review: After the completed sprint work is presented to the stakeholders including cus-
tomer to received feedback. At the end of the sprint review takes place.
Sprint Retrospectives: Meeting where development team with scrum master exchange experi-
ences and discuss the feedback from the previous sprint and plan how to improve work in next
sprint

2.9
Deﬁnition Of Change Management
According to the literature, change is deﬁned in numerous ways. One way is to described it as
an event evolving through phases of "frozen, unfrozen and refrozen" (29). Another way is to
say that change is an innovative phase that contrasts with the previous one. (30). Moreover, a
theory claims that change is an empiric prove of various states, roles, etc. These states might be
seen in the way of the team’s work, approaches to solve the problem, the ﬁnal outcome of the
company’s project, or functioning of the company (31). Whatever deﬁnition is taken as a right
one, they all agree that any change is possible without transformation in one or more areas of
company structure and activities. The general experience of every change is that it depends on
a number of factors (32).
2.10
History of Change Management
Change Management is a discipline developed by years of research in various areas such as
psychology, management, and leadership (6). Due to its versatility, it has been adapted in an
organization’s life-cycle all over the world throughout the years. It has to oﬀer many change
models, strategies, and outcomes that allow using their practices in companies regardless of their
current situation, industry, size, and need for change (6)

Change types group characterise scale and duration of the change. This is crucial to identify
the nature of the change. Whether transformation will impact entire organization (big) or just
a part of it (small). Also the period of time that change process will be present in the daily
company’s performance. Having this aspects clearly identify managers are able to structure work
as well as choose the most suitable method to conduct the process (33). "Understanding where
your organization sits today and what processes it needs to improve, change or transform is the
ﬁrst step toward introducing business process change discipline" (34).
Change scale deﬁnes what part of the company is needed to be inﬂuenced by the process to
reach the expected outcome. It interferes in processes and employees’ behavior (35) but also
many other stakeholders, including leadership, and requires strong collaboration (36). Due to
the complexity of the large-scale change, the strategy insists on the culture alignment on the
lower level of the organization such as departmental and units (37). Changes conducted on a
small scale are marked by lower complexity level and does not require leadership involvement
and are signiﬁcantly easier to conduct and manage (6).
Change duration refers to the period of time the change process is going to last. Long-time
changes are much more challenging and require a greater investment of resources from the or-

ganization. Leadership must be fully committed to the change process and make sure the em-
ployees are also strongly supportive. Understanding people’s experiences allows designing the
change strategy to be keen to be involved and might positively inﬂuence and ﬁnalize change with
success (38). On the other hand, short-term changes have a much higher rate of success (38).
The need for small change recognized quickly and in favorable conditions gives an advantage over
the competition on the market and makes adaptation in a new environment easier (39). Wisely

Change enablers are the factors that have to been taken care of during change in order to

align in order to conduct comprehensive change: content, people and process. Content covers
matters related to strategy, technologies, systems and work practices. As a technology plays sig-

The research process started with a literature review of relevant publications. Hence, the im-

Their profession’s speciﬁcation allows seeing the overall picture based on the experience of con-

in diﬀerent companies, which enriches data used to analyze the problem.
3.2
Interviews
Interviews are the primary method of collecting data. This method allows to see the same issue
from diﬀerent perspectives hence, every person perceives a certain situation diﬀerently because
of their experience. The discussion involves personal information like opinion, beliefs and judg-
ments (47). To create the most comprehensive picture of the implementation process, interviews
with consultants seem to be the most objective method. It presents the experience from diﬀerent
environments.

interview and gave time to prepare. That approach allows to maintain a ﬂow of the conversation
and presents consultants’ thoughts. Thus, it was expected to receive diﬀerent answers all phases
were discussed with every single interviewee separately before the following phase was discussed.

Adaptation Phase
The adaptation phase is a moment in the implementation process when all arrangements and
training are tested in real-life environment. It may be tested in two ways. The ﬁrst approach is
"go all-in," which means working in an agile way by all employees and projects that have been
decided to transform. It brings a lot of confusion and a reduction in the quality and achievability
of objectives in the beginning. This is caused by a lack of experience and a time-consuming
learning process. However, in a longer time perspective, more people will be familiar with the
methodology. Potential ﬂuency in working agile will be possessed sooner by the company’s entire
desired area. The second approach is "start small" which requires select a so-called pilot team.
This is the group of employees fully allocated on one project and working only in agile. This
approach allows practicing how employees understand their new roles and tests other solutions
and adjustments on a small team. A pilot team allows adjusting employees to the methodology
without impacting the company’s daily business on a big scale.
“In theory it simple. You just let them work for some weeks or maybe months and see how it
works. You have to give some time to the team to align and probably do the training meanwhile
and discuss how it can be implemented before doing it.”

Discussion
This chapter aims to discuss and interpreter the result of ﬁndings presented in the previous chap-
ter. Hence, there has been presented two models in the last chapter so both will be analysed and
compared.
First case company "X" starts the implementation process by gathering information from
the management board. The meeting during the preparation phase includes discussion about
the current company’s situation and follows burning platform analysis. The strategy that de-
scribes an overall situation in the company. As an outcome, there is a clear view of the reason
to transform and the consequences of not changing the present state. The reason behind cre-
ating burning platform is to improve the awareness of the threat by not challenging the status
quo and engagement among management board members. Creating one consolidated overview
by consultants and management board aligns the vision of the future state after transforma-
tion and enables the set of the main objective. The next step is to create a framework for the
change. Consultants coordinate the entire process and support every involved employee during
the transformation however, to minimize resistance, it needs to be checked what qualiﬁcations
are possessed by the employees and adjust the plan to it. During the meeting with employees,
there are also discussed matters related to the project’s speciﬁcations. The main focus is on
the struggles faced and ﬁnding the bottlenecks. This information helps decide whether the agile
methodology is able to provide the desired solution. Depending on the enterprise’s size and
way of running, business partners to interview are chosen. It might be stakeholders within the
company or the clients. Gathered input is analyzed, and it is decided if the agile methodology
is suitable for the client and if it can help. If the decision is positive among the management
board, it is selected the methodology and the main responsible person to coordinate and decide
implementation activities. Subsequently, there is developed an announcement strategy. Hence,
it is expected to face resistance the number of employees to inform at each meeting is reduced to
the minimum, and it is agreed that the person to announce the change will be from the closest
environment.
On the other hand, case company "Y" conducts preparation meeting in a slightly diﬀerent way
and less decision has been made. As a baseline, it is discussed the main goal to achieve by the
transformation. The implementation process cannot be started without one clear vision approved
by the management board. It is discussed what areas agile methodology should improve and
how the work after transformation should be. The consultants from company "Y" must under-
stand what the client expects but also familiarize the client with the expected implementation
process. What action will be taken, and what reaction is expected from the employees. To do
that, consultants refer to innovation adaptation curve.

It can be seen that range of activities taken during preparation phase varies between compa-

On the other hand company "Y" treats preparation phase as phase to make client’s familiar

companies is an initial meeting with management board where it is developed a company’s vision
and goal to reach by the transformation. Both companies believe that aligning the objectives is

It does not strictly deﬁne where one phase ends and the other begins. Some phases overlap
others, like the preparation phase and employees’ analysis phase during implementation by com-

a change plan and potentially involve employees to support the process. Also, in that phase,
employees are notiﬁed about the change happening in the company, and the change team faces
expected resistance from the employees’ side. Consultants and responsible person from the com-
pany are in charge of ﬁnding out the reason for resistance and explaining why the change is
necessary. This process aims to build awareness among the employees about the change and
try to convince them to cooperation. However, there is an attempt to convince, but without
much eﬀort from the change team, employees cannot stop the implementation, so it would be
very supportive if they accept the change and cooperate, but their approval is unnecessary. It
is essential to inform employees about happening changes. Company "Y" promote employees

preferences. To gain insight into what is desired consultant with selected person who is high in

communication, relations in the department and projects speciﬁcation. During project analysis
the main focus is on the problems and areas where employees see a need of improvement. These
information are necessary to consider in the meeting among consultants whether agile can help
and to develop workshop framework.
Both companies perceive employees’ role in the process diﬀerently. Company "X" treats em-
ployees as an additional factor that needs to be considered in the implementation. Purpose of
discussion with employees is to help consultants organize the change. Whereas, company "Y"
designs process and methodology tailored to the employees. During workshops consultants pre-
sented a framework, how to work according to a new methodology but the most important was
employees’ behavior, attitude, personality and preferences. Participants’ opinion was valuable
and deeply analysed with other input gathered during workshops and has a signiﬁcant impact
on the ﬁnal shape of the methodology.

Limitations and Further Research Suggestions
6.1
Limitations
The consulting companies case study as a research subject has several limitations that might
impact the ﬁnal result. The case study might be diﬃcult to redo, which results in low accuracy
of generalizability of the results. Additionally, personal opinions and discussed examples from
consultants’ experience might cause researcher bias.
Consulting companies characterize in working at client’s site which requires the additional
agreement of cooperation between researcher and client which was reached only in a minor part
of the research. Due to safety matters related to the COVID-19 restriction, clients preferred to
limit the number of external visitors hence, the presence of a researcher at the site was impossi-
ble. That constrain aﬀected size of the analysed data. Most data were collected via interviews
and presents consultants personal opinion regarding researched topic. Recognition of the most
appropriate interviewee was principally challenging by reason of conﬁdentiality agreement with
clients and eagerness to share relevant information. Moreover, due to interviewees working time
experience and industry the point of view vary. As a consequence the ﬁnal result indicated com-
bination of real case and personal experience.
6.2
Further Research Suggestion
Consulting companies might be bias during interviews and provide a data presenting own work
in a more favorable position than it is in reality. Dissertation research is also a good PR op-
portunity for the company, so consultants can describe certain situations or activities during
implementation without the mistakes that have been made. To get an objective opinion regard-
ing consultants’ work, the solution can be to interview the client where the researched consulting
company conducted the implementation. It could also reveal consultants’ approaches in a few
dimensions. The client might provide an opinion about interpersonal relations, the diligence of
work, and whether the used techniques led to ﬁnalize implementation with success or failure.
Furthermore, to get a holistic overview of the consultants’ entire implementation process, it
is advised to research within one company that is currently going through the transformation or
did that recently. This can improve the reliability of the interviewees. The recent memories are
more detailed and show a connection between activities in a speciﬁc environment.

Conclusion
The purpose of this section is to highlight major conclusion of the research questions
The research questions in this master thesis are:
What are the diﬀerences and similarities in the implementation of agile project management
process between consulting companies?
How consulting companies manage employees during implementation of agile project manage-
ment process?
The main ﬁndings are:
Throughout the entire implementation of the agile project management process, both compa-
nies present certain similarities and diﬀerences. Also, the way of managing employees is diﬀerent,
and shapes inﬂuence the activities taken during the implementation. In both approaches, the
process starts with a preparation meeting that discusses common vision goals and the purpose
of the implementation. These aspects are perceived as essential for a successful transformation.
This alignment provides a sense of support for the consultants and enables to structure the
following activities. Other matters discussed in the initial phase are to show the diﬀerences in
the approach and put some light on what strategy is taken by the consultants. Company "X"
prefers to develop the entire process, including selecting the methodology with the management
board, whereas company "Y" explains the adaptation process of innovation by employees and
leaves the decision about change execution and methodology selection to develop in cooperation
with employees. Company "X" announces the change to the employees piece by piece and waits
for announcement until consultants are ready to take the next step in the process and the em-
ployees are required to participate. Company "Y" put employees in the center of the process.
It empowers the employees to run the change, gather input, have an impact on the process and
value their opinion as a signiﬁcant factor. The goal of the employees’ analysis for the company
"X" is to learn what experience employees have regarding the change. This approach aims to
reduce confusion among employees and the management board. Change activities that are famil-
iar for employees make the process smoother to conduct by consultants and track the progress
of implementation of the methodology chosen during the preparation meeting. Company "Y"
decides the methodology based on conducted workshops during employees’ analysis phase where
employees are getting familiar with the methodology and evaluated their personality and engage-
ment, which result in assigning to a certain role after implementation. During adaptation and
implementation phase both companies align again. Between two strategies, "go all in" and "start
small" there is selected the latter one. First is selected pilot team to work in a new methodology
and subsequently the project. Based on the arose issues related to the performance and work
