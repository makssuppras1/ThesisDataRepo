Abstract 
 
 
 
Silicon (Si) is the most widely used semiconductors in power devices. But Si-based power devices 
are approached their material limits, which has led to find alternatives to Si-based power devices 
for better performance. Silicon Carbide (SiC) power devices have evolved to be a viable alternative 
to Si-based power devices in high power and high efficiency applications. They have several 
advantages: high breakdown voltage, high operating temperature, high switching frequency and 
low loss. Despite these excellent advantages, the reliability of gate oxide and stability of threshold 
voltage in SiC devices such as SiC MOSFET has not been verified. 
This project presents different characteristics of SiC MOSFETs compared with Si MOSFETs and Si 
IGBTs. Moreover a heat control circuit is made to measure switching loss of a transistor in 
different junction temperatures. Then the switching loss in four devices: a Si IGBT, a SiC MOSFET 
and two normally-on SiC JFETs is measured with double pulse tester (DPT) at different junction 
temperatures.  
Result shows that SiC devices have lower switching loss compare with Si IGBTs and their switching 
loss stays almost constant in different temperatures while switching loss in IGBTs increase 
significantly with elevated temperature. Regardless of reliability, the advantages of SiC MOSFET 
make it a supper alternative to Si devices in power conversion systems such as DC-DC converters 
in order to achieve higher efficiency.

 1    Introduction 
 
 
 
 
 
1-1              Background  
 
As the demand of electric energy increase, the importance of renewable energy systems and 
energy efficiency appliance such as hybrid vehicles increases. In these systems, power conditioner 
such as converters have a great influence on the efficiency. A main part of efficiency of these 
power devices is related to the amount of loss in the switches that they use. For many years Si-
based switches such as IGBTs and MOSFETs have been used in power conditioners which produce 
high loss. As the demand of more compact and highly efficient equipment increases Si-based 
switches cannot meet the demand; then the wide band gap switches such as SiC devices are taken 
into consider due to their low conducting and switching loss generation.  
Researching on SiC switches started at 1980s and in the following two decades the performance of 
these devices improved immensely [1][2]. The first SiC power MOSFET was proposed by Palmour 
in 1992 and in 1996 SiC MOSFET structure was developed by Shenoy successfully [3]. As the 
improvement of this device continued, SiC MOSFETs have become one of the most promising 
candidates for the next generation of power switches [4].  
 
1-2           Silicon Carbide power MOSFETs 
 
Devices based on Silicon (Si) have been used as dominate power semiconductors in past decades 
such as diodes, metal-oxide semiconductor field-effect transistors (MOSFETs), bipolar junction 
transistors (BJTs), insulated-gate bipolar transistors (IGBTs) and etc.  As in the market the demand 
of more compact and highly efficient equipment is growing, Si-based devices cannot fulfill this 
demand due to their inherent limitation of Si semiconductor material such as low breakdown 
voltage, low thermal conductivity and low switching frequency operation. For this reason 
researchers and manufactures have had more attention to the wide band gap semiconductors 
such as Silicon Carbide (SiC) and Gallium Nitride (GaN). Currently, GaN is very expensive to be 
competitive with SiC, therefor SiC semiconductors are more considered [5].    
==PAGE:11==
Page 11 of 78 
 
SiC MOSFETs is one of the attractive switches among SiC devices since they are simple designed, 
normally off and voltage-controlled gate. Currently the maximum voltage rating of IGBTs are 
around 6.5 KV [6]. Power systems use IGBTs in low operation frequency due to their tail current 
which leads to high switching loss. Si MOSFET are able to operate in frequency in range of MHz 
because of their low switching loss but they suffer from high conducting loss due to that their on-
resistance increases with blocking voltage [7]. SiC MOSFETs do not have tail current and exhibit 
significant lower switching loss and as well lower conducting loss because of their lower on-
resistance. Despite SiC MOSFET s exhibit excellent advantages, but the reliability of gate oxide and 
stability of threshold voltage in these devices have not been verified.  
Regardless of low reliability, capability of SiC MOSFETS in reducing significantly switching and 
conducting loss while offering operation in higher junction temperature and higher blocking 
voltage make them excellent substitute for Si devices and enable technology to improve the 
power electronic systems.

Main properties of SiC semiconductors are summarized as following: 
 Low on-resistance: The lower on-resistance of SiC-based devices results low conducting 
loss and thus higher efficiency in power electronic systems. 
 Low switching loss: The low switching loss in SiC switches enable these devices to operate 
in higher switching frequency. This brings reducing the size of passive components, 
==PAGE:13==
Page 13 of 78 
 
reducing in size, weight and cost of the power electronic systems. The size reduction is 
proportional to the increase in frequency. 
 High thermal conductivity: SiC semiconductors have thermal conductivity around three 
times higher than Si semiconductors. Due to it, heat dissipation that caused by loss can be 
conducted within the semiconductor faster. This property simplifies the cooling system. 
 High breakdown electric field: This allows the design of SiC-based power devices with 
thinner layer which leads to decreasing in the size of the device. 
 Large bandgap:  this feature results operating in higher temperature. 
 
 
2-2              SiC power MOSFET 
 
SiC power MOSFET is a popular switch among SiC switches since they are simple designed, 
normally off switch and voltage controlled. 
In high voltage and high current application, IGBT is the most popular power transistor. IGBTs have 
high breakdown voltage and low on-resistance in cost of high switching loss. In the structure of 
this device minority carriers are injected to the drift region in order to reducing the on-resistance; 
but when the transistor turns off, takes time for this minority carrier to dissipated, thus IGBTs have 
turn-off tail current which increases their switching loss. In contrast, SiC MOSFET are majority 
carrier devices and do not have tail currant. Due to that, SiC MOSFETs have all desirable 
characteristics of power switches: high blocking voltage, low on-resistance and low switching loss. 
Therefor they are excellent candidate to substitute IGBTs. Figure 2-1 indicates that conducting and 
switching loss in SiC MOSFETs are lower than Si IGBTs.

Chapter 2-4 described SiC MOSFETs have lower drift layer resistance compare with Si MOSFETs

Since SiC MOSFETs have low threshold voltage and it even decreases with increasing the junction 
temperature, it is recommended to apply negative gate voltage when the device is in off state to

2-7              Thermal stability  
 
Switching and conducting loss in power switches cause rising in junction temperature. Increasing 
the junction temperature leads to increasing the on-resistance and consequently conducting loss. 
When the device conducts significant current, this interaction can cause a positive feedback 
between the on-resistance and the junction temperature which leads to further increasing in both. 
This mutual promotion leads to sharp rise in temperature which is so called thermal runway. Then 
the junction temperature of the device can increase much higher than critical temperature of the 
materials and damage the device.  
Many papers have reported that SiC small signal devices can operate in maximum junction 
temperature of 600° C, and power SiC devices in pulsed testing operate in maximum junction 
temperature of 300°-400°C [15].  Literature [16] investigated the maximum steady state junction 
temperature of SiC MOSFETs measurement is equal to 250°C and the thermal runway temperature 
is closed to 300°C. This temperature is about 100° C higher than SiC JFET and Si power MOSFETs. 
The higher temperature stability of SiC MOSFETs is beacause on-resistance of these devices 
behaves differently to other SiC switches. The on-resistance of SiC MOSFETs divided into two 
parts: channel resistance     and residual resistance    which includes JFET resistance, drift 
region resistance, substrate resistance and contact resistance [17]. The temperature coefficient of

temperature coefficient means that     decreases with elevated temperature. As     has a 
significant portion of the on-resistance, on-resistance does not increase much when the junction

3-4              Gate reliability of SiC MOSFET 
 
Despite SiC MOSFET s exhibit excellent advantages, but the reliability of them has not be verified 
under short –circuit.  
SiC MOSFETs are designed with thinner gate oxide. The reason is that doping concentration in SiC 
is higher than Si materials therefore the threshold voltage in SiC semiconductor is higher. As high 
threshold voltage leads to higher on-resistance[10], in order to achieve desired low threshold 
voltage, SiC MOSFETs is designed with thinner gate oxide compare with Si MOSFETs. The relation 
between doping concentration and threshold voltage in SiC and Si MOSFETs is depicted in figure 3-
6. 
 
 
 
Figure 3-6: Relation between the threshold voltage and doping concentration in SiC and Si MOSFETs [10]

As the maximum electric field in SiC semiconductor is 10 times higher than Si, the electric field in 
the gate oxide of SiC devices is higher than electric field in the gate oxide of Si devices. 
The thinner gate oxide and high electric field in the gate oxide in SiC MOSFETS, causes leakage 
current to flow through the gate- source when a short-circuit happens. This leakage current causes 
reduction in the gate voltage as shown in figure 3-7.  After number of short-circuit the gate can be 
broken while the SiC MOSFET is able to working. 
 
Due to the results of different test that mentioned in this chapter, SiC MOSFETs are rather robust 
against single pulse short-circuit up to 50% of their voltage rating. If higher voltages are applied, 
the device cannot withstand longer than 10 µS. The withstand limitation of the device is relevant 
to degradation and eventual failure in the gate structure.[8]

4  Heat controller circuit for transistor 
 
 
 
 
To measure characteristics of a transistor in different temperatures, is needed to implement heat 
to the transistor and measure its characteristics in required temperature. There are different ways 
to do this such as using heat chamber which required putting the whole system in it; or using heat 
plate that has also limitation. In this project what is desired is heating up just the switch (DUT). 
The way that is chosen to achieve this, is applying power to a resistor that this power dissipate in 
the resistor and produce heat. If the resistor is attached to the DUT then can heat up just the 
switch.   
A prototype of such a heat control circuit is already designed and built by a PhD student from DTU. 
As there is not documentation and schematic of the circuit, the PCB of the circuit is designed and 
made in Altium Designer software in this work and the performance of it is tested in the lab.

components in a control system. Figure 4-1 shows the basic block diagram of a closed loop control 
system.

The Controller block, monitors the output and compares it with the reference to drive the Plant. 
The difference between the measured output and the reference is called error signal. This error 
signal is applied as a feedback to the controller and the controller changes the input of the system 
to change the output and makes it closer to the reference.   
The feedback loop takes the system output into consideration, which enables the system to adjust 
its performance to meet a desired output response. 
 
  4-2              Block diagram of the heat control system 
 
Due to the definition of basic control system in chapter 4-1, a block diagram for the heat control 
system is designed to control the temperature of a transistor. It can be seen in figure 4-3. In this 
block diagram Plant is resistor R.  If some power applies to the resistor R then it dissipates in the 
resistor and produces heat. If the resistor R is connected to a switch, it can heat up the switch to 
achieve the desired temperature. 
The Control block consists of an error amplifier and a PWM control amplifier. The MOSFET 
supplies power to the resistor where the amount of this power can be controlled by controlling 
the duty cycle of the PWM signal in the gate of the MOSFET. The error amplifier gets an error in 
the input, doing calculation and producing an output that effect on the duty cycle of the PWM 
signal to make the error becomes zero. The error is the difference between required temperature 
(or the referenced voltage)      and the measured temperature that converts to voltage by the 
thermocouple amplifier      .  
The feedback loop includes a thermocouple and a thermocouple amplifier.  The thermocouple 
senses the temperature in the resistor R and the thermocouple amplifier converts the 
temperature to voltage, which the error amplifier can understand it.

7-1              Junction temperature 
 
Junction temperature and case temperature in a switch are different but in these measurements is 
assumed that they are equal. The reason is described in this section.   
To illustrate the thermal resistance of a transistor, the analogy of an electrical resistor is used 
where the electrical resistance is defined by the potential difference across the resistor divided by 
current through the resistor (ohm low). In a thermal resistor in a transistor, the thermal resistance 
    is defined by the thermal potential difference (temperature) divided by the thermal current 
(heat) through the thermal resistor. Figure 7-2 and equation 7-1 show this concept in case of the 
junction to case thermal resistance where    is the junction temperature and    is the case 
temperature.

8    Conclusion  
 
 
 
 
In this project the characteristics of SiC MOSFET and robustness of it under short-circuit is 
described. The PCB of the heat control circuit is made in order to control the temperature in a 
transistor to measure the characteristics of the device at different junction temperatures. 
Moreover the switching performance of four 1200 V power devices (Si IGBT, SiC MOSFET and two 
normally-on SiC JFET) is measured at different junction temperatures with the double pulse tester 
and the heat controller circuit; and their switching performance is compared.  
The result of the measurements highlights that the normally-on SiC JFET from Infineon has the 
lowest switching loss compared with other three devices. As expected, the Si trench IGBT has the 
highest switching loss compared with other three SiC devices and its switching loss increases 
significantly with elevated junction temperature, while switching loss in other three SiC devices 
stays almost constant when the junction temperature increases. Low switching loss and stability of 
switching loss in elevated temperature in SiC devices result to switching in higher frequencies, 
making possible to reduce the size and weight of passives while enabling to achieve simpler and 
less expensive cooling systems. 
The combination of excellent switching performance, high breakdown voltage and low on-
resistance makes SiC devices ideal alternative for Si IGBTs and Si MOSFETs. However, the SiC 
MOSFETs are claimed to have long term gate reliability which is a major reason to not being 
commercial product in the market. Normally-on JFET does not have the gate reliability issue but if 
its gate driver is failed then this device goes to on-state. This is a reason to not using it to replace 
Si devices.  
By taking advantage of the properties of SiC devices, it becomes possible to reduce the size of 
power systems as well as simplify cooling system and as a result reducing in cost. Literature [26] 
investigated that, in general, power systems using SiC power devices are on average 10% more 
efficient.  
SiC devices are still under developed. The reliability issues of these devices such as gate reliability 
and instability of threshold voltage in SiC MOSFETs constitute the biggest challenge in the future 
work for these devices.
