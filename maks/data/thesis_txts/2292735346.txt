Abstract
Probes used in hearing diagnostics often use armature loudspeakers or receivers. This probes require a
wide frequency response and a faster impulse response to make an accurate diagnostic of the hearing loss,
but due to the construction parameters of this equipment this is not often possible. A Optimization in the
frequency response was made extending the range in frequency and equalizing the response, This was done
by a digital ﬁlter that will amplify or reduce the standard response of the probe at target frequencies. A
problem that is not usually taken in consideration is the increase of the distortion due to the ampliﬁcation. In
order to accomplish the compensation and then evaluate the impact of the distortion in the frequency response
two method were used in order to create a design tool box. The ﬁrst is a fast deconvolution regularization
use to equalize the response of the probe, and the second a extraction of nonlinearities method that use an
approximation to a Voltera series to extract the contribution of a given number of nonliniarities in the input
signal.

CHAPTER 1
Introduction
Hearing is an important part of our day a day retinue, from listening music to hearing conversations and
perceive the sound vibrations of the environment. Due to multiple causes the auditory perception can be
lost or halved temporally or permanent, thus it is important for medic personnels to be able to diagnostic
the causes and location of hearing impediment. Through out the years many tools and method have been
developed to discern the causes of hearing lost.
For this purpose probe are used to emit sounds directly to the ear. Some of this probes use balance
armature loudspeaker that are commonly used on hearing aids. This compact transducers are highly efﬁcient.
The drawbacks of type of loudspeaker is that the response is limited in time and frequency and have high
distortion.
Since many of the test require that the sensitivity range of the probe to be able to cover most of the
hearing range of the human hearing and produce very sharp impulse responses it is necessary to optimize the
behavior of the system. Digital ﬁlters is a powerful tool that allow to manipulate the response of a system
from the input to a desired behavior, a problem that is not often consider in this type of optimization is the
increase of the distortion due to the bust in amplitude product of the compensation. The distortion added by
the compensation could affect the diagnostic given by the equipment, so it would be an important to develop
a design tool that could compensate the loudspeaker and evaluate the distortion performance in order to
assets the increase of the contamination due to de nonlinearities of the system
In this project will optimize the behavior of the probe using a fast deconvolution regularization, a method
that provides an equalization of the probe using deconvolution of the input signal and the ﬁlter. And in order
to evaluate the distortion a method that extract the contribution of the orders of distortion using a Voltera
series approximation. The advantages of this method over traditional method like the sine sweep is that, the
contribution of the odd order harmonics in the fundamental can be observed and can be use on any tipe of
signal. This will make easy to evaluate the effects of the distortion in impulse responses often use in hearing
diagnostic techniques.
In Chapter 2 of this thesis a brief theoretical background will be presented, including the fast decon-
volution regularization and extraction of the nonlinearities order method. Chapter 3 depicts the series of
experiment use in the processes of characterization of the probe used and the application of the two method
mentioned before, wile chapter 4 will show the result obtained during the experiment. Chapter 5 provide an
analysis o the results and discussions about the implementation of the method. Finally, in chapter 6 the main
conclusion of the project will be summarized.

CHAPTER 2
Background
Hearing diagnostics is an important part of ear heath, it helps medics and nurses to assess if a hearing
loss is temporary or permanent, and also provides information as to where the loss is located. Many of the
hearing diagnostics probes are comprised of armature loudspeakers and microphones commonly used in the
construction of modern hearing aids. This loudspeaker often need to be compensated in order to increase the
frequency range and thus reduce the impulse response, sometimes, the response also needs to be linearized
to have a consistent frequency response across some important frequencies
2.1
Armature Loudspeaker
As mention before armature loudspeaker are commonly used on hearing aids and hearing diagnostic
probes. The typical construction of a receiver can be seen on the cross-section view depicted on Figure 2.1.
The main difference with a regular loudspeaker is that the coil doesn’t not move along side the armature.
The armature is balanced between two permanent magnets that will apply opposite magnetic forces wen
the current in the coil is zero. A coil that surround the armature magnetizes it when the coil is exited by
an electrical current. Its important to note that the mechanical stiffness of the loudspeaker ensure that the
armature will not ﬂip from the resting position. The acoustic radiation efﬁciency of the armature is increased
by the rigid connection to a light diaphragm Joe Jensen and Ja (2011).
Figure 2.1: Cross-section view of an armature loudspeaker Joe Jensen and Ja (2011)
The nonlinear behavior of the loudspeaker is mainly due to the variation of the magnetic ﬂux that will
depend of the displacement of the armature. Also some of this armature loudspeaker will be enhanced
with a damping ferro ﬂuid that will affect the response of the loudspeaker but is dependent of factors like
temperature.

2.2
Fast deconvolution Regularization
2.2.1
Overview
The probe needs to be optimized and since the response is limited in the frequency range and irregular
due to the construction of the armature loudspeaker, it was decided to linearize and extend the frequency
response by applying a digital ﬁlter. For this the Fast Deconvolution Method propose by Ole Kirkeby , Per
Rubak and Angelo Farina was selected. This method is based on the fast Fourier Transform to design a
matrix of casual ﬁnite impulses response ﬁlter that are optimized at a large number of single frequencies.
Digital ﬁlters design in general follows two mayor parts, the approximation and the realization problem.
The approximation part will deal with the choice of coefﬁcients or parameters in the transfer function of the
ﬁlter to in order to obtain a desired response, wile the realization part will take measures dealing with the
structure to implement the ﬁlter (?).
The general block diagram for the discrete-time multichannel deconvolution problem is shown on Figure
2.2. The different elements are deﬁned as follows:
The vector u(z) is constitute of observed signals, v(z) is a vector of source input signals, w(z) is a vector of
reproduced signals, d(z) is a vector of desired signals, and ﬁnally e(z) is a vector of R performance error
signals. The multi-channel ﬁlters are represented by the matrices A(z), C(z), and H(z). A(z) is a target
matrix, C(z) is a plant matrix, and H(z) is the ﬁlter matrix that will be calculated. It is important to note that
the elements of A(z), C(z), and H(z) are assumed as ﬁnite impulse response (FIR) ﬁlters. The modeling
delay is implemented by the z−m component . The case of interest in this project is a single-channel
equalization, so the dimension of the vectors and matrix will be one, and requires that A(z) to have ﬂat
magnitude response. For the single-channel equalization A(z) will have a value of one (Kirkeby Ole 1999a).
Figure 2.2: Block diagram of the discrete-time deconvolution problem provided by Kirkeby Ole (1999a)
The algorithm design will focus on minimize a cost function of the type shown on Equation 2.1 in the
frequency domain.
J = E + βV
(2.1)
Where E is a measurement of the performance error e, V correspond to the measure of the effort v.
β is a regularization parameter that determines how much weight to assign to the effort term and must
be a positive real number. As β is increased from zero to inﬁnity, the solution changes gradually from
minimizing E to only V . The method makes the regularization frequency dependent in order to control

2.2.2
Implementation
Even though the z-transforms is easy to manipulate, it cannot be expected that the time sequence
corresponding to an arbitrarily chosen z-transform to be both causal and stable (Ole Kirkeby and Farina
(n.d.)). The algorithm use H(z) as a continuous function of frequency by sampling Equation 4.13 for Nh
points. The FFT is used to get in and out of the frequency domain. The inverse FFTs of the optimal frequency
responses is used to implement a modeling delay since using the FFT effectively means that the system is
operating with periodic sequence. If an FFT is used to sample the frequency response of H(z) at Nh points
without including the phase contribution from the modeling delay, then the value of H(k) at those frequencies
is given by Equation (4.14).
H(f) = [C∗(f)C(f) + βB∗(f)B(f)I]−1C∗(f)
(2.3)
Where ∗denotes a complex conjugation of the argument, and f is the frequency band were the H(f) is
calculated and C(f) is the FFT of the response that the ﬁlter will compensate. The following steps must be
follow in order to calculate the ﬁlter:
1. Obtain the C(f) from the fft of the impulse response of the system that is going to be equalized.
2. Select a shape factor that will affect the regularization parameter at selected frequencies .
3. Select a regularization parameter β
4. Calculate one period of h(t) by taking Nh-point inverse FFTs of the elements of H(f)
5. Implement the modeling delay by a cyclic shift of m samples of each element of h(n)
The shape factor is assemble as a function of frequency with the general shape shown in Figure 2.3,
where the mid-frequency region, |B| is one. BL and BH are usually much greater than one, and have a
lowfrequency asymptotic value BL, and a high-frequency asymptotic value BH. The frequencies fL1, fL2,
fH1, and fH2 deﬁne the transition bands.

2.3
Elimination of loudspeaker distortion

to the system may cause the receiver to be exited with values higher that ﬁrst intended, producing signiﬁcant

2.3.1
Selection of the number of orders
As can be seen, it’s a key factor to chose the number of orders that are going to be extracted. So the next
case is an example to observe the effect of a wrong selection of the order.
Estimation of orders
As mention before the method requires to assume the number of nonlinearities present in the system in
order to extract them, but this can be difﬁcult to assets in real systems. Also it has been establish that the
higher nonlinearities will be usually more contaminated by noise than the lower ones. A trade off must be
done between the number of orders and the amount of noise added to the system.
The following example shows how the underestimation and overestimation can affect the extraction of
nonlinearities.
Two example cases were assemble using Equations 2.17 and 2.18.

(2.17)
y(t) = ai sin(2πft)
(2.18)

CHAPTER 5
Discussion and Future Work
The objective of this thesis was to sought out to improve and optimize the performance a hearing
diagnostig probe and test the effect of the equalization in the distortion of the transducer. Because two
method were combined in order to accomplish this is imprtant to discuss them separately ﬁrst. The method
used were a Fast deconvolution regularization derived by Ole Kirkeby and Farina (n.d.) for the equalization
of the probe and the extractions of nonlinearity orders propose by Finn T. Agerkvist (2015) for the distortion
analysis.
Fast Deconvolution Method
There are multiple methods out there to design ﬁlters in order to do the equalization of loudspeaker. The
fast deconvolution method suggested by Ole Kirkeby and Farina (n.d.) offers a versatile and fast process
to obtain and apply the ﬁltering in the input of the loudspeaker. The calculation of the ﬁlter was quite
straight forward with the only inconvenient being the selection of the constrain factor and shape factor. The
application on the selected probe yield satisfactory results in the frequency domain, the range was extended
around 10kHz from 4kHz to 14kHz.
Even though the ampliﬁcation of the amplitude of the impulse response is inconvenient because the input
voltage has to be limited, it was expected because the ﬁlter needs to bust many of the frequencies in order to
get the desired response, driven the receiver beyond the original point.
Due to the high number of samples used in the modeling of the ﬁlter and the convolution implementation
the delay introduce by the equalization is quite high. Some acoustic diagnostics test require a sequence of
impulse, so in order to reduce the delay time fewer number of samples can be taken in consideration in the
calculation of the ﬁlter and since the impulse response died out very quickly it shouldn’t be a problem.
In future projects a method could be derived to optimize the selection of the shape and constrain factor
an in order to obtain a good balance between the desired shape and the gain introduce to the original signal
giving more room to variate the impulse value. Also this project didn’t consider the maximum delay that
will allow a correct implementation in acoustics measurements so it will be necessary to research the trade
off between the sample time and the calculation of the ﬁlter and how will affect the ﬁnal result of the ﬁlter
Extraction of nonlinearities
In contrast with the classical distortion characterizations method like tone sweep, the Voltera series
approximation provide a more accurate information on the behavior of the distortion. In a pure tone case the
contamination of the higher orders harmonics in the lower and fundamental can be quantiﬁed and taken in

One of the problems encounter with the application of the method in experimental measurements is the
correct estimation of the orders. From the simulations shown in the background chapter and the measurement
done with a pure tone can be noted that an incorrect estimation of the orders will result in a residual distortion
on the fundamental that will carry on to the calculation of the other harmonics contaminating them. Wile the
correct estimation or overestimation would be preferable the method will ampliﬁed the noise of the signal,
so a trade of must be made between the number of harmonics and the noise gain.
An advantage of this method is that the extraction can be applied to any signal, providing information of
the behavior of the distortion for that particular excitation. In the case of this project an impulse signal was
used. The result shows that the harmonic distortion calculated for the impulse signal is lower than the THD
of a pure tone. A reason that explain this is that even though the system is driven at the same voltage level,
the impulse will distribute the energy of the signal across all the frequency spectrum so, wile the pure tone
all the energy is focus in one frequency beam producing more distribution at set point. A problem brought
by the impulse case was that due to the low level measurements, the noise gain got close to affect the results
of the extraction, and in some frequency beams even affect the extraction,
An idea for future studies could be test and derive the best relationship between the number of mea-
surements, the noise gain and tthe sufﬁciend amount of orders considered in order to get an acceptable
extraction to reduce de contamination of the extracted armonics. Also it will be important to study the correct
distribution of levels in order to avoid an ill conditioned invention of the level matrix.
Combination of the method
By combining both method the effects of the compensation in the distortion were evaluated. Figures

and easily quantiﬁed with the Total distortion of both cases. The increase in the distortion is caused by the
energy injected by the ﬁlter in order to boost the gain at some frequencies, driving the loudspeaker beyond it
nominal working values.
Even though this project shown that the combination of both method is useful in the optimization of
the probe the process needs to be reﬁned in order to be practical in the production of equipment. Future
studies could focus on the craft a toolbox that use the efﬁcient number of measurements, averages and shape
and regularization factors to quickly calibrate the probe in order to be used in hearing diagnostics. Also the
effects in the equalization and distortion due to the variation in the parameters of the probe caused by use,

CHAPTER 6
Conclusions
This project began with the objective of deriving a method to optimize the behavior of probes used in
hearing diagnostic equipment and assess the impact of the compensation approach in the distortion of the
equipment. Two method were tested and Analise.
To optimize the probe a fast deconvolution ﬁlter was applied in order to extend the frequency response
and equalize the maximun number of frequencies. The results shown in this project got to a successful
equalization of the frequency response of the receiver, with a variation of less than 1dB in the targeted
frequencies and a good approximation to the design ﬁlter. The frequency response was increased around
10kHz witch will prove useful at the time of hearing diagnostic test. Even though the delay introduced by
the ﬁlter process wasn’t taken into account future studies could focus in the design part of the deconvolution
process that is responsible of applying the ﬁlter and introducing the delay.
A resent approach of extraction of the nonlinearities was tested. In this project the method was used
in a practical and experimental way, something that wasn’t done before, and provide a valuable insight in
the different factors that will affect the extraction. In the future, factors like the noise gain and number of
order extracted must be studied in order to minimize the effect of the contamination of the extracted orders
of nonlinearities due to an overestimation or the noise gain. Also it provided a good information of behavior
of the nonlinearities in the impulse response of the receiver, and how the distortion will behave across the
frequency spectrum
Even though the process must be optimized in order to be applicable on the ﬁeld it shown great promise
equalizing the response of the probe and evaluating the impact in the distortion of the probe.
