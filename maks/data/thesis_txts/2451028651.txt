Abstract (english)
Assessing the interior conductivity of a two-dimensional object from the outside is
a hybrid inverse problem of special interest in medical imaging. The reconstruction
procedure is characterized by obtaining internal data from boundary measurements
followed by reconstruction of the conductivity from the internal data. The conductiv-
ity is linked to the model for the voltage potential inside some domain that is occupied
by the object. For two different Neumann conditions enforced on the boundary of
the domain, the latter step of the reconstruction procedure is wellposed when the
matrix composed of the gradient of the potentials, satisfying the Neumann problems,
is invertible. In this project we investigate conditions on the Neumann boundary
functions such that the determinant of this matrix is nonzero and thus the matrix is
invertible. Furthermore, we investigate what more can be said about the determinant.
We find that the determinant is nonzero when the boundary functions and any linear
combination between these are constructed such that the boundary of the domain can
be split in two arcs, where for the first arc the boundary functions are nonnegative
and nonpositive for the second.
This is satisfied for the specific choice of bound-
ary functions defined as the first and second coordinate of the unit outward normal.
Apart from the determinant being nonzero, for this boundary function choice we can
furtherly say that the determinant is positive in the domain and there exists a posi-
tive constant bounding the determinant from below in subsets for which the closure
is compact in the domain. By this result we have local stability of the reconstruction
problem for the conductivity, but this result is restricted to sufficient regularity of
the conductivity and regularity on the boundary of the domain; furthermore, the
potential is restricted to integrate to zero along the boundary.

At bestemme ledningsevnen i det indre af et to-dimensionelt objekt fra målinger på

det indre af et domæne, som er optaget af objektet. Når to forskellige Neumann
betingelser påtvinges på randen af domænet, så vil det sidste trin i rekonstruktions
processen kun være veldefineret, hvis matricen givet ved gradienten af de to spænd-
ingsforskelle, der opfylder Neumann problemerne, er invertibel. I dette projekt under-

matricen er forskellig fra nul og matricen dermed invertibel. Derudover undersøger vi,
hvad der ellers kan siges om determinanten. Vores resultater viser, at determinanten
er forskellig fra nul, hvis randværdierne og enhver linear kombination imellem dem
er konstrueret således, at randen af domænet kan splittes op i to dele, hvor rand
funktionerne henholdsvis ikke er negative og ikke er positive. For det specifikke valg
af første og anden koordinaten til normalvektoren som randværdier er de ovennævnte
betingelser opfyldt. Udover at determinanten er forskellig fra nul kan vi sige, at de-
terminanten må være positiv i domænet og at der findes en positiv konstant som
begrænser determinanten nedefra i delmængder, hvilke opfylder, at deres afslutning
er kompakte i domænet. Fra dette resultat følger, at rekonstruktions problemet for
ledningsevnen er stabilt lokalt, men dette resultat gælder kun, hvis ledningsevnen er
en tilstrækkelig regulær funktion og randen er tilstrækkelig regulær; derudover skal
spændingsforskellen integrere til nul langs randen.

Introduction
Inverse problems arise, when one wants to assess interior information of an object
solely from exterior information. Hybrid inverse problems are one type of such prob-
lems that typically involve two reconstruction steps: Obtain internal data from mea-
surements on the boundary of the object and then reconstructing the desired infor-
mation from the internal data. Throughout this project the type of hybrid inverse
problem under consideration is reconstructing the conductivity inside an object. El-
liptic methods come into play, as the conductivity matrix a(x) ∈R2×2 investigated
in the following chapters satisfies the uniform ellipticity condition

The main focus of this project is to investigate explicit conditions on the bound-
ary functions for the Neumann problem (2) such that the determinant of [∇u1 ∇u2]
is nonzero. Furthermore, it is investigated what more can be said about the deter-
minant of [∇u1 ∇u2] if fixed boundary functions, that satisfy these conditions, are
considered. These investigations for the Neumann problem are outlined in chapter 3.
To accomplish the previous steps the same steps are derived for the Dirichlet problem
(1) following the approach of [AC18, Chapter 6]. Here it was shown for the choice
of boundary functions ϕ1 = x1 and ϕ2 = x2, where x1 and x2 are the coordinates of
the points on the boundary of ∂Ω, that

in Ωand that there exists a constant C > 0 such that
det[∇u1 ∇u2] ≥C,
in subsets of Ωthat are compactly contained in Ω. Existence of such a constant
C > 0 yields local stability of the reconstruction problem for the conductivity. To
obtain these inequalities for the determinant, specific regularities of the conductivity
a(x), the boundary ∂Ωand the boundary functions ϕi were considered, yielding
C1,α-regularity for the solutions ui and a C1,α estimate. The derivations of [AC18,
Chapter 6] are presented in more detail, with more illustrations and with an example
in chapter 2. Following from the obtained inequalities for the Dirichlet problem, in
chapter 3 concerning the Neumann problem, it is investigated if there exists a specific
choice of boundary functions and regularities of the conductivity and the boundary
such that one can obtain C1,α-regularity of the solutions and a C1,α estimate. In
this way we want to obtain similar inequalities for the determinant such that we have
local stability for the reconstruction problem for the conductivity.

CHAPTER 1
Acousto-electric
tomography
Acousto-electric tomography (AET) is a hybrid data imaging modality that combines
Electric Impedance Tomography (EIT) and the information obtained by penetrating
an object with acoustic waves. The approach of EIT is to reconstruct the conductivity
inside the object solely from boundary measurements, but this yields a fairly illposed
reconstruction problem [JKK19]. Therefore also acoustic waves can be considered to
obtain more information about the interior.
We will in the following motivate how the internal data for AET can be obtained
and how the conductivity can be reconstructed from the AET data. For motivational
purposes the conductivity in this chapter is a scalar function σ(x), while in the fol-
lowing chapters it is a matrix-valued function a(x). In section 1.1 we illustrate how
the internal data for AET can be obtained from boundary measurements based on
the approach by [JKK19]. Furthermore, section 1.2 addresses how to reconstruct the
conductivity σ from internal AET data, to motivate why it is important that the de-
terminant of the matrix [∇u1∇u2] is nonzero, which is the topic of the next chapters.
This section is based on [AC18, Section 10.1 & 10.2].

4.2
Comparison of Dirichlet and Neumann approach
In comparison, the condition ∇ui ̸= 0 is satisfied for the Dirichlet case, when ∂Ωcan
be split into two arcs on which ϕi is nonincreasing and nondecreasing, while for the
Neumann case this is satisfied, when ∂Ωcan be split into two arcs, on which gi is
nonpositive and nonnegative. For the Dirichlet case by the maximum principle any
interior critical point would induce at least two nonincreasing and nondecreasing arcs
on the boundary, therefore the number of arcs must be one for Ωnot to have interior
critical points. However, for the Neumann case the difference between the number
of arcs on which gi

and the number of rotations α =

Ωnot to have interior critical points. As α makes one rotation along the boundary,
the number of arcs must be one.
In order for the determinant det[∇u1 ∇u2] to be nonzero not only the boundary
values, but any linear combination between the boundary values must satisfy the
respective conditions as well. For fixed boundary values ϕ1 = x1 and ϕ2 = x2 and
g1 = n1 and g2 = n2 in the Dirichlet and the Neumann case respectively these condi-
tions are satisfied for both cases, when Ωis convex. However, in the Neumann case
the boundary values and any linear combination must also satisfy the condition of
integrating to zero along the boundary to guarantee existence of a solution. Therefore
in the Neumann case further sufficient conditions for Ωare that Ωis centered at (0, 0)
and that Ωcan be rotated such that it is symmetric around the x1- as well as the
x2-axis.

information for the Neumann case, we considered fixed Neumann boundary values
g1 = n1 and g2 = n2. The Dirichlet data was the simplest boundary value choice
satisfying the conditions of the previous to paragraphs, while the Neumann data
choice was motivated by the fact that we could use the same approach as for the
Dirichlet data to determine if the determinant of [∇u1 ∇u2] is positive. To approach
further information about the determinant of [∇u1 ∇u2] it was then helpful to have
an C1,α-estimate for the Neumann solution. For this purpose we derived an estimate

existence of a constant C > 0 such that det[∇u1 ∇u2] ≥C followed from the fact that
any bounded sequence of conductivities an converges in a suitable space. Therefore
for the Neumann case we similarly needed to choose a space for a such that any
bounded sequence an converges in a suitable space. As convergence is assessed for a
function where a appears in the right hand side, a must converge in H2, such that the
derived estimate can be applied. Furthermore, the space should be a subset of C2 to
obtain the desired regularity for the C1,α estimate. Hence, we selected H4-regularity
for a. In this way we achieved that both for the Dirichlet and the Neumann case
det[∇u1 ∇u2] > 0 and det[∇u1 ∇u2] ≥C for some constant C > 0. Here we needed
more regularity for the boundary of Ωand the conductivity a in the Neumann case
compared to the Dirichlet case: C3 boundary instead of C1,α and the conductivity
being a H4-function instead of a Hölder continuous function.
4.3
Application
As motivated in chapter 1, invertability of the matrix [∇u1∇u2] for solutions ui to the
Neumann boundary value problem (4.3) are necessary for the reconstruction process
of the conductivity. This is important for the approach of AET, but also the simpler
approach of Electric impedance tomography (EIT). For both AET and EIT it is im-
portant to obtain precise boundary measurements and therefore the interface between
electrodes on the boundary and the object must be modeled properly. One frequently
applied model as in [Hyv04; Bov+07] is the complete electrode model presented by
[Kuo+89]. For this model away from the attached electrodes on the boundary, Neu-
mann conditions are enforced similar to the investigations of this project.
4.4
Future work
As done for the Dirichlet problem in [AC18, Section 6.4] potential future work of the
Neumann problem is to generalize the results for less regularity on the conductivity
and the boundary of the domain. Also, as motivated in chapter 1 it will be useful

CHAPTER 5
Conclusion
By presenting the AET approach for reconstruction of the conductivity inside an
object, we successfully motivated why it is relevant for the matrix composed of the
gradients of solutions to the Dirichlet and the Neumann boundary value problem to
be invertible. Furthermore, we motivated that existence of a constant bounding the
determinant of this matrix from below, ensures that the reconstruction problem is
stable.
For the Dirichlet case we outlined and rederived the findings about the mentioned
matrix by [AC18, Section 6.1 & Section 6.2]. Hence, when the boundary values are
chosen such that the boundary of the domain can be split in two arcs on which the
boundary values are nondecreasing and nonincreasing functions, then there are no
critical points in the interior of the domain. When any linear combination between
the boundary values satisfies this condition as well, it was shown that the determi-
nant of the matrix is nonzero and hence the matrix invertible. For the fixed boundary
value choice of the identity mapping, where the boundary values are defined as the
first and the second coordinate for a point on the boundary respectively, all previous
conditions are satisfied when the domain is convex. Furtherly, for a convex domain
and the boundary values set to the identity mapping, it was found, that the deter-
minant of that matrix is positive in the domain and in fact, there exists a positive
constant bounding the determinant from below in subsets, for which the closure of
these subsets is compact in the domain.
For the Neumann case a similar approach was considered. Based on [AM92; AM94]
we outlined conditions on the boundary values and their derivations such that there
are no interior critical points in the domain. The conditions are defined as following:
The boundary of the domain can be split in two arcs on which the boundary values
are nonnegative and nonpositive functions. Similary, it was shown that the determi-
nant of the matrix is nonzero when any linear combination between the boundary
values satisfies this condition as well. The main achievement of this project was to
achieve results for the Neumann case similar to the Dirichlet case for a fixed choice of
boundary values. This was accomplished by considering the boundary values defined
by the first and second coordinate of the unit outward normal. When considering
this choice of boundary values and a convex domain the previous conditions are sat-
isfied. Furtherly, we derived an estimate for the solutions such that we could use the
==PAGE:70==

same approach as for the Dirichlet case to obtain that the matrix is positive inside
the domain. Also, we could use the approach to prove that there exists a positive
constant bounding the determinant from below in subsets, for which the closure of
these subsets is compact in the domain. In this way we locally obtained stability of
the reconstruction problem for the conductivity. However, this result is restricted to
sufficient regularity of the conductivity and regularity on the boundary of the domain;
furthermore, the solution is restricted to integrate to zero along the boundary.

APPENDIX C

C.1
Project Plan
C.1.1
Theoretic background
This project concerns the conductivity equation
{
div(a∇ui) = 0
in Ω,
ui = ϕi
on ∂Ω,
(C.1)
for a real-valued conductivity coefficient σ and i = 1, 2, ..., d. Areas of application
are hybrid inverse problems such as Acousto-Electro Tomography (AET). For the
latter mentioned procedure and d = 2, 3 one studies the problem of reconstructing
the conductivity σ from internal data
Hij(x) = σ(x)∇ui(x) · ∇uj(x),
which is a useful approach in e.g.
medical imaging.
However the reconstruction
procedure requires conditions on the boundary values ϕi such that the corresponding
solutions ui satisfies
det[∇u1 . . . ∇ud] > 0.
This inequality ensures that the map H 7→σ is invertible.
C.1.2
Purpose of the thesis
In previous work [AC18] the above was addressed for the Dirichlet boundary value
problem (C.1). The purpose of this Thesis is to address the above for the problem
(C.1) with Neumann boundary conditions n · a∇ui = ϕi:
{
div(a∇ui) = 0
in Ω,
n · a∇ui = ϕi
on ∂Ω,
(C.2)
==PAGE:84==
68
C Project Plan and Evaluation
with n denoting the normal to the boundary ∂Ω. The overall question of this thesis
then defines as follows
1) Does there for the two-dimensional case exist explicit conditions on the bound-
ary values ϕi in problem (C.2) so that the corresponding solutions ui satisfy
det[∇u1 ∇u2] > 0? If that is true, how are these conditions defined?
We will optionally consider two other perspectives of the previous question:
2) What is the answer to question 1) for the Dirichlet boundary value problem (C.1),
in a limited-angle scenario, i.e. when the boundary conditions only are supported
on some part of the boundary?
3) What is the answer to question 1) for the Dirichlet boundary value problem (C.1),
in the three-dimensional case?
C.1.3
Tools and Methods
In order to address the question(s) in the previous section, the following methods are
considered
1) Study literature about elliptic PDEs and the conductivity equation (e.g. [AC18]).
2) Consider specific PDEs and calculate concrete solutions.
Furthermore, the aim is to learn Python to be able to use the following tool
1) Use the Python platform ”FEniCS” to calculate numeric solutions to specific
PDEs.
Method 1) is considered to address the theoretical part of the question(s), while
method 2) and the tool 1) are considered to illustrate and understand different aspects
of the answer to the question(s).
C.1.4
Output
The output of the thesis is a report with an expected range of 50 to 70 pages, focusing
on quality not quantity.
C.1.5
Supervision
The supervisor is Associate professor Kim Knudsen at the department DTU Compute.
I am looking forward to receive guidance with respect to theoretical understanding
of the literature (when necessary), to get inspiration for interesting PDE problems,
that are relevant for the thesis and supervision on how to approach unexplored fields
and in the procedure of creating new results.

C.2
Self evaluation
We successfully addressed the main goal formulated in question 1) in the original
project plan: ”Does there for the two-dimensional case exist explicit conditions on the
boundary values ϕi in problem (C.2) so that the corresponding solutions ui satisfy
det[∇u1 ∇u2] > 0? If that is true, how are these conditions defined?” We found
explicit conditions such that the corresponding solutions ui satisfy det[∇u1 ∇u2] > 0
and furthermore we proved existence of a constant C > 0, such that locally it holds
that det[∇u1 ∇u2] ≥C. This was carried out more or less as planned in the original
study plan.
We visualized our theoretical results with some illustrations and an
example, but we didn’t use the Python platform ”FEniCS” to calculate numeric
solutions to specific PDEs. This was due to the fact that it took a lot of time to
establish the theoretical results and we wanted to focus more on verification of these
results instead of calculating numerical results. Furthermore, we didn’t have time to
address the optional questions 2) and 3) in the original project plan. The deadline
was moved to the 22nd of June.
