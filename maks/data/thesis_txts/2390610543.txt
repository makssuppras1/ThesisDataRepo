Abstract
The aim of this Master Thesis is to derive simple geometric criteria on the existence
and multiplicity of sliding vector пђБelds along the intersections of codimension-1 dis-
continuity sets of piecewise smooth dynamical systems. The sliding vector пђБelds are
expressed in the form of convex combinations of the vector пђБelds around these in-
tersections, corresponding to an extension of the Filippov Convention for the case of
sliding on codimension-1 discontinuities. The possible kinds of stability of the sliding
пђВow are classiпђБed, and two possible reasons for which sliding can appear or disappear
across the intersection are demonstrated.

Chapter 1
Introduction
Discontinuous models are used to describe physical systems in various branches of
science and engineering, e.g. in mechanics (friction and impact), electronic & control
systems (systems with switches) and biology. The mathematical models describing
such systems assume that the transition among smooth regions occurs in a discontin-
uous manner, thus the term piecewise smooth systems (PWS) is used. However, for
actual systems in nature this transition is perhaps better perceived as continuous but
very steep, and thus discontinuous models are used as simpliпђБcations of reality. A
typical example is the case of mechanical friction, when force is applied to an initially
stationary object (Figure 1.1). The value of friction increases linearly and then drops
suddenly when the object starts moving.
Figure 1.1: As long as an object is stationary, the value of friction depends linearly on the force
applied to it (static friction). When the object starts moving, the value of friction drops suddenly
to a constant value (kinetic friction). Although the mathematical model is discontinuous, it
could be that in reality the transition occurs in a smooth but very steep manner (gray line).
Under the above assumption that discontinuity is a simpliпђБcation of reality, it is
reasonable to associate piecewise smooth system with smooth ones. This is done by
connecting the two regions by smooth transitions, using so-called regularization func-
tions [10], as shown in Figure 1.2. Then, by studying this smooth regularized system

Trajectories originating from a smooth region could in principle reach the a discon-

extendable on it, a degeneracy occurs and solutions can not be uniquely deпђБned. For

пђБelds on either side, solutions could either вАЬcrossвАЭ the discontinuity or вАЬslideвАЭ along it
(notions to be described in detail in Chapter 2).
In order for sliding vectors to be deпђБned, the normal components of the vector пђБelds
on either side of the discontinuity have to be oriented towards opposite directions. In
case they both point towards the discontinuity the sliding is characterized as stable,
and in case they both point away from the discontinuity the sliding is characterized as
unstable. Following the Filippov Convention, the sliding vector пђБeld is deпђБned as the
convex combination of the vector пђБelds on either side of the discontinuity, such as it is
tangent to it. Geometrically, its endpoint is located at the point where the discontinuity
intersects the straight line connecting the endpoints of the two vector пђБelds. The above
hold for the general case of codimension-1 discontinuities, as illustrated in Figure (1.4).
However, on the codimension-2 intersection of discontinuities (Figure 1.5), it is not

Chapter 6
Conclusion
In this thesis, a resolution of piecewise smooth systems with intersecting codimension-
1 discontinuity sets was performed. The ultimate goal was to deпђБne the sliding пђВow
as an extension of the Filippov Convention and derive conditions on its existence and
multiplicity. The foundation for the analysis was set by regularizing the system using
Sotomayor and Teixeira regularization functions and exposing its slow-fast nature.
The expression of the sliding vector пђБeld was given in Chapter 3 in the desired form
of a convex combination, corresponding to the dynamics on the critical manifold of the
slow-fast regularized system. Apart from deпђБning the above, the algebraic approach of
this chapter was the пђБrst step towards conditions on the sliding пђВowвАЩs existence, and
its main attribute was a condition related to the existence of two sliding vector пђБelds,
which would be complicated to derive geometrically. Furthermore, in the пђБnal section
of this chapter, results that allowed for general remarks on stability were produced and
put to use in the following chapter.
Chapter 4 contains the main contributions of this work. Although the convex com-
bination describing the sliding пђВow has been deпђБned in the references by alternative
approaches, the parametric surface induced by the regularization had not yet been

6. Conclusion
Although the main objective of this work was clear from the beginning, new ques-
tions were emerging at every step. Every new result lead to further understanding of
the nature of the problem, and the connection between different parts was revealed. It
turned out that various approaches should be combined in order to derive conclusions,
which in turn revealed potential for further study. The topics that came to surface after
achieving the initial goal such as appearance and disappearance of sliding across the
intersection and hidden dynamics, along with challenging questions such as intersec-
tions of more than two discontinuities, are exciting possibilities for future work.
