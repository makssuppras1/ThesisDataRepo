ABSTRACT
Nowadays, wind power has become one of a leading form in the renewable energy, and
it is still developed with a very high speed. However, the integration of wind power is
still an issue for the power system especially when more and more wind farm are
installed. Unlike the normal convention energy, wind power has more fluctuations when
integrated into the power system due to its stochastic characteristic, and it will brings a
lot of troubles for the decision maker to schedule the system. In order to run the power
system in an optimal way, the stochastic unit commitment is introduced which will be
detail presented in this master thesis. The whole work can be divided into two part:
scenarios generation of wind power and the stochastic unit commitment.
The first part is given with an already forecast data from a real wind farm in Australia,
and this data will be implemented in the scenarios generation.After getting the results,
the correctness of the data will be implemented. And it is found that all the 19 bars
which stands for each interval of quantiles (5% to 95% with 5% of interval)are all
around 5% as expected which proves that the method of scenarios generation is feasible.
After checking the feasibility, the second part mainly deal with the stochastic unit
commitment based on a 6-bus power system. Here the cases of 7,20, 30 scenarios and
the one from the real measurement will be calculated by two different solvers: “BNB”
and “CPLEX”. But it is found that “BNB” runs too slow to solve the problem and the
accuracy is worse than the “ CPLEX ” , so the second solver is chosen for the final
simulation.Comparing the results, the generators status from 4 cases are all the same
which is not expected since there do exist some distinct differences of wind power data
in the case N=7 compared with others. So making the other simulation for a more
complex system (30-bus) and then the difference can be observed from the status results.
Therefore, it is suggested that more scenarios should be generated when inputting to the
stochastic unit commitment since it contains more detail information of the forecast
interval.

INTRODUCTION
In this chapter, the project is introduced by firstly outline the current global energy
challenge and the importance of wind power. In order to integrate the wind power to the
power system,power generated from the wind has to be forecast and the scenarios
should be generated since the stochastic unit commitment require to do so. The
following sections will briefly give an introduction to them.
1.1Global energy challenges
Since human started to know how to use the fire, energy has changed our lives a lot and
it affects the daily lives deeply in every aspect. Among them, electricity is one of the
most useful forms and it will cause the chaos in the modern world if any serious
breakdown happens in the power system.
Currently the fossil fuels still dominate the energy market, accounting for around 80%
of the energy consumption[1,2]. However, this kind of energy is unsustainable, and it
will bring the horrible pollution problems to our living environment. Analyzing from
the energy depletion perspective, the reserves of existing fossil fuels decrease rapidly
while the energy demands are continuously increasing. According to the energy reserve
data shown in figure 1 , the coal deposits will be empty before 2088 and the worse
situation happens to the oil and gas which will be depleted in 2052 and 2060
respectively[3]. The amount of fossil fuel are finite but the consuming rate still climes
and has already reached to 4 billion tonnes per year. Such a contradiction between
demands and supplies will lead to the prices rising of fossil fuels and eventually the
conflicts regarding the fossil energy.
Analyzing from the environmental protection perspective, the main traditional way to
use coal and oil for electricity is burning, but the combustion of coal and oil will emit
some harmful gases like CO2, SO2 and so on. As already know, CO2 is the major cause
for the global warming, the sea ice in Arctic starts to melt and it will cause the rising of

In order to improve the environment, the development of green energy could be an
effective method. Sources like wind, solar, hydro and geothermal energy are classified
as green energy and this kind of energy is produced in a way to minimize the bad effect
to the environment and it will make little change to the climate. Among them, wind
energy is one of the leading forms in renewable energy and it is still in a rapid
development. Since this master thesis is mainly deal with the power system which

1.3Wind power forecasting
Although the wind is a clean and infinite resource in nature which is harmless to the
environment, its drawbacks still can not be ignored. The worst one is the instability
characteristic: the strength is extremely unstable which can vary from 0 to a storm level
and it will brings a large fluctuation to the power output of wind turbines. Due to the
intermittent characteristic of wind, it would lead to a lot of challenges for the power grid.
Especially for the system unit commitment and the power operation dispatching which
are strongly bounded to the economic optimization of the power system. Moreover, the
higher penetration of wind power is, the bigger influence will be suffered by the grid[7].
For the transmission system operator (TSO) the power production has to be scheduled
in advance to match the load profile, electricity production must balance the
consumption at any time. Normally the load profile can be predicted, and it can be
associated with a high accuracy, so the production side should be also scheduled to
match the corresponding values. Since the wind is unstable and hard to control, the
power output of wind power station then has to predict beforehand so that the

1.4Unit commitment
As already written in the previous section, the power operation planning aims at
scheduling the power production in advance to match the predicted load profile for the
TSO. And normally this power planning can be described by three distinct sets of
decisions according to length of the planning periods: long-term(for years), medium-
term(for days to weeks) and short-term(for seconds to hours) decisions. For long-term
decisions the types ,capacities and numbers of generator should be determined while the
short term handle the economic dispatching problems. In this thesis, the medium term
decisions are analyzed and the existing power plants have to be commit for the planning
time horizon and this type of problem is normally called the unit commitment. Due to
the fluctuation of the load, the generators need to produce different amount to follow the
load profile and based on the reason that the generators can not be turned on instantly,
power operation has to be planned in advanced to see whether to turn on or switch off as
well as the economic constraint and that is the goal of unit commitment. The difference
between economic dispatch is that the unit commitment also consider the status of
generator.
To explain it more specific, an example from literature[9] is presented in the figure 2 to
detail describe the goal of unit commitment problem. The example contains only one
simple load scenario for a three generators system. As can be observed, the cost of each
generator varies. In order to achieve the optimal cost, the status of generators have to be
considered according to the load requirement.Unit 1 is the cheapest one so that it is
turned on all time to match the load while the more expensive one (unit 2) and the most
==PAGE:17==
5
expensive one (unit 3) are turned on only if needed respectively. The actual unit
commitment will be more complicated since more constraints need to be considered, but
the overview concept is follow the description above.
For normal power station, the power output is easier to control but not the wind due to
its characteristic. So the wind power forecasting scenarios has to be generated in
advance for the unit commitment and this master thesis will mainly deal with this kind
of power operation problems(referred as stochastic unit commitment). About the
construction of stochastic unit commitment, two elements are essential: objective
function and constrains. For the objective function, it is mainly formed to express the
function for minimizing the cost while the constraints takes generators,transmission,
security constraints and power reserve requirement into account to meet the load profile,
and the detail procedures of the formulation will be presented in the chapter five.

1.5Thesis objective
The main purpose of this master thesis is to study how to deal with the stochastic unit
commitment problem for a power system which contains a wind farm base on the wind
estimation data. Applying advanced solver to solve the mixed-integer programming
problems and schedule the power production in order to reach the most-optimal
operation as well as fulfill the load profile. During the simulation, two different solvers
will be used and the performance will be evaluated. Additionally, different numbers of
scenarios will be also compared to study how the numbers of scenarios will affect the
result of stochastic unit commitment.

The following sections will mainly introduced these types. Section 2.1 will firstly give

For forecasts, there are commonly exist two kinds of approaches: expert judgment and
model-based approaches. Different approach will have different usage and advantages.
For example when dealing with load forecasts, expert judgment approaches can surpass
nearly any types of model-based approaches. But when it comes to the renewable
energy estimation, model based are more preferable due to the difficulty of foreseeing
the wind development and its influence on the power generation. Since we are analyzing
the wind power estimation, model-based approaches should be paid more attention, and
a common definition of the model-based approach is presented in the following
paragraph[10].

2.3The probabilistic forecasts
Point forecasts can not show the full view of what would really happen when dealing
with operation issues especially the case when the cost caused by the entire potential
range of realization need to be considered. Even improving the chosen model, point
forecast still can not provide users with the permission to make the optional decisions.
Therefore, the methods of the probabilistic forecast which can deal with the actual
operational problems should be developed. Comparing with point forecast, the
probabilistic forecast is used more commonly and is applied to practice now. It can
provide the user with the full information of the coming results in the future. There are
several types of probabilistic forecasts like quantiles, density forecast,prediction interval,
and they would be briefly introduced in the following sections.

When dealing with power system operation scheduling problems, there is always a
question that will bothers the decision-maker about which and when generators should
be operated and whether the operating level is optimal and security enough for
balancing the load demand. Moreover, the problem will become even more complicated
when integrating the wind farm into power system. Therefore, in order to deal with
these problems, stochastic unit commitment should be introduced. Before implementing
the simulation, the regarding theory is presented in this chapter. Section 3.1 will give an
overview of unit commitment while the optimization method used will follow behind in
3.2 and the last section 3.3 is the description about the stochastic unit commitment.

the following part will detail describe it.

3.3.2
Cutting-plane technique for MIP
The idea of cutting-plane technique is to associate the constraints with the linear
program until an optimal integer result is found feasibly. In other words,This technique
consider MIP as LP relaxation firstly and gradually narrowing the region down when a
feasible solution (the most optimal integer results) is found to satisfy the constraint.
When solving a MIP problem, the cutting-plane technique will firstly regards the
problem as an non-integer linear programming problem and it will have a feasible
region which contains all the possible solution for MIP. Once forming the region,
“simplex method” (a commonly used algorithm for solving the linear programming
problems which operates in the standard form) can be used to solve the LP relaxation
==PAGE:43==
31
finding the optimal solution. But if the optimal solution is not integral, then an
additional cutting plane constraint (a cut) will be added to form a new region of LP
relaxation so that a new optimal solution can be implemented. Repeating this process
until an integer solution is found and this algorithm can be concluded in the figure
16[20].

Basing on the theory presented in the chapter two, this chapter is an implementation of
scenarios generation. As mention previously, probabilistic forecast which is associated
with estimation uncertainties is very valuable for many decision making for power
operation. But it does not show any message about the interdependence of each look
ahead time. In order to achieve that, Gaussian Copulas model is applied in this chapter.
With a correlation factor of 0.86 for each neighbor point, the covariance matrix is
constructed and then the multivariate Gaussian distribution is build according to the
expression of Xk~N(μ0,∑).
After forming the relationship, the model has to be implemented for the actual wind
forecast data. As mention in the quantiles forecast introduction, the forecasting data
which is defined by quantiles should follow the uniform distribution U~[0,1]. In order
to link forecast data and the data in multivariate Gaussian distribution, a cdf
transformation is firstly applied to transform the data in multivariate Gaussian
distribution to the uniform domain. Since the data is already change to the uniform
domain, we then link it with the cdf of actual wind distribution. In order to achieve it,
an inverse cdf transformation is applied. Then the wind power values which is
associated with relationship of each look ahead time can be generated. And that is the
way for generating the scenarios.
Plotting both the scenarios and the real measurement data, we find that the measurement
data completely falls in the interval of scenarios which means that the real wind power
case is well considered among the scenarios.Since we know the real measurement wind
power at the time of t+k will have the equal probability to fall into the interval consisted
by any two successive quantiles. Then we make a histogram to evaluate the results base
on this evaluation point. As can be observed, all the 19 quantiles interval(5% to 95%)
have around 5% in numbers of the data which is as expected and that can prove the
accuracy of the scenarios generated in this master thesis. Since the scenarios data
generated is correct, the method then can be used in the following chapter of stochastic
unit commitment.

BNB
The first optimization tool used for stochastic unit commitment in this master thesis is

programming problems. The algorithm of “BNB” for mixed integer programming is the

the minimum of constrained problem) will be called to solver the sub problems.

branched. But “BNB” will still try to branch it so that it can be solved again to see
whether a solution can be found or not. An example of formulation of problems that can

5.2.2
CPLEX (Branch & Cut)
CPLEX is a software developed by IBM which can solve linear programming (LP),
mixed integer programming(MIP) and also the concerning problems in the languages of
C, C++, Java and Python. To be more specific, it handle the problem with linear or a
quadratic objective function associated with the linear or quadratic constraints and the
variable inside the problem can be either integer or continuous or mix of them.[24]
Compared with “BNB”, the algorithm in“CPLEX” is different which is called branch
and cut algorithm. It is a method which combines the advantages of both “cutting plane”
and “branch and bound” to improve the efficiency of the optimization process. The cut
during the process is used to tighten the initial LP relaxation.[25]
Figure 34: Algorithm of branch and cut [26]
A flow chart showing the algorithm of the branch and cut is presented in the figure 34.
As can be observed, the first step is “Pre-processing” which is firstly used to identify the
infeasibility and redundancy of the problems.With the step, the calculation speed is
greatly improved since it can ameliorate the bounding process and simply reduce the
size of the problems. After the “Pre-processing” step, the problems will be initially
solved applying simplex without integer constraint. When the solution is non-integer, a
cut will be added to cut away the zone of the feasible region of the relaxation which
==PAGE:62==
50
involves the fractional solution. After getting this incumbent, the similar step in branch
and bound then is applied to divided it into sub-problems. During these step,
the“Heuristic” is activated and it aims to find the solution for the relaxation of the
current node.One should be noted is that the branching step will be not be replaced by
“Heuristic”, but sometimes the “Heuristic” is faster in locating the new feasible solution
inexpensively which can also help to improve the calculation speed.[27].

5.4.2
Result from “CPLEX”
The same equality and inequality constraints can be directly used for “CPLEX”, but the
objective function is not longer described in a string form, it should be presented by
using the quadratic matrix “H” and vector “f”. And the type of the variables should be
listed in the form of string (introduced in the section 5.2.2). Due to the additive
procedures of branch and cut algorithm, the “CPELX Optimizer” is really fast when
solving stochastic unit commitment problems compare with “BNB”. The same size of
the calculation (7 hours × 7 scenarios) only takes 0.125s which is a really huge progress
compare with the solver “BNB” (designed in 1998) and the detail comparison for the
same case will be presented in the section 5.4.3.

CONCLUSIONS AND PERSPECTIVES
When wind penetration of the power system is increasing, the problems of scheduling
the power operation will become much harder. Unlike the normal convention energy,
wind power has more fluctuations when integrated into the power system due to its
stochastic characteristic, and it will brings a lot of troubles for the decision maker to
schedule the system. In order to run the power system in an optimal way, the stochastic
unit commitment is introduced. And this master thesis is mainly deal with the stochastic
unit commitment which based on the wind forecast interval. The whole work can be
divided into two part: scenarios generation of wind power and the stochastic unit
commitment. The corresponding theories are presented in the chapter two and three
while the simulation processes versus the results are presented in the chapter four and
five.
6.1Scenarios generation
The first part is given with an already forecast wind power data which are originally
from a real wind farm in Australia. Since it is only a probabilistic forecast which do not
involve the information about the interdependence for each look ahead time, so the
scenarios generation should be implemented. As mentioned previously in the chapter
four, the whole process of scenarios generation can be described by three main
procedures. The first step is to construct the correlation by using the Gaussian Copulas
model which form the correlation for these data in a multivariate normal domain.In
order to link these data with the data in the actual wind distribution(quantiles forecast),
the second step of cdf transformation should be applied to transform the data in
multivariate Gaussian distribution to the uniform domain. Since the data is already
change to the uniform domain, then link it with the cdf of actual wind distribution by
applying an inverse cdf transformation.
After these processes, the scenarios then can be generated and the correctness should be
evaluated. As already mentioned, the generated wind power data at the time of t+k will
==PAGE:84==
72
have the equal probability to fall into the interval consisted by any two successive
quantiles(5% to 95% with 5% interval). So for this case, we can check whether the
proportion of generated data falls into two neighbor quantiles will equals to 5% or not.
Increasing the number of scenarios to 500 and make a histogram, all the 19 bars in the
figure 32 which stands for each interval are all around 5% as expected which proves the
correctness of the scenarios. After checking the feasibility of the scenarios generation,
the method described in the master thesis then can be used for the stochastic unit
commitment.
6.2 Stochastic unit commitment
The second part mainly presents the procedures of forming and simulating the stochastic
unit commitment. During the process, a data package of a 6-bus system “ case6ww ”
from “ Matpower ” is called and whole simulation is implemented by assuming the
system is in DC power flow (no active power losses), regarding P2 as wind farm with a
total 23.81% of wind penetration and randomly generating the load.
Basing on the scenarios generation method described, three cases which contains
different number of scenarios (7, 20 and 30) are implemented here and it is done by two
different solvers.The result from these solvers are also compared since two different
algorithms are used. The first solver is “BNB” and it apply the “branch and bound”
algorithm to do the mixed integer programming calculation, but it is found that this
solver requires too much time (nearly three hours) to solve a simple case with only 7
scenarios over 7 hours which is not efficient. Therefore a more mature solver “CPLEX”
is introduced which only need 0.125s to do the same size calculation. The algorithm
used in “ CPLEX ” is the “ branch and cut ” which has additional steps of “ Pre-
processing” “cut” and “heuristic” compared to the one in “BNB” and these steps will
greatly help to improve the efficiency and accuracy of the calculation for MIP problems.
After choosing “CPLEX” as the final simulation solver, the cases of 7, 20 ,30 scenarios
and also the real measurement are calculated. Comparing the results from these cases,
the generators status for each hour are exactly the same as well as the real measurement.
Plotting the real wind data versus the scenarios from three cases, it is found that the
cases of 20 and 30 scenarios works really good since the real measurement curve is
involved in their cover regions. While the situation is not so good for case of 7 scenarios.
As can be observed from the figure 39, two distinct difference appear at the time t=3

and t=8 which could have influence in the results of generators status. However, the
status results from N=7 are still the same as the one from N=20 and N=30. The possible
reason for that could be the system structure, the system in“case6ww”only contains
three generators which is not complex and the wind penetration is only 23.8% which
has limit influence to the power system.So even the difference exists in the cases of 7
scenarios and 30 scenarios, the generator status will still remain the same. But when the
system becomes more complicated or the wind penetration is increased, the result could
be different.
In order to prove the conclusion, a more complex system of “case30”(see figure 45) is

expected. Therefore we can conclude that: more scenarios should be generated when
inputting to the stochastic unit commitment since it will involve more information about
the forecast interval and will consider more potential situations that will happen in the
future especially when the wind farm has great influence in the power system.
6.3 Perspective and future researches
The topic of stochastic unit commitment is far more extensive than what have done in
this master thesis and there are several ways that can enrich this thesis which are
presented as below:

The Copulas model used in this master thesis is the most simplest one which is
Gaussian Copulas. There do exist some other model that can applied. In the future
research, models like Frank’s Copulas, Diagonal band Copulas and the Minimum
information Copulas should be investigated and the corresponding results can be
compared to figure out which one preforms better in stochastic unit commitment.

The stochastic unit commitment in this master thesis only have 5 constraint which
is a simplify case. But this is far not enough for the real work since the real power
system will have more considerations. Constraints like the spinning reserves and
ramp-up limit should be considered in the future analysis.

The influence of different wind penetration to the power system is not analyzed in
this master thesis. So in the future work, the result from different wind penetration
will be also compared.
