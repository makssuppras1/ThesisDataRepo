Abstract
Skin cancer is a massive health problem, where non-melanoma skin cancer is the most
common type of cancer seen among both men and women. In 2018 over 1 million
diagnoses were made worldwide. Misdiagnosis of skin cancer can be critical for the
patient.
Uncontrolled proliferation of cells requires more blood supply than normal cells. This
results in changes in the vascular morphology of the blood vessels in the skin. These
changes can be used as bio-markers for clinical staff to diagnose neoplasia more ef-
ficient. These morphological structures can be diﬀicult to detect without invasive
agents. Optical Coherence Tomography is a non-invasive method of looking at the
composition of the skin. OCTA is an extended modality within OCT that can be
used to visualize the vascular networks in the tissue.
This study aims to present methods to elucidate how an ultra-high-resolution SD-
OCT system can improve the current resolution and sensitivity of OCTA and how
this can benefit clinical staff in diagnosing diseases in dermatology. Data used in
the thesis are collected from the OCT system in the Department of Photonics at the
Technical University of Denmark. Microscopic studies have been performed on to
elucidate which compositions in the fluid makes OCTA possible. Blood velocity and
the importance of scanning speed will be evaluated for OCTA detection. Handling of
artefact signals such as speckle noise and tissue movements will be made to improve
the resolution of the OCTA algorithms.
Furthermore will OCTA signal results from milk be compared with blood to eval-
uate if the fluid type has any importance for flowdetection. Finally, these methods
have been tested on tissue to visualize the vascular network in the skin.

Resume
Hudkræft er et massivt helbredsmæssigt problem i hele verden.
I 2018 var non-
melanoma hudkræft blandt de hyppigste typer cancer man så blandt både mænd og
kvinder. Statestikker fra det pågældende år viste over 1 million diagnosticeringer
over hele verden. Fejldiagnostiseirng af kræft kan derfor være kritisk. Ukontrolleret
differentiering af celler kræver en større blodforsyning, hvilket ændrer morfologien af
blodårerne i huden. Disse ændringer kan bruges som biomarkør for klinisk personale,
som hjælp til diagnosticering af hudkræft. Disse morfologiske strukturer kan være
svære at detektere uden invasive midler.
Optical Coherence Tomografi er en non invasiv metode til at observere kompositionen
af huden. OCTA er en udvidet modalitet indenfor OCT, som kan bruges til visualis-
ering af de vaskulære netværk i væv.
Dette studie har til formål at præsentere metoder til at belyse hvordan en ultra high-
resolution SD-OCT system kan forbedre den nuværende opløsning og sensitivitet af
OCTA, og hvorledes dette kan gavne det kliniske personale med at diagnosticere
sygdomme indenfor dermatologi. Data brugt i afhandlingen er indsamlet af OCT-
systemet i afdelingen for fotonik på Danmarks Tekniske Universitet. Der er foretaget
mikroskopiske undersøgelser af kompositionen af væsker og deres betydning for detek-
tering af OCTA signaler. Herudover har blodets hastighed og scanningshastigheden
også indflydelse på detekterings udfald. Desuden vil der undersøges, hvorvidt håndter-
ing af artefakt signaler som speckle støj og vævs bevægelser vil forbedre detektering
af OCTA.
Yderligere sammenlignes OCTA signaler fra mælk med blod, for at se hvorvidt kompo-
sitionen har en betydning for detektering af et flow signal. Til sidst er disse metoder
testet på væv, for at visualisere det vaskulære netværk i huden.

Preface
This Master thesis has been prepared at the Technical University of Denmark for the
Department of Health Technology for the acquisition of a Master of Science degree
in Biomedical Engineering. This thesis presents methods for visualizing blood vessels
in human skin.
The project started on 22.10.2020 and ends on 21.04.2021. This thesis contains a
description and analysis of the various experiments that have been performed with
the methods and algorithms for blood flow detection.
Kongens Lyngby, 21st April 2021

CHAPTER 1
Introduction
1.1
Motivation
Skin cancer is a major health problem worldwide. I 2018 non-melanoma skin cancer
was ranked 5th commonly occuring cancer seen in men and women. In that year over
1 million diagnoses were made worldwide and could be an underestimation. [9] Neo-
plasm, which is the development of a new tumour, is divided into two classes: benign
and malignant tumours. What they have in common is an uncontrolled proliferation
of cells. With an increasing amount of cells, blood supply plays a critical role as
more cells require a greater need for nutrients and oxygen. Which results in altered
vascular morphology and density. The vascular network and many other subsurface
microstructures are diﬀicult to evaluate without invasive agents and are necessary
to identify. These morphological changes appear as a biomarker for the clinics and
can be used among others, to diagnose and eventually treat skin cancer. Fluorescein
angiography is considered to be the golden standard when it comes to elucidating
blood flow in tissue. This method of imaging blood flow has been practised for over
50 years. [10] The major concern with this technique is that it is invasive and requires
dye injection. Blood vessels could therefore be obscured if there is a leakage of dye
within the tissue. Besides, haemorrhage may also occur during the injection, which
may cause additional opacities in the image. fluorescence angiography is also limited
when it comes to depth location in tissue and has thereby poor stereopsis. This means
that the delineation of neovascularization is not possible. Since the FA images are
not depth-resolved, it is diﬀicult to perform segmentation from the axial plane of the
scan. Another drawback of this method is that it is expensive and time-consuming
and requires a lot of safety management as some people could gain an allergic reaction
from it. [11] Optical coherence tomography OCT, on the other hand, is non-invasive
and can perform high-resolution images with high-speed imaging.
The method is
in vivo and performs cross-sectional scans as well as 3-dimensional scanning. It is
label-free with good stereopsis. The commonly used OCT systems are swept source
and spectral domaine OCT.
The vascular network is not resolved by itself in an OCT image. To visualize the
vascular network an extension of OCT is used called Optical Coherence Tomography
Angiography. The method is still in the development phase, but with unprecedented
resolution. This imaging technology works by scanning multiple B scans at the same
cross-sectional location and detects changes within them.
These signal variations

1.2
Problem statement
As mentioned, OCTA is in its early development when it comes to dermatology, where
various skin conditions can benefit from this form of technology. Besides, there are not
many experiments regarding OCTA with ultra-high-resolution OCT systems. There
is a need to advance the recent resolution and sensitivity of OCTA for skin diseases
and improve its potential in diagnosing skin diseases.
To elucidate the problem Bispebjerg Hospital Department of Dermatology are
interested and willing to provide resources for the thesis. The OCT modality used in
the hospital has not the best resolution or sensitivity, which limits the true potential
of OCTA by detecting the smallest vascular networks in skin.
This study aims to develop state of the art OCTA numerical model to generate
subsurface vascular images in human skin. A supercontinuum based spectral-domain
OCT system developed at DTU Fotonik is used to utilize its ultra-high resolution.
This study is a pre-clinical aid for clinics. We present OMAG, phase variance and
speckle variance which are OCTA techniques. Subsequently also test the model with
some acquired data of skin tissues and assess the models regarding the scientific
prospect of application. Chapter 1 contains a presentation and motivation for the
study. Chapter 2 introduces the clinical background of the project with the used
scientific terminology regarding the OCT system and OCTA techniques used. There
will furthermore be a description of the integumental and neoplasia which is the area
of application for this study.
Chapter 3, includes state of the art methods for detecting blood flow with OCTA,
best resolution and sensitivity for the system. Chapter 4 discusses the methods re-
quired to develop the OCTA imaging system. A brief description of the different
types of data the algorithm was tested on. How to handle bulk tissue motion by reg-
istration, artefact handling, application of motion contrast, thresholding and finally
visualization of blood flow. Chapter 5 and 6 will evaluate the results of the study and
draw conclusions based on the data and developed algorithm.

2.0.1
Integumentum
The Integumentum is an apparatus that separates the internal organ from the external
environment. The skin is the most versatile organ as it has many biological functions.
It acts as protection against physical damage from the environment and a barrier
for microorganisms to enter the body.
It helps maintain body fluid and protects
against sunlight and synthesis of vitamin D. It also regulates blood temperature with
respect to the environment and provides accommodation for inflammatory response.
Integumentum consists of two main layers. The first is the epidermis which is the
superficial layer and the thinner one. This layer is called epithelial tissue layer. The
second layer is called dermis. Dermis is a deeper and thicker layer and is referred
to as the connective tissue layer. Between these two main layers, you will find the
basement membrane, which also is called the hypodermis. Basement membrane acts
as a connective tissue layer between the main two layers.
2.0.2
Epidermis

are keratinocytes, melanocytes, Langerhans cells and Merkel cells, but where the main
composition is from the keratinocytes. Melanocytes are dendritic cells that produce
pigment granules melanin which gives the skin its colour. Langerhans cells are also
dendritic cells and are responsible for the immune system and have antigen-presenting
cells. The Merkel cells are epidermal cells and produce a cutaneous sensation. Ker-
atinocytes are the most dominant cell type and produce keratin. Keratin is a fibrous
protein. This functions as protection and is otherwise involved in wound healing and

2.0.3
Dermis
The dermis is composed of connective tissue layers and acts as the primary source of
nutrition for the cells in the skin. The dermis is mainly composed of collagen which
is a fibrous protein and is stress-resistant. Glandular and hair follicles are embedded
in the dermis. The dermis is divided into two groups. The first is the superficial
papillary dermis and the second one is the deeper reticular dermis. The papillary
dermis is thin and interdigitates with the epidermis, where it is still separated by
the basement membrane in between. The papillary dermis contains a lot of blood
vessels, ranging from capillaries, arterioles and venules. The reticular dermis is thicker
than the papillary dermis and consists of dense collagen which is interconnected with
elastic fibres and ground substance. Immune cells are also a common cell type found
in the dermis. It is mainly composed of dermal dendrocytes and acts as a part of
wound healing and inflammation. In addition, it is responsible for blood clotting and
consists of macrophages, T cells and mast cells. [13]
2.0.4
Basement membrane
The basement membrane is composed of intercellular and extracellular matrix and
act as an interface between the epidermis and dermis. The basement membrane layer
acts as a filter for molecular transport between the epidermis and dermis. Molecule
transport occurs mainly from the epidermis to the dermis. The membrane consists
of lamina lucida which are organized macro molecules that are connected to the

2.0.5
Vascular network
The vascular network of the dermis is divided into two main areas in the integumen-
tum. The first area is the superficial papillary plexus. This area is located between
the papillary and reticular dermis. The second network layer lies in the substantial
reticular plexus which is just above the junction and between the subcutaneous tissue
and the dermis. This network supplies hair follicles and sweat glands with oxygen and
nutrition. The vascular network is of significant importance as it is responsible for
the delivery of oxygen and nutrients to the tissue cells. In addition, it removes subse-
quent waste product and maintenance fluid homeostasis. The two plexuses are found
between the dermis and subcutaneous tissue and the other between the papillary and
reticular layers of the dermis.
2.0.6
Neoplasia
Neoplasia is defined as an abnormal growth of cells, also known as a tumour. There
are two types of neoplasia which are categorized by benign and malignant neoplasms.
In this chapter a basic etiology of cancer as well as clinical manifestations will be
given.
Neoplasia is a disorder with altered cell differentiation, while neoplasm is
defined by recent growth of tumours. The cells of neoplasia are uncoordinated and
lack autonomous regulatory control over the growth and division of their own cells.
The repair and renewal of cells are controlled by two processes called proliferation and
differentiation. Proliferation is a process of cell division and is an adaptive mechanism
of cell replacement. Differentiation on the other hand, is a process that determines
the structure of the cells and gives the cells its functional characteristics for their
location from the cells they replace.
Apoptosis, which is programmed cell death,
does not occur for neoplasm. This means the cells keep growing and thus have a
greater need for nutrition and oxygen to survive. This results in angiogenesis which
means the formation of new blood vessels for the blood supply.
Benign neoplasm is well-differentiated, but has lost control of proliferation. This
type of tumour has slow cell growth and does not spread to other parts of the body
by infiltration, called metastasis. Tumour is characterized by benign ends in –oma
after its parenchymal tissue from where the growth of the cells occurs.
Malignant neoplasm does not have control over either the differentiation or the
proliferation. It has a much faster rate of cell growth and is involved in evasion and
spread to other parts of the body. Since this type of neoplasm invades and infiltrates
other parts of the body, it can result in the development of ischemia. Ischemia is a
restriction of blood supply as cell division will result in blockage of blood vessels. In
addition, tissue injury may also occur as the compressed blood vessel cannot transport

2.1
Optical Coherence Tomography
OCT is an optical imaging modality in biomedical optics. OCT is characterized by
taking high-resolution cross-sectional images. In addition, one is also able to take
3-dimensional volumetric images. Optical coherence tomography works by sending
light into a tissue, which echoes backscattering light from the tissue sample. The two
components that are taken into account in an OCT scan are magnitude and echo
time delay. With this highly sensitive optical modality, one does not need to remove
tissue to examine specimens known as in vivo. In addition, this modality can benefit
excisional biopsies, which are hazardous or impossible to perform otherwise because
of their invasive behavior. It can also help to detect false-negative assumptions if one
could have overlooked something during the biopsy. The data from the scan appears
in grey scale or false colour.
A single scan along the direction of the axial scan is called an A-scan. By per-
forming multiple A-scans by a cross-section scan, one can obtain a 2-dimensional data
set known as a B-scan. A raster scan on the other hand will obtain a 3-dimensional
data set called a C-scan. The X-axis will be the transverse fixed scan direction. The
y-axis will be the direction of the transverse slow scan and the z-axis will be the axial
direction of the beam.
2.1.1
Spectral-domain OCT (SD-OCT)
SD-OCT is an OCT system that uses a broad bandwidth light source and a spec-
trometer. It measures the interference spectrum of the interferometer with a line
scan camera. SD-OCT acts as a periodic frequency filter, where the period itself
depends on the path length difference between the light from the sample arm and
the reference arm. Path length difference indicates the relative time delay between
the sample and reference arm. This is used to determine the depth information in
tissue. [16]
In figure 2.3 one can see the schematic structure of an SD-OCT system. The light
comes from a low coherence source and goes through a fibre optic coupler that splits
the incident power into two parts. One part is fed to a reference arm and the other to
a sample arm where the specimen is. The beams from the reference and sample arms
are reflected back with a relative delay. The interference pattern of the two signals is
detected in the spectrometer from which an A-scan is formed. The reference arm is
fixed in an SD-OCT system. The SD-OCT looks at spectral content that comes from
the spectral interference that is encoded. The well-known artefacts of an SD-OCT
system are mirror artefacts in the image, where the SD-OCT system cannot solve the
difference between positive and negative values according to zero delay. Sensitivity

2.2
Optical Coherence Tomography Angiography
OCTA is an extended modality of OCT that visualizes blood flow in the OCT images.
This is done by performing repeated cross section scans over the same location, but
at different time points. Blood flow can be visualized by variation in OCT signals
from the different B scan. This variation in signal strength is due to the fact that
blood has moved between the two B-scans, whereby there have been pixel changes at
these particular positions, which illustrates moving particles. The remaining tissue
components will be steady.
The red blood cells will therefore act as an intrinsic
contrast agent for blood flow as they change position over time. One can thus visualize
microvascular networks without the use of intravenous dye injection. OCTA is done
by two main pieces of information from the OCT scan. It is the amplitude and phase
information in the image. In addition, it is important to take motion compensation
into account along the axial shift of the B-scans, as tissue movement can give false
positive information about blood flow.

Phase based OCTA can quantify the axial velocity and is based on the Doppler effect.
The primary approach is to look at the phase difference between the scans.

4.5.0.1
Variable inter-scan time analysis (VISTA)
In order to distinguish flow In an OCTA signal, we will conduct experiments regu-
lating the speed of blood and the inter-scan time. We know there is a time interval
between two consecutive B-scans. During this interval blood particles would have
moved a certain distance. There changes are what we observe in an OCTA signal.
The interval between two consecutive B-scans can be subdivided. Acquisition time
is the time it takes to make a B-scan. in other words, it is the time for an A-scan
times the number of A-scans in a B-scan. After a scan has been made, the laser
must relocate to perform a new scan without retrieving data. The time for relocation
is called fly back. This time corresponds to 10-20% of the total time for making a
B-scan. The sum of acquisition time and fly back is called inter-scan time. This is
==PAGE:46==
26
4 Material & methods
the time delay ∆T, where a new B-scan can be performed. Inter scan time plays a
critical role in motion contrast detection. The distance the fluid particles can travels
between scans is depended om the inter-scan time. If the change in blood cell position
relative to the OCT beam is small. The OCTA signal will then also be weak and
vise versa. A longer interscan time increases sensitivity to motion because more time
has been elapsed between the repeated B-scan. Shorter interscan time will reduce
sensitivity to exercise as the blood particles have barely moved but is more sensitive
to flow impairment. The distance traveled is also determined by the speed of the
particles. One can the use different inter-scan times to discriminate relative blood
flow speed. This technique is called Variable interscan time analysis (VISTA). [10]
Most commercial instruments used have an interscan time of 4-5 ms, where high-
speed research instruments can go all the way down to 1.5 ms interscan hour or
less. Interscan time is governed by certain parameters. First, the A-scan rate of the
instrument is of great importance for interscan time. The density of A-scan sampling
and B-scan dimension are also of significant importance. A slower A-scan rate will
result in a longer interscan time for the given A-scan density and B-scan dimension.
Current commercial instruments use long interscan time and thus have improved
sensitivity for slow detectable flow. Faster flow with a long interscan time will not
be possible as the OCTA signal will be saturated and thus can not be differentiated.
Long interscan time has a low saturation limit and can not distinguish the difference
in faster flows. Long interscan time has the advantage that they can detect a large
part of the blood vessels, but no difference in flow rate.
In addition, if the flow
rate is greater than the fastest distinguishable flow or saturation limit, there will be
no difference in OCT signal and flow impairment and the OCTA signal cannot be
detected. Short interscan time, on the other hand, has poor sensitivity to detect slow
flows, but a high saturation limit to distinguish the difference between flows. Faster
flow is no longer saturated enabling detection of flow impairment. The size of the
erythrocytes and the OCT beam are of great importance when it comes to the OCTA
of blood vessels. [10]

4.6.1
Bulk tissue motion
Following data acquisition, bulk tissue motion needs to be addressed as it reduces the
angiographic signal within the image. Since the OCTA algorithms look at the same
cross section, dispeaclement of the tissue between scans will affect the final detection
of blood vessels in the image. There is two types of bulk tissue motion, in plane and
out of plane.
4.6.1.1
Out of plane motion
This movement is found along the x-y direction, known as in-face motion. Out of
plane motion is essential to handle when examining eyes as they move side to side.
Out of plane motion is also hard to rectify and needs to be addressed with external
softwares. Since we are looking at skin in this thesis, out of plane motion correction
is not needed to be corrected as long as the skin does not move physically while
scanning.
4.6.1.2
In-plane motion
In-plane motion correction is called image registration.In image registration, you must
align the images so that in-plane motion is minimized. This motion is observed along
the direction of the fast scan, i.e. the depth in the image as in a B-scan. This comes
from changes in the focus point. Image registration is divided into two categories.
The first one is feature based and the second one is intensity based registration.
Feature based registration is popular, but requires utilizing certain features that can
be tracked from scan to scan. Unfortunately, this requires a lot of processing power
and the identified features are not usually completely identical between scans. Low
contrast in the image can make the features undetectable or if the image is blurred
by speckle noise. Feature based image registration can therefore be unreliable. The
other method, which is intensity based, uses cross correlation to detect similarities

between images. Some cons of this method is that if the B-scan has a high resolution
with a lot of pixels, the cross correlation process can take a lot of time. There is a
trade off between performance and cost when choosing the algorithm for bulk tissue
motion. A new method is then used for image registration. In motion correction, you
generally look at displacement of the image and if there is need for rotation or scaling
of the image. For this approach we are only take account of displacement along the
z-axis.
The highest intensity in an OCT image comes from the surface of the tissue, which

4.7
Speckle artifact
Besides bulk tissue motion, artifacts can also disrupt the performance of the OCTA
algorithm. In this thesis we look at speckle noise. Speckle noise is able to amplify
signals of static tissue components and thus greate false positive signals of of flow
Coherence is essential for making a OCT image, but is also to be blamed for the
formation of speckle. Speckle will degrades the quality of the image. this is done
by reduces the contrast and obscures the boundaries of scattering structure in the
image. This makes it diﬀicult to resolve the structures visually. Speckle is a result
of autocorrelation where the signal in the sample is backscattered multiple times by
superimposed tissue.
It contains signal information, but the high frequencies of the signals will be deter-
mined as noise. Speckle has no relation to the texture of the tissue. It is characterized
as interference between random reflected waves which otherwise is coherent. Speckle
is controlled by some specific parameters. Here among the size of the light source,
temporal coherence, multiple scattering, aberrations of propagating beam, multiple

that if you remove all speckle from the image, there will be no image left. We therefor
have Signal-carrying speckle which we like to accentuate and signal-degrading speckle
which we would like to attenuate.
There are 4 different methods to deal with speckle noise. Polarization diversity
based, spatial compounding, frequency compounding and digital signal processing.
Among these methods, digital signal processing is the most widely used and wavelet
appears to be the optimal method to handle speckle noise. Wavelet can improves
contrast to noise ratio of highly scattering features. [24]
4.7.1
Denoising
The OCT images are denoised to reduce speckle noise. Fourier transform is a powerful
tool for examining frequency signals. Fuorier transform looks at the sum of waves and
is not functional for signals with abrupt changes that speckle pattern contains. To
minimize signal loss,wavelet is then used. Wavelets are localized in time and frequency.
It looks at trends in a signal and can handle abrupt changes. There are different types
of wavelet filters, but in common they have zero mean and are selected in relation to
the specific application. In the literature, the most commonly used wavelets for OCT
are Daubechies, Symlet, Coifiet, and Discrete Meyer. For this study, a Deaubechies
type 4 has been selected. The wavelet has a bandpass characteristic and is adaptable
by scaling and shifting. Scaling is about stretching the wavelet function in time. If you
scale it up by 2, you will reduce the frequency by half. By stretching the wavelet you
look at slow changes in the signal, while with a compressed wavelet you can examine
abrupt changes in the signal. Shifting or delaying is about leveling the wavelet with
the feature you are looking at in the signal.
There are 2 types of wavelet methods. The first is Continuous Wavelet. This
method is used for time frequency analysis and filtering time localized components.
The second method is Discrete Wavelet which is used for denoising and compression of
images and signals.Discrete Wavelet uses few coeﬀicients and hence the name discrete.
In this case, the discrete wavelet is used as we work with images. Wavelets have
bandpass characteristics. they use low-pass and high pass filters to divide the signal
into 2 bandwidths where half of the samples are removed due to the nyquist criterion.
The low-pass filtered part of the signal is called the approximation level and the high-
pass filtered part is called the detail level. Decomposition level is the amount of time
the signal is divided. We only divide the approximation level into a new detail and
approximation level.For the OCT signal, a decomposition level of 4 was selected. The
structure of the decomposed signal is given in figure 4.7

Clinical application
The algorithm is not ready for clinical application yet. For the phantom model we
could regulate the flow speed and calibrate the inter-scan time accordingly. In real life
when measuring blood flow in the skin, we have different sizes of vein with different
blood speed within. Some of the veins will be resolved in OCTA with a specific inter-
scan time and some of them will not. It is yet not possible to use an inter-scan time
that can resolve all the vein structures in the skin. Some inter-scan times results in
poor image resolution because of fewer A-scan rate in the image.There is therefore
also a trade off between resolving the image and flow detection. The OCTA algorihms
also shows tissue structure. This is a problem because it can give asumption of vessel
structure, when it could be skin. There is also a trade off when choosing a threshold
for the denosing as we may loose some flow information in the process. to reach
clinical application look at the sections for future work for the phantom and for skin.

Conclusion
This study aimed to investigate how an ultra-high-resolution SD-OCT system could
advance the present resolution and sensitivity of OCTA, and how this would benefit
diagnosing diseases in dermatology. characterization of the ultrahigh-resolution OCT
system was made in terms of resolution and sensitivity. The axial resolution was
calculated to be 3.25 micrometres and a lateral resolution of 6.31 micrometres. The
sensitivity of the system was calculated to be 82.81dB
The OCTA algorithms have been tested on simulated flow imaging data, where
all three OCTA methods, OMAG, speckle and phase variance performed well.
Conduction of flow phantoms for OCTA signal analysis was made. In relation to
the choice of liquid for OCTA, milk works well, as it consists of small fat particles
of 3 micrometres, which can be resolved by the lateral resolution of the system. In
addition, they reflect more light than blood, as the particles were more visible in the
OCT image without OCTA processing.
OCTA for blood requires a long interscan time, as erythrocyte particles have a
diameter of 7 micrometres and requires a greater travel distance to be detectable.
This longer interscan time may result in more visible bulk tissue motion, as more
time has elapsed. The OCT system is more sensitive to smaller particles than to
larger particles compared to milk. Interscan time and blood speed play a critical role
when it comes to detecting OCTA signals. Interscan time also affects the resolution
of the image, as a short interscan time has fewer A-scans for a B-scan. There is a
trade-off between image quality and flow detection.
A numerical model of the OCTA algorithms was proposed. OMAG showed the
best performance compared to the speckle and phase variance methods. This could
be clearly observed in the CNR values for the phantom measurements. This was also
expected, as OMAG processes the intensity and the phase information in the image.
The number of B-scans required for OCTA was critical and determined whether
one could detect flow signal or not. The number of B-scans depends on the fluid used,
as the reflectivity of the fluid varies.
The denoising algorithm attenuates speckle-noise a lot and improves the OCTA
resolution of the image, but some of the flow signals can be lost. There is a trade-
off between the preservation of signal information and the attenuation of speckle-
noise. In addition, some phantoms show phase information after denoising, which
was otherwise not possible.
Conduction of OCTA visualization of human blood vessels was done, but it is not

There is a need for an inface examination of the tissue. There also a need to remove

A.16
Titanium phantom
In this appendix you can see the first phantom model that was made. the purpose
was to create a fluid with a refractive index corresponding to blood between 1.4-1.43.
In addition, the liquid had a viscosity reminiscent of blood corresponding to 4.4 + -
0.5 cP at 37C. The liquid was prepared with 47.38% water. 36.94% glycerol. 15.68%
sodium iodine where 0.5% sodium must be added every half hour to maintain the
correct refractive index.
After calibrating the abbe refractometer I tried to determine the refractive idenx
of the titanium liquid. but it did not give a clear result.
I also tried to determine the refractive index of liquid mixtures of cream and milk.
In addition, I tried to assess refractive index of yogurt and milk, but it did not
measure properly.
Below you can see the experiment with the titanium liquid.
The titanium liquid got stuck in the silicone hose and reflected strongly. which
gave error measurements as you can see.
The titanium in the fluid blocked the opening of the syringe which meant that no
liquid ran through the silicone hose. One had to constantly shake the syringe, which
gave noise signals at the measurement.
