Abstract
High Luminance Laser Light Devices have received a lot of attention in recent studies.
This is due to the great brightness that these devices can achieve. They consist of a high-
power laser diode, a superior optical system of lenses, and phosphor materials. Phosphor
materials are able to absorb and excite different ranges of wavelength of the light (due
to Stoke shift effect ) depending on their consistency. In this study, the photometric and
colormetric characteristics of three differenrt phosphor materials are examined, as well as
the limitation of brightness that phosphor material produce.
The three different phosphor material are CASN:Eu ,YAG:Ce,SCHOOT.Due to different
bandgaps as well as different doppants can produce different range of wavelength in the
output light .CASN:Eu produce light in the red range of wavelegths ,YAG:Ce and SCHOOT
produce yellow range light which is mixed with the scattered blue light and the output light
is white.The luminance is changing as the input power of the laser and the laser spot
size changes.Due to saturation of the phosphor materials in elevating input power the
luminance that those devices can achieve is limited

Introduction
Recent studies shown that there is a rapid increase in replacement commercial light de-
vices with LEDs.LEDS are known as high efficiency and high brightness devices that can
lead to a reduction of greenhouse gas emissions.They are capable to produce different
colours and different brightness as an input power changes. LEDs are widely spread in
the industry and they have replaced a significant amount of light emitted devices over the
last century[1].One method to produce LED is to excite a phosphor material with a 455nm
LED .The light that is emitted by this set up is a mix of fluorescence light of the phosphor
and excitation blue light from the LED [2]. The main issue with the commercial LEDs
is that they suffer from a notable drop in the efficiency rate at high input currents.A new
method to reduce the drop in the efficiency at elevating power lever is to replace the LED
as a light source with a laser diode [3].The figure 1.1 represents the comparison of power
efficiency with the use of LED as light source and with the use of LD as a light source
versus input power density with a wavelength 450nm
Figure 1.1: Comparison of power conversion efficiency versus input power density for a
LED and a LD at 450nm [4]
Figure 1.1represents the comparison of the conversion of the input power between the
LED and the LD.At high power density there is a drop in the efficiency of the LED .In
comparison LD efficiency which the efficiency drop is at higher input power density.
The production of LEDs with the use of the Laser Diodes (LD) as a light source are promis-
ing for significantly high luminance applications at elevating input powers densities .How-
ever, there is a big disadvantage in the Laser Lighting System, the efficiency drop at a high
irradiation of LD[2]. This phenomenon is called ”luminescence saturation” and it is differ-
ent for different phoshor materials[1].The efficiency drop is taking place mainly due mainly
to thermal quenching where most of the transition inside the phosphor is a non-radiative
.This provokes a decrease of the emitted light due to an increase of the temperature of the
phosphor.A simple way to avoid part of the thermal quenching is to increase the thermal
conductivity of the phoshor .This can be done by mixing glass into the phosphor pow-
der.Another way is to produce ceramics phoshor with aluminum oxide (Al2O3) which can
rise the thermal conductivity.However,the thermal quenching of the phoshor in laser light-
ing systems is an important issue and it need phoshor with high thermal conductivity[5]. .

2.1
Radiometric and Photometric Quantities
Human eye is a very sensitive detector in receiving environmental data. However, the
information that a human eye collects are limited to the visible spectrum .Detectors can be
used for measuring the optical radiant energy in the visible spectrum as well as outside of it
.Hence the two basic techniques of measuring the light can roughly separate in radiometry
and photometry
• Radiometry is the measurement of energy per time (= power, expressed in watts)
released by light sources and impinging on a certain surface.This measurements
include physical quantities such as photon energy , Number of photons
and
Radiant flux e.c.t.
• Photometry is the measurement of optical quantities of light that are closely con-
nected to human vision sensitivity.
Both techniques are required to identify the light.
2.1.1
Basic Photometric Quantities
In this thesis photometric quantities are very important to characterize the output light
.Some of the basic photometric quantities are :
• Luminous Flux describes the overall radiance that is emitted by a light source and
captured by human visual system .The visual system is more sensitive to the green
yellow spectrum (500-600 nm ) than the red one(650nm ),so the absolute power
of radiation would produce a significantly stronger sensitivity in the green spectrum
than in the red one .The units of this quantity are lumens (lm)
• Luminous Intensity is a measurment of power of the light source in a specific direc-
tion per unit of a solid angle based on luminosity function.The units of the quantity
are lumens per steradian (lm/sr) or candela (cd) which relates to the light intensity
emitted by a plumber’s candle of a specific size
The luminus flux of a 1 cd luminous intensity is 4π lm=12.57lm if we assume that
there is an isotopic light source
• Illuminance is defined as the energy of light source that is incident and spreading
on a surface that is weighted by the eye’s sensitivity function.The metric units of
illuminance are cd/m2.
• Luminance is describing the photometric quantity of the luminous intensity per unit
area of the light which is traveling in one direction .The metric unit of luminance is
cd/m2
• Luminous exitanceis a unit of measurement for the luminous flux per area emitted
or reflected from a certain spot on a surface.The unit is lm/m2.
The transformation between radiometric to photometric units is given by eye sensitivity
function V (λ).This function has a maximum sensitivity at yellow green spectra where the
function has a value of unit V (555) = 1.0[6]

objects to the natural color of the object .The value of the CRI is determinate by the com-

Phoshor Materials
Phoshor materials are materials that can absorb an optical radiation and emit a secondary
optical radiation in different wavelength.The mechanism that a phoshor emits a secondary
optical radiation is photoluminescence..This can be divided in two categories: 1) Fluores-
cence, which is caused by spin allowed transitions and has lifetime 10ns to 100ns .The
emitted light of fluorescence has longer wavelength so lower photon energy of the initial
radiation.Those transitions are illustrated in the picture 2.4 2)Phosphorescence which is
due to spin forbidden transition so it has longer lifetime >100ns. In the LED technolo-
gies the mechanism is mostly the flurescence because they do not saturate fast at high
intensity radiations.

2.2.1
Composition of Phosphor Materials
Phoshor materials consist of a host lattice and which is doped with impurities.The host
lattice has a large energy bandgap that can excite lattice vibration.The impurities can be
separated in two categories Activators and Sensitizers
Activators are atoms inside the phosphor material added as doppans in the crystal of
the material.This materials absorb a specific wavelength photon and emittes a longer
wavelength photon. The radiative lifetime of the activators is short, in order high rate
transition of the ground state to occur.The atom that can be optically active doppanes in
a phoshor material is mostly rare-earth atoms -lanthanids-such as cerium(Ce), europium
(Eu)..
Sensitizers are dopped atoms that contribute in shifting the emitted photon in the desired
wavelength.In the process of absorption the sensitizer transmit the excited electron to to
the activator where it recombines.The properties of phoshor material define the proper
sensitizer .Sensitizer can be district atoms like Mg in other cases can be oxygen atom (O)
which is adjust to the closest activator atom.However hot lattice can also act as activator.

2.2.3
Temperature quenching
Temperature quenching is referring to the drop of the phosphor efficiency as the temper-
ature increasing. The thermal quenching of YAG:Ce is a good example of describing the
mechanism of this phenomena. The electron configuration of the activetor Ce is [Xe] 4f¹
5d¹ 6s² . The thermal quenching is take place at 4f-5d transitions.In the 5d state due to
strong crystal field of the host material are splitting into 2 stages 5d1 and 5d2. At very high
temperatures the electron are located in 5d states will may be excited in the conduction
band of the host material. As a result the electron is diffused in the host material and may
be trapped in a defect point of the crystal where the non radiative process is dominated.
The propability of this process has a Boltzman distribution ∆E/kT where ∆E is the en-
ergy difference between the states of Ce and the lowest level of the conduction band this
called ionization energy and T is the absolute temperature. The mechanism of thermal
quenching in YAG:Ce is illustrated in figure 2.12
Figure 2.12: Thermal quenching of YAG:Ce [9]
The thermal quenching depend on a lot of parameters. One of them is the location of
Ce inside the host material. An other one is the energy gap of the host material. As the
difference between the energy level of the activator and the energy bandgap of the host
material is smaller the thermal quenching is stronger.The figure 2.13 illustrates different

devices.As known LD are combined of quantum wells and they can emit photon with range
from ultraviolet (UV) to infrared (IR).The big challenge in laser lighting system which are

one . The circulazation of beam of LD can be done with the two techniques techniques
[15].
• Aspheric Lens
• Cylindical Lens or Anamorphic Prism Pair

that coupled in 100µm cores size fiber.Due to the eliptical shaped of the output beam of

• CASN:Eu phorshor material is a combination of a ratio of glass layer and CASN:Eu
with glass/CASN ratio 60% respectively
• YAG:Ce phorshor material is a combination of a ratio of glass layer and YAG:Ce
with glass/YAG:Ce ratio 60% respectively .
• SCHOTT is a commercial static ceramic converted which include YAG:Ce and glass

5
Conclusion
This thesis aims to examine photometric and colormetric properties of three different phos-
phor materials and to investigate the limitation of high luminescence in them .Based on
two different optimal set up and a high power diode laser that is coupled to a fiber , the
phosphor material was exited in with different laser spot diameters and in different power
levels .The output light was gradually collected and analysed.
The phosphors that is analysed are the CASN:Eu,which emits in the range of red, the
YAG:Ce and the SCHOTT which both emits in yellow range.The output light of the last
two phosphor materials is close to white.
At the beginning there is an investigation of the spectra of the output light and the tem-
peratureof the phosphor material as the input power of the laser is increasing.At higher
input power levels the temperature is increasing due to thermal quenching and that cause
the luminescent part of the light to decrease.The relative luminescent flux as well as the
relative total flux is calculated with the integration of the area inside the spectra .The flux
of the output light shows saturation of the phosphor at higher power levels due to thermal
quenching. The ratio between the relative total flux and the relative luminescent flux is
decreasing because at high input laser power the blue part of the spectrum is dominated
.
The spectra is different for the three different phosphor material.For CASN:Eu the peak of
the luminescent part is close to 630 nm which is at the range of red but in the YAG:Ce and
the SCHOTT is close to 570 which is in the yellow range.The temperature in the CASN:Eu
is increasing rapidly with the input laser power in comparison with the other two phosphor
materials which is correlates to the fast decreasing in the luminescent part of CASN:Eu
of the spectrum at high power levels.The relative luminescent flux as well as the relative
total flux is higher at YAG:Ce and SCHOTT than in the CASN:Eu due to less saturation
at high power levels
For the photometric properties of the three different phosphor materials where also in-
vestigated for different laser spot sizes.The tempearture in the small laser spot sizes is
increasing rapidly in comparison witht the bigger laser spot sizes for all of the phosphor
materials this is because the heat tranfered in all direction for the smallest spot sizes in
comparison with the bigger ones.For CASN:Eu the temperature is reaching the highest
values at all the spot sizes in comparison with the YAG:Ce and the SCHOTT because
the saturation in the CASN:Eu is taking place in all the spot sizes and in the YAG:Ce and
SCHOTT the saturation occur only in the small laser spot sizes .The saturation threshold
for the phosphor materials is increasing as the laser spot increases and for the YAG:Ce
and SCHOTT there no saturation at the bigger laser spot sizes
The colormetric properties of the three different phosphor are also investigated.The CCT
is calculated for different laser spot sizes and different input power levels .The CCT is
increasing as the input laser power increases .Also it is higher for the smallest spot sizes
and lower for the bigger ones,this is because at bigger spot sizes the saturation occurs
in high input power levels so less blue light is converted to red in case of CASN:Eu and
yellow in case of YAG:Ce and SCHOTT.This is also shown in the CIE diagrams as the
input power increases the output light moves more to blue part .The luminous exitance
was also calculated in different laser spot sizes and different power levels and it seems
that the smaller laser spot size has the highest luminous exitance.For the CASN:Eu is
obvious the different in luminous exitance for the different laser spot sizes ,this is due to
better confinement of this phosphor material.Finally ,the illuminance was measured in 30
