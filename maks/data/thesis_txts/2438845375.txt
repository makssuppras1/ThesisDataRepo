Abstract
English
The goal of the thesis is to investigate the thermal stability of electrodeposited
Fe-C. This is done by constructing adding single layers and multilayer con-
structions of electroplated Zn, Ni or both. Besides a reference the samples are
diusion annealed at 300 ◦C for 1h, 24, or 48h. Techniques of LOM, SEM, EDS,
XRD and Vickers microhardness indentations are used to extract data. The
results show appearing intermetallic Fe-Zn phases with heat treatment Γ2, η +
δ and nally Γ1. The NiZnFe-C diusion couple matches a ζ phase and
ternary phase after 24h annealing. A possible match of the FeNi phase is found
for the NiFe-C couple. Hardness measurements of the bulk Fe-C at dierent
congurations shows an increase with annealing and a maximum around 24h
for the Zn single layers.

Chapter 2
Introduction
Steel is a widely used material. It is essential for industrial applications ranging
from massive constructions within transportations and buildings to precision
instruments. It is an alloy of iron and carbon with additional elements added
for specic properties. The material is chosen for its high strength and capability
to adjust its properties by various treatment possibilities.
The properties of the steel can be improved through hardening treatments of
both heating and and coldworking depending on its composition, while its re-
sistance to corrosion can be improved by diusing it with a protecting material
such as Zn. Zn is soft and has a low melting point and is corroding very slowly.
Steel can be alloyed with various materials, while knowledge on solid diusion
couples of electrodeposited steels is still limited and possible to expand. Lit-
erature shows examples of galvanised samples, while limited work is found on
electrodeposited couples of Fe-C and Zn.
While galvanising is a common method of protecting steel, there is little material
on connecting electrodeposited steel as a diusion couple while maintaining the
hardness of the material. A good initiation point could be coupling with Zn due
to its anti corrosive application known in advance.
In order to investigate the diusion of electrodeposited Fe-C and thereby the
thermal stability of the these, microstructure, hardness, chemical composition
and phase analysis needs to be performed. Construction types of simple single
layers of either Zn or Ni deposited on Fe-C are investigated as well as the eect
of a decreased diusion path in multilayer constructions of either Zn, Ni or a
combination of the three are investigated in accordance to the annealing time

The Fe-C coatings have a smooth black wavy surface while the substrate side
show both lines possibly from the substrate topology and spherical shapes
may be caused by hydrogen development during the deposition.
