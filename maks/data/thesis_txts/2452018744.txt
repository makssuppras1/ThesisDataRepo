Summary
In this thesis, a new guiding system in the field of deep learning for MIDI keyboard is
presented. The system employs Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) for predicting the
next upcoming note with respect to the inputs implemented by users. The keyboard
contains 88 keys with individual LED attached to indicate the signal from RNNs.
This project was inspired by Jeremy’s initial idea for designing a special instrument
for people suffering from hearing issues; for whom, only certain ranges of sound frequency
is detectable and ordinary musical instruments are not applicable for them. Since the
existing music pieces are not suitable for hearing loss people to play. Computational
algorithm can be applied for deleting certain frequencies and its utilization must assure
that the note is harmonic with previous melody. Moreover, music plays an important role
in human’s life via entertaining people. Listening to music and playing the instruments
improve people’s mental health. One of the most vital reason for disabled people is
that they try to hide their disability because they do not want to be treated differently
from normal people. However, this brings them pressure that cause mental unhealthy.
As a result, one of the main objectives of the current work is to remove the margin of
shame between people with the cited issues. Owing to the significance of this field, more
research and development is expected to occur.
The cited model model was trained on 30 pieces of MIDI music from Internet, the
dataset consists 3 mixed genres including : western classical music, traditional Chinese
music and modern pop music. This is a relatively new field of research, which combines
RNNs with hardware devices. The cited model allows user to play with the keyboard
interactively without any restriction. The idea of project is theoretical feasible; in which,
the age constraint is removed. To begin with, the model should be able to learn from
dataset and make a reasonable prediction. Afterwards, one must assure that components
can communicate with each other through Raspberry Pi.
It must be stated that in the current work, RNNs have been applied on MIDI key-
board, which can be noted as a novel route with not a wast history of utilization. More-
over, the current work can be used as a base for adding various features to the interactive
keyboard in future studies.

CHAPTER 1
Introduction
This thesis covers all my work on my final master project: Inspiration for music im-
provisation through Deep learning (RNNs). The purpose of this project is to design a
inspiration system, which takes a certain length of (musical) notes as input and offers
two options of the following note for users via Recurrent Neural Network(RNN) model.
Individual guiding LEDs is attached to each key on the keyboard indicating the two
options. Users can always master the music style by pressing any key other than the
provided options without any restriction. An perception evaluation will be introduced
to assess the quality of music generated by RNNs.
1.1
Motivation
“If I were not a physicist I would probably be a musician.”
“Life is like a Piano, what you get out of it depends on how you play it.”
“It occurred to me by intuition, and music was the driving force behind that
intuition. My discovery was the result of musical perception.”
— Albert Einstein
The quotes above show how vital music is to Albert Einstein, one of the most out-
standing scientists in 20th century, who regards music as an indispensable part of his
life. Many great musicians such as Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, Ludwig van Beethoven,
and Johann Sebastian Bach, left their masterpieces by their whole life devotion, which
is the great cultural treasure to human-beings.
Nowadays, more and more people have the chance to learn music. Musicians take
music as a medium to express their understanding and attitude towards life. Therefore,
how to compose the melody matching up with idea and emotion they want to express
becomes increasingly significant.
However, actually, composing is not an easy task.
It requires the composer has a high quality of knowledge accomplishment on music.
Provided you have a piece of music popping out of your mind, and you intend to make
a song but unfortunately you are stuck in the rest of the song, would it be helpful to
have an inspiration from existing songs and avoid plagiarizing? Is there something could
learn the pattern in music and provide suggestions? Deep learning will explain how this
goals can be achieved.

1 Introduction
However, not all people have the opportunity to learn how to play an instrument due
to various reasons. Enough practical time is required for human to master an instrument.
But what if a person without musical training wants to play a song on the piano? Or a
singer plans to compose his or her own song but stuck in the way of composing? Would
it be helpful to have a system providing some hints? How could this system be smart
enough to offer a reasonable option for user?
Technology boom allows scientists to explore more in interdisciplinary research. This
brings possibility to explore music in a scientific perspective. Since this project aims at
getting inspiration for music improvisation, the method RNNs is able to handle musical
signal in a mathematical way. In the coming chapter, we will explain the theory behind
RNNs thoroughly.
Recent years, deep learning and artificial neural networks (ANNs) gain extensive
attention among researchers and scientists for its powerful learning ability by mimicking
human neuron pervasive application in every aspect of modern life. Recurrent neural
networks (RNNs) is a specific sequence model differs a bit from ANNs. RNNs can store
previous inputs as memory for later process. RNNs plays an essential part in various
fields such as robot control, rhythm learning, music composition, speech recognition,
natural language processing (NLP), etc. RNNs is a sequence model in deep learning.
Since the purpose of learning how to use RNNs is to generate a clip of music by training
certain audio data set, which is a sequence data set, RNNs is an appropriate method for
the music generation.
1.2
Literature review
Music combines various tones for an hearing-pleasant melody. It plays an essential role
in social, physical, emotional and cognitive aspects. The quote ”Where words fail, music
speaks” clearly described music as an universal language of humanity. There are some
researches having been done on music generation by neural networks. However, in fact,
the working principle of how neural networks learn the pattern of music is a pandora
box for many people. In order to uncover the mystery of music generation via RNNs,
here are some related works[1][2].
1.2.1
Related works
The utilization of Long Short-Term Memory Network could be traced back to the stud-
ies conducted by Nayebi and Vitelli [3] , which was aimed for generating music on the
wave form of the music files. It must be stated that parallel to the cited research, the
investigations regarding music generation through gated recurrent unit networks could
be also witnessed within their work. The application of variational recurrent network
together with autoencoder with the objective of taking into account the benefits of lan-
guage model for generating music (utilizing raw files) have been implemented by Van den

trained through Johann Sebastian Bach’s chorales. The main objective of the mentioned
project is to generate and harmonize the chorales in a way that prevents them to be
recognized from Bach’s work. Moreover, the generation of wandering chorales would be
made within the conditions in which, at least one of the present voices is not fixed. The
cited project could be utilized for adding chorales to an already generated melody. The
research team of Degroote et al. [10] have been working on a project entitled as ‘Flow
Machines’ , which is aimed to be used for retaining the artists within their margin of
creativity. Throughout the cited project, with the applicability of utilizing a database
containing 13000 lead sheets, generating the leaf sheets could be fulfilled with respect to
implemented style of the composer. It must be stated that Markov constraints, which
are a type of neural network technique, have been used in the cited project. As another
work conducted by Google, one can state the socalled project of ‘Wavenet [11]’ ; in
which, Convolutional Neural Networks that are mainly applied for image generation and
classification, have been utilized. It must be stated that one of the most significant aims
of the illustrated project is the attempt which has been made for enhancement of text to
speech applications via a refined flow generation within the produced vocal sounds. For
generating music in a raw audio, the cited project could be applied both in the shape of
the input and output of the model.
The relevant studies of Aran Nayebi et al. [12] at research centers of Stanford uni-

1.3
Self evaluation
The thesis goal is mostly accomplished. However, music relies heavily on human’s per-
ception, and it is a challenge to account for the perception of music via mathematics
accurately. Perception lies on sophisticated functions of the nervous system in human
brain. Individuals’ perception depends on culture, education, mood, etc. Even genes
play a part in musical perception.
It is an ambitious and challenging project to compile software and hardware since
there is no such a system on keyboard with the implementation of neural networks.
Besides, references about inspiration for music improvisation in an interactive, real-time
manner are quite limited. Moreover, most of the code on Raspberry Pi is written by
myself. For instance, controlling the individual LED on the LED strip with the detection
of key-press by user. The interval between two neighbour individual LEDs on LED strip
differs from each other, and there is no such a product on market matching the LEDs
to keys exactly.
The RNNs built in this project learns the common pattern of the mixed-style music

4.2
Pitch, Notes and Chords
Pitch refers to the frequency of the sound, pitch name is represented with the letters
”A, B, C, D, E, F, G”. Pitch is a crucial and most basic aspect of music: sequences
of pitches form a melody, and simultaneous combinations of pitches form chords – two
foundations of western music.
Pitch is a psychological variable rather than a physical property of a sound. Dating
back over 150 years, there had been discussions about pitch from both perceptual and
neural perspective [28]. The term pitch has a standard definition having varied over the
years, perhaps reflecting the difficulty in unifying the concept. In 1951, the American
Standards Association (ASA) defined the pitch as ”that attribute of auditory sensation
in terms of which sounds may be ordered on a scale extending from low to high, such as
musical scale” [29]. In 2013, the standard was revised again, reintroducing the concept
of music and referring to pitch as ”that attribute of auditory sensation by which sounds
are ordered on the scale used for melody in music” [30], The definition is of convenience
for describing the role of pitch within music.
Note objects contain information about the pitch, octave, and offset.

The Sigmoid function squashes a vector into the range (0, 1). It is applied inde-
pendently to each element of the input vector. Sigmoid function is also called logistic

5.2
Data Splitting and Cross Validation
Data splitting is a machine learning technique for the sake of building a computational
model with high generalization ability and prediction. On the other hand, cross valida-
tion is one of the methodologies. Additionally, over-fitting can be avoided by statisti-
cally analyze the result of cross validation. The tool of analyzing is calculating the mean
square error (MSE).
In this thesis, K-fold cross validation was employed for assessing the model. Firstly,
the original dataset will be subdivided into k equal size subset. One of the subset will be
kept for testing, in the other words, the chosen subset is called test set accordingly. The
rest k-1 subsets are called training set, which will be used for training model. Secondly,
the training and testing process will be executed k times, every subsets of the k equal
size subsets will be selected as test set during cross validation procedure. Each iteration
will give a MSE value, and finally, a single estimation will be calculated in Equation 5.9
by averaging the k MSE values. The advantage of this method over repeated random
sub-sampling is that all observations are used for both training and validation, and each
observation is used for validation exactly once. Generally, K is an unfixed parameter,
which can be defined for various purpose, while 5-fold and 10-fold cross-validation are
commonly used in practice.
MSE = 1
kΣ(y −y)2
(5.9)
Note that in the Equation 5.9, k is the number of iteration or the number of folds, y
is the real label for each input accordingly and y is the output from the model.
A simple example, given k equals to 10, then the k-fold cross-validation is 10-fold
cross validation in this case. In the 10-fold cross-validation, the dataset will be split into
10 subsets randomly, so that all sets are equal size, see Figure 5.8. Then the model will
be trained on 9 subsets and validated on 1 subset separately, repeat 10 times. Normally
and conceptually, the split rate is 80%, which means taking 80% of the whole dataset
for training, the rest 20% is for evaluating the model’s performance. By having training
set and test set independently, over-fitting could be avoided effectively.

5.7
2IFC Evaluation
In the current work, three models of recognition memory have been compared with
respect to their ability in generalizing across yes/no and two-alternative forced-choice
(2AFC) testing.
The unequal-variance signal-detection model takes in to account a
continues process of memory strength, while, in terms of dual-process signal-detection
model, a threshold-like-recollection process has been added to a continues process of
memory strength. On the other hand, the old item distribution is comprised of a mix-
ture that takes in to account two distributions with various means. In the previous
studies regarding the compare of model capability in characterizing data from both of
the test formats, the significance of parameter reliability has not been considered.The
cited manner could be critical in terms of comparing models with various flexibility.
The investigations regarding the Parametric bootstrap simulations illustrated that the
regressions of the parameters with respect to separate fits for each type of test, merely
worked for identifying the least flexible model. On the other hand, the various fits of
ROC data from both types of the tests with the goodness-of-fit amended utilizing AIC,
had a successful recovery of the true model; from which, data was generated. Using
AIC and conducting simultaneous fits with the real data,the unequal-variance signal-
detection model was believed to be the most appropriate account among across yes/no
and 2AFC testing.
The Two-alternative forced choice (2AFC) could be defined as an unbiased method
that could be utilized to investigate the subjective personal experience of people account-
ing the pattern of choices and response times. The cited subject is introduced using two
alternative MIDI songs; among which, merely one includes the target model.The at-
tempt is to opt the correct MIDI file from the target model. Both of the MID files
would be illustrated successively to the person who performs the test.The cited route
is also named ’two-interval forced choice’, 2IFC). It must be stated that 2AFC test
is usually confused with the more commonly utilized yes/no task; in which, the test
conducting person must opt the category that the present item belong to.

CHAPTER 8
Discussion
In this chapter, firstly, the results will be discussed in relation to previous studies in
music generation through RNNs and theories presented in Chapter 5 and Chapter 4.
The feedback from perception test will also be discussed.
8.1
Results Discussion
Clearly, all models trained above had low test accuracy, usually around 0, even though
some models had high training accuracy. Furthermore, the model with high training
accuracy generated high quality music. Hence the performance of model can be explained
by training accuracy. Besides, all models reached a high accuracy in a fairly short period
during training. Theoretically, the training should stop to avoid over-fitting. However,
the music quality increased in spite of training accuracy increase slowly after reaching
its peak value or even sometimes decreased. As is known to all, over-fitting means the
model performs perfectly on training set but performs badly on test set, which is exactly
the same situation in this project.
Interestingly, the more over-fitting, the better music quality. Under normal circum-
stances, data should be split into a training set and a test set, or implementing a cross
validation to avoid over-fitting. The reason why over-fitting should be averted is that the
model is expected to have strong generalization ability. High training accuracy accounts
for the model has the ability to learn pattern within data set, low test accuracy means
the model cannot predict well. However, having low test accuracy does not mean the
quality of music generated via RNN is bad. In fact, the model aims at inspiriting music
improvisation rather than predicting existing music. As long as it has the power to offer
a harmonic prediction based on previous notes, the model should be regarded as a good
model.
As mentioned in Chapter 7, during training process, the accuracy dramatically
dropped around 200 epoch.
Note that the dataset consists of mixed style of music.
Especially the Chinese folk musics has a totally different style that makes it hard for
RNN to learn. The sudden drop during training process could be the repetition chords in
Chinese folk songs, since batch size was 64. Therefore during the training, some batches
were out of balance with respect to all classes.
==PAGE:70==
56
8 Discussion
8.2
Optimizer
At the beginning of this project, RMSprop optimizer was employed because of its nice
performance in the previous work and ”not bad” performance on the larger and mixed-
style dataset. Also, RMSprop is recommended for RNNs training. Moreover, RMSprop
is based on gradient-based optimization dealing well with the cumulative nature of neural
networks, tending to either gradients explode or gradients vanish. It takes all squared
gradients average every time into accounts to normalize the gradient. RMSprop adds
weight for small gradients to avert vanishing or subtract weight for large gradient to
avoid exploding. Although during the training process, all models have dramatic drop
at around 200 epoch after reaching peak values of accuracy, or the minima of loss, the
performance at the end of training was relatively well.
Among the 3 models with RMSprop optimizer, only the one with 0.2 dropout rate
performs stably after one big oscillation. The rest two had several oscillations before
1000 epochs, while the optimizer was expected to balance the very large or small changes
in gradients. The generated music sounds good but maybe the model can be improved
by changing the optimizer even though RMSprop optimizer is recommended for training
RNNs.
Adam optimizer, another optimizer based on SGD, tends to find one invariant direc-
tion of gradient when other directions are oscillating, which can find minima efficiently.
Empirically, Adam optimizer works well practically, and compares favorably to other
optimizer. When Adam optimizer was implemented, the one with 0.1 dropout rate failed
to learn. Adam optimizer with 0.2 dropout rate works better than any other models,
although it also dropped at around 200 epochs.
Note that Adam optimizer with 0.3 dropout rate was the only one who did not
drop around 200 epoch, whereas, it started to be unstable from 500 epoch and then the
training accuracy dropped back to 0 around 800 epoch.
8.3
Dropout Rate
From previous chapters, the dropout rate plays an important role during the training
process. Even a small change in dropout rate results in totally different results. For
instance, the model with Adam optimizer and 0.1 dropout rate prevented the model
from learning, which might be due to high regularization.
8.4
Analyzing Feedback from Perception Test
2AFC score from the survey was 0.62 which is above 0.50, indicating that we can some-
how believe that music quality increases as the model continues training.

CHAPTER 9
Conclusion
In the thesis we built an RNN that can learn the pattern of 30 pieces of mixed genres
MIDI files and then predict the following note by taking in user’s input. The RNN was
implemented on Raspberry Pi with a LED strip so as to offer real-time prediction.
First, we went through the baseline of this project from a pilot special course that
learned one certain genre of music to see whether the RNN has adaptive learning abilities
to learn from mixed styles of music. Then, We reviewed theories behind this thesis in
Chapter 1 and introduced the libraries or applications utilized on Raspberry Pi.
Most previous studies in music generation has proved that deep learning can provide
a way to generate music. However, most of them only have a training set and did not
provide interactive prediction in real-time manner. Note that a product in the market
also uses LED to guide user but it serves as a teaching assistant for starters to practice
the music theory. This thesis provide a convenient way for people who wants to play
the piano but has no music theory to play with the keyboard without restriction. For
composers, it offers inspiration for their improvisation.
Due to the limitation on the HPC at DTU as mentioned previously, only 8 models
were trained in this project. Two for Raspberry Pi with ten sequence length and six
for analyzing to figure out the best combination of hyper-parameters. Hence the testing
number of combination of hyper-parameter was reduced. The more previous information
we used for prediction, the better result we get out of the model. After training all
models, the Adam optimizer works slightly better than Rmsprop optimizer.
Another essential part of this thesis is to implement the RNNs model on Raspberry
Pi and compile LED strip, keyboard with Raspberry Pi. Although there were many
limitation for finding reference since the amount of research on guiding LED is fairly
limited, this thesis managed to compile all the components together. In the future, more
and more features could be added in the guiding system. It brings convenience for both
musicians and non-musicians. For musicians, this project offers notes based on the input
from musicians so as to inspire composers. For non-musicians, this project allows them
to play the keyboard without any command of music theory. In addition, it can improve
the efficiency of learning how to play the keyboard.
However, like all technologies has both sides, when it comes to improvisation, it does
can inspire composers, but the composers should not copy the outputs from RNNs. It
is hard to judge whether the melody belongs to the composer or to RNNs. Hence more
regularization and restriction should be put on the using music generation via RNNs.

9 Conclusion
The best combination of hyper-parameters among all the combinations has been dis-
cussed in the thesis. In spite of some functions which are recommended empirically, some
adjustments should be made based on the dataset. For instance, RMSprop optimizer
is recommended in many music generation project. However, this project revealed that
Adam optimizer works better than RMSprop optimizer, both in accuracy and stability.
There could be better a combination of parameters that are not tested in the project.
Thus, if it is possible, all pairs of hyper-parameters could be implemented to find the

be accurate but music prediction only needs high quality, which does not require an
absolutely accurate result.

added to test the performance of the system by asking tester to play with the interactive
keyboard. More discussion detail has been in chapter 8.
