Abstract
The following thesis explores the challenges faced by healthcare professions in data retrieval process,
with specific focus on the Danish healthcare research company CHIP. The healthcare professionals
currently rely on IT teams to navigate and understand the complex healthcare databases for data re-
trieval for their research projects. To address these challenges, this thesis adopted a User-Centered
Design approach, integrating the Double Diamond Framework and Agile Development to focus on
the development of metadata-driven workflow application and usability testing of the prototypes, a
final prototype was developed. The final prototype highlighted the identified key metadata attributes
and UX design elements that enhanced the current manual data retrieval workflow to support inde-
pendent navigation and understanding of healthcare databases by healthcare professionals without
relying on technical knowledge and IT teams. By conducting iterative prototyping and usability test-
ing, the final prototype had achieved a high usability with a System Usability Scale(SUS) score of
93.43, rating it as excellent, implying that the user involvement and UCD approach will lead to high
usability applications development.

Chapter 1
Introduction
The introduction chapter discuss the current workflows, data retrieval challenges in the company
CHIP. The research particularly discuss the limitations in the data retrieval for healthcare profession-
als. It explores the current manual workflow followed for data retrieval and highlights the dependence
on IT teams due to the complex healthcare database structures. Furthermore, this chapter outlines
the motivation for developing the user-centric workflow application to address these challenges and
streamline the data extract request workflow. Lastly, it discuss the research objectives, significance of
the study, and the structure of the paper.
1.1
Context
The context of this study is primarily focused on addressing the challenges encountered by the health-
care professionals in retrieving the required research data efficiently from the complex healthcare
databases. The study is carried out within the company CHIP, a Danish healthcare research com-
pany, and focuses on designing and developing a user-centric data retrieval workflow application to
streamline the data retrieval process.
1.1.1
The Company CHIP
The Danish company CHIP[7], mainly operates in healthcare research domain and employs vari-
ous healthcare professionals(medical researchers, bioinformaticians, doctors,..) and IT professionals.
This company mainly works on the patient’s data who are suffering from immune dysfunction infec-
tions such as HIV, organ transplantation, genome data, protein data, blood bank data, biobank data

by retrieving data from multiple interconnected healthcare databases. Healthcare professionals col-

1.1.2
Current Workflow for Data Extract Requests
The current workflow for retrieving required data by healthcare professionals is mostly manual and
completely dependent on collaboration with IT team. The healthcare professionals make their data
extract requests using a word document file created by the IT department. The word document file
contains a pre-defined template that outlines the research project requirements and is submitted to
IT team, which is sent to the medical research group leader for approval. If the approval is granted
the IT team will create a specific Stored Procedure or SQL queries based on the complexity of the
data extract request. If the IT team does not get a clear overview of data extract request, they will be
meeting with the data requester and clear the doubts with each other and then the data retrieval will
be completed by IT team.
1.1.3
Challenges in Data retrieval at CHIP
The challenges in data locating and retrieval at CHIP arises from the healthcare professionals re-
stricted access to databases, understanding of the database schema, limited SQL knowledge, complex
relationships between tables and likewise the IT team has limited knowledge in the medical domain.
Due to which, the IT team must interact with healthcare professionals on identifying the exact re-
quired tables, spending extra time and efforts, leading to delays in delivering the precise requested
data.

1.2. Motivation
The challenges in data retrieval identified at CHIP can be overcome with development of a user-
centered workflow application that helps the healthcare professionals to understand and navigate
through the complex database structures by implementing intuitive interfaces, helping them to request
data with more precision and reducing dependency on IT teams. The research [42] also highlights
the importance of user-centric applications in addressing healthcare research data challenges. These
applications helps in making collaboration between technical and healthcare professionals, which
results in improving the data retrieval process. In the end, this approach contributes to streamline
operations, improved decision-making and accelerate the research advancements in the healthcare
research projects.
1.2
Motivation
For development of an effective medical research studies, there should be a smooth flow of data
retrieval that is supported by non-technical users, who find it difficult to handle complex healthcare
databases. In this section, the challenges in data retrieval and possibility for the development of
user-centric solutions to these problems are discussed.
1.2.1
Challenges for Non-Technical users
The healthcare professionals at the company CHIP has restricted database access, lacks SQL knowl-
edge, unable to understand the complex healthcare database structures and relationship between vari-
ous tables. Jeddi et al.[42], also discuss that the non-technical users such as healthcare professionals
often lacks the knowledge in SQL queries, forcing them to rely on the IT team for the data retrieval.
Such dependencies will result in slowing down the research process, and might effect the data accu-
racy for research projects.
This study addresses the challenges for non-technical users such as healthcare professionals in locat-
ing and retrieving data in the complex database systems. Existing systems often lack user-friendly
interfaces, with data spread across multiple databases, making it difficult for users to navigate the
database systems[42]. These healthcare database systems are mostly designed for technical users,
making it difficult for non-technical users to locate and retrieve the data from these systems effec-
tively.

1.2.2
Importance of user-centric design application in improving data retrieval
The user-centered application plays a major role in overcoming the challenges with the data retrieval
process by non-technical users in complex healthcare databases. The research [5], specifies that the
end user’s involvement in the application design and development process will create more relevant
and useful applications. The end-user involvement will help in developing highly user-friendly appli-
cations catering to a wide range of stakeholders.
Meloni et al.[27], explain that the display of metadata will facilitate the non-technical users in un-
derstanding and locating the data they need in complex database structures. Furthermore, the author
highlights that the metadata will be used as a descriptive layer explaining the necessary details of
the complex database systems. The display of metadata with user-centered design interfaces such as
simple layouts, interactive interfaces, metadata searching, and filtering capabilities will be helpful for
non-technical users such as healthcare professionals to understand and navigate through the health-
care databases on their own without relying on the IT team.
Furthermore, Asnicar et al. [3], discuss that the visual and interactive design elements with the inter-
active dashboards, clear labeling, and well-organized data help users in understanding and utilizing
metadata. The user-centered design approach will help in developing the application to improve data
retrieval for non-technical users such as healthcare professionals, thus resulting in reduced depen-
dence on the IT teams.

Chapter 2
Literature Review
The Literature Review chapter offers a comprehensive analysis of the existing literature related to the
data retrieval challenges in healthcare database systems, the use of FAIR principles for better data
accessibility and the impact of UX design on metadata systems for data accessibility and retrieval.
The literature highlights the importance of a user-centric approach that includes both technical and
non-technical users, resulting in healthcare data systems that are accessible and effective for a various
types of stakeholders. This chapter highlights challenges in existing methods, and aims to establish
the context for the current study and highlight its relevance.
2.1
Challenges in Data Retrieval from Healthcare Databases
The healthcare databases are often organized for clinical operations, creating challenges for academics
seeking access to raw data for analytical and research purposes [42]. The literature highlights the
difficulties related to the design of large databases, the technical skills required for their navigation,
and the discrepancy between visualization tools and healthcare professionals preferences. This section
analyses these challenges, citing relevant literature and assessing their impact.
2.1.1
How Data is Structured in HealthCare Databases
Healthcare services generate vast amount of data, including patient records, treatment, diagnoses, pre-
scriptions and administrative data. The structure of healthcare databases is mainly suitable to perform
clinical operations and provide access to the patient information. In this structure the normalized
schemas are used where patients data is stored across multiple interconnected tables. This way of

Tarigan et al. [38] further emphasizes that database is one of the widely used storage system to store

2.1.2
Data Retrieval with Limited SQL Knowledge
The main challenge encountered by healthcare researchers is the lack of proficiency in SQL, a crit-
ical language tool used for effectively querying the databases. The lack of SQL knowledge among
researchers complicates not only retrieving data but also in understanding the organizational structure
of that data across multiple tables and columns.
Jeddi et al. [42] discusses that healthcare professionals depend on the IT teams for their required data
retrieval because of lack of SQL skills. Due to the dependency on IT teams, the healthcare profes-
sionals not only experience delays but also reduces their autonomy, which is effecting to conduct the
real-time updates to their research projects. This dependence on IT specialists for fundamental data
extraction activities escalates operating expenses and strains IT departments, reallocating resources
that may be more effectively employed in other areas.
Jeddi et al. [42] also proposes the creation of interactive web interfaces that includes visual query

2.2
FAIR Principles and Their Role in Data Accessibility
The FAIR principles are mainly introduced to make the data being Findable, Accessible, Interoper-
able, and Reusable, are recognized as a paradigm for improving data management and accessibility
across multiple domains, including healthcare. The use of FAIR principles in healthcare databases
helps to deal with the critical issues of data locating, sharing, and reuse, improving the accessibility of
healthcare data for academics, doctors, and various stakeholders. This section highlights the impor-
tance of FAIR principles in healthcare, analyzing its practical impact as referenced in the literature.
2.2.1
Overview of FAIR Principles
The FAIR principles were created to meet the ever increasing demand for efficient data management
and retrieval in various scientific research fields. Wilkinson et al. (2016) [39] initially introduced these
principles, which offers a systematic approach designed to enhance data retrieval, data management,
data sharing. Wilkinson’s study highlights the need for data to be Findable through standardized
metadata and identifiers, Accessible through explicit protocols and licensing, Interoperable through
standardized formats, and Reusable through clear documentation and usage conditions.
1. Findable: The data should be easy to find using the rich metadata and globally unique identi-
fiers.
2. Accessible: After discovering the desired data, the user should be able to access it through open
or authenticated access.
3. Interoperable: The data should be formatted in such a way to that allows it to work with other
systems or datasets.

4. Reusable: The data should be well-documented, formatted and maintained such that the data
will be used in new contexts or future research projects.
In the healthcare sector, the FAIR principles are highly relevant because of the complexity and sensi-
tivity of medical data. Sinaci et al. [35] constructs upon Wilkinson’s fundamental study by explicitly
relating these principles with healthcare data, recognizing the necessity for uniform and structured
metadata to improve data discovery across multiple healthcare systems. This plays major role in sys-
tems where data fragmentation create problems in effective access and retrieval. Making sure that data
is well documented and following well defined formats improves the accessibility of FAIR-compliant
healthcare data for researchers, which results in reducing dependence on IT teams and other interme-

Consequently, although Wilkinson’s work establishes a crucial benchmark, the literature [28] indi-

Despite its great potential, the practical implementation of FAIR principles presents difficulties, in-

duplication of metadata in healthcare systems. These initiatives highlights the importance of collabo-

5.1
Summary of Key Findings
The following key findings highlights the user-centered design process, and identification of metadata
attributes that helped in improving the data retrieval process in healthcare for non-technical users.
5.1.1
Challenges in Raw Data Access
One of the finding of this study was that healthcare databases are difficult to access by non-technical
users including medical researchers and bioinformaticians. The main barrier is their inability to use
SQL and have limited access to the databases directly due to data security and privacy, as highlighted
by Jeddi et al. [42]. Therefore, these users most of the time rely on IT departments for their data
requests and this may cause delays and bottlenecks in their research activities. User interviews and
design workshops consistently highlighted the need for an intuitive system that minimizes reliance on
IT support and helps users to locate and retrieve the necessary data independently.

5.1.2
Importance of Metadata Attributes
The research highlighted the importance of well-defined metadata attributes in improving the data re-
trieval process for non-technical users in healthcare. The researches [42], [27], highlight that metadata
provides valuable context to help non-technical users understand and navigate complex database in-
dependently. This study validated that presenting metadata attributes like descriptions of data sources,
tables, columns, sample views of the data, and display of relational aspects between various tables in
an intuitive web interfaces enables users to locate and extract required data. Such metadata attributes
are very important for helping users to get a overview of the databases easily without access to the
real database systems.

The research also highlighted that the users showed a preference towards simple and organized inter-
faces that contained fundamental design features such as data tables, expansion panels, date picker,
checkbox selection, tooltip component for information context, snackbars. Such design features were
instrumental in decreasing the mental effort required, thus enabling the users completion of research
activities instead of interacting with the complex interfaces. The inclusion of end-users in the process,
as Carayon et al.[5] suggest, resulted in development of a more user relevant and usable application.
The users pointed out how important it was for the design to be simple and user friendly, aiming to
decrease the time and effort when trying trying to interact with the application.

Iterative Development with the Double Diamond Framework
The Double Diamond Framework is applied in the development of application to align the final pro-
totype with the user expectations. The application started with the phases of Discover, Define so that
the user problems are understood clearly and then the phases Develop, Deliver were conducted using
Agile framework which allowed to conduct multiple iterations of development and receive feedback
at the end of every iteration to refine the application to align with user expectations. This approach
aligns with the study of Przybilla et al. [31], who advised for combining double diamond design
thinking and Agile development to address the challenges of IT projects effectively.
The user interviews, design workshops, user persona, flow model, work activity notes, user stories,
user story map, and usability tests were part of that process which helped in improving the prototypes.

3. The participants 1,3,5,6 has mentioned that the display of domain descriptions in Tooltips has

4. All the participants are impressed with the inclusion of Filter Options to search for the tables,
columns based on keywords.
5. Most of the participants highlighted the use of Dialog Boxes for displaying the sample data,
table relationships which helped to get a good overview of the tables while staying in the same
viewpoint without redirecting to another interface.
6. The participants 2,3,4 has mentioned that the design elements Checkboxes and Date Pickers
has enabled them to select the tables, columns they need and also helped to provide the date
inputs in one format across the application.
7. The design element Textarea facilitated the participants to provide large text as comments to
help with the data retrieval process.
8. All the participants has mentioned the use of DatePickers at column level is not useful since
they request a data of certain time period at table level rather than at each column level.
