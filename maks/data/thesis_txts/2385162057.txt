Abstract 
 
Lipopolysaccharids (LPS) are an important indicator of gram negative infections. As fast diagnosis 
is crucial in combating diseases, diagnostic tools undergo constant improvement. One niche is the 
development of binding antibodies and antigens to LPS thereby enable more reliable and easier 
analyses of infections.      
The project investigated “Preparation of surfaces for the binding of LPS” and focused on the 
synthesis of a branched quaternary ammonium salt. This thesis presents the outline of the method to 
synthesis a branched quaternary ammonium salt, using betaine to make N-acyldithiocarbamate. 
There is evidence for the quaternary ammonium salt has been synthesized successful. The work-up 
procedures need to be improved and further test needs to be performed before the product is ready 
to be couple onto magnetic micro beads. Here the binding affinity can be tested and compered it to 
previous made quaternary ammonium salts.

Introduction 
 
Immunoassays such as ELISA have been used for many years as a way to identify the different 
biomarkers and cytokines from invasive microorganisms as a key element in treatment and disease 
control. Due to the constant need for a faster, more precise and cheaper way to diagnose diseases, 
new methods are being researched and developed. The use of multiplex bead technology has caught 
the eyes of many, as a competitor to the usual methodsi. 
 
Immune system  
The immune system is the body's natural defence against invasive microorganisms. It is divided into 
two groups: the innate and the adaptive system. The innate system is the one, we are born with and 
it provides a quick response, but is not very specific. The adaptive system is the one we develop 
throughout our lives, which evolves to recognize specific diseases, but takes a while to activate 
fully. The first step is recognizing the threat. This happens when our skin gets penetrated or when 
the mucosal entrances comes in contact with diseases. Sometimes the skin gets breach as a result of 
a cut or burn, making the immune system only fixing the damage and being in alert state but not 
moving on to step two. Step two is elimination and containment of the invasive cells. After 
elimination, regulation of the immune response must happen. If not doing so properly, the body can 
develop allergies and autoimmune diseases. The final step is developing memory cells, so that the 
particular infection can be dealt with a lot more efficient, if reinfection should occur.ii 
 
Antibodies and antigens 
Antibodies are glycoproteins that targets a vast range of substances, mainly from invasive organism. 
As these substances stimulates antibody generation, they earned the name antigens. Antibodies, also 
known as immunoglobulin, are divided into five main groups: IgM, IgD, IgG, IgE and IgA, 
furthermore IgG and IgA has several subclasses. The different classes are all made up of two heavy 
chain and two lighter chains, making the shape of a “Y”. Their classes are depended on their heavy 
chain. The bottom part of the antibody, the C region, interacts with effector cells and molecules and 
is stable. In the other end of the “Y” is the V regions, and they are less stable, as this is where 
==PAGE:7==
Page 6 of 92 
 
antibody-antigen interaction happens.iii  The forces involved are: hydrophobic, electrostatic, 
hydrogen bonds and van der Waals forces.iv 
Three kinds of cells can present foreign antigen: dendritic cells, macrophages and B lymphocytes. 
Macrophages are mainly a part of the innate immune system. They provide phagocytic effect, which 
mean they digest foreign microorganisms into smaller harmless pieces. Dendritic cells are found in 
the peripheral tissue in their immature state. They are activated when their pattern recognition 
receptors (PRR), binds with a so-called PAMP. PAMP stands for pathogen-associated molecular 
patterns and they most commonly known are DNA, RNA, lipopolysaccharides (LPS) and 
peptidoglycans. After activation, the matured dendritic cells migrate to the lymph nodes. Here they 
present the antigen to naïve T-cells and stimulate these to proliferate and production of effector 
cells. Depending on the antigen, the T-cells will differentiate into TH1, TH2, TH17 or Treg. Only TH1, 
TH2 are relevant in relation to antibody production. If it is an infection caused by virus, that 
activated production of TH1 cells, these stimulate production of cytotoxic cells and more T-cells as 
well as macrophages. If TH2 cells were produced as a cause of intracellular bacterial infection, the 
T-helper cells present the antigen to B-cells and activate these to proliferation and differentiation. 
These B-cells will then produce IgG2 and IgG3. If TH2 cells were produced as a cause of 
extracellular bacterial or virus infection, the T-helper cells also presents the antigen to B-cells and 
activate these to proliferation and differentiation. These B-cells will produce IgG4, IgA and IgE and 
plasma cells, that will make it easier to deal with eventual reinfection.v

LPS 
Gram negative bacteria have, contrary to gram positive bacteria, an outer membrane. This 
membrane has many of the same features as a normal membrane, but it also has liposaccharides, 
which are an antigen. These are attached to the membrane by a number of fatty acids. They are 
connected to the core part of the LPS that is made up by polysaccharide, which has two phosphate 
groups attached – one in each end. From the core, a tail of repetitive glycan polymer is attached. 
This is often referred to the O antigen, and it varies a great deal, even within the same species of 
gram negative bacteria. The presence of LPS attracts antimicrobial peptides (AMP) and provokes an 
innate immune response in humans as well as animals. This response can as previously mentioned 
activate the adaptive immune system, to combat the infection. As gram positive bacteria do not 
have LPS, the AMP's will instead be attracted to the teichoic acid bound in the cell wall. vii

Immunoassay 
Immunoassays are an important tool used widely in the pharmaceutical industry. This group of 
assays uses antibodies to detect the presence of molecules in a given sample. Antigens can also be 
used, if it is antibodies that are screened for. Furthermore, immunoassays can determine the amount 
of targeted molecules in the sample. The assays can be used effectively as diagnostic tools, due to 
them being fast and precise even at early stages of diseases, which are key elements in treatment. 
==PAGE:9==
Page 8 of 92 
 
 Immunoassay technology was first developed in the 1950’s by Rosalyn Sussman Yalow and 
Solomon Berson when they were working with diabetes. They developed an assay technique known 
as radioimmunoassay, where the targeted molecule is tagged with a radioactive label. 
Radioimmunoassay is but one of many different immunoassays that exist today. ix  The most 
commonly used are within the enzyme immunoassay-group, where Enzyme-linked immunosorbent 
assay, ELISA is the most well-known. Different versions of ELISA evolved from the basic direct 
ELISA, whereas the “sandwich” is most commonly used, due to being robust and sensitive. The 
principle of this ELISA is as following; Antibodies gets attached to the bottom of wells, antigens 
are added and the wells gets washed leaving only bound antigens. Specific antibodies are added and 
then enzyme-conjugated antibodies, the wells gets washed again. A substrate is then added resulting 
in a reaction either detectable by fluorometer, spectrophotometer or luminometerx. The principle of 
the ELISA methods emanated to multiplex immunoassay technology using magnetic microbeads.  
 
Figur 3: Different ELISA methodsxi 
 
 
Multiplex analysis using magnetic microbeads 
The use of multiplex bead array assay, MBAA, mostly surfaced around the 21st century. Multiplex 
analysis makes it possible to test different analytes that are commonly grouped, at the same time.  
The principle behind the use and detection of the beads dates back to 1977 where Science published 
Paul K. Horan and Leon L. Wheeless Jr. article on the matter of detection of cellsxii. Since then, the 
methods have evolved giving birth to more sophisticated and specific methods. Today multiplex 
analysis allows for the simultaneous measurement testing of many analytes one such method is the 
use of multicoloured beads. One of MBAA that uses multi-coloured beads, are the Luminex 
technology. It is based on the principle of a sandwich ELISA. The beads are coloured differently 
==PAGE:10==
Page 9 of 92 
 
and will have specific analytes bound to the surface, whereas the second antibody to the specific 
analyte has been bound to a fluorescent label. Afterwards the beads are run through a dual detection 
cytometer flow. This method can by the colour of the bead itself identify the individual assays and 
by the label determine the concentration.xiii 
 
Affinity binding 
Many types of binding and interactions exist and occur when atoms and molecules arrange 
themselves in the most stable conformations. Best understood is probably chemical binding, but 
other types of interactions exist, such as non-covalent interactions. These are the types of 
interactions occurring in intermolecular bond. Non-covalent interactions are classified into different 
categories, such as van der Waals forces, π-effects, hydrophobic effects and electrostatic effects. 
The two effects there influences affinity binding is hydrophobic effects and electrostatic.  
 
Hydrophobic effects 
Hydrophobic effects occur when nonpolar molecules group to elude as much contact with the 
aqueous solution. Of hydrophobic molecules can be mentioned such as the long carbon chains in 
fat, LPS or other nonpolar substances. The hydrophobic effect is caused by the strong water–water 
interactions leading to the poor solubility of nonpolar groups in water causing aggregation of these 
groups (hydrophobic interaction) and the formation of self‐assembled structures such as micelles 
and lipid bilayers.  
 
Electrostatic interactions 
Electrostatic phenomena arise from the forces that electric charges exert on each other. Different 
atoms have different electronegativity and binding between atoms that are far apart, resolves in an 
ionic binding where the atom with the greatest electronegativity pulls the electron closer to itself. 
The difference between ion binding and covalent binding can thereby be described by the 
electronegativity difference. For nonmetals, if the electronegativity between the atoms is less than 2, 
it is a covalent bond. The closer the number gets to 2, the more polar abilities the molecule gets.xiv 
If the difference is bigger than 2, the molecule will be in a cation/ anion form. An anion is an atom 
==PAGE:11==
Page 10 of 92 
 
or a molecule which is negatively charged, i.e. has more electrons than protons, whereas cations are 
the complete opposite. The attraction force between ions is described by Coulomb's law. Even 
though electrostatically induced forces seem to be rather weak, some electrostatic forces, such as 
the one between Na+ and Cl-, are rather strong. xv 
 
Affinity binding in bioassays 
When testing LPS in bioassays, such as ELISA, it is normal to bind LPS with affinity binding, 
where the antigen or antibody binds directly to the well. This approach is not possible in bead based 
assays and here the standard way is to use covalent binding to bind LPS directly to the beads. When 
binding LPS covalent with EDC(1-Ethyl-3-(3-dimethylaminopropyl)carbodiimide) coupling it can 
either bind to the amino group or an OH group on the carbohydrate.  
 
Aim of study 
For bioassays, it is important to be able to fixate LPS and other immunogens. By using ELISA, it is 
possible to bind the antigens or antigen specific antibodies directly to wells. This is not possible in 
bead based assays; here it is necessary to bind with covalent bonds. This works great in some cases, 
but is not an option in all cases. In some cases, the covalent bond might hinder the antibodies in 
binding to the target antigen. The purpose of this study, was therefore to find new ways to bind 
antigen to beads and thereby improving the multiplex bead array assay technology. The idea was to 
use adhesion to link the LPS to the beads. Building on the WO2017042303 patent: “Binding of 
hydrophobic antigens to surfaces”,xvi the concept was further perused. The hope was that unspecific 
binding, such as adhesion, would be a viable alternative to the covalent binding, that is currently 
being used.  
The idea of testing multiple analogues adhesion to LPS, in order to find the best unspecific linger in 
bioassays, suggested that branched quaternary ammonium salts could be an interesting study. To 
properly use these salts with reference to MBAA, it is crucial to find a simple and reliable way to 
synthesize it. This thesis will examine the synthetic attempts, chemical approaches and present their 
respective outcomes.

Quaternary ammonium salts 
Quaternary ammonium salts are an interesting niche of organic chemistry. They have unique 
abilities, as the quaternary ammonium cations are permanently charged independent of the pH of 
their solution. This ability increases the potential ionic binding. 
 
Betaine 
Betaine is any neutral compound with a positively charged cationic functional group such as a 
quaternary ammonium or phosphonium cation which bears no hydrogen atom. They also have a 
negatively charged functional group such as a carboxylate group which may not be adjacent to the 
cationic the first betaine found. Trimethylglycine (TMG) is an amino acid derivative that occurs in 
plants. Originally it was simply called betaine because, in the 19th century, it was discovered in 
sugar beets.xvii Since then, many other betaines have been discovered, and the more specific name 
glycine betaine distinguishes this one from the others. 
Trimethylglycine is an N-trimethylated amino acid. This quaternary ammonium exists as the 
zwitterion (with both positive and negative electrical charges) at neutral pH. Strong acids such as 
hydrochloric acid convert TMG to various quaternary ammonium salts, with HCl yielding betaine 
hydrochloride:

Future aspects 
 
There is a great amount of considerations needed to complete this project. Every reaction step needs 
to be evaluated in order to determine, if it is possible to get improved results. Especially the work-
up procedure of trisazid needs to be revised, and an alternative must be considered if a better work-
up procedure cannot be found. It should be examined, whether the reaction could still occur with 
less of a surplus of tris, which could provide an easier work-up procedure. The successful coupling 
with N-acyldithiocarbamate proves that the chemical approach, of using betaine to introduce the 
quaternary ammonium salt, works, but the separation of the product from the reactants needs some 
adjustment. A possible way to go about this, would be to use flash chromatography. Analytically, it 
would be optimal perform different work-up procedure. Possibly also looking into the usage of 
preparative HPLC; NMR and HPLC-MS should continuing be used to prove the structure and 
purity of the products. 
Another idea for future work, would be to use a chemical similar to 2,3-Diaminopropionic acid, 
instead of tris. One would expect this to be relative easy to react with betaine, as it can be reacted 
directly with the N-acyldithiocarbamate. Afterwards, it could be reacted with ethylenediamine or 
coupled onto the bead in another way. If this deemed possible, the problems of purifying trisazid, 
would be bypassed, providing an easier way to synthesize branched quaternary ammonium salts. 
After procuring the branched quaternary ammonium salts, the next step would be to couple it to 
magnetic micro beads and tests the binding affinity. Thereby comparing it to previous made single 
quaternary ammonium salts such as 2-amino-N,N,N-triethylethan-1-aminium iodide.  
Another way to utilise the adhesion binding concept could be in strip tests, where antibodies needs 
to be bound to the strip. If this proved to be effective, it might revolutionary the binding of 
antibodies, thus making it possible to use as an easy and new way to identify diseases.

Conclusion  
 
The project investigated “Preparation of surfaces for the binding of LPS” and focused on the 
synthesis of a branched quaternary ammonium salt.  
The first step was a nucleophilic acyl substitution with a reaction ratio of 1 metylazid to 3 tris. This 
reaction was hard to work-up and a really good procedure was never found. The next was to react it 
with methyl iodide, which proved challenging and did not succeed. The second strategy was using 
betaine-HCL to introduce the quaternary ammonium salts. Many different approaches were tried 
out, such as via an acyl chloride intermediate. Different coupling reagents were used, such as 
PyBOP and TFFH, which also did not succeed. The problem was deduced to be the betaine-HCL 
therefore the acetate anion of betaine, trimethylglycine, was used instead.  A betaine derivate (N-
acyldithiocarbamate) was produced and reacted directly with trisazid. Due to time pressure the 
product was not purified and conclusive evidence could not be obtained. To prove the reaction of 
N-acyldithiocarbamate, based on the abovementioned strategy, it was reacted with 
triphenylmethanamine.  This reaction gave a distinct HLPC-MS peak with the expected mass.  
The successful couplings N-acyldithiocarbamate proves that the chemical approach of using betaine 
to introduce the quaternary ammonium salt works. Although it still needs more work, especially the 
purification, this project has showed that coupling using quaternary ammonium salts has potential.
