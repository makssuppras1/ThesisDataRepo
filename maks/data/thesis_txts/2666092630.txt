ABSTRACT
The increasing concern over the global warming and therefore the possible harmful impact
of fossil-fuel-based conventional power generation, many countries round the world began
to implement renewables energy (RE) sources in their grid to decarbonize the power
sector. The decrease in wind turbine and solar PV prices and advanced technologies have
also contribute in recent growth of renewables plant installations. Therefore,lead to an
attractive solutions for the energy companies to mix the energy mix within the power
system and form a hybrid power plant (HPP).
The intermittent nature of the wind and solar sources introduce signiﬁcant challenges for a
stable and reliable power system. A feasible technical solution is to hybridize battery energy
storage system (BESS) with the renewable energy source .Another, to add control strategies
to achieve smooth power output at point of common coupling (PCC). The implementa-
tion of the control strategies will enhance and provide greater reliability to the power system.
In a grid-connected plant,the transmission system operartor (TSO) don’t have control
over the plant. The developers holds the controllability of the entire HPP assets and
how the HPP owners perform the energy management as a single conventional plant with
the external market/operators like TSO, energy market and ancillary service market is
essential. For this the project author deﬁne a HPP operator named energy management
system (EMS) behind the point of common coupling. In the design, the WPP and SPP are
considered as main power resources, whereas BESS is used as an additional support. The
whole control strategy are often implemented in two modes like power reference following
and maximum power point tracking (MPPT) priority mode.
The main scope of the current work is to develop control strategies for hybrid power
plant consists of WPP, SPP and battery energy storage system (BESS) that allows to
regulate the active power injected into the grid. The HPP control conﬁguration has a
hierarchical structure with a central control level, farm control level and a local control level.
However,the focus is on the higher central level i.e HPP controller. The HPP controller
sends out reference signals from the EMS to all the farm level controllers. To validate and
evaluate the operation of the two control strategies, the proposed HPP control modes are
simulated within the Matlab Simulink environment.

INTRODUCTION
This chapter presents the background and the problems statement associated with control
strategies for hybrid power plant.
It clearly states the objective of the thesis and the
assumptions /limitations that are considered for the study.In general,it provides the main
structure for the entire project work.
1.1
Background and Motivation
In the recent years, renewable energy sources such as wind and solar PV have grown
signiﬁcantly, to reduce CO2 emissions from the fossil fuels based conventional power plant
on the climate [8][1]. Many policies and acts are introduced. An example is the Paris
Agreement where many countries has committed to increase renewable energy connection
to the grid by 40% of its total electricity generation by 2030, to de-carbonisation of the
energy sector. Wind power and solar power are having a signiﬁcant role in achieving the
climate change and energy commitments in the power sector. In Europe, wind energy has
been a major source and it has experienced an exponential growth[1][9].
However, the continuous decrease in wind turbines and PV panels prices along with the
recent policies and incentives for increasing the application of renewable energy sources
makes an attractive solution for the plant developers to consider the energy mix in power
system and form a hybrid power plant [8]. From an environmental point of view this is a
favourable development, but its implementation bring challenges such as power ﬂuctuations
due to change of the external conditions like wind speed, temperature, solar irradiance,
etc [10]. The variability and ﬂuctuations power from renewable sources makes the power
system more volatile and aﬀects the grid stability. One of the practical solutions, to reduce
the power variability caused by the renewable sources, plant developer have added the
energy storage system [10]. Second, due to grid constraints, the HPP may not able to
export more at the Point of Common Coupling (PCC) [11].
The BESS can be utilized to store parts of the generated power that would otherwise
get curtailed and release it at times when the grid has the capacity to take this spare
generation at the point of common coupling (PCC). In this manner, it is possible to
limit the impact of variability eﬀects improved power quality and energy availability.This
enhance the full utilization of the resources and making wind power plant (WPP) and
solar power plant (SPP) look more like a conventional power plant[12]. The increase of
RE installations can replaces the conventional power plant,however it leads to an increase
imbalances between generation and demand. TSO requires HPP to take part in ancillary ser-
vices and contribute to frequency regulation by injecting additional power into the grid [13].

1.2
Problem Statement
Developing a simple and accurate control strategies for HPP posses many challenges.
The power produced by the renewable’s generator varies, due to the change of weather
conditions like solar irradiance and wind speed etc. This makes the power system more
volatile and introduces imbalance between the power generation and the demand. Therefore,

The integration of battery energy storage with WPP and SPP can handle power ﬂuctuations

1.3
Objectives
The main objective of this work is to design control strategies for hybrid power plant
consists of WPP, SPP and battery energy storage system (BESS) that allows to regulate
the active power injected into the grid. The focus of the present work is to propose a
higher level central HPP controller that sends out reference signals from the EMS to all the
farm level controllers. In this context, this research work proposes and develops following
main tasks.
• To develop supervisory the HPP controller to ensure that HPP can track the power
reference issue by the energy management system.
• To develop diﬀerent control modes
• To test the developed control algorithm on a power plant model developed under
Matlab-Simulink

1.5
Assumptions and Limitations
In order to simulate the system as precisely as possible, but also not increasing the
complexity and simulation time, the following assumptions are considered.
• Sizing of hybrid power plants with WPP,SPP and BESS can be very complicated
task. It depends of many factors like geographical location, available area, cost, plant
operation and market participation. So, for this thesis work it is assumed that the
WPP, SPP and BESS size is provided. All the wind turbines and PVs are of same
type.
• The utlility scale HPP consists of large number of wind turbines and PV units. For
simulating larger systems, an aggregation of several wind turbines and solar PV are
favoured, to increase simulation speed and to reduce computation resources.
• Only electrical models of the wind turbine and PV system will be studied without
considering power converter topology, therefore the type of the current does not have
a relevance in this study.
• Markets aspects are not considered.
• Wind speed and solar irradiance are assumed to be equal for all wind turbines and
solar PVs , although for WPP and SPP there are diﬀerences due ﬂuctuating weather.
• The eﬀect of the temperature is not taken into account on the PV module and battery
model.
• Communication delay is not considered in the model

2.1
Wind Turbine Technology
The concept of wind turbine has been developed signiﬁcantly over the decades. Wind
turbine has been classiﬁed into ﬁxed and variable speed depending upon the rotational
speed and power control ability, [2][14][15]. A ﬁxed-speed wind turbine has a rotor speed
which is ﬁxed, regardless of the wind speed, and is designed to achieve maximum eﬃciency
at a particular wind speed. Fixed-speed wind turbines are equipped with an induction
generator (squirrel cage or wound rotor) that’s directly connected to the grid with a
soft-starter and a capacitor bank. They have the advantage of being simple, robust and
low cost, however they are an uncontrollable, reactive power consumption, mechanical
stress and limited power quality performance.
Variable-speed wind turbines have dominated during the past several years. Variable-
speed wind turbines are typically equipped with an induction or synchronous generator
and are connected to the grid through a power converter. The power converter controls
the generator speed against wind variations in order to increase energy capture, improve
power quality and reduce mechanical stress on the wind turbine.
There are four diﬀerent dominant types of wind turbines, classiﬁed by their ability to
control speed, as illustrated in ﬁgure 2.1.A detailed comparison of the diﬀerent concepts
has been addressed in [16][17]. Among of these wind turbines type 3 and type 4 has been
used extensively in the market. Type 3 is known as the doubly fed induction generator
(DFIG) concept, with a partial-scale frequency converter on the rotor circuit and pitch
control. The stator is directly connected to the grid, while the rotor is connected through
a partial-scale power converter. The presence of power electronics enables the generator to
operate in a wide range of speeds, which is typically around ±30%. The power electronics
converter is rated at only 25-30% of the generator capacity.
Finally, type 4 WTG has both its rotor and stator connected to the grid through a
full-scale converter. This permits for operation on a broader range of speeds compared
to type 3. Additionally, the full-scale converter eliminates the necessity for a gear-box,
demonstrating multiple beneﬁts, such as simpliﬁed drive train, high overall eﬃciency,
and high reliability. Type 4 generator is of two types, either electrically excited or with

2.2
Solar PV System
According [18] solar PV consist of solar cells that are arrange in series or parallel orders
to produce DC voltage and current.The output of the PV module depends abundantly
on atmospheric conditions like solar irradiance and temperature. Therefore, the power
generated by the PV module is not constant.Thus it requires MPPT design to extract

Literature Review
This literature review summarises work done associated with utility scale integration
of hybrid power plant in general.In order to implement on utility scale, renewable energy
sources are required to behave like conventional power plants. However,for a HPP, the
concept of utility-based hybrid power plant including wind, solar and battery storage is in
early state since there are limited numbers of existing HPPs around the world or HPPs
under developments[28]. As a result, the control strategies for HPP regarding the power
and frequency control or ancillary services haven’t been extensively covered in scientiﬁc
literature. A number of researches have investigated how wind turbines/solar plant and
BESS can fulﬁl the requirements demanded by power system operators.The standard model
proposed by the IEC Committee in IEC 61400-27-1 and WECC are commonly used to
study dynamic power system phenomena for wind and solar model. Organizations such

as WECC and IEC have separate working groups or committees for developing generic
models for wind and solar farm [29][30][31][32].
This section describes the on-going and previous work of a research, whose overall
objective is to analyse and assess the possibilities for control of HPP concepts. Researchers
have reported diﬀerent types of control strategies designed mainly to manage power
exchange between the grid and the HPP in their work.In [33], an optimization strategy was

the controller can proritized WPP and SPP for the maximum power point tracking and

which communicates with the TSO and HPP controller. This makes EMS accountable
for oﬀering its resource’s services to the market/system Operator, and is responsible for
holding the HPP accountable for meeting its commitments at the PCC. Further, HPP
control strategies are called on to either maximum power point tracking (MPPT) or track
a power reference set by EMS.

3.1
HPP Control Conﬁguration
Figure 3.1 depicts the general proposed control architecture for the HPP that consists
of WPP, SPP and BESS plant. The HPP control conﬁguration is categories into four
following major levels like energy management system (EMS), HPP central control level,
farm control level and assets level. Each level is controlled by following a selected hierarchy.
3.1.1
Energy Management System (EMS)
Literature [1] illustrated that in a grid-connected HPP, the plant developers holds the
controlability of the whole HPP assets.So as to realize this controlability the HPP owners
requires an operator within the HPP plants to perform energy management strategies. For
this the author deﬁne a HPP operator named energy management system (EMS) behind
the point of common coupling. An EMS basically communicate with the trading center
like TSO and market agent and HPP control level (HPP controller). Depending on the
actual network status, the EMS issues speciﬁc demands to the HPP central control level,
which prepares and sends further reference signals to each wind farm control level.
Here,
EMS is accountable for oﬀering the hybrid resource’s services to the
Market/System Operator,then delivering those services just like a Conventional Resource.
EMS will internally determine the amount of energy that the hybrid resource can provide
with a high degree of conﬁdence and can oﬀer this volume as energy and/or ancillary
services for each hour in the day ahead and real time markets. The algorithms how EMS
will internally allocate the energy isn’t within the scope of this project.
3.1.2

tracking the power reference from EMS for each market interval and to control the active
power exchange between the HPP and the grid centrally. It also helps in minimizing the
curtailments through the use of BESS. The HPP controller contains typically the HPP

3.2
HPP Control Algorithm
Control strategies for the HPP controller is intended to supervise active power among
various energy resources and also the storage system. Within the design, the WPP and
SPP are considered as main power resources, whereas BESS is used as an additional
support.The BESS will deliver power only there’s mismatch between the reference and
measured power. The HPP controller allocates the active power to be generated by the
WPP,SPP and BESS at the point of common coupling. During the controlling process, the
strategy decides when WPP, SPP to operate at MPPT, execute charging and discharging
of the BESS and power curtailments. In order to achieve the active power control,in this
work two control modes are proposed called
• Energy management system, power reference following (EMS-PRF)
• Energy management system, power reference following with MPPT priority (EMS-
PRF MPPT priority)

CONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORK
This chapter discusses its ﬁndings and it proposes diﬀerent tasks for future research.
6.1
Conclusions
In the present work, the attention is mainly drawn to the capability of a HPP control
strategies to regulate the HPP’s active power production at PCC. For this, this work has
proposed two control modes: one is power reference tracking set by the energy management
system and also the second MPPT priority that helps in injecting surplus power into the
grids with no power curtailment. The proposed HPP model includes a supervisory central
HPP controller that are essential for the eﬀective operation of both the HPP and its assets.
Since the project is based on simulations in a MATLAB simulink tool, the aspect of
modelling WPP,SPP and BESS are of great importance. The eﬀectiveness of the proposed
control algorithm are achieved by variations in wind speed and solar irradiance, respectively,
as well as changes in active set points. It is observed that the sudden variation within the
solar radiation and wind speed and power set points causes considerable impact on the
power injection into the grid. The proposed controller also shows capability of mitigating
transients due to sudden variation in the wind speed and radiation level. The simulation
results indicate good performance in relavance the proposed control strategy. It is noticed
that BESS power witness the compensating power for the variation in solar irradiance and
wind speed as long as availability of the BESS permits.
The major ﬁndings from the current work are:
• In normal operating condition, the HPP controller ia capable to inject the maximum
power into the grid,i.e let renewable plants to operate at MPPT.
• The control strategies has capabilities to decide MPPT operation for WPP and SPP,
execute charging and discharging of the BESS to assist in tracking the set points
and power curtailments.
• For simulations it is essential that the models is initialised properly, otherwise
imprecisions within the initialisation result in transients occur at the start of the
simulation.
• Tuning of controller should be performed to achieve better tracking performance and
reduce the peaks and oscillations. As the response time for WPP, SPP and BESS
are not identical, this might aﬀect the design of the PI controller
• For simulating larger systems, an aggregated modelling of several wind turbines
/solar PV units are favoured with re-scaled power capacity
53
==PAGE:64==
6. Conclusion and Future work
• BESS is a very important component to assess the eﬀectiveness of the control
algorithm in a hybrid power plant. The power diﬀerence between the power set
points and the measured power will be used to control the charging and discharging
of the BESS.Therefore requires good sizing.
6.2
Future Work
In future, the HPP control strategies can be used for the following purposes
• Add other functionalities like reactive power control, voltage/frequency regulation,
etc.
• Add the communication delay between controllers and assets since, reference values
and measurement values of the plant controller need to be transported over a long
distance between measurement point and plant controller locations. As a result
of this there will be delay and therefore the power response within the wind/solar
/BESS is slow.
• Proper sizing of BESS can be added on the WPP and SPP ,for the better performance
of HPP.
• Control algorithm presented in this work can be modiﬁed to incorporate a proper
ramp rate limitation for smooth operation.
