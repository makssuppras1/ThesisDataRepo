Abstract 
This Master’s thesis report is about the structural analysis of one of the buildings included in the ongoing 
residential project in Aarhus Dockland. The Aarhus Docklands is new neighborhood project in the second 
largest city of Denmark. The project had its inception in the year 2007 and in the coming year the projects 
aims to convert the present area into multifunctional and modern landmark of the city.  
The project includes lighthouse which consist of five buildings and are shaped as the letters L, I, G, H and T. 
This thesis focuses on building T of the lighthouse. Building T will be a 160 meters high tower and will 
mainly have offices in most of its 38 storeys. This tower will have view of the sea and large parts of Aarhus. 
The main objective of the thesis is to present a structural analysis of the Building T thorough the means of 
Hand calculation and software calculations. The software used in the structural analysis of the building is 
StruSoft FEM-design. This software was developed by Strusoft AB and has a very good user friendly 
interface which makes its very pleasing to use. This makes it quick and easy to use in simple and intuitive 
manner. The codes mainly used in the design and analysis are the Danish codes, Eurocodes. For the critical 
joint connections Abacus was used. 
The height of the Lighthouse building T is 160 m and the shape is almost rectangular, with 38 floor levels 
(28 office + 7 service floors + ground floor + recreation + top), 2 underground parking levels and each floor 
height is equal to 4,0 m and the floor footprint is equal to 1250 𝑚2. The building T has many important 
elements which are highly important to know. The foundation system of the Lighthouse building T is made 
of three types of foundations. A Piled Raft foundation was made for the cores, the Mat foundation was for 
the facade columns and the Point foundation is made for the parking stories columns.  
For the Lighthouse building T up to 9 different columns diameters were designed, where the columns 
dimension changed after every 5 levels and 3 structural walls thicknesses were also designed. The column 
diameter ranged from 450mm at the top to 950mm at the bottom.  The beam used in the project were two 
type of delta beams, beam used along the façades were the edge beams of type DR22-250 and DR26-325 
and the one used between the cores were two types of composite beams and they were D22-400 and D26-
400. 
The footprint of each floor for the lighthouse building T is equal to: 1250 𝑚2 and the concrete column was 
located in all four façade sides. The steel delta beams were used along the façade, between the columns 
and between the cores. Two types of floor slabs were used, corresponding to hollow core slabs elements as 
HCS 220 and HCS 270 and the span direction of floor slabs. The main structural elements of the roof were 
made by the steel trusses on the facade, and covered with glass.  
The loads acting on the building were divided into dead load, imposed loads, snow load, wind load, 
accidental actions load and horizontal mass load and the calculated according to Eurocode, Danish code 
and DTU Course 11320: Structural analysis of Buildings. Dead load is the load that includes the weight of 
all structures and other building parts, which is expected to be present and works throughout the lifetime 
of the structure. The dead load determined according to structural elements and design of the structure on 
architectural drawings.

Imposed loads on buildings are loads that comes from people, furniture, fittings and machinery etc. 
Imposed loads are divided into different load categories, depending on what floor area is used for. Imposed 
load is applied on floor area as either flat load, line load or point load, or a combination of these loads.  
 
There were used three load combination factors ψ0, ψ1 and ψ2 by the combination of variable loads and in 
connection with dimensioning for accident loads and serviceability limit state. The snow load and dead load 
of the roof structure is taken up by the slab of the glass roof, which is then further carried by the structural 
load bearing elements concrete columns and composite and edge beams.  
 
The floor slabs transferring the loads as a line load to the load bearing walls, beams and columns. From the 
Wind load and Mass load calculation it was seen, that wind load was dominant on long-side edges of façade 
while Mass load was dominant on the short-side edges of façade in building T. 
 
The horizontal loads on the building was usually either wind load or mass load. Structural Stability in the 
longitudinal, X-direction was ensured by the 8 walls as: (W01, W02, W03, W04, W05, W06, W07 and W08), 
these walls are load-bearing. Structural Stability in the transverse, Y-direction was ensured by the 6 walls 
as: (W09, W10, W11, W12, W13 and W14), these walls are also load-bearing.  
 
Force was transferred by diaphragm action in the roof and floor separations to the stabilizing walls where 
the force is transmitted by shearing. After that the horizontal forces by diaphragm action to stiffening walls 
in the basement. The vertical loads included the structural loads, as snow-, wind-, dead- and imposed loads. 
Those loads passed through structural column, beams and walls. The structural elements were verified for 
their adequate strength in their ultimate limit state, service limit states. All the structural elements checked 
met the load coming on it. 
 
The critical joint connection between column and beam, is modelled with 3D solid elements in Abaqus, 
where the column consists of concrete while the beam and Peikko joint consist of steel. The critical joint 
consists of PCs7 UP. Autodesk Revit was used to make the 3D and 2D drawings of the building T. These 
were then further imported to Strusoft. Some of them worked but there were some drawing which didn’t 
get imported in Strusoft as desired.  
This made the drawing to be remade in Strusoft again. Which took a lot of time. There imported drawings 
and those which were made in Strusoft were used to carry out the software structural analysis of the 
building T and its elements.  
Once the results from the hand calculation and the software were obtained, they were later compared. It 
was seen that some the results were matching while some different between hand and software 
calculations. The dissimilarities could be due to some difference in the method used in the two different 
calculation processes. 
From the results seen it can be concluded that there are going to differences in the results obtained from 
the hand and software calculation. To be safe the results which give more critical results among the hand 
and software calculation should be used.

1 Introduction 
The Lighthouse consists of a total of five building, which are shaped as the letters L, I, G, H and T. In this 
present project will deal with the new Lighthouse building T which is an efficient high rise building and the 
new icon for the city of Aarhus Harbor in Denmark. 
In this project the focus has been on the project Lighthouse which is currently under construction. 
Lighthouse is an office building performed in Ø1 and can be seen in Figure 03. The office building is called T 
and is designed according to all standards of design requirements. Using hand calculations, the type and 
size of building elements are determined and then used in StruSoft FEM-design program.  
By implementing the selected dimensions and the types of elements in StruSoft a number of changes were 
made to the elements. In the figure 01 below, the StruSoft model of the floor level 3 of the Lighthouse 
Building T is illustrated.  
 
Figure 01 – StruSoft model of Floor level 3 in the Building T 
It turned out that the diameters of the structural concrete columns were reduced in diameter for every five 
floors. The reason for this was to ensure sufficient load bearing capacity. At the foundation the diameter of 
columns equal to Ø950 mm which is reduced by 100 mm, for every 5 floor.  
This corresponds to the diameter of the level 37 floor is Ø450 mm. Circular steel bars with diameter of 
Ø273 are used for roof construction at the Sky Garden. 
Similarly, the thickness of the bearing walls are changed three times. From foundation to level 12, the wall 
thickness is 700 mm, after that follows a thickness of 600 mm to level 25 and from level 26 to 37, the 
thickness is 500 mm. 
Beam elements that are used are of the composite and edge beam. The composite beam corresponding to 
D22-400 and D26-400. The edge beam corresponding to DR22-250 and DR26-325. The floor slabs are called 
hollow core slabs and is of type HCS 220 and HCS 270. HCS 220 is used at level 37.

2.2.4.5 Structural Load bearing dealt beams 
 
The structural design goals of the beams are to be able to resist all assigned loads from floors and facades 
at ultimate limit state and be capable of transferring them to the connected elements, columns and cores. 
They should also fulfil requirements of deformation on serviceability limit state. 
The beams will receive loads from the floors and the facades and deliver the loads to the columns or in 
three cases to the cores. The beams are considered simply supported against vertical loads but considered 
as a part of the stiff floor diaphragms against lateral loads. 
Delta beam and edge beams are shown in the following figure 24 to figure 27, where the delta beam has 
been between the cores and the edge beams have been used along the long-side façade edges and short 
façade edges. 
In figure 26 and figure 27 below, the vertical section of structural load bearing delta- and edge beams for 
level 37 are shown. 
 
     Figure 26 – Delta beam D22 – 400                                           Figure 27 – Edge beam D22 – 250

5.2.3 Defining Loads in StruSoft FEM-design 
Defining the loads and load combinations in the StruSoft FEM-design program are under the load tab. By 
using the element geometries and material parameters, StruSoft program automatically determines the 
self-weight of the structural elements.  The self-weight of the nonstructural elements according to the table 
in sub chapter 3.3.1 and 3.3.2 and it is manually defined in the program. The Snow load applied on the roof, 
the imposed loads applied in the all storey-levels and the wind load on short-, and long-side of the façade 
applied and defined manually in the program. 
5.2.4 Structural Analysis in StruSoft FEM-design 
The calculation is run under the Analysis tab where the general process of designing the building under the 
serviceability limit state and the ultimate limit state is performed. By using the Analysis tab, the internal 
forces in the structural elements as beams, slabs and columns are determined for the defined load 
combinations.  
StruSoft FEM-design has an automatic and powerful mesh generator that calculates the optimal average 
element size. The Finite element tab vas skipped according to the program that can automatically run the 
mesh generation during analysis. Only the analysis tab is used in order to obtain results comparable with 
the hand calculation.

7 Conclusion 
In this project, structural analysis of one of the buildings in Aarhus Dockland was carried out. The analysis of 
the structure was done through the means of hand calculation and software. The software used for analysis 
was StruSoft FEM design and Abacus. All the calculations were done according to the Eurocode and Danish 
Codes.  
The building used in analysis was Building T of the Aarhus towers. This building when completed is 
expected to be the tallest in Denmark and will have 38 storeys. The 38 storey tower will have 28 office + 7 
service floors + ground floor + recreation area and 2 underground parking levels. Each floor height of the 
tower is equal to 4,0 m and the floor footprint was equal to 1250 𝑚2. Since it was a very tall 160m tower, 
the load experienced by the structural elements was very high.  
Due to the high structure the load experienced by its structural members at different levels varied a lot. As 
the structural members got lower in the building the more load they experienced. To cater to this 
increasing load from top to bottom the structural member’s dimension was modified so that they could 
carry the load.  
For the project itself most of the hollow core slabs were provided by Hollow core Slab Company. The 
hollow core slabs used were HCS 220 and HCS 270 and they spanned the direction of floor slabs. The hollow 
core slabs used were then compared with the amount of load they could resist. It was found that the 
hollow core slabs used in the construction satisfied the required design load. The prefabricated hollow core 
slabs used in construction were safe to use.  
The beam elements used in the construction were provided by Delta beam. Two types of beams provided 
by them were used. Both were of composite in nature but had different geometrical design. The delta 
beams used were of two types. The one used along the façades were the edge beams type DR22-250 and 
DR26-325 and the beams which were used between the cores were two types of composite beams D22-
400 and D26-400.  
The column used varied from top to bottom as the load on the column increased constantly with every 
floor and they were circular in geometry. At the top the column diameter was 450mm and since the load 
increased constantly at each level hence to cater to this increased load at each 5 floor level the diameter of 
the column was increased by 50mm. This helped to get satisfactory results when the load acting on the 
columns were applied. Overall in the tower there were columns with 9 different diameters and they ranged 
from 450mm at the top to 950mm at the bottom.  
The walls used in the building were of load bearing type. They were located in the cores of building T. the 
dimension of the wall varied as the load resistance required. The wall thickness initially was at 500mm at 
level 37 and this was increased at level 26 to 600mm and the next change was at level 13 with a thickness 
of 700mm and this wall thickness was used till the level -2. This was done so that the wall could carry the 
load coming on it.  
The design of the building structure was mostly carried out in REVIT. The software was found to be very 
user friendly as it was easy use when designing a building. The building design completed in REVIT could 
==PAGE:148==

 Spring 2016

easily be transferred to StruSoft. There were at times some errors in transferring in building design to 
StruSoft but most of the time it worked.  
The designs which were not transferred to StruSoft had to be made again in it. This increased the time it 
took the work on the project. The StruSoft interface was similar to REVIT and hence designing the building 
elements was not as difficult as thought when the errors arose. StruSoft had its own problem, in the 
beginning when simulation of the design was being done in StruSoft; the simulations took almost a day to 
finish.  
As time passed slowly the improvements done in the model in StruSoft helped reduce the simulation time 
from a day to an hour or two hours. Overall the StruSoft was satisfying to use as the learning process was 
not difficult.  
Abacus software was used in in the analysis of the critical joints being used in the building. The joints 
between column and beam were the critical connections. The joints were between the concrete column 
and steel beam. As per the experience the software Abacus is difficult to use and takes a lot of time to 
learn.  
Hand calculation method followed in its entirety the calculation process which was learning while studying 
Course 11320: Structural analysis of Buildings. This made a lot easier to carry our hand calculation as 
procedure was streamlined and saved time. The loads acting on the building are divided into dead load, 
imposed loads, snow load, wind load, accidental actions load and horizontal mass load. The critical load of 
the column was calculated for three conditions. The three conditions were before fire, during fire and after 
fire.  
Once the results from the software and the hand calculation were obtained, they were then checked with 
the structural elements which were to sustain the load. From the results it was seen that all the structural 
elements satisfied the design criteria. From the results it was also observed that the hand calculation and 
the software results varied in their results.  
The dissimilarities between the hand calculation and the software results could be due to some difference 
in the method used in the two different calculation processes. From the results seen it can be concluded 
that there are going to differences in the results obtained from the hand and software calculation. To be 
safe the results which give more critical results among the hand and software calculation should be used.

Annex A2 - Horizontally Mass load ``Vandret Masselast`` 
The horizontal mass load is the minimum horizontal load, that structures must be assumed to affect. It has 
centers of gravity of the associated vertical loads, and assumed to be capable of acting in any horizontal 
direction. The prescribed value of the horizontal mass load is 1.5% of the design value of the vertical load, 
as the mass load originated. 
A horizontal mass load is assumed to occur simultaneously with the associated vertical load, i.e. it cannot 
be calculated while the horizontal wind loads. The horizontal mass load has the point of attack in individual 
structural components of gravity. 
It should be examined whether the horizontal mass load works to more unfavorable to the structure than 
the wind load.
