Abstract
The deep energy renovation(DER) of existing buildings is an important aspect for building
sustainability promotion, under the background of global climate change and energy shortage.
However, the DER may lead to occupant comfort problems with lower indoor environmental
quality after renovation. To solve this issue, the idea of PredicTAIL rating scheme is purposed
for the renovation design process. This rating scheme includes the indoor environmental qual-
ity parameters of the thermal (T), acoustic (A), indoor air quality (I), and luminous (visual)
environment (L), and the overall IEQ will also be rated with the rating results of the four com-
ponents. The rating is based on the simulation of the retroﬁtted building, and it is expected
to provide designers with predictable indoor environmental quality. To examine the sensitiv-
ity and feasibility of the thermal quality scheme of the PredicTAIL scheme, the renovation
scenarios were designed for two existing buildings, and the corresponding thermal simulations
were performed, whose results were rated and analysed according to the scheme. The results
showed that the PredicTAIL thermal scheme is sensitive to the renovation, for the rating level
showed diﬀerent variations under diﬀerent conditions. However, there still exist some interfer-
ence factors that will aﬀect the rating results, and the scheme also needs further improvement
on the rating method of the thermal environment. It is concluded that the scheme has enough
sensitivity in the rating of diﬀerent simulated thermal environments, but it currently can only
be a direction guidance of the building renovation design, since the renovation measures are not
the only factor aﬀecting the thermal simulation results, which makes the scheme not reliable
for quantitative assessment of thermal environmental quality.
Key words: PredicTAIL, indoor environmental quality, building renovation, building sim-
ulation, thermal comfort

Introduction[1]
1.1
Background of PredicTAIL
To mitigate global climate change and reduce energy consumption, the improvement of the
building energy performance is necessary, for building energy consumption usually takes up
around one-third of the total energy consumption. Except for the energy eﬃcient design of the
new buildings, the deep energy renovation of the old building is also a way to reduce the energy
cost by measures like improving the building envelope thermal performance to reduce the load
of heating and cooling systems. The ALDREN project, which stands for the ALliance for Deep
RENovation in buildings, is conducted to promote the deep renovation of buildings, making it
more attainable and attractive in the building market.
Although the building retroﬁt usually takes energy saving as the primary task, the indoor
environmental quality is also signiﬁcant for the building owners and occupants. To make the
IEQ level can be deﬁned, described, and rated, a comprehensive and normative rating standard
is requisite. As a result, the TAIL scheme was developed under the framework of the ALDREN
protocol. It consists of four main factors aﬀecting IEQ, and the four letters ’TAIL’ represent
the thermal environment, acoustic environment, indoor air quality, and luminous environment
respectively.
The TAIL scheme is mainly used for the IEQ measurement of hotel and oﬃce buildings. How-
ever, only the indoor environment of existing buildings can be monitored and measured prac-
tically, and the impact of renovation can only be known after the retroﬁt is ﬁnished. In other
words, even if the renovation leads to a degraded IEQ level, this consequence can only be
detected after the renovation construction. To avoid this condition and make the renovation
result more predictable, the concept of PredicTAIL is proposed to assist the decision-making
in the design stage.
Instead of the data from measurements in reality, the simulation results of IEQ parameters
are the basis to rate predictive IEQ levels of retroﬁtted buildings. The detailed requirements
of the PredicTAIL rating scheme will be shown in the following section.
1.2
PredicTAIL rating scheme
1.2.1
PredicTAIL quality level
The TAIL scheme has four quality levels: green (high quality), yellow (medium quality), orange
(moderate quality), and red (poor quality). The quality level will be rated for all simulated
IEQ parameters included in the scheme respectively, according to their own range values and
percentage of unsatisﬁed time.
There are 12 IEQ parameters in the TAIL scheme: indoor air temperature and relative humidity,
sound pressure level, ventilation rate, visible mold area, concentrations of CO2, formaldehyde,
benzene, radon, PM2.5, daylight factor, and illuminance. For the PredicTAIL, the visible mold
area and ventilation rate are not considered in the PredicTAIL assessment, for these two pa-
rameters have no proper method to simulate in this project. In terms of the visible mold area,
it currently can only be predicted in a qualitative manner and cannot be simulated. For venti-
lation rate, the mechanical ventilation is a constant setting of the ventilation system, and the
natural ventilation is not considered in this study, since the unstable ventilation rate will make
the IAQ simulation too challenging, making the rating of ventilation rate in the simulation

The overall IEQ level of the building is determined by the worst quality level among the
thermal, acoustic, luminous environments, and the IAQ. For each component, the level is also
determined by the worst quality level of the parameters belonging to that component category.
In this way, the renovation design can be encouraged to attach importance to every factor
aﬀecting IEQ equally, and try to improve the indoor environment comprehensively.
However, in terms of simulation, it is unpractical and ineﬀective to simulate all rooms in the
target building. Instead, some rooms are chosen as the target rooms to represent the whole
building. Usually, the rooms with representative characteristics are chosen, like the standard
room on the standard ﬂoor, which is commonly occupied and has a large number of same type
rooms in the target building. The PredicTAIL levels are calculated for each target room, and
the interim rating result of the rooms will determine the level of the corresponding parameter,
which is shown as the following.

Objectives
The primary objective of the project is to examine the sensitivity and feasibility of the thermal
component ’T’ in the PredicTAIL scheme under building renovation conditions. The scheme
is mainly used for oﬃce buildings and hotel buildings, and both types will be simulated, and
the corresponding thermal environmental quality level will be calculated to see how the rating
level will vary with the building retroﬁt. The rating of the other components will be done by
cooperating partners to obtain the overall PredicTAIL level.
The second objective of the project is to analyse the reliability of the thermal component
of the PredicTAIL scheme, by comparative simulations between diﬀerent simulation tools and
models. The project aims to ﬁnd out whether interference factors will aﬀect the rating result
and leads to the error of quality level rating, the direction of the scheme improvement and
advice of the scheme application.

3
Method
3.1
Overall method
To examine the sensitivity and feasibility of the PredicTAIL scheme, it is necessary to perform
exact ratings for the simulation result of the building renovation cases, which will show how
the building performance and its quality level vary with the retroﬁt.
The simulation is performed based on the real existing buildings which need to be renovated.
One is a hotel building in Alicante in Spain, another one is an oﬃce building in Lecco in Italy,
whose detailed building information will be elaborated in the following section.
The speciﬁc renovation scenarios are designed to investigate the variation of IEQ levels af-
ter the renovation. There are three diﬀerent simulation scenarios in total, which have diﬀerent
renovation extents respectively: base case, case 1, and case 2. Comparing with the results of
diﬀerent simulation scenarios, the corresponding thermal environmental quality level can be
calculated, and the scheme sensitivity can also be assessed according to the ﬁnal result.
However, the four components of the scheme cannot be simulated in one software together
currently, and the simulations of them are performed respectively. This project mainly focuses
on the simulation and rating of thermal environmental performance in retroﬁtted buildings.
And the simulation and rating of the other IEQ parameters including acoustic environment,
IAQ and luminance environment are ﬁnished by cooperation partners from Scientiﬁc and Tech-
nical Centre for Building (CSTB) in France.
Although the scheme is applied for all seasons in the whole year, the project only focuses
on the shoulder season without any supplementary heating and cooling, and the thermal envi-
ronment in the heating and cooling season is assumed to always stay at the green level. Since in
the simulation the power of heating and cooling devices can be adjusted optionally, the thermal
environmental quality level can also be controlled easily. In the shoulder season, the heating
and cooling devices can even easier to control the thermal level in the relatively moderate out-
door thermal environment.
As a result, to avoid the potential bias caused by the heating and cooling, the building in
the simulation is set as a free-running building, which means the building has no heating and
cooling all year round. The original building thermal characteristics can also be revealed with-
out extra heating and cooling supply. However, considering the heating and cooling season
may have an extreme thermal condition under the free-running condition, which may cover the
possible changes after renovation in the shoulder season, the heating and cooling seasons are
assumed to have a perfect thermal performance, and only the shoulder season is taken into
consideration when performing the quality rating.
3.2
Renovation scenario design
The simulation scenarios are designed and their general design strategies are shown in the
following paragraphs. The scenario design considers all four aspects of the scheme. For in-
stance, the air inlet will be renovated according to the noise level, which will aﬀect the acoustic
environmental quality. However, in this report, only the renovation inﬂuencing the thermal
environment will be elaborated.

In base case, the simulation will be performed based on the condition before the renovation,
and the model is built up according to the original building. The original envelope construc-

3.3
IDA ICE model establishment
The sensitivity analysis is performed on two existing buildings. One is a hotel building in Spain,
and another is an oﬃce building in Italy. The building information has been collected before
and will be the reference of the retroﬁt design and IEQ analyses.
The thermal environment is simulated by IDA ICE. The simulation model of the base case
is built up according to the collected building information, including the original building di-
mensions, envelope construction, ventilation system, and internal heat gain. And the retroﬁtted
builds are simulated by changing the construction and system settings of the base case model,
and the setting of internal heat gain like occupancy schedule will keep constant in every sce-
nario. From the simulation, the hourly main air temperature in the target zone all year round
can be obtained, which is a reference for the thermal environmental quality rating according to
the PredicTAIL scheme. The detailed setting and controls of the IDA ICE model are presented
in the following sections.

5.4
Limitation
According to the previous research results, there are some limitations that need to be discussed
for the further improvement and application of the PredicTAIL scheme, which will be elaborated
in the following sections.
5.4.1
Diﬃculties of simulation accuracy and cooperation
The results of models with diﬀerent simpliﬁcation extent have shown that the strategy of the
model establishment will inﬂuence the ﬁnal rating of the PredicTAIL scheme. In order to make
the diﬀerence between the rating result and the condition of reality as little as possible, the
inaccuracy of the simulation aﬀecting the rating level should be avoided to the maximum extent
ideally.
However, there are several diﬃculties in the simulation which need to be solved.
First of
all, with the improvement of accuracy and complexity of the simulation model, the diﬃculty of
keeping consistency between simulations of diﬀerent PredicTAIL components is also increased.
For instance, during the thermal simulation, if the hotel model is built up with surrounding
rooms including the bathroom and living room, the exhaust air in the bathroom will not be
set in the target bedroom directly. Instead, the bedroom air exhaust will be inﬂuenced by the
combined eﬀects of air pressure balance between both the living room and bathroom, as well
as the leakage factor and opening of the bedroom door on the internal wall. In other words,
the exhaust air amount may not be as consistent as the simpliﬁed model in this research. As
a consequence, the IAQ simulation will be inﬂuenced because of the changed air exhaust rate,
and the diﬃculty of the simulation will be improved signiﬁcantly. In terms of the acoustic envi-
ronment, the noise in the other rooms will also need to consider because it can get through the
internal walls. The thermal environment simulation will also aﬀect the assumption of noise level
and acoustics insulation characteristics, which are closely related to the building and HVAC
system construction.
In a word, the increase of the simulation accuracy will highly increase the diﬃculty of sim-
ulation and cooperation between each IEQ component, and the feasibility and necessity for
accuracy improvement still need to be investigated considering the application of the Predic-
TAIL scheme in reality.
Besides, the improvement of thermal environment control may also lead to the problems of
the other IEQ components control. For instance, natural ventilation has the possibility to im-
prove the thermal environment to a large extent, which can be realized by intelligent control
Page 66 of 72
==PAGE:68==

Limitation

systems, like the temperature control of the window opening. Nevertheless, the natural venti-
lation will inﬂuence the IAQ parameters with inconsistent ventilation rate, and the acoustics
and luminance environment will also be aﬀected because of the window opening. As a result,
the synchrony and cooperation issue between diﬀerent components simulations becomes more
and more diﬃcult with the increase of simulation complexity, and it is necessary to ﬁnd the
balance point between simulation accuracy and feasibility.
5.4.2
The gap between simulation and reality
Except for the model simpliﬁcation for the simulation process and the cooperation between
diﬀerent components, there are still some unavoidable factors that may lead to the diﬀerence
between simulation results and the real performance in reality.
Even though the model is completely built up according to the real geometry without any
simpliﬁcation, the lack of building information will still result in errors. For example, in the
hotel case, the thermal characteristics of original envelope materials are missed, and the simula-
tion has to be processed based on the assumption of the unknown data. Although the database
can be a reference for the assumption, the parameters of materials with the same name vary on
a wide range. In addition, even though the building has detailed information on construction
materials, the characteristics can still be diﬀerent because of aging.
Besides, the setting diﬀerences of the usage schedule will also cause the deviation.
In the
simulation, the schedules of occupancy, equipment, and lighting are usually kept constant and
regular during the simulation period. However, in reality, the usage pattern can be diﬀerent to
a large extent, which may lead to the diﬀerent IEQ compared with the simulated level.
Despite the construction of the building model itself, external factors like climate change are
able to aﬀect the IEQ performance too. As a consequence, it is hard to verify the accuracy of
the PredicTAIL level which is rated according to the simulation results. From the simulation
and analysis above, the result can be easily aﬀected by many factors, including the simulation
software and the way of model establishment. They may lead to errors, and it is still an issue to
examine the inﬂuence of the error, which is related to the feasibility of the PredicTAIL scheme,
since it is a rating system based on the IEQ simulation.
5.4.3
Missing analysis of heating and cooling season
In the previous study, the heating and cooling devices are all turned oﬀto see how the thermal
environment varying with the retroﬁt during the shoulder season. However, this method misses
the analysis of the thermal environmental performance during the heating and cooling season,
when the outdoor thermal condition is more diﬃcult for maintaining a comfortable indoor ther-
mal environment.
To further improve the simulation, the design of the heating and cooling systems can be done
according to the real condition. The power, control schedule, and temperature setpoints of
heating and cooling devices can be detailed designed, and optimized according to reality. In
this way, the feasibility of the PredicTAIL scheme in the renovation of the heating and cooling
system can also be examined.
Still, considering the purpose of DER is reducing energy consumption, the simulation result
of the free-running building can also be the reference for the further optimization of renova-
tion design on the other hand. According to the previous study, the oﬃce building in Lecco

5.5
Improvement
Apart from the previous analysis of the results and discussion of the limitations, there are also
some improvements in the thermal rating scheme, which can be done to further improve the
PredicTAIL scheme.
5.5.1
Improvement of thermal environment assessment parameter types
In the thermal environment assessment, the mean air temperature is the only parameter used
for the rating. However, there are still a lot of other factors inﬂuencing thermal comfort. The
research of Olesen[13] points out that the general thermal comfort is expressed by operative
temperature, air velocity, and humidity. Local thermal comfort or non-uniformity is expressed
by draft, vertical air temperature diﬀerences, radiant temperature asymmetry, and surface tem-
perature of the ﬂoor. In terms of operative temperature, the PredicTAIL uses the mean air
temperature instead considering the diﬀerence between these two types of temperature is slight
in a comfort building.
However, the factors such as the air velocity and vertical temperature diﬀerence will still inﬂu-
ence the thermal comfort of occupants, which cannot be replaced by the mean air temperature
directly. Under the same indoor mean air temperature, the high air velocity may help to in-
crease thermal comfort in the hot weather, but will be a problem during the heating season and
increase the cold feeling. In terms of the vertical temperature diﬀerence, there has a relatively
high risk of this problem in the room with devices like ﬂoor heating and cooling. Besides, in
the study from Melikov[14], it is also pointed out that there has a high risk of both draft and
vertical temperature diﬀerence displacement ventilation, which results in thermal discomfort in
nearly half of the occupied zone in the measurement.
Besides, considering the thermal environment can be controlled at the expected level if the
heating and cooling devices are powerful enough, it is still necessary to take the energy con-
sumption as a parameter when rating the thermal environmental quality. On the other hand,
the required amount of energy consumption to reach the satisfying thermal environment at the
green level can also show the eﬀectiveness of renovation measures, helping the owner to examine
and improve the renovation strategy.
In conclusion, it is necessary to further improve the thermal assessment methods of the Pre-
dicTAL scheme by increasing the rating components including the air velocity and vertical
temperature diﬀerence, for they can aﬀect the thermal comfort and cannot be represented by
the mean air temperature.

Conclusion
According to all the investigation and analysis of the PredicTAIL rating scheme application in
the building renovation simulation, the conclusions of the research can be made as the follows:
• In the renovation simulation of the hotel building in Alicante, the PredicTAIL scheme is
sensitive to the renovation in general. The annual indoor mean air temperature shows
an obvious variation during the shoulder and heating season, but the temperature in the
cooling season nearly has no diﬀerence between the base case and the two renovation
cases. According to the PredicTAIL rating scheme, the thermal environmental quality
has been improved from red to yellow level in renovation scenario 1, but renovation
scenario 2 shows no improvement in the aspect of the thermal environment. In terms of
the acoustic environment and IAQ, the level also varies in diﬀerent scenarios. For the
luminous environment, the level is steady because of the night environment.
• In the renovation simulation of the oﬃce building in Lecco, the PredicTAIL scheme shows
less sensitivity compared with the hotel case. The thermal environmental quality always
stays in the red level in all target rooms because of the overheating inside the room, and
the diﬀerence between the base case and renovation scenarios is slight in the standard
oﬃces. The luminous environment level also shows no change. Still, the acoustic environ-
ment and IAQ level are improved in scenario 1. However, with further improved natural
ventilation, the thermal environment level can be improved signiﬁcantly in standard oﬃce
1,3 and 4.
• Regardless of the renovation strategy itself, the thermal rating level of buildings can
be aﬀected by many external factors.
The simpliﬁcation and assumption during the
simulation, and the tool of thermal simulation will aﬀect the ﬁnal rating level of the indoor
thermal environment in the shoulder season. With the same inputs of the simulation
models, the IDA ICE and TRNSYS still end up with diﬀerent results. Although only the
rating level of case 1 has the diﬀerence that TRNSYS model has the orange level rating
while IDA ICE model has the red level rating, the plots of annual temperature show a
large diﬀerence between the results from two diﬀerent thermal simulation tools, which
can be a risk leading to rating errors. In addition, diﬀerent assumptions and the extent
of simpliﬁcation of the model establishment can even result in a signiﬁcant diﬀerence of
the thermal environmental quality rating.
• It is necessary to further investigate the sensitivity and feasibility of the ’T’ rating scheme
for the building with heating and cooling systems working during the heating and cooling
season.
However, it is still meaningful to run a free-running building without active
heating and cooling devices, whose results can be the reference for the retroﬁt design of
the HVAC systems in terms of energy eﬃciency considerations.
• Although the PredicTAIL scheme has already been very comprehensive to include many
main aspects aﬀecting indoor environmental quality, further improvement is still neces-
sary. In terms of thermal environment rating, the vertical temperature diﬀerence and air
velocity should be taken into account, for the mean air temperature can not represent
these two factors that also inﬂuence thermal comfort.
• Considering the value of PredicTAIL scheme to be a guidance of the building retroﬁt,
the result presentation of the rating can be further improved to show more exact reasons
leading to the unsatisﬁed indoor environment.

All in all, the PredicTAIL scheme shows enough sensitivity and feasibility in the renovation
scenarios, and further investigation of the comparison between diﬀerent models and simulation
tools. The thermal environment level varies sensitively with the change of retroﬁt measures,
model establishment strategy, and simulation tools. The results of the other scheme compo-
nents’ rating results are also reasonable and explicable combined with the scenario conditions.
However, with further analysis and simulation based on the two existing building renovation
thermal simulations, it is proved that the limitation of the PredicTAIL scheme. The ﬁrst and
the most signiﬁcant limitation is the instability of the building indoor environment simulation
itself, because the results can be aﬀected by many factors, and ﬁnally change the PredicTAIL
rating levels. The factors include the model establishment strategy, the choice of simulation
tools, the completeness of building information collection, and so on, which leads to the un-
certainty of the rating. Since the rating result can be inﬂuenced by external factors but not
only the building renovation measures, it is diﬃcult to use the simulation results to evaluate
the indoor environmental performance after renovations quantitatively.
Still, although the PredicTAIL scheme cannot precisely predict the thermal environmental
performance, the rating results are able to show the trend of thermal environmental evolution,
which can be a guidance of renovation strategy design and decision making qualitatively.

it possible to be a guiding tool during the building’s DER design. However, it is still not re-
liable enough to be a criteria rule in the renovation design stage, for the rating results can be
aﬀected by many interference factors, and there has no method or standard to control those
factors currently. As a result, there need further investigations to solve those issues to improve
the reliability of the scheme. Besides, since the project only took two buildings as objects to
examine the sensitivity, it is also necessary to pursue more investigations for buildings with
diﬀerent constructions, geometries, and weather conditions in the future, to ensure the scheme
can be applied in a wide range.
