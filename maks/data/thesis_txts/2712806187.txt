Abstract  
 
The building industry is constantly evolving and more and more tools and information is constantly 
required for building projects. In such an environment it is important to manage the dataflow and 
information met on building projects. With a growing interest for having more information and 
requirements for projects, both nationally and internationally, it is important to have a uniform 
tool to manage project information and dataflow across parties on a building project.  
The ISO 19650 standard is beginning to affect many countries in both Europe and internationally. 
Not only as a mandatory standard, but also of the countries and the industries interest in investing 
in new and better tools to manage information across the entire lifecycle of a building project. An 
investigation is then needed for the Danish building sector, a country that currently stands as one 
of the most digital and leading in digital building, to see if the ISO 19650 is of interest in investing. 
This thesis aims to investigate and evaluate the needs of the ISO 19650 in the Danish building 
sector, as well as the influence it may have for Danish organizations.  
The analysis is caried out by research and interview made by and with leading experts with their 
respective fields, and individuals that are part of the Danish building sector. It furthermore builds 
on the knowledge gained by two previous courses performed at DTU (Technical University of 
Denmark), that investigated comparison of the ISO 19650 series and current ICT documents from 
Denmark, as well as an investigation of ISO 19650 on the Danish building sector.  
The analysis has shown that there is an interest in implementing the ISO 19650 series in Denmark, 
and that there currently is an interest in making a scalable version of the ISO 19650 series to 
better suit the current needs and demands for both smaller and larger building projects in 
Denmark.  
The current perspective of implementing the ISO 19650 series on the Danish building sector is that 
it is divided into larger and smaller organizations, where the larger organizations are trying to 
make a scalable solution, so that the smaller organizations can also benefit from using the ISO 
19650 series. Also, since the ISO 19650 series is something that has been mentioned from larger 
international organizations, a fear that it becoming a European norm is a motivating factor to 
starting the implementation of the ISO 19650 series early on the Danish building sector.

1. Introduction  
1.1  
Scope of the thesis  
This thesis will investigate and look at the Danish market’s usage, advantages, and boundaries for 
utilizing and properly implement and use the ISO 19650 series. The scope will be to investigate, 
and further propose suggestions on how to use the ISO 19650 series on the Danish building sector.  
The usage of ICT agreements on Danish building projects are of common use, and with the 
introduction of this new ISO standard series, a new way of looking at an open market for making 
projects more international, and to have companies do projects across borders with the same 
toolsets for ICT agreements and information handling.  
This study aims to investigate why the ISO 19650 series is not already commonly used on projects, 
and what has blocked it from entering the Danish building sector and reaching out to the intended 
clients that this ISO series aims to aid.  
Furthermore, the report contains an analysis of the Danish building sector and the aspects to 
consider when implementing the ISO 19650 series. With this, there will also be a discussion of a 
scaled solution of the ISO 19650 series to implement to the Danish building sector.  
This report analysis is carried out by interviews, phone calls, emails, and articles from and with 
experts and involved individuals from the Danish building sector. Some of which are ICT specialists 
and specialists within the ISO 19650 series. Some of which are companies that uses their own ICT 
specifications and agreements, and some who are interested in using the ISO 19650 series on 
projects. The articles used in this report has been carefully chosen from their standpoint and 
knowledge with projects using either ICT agreements or specifically regarding the ISO 19650 series 
influence and importance or conflicts.  
Furthermore, an analysis of what affects in both a negative and positive direction for the usage of 
the ISO 19650 series on the Danish building sector. To this, a recommendation for what actions is 
needed to make a direct change, both politically and individually.  
The analysis in this report contains a discussion of methods to consider when implementing the 
ISO 19650 series and an industry perspective to the implementation of the ISO 19650 series.  
lastly, an implementation plan is described to how the ISO 19650 series can better be understood 
and used for projects of various scales in the Danish building sector.

3.  
Introduction to the ISO 19650 series 
The following chapter has been made from a previous project made by the author. It is 
therefore highlighted and referred to in the annex.   
The ISO 19650 series have been created to ensure a common shared document that can make a 
standard formular for an ICT document. This series of standards 19650-1,2,3,5 have been created 
with the purpose of being applied to all companies and projects across country borders, thus 
helping the process of an open market for making projects with a standard ICT document, apart 
from the ones used in individual countries. The ISO 19650 series contains project requirements for 
sharing and delivering information across parti members. It creates a possibility for all working on 
a project, to verify and ensure a common understanding of the expectations for project 
information. The following chapters will try and explain what each of the individual standards in 
the ISO 19650 series are containing. 
 
ISO 19650 – 1 ([01], 2019) 
The principals for ISO 19650 – 1 are that the contracting party should set points of interest that 
can be shared and passed on to other organizations. Information requirements associated with 
the delivery phase of the asset should be reflected in the descriptions of the contracting party. 
Within these information requirements we have six information models, shown in figure 1 below. 
 
Figure 1 Type of information requirements and information models ([01], 2019) 
If a delivery team consists of more than one party, information requirements should be passed on 
to where the information is most readily available. 
Information development involves the sharing and coordination of information through the CDE 
using open standards and should be clearly defined for operation to enable a consistent approach

for parties involved. All project-related information should be specified by the contracting party 
through a set of information requirements. The lead delivery party should prepare a response to 
each requirement. Feedback loops provide an opportunity to revise information before it should 
be handed over to the contracting party. Submissions and disclosure of information should be 
verified, otherwise remedial work may not be required. 
 
Figure 2 Information delivery cycle ([01], 2019) 
ISO 19650 – 2 ([02], 2020) 
ISO 19650 – 2 is highly focusing on what information is needed for the delivery phase, and who 
will be responsible for those actions during the project. This document puts the contracting party 
in focus, and tries to explain what needs the contracting party must have for a project teams 
information delivery. Figure 3 illustrates the process that must be proceeded from the contracting 
party and project leaders’ part, in order to meet the criteria of information delivered from the 
various parties on the project.

ISO 19650 – 3 ([03], 2020) 
ISO 19650 – 3 is in many ways very similar to the processes and phases from ISO 19650 – 2. In this 
document, the key point is that the project phases are triggered by an event, that starts a phase in 
the overall information exchange process. As seen in figure 4, we have 3 variations of the process 
for an asset. The first one (event B in Figure 4) will be that there might occur a trigger event or a 
decision within the project process. This means that during the project, an event might happen 
along the way, that can change the way that information needs to be handled further for the 
project, and thus changing the delivery requirements for the asset. The second event (event D in 
Figure 4) might occur from an operating organization overtaking the project and its information 
and delivering the information to the contracting party at the end of the project. The third option 
(event C in Figure 4) would occur before the project starts, and the trigger event might be the 
desire to start a certain project, and then work with the same model presented from  
ISO 19650 – 2 that follows a specific process for the information management on the project.

ISO 19650 – 5 ([04], 2020)  
ISO 19650 – 5 is specifying the principles and requirements for security minded information 
management at a mature stage, also described as “Building Information Modelling” (BIM). In 
according to the ISO 19650 series, it is to manage sensitive information obtained, created, 
processed or stored as a part of, or in relation to, any other imitative, projects, assets, products or 
services. It addresses the steps required to create and cultivate an appropriate security mindset 
across organizations with access to sensitive information. It highly draws parallels to ISO 19650 – 1 
in terms of how to manage and deal with information across parties or organizations, and how to 
properly store and share this information. BIM and other digital collaborative work methods and 
technologies generally involve collaborative sharing of information across a broad range of 
independent organizations within the build environment sector.

4.  
Denmark and compagnies with interest in the ISO 19650 
series  
The Danish building sector has long known about the ISO 19650 series. An interview with Gunnar 
Friborg (Project Manager and Standardization Specialist) verifies that the ISO series has been on 
the international market for about 10 years. There has been many reports and interviews 
regarding usage of the ISO series in a Danish context. Alongside this, Dansk standard (DS) has 
made a committee with the purpose of expanding the knowledge and usage of the ISO 19650 
series in a Danish context, so that it can be applied to Danish projects, and further be used as a 
tool to help smaller and bigger companies to bid in on international projects, or work with 
international companies.  
The following sections contain information collected from various individuals, who have all been 
introduced or who are working with the ISO 19650 series. All of which give a better understanding 
of what the user advantages and the general disadvantages can be when trying to implement and 
use the ISO 19650 series. 
4.1  
MOLIO 
An interview with Ole Berard (Digitization Manager) says the following about the maturity and 
strategy to implementing the ISO 19650 series: “The way to implement something in this industry, 
it is if the client makes demands, or if the industry makes demands, then people should probably 
get started and think creatively. Apparently, what works best is to force it through and use some 
tools. Our industry needs the unifying player that many other industries have.”[C] What this 
indicates is, that with many of the companies involved in the development and implementing of 
the ISO 19650 series are perhaps not yet ready to include the usage of ISO 19650 series on their 
building projects. The question weather to push the standard into the market, or to take actions 
individually in the business, has led to a response from Ole Berard, that states, that he himself 
think that the most likely way that the series will be implemented would be, if it was a demand by 
the client. In the interview, Ole Berard says that he thinks, that if the client or the industry makes 
the demand to use the ISO 19650 series on projects, the smaller and larger businesses will have to 
apply to these demands and deliverables on the projects. MOLIO does not have a strategy for 
implementing the ISO 19650 series. However, Ole Berard says the following statement to usage of

COWI

solution where the Danish building sector can easily implement and understand the ISO 19650 
series. An interview with Niels Tredal (Digital Transformation Director) highlight some of the 
curiosity and challenges that COWI is having towards the ISO 19650 series. “The good thing about 
it is, that for the first time you get to sit down internationally, and overall have agreed on how a 
project should proceed from A to Z. What makes it a bit complicated in a Danish context is, that 
there is something we have or has been doing for 10 years, but it is also good because we have a 
great deal of agreement with many of the things that are in the standard.”[E] Some of what the 
interview reflects on shows that COWI is very likely to proceed in the investment of using ISO 
19650. Niels Tredal also says the following when asked about COWI interest in the ISO 19650 
series “We are very interested in this if the ISO standard is used, then it is of great value that it is 
implemented correctly. We are part of a committee at DS where we also help to shape the 
conversation and development in a direction that can create value for ourselves in the long run. … 
We believe, that it will be in demand very soon, and therefore it is important for us to be at the 
forefront of the ISO standard. This is a huge advantage, as there are too many international 
customers that we can bid on.”[E] COWI has a great influence in the work that has followed with 
DS in their committee S808, where they help to push the work towards a more practical

4.3  
Give Elementfabrik A/S  
Give Elementfabrik A/S is today a leading supplier of concrete and lightweight concrete elements 
for agriculture and industrial construction. They produce, deliver, and assemble prefabricated 
modular concrete elements throughout Denmark, and have a significant export to other Nordic 
countries. Give Elementfabrik A/S has its largest market in Denmark, but also supplies elements 
for construction in Norway, Sweden, the Faroe Islands, Germany and Poland. An interview with 
Gustav Skaanes (Quality manager) highlighted the usage of ISO 19650 on projects. Gustav Skaanes 
says that it has been the CEO’s intention to apply ISO 19650 on projects, to better set demands to 
project, and to the clients that demand products. In the interview, Gustav says that they quite 
often receive both PDF, Word, Excel files, as well as simple drawings of demands for elements to 
produce. Since Give Elementfabrik A/S is able to produce almost any concrete element that the 
client demands, it also puts some pressure for their projects to contain a lot of information and 
planning for deliverance. Their production line is working very close with the client to produce and 
manufacture the elements that they desire. Their difficulty has been that the drawings and 
information they receive has been of various format, and so to have a consistent production 
format has been a struggle. Their intention to apply the ISO 19650 series has been to try to make 
some general demands, that their clients could follow and so their own demands from client 
information will be more settled and uniformed. The biggest difficulty that Gustav mentions has 
been that since this was an idea that their CEO has tried to implement. Not all employees has been 
satisfied with the change, and shortly before the interview with Gustav, their CEO left the 
company. This has also taken their current stage of applying ISO 19650 to a hold. As Gustav 
mentions, Give Elementfabrik A/S wanted to be more innovative and be better at setting 
demands, but no one was quite ready for the change to use the ISO series, and so there was a lot 
of push back from the employees of the company. Since he himself was in charge of quality 
management and was looking into receiving models and information to process from clients, the 
vast diversity has been a daily challenge for him. Gustav tells that since every client is delivering 
either a 3D model, hand drawings, or just simple 2D drawings with measurements, none of these 
are compliant with their own software. For Gustav, it would help his process a lot, to set better

4.4  
Københavns Kommune 
An interview with Michael Friis Ørsted (Head of Digitization) gave a perspective from a builder’s 
point of view. Michael Friis Ørsted said in an interview, that Københavns kommune was not yet 
ready to implement the ISO 19650 series. Although further questioning led to a standpoint, that 
since it was not yet mandatory to use the series on projects, their implementation of the ISO 
19650 series was not yet a perspective they have investigated. Michael tells that Københavns 
kommune was interested in the ISO 19650 series and could most definitely see potentials to use it 
on projects. Their involvement with the series has been very passive, and thus they are currently 
waiting for the industry to adopt the series more into projects. Byggeri København is one of 
Denmark's largest public builders, and they expected to have a turnover of DKK 1.9 billion in 2021. 
They are responsible for a portfolio of construction projects with a value of approx. 10 billion kr. 
Their involvement in building projects in Denmark is of a high importance, and so to implement 
and use the ISO 19650 series would effectively also apply to other builders and contractors. As 
Michael tells in the interview, if Københavns kommune were to use the ISO 19650 series, it would 
be because the value of using it was to have a uniform way of handling project information across 
all that is involved in the building projects.

7.  
PESTEL on the ISO 19650 series on a Danish market  
An PESTEL of ISO19650 onto the Danish building sector has been made, to see its impact and 
possible implementation. The concept of a PESTEL analysis is to view the topic from 6 different 
aspects, to understand the potential outside influence, as well as addressing the different topics of 
the PESTEL.  
 
7.1  
Political 
The political aspect of implementing the ISO 19650 series can have many outcomes. One of them 
being that Plan, Bygge og Boligstyrrelsen makes the usage of ISO 19650 mandatory for all building 
projects in Denmark, thus forcing all companies within the Danish building sector to adapt to the 
situation. This can either give a strong political adversity, or it can benefit those who were 
prepared to make the change.  
Another aspect can be that the Danish building sector is mature enough to self-implement the 
usage of ISO 19650 on their projects, thus it being the AECOO companies that takes the first step 
and starts to demand, that their clients and partners to use ISO 19650. This way, there can be an 
easy integration into using the ISO 19650 series, rather than being forced into it. It can also help 
create a better environment for the practice of ISO 19650 for building projects, as to the users 
being the ones that enrolls the new change to the market. This can be of a political standpoint, to 
demand all international companies to then having to also use the ISO 19650 series for Danish 
building projects.

7.2  
Economic 
The Economic aspect is looking at the costly benefits, as well as challenges when implementing the 
ISO 19650 series onto the Danish building sector. There are two actions to be considered here. 
One is the pre-implementation phase, and the other being the post-implementation phase. The 
pre-implementation phase will require the ISO 19650 to be suited for the Danish building sector, in 
order to be suited for implementation to the companies that will have to use it. There will have to 
be a learning phase, where courses and intros will be available. The implementation of the ISO 
19650 into the individual companies will then depend on their time, recourses, and size. The 
general economic cost of this phase will then be determined by the work needed to be done with 
the ISO 19650 series before being implemented, and the companies individual size and resources.  
The post-implementation phase will then be divided into two parts, where the first being the 
learning phase. The learning phase will be driven by individuals’ willingness to adapt and use the 
ISO 19650 series, in comparison to previous methods. The learning phase will be costly, depending 
on the individuals time and needs, in order to be able to apply and use the ISO 19650 series, as 
well as understanding the market demands for the usage of the ISO series. The post learning 
phase will then be the profitable phase for the ISO 19650 series implementation. The argument for 
this will be as shown from interviews and research, that once there has been established a 
common international practice, it will reduce cost for the companies, as well as give both smaller 
and larger companies the possibility to bid in on international projects, and not only national. For 
companies such as COWI and other large companies, this would mean that they can now work 
with and help out their international branches placed in other countries.  
The ISO 19650 series has its strength in communication of data and information. It’s improving the 
communication of the building asset throughout the life cycle. The quality of the building asset is 
improved based on this logic of having to set better demands in the earliest phases throughout 
the entire life cycle of the building. This would save the company money, time, and spare conflicts 
for any building project.

7.3  
Social 
The social aspect of introducing the ISO 19650 series into the Danish building sector can have two 
outcomes. The first being that it will be a great success, and that there’s a smooth and easy 
transition from the old methods, into the usage of ISO 19650. 
The other being that the Danish building sector and the employees having to use the ISO 19650 
are not content with the situation. In this situation there can be that the methods used for ISO 
19650 can clash with the ones commonly used for Danish building projects, thus making this an 
unwanted change. The employees could potentially then try to uphold their current methods, and 
not be willing to make a change to the usage of the ISO 19650.  
In a situation like this, it will be important to illustrate and explain the values of the change, and 
the benefit that follows. It is important to motivate the employees to have a good practice and 
usage of the ISO 19650 series, in order to establish a good social acceptance of the ISO 19650.  
 
7.4  
Technology 
ISO 19650 is a technical document that can be used for built assets and construction projects of all 
sizes and all levels of complexity. ([01], 2019)The strength of the series, lies within its technical 
specification to application of building assets. With the market interest and gain from industry 
organizations, the ISO 19650 series is of a new method to be applied on the Danish building 
sector, and help Danish organizations gain stronger digitalization both international and national. 
As it has been mentioned earlier, there is also much to gain with an early involvement in ISO 
19650, to help with competitiveness for Danish organizations abroad. More and more countries 
lean towards a future of integrated BIM workflows for their building projects, and it would only be 
of great value for the Danish building sector, to be in the frontier of the global industry.

7.5  
Environment 
ISO 19650 is built upon data transactions and information transactions between parties. An 
environmental aspect of this can be, to implement information regarding CO2 emission and 
product properties along the project. With the investment of using reusable materials, and 
document their information and properties correctly, the building asset future can be determined 
to have a lower carbon footprint, and thus creating a both safer and less harmful environment for 
the building asset and its users.  
With the constant push from governments all over the world to decrees the carbon footprint, this 
action could very well be one requirement for the future usage of ISO 19650 to implement this 
information in the data transactions between parties.  
7.6  
Legal 
As it stands right now, the S808 committee is trying to establish a good understanding and 
practice for the usage of ISO 19650 on the Danish building sector. With the intention to make this 
ISO series a more common practice for Danish organizations, it could be of high possibility that 
there will be larger needs for the government to take actions to have the ISO 19650 series as a 
standard practice. As it stands right now, the Danish government haven’t made any direct plans to 
make the ISO 19650 series mandatory on building projects. As it has been verified and studied in 
other countries, to push the mandatory legalization of the ISO 19650 series can either be of great 
benefit or divide the Danish building sector. As mentioned throughout in this report, the countries 
such as UK that has made this series mandatory, is also affecting other nations close to it that has 
the same practice of BIM, within their building sector. This could mean, that other close by 
countries can look to this as an inspiration of either good or bad, and learn from those experiences 
gained from mandatory legalization of the ISO 19650 series.

Actions and recommendations  
This chapter will be including research described throughout the report. The actions and 
recommendations will be based on the authors gained knowledge of the topic from the research 
done in this thesis. The chapter will explain what actions must be made to implement the ISO 
19650 series on the Danish building sector, and what recommendations to consider of 
implementation methods.  
From the discussion made in this report, a few aspects must be considered before taking actions 
of implementing the ISO 19650 series. First it must be considered, if this is going to be 
implemented from a government or organization action. 
If a governmental approach is considered, then one of the first things that must be acted on, is to 
have initial meetings to understand what the ISO 19650 series contain. The Danish government 
must first understand the differences and similarities that the ISO 19650 series shares with already 
existing documents in the Danish building sector. Then an exercise must be made to evaluate the 
outcome of what will be affected if the ISO 19650 series is implemented. A market research must 
be made to see, if other nations already have the ISO 19650 implemented, or is going to very 
soon. It must then be considered if the ISO 19650 series is going to be a European norm, and if so, 
actions must be taken to implement this early on, in order to be in advance of the future 
legislation. From a governmental standpoint, the actions that must be taken to implement, is to  
make this mandatory for all Danish building projects in the future. The government must 
encourage organizations to use the ISO 19650 series early on, in order to ensure that the 
organizations is well prepared to make the transition into implementing the ISO 19650 series. The 
ISO 19650 series must not be a mandatory tool to have on projects as first, but organizations must 
be aware that it will in the future, thus encouraging the implementation and usage of the ISO 
19650 series. This way the ISO 19650 series is not implemented immediately, but slowly 
integrated into the Danish building sector over time. The cause of this will be, that Danish 
organizations will then get a chance to test and develop skills using the ISO 19650 series. This will 
most likely give an easier transition period to the implementation phase of the ISO 19650 series. 
The consideration to have for this strategy is, that if this is something that comes from the top-
down, then it can create tension in the Danish building sector. As the research in this report has 
highlighted, then there can possibly be a push back from organizations that the ISO 19650 series 
==PAGE:46==
Alexander Gale Heiede  
Technical University of Denmark (DTU)

will be applied to. The risk is that not all will be comfortable using the ISO 19650 series, and then 
try to uphold the current methods and standards used. To prevent this, the government must 
include industry relevant individuals, who are familiar with the current methods and standards, 
and ask for help and reflections on how they would prefer the implementation to occur. A way to 
secure more satisfaction when implementing, is to include both larger and smaller organizations, 
as it will not only be one or the other that this will affect.  
The other recommendation on how to implement the ISO 19650 series is to have it implemented 
from bottom-up, meaning that it will be the organizations themselves that take actions in 
implementing the ISO 19650 series. Examples of this is already showing, since the S808 in DS is 
working with a committee from industry organizations dedicated to understand and solve the 
possible conflicts that the ISO 19650 series may have with current Danish methods. One of the 
first things to consider when doing this is to establish a good understanding of the ISO 19650 
series, and what information it contains. Then a comparison to the already existing standards and 
documents can be made in order to map out what is similar and what is not in a Danish practice. 
To better understand this, the Danish building sector must form a committee, such as the S808, 
and compare the existing documents to one another, in order to see what’s missing to fulfill the 
ISO 19650 series. When this has been done, an exercise can be made, to see how well the ISO 
19650 series can fit to Danish building projects, and to see if it can be applied to all. The exercise 
will most likely give a return of knowledge, and an understanding of the ISO 19650 series, that can 
lead to a solution, where the ISO series can be managed to fit into the information requirements 
of all Danish building projects. A good example of this is as mentioned in this report, that 
companies such as MOLIO, is trying to make a scalable solution for the ISO 19650 series, thus 
making it able to be applied to both small and larger scaled building projects. A scalable solution 
for the ISO 19650 would mean, that not all building projects are the same, and not all the 
information requirements can be applied from the ISO 19650 series onto projects. This means, 
that if there is not a scalable solution, then the ISO 19650 series will most likely cause more harm 
than good for any building projects. The aim is to make a solution that can save both time and 
money, and that organizations can understand and integrate into their practice. When a practice 
of using the ISO 19650 series has come to a point, where organizations are comfortable and 
satisfied with the usage of the ISO 19650 series, it can be proposed as a mandatory method to

have on all building projects. This action requires that both smaller and larger organizations are 
willing to see a change, and understand the risks of not making it. If a proposal from the EU makes 
the ISO 19650 series a European norm, then not being able to integrate and use the ISO 19650 
series can have both costly and time-limited consequences. It is then in the Danish building sectors 
best interest to invest in the expansion of the ISO 19650 series to be a common practice. To help 
this, actions such as proposal to the Danish government of implementation needs and an industry

Conclusion  
The conclusion of this report will be based on the discussion of implementation methods, and 
industry perspective on the ISO 19650 series and results from the actions and recommendations. 
From the research presented in this report, the industry perspective of ISO 19650 is not yet 
determined to be implemented immediately. The perspective of the usage of the ISO 19650 series 
is in a stage, where larger companies are investing in trying to find a solution on how to implement 
the ISO 19650 series, and smaller organizations are not yet prepared to take actions in investing 
and implementing the ISO 19650 series. The work that is ongoing in both individual companies and 
the S808 committee, shows that a scalable version of the ISO 19650 series is under evaluation, and 
that it can help both smaller and larger organizations to better implement and use the ISO 19650 
series. This report has produced documented research that usage of the ISO 19650 series, and 
standards in general, can help heighten quality, services, dataflow, information shacking, 
coordination and communication for building projects. It also shows that a governmental 
approach to make a mandatory legalization of the ISO 19650 series can have consequences such 
as industry push back, and lack of engagement from individuals in organizations. The 
recommended action to take from the research and documentation, is to have a bottom-up 
implementation of the ISO 19650 series, making it the industry organizations interest to 
implement it, rather than having the government making it mandatory. By doing so, the actions 
for integration of the ISO 19650 series on the Danish building sector, ensures that it is driven by 
the industry organizations interest in the implementation of the ISO 19650 series. This will also 
respond in a better outcome and satisfaction for the usage of the ISO 19650 series. As a 
perspective of the possible conflicts, gains, and potential losses of using ISO 19650 on the Danish 
building sector, this report shows documentation, that clarifies the outcomes of implementation. 
The consideration of implementing the ISO 19650 series requires both time and resources, and is 
not yet guaranteed to succeed. From the discussion made in this report, if the ISO 19650 series 
becomes a European norm, then it is in the Danish building sectors best interest, to be well 
prepared to make the change, and having the option of an implementation strategy, such as a 
scaled version of the ISO 19650 series, to better suit current and future Danish as well as  
international building projects.

Future research  
If future research were to be made, it could be of interest to have more interviews with industry 
relevant individuals from smaller organizations. This could potentially heighten the knowledge and 
interest that smaller businesses have in the usage of the ISO 19650 series. It could also bring 
forward a wider discussion, to conclude if the implementation of ISO 19650 is just for the benefit

ISO series.  
A future research can also be to dive deeper into the European market, to see how many countries 
and organizations within are using the ISO 19650 series already. This could then give a perspective

it can also give the research in this report a further reflection, as to the fact that if more countries

Danish building sector can be of interest.
