Abstract
Asthma, a chronic inflammatory condition affecting the respiratory system, impacts more than
250 million individuals globally, with cases continuing to increase. In addition to causing respiยญ
ratory symptoms, it is frequently correlated with impaired cognitive function in affected individยญ
uals. The underlying mechanism remains unclear, but several studies have investigated how
immune activity in peripheral tissues influences the central nervous system. In particular, the
meninges have been shown to harbor immune cells associated with asthma, including innate
immune cells such as mast cells, which can interact with nearby nerves and affect learning and
behaviour. Based on this, the aim of this study was to investigate the immune landscape of
the dura mater in a mouse model of chronic allergic asthma and to compare these findings with
immune responses in the airways.
To do so, a wholeยญmount immunofluorescence assay was developed and optimized through evalยญ
uation of fixation methods, blocking solutions, antibody titration, and coยญstaining with vascular
markers. This assay allowed detection of dural CD45+ leukocytes, mast cells and eosinophils in
allergic asthma. Mice were sensitized and challenged intranasally with house dust mite (HDM,
10 or 25 ยตg) to induce asthma and were subsequently examined using both flow cytometry and
the optimized immunofluorescence assay. In addition, a cohort of mice was analyzed after a
fourยญweek recovery period to assess resolution of inflammation.
Flow cytometry confirmed significant increases in lung leukocytes, with up to 1.32ยญfold increase
after exposure to HDM. CD101+ eosinophils increased dramatically in both lung tissue and
bronchoalveolar lavage fluid (BALF) (up to 5.89ยญfold), and mast cells were also increased in the
lungs. In contrast, no corresponding changes were detected in the dura. After the recovery
period, airway inflammation subsided to control levels, although leukocytes remained slightly
elevated in BALF. Immunofluorescence of the dura revealed leukocytes, with sparse eosinophils
and mast cells in both groups. The eosinophils were confined to the dural sinuses and no group
differences were observed, apart from a single CD45+ immune cluster in an asthmatic sample.
In conclusion, this study demonstrates that the chronic allergic asthma model induces a strong
inflammatory response in the airways but does not induce detectable inflammatory changes in
the meninges, although further studies are required to investigate potential subtle or localized
effects.

Introduction
In todayโs world, the rising prevalence of allergies and asthma has become a major global
health concern, affecting millions of people [1]. A study on the finish population found a 12ยญ
fold increase in asthma cases from 1966 to 2003, with similar trends observed in many areas of
Europe, America, and Asia [2]. The 2019 Global Burden of Diseases, Injuries, and Risk Factors
study estimated that 262.41 million people worldwide lived with asthma [2].
Asthma is a chronic inflammatory respiratory disease often with onset in childhood. It is charยญ
acterized by inflammation of the airways, leading to tightening of the surrounding smooth musยญ
cles, narrowing of the airways, excessive mucus production, remodeling of the airway walls,
and bronchial hyperresponsiveness. These processes lead to the hallmark asthma symptoms,
including wheezing, coughing, and shortness of breath, also termed dyspnea [3]. Although
asthma is a disease that affects lung function primarily, multiple studies have also highlighted
its negative impact on cognition and memory in children. A study from 2024 by Christopherยญ
Hayes et al. sought to uncover whether childhood asthma was associated with lower memory
and cognitive function in children [4]. In the cohort study involving 474 children, they found that
asthmatic children showed significantly lower development of episodic memory. Furthermore,
they found that asthmatic children performed worse in tasks evaluating processing speed and
inhibition and attention [4]. A similar picture was observed in a 2017 metaยญanalysis, which reยญ
ported that cognitive deficits were more pronounced in people with severe asthma and among
those of a younger age [5].
The underlying mechanism of cognitive dysfunction in asthma is not fully understood. Studies
have suggested that cognitive impairment could root in systemic inflammation, however, since
the brain is highly dependent on oxygen, hypoxia could also play a role [6][4]. The research
interestingly points to the meninges as a significant location for immuneยญneuron interactions,
revealing a potential route by which peripheral inflammation could affect brain function [7].
1.1
Asthma
Asthma was traditionally classified as intrinsic (nonallergic) or extrinsic (allergic), based on
whether the disease was triggered by nonallergic factors including stress, infections and polยญ
lution or external allergens, such as house dust mites (HDM) [8]. However, modern research
into the disease course and clinical features has revealed that it is a heterogeneous condition,
characterized by distinct observable clinical characteristics (phenotypes) and driven by different
underlying biological mechanisms (endotypes). It is broadly classified into two main endotypes:
Type 2ยญhigh (T2ยญhigh) and T2ยญlow, each covering different phenotypes [9]. As indicated by its
name, T2ยญhigh asthma is characterized by a typeยญ2 immune response with overexpression of
cytokines like interleukinยญ4 (ILยญ4), ILยญ5, and ILยญ13, and in more severe instances, Interferonยญฮณ,
along with mediators and biomarkers such as elevated blood eosinophils and allergenยญspecific
IgE. In contrast, T2ยญlow asthma is characterized by the absence of T2ยญhigh markers and neuยญ
trophil inflammation [10][3].
This thesis will involve work conducted using a HDMยญinduced allergic asthma mouse model,
which is frequently used for studying a T2ยญhigh endotype. Therefore, only this endotype will be
discussed further.

Allergenยญspecific IgE is a key mediator in the cascade of allergic reactions, including asthma.

1.1.2

1.1.3

Eosinophils were discovered in 1879 by Paul Ehrlich and got their name from their affinity for the
acidic dye eosin, which stained their granules [23]. Their large granulates, known as secondary
granulates or cytoplasmic crytsalloid, distinguish them from neutrophils and basophils. The
eosinophil crystalloid granules contains preformed cationic proteins important in both allergic
reactions as well as defense against parasitic helminths [24]. Eosinophils are central effecยญ

Unsurprisingly, elevated numbers of EoPs are found in both the blood and bone marrow of

and GMยญCSF, with ILยญ5 being particularly important for the induction of airway eosinophilia. In
allergenยญchallenged mice, ILยญ5 promotes an increase in EoPs, and ILยญ5 knockout mice fail to deยญ

Brain borders and meningeal immunity
The central nervous system (CNS), comprising the brain and spinal cord, is the part of the
nervous system, responsible for receiving, processing, and responding to sensory information,
thereby coordinating functions and behaviour. For centuries, the CNS has been viewed as
an immunologically privileged site. This concept was rooted in several studies in the early to
midยญ1900s, which demonstrated that implanted foreign tissue was tolerated in the brain, but
not in other regions of the body. Together with the discovery of the bloodยญbrain barrierโs (BBB)
tight junction structure, and the lack of lymphatic vessels in the brain parenchyma, the concept
became widely accepted, also with regard to the borders surrounding the CNS [49].
Now it is known that the brain and especially the borders and CNS ventricular spaces contain a
multitude of immune cells. During steadyยญstate, the brain primarily reside resident macrophages,
termed microglia, and these rarely interact with peripheral T cells. The outer border of the brain
and spinal cord, known as the meninges, is in contrast infiltrated by cells from the innate and
adaptive immune system. Through immune surveillance known as meningeal immunity, the
meninges serve as a protective barrier for the CNS [7]. New findings in neuroscience have
revealed the meninges as a critical interface for neuroimmune interactions, where immune cells
secrete cytokines that influence brain functions such as cognition, memory, and spatial learning
[7][50].

Mast cells and eosinophils role in neuroinflammation and behaviour
Mast cells are increasingly recognized as central regulators of meningeal immunity and CNS
homeostasis. Few mast cells reside in the brain parenchyma, these cells migrate along the
blood vessels during development, but can also migrate from the periphery to the brain. The
vast majority of mast cells are located in the meninges, particularly around the dural sinuses

[65] [67]. Upon experimental activation by c48/80 (a secretagogue targeting the connective

Immunofluorescence: principles and optimization strategies
Immunoassays are essential tools for characterizing the immune landscape in asthmatic mice,
enabling the detection of specific cellular and molecular markers involved in inflammation. In this
thesis, immunofluorescence (IF), a type of immunoassay, was adapted and optimized for wholeยญ
mount staining of mouse dura to investigate the meningeal immune response following allergen
exposure. IF utilize antibodies, conjugated to fluorophores, to detect specific antigens, providing
semiยญquantitative data on protein expression, cellular distribution and localization [82]. This is
particularly valuable for studying subtle or spatially organized immune infiltration, as wholeยญ
mount IF preserves the threeยญdimensional architecture of the meninges, including vasculature,
which is often disrupted in sectioned tissue.
IF is a relative straightยญforward technique, but do require extensive optimization to get a sensitive,
specific and reproducible results, all which dependent on tissue type and antigen properties.
Key parameters to optimize include fixation conditions, antigen retrieval, blocking strategies,
selection of antibodies, and their corresponding concentrations [82].
Fixation, whether by perfusion or immersion, is essential for preserving tissue morphology and
preventing degradation. Common fixatives include aldehydes, alcohols, and acetone [82]. In
this study, fixation was carried out using 4% paraformaldehyde (PFA) and 10% neutral buffered
formalin (NBF) in PBS, both of which act by forming methylene bridges between amino groups in
proteins, hence stabilizing them. While antigen retrieval is sometimes necessary, especially for
formalinยญfixed, paraffinยญembedded tissues due to extensive crossยญlinking and epitope masking,
it was not required in this study, as antigens remained accessible following fixation. Blocking
nonยญspecific binding is important, as antibodies, often secondary antibodies, may bind to nonยญ
wanted Fcยญreceptors or charged proteins. A common approach is to use serum from the same
species as the host of secondary antibodies, as it contains antibodies that bind to otherwise
reactive sites [82]. Autofluorescence is another important factor to consider in tissues high in
collagen and elastin like the dura mater. These exhibit strong autofluorescence in the green

2.1
Background
Asthma has been shown to have a negative effect on cognitive performance, affecting millions of

the muchยญoverlooked question: Does asthma affect cognition through meningeal immunity?

Discussion
This study had two main objectives. First, optimize a whole mount IF protocol for the detection
of CD45+ leukocytes, tryptase+ mast cells, and SiglecยญF+ eosinophils in meningeal tissue. Secยญ
ond, to apply this protocol to investigate immune responses after HDM exposure. A major techยญ
nical challenge was to minimize interference from the inherent autofluorescence of meningeal
tissue, particularly in vascular structures. Antibody titration, fixation, and blocking conditions
were systematically tested to improve signal specificity and reduce background. The resulting
protocol was then used, in combination with flow cytometry, to assess how exposure to two
different HDM concentrations influenced immune cell composition in the meninges, lungs, and
BALF. This dual approach provided complementary spatial and quantitative data, allowing both
the identification of target cell populations within tissue architecture of the meninges and the
evaluation of broader immune dynamics across compartments.
5.1
Optimization of immunofluorescense
5.1.1
Intrinsic tissue autofluorescence
In wholeยญmount IF staining of the meninges, erythrocytes and blood vessels exhibited the strongest
autofluorescence.
The blood vessels comprise smooth muscle cells, collagen, and elastin,
which collectively maintain the structural integrity essential for vascular performance [90]. A
study by Luo et al. (2018) demonstrated, using fluorescence lifetime imaging microscopy, that
elastic fibers exhibit autofluorescence between 465 and 490 nm under UV excitation. [91] This
spectral range overlaps with the detection window of our AF488 channel (Figure 4.1). Likewise,
Kittelberger et al. (1988) reported that blood vessels, including veins, though to a lesser extent,
show strong yellowยญgreen emission [83]. Together, these findings support the interpretation that
the intense autofluorescence observed in the shorter wavelength channels of our images is preยญ
dominantly vascular in origin, likely arising from the intrinsic emission properties of elastin and
other components associated with vessels. Erythrocytes are known to exhibit broadยญspectrum
autofluorescence and were detectable in both green and red channels. [92]. In the IF images,
erythrocytes were largely found in the small vessels in nonยญsinus regions, which reflects more

Fixation and blocking
Fixatives are a known source of autofluorescence in IF protocols [84]. Studies have shown

the green and red channels, compared to 10% and 20% formalin and 95% ethanol, shows the
lowest autofluorescence in both green and red channels [93]. Importantly, 4% PFA and 10%

PFA and NBF fixation yielded comparable results, with NBF giving slightly less background.
In this study, fixativeยญinduced autofluorescence was addressed by incorporating glycine into the

Fluorophore choice, antibody concentration, and incubation time all influenced the quality of

The antibody concentration proved critical for obtaining clear and specific staining. Highly conยญ

this study. Incorporating such quantitative methods could improve the assessment of blocking

Woo et al. (2018) investigated HDMยญinduced allergic airway with both 4ยญweek and 8ยญweek expoยญ

The eosinophilic CD101+ subgroup in BALF and lung was the only subgroup that expanded

potentially representing a restrained or โexhaustedโ mast cell state that limits excessive activaยญ

5.2.2
The meningeal immune landscape appears minimally altered by allergen

Compared to the robust immune cell influx observed in lung tissue and BALF, the meningeal
immune environment appeared largely unaffected by asthma. Flow cytometry analysis revealed

eosinophils were scarcely detected at both time points and HDM groups.
Few meningeal immune cells were recovered in the flow cytometry protocol. At the Post Expoยญ

11 000 cells (Figure 4.11A). In contrast, Niu et al. (2023), who used mechanical isolation of dura
cells followed by red blood cell lysis, reported more than 100 000 CD45+ live single cells [106].
This difference may indicate that the sample preparation for flow cytometry was less efficient
at recovering leukocytes. In addition to mechanical digestion, Niu et al. maintained cells under
chilled conditions throughout the procedure and centrifuged at higher force โ factors that may
be necessary for viability and to pellet all cells effectively.
The meninges contained approximately 150 mast cells in this study, a number substantially
lower than the ~4000 mast cells reported by STAR protocols in wildยญtype mice using flow cyยญ

permeabilization, or overnight intracellular staining, steps that may reduce mast cell recovery in
the present protocol. Even though the number of mast cells was relatively low, their frequency
within the CD45+ population ranged from 0.47% to 2,89% across all groups, which aligns closely
with the 1,1% found in the literature [64].
To the best of current knowledge, no studies have investigated eosinophils in meninges by

rather than resident meningeal cells.

5.2.3
Limitations
This study has several limitations that should be considered when interpreting the results. The
sample size was limited, with only one mouse per group for IF and four to five mice per group for
flow cytometry, thus reducing the statistical power. In addition, the flow cytometry protocol for
the meninges yielded markedly fewer CD45+ cells and mast cells than previously reported. Flow
cytometry likely underestimated the true abundance of meningeal leukocytes due to reduced
cell recovery caused by suboptimal tissue dissociation, centrifugation, and temperature control.
Another important limitation relates to flow cytomery gating. Fluorescence minus one controls
were not incorporated, making it difficult to set accurate gates, which could lead to falseยญpositive
or falseยญnegative results.
There are also various limitations associated with the IF data. The prolonged storage of meningeal
samples from asthmatic mice, compared to the more immediate processing of samples used
during IF optimization, may have led to reduced antigen stability and impaired detection of
tryptase and SiglecยญF. This raises the possibility that the apparent absence of these cell types
reflects a protocol artifact rather than a true biological absence. In addition, the absence of
positive controls or coยญstaining with additional lineageยญspecific markers in IF staining limits the
confidence in identifying mast cells and eosinophils. Including a definitive positive control would
allow for a reliable determination of intensity thresholds when classifying true positive cells. Deยญ
termining whether the eosinophils found in the dura were circulating or resident was not possible,
thus limiting conclusions about their origin and functional role. Finally, many of the optimization
steps relied on visual interpretation, which introduces personal bias and limits the strength and
reproducibility of the optimization strategy.

Conclusion
This study aimed to investigate the immune landscape of the dura mater in a mouse model of
chronic allergic asthma and to compare these findings with immune responses in the airways.
A central part of this work was the development of a wholeยญmount IF protocol to detect dural
CD45+ leukocytes, mast cells, and eosinophils, complemented by flow cytometry to assess
airway inflammation.
The IF assay was systematically optimized by testing fixation methods, blocking solutions, and
antibody titration strategies. An important methodological insight was that perfusion fixation
proved critical for preserving mast cell tryptase staining, underlining the importance of fixation
conditions for reliable detection.The final protocol successfully detected immune cells within
the dura, despite the autofluorescence of inherent tissue elements being relatively prominent in
channels with shorter wavelengths.
Flow cytometry demonstrated that chronic allergic asthma induced pronounced airway inflamยญ
mation, while the meningeal compartment did not show the corresponding inflammatory changes.
In the airways, there was a significant increase of leukocytes, and mast cells. Furthermore, alยญ
lergic asthma led to nearly sixfold increases in eosinophils, completely driven by the CD101+
subset. In the meninges, eosinophils were not detectable by flow cytometry. IF confirmed leukoยญ
cytes in both sinuses and nonsinus, with no changes after asthma induction, except for a small
cluster of immune cells in one asthmatic sample, suggesting that localized immune activity may
occasionally occur. Mast cells and eosinophils were scarce in steadyยญstate and asthmatic mice,
with eosinophils confined to the sinuses, indicating that eosinophils are not resident in the dura,
but rather reflect circulating populations. However, their scarcity may reflect storageยญrelated
loss of signal, rather than a true absence. After a four week recovery period, airway inflamยญ
mation largely resolved, with only persistence of leukocytes in BALF and mast cells in lung
parenchyma, while meningeal immune populations remained unchanged.
Together, these results indicate that chronic allergic asthma elicits strong immune activation in
the airways without detectable alterations in the meninges. Subtle or localized changes cannot
be excluded and may require higherยญresolution approaches to uncover. Overall, this work esยญ
tablishes a methodological foundation for studying meningeal immunity and sheds light on the
impact of chronic allergic asthma on the dural immune landscape.

Future perspectives
The findings in this study provide a foundation for further investigation of the meningeal immune
landscape and its relevance in asthma. By advancing technical approaches, integrating addiยญ
tional methodologies and considering alternative biological explanations, future work can build
on this foundation to uncover new dimensions of neuroimmune interactions in asthma.
7.1
Considerations to furture immunofluorescence analysis
Future steps should focus on further refining the IF protocol to improve reliability and sensitivยญ
ity. Methodological refinements could include the use of alternative blocking agents, such as
chemical quenchers, to reduce autofluorescence. In addition, quantitative image analysis tools
should be applied systematically to assess SNRs and optimize staining conditions in a more
objective and reproducible manner.
Including additional markers in future studies could expand our understanding of the true comยญ
plexity of the immune landscape. Other cell types, like Th 2 cells or ILC2 cells are proven to
habor the meninges, and their role in behaviour and cognition have been investigated in other
relations. Including markers for these may enable to capture a broader picture of the immune
landscape. In addition, incorporating markers for mLVs like LYVEยญ1 would enable investigation
of how immune cells interact with draining pathways, and if there are asthma induced changes
in the immune trafficking. Creating a more complex (multiplexing) staining protocol should be
accompanied by advanced imaging strategies, to avoid fluorophore bleedยญthrough. Spectral
imaging, also known as lambda scanning, records emission spectra at multiple wavelengths
identifying and differentiate between fluorophores with overlapping emission spectra, and unmix
the signals to identify individual markers. This study also sets the stage for further investigaยญ
tion of eosinophils detected by IF, where future work could distinguish circulating from resident
populations, e.g. by applying intravascular labeling approaches.
7.2
Additional techniques to investigate the meningeal immune
landscape
Molecular profiling techniques, like scRNAยญseq would be a great addition to the research. Deยญ
spite itโs technical demands, it would allow one to identify the activation states of multiple cells of
interest and uncover novel immune subsets that are not detectable by antibody staining alone.
If specific populations of meningeal cells are altered in asthma, genetically modified mice, such
as mast cellยญdeficient or cytokineยญdeficient lineages, or antibodyยญmediated depletion strategies,
could provide information on how these cells contribute to meningeal inflammation in asthma.
Th2 cytokines such as ILยญ4 have been shown to be important in astrocyte regulation, as well as
memory and learning, and models targeting this might provide interesting insights. Then addiยญ
tional behaviour and cognitive function assessments could be applied to assess the functional
consequences of these alterations.
7.3
Exploring further than the meninges
Perhaps the explanation for cognitive impairment is not limited to the meninges. Brainยญresident
mast cells are located close to the areas of the brain where learning and behavior are mediยญ
ated, and can be influenced by peripheral inflammation in asthma. Their activation is known
to cause neuroimmune responses and increase BBB permeability, providing a link between airยญ
way inflammation and altered brain function. Another possible mechanism to be investigated is
