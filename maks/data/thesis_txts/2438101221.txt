Abstract
The study of loudspeaker diaphragm geometry is aimed to focus on how does the distri-
bution of mass in the radial direction changes the dynamic response of the diaphragm.
The geometry is subjected to concentrating the mass by changing the shape of our in-
terest in a 2D rectangular geometry in COMSOL, while the total volume is kept as
constant (Total mass is constant). Since the total volume is kept as constant, it is easy
to control the shape of the plate by splitting it into segments and possibly inferred as
thickness variation in x axis of the 2D model and in radial direction in an axisymmet-
ric model. This study signiﬁes the importance of the Eigen frequency pattern changes
versus position of concentrated mass. This study extends from a 2D plate model to 2D
circular plate and cone shaped plate diaphragm.
The work starts from the validation of the numerical method used in the COMSOL,
and a study of very simple plate model that has simply supported end conditions. This
study was considered as a base for the development of the model that resembles the
loudspeaker conditions. This basic study also provides an idea about distribution of
mass by controlling the area in the 2D COMSOL Geometrical Operations.
2D (Rectangular), 2D-Axisymmetric (Circular) plate models were implemented using
COMSOL 2D Geometry Operations and controlling this geometry is performed by con-
trolling the volume. For a 2D (Rectangular) plate this operation is straight and easy
while 2D-Axisymmetric (Circular) case the volume is subjected to polar coordinates,
where Integral calculus is applied with control variables to control the geometry in
COMSOL.
Maintaining the total volume to be constant leads to an importance of keeping the mass
constant for the modal analysis and thus enables the study of geometry modiﬁcation
and its inﬂuence on modes and mode shapes. It was also our interest not to increase
the mass since that directly inﬂuences the sensitivity of the driver and this is not the
interest of the study.
The computations performed in this analysis will be studying
the modes and mode shapes and how does the modes increases or drops with geometry
modiﬁcations. It would be further extended with the interest of controlling a geometry
with speciﬁc interest of frequency contents.

Chapter 1
Introduction
This Thesis was aimed to study the ’Loudspeaker Dynamics with Geometry Modiﬁca-
tions using Numerical Techniques’. The work starts with Validation of numerical method
which is going to be used throughout this thesis work. From the starting of validating
the numerical technique, the work ends with a modal study of conical plate subjected
to mass distribution. The results of every chapter has been considered to start the next
phase of work. Since the time was limited the phase of work was pre planned so that it
can be executed properly with objectives and time constraints.
Chapter2 is validation of numerical method with objective of validating the result ob-
tained from the ’Eigen Frequency Analysis’ of a simple 2D plate model that is subjected
to simply supported end conditions. The process of obtaining the results and devel-
opment of the analytical model for a simple 2D plate also causes for errors in the co
relations of analytical and numerical results were discussed deeply. The 2D model sub-
jected to study in this section is just ﬂat plate geometry that has a length of 100mm
with thickness of 0.5mm subjected to simply supported end conditions. This is a Free
vibration analysis that the model is not subjected to any external force.
Chapter3 is Modal analysis of same model that was used in the chapter 1 but subjected
to mass distribution or concentration. Controlling the total mass to be constant and
distribution of the mass along the x directions were discussed. This mass concentration
process with a 2D rectangular plate is quite an easy process as it is performed with
segmentation of geometry and also controlling the mass at particular segment become
much more easier and enables the mass concentration along the x direction. The results
obtained from the modal analysis were discussed and also the mode shapes that are

2.1
Introduction
The objective of the writing is to carry out the numerical analysis on a 2D plate cross
section using comsol multiphysics, a numerical tool. The results obtained in the numer-
ical analysis is later veriﬁed by the analytical method described in one of the reference.
This chapter further extends to analyze the relative error between the numerical and
analytical methods. It is also important to decide to what extend that this numerical
results can be trusted based on the number of degrees of freedom described and relative
error, which adds more perception on the extended use of numerical tool in this project,
and this chapter gives an idea of numerical tool’s accuracy and accountability for the
project to use numerical method in the upcoming work.
The numerical analysis is aimed to simulate the mode shapes of the plate and obtaining
the ﬁrst ﬁve eigen frequencies. The same mode shapes and natural modes are going to
be simulated in MATLAB using analytical formulas which are going to be described in
the next section.
It is possible to validate modes found by the numerical models using analytical methods.
Modal frequencies are fundamental or natural frequencies of a structure, when exited
with same frequency the structure resonates. It is usually a frequency where the struc-
ture preferably not excited since it leads to higher deﬂection that is most often not an
interest.

Conclusion and Future Scope
The thesis work aimed to be able come up with an ideology of how the mass distri-
bution could aﬀect the ’Dynamic Response of the Loudspeaker Diaphragm’. Started
with Validation of Numerical Method, which was used throughout this thesis work to
study the modal performance of the various interesting cases. After having validated
the numerical method it was decided to have to establish diﬀerent decks that performs
all the necessary studies through COMSOL and post processing done using MATLAB.
There are around 10 diﬀerent decks were established and used during the course of this
thesis work. few decks were helpful in ﬁnding the stiﬀness values of the geometries and
other decks were subjected to ’Eigen Frequency Analysis’.
The main objective of the study is achieved after analyzing various cases of 2D rectangu-
lar and circular plate and especially circular plates that are subjected to tilted conditions
and that represents the conical shaped loudspeaker diaphragm. When the mass is placed
at particular radial location the bending resistance suddenly increases around the region
and observed with reduced amplitude of displacement and the natural frequency mode
that causes bending on the concentrated mass gets reduced meanwhile if that causes
highest amplitude on the mass concentrated are, that would cause increase of natural
frequency.
Natural frequency of 2D axi symmetrical model ﬂat geometry has shown ﬁrst mode
around 14Hz to 17Hz while the ﬂat geometry subjected to tilting has shown around
60Hz to 70Hz. This increase is observed because of the conical structure that get higher
stiﬀness due to spatial positions with end conditions also with position of excitation.
When the excitation through voice coil happens to vibrate conical structure, the incli-
nation angle of conical structure to the exciting voice coil is reducing over the increase of
