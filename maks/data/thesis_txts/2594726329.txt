Abstract
There is a well-known problem over two decades for the simulation of breaking
waves with computational ﬂuid dynamics. In the process of both pre- and post-
breaking, the turbulence level is always over-estimated in the simulation results.
This problem is caused by the unconditionally instability of these popular turbu-
lence models using Reynolds-averaged Navier-Stokes (RANS) equations, which re-
sult in the exponential growth of the turbulent kinetic energy and eddy viscosity
from previous research. This thesis would extend the work into the low Reynolds
number k −ω model while the standard k −ω model for fully turbulent ﬂow has
been already proved with unconditionally instability in previous studies. The low
Reynolds number k −ω model is based on standard k −ω model but with non-
constant closure coefﬁcients. With the corresponding analysis on the stability, the
low Reynolds number k −ω model is found with unconditionally instability due to
the always positive value of exponential growth rate. The stabilization stress limiter
introduced in previously research on the standard k −ω model is adopted for the
stabilization of low Reynolds number k −ω model.
The stabilized low Reynolds number k −ω model is created and implemented
in the OpenFOAM with the modiﬁcation on the closure coefﬁcients and the diffusion
term in the k- and ω- equations. The simple steady boundary layer case validates
the successful implementation of this model in OpenFOAM with the good performance
in the viscous sub-layer. The simulation of propagating wave exhibits the difference
between the stabilized model and non-stabilized model where the stabilized model
successfully keep the ﬂow stable with exponential decay of the turbulent kinetic en-
ergy while the non-stabilized one have tendency on the exponential growth of the
turbulent kinetic energy.
The application of low Reynolds number k −ω model on simulation of break-
ing waves demonstrates that the non-stable model have serve pollution on the eddy
viscosity while the stabilized model gives good improvement on this. The stabilized
model have a further breaking point on shore from the observation on the surface
elevation envelopes. But the stable model demonstrates the turbulent kinetic energy
proﬁle out of our expectation and also undertow proﬁle similar with laminar model
results in the surf zone, which additionally explain the eddy viscosity in very low
magnitude in the full region.
Keywords: Turbulence modelling; Low Reynolds number model; surface grav-
ity waves; Breaking waves; OpenFOAM

Chapter 1
Introduction
Nowadays, with the development and exploitation on the sea, more and more en-
gineering activities are carried out in aquatic environment. High importance are
attached to the study on breaking waves (or breaker) which cause severe damage to
coastal structures. On the coastline, the breaking of water free-surface waves is the
most common natural phenomena. While the water waves spread from the deep
water to the coast, it leads to the decreasing of water depth and the increasing of
wave amplitude during this process. As a result, the turbulence is generated with
large amounts of wave energy being transformed into turbulent kinetic energy. At
this point, the linear wave dynamics often become invalid in the description of wave
behaviours. Such breaker-induced turbulence is mainly inﬂuenced by the breaker’s
steepness and beach slope, which gives the Iribarren number, ξ0, (Battjes, 1974;). The
breaker is classiﬁed into three types according to the value of the Iribarren number,
as spilling (ξ0 <0.5), plunging (0.5< ξ0 <3.3) and collapsing (3.3< ξ0).
The key point on the study of breaking waves is to give more accurate descrip-
tion on the turbulence of this process. A number of relevant experimental investiga-
tions have been conducted on a sloping beach by both spilling (Ting and Kirby, 1994;
Ting and Kirby, 1996) and plunging breakers (Ting and Kirby, 1994; Ting and Kirby,
1995). With the fast development of computer power, the computational ﬂuid dy-
namics (CFD) is now widely used in the study of the breaking waves. Various meth-
ods for numerical simulation of this problem are developed. Direct Navier-Stokes
simulation (DNS) is to seek the solution of the primitive Navier-Stokes equations
and the continuity equation with the base of large computation expense. Therefore,
it is only possible to be applied in relatively low Reynolds number ﬂow. For the sake
of reducing the computation requirement, the simpliﬁcation is obtained through
Reynolds averaged Navier-Stokes equations (RANS). The classic turbulence closure
problem is proposed based on the continuity equations and three RANS equations
governing the mean ﬂow of turbulence. As there are four equations with ten un-
knowns, the system is not closed. In order to close it for obtaining the solution, more
equations describing turbulence on the mean ﬂow are required. Various turbulence
model is proposed for this aim with being coupled with two-equations turbulence
closure models, like the widely used k −ω model (Wilcox, 1988) and k −ϵ model
(Launder and Sharma, 1974) where k is turbulent kinetic energy, ω is speciﬁc dissi-
pation rate and ϵ is dissipation. These two equations turbulence model are used to
model the turbulence generation and dissipation in the surf zone.
However, present turbulence models generally overestimate the level of turbu-
lent kinetic energy from breaking waves. The cause of this problem is not clearly rec-
ognized yet. Devolder, Rauwoens, and Troch, 2017 pointed that the over-production

on turbulence beneath the computed surface waves is from the too high eddy viscos-
ity near the air-water interface, hence they included the buoyancy production term
to combat this in k equation. It to some extent helps limiting the over-production but
does not solve it fundamentally. More likely explanation for the over-production
problem in RANS models is from Mayer and Madsen, 2001, which proves the in-

that the standard k −ω model (Wilcox, 1988) is unconditionally unstable (exponen-

method to stabilize it. According to the stabilization method posed in Larsen and
Fuhrman, 2018, the turbulence in the fully turbulent region is more consistent with
the experiments (Ting and Kirby, 1994) improving the over-production problem.
1.1
Structure of the Thesis
For the standard k −ω model with the stabilization effectively improve the over-
production problem, low Reynolds number k −ω model for the overestimation on
the turbulence level for the simulation of breaking waves would be studied in this
thesis. This article would start from this point to introduce the governing equations
and closure coefﬁcients in low Reynolds number turbulence closure model. The sta-
bility analysis on low Reynolds number turbulence model would be imposed. Af-
ter the stability analysis, we would purpose the stabilization method on this model
when it is not stable. The stabilized low Reynolds number model would be imple-
mented with successful practice with the interFoam solver in the open source CFD
software OpenFOAM. Afterwards, the surface waves case and breaking waves case are
respectively simulated with the stabilized low Reynolds number turbulence closure
model for the further investigation.
The following is the structure of this thesis:
Chapter 1 Introduction: this chapter focus on the relevant literature with the
topic of the breaking waves simulation and turbulence modelling. The current de-
velopment of these subjects is also introduced and leads to the research focus of this
article.
Chapter 2 Basic Theory: this chapter would start from the basic equations of
RANS turbulence theory to introduce relevant theoretical background for standard
k −ω model and low Reynolds number k −ω model. The difference between the
standard model and low Reynolds number model would also be stated. The non-
constant closure coefﬁcients would be introduced and studied on their development
with turbulence Reynolds number ReT.
Chapter 3 Stability analysis and stabilization of low Reynolds number model:
The standard k −ω model stability analysis (Larsen and Fuhrman, 2018) would be
repeated ﬁrst. The low Reynolds number k −ω model is then analyzed in the same
strategy. The ODE numerical modelling with the base of analysis would also be
implemented to verify the analytical results. The stabilization of this low Reynolds

Chapter 2
Basic theory
This chapter is going to introduce the basic equations governing the turbulence clo-
sure model. The standard k −ω model would be then presented with the two equa-
tions whose every single term in the equations is explained physically. Inheriting
from the standard k −ω model, the low Reynolds number k −ω model are pre-
sented.
The non-constant closure coefﬁcients specially in low Reynolds number
model would be analyzed to show their development and how they inﬂuence the
model equations.
2.1
The turbulence closure problem

The sign of Pk and Pω directly determines the tendency of development on the k

without viscous modiﬁcation, Pk turn positive at Reynolds number Rek = 8100 and
Pω turn positive at Reynolds number Reω = 12254 in his case. Hence starting from
laminar ﬂow, the following sequence of events occurs in the transition period:
1. It starts in a laminar region with k = 0 in the boundary layer and a small
freestream value of k
2. At ﬁrst, the Pk and Pω are both negative. The dissipation of k and ω is larger
than production. the boundary layer remains laminar.
3. When Re reaches the Rek, Pk becomes positive but Pω stays negative. Turbu-
lent kinetic energy k starts being ampliﬁed. At some point in this process, the eddy
viscosity grows rapidly indicating the beginning of transition.
4. When Re reaches the Reω, the Pk and Pω are both positive. ω gets ampliﬁed
and continues growing until a near balance between the production and dissipation
in k equation. The reaching of the near balance indicates the end of transition. The
ﬂow gets completely turbulent.
The whole process is very sensitive with freestream value of k and ω. There are
three observations could be concluded. Firstly, the previous model predicts transi-
tion at much too low Reynolds number which should be 90000 for minimum crit-

Implementation of low Reynolds number k −ω model
For the core aim of this chapter is to construct and validate the desired low Reynolds

on the standard k −ω model which could be obtained from OpenFOAM turbulence
model library, the desired model would be constructed by modifying the standard
model step by step. After every small operation, the compilation for every upgrades
are required to be made for easier locating on the errors.
Firstly, the closure coefﬁcients in the standard model are constant, but in the
low Reynolds number are partly dependent on turbulence Reynolds number ReT as
mentioned in section 2.3. So the additional closure coefﬁcients constants including

function after assigning the local reference.

could be easily switched off by assigning the value as 0 under the constant/turbulence
properties ﬁle.

Chapter 7
Conclusion
For the aim of the implementation of the stabilized low Reynolds number k −ω
model is to solve the common over-production problem on the turbulence level for
breaking waves simulation while using low Reynolds number k −ω model, the con-
clusion would contain every milestone in the whole process of this project imple-
mentation.
Firstly, the low Reynolds number k −ω model is analytically proved uncondi-
tionally unstable based on stability analysis with the simpliﬁed k- and ω- equations
of low Reynolds number model. It shows that turbulent kinetic energy k and eddy
viscosity νT have the exponential growth rate for all range of ReT in the low Reynolds
number k −ω model which result in the over-production problem. The ODE numer-
ical modelling based on the the simpliﬁed k- and ω- equations conﬁrms the analysis
result. Furthermore, the stabilization using stress limiter λ2 is introduced into the
low Reynolds number model. It successfully stabilizes the model and gives the neg-
ative exponential growth rate of eddy viscosity. The decaying of νT shown in the
ODE numerical modelling with the stabilization applied conﬁrms the successful im-
plementation of stabilization.
Based on the model equations and its special deﬁnition of turbulence closure co-
efﬁcients, the low Reynolds number k −ω turbulence model could be established
in the OpenFOAM through the modiﬁcation on the base of the OpenFOAM-version k −ω
turbulence model. After successful compilation of the low Reynolds number k −ω
model, the performance of it is tested in the steady boundary layer cases. The low
Reynolds number k −ω model exhibits the much better correspondence with the
experimental data in the viscous sub-layer region (y+ < 100) with the comparison
of the standard k −ω model for fully turbulent ﬂow. So far the low Reynolds num-
ber k −ω model could be regarded as successful implementation and application on
simple case.
Afterwards, on the base of successful validation in steady boundary layer case,
the simulation of progressive surface waves is demonstrated in OpenFOAM. The simu-
lation cases are implemented with respectively non-stabilized low Reynolds number
k −ω model (λ1 = 0, λ2 = 0) and stabilized low Reynolds number k −ω model (λ1
= 0, λ2 = 0.05) under three different initial turbulence level. As for the initial turbu-
lence level, the simulation results shows that too low initial turbulence level would
not be able to trigger the transition in the simulations. It is not conﬂict with the an-
alytical results showing all range ReT would have the exponential growth for there
is a potential hypothesis that it is in the transition. In the aspect of stabilization, the
simulation using non-stabilized model shows good correspondence with the ana-
lytically predicted exponential growth rate for eddy viscosity, while the result with

stabilized model successfully gives exponential decaying of eddy viscosity. Addi-
tionally, the eventual levelling off of eddy viscosity in the simulation with non-stable
model could be explained through the decaying of surface elevation. The turbulence
level keeps increasing, the wave energy is hence extracted and result in the decreas-
ing of the surface elevation. The surface elevation ﬁgures for stable model illustrates
that the surface elevation is kept constant for the stabilized turbulence level. Hence
the effectiveness of stabilization could be conﬁrmed in progressive surface waves
simulation.
As ﬁve spilling breakers cases are simulated using low Reynolds number k −ω
model with different value of λ1 and λ2 to control the switch of these two stress lim-
iters, the experimental data from Ting and Kirby, 1994 are used for the comparison.
In the aspect of the eddy viscosity, these snapshots with νT indicates that the non-
stabilized model have serve over-production problem on the eddy viscosity while
the stabilized model delivers low magnitude value of eddy viscosity for the whole
region containing both pre- and post-breaking. Regarding the surface elevations, the
stabilized model demonstrates the further breaking points on shore compared with
non-stabilized model results. For the investigation on the turbulent kinetic energy,
the overestimation could be easily observed for stable model at those positions af-
ter breaking. The undertow proﬁle demonstrates that the stabilized model performs
similar to the laminar model from Brown et al., 2016. The high undertow velocity
is regarded as the reason for the high value of k. Additionally, the laminar model
performance could again explain the low magnitude of eddy viscosity for stabilized
model. The observation on the full size of k and ω proﬁles also explains that the
high magnitude of speciﬁc dissipation rate leads to the low eddy viscosity in the
whole region, especially for the inner surf zone. As for the snapshots of the breaking
process, it shows that the waves are maintained more stable and progressive in the
stable models than in the non-stabilized models. The stable models do stabilize the
breaking wave simulation in some aspects.
7.1
Further works
Due to time limitation and some mistakes on the operations, the spilling breaker
cases are only simulated for 75 periods, 60 of which are regarded as warm-up. For
the investigation of the data only covering 10 periods, the averaged data is not stable
enough. The full simulations might gives out better results. Secondly, because the
spilling breaker results could not be well matched by the analysis results and the
behavior of the stabilized model is similar with laminar model in terms of the , the
applicability of the stabilized low Reynolds number k −ω model is not fully deter-
mined. Even though the successful reduction on the eddy viscosity overestimation
is obtained, the abnormal high turbulent kinetic energy development and the main-
tenance of high speciﬁc dissipation along the depth is not ﬁgured out yet. More
explanation on the mechanism of the low Reynolds number model stabilization is
needed.
