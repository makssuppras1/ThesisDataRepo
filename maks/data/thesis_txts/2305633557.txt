Abstract 
 
Quality control is an important process in a production line by which the needs and ex-
pectations of a customer are achieved. Functionality of assembled parts can be assured 
only through high quality production processes being practiced throughout different de-
partment. To verify if the parts fulfil the required quality, tolerance verification has to be 
carried out. 
 
As a state of the art of Ortofon in manufacturing of TPE micro-rings, the present research 
work contains verification of critical component ensuring it fits to the cantilever assembly 
and delivers certain damping ratio. This project is focused mainly on two critical dimen-
sions: Outer Diameter (OD) of 1, 5 mm with dimensional tolerances of ± 10μm and Inner 
Diameter (ID) of 0, 45 mm with dimensional tolerances of ± 20μm. Presently the TPE 
micro-rings are produced by micro-injection molding where keeping the above mentioned 
dimensions is of prime importance. To find the best quality of the micro-rings, 36 batches 
of micro-rings were procured from Ortofon A/S which were produced with different ma-
chine settings.  
 
These samples were measured and analyzed by different software for right machine set-
tings. It gave conclusive results with choice of software and the machine itself for such 
evaluation. Firstly, the DeMeet CMM machine is used for such measurements. The soft-
ware “Approve for DeMeet” was used for analyzing the measurements. Subsequently 3D 
optical coordinate measurement system was used where two software (SPIP and Moun-
tainsMap) have been considered as possible tools to analyze measurements. 
 
Secondly, different effects which can influence on the process production were analyzed 
by Design of Experiment (DOE) in order to find relationship between factors affecting a 
process and the output of that process (viz. outer and inner diameter). Five factors have 
been considered as most influential on the process production: injection speed, packing 
pressure, melt temperature, mold temperature and variation of 4 cavities. 
 
Finally, as micro-injection molding is a relatively new process regarding the manufactur-
ing of TPE micro-rings in Ortofon. Hence a separate experiment which included 25 
batches of micro-rings with same machine settings were studied for dimensional preci-
sions with an aim of evaluating process reproducibility. This time, the thickness of the 
part was also measured apart from ID and OD. As a last step, the variation of the results 
were compared with results obtained by Ortofon operator.

Material with good vibration properties is necessary for production of suspension rings.

Background 
Over the past decade the research for micro parts has been increasing every year. Nowa-
days, miniaturization of the parts play an important role in society, both from economic 
and ecological point of view. The most significant micro product fields are information 
technology, medical and biomedical components like for example hard disk, jet printers 
and so on. Also automotive and telecommunication industries represent wide range of 
micro products.  Micro-manufacturing is the field or process of fabrication of miniature 
parts of micrometer scales. Is it a bridge between Macro-manufacturing and Nano –man-
ufacturing where at least one functional feature is in the range of μm. There is a huge 
difference in micro-market and the most common terms and main types of micro-products 
involve: micro-electronics products, micro-optical electronic systems, micro-electronics 
mechanical system and micro-optical electronics mechanical system. There are different 
methods which can be used for manufacturing of given product. In order to categorize 
these methods, micro-manufacturing techniques are divided into two groups: Micro-sys-
tem technology (MEMS) and non-MEMS manufacturing. In the Table 1.1 can be seen 
differences of different techniques between MEMS and non-MEMS manufacturing. The 
reason for dividing these two methods is not only because of MEMS cannot handle some 
types of materials which can be manufactured only by non-MEMS but also because of 
scaling down the process, tools and machinery in order to achieve much smaller dimen-
sions or features. In some literatures can be found that micro manufacturing is categorized 
as a silicon-silicon based manufacturing and non-silicon material. [4] 
 
In addition, due to small sizes of micro-products, it faces more complex problems com-
pare with macro products and it is mainly because of: 
 Material properties: material is too hard or too weak for process manufacturing 
 Structural strength and stiffness: material is too brittle to retain all of the forces 
which influence on the processing  
 Dimensions: due to small sizes it may be difficult to realize or cut part without 
any deformation  
 Stresses: due to residual stresses and surface integrity induced by different factors 
(plastic deformation, thermal gradient and so on) may influence on the quality of 
the product [4]

BACKGROUND 
2.1 
Introduction 
First introduced between the years 1985 and 1995, when no appropriate machines were 
suitable for manufacturing of micro parts, the term “micro injection molding (μ-IM)” has 
been widely accepted. Micro-injection molding is considered as a one of the key technol-
ogy in micro-manufacturing.  It is because of its ability of mass-production, reliability 
and relative cost effective manufacturing process to form micro-parts made from poly-
mers. Increasing demands for micro-devices depend highly on manufacturing processes 
that can produce large amounts of micro parts in reliable and economical way. Parts made 
by micro-injection molding fall in one of the following two categories [6]: 
a) Type 1-components with overall size less than 1mm 
b) Type 2-components which have larger overall dimensions but their micro-fea-
tures have sizes smaller than 200 μm 
 
Technological differences of the μ-IM in comparison with the injection molding (IM), is 
not just scaling down of the conventional injection process. It also requires a rethinking 
of each step process with special requirements. By comparing these two technologies it 
clearly shows that μ-IM is new technology because of: [6] 
 
 Construction of the mold and its cavities 
 Nano rheology 
 The applications 
 Raw materials variation 
 Software process simulation modeling  
 Precision Technology 
 Process monitoring and measurement 
 Product properties 
 Different machine settings 
 Different control system

2.2 
Process of Micro-injection molding 
To control the process production, the new technology has been made for micro-injection 
molding. In order to control metering volume and the homogeneity of the small parts 
during the process, a screw extruder has been developed. Screw diameter is typically less 
than 20 mm with limitation approximately to 12 mm not only because of the size of con-
ventional polymer pellet but also because of the weight as a limiting factor. 
The concept of μ-IM consisting of splitting four units [6]: 
 
 Plastification,  
 Matering 
 Locking  
 Injecting 
 
The plastification takes place in separated way from the injection unit.  Small amount of 
plastic is fed into the injection cylinder by plunger. The plastification can be done in two 
similar ways by using two plasticizing systems. The first system uses plunger and hot 
cylinder and the second system uses the conventional small screws and barrel. After plas-
ticizing, a second plunger with diameter of 2-5 mm, injects the molten material into the 
cavity. 
The μ-IM consists of following five process steps [9]: 
 
1) In the first step polymer pellets are plasticized by fixed extruder screw and fed 
into the metrering chamber. (Figure 2. 4a) 
2) In the next step the shut-off valve closes in order to avoid back flow of  polymer 
from the metering chamber 
3) After having a predefined volume of polymer, the plunger in the dosage barrel 
delivers the shot of polymer volume to the injection barrel. .( Figure 2. 4 b) 
4) Polymer melt is pushed into to mold cavity by injection plunger.( Figure 2. 4 c) 
5) After this plunger completes its movement, holding pressure is applied to the melt. 
The movement of injection plunger is maximum 1 mm forward.

2.3 
Materials used in micro-injection molding 
Several materials have been used for production of micro-parts made by μ-IM. However 
polymers are showing better properties compare with glass and silicon. It is due to number 
of reasons: price of polymers, wide variety of characteristic material properties (mechan-
ical strength optical transparency, chemical stability and biocompatibility), excellent rep-
lication fidelity, compare with the silicon, polymers offer good electrical insulation. 
The factors mentioned above necessitate the next sections of the report, which deals with 
the polymers to be considered as a best material for μ-IM. Therefore all of the comparison 
between materials and processes will be connected to polymer. 
The properties of a given polymer highly depends on its flow ability, heat transfer ability 
and cooling shrinkage which affect its molding efficiency. Micro-injection molding is the 
process within many operations. During the flow of the polymer to micro cavities, radical 
changes take place in material properties of selected polymer. Also it has been reported 
that polymers containing aggressive chemicals can lead to corrosion in the mold insert 
Considering all of these factors; problems which can happen during the process of mold-
ing, it is necessary to choose the most suitable material before the process will start.

2.4 
Process parameters influence on the parts quality  
Factors affecting on the process of micro-injection molding have big influence on the 
final product. Due to complex system it is important to study factors that affect the repli-
cation capabilities of μ-IM. The quality of the replicated micro-parts is a significant factor 
of any micro-process.  It is necessary to study optimization of the process parameters for 
producing parts with acceptable quality. The most common variables influenced on the 
part quality are [12]: 
 Mold temperature 
 Melt temperature 
 Injection speed 
 Injection pressure 
 Holding pressure 
 Holding time 
 Cooling time 
 
In addition part geometry is also significant factor that affects the quality of filling for 
micro-components. Studies are showing that the holding pressure, injection speed and 
mold temperature are the most affecting variables which influence on the quality of com-
plex micro-parts.

Finally the surface roughness of the mold is next parameter which has to be taken into 
account. Investigations show that interaction between melt flow and tool surface has sig-
nificant impact on the final quality of the micro-product. The reason for this is that the 
level of turbulence in the polymer flow is different by using same cavities with same 
geometry but with different surface finish [13]. 
Also quality assurance is the request for any type of the micro-manufacturing process.  In 
order to measure robustness of the process the control plans addressing critical features 
have to be achieved. To ensure that the parts are meeting with their specific limits and the 
samples do not have possible defects which can influence the performance of given prod-
uct, it is necessary to carry out quality control. To verify precisely tool and part dimen-
sions, the high quality machine is requested (for example high magnification microscope 
or CMM). However to be sure that product meets the customer satisfaction and arrives to 
customer undamaged, the product handling is necessary to consider as important factor 
in quality assurance [14]. 
 
Simulation

 Visualization of the flow 
 Optimization of the design of the mold

2.5 
Micro-molds & Inserts 
In principle micro-molds are similar to conventional molds. Depending on the desired 
design, the micro-mold consist of a fixed part with one or more moving parts. Compared 
to the conventional machines, micro-injection molding machines use special features like 
Variotherm process (raising the mold temperature during filling and rapidly cooling at 
the end of filling = shortening cycle time) [9]. 
The molds made for μ-IM are created on the base of inserts.  Conventional molds are 
made of steel while molds made for μ-IM are made from different materials. It means that 
inserts are fitted into the “body” of the main mold.  By fitting insert to the body of the 
mold the cost of the final mold will decrease.

The manufacturing of the inserts with micro cavities is complex process. It depends on 
the dimensions, accuracy and fabrication cost. Therefore different techniques are used to 
produce different inserts. It includes e-beam writing, lithography, electroforming pro-
cesses (LIGA), precision engineering techniques (EDM). 
In more complex inserts it becomes necessary to use combination of different processes 
by using different techniques.

Machines

-0.1g while conventional machines are able to make “only” micro components with part

2.7 
Micro-handling system 
Nowadays a lot of companies are using fully automatized process in order to increase 
speed of the production .Therefore an efficient transportation of the components from 
one station to another is necessary to be achieved. To do such operations the handling 
process is needed. Key problems in micro manufacturing are high-precision micro han-
dling machinery and lack of flexibility of the manipulators. These problems have to be 
solved in the way that process will run as smooth as possible.  Micro-manufacturing 
includes 3 main handling processes: transport of the samples from one location to an-
other, Sorting and Orientation control.  
 
Manipulators 
It should be noted that conditions for manipulating with micro-parts require higher ma-
nipulation care and relatively clean environment because of high fragility of micro 
components. Presently the trend is to improve the system’s immunity in order to pre-
vent machine from vibrations and thermal drifts. As the most important parameters of 
industrial manipulators which have big influence on the overall process of handling are 
precision, velocity, load capacity and workspace. The state of the art of manipulators 
can be found in [15].  
 
Sorting-Gripping System 
Another problem is gripper system and its influence on the process due to gripping or 
releasing the part. Some well-known gripper system are: friction, pneumatic and mag-
netic. The main principle of friction gripper is that the sample is centered between the 
gripping jaws which allows quite precise handling of micro-components with high de-
gree of accuracy and speed. The pneumatic gripper system works based on using micro-
vacuum tools with robotic systems. The advantage of such machine is its small size due 
to only one gripping surface [16]. The magnetic grippers are using electromagnets for 
handling samples. Different design for different parts is not needed for this type of 
machine. However usage of these machines has to be reconsidered due to their limita-
tions. Some of the limitations are introduced: regarding the friction griper the problem 
occur when different parts are used in the production due to limitations of gripping 
jaws. When the holes are presented in the components, vacuum grippers are not able to 
grip the part due to shortage of pressure [17]. As an example using of plastic material 
in the production do not allow to magnetic grippers grip the plastic part because mag-
netic grippers can be used  only for ferromagnetic materials[18].

Limitations: 
Process of micro-injection moulding needs a special machine compare to the conven-
tional one in order to carry out the control of the small injection volume. Also designing 
the mould is a tough process which has to be done precisely in order to allow the gas 
evacuation. In addition packing and manipulation of the small finished part is a complex 
process. The inspection of the final part is necessary. Finally choosing the right material 
has to be taken to account, because behaviour of the material in μ-IM machine is different, 
mainly because of injection speed with shear being higher and cooling speed being faster. 
From this point of view main, the limitations for micro injection moulding are: 
 Injection moulding machine  
 Mould 
 Raw plastic material 
 Manipulation and micropieces control quality

3 FOCUS VARIATION INSTRUMENTS-OPTICAL 
3D SURFACE METROLOGY 
3.1 
Introduction 
To guarantee the quality of a product, 3D measurement of technical surfaces have become 
a standard tool in engineering field in last decades. The part checking, controlling the 
properties and the function of the materials is necessary for production of high quality 
parts. Traditionally, 3D measurements are using tactile devices for measuring the part 
quality and can be divided into two groups:  
1) Contact stylus system – measurement is provided by stylus tip which is traced along a 
profile of a small scale surface. The output of this measurement are roughness parameters 
(Ra, Rq, Rz ).  
2) Coordinate measurement machine (CMM) - It is similar with a contact stylus system, 
but it is more complex system. Difference is that CMM are using synthetic ruby ball as a 
stylus tip and can measure large scale feature. The output of the measurement (s) can be 
different parameters as for example sphere radius, cylinder diameter and so on  [22]. 
 
However optical measurement devices have become more popular nowadays. Compared 
with traditional 3D measurements, optical ones can perform measurements with addi-
tional advantages: 1) Measurements are performed without any physical contact and 
therefore there is no damage of the surface. 2) There is no request for such maintenance 
as compare with tactile instrument because there is almost no parts which have to be 
replaced. [22] 
 
In the field of optical measurements, can be found many different type of devices. 
ISO 25178-6:2010 (Classifications of methods for measuring surface texture) offer de-
scription of specific methods for specific use. 
Each of such methods have their own advantages and disadvantages, depending upon the 
kind of surfaces that are to be monitored and the applications there of. Nonetheless, opti-
cal instruments have gained a considerable ground and their superior features over stylus 
based instruments in terms of speed of measurements is only further alluring [23].

3.2 
Criterion for choosing a method for measuring sur-
face texture 
 
When selecting a certain measurement equipment for evaluating and monitoring sur-
face texture of a specimen, a variety of factors are needed to be considered. Depend-
ing on the specimen’s application and manufacturing processes involved, different 
measurement techniques can be employed. Since this project deal about optical meas-
urements and most of the measurements are done by focus variation (FV) instrument, 
next section will be focus mainly on this instrument. Some of the factors which are 
pivotal in preferring one method over the other are enlisted below: 
 What type of surfaces are to be measured? Surface Geometry and Material 
hardness 
Depending on the necessity of the spatial dimensions and depth to which the surface to-
pography is to be mapped, a compromise will have to be made between choosing SPM 
or SEM method and stylus mounted method. SPM and SEM methods prove to be best for 
spatial spectrum of 500 nm or low. However, the limitation comes on the height ranges. 
Stylus instrument, on the other hand is a wrong choice if a surface is soft, as it can inter-
fere with the surface texture due to direct contact.  
As in this project case, it is a micro ring, which is quite small, usage of stylus is difficult. 
Also, the focus variation instrument gives best results as the size is small and the meas-
urements are faster. [24] 
 
 What is the size of the sample to be measured? 
Even though, optical measurements are faster, one of the limitations is the size of object 
that they can accommodate [24]. However, in our case, since, it is a micro ring which is 
to be measured, this problem is solved. 
 
 Speed of measurements? 
As discussed earlier, speed is also one of the important factors in measuring an object 
surface. In our study, since there were 36 samples to be measured for the inner and outer 
diameter, this became one of the driving factors to choose an infinite focus optical micro-
scope.  
 
 Expected Measurement Uncertainty? 
This consideration is one of the most important as directly correlates with the tolerance 
levels in the final product. As a micro ring plays a very crucial role in the DJ equipment, 
it is expected to have a high grade quality. For this reason it is important to obtain the 
lowest uncertainty as is possible. Hence, InfiniteFocus Microscope was used.

Focus variation vs. Imaging Confocal Microscope 
 
The most common differences between these two devices is the width of curve for max-
imum sharpness calculation. It means that intensity of confocal curve has a smaller width 
in the scan direction as compare with the confocal one.  The second difference is that 
Focus variation instrument is not limited to coaxial illumination which can lead to limited 
maximum measurable slope angle as in the case of confocal instrument. 
 
Focus variation vs. Point Auto Focusing Techniques 
 
The main difference between these two instruments is in the lateral scanning. While Focus 
variation microscope measure many points with one vertical scan, the point auto focus 
measure only a single point  and the sample has to be moved laterally in order to get a 
depth profile.

5 PROCESS OPTIMIZATION 
 
5.1 
Introduction 
The optimization of micro injection molding is important fact which can influence on the 
final product. To achieve minimal cost of the product is necessary to understand the pro-
cess and identify effects which can influence on the process production. In order to 
achieve the highest quality and reliability constraints, the reduction of variability due to 
changes in the process parameters is the fact that is necessary to achieve during the oper-
ation. As a key method for investigations of different effects which can influence on the 
production is Design of Experiment (DOE).  An experiment is a method where a series 
of steps or a procedure carried out under certain predefined conditions for establishing a 
standard conclusion can be defined. In engineering, often we are faced with a situation 
where an analysis of a process is to be carried out. Such analysis is done by studying 
which input variables affects/impacts the final output. DOE refers to the choice of an 
adopted way of testing and analyzing a process. As most of this whole thesis is based on 
experimental testing and documenting the obtained results, it becomes imperative to 
know the importance of the way these studies were done and how we arrived at such an 
experimental setup. Designing a proper DOE can prove to be of great importance, as sig-
nificant cost savings can be achieved through proper usage of resources. This can usually 
be achieved by choosing best chronological order of performing these experimental steps 
or by choosing the most effective software for studying a given physical phenomenon 
[34, 35]. 
By performing DOE experiment many questions can be answered like for example [36]: 
 What are the most important factors in the process? 
 What could be the best possible settings in order to achieve the highest process 
performance? 
 What are the effects which interact the most during the process?

the micro injection molding process. The empirical and analytical methods have been 
used for this project. Due to complexity of the process and large amount of the factors

cavities will be also the subject of the following studies as a possible factor affecting on 
the quality. 
 
Before going to details the general understanding of cavity air flow behavior in μ-IM is 
introduced here. The evacuation of the air or gas from the micro cavity is one the most 
important factor which is posed to researchers. To prevent micro-parts from possible de-
fects, vacuum mold venting system has to be implemented in order to remove air pockets 
trapped in micro cavities. By removing air/ gas from the cavities such problems as burn 
marks and short shots can be avoided. In many cases burn marks are caused by trapped 
air in the cavities. In particular, when the air inside the cavity is compressed so high by 
the injected plastic that a diesel ignition occurs [38]. Short shots occur when the polymer 
melt cannot fill entire cavity because the trapped air inside the cavity resist the melt flow. 
It is caused by air/gas that failed to evacuate from the cavity. Designing of the venting 
system is complex system and has to be consider with respect to temperature and pressure 
in the cavities. Hence the flashing of a part is the result of filling of venting gap, the 
critical gap width has to be considered in order to prevent polymer from defects. Consid-
ering high pressure and temperatures in the cavity, in many cases it is challenge for the 
companies or producers to achieve proper replication of the product. One of the solution 
for improving the venting system could be applying the negative vacuum which can help 
with evacuation of the entrapped air/gas.

7 CONCLUSION 
Micro injection moulding will play most likely an important role in the near future with 
the request for mass production of micro parts. 
The research undertaken in this M.Sc. project has been designed with respect to under-
stand the process of micro-manufacturing and instrumentation used for validation of mi-
cro-products in micro injection moulding. Based on the results the influence of process 
parameters on dimensional variations of TPE micro-rings was carried out. The most rel-
evant results have showed following observations. 
. 
 Measurements by two different machines have showed that InfiniteFocus machine 
is more precise as compare to DeMeet machine. On the other hand InfiniteFocus 
is slow device when a big amount of samples have to be measured. Choosing the 
MountainsMap software for analysing the diameters of micro-rings has showed 
the fastest and best way of measuring due to circular interpolation which this soft-
ware offers. On the other hand, measurement of thickness has been provided by 
SPIP software due to fact that MountainsMap do not offer “precise” tool which 
could be used for measuring of this measurand. 
 
 From the visual inspection of inner diameter was conclude that in the cavity 2 and 
cavity 3 are misalignments in all 36 batches. In all four cavities were found that 
by combination of 40 ˚C of mold temperature and 210 ˚C of melt temperature the 
mass of the protrusion is quiet high. The best quality and size of the inner diameter 
was found by combination of 30 ˚C of mold temperature, 225 ˚C of melt temper-
ature and 90 mm/s of injection speed. 
 
 The process optimization used for finding the optimal process parameters have 
showed in DOE_1 that different height of the cavity and different pin-core diam-
eter may affect the air entrapment. The second observation was that a higher MoT 
has influence on expansion of material. Second experiment has showed that pol-
ymer flow in the cavity 2 behaves different compared with other cavities by using 
50 mm/s of injection speed. This different behavior can be caused by defect in the 
pin-core. The last part of the process optimization has showed big deviation within 
cavities. These deviations indicate that tool is not probably precise. The main rea-
son for this is that influence of the pin-core in cavity 2 and 3. This affects the 
dimensional variation due to defects which can be caused by misalignment of the 
pin core, bended pin-core, different pressure in the cavities, ejection side plate is 
not parallel with moving middle plate or injection plate. General conclusion for 
==PAGE:60==
 
54 
 
DOE_2 and DOE_3 can be that by increasing inner diameter, outer diameter will 
decrease and vice versa. 
 
 The results from process reproducibility shows that thickness around the weld line 
is relatively small (0.880-0.900 mm) compare with specific value. The values ob-
tained from 25 batches have showed that outer diameter is not stable and obtained 
values are low with respect to specific limit. Difference between the highest meas-
ured value and lowest measured value is approximately 30 μm. On the contrary 
the inner diameter has showed good results. The values are centralized around the 
lower tolerance limit.

8 OUTLOOK 
 
The research presented in this thesis also provide a strong foundation for future work in 
the field of micro-injection moulding. The following points could be recommended 
where possible developments can be made.  
 
 Since investigation in the last chapter (process reproducibility) has showed big 
variation of using different software it should be reconsidered which software is 
more capable for such measurements by making more comparisons between them. 
A strongly-driven and focused research should be carried out on the software front 
for obtaining the best tool for such analysis.  
 One of the most challenging part of this work was to carry out measurements for 
a large sample data. The InfiniteFocus Microscope used delivered good results, 
but with a very slow speed. Faster computational algorithms to develop the final 
image of a given sample from the height variation could possibly solve this prob-
lem.  
 As has been found that the mold temperature is the key parameter influencing the 
properties of polymer, it becomes utmost important to control and maintain an 
accurate mold temperature.
