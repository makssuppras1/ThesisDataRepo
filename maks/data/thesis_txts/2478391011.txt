1      INTRODUCTION 
 
This project was undertaken for the purpose of acquiring knowledge and experience in 
the vast world of power electronics. As the future is going towards more reliable and 
green systems, which is the need of the world, also inspired us to try to make something 
inhouse more efficient for the vehicle market. In this report we will go through the de-
sign and building of SiC MOSFET based Inverter Drive. 
1.1 Background 
An inverter is a device used as power conversion module from DC to AC. It is used in 
several applications and with the world going towards electric systems rather than fossil 
fuel-based systems, these kinds of modules being AC to DC or DC to AC convertors have 
become very prevalent around us. Being in household as power backups or in the indus-
try as high power motor drives can be found readily around us.

1.2 Voltage Source Inverter Model 
 
In fig (1) we can see the basic model of an inverter with six MOSFET’s making 3 half 
bridge legs connected in parallel to a DC bus capacitor making it a voltage source in-
verter. 
The BLAC motors to be used as load, has a very low stator resistance but has high in-
ductance which is needed in voltage source inverters as it has high leakage inductance 
as well. This system is used with semiconductors that can handle reverse conduction 
due to back emf from the motor i.e with MOSFET’s or IGBT’s. 
The gate drives need to be isolated from the main power side of the circuit to maintain 
galvanic isolation.  
1.3 Modulation Techniques 
The output sine wave Vo = Van needs to follow a predefined waveform while switching 
the MOSFET’s at a high frequency. This is also called carrier based PWM modulation as 
a predefined sine waveform is compared with a triangular waveform at much higher 
frequency and at the point the waveforms are equal or more than the other an output 
PWM is achieved.

1.6 Control System 
 
The purpose of building a high frequency VSI is to control the speed and torque of a 
rotating machine. So, variable frequency drives are used to control these systems with 
mainly two types of control loops, 
 
1. Scalar Control 
In this control strategy two components are varied together i.e. frequency and 
voltage to control the output torque. These systems are controlled my varying 
any operating parameters of the machine. 
It’s easy and cheap to design these systems but at the cost of efficiency and ac-
curacy. V/Hz open loop control can also enable the operation of multiple ma-
chines in parallel. 
 
2. Vector control or FOC (Field oriented control) 
This is a very sophisticated way of controlling an AC drive than a standard V/Hz 
control. Vector control is done by maintaining the instantaneous magnetizing 
currents space vector constant, to maintain the rotor flux linkage constant. The 
voltages and currents are constantly observed through ADC are converted to its 
orthogonal components. The real and imaginary components of the space vec-
tors are separated, resulting in separate direct and quadrature axis equivalent 
circuits but with equal parameters in the two axes. The torque is controlled by 
the quadrature current iq and the direct current component is generally used for 
excitation circuit in Induction motor nut as we are using PMSM motor the mag-
nets are used to create the flux which is considered constant. So, id can be used 
to induce field weakening control.

2.2.1 Why choosing SiC MOSFET’s over IGBT’s? 
 
Over the years SiC MOSFET’s have become very prominent in high power electronics 
due to its capability of handling high voltages and in some cases even higher than Si IGBT 
devices. This is due to higher band gap energy and higher critical breakdown voltages in 
SiC MOSFET’s. (7) 
Higher switching frequencies can be achieved in the new SiC MOSFET’s with lower 
switching losses and with higher Thermal conductivity than any IGBT in the market, 
which reduces the size and cost of the cooling system required. 
Main Points for Selection: -
 
Availability -
 
Capability to handle wide range of voltage and currents as in Table (1) -
 
Price (as SiC MOSFET’s are quite expensive) 
 
After going through quite a lot of datasheets and topologies available in the market the 
most suited for our requirement was CAS120M12BM2 half bridge module. 
 
Features (8) 
• Ultra Low conduction and switching Loss 
• High-Frequency Operation 
• Zero Reverse Recovery Current from Diode 
• Zero Turn-off Tail Current from MOSFET 
• Normally-off, Fail-safe Device Operation 
• Ease of Paralleling 
• Copper Baseplate and Aluminium Nitride Insulator

4.2.8 Gate Resistor Selection 
 
Choosing a correct gate resistor is a game between having more of switching losses or 
voltage ripple levels due to fast turn on. So, a balance must be made between the two. 
If the gate resistor is low the dv/dt of the MOSFET will be high that means the time it 
takes to go from -5V to 20 V will be very fast. It will also be the case for Vds. This can 
Figure 50 DESAT circuit with Rext 
==PAGE:58==
Inverter Layout 
56 
cause high current ripples in the output and as well as can be seen on the gate driver 
side which can put the MOSFET into heavy stress. This happens due to parasitic induct-
ances in the gate drive loop. 
If the gate resistor is increased the MOSFET starts to go into Switching losses as the gate 
takes more time to fully turn on but can reduce the gate source ringing significantly. 
Adding some deadtime to the High side and low side MOSFET’s can also help in reducing 
this current draw. 
During initial testing the gate resistor was set at 4.7ohm to see the switching character-
istics of the MOSFET but as the voltage on the MOSFET was increased so did the voltage 
ringing on the gate signals which would heat up the system. 
The gate resistance then was taken to 6.5 ohms to reduce a bit of the voltage spikes.

6.4 Gate Signal Loop Inductance 
 
During turn on the voltage at the gates have a very high dv/dt and due to the gate signal 
path making an RLC circuit is susceptible to ringing at its resonance frequency. The 
higher the inductance in the loop higher will be the ripple. By putting a resister in series 
dampens the oscillation. 
Due to this the MOSFET can go into more conduction losses. To not getting into this 
problem few measures can be taken: -
 
Increase the gate resistance if there is ringing, damping the signal -
 
Reduce the length of wire till the gate connection -
 
Increasing trace width

When the supply turns on the gate drive board need to start before the MOSFET 
gets voltage on its Vds. Due to parasitic capacitances the gate is susceptible to 
false turn on and if at the time of turn on MOSFET gets voltage before the gate
