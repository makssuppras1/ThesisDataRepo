Abstract
During the winter season of 2022-2023 in Denmark and in many other European coun-
tries, it was decided to reduce the set point in public buildings to adapt to the energy crisis.
In Denmark, the temperature set point was reduced to 19◦C, 2-3 ◦C lower than the tradi-
tional set point. This report presents the study investigating the consequences of lowering
the temperature set point on thermal comfort (and other responses) of occupants of an office
building. It was examined whether thermal comfort would be negatively affected by the tem-
perature reduction or not. Two buildings were chosen: one public office building where the
set point of temperature was reduced to 19 ◦C (called K-19) and one private office building
where the temperature set point was retained at 22 ◦C (called K-22). Measurements were
performed and included monitoring of conditions in offices by deploying calibrated sensors
with loggers collecting data on air temperature, relative humidity, and carbon dioxide and
collecting responses from occupants using an online survey. The measurements started on
06/02/2023 and ended on 28/03/2023. During the 7 weeks conditions were monitored con-
tinuously and a survey was presented once a week. The results showed that temperatures
were similar in both buildings (generally 22 ◦C and above).
Yet occupants in the K-19
building reported that they felt colder and they would rather have warmer temperatures
even though they wore more clothing. Several hypotheses were put out to explain these
unexpected results. Among others, it was re-checked whether the sensors had systematic
imprecision and whether the results could be explained by differences in biological sex, age,
and variabilities between the different locations in the buildings, as well as lower morning
and weekend temperatures. All were rejected. Thermal responses of people in the K-19 and
K-22 buildings were estimated using the thermal comfort model by Fanger using collected
clothing insulation and metabolic rates and measured temperatures.
The results showed that occupants in K-19 should have had a similar thermal response
as occupants in the K-22 building, even slightly warmer. Further, it was estimated what
air temperature should have been in the K-19 building using thermal sensation votes of
occupants and they were below 19 ◦C. Also, the mean radiant temperature was estimated
and it should have been below 15 ◦C, which is unlikely considering that the building meets
energy Class C and was energy retrofitted. The energy consumption of both buildings was
very similar and both buildings were expected to save around 10.000 m3 of gas compared
with the heating season of 2021-2022. Heating degree days were also investigated and the
results indicated that February and March 2022-2023 were slightly colder compared to 2021-
2022, yet it is expected that energy consumption would be lower compared to 2021-2022.
Though it should also be mentioned that for 2021-2022 we have the actual measured en-
ergy use but for 2022-2023 it is the expected energy use. This could probably approve the
hypothesis that the temperature was reduced in both buildings by around 1 ◦C. Also, no
differences in outdoor temperatures in different building locations were observed; outdoor
temperatures were generally above 0 ◦C on average. In conclusion, we assumed that the
observed differences were due to psychological effect (so-called nocebo effect): occupants in
the K-19 building were informed the temperatures would be reduced and they developed a
psychological response (fear of cold) which could affect their physiology. Similar effects were

seen for asthmatics who received placebo treatment but were told that they would reduce
their symptoms. No physiological measurements were actually made to confirm the nocebo
effect though it was observed that the K-19 building occupants reported cold hands and feet
which is a marker of physiological response to cold. More studies are needed to confirm the
observed results.

Resume
I vintersæsonen 2022-2023 i Danmark og i mange andre europæiske lande blev det
besluttet at man skulle reducere setpunktet i offentlige bygninger for at tilpasse sig en-
ergikrisen. I Danmark blev temperatursetpunktet reduceret til 19 ◦C, 2-3 ◦C lavere end
det traditionelle sætpunkt. Denne rapport præsenterer undersøgelsen i konsekvenserne af
at sænke temperaturindstillingspunktet påtermisk komfort (og andre reaktioner) hos be-
boere i en kontorbygning. Det blev undersøgt, om den termiske komfort ville blive negativt
påvirket af temperaturskningen eller ej. To bygninger blev valgt: en offentlig kontorbygn-
ing, hvor indstillingspunktet for temperatur blev reduceret til 19 ◦C (kaldet K-19) og en
privat kontorbygning, hvor temperaturindstillingspunktet blev bibeholdt på22 ◦C (kaldet
K-22). Målinger blev udført og omfattede overvågning af forholdene påkontorer ved hjælp
af indsatte kalibrerede sensorer med Hobo loggere, der indsamler data om lufttemperatur,
relativ fugtighed og kuldioxid og indsamler svar fra beboere ved hjælp af online-undersøgelse.
Målingerne startede den 06/02/2023 og sluttede den 28/03/2023. I løbet af de 7 uger blev
forholdene overvåget kontinuerligt, og en undersøgelse præsenteret en gang om ugen. Re-
sultaterne viste, at temperaturerne var ens i begge bygninger (generelt 22 ◦C og derover).
Alligevel rapporterede beboerne i K-19-bygningen, at de følte sig koldere, og at de hellere
ville have varmere temperaturer, selvom de havde mere tøj på. Adskillige hypoteser blev
fremsat for at forklare disse uventede resultater.
Det blev blandt andet efterprøvet, om
sensorerne havde systematisk unøjagtighed, om resultaterne kunne forklares med forskelle i
køn, alder og variationer mellem de forskellige placeringer i bygningerne samt lavere morgen-
og weekendtemperaturer. Alle blev afvist. Termiske reaktioner fra mennesker i K-19 og
K-22 bygningerne blev estimeret ved hjælp af termisk komfortmodel af Fanger ved hjælp af
indsamlet Beklædningsniveau og stofskiftehastigheder og målte temperaturer. Resultaterne
viste, at beboerne i K-19 burde have haft en lignende termisk respons som beboerne i K-22
bygningen, endda lidt varmere. Yderligere blev det estimeret, hvilken lufttemperatur der
skulle have været i K-19-bygningen ved hjælp af termiske sensationsstemmer fra beboerne,
og de var under 19 ◦C. Den gennemsnitlige strålingstemperatur blev ogsåestimeret, og den
skulle have været under 15 ◦C, hvilket er usandsynligt i betragtning af, at bygningen opfylder
energiklasse C og blev energieftermonteret. Begge bygningers energiforbrug var meget ens, og
begge bygninger forventedes at spare omkring 10.000 m3 gas sammenlignet med fyringssæ-
sonen 2021-2022. Varmegraddage blev ogsåundersøgt, og resultaterne indikerede, at februar
og marts 2022-2023 var lidt koldere sammenlignet med 2021-2022, men det forventes, at
energiforbruget vil være lavere sammenlignet med 2021- 2022. Det skal ogsånævnes, at vi
for 2021-2022 har det faktiske målte energiforbrug, men for 2022-2023 har vi det forventede
energiforbrug. Der blev heller ikke observeret forskelle i udendørs temperaturer påforskellige
bygningssteder; udendørstemperaturer var generelt over 0 ◦C i gennemsnit. Som konklu-
sion antog vi, at de observerede forskelle skyldtes psykologisk effekt (såkaldt nocebo-effekt):
Beboerne i K-19-bygningen blev informeret om, at temperaturerne ville blive reduceret, og
de udviklede psykologisk respons (frygt for kulde), som kunne påvirke deres fysiologi. Lig-
nende virkninger blev set for astmatikere, der fik placebobehandling, men fik at vide, at
de ville reducere deres symptomer. Der blev faktisk ikke foretaget fysiologiske målinger for

Flere undersøgelser er nødvendige for at bekræfte de observerede resultater.

1
Introduction
1.1
Energy crisis
The energy crisis in Europe is a growing concern due to the continent’s reliance on
imported energy sources.
Europe is heavily dependent on energy imports from Russia,

Potential Impacts of the Energy Crisis
The potential impacts of the energy crisis in Europe in 2022 are numerous. The most
immediate impact would be higher energy costs for consumers. This could lead to a decrease
in consumer spending, which could hurt the continent’s economy. In addition, the energy
crisis could lead to an increase in air pollution, as more energy would need to be generated

Finally, the energy crisis could lead to an increase in energy insecurity. This could lead to a

associated [1], [2], [3].

1.4
Theory of thermal comfort
Hansen [10] defines thermal comfort as "a state in which there are no driving impulses to
correct the environment by the behavior" [11]. The American Society of Heating, Refriger-
ating and Air-Conditioning Engineers (ASHRAE) defines thermal comfort as "the condition
of the mind in which satisfaction is expressed with the thermal environment" [12]. Thermal
comfort can be affected by: mood, culture, and other individual personal differences, organi-
zational and social factors. Based on the definitions above, comfort is not a state condition,
it is a state of mind. The definition of thermal comfort remains open as to what it should
be understood as the state of mind or satisfaction. It emphasizes that comfort assessment
is a cognitive process that involves many factors, which are influenced by physiological,
physical, physiological, and other factors.[13]. Even with the same thermal environment,
thermal sensations are different among people. Looking at a group of people in a similar
space, subjected to the same environment and climate, belonging to the same culture, they
will issue very different opinions on thermal comfort.
And it is due to the combination
of different factors that can affect the perception of human beings [14]. "Satisfaction with
the thermal environment is a complex subjective response to several interacting and less
tangible variables" [15], [16]. In other words, it can be discussed that there is no absolute
standard for thermal comfort. According to Macpherson [13] six factors can affect thermal
sensation. Four physical variables: Air velocity, air temperature, mean radiant temperature,
and relative humidity and two personal variables: activity level and clothing level[15].

1.6
Human thermal physiology and thermoregulation
Parsons [17] explains that the temperature of the human body is an important indi-
cator of its condition,(heat or cold stress, comfort, ability to perform tasks). Humans are
homeotherms, which means they attempt to maintain their internal body temperature at
about 37 ◦C. Serious consequences may result from deviations from this value by greater
than a few degrees. Therefore the human’s body temperature will be greatly affected by
the temperature of solids or fluids surrounding it. These will influence the heat transfer to
and from the body. People constantly respond to their environment to maintain optimum
thermal conditions. Human thermoregulation is explained as there are mechanisms in the
human body that will detect conditions, interrupt their consequences and take action. An
action could be in the state of the body such as sweating or a change in the environment

1.7
Indoor environmental quality and work performance on health
Sick building syndrome (SBS) describes a situation in which the occupants of a building
experience acute health- or comfort-related effects. These symptoms seem to appear when
people spend time in the building, though no specific illness or cause can be identified. This
feeling of ill increases the sickness absences which results in a decrease in productivity or the
workers [18]. Poor indoor environmental quality (IEQ) is related to increases in sick building
syndrome, respiratory illnesses, loss of productivity, and sick leave. Studies show that the
cost of poor indoor environmental quality is higher than energy costs for space conditioning
and ventilation [11]. Some sample calculations have shown that improving the indoor air
environment is cost-benefit when productivity and health benefits result from an improved
indoor climate [19], [20].
1.8
Effects of cold environments on manual performance
There have been several studies about the effects of cold environments on manual per-
formance. In all those papers there are repeatable findings that performance is decreased.
The cold causes vasoconstriction and lowering of tissue temperatures which cause numbness
and a decrease in manual dexterity and strength. Fox (1967) did an extensive review and
it indicated that there is a clear relationship between hand skin temperature and manual
performance. Horvath Freedman‘s (1947) study, subjects were exposed to cold for 14 days,
and it showed that finger dexterity and hand strength were noticeably diminished, even after
a short exposure. The results of Meese et al. (1984) study were that finger strength and
speed and manual dexterity decreased as the temperature fell from 24 °C through 18°C,
12°C, 16°C [17].

2
Objective
During the winter season of 2022-2023 in Denmark and in many other European coun-
tries, it was decided to reduce the set point in public buildings to adapt to the energy crisis.
In Denmark, the temperature set point was reduced to 19 ◦C, 2-3 ◦C lower than the tradi-
tional set point. This report presents the study investigating the consequences of lowering
the temperature set point on thermal comfort (and other responses) of occupants of an office
building. It was examined whether thermal comfort would be negatively affected by the
temperature reduction or not.

0.05. The effect size was also calculated for numerical data. Effect size is a way of quantifying
the difference between the two data sets and it is an indicator of whether the difference is of
practical importance [27]. Small effect size indicates that the difference is negligible, while

5
Discussion
The objective of the study was to examine whether there were any negative consequences
for office employees and their thermal comfort in public office buildings. As the temperature
results show, the two buildings almost have the same air temperatures, with a maximum
half-a-degree difference. Other results such as satisfaction, whether they feel cold or not,
symptoms indicate that people in K-19 feel a lot colder compared to people in K-19, who are
unsatisfied with the temperature, and would rather have a warmer temperature and they
experience having a runny nose, cold hands, and feet or both more often. In order to find
the cause of these differences, some more analysis had to be done. 5 main hypotheses were
formed and each needed to be investigated.
• Differences due to the sensor imprecision
• Differences between population, male or female, sex, zones, etc.
• Differences due to lower morning, night, and weekend temperatures
• Energy consumption, was the temperature actually reduced?
• Physiological effect (CBE thermal comfort tool, The nocebo effect)

5.6.2
The nocebo effect
"Placebo and nocebo effects are the effects of patients’ positive and negative expecta-
tions, respectively, concerning their state of health" [29]. The nocebo effect is a phenomenon
wherein negative expectations or beliefs about a treatment or intervention can lead to ad-
verse outcomes or increased symptoms, even if the treatment itself is inert or harmless. The
nocebo effect is a phenomenon wherein negative expectations or beliefs about a treatment or
intervention can lead to adverse outcomes or increased symptoms, even if the treatment itself
is inert or harmless. Similar to its counterpart, the placebo effect, which demonstrates the
positive impact of positive expectations, the nocebo effect highlights the power of negative
beliefs on health outcomes. When individuals anticipate negative side effects or perceive
treatment as ineffective, their mindset can activate physiological and psychological mecha-
nisms that amplify symptoms or even induce new ones. This can occur regardless of whether
the treatment is actually causing any harm. The nocebo effect underscores the intricate re-
lationship between the mind and body, highlighting the influence of psychological factors on
our physical well-being [29], [30]. Dalton [31] did a study about asthma and odors: The role
of risk perception in asthma exacerbation [31]. Study findings suggest that expectations trig-
gered by perceiving a potentially harmful odor can influence airway physiology, potentially
exacerbating asthma symptoms.
Different aspects were investigated to find the reason behind the different responses
despite the same temperature in the two buildings. The physiological responses were not
measured for this study but the questionnaire included questions such as having cold hands
or feet, having a runny nose, etc. People in K-19 had more clothing on, yet they reported
feeling colder and on top of that they showed symptoms such as having a runny nose, cold

==PAGE:59==

hands, feet, or both.
In conclusion, we assumed that the observed differences were due to psychological effect
(nocebo effect): occupants in the K-19 building were informed the temperatures would be
reduced and they developed a psychological response (fear of cold) which could affect their
physiology. Similar effects were seen for asthmatics who received placebo treatment but
were told that they would reduce their symptoms [31]. No physiological measurements were
actually made to confirm the nocebo effect though it was observed that the K-19 building
occupants reported cold hands and feet which is a marker of physiological response to cold.
More studies are needed to confirm the observed results.

Limitations
The present study, like any research endeavor, is not without its limitations. In this
section, we aim to identify and discuss the factors that may have influenced our findings and
the boundaries within which our conclusions can be interpreted.
• It was not possible to start the study during cold months (December, January, and
February), since the thesis started in February and therefore the outdoor temperature
was moderate and not cold.
• It was not possible to measure the physiological responses of the employees and plus
the employees had the whole week to answer the questionnaire and that caused they
could have answered on different days which could affect the responses. It should also
be mentioned that not the same people answered the questionnaire every time, which
is another limitation.
• The temperature in K-19 was not actually 19 ◦, but around 22-23 ◦C. Because of that,
it was not possible to see how the 19 ◦C impacts employees and what consequences it
actually has.
• It should also be mentioned that this study only had one building with a temperature
of 19 ◦C and no control group with a building with actual 19 ◦C, where people were
not told that the temperature is reduced to 19 ◦C.
• Another limitation is that we do not have the actual energy consumption but the
expected one.

7
Conclusions
The objective of the study was to examine whether the thermal comfort of occupants in
public office buildings would be negatively affected when the set point of temperature was
reduced to 19 ◦C; a similar office building where the set point was not reduced was selected as
a control. It was observed that there were no differences in temperatures in the two buildings
but there were differences in thermal responses. Therefore some investigations were done in
order to find out the reason behind these unexpected results. Several hypotheses were formed
and it was found that there were no differences in thermal responses due to population, age,
sex, or zone differences, nor due to sensor imprecision. Based on all analyses, the most likely
hypothesis is the nocebo effect. People in the K-19 building were told that the set point of
temperature would be reduced to 19 ◦C; even though it was reduced the actual temperature
remained at around 22◦C or even higher. Yet they reported feeling cold, wanting warmer
temperatures, and reporting some physiological physical symptoms such as having a runny
nose, having cold hands, cold feet, or both cold hands and feet.

ENERGIMÆRKET
FORMÅLET MED ENERGIMÆRKNINGEN
Energimærkning af bygninger har to formål:
1.
Mærkningen synliggør bygningens energiforbrug og er derfor en form for varedeklaration, når
en bygning eller lejlighed sælges eller udlejes.
2.
Mærkningen giver et overblik over de energimæssige forbedringer, som er rentable at
gennemføre – hvad de går ud på, hvad de koster at gennemføre, hvor meget energi og CO₂ man
sparer, og hvor stor besparelse der kan opnås på el- og varmeregninger.
Mærkningen udføres af en energikonsulent, som måler bygningen op og undersøger kvaliteten af
isolering, vinduer og døre, varmeinstallation m.v. På det grundlag beregnes bygningens energiforbrug
under standardbetingelser for vejr, familiestørrelse, driftstider, forbrugsvaner m.v.
Det beregnede forbrug er en ret præcis indikator for bygningens energimæssige kvalitet – i modsætning
til det faktiske forbrug, som naturligvis er stærkt afhængigt både af vejret og af de vaner, som
bygningens brugere har. Nogle sparer på varmen, mens andre fyrer for åbne vinduer eller har huset fuldt
af teenagere, som bruger store mængder varmt vand. Mærket fortæller altså om bygningens kvalitet –
ikke om måden den bruges på, eller om vinteren var kold eller mild.
BYGNINGERNES ENERGIMÆRKE
På energimærkningsskalaen vises
bygningernes nuværende energimærke.
Nye bygninger skal i dag som minimum leve
op til energikravene for A2015.
Hvis de rentable energibesparelsesforslag
gennemføres, vil bygningerne få
energimærke B
Hvis de energibesparelser, der kan
overvejes i forbindelse med en renovering
eller vedligeholdelse også gennemføres, vil
bygningerne få energimærke A2010
