  Abstract 
 
Pre-cooling radiant floor systems takes advantage of using less expensive electricity prices and 
cooler outside conditions during the night and early morning. Often this allows the use of cooling 
towers instead of chillers. The objectives of this thesis are to demonstrate the possibility of using 
pre-cooling floor slab as an energy efficient cooling system and finding an energy efficient 
cooling floor slab with appropriate properties. 
It is always difficult to figure out which control system is the best option which can be used for a 
HVAC system or which properties affect more on removing loads in a zone, by experiment. 
Using simulation software is the best tool to exploring the proper system and building physics. 
We used IDA ICE to simulate the operation of cooled radiant floor system. The model was 
calibrated using data collected at the LBNL FLEXLAB facility. More than 200 simulations in 
different climates, different slab properties and different control systems have been examined to 
compare and find the appropriate slab property and control strategy that yield the highest energy 
performance. The system uses a radiant cooled floor to remove the majority of the cooling load, 
although there is also a ventilation system, which applied to remove sensible and latent heat 
gains. This research shows cooling radiant floor system works better in warm and dry climates. 
Cooled slab is able to remove up to 68% of cooling loads in dry climate like Sacramento while 
removing up to 55% of cooling loads in moist climate like Orlando.  several water supply 
temperatures evaluated to observe the difference in the floor surface temperatures and floor 
surface heat gains. Appropriate night time pre-cooling strategy examined in different climates to 
keep the building cold during the day time while solar heat gain added to other internal heat 
gains. Operative temperature and floor surface temperature are the main factors that can illustrate 
how water supply temperatures affect heat transfer. The paper compares performance of the 
hydronic system under different control systems floor slab properties and climates. 
The nighttime pre-cooling control strategy has a significant effect on energy efficiency compared 
to the daytime control strategy. The results show that slab properties (for example; material and 
depth of the embedded pipe in the concrete) have a significant effect on energy efficiency and 
comfort, while water supply temperature in the hydronic system has less of an effect. 
==PAGE:4==
 
 
Radiant floor which applies night time Pre-Cooling control strategy combined with an 
appropriate air system is more energy efficient and has more peak power savings potential than 
controlling the radiant system during the day for example 67% peak power demand saving in dry 
climate like Sacramento and 56% saving in moist climate like Orlando. 
This research illustrates that office buildings using cooling radiant floor slab can meet the 
category 2 standard EN-15251 for occupant comfort with night time Pre-Cooling strategy.

1     Introduction 
 
 Literature review 
1.1
The use of radiant heating systems has several thousand years of history.  The early stage of 
radiant system was used for heating purpose, where hot air from   a duct which conveyed the 
heat from ovens to floor of a room and then fume exhausted out to the atmosphere. An example 
of such a system is Kang in China and Korea which consisted of a brick platform built across one 
side to the end of a room in houses in northern China or Manchuria. This brick platform had 
been warmed by a fire beneath, and used for sleeping. An example of radiant cooling system is 
snow-packed wall which were using in Middle East to remove heat gains in buildings or cool 
stores (Bean et al. 2010). Earlier stage of using radiant systems was mainly for residential 
buildings because of its comfort and free use of floor space without any problem from 
installations. Also, because of peak load of reduction and saving energy, radiant systems are 
being widely used in commercial and industrial buildings.  
Nowadays, radiant systems are getting more popular because they provide better thermal comfort 
and they are more energy efficient than convection systems. The radiant system is defined as a 
system where at least 50% of the heat transfer takes place by radiation. (Feng 2014) Since 
radiant systems can work with circulating water instead of air this system has been considered 
energy sufficient. (Stetiu 1999, Raftery et al. 2011)  
Also regarding comfort condition, radiant systems provide a uniform thermal environment. With 
using large surfaces for heat exchanging, the temperature of the cooling/heating water can be 
only a few degrees different from the room air temperature. This gives the possibility of using 
heat pump with very high coefficient of performance (COP) in the case of neglect different 
temperature in a room (Gayeski 2010), or a system that allows the use of alternative low-energy 
cooling/heating sources, for example, solar panels, evaporative processes, or ground heat 
exchangers (Babiak et al. 2007). In addition, thermally active building systems (TABS) that have 
pipes embedded in building’s slab can take advantage of the high thermal inertia. A significant 
reduction of peak loads can be achieved by pre-cooling/heating building structures during 
==PAGE:11==
2 
 
nighttime hours, when utility rates are lower (Rijksen et al. 2010). All the aforementioned 
features enable TABS to transcend many of the inherent limitations in current HVAC 
approaches. 
Radiant cooling follows the same principles as radiant heating, but in reverse.  Thermal energy is 
exchanged between the heat loads present in the space, and the cool surface.  To ensure air 
quality and removal of the moisture load in the room, radiant cooling panels need to be used in 
conjunction with a small ventilation system.  However, since radiant cooling systems do not use 
forced air flow to facilitate cooling, a uniform cooling gradient in the room is created.  With this 
reduction in draft, radiant cooling panels provide a comfortable environment for the occupants of 
the cooled space. 
From a thermal comfort perspective, the large heat exchange surfaces in radiant systems have the 
advantage of convective coupling to the room air and radiant coupling to the room surfaces and 
occupants. For most buildings, the interior surfaces of the exterior partitions, exposed on their 
outer surfaces to the weather, will have the most extreme temperatures in the enclosure. Radiant 
conditioning balances the radiant interaction between occupants and enclosure, both by offsetting 
the radiant effects of the exterior partitions and by interacting radiantly with these surfaces to 
bring them closer to the desired temperature. Because of the radiant coupling between the 
surfaces and occupants, the cold interior surface temperature of extensive glazed areas or other 
lightly insulated partitions can be offset by warm ceilings or floors. Residential occupants have 
long been familiar with the thermal comfort of radiant heating floor systems. Research has 
shown that radiant systems have the potential to provide similar if not better thermal comfort 
when compared to air systems (Imanari et al. 1999, Diaz and Cuevas 2011, Saelens et al.)

Thermal Activated Building System (TABS) 
1.3
Building elements such as walls, floors and ceilings can be thermally activated by circulating 
water, air or electric current through a circuit embedded in or attached to the structure.  
As far as this system extremely depends on radiant heat transfer, they literally are called radiant. 
There are different types of TABS which will be discussed further.  
In this project a TABS has been used to control the thermal environment of the building. TABS 
can be defined as water based system, used to heat up or cool down the building using embedded 
pipes incorporated in the thermal mass of the building. 
 
Advantages of Radiant Cooling Floor 
1.4
 As far as a radiant cooling floor works with high temperature cooling water, there is no 
need to use chiller and compressor cooling system which is basically using lot of 
electricity. Water cooling system can be a cooling tower which is using electricity just for 
the pump to circulate water in its circuit. 
 Operational costs are reduced for the mechanical chilling system since cooled floor 
operate at relatively high temperatures. 
 Chillers can operate at higher temperatures resulting in an increase in efficiency and 
reduction in energy costs. 
 Radiant slabs can be used as both heating and cooling panels reducing the amount of 
equipment and piping required compared to conventional heating and cooling system. 
 Radiant floor are silent and basically draft free since air flow volumes are reduced 
compared to conventional systems (typical radiantly cooled office building less air 
changes per hour compared conventional systems). 
==PAGE:13==
4 
 
 There is possibility to use radiant cooling panels separately into the false ceilings of older 
buildings as the plenum space requirement is minimal relative to fan coil units or VAV 
systems 
 Smaller plenums result in savings in building height (1' per floor!) compared to air 
conditioning systems. 
 Advantage of using cool floor surface for area with long day time and mostly sunny. 
The big advantage of using floor cooling is when there is direct sun radiation on the floor. In 
this case the cooling capacity of the floor significantly rises. This is also why floor cooling is 
increasingly used in spaces with large glass surfaces like airports (Simmonds et. al. 2000). 
 As the reaction time of the building and the activated slab is very long, it may be an 
advantage to control the water temperature according to the weather forecast of external 
temperature (Olesen 2000). 
 
 Disadvantages of Radiant Cooling Floor 
1.5
 • 
TABS can provide an acceptable thermal indoor environment but cannot provide the 
occupants with fresh air while removing pollutants, odors and CO2 from the indoor air. 
Therefore a mechanical ventilation system (with a smaller flow rate) is still needed to provide a 
fresh air. As a result, a well-engineered solution where the ventilation system is providing fresh 
air and the TABS is dealing with thermal indoor environment is required

Cooling system 
1.8
Compared to traditional air system, radiant systems can reduce energy use. 
Radiant systems can save around 30% of energy use and 27% on the peak loads which has more 
expensive electricity tariff than regular hours (Stetiu 1999). Another study showed that the 
combination of under floor air distribution (UFAD) and radiant systems reduced by 22–23% in 
total energy use during the peak cooling months (June to August) and a 31% reduction in peak 
hourly electricity demand when compared to the single UFAD system, these reductions are 21–
22% and 24% respectively. Study also illustrated that the system combination UFAD and radiant 
system compare to a single UFAD system also improves occupant thermal comfort (Raftery et 
al. 2011) 
       One reason for energy use reduction is attributed to conveying heat by circulating water 
compared to circulating air (Stetiu 1999, Raftery et al. 2011). Secondly, for having a successful 
heat transferring by a hydronic system, a bigger heat transfer surface is required, which is 
==PAGE:19==
10 
 
normally the ceiling or the floor.  The heat exchange coefficient between a cooled floor and the 
room is typically around 7 W/m2K, where 5.5 W/m2K is radiant heat transfer (Olesen 1977).  
One important factor for the cooling capacity of hydronic systems embedded in concrete slabs is 
the heat exchange coefficient between the slab surface and the space. Slab surface temperatures 
should be in the reasonable range based on comfort and consideration of the dew point in the 
space and heat transfer between the pipes and the surface (Olesen 2000). 
From the thermal comfort viewpoint, in radiant systems the large heat exchange surfaces have 
the advantage of convective coupling to the room air and radiant coupling to the room surfaces 
and occupants. Research has shown that radiant systems make better thermal comfort compared 
to air systems or in the some cases it might be equal but definitely not worse than air systems 
(Imanari et al. 1999).  
There is a disadvantage for radiant systems. As far as, radiant systems provide sensible 
cooling/heating and typically if need to have an air system to provide latent cooling/heating as 
well and the main problem in cooled surfaces is condensation which appears on the surface due 
to the moisture in the air in it does directly depend in dew point. Study shows, building equipped 
with a radiant cooling system can be operated in any US climate with low risk of condensation 
(Stetiu 1999). 
Generally, there are four types of water based radiant system and all four are pipes embedded 
systems which are insulated from main building structure like floor, walls and ceiling.(figure1.5) 
Slab floor which is included in a heavy weight construction which embedded pipes mounted in 
this slab and in one side facing the insulation and the side facing the room.  
Radiant ceiling panels are suspended under the ceiling. Slab walls are installed on the wall and 
include embedded pipes in a construction which is facing the room in one side and insulated 
from wall on the other side. 
Active thermal slabs are embedded pipes mounted in construction and both side facing 
conditioned spaces. (figure1.6)

2.1.1 Cooling Capacity 
Major parameter to a hydronic system with embedded pipes in concrete like the one used in this 
experiment, is heat exchange coefficient between slab surface and the room space. The heat 
exchange coefficient depends on the position of the surface and also to the temperature whether 
is cold or hot.  
For the test with solar radiation, dynamic heat gain through equipment and also solar radiation 
has been considered. In this experiment, since there were two different systems (hydronic and 
ventilation) working at the same time, it was required to calculate the cooling capacity for both 
and the sum of these two was considered as total cooling capacity which could show how much 
heat gain was removed by the cooling system.

5. Conclusion 
The aim of this study was assessing the heat gain in a cooled floor surface during the day with 
solar gain and without solar gain. Also this study was going to investigate heat gain in a cooling 
radiant panel with different water supply temperature. For optimizing of radiant cooling system 
all the experiment data simulated by IDA ICE.  
 
Since it has been mentioned in the discussion, there are some limitations in the IDA ICE, which 
affect directly on the results and cause not to have reasonable results when comparing to 
experiment. Results of window surface temperature simulation and experiment have almost the 
same behavior. Therefore the results which obtained from IDA ICE are valid. 
Results of operative temperature in the experiment illustrate that solar heat gain can be perfectly 
removed by combination of radiant cooling system and air system. However due to longer 
response time in simulation, for the hours we have solar radiation on the floor surface, the 
operative temperature cannot stay constant at the set point. 
Results in floor surface temperature illustrated that when the radiant system has changing in its 
own temperature, IDA ICE has problem to achieve a reasonable results due to lack of initial 
temperature setting. 
Radiant slab simulation by IDA ICE has also another limitation which goes back to the 
specification of setting. For example, there is no possibility to set the length of pipes in each 
loops and also there is no place to define a temperature variation.

6. Night time pre-Cooling strategy hydronic system 
6.1 Introduction 
Achieving the energy reduction in the building sector is not possible unless HVAC systems 
being more efficient. The Building sector consumed 41% of all primary energy produced in the 
United State (Feng et al. n.d.). Possibility of using Pre-Cooling night time for hydronic system as 
thermally activated building envelopes leads to peak power savings of radiant cooling systems 
which can be used in commercial buildings.  Employing a radiant cooling system instead of a 
traditional all-air system in office buildings also can save an average 30% of the energy 
consumption and 27% of the peak power demand (Stetiu 1999).  
 
6.2 Methodology 
A single office building was simulated at IDA ICE as conditioned by a floor slab radiant cooling 
system employing a variable air ventilation system (VAV). Almost 200 simulations conducted to 
improve the radiant floor system. Several slab properties and also several cooling slab control 
systems examined. For investigating different out door conditions, climate files from cities like 
Singapore, Las Vegas, San Francisco, Tehran, Orlando and Sacramento are applied. 
Based on the differences and similarities, Sacramento California and Orlando Florida have 
chosen. Because Sacramento has sunny and dry climate during summer while Orlando has humid 
climate and less sunny but both cities have a very hot climate during summer which has basically 
more challenging for investigating the cooling radiant floor system. Basically, cool climates has 
lower potential in saving energy in the peak load demanded times and using night time Pre-
Cooling control strategy than the hot climates and also possibility of peak load electricity

6.2.2.2 
Slab control system 
Several control systems have been investigated and the most accurate one which gave us the best 
result was the control system which is consists of a proportional controller with set point signal 
that combined with a time schedule signal. Conventional control methods, such as PI control, 
may not be able to maintain thermal comfort and lead to high energy consumption caused by 
constant switchover between heating and cooling mode in an effort to maintain a constant room 
temperature (Tian and Love 2009). The proportional controller was limited in the range of low 
and high temperature which has been defined in the set point and also received signal from the 
zone when the zone temperature was warmer than the set point. Compared to air systems, there 
are many challenges in the control of heavyweight slabs. Water pipes embedded in radiant 
systems are slow to respond to control signals due to large thermal inertia (Feng 2014). The 
response time depends on the depth of the tube and the heat capacity of the radiant slab, and 
usually the time constant can be up to couple hours (Babiak et al. 2007).

Conclusion  
The potential savings of the simulated radiant cooling system are lower in the moist climates 
than the hot and dry climates. 
==PAGE:52==
43 
 
Air system which combined with a hydronic system with Pre-Cooling night time control system 
use less energy than the air system works with day time controlled hydronic system.  
Pre-Cooling night time system in hot and dry climate like Sacramento use 0.7% more energy 
than day time system control which can still have much more saving in the electricity price while 
in hot and moist climate like Orlando Pre-Cooling night time use 0.08 less energy but lower 
thermal comfort. 
Totally, cooling radiant floor system with Pre-Cooling night time control system can be used in 
all warm and hot climates in the USA. 
This study illustrated that using cooling radiant floor system with night time cooling strategy 
combine with air system has potential of saving energy up to 67% in peak power demand in dry 
climate and 56% saving energy at moist climate.
