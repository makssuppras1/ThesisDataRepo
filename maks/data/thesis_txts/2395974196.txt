Abstract
This thesis explores the possibilities of concurrently evolving mul-
tiple design пђБelds with the aim of minimizing dynamic compliance
measures of composite structures, and is build upon two recent de-
velopments: A coating approach to obtain an optimized shell that is
пђБlled uniformly with a prescribed porous base material (shell-solid),
and an inпђБll approach which generates optimized, non-uniform inпђБll
within concurrently optimized single-material shell (shell-inпђБll).
The composite material models are developed in succession within
this thesis, with the пђБnal composite containing a shell of two distinct
materials, surrounding a non-uniform inпђБll. A novel formulation of
the material interpolation scheme of composite structures, based on
a RAMP/SIMP approach, is presented in the context of minimum
dynamic compliance topology optimization. A total of three design
пђБelds are utilized to evolve a dual shell-inпђБll design. One set deпђБnes
a shell of a pre-deпђБned thickness пђБlled with solid material denoting
the base, while the remaining two act as enrichment пђБelds on either
the shell or base region. These deпђБne the local material distribution
of the base and the distribution of the two materials in the shell.
The eпђАect of introducing the enrichment пђБeld that alters the stiпђА-
ness, density or damping of parts of the shell investigated. Nu-
merical results of optimizing with respect to dynamic compliance
measures obtained a reduction in the dynamic response for all ma-
terial models and have been presented for diпђАerent load cases and
special material ratios.

Introduction
Topology optimization has become an important and recognized design method
when the пђВexibility and potential of additive manufacturing is to be fully exploited.
In the last decade additive manufacturing has seen an increase in use and devel-
opment that has enabled the production of arbitrary shapes, internal holes and a
wide range of combinations of materials, where conventional manufacturing strug-
gles. Therefore additive manufacturing, with its immense design freedom, allows
topology optimization to utilize its capabilities and explore designs that are not
constrained by conventional manufacturing. With the use of topology optimization
near optimal designs, to a given load case, can be obtained and realized by the use
of additive manufacturing.
Excessive vibrations and noise can be a major concern when designing structures
subjected to time-harmonic loading. To combat this, an incorporation or addition
of damping materials in structures has been widely used in many engineering solu-
tions, to oпђАers a way of passively reducing or cancelling unwanted vibrations. This
includes applications for noise and vibration hazards in electric- and mechanical-
components to airplanes and cars. Here a relatively highly dampening component,
such as coating layers or simple rubber bands, can be added post production.
Applying a coating layer in the post-process on an optimized design, will not pre-
serve the structural characteristics. This can cause sub-optimal designs as the base
component was not optimized with the coating layer in mind. Therefore resources
on both materials and the design optimization process could be utilized better. This
investigation is motivated by coupling the post-process vibration reduction with the
structural base design, in order to further leverage on the capabilities of additive
manufacturing and in turn guarantee near optimal пђБnal products.
A recent paper investigated composites, with the aim of obtaining optimal shell-
solid designs, and proposed an extended interpolation scheme by using a density-
based topology optimization approach. Clausen et al.(2016)[1] proposed a method
of designing coated structures, consisting of a composition of a solid base and a solid
shell component with a prescribed thickness. Here a method to identify and apply
the shell component relative to the base component was presented and utilized to
concurrently develop an optimal design from a single design пђБeld.
A second development within optimization of composites in density-based topol-
ogy optimization, was enabled by Wu et al.(2017)[7], to generate bone-inspired
micro-structures as porous inпђБll. This was achieved by imposing an upper bound
as a local volume constraint, to regulate the local material distribution in a vari-
able density пђБeld. This resulted in a design пђБlled with porous inпђБll with crossing
sub-structures in the entire base region.
These two developments were further coupled in Wu et al.[6] to optimize com-
posite structures composed of inпђБll within a prescribed shell. Repetitive unit inпђБll
cells are commonly used as inпђБll patterns in additive manufacturing. But it was
shown that the optimized inпђБll performed much stiпђАer than repetitive unit cells.
The shell-inпђБll model utilized multiple пђБltering operations and added up to a total
of four smoothing operations, three projections and a gradient norm operation to
enable the interpolation of two design пђБelds, and yielded one of the most advanced
interpolation scheme published at the time of August 2017.

Introduction
The objectives of this thesis are summarized here, with the main objective being
to optimize the dynamic response in low frequency domains of composite structures
by utilizing density-based topology optimization. This statement has been divided
into three objectives:
вАҐ Implement and Extend the work of Clausen et al.[1] to allow optimization of
dynamic compliance of damped structures.
вАҐ Develop and present a methodology to apply and concurrently evolve a coating
composed of two materials, and optimize designs with locally damped coating
when subjected to a time-harmonic load.
вАҐ Extend the methodology presented by Wu et al.[6] to concurrently optimize
a dual material shell in conjunction with a non-uniform inпђБll occupying the
base region, with respect to dynamic compliance.
This thesis is inspired by the paper by Clausen et al. [1]. We extend the linear
elastic material interpolations in a density-based optimization approach, with a focus
on the dynamic response of damped coated structures. This includes a complete
remake of the work by A. Clausen et al.[1] and presents an interpolation scheme
covering the minimization of the dynamic compliance, when dissipation is added
to the system, in the form of complex stiпђАness damping. The initial foundation
that laid basis for the developed script throughout this thesis was the code "An
82 line topology optimization code based on PDE пђБltering" (Nov. 2010, corrected
03.04.2011) by E. Andreassen et al. [11].
A novel formulation of a density based interpolation scheme of a multiple material
shell in the shell-solid model is presented and concurrently optimized. Additionally
a shell-inпђБll approach is investigated when subjected to dynamic loading, by intro-
ducing an enrichment пђБeld as an integral part of the base region and incorporating
an upper bound constraint to control the local material distribution of this enrich-
ment пђБeld, and further adding a lower bound constraint on this variable пђБeld. Here
the aim is to simultaneously develop a non-uniform inпђБll and a multiple material
shell to obtain a combined dual shell-inпђБll design when subjected to time harmonic
loading.
To accomplish this an interpolation of 3 design пђБelds is utilized to deпђБne the
physical stiпђАness, mass density and loss factor. In addition a massive number of пђБlter
operations are utilized to develop the variable пђБelds. A total of 10 пђБlter operations
are utilized, to enable a dual material shell-inпђБll optimization. This includes the пђБve
consecutive пђБlter operations from the two-step пђБltering process[1].
The thesis has been divided into the main chapters: Chapter 2 presents the
theory used and has been divided into three sections describing the theory of each
of the three sub objectives described above. This involve the optimization problem,
пђБltering and interpolation schemes for all three material models starting with the
optimization of a shell-solid design and is sequentially extended to include dual shell-
solid and dual shell-inпђБll designs. Chapter 3 presents the results generated from the
three material models and is hereafter discussed in chapter 4. Finally chapter 5
concludes the thesis and chapter 6 suggests and discusses further work.

2.1
Optimization problem
The approach used in this work is a density-based topology optimization approach,
where material properties are deпђБned by interpolation of the variable density пђБeld
xe. In order to determine the best locations to place material an objective function
is minimized by utilizing sensitivities of the objective function in each element in
the design domain. The objective function is subject to a governing equation and a
number of constraints that may be imposed. A commonly used type of constraint
is a global volume constraint, which is useful for situations when more material is
always better, such as a standard compliance minimization problem subjected to
static loading. The design variables are updated iteratively. For the purpose of
this work the design update is performed using the Method of Moving Asymptotes
(MMA) by K. Svanberg [9]. The method does not perform a global search for minima
and therefore it is possible for the algorithm to get "stuck" in a local minima. The
solution obtained can therefore not be said to be "optimal" only optimized.

Discussion
This chapter contains a discussion of the implementation and modeling of the meth-
ods developed and utilized in this thesis. In addition the results obtained are com-
mented and discussed in sequence with the investigated material models.
The shell-solid model results compared to the results presented in the pa-
per by A. Clausen et al. [1], were similar, for the static MBB-beam case. Designs
with minimized local dynamic compliance obtained a decreased response at the op-
timization frequencies, But localized modes and gray-scale region in the void were
encountered due to the stiпђАness density ratio being badly scaled. The global compli-
ance formulation was tested for this model, however the results werenвАЩt satisfactory
and no further test of this formulation were presented.
For suпђГciently small base feature widths coating defects were encountered in
the optimized designs. The computational time was one of the reasons the robust
method [3] was not utilized, as additional solutions to the governing equation are
needed. J. Wu et al.[7] utilized a variant of the robust method to ensure a suпђГcient
feature width in the base region. This variant of the method reduces the added
computational time, as it only utilizes the eroded design. This variant is only valid
under the condition of static loading. If a robust method should be utilized this
would not be suпђГcient.
The ratio between the YoungвАЩs modulus and the physical density is important
when dynamics are considered. Initially the theoretical values of E0 = 1 and ѕБ0 = 1
were used. The YoungвАЩs modulus is later increased to achieve a more appropriate
ratios of 106, however the minimum value in the void regions remained in the ratio
1. This is suspected to be the cause of the gray-scale problem the global compliance
measure suпђАered from when subjected to dynamic loading. However this problem
also occurred for the static load case, where the ratio of minimum stiпђАness and mass
is unimportant. The problem occurred when the global compliance was minimized,
for all support and load cases. An MND correction scheme could potentially solve
this problem.
The dual shell-solid model revealed that the mesh dependency was dependant
on the ratio between the stiпђАness and mass. For ratios where the maxima occurred
for values below ѕИ = 1, the second coating didnвАЩt achieve the required material
properties, however the optimized designs showed mesh independency. When the
ratios were within the limit, where the maximum stiпђАness-density ratio was at ѕИ = 1
the mesh-dependency became more severe. A possible cause is that the gray-scale
allows the optimization more freedom to distribute the material, where the discrete
second material easier fell into diпђАerent local minima. Another reason could be the
large amount of non-linear пђБlters, however the dual shell-solid model, where the
stiпђАness-density ratio is below the found limit, contained the same amount of non-
linear пђБlters. The reason for the mesh dependency issue was in-conclusive, and a
further investigation should be conducted.

Discussion
The dual shell-inпђБll model displayed mesh-dependency issues, this was how-
ever expected. The inclusion of the inпђБll pattern contributes to the addition of many
new local minima that the optimization can converge towards.
The optimization did not adhere to the upper bound of the local volume when
subjected to dynamic loading, due to the generation of the inпђБll opposing the com-
pliance minimization objective. The issue was alleviated by scaling the sensitivities
of both the local volume constraints. This forces the optimization to update the
design пђБeld, v, and adhere to the upper bound constraint. The need to force the
constraint to generate the non-uniform inпђБll could potentially be a consequence of
substructures being even less beneпђБcial when dynamics are considered.
A method to minimize the the dynamic response of the dual shell-inпђБll model was
presented. Here the method included two cases of initializing the optimization with
the non-uniform inпђБll and a reset inпђБll. This was initially done to avoid local com-
pliance minima when initiated with the inпђБll, hence when reset the macro topology
of the structure could avoid local minima. Both methods showed optimized designs
with discrete solutions, though without resetting minor defects were encountered.
But when the inпђБll is reset to unit size values the stiпђАness and mass will increase
and with it its natural frequencies. Therefore when doing eigenfrequency optimiza-
tion with the aim of moving the natural frequencies away from a single or multiple
speciпђБed optimization frequencies should be performed with care.
The dual shell-inпђБll approach was rather unstable. After around 200 iterations,
it would begin to oscillate between two values of the objective. The oscillations were
followed by the MMA update reaching the maximum number of iterations. However
overall the compliance did decrease, even after these oscillations started. This also
contributed to that non of the optimized designs with the non-uniform inпђБll reached
the convergence criteria within the speciпђБed maximum iterations of 1200 iterations.
The substructural inпђБll does not converge towards a single minima throughout the
optimization process, which can be the cause of oscillation.
The enforcement of the non-uniform inпђБll with the isotropic Helmholtz PDE
пђБlter limits the generation of intersecting substructures. This causes sub-optimal
inпђБll patterns, that consists of parallel rods following the major principal stress
directions, hence making other intersections between rods in alternative directions
minimal.
To enable an improved sub-structuring within the inпђБll a anisotropic
пђБltering should be utilized[6].

Conclusion
The objective of a thesis has been to optimize composite structures when subjected
to dynamic loading by use of density-based topology optimization. A novel formu-
lation of three material models of composite structures has been developed within
this thesis. The foundation of these models were the interpolation scheme and пђБl-
tering technique developed by[1]. The shell-solid model the interpolation scheme
has been extended to include complex damping and allow an optimization of the
dynamic response.
Further appropriate boundaries were imposed in the form a
domain extension. Numerical studies showed mesh independency and controllable
coating thickness and optimized designs subjected to dynamic loading were found
with respect to a global and a local compliance measure.
The second model, developed within this thesis, presents a topology optimization
method of concurrently evolving a shell containing two materials with a solid base.
This dual shell-solid material model enabled optimizationвАЩs of structures contain-
ing two distinct materials within a shell of continuous prescribed thickness and was
achieved by interpolation of a пђБltered 0/1 variable пђБeld to enrich the coating. To con-
strain the material distribution within the shell a coating volume ratio was imposed.
Numerical results showed that the material distribution within the coating could be
controlled, however optimized designs showed no mesh independency. Furthermore
an invalid stiпђАness-density material ratio region, caused by the penalization in the
interpolation scheme, was identiпђБed. Minimization of the dynamic response was
demonstrated for diпђАerent load cases, special cases of material ratios and objectives.
In addition our results demonstrated that the concurrently optimized two material
coating performed better than a reversed coating design.
The material interpolation scheme was further extended to include non-uniform
inпђБll in the base region.
This enables a density-based topology optimization to
concurrently evolve a two material shell and an internal base region composed of
bone-like sub-structures, which is achieved by regulating the local element densities
in a smoothed пђБltered 0/1 variable пђБeld with an upper bound. To allow minimization
of the dynamic response a lower bound was utilized and justiпђБed through numerical
results.
This enabled a topology optimization to simultaneously optimize a two
material shell with non-uniform inпђБll subjected to dynamic loading.
By assessing the objective of the thesis it is concluded that the project goals have
been reached in the form of minimization of the dynamic response for shell-solid,
dual shell-solid and dual shell-inпђБll composite structures.

Further work
Topology optimization of the composite structures presented in this thesis utilizes
an already massive amount of пђБlters and a large interpolation schemes. A multitude
of directions and approaches can further be explored to improve and/or extend upon
the methodology presented. Some extensions to the methodology are summarized
in no particular order below:
вАҐ The issues of the non-uniform inпђБll being composed of parallel features can
be alleviated by utilizing an anisotropic smoothing.
The anisotropic PDE
пђБlter smooths elliptically, hence enabling a composition of proper crisscrossing
substructures. The appropriate smoothing пђБlter should be modiпђБed according
to the description of anisotropic Helmholtz PDE пђБltering in B. S. Lazarov et
al.[5].
вАҐ 2-D applications of the composite models presented are limited. By extending
a model into a 3-D formulation expands the пђБeld of possible application. By
straight-forward extending the пђБlters into a 3-D formulation the пђБlter technique
and interpolation scheme is expected be reused. However the computational
time will increase, hence a parallel implementation would be advised due to a
increasing number of elements.
вАҐ Length scale control by implementing a robust method as described in Wang
et al.[3] can alleviate and potentially completely avoid minimum base feature
defects and ensure manufacturability.
вАҐ A modiпђБcation of the interpolation scheme to allow two distinct material char-
acterizations within in the base region is a possibility. This can be done either
by an interpolation of the пђБeld ѕИ, or an interpolation of a new пђБltered 0/1
design пђБeld with similar interpolation scheme as the second coating.
вАҐ Alternative dissipation models are available, such as Rayleigh damping. The
complex stiпђАness method is a special case of Rayleigh damping and was chosen
due to its simplicity. Expanding the damping formulation to include mass
proportionality would be a trivial extension, but was not deemed necessary in
the scope of this thesis. Rayleigh damping could be a supplement in a more
in-depth investigation of local dampening within the shell.
вАҐ The PDE Helmholtz пђБlter enables a smoothing of a density пђБeld with a vary-
ing пђБlter radius.
Including the пђБlter radius R2 as a design variable would
enable a variable coating thickness and cojoined with the presented model en-

methodology of a пђБlter parameter are, to the knowledge of the authors, un-
