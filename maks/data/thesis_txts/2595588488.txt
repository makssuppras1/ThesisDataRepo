GRAVITY YUKON description:  
 
Gravity Yukon is the new range of liquid cooler developed by Enex for chilling water and 
water/glycols mixture.  
The new Yukon family chiller uses CO2 as refrigerant: it is a natural fluid with a zero environmental 
impact and it is neither flammable nor toxic, not affected by F-GAS rules.  
Gravity Yukon chillers are designed following a new concept: the “gravity” circulation of CO2 
refrigerant in the evaporator occurs thanks to the difference of density between the pure liquid 
incoming and the outgoing vapor from the evaporator, so the new design allows overfeeding of 
evaporators, assuring maximum efficiency and minor power consumption. 
Yukon frame is in welded steel painted RAL 7035, while all the internal pipes are in SS304 TIG 
welded and insulated.   
All the vessels (liquid, oils) are supplied with double insulation, Barrel vapour and rubber insulation. 
Dedicated system in order to avoid liquid back to the compressors. 
Dedicate PLC handle the Inverter and compressors for different loading needs. 
Operation range from -10 to +40°C, special version can be developed. 
Complete control panel on board ready for remote control 
The several options and variants permit to optimize the use in particular applications:  
• Version with inversion chiller/heat pump with COP optimized according to the required water 
temperature • Heat recovery hot temperature

Yukon Gravity (standard) – Operation principle 
 
LEGENDA  
- The refrigerant is compressed in high pressure (1)  
- The refrigerant is sent (2) to the gas cooler  
- The refrigerant is cooled with external air in the gas cooler (3)  
- The refrigerant flows to the high pressure valve (4) through which it reduces the pressure  
- The refrigerant is sent to the liquid receiver in low pressure, the liquid remains at the bottom and the vapor       
separates in the upper zone (5)  
- By gravity the liquid refrigerant circulates in the evaporator (6)  
- The steam is sucked by the compressors through the regenerative exchanger (7)
