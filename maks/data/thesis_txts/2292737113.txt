Abstract
In the last decades, because of its feasibility and reliability, organic Rankine cycle (ORC)
has received widespread attentions and researches. The expander is one of the main parts
that composed an ORC plant and a wrong choice of that can implicate a great lack of
eﬃciency. In this paper I provides a comparison between the scroll expander with another
an axial turbine ﬂow in order to ﬁnd which condition are more suitable for each machines.
The scroll machine has received a large success for micro scale ORC but is unknown the
behaviour of this kind of machine with greater power size. For this reason I propose a
New Model scroll able to deﬁne easily the optimal geometry of a scroll expander for a
wide range of mass ﬂow rate. After that the model proceeds with the evaluation of all
thermodynamic variables and calculates the performances. In this way I compare an axial
turbine and a scroll expander each times with a optimize geometry for a wide range power:
few hundreds watts to two hundreds kilowatts. After that I make some considerations
about the guidelines to help the choice of an ORC expanders between those two type of
machines.

Chapter 2
Introduction
The Organic Rankine cycle (ORC) system has been object of a large number of studies in
the last decade. It is a simply plant able to convert heat energy made by several diﬀerent
sources. The low number of part that composed it and the skill to answer a large number
of scenarios are the reasons for the lately success of that kind of plant. The expander,
is one of the most crucial part of an ORC plant, due its high cost and relatively low
eﬃciency. Expanders, in general, can be categorized into two types: one is turbo machine,
such as axial or radial turbine expanders; the other is the volumetric type, such as screw
expanders, scroll expanders, and reciprocal piston expanders.
2.0.1
Aim
The aim of this work consist to compare the performances of two diﬀerent expander
machines in order to ﬁnd a guide-line for the choice in several situations. I took in account
just one machine for type, an axial turbine and a scroll volumetric expander. Several studies
have been made in the last years upon the scroll machine. This volumetric machine is
primarily used like compressor in the refrigerant plants, but it also shown suitable working
like expander. The comparison with the axial turbine, the most common turbo-machine,
would evaluate the possibility of the scroll architecture against a solid alternative like a
axial turbine. The choice of an expander is crucial and take in account a huge amount of
factors, the aim of this work was not categorized the two type of machine but it would
discover through numerical simulation the advantages of each type of machine in diﬀerent
conditions.
2.0.2
Methodology
The ﬁrst part of the work was studied the ”Turax” model, a model to design an axial tur-
bine provided by the department of mechanic of Technical University of Denmark (DTU).
Then I made some changes at the model to allow the design of turbine with low mass
ﬂow rate. In the second part I proposed a new model of scroll expander based on the
Lemort’s semi-empirical model. This Newmodel scroll should be capable to design scroll
expanders with high mass ﬂow rate. Both the models were integrated in an algorithm for

Chapter 3
Expanders for ORC cycles
Low grade heat is abundantly available in renewable energy sources and also released
to the environment by many process. Such low-grade heat sources include solar-energy,
geothermal, ocean thermal, biomass combustion and heat rejected by fossil fuel-based
power plants, and industrial and agricultural process. Many strategies and technologies
have been adopted to recover this energy. Currently, ORC is widely applied because of its
non-complexity and easy maintenance [3]. ORC works on the same principle of a tradi-
tional Rankine cycle except the working ﬂuid used in ORC is of organic type. Therefore,
the basic components are the boiler, a work-producing expansion device, a condenser and
a pump. Due to low boiling temperature and pressure, refrigerants are found to be suit-
able in extracting low-grade heat from whatever energy sources. Most research interest
has been focused on reaching an optimal cycle performance, from a thermodynamic point
of view, hence often in this optimization the expander was take in account only as a plant
part with a ﬁx eﬃciency [4].
As a core component of the ORC system, expander has a vital role in the overall system
performance. To date, two main types of expanders are applied in ORC, turbo-expanders
and volumetric expanders. Turbine expanders are usually applied in relatively large-scale
systems with the output power above 50 kW. However, turbine expanders become a strug-
gle for small scale units, mainly because their rotational speed increases exponentially with
the decrease in turbine output power. Platell et al.[5] indicates that the volumetric ex-
panders are more suitable than the turbine expanders for small-scale units. Over the past
few decades, scroll machines, a type of volumetric machine, have been widely used in the
ﬁelds of air conditioning and refrigeration. With the development of manufacturing tech-
nologies and continuous innovation and improvements, the scroll machine technology has
also become more and more matured in the residential and commercial markets [6]. The
scroll expanders shown solid performances up to 10 kW but they were almost modiﬁed
from scroll compressors [7].
Here follows a brief summary description of both type of machine, a complete review of
these machines can be found et al. [22] for the scroll machine and et al. [8] for the axial
ﬂow turbine.

3.1
Axial ﬂow Turbine
Turbo-machineries are apparatus made up by several guide vanes that composed the stator,
each of them are in series with a rotating vane, all the rotating vanes compose the rotor.
The word “vane” indicates the physical space between two consecutive blades. The rotor
is coupled with a shaft in order to exchange mechanical power with the outside. The main
function of the stator vanes is to accelerate the ﬂuid to produce work. The work is collected
in the rotor vanes where the deviation of velocity vector of the ﬂuid produces a momentum
on the shaft. For this reason the study of the velocity diagram is fundamental to design a
stage turbine.
3.1.1
Velocity diagrams of the axial turbine stage
How shown at Figure 2.1, ﬂuids enter the stator with the absolute velocity c1 at angle α1
and accelerates to an absolute velocity c2 at the angle α2. From the velocity diagram the
rotor inlet relative velocity w2, at the angle β2, is found by subtracting, the blade speed
U from the absolute velocity c2. The relative ﬂow within the rotor accelerates to velocity
w3 at the angle β3 at rotor outlet; the corresponding absolute ﬂow (c3,α3) is obtained by
adding the blade speed U to the relative velocity w3.

Scroll Machine
The working principal of a scroll machine has been known since the beginning of the 20th
century when was invented by Creux at (1905). But it was not until the mid 1970’s that it

required. First of all the scroll technology was used to build compressors. The scroll com-
pressor’s inherent characteristic such as few moving parts, low level of noise and vibration,

The deﬁnition of the geometry is the same for both applications , the main diﬀerence was
swapping the inlet chamber with the exhaust.
Fig. 2.2 Scroll section.
The Figure 2.2 shows the principal part of a scroll. The two scroll wraps, called ﬁxed
scroll and orbiting scroll, have a particular shape that is an archimedean spiral. They are
identical but they are assembled symmetrically. These two scrolls are oﬀset of 180◦and are
in conjugacy. The orbiting scroll “rotating” around generates with the ﬁxed scroll several
contacted points. These points circumscribe diﬀerent chambers where is collected the ﬂuid.
The orbiting scroll, when work as a compressor, thrusts the ﬂuid toward the center, where
the chambers are always smaller and so the ﬂuid pressure increase. The Oldham-coupling
permits the moving scroll only to orbit and prevents the rotation on itself.

For the expansion process the ﬂuid is admitted at the center and trapped in a pocket
that is progressively expanded while travelling to the periphery, where the working ﬂuid
is ﬁnally discharged. Two types of losses can occur if the system speciﬁc volume ratio is
not equal to the expander nominal volume ratio Fig 2.3.
Fig. 2.3 Under and over expansion losses [42].
Under-expansion occurs when the internal volume ratio of the expander is lower than the
system speciﬁc volume ratio. In that case, the pressure in the expansion chambers at the
end of expansion process is higher than the pressure in the discharge line. Likewise over-
expansion occurs when the internal volume ratio imposed by the expander is higher than
the system speciﬁc volume ratio.
These two eﬀects can be considerably reduce the eﬃciency of the expansion process. To
optimize the performance of the expander the built-in volume ratio should match the
operating conditions which suggest the development of adapted design of such expanders
rather than retroﬁtting existing compressor.

The axial turbine model used for the comparison was provided by the Department of Me-
chanical Engineering of Danmarks Tekniske Universitet (DTU). The model, called “Tu-
rax”, starts from a one-dimensional analysis that contemplates only the mean radius of
the turbine with the aim to design of axial turbine stage. The inputs for the design process
included a set of boundary conditions imposed by the thermodynamic cycles and eight ge-
ometrical parameters, these are able to deﬁne the geometry of the turbine stage Figure 4.1.

Initially, a preliminary calculation of velocity triangles, thermodynamic properties and
blade geometry was based on a guessed stage eﬃciency. Afterwards, ﬁxed the blade an-
gles, velocity triangles and thermodynamic properties were update. In order to carried
out the losses of the stage, the model follows the guidelines suggest by Craing & Cox et
al.[10].The method proposed by Craig & Cox appears the most complete, coherent, and
realistic to study the eﬃciency of organic Rankine cycle turbine [9]. They claimed that
the method was valid both for gas and steam turbines and they stated that for most
of the tests the eﬃciency was predicted within a range of ±3%. They recommended to
determinate the losses at more than one radius, at least: root, mean and tip radius. For
this reason the model has to introduce some elements of 2-D analysis. In particular the
model implemented the “Free Vortex Theory” analysis as described in Saravanamuttoo
et al.[11]. The losses evaluation let to calculate the stage eﬃciency and at this point an
iterative cycle based on the stage eﬃciency started. The iterative loop stops when the
diﬀerence between the new obtained eﬃciency and the one calculated in the preceding
iterations is lower than a tolerance, it usually sets at 10−4.
After that is also possible to include an optimization algorithm in order to ﬁnd the most
suitable geometrical parameters for given inlet conditions, more details about the opti-
mization will proceed after that.
Although the model is able to design a multi-stage turbine I decided to focus my sim-
ulation only upon a single stage turbine. This kind of turbine are also called ”impulse”
because they often highly loaded, they produce a big expansion ratio in a single stage.
The impulse turbines are the simplest design, suitable for small scale applications and so
with the range of power taken in account in this work.

4.1
Partial admission
The dimension of turbo-machinery blades are primarily a function of the volume ﬂow
rates which pass through the machine. When a machine operates with dense ﬂuid, like
organic ﬂuid, and/or low power levels, small volume ﬂow rates take place and ﬂow passage
channels could become too small. Two ways are available to turbine designer to avoid this
phenomena: the adoption of very high speeds of revolution and/or the partial admission
of the machine. Turax model is also able to design a turbine with partial admission.
The partial admission lets to have greater dimensions of turbo-machinery blades but it
introduces other three main losses: blade pumping loss, sector loss, leakage loss. In the
model is possible to decide which relations to use to take in account these losses among
the work made by three diﬀerent authors. In Table 2.1 are summarized the parameters

A Semi-empirical model involve a limited number of physically meaningful parameters
that can easily be identiﬁed from performance measurements, while deterministic model
require an exact knowledge of the geometry of all the components. Semi-empirical models

tied at the ranges of validation. This is deﬁned by the experimental data used to verify
the model. Below is shown the concept of Lemort’s model, a more detailed description of
that model is provided et al.[20].

Before to proceed with the comparison of the two models is important to ﬁnd a way that
leads trough the choice of the optimal machine. In both cases I used a genetic algorithm
provided by Matlab. It allows to solve single and multi-objective optimization problems.
The present study only dealt with a single objective function. In particular, the eﬃciency
of the axial-ﬂow turbine deﬁned previously at Eq. 2.0.2 and of the Scroll machine Eq.4.2.32
represented the objective of the optimization process. The implementation of the genetic

• Deﬁnition of a set of boundary conditions. The input parameters given to the genetic

• Deﬁnition of a lower and upper boundary for each optimizing variable.
• Deﬁnition of the ﬁtness function. It is the mathematical function that describes the
variables to be optimized. This point required the deﬁnition of all the inputs for the
function, specifying the optimization parameters.
• Call of the genetic algorithm through the expression

Methodology
The comparison between such a diﬀerent kind of machine is not simple, in particular the
task become more diﬃcult considering an ORC plant, where usually the entire plant is
optimized taking in account all parts together. In order to make a fair comparison, the
methodology used in the present work is to take some inlet condition and ﬁnd the best

for given heat source for pure ﬂuid: [52], [53] and for zeotropic mixtures: [54], the mixtures
lately are shown a better thermodynamic eﬃciency for ORC plants. However I decided to
take in account only pure. The ﬂuids choice followed the work made by Larsen et al.[55],
because he covered a wide ﬂuid selection and temperature range Fig.6.1.

Chapter 8
Conclusion and Further Work
The computational model NewModel provided a tool for the prediction of the performance
and the geometry of a scroll machine without numerical limits of capacity or size, these
are characteristic of a semi-empirical model. Nevertheless the NewModel was easier than
a complete analytical model, thanks at the hypothesis to consider the scroll as an unique
chamber. However the value obtained were good and the trend of the variables were close
with the model seen in literatures.
At the axial turbine model “Turax” was included the Traupel’s correlation to allow the
evaluation of turbine with partial admission.
The comparison between a scroll expander and a axial turbine was made in order to
estimate the advantages of one technology against the other. The simulations covered a
range of mass ﬂow rate between 0.1[kg/s] and 3[kg/s], and a range power from few hundred
watt to two hundred kilowatt. The analysis accounted ﬁve diﬀerent organic ﬂuids as much
inlet temperatures.
The main conclusion of the comparison can be summarized as follows:
• For machine with a low capacity, as such 1 kg/sec the turbine needed a partial
admission to avoid blade height too small and that involved new losses. Therefore
in this ﬁeld of capacity the scroll model indicated a eﬃciency between 0.65 and 0.85
against a turbine eﬃciency of 0.3 caused by a partial admission of 20% with eﬃciency
peak of 0.7 with a partial admission equal to 80%. That happened for all kind of
ﬂuids.
• The dimensions of the scroll expanders were bigger than the axial ﬂow turbines, and
the gap increased with higher pressure ratio and mass ﬂow rate. Therefore the power
expressed to unit of volume for a turbine was an order of magnitude bigger. This
fact could be crucial for application where there is a lack of space.
• The performance of both machines with higher mass ﬂow rate were close and they
reached the eﬃciency of 0.95 for the scroll expanders working with the ﬂuids R134a
Ipentane, Toluene. The ﬂuids R245fa and Isobutane gave for both machines perfor-
mance slightly lower: respectively the eﬃciency reached the peak around 0.92 and
0.9 for the scroll, 0.84 and 0.8 for the axial turbine.
53
==PAGE:59==
8 – Conclusion and Further Work
• The scroll expander shown, for each analysis, the best performance for a value of
pressure ratio around 3.3. This is an important factor to take in account when
someone design an ORC plant with a scroll expander, independently from the ﬂuid
or the temperature.
• In order to produce electric energy with an alternator both type of expanders recom-
mended a gearbox for the power range investigated in that work. The turbine needed
a rotational speed equal to 30000 [rpm] to have good blade heights. The scroll ex-
hibited bad performance with partial loads, a gearbox should allow to balance the
volumetric ﬂow rate to keep the best inlet conditions.
In order to make more signiﬁcant the data from each models a validation of the scroll
model and the turbine with partial admission should be provided. The performances ob-
tained with the scroll machine were good and that incentives to continue the study of
the scroll as an expander also for power higher than few kilowatts. Moreover, although a
validation for big scroll expanders was tough because until now the biggest scroll built has
a power of 10 [kW] et al. [61].
Almost all scroll expanders found in literature were made with scroll compressor modiﬁed.
The new presence in the market of scroll expanders made by the constructors which are
experts in the scroll compressor ﬁeld means a reliability of this technology. However until
now the greatest scroll expander has been selling by Airsquared had a power of ten kilo-
watts [62]. The thrust on the development upon scroll expanders is due at the fact that
this kind of machine is really cheaper than a turbo-expanders and reach eﬃciency better
than all the other volumetric machines. These factors are really important with small
ORC plants. I think with a geometry design focused exclusively on the scroll expander is
possible ﬁnding a good solution also for a power of few tens kilowatts. Nevertheless I think
the range power of hundreds kilowatts is more suitable for radial or axial turbine.
At the end some advices are here provided in order to make the Newmodel able to evaluate
a more precise optimized geometry, and valid performances.
8.0.1
Further Work
• For the mechanical losses, the NewModel could be including also the frictions due
by the crank bearing and the dynamic behaviour of the machine [24].
• The NewModel provided a geometry of the scroll, a numerical simulation with
CFD of that is the only way to verify the deformation caused by the forces and the
temperature variation. It is necessary to optimized the thickness of the machine.
• The scroll machine works with high contact pressure between the two wraps, an
hertian contact stress analysis is needed to increase the reliability in order to design
bigger scroll.
• The validation of the model is necessary to trust of the result made with it but for
this more experimental results are needed.

• The compactness factor is the key to obtain scroll with high performances. It is pos-
sible rethink the fabrication process to consider more expensive processing technique
than milling but with lower cutting limits.
