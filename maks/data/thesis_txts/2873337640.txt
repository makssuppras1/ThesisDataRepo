Abstract 
Landﬁll aera*on is commonly applied during the a6ercare phase to accelerate waste 
stabiliza*on and reduce methane emissions. However, it is s*ll unclear how much 
methane can be released from previously aerated waste under diﬀerent 
environmental condi*ons. In this study, we examined the gas genera*on poten*al of 
low-organic-content waste that had undergone one year of aera*on. Three types of 
samples were tested in Landﬁll Simula*on Reactors: aerated, nitrogen-ﬂushed, and 
untreated. All samples were sealed and incubated at 30 °C and 70 °C for 51 days. 
CO₂, CH₄, and O₂ concentra*ons were measured regularly to assess stability. Results 
showed that CO₂ levels in the aerated group remained lower than those in the other 
two groups at both temperatures. CH₄ produc*on was nearly zero, sugges*ng that 
methanogenic ac*vity remained suppressed, and that available organic maTer had 
been largely consumed during aera*on. Overall, the results indicate that aera*on can 
eﬀec*vely reduce residual methane poten*al, especially under thermophilic 
condi*ons, and may support improved long-term landﬁll management.

2.3.1 Influence of Temperature and Moisture on Methane Generation 
CH₄ genera*on in landﬁlls is controlled by the ac*vity of methanogenic archaea, that

Methane produc*on in landﬁlls is highly temperature dependent. As shown in 
laboratory studies, methane genera*on rates can increase drama*cally, which up to 
100 *mes, when temperature rises from 20°C to 30–40°C [Christensen & Kjeldsen, 
1989]. This enhancement is aTribute to the ac*va*on of mesophilic methanogens,

sustained under moderate water ﬂux, thereby crea*ng favourable condi*ons for

inhibit methanogenic ac*vity even under anoxic condi*ons [Cossu et al., 2019].

Suﬃcient moisture and water movement in the landﬁll are vital to sustaining

maintain moisture levels and allows for even distribu*on of soluble compounds

Municipal solid waste (MSW) typically contains 30–60% moisture by weight, with

solids. However, for microbial processes to ini*ate and sustain, a baseline moisture

2.4.2 Implications for post-aeration waste  
The aera*on of landﬁlls fundamentally alters the physicochemical and biological
