Chapter 1
Abstract
Abstract
This thesis explores the integration of eye-tracking technology with real-time adaptive
text display to enhance the reading experience for individuals with disabilities. Tradi-
tional eye-tracking applications have focused primarily on data collection for post-hoc
analysis without utilizing the data dynamically. ”Reading the Reader” aims to fill this
gap by developing a system that adjusts text properties such as font size, spacing, and
color instantaneously based on live eye-tracking data, thus reducing cognitive load and
improving accessibility. The project includes a comprehensive evaluation of current tech-
nologies, selecting the optimal frontend, backend, and database solutions through rigorous
comparative analysis. Additionally, a proof of concept was implemented to demonstrate
the feasibility of real-time text adaptation. While the prototype shows promising results
in real-time text adjustment and user-centric interface design, limitations such as the
non-integration of a fully operational machine learning model and incomplete user feed-
back mechanisms highlight areas for future development. This work lays the groundwork
for future enhancements that could significantly advance the field of adaptive reading
technologies, providing a novel approach to integrating eye-tracking with real-time, user-
responsive text display systems.

Chapter 2
Introduction
The act of reading has fundamentally remained unchanged since the invention of the first
word press. Traditional reading materials are static, offering the same content in the same
format to all readers, regardless of their individual needs, abilities, or preferences. In to-
day’s digital age, where dynamic content and personalized user experiences are becoming
the norm, the static nature of traditional reading methods stands out as an area fit for
innovation. This thesis explores the development of a novel reading platform that utilizes
advanced machine learning techniques to offer a dynamic, adaptive reading experience
tailored to the unique preferences and requirements of individual users.
2.1
The Static Nature of Traditional Reading
Historically, reading materials have been designed and printed with a one-size-fits-all
approach. Whether it is a book, a newspaper, or an instructional manual, the content
remains fixed, with no consideration for the diverse needs of its readers. This static nature
poses particular challenges for individuals with reading impairments, such as dyslexia or
visual processing disorders, who might require different text formats or styles to read
effectively and comfortably.
Moreover, with the advancement of digital technology, expectations for personalized
digital interactions have significantly increased.
Users of digital platforms experience
highly tailored content recommendations, interactive and adaptive interfaces, and respon-
sive web design that caters to individual preferences. This shift in user expectations sets
the stage for rethinking how text-based content can be dynamically presented to enhance
comprehension, retention, and engagement.
2.2
Leveraging Machine Learning for Dynamic Adap-
tation
This project introduces a dynamic, adaptive reading system that leverages machine learn-
ing to revolutionize how text responds to individual readers in real-time. The core of this
system is a robust machine learning backend that processes and classifies features ex-
tracted from real-time, complex, and multimodal data collected through an eye tracker.
This technology allows the system to detect subtle cues in a reader’s interaction with text,
2
==PAGE:7==
such as eye movement patterns, reading speed, and focus areas, which are indicative of
their reading habits and difficulties.
The machine learning model acts as the intelligence behind the system, using the
collected data to dynamically adjust the text presentation based on the identified needs
of the reader. For instance, the system can modify font size, spacing, and color contrast
automatically to accommodate specific visual preferences or to alleviate common reading
strains.
2.3
The Role of Frontend Development
The creation of a frontend platform that interacts seamlessly with the machine learn-
ing backend is central to this project. My role in this initiative focuses on developing
this user-facing interface. The primary goal is to ensure that the interface is intuitive,
user-friendly, and capable of delivering a personalized reading experience that is both sup-
portive and effective. The frontend platform, developed using React, provides a responsive
and adaptive environment where text content is not only consumed but interacted with
and adapted in real-time.
This interface is not merely a passive display but an active participant in the reading
process.
It includes features that allow users to customize their reading environment
further and provides feedback to the backend, which continually refines its understanding
of the user’s preferences. The frontend thus plays a crucial role in making the benefits of
real-time adaptive machine learning tangible and accessible for readers.
2.4
Expected Outcomes and Future Considerations
The expected outcome of this project is the development of a practical and effective
adaptive text system that exemplifies the successful integration of machine learning with
frontend web development. This system aims to set a new standard for digital reading
platforms, offering a model that could be adapted for various types of textual content
across different platforms and devices.
Looking forward, the scalability and distribution of this system are paramount. The
project considers future enhancements that could extend the system’s capabilities and
make it accessible to a broader audience. The adaptability of the solution to various ed-
ucational and professional contexts, as well as its potential to support multiple languages
and reading levels, are key considerations. Ultimately, the goal is to establish a reliable,
user-friendly tool that can significantly enhance the reading experience for users around
the globe.
By bridging the gap between static text and dynamic user needs, this thesis aims
to contribute significantly to the field of educational technology and accessibility.
It
not only addresses a long-standing limitation in how text is presented but also offers a
forward-looking solution that could transform reading into a truly personalized, interactive
experience.

Chapter 3
Related works
This chapter examines existing technologies that integrate eye-tracking and machine learn-
ing to adapt digital content dynamically. It contrasts these systems with the novel ap-
proach of this thesis, which aims to enhance the reading experience by adapting text in
real-time based on user-specific data.
3.1
Eye tracking: Background
In this section, I will explore the existing literature behind what eye tracking is and how
it works, what it has been used for in the past, as well as provide background information
needed to completely grasp what this thesis is about, alongside some limitations and
restrictions.
3.1.1
Review
[Benjamin T. Carter, 2020] Eye tracking is a technique that is used to measure the point
of gaze. Gaze could be defined as a point where an examinee is looking at a certain point
in time. Some argue that gaze can also be defined as the motion of the eye relative to the
head. The method in itself is of utmost importance when it comes to understanding and
analyzing visual perception, cognitive engagement of an examinee, as well as interactions
of the examinee with the environment.
3.1.2
Historical context
In the past, eye tracking began with simple observational techniques. The researchers
were known to use direct observation in order to study visual patterns. Noticing the
importance of the practice, the technology used to study visual patterns evolved and
new mechanical methods were developed. At one point in time, there were devices that
physically contacted the eye, in order to track its movements. With further technological
advances, new methods were introduced. In the first place, photographic based systems
were introduced. Soon after, video based systems followed in the 20th century. Both
of these methods allowed for drastically more precise and less intrusive tracking of eye
movements. In order to achieve this, these mechanism incorporated reflective markers or
direct imaging in order to achieve more precise measurements.
4
==PAGE:9==
3.1.3
Role in research
These methods, and eye tracking technology in general has been adopted in a wide range
of scientific and non-scientific fields. These include, but are not limited to: marketing,
human-computer interaction, neuroscience mathematics, computer science and psychol-
ogy. The reason for such a wide adoption of eye tracking technology is because of the
direct insight it provides into the attentional and visual processes of individuals. By de-
termining where a person is gazing, researchers can deduct a great deal about a person’s
state of awareness, attention, and cognitive processes, at any given moment.
3.1.4
Technological components
Modern eye trackers are highly sophisticated devices. They use infrared light technology
in order to create reflections on the cornea. The reflection is then captured, and recorded
by cameras.
Through the use of this technology, precise measurement is achievable.
Modern eye trackers usually measure the point of gaze and the movement of the eye,
with minimal contact to the user. The data that is collected with the use of these eye
trackers are processed in order to extract some meaningful metrics. These usually include,
but are not limited to: fixation points, pupil dilation and saccadic paths. These will be
covered and explained at a later point of the paper. Through these metrics, it is possible
to synthesize critical information for analyzing visual attention and processing.
3.1.5
Impact
When it comes to human-computer interaction, eye tracking data helps improve the de-
sign of UI(User Interface) by providing real data on how certain users interact with the
technology in front of them. In the case of psychological and neuropsychological research,
eye tracking enables researchers to delve into the mind of examinees, and helps to map
out how visual information is processed and integrated with cognitive functions.
3.2
Basics of Eye Anatomy and Movements
==PAGE:10==
Figure 3.1: Eye anatomy
The human eye works incredibly similarly to a modern camera. The cornea acts as
a lens cover, focusing the light as it enters the eye. Behind the cornea, the lens is used
to adjust the focus, and the retina at the back of the eye works like a photographic film,
capturing the image. The retina is a layer of tissue that senses light and sends the images
received to the brain. Central to the retina is the fovea; ”The fovea captures a detailed
image of whatever the eyes are currently pointed at, which is crucial for high-resolution
vision”[Benjamin T. Carter, 2020] . This area is crucial for tasks requiring detailed vision,
such as reading or visual inspection.
3.2.1
Types of Eye Movements
Eye movements are a complex phenomena. They can be categorized into several types,
and each type serves different visual and cognitive functions.
• Fixations
– Description: Fixations occur when the eyes stop along points of interest to
examine and take in information. The eye will most likely be relatively still
during fixation in order to accurately process details
– Importance: During data processing, fixations indicate, and give insight into,
what a person is paying attention to and are usually critical for understanding
how individuals process visual information
• Saccades
==PAGE:11==
– Description: Saccades are usually characterized by rapid and ballistic move-
ments of the eyes that abruptly change the point of fixation. Saccades are also
classified as the quickest movements produced by the human body
– Importance: In research, saccades are used to study how the attention of the
user is shifted and how information is integrated across different fixations
• Smooth Pursuit Movements
– Description: movements that allow the eyes to smoothly follow moving ob-
jects
– Importance: Smooth pursuit is important for activities where one needs to
track moving objects, such as driving or watching moving targets in sports.
• Vergence Movements
– Description: Movements involving the simultaneous inward or outward turn-
ing of the eyes, enabling single binocular vision when looking at objects that
are either close, or far
– Importance: Vergence movements are essential when it comes to depth per-
ception and engaging with objects at varying distances.
• Pupil Dilation and Constriction
– Importance: Changes in pupil size can indicate arousal, cognitive load, or
changes in emotional state. Monitoring pupil size can provide insights into
user engagement and mental effort during various tasks
3.2.2
Metrics Used in Eye Tracking
Eye tracking not only involves monitoring where and how the eyes move but also uses
various metrics to analyze these movements:
• Gaze Path: It is defined as a sequence of points that the gaze visits over time,
useful in understanding reading patterns and analyzing how users might navigate
through interfaces
• Dwell Time: A measurement that indicates the total amount of time spent fixating
within a specific area of interest. This metric is often analyzed in usability studies
to determine which areas attract more or less attention and are therefore potentially
more or less engaging or informative
• Blink Rate: As the name states, the rate at which a user blinks, which can provide
indirect insights into fatigue and cognitive load. If the blink rate is increased, it
might suggest an increased cognitive load or fatigue
• Heatmaps: A visual representation of data where values are represented as colors.
When it comes to eye tracking, heatmaps often show where the users have looked
on a screen the most, or the least, indicating hot spots where visual attention is
concentrated
==PAGE:12==
3.2.3
Physiological Basis of Eye Movements
Movement of the eyes are controlled by a complex system in our nervous system. It in-
volves various brain regions, including the frontal eye fields, superior colliculus, and cere-
bellum. All those parts coordinate together to determine when, where, and how quickly
the eyes should move. In doing so, the brain continuously receives and processes visual
and proprioceptive feedback in order to adjust eye movements. This ensures precision
and adaptation to changing visual demands.
3.3
Eye Trackers
3.3.1
Overview of Eye Tracking Hardware
Eye tracking systems commonly consist of several hardware components that work to-
gether to measure and record where and how the eyes are moved by the user. There
is a number of different eye trackers, and understanding these components is crucial for
selecting the right eye tracker for specific research needs.
3.3.2
Key Hardware Components
• Infrared Illuminators
– Function: The illuminators emit an infrared light to highlight the eye of the
user. In the case of an eye tracker, it specifically highlights pupils and corneal
reflections, which are crucial for tracking
– Use: They are essential for creating distinguishable features on the eye, which
are captured by cameras in order to track the eye movements. This is extraor-
dinarily important in low-light conditions.
• Cameras

• Design Principles: In modern software development, great emphasis is put on cre-

scalability, maintainability and performance. A well designed system is supposed
to adhere to these principles, not only to meet the current requirements, but rather
accommodate future expansions and possible adaptations.
Modularity is a design principle that involves segmenting a system into separate
components, that are each responsible for a distinct aspect of functionality. With
the utilization of this approach, there is a focus on clarity, the reduction of com-
plexity, which is achieved by isolating functionality into manageable, discrete blocks.
For instance, in a system designed for reading enhancement using eye-tracking and
machine learning, modularity allows for distinct handling of eye-tracking data input,
processing of this data to derive meaningful insights, and the dynamic adjustment
of text display based on these insights. Each of these modules interface with others
through simple web socket communication, which encapsulates their functionalities
and shields their internal workings from other parts of the system. Utilizing this
22
==PAGE:27==
approach not only simplifies debugging and development, but furthermore enhances
the ability to test components individually, making sure that possible issues in one
module do not spread out and affect others.
Scalability refers to the capability of a certain system to handle a growing amount
of work, which is usually achieved by adding resources to the system. In the con-
text of this application, scalability has been a core consideration, especially given
the potential of great variability in the volume of data being processed, as user
engagement scales. Therefore, this system is designed to handle increased workload
without significant reconfiguration.In this system, scalability has been approached
by optimizing the internal management of stateful components, which maintain
continuous sessions and user-specific data across interactions. Given the stateful
nature of the system, careful attention has been paid to how data is stored and
retrieved in a manner that optimizes performance and minimizes the overhead on
each component.
Maintainability in essence, is about designing a system that facilitates updates,
upgrades and debugging with minimal operational costs. This specific attribute is
crucial for the long term success and viability of software. The system’s architecture
promotes maintainability through clear, concise, and well documented code bases
along with adherence to coding standards. These standards promote readability
and reuse. In the future, regular refactoring is to be expected, as to adapt to new
requirements, and avoid possible technical dept. This approach makes the system
easier to maintain by original developers. Furthermore, anyone who might need to
work on the system in the future can be sure that enhancements, updates and bug
fixes can be implemented swiftly and safely.
Performance considerations are commonly critical when designing software, espe-
cially in systems dealing with real-time data processing, as is also the case when
dealing with eye tracking data. The system is optimized for performance through
efficient algorithms and data structures. This ensures quick processing of input data
to yield outputs with minimal lag or latency. In an app such as this, it is crucial to
ensure quality performance in order to maintain user engagement and the system’s
responsiveness.

• Choice of Architecture Style: In determining the optimal architectural style

6.4.3
REST API
The REST (Representational State Transfer) API is a widely-used protocol for web
applications, characterized by its statelessness and use of standard HTTP methods
[Doglio, 2015]. In a REST API, each request from the client to the server is in-
dependent, with a new TCP connection established for each HTTP request. This
stateless nature simplifies server design and scalability, allowing both horizontal and
vertical scaling.
Some distinctive characteristics of a REST API in comparison to some other commu-
nication layer designs is the the stateless protocol. That means that each request is
independent, and does not hold on to any information. Communication is distinctive
with its uni-directional feature. It allows only the server, or the client to commu-
nicate at any given point in time. This adheres to the traditional request-response
model. Furthermore, HTTP requests include headers that contain metadata, which
also require a new TCP connection for each request.
This protocol’s simplicity and widespread adoption are significant benefits, making
it very accessible, and easy to implement. Its statelessness simplifies server design,
as well as enhances scalability, because it supports both horizontal and vertical
scaling.
On the other hand, there are some drawbacks to it as well. One such example is
the overhead of establishing a new TCP connection for each request, which can
introduce latency. Moreover, message transmission is slower in comparison to some
other communication layer solutions. Finally, the protocol is limited to standard
HTTP methods, such as: GET¡ POST¡ PUT and DELETE.
6.4.4
WebSocket API
The WebSocket API is designed for real-time, low-latency communication, making
it ideal for applications requiring instantaneous data exchange, such as chat ap-
plications, live notifications, and gaming.[Liu and Sun, 2012] Unlike REST, Web-
Socket establishes a single persistent TCP connection, allowing for continuous, bi-
directional communication between the client and server.
Web sockets are characterized by their stateful protocol, meaning they maintain
state across connections. Furthermore, they enable bi-directional communication,
allowing both the server and the client to send and receive messages simultaneously.
This protocol also follows a full-duplex mode, which means it enables concurrent
two-way communication,which is particularly suitable for real time applications.
The reason for this is because it is optimized for minimal latency.
WebSockets
achieve this by using a single persistent TCP connection, enhancing communication
efficiency.
The benefits of WebSockets include real time communication, making them ideal
for low-latency, interactive applications. They offer a significanyly better efficiency
with reduced overhead by maintaining a single connection. This results in a faster
message transmission compared to REST APIs.
==PAGE:42==
Nevertheless, there are some drawbacks as well.
To name a few, they increase
implementation complexity due to their stateful nature, and continuous connection
management. Furthermore, WebSockets consume way more memory, and buffer
resources. Another big downside is that they predominantly support vertical scaling,
which can limit scalability.
6.4.5
Choosing the Right Protocol for Real-Time Applica-
tions
Given the requirements of a real-time application, the WebSocket API is the pre-
ferred choice for the communication layer between the frontend and backend. Its
ability to provide low-latency, bi-directional communication makes it suitable for
scenarios where timely data exchange is critical. The WebSocket protocol’s effi-
ciency in maintaining a single persistent connection reduces overhead and ensures
faster message transmission, which is essential for a responsive user experience in
real-time applications.
However, it is important to consider the increased complexity and resource con-
sumption associated with WebSockets. While REST API offers simplicity and ease
of use, its limitations in speed and overhead make it less suitable for applications
requiring real-time interaction.
In conclusion, the WebSocket technology provides the necessary infrastructure for
achieving efficient, real-time communication in modern web applications, making it
the optimal choice for the communication layer in the case of ”Reading the Reader”.

6.5
Data Architecture
• Data Storage Solutions: When making decisions for data storage solutions, in
the case of ”Reading the Reader”, there were a number of possible DBMS(database
management systems) that were taken into consideration. As of now, the system
does not require perfect optimization, due to the low load of actions it is required
to accomplish, but in doing this, future implementations and upgrades were taken
into considerations.
Firstly, three different DBMS systems were chosen, because of their distinctive
differences.
These were PostgreSQL, MySQL and MongoDB. A research paper
[Putra et al., 2022] did an experiment in which they analyzed performances of these
DBMS technologies, because of their wide use. The study included experiments
that would test databases in a range of queries including ’INSERT’, ’SELECT’,
’UPDATE’, ’DELETE’, ’SUM’ and ’COUNT’ on a chess dataset from Kaggle. The
dataset features 16 columns and 20058 rows. The response times for the aforemen-
tioned operations were measured and compared.
When it comes to SELECT Queries, PostgreSQL consistently had lower response
times compared to MYSQL and MongoDB across different data limits, indicating
superior read performance. It is also important to notice that response times of
MongoDB were competitive at lower data volumes, but increased as the data volume
increased. As for INSERT Queries, PostgreSQL displayed remarkably lower response
times across all data sizes, showcasing its efficiency in handling insert operations.
MongoDB and MySQL showed higher and more variable response times as data
volume increased.
UPDATE Queries showed similar results, where PostgreSQL
outperformed the other two, with MongoDB having a slightly faster response time
in comparison to MySQL at larger data sizes.
DELETE Queries demonstrated
pretty much the same, with PostgreSQL outperforming its competition, while the
other two had similar performance, with slight variations depending on data volume.
Finally, SUM and Count Queries exhibited essentially the same when it comes to
PostgreSQL, whereas MongoDB performed the slowest in these operations due to
less efficient handling of aggregation queries.
In conclusion, PostgreSQL outperformed its competitors in every scenario, with
MySQL shoeing competitive performance across different query types and volumes.
MongoDB performed well in smaller datasets but struggled with higher complexity
==PAGE:45==
queries.
Since ”Reading the Reader” is currently dealing with smaller datasets, and non
complex queries, it has been decided that the characteristic of a DBMS to perform
with smaller loads has priority, and because of that, PostgreSQL and MongoDB
continue in the next round of selection.
Taking this into consideration, another potential candidate for a DBMS system
emerged in Redis[Eddelbuettel, 2022]. Some of the advantages of this DBMS, and
the reason why it is being considered is because it is extremely fast. The reason for
this is because it stores its dataset in memory. It provides support for data types
such as strings, hashes, lists and stored sets with range queries. Another advantage
of this technology is that it supports master-slave replication and allows for hori-
zontal scalability. Essentially, the reason why Redis was considered, is because of
its incredible speed, which is essential for real time applications such as ”Reading
the Reader”. Unfortunately, there are some disadvantages to using it as well. The
size of the dataset is generally limited by memory of the hardware. The bigger
drawback, and the main reason that this will not be used as DBMS solution is be-
cause of data persistence. While Redis has options for durability, ensuring data is
not lost if a power failure occurs, can be way more complex compared to disk-based
databases like PostgreSQL. With the intention of persisting the data that can be
used for continuous training of the model, this DBMS option will not be considered
as a contender.
Finally, a study compared MongoDB, Cassandra and PostgreSQL [Ohlsson and Persson, 2019]
in order to to compare the three to determine which one is the best for stor-
ing semi-structured data, something that might be useful in future development
of ”Rearing the Reader” app. The study outlines the methodology for selecting the
databases, setting up the benchmarking with Yahoo Cloud Service Benchmarking
System (YCSB), and the specific focus on read operations for performance com-
parison. The experiments utilized different workloads and different data operations
that reflect real-world usage. There were six different simulations: update heavy
(50% reads, 50% updates)[A], read mostly (95% reads, 5% updates)[B], reads only
(100%)[C], read latest (95% reads, 5% inserts)[D], short ranges (95% scans, 5% in-
serts)[E], and read-modify-write (50% reads, 50% updates)[F]. These experiments
were conducted to evaluate the performance across varying numbers of operations,
from ten thousand to one million, to scale up the complexity and size of the tasks.
As for the results, Load performance during the initial data loading phase, Post-
greSQL consistently showed the lowest loading times, suggesting efficient data inges-
tion capabilities. In experiment A, PostgreSQL and MongoDB performed better for
smaller number of operations, but as the scale increased to one million operations,
PostgreSQL showed better handling of large mixed operations. Experiment B and C
demonstrated that PostgreSQL is superior in performance when handling read-heavy
operations across all scales. MongoDB was slightly behind, whereas Cassandra was
revealed as the slowest. Experiment D indicated similar trends, with PostgreSQL
leading in performance, especially as the data operations scale increased. Exper-
iment E was particularly challenging for Cassandra, which showed exponentially
higher execution times as the number of operations increased. PostgreSQL again
maintained a constant lead in performance. Finally, in experiment F, all databases
==PAGE:46==
showed a gradual increase in execution times as the operation count increased, but
PostgreSQL maintained the lowest times, suggesting better transaction handling.
In conclusion, PostgreSQL results highlighted its superiority over other DBMS tech-
nologies, for scenarios involving high read operations and transactional workloads.
The efficiency and speed of PostgreSQL in handling varied and intensive data op-
erations suggest it is well suited for environments requiring high reliability and
performance.
Based on the comprehensive results from the research comparing a number of DBMS
technologies, PostgreSQL emerges as the favoured technology for ”Reading the
Reader” application. These findings clearly indicate that PostgreSQL provides su-
perior performance in read operations, thanks to its robust parsing and transaction
handling mechanism, which are critical for ensuring data integrity and accuracy.
This makes it especially suitable for applications that demand high reliability and
precision in data retrieval and processing, which is the use case in question.

This project aims to transform reading into a more interactive and personalized experi-
ence. This is done in order to fill the gap that currently exists in the application of eye
tracking technology.
9.5
Limitations
One of the key limitations of the ”Reading the Reader” project lies in its dependency on
the integration and effectiveness of a machine learning model, which was not within the
scope of the project to develop or implement. As a result, while the system’s architecture
and frontend were designed to support real time text adaptation based on eye tracking
data, the absence of an active, trained machine learning model means that real time
adaptation is more theoretical than practical at this stage. Furthermore, while the system
==PAGE:68==
is designed to adjust text based on style changes, the current implementation only partially
handles contextual awareness. The method to track where each word is exists, but remain
utilized. If the text is large or the style change is drastic the computational demand
becomes excessive, which can degrade the performance and user experience.
9.6
Future works
Looking forward, several enhancements are poised to elevate the capabilities and impact
of the ”Reading the Reader” project. First and foremost, developing and integrating a
robust machine learning model tailored to analyze eye tracking data and implement real
time text adjustments is crucial. This development would fully activate the project’s core
functionality, allowing for a genuinely adaptive reading experience. Moreover, Enhancing
the contextual awareness of the application by fully utilizing the location of all words can
offer a more seamless reading transition. Introducing a feedback mechanism would allow
users to provide direct responses to adaptations. This could be used to further refine and
personalize the reading experience. Expanding the undo/redo functionality to include
a redo option and improving the overall system’s adherence to WCAG 2.1 accessibility
standards are also vital steps to ensure the application is inclusive and as user friendly,
as possible.

Chapter 10
Conclusion
10.1
Conclusion
The thesis ”Reading the Reader” has advanced the application of eye-tracking technology
to enhance reading for users with disabilities, integrating this technology within a real-
time, user-centric interface. This project not only involved the development of a prototype
but also extensive research to select the most suitable technologies for frontend, backend,
and database systems, ensuring an optimal setup. Despite its limitations, such as the
absence of a fully integrated machine learning model, it lays a solid groundwork for
future enhancements.
The foundational system design and comparative analysis with
existing technologies underscore its innovative approach to real-time text adaptation.
Future work will focus on fully integrating a machine learning model and expanding
user interaction capabilities, promising significant improvements in accessibility and user
experience. This thesis not only highlights the potential of adaptive technologies but
also establishes a strong basis for significant future contributions to the field of accessible
reading solutions.
