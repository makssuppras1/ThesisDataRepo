Abstract
It has originally been the purpose of this project to investigate the behaviour of cracks in con-
crete. It has especially been desired to investigate the unloading of cracks. It is desired to
model this behaviour, in order to be able it take it into consideration, when modelling larger
concrete structures. The focus of the modelling in this project have been on concrete beams.
Experiments have been conducted, where reinforced concrete cylinders have been loaded in
tension, in order to investigate the crack development. Unfortunately the cracks develops too
fast to measure, which means unloading becomes irrelevant in this set up. The purpose of the
project have therefore been revised.
The beam model made for the unloading are capable of considering the unloading and diﬀerent
load types acting on the same structure. It does however require a longer computation time.
This is to a required smaller load step size, as the tolerance related to the equilibrium iterations,
have to be larger in order to be able to handle the sudden changes in tangential stiﬀness of the
individual concrete ﬁbre.
The beam model have been applied for a structural analysis of a beam in three point bending.
The modelling results have been compared with an analytical solution, where both the rein-
forcement in the compression and tension have been taken into account. The two calculations
yield similar results, when considering the load bearing capacity.
The purpose of the project have been changed into being able to model the cylinder tests
performed in the project, with focus on investigating the values of the fracture energy of the
concrete and τ, which describes the interaction between the concrete and the reinforcement.
This have been done successfully by introduction of a so called τ-model.

Resume
Oprindeligt har det været formålet med dette projekt at undersøge opførslen af revner i beton.
Specielt har aﬂastning af revner været ønsket undersøgt. Det er ønsket at kunne indkludere
efekterne af disse ved modelering af større konstruktioner. Fokus har i dette projekt været på
betonbjælker.
Der er blevet udført forsøg, hvor armerede beton cylindre er blevet lastet i træk. Dette er blevet
gjort for at undersøge revneudviklingen. Desværre har denne vist sig at ske for hurtigt til at
der kan måles på dette med den benyttede opstilling. Dette har gjort aﬂastning af cylindrene
irrelevant. På baggrund af dette er projeketets formål blevet revideret.
En bjælke model, som kan håntere aﬂastningen, er blevet opbygget. Desuden er det vist at
modellen kan håntere forskellige last typer på den samme bjælke. Både aﬂastning og diﬀeren-
tierede lasttyper kræver dog, at en højere tolerance relateret til ligevægts iteration kan tillades.
Dette kræves for at modellen kan håntere de pludselige ændringer i stivheden af den enkelte
betonﬁber.
Bjækemodellen er blevet benyttet til at foretage en konstruktionsanalyse af en bjæke i trepunk-
tsbøjning. Dette er sammenlignet med en analytisk løsning, hvor både armering i tryk og træk
er regnet med. Ved brug af bjælkemodellen opnås en bæreevne lignene dem som opnås ved den
analytiske løsning.
Formålet med projekt er blevet ændret til at forsøge at kunne modelere cylinder testene. Der
er blevet særligt fokus på at undersøge værdierne af brudenergien Gf og parametren τ, som
beskriver interaktionen mellem beton og armering. Dette er blevet sket med succes, ved blandt
andet at inføre en såkaldt τ model.

3. Introduction
It is within the scope of the project to attempt to model unloading of a crack in concrete.
Furthermore it is with in the scope to investigate two parameters related to the modelling of
the crack development. It is desired to investigate the fracture energy of concrete and the shear
parameter τ, which describes the interaction between concrete and reinforcement. These are to
be investigated through experiments. It is desired to apply the developed model in a structural
aspect. In this project the focus is on beams, so the model should be tested for a structural
beam, and compared to an analytical calculation.
3.1. Structure of the report
This report contain 11 sections, where the ﬁrst 2 before the introduction contains a list of the
content of the attached USB drive and a list of the symbols used in the project. In section 4 the
Non-linear fracture mechanics, which are the base of the modelling i this project, is presented.
Then follow two sections where the development of the various models used in this project is
presented. In section 8 the experimental work performed is described. Then in section 12 and
13 models are applied. And lastly in section 11 the report is wrapped up in the conclusion.
After that is the bibliography and then the appendices.

4. Non-Linear fracture mechanics
In this section the Non-linear fracture theory applied in the modelling are described, based on
(Karihaloo, 1995) and (Olesen & Poulsen, 2011). Non-linear fracture mechanics allows one
to take in to account the micro cracking in the zone ahead of a pre-existing crack. This zone
is called the fracture process zone, and for concrete this zone occupies most of the Non-linear
zone at the tip of the crack, as opposed to for example a ductile material like steel, where the
fracture process zone, is small compared to the non-linear zone. This is illustrated in ﬁgure 1
retrieved from (Karihaloo, 1995, p. 61).

(c) a quasi-brittle material eg. Concrete. L refers to the linear elastic region, while
N refers to the non-linear zone and F to the fracture process zone

5. Concrete hinge
In order to describe the crack development in a concrete beam a "hinge" is considered.
A
hinge represents a part of the beam, which contains exactly one crack. The eﬀects of this
crack is considered as smeared evenly over the hinge. The ends of the hinge is positioned right
in the middle of two cracks, which illustrated in ﬁgure 5. The crack distance is assumed to
approximately equal to half the height of the beam. However for more detailed calculations the
length of the hinge s is calibrated to ﬁt the individual model.
Figure 5: Hinges in a concrete beam
The idea that the eﬀects of the crack are smeared over the hinge, means that the stresses and
strains are constant over the length of the hinge. This is used to relate strain and curvature
to the moment and axial force acting on the hinge.
Perpendicular to the beam the stress
distribution of a beam in bending is shown for two phases in ﬁgure 6.
Figure 6: The assumed stress distribution in phase 1 (left) and phase 2 (right) by (Olesen &
Poulsen, 2011)

ﬁgure 6 is seen three stress parameters (σ1, σ2, σ3) are illustrated. These represents the points,

6. Modelling of interaction
In order to model the cylinder tests a model taking the interaction between concrete and
reinforcement into consideration has been developed. This is described by the parameter τ. τ
is deﬁned as the amount of force transferred between reinforcement and concrete per contact
area between the two. τ is assumed constant, which experiments by (Bernth & Jensen, 2004)
show is a decent assumption.
A "τ model" have been developed, which is described in (Olesen & Poulsen, 2016). The τ-model
is based on a reinforced concrete cylinder in uniaxial tension, and like the hinge model it divides
the cylinder into cells formed around a crack. It is assumed that when a crack is initiated a
new equilibrium has to be established. Further away from teh crack the original equilibrium
based on the strains are upheld. The cell length is deﬁned such that the stress in the concrete
is equal to ft. This also means that the minimum distance between the cracks are equal to half
the length of a cell. This is illustrated in ﬁgure 11 from (Olesen & Poulsen, 2016).
Figure 11: A slender concrete cylinder and the assumed stress distribution around a crack
An important part of the modelling is to determine the length of a cell. This is determined

are applied. For this purpose the Newton-Rahpson method is applied. The Newton-Raphson
method is described in (Krenk, 2009) and the concept is illustrated in ﬁgure 13.

7.1. Relief
In order to enable the model to handle relief the functions beamhinge NM.m and beamhinge
Dep.m have been modiﬁed. The functions now determine whether the ﬁbre is being loaded
or unloaded and then it calls the proper function, which then calculates the correct moment,
axial force or tangential stiﬀness in the case of beamhinge Dep.m. Preliminarily it is assumed
that the stress in the ﬁbre is equal to zero (σfib = 0) when the crack is closed (w = 0). It
is furthermore assumed that the stress drops linearly towards σfib = 0. When applying the
unloading to the beam model the sudden changes in stiﬀness prove a challenge. This yields a
rather large residual, which take a lot of iterations to even out. However by allowing a larger
tolerance of the residual and using smaller load steps the model produces reasonable results.
Also when introducing a "ﬂag" telling whether the beam is being loaded or unloaded, and
forcing the model to apply the correct stiﬀness, reduces the needed tolerance further. Running
the model with two relief and reloading stages results in the global response shown in ﬁgure 15.

7.2. Varying load types
It might be needed to expose the structure to diﬀerent load types during the calculation, so this
is tested in the beam model. For example a cantilever beam might be subjected to moment at
the free end. Then to introduce relief to the concrete in tension the beam could be exposed to
an axial load. Lastly a concentrated transverse load might also act on the free end. This have
been modelled with the beam model and the global response are shown in ﬁgure 17.
Figure 17: Global response of the beam subjected to changing load types
As when calculating for unloading of the beam a "ﬂag" has to be used to force the programme
to select the correct expression for the stiﬀness. Also a larger tolerance have to be allowed, in
order for the model to handle the sudden changes in tangential stiﬀness.

The purpose of this experiment is to investigate the behaviour of cracked concrete during the
crack opening and furthermore during unloading of the cell.
Also the interaction between
concrete and reinforcement, when the crack is initiated. More precisely the shear parameter
τ should be investigated, which determines the amount of stress transferred from concrete to
steel by shear.
The tests results however left much to be desired, as the development of the individual cracks
was not visible. Only the point just before the initiation of the crack and a point after the crack
had become stress free where found. This is more thoroughly described in section in section
8.4.
8.1. Specimen
The test specimens are concrete cylinders reinforced with a single steel bar. There are two types
of specimens. Three cylinders contain a 6 mm reinforcement and are called d6-cylinders, and
Three cylinders contain a 8 mm reinforcement bar and are called d8-cylinders. Furthermore one
extra of each cylinder type was cast as a dummy. However the initial casting of the cylinders
went wrong, which means that four extra cylinders of each type where cast to use as dummies.
This also means that all the obstacles met in the experimental work, was solved by testing the
solutions on a dummy. This have given two times three good cylinder tests.
8.2. Test set-up
The tensile forces is applied to the reinforcement, which then transfers the tensile force to the
concrete by shear. At the ends of the cylinder the reinforcement will take most of the force, but
further from the ends the concrete is going to take more stress through shear. It is assumed
that the force is balanced in a way that the strain of the concrete follows the strain of the steel.

9. Results of modelling
When attempting to model the test results with the ﬁbre hinge model and τ-model only the
cells containing earliest crack are considered. This is due to the fact that the axial stiﬀness are
being reduced by the crack. This means that the cell containing the earliest crack are going to
deform more than the rest of the cylinder. Due to this the measured strains i the secondary
cells should be lower, than if no cracks exists, thus yielding a fake larger stiﬀness of the cell.
The models have been ﬁtted to the test results.
This have been done by two diﬀerent
methods. In one method the fracture energy Gf have been calculated based on the length of
the individual cell s, which have been chosen in Aramis. Gf have been calculated by eq 13,
which is isolated from (eq. 8, Olesen & Poulsen, 2016).

axial force in the reinforcement.
The analytical solution takes both reinforcement in compression and tension into consideration.
Furthermore no safety coeﬃcients have been used, as this then will make a better reference for
the model. The analytical calculation can be seen in the appendix . The result of the analytical

10.2. Discussion of models
The models should brieﬂy be evaluated. The ﬁbre hinge model is by itself not very useful in
practice, as one have to assume the deﬂections. It is however quite useful for learning purposes.
Working on the ﬁbre hinge model gives a good understanding on how the modelling works. This
is very useful when working on the more advanced beam model. The beam model is quite useful
for modelling of structures. Some parts of the beam model however does need some work. The
model needs to be improved regarding unloading of the beam. The model should be able to
adapt to the sudden changes better than what is case at the moment. Also the implementation

of τ in the beam model is incomplete and in the future this should be expanded to not only

11. Conclusion
By implementing a layered solution to the original hinge model, the computation time is re-
duced. It requires 20 layers for the beam considered in this project to yield accurate results
compared to the original hinge model. The work with the beam model, has shown that it is
possible to model the behaviour of the beam when it is unloaded. It does however require a
larger tolerance than traditionally with the model. Furthermore it is recommended that the
step size are reduced in order to limit the eﬀect of the larger errors, related to sudden changes
in stiﬀness. The beam model can be applied to perform a structural calculation on a concrete
beam. The used of the beam model yields a ultimate load, that corresponds well with one cal-
culated by an analytical calculation. The experiments have shown that when a crack is opened
it suﬀers a disequilibrium, which is cause for a discontinuity in the response of a cylinder in
tension. This discontinuity has in these experiments been too large for unloading to be relevant.
The scale τ-factor have been determined to being in the interval of 1 to 3 MPa. τ does however
rely heavily on the fracture energy Gf. When testing concrete in tension misalignments should
be considered in the design of the experiments. Both misalignments in the concrete and in the
test set up should be considered as the bending caused by them have a signiﬁcant eﬀect on the
test results. It is possible to apply the ﬁbre hinge model and the τ model for modelling the
cylinder tests. It is however hard to model the stage immediately after the crack initiation,
as the test results are completely blind at this point. The τ model gives a better ﬁt than the
hinge model, as the introduction of τ oﬀsets the ﬁnal stage to ﬁt the test results. Two methods
can be used for the modelling of the cylinder tests. One where the length of a cell is known
beforehand and are used to determine the fracture energy, which then are used to determine τ.
And another where the fracture energy is determined to yield a slope matching the experiments
at the discontinuity. Both methods give are reasonable at modelling of the cylinder tests.

This interpretation of the uniaxial behavior of concrete was ﬁrst introduced in Hiller-
borg et al. where it was named the Fictitious Crack Model (FCM). The modeling of the
localization of micro cracks into a line separating the specimen into elastic parts is one
of the basic assumptions of the FCM. Another assumption is that stresses bridge this
ﬁctitious crack according to the softening curve which relates the bridging stresses to the
crack opening. The softening curve may be seen as the constitutive relation for the crack.

Assignment
The purpose of the assignment is to extend a Matlab code which solves the problem of
crack propagation in a concrete beam subject to bending, and to apply this code in an
analysis of the response of a reinforced concrete beam.
The following Matlab code which covers the hinge phases 0 and I is available as a starting
point:
hinge1.m Matlab script which produces the moment-curvature curve for the hinge
with N = 0 by calling the function e0Mhinge.
e0Mhinge.m Function script which produces ¯ε0 and M as a function of N and ¯κ
by iteration using built-in function fzero to ensure a prescribed normal force N
produced by calling NMhinge.
NMhinge.m Function script which produces N and M as a function of ¯ε0 and ¯κ
For the tasks of the assignment the following data should be used:
• Beam dimensions: h = 0.2 m, t = 0.1 m, s = h/2
