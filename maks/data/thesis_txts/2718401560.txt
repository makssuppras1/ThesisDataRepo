Abstract 
 
During this project, a microfluidic chip with unique features has been designed and 
developed step by step to being a part of a multi-step biochemical reaction cascade. The 
channel design of the chip has been simulated and prototyped to reach to the most 
efficient performance in the aspects of making a high flow velocity, high amount of 
mixture points and huge surface contact with fluid running through the chip, besides 
precisely considering high flow velocity. 
The chip has been developed, prototyped, and manufactured properly and each design 
feature e.g. electrode place, was added, tested, and prototyped. There were many 
obstacles and difficulties happened during the manufacturing process that most of them 
has been solved with professional design modification and frequent remodeling and 
prototyping. 
After the chip design and manufacturing, the biochemical reaction was being 
experimented to reach to various trade-offs which are necessary for the cascade of 
reactions e.g. finding the best voltages for electron transferring or finding the best co-
factor for this transference. Protein implementation and immobilization on the channel 
surfaces has been done, different parameters have been analyzed and the chip design has 
been finalized and all the data is ready to start creating and utilizing one of the 
biochemical reaction cascades for one specific pathway.

1. Introduction 
 
Microfluidic chips and systems now are categorized as a serious and substantial Micro-
electro Mechanical systems (MEMS) due to unify micromixers and micropumps with 
channels for passing the fluid. Nowadays, having micromixer features inside a 
microfluidic chip are very practical and widely useful in biopharmaceutical and chemical 
industries; they are simple and quickly manufacturable, even with 3D printing and they 
can be combined with different electrical or mechanical systems to become more closer 
to the pleasant applications that the user needs them. [1] 
Two kinds of micromixers can be utilized for Lab-on-Chip applications. Based on the 
operation, the first one is active and the second one is passive form. The active 
micromixers are the ones that can co-operate with the other electrical and mechanical 
systems and the passive forms just focus on improving the mixing of fluid inside the 
channels and other internal features to enhance the performance of any desirable 
application e.g. the channel surface area. Besides that, regarding having other aspects of 
systems e.g. mechanical and electrical, the active micromixers are more complex and 
harder to control and fix it to work constantly without having any disturbance in the 
systems e.g. it is not possible to keep the flow rate stable when making a fluid circulation 
inside the channel by using MPs. [1] 
In this project, we have created a microfluidic chip with various design features to 
manually adjust the flow behavior of the fluid inside the channels and tried to simulate 
the flow behavior to observe the effects of the modifications (or any added features) on 
the flow. The chip performance has been assessed with a modified version of TESLA 
micromixer in aspects of mixing efficiency, flow rate, and surface area. Some relevant 
biochemical experiments such as enzyme electron transferring has been done on the chip 
after manufacturing. 
2. Backgrounds 
 
2.1. Interior and Exterior Design of the Chip 
 
In the previous studies, the microfluidic chip design was limited to simple shape of the 
channels and just 2D approach, the maximum design modification was that to increase 
the number of the channel to make multi-channel or planar chamber. Regarding the 
fabrication, most of the studies manufactured microfluidic chips with PDMS and mold 
printing. [18] The chips were manufactured by PDMS, with limitations of a six-step 
procedure which is time consuming and difficult process for the user. [4] More errors 
might happen on each step when the steps of the manufacturing process are increasing. 
==PAGE:10==
 
 
 
2 
 
Although 3D printing is a suitable method for fabricating the microfluidic chips, it still has 
some limitations regarding having a precise control in microscale, compatibility with a 
specific resin and mass production. [4] With a high accuracy printer, resin compatible 
and easy-manufacturable design, we can minimize the negative points and limitations of 
3D printing for microfluidic chips fabrication. 
The start point for this project is designing the chip, creating a simulation for the flow, try 
to manufacture the design and prototyping, and making design modifications whenever 
it appears that there is a problem regarding the chip or channel design in the prototyping 
stage. 
2.2. The Idea Back of the Chip 
 
In the previous studies, various shape of the passive micromixers has been analysed and 
assessed. [19] The idea for designing the chip needs to possess three main features; at 
first, we should design the inside chambers in the way that they are contacting with fluids 
as much as it is efficient. It means that we must design the inside chambers to have the 
most possible connection with fluids as we need the highest area for the inside chambers.  
Secondly, the reactions inside the chambers are going to become more efficient if the 
mixture level of the fluids stands on the maximum. It means that the flow simulation is a 
significant factor that we have to put an eye on it and try to increase the flow 
disorderliness due to creating vortexes in all three dimensions.  
Thirdly, disordering the flow direction and altering the flow rate during the fluid 
circulation inside the chip might be advantageous and effective in our reaction efficiency. 
In overall, the flow behavior is mostly laminar in the normal straight chamber, it would 
be pleasant for us to convert the flow behavior from laminar to turbulent, with flow rate 
variation beside having numerous vortexes and whirlpools and all x, y, and z directions. 
2.3. Finding the Required Design Features 
 
For testing various design features, we have tried to assay ten various concepts with 
different features to test that which features are effective for reaching to our desirable 
type. We have tested them in the previous project (special course) and derived the best 
design features from the flow simulations. We got that we could add small and big column 
barriers to change the flow behavior and direction, then using snaky channel design to 
increase the channel surface area and volume (Figure 1).

2.5. Flow simulation assessment 
 
For creating a precise flow assessment, we need a powerful and professional software to

we used for designing the chip, in extra it has a feature for flow simulation that the user

the files into a new software e.g. exporting to STL results in that the software converts 
the design into small triangular meshes and the simulation will be done on this file which 
is imported to a new simulation software such as COMSOL Multiphysics. 
 
3. Design features setup and progressive chip development 
 
3.1. Channel development 
 
In this section, the channels have been designed and developed step by step, based on the 
design features’ influence on the flow behavior. The flow has been simulated and 
visualized in each level, then the channels become more developed progressively. 
3.1.1. Normal channel 
 
With having the normal channels, we have a laminar flow inside the chip with having no 
disorderliness of the flow direction and no vortexes and whirlpools for higher mixture, 
so we start the development process by adding a single column barrier on the way of the

As it is observable in Figure 3, putting a column barrier leads to an alteration in the flow

be considered as it has direct effect in the fluid mixture, the center flow rate in this design 
has been decreased after passing the small barrier and entered to the vortex. In the next 
step we try to increase the number of the barriers and see how the results are going to be 
different.

3.1.3. Organized and disorganized barriers 
 
In this step we are adding more barriers on the way of the flow. At the first step we add 
four more column barriers and analyze the effects on the flow behavior alteration. It can 
be seen that the flow rate is constant on the sides and lower between the barriers, which 
is unpleasant because what we would like to achieve is that to increase the flow rate 
beside having whirlpools and vortexes. With disorganizing the barriers we will achieve a 
good trade-off between the flow rate, which is constant now, and the flow behavior and 
direction alteration. Despite the fact that we have some flow changing, we need to make 
more influence on the flow. Figure 4 and 5 can show the flow simulation results.

3.1.4. Big barrier influence 
 
With adding a big barrier and narrowing the flow way, we can see a very high flow 
changing and vortex creation in front side of the barrier. The result is mostly favorable, 
especially when the flow rate has been increased because of the pressure increasing 
inside the channel with narrowing it. It shows us that with narrowing the flow way, we 
can reach to a better mixture and higher flow rate, enhancement from approximately 0.02 
m/s to 0.18 m/s which means about 9 times improvement in the mixture ratio (Figure 6).

3.1.5. Big barrier on turning points 
 
As it could be seen in the previous simulation, adding a big barrier and in fact, narrowing 
the channel contributes to a very high mixture of the fluids and rising the flow rate up 
into a two-dimensional environment. The idea for the next development is that if we try 
to make circular channels and add a big column barrier into a channel turning point, the 
flow alteration will be in three-dimensional scale or not. The simulation can easily show 
that the flow behavior has been totally changed after passing the big barrier, the flow rate 
has been even enhanced and the mixture point number and scale have been increased to 
great extent in three-dimensional environment. 
The flow rate when the fluid coincided with the barrier has been sharply increased and 
three-dimensional vortexes and flow rotation has been happened, and the flow rate has 
been tremendously increased when it is passing the barrier from the side. The flow rate 
increment is closely from 0,03 to 0.4 which means about 13 times higher than the starting 
flow rate (Figure 7 and 8).

being directly in contact with the fluid. 
We need an electrode to be inserted into the chip, exactly at the middle of the fluid way 
to make the electron transferring process (Figure 10). This is a challenging part of the 
design process because we have manufacturing limitations for the chip, and we are going 
to talk about it in the manufacturing section.

5. Chip Manufacturing and Assembly 
 
5.1. 3D Printing 
 
We are using Asiga Pico II as a main 3D printer for manufacturing the chip. The printer is 
compatible with BioMed resin and has the Z resolution of 1 micrometer which is very 
desirable for our function (more information regarding the 3D printer can be find in the 
appendix). Although, one of the negative virtues regarding the printer is the platform size, 
which we can just print one chip per print and that is time consuming regarding the 
manufacturing, in overall the 3D printer performance is one of the best for our 
application.  
5.2. Cleaning and Curing 
 
For curing the components after printing we need a UV curing device which we are using 
Form Cure from FormLab Company. The technical specification is available below in the 
appendix. We cure each chip in 60° for 10 minutes, after curing with these parameters no 
liquid resin can be found inside and outside of the chip. 
5.3. Assembly 
 
Design of the assembly system is like that we use one chip as a start point of the reaction 
and try to connect them vertically to the other ones, with rotating one of the chips for 180 
degrees through Z-Axis. As we mentioned before, we have changed the place of the outlet 
to the bottom surface of the chip, for the next step we need to add three vertical column 
and three parts on the top surface. 
6. Prototyping and Evaluating the Manufacturing Mechanical Aspects 
 
6.1. Before Manufacturing 
 
The features that we are expecting to happen for the chip after manufacturing are that at 
first, the chip interior and exterior design should be manufactured in a proper way with 
having least artifacts and errors on the final results, secondly, no leaking must be 
happened during inserting the fluid inside the chip, and thirdly, the final component must 
be accurately printed in terms of dimensions and design features. 
6.2. After Manufacturing 
 
The printed component must be totally cleaned from the liquid resin and the surface of 
the interior sections (mostly channels) must be washed with IPA (as a solvent for resin 
residues) to solve and remove all the resin residues and fully open the channels. Beside 
==PAGE:21==
 
 
 
13 
 
using IPA to remove the resin, we can use a nitrous oxide pump to ventilate and purify 
the interior surface. Since we are going to implement proteins on these surfaces, it should 
be noted that the interior surfaces are very significant and vital part for us, thus, we wash 
and clean each chip five times, firstly with IPA and secondly with 𝑁2𝑂 pump. Moreover, 
we can also fill the interior channels with IPA and drown the chip into a tank of IPA inside 
a laboratory shaker and leave it for 10 minutes. It makes this opportunity for the interior 
resin residues to be solved into the inserted IPA and going out of the chip after the 
mentioned time with 𝑁2𝑂 pump. 
6.3. UV Curing 
 
After washing all the resin residues and cleaning the chip and drying it from any humidity, 
it is the time for curing it by using the curing machine. We used Form Cure from FormLab, 
put the chip inside the chamber and start the curing machine for 10 minutes for each chip 
on 60 Celsius degrees. 
 
Figure 18. Asiga Pico II and Form Cure, from left to right, respectively.

7. Micro Pump Setup 
 
For making a fluid circulation inside the chip, we need a micro pump to connect it from 
inlet and outlet of the chip and create an adjustable circulation. The maximum flow rate 
that we want is one microliter per minutes, hence we find Bartels-Microtechnik company 
which they are producing and marketing a pump system that can lead us to reach to this 
number. We have chosen mp-advance kit for our usage which has been shown in Figure 
21. 
7.1. MP-Advance Kit 
 
 
Figure 21. MP- Advance Kit for making a complete microfluidic system from Bartels-Mikrotechnik® company. [9] 
The MP-Advance kit is a micropump kit that contains the systems components for a 
complete microfluidic system. This package contains three various drivers for different 
flow rate durations, a sample microfluidic chip, tubes, and attachment part and a 
multiboard which is an evaluated board that allows the user to control the pump drivers’ 
frequencies and amplitudes, and the micropumps can be attached to it and making a 
connection between the drivers and the pumps. The complete system is controllable with 
Multiboard Software through connecting the board to PC or Laptop with Windows OS. All 
the other necessary accessories for making a complete microfluidic system are available

9. Protein implementation inside the chip 
 
9.1. Lactate Dehydrogenase enzyme 
 
Lactate dehydrogenase (LDH) is a significant enzyme in an anaerobic pathway which 
utilizes pyruvate for the final product of glycolysis. The enzyme function is catalyzing the 
conversion of lactate to pyruvate and vice versa, which means that the reaction is 
reversible, and it reacts with the reduction of NAD+ to NADH. The enzyme is very wide in 
nature and its presence can be observable in various organisms e.g. animals and plants. 
[2] 
9.1.1. Function and Mechanism 
 
This enzyme is a H transfers enzymes that can catalyze the reversible reaction of pyruvate 
to lactate conversion using NADH. [2, 6] 
 
Pyruvate + NADH + H+ → Lactate + NAD+ 
 
LDH is able to catalyze pyruvate to lactate and NADH to NAD+ and it can improve the 
reaction speed by 14 times. The reaction progresses with hydride ion transferring from 
NADH to pyruvate. Hydride transferring is quickly happening in each direction. [2, 6] 
9.2. Chip Activation and Protein Implementation Process 
 
For the chip activation, we are following the procedure step by step to establish 
Glutaraldehyde on the surface of the channels and immobilized the protein on the 
==PAGE:26==
 
 
 
18 
 
terminals. [13, 23] The first step is that after printing the chip we must wash the chip with 
distilled water for three to five times and the dry it with injecting 𝑁2𝑂 flow with 𝑁2𝑂 
pump. Then we mix Ammonium Hydroxide with Hydrogen Peroxide with the ratio of 3 to 
1 respectively, equal to 750 µl AH with 250 µl 𝐻2𝑂2. These chemics are toxic, hence we 
must wear mask and gloves when we are working with them. We inserted both of them 
in a microtube with mentioned ratio and put it in laboratory oven with temperature 
between 50° to 60° until observing small bubbles on the surface of the microtube. In this 
situation we inserted this combination to the chip and put it for 5 minutes in the 
laboratory oven with the same temperature. Then we wash the channels two times but 
not drying it with 𝑁2𝑂 this time. we insert 3-Aminopropyl trimethoxysilane (APTS) with 
ratio of 1 to 9 with water, equal to 900 µl water and 100 µl APTS. After insertion, we give 
1 to 2 hours to the channels to having connections to each other during this time. Then, 
we again wash the channel 3 to 5 times with water and adding Glutaraldehyde with 1 to 
4 ratio, equal to 200 µl of Glutaraldehyde and 800 µl of water, then inserting it to the 
channels and wait for one hour. After this step, we wash the channels with PBS 1X, 3 times 
and the chip are ready for inserting proteins. 
There are many techniques are available to immobilize an enzyme through a reagent 
(which here is Glutaraldehyde).  [13] To do this procedure, the next step is to add protein 
inside the chip for making the attachment with Glutaraldehyde branches which are ready 
for making connection with proteins. For instance, we immobilize β-Galactosidase with 
the ratio of 60 µl to 540 µl PBS 1X (the channels volume is approximately 600 µl). We 
insert this amount to the chip and wait for the reaction overnight. Based on the 
immobilization result which will be read with plate reader, we can increase the density 
of the protein and then immobilize it to the channels.

confirm that the reaction efficiency can be increased by adding PAMAM inside the 
channel to increase the branches for enzyme implementation.

14. Conclusion 
 
During this project, the microfluidic chip with high mixture possibility, high inside flow 
rate and with surface areas for attaching proteins has been designed and developed. At 
the first point, there was basic ideas for designing this chip. In progressive chip 
development, each design feature has been added step by step and the features have been 
applied to the chip and have been simulated and analyzed for reaching to an appropriate 
design. Ten various concepts with distinctive design features have been simulated and 
tested (in previous special course) and the best design features have been progressively 
applied to the chip. After the basic chip design and channel design, the flow simulation 
has been analyzed on the chip and the superior results have been observed which can 
direct the project to the proper way. The flow simulation represented that the channel 
design is able to result in high surface contact between the chip and inside fluid running 
through the channels for making the reaction in future. The place for inserting electrode 
for electron transferring process, Fluid Tanks for making connection between fluid and 
the electrode, and heater place for attaching heaters directly to the chip surface to control 
the temperature for each reaction has been added to the main chip design. For making a 
cascade, the design has been modified and it is possible now to join more than one chip 
together to create a biochemical reaction cascade which is vertical and start from top chip 
and going downwards. One reaction per chip has been considered for the reaction 
cascade, and for each reaction and each chip, the user can add specific heater with specific 
temperature and add various voltages to various chips through placing the electrode 
inside the chips in the cascade. 
In each stage, the critical point that must always be considered is the manufacturing of 
the chip. At the first point the resin must be biocompatible and possess this capability 
that the protein will be attached to its surface. Secondly the design should be in a way 
that the 3D printer can manufacture it in the best way without having artifacts and errors. 
Some parts of the design and design features are critical to be manufactured with 3D 
printing (e.g. electrode place). Cleaning and curing the chip after printing has been 
completely described, and the chips can be assembled on top of each other for making a 
cascade after printing, washing, and perfectly curing. There were lots of manufacturing 
failures happened during the chip prototyping, and each time that there was a failure, the 
design has been upgraded and modified to reduce the failure probability. Then, the 
electrode system has been set up to provide electron transferring and a microfluidic 
pump has been chosen and purchased to make a fluid circulation inside the chip. 
For the next step, the chip has been assessed and compared with a new design which was 
a combination of TESLA design with having more column barriers to increase the surface 
==PAGE:45==

contact and the results from our design was perfectly fine, the chip activation result for 
both chips was approximately similar to each other, with considering these points that 
our chip has higher fluid volume and surface area than the TESLA chip and while the 
bubbles was not ejected from the channels, and the flow behavior was not pleasant in the 
turning points. 
At the last step, we focused on doing the various biochemical reaction to prepare the data 
and material for creating a biochemical reaction cascade in a proper way. We have tested 
the BioMed resin in aspect of protein immobilization possibility to the channel surfaces. 
The electron transferring process has been done to find the best co-factor for this 
reaction. we replaced PBS buffer to Tris 20mM to increasing the toleration rate of the 
fluid combination when it is on the electrode and the electron transferring are performing 
on it. The GFP protein implementation and immobilization with higher efficiency by using 
PAMAM has been done and assessed by analyzing the chips with microscope and 
observed the PAMAM effect on protein immobilization on the channel surfaces. This 
microfluidic system can be hugely useful for multi-level biochemical reaction cascades 
and with adding heaters and electrodes, the parameters of each chip (which contains one 
reaction) are adjustable for reaching to the best and most efficient performance and 
getting the better results.
