Summary
In order to realize decarbonization gradually, the development of wind energy has
been boosted in Denmark with natural weather advantage, increasing the demand to
solve the storage problem with high penetration of intermittent wind energy.
In this case, P2H(Power-to-Hydrogen), converting electricity from renewable energy
into green hydrogen by water electrolysis has been developed. To increase the work
eﬀiciency of P2H, extra heat of electrolysis process is recycled at the same time when
producing hydrogen, which is P2HH. In this thesis, P2HH has been applied in an
alkaline electrolyser that works as a prosumer consuming electricity, and producing
hydrogen and heat simultaneously.
The mathematical model of the alkaline elec-
trolyser has been built. The economical performance of a large-scale grid-connected
alkaline electrolyser with or without wind energy has been investigated by applying
economic model predictive control (EMPC) strategy for its optimal dispatch.
With different configurations of weights for hydrogen price, and constraints on re-
cycled heat and working temperature, different controller performance, total electric-
ity consumption, hydrogen production, and recovered heat have been obtained. By
scaling the hydrogen price to be lower corresponding to the changing trend of green
hydrogen price, the EMPC controller is able to achieve optimal dispatch. More prof-
its are likely to be obtained when there are negative prices in the electrical power
system, and the recycled heat dose not have any constraint. The suitable working
temperature of the electrolyser is 70-80◦C to reduce physical loss caused by high
temperature, even if more profits canbe gained with working temperature 60 -90◦C.

CHAPTER 1
Introduction
1.1
Background
In order to decrease carbon dioxide emissions and maintain sustainable development,
electricity generation from renewable energy sources (RES) such as solar, wind, and
biomass energy consumed without emitting greenhouse gases has become a great
concern recently.
It was reported that Denmark created a new world record for
a proportion of electricity produced from wind power with a little more than 50%
generation in 2020[1], which is just the start of a vision to realize its electricity sector
fossil fuel-free by 2030[2]. As Denmark vigorously develops wind energy with natural
geographical advantages, enormous amount of electricity from wind can be produced.
But wind power generation is greatly intermittent and dependable on seasons and
energy demand also varies by season and time of day. The traditional power grid
operates in a way that electricity is consumed instantly after being generated, which
means the supply and the demand of electricity is in balance to keep the grid stable.
Introducing more and more share of intermittent, unexpected and uncontrollable
wind power generation is prone to increase the risk of breaking the balance since the
availability and flexibility cannot be guaranteed by power generation. On the other
hand, there is an increasing negative prices hours shown in Figure1.2. It is obvious
that a number of negative price hours have increased generally in DK1 and DK2
respectively from 2014 to 2020 because of the increasing penetration of renewable
energy resources shown in Figure1.1. With traverse of the electricity price data from
the oﬀicial website[3] by utilizing python, negative price hours usually occur in winter
since the supply exceeds the demand.

from 2014-2020[4].

Under these circumstances, hydrogen referred to as the energy carrier of the future
can be a promising candidate to store intermittent RES. According to the production
sources, the World Energy Council classifies hydrogen into grey, blue, and green hy-
drogen. Grey hydrogen refers to hydrogen produced from fossil fuels like natural gas
and coal. It has the lowest production cost but high carbon emission and is widely
used in the chemical industry. Blue hydrogen is an upgraded version of grey hydrogen.
In conjunction with carbon capture and storage technology in hydrogen production,
carbon emissions can be reduced by about 90%, but the cost is correspondingly higher.
Green hydrogen is produced from water electrolyser by consuming renewable energy
without carbon emissions, which has a broad development prospect because the goal
of bringing global energy-related carbon dioxide emissions to net zero in 2050 has
been stated in NET ZERO BY 2050: A Roadmap for the Global Energy Sector[5].
Using green hydrogen instead of grey hydrogen as chemical raw material is an im-
portant way to realize deep decarbonization in the chemical industry. In addition,
green hydrogen can help solve the problem of excessive renewable energy generation,
mitigate wind abandonment, and improve the utilization rate of renewable energy.
Up until now, power to hydrogen (P2H) converting power to hydrogen by means
of electrolyser can be perceived as the most promising technology to obtain green
hydrogen. P2H belongs to power to X (P2X) which refers to convert excess electrical
energy into long-term storage of chemical energy such as hydrogen or methane, etc.
With the phasing out of nuclear energy and the development of renewable energy,
the demand for P2X solutions as a renewable power storage option will rise sharply
since it is able to make a great contribution to the integration of electricity, gas, and

thermal energy networks and the decarbonization of energy systems. This technique
is aﬀirmed with strong support by Danish governments who allocated 850 million

1.2
The state of art
How to integrate water electrolysers into the energy system has been attracted more
attention for research and development (R&D) in recent years. A mathematical model
for an advanced alkaline electrolyser was researched and a one-year simulation of a
photovoltaic-hydrogen system was identified with the developed system simulation
model in [7]. The model developed in this paper can be used for alkaline electrolyser
system design and optimization of control strategies. A thermally compensated elec-
trolyser model coupled with a pressurised hydrogen storage model was proposed in
this paper[8], making it accessible to evaluate the performance of an operating real
electrolyser system such as working temperature and hydrogen production and check
the presence of hydrogen leak. The static and dynamic behaviour of a commercial
advanced alkaline water electrolyser was investigated in [9] to analyse the permanent
and transient performance and develop a complete and integrated static dynamic
electrical model with high accuracy. The paper[10] focused on MATLAB implemen-
tation of an alkaline electrolyser directly connected with a photovoltaic module and
concluded that the electrolyser eﬀiciency is around 68.48% in summer and 63.32%
in winter. Thus, during the conversion process of alkaline electrolysis, the transfor-
mation eﬀiciency is only 60%-70% and 20%-30% of the energy is converted as excess
heat[11]. To improve the eﬀiciency of traditional P2H, the conception of power to hy-
drogen and heat (P2HH) was proposed[11]. In this case, the electrolyser not only can
work as a flexible load to consume electricity but also provides excess heat for heat
load to reduce energy waste and improve energy eﬀiciency. In this paper, P2HH was
applied in an integrated energy microgrid(P2HH-MG) and a model predictive control
(MPC) strategy was applied to realize optimal dispatch with multiple objectives. The
paper[12] introduced artificial intelligence together with MPC to realize minimal en-
ergy exchange between the power grid and the hybrid renewable energy (RE) storage
system. The recent decline of the renewable energy cost suggests that the production
of green hydrogen through P2H process is likely to be more economical[13] since the
capacity can be optimized in real time by taking advantages of fluctuations in electric-
ity prices and intermittent renewable power generation. Therefore, it is meaningful
to research the economic performance of P2HH in a hybrid energy system combining
a RES with the electrolyser because it is able to make more profits compared with
P2H by recycling excess heat.

1.3
Objective
In this thesis, a wind and hydrogen hybrid system consisting of a wind power resource
and an alkaline electrolyser is mainly investigated for the large-scale grid-connected
application. The alkaline electrolyser is considered as a prosumer that consumes elec-
tricity from grid or wind power and supplies hydrogen and heat by using its waste
heat at the same time. With the objective of lower energy cost, higher profits and
optimal electricity usage, economic model predictive control (EMPC) strategy is ap-
plied to make most of the wind energy and achieve the best economic performance
via exploiting the variation of electricity, heat, and hydrogen price. The developed
EMPC controller will be implemented on different scenarios and the performance of
the controller and the results of them will be compared and discussed.
The main research contributions of this thesis are:
• Analyzing the mathematical model of the alkaline electrolyser and investigating
its dynamic characteristics.
• Designing an EMPC controller for the hybrid system to realize optimal opera-
tion.
• Evaluating the performance of the EMPC controller and testing optimal eco-
nomic operation of the wind and hydrogen hybrid system to achieve optimal
electricity consumption, energy cost savings and the best economic benefits.
1.4
Thesis Outline
This thesis is organized as follows:
Chapter 2 illustrates various types of electrolysers and the mathematical modelling
process of the alkaline electrolyser.
Chapter 3 introduces basic knowledge of MPC and the design of EMPC controller.
Chapter 4 implements different scenarios and cases, and compares and analyzes
their results.
Chapter 5 is the conclusion and future work.

CHAPTER 2
Electrolyser
In this chapter, first, the simple principle of water electrolysis is illustrated and three
different kinds of electrolyzers based on this theory are introduced and their spe-
cific working principles are explained. Then their advantages and disadvantages are
clarified and compared. Next, the mathematical model of the alkaline electrolyzer
selected as the main focus of this thesis is built and its characteristics are investigated.
Finally, the conception of P2HH is introduced and the mathematical model of that
is established.
2.1
Introduction of Electrolyser
With the principle of water electrolysis, water can be separated into hydrogen and oxy-
gen by connecting a direct current (DC) to two electrodes in an electrolyte. Hydrogen
and oxygen is produced from the cathode and anode respectively. This process occurs
in a unit named electrolyser whose size can range from small, appliance-size equip-
ment suitable for small-scale distributed hydrogen production to large-scale, central
production facilities tied directly to RES. In accordance with the distinct electrolyte
materials and working temperature, there are three different kinds of electrolysers
which are alkaline electrolyser, proton exchange membrane (PEM) electrolyser, and
solid oxide electrolyer (SOE)[14].
Alkaline water electrolysis shown in Figure 2.1 is a well-developed technology first
proposed in 1789[15]. An Alkaline electrolyser is characterized by having two elec-
trodes operating in a liquid alkaline electrolyte[16]. It is operated at lower temper-
atures such as 30–90 °C with an aqueous solution of potassium hydrogen(KOH) or
sodium hydroxide (NaOH) as the electrolyte, the concentration of its electrolyte is
20% to 30%. The speed of the electrolysis reaction can be increased by means of using
strong electrolytes delivering ions with high mobility to strengthen the conductivity
of the solution. Hence, the concentrated solution of potassium hydroxide is usually
considered as the electrolytic solution due to its high conductivity and less corrosion
compared with others. During the electrolysis process, the diaphragm located in the
middle of the cell is responsible for separating the cathode and anode as well as sep-
arating the produced gases from their respective electrodes.

PEM electrolyser creates a reaction using an ionically conductive solid polymer in-
stead of a liquid[17]. The solid polymer electrolyte (SPE) takes the responsibility to
conduct the protons, separate product gases and insulate the electrodes. It is also
operated at low temperature(20–90 °C). The working theory of PEM electrolysis is
quite simple shown in Figure2.2 . The water breaks down at the anode into protons
and molecular oxygen. The oxygen is evacuated by circulation of water and the pro-
tons move to the cathode where they are reduced to molecular hydrogen under the
impact of the electric field. Apart from the simple operation, PEM electrolysis has
lots of other merits such as compact design, high eﬀiciency, fast response, small foot-
print and produced ultrapure hydrogen and also produced oxygen as a byproduct[15].
However, the electrodes of PEM electrolyser are typically iridium and platinum be-
longing to noble metals which are more expensive than electrodes used in alkaline
electrolysis.

3.1
Overview of Model Predictive Control
The model predictive control (MPC) for the system with constraints is the most
successful industrial process control method that has appeared in the past three
decades.[23]. It utilizes the model of a system to predict its future behaviour and
solves an online optimization problem to select the best control action that drives
the predicted output to the reference. The advantages of MPC compared with other
control strategies like PID control are as follows.
• It is capable of handling multi-input and multi-output systems that might have
interactions between inputs and outputs.
• It takes constraints into consideration because the optimal operating point of a
typical process unit often lies at the intersection of constraints[24].
• It can be used to deal with a high variety of process types and is easy to tune.
The number of predicted future time steps is called prediction horizon(P) and it
shows how far the controller predicts into the future. As shown in Figure3.1, the black
dots represent the discrete time steps in the past and the solid line shows the past
control inputs. At current time k, the system state variables are measured directly or
estimated by the Kalman filter so that the control inputs u within prediction horizon
can be calculated by the least squares objective function with the purpose of tracking
the set point as much as possible. Although the inputs for prediction horizon are
obtained, only the calculated input uk at time k is applied in this system. At next
step k+1 the same prediction horizon moves one step further so the length of it keeps

3.2
Define the MPC problem
The quadratic cost of conventional MPC is capable of tuning closed-loop response.
Nevertheless, it is not adequate to manage real-time process operation regarding the
process economic performance [27]. Economic Model Predictive control(EMPC) is
a receding horizon controller that minimizes an economic objective function instead
of a weighted least squares objective function in MPC. As a consequence of the gen-
eral stage cost used, EMPC is likely to operate a system in a time-varying fashion
to optimize the economics[28]. Therefore, EMPC strategy should be taken into con-
sideration, since in this thesis the economic profits of an alkaline electrolyser are
investigated.

5.1
Conclusion
In this thesis, three different kinds of electrolysers have been discussed in detail. By
analysing and comparing the advantages and disadvantages of them, the alkaline elec-
trolyser with high maturity and lower cost is mainly investigated combined with RES
and grid-connected application. The mathematical model of alkaline electrolyser has
been built and the concept of P2H and P2HH has been illustrated in detail. An
EMPC controller for the optimal economical dispatch of an alkaline electrolyser con-
nected to the grid without local wind, and an alkaline electrolyser applied for wind
and hydrogen hybrid system has been presented. The EMPC controller has been im-
plemented for the different cases and scenarios. By different configurations of weights,
constraints and different working temperature to the EMPC controller, different re-
sults can be obtained to evaluate the controller performance. The performance of
EMPC controller has been analysed and compared in each case and the general elec-
tricity consumption and heat and hydrogen production have been concluded in form
of Tables. According the obtained result in each case, P2HH is capable of improving
the whole eﬀiciency effectively by recycling the produced heat and the work eﬀiciency
in this case is up to around 90%. From the obtained results, the designed EMPC con-
troller is able to optimal dispatch the operation the electrolyser to realize the fewer
energy cost and higher benefits by controlling the operation process according the
changing prices of electricity, hydrogen and heat. The results can be concluded that
it is likely for the electrolyser to make most profits when there is suﬀicient electricity
from RES and the electricity price has negative values. Moreover, working at the
temperature 60-90◦C makes it possible for the electrolyser to earn more profits com-
pared with working at the temperature 70-80◦C since the temperature(60-90◦C) can
be adjusted more flexible to recycle more heat. However, larger range of temperature
is prone to make physical loss for the electrolyser, which has an negative influence
on the life of the electrolyser. Therefore, the temperature from 70◦C to 80◦C is con-
sidered as the suitable working temperature for the alkaline electrolyser. According
to EMPC performance, the controller follows the hydrogen price obviously to make
more profits when the hydrogen price is not scaled by adding weight, covering the

5.2
Future work
In this thesis, the hydrogen eﬀiciency of the alkaline electrolyser has been assumed
to be 70% to simplify the system. However, in the more precise system, the hydro-
gen eﬀiciency will be affected by the temperature easily and more hydrogen can be
produced at higher working temperature with same current.
In addition, there is an error between heat loss and heat from heat districting net-
work when the electrolyser stops working and is provided with heat by external heat
network to keep its minimum temperature. The error is caused by the linearization
error from fit method. Therefore, in the future work, fragmented fitting method will
be used to obtain more accurate linear relationship between TPHH.
