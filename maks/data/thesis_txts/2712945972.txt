Abstract
An experimental investigation is performed on solar driven open sorption cooling system in
an indoor test facility. The major components of the system are: solar air dehumidifier, cold
storage with silica gel inside, and ventilation duct. Complete cycle in the operation of the
cooling system involves drying or charging of the air dehumidifier , charging of the storage ,
and discharging of the storage. The solar air dehumidifier is charged in the day using solar
energy, while the storage is charged in the night time with the help of the air dehumidifier
which dehumidifiers inlet air to the storage . During the day when there is need for cooling
, the charged cold storage is discharged to supply cool and dry air for comfort cooling.
During the experimental investigation, the performance and behaviour of the cooling system
at different climate conditions, different air volume flow rates , and possibility of operations
without the solar air dehumidifier are investigated.
The results of the investigation show that the system performs best in terms of discharging
power and and temperature drop ( difference between inlet and discharge temperature in
the storage) for operations at hot and humid climate condition and high air volume flow
rates. The maximum discharging power and temperature drop achieved in the system are
0.85 kW and 15 °K respectively. Operations at hot and humid climate condition outperforms
operations at hot and dry climate condition. The system performs poorly for operations
without solar air dehumidifier at hot and humid climate condition.
Operations without
air dehumidifier at hot and dry climate condition shows better performance compared to
the operation without air dehumidifier at hot and humid condition. Generally, operations
without solar air dehumidifier is less efficient compared to the operations with solar air
dehumidifier.

Introduction
One of the consequences of global warming is the increase in demand for air conditioning
and refrigeration. The environmental impact and burden on energy consumption caused by
this increasing demand can be reduced by utilization of solar energy for cooling[1]. Using
Solar driven cooling system instead of conventional vapor compression cycle for cooling will
help to avoid excess energy consumption, mitigate global warming and depletion of ozone
layer by avoiding harmful refrigerants used in vapor compression cooling systems.
The
interest and attention to solar energy by the scientists have been increasing in the recent
years. The demand for utilization of solar energy in the application like heating, cooling and
refrigeration increased over the years [2]. As a result of strong correlation between supply
of solar resources and demand for cooling during day time, solar cooling technologies have
enormous potential to meet the world cooling demand. Efficient cold storage technique can
also fully or partially cover the cooling needs during the day time [3, 4]
1.1
Background of the study and working principle of the system
Open cycle solar cooling is a desiccant cooling technique that make use of solid or liquid as
the refrigerant in direct contact with air. This technology got its name from its mode of
operation which involves discarding the refrigerant from the system after cooling as it comes
in contact with air being conditioned. Solar driven open sorption cooling cycle consists of
evaporative cooling with air dehumidification by desiccant material. Solid system uses water
as refrigerant and silica gel or other solid sorbents as sorption material [5].
Temperature and relative humidity are the most significant thermal comfort parameters to
be controlled [6]. The spread of infections depend on humidity ,therefore relative humidity at
the indoor environment of a building must be within a range for human comfort. Desiccant
based air conditioning is characterized by effective humidity control, thus it is an efficient
way to improve indoor air quality [7].
Working principle of the system: The system for investigation is a solar driven open
sorption cooling. The major components of the system are solar air dehumidifier, humidifiers,
ventilation ducts, and the storage. The solar air dehumidifier work as a thermal compressor
while storage work as interchanging evaporator and condenser. Figure 2 shows a simplified
operational diagram of the system. The operation sequences which is shown on Figure 2 are
described below:
A) The solar air dehumidifier is regenerated or dried during the day when sun is shining.

Through solar energy. warm ambient air is supplied through the solar dehumidifier
where it is heated before it passes through the implemented desiccant layer. When the
heated air comes in contact with the desiccant material, (Silica gel), it results in the
release of bound water to the ambient. Through this process, the solar air dehumidifier
is dried. During the drying or charging of the solar air dehumidifier, it becomes hot
and need to be allowed to cool before it can perform dehumidification function again
[1]. During the regeneration or drying of the solar air dehumidifier, air volume flow rate
and regeneration temperature are important parameters which determines the rate and
efficiency of the drying [8]. Figure 1 shows the solar dehumidifier in charging mode.

B) During the night time, the charged or regenerated solar air dehumidifier is discharged

by using it to charge the storage.
During the processes, cool and humid air from
the ambient enters the solar air dehumidifier where the air is dried as a result of the
implemented desiccant layers. The dried cool air moves from solar air dehumidifier
to the storage which is packed with desiccant material (Silica gel). At this state, the
storage has previously condensed water attached to the silica gel. When the dried cool
air passes the desiccant storage from the bottom to the top, the water at the silica gel
absorbs energy from the cool dried air and vaporize. The vaporization results in cooling
of the storage [10, 11]. Partial vapor pressure difference between the air volume in the
storage (high) and inlet air steam (low) is the driving force for the process. After some
time of operation, the storage dries and cool close to ambient (night) temperature.
C) when cooling is needed, usually in the warm daytime, warm and humid ambient air is
ventilated through the cold storage. When the warm humid air comes in contact with
the desiccant material in the storage, adsorption of water by the silica gel will take
place. This results in condensation and release of heat of condensation in the storage
[12]. The heat will create warmer air in the storage. As a result of presence of warm
and cool air in the storage, thermal buoyancy will set in [13]. The warm air which is
less dense than cool air will float on top, thus pushing the cool air down, which results
in the discharge of the cool air from the storage. The process starts from the top to
the bottom of the storage. Thus, the warm air gradually occupies the storage as cool
and dried air is being discharged from the storage. After some time, the whole storage
becomes warm and there will be no more cool and dried air to discharge. At this state,
there is a need to recharge the storage and the whole cycle will be repeated. It takes
about 11 hours to charge the dehumidifier, one hour and 30 min to charge the storage
and about an hour to discharge the storage. The output cool and dry air is used for
ventilation and cooling of the target space. The system passes through 3 stages to
complete a cycle. First stage: charging or regeneration of dehumidifier, second stage:
charging of storage and discharging of dehumidifier, third stage: discharging of the
cold storage

1.2
Aims and scope of the study
The purpose of the study is to investigate a solar driven open sorption cooling system.
Nielsen et al[9] started the work on the cooling system. Thus, this thesis is a continuation and
consolidation on the work done by Nielsen et al. In the current study, a new humidification
system is used to investigate the behavior and performance of the system under various
climate inlet conditions. Following the aim of the system which is to control temperature
5
==PAGE:14==
and humidity in a given space, This investigative study aims at the following:
1. Study the effect of various inlet temperatures on the cooling system.
2. Study the effect of different inlet relative humidities on the cooling system.
3. Study the effect of various air volume flow rates on the system.
4. Study the effect of not using solar air dehumidifier in the cooling system.
The investigation involves an experimental study of the cooling system. Series of experiments
are performed in order to study the effects of the selected parameters on the cooling system.
The obtained data from the experiment are processed and analysed in excel worksheet. The
results of the analysis are presented and discussed.

Solar cooling technologies
Almasri et al [14] classified cooling technologies as shown in figure 3:
Figure 3: Classification of solar driven solar technologies[14]
Solar driven cooling can operate based on adsorption cycle, absorption cycle, Rankine cycle,
desiccant cooling cycle, ejector cycle, Stirling cycle, and photovoltaic principle [15, 16, 14, 1].
7
==PAGE:16==
Closed cycle for chilled water system and open cycle for direct treatment of air for tempera-
ture and humidity comfort are sub classifications of solar driven cooling technologies [4]. In
thermo-mechanical solar cooling, concentrated solar collectors or evacuated tube collectors
are used to generate steam. The generated steam is processed in a thermodynamic cycle to
produce cooling effect. The steam is passed through steam jet ejector , this results in the
reduction of evaporator pressure which leads to the vaporization of of water in the evaporator
by absorbing heat from cold water. Alternatively, the steam can be used to produce mechan-
ical power which drives the prime mover of vapor compression cycle [14]. Adsorption and
absorption solar cooling are classified as closed systems. Major components of adsorption
and absorption cooling systems include: evaporator, adsorbent for (adsorption system), a
throttle, condenser, solar heating module and cooling module[14]. In absorption, absorbent
material helps to achieve sorption process as a volumetric phenomenon while sorption pro-
cess is carried out in adsorption by solid adsorbent material as a surface phenomenon [1,
14].
Absorption technology has a dominance of 82-84% in the solar cooling technology when
compared to adsorption and desiccant cooling technologies which share 9-11% and 7% re-
spectively. Absorption technology happens to be the most popular and matured solar cooling
system in the world[17]. Absorption cooling systems are popular and most suitable for cool-
ing of large buildings while comparatively small buildings are usually cooled with adsorption
cooling system [18]. Gado et al [19] presented operating temperatures and COPs for various
cooling technologies as shown the table 1

2.2
Solar Open Sorption Cooling technologies
Desiccant evaporative cooling (DEC) is classified as open cycle cooling system which is
directly connected to the surrounding air. They are different from closed system, which
consists of adsorption and absorption. Depending on the operational conditions and design
8
==PAGE:17==
constraints, either liquid or solid desiccant can be used in Desiccant evaporative cooling
system . Unlike solid desiccant cooling system , liquid desiccant cooling system requires
pump for the circulation of the liquid, thus more energy consumption. Liquid desiccant
solar cooling system can operate at lower regeneration temperature compared to the solid
system[20, 14].
Open cycle which involves direct treatment of air for temperature and
humidity control are simpler than closed sorption cycle. They do not require condenser,
evaporator, water storage reservoir and complex control strategy[21]. According to Nielsen
et al [9], a desiccant evaporative cooling system can be made up of the following components
• Air solar collector with a desiccant layer which serve as dehumidifier.
• A cold storage with desiccant material.
• Fans(ventilator) powered by Photovoltaic panel with battery.
• Ventilation duct system with valves and control system.
The working principle of Desiccant evaporative cooling system can be refereed to as open
adsorption heat pump in discontinuous operations [9].
Wang et al [22] presented 5 typical configurations of desiccant dehumidifiers as follows:
liquid spray tower, solidly packed tower, multiple vertical bed and rotating honey comb.
Rotating wheel is the most applied desiccant dehumidifier. Figures 2, 4, and 5 show various
configurations of solid desiccant cooling systems. Figure 4 shows a packed bed solar driven
open sorption cooling system. As shown in the figure, air is passed through the dehumidifier
where it is dehumidified and it becomes warm and dry. The warm dry air can pass through
heat exchange for pre-cooling before passing through evaporative cooler where it is further
cooled and delivered for space cooling. Alternatively, the dehumidified air can bypass heat
exchange and move to evaporative cooler where it is cooled.

Figure 4: Packed bed solar desiccant evaporative cooling system[14]
Figure 5 shows a solar driven open sorption cooling with rotary wheel desiccant dehumidifier.
Process air passes through the desiccant wheel where it is dehumidified. The dehumidified
hot air is passed through the heat recovery wheel for pre cooling, then through evaporative
cooler and heat exchange for further cooling and moisture removal. The output cold dry
air is sent to the place where is used for space cooling. The warm and humid air from the
cooled space is passed through the evaporative cooler in opposite direction where it is cooled
and humidified before passing through the heat recovery wheel where cold is recovered. The
exiting air is further heated by the heat exchanger before being used for regeneration of the
desiccant wheel air dehumidifier.

been increasing focus on liquid sorptions applications. This is because liquid sorption ma-

layer of desiccant materials like silica gel, zeolites, on a support structure called desiccant
wheel. The desiccant wheel rotates slowly between the process and regeneration air[1, 23].

Theoretical Framework of the Study
3.1
Desiccant materials
Desiccant material is an important part of an open sorption cooling system because the
choice of desiccant has significant effect on the cooling capacity of the system [9]. Sorbent
materials have ability to attract and retain gases or liquids. Desiccant belongs to the family
of sorbents. Almost all materials behave like desiccant materials, they can attract, retain
and release moisture the same way standard or commercial desiccants do. However, those
materials like wood, clays, and natural fibre lack holding capacity as standard desiccants.
Desiccants also differ from other moisture absorbing materials in their ability to continue to
attract moisture even when surrounding air is dry[65].
3.1.1
Types of desiccant materials
Desiccant can be in liquid or solid form. Adsorbents are usually solid while absorbents are
usually liquids. Meher, S [66] presented classification of desiccant materials as shown in the
figure 6.
18
==PAGE:27==
Figure 6: Types of desiccant materials
Among the solid desiccants are aluminas, hydrated salts, polymers, silica, zeolites, and mix-
tures (combination of 2 or more different desiccants). Liquid desiccants include calcium chlo-
ride, lithium bromide, lithium chloride, sodium chloride, Tri-ethylene-glycol and mixtures.
Silica gel, zeolite and activate carbons are among the most popular desiccants. Between the
two widely used desiccants (Silica gel and Zeolite). Silica gel is more efficient because of its
ability to take more moisture and regenerate at less temperature compared to zeolite. It is
difficult to regenerate zeolite because of the strong bond it has with water molecules [67, 9].
Due to the surface of activate carbons being hydrophobic, they have tendency for abrupt
decrease in adsorption amount at low relative humidity. However these shortcoming can
19
==PAGE:28==
be improved by adding silica gel to activated carbon in order to improve its hydrophobic
surface[67].
3.1.2
Desiccant operation and cycle
Desiccant ability to adsorb moisture from air results in its application in air conditioning.
Attraction and retention of moisture by desiccants takes place in form of absorption or
adsorption phenomena. During absorption phenomenon, desiccants undergo change after
absorbing water. For adsorption phenomena, desiccant does not change form when it adsorbs
moisture, instead, there is only change in weight due to adsorbed moisture. During sorption,
the sensible heat generated is equal to the latent heat of water taken up by the desiccant,
thus heat of sorption varies between 5 and 25% of the latent heat of water vapor [65].
All desiccant operational principle is by moisture transfer which results from the difference
between water vapor pressure at their surface and the surrounding air. Adsorbents are solid
materials that has high internal surface area per unit of mass which makes it possible for
it to attract and hold moisture.
The vapor pressure between adsorbent surface and air
determines its ability to attract moisture. The adsorption behavior of desiccants is subject
to the following properties - total volume of capillaries, range of capillary diameters and
total surface area. An adsorbent with large surface area results in large capacity at low
relative humidity while large capillaries results in high capacity at high relative humidity.
An adsorbent with narrow range of capillary diameters become more selective on vapor
molecules it hold [65].

3.1.3
Desiccant ageing and its effect
Like every other materials, desiccants can degrade after many cycles or can experience early
degradation due to adverse situations. Desiccants degradation results in decrease in desiccant
material’s adsorption capacity. Decrease in rate at which desiccant material adsorb moisture
is also consequence of desiccant degradation.
Hydrothermal damage and pore blockage
are among common cause of decrease in adsorption rate in desiccants. High water partial
pressure in the pores of desiccant at high temperature results in hydrothermal damage . The
consequences of hydrothermal damage are; distortion of crystal structure of the desiccant
which results in pore wall collapse and surface area loss. Silica gel and activated alumina are
prone to this issue. Pore blockage is usually as a result of particle build up on the external
surface of desiccant pores. This leads to blocking of access to active adsorption sites, hence
desiccant deterioration. Experiments show that activated alumina and silica gel lost 41% and
60% respectively of their adsorption capacity after 50,000 cycles at 60% relative humidity
and regeneration temperature of 200◦C [70].

3.3
Psychometrics of desiccant cooling
Psychometry involves the science of study of different properties of air, how to control tem-
perature and moisture content or humidity of the air.
Air is made up of mixtures of various gases and water vapor or moisture. Nitrogen and
Oxygen gases which are major constituents of air exist in superheated condition as gas at
normal atmospheric conditions. Since they have low boiling points of −195◦C and −182.7◦C
respectively. During air conditioning process, heat that is added or removed from the air
is usually sensible heat. No latent heat is involved since the boiling point temperature of
oxygen and nitrogen are very low.
When water vapor which exists in super heated condition is heated or cooled, it undergoes
phase change. As a result of the phase change, it absorbs or liberates sensible heat as well
as latent heat. Cooling of moisture results in its condensation, while its heating results in
super heating[79].
3.3.1
Psychrometric properties of air
There are some properties which are used to describe and understand air in air conditioning
process. Some of the properties of air are [79, 65, 6];
Dry bulb temperature (DBT): This refers to temperature of air measured by ordinary
thermometer. It is the atmospheric temperature.
Heat content of air: The total heat content of air or enthalpy of air consists of sensible
and latent heat. Latent heat is the heat lost or absorbed during the change in phase of water
vapor present in the air. During change in temperature of air, sensible heat is absorbed or
lost. Sensible heat of air is usually as a result of dry air present in the air. Dry air has
no latent heat since it does not undergo phase change. The water vapor present in the air
experiences phase change when the air is either cooled or heated. The amount of water
vapor present in the air determines its latent heat. The water vapor in the air also absorbs
or liberates sensible heat but the quantity is negligible and usually ignored.
Enthalpy: It is a useful concept in air conditioning. It helps to understand how much of the
27
==PAGE:36==
stored energy will be consumed or released when the temperature or the moisture content is
changed. Air with some amount of energy may either be dry hot air (high sensible heat) or
cool moist air (high latent heat).
Enthalpy = Sensible heat + Latent heat
Relative Humidity: It is a measure of water in the air relative to the maximum amount
of water air can hold. Warm air can hold more moisture than cold air. There is quick water
uptake when the relative humidity is low and temperature is high. So. the cooling effect is
more pronounced than in situation where relative humidity is high, in which case the moisture
uptake will be lower. Relative humidity varies significantly with dry bulb temperature. It is
expressed in percentage.
Absolute humidity: It is the actual amount of moisture in the air. It can be expressed as
the amount of water present in a unit mass of moist air at given temperature and pressure.
It is measured in g/kg or kg per kg of dry air. It is also known as moisture content or
humidity ratio.
3.4
Psychometric Processes
In air conditioning, air must pass through one or more of the following: cooling, heating:
temperature control, dehumidification and humidification: humidity control. Some psycho-
metric processes have to be performed on the properties of air so as to achieve certain values
of temperature and humidity of air within an enclosed space. The processes are very useful
in the domestic and industrial air conditioning. Some of the processes are discussed below
[79, 65]
Sensible heating and cooling: During sensible heating, humidity ratio, vapor pressure and
dew points remains constant. However, the following will increase: Dry bulb temperature,
specific volume, enthalpy. Heating will result in lower relative humidity. During sensible
cooling; dry bulb temperature, enthalpy, specific volume decreases. Humidity ratio, vapour
pressure and dew point of temperature remains constant.
Humidification (heating or cooling):
It is a process of introducing moisture into the air stream. In summer, humidification is
usually performed as part of an evaporative cooling system (for non desiccant cooling).
Heating and humidification is a process of simultaneously increasing both the humidity ratio
28
==PAGE:37==
and dry bulb temperature of the air. The total heat gained in moving from the initial to
final condition can be divided into sensible and latent heat.
Cooling and dehumidification:
When air is cooled below the dew point temperature, it results in condensation and removal
of moisture from the air stream.
Thus the air stream exists at lower temperature and
humidity ratio than the incoming air stream. The cooling by condensing water from the
air is called latent cooling or dehumidification. It involves both sensible and latent cooling.
During cooling by dehumidification; dry bulb temperature, humidity ratio, vapour pressure
and enthalpy decreases. These is a decrease in energy level and the less energy results in
condensation.
Heating and dehumidification (Chemical dehydration)
When air stream comes in contact with desiccants, water vapor molecules are adsorbed which
results in dried air. During this process, the heat of condensation will enter the air stream
and increase its temperature. The process is adiabatic or isenthalpic.

Experimental Procedure: The experiments were performed at different operation modes,
air volume flow rate, and different inlet conditions (ambient temperature and ambient relative
humidity) as shown in tables 6-17. A cycle of the experiment consists of three experiments
and four operation modes.
During the experiment, the ventilation air is heated to the desired temperature with the
help of the heating element which is controlled by a PID controller, see Figure 12c. The
water content of the air stream is modified by a humidifier which is set to inject a given
amount of vapor into the air stream. The humidifier function by evaporating the water filled
in its water component. Demineralized water was used in the humidifier in order to avoid
formation or accumulation of calcium carbonate in the humidifier and the ventilation ducts.
The experimental procedure for the four operation modes are explained as follows:
• Charging or regeneration of solar air dehumidifier: The desired inlet conditions
like air volume flow rate, inlet relative humidity and air stream temperature were con-
ditioned using the fans, humidifiers, heating element and PID controller. The heated
air stream injected with water vapor passed through the mixing ventilation duct for
proper mixing, see figure 11b (Ventilation duct). The properly mixed air stream passed
through the ventilation duct to solar air dehumidifier. During this period, the heat-
ing wires for imitating solar irradiance were switched on. The inlet conditions were
used to mimic ambient condition during the charging or regeneration of the solar air
dehumidifier. As the silica gel in the solar air dehumidifier get dried, warm and humid
air stream left the dehumidifier into the ambient. Figure 13 shows schematics of solar
air dehumidifier during charging. The charging takes place for about 11 hours, which
results in a very dried silica gel ready for adsorption of water vapor.

Discussion
Dehumidification capability of the solar air dehumidifier
The result analysis shows that approximately 0.3 kg of water is being removed by the air
dehumidifier from the inlet air to the storage, irrespective of air volume flow rate or climate
conditions. This behaviour can be a signal that the capacity or dehumidification ability of
the dehumidifier is being limited by one or more factors. Temperature rise in the dehumid-
ifier during dehumidification contributes immensely in the limitation of the dehumidifier’s
capacity. This is because, the rise in temperature results in the increase in surface vapour
pressure on the surface of the silica gel in the dehumidifier [12]. Such increase in vapour
pressure will make it to quickly attain equilibrium with water vapour pressure of the air
being dried. When equilibrium is attained dehumidification process will stop, even through
the silica gel is not saturated yet, its capacity to adsorb water is not fully utilized.
System performance in relation to climate condition and air volume flow rate
It is observed that operations at different climate conditions and air volume flow rate per-
formed differently. Higher temperature reduces performance and lower temperature enhances
performance [82]. The higher inlet temperature for operation at hot and dry climate condi-
tion compared to hot and humid climate condition could be the explanation for the better
performance of hot and humid climate condition. This is because, at low inlet temperature
desiccant material is not heated up. Low desiccant temperature means low surface vapor
pressure. At low surface vapour pressure, the desiccant material ability to adsorb moisture
from the inlet air during discharging of storage is increased [12, 82]. Adsorption of more
moisture from the inlet air results in high availability of cool and dry air for discharge. Also,
moisture adsorption from the inlet air results in higher temperature drop (better cooling of
inlet air). The temperature drop is as a results of condensation of water vapor present in
the inlet air. During the condensation, inlet air releases energy to cool down. More moisture
adsorption results in higher heat of condensation. This phenomenon also explains the higher
temperature rise in storage operation at hot and humid climate compared to the operations
at hot and dry climate during discharging of storage. see figures 21 and 26. Higher air vol-
ume flow rate helps to quickly move away dehumidified air from the interface, which helps to
reduce humidity gradient between the desiccant material and the inlet air during charging
of storage, thus mass transfer coefficient is improved [83]. This explains why temperature
drop for discharging of the storage at both hot and humid , hot and dry climate conditions
increases with increase air volume flow rates.
The results show that discharging power increases with increase in flow rate. This behaviour
82
==PAGE:91==
can be explained by the fact that moisture removal rate increases with increase in air volume
flow rate [84], see figure 46. This behaviour is because, the higher the amount of air sent
to the storage, the larger the volume of moisture of such air to be absorbed in the storage
per unit time [85] . Thus, dehumidification of large volume of air at short period leads to
availability of large volume of dehumidified air available for discharging.
System performance with air dehumidifier and without air dehumidifier
Experiments without dehumidifier results in the accumulation of water in the storage. The
accumulation affects discharging power and temperature decrease in the system. This is as
a result of the inability of the silica gel in the storage to further adsorb moisture during the
accumulation. Operating the system without solar air dehumidifier will result in initial cost
saving on capital expenditure. However, the cost saving comes with two major problems.
First, the system will operate inefficiently. The discharging power and achieved sensible
cooling will be low. Second, after few cycles, there will be need to replace the silica gel in
the storage. The cost will involve buying of new silica gel and cost of labour to replace the
silica gel.
The higher temperature drop shown by operation at hot and dry climate condition , without
air dehumidifier compared to operations with air dehumidifier is as a result of cool desiccant
material, see figures 43 and 49.
Operation for experiments with air dehumidifier shows
lower drop in temperature because the desiccant material is heated up as a result of sending
hot air to the storage during charging with air dehumidifier. The poor performance in the
temperature drop for operations at hot and humid climate condition compared to operation
at hot and dry climate condition , both without air dehumidifier is as a result of low moisture
adsorption at hot humid climate condition. Surface vapor pressure of desiccant material is
low at low temperature and low water content [82]. During operation at hot and humid
climate condition, a lot of water accumulates in the desiccant material thereby making its
surface vapor pressure to rise, eventhough it has low temperature. As a result of the high
surface vapor pressure, the desiccant material could not adsorb enough moisture from the
inlet air to cool it down.

Conclusion and Further Study
Investigation on solar driven open sorption cooling is performed by conducting several ex-
periments. The investigated system is made up of solar air dehumidifier, ventilation ducts,
and cold storage as major components. The effects of different air volume flow rates at
different climate conditions are studied in the experimental investigation. The possibility of
operating the investigated system without air solar dehumidifier is also studied. Based on
the experimental investigation the following conclusions are drawn:
• Temperature drop (difference between inlet and discharge temperature during dis-
charging of the storage) increases with increase air volume flow rate and lower inlet
temperature during discharging of storage.
• Pressure drop in the storage increases with increase in air volume flow rate.
• Inlet relative humidity, temperature , and air volume flow rates affect the performance
of open sorption cooling system.
• Operations at hot and humid climate condition for same flow rate results in better
sensible cooling than operations at hot and dry climate condition.
• Operations at hot and humid climate condition for same flow rates gives higher dis-
charging power than operations at at hot and dry climate condition.
• Moisture removal rate from the air that passes through the air dehumidifier depends
on the water content of the air and air volume flow rate. The higher the moisture
content, the higher the moisture removal rate. Also, moisture removal rate increases
with increase in air volume flow rate.
• Amount of water removed from the air that passes through air dehumidifier increases
with increase in moisture content and decreases in air volume flow rate.
• Solar driven open sorption cooling cannot function well without solar air dehumidifier
at hot and humid climate condition. Operations without dehumidifier leads to accu-
mulation of water in the storage. The water accumulation will result in the break down
of the system after few cycles.
• Solar driven open sorption cooling can function without solar air dehumidifier at hot
and dry climate condition. The absolute humidity of air at night condition is expected
84
==PAGE:93==
to be at maximum of about 5 g/kg of dry air for the system to function well.
In Summary, the cooling system performs better for operations at hot and humid climate
condition with solar air dehumidifier, discharging and charging of storage at 74 m3/h when
compared to operations at hot and dry climate condition, without solar air dehumidifier,
charging and discharge of storage at flow rates below 74 m3/h.
Suggestions for further study
The following studies can be carried out on the investigated system in order to gain more
insight on the system performance.
• Study the effect of higher air volume flow rates up to 300 m3/h
• Discharging of storage at higher temperature like 45°C to see the system response.
• Thermodynamic modeling or simulation study of operations in the storage during
charging and discharging (Heat and mass transfer study). This can help in the opti-
mization of the system for better performance.
• Study the most efficient way to adapt the system for heating during winter, since heat
is generated during charging and discharging of storage using cool humid air.
Suggestions for improvement of the test facility
In order to perform more efficient experiment and study wider range of parameters, the
following improvements can be done on the test facility.
• Installation of valve before the flow meter. This will help to adjust the values of inlet
air volume flow rate when it cannot be done from the ventilator.
• Installation of higher capacity ventilator to enable study of effects of higher air volume
flow rate in the system.
• The heating capacity of the heater for heating the inlet air need to be increased so that
the effect of higher temperature in the system can be studied.
• The solar air dehumidifier need to be cooled during sorption or air dehumidification
process. This will help to avoid sending hot air into storage during charging. It will also
help to increase the air dehumidifier adsorption capacity. The cooling can be achieved
by circulating cooling medium within the dehumidifier. Alternatively, installation of
heat exchange to cool down the air coming from air dehumidifier before being sent to
storage. However, implementation of this will only ensure that cold air is sent into
