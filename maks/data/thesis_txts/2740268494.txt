Abstract
This research explores the integration of Large Language Models (LLMs) into the characбґ
ter creation process for Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition (D&D 5e), addressing challenges
of efficiency, effectiveness, usability, and user satisfaction. Through iterative design and
user testing, an LLMбґpowered tool was developed to enhance the onboarding experience
for both new and experienced players.
The study employed a mixedбґmethods approach, combining quantitative measurements
of efficiency and effectiveness with qualitative user feedback. The final prototype demonбґ
strated significant improvements over existing digital solutions in all measured areas.
Character creation time was reduced to an average of 29 minutes, compared to 50 minбґ
utes for D&D Beyond and 42.5 minutes for Roll20. Effectiveness ratings increased to 4.13
out of 5, surpassing both D&D Beyond (3.35) and Roll20 (3.07). Usability, measured by
the System Usability Scale (SUS), improved to 77.5, a substantial increase from D&D Beбґ
yond (51.5) and Roll20 (57). User satisfaction ratings reached 4.35 out of 5, significantly
higher than existing digital tools.
These results indicate that LLMбґpowered tools can successfully address key challenges
in TTRPG character creation, offering personalized guidance, reducing complexity, and
enhancing accessibility. The research provides a foundation for future development of
AIбґassisted TTRPG tools, potentially broadening the appeal and accessibility of complex
roleбґplaying games like D&D 5e.

Introduction & Motivation
Tabletop roleбґplaying games (TTRPGs), such as Dungeons & Dragons (D&D), have grown
from niche hobbies to global phenomena1. Since its creation in the 1970s, D&D has seen
significant growth, especially after the release of its 5th edition in 2014. This edition, along
with increased media representation and the mainstream acceptance of geek culture, has
attracted over 50 million players worldwide2.
However, TTRPGs continue to face challenges. The complexity of rules and intricate
gameplay mechanics can hinder new player engagement and retention, and also create
usability issues for experienced players3. Before even starting a game, players often need
to invest considerable time in preparationБ─■learning extensive rules, creating characters,
and understanding the gameБ─≥s mechanics and constraints. This substantial upfront effort
can be discouraging for newcomers and burdensome for seasoned players, highlighting
a persistent tension between gameplay depth and accessibility in the TTRPG community.
Large Language Models (LLMs) have shown impressive abilities in simplifying processes
and addressing challenges across various fields[1]. Applying LLMs to TTRPGs presents
an intriguing opportunity to offer solutions to longstanding issues within the TTRPG space.
This thesis explores how integrating LLMs into TTRPGs can address these traditional
complexities, aiming to enhance accessibility and enjoyment for players at all levels. While
specific applications will be discussed in later sections, the overarching goal is to unбґ
derstand how advanced language models can improve different aspects of the TTRPG
experience.
Recent studies have begun to investigate the use of LLMs in TTRPGs, indicating promisбґ
ing avenues for innovation that have yet to be fully realized in mainstream gaming[2].
Additionally, there is a gap in user research regarding the design and user experience
implications of integrating language models into TTRPGs[3]. This research aims to build
upon these initial findings by specifically tailoring LLM capabilities to enhance the TTRPG
experience.
Б─°How can integrating LLMs into TTRPGs address traditional gameplay comбґ
plexities and enhance the overall player experience?Б─²

Б─╒ Design and Development of an LLMбґIntegrated Prototype: Design and develop

Tabletop RoleбґPlaying Games (TTRPGs) and Dungeons &

Tabletop RoleбґPlaying Games (TTRPGs) are a genre of games where players assume

cater to both new and experienced players, offering rich and immersive experiences 1.
The fifth edition of D&D (D&D 5e) is particularly recognized for its streamlined and less
complex system compared to previous editions and other TTRPGs, making it more acбґ

Players and Game Masters
In TTRPGs, gameplay is structured around two primary roles: Players and the Game
Master (GM), referred to as the Dungeon Master (DM) in D&D.
Б─╒ Players: Players create and control characters known as Player Characters (PCs)
within the game world. Decisionбґmaking for these characters is guided by the rules
and options provided in the gameБ─≥s rulebooks, such as the PlayerБ─≥s Handbook in
D&D. Players must invest significant effort into understanding these rules to effecбґ
tively navigate the complexities of the game 2.
Б─╒ Game Master (GM)/Dungeon Master (DM): The GM or DM acts as both storyteller
and referee, controlling NonбґPlayer Characters (NPCs), describing environments,
and presenting challenges. They utilize resources such as the Dungeon MasterБ─≥s
Guide and Monster Manual, which provide additional rules and guidance for worldбґ
building, encounter design, and maintaining game balance 3.

Ability Score Assignment Methods
Assigning ability scores is a fundamental step in creating a character in Dungeons &
Dragons 5th Edition (D&D 5e). The ability scoresБ─■Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelбґ
ligence, Wisdom, and CharismaБ─■determine a characterБ─≥s capabilities and influence their

Standard Array Method
The Standard Array method provides a balanced set of ability scores that players can

six abilities based on their character concept, ensuring a wellбґrounded character without
extreme strengths or weaknesses.

2.7.3
Rolling Method
The Rolling method introduces an element of chance into ability score assignment, leading
to unique character strengths and weaknesses. The most common rolling method in D&D
5e is as follows14:
1. Roll four sixбґsided dice (4d6) for each ability score.
2. Discard the lowest die roll from each set.
3. Sum the three highest rolls to get the ability score.
This method can result in higher or lower ability scores compared to the Standard Array
or Point Buy methods, adding unpredictability and excitement to character creation.

Б─╒ Combat: Tactical and turnбґbased, combat is often played on a grid map with miniaбґ
tures. Players must carefully consider their actions, using dice rolls to determine the

Б─╒ Exploration: This phase involves navigating the game world, uncovering secrets,

Б─╒ Roleplay: Roleplaying allows players to embody their characters fully, making deбґ
cisions and interacting with NPCs based on their characterБ─≥s personality and goals.
This phase is central to the storytelling aspect of TTRPGs 17.

4.11
System Usability Scale (SUS)
The System Usability Scale (SUS) is a standardized Questionnaire[9] designed to evalбґ
uate the usability of a system. Developed by John Brooke in 1986, SUS is a reliable
tool that provides a quick measure of a systemБ─≥s overall usability, even with small sample
sizes. It has been widely adopted in various fields due to its simplicity and effectiveness.
The SUS consists of ten statements that participants rate on a fiveбґpoint Likert scale rangбґ
ing from 1 (Strongly Disagree) to 5 (Strongly Agree). The statements are:
1. I think that I would like to use this system frequently.
2. I found the system unnecessarily complex.
3. I thought the system was easy to use.
4. I think that I would need the support of a technical person to be able to use this
system.
5. I found the various functions in this system were well integrated.
6. I thought there was too much inconsistency in this system.
7. I would imagine that most people would learn to use this system very quickly.
8. I found the system very cumbersome to use.
9. I felt very confident using the system.
10. I needed to learn a lot of things before I could get going with this system.
The scoring process for the SUS is as follows[10]:
Б─╒ For oddбґnumbered items, subtract 1 from the userБ─≥s response.
Б─╒ For evenбґnumbered items, subtract the userБ─≥s response from 5.
Б─╒ Sum the adjusted scores for each item. This will yield a total score out of 40.
Б─╒ Multiply the total score by 2.5 to convert it to a scale of 0 to 100.

Limitations and Future Work
While the research achieved significant progress, several limitations and areas for future
work were identified:
1. Measurement Comprehensiveness: Early prototypes did not address all measures
outlined in the final problem statement, focusing primarily on efficiency and effectiveness.
Future iterations should incorporate a more comprehensive evaluation framework from
the outset.
2. MVP Approach: Initial prototypes deviated from a true Minimum Viable Product apбґ
proach, potentially slowing down the iterative process. Future research could benefit from
a stricter adherence to MVP principles in early stages.
3. Local vs. Global Optimizations: The research primarily focused on local optimizaбґ
tions in early iterations, potentially missing opportunities for more significant global imбґ
provements. Future work could explore a balance between local and global enhanceбґ
ments from earlier stages.
4. LLM Integration: While the final prototype leveraged advanced LLM capabilities, earбґ
lier iterations could have benefited from integrating existing LLM solutions like ChatGPT
to accelerate development and insights.
5. Statistical Significance: The relatively low number of participants in each iteration
made it challenging to achieve statistically significant results in A/B testing. Future reбґ
search could benefit from larger sample sizes to draw more robust conclusions.
6. User Segmentation: While the tool showed improvements for both experienced and
inexperienced players, thereБ─≥s potential for further optimization by developing more tarбґ
geted solutions for specific user segments, particularly new players.
7. Efficiency Metrics: While time taken was a useful efficiency measure, it may not
fully capture the quality of the user experience, as engaged users might spend more time
exploring options. Future work could explore multiбґfaceted efficiency metrics that balance
speed with engagement and quality of outcomes.
These limitations provide valuable directions for future research, offering opportunities to
further refine and expand the capabilities of LLMбґpowered tools in enhancing the TTRPG
experience.
