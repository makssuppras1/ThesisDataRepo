Abstract
The world is moving away from heavy fuels and there is demand for alternatives fuels. The
maritime industry is looking to take the ’¼ürst step in using fuels with a low Global Warming
Potential(GWP). The maritime industryŌĆÖs answer to alternative fuel is DME(Dimethyl Ether),
which has a low GWP and it is relatively easy to transport.
The current solution for transporting DME is the traditional cylindrical pressure vessels. The
cylindrical shape of the pressure vessels is not optimal in the maritime environments where
space is scares and all the transport containers are rectangular.
A rectangular cuboid shaped pressure vessel will have more available space which more fuel can
be carried. This problem is solved by topology optimization and by combining di’¼Ćerent methods
together. Method of Moving Asymptotes(MMA) [1] is used to optimize the design, combined
with other methods such as Method of Robust optimization [2], method using Flexible void [3]
and some other methods the cuboid pressure vessel is designed.
A 24 percent increase in the 2D cross section area is achieved by using combining the theories.
Some studies are done in order to understand the di’¼Ćerent parameters used in the optimization
and getting the best suitable design. The program can be used as a tool for designing the 2D
model of the pressure vessel and the input parameters can be changed to suit each speci’¼üc
problem.

Motivation
The current fuel solution for the most merchant vessels are oiled based. As the world turns
more towards green solutions, companies like M├”rsk wish to move away from marine gas oil
due to the high CO2 commissions, therefore they are looking for a new alternative fuel that can
replace the marine oil. Currently there are a lot of ongoing researches on the alternative gasses
such as methanol, ethanol, ammonia and DME(Dimethyl Ether). Out if these alternatives
DME stands out the most due to its unique properties. DME is a non toxic propellant with low
Global Warming Potential(GWP), currently DME is used in some products such as hairsprays.
One of the most important properties of DME is that it becomes lique’¼üed below -25 degrees or
above 5 bar of pressure, compared to other gasses like methanol or methane, 5 bar of pressure
is very low which is important in transportation of it. Another important property of DME is
its heating value. DME has a lower heating value of 29MJ/kg which is lower than its competi-
tors i.e. methane. The small lower heating value of DME means that it does not require pilot
ignition. Gasses like methanol or methane require pilot ignition and donŌĆÖt ignite by them selves.
The Chinese have some experience with DME running engines. They have had DME based
busses since 2005 for reducing the air pollution. Due to the high cost of of DME production
they lost the battle against fossil diesel and LPG. Now the main goal is changed to reduction
of global warming where DME is cheaper compared to other green fuels.
The maritime sector is the ’¼ürst mover in the industry. Therefore an important challenge is
to be able to carry DME without sacri’¼ücing any space for containers. There are two types
of tank systems available for carrying DME, insulated tank system and modular tank system.
The insulated tank system is very expensive compared to the modular tank system, therefore
a modular tank system is preferred. The currant modular tank systems are cylindrical. The
cylindrical tanks are not e’¼ācient in use of space. As space is scarce in a vessels, a rectangular
cuboid is the optimal solution for the modular tank systems. The current cylindrical vessels
are put in a rectangular cuboid for easier transportation as shown in ’¼ügure 1.
