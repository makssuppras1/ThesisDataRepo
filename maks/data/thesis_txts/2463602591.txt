Abstract
In powder bed fusion (PBF), one of the most complex systems is the powder handling
system. In this project, the work proposes a new strategy to simplify the powder
handling system by ﬂuidisation. A design phase was initiated to come up with multiple
solutions ideas and to create a concept. The ﬁnal design proposed ﬂuidisation by
linear vibrations and rendered away the need for a powder feed and a powder overﬂow
system. Calibration of the system ensured valid data. Experiments were carried out
to characterised the powder handling system. The investigations revealed that voltage
and the mass added to the system had an impact on the amplitude of the vibrations.
Finally, a proof of concept was carried out, and it was found to be possible to submerge
a build plate into ﬂuidised sand powders, however, this was not the case with 316L
powder in which case the solenoids was not powerful enough.

Introduction 1
PBF is the additive manufacturing (AM) method by which powder is selectively
welded to form solid matter. One of the most complex subsystems in such a machine is
the powder handling system. This project presents the work to simplify the subsystem
by proposing a methodology which is fundamentally diﬀerent from what currently
exists.
In PBF the function of the powder handling systems (Figure 1.1) is to deliver powder
to the build chamber. A sieve (1) processes the powder before entering the build
chamber (2) to eliminate debris and big particles. The powder feed mechanism evenly
distributes the powder layer. The entire system is held in an inert atmosphere.
Figure 1.1: Powder handling system from SLM solutions[1].
The four critical challenges in PBF powder handling comprise of; safeguarding powder
quality; reducing manual handling tasks; establishing fast, controlled and repeatable
processes; and ensuring containment of the materials used.
By conﬁning the powder to only one chamber, it would eliminate the purpose of
an overﬂow system and a powder feed mechanism.
This project considers a new
principle for powder handling: Fluidisation. Fluidising the powder will enable the
build plate to be submerged into the powder in a similar fashion to the principle of
Stereolithography as seen in Figure 1.2. When the build plate has reached its new
position, the ﬂuidisation stops, and the powder transforms from a liquid state to a
==PAGE:18==

solid state. A laser will then fuse the current layer with assistance from a powder bed
heat source.
Figure 1.2: The principle behind Steolithograhpy.[2]
In order to achieve ﬂuidisation, either aeration or vibrations will be used. The ad-
vantage and disadvanges of each of these two methods will be investigated through a
literature study in order to determine which system to employ.

Scope of the
project 2
In powder handling systems the powder is transferred to its destination by mechanical
mechanisms. A feed hopper delivers a speciﬁed amount of powder(Figure 2.1). The
recoater transfers the powder towards the build plate and evenly distributes a ﬁne
layer. The recoater continues to remove the excess powder to the overﬂow system.
Feed hopper
Recoater
Over ow
Figure 2.1: The principle of a PBF powder handling system.
This consideration leads to the main hypothesis of this work: Will it be possible to
render these steps, and as a result of this, simplify the PBF powder handling system
by ﬂuidisation? The principle drawing (Figure 2.2) shows the ﬂuidisation of a powder
bed, changing the rheology of the powder. By introducing a method used to create a
ﬂuidised state of solid particles, it would make it possible to submerge a build plate.
Figure 2.2: The ﬂuidisation could remove the need for some of the mechanical mechanisms such as the feed hopper
and the overﬂow system.
In PBF there are several variables that can alter the properties of the powder and
debilitate the quality of the powder - one of these eﬀects is spatter[3]. When welding,
spatter forms and travels away from the melt pool into the powder resulting in debris.

3.2 Fluidisation
The ﬂuidisation process is when powdered solids behave like ﬂuids by introducing a
gas or liquid up through a container ﬁlled with solids or by vibrating the powder.
This process is extensively used in industry. The application of ﬂuidisation can be
divided into two categories [7]
• physical operations, such as transportation, heating, absorption, mixing of ﬁne
powders
• chemical operations, such as reactions of gases on solid catalysts and reactions
of solids with gases
The process is also used in oil reﬁnery plants as it is an excellent contacting method.
It is suitable for both small and large scale operations, and one of the advantages is
the eﬃciency of mixing the particles.[7]

the frictional forces, which increase as the ﬂuid ﬂow will increase, nothing happens.

drag-force equals the weight of the particles. If pressure continues to rise, then the
particles will levitate due to the ﬂuid ﬂow, and the separation distance of the particle
will increase as well. An equation has been made to describe the force balance across

The repetitive motion of objects relative to a stationary frame of a nominal position
also often mentioned as the equilibrium or a reference position is the study of vi-
bration[10]. Vibrations are everywhere, and often a phenomena engineers will try to
avoid or minimise as much as possible. Nonetheless, vibrations can also be used as a
tool to ﬂuidise a powder or to optimise a process, such as segregation[11].
The phenomena vibrations are explained physically by an interplay between the ki-
netic and the potential energy. More speciﬁcally, the vibrating system stores potential
energy in a component and releases that energy as kinetic energy as a motion causing
the vibration.[10]

A vibration can be categorised into three diﬀerent types of vibrations; free vibrations,
forced vibrations and damped vibrations.

indeﬁnitely.
The solution to prevent this, is to absorb the energy by introducing
damping.
Damping will result in the oscillation eventually dying out due to the
energy loss.

Whilst introducing vibrations into a powder handling system can cause the metal

voids where the smallest particles will travel to ﬁll the voids. Over time this process
will force the larger sized particles to "travel" up towards the surface, and there will
be a ﬁne line which separates the small particles from the larger particles.[13]
Three essential factors are believed to enable the BNE
• Particle size
• Particle shape
• Density
Ludewig and Vandewalle showed in their experiment that BNE is reversible; but also
a possibility to mix the diﬀerent sized powders. [13]
However, introducing vibrations is one eﬀect that might give an disadvantage, the
BNE. The vibrations will make powder bed more dense but if it results in a segregation
and hereby remove the ratio of the diﬀerent powder sizes.
Ultrasonic vibrations
Ultrasonic vibrations can be used to ﬂuidise powder and has already been proven by
several studies. Nonetheless, the studies reveal that the sound levels need to be high;
in the most extreme cases, the sound levels reach up to 150db. Thus, there have
been various levels at which optimum ﬂuidisation occurs; giving no concrete frame of
reference.
However, there is one explanation for this divergence in result, which is that the
powders used in the experiments had varying mass hence, the did not achieve same
data. In the experiments, the powders used are characterised as nanopowder with a
diameter less than 100nm. Another ﬁnding was that some materials would not be
aﬀected by ultrasonic vibrations. [14]
Concluding the literature study of ultrasonic vibrations can create a ﬂuidisation of a
powder bed, there are some keys ﬁndings:
• The particles size are in nanoscale < 100nm
• Not all materials can be ﬂuidised by ultrasonic vibrations
• The sound will be in a range which is dangerous to the human ear

Discussion 6
This project has intended to create a new strategy for PBF. A strategy where ﬂuidis-
ation is introduced to render away some of the mechanism of the commercial systems.
This led to a study on how to generate ﬂuidisation by vibrations. Brainstorming was
used to come up with ideas of which one was proposed and designed. The design had
to be able to function as an experimental platform by itself and be able to connect
to the experimental PBF platform. Calibrations and experiments ensured collection
of trustworthy data and allowed for testing the proof of concept.
6.1 Design
The powder handling system is designed to be able to function independently without
needing to be implemented in the experimental PBF. The idea of the design was to
eliminate the need for a powder feed mechanism and the overﬂow system.
Powder feed mechanism
By having the amount of powder needed for a task in the build chambert he feedstock
could be removed from the design. With a solution like this, the downtime could
minimised if the powder chamber in a new design iteration is created as a detachable
system. This allows for having multiple containers that can be prepared, with diﬀerent
powder materials. There will be no cleaning and furthermore, the downtime is close
to the time of changing the chamber.
Overﬂow system
The powder handling system has the correct amount of powder from the beginning
of the task why there is no need for an overﬂow system. Some might argue that the
advantages of an overﬂow system in this case as the excess powder in the powder
handling system is contaminated by debris. To be able to reuse the powder left in
the powder chamber after a ﬁnished task, it will need to be sieved.
==PAGE:69==
53
6.2 Experiments
Other arguments
By controlling the vibrations, there might be a possibility to control a segregation
sequence by looking at the study of RBNE[13]. Oversized particles and debris would
submerge to the bottom of the powder.
6.2 Experiments
To test the design of the powder handling system, a test setup with an accelerometer
to measure the vibrations was used. This was used to ﬁnd out the eﬀect of the voltage,
the eﬀect of the mass added and to ﬁnd the frequencies where the amplitudes reached
its maximum.
The voltage is a parameter that changes the eﬀect of the amplitude. A power supply
with the possibility of delivering much higher voltages would be a must. A more
in-depth study of the voltage .could led to a good approximation of which voltages
should be used according to the mass
A study of the mass showed that adding mass would act similarly to a damper as the
amplitudes decrease. It was evident that moving more mass equals more energy. It
revealed that the change in mass would change the optimal frequency as the weight
makes the peaks shift to the left with added mass.
6.2.1 Observations
The experiments conducted in this project, some observations were made. With 46V,
it was possible to observe frequencies that would make the powder travel, indicating
that there might have been some ﬂuidisation when using sand. 316L did move; how-
ever, this is more believed to be a normal reaction due to vibrations than actually a
ﬂuidised stage.
The phenomena BNE was observed with both of the powders. When the powder
chamber was cleaned and added with a new type of powder, the layer was of ﬁne
particles. As the test was running, larger particles would reach the surface, which
is why this eﬀect is something that needs attention. The literature dictates that a
speciﬁc ratio of diﬀerent sized particles is needed to increase the density of a powder
bed. This ratio could be eliminated due to the segregation of the BNE.
6.3 Fluidisation
In this project, it could not be proven that the vibrations caused the particles to
ﬂuidise. The sand showed some ﬂuidisation by observing how the sand started to
==PAGE:70==
6 Discussion
54
travel in the chamber; nonetheless, there was no scientiﬁc equipment to prove this
fact. A viscometer would ﬁt the purpose of measuring the eﬀect of the vibrations.
Fluidisation could eliminate one more mechanism of the commercial powder handling
systems - the recoater. If an optimal ﬂuidisation occurs in the powder bed, then the
powder starts to behave similarly to water. This should be possible to level the surface
and as a result of this, simplify the system even more. If the linear vibrations are
powerful enough, the vibrations could fade out as a sinus wave levelling the surface
as the loose particles would travel into the nearby voids.

Conclusion 7
This project has proposed a new powder handling strategy for PBF. The ﬁnal solution
was obtained though a design phase resulting in a system that ﬂuidises powder by
vibrations. Calibration of the device ensured valid data. Experiments were carried
out to characterise the system. Finally a proof of concept was conducted. In the
scope of the project four research questions were proposed and this is the conclusion
of this work:
I By simplifying the powder handling system by ﬂuidisation two mechanical mech-
anisms was eliminated; the feed stock and the overﬂow system.
II Powder can be ﬂuidised by linear vibration. The proof of concept was carried out
with sand and 316L. The build plate successfully submerged into sand, however,
it did not submerge with 316L powder.
A more powerful solenoid system is
expected to solve this.
III The powder handling system is redesigned with two puposes; to stand as an
independent experimental platform and to be installed in the experimental PBF
platform.
IV It was possible to construct and demonstrate the new powder handling strategy
within the framework.

Future work 8
The work of this project has led to some questions that could be answered by future
work.
8.1 BNE
Studying the BNE and how it aﬀects the density of the powder bed. In the ﬁrst
phase, a transparent chamber could show if segregation occurs. Next step would be
MRI scanning of the bed.
8.2 Measuring ﬂuidisation
Measure the vibrations capability of ﬂuidising powder by the use of a viscometer
8.3 Cymatics
Cymatics to levelling the surface. Diﬀerent patterns are formed at diﬀerent frequen-
cies. This could be a method to control the surface of the powder.
8.4 Sanding
In the welding process of PBF, small defects can occur due to balling, dross formation
or due to loose powder getting sintered to the sides of the workpiece unintendedly
due to the fusion process. It should be tested if the vibrations could act as a sanding
mechanism. If experiments show that sanding generated by vibrations works, the
powder handling system will have an internal ﬁnishing operation.
