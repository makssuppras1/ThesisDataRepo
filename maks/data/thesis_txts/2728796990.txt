Abstract 
Cardiovascular diseases are associated with a wide spectrum of challenges, resul8ng in high 
mortality rate, aﬀec8ng overall health, and quality of life globally. Nitric oxide (NO) acts as a 
vasodilator, improves blood ﬂow, and regulates blood pressure, all of which is essen8al in 
preven8ng cardiovascular disease, making it an important drug target. Herein, metal-based 
nanozymes with the poten8al to trigger NO-release from endogenous and synthe8c NO-
donors were inves8gated. A successful synthesis of gold nanopar8cles (AuNPs), pla8num 
nanopar8cles (PtNPs), and cerium oxide nanopar8cles (CeO2NPs) was reported, and their 
crystalline structure, as well as size, charge, and valency were inves8gated. All three 
nanozymes have demonstrated the ability to trigger NO-release from two diﬀerent S-
nitrosothiols and diazeniumdiolates. The NO-triggering ac8vity was retained in presence of 
human serum, with the PtNPs exhibi8ng strongest ac8vity amongst the three nanozymes. 
Subsequently, encapsula8on and coa8ng strategies of the PtNPs were explored. Two 
diﬀerent PtNP-containing plaLorms were synthesized. A layer by layer method allowed to 
synthesize poly(lac8c-co-glycolic acid) (PLGA)/poly-L-lysine par8cles coated with PtNPs. 
Furthermore, using the double emulsion solvent evapora8on method resulted in synthesis 
of PLGA-encapsulated PtNPs. Both strategies proved to be eﬃcient as Pt-carrying plaLorms 
and were able to demonstrate NO-triggering ac8vity.

4 CONCLUSION 
 
Cardiovascular diseases remain one of the leading causes of mortality, emphasizing the need 
to develop new approaches to improve the treatment strategies. Lowered NO biosynthesis is 
the main causa8ve agent contribu8ng to development of endothelial dysfunc8on, and other

novel pla8num-based nanozyme, with the ability to trigger NO release from endogenous

experimental condi8ons, the PtNPs were able to trigger NO release under physiological 
condi8ons and retained their ac8vity in PLGA-encapsulated and -coated form.  
 
Ini8ally, three diﬀerent NZ were synthesized and characterized. Synthesis of AuNPs, PtNPs 
and CeO2NPs proved to be successful, and resulted in monodisperse NZs with the size of <10 
nm. The presence of par8cles and their crystallinity was conﬁrmed using TEM, XRD, and XPS, 
which were in accordance with previously reported values.  
NO-triggering ac8vity was achieved using diﬀerent NO-donors, with amount of the ac8vity

RSNOs, with AuNPs demonstra8ng the highest NO-triggering ac8vity from GSNO, and PtNPs

Despite possible interac8ons with serum, all three NZs were able to demonstrate NO-
triggering ac8vity in presence of human serum, with PtNPs demonstra8ng highest ac8vity 
amongst the three NZs. Based on this, PtNPs were chosen as the most NO-ac8vity containing 
NZ and were further inves8gated.  
Encapsula8on strategies were of interest due to the small size of the PtNPs, which upon 
intravenous delivery would lead to a short circulatory half-life. Our results have 
demonstrated a successful LbL strategy for produc8on of PLGA/PLL/Pt-NPs as a PtNP
