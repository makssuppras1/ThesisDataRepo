Abstract
Solar energy is a renewable and clean energy source. Heat pumps are also an energy┬¡efficient
and sustainable way of producing energy, the integration of heat pumps and solar collectors
can achieve complementarity in energy production. Besides, a storage tank can improve the
utilization of thermal energy. Thus, in the Danish city ├ÿrum, a solar district heating system
integrated with an air┬¡to┬¡water heat pump, an accumulation tank, and gas boilers was built.
Therefore, the data processing and analysis of the ├ÿrum district heating plant were conducted
in this masterÔÇÖs project. The original data was pre┬¡processed using Python, and various indi┬¡
cators such as solar collector efficiency, heat pump COP, and solar fraction were proposed to
comprehensively evaluate the overall thermal performance of the system. By analyzing energy
flows, synergistic temporal and spatial operation strategies for different components were de┬¡
termined. Additionally, this project established a coupled model in TRNSYS. Comprehensive
validation of all models and connections, including solar collectors, thermal storage tanks, and
air source heat pumps, was conducted based on measured data.
The data analysis is based on the measured data in 2020, 2021, and 2022, which includes
the stage with heat pump operation and without heat pump operation. During the three years,
the solar collector field had the highest yearly collector efficiency at 36.33% in 2022, and the
highest yearly COP of the heat pump reached 3.54 in 2021. Furthermore, the energy diagrams
for each year illustrate the energy structure became more sustainable. The HP covered over
50% of heat demand in 2021 and 2022, while the heat contribution of gas boilers decreased
from 70% to less than 25%.
The TRNSYS model validation is necessary to ensure the accuracy of the parameters setup.
After validating the collector models, it was found that the simulated collectors had an average
of 10% higher outlet temperature. It is considered to be caused by the soiling, aging of the
collectors, etc. A control strategy of the solar loop was also discussed. The solar collector
field starts working when the solar radiation reaches 200 W/m2. There is an internal circulation
before the collectors supply heat to the heat exchanger with a constant flow rate of 46 m3/h. An
optimal flow rate control model was built based on an outlet temperature┬¡controlled strategy in
TRNSYS, and it shows that solar heat production could be promoted by a maximum value of
36% in a month.
This thesis is the first comprehensive investigation of the ├ÿrum district heating plant, and it not
only serves as the database for future research but also provides experience for future design.

Introduction
Heating is a necessary way to create a comfortable indoor environment in winter. Still, the
CO2 emission caused by the heating source should be more concentrated at the same time.
Globally, heat accounts for nearly half of building energy consumption and 40% of energy┬¡
related carbon dioxide emissions[1]. As a renewable power source, solar energy has a vital
role in reducing greenhouse gas emissions and mitigating climate change[2]. Comparing the
footprint of different types of generation sources[1], it was shown that solar thermal only had
the lowest CO2 equivalent per kWh of heat at 10┬¡35, while gas boiler and oil boiler had higher
value at 210┬¡380 and 210┬¡550, respectively.
In 2021, heating accounted for 50% of energy demand in the global market, but only 11% of
the heating energy is supplied by renewable energy [3]. District heating contributes a large
percentage of the heating supply all over the world, especially in Denmark (45%) [4]. However,
fossil fuels still dominate district network supplies globally (about 90% of total heat production),
especially in the two largest markets of China and Russia. Solar thermal utilization currently has
a relatively low share in overall energy consumption. Nevertheless, with abundant and limitless
solar resources, it holds promising prospects for expediting the transition to carbon neutrality.
Thus, combining solar energy with district heating is a good way to realize decarbonization.
1.1
The development of solar heating
A solar collector is a device that collects or concentrates solar radiation from the sun [5]. Nowa┬¡
days it basically includes flat plate collectors, evacuated┬¡tube collectors and concentrating col┬¡
lectors. Flat plate collectors and evacuated┬¡tube collectors are normally used to collect heat for
space heating, domestic hot water, or cooling with an absorption chiller [6], and the temperature
range of the collector fluid is usually around 50 ┬░C to 110 ┬░C. Concentrated collectors such as
parabolic trough collectors and solar towers are more likely to be used in solar power plants
to generate electricity by heating a heat┬¡transfer fluid to drive a turbine connected to an elec┬¡
trical generator [6] because the collector fluid temperature can be heated up to 400 ┬░C. Large┬¡
scale solar thermal systems can be centrally controlled and distributed, and the series┬¡parallel
connection of collectors can achieve the dual optimization of hydraulic and temperature perfor┬¡
mance. Large┬¡size collectors offer better economic benefits, making them suitable for gradually
replacing fossil fuels in regional heating systems and reducing carbon emissions. Large┬¡scale

The IEA Solar Heating & Cooling Technology Collaboration Program was established in 1977,
and over 10 million solar heating systems have been installed in Europe after many years of
research [7]. The application of solar heating has been developed from individual systems on
houses to large plants, also the type of solar collectors applied in heating plants varies from flat
plate collectors to hybrid collectors. As solar heating technology develops, more and more re┬¡

energy consumption caused by building services. According to the energy transition strategies
from the Danish energy and supply sector, solar district heating (SDH) is helpful to achieve the
target of covering the whole of net energy consumption with renewable energy by 2050 [8].
As early as 1997, a central solar heating plant was set in operation at Marstal to cover the
whole heating demand in the summer season and preheat the district┬¡heating medium outside
the summer season. The solar collector field was composed of 95% flat plate collectors and 5%
focusing collectors, vacuum tube collectors and roof┬¡integrated solar collectors. Then a rele┬¡
vant TRNSYS model was built to investigate the system thermal performance under alternative
control strategies [9]. In 2015, based on this plant and its corresponding model, a similar model
built by Thorsten Urbaneckto et al. demonstrated the solar fraction can be reached at about
10% in a cogeneration┬¡dominated east Germany city [10]. With the development of technolo┬¡
gies, larger and more complicated solar district heating plants were built to enlarge the systemÔÇÖs
solar fraction. Taars hybrid solar district heating plant was constructed by Aalborg CSP in 2016,
and it is the first large┬¡scale demonstration project worldwide with flat plate collectors (FPC)
and parabolic trough collectors (PTC) for district heating [11]. The yearly solar heat yield of
FPCs and PTCs reached 450 kWh/m2 and 354 kWh/m2 for the period Sep. 2015 to Aug. 2016,
respectively.
1.2
Solar district heating with assisted heat pump
The heat pump is also an energy┬¡efficient way of providing heat. At an ambient temperature
of 8 ┬░C, the coefficient of performance (COP) of air┬¡source heat pumps typically ranges from
between 2.0 and 5.4═¥ the COP range changes to 1.1 to 3.7 when the ambient temperature is
┬¡8 ┬░C [12]. According to the weather condition in Denmark [13], the air temperature during the
winter maintains around 0 ┬░C so that the air┬¡source heat pumps can work in a relatively high
COP under winter conditions. Furthermore, this kind of electricity┬¡driven heat pumps have the

potential to be more economical than gas boilers due to high COP.
Thus, it is beneficial to combine the heat pump with solar heating systems. The relationship
between solar heating and heat pump in a coupled solar heating/heat pump system can be
defined as three types: no interaction, moderate interaction and strong interaction and integra┬¡
tion. Where no interaction means the parallel operation of the solar collector and heat pump.
There are many advantages of solar heating combined with a heat pump, such as an alternative
to concepts using very large heat storage, increased solar fraction for heating and hot water,
higher inlet temperature to the heat pump, and also the direct use of solar energy for hot water
or heating when the temperature rises up higher enough.
The coupling solar heating and heat pump system can be designed on a small scale which only
services a single┬¡family house, but also it can be set up on a large scale with many system
components. Thus, lots of research was done to explore the systemÔÇÖs performance. Li et al.
did investigations on the technical and economic performance of winter heating systems of air
source heat pump┬¡assisted solar evacuated tube water heaters in a single building located in
Lvhua Village, Lanzhou City. The results show that in the whole heating season, air source heat
pump┬¡assisted solar energy systems greatly improved technical and economic performance in
comparison with heating by solar water heater and air source heat pump [14]. As mentioned
before, in a large┬¡scale coupling system, the heat pump may have no interaction, moderate
interaction and strong interaction with solar heating. On the one hand, heat pumps can generate
heat and supply it directly to the heating network. In 2019, a solar┬¡assisted ground source heat
pump was designed to meet the heat demand of space heating in winter and cooling in summer
for all buildings in a village located in north China[15]. The heat pump extracted heat from the
soil via borehole heat exchangers during winter, and it can release heat to the soil in summer.
On the other hand, since heat production and heat demand are not matched at every moment,
a heat pump system is able to play an important role in heat storage. Dronninglund solar district
heating plant in Denmark was built in 2016, the system has a supporting seasonal heat storage
system which was conducted by a water pit. The hot water was supplied by a solar plant and
auxiliary boilers, and the hot water produced by the solar plant was also used to increase the
temperature of the upper┬¡part water in the pit. It is worth mentioning that the heat pump can
decrease the return water temperature to below 20 ┬░C, which means it enlarged the maximum
heat capacity of the water pit thermal energy storage system and brought higher utilization
efficiency of the solar collectors [16]. Thus, the aim of seasonal storage of solar energy was
achieved with the help of heat pumps.

1.3
Technical and economic analysis method
Not only are the technologies of renewable energy supposed to be concerned, but also thermal┬¡
economic optimization should be done to improve the systemÔÇÖs feasibility under real working
conditions. The IEA SHC task 54 focused on the price reduction of solar thermal systems, and
price reduction can be considered in the costs of material, component and system level [17].
Basically, the total cost consists of the initial investment and the operative cost. Bellos et al. [18]
did the energetic and financial evaluation of a solar┬¡assisted heat pump space heating system.
The calculation was based on the simulation results from TRNSYS, and the evaluation index
was selected as the total cost, which is the sum of the capital cost of every investment and the
variable cost for all the investment years. Furthermore, an advanced method is to calculate the
Levelized cost of heat (LCOH), which is more helpful to make decisions on systems planning and
design based on the optimization routine. Besides the cost investigations, the net LCOH and
system LCOH were calculated based on a TRNSYS┬¡GenOpt model for a solar district heating
plant with both FPC and PTC technologies in Denmark [19]. The calculation was in the light of
optimal design, which was helpful to reduce the cost of the installation and increase the thermal
performance of large solar heating plants simultaneously.
As there are some large solar heating plants connected to district heating networks that have
been of great success in Europe, especially in Denmark [20], analyzing the measured data of a
mature commercial solar district heating plant can provide the experience to set up the system
operation strategies. Besides, dynamic simulation is necessary to verify the feasibility during
the design stage, and it also extends the data range for system performance evaluation. Then,
the corresponding economic evaluation should be done based on the obtained data to show
the systemÔÇÖs potential on the market.
Briefly, it is worthwhile to investigate the thermal and economic performance of a system that is
combined with SDH and heat pump. A solar district heating system was newly established in
├ÿrum, Denmark. The main system components consist of a solar collector field, a tank thermal
heat storage, an air┬¡to┬¡water heat pump, and auxiliary gas boilers. To investigate the system
performance of this solar district heating plant, this paper will document the following aspects:
1) Measured experimental data analysis in system thermal performance and energy structure═¥
2) TRNSYS simulation of the SC loop, data validation═¥
3) SC loop flow rate control strategy analysis and optimization.

2.1
System introduction
In the Danish city ├ÿrum, the local district heating facility has an area designated for eco┬¡friendly
energy production. Initially, this area consisted of a solar collector (SC) field, an accumulation
tank and a gas boiler. The SC field can fulfill the heat demand during the summer, and auxiliary
gas boilers are used to reach the increasing heat demand during winter. A heat pump (HP)
was added to the system to reduce the usage of gas┬¡fired engines/boilers throughout the year.
There is also an economizer (Eco) added to the whole system, which is designed to utilize the
hot water from the tank to boost the performance of the HP compressor. By integrating the HP
with the solar thermal system, the two systems are able to boost each otherÔÇÖs performance.

2.2
Solar collector field
The SC field at ├ÿrum consists of 506 Arcon┬¡Sunmark HT┬¡Solarboost flat plate collectors and
covers an area of 6355.36 m2. The layout of the SC field is shown in Figure 2.4, and the technical
parameters of the SC are shown in Table 2.3. The 506 SCs were divided into 46 arrays, these
46 arrays provide the heated mixture of glycol/water (30%) to the heat exchanger in parallel. In

Future work
Though the numerical analysis of measured data of the main loops in the whole SDH plant
was investigated, some detailed exploration could still be carried out in the future. The anti┬¡
frozen loop in the SC field had an unignorable impact on the heat production of the SC field in
wintertime. By applying proper anti┬¡frozen operation in the SC loop, the potential deduction in
SC heat generation caused by rain, snow and high┬¡temperature difference will be minimized.
Thus, it is recommended to have a detailed analysis of the strategy of anti┬¡frozen loop control.
As mentioned in Chapter 1, it is also valuable to study the carbon emission in 2020, 2021, and
2022 in the whole system. The performance of each component can be compared by evaluating
the carbon emission per MWh heat output, especially the gas boiler and the HP, and then the
whole system is evaluated. By studying the carbon emission in the components as well as the
overall performance of the system, it can be seen how much carbon emission was reduced over
the 3 years. Furthermore, The LCOH of the whole system is also worth studying, because it is
beneficial for system design optimization.
As for the simulation work in TRNSYS, the tank model could be investigated more accurately.
As mentioned at the end of Chapter 4, the storage tank could have a significant impact on the
energy distribution to the whole system. An accurate tank model can be helpful in simulating
the end┬¡supplying temperature more accurately.
Furthermore, the model of HP would also be suggested for more investigation in the future. The
energy structure of the whole system is dependent on the performance of the HP because it
covered over 50% of the heat demand. By integrating more accurate SC, HP, and storage tank
models in TRNSYS, the whole system could be explored and optimized more technically and
economically.

Conclusion
The measured data analysis and TRNSYS simulation in this paper are the innovative study
of the ├ÿrum SDH plant. The most significant components in this plant are the SC field, air┬¡
to┬¡water HP, and storage tank. The measured data analysis is conducted to investigate the
system working principle and their contribution to the energy structure of the whole system.
The TRNSYS simulation is focused on the SC field thermal performance and its control strategy
study. The conclusions can be drawn as followed:
1. The solar heat production in 2020 and 2022 was higher than 3000 MWh, and the yield in
2021 was relatively low because of poor solar irradiance below 8000 MWh this year. The SC
field heat output in 2022 was approximately 1000 MWh higher than in 2021. The yearly SC field
efficiency is about 35%, and it was usually higher in summer. Most of the heat supplied to the
city was produced by the HP since 2021, the yearly heat output of the HP was higher than 7000
MWh in both 2021 and 2022. The yearly COP of the HP in 2021 and 2022 was about 3.6.
2. The storage tank was used for short┬¡term thermal storage, and the storage utilization cycle
increased from 21.39 to 36.26 from 2020 to 2022, because part of the stored heat was used
to increase the COP of the heat pump. There was about 15 MWh heat loss every year for
the tank. The stored thermal energy of the tank was almost supplied by the SC field, thus the
average water temperature was always kept at a high level during the summer. The high solar
heat production in 2022 made the tank be charged at 3505.45 MWh and discharged at 3499.63
MWh, which was the highest compared to 2020 and 2021.
3. The heat production of the gas boiler in this system was not only influenced by the gas
price, but also the heat supply ability of the SC field, HP, and tank. The gas boiler accounted
for over 70% of the total heat supply in 2020, and with the existence of the HP, it decreased
to only 21.27% in 2022. The HP became the main backup energy source in this system since
its installation and operation, which covered over 50% of heat demand in 2021 and 2022. The
whole system energy structure became more sustainable since the HP operation.
4. The SC validation in TRNSYS shows a 10 % higher outlet temperature than the measured
values. It is considered to be caused by the soiling, aging of the collectors, etc.
5. The SC loop start/stop control was determined by solar radiation. The SC loop starts work┬¡

duction was increased. The highest percentage of solar heat production promotion in 2022 was

In conclusion, this thesis can provide an analytical idea of the ├ÿrum SDH plant. This TRNSYS
