Abstract
To achieve longВӯterm greenhouse gas neutrality in aviation, replacing fossil aviation fuels
with Sustainable Aviation Fuels (SAF) from renewable sources is essential. SAF producВӯ
tion from reenwable Hydrogen and Carbon Dioxyde, was designed using Aveva Process
Simulation, followed by comprehensive economic and environmental assessments.
The designed process leads to an annual production of 37 kT of SAF, with 97% of the
molecules featuring a carbon chain length between 8 and 16. This output indicates a roВӯ
bust and targeted production capability.
It was found that the profitability of the plant aligns with other SAF studies, demonstrating
a Minimum Selling Price of 2.46$/kg after Heat Integration. Despite this, the price remains
higher than the current cost of fossil jet fuels, posing a competitive challenge.
Additionally, the environmental impact of SAF production was assessed. The primary metВӯ
ric evaluated was the carbon footprint, which emerged as slightly lower than that reported
in previous SAF production studies. This finding underscores the potential environmental
benefits of the designed process.
Therefore, the production of SAF using the methanol pathways appears to be an alterВӯ
native to other SAF production pathways in term of economical profitability and environВӯ
mental impact.

1
Introduction
1.0.1
Background
To limit global warming to 1.5В°C, the Paris Agreement of 2015 established goals for reducВӯ
ing greenhouse gas emissions [1]. The EU aims to gradually achieve carbon neutrality
by 2050.
The transportation sector is a major contributor to emissions, and the aviation field is no
exception, accounting for 2.5% of global emissions [2]. To achieve longВӯterm greenhouse
gas neutrality in aviation, replacing fossil aviation fuels with Sustainable Aviation Fuels
(SAF) from renewable sources is essential.
By 2025, SAF production will be predominantly through the Hydrotreated Esters and Fatty
Acids (HEFA) pathway, which will account for 99% of production (42.1 MT) [3]. The HEFA
process can reduce greenhouse gas emissions by up to 85% compared to fossil jet fuels,
whereas PowerВӯToВӯLiquid processes have the potential to achieve a 100% reduction [3].
One PowerВӯToВӯLiquid pathway involves using renewable hydrogen and carbon dioxide to
produce methanol, which can then be converted into SAF using the ExxonMobil process
[4]. Although SAF production from H2 and CO2 is still under development, it is necessary
to evaluate the economic and environmental potential of this process.
1.0.2
Objectives
The main purpose of this thesis is to evaluate the profitability and the environmental impact
of a process producing SAF from H2 and CO2. This is done by fulfilling the 5 objectives
listed below:
вҖў Process modeling of the plant from from H2 and CO2 to SAF on Aveva Process
Simulation.
вҖў Sensitivity analysis of key parameters of the process such as methanol formation
reactor or final distillation column of SAF.
вҖў Heat integration of the process and Heat Exchanger Network Synthesis for reduction
of the energy consumption of the plant.
вҖў Economical evaluation of the plant including sensitivity and uncertainty analyses of
the main inputs parameters such as Fixed Capital Cost, Operation Cost, Reactant
and SAF prices.
вҖў Life Cycle Assessment of the production of SAF from H2 and CO2.
1.0.3
Report structure
The report is structured as follows. The literature review summarizes existing SAF projects,
various production methods for SAF, and specific studies on methanol production from
hydrogen and carbon monoxide, as well as SAF production from methanol.
The second section details the methodological assumptions and input parameters for the
five listed objectives.
Finally, the results for these objectives are presented, evaluating the validity of the design,
the economic and environmental potential of the plant, by comparing these findings with
existing projects and studies.

Even though the development of Sustainable aviation fuels is still recent, several projects

MTOВӯMOGD process
In general, two main types of catalyst are used in MTOВӯMOGD process: ZSMВӯ5 or SAPOВӯ
34, SAPOВӯ34 deactivates more easily than ZSMВӯ5, which could cause mass production
issues. Additionally, SAPOВӯ34 usually requires to use the mixture of water and methanol

less susceptible to deactivation, does not require water in the feed and enables nearly
complete methanol conversion [33]. For the MTOВӯMOGD process, conversion reactor
are used, The conversion is based on experimental data over ZSMВӯ5 catalyst [4].

Sensitivity analysis
A sensitivity analysis needs to be performed on several parameters of the process. It
enables the identification of which input data has the greatest impact on the result. This
also helps to verify design decision and assumptions.
For this process, it has been decided to perform the sensitivity analysis on two primordial
equipments of the process. First, the methanol formation reactor necessitates a thorough
analysis. Several kinetics model are proposed in the literature, and it is important to
represent the reactor as close as possible to the reality. 2 different kinetics models are
then studied for the methanol reactor, as well as sensitivity analysis for several input
parameters and their impact on carbon conversion. In a second time, sensitivity analysis
has been performed on the last distillation column of the process. This column separates
the distillate from the kerosene product and has therefore a tremendous impact on the
quantity of final product and its purity.
3.2.1
Sensitivity tool
The sensitivity analysis has been performed thanks to a modified Python script provided
in the DTU Process Design class. The SimCentralConnect package is also used to be
able to modify parameters of the process on Aveva Process simulation with the python
script. The script works as such: a range of input parameters is entered and the wanted
output parameters computed by Aveva are then displayed with the variation of the input
parameters.

The FCI and working capital are directly taken from the economics library of Aveva ProВӯ

the process. CAPEX includes the purchase of the reactors, columns, heat exchangers,
compressors and pumps and the cost of installation of these equipments. A list of the
equipments of the process is presented in appendix. The total purchase cost is equal to
145.0 $million. It is assumed to be splitted equally between the first two years before
launching the plant.

This project has detailed the process modeling of SAF production from renewable hydroВӯ

The methanol formation reactor has been an important subject of this study with the comВӯ

5.0.1
Perspectives and potential next steps
Process modeling
Process modeling was based on RambГёll internal model for the methanol formation and
on the ExxonMobil process for the MTO and MOGD part. While the methanol formation
has been the subject of an inВӯdepth study, the MTO and MOGD process could be also
optimized. The reactors were modeled as conversion reactor based on the Ruokonen
article values [13], whereas the impact of catalyst and reactor volume could be studied by
using a detail kinetics model. Optimization has been performed on the methanol formation
reactor and the final distillation column, with potential for further optimization in other parts
of the process. For example, product yield could be increased by incorporating recycles
into the reactors. Additionally, the unsold fuel gas could be used as an alternative to
steam to reduce the OPEX. However, this would require an inВӯdepth analysis of the fuel
gas composition and the heat capacity of each component, as well as an assessment of
the impact on CAPEX due to the need for different heat exchangers.
Economical assessment
Even though the economical assessment has been supported by a MonteВӯCarlo SimulaВӯ
tion with literature based value for prices, prices of reactants and products may substanВӯ
tially vary in the future. This requires further studies with new prices. On the other hand,
a way of assessing more accurately the profitability of the project could be to include the
formation of Hydrogen and the Carbon Capture and Storage within the system boundВӯ
aries. Moreover, the prices of coВӯproduct was not part of a benchmark analysis similarly
to the reactant and SAF price: this prices may also vary tremendously in the future which
would require new economical assessment.
Some other parameters could be also studied further to assess the performance of the
plant. In a recent study, Bube et al [14] compared the carbon efficiency of the formation
of SAF between FischerВӯTropsch process and SAF from methanol. While the FT pathВӯ
way has a higher carbon efficiency for the total products (99% against 85%) the methanol
pathways remain more efficient when only the SAF is considered (76% against 72%).
This result is another argument in favor of the methanol pathway as an alternative to the
