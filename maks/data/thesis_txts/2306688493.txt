Resume 
I dette studie har vores mål været at undersøge hvordan de IL-17 producerende RORγt+ γδ T cellers 
population er fordelt mellem forskellige organer. Vi fandt frem til at frekvensen af disse celler er højere 
i huden og lamina propria, sammenlignet med de perifere lymfeknuder (pLK) og de mesenteriale 
lumfeknuder (mLK). I begge LK grupper fandt vi at Vγ4+ populationerne udtrykte mere CCR6 og 
CCD9, mens Vγ4¯ populationerne havde en højere frekvens af α4β7+ celler. Stimulation af γδ T celler 
isoleret fra pLKmed retinoic acid (RA), ledte til en forøget tarmsøgende fænotype med reduceret 
ekspression af CCR6 og øgede frekvenser af både CCR9+ og α4β7+ γδ17 T celler. Ydermere, efter fire 
dages stimulation var frekvensen af IL-17 producerende celler faldet. Vi vurderede funktionen af type I 
interferoner (Type I IFNs) på disse celler med brug af en interferon alpha receptor1 knockout model. 
Vi fandt ud af at den totale mængde af γδ17 T celler blev forøget i begge LK afdelinger mens kun 
Vγ4¯ populationen blev forhøjet i huden. Vi observerede også et tab i IL-17 produktionen i Vγ4+ 
populationen og et overordnet tab i IFNγ producerende celler under homeostasis. Efter påførelse af 
Imiquimod (IMQ) på ryghuden, observerede vi mindre rødmen og epidermis fortykning i INFAR1 
musene, samt en reduktion i IL-17 produktion fra Vγ4+ γδ T cellerne. Vi konkluderede deraf at type I 
interferoner virker som en positiv regulator for produktionen af IL-17 under IMQ påførelsen.

Abstract 
In the current study we aimed to investigate how the IL-17 producing RORγt+ γδ T cell (γδ17) 
populations are distributed between various organs. We found that the frequencies of these cells are 
higher in the skin and lamina propria compared to the peripheral lymph nodes (pLN) and mesenteric 
lymph nodes (mLN). Within both LN compartments we find that the Vγ4+ populations express more 
CCR6 and CCD9, while the Vγ4¯ populations had a higher frequency of α4β7+ cells. Stimulation of γδ 
T cells isolated from the pLN with retinoic acid (RA) led to an increased gut homing phenotype with 
reduced CCR6 expression and increased frequencies of both CCR9+ and α4β7+ γδ17 T cells. 
Furthermore, after four days of stimulation the frequency of IL-17 producing cells had decreased. We 
assessed the function of type I interferons (Type I IFNs) on these cells using an interferon alpha 
receptor1 knockout model. We found that the total number of γδ17 T cells was increased in the LNs 
while it only the Vγ4¯ population increased in the skin. We also observed a loss in IL-17 production in 
the Vγ4+ population and an overall loss in IFNγ producing cells during homeostasis. After Imiquimod 
(IMQ) application on the dorsal skin, we observed less reddening and epidermis thickening in the 
INFAR1 mice, together with reduced IL-17 producing by Vγ4+ γδ T cells. We thus conclude that type I 
interferons act as a positive regulator of IL-17 production during IMQ application.

Introduction 
Psoriasis is an autoimmune disease, and is reported to affect up to 10% of the population in certain 
countries1. There are five different types of psoriasis varying in both severity and location of the skin-
lesions2. It is still unknown what causes the actual disease onset, however two factors have been 
identified: The gamma delta T cells and type I interferons. The γδ T cells only consist of a relative 
small proportion of the total lymphocyte population; however they are highly localized in epithelial 
surfaces such as the skin. These cells are classified as innate-like lymphocytes and are thus able to 
respond directly to cytokine stimulation rather than being activated in the lymph nodes like the 
conventional T cells. Especially the IL-17 producing sup-set have been associated with the 
pathogenesis of psoriasis which are found in high numbers in the skin. There is still much we do not 
know about these cells, thus it is important to further expand our knowledge. Not only on their function 
and role during psoriasis and inflammation, but also on their overall function and distribution during 
homeostasis. In this study we aim to characterize the IL-17 producing sup-set in four different tissue 
locations: the skin, peripheral lymph nodes, mesenteric lymph nodes and the lamina propria based on 
frequency and expression of homing molecules. Furthermore, using an interferon alfa receptor1 
knockout mousemodel, we aim to understand how type I interferons affect these cells during 
homeostasis and inflammation. Inflammation will be induced using the commercial available 
Imiquimod crème which induces psoriasis-like skin lesions reducing the distance between the model 
and real life. 
1st. 
Chapter: γδ T cells   
1.1 The different γδ T cell sub populations and their phenotype 
The immune system consists of two main branches, the innate and the adaptive. The innate immune 
system acts as a first line of defense and is characterized as being fast and unspecific in its immune 
response. The adaptive immune response is initiated with the binding between professional antigen 
presenting cells (APCs) and antigen-specific naive T-cells. As opposed to the innate, the adaptive 
immune response is much slower but more accurate in its effect 3. 
The most abundant population of T cells in humans and mice are the alfabeta (αβ) T cells. These cells 
can be recognized based on their expression of the T cell receptor αβ (TCRαβ) and co-receptors such as 
CD4 and CD8 4 and divided into various sub-populations such as CD4+ T helper cells, CD8+ cytotoxic 
==PAGE:8==
Page | 8  
 
T cells, memory T cells, and regulatory T cells 3. Besides the αβ populations, there is also a less 
frequent T cell population expressing the gammadelta (γδ) TCR, which only accounts for 2-10% of all 
the circulating T cells in healthy humans and mice 5,6. 
The γδ T cells differ in their TCR subunit composition compared to the classic T cells, as it consists of 
a combination of a γ- and δ-chain 3. Variation in the recombination of Vγ and Vδ gene segments is part 
of the development of a diverse and flexible TCR repertoire 7. Additionally, while αβ T cells require 
presentation of foreign peptides through the major histocompatibility complex (MHC), γδ T cells are 
able to bind directly to the antigen superstructure, directing the intracellular signaling through the 
associated CD3 complex 5. As a result of this, the CD4 and CD8 co-receptors are uncommon in 
association with the TCRγδ 8.  
Another key feature of the γδ T cells is their capacity to rapidly activate and respond to an external 
stimulus together with the innate response. This ability to generate a fast paced response allows these 
cells to contribute to lymphoid stress surveillance, inflammation and autoimmunity 9. 
γδ T cells originate in the embryonic thymus, where they undergo their early developmental steps (this 
will be discussed in detail in sec 1.2) prior to migrating to the epithelial tissue where the final 
maturation is completed 10. As mentioned earlier, γδ T cells only represent a minor part of the total T 
cell population in the peripheral blood and lymphoid tissue, however they are abundant in certain 
epithelial tissue such as the skin, lungs and gut 11. Although the γδ T cells found in various tissue 
belong to the same lineage, they show great variation in their phenotype between the different organs 
12. The different γδ T cell populations can be characterized either through the expression of surface 
molecules or their cytokine profile. Additionally, the γδ T cell population can be separated into several 
different populations based on the TCR Vγ usage (Heilig and Tonegawa nomenclature 13). The 
different Vγ groups are often distributed in specific tissue locations, i.e. the Vγ1 and Vγ4 in the 
peripheral blood and lymph nodes 14, Vγ6 in the dermis 15, and the Vγ7 in the gut.

In the peripheral blood and lymph nodes (LN) of mice, the γδ T cells can roughly be separated into two 
populations (Fig 1a).  Population A can be recognized based on the master transcription factor retinoic-
related orphan receptor γt (RORγt) associated with the ability to produce and secrete interleukin-17

1.3 Skin γδ T cells 
Moving into the skin, the murine epidermis contains a large number of γδ T cells, approximately 2-4% 
of all the epidermal cells, called dendritic epidermal T cells (DETCs) and contribute to almost all the T 
cells found in the murine epidermis (>99%) 22. DETCs phenotypically express CD44 and are CD27−, 
despite that CD27  is associated with the IFNγ producing γδ T cells found in the lymphoid organs 23. 
Once activated, the DETC responds by secreting molecules such as IL-2, IFNγ, and tumor necrosis 
factor α (TNFα) 24. Besides having a function during inflammation and stress 22, the DETCs also have a 
function during homeostasis. Mice lacking DETCs have increased epidermal apoptosis among the 
keratinocytes compared to wild-type (WT) mice, indicating that these cells play a key role in 
maintaining a healthy barrier during homeostasis 25. Another crucial function of DETCs is their 
cytotoxic capability towards the natural killer-sensitive target YAC-1, firbosarcomas, melanomas, and 
transformed keratinocytes 26 giving these cells anti-cancer potential. Opposed to the murine epidermis, 
the human epidermis contains no equivalent to the DETC population, and only very few T cells, 0.5-
1% of the epidermal cells 27.  
Compared to the epidermis, the total T cell population in the dermis is lower (0.5-1%) and 50% of 
resident murine dermal T cells are of the γδ T cell linage 24. Additionally, the γδ T cell population in 
the dermis is not as homogenous as in the epidermis.  In the dermis the two main γδ T cell populations 
detected during homeostasis are the Vγ4+ and Vγ4¯. Unlike in the pLN these cells express no CD27 and 
can be recognized based on their expression of CCR6, and RORγt and their ability to secrete IL-17A. 
The Vγ4¯ population consists primarily of Vγ6+ γδ T cells. The dermal γδ T cells are self-renewing, as 
they are able to proliferate in the skin. Furthermore it has been shown that these cells are motile and 
able to move between the dermis and LNs 28,29.  
1.4 Gut derived γδ T cells 
Much like in skin, the γδ T cells in the intestine are localized to two different compartments; inside the 
epithelium membrane and in the lamina propia. The intraepithelial lymphocytes (IELs) in the murine  
small intestine consist of approxitmaly 50-60% γδ TCR-expressing T cells 30. These cells are involved 
in maintaining homeostasis of the intestinal epithelium, protecting against intestinal inflammation, 
==PAGE:11==
Page | 11  
 
restricting trafficking of symbiotic microbiota, and preservation of epithelial tight junctions during 
infection 30. In the lamina propria γδ T cells account for approximately 10-20% of all T cells 31. These 
cells are suspected to play an immunoregulatory role in the homeostasis of αβ T cells, as deletion 
studies have shown altered inflammatory responses in the αβ population 32. Additional shared 
characteristics are the capacity to produce IFNγ and cytotoxic killing potential 30. An important 
mediator for γδ IEL homing towards the intestinal epithelium is chemokine receptor 9 (CCR9). Studies 
with a defect in CCR9 have shown that the number of γδ IELs are reduced, while the amount of γδ17 T 
cells IEL precursors increases in the periphery 30. Similar observations have been done for the homing 
of conventional T cells towards the gut in which loss of expression of either CCR9 or α4β7 lead to a 
reduction in payer’s patch size as a result of fewer lymphocytes 33,34. 
The outer layers of the skin and the intestine both contain the IFNγ producing DETCs and IELs, 
compared to the IL-17 producing γδ T cells found in the dermis and lamina probria 35. Additionally, 
some subsets also have regulatory effects on neighboring cells. Thus, DETCs are able to regulate the 
apoptosis rate of other cells in the epidermis through the production of insulin-like growth factor 1 
(IGF-1) 25. Additionally, both the skin resident γδ T cells and the IELs of the gut are able to produce 
keratinocyte growth factor (KGF) after activation, in contrast to activated αβ IELs. KGF is part of the 
fibroblast growth factor (FGF) family, and is recognized through the FGF receptor (FGFR) expressed 
by the epithelial cells. Recombinant KFG is able to promote the growth and differentiation of epithelial 
cells. Especially the epithelial cells lining the gastrointestinal tract are highly responsive to this 36. This 
allows the γδ IELs to respond to the epithelium damage, and promote epithelial restitution to prevent 
infection through this barrier 37. 
1.5 γδ T cells and their role in diseases 
The pathogenesis of various autoimmune diseases has been connected to γδ T cells such as psoriasis, 
allergy, multiple sclerosis (MS), and rheumatoid arthritis (RA). In the dermis, the IL-17 producing 
RORγt+ γδ T cells have been connected to the development of psoriasis. Psoriasis is characterized by 
hyperproliferative keratinocytes and massive infiltration of leukocytes, due to the production and 
secretion of several different interleukins including IL-23, IL-21, IL-22 and IL-17 38. The IL-17 
producing γδ T cells have also been connected with RA, a chronic autoimmune disease that leads to the 
destruction of cartilage and bone joints 39. Interestingly, IL-17 also serves as a cytokine involved in 
==PAGE:12==
Page | 12  
 
bone-recovery and tissue healing, and have been detected at elevated levels immediately after bone 
injury after production by Vγ6+ γδ T cells 40. 
γδ T cells have also been associated with cancer, both as inducers of tumor growth and as targets for 
immunotherapy. The CD27¯ Vγ6 population was investigated in a murine model for ovarian cancer 
(ID8 transplantable cell line), and started to accumulate in the peritoneal cavity. From here they 
produced IL-17 which was found to be a key cytokine in the pathogenesis. They were able to recruit 
small peritoneal macrophages (SPM) to induce the tumor growth, thus counteracts the cancer 
immunesurveillance 41. Similarly a study in human breast cancer patients illustrated a correlation 
between the frequencies of γδ T cells in the tumor infiltration lymphocytes. They discovered that with a 
high frequency of γδ T cells in the tumor environment there was a negative correlation in the clinical 
outcome 42. Although some γδ T populations have been associated with the cancer pathogenesis, are 
others being heavily investigated as potential immunotherapy as they also induce protection towards 
cancer. For instance have Vγ4+ cells been identified as inducing protection against the aggressive B16 
melanoma, through the production of IFNγ and perforin. In the same study they also observed that the 
protection was highest when treated with Vγ4+ compared to Vγ1+ 43. Currently the most investigated 
therapeutic target is the human Vγ9Vδ2 strain due to its abundance in peripheral blood and availability 
for selective expansion and activation both in vivo and in vitro. This cell type can act both as an 
effector cell producing cytokines, but also as a potent antigen-presenting cell, thus priming the adaptive 
immunity as well 44. 
1.6 Development and maturation of γδ T cells 
γδ and αβ T cells share the same  pre-thymus development stages up to the double negative 2 (DN2) 
stage, in which the initial gene arrangement of the TCR begins 45,46. γδ T cell selection is followed by 
an upregulation of surface molecules such as CD27, before committing to this linage 45. One of the 
unique features of γδ T cells is their ability to induce specialized responses in numerus different tissue 
sites. This can be partly explained developmentally as they leave the thymus in separated waves 
embryonically and post-natally 47. During the first wave the epidermal DETCs leave the thymus, the 
second to the genital tract and the subsequent waves to tissue sites such as the lungs and gut 35. 
In the fetal thymus DETCs engage thymic epithelial cells (TEC) expressing Skint-1, which suppresses 
the expression of the SRY-related high mobility group box transcription factor 13 (SOX13) and RORγt 
==PAGE:13==
Page | 13  
 
that would otherwise lead to an IL-17 effector cell type. Instead they activate early growth response 
3(Eg3), nuclear factor of activated T cells (NFAT), and NF-κB regulatory networks committing the 
cells to an IFNγ producing cell type by promoting the expression of T-bet. Another result of this 
positive selection of the DETCs is the coordinated switch of several homing molecules; one of them 
being the upregulation of CCR10 and another downregulation of CCR6. Using a transgenic 
CCR10EGFP/EGFP knock-in mouse model, it was discovered that the seeding into the epidermis was 
severely impaired while an increased number of DETCs was found in the dermis. This proves the 
importance of CCR10 in epidermis migration in contrast to CCR6 for dermis migrations 15,48 
The second wave to leave the thymus is the Vγ6+ positive cells and interesting feature about these cells 
is that they all carry the Vγ5 gene as well 49. It was discovered that both the Vγ5 and Vγ6 γδ T cells 
originate from a common thymic lineage. These precursors would initially rearrange the Vγ5 gene and 
if this succeeds it would lead to expression on the surface. However, if the rearrangement is 
unsuccessful, the cell will have a chance to rearrange either Vγ5 or Vγ6 in the other allele 49. If Vg6 is 
rearranged these cells will be unable to engage Skint-1 leading to activation of SOX13 and eventually 
RORγt giving these cells an IL-17 effector cell type. SOX13 is part of a major box transition factor 
group together with SOX4, HNF1 homeobox A (TCF1) and lymphoid enhancer binding factor 1 
(LEF1). Development of Vγ6+ cells requires only the expression of SOX13, whereas a combination of 
SOX13 with SOX4 is essential for the development of IL-17 producing Vγ4+ cells. As such Skint-1 
acts as the mediator for the regulatory network deciding between either the IFNγ or IL-17 phenotype. 
Activation of this network was associated with a strong TCR signal and promotes the IFNγ population, 
whereas a weak signal was associated with the IL-17 population 50,51. 
A recent study by Muñoz-Ruiz et al. showed the importance of the TCR in development of specific γδ 
T cell effector populations. By using a CD3δ double knockout mice they show that the IFNγ producing 
γδ T cell population is depleted along with the IL-17 producing Vγ6 population due to the loss of TCR 
signaling 52. Furthermore, TCR signal strength is affects the thymic commitment to either the γδ versus 
αβ lineages 45. A strong signal through the TCR would result in a higher activation of the extracellular 
signal-regulated kinase-mitogen-activated protein kinase (ERK-MAPK) pathway, leading to an 
increased induction of Egr1 and Egr3 transcription factors and their target, Inhibitor of DNA binding 3 
(Id3). Following this sequence, the Id3 proteins are direct inhibitors of the transcription factor E2A 
==PAGE:14==
Page | 14  
 
which acts as a checkpoint regulator monitoring thymocyte development. Inhibition of E2A stalls the 
V(D)J recombination of the TCR, which is required for the successful development of these cells 53. 
Hence, a strong TCR signal would result in a larger accumulation of the Id3, resulting in a greater 
inhibition of the E2A 47 promoting the γδ lineage. Consistent with this Muñoz-Ruiz et al also found a 
reduced number of γδ thymocytes 52. 
Another factor which influences the development of γδ T cells is trans-conditioning. Trans-
conditioning is the interaction between double negative (DN) γδ T cells and double positive (DP) αβ T 
cells45. The DN γδ T cells responds to signals through the lymph toxin beta receptor (LTβR) delivered 
by lymphotoxin (LT) expressing DP αβ T cells 54. Depletion of the αβ T cell population only hindered 
the IFNγ producing γδ subset. This indicates that the interaction is only required for the development of 
this specific subset rather than complete γδ development. 
2nd. Chapter: Type I interferons 
As mentioned earlier, γδ T cells are classified as innate-like lymphocytes due to their fast response to 
cytokine activation. One of the cytokine families that can activate these cells are the type I interferons 
(IFNs). They are an essential group of cytokines involved in initiating the innate immune response and 
are secreted by immune and non-immune cells primarily in response to viral infection. The type I IFN 
family is composed of multiple IFN alpha (IFNα) members (α1 to α13 in humans 55) and a single IFN 
beta (IFNβ) 56. They all cluster together in a 400 kb region, while the individual IFNα subtypes are 
composed of 188-189 amino acid residues with a 70% to 80% amino acid homology 57. There is also a 
high similarity of the IFNα subtypes between human and mice indicating how conserved these 
cytokines are cross-species 58. While there is a direct difference in the effector function of the various 
IFNα subunits is yet to be described, it has been shown that they possess a different affinity for the 
IFNα receptor (IFNAR), demonstrating that the binding strength could influence the cellular 
mechanisms initiated after binding 58. 
2.1 Type I interferon signaling 
The signaling pathway for type I IFN molecules is initiated by the binding to the homodimer receptor 
complex called IFNAR, consisting of two subunits – IFNAR1 and IFNAR2. The current understanding 
is that the IFN molecules bind to IFNAR2, as IFNAR1 only show a weak affinity for the type I IFNs 59.

addition to STAT1 and STAT2s several other STAT family members, which act as mediators of the 
response to type I IFNs. These other STATs (ranging from 3-6) have been reported to form a 
combination of homo- or heterodimers before they also translocate to the nucleus 60. However, the 
biological effect of these molecules can have completely opposite effects. While STAT1 is a growth 
inhibitor then STAT3 is able to stimulate growth. Similarly, whereas STAT4 is required for IFNγ 
production, STAT1 is a negative regulator for type I IFN-dependent induction of IFNγ 62. However, the 
presence of these dimeric transcription factors is likely to vary substantially depending on the cell type 
and which state the cell is in and is likely to have a major impact on the response to the type I IFNs. 
Stimulation of the IFNAR/JAK/STAT1 pathway can also lead to the upregulation of the TAM receptor 
Axl, which upon accumulation on the cell surface associates with the IFNAR and switches off the 
IFNAR-STAT1 pathway 63. It does so by stimulating the transcription suppressors of cytokine 
signaling (SOCS). The SOCS proteins induce a negative feedback loop, and thereby extinguish IFNAR 
signal and by doing so they help to keep the type I IFN effects under control 64,65. 
Besides the IFNAR/JAK/STAT1 pathway, there are also other pathways that can respond to type I 
interferon signals, such as the phosphoinositide 3-kinase (PI3K) pathway, and the NF-κB pathway 66. 
Activation of these two pathways generally leads to either downregulation of several proteins involved 
in the control of cell division and proliferation and regulation of prosurvival signals 60. 
2.2 Production of type I IFNs 
There are various ways by which production of type I IFNs can be initiated. One is the response to 
detection of virus via the toll like receptor (TLR). Several of which are able to induce the production of 
type I IFNs 67. The mRNA expression of IFNβ is dramatically increased in response to viral infection 
through activation of the IFNβ promoter. This promoter consists of four positive regulatory domains 
(PRDI-IV), which are occupied by four overlapping transcription factor complexes 68.  Successful 
binding of these components will lead to activation of the promoter and thus initiate the production 69. 
While the amount of cells that are capable of producing are vast, specialized cells like the plasmacytoid 
predendritic cells (pDCs) are potent for the production of large quantities of type I 70.  However, type I 
interferons are not solely produced during inflammation.

2.3 Type I interferons during homeostasis 
It has been proposed, that IFNβ is being constitutively produced even during homeostasis as a way to 
maintain a steady state level of STAT1 68. While the viral induction of IFNβ has been thoroughly 
investigated, less is known about the transcriptional regulation of constitutive production. During the 
viral activation especially interferon regulatory factor 3 (IRF3) and 7 are essential for the induction, 
while they have no apparent effect on the constitutive production 71. Instead the activation transcription 
factor 2 (ATF-2) and c-Jun along with the NF-κB p50-RelA heterodimers appear essential for this 
induction, as deletion studies have shown a loss in constitutive IFNβ levels 72,73. The constitutive 
expression has a different effect on different kind of immune cells illustrating how diverse this 
molecule can act. For instance it augments macrophage homeostasis, and is required for normal 
macrophage function. Overall it maintains the entire myeloid linage at normal homeostatic numbers, 
affecting both the innate immune system and maintenance for the skeletal system 68. On the other hand, 
CD8+ cells isolated from IFNAR1-/- mice show no change in numbers during homeostasis but after 
immunization these mice accumulate twice the number of antigen-specific CD8+ T cells. A hypothesis 
for the reason behind this, is the ratio between STAT1 and STAT4 as IFNAR1-/- mice have reduced 
STAT1 expression, hence enabling the JAK-STAT pathway to prioritize STAT4 which is known to 
stimulate T cell proliferation 68. 
Regulatory pathways are required for both the constitutive IFN production as well as the responsive 
IFN to a pathogen. Under expression can lead to loss of function, rendering the reaction wasted, 
whereas the opposite can lead to autoimmune diseases. The transcription factor interferon regulatory 
factor-2 (IRF2) is able to negatively regulate the constitutive production of type I IFNs, by attenuating 
the transcription from the DNA which it binds. It have been observed that IRF-2 null mice, 
spontaneously develop a psoriasis like skin inflammation 74. Similarly psoriasis in humans is induced 
by the migration of PDCs to the skin, where they start producing IFNα.70. This leads to the activation of 
T cells, which in turn start producing cytokines themselves to further empower the immune response. 
Drugs against this disease are designed target inflammatory cytokines like TNF, Th17-associated 
cytokines like IL17 and IL-23, or specific bindings in the JAK/STAT1 pathway after IFN activation75. 
So to successfully treat this disease further research is required, both into what drives the initial onset 
of the disease but also which cell types are crucial throughout the disease period.

2.4 Type I interferons during inflammation 
Type I interferons are crucial in the response to both viral and bacterial infections. TLR-4 can induce 
IFNβ response when recognizing bacterial cell-wall components. IFNβ then promotes the maturation 
and differentiation of conventional dendritic cells (cDCs) from blood monocytes and induces the 
expression of co-stimulatory molecules on macrophages and cDCs, enabling them to act as effective 
APCs3. Furthermore, type I IFNs induce the production of several chemokines which recruits T 
lymphocytes to the site of infection and increases the expression of MHC class I molecules on all cells. 
This helps the Cytotoxic T cells recognize the virus infected cells through recognition of viral peptides 
presented on the MHC class I molecules 3.  Besides this are type I IFNs also able induce proliferation 
of the cytotoxic cells  
Some of gene induced by viral infection are the Mx proteins 76 and the protein kinase (PKR) 77. Both of 
these they are able to reduce the risk of healthy cells being infected by virus. Expression of PKR leads 
to the phosphorylation of the eukaryotic protein synthesis initiation factor 2 (eIF-2) inhibiting 
translation which leads to further reduction of viral replication77. The Mx proteins serves a similar 
function by interfering with the viral transcription 76. Type I IFNs have also been associated with 
induction of IL-17 production by γδ T cells in response to bacterial infection. Henry et al. reports that 
the lack of IFNAR led to a loss of neutrophil recruitment and thus worsens inflammatory response to 
the bacterial infection 78.

5.4 Retinoic acid, a modulator of the γδ T -cell phenotype 
Next we wanted to investigate why the γδ17 T cells that localize in the gut had a higher frequency of 
CCR9+ and α4β7+ cells. There are several reports describing that retinoic acid (RA), a vitamin A 
metabolite, is able to induce a CCR9+α4β7+ phenotype on conventional T cells 34,80,81. We then 
hypothesized that RA could have a similar effect on γδ17 T cells. To investigate changes in the 
expression of CCR9 and α4β7, γδ T cells isolated from the pLN were cultured overnight (ON) with RA 
or for four days with IL-7 and RA. 
We found that short (overnight) exposure to RA increased expression of CCR9 and α4β7 on γδ17 cells 
and reduced CCR6 expression (Fig 6. A+B). In addition, we observed that after four days RA 
stimulation CCR6 and CCR9 expression remained stable while the frequency of α4β7+ cells continued 
to increase (Fig 6 C+D). Thus, RA imprints a gut-like phenotype on γδ17 T cells.

5.5 Loss of IL-17 producing potential in γδ17 T cells through RA stimulation 
In addition to enhancing a gut homing phenotype RA negatively regulates the differentiation of Th17

5.7 Type I interferons act as a positive regulator for IL-17 production in γδ17 T during 
inflammation 
Next we wanted to analyze how γδ17 T cells in the IFNAR1−/− would respond to inflammation. Type I 
IFNs are crucial for mounting an adaptive immune response in conventional T cells, against both viral 
and bacterial infections56. KO studies in the IFNAR1 strain have shown increased to sensibility to viral 
infections87, highlighting the importance of this receptor complex. However, the type I interferons are 
also known to inhibit the production of IL-17 in the Th17 population88, and so potentially increasing 
the IL-17 production of the γδ17 T cells during inflammation. Conflicting reports have previously been 
published on this matter with reports showing both protection89 and no difference90. Thus we wanted to 
investigate which of these reports were correct. To test this, WT and IFNAR mice received daily 
applications of commercial Aldara cream containing 5% imiquimod (IMQ) at the dorsal skin. After 
four applications skin thickening was evaluated by H&E staining and cell populations by flow 
cytometry. IMQ has been extensively used to induce skin inflammation, as it promotes an IL-17 driven 
response and results in a γδ 17 T cell driven psoriasis like disease 91,92.

6th. Chapter: Discussion 
The present study had two primary goals; investigate the distribution and characterize the RORγt+ γδ T 
cell populations found in the pLN, mLN, ears and LP of RORγt-GFP mice. Along with identifying the 
role of IFN-I in regulating homeostasis and inflammation in the IL-17 producing γδ17 T cells with 
special focus on the skin and draining lymph nodes. We found that the frequency of RORγt+ γδ T cells 
were higher in the epithelial tissue compared to the lymph nodes. γδ T cells are present in both LNs, 
but the RORγt+ cells are almost absent in the mLN. Furthermore the distribution between Vγ4+ and 
Vγ4¯ cells also varied between the organs with the LP having almost sorely Vγ4¯ while the remaining 
three had a higher frequency of Vγ4+. The expression of homing molecules also varied between the 
organs, and even within the organs themselves. CCR6 had the highest expression in the pLNs with the 
Vγ4+ had the highest expression in both the pLN and mLN. A similar finding was done for CCR9 in 
which the frequency was highest in the Vγ4+ RORγt+ population. The opposite was found with the 
expression of α4β7 which was almost exclusively expressed in the mLN and primary found on Vγ4¯. 
==PAGE:37==
Page | 37  
 
We then discovered that stimulation with RA induced a gut topic phenotype, both in regards to altering 
the expression level of homing molecules and the cytokine expression. 
Using an IFNAR1-/- mouse strain we observed increased numbers of both Vγ4+ and Vγ4¯ γδ17 T cells 
in the pLNs, while we only identified an increase in the Vγ4¯ population in the skin. The γδ17 T cells 
in both locations also had a significantly reduced expression of CCR6. The cytokine profile also 
changed as we observed a loss in the frequency of total IFNγ+ and Vγ4+ IL-17+ γδ T cells. During 
inflammation the IFNAR1-/- mice showed increased protection due to; reduced reddening, reduced 
epithelial thickening and a lower number of Vγ4+ γδ17 T cells that was able to activate and produce IL-
17. 
One of the things we have shown in this study is that the frequency of γδ17 T cells can vary between 
different tissue sites. Especially the dermis and lamina propria have a high proportion of these cells 
compared to the LNs. This is likely to correlate with IL-17s function in the clearance of both bacterial 
and fungi infections such as Staphylococcus aureus and Pneumocytis carnii 18.  This is opposed to the 
LNs were the γδ17 T cell population only represent a small fraction of the total lymphocytes. Within 
the LNs the expression of homing molecules varied not only between the pLN and mLN, but also 
within each compartment. We show that the Vγ4+ cells generally express higher amounts of CCR6 and 
CCR9 independent of their lymph node location, whereas the Vγ4¯ has the highest expression of α4β7. 
This indicates that the two populations are programmed towards different locations. 
Following this we show that ON stimulation with RA of γδ17 isolated from the pLN is able to induce a 
gut homing phenotype. Through reduction CCR6 MFI and an increased frequency of both CCR9+ and 
α4β7+ γδ17 T cells.  After four days of stimulation the CCR6 MFI and CCR9+ frequency was stable 
while the α4β7+ frequency continued to increase. CCR9 and α4β7 requires binding to their respective 
ligands CCL25 and MadCAM-1, to successfully enter the small intestinal mucosal. CCL25 is 
constitutively expressed in the small intestinal mucosal while MadCAM is constitutively expressed on 
vascular endothelium in the intestinal lamina propria81. Combining this with our findings, indicate that 
RA helps to induce the expression of these molecules and thus promote the transport of γδ17 T cells 
into the LP.  
==PAGE:38==
Page | 38  
 
Considering the expression of CCR6 in the IFNAR1-/- model then we found that the MFI was reduced 
compared to the WT mice. It has been shown that activation of the TCR in naïve and effector T 
lymphocytes lead to a downregulation of the CCR6 expression. This could indicate that the γδ17 T 
cells isolated from the IFNAR1-/- could be activated compared to the WT. This could be an explanation 
for the increased numbers of Vγ4+ and Vγ4¯ cells in pLN, and an increased Vγ4¯ population in the 
dermis. However, a similar finding has been reported previously in which they use a CCR6-/- model. 
They show that this leads to increased numbers of Vγ4¯ in the skin and increased Vγ4+ in the pLN15. 
This illustrates that CCR6 plays an important role for the migration of specific subpopulations to and 
from the dermis. With the Vγ4¯ population being CCR6 independent to enter the dermis while Vγ4+ 
needs it to migrate there resulting in the observed increase in numbers in the respective tissue. While 
homing issues could be the explaining factor for the difference in population size, it cannot be ruled out 
others factors also influence the γδ17 T cell numbers as well. Type I IFNs are known to be involved in 
a lot of processes and have anti-proliferating effects. As such removal of the constitutive type I IFN 
production could remove a blockade hindering proliferation of the γδ T cells. While the effects of tonic 
IFN signaling on proliferation of γδ T cells have yet to be investigated, studies like this have been 
conducted on both CD8+ blasts and NK cells. Showing how the constitutive production can lead to 
either enhancing or inhibition of the two cells types respectively68. A third option which could explain 
the increased numbers is due to developmental effects. Others have shown that removal of IFNAR1 led 
to defects in the development of regulatory T cells94 and NK cells95 making this an explanation in our 
setting as well. 
Application of Imiquimod on the IFNAR1-/- model showed increased protection through reduced 
reddening and reduced epidermis thickening. Imiquimod application is a commonly used method to 
investigate γδ T cell induced inflammation in the skin91,92. It is well established that this method rely on 
the production of IL-17 to induce the skin lesion, with the Vγ4+ γδ T cell being the main producer of 
IL-1715.  Our findings indicate that a loss of expansion in size along with and subsequent reduced IL-17 
production by the Vγ4+ induces the protective phenotype in the IFNAR1-/- mice. Other groups have 
done similar experiments but with variating results. Some have applied the IMQ crème to IFNAR1-/- 
mice with no protection90 while others have seen the same phenotype as observed in this study89. The 
question then remains, which of the two findings are correct. One explanation revolves around the 
==PAGE:39==
Page | 39  
 
dermal Vγ6 population. We show in this study that these cells have increased numbers in the skin 
during homeostasis and that the Vγ4¯ population in the LNs is increased during both homeostasis and 
inflammation. While they prodouce less IL-17 during inflammation in the WT it is possible that they 
can compensate for the lack of a functional Vγ4 population. However, the Vγ6 cells are not as effective 
as the Vγ4 in producing IL-17. The protocol for ear application often involves a longer period of 
application which in turn could active the slower Vγ6 cells through a type I IFN independent pathway 
and induce the psoriasis like skin lesions. 
7th. Chapter: Conclusion 
Based on the results presented in this study we show how diverse the γδ17 T population is, not only 
between different tissue locations, but also within a single organ. With the expression level of homing 
molecules like CCR6, CCR9 and α4β7 being highly dependent on the locations which the cells are 
located. Through stimulation with RA we demonstrate how external molecules located in the different 
tissues, are able to modulate the effector type and mediate a change in the expression of homing 
molecules. Furthermore, we found that the removal of IFNAR1 results in an increased γδ17 T cell 
population in both skin and LN, and a drop in the frequency of IL-17 producing cells. We also 
demonstrate how this knockout has induced protection against IMQ induced psoriasis-like skin lesions 
due to the lack of IL-17 producing Vγ4+ γδ17 T cells.
