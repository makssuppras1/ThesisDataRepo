Abstract
Internet of Things (IoT) is a fast growing technology that its aim is to serve the demand of
the market for massive connectivity between objects to objects (things) or/and objects to
people. Also, the aim of IoT technology is to give easy and efﬁcient access to everyone to
monitor and conﬁgure objects by connecting them to a network (mainly on the Internet).
Finally, one aim of the IoT technology, is to succeed in those requirements by keeping the
prices of manufacturing the physical devices, in low levels and at the same time to keep
the devices in a long term operating period with a low level of power consumption.
Narrow-Band Internet of Things (NB-IoT), is one of the low power wide area network (LP-
WAN) technologies that aims to fulﬁll all those requirements that mentioned before. NB-
IoT technology has been selected among other LPWAN technologies because it fulﬁlls,
also, the requirement of a fully standardized technology and at the same time is a technol-
ogy based on the already existing mobile network technologies.
Moreover, Quality of Services (QoS), is an important parameter when a standardized tech-
nology is the object of the investigation for several reasons. For this project, an investi-
gation of the NB-IoT technology is held in order to deﬁne and analyze some of the most
remarkable parameters that can underline the technology. Also, an investigation of the
QoS in NB-IoT is held in order to analyze and underline the parameters that "play" a key
role in its implementation.
To continue, a NB-IoT device and a signal generator that emulates all the functions of a
"real" network are used, in order to evaluate the QoS of the technology. As a result, ex-
perimental measurements and calculations lead to the analysis of the aspects that make
promising the NB-IoT technology in terms of QoS. Finally, all the results and several func-
tions of the NB-IoT technology are compared between them in order to analyze how the
principle aims of the technology can be fulﬁlled.

CHAPTER1
Introduction
In this work an analysis of the NB-IoT technology is presented. Initially, the theoretical
milestones of NB-IoT are covered and then, experimental measurements are analyzed in
order to investigate parameters that can estimate, analyze and ﬁnally summarize the abil-
ities of the technology in terms of Quality of Services (QoS).
To begin with, the term Internet of Things (IoT) deﬁnes the demand for connectivity be-
tween the people and the things or objects. The networks are designed to connect people
however, it is also needed and will be more demanding in the future to serve objects as
well, due to the increasing amount of devices and applications. It is not a new method,
however the last years it seems that the demand for IoT is increasing rapidly.
To continue, Narrow-Band IoT (NB-IoT) is a Low Power Wide Area Network (LPWAN) radio
technology standard developed by the 3rd Generation Partnership Project (3GPP) to en-
able a wide range of cellular devices and services. Is a massive LPWAN technology which
can support massive connections, ultra-low power consumption, wide area coverage and
bidirectional triggering between signaling plane and data plane. The main characteristic
of the NB-IoT concept, which makes this technology promising, is that is supported by the
cellular communication networks based on the LTE technology.
1.1
Thesis Scope
The aim of this M.Sc thesis is to investigate and analyze the aspects of the NB-IoT technol-
ogy in terms of deployment, implementation requirements, key features and more. Also,
an investigation of the QoS that the technology provides are tested in order to examine the
several functions and challenges. All the possible challenges are taken into consideration
and ﬁnally experimental measurements and calculations are implemented in order to an-
alyze and identify the results.

In Chapter 3, a QoS approach is presented. In the beginning as a general concept, and
then focused on the NB-IoT technology in order to remark the importance of the QoS in

underline the motivation of this project and present relative work that have been done by
other researchers, scientists and writers.
In Chapter 4, the ﬁnal goals of the report are presented. Also, the conﬁgurations required
and the set-up of the experiments are presented. Finally, the scenarios set-up is presented
in order to fulﬁll the goals.

tions are presented. Also, the analysis of the results is taken place in this chapter.
In Chapter 6 a conclusion of the report is presented. Also, possible cases for future work,
further investigation and improvements are mentioned in the last section of the chapter.

CHAPTER2
NB-IoT Technology
2.1
Standardization & Development History
Back to 2005, 3GPP started an investigation on cellular networks for Machine-Type Com-
munication (MTC) services in order to include MTC as an important element to 5G net-
works.
[23] Based on the early deployment of MTC, the preliminary work of 3GPP (R8-R11) fo-
cuses on issues such as overload and congestion on data and signaling planes, numbering
and addressing of resource shortage during synchronous access of numerous terminals to
the network.
[23] After further reﬁning and specifying of demands and features of MTC service, in R12
3GPP announced the enhancements of GSM access network related to the design of low-
cost MTC terminals, requirements for security and network system architecture.
[9] In R13 3GPP set 5 objectives for MTC, including enhanced indoor coverage, supported
massive small data terminals, lower terminal complexity & cost and higher energy efﬁ-
ciency. The R13 also deﬁnes 3 kinds of new narrow-band air interfaces, including GSM-
compatible EC-GSM-IoT, LTE-compatible eMTC and brand new NB-IoT technology.
[23] R14 proposes functional requirements in terms of localization support, multi-cast,
mobility, higher data rate and link adaptation in order to make cellular IoT possess more
suitable objects and application range. In short, 3GPP adopts two-step strategy to cope
with technological challenges brought by MTC services. The ﬁrst step is a transition strat-
egy that aims to utilize and optimize the existing network and technologies to provide
MTC services. The second step is a long-term strategy based on introduction of a new air
interface technology for NB-IoT, in order to support large-scale growth of MTC services
and to maintain its core competitiveness toward non-3GPP LPWA technology.

2.2
Principle Aims
In the following section, the goals of the NB-IoT and the most signiﬁcant features of the
technology are presented. The principle aims of NB-IoT technology is to support low
power consumption for low cost devices, to assure long range coverage with massive con-
nectivity for those devices and all those aims in terms of reliability and safety.
Figure 2.2: NB-IoT Principle Aims
Massive connectivity is one of the main aims of the NB-IoT technology and in numbers it
means that 50.000 connections could be supported by one cell. Low power consumption
devices should be able to transmit small packets in real-time and at the same time to be

part of the network without causing conﬂicts and huge congestion to it.
Low power consumption on the other hand, is another main goal of the NB-IoT technol-
ogy. In order to achieve those low power consumption, power saving mechanisms are
introduced and are explained further in this report. In numbers, the terminals should be
able to have a lifetime of up to 10 years with their battery sufﬁciency of 5 Wh.
To continue, one more goal of the NB-IoT technology is to provide the users and the termi-
nals by a long range/wide coverage and mobility. This is also one of the remarkable points
of NB-IoT technology that is based on the cellular networks and takes advantages of the
already existing infrastructure of the mobile networks.
Moreover, the NB-IoT technology aims to support low cost production devices, which
means that the devices cost from 5-20 $ and they are compatible with the requirements of

[23], [11], [30] The modulation technique of the NB-IoT technology is QPSK in the down-
link with OFDM encoding method and sub-carrier spacing 15 kHz. On the other hand,
the modulation technique in the uplink is BPSK or QPSK with single frequency-division
multiple access scheme (SC-FDMA). Also, a single sub-carrier with sub-carrier spacing of
3.75 kHz and 15 kHz is supported by the IoT terminals.
[23], [11], [30] For sub-carrier spacing of 15 kHz, 12 continuous subcarriers are deﬁned.
Accordingly, 48 continuous sub-carriers are deﬁned for sub-carrier spacing of 3.75 kHz.
Multiple subcarrier transmission supports sub-carrier spacing of 15 kHz and deﬁnes 12
continuous sub-carriers, which they are combined into 3, 6, or 12 continuous sub-carriers.
The coverage ability for 3.75-kHz spacing is higher than for 15-kHz spacing, because of
higher power spectral density. The cell capacity for 15-kHz spacing is 92% of that for 3.75-
kHz spacing, but the dispatching efﬁciency and dispatching complexity are superior.
[23], [11], [30] Since the Narrow Physical Random Access Channel (NPRACH) has to adopt
single sub-carrier transmission with spacing of 3.75 kHz, most of the equipment prefer-
entially supports single sub-carrier transmission with spacing of 3.75 kHz for uplink. Af-
ter introducing single subcarrier transmission with spacing of 15 kHz and multiple sub-
carrier transmission, the choice is made adaptively according to the terminal’s channel
quality. The minimal dispatching unit for the Narrow Physical Downlink Shared Channel
(NPDSCH) transmission is the resource block (RB), and the minimal dispatching unit for
the Narrow Physical Uplink Shared Channel (NPUSCH) transmission is the resource unit
(RU). In the aspect of time domain, for single sub-carrier transmission, the resource unit
is 32 ms for sub-carrier spacing of 3.75 kHz and 8 ms for sub-carrier spacing of 15 kHz. For
multiple sub-carrier transmission, the resource unit is 4 ms for spacing with 3 sub-carriers,
2 ms for spacing with 6 sub-carriers, and 1 ms for spacing with 12 sub-carriers.
[23], [11], [30] The protocol of NB-IoT in high layer is formulated through modiﬁcation of
some LTE features, such as multi-connection, low power consumption and few data. The
core network of NB-IoT is connected through S1 interface.

Low Power Consumption
Two power saving modes techniques are introduced in NB-IoT technology, in order to as-
sure low power consumption. The ﬁrst is the Power Save Mode (PSM) which is included to
the release R12 and the other is the Extended Discontinuous Reception (eDRX) technique
which is included in the release R13.
[12] The PSM technique applies the method, in which the terminal announces to the net-
work that a "sleep" period will follow in order not to be reached by signaling processes.
On that period, the terminal is still registered to the network, however cannot be reached.

NPBCH is responsible for Master Inforation Block (MIB) transmissions and is transmit-
ted on the ﬁrst subframe (#0) of each frame, while the rest of the signaling information
and data are transmitted by the NPDSCH. Also, information for downlink and uplink data
channels are contained to NPDSCH, which is responsible of controlling the data transfer
between the UE and the eNB.
Furthermore, for the UE which there is data in the NPDSCH, the "place" where to look
up for them and how often they are repeated, are pointed out from NPDCCH. Also, the
resources that the UE is using for data transmission in the Uplink and further information
(e.g system information update) are contained to the NPDCCH.
NPSS is transmitted on the 6th (#5) subframe every 10ms and together with the NSSS are
responsible to make processes which include time synchronization, frequency synchro-
nization and cell identity detection. These channels are important from the UE’s side, as
they are the channels containing the information from where the cells are searched.
Moreover, they are similar with the PSS & SSS from LTE implementation. One more signif-
icant case of NPSS and NSSS is that during the ﬁrst synchronization, the operation mode
of the NB-IoT is probably not known to the UE, as a result the synchronization time is the
same for all the modes.

and it does not matter if there are data transmitted. In other words, NRS is "hepling"
the NB-IoT device to estimate the dowlink channels, dowlink signal strength and quality
measurements. NRS is used in both connected and idle mode, in order to perform the
measurements as they referred before.

[38], [20], [24], [18] NPRACH is the channel used by the UE and is responsible for the con-
nection’s initialization. Also, during the NPRACH transmission, one important process
takes place. That process is the estimation of the Time of Arrival (ToA) by the eNB that is

serving the UE. [20] From that process, the round-trip propagation delay (UE-eNB trip) is
expressed as well. This is important in order to align the received signal for every NB-IoT
device and preserve the orthogonality between different frequency-division multiplexed
devices. As a result, the Timing Advance (TA) can be decided by the eNB.
Moreover, it is important to mention the procedure that is followed from the NPRACH in
order to reach the extreme case coverage classes and/or establish successfully a connec-
tion.
First of all, [38], [20], [24], [18] a preamble is transmitted in the NPRACH. The preamble
is structured as symbol groups in which every symbol group is designed by a cyclic preﬁx
(CP) followed by ﬁve single-tone symbols of 3,75 kHz spacing and total duration of the ﬁve
symbols 1,333ms. Also, there are two formats of NPRACH preambles. NPRACH preambles
Format 0 with CP duration 66,7 µs and NPRACH preambles Format 1 with CP duration
266,67 µs, which they cover, up to 10km and 40km cell radius respectively.
The following ﬁgure (Figure 2.17) illustrates the two preamble formats of NPRACH.
Figure 2.17: Preamble Groups of NPRACH
Then, [38], [20], [24], [18] the preamble symbol groups are transmitted without having
"gaps" between them and they are formulated in four symbol groups for every repetition.
Also, every symbol group is transmitted in different subcarrier (12 subcarriers in total).
Next, the cell is the one that decides how many times the UE should repeat the preambles
with a maximum repetition of 128 times (1,2,4,8,32,64,128) depending on the coverage
level. The transmission power is the same for all the repetitions. Moreover, resources of
NPRACH are supplied individually depending on the coverage level.
Figure 2.18 illustrates an example of NPRACH frequency hopping. It can been seen that
one repetition is made up of four preamble symbol groups. The ﬁrst preamble symbol

group is transmitted in a subcarrier when the second is transmitted right after above in
the next subcarrier. The third preamble symbol group is transmitted in a different sub-
carrier and with a +6 tone difference from the ﬁrst preamble symbol group. The fourth
similar to the third with a +6 tone from the second and one tone above the third preamble
symbol group.
Figure 2.18: NPRACH Frequency Hopping
The whole procedure (tone hopping) aims to a Timing Advance (TA) estimation for the
eNB in order to "meet" the unknown’s device residual frequency offset and mainly distin-
guish two (or more) "unknown" devices. In other words, even if the same frequency is
used, a time-frequency relationship is created between the four symbol groups.
[38], [20], [24], [18] As a result, 12 possible set of NPRACH are created into a repetition.
Also, in order to succeed in the NB-IoT long range coverage classes, repetitions are impor-
tant as described before. At this point, Pseudo Random Hopping is necessary between the
repetitions in order to avoid collisions/interference with other cells. In Figure 2.18 those
Pseudo Random Hopping are illustrated. To analyze more, a pseudo random hopping inte-
ger tone offset χ is applied to every preamble and an index (from 0-11 as it is a 12 tones) is
created. Also, cell ID and repetition index are the values that decide the tone offset χ. As a
result, for two different (neighbour) cells, the same NPRCH preambles’ patterns is avoided
and the collisions/interference from trying to connect to an eNB is avoided as well.

Low Cost
It is expected that the NB-IoT devices are low cost manufactured. However, in order to de-
crease the cost of the devices there are several adjustments that are made to the network’s
side, the implementation and the functions of the NB-IoT technology. In this section, the
features in order to make the technology less complex and fulﬁll the low cost requirements,
are presented.
In NB-IoT technology Half-Duplex data transmissions are implemented, in comparison to
Full-Duplex data transmissions in LTE. In other words, the data transmission method in
NB-Iot technology is simpliﬁed in order to avoid simultaneously transmissions that cause
the devise’s complexity.
The low bandwidth that NB-IoT is deployed is a key role in the low cost aim. NB-IoT
is deployed in a maximum bandwidth of 180kHz and as a result it can be implemented
through the already existing channels of the already existing technologies. Also, low band-
width can be translated to more devices per cell as they occupy a small amount of the total
bandwidth. Moreover, with low bandwidth the NB-IoT devices can be deployed with lim-
ited hardware equipment. For instance the antennas’ design of the NB-IoT devices can be
kept simple.
Also, NB-IoT technology does not support handovers during the connected mode. The
cell selection and reselection is done only during the idle mode of the device. That means
that the complexity of the device is remaining simple and the cost of the device low as well.
Another remarkable ﬁgure of the NB-IoT technology is that introduces power saving modes.
The technology is based on small packet transmissions, which means that after every com-
pleted transmission the device can return to power saving mode. As a result, the saving
modes "help" the device to save energy when transmissions are not needed and eventu-
ally keep the initial’s and maintenance’s cost low.
Moreover, the size of the blocks (TBS) in the physical channels is limited by the technol-
ogy in order to keep the cost low. The maximum TBS of the downlink channel (NPDSCH)
is 680 while for the uplink (NPUSCH Format 1) is 1000. The importance of those limita-
tions can been translated into coding and decoding process. With those limitations, is not
needed to use complex and "fast" processors, so the device’s complexity and the cost can
remain low.
In the architecture’s point of view, NB-IoT is simpliﬁed as well. In the core network the
introduction of the SCEF element provides ﬂexibility and low cost aspects. Apart from the
abstract interface for the network’s services that can be provided from SCEF, non IP data
transmissions can be executed as well.

Challenges
As a not-widespread technology, NB-IoT has to face a few challenges. Most of the already
deployments of the NB-IoT are experimental and based on research and not massive pro-
duced.
One important challenge that NB-IoT technology has to face is the narrow band. As the
name of the technology deﬁnes, the NB-IoT is based on the narrow bands. This is a chal-
lenge if it is taken into consideration that the transmission of the narrow band can be
blocked easily from trees’ leaves to houses’ walls. At the same time the aim of the NB-IoT
is to provide services for a long range coverage and maybe the narrow band is an "issue"
again.
Another challenge that NB-IoT face is the low latency. IoT devices are devices that in
general transmit small packets. However, most of the IoT application require real-time
transmission and in some cases the information is really sensitive. For instance, the tem-
perature measurements of the boiler in an electric power plant in order to take action
automatically is a sensitive information that requires accurate transmission. As a result,
the transaction of data within an acceptable latency period and avoidance of lost packets
are important requirements that NB-IoT should cope with.

Quality of Service
In this point, is deﬁned what Quality of Service (QoS) is. Quality of Service is an important
parameter for the networks (every kind of network e.g mobile network) and depends on
several parameters (delays, loss of packets, throughput, latency and more). Is deﬁned by
the totality of characteristics of a network operator that bear/suffer on its ability to satisfy
stated and implied needs of the user of the network. So, QoS is a group of parameters for
measuring how the network works and most of the times speciﬁes how it can work under
limited capacity.
3.1
QoS General Concept
In general, QoS can be categorized to many different classes depending on the imple-
mented technology, on the client’s demand and obviously the operator’s abilities/facilities.
One of the most well-known separation of QoS that can be offered is the Deterministic
guarantees or Statistical guarantees. In the ﬁrst case, deterministic services provide sim-
ple model to the applications, they tend to overload resources because they answer for the
worst-case scenario. As a result, signiﬁcant portions of the network resources are wasted.
If the parameters are well deﬁned deterministic QoS provides high performance. On the
other hand, statistical services, take proﬁt of user statistics in order to perform the multi-
plexing. Statistical multiplexing increases signiﬁcantly the efﬁciency of the network. The
drawback of statistical services is that in case that strong guarantees are needed, low efﬁ-
ciency is occurs because packets are occasionally dropped or excessively delayed.
One more well-known mechanism in order to achieve QoS in a network is the DiffServ
(Differential Services) and IntServ (Integrated Services) Services. Those mechanisms are
mainly used in ATM technology and IP networks.
One way to provide guaranteed services is to control input trafﬁc by admission control
and policing, and to schedule packets based on the requirements of the connection they
belong to. This approach is obtained by the Integrated Services (IntServ). In IntServ, con-
nections are controlled both by admission control at connection establishment time and
==PAGE:48==
3.1 QoS General Concept
35
packet scheduling during the lifetime of the connections.
In IntServ, the QoS is supported by reserving capacity in routers. For implementing IntServ
the router must be able to perform the next functions:
• Classify: identify ﬂows that should receive a certain level of service.
• Packet Scheduling: forwarding different packet ﬂows based on QoS needs.
• Admission control: determine if router has the necessary resources to accept a new
ﬂow.
On the other hand, DiffServ is based on classifying and managing network trafﬁc and pro-
viding quality of service (QoS) on modern IP networks. For instance, DiffServ can be used
for streaming media or voice services in order to provide low-latency to critical trafﬁc,
while, at the same time non-critical services such as web trafﬁc or ﬁle transfers can be pro-
vided by best-effort service.
The QoS mechanisms for ordering packets and allotting bandwidth are queuing and band-
width management respectively. Before they can be implemented however, trafﬁc must
be differentiated using classiﬁcation tools. The classiﬁcation of trafﬁc according to policy
allows organizations to ensure the consistency and adequate availability of resources for
their most important applications.
Trafﬁc can be classiﬁed crudely by port or IP, or using a more sophisticated approach such
as by application or user. The latter parameters allow for more meaningful identiﬁcation,
and consequently, classiﬁcation of the data.
Next, queuing and bandwidth management tools are assigned rules to handle trafﬁc ﬂows
speciﬁc to the classiﬁcation they received upon entering the network.
The queuing mechanism allows for packets within trafﬁc ﬂows to be stored until the net-
work is ready to process it. Priority Queuing (PQ) is developed to ensure the necessary
availability and minimal latency of network performance for the most important batches
of applications and trafﬁc by providing an assigned priority and speciﬁc bandwidth to
them based on their classiﬁcation. This ensures the most important activities on a net-
work are not starved of bandwidth by activities of lower priority. Applications, users, and
trafﬁc can be batched in up to 8 differentiated queues.
Bandwidth management mechanisms measure and control trafﬁc ﬂows on the network
to avoid exceeding its capacity and the resulting network congestion that occurs. Mecha-
nisms for bandwidth management include trafﬁc shaping, a rate limiting technique used
to optimize or guarantee performance and increase usable bandwidth where necessary,
and scheduling algorithms, which offer varied methods for providing bandwidth to spe-

ciﬁc trafﬁc ﬂows.
Depending on the provider, the above services and controls can be managed and consoli-
dated down to a single box. Such is the case for QoS via Palo Alto Networks ﬁrewalls. Thus,
to communicate QoS measures and classiﬁcation outside the box and downstream net-
work infrastructure, Differentiated Services Code Point (DSCP) is implemented. DSCP
marks each packet based on its classiﬁcation, and communicates this to each box the
packet travels through, ensuring a consistent implementation of QoS policy.
3.2
QoS Metrics
As it is already mentioned, there are several mechanisms and several metrics in order to
measure the QoS of a network. In the following paragraphs, some of the most signiﬁcant
metrics of QoS will be presented/deﬁned.
Bandwidth: Bandwidth is the metric that deﬁnes the amount of data that can be trans-
ferred through a network for a speciﬁc time/period. It is not the "speed" of the network,
however the biggest the bandwidth is the better the QoS can be, due to source availability.
Throughput: Throughput is the metric that deﬁnes the successfully transmitted packets
for a period and the unit is most of the times bps. Throughput can be characterized as the
actual bandwidth that is available to a network and the one that the users can experience.
Latency: Latency is the needed time for a packet to be delivered thought the network from
one point to another (sender-receiver). The desired latency is as closest to zero as possible
and is a parameter that impacts the throughput of the network as well.
Availability:[34]Availability is another important parameter that has to be taken into con-
sideration in terms of QoS. Availability can be deﬁned as the period that a network is work-
ing properly (the preferable value is ﬁve nines 99,999). The two main parameters that
claim the availability of the network are the Mean Time Between Failure (MTBF) which is
relative to the Time to Fail (TTF)(statistically is random) variable and the Mean Time To
Repair (MTTR) which deﬁnes how much time is needed to repair the failure.
Network Connection Time: [22] Server Connection Time is time taken by the server to
reply to a data request made by some other device. Connection timeout can occur when
the server’s request is not serviced in given time.
Jitter: Jitter is a parameter that represent the time between two or more packets to be
delivered to a destination. The delay between different packets can vary and as a result
packet losses or network congestion can be caused because of jitter. In order to avoid jit-
==PAGE:50==
3.3 QoS in NB-IoT
37
ter and provide high QoS, high bandwidth and low latency are required.
Security and Privacy: [22] Security and Privacy are also two important parameters due to
QoS. Security can be deﬁned as the metric of how secure is a network in order to prevent
or stop external attacks that are taking place inside the network. Firewalls and encryption
are examples of how operators can protect their users and provide higher security. On the
other hand, privacy can be deﬁned as a metric of how network operators protect the per-
sonal information/data of their users. They are two different parameters, however can be
really relevant and close related.
Packet Loss: Packet Loss is another important parameter in terms of QoS. As a term,
packet loss deﬁnes the amount of the transmitted data (packets) that fail to be delivered to
the potential receiver. There are several reasons why the packets can not be deliver, such
as software or hardware failures and the less the packet losses the higher QoS is achieved.
Reliability: One more parameter that can inﬂuence the QoS is the reliability, which de-
ﬁnes for how long a network remains operational without failures. There are are a lot of
different parameters that can occur the reliability of a network. Some of them are the
Hardware (cables in the links), the Software (conﬁgurations such as IP settings or routing),
or even more human errors (during maintenance).
Resiliency: Resiliency is one more parameter that can cause the QoS of a network. Re-
siliency can be deﬁned as, how ﬂexible a network can be and/or how fast failures and
damages can be ﬁxed.

synchronization, the time to perform the Random Access procedure and setup the con-
nection, the time to transmit the data and the time to release the connection. As a result,
the highest the latency the highest power consumption is occurred.
Latency is related to high capacity and massive connectivity as well. That can been ex-
plained if it is taken into consideration the available resources. For instance, the highest
amount of connected IoT devices to the network the highest latency can occur. As a result,
low latency can give the chance to more devices to be "served" better from the network
and fulﬁll the need of extreme coverage as well, as more resources will be available.
Power Consumption: Power Consumption is one more important metric in terms of QoS
when NB-IoT technology is investigated. Power consumption is very crucial parameter
because of the long term battery life that the IoT devices should support. However, apart
from the long life of the devices that is required, as power consumption, can be distin-
guished in terms of source power (e.g solar power, battery power and more).
Also, power consumption is close related to the NB-IoT technology’s power saving modes,
transmission modes and signaling procedures. Furthermore, power consumption is re-
lated to the coverage classes and the deployment modes. For instance a device with a
"good" coverage level can transmit "faster" than a "bad" coverage class and then return to
the "sleep" mode.

In the following section, a "State of the Art" is presented. The purpose of this attempt
is to search and present the most signiﬁcant "job" that have been done so far by other
researchers, writers and scientists that they "spent" time investigating and analyzing the
NB-IoT technology via several aspects.
There are several papers that present the NB-IoT technology and how all the parameters of
the technology are combined to be part of the LTE technology. More speciﬁc, there are doc-
uments such as [11] "On the performance of narrow-band Internet of Things (NB-IoT)",
[30] "NB-IoT system for M2M communication" that analyze the deployment modes of the
NB-IoT technology. Also, documents such as [39] "The 3GPP NB-IoT system architecture
for the Internet of Things" that illustrate all the requirements of the NB-IoT technology
and analyze further the required adjustments to LTE technology in order to be compati-
ble with the IoT technologies. One more paper that presents the aspects of the NB-IoT
technology with title, [28] "Key Aspects of Narrow Band Internet of Things Communica-
tion Technology Driving Future IoT Applications", is used as reference. In this paper an
approach of how the technology can be utilized in order to enable better integration of
low power devices into future Internet of Things is presented.
==PAGE:54==
3.5 State of the Art

Other documents, whose aim is to compare the LPWAN-IoT technologies in terms of cov-
erage, endurance and power consumption are investigated. The main comparisons are
between the NB-IoT technology with LoRa, SigFox etc. More speciﬁc, a comprehensive
survey on NB-IoT and LoRa as efﬁcient solutions connecting the device is provided on
[29] "A survey on LPWA technology: LoRa and NB-IoT". Another comparison between LP-
WAN technologies is presented in [37] "Coverage and capacity analysis of sigfox, lora, gprs,
and nb-iot" and [26] "NB-IoT and LoRA connectivity analysis for M2M/IoT smart grids ap-
plications". In both documents, real site deployments are tested in order to analyze the
differences in terms of coverage and capacity of several technologies.
Furthermore, approaches have been done in order to deﬁne and analyze the QoS of NB-
IoT. A theoretical approach of how the QoS of NB-IoT can be categorized due to archi-
tectural parameters have been investigated in [22] "Quality of Service (QoS) in Internet of
Things". One more document is investigated in terms of QoS in the LPWAN. More speciﬁc,
in [13] "QoS Metrics Measurement in Long Range IoT Networks", QoS metrics measure-
ment in LoRaWAN networks are presented by results of experimental study. Also, in [17]
"Modelling QoS in IoT Applications", an investigation of QoS is done. This paper inves-
tigates the QoS of delay sensitive things and the corresponding trafﬁc they generate over
the network.
In terms of power consumption, two papers are investigated. The ﬁrst is [19] "An Empir-
ical NB-IoT Power Consumption Model for Battery Lifetime Estimation". In this paper,
is presented the ﬁrst publicly available empirical power consumption measurements on
two NB-IoT devices. The target is to provide a power consumption model for Internet of
Things battery lifetime estimation. The other paper is [21] "Radio Resource Management
Scheme in NB-IoT Systems". In this paper, ﬁrst a theoretical framework for the upper
bound on the achievable data rate is formulated in the presence of control channel and
repetition factor and then a proposal of an interference aware resource allocation for NB-
IoT by formulating the rate maximization problem considering the overhead of control
channels, time offset, and repetition factor. The proposed algorithm is then evaluated to
investigate the impact of repetition factor, time offset and ICI on the NB-IoT data rate, and
energy consumption.
An extra motivation for this project is the book [20] "Cellular Internet of Things: Tech-
nologies, Standards, and Performance". This book, consist of a detailed description of the
three (EC-GSM-IoT, NB-IoT & LTE-M) technologies of Cellular Internet of Things. Apart
from the key aspects of the technologies, measurements through simulations are held and
analyzed. Few parameters and results are presented, however a "picky" selection of them
is done. As a result, more motivation occurs in order to investigate more aspects, through
this book. One more, motivating book is the [24] "LTE and IoT Systems and Signal Quality".
This book is a blend of practical aspects, system analysis, signal quality issues and basic
mathematics. Also, is a LTE and IoT oriented literature, where all the aspects are presented
detailed.

achieved "faster" and then the device can return from the connected mode to idle mode
in order to "save" energy and avoid unneeded activity.

the NB-IoT is to achieve massive connections and long range coverage. As a result, the
choice of the NPUSCH Format 1 depends on a the implementation and the needs of the
network.

5.5
Discussion
In this section, a discussion of the results and the ﬁnal presentation of them, is following.
During the experimental measurements all the different aspects of the NB-IoT technol-
ogy, in terms of coverage condition of the device, available deployment modes, physical
resources and several implementations are investigated.
In terms of coverage, the measurements are held in the limits of the coverage classes and
with the worst case scenario under investigation. For instance, the Tx Power of all the
measurements is 23 dBm which means that the results represent the worst case of ev-
ery situation, as 23 dBm is the maximum transmitted power of the device. For this rea-
son, in the graphs the results are presented as repetitions and not as Maximum Coupling
Losses. When the device is in a "good" coverage condition the messages are repeated less
time. While the device is in a "bad" coverage condition the repetition of the messages is
increased in order to robust the signal and transmit successfully those messages.
In terms of deployment modes, all the three available deployment modes( Stand-Alone,
In-Band and Guard-Band) are investigated during the experimental measurements. How-
ever, it is observed that the deployment modes do not have extreme differences between
them as it was expected due to different link budgets and shared resources with LTE (i.e
In-Band). This can be explained if it is taken into consideration that the measurements
are held in the safe environment of the lab and that in the network the device that is under
investigation is the only UE of the network. As a result, the resources are allocated to this
device without the interference, collisions and the need to share them with other devices.
So, the graphs in Chapter 5 represent the overall measurements of the NB-IoT technology
in terms of deployment modes.
One more parameter that is important to be mentioned and impacts the results, is the re-
source allocation that is implemented to the measurements and the results of this chapter.
For instance, the Resource Unit, that is selected to form the 1000 bits TBS, is 8 for the Up-
link formation. Several values were tested before the selection of this formation, however
this value is selected as it is able to allocate the maximum resources to all the different
cases in terms of subcarriers. In other words, higher resource unit was tested in order to
form a higher resource allocation with RU=10 and TBS=1000. However, the single tone
transmissions were unable to handle this parameter and the device was detached from
the network. As a result, the ﬁnal resource unit was selected in order to compare all the
cases under the same conﬁgurations, as the point of this project is not to investigate the
best optimized solution for every case.
Finally, according to RF settings, all the measurements are held in the same band (Band
20). Moreover, for the case of the In-Band and Guard-Band deployment modes, the LTE
Bandwidth is 10 MHz. Also, the way that the conﬁgurations are set, interference in the
radio frequency parameters is not applied. In a more realistic approach, it is obvious that

CHAPTER6
Conclusion
As the demand for massive connections of "things" is increasing day by day, new technolo-
gies are introduced in order to cope with those challenges. Those technologies are deﬁned
as "Internet of Things" and their aim is to interconnect the "things" with the Internet in or-
der to transfer data over a network. One main category of the IoT systems is the low power
wide area network technologies (LPWAN), whose aim is to serve the massive connections
using low power devices and achieving long range coverage. As the data trafﬁc and the
amount of the connected devices in the network are increasing, the need of Quality of Ser-
vices (QoS) is more demanding than ever.
QoS is related to the performance of the network. When IoT technologies are the object of
investigation, high coverage and high capacity of the network is required in order to cope
with the massive connectivity of the devices and at the same fulﬁll the aim of the technolo-
gies for extreme coverage.
One of the most prominent LPWAN technologies is the Narrow-Band IoT (NB-IoT). The
unique feature that sets apart NB-IoT from the rest of the IoT technologies is, that is a
licensed, fully standardized by 3GPP technology and its implementation is based on the
already existing mobile communication technologies. This features make NB-IoT unique
technology in terms of QoS.
QoS in NB-IoT can be achieved by resource allocation. The network (eNB) is responsible
to allocate the resources to the devices (UE), depending on the available capacity and the
device’s coverage condition. The primary concept of the NB-IoT technology is to trans-
mit the packets as "fast" as possible through the network and then return to the power
saving mode. Due to this concept, the network prioritizes the devices in "good" coverage
by allocating more resources in order to achieve transmissions with low latency and high
throughput.
Experimental measurements were held within the scope of this project, in order to inves-
tigate and ﬁnd out how the NB-IoT performance is mapped under several cases and how
QoS can be achieved by allocation of the available resources. The impact of several pa-
rameters was investigated, including the coverage classes, the deployment modes and the
transmission methods. The performance of the NB-IoT technology is examined for a sin-
gle cell and a single NB-IoT device (UE).
==PAGE:99==
6.1 Future Work
86
The results show that the performance is dependant on several parameters and QoS can
be applied depending on the situation of the network in terms of coverage & capacity.
"Good" coverage condition of the device can achieve higher QoS as more resources can
be allocated. High throughput and low latency transmissions can be achieved and as a re-
sult the devices can return to the idle mode. On the other hand "bad" coverage condition
of the device can lead to less available throughput and more latency for the transmissions.
Also, high capacity can lead to less resource allocation in terms of transmission methods,
as the transmission method for the uplink is a choice of the network. When more devices
are connected to the network, less subcarriers are preferred in order to increase the capac-
ity. This choice leads to less resources available but ensures massive connections.
All in all, the results show that there is a variety of options in order to achieve QoS in the
NB-IoT technology. Also, when QoS is applied, the principle aims of the NB-IoT can be
achieved. Those are cheap and easy to use devices, consume a small amount of energy,
deal with massive connections and provide big coverage.
6.1
Future Work
Regardless of the promising aspects of NB-IoT technology demonstrated in this work,
there is space for improvements and additional investigation can be held..
To begin with, more measurements in terms of shared resources and different radio fre-
quency parameters can be held. In this way, more accurate and precised results can be
extracted. Also, a better approach of the technology’s capabilities can be investigated.
More speciﬁc, massive connections is a factor that can impact the performance of the
NB-IoT, as more connected devices can generate more trafﬁc and the available resources
should be shared between all those NB-IoT devices. As a result, testing the performance
of the network under limited capacity can extract more realistic results.
One more scenario that can be implemented is to test the performance of the NB-IoT
technology in a "real" network. It is mentioned that the NB-IoT technology provides de-
ployment modes that the resources are shared with the LTE technology. As a result, if a
scenario in a "real network" is implemented, it is expected to observe differences between
the deployment modes.
Also, by testing the NB-IoT technology in a "real" network, the interference that can occur
in real environmental conditions can impact the performance of the NB-IoT and more re-
alistic results can be extracted.
