-­‐	  1	  -­‐  
Abstract 
	  
The	  aim	  of	  this	  thesis	  was	  to	  develop	  a	  new	  tracer	  method	  to	  observe	  changes	  in	  metabolic	  
pattern	  in	  E.	  coli	  (BL21	  strain).	  The	  characterization	  of	  metabolic	  fingerprint	  was	  possible	  due	  
to	  observations	  of	  metabolite	  changes	  during	  time	  and	  different	  limited	  growth	  mediums.	  Cell	  
extraction	   methods	   as	   total	   metabolite	   assay	   and	   extracellular	   metabolite	   assay	   was	  
performed	   and	   analysed	   by	   dDNP	   13C	   NMR	   or	   thermal	   NMR.	   To	   compare	   the	   metabolic	  
differences	  within	  phenotype,	  a	  closely	  related	  E.	  coli	  strain	  (MG1655)	  was	  additionally	  used.	  	  
To	  observe	  metabolite	  changes	  in	  a	  longer	  time	  frame	  than	  is	  normally	  possible	  with	  real	  time	  
dDNP,	  the	  steps	  in	  the	  experiment	  was	  turned	  around.	  This	  made	  it	  possible	  to	  incubate	  the	  
cells	   for	   longer	   time	   than	   few	   seconds.	   There	   were	   observed	   clear	   metabolic	   change	   in	  
phenotyping	  by	  applying	  different	  limited	  carbon	  sources.	  Closely	  related	  E.	  coli	  strains	  (BL21	  
and	  MG1655)	  showed	  different	  phenotypic	  patterns.	  The	  change	  in	  phenotypic	  pathway	  can	  
be	  used	  for	  further	  investigations	  of	  pathogens	  strategy	  in	  limited	  nutrition	  environments.	  	  
	  
	  
	  
	  
	  
Resumé  
 
Formålet	   med	   specialet	   var	   at	   udvikle	   en	   ny	   tracermetode	   til	   at	   observere	   ændringer	   i	  
metabolisk	  mønster	  på	  E.	  coli	  (BL21	  sammen).	  Karakteriseringen	  af	  metabolisk	  fingerprint	  var	  
muligt	   på	   baggrund	   af	   observationer	   af	   metabolitændringer	   under	   tid	   og	   forskellige	  
begrænsede	   vækstmedier.	   Celleekstraktionsmetoder	   som	   total	   metabolitanalyse	   og	  
ekstracellulær	  metabolitanalyse	  blev	  udført	  og	  analyseret	  ved	  dDNP	   13C	  NMR	  eller	  termisk	  
NMR.	  For	  at	  sammenligne	  metaboliske	  forskelle	  inden	  for	  fænotyper	  blev	  en	  tæt	  beslægtet	  E.	  
coli	  stamme	  (MG1655)	  yderligere	  anvendt.	  	  
For	  at	  observere	  metaboliske	  ændringer	  i	  en	  længere	  tidsramme	  end	  der	  normalt	  er	  muligt	  
med	  real-­‐time	  dDNP,	  var	  trinene	  i	  processen	  vendt	  om.	  Dette	  gjorde	  det	  muligt	  at	  inkubere	  
celler	  i	  længere	  tidsperiode	  end	  få	  sekunder.	  Der	  blev	  observeret	  en	  klar	  metaboliske	  ændring	  
i	  fænotypen	  ved	  anvendelse	  af	  forskellige	  begrænsede	  kulstofkilder.	  Tæt	  beslægtede	  E.	  coli	  
stammer	  (BL21	  og	  MG1655)	  viste	  forskellige	  fænotypiske	  mønstre.	  Ændringen	  i	  fænotypiske	  
mønstre	  kan	  anvendes	  til	  fremtidige	  undersøgelser	  med	  viden	  inden	  for	  patogenstrategi	  i	  
begrænsede	  næringsmiljøer.

1   Aim	  
The	  aim	  of	  this	  project	  is	  to	  investigate	  a	  new	  tracer	  method	  to	  observe	  a	  change	  in	  the	  
metabolic	  pattern	  in	  Enterobacteriaceae.	  Escherichia	  coli	  (E.	  coli)	  is	  chosen	  as	  a	  model	  system.	  
The	  observation	  of	  metabolic	  change	  is	  based	  on	  various	  nutrition	  sources.	  	  
Quantitative	  data	  from	  metabolites	  changes	  will	  be	  obtained	  by	  analysis	  of	  dDNP	  and	  13C	  NMR	  
data.	  
1.1   The	  objectives	  
	  
To	  develop	  a	  new	  tracer	  method	  for	  metabolic	  fingerprinting	  in	  E.	  coli	  based	  on	  NMR	  	  
The	  main	  aim	  is	  to	  develop	  a	  13C	  isotope-­‐enriched	  tracer	  method	  for	  elucidating	  metabolic	  
pathways.	  Analysis	  of	  the	  metabolomics	  output	  is	  performed	  with	   13C	  NMR	  technique.	  To	  
enhance	  the	  obtained	  signals,	  a	  novel	  tracer	  based	  NMR	  technique	  called	  dDNP	  is	  applied.	  
	  
The	  method	  development	  includes:	  
	  
•  
An	   evaluation	   of	   a	   new	   reverse	   step	   version	   of	   the	   usual	   real-­‐time	   cellular	   dDNP	  
experiment.	  
	  
•  
Setting	   up	   a	   method	   of	   incubating	   and	   arresting	   the	   cell	   metabolic	   system	   and	  
optimising	  different	  experimental	  parameters.	  The	  phenotypical	  output	  of	  metabolic	  
system	  will	  be	  based	  on	  total	  metabolites	  (intra	  and	  extracellular).	  	  
	  
	  
	  
To	  characterize	  the	  metabolic	  fingerprint	  under	  different	  growth	  conditions	  in	  E.	  
coli.	  	  
The	  developed	  metabolic	  tracer	  method	  will	  be	  used	  to	  characterize	  metabolic	  fingerprint.	  
The	   purpose	   is	   to	   observe	   a	   change	   in	   metabolic	   phenotyping	   under	   different	   growth	  
conditions	  in	  E.	  coli.	  	  
	  
Characterization	  of	  metabolic	  fingerprint	  includes:	  	  
	  
•  
Using	  the	  developed	  tracer	  method	  in	  combination	  with	  different	  growth	  on	  carbon	  
sources.	  The	  purpose	  is	  to	  observe	  change	  in	  metabolite	  outcome.	  	  
	  
•  
Comparing	  two	  E.	  coli	  strains,	  BL21	  and	  MG1655,	  that	  are	  used	  as	  model	  systems	  to	  
see	  the	  variation	  of	  metabolic	  phenotypic	  pattern.

2   Background	  	  
	  
This	  section	  describes	  fundamentals	  in	  the	  theory	  and	  background	  knowledge	  within	  the	  area	  
of	  E.	  coli	  energy	  metabolism	  and	  about	  analysis	  of	  metabolites	  and	  phenotype	  detection	  by	  
the	  dDNP	  NMR	  method.	  	  	  
	  
2.1   Metabolic	  phenotyping	  
Metabolic	  pathways	  are	  regulated	  by	  genetic	  and	  enzymatic	  and	  biochemical	  reactions,	  in	  
which	  the	  phenotypical	  outcome	  of	  these	  processes	  leads	  to	  production	  of	  chemicals	  called	  
metabolites1.	  
Bacteria	  can	  sense	  different	  chemical	  signals	  for	  different	  environments	  like	  the	  intestine	  for	  
bile	  salt	  1,2.	  The	  bacteria	  uses	  the	  signals	  to	  modify	  the	  gene	  expression	  and	  the	  metabolic	  
network	   in	   order	   to	   optimize	   bacterial	   growth	   and	   be	   beneficial	   in	   its	   survival	   in	   the	  
microenvironment	  1.	  The	  environmental	  changes	  include	  concentrations	  of	  nutrients	  such	  as	  
carbon,	  nitrogen,	  phosphate,	  ion	  sources	  as	  well	  as	  growth	  conditions	  like	  pH,	  temperature	  
and	  oxygen	  available2.	  Bacteria	  are	  adaptable	  to	  culture	  environments2,	  but	  the	  physical	  and	  
morphological	  changes	  affect	  the	  organism’s	  ability	  to	  adapt	  effectively	  and	  secure	  survive3.	  
However,	  bacteria	  are	  qualified	  to	  adapt	  to	  new	  environmental	  changes	  4.	  	  
	  
A	  subgroup	  of	  bacteria,	  intracellular	  pathogens	  has	  evolved	  to	  survive	  and	  multiply	  inside	  a	  
host	  cell1.	  The	  nutrition	  supply	  depends	  on	  what	  is	  taken	  up	  by	  the	  host	  cell	  and	  representing	  
a	   special	   interesting	   case	   of	   metabolic	   interplay.	   The	   pathogen	   is	   usually	   adapting	   to	  
eukaryotic	  hosts	  after	  an	  infection,	  while	  the	  host	  is	  trying	  to	  eliminate	  the	  pathogen.	  The	  
pathogen	  is	  searching	  for	  beneficial	  nutrition	  when	  the	  host	  cell	  will	  be	  damaged	  5.	  A	  study	  
from	  Kentner	  et	  al.	  (2014)	  describes	  the	  pathogenic	  Shigella	  growing	  on	  metabolites	  produced	  
by	  its	  human	  host	  (HeLa	  cell	  line).	  Pathogens	  that	  are	  targeting	  intracellular	  environments	  in	  
the	  host	  cell	  are	  taking	  advantage	  of	  the	  host	  cells’	  micro-­‐	  and	  macronutrients	  such	  as	  carbon	  
source	  and	  amino	  acids.	  In	  this	  case,	  the	  pathogen	  is	  competing	  for	  the	  nutrition	  in	  the	  host	  
cell	   and	   is	   more	   likely	   to	   initiate	   infection.	   The	   pathogen	   needs	   to	   adapt	   to	   the	   new	  
environment	   for	   survival	   in	   the	   host	   before	   the	   host	   immune	   system	   eliminates	   the	  
pathogen1.	  	  	  
	  
The	  knowledge	  and	  understanding	  of	  interaction	  of	  pathogens	  in	  intracellular	  hosts	  has	  lagged	  
earlier	  due	  to	  technical	  challenges5.	  Different	  nutrition	  sources	  and	  limitations	  can	  lead	  to	  
changes	   in	   the	   pathogen’s	   metabolic	   strategy;	   studying	   these	   changes	   in	   metabolites	  
production	  being	  the	  aim	  of	  this	  study.	  Metabolites	  are	  observed	  as	  the	  outcome	  of	  metabolic	  
pathway.	  The	  knowledge	  of	  the	  metabolic	  shifts	  in	  the	  pathogen	  is	  a	  good	  model	  to	  investigate	  
metabolic	  changes	  in	  a	  more	  complex	  in	  vivo	  system.

2.2   Metabolic	  tracer	  assay	  	  	  
An	  opportunity	  to	  use	  isotopic	  tracer	  is	  to	  explore	  metabolic	  changes	  in	  the	  pathway	  and	  
network.	  Isotopic	  tracer	  gives	  specially	  the	  opening	  to	  use	  nuclear	  magnetic	  resonance	  (NMR)	  
analysis	  for	  metabolic	  understanding.	  The	  benefit	  that	  isotopic	  tracer	  is	  offering	  is	  to	  enrich	  
the	  13C	  to	  enhance	  detection	  sensitivity	  in	  NMR	  analysis6.	  The	  tracer	  is	  able	  to	  be	  exposed	  13C	  
from	  the	  background	  noise,	  which	  make	  it	  unique	  and	  detectable.	  	  	  	  
Until	  now,	  mass	  spectroscopy	  and	  NMR	  have	  been	  used	  as	  isotopic	  tracer	  assays	  for	  analysis	  
of	  the	  metabolites	  from	  cells	  with	  a	  focus	  of	  change	  of	  metabolite	  patterns.	  	  
	  
Due	  to	  low	  sensitivity	  in	  13C	  NMR	  the	  ability	  to	  detect	  metabolites	  have	  been	  limited.	  	  It	  was	  
not	  possible	  to	  track	  metabolic	  changes	  by	  13C	  NMR	  by	  non-­‐invasive	  in	  situ	  procedure	  in	  living	  
organisms	  due	  to	  low	  sensitivity	  of	  the	  technique6.	   13C	  NMR	  has	  earlier	  been	  used,	  but	  it	  
required	   time	   resolution	   and	   sensitivity	   for	   detection	   of	   metabolites	   in	   the	   intermediates	  
pathways7.	  This	  can	  be	  solved	  by	  using	  the	  hyperpolarized	  DNP	  8.	  Dynamic	  nuclear	  polarization	  
(DNP)	   technique	   that	   was	   used	   in	   DNP	   nuclear	   magnetic	   resonance	   (DNP	   NMR)	   was	  
developed.	  Signals	  that	  were	  difficult	  obtained	  earlier	  by	  NMR,	  could	  now	  be	  detected.	  This	  
method	  has	  been	  an	  advantage	  for	  metabolite	  determination	  in	  vivo	  assays,	  and	  now	  also	  for	  
in	  vitro	  assays,	  because	  of	  hyperpolarization	  by	  DNP	  allows	  strong	  short-­‐time	  signals	  to	  be	  
detected,	  when	  it	  is	  dissolved	  8,9.	  Real-­‐time	  DNP	  has	  been	  used	  in	  relation	  to	  metabolic	  flux	  
studies10.	   In	   real-­‐time	   DNP	   the	   hyperpolarized	   solution	   is	   directly	   transferred	   to	   a	   NMR	  
instrument,	  that	  already	  has	  the	  biological	  sample	  ready	  to	  be	  analysed6.	  The	  disadvantage	  of	  
doing	  hyperpolarized	  DNP	  is	  to	  have	  a	  short	  time	  slot	  and,	  low	  relaxation	  time	  (T1),	  which	  
challenge	  the	  experiment7.	  	  
	  
A	   new	   proposal	   to	   conduct	   the	   13C	   glucose	   tracer	   method	   is	   to	   first	   prepare	   a	   biological	  
extract,	  and	  later	  prepare	  it	  with	  polarized	  medium	  to	  a	  solid	  phase	  of	  polarization	  followed

order	  gives	  the	  possibility	  to	  incubate	  the	  cells	  for	  longer	  duration	  (more	  than	  few	  seconds).	  
The	  hyperpolarization	  will	  offer	  enhancement	  of	  signals	  of	  the	  biological	  components,	  and	  
make	   it	   able	   to	   detect	   metabolic	   pathway	   in	   vivo.	   The	   challenge,	   which	   is	   a	   technique-­‐
dependent	  issue,	  is	  to	  make	  this	  assay	  quantitative	  by	  controlling	  the	  short	  relaxation	  time	  
and	  the	  fast	  disappearance	  of	  signals.	  	  
	  
To	  apply	  this	  new	  proposed	  method	  for	  a	  metabolic	  tracer	  assay,	  optimization	  of	  the	  assay	  is	  
first	  required.	  These	  optimization	  parameters	  include	  (1)	  incubation	  times	  and	  concentration,	  
(2)	  metabolic	  quenching	  methods	  and	  (3)	  DNP.	  The	  optimizations	  are	  used	  afterwards	  used	  in	  
cell	  model	  system.

3.1   Detectable	  nucleus	  	  
Nuclear	  magnetic	  resonance	  (NMR)	  is	  a	  technique	  that	  can	  determine	  the	  structure	  of	  organic

nucleus	  can	  therefore	  be	  seen	  as	  a	  bar	  magnetic	  and	  will	  orient	  it	  selves	  in	  a	  spin	  rotation	  axis,	  
which	  is	  called	  the	  nuclear	  spin	  (I).	  For	  example,	  1H	  nucleic	  (proton)	  has	  two	  possible	  magnetic

or	  –	  ½	  numbers.	  There	  is	  a	  rule	  that	  says	  that;	  a	  nucleus	  with	  an	  even	  mass	  A,	  charge	  Z	  and

expressed	  as	  n/2	  (n=	  odd	  integer)	  like	  the	  following	  isotopes,	  1H,	  13C,	  15N	  and	  17O,	  which	  can

After	  bringing	  the	  signal	  out	  of	  equilibrium	  and	  in	  to	  the	  x,y	  plane	  it	  will	  return	  to	  its	  original	  
state	  along	  the	  z-­‐axis.	  This	  time	  constant	  is	  called	  T1.	  	  
T1,	  also	  called	  the	  spin-­‐lattice	  relaxation	  time	  (or	  nuclear	  relaxation	  time),	  is	  the	  time	  that	  are	  
needed	  for	  the	  system	  to	  return	  63%	  of	  the	  way	  near	  thermal	  equilibrium22.	  When	  the	  T1	  value	  
is	  long,	  the	  nucleus	  is	  relaxing	  slowly	  and	  gives	  a	  sharp	  signal,	  and	  when	  the	  signal	  is	  broad,	  
the	  nucleic	  are	  relaxing	  rapidly21.	  	  
T1	  value	  is	  depending	  by	  the	  particular	  type	  of	  nucleus	  and	  the	  location	  of	  the	  nucleus	  in	  the	  
molecule.	  For	  liquids	  the	  usual	  values	  lies	  between	  10-­‐2	  –	  102	  seconds	  21.

Ardenkjær-­‐Larsen	  et	  al.	  (2003)	  did	  describe	  dissolution	  DNP	  (dDNP)	  as	  the	  first	  one.	  It	  was	  
here	  shown	  that	  strong	  polarization	  of	  nuclear	  spin	  in	  solution	  could	  increase	  SNR	  for	  more	  
than	  10,000	  times	  in	  liquid	  state	  7.	  
	  
3.3.1   Sample	  polarization	  
A	   challenge	   for	   DNP	   is	   that	   the	   samples	   need	   to	   be	   prepared	   in	   the	   solid	   phase.	   Sample	  
preparation	  consists	  of	  polarization	  medium	  that	  helps	  the	  sample	  to	  polarize	  efficiently	  by	  
securing	  a	  homogeneous	  distribution	  of	  the	  radical.	  When	  designing	  a	  DNP	  probe,	  the	  position	  
of	  the	  13C	  labels	  is	  placed	  where	  the	  relaxation	  that	  is	  effecting	  13C	  nuclei	  is	  maintained.	  	  
	  
Dipolar	  coupling	  is	  the	  most	  frequent	  source	  for	  relaxation	  processes,	  e.g.	  1H	  –	  13C,	  which	  limit	  
the	  use	  of	  the	  DNP	  probe	  by	  decreasing	  the	  hyperpolarized	  T1.	  For	  solving	  this	  problem,	  the	  
most	   useful	   DNP	   probe	   have	   13C-­‐labelling	   on	   carbonyl	   position.	   Alternatively,	   1H	   can	   be	  
replaced	  with	  deuterium	  (D),	  since	  the	  dipolar	  interaction	  is	  proportional	  to	  the	  gyromagnetic	  
ratio	  (γ).	  	  
	  
The	  polarization	  medium	  consist	  of	  Gadolinium	  (Gd3+),	  radical	  (OX063),	  glycerol	  and	  water.	  	  
Gadolinium	  is	  a	  paramagnetic	  molecule,	  which	  has	  the	  function	  to	  increase	  the	  polarization	  
level	  of	  13C-­‐labeled	  compounds24.	  Gadolinium	  are	  reducing	  the	  T1	  for	  the	  electrons,	  which	  is	  
done	  in	  purpose	  of	  faster	  returning	  of	  pulse	  so	  a	  new	  13C	  can	  be	  pulsed.	  
The	  ratio	  between	  1:500-­‐1000	  (conc.	  1-­‐50mM)	  for	   13C	  nucleus	  and	  radical	  is	  needed.	  The	  
radical	  is	  important	  to	  be	  added,	  because	  there	  are	  needed	  unpaired	  electrons,	  since	  DNP	  is	  
established	  based	  of	  electrons	  polarization	  being	  transferred	  to	  nuclear	  polarization	  in	  the	  
solid	  state24,25.

The	  sample	  preparation	  is	  placed	  in	  a	  sample	  cup,	  which	  is	  inserted	  into	  the	  DNP	  instrument

3.3.4   Pros	  and	  cons	  for	  DNP	  	  
NMR	  is	  such	  a	  powerful	  technique,	  because	  it	  is	  quantitative.	  The	  problem	  is	  that	  it	  is	  not	  
sensitive	  because	  of	  Boltzmann	  distribution.	  dDNP	  can	  increase	  the	  sensitivity	  by	  changing	  
the	  conditions	  in	  Boltzmann	  distribution.	  The	  method	  is	  difficult	  to	  use	  quantitatively,	  both	  as	  
real-­‐time	  assay	  and	  as	  a	  tracer	  assay.	  
The	  difference	  in	  relaxation	  time	  creates	  a	  variation	  in	  the	  signal	  amount.	  By	  using	  the	  tracer	  
assay	  method,	  it	  is	  possible	  to	  compensate	  by	  using	  an	  internal	  standard	  reference	  and	  survey	  
the	  relaxation	  time	  for	  the	  compounds	  that	  is	  observed.	  	  
Despite	  of	  the	  problem	  based	  on	  the	  quantification,	  the	  dDNP	  give	  the	  possibility	  to	  observe	  
metabolites	  from	  biological	  experiments	  that	  cannot	  be	  seen	  with	  13C	  NMR.

5.1   DNP	  isotope	  tracer	  assay	  	  
The	  fundamental	  problem	  with	  dDNP	  regarding	  relaxation	  time	  and	  transferring	  of	  dissolution	  
components	  within	  a	  certain	  short	  time	  frame	  need	  evaluation,	  which	  is	  accomplished	  below.	  	  
A	  new	  assay	  was	  developed	  for	  measuring	  metabolites	  using	  the	  sensitivity	  gain	  from	  DNP	  
NMR.	   This	   work	   is	   described	   in	   its	   full	   length	   in	   appendix	   1	   comprising	   a	   manuscript	   in	  
preparation.	   Here,	   the	   technical	   site	   of	   this	   work	   is	   explained	   in	   more	   detail.	  
	  
	  
5.1.1   Technical	  reproducibility	  	  
Technical	  reproducibility	  includes	  two	  different	  focus	  area:	  evaluation	  of	  solid	  and	  liquid	  stare	  
polarization	  level	  and	  component	  liquid	  state	  relaxation	  time.	  
	  	  	  	  
5.1.1.1   Evaluation	  polarization	  level	  
First	  DNP	  conditions	  were	  optimized.	  This	  was	  done	  by	  looking	  at	  signal	  development	  from	  
stock	  of	  13C-­‐labeled	  compounds.

unnecessary	   time,	   a	   technical	   study	   for	   optimal	   polarization	   technique	   was	   conducted.	  
Technical	  experiments	  demonstrate	  the	  most	  optimal	  polarization	  condition	  for	  solid-­‐	  and	  
liquid-­‐state	  
polarization,

is	  
the	  
reason	  
why	  
they	  
are	  
made.

polarized	  for	  30	  min.,	  90	  min.	  and	  240	  min.	  (n=3)	  and	  the	  solid-­‐state	  signal	  was	  followed.

5.2   	  Metabolic	  isotope	  tracer	  assays	  	  
It	   was	   then	   investigated	   what	   type	   of	   metabolites	   could	   be	   observed	   from	   the	   chosen

various	  nutritional	  sources.	  The	  analysis	  of	  metabolites	  was	  facilitated	  by	  dDNP	  NMR	  that	  
enhanced	  the	  metabolic	  signals	  to	  make	  them	  detectable.	  	  
Firstly,	   the	   technical	   issues	   regarding	   to	   optimal	   use	   of	   DNP	   NMR	   was	   explored	   and	  
considerations	  concerning	  DNP	  conditions	  was	  optimized	  to	  achieve	  the	  best	  and	  highest	  
signals.	  Next,	  the	  detections	  method	  for	  metabolites	  extractions	  was	  tested	  and	  optimized	  
based	  on	  the	  variation	  in	  different	  parameters.	  Finally,	  the	  detections	  method	  reached	  the	  
optimal	  conditions	  to	  conduct	  the	  assay	  and	  observe	  the	  pattern	  in	  metabolic	  system	  based	  
on	  nutrition	  source.	  	  
During	  these	  optimizations	  of	  assays	  there	  has	  been	  some	  challenges	  and	  some	  decisions	  
were	  taken	  to	  implement	  the	  assays	  in	  the	  best	  way,	  which	  is	  explain	  more	  in	  detailed	  in	  this	  
discussion	  part.	  	  
	  
6.1   Quantitative	  dissolution	  DNP	  	  
The	  technical	  and	  biological	  results	  were	  reproducible,	  which	  is	  important	  in	  considerations	  
of	  demonstrating	  new	  methods	  and	  allow	  the	  reader	  of	  this	  thesis	  to	  give	  the	  opportunity	  to	  
compare	  results	  from	  this	  new	  method.	  Based	  on	  reproducibility	  from	  the	  first	  experimental	  
results	  the	  following	  experiments	  was	  only	  performed	  on	  one	  sample.	  Analysing	  the	  biological	  
samples	  by	  DNP	  NMR	  and	  thermal	  NMR	  do	  take	  long	  time	  to	  perform.	  It	  is	  prioritized	  to	  focus	  
on	   development	   of	   the	   method.	   It	   is	   noticeable	   that	   the	   biological	   results	   did	   not	   act	  
randomly,	  when	  they	  were	  in	  controlled	  environment.	  
Thermal	   NMR	   has	   the	   advantage	   be	   quantitative	   and	   fast,	   but	   has	   the	   limitation	   not	   to	  
enhance	  the	  metabolite	  signals.	  The	  hyperpolarized	  advantage	  that	  enhance	  the	  signals	  can	  
solve	  the	  weakness	  of	  NMR	  and	  still	  maintain	  the	  quantitative	  benefit	  of	  the	  NMR	  technique.	  
A	  limitation	  for	  dDNP	  is	  the	  relaxation	  time	  of	  components	  in	  a	  short	  time,	  which	  decrease	  
the	  signal.	  It	  is	  manageable	  to	  maintain	  the	  two	  closely	  related	  techniques	  unique	  functions	  
by	  combination.

7   Conclusion	  
	  
Experiments	  was	  implemented	  successfully	  and	  new	  methods	  for	  metabolic	  fingerprinting	  
was	  generated	  with	  promising	  results	  for	  a	  new	  initial	  research	  area.	  Combined	  technique	  
that	   included	   cell	   preparation	   and	   afterwards	   analysis	   of	   biological	   component	   made	   it	  
possible	  to	  develop	  a	  method	  that	  is	  leading	  to	  a	  metabolic	  fingerprint	  in	  E.	  coli	  strains.	  	  
	  
Three	  assays	  showed	  each	  variations	  in	  metabolites	  based	  on	  different	  methods.	  U-­‐(13C,	  D)	  
glucose	  was	  used	  as	  a	  tracer	  in	  cell	  incubations	  and	  further	  hyperpolarized	  with	  dDNP	  or	  NMR	  
to	  obtain	  metabolic	  data.	  The	  dDNP	  made	  it	  possible	  to	  measure	  the	  metabolites,	  which	  by	  
regular	  NMR	  instrument	  was	  not	  detectable	  because	  of	  low	  sensitivity.	  The	  solution	  for	  the
