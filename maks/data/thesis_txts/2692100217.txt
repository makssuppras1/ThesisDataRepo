Abstract
This study reviews and implements a Robust MPC based controller for the purpose of
trajectory tracking in MAVs specifically quadrotors under wind disturbance. A tube based
MPC scheme is designed on a system linearized around the hovering condition. A casВӯ
caded control architecture is used with an inner loop for faster attitude control and outer
loop for position control using MPC. Recursive feasibility and stability of the system is
ensured by exploiting the property of invariance. The disturbance is modelled as being
additive and bounded in nature. The CVXGEN solver is chosen after a review of available
solvers for the optimization problem. This method is applied to test on different simulation
environments using benchmark trajectories to evaluate the performance and a compariВӯ
son with Nominal MPC scheme is presented.
Keywords: Tube MPC, Trajectory Tracking, Quadrotor, Robust Control, CVXGEN

Introduction
Multirotor systems such as quadcopters have seen exponential growth in their use in many
applications. Modern Unmanned Ariel Vehicle (UAV) systems are experiencing increased
sensing and communication capabilities, while at the same time cost is decreasing. FeaВӯ
tures such as Vertical TakeВӯoff and Landing (VTOL), good maneuverability combined with
a simple mechanical design are some reasons why the use of UAVs has become central
in many industries and businesses such as farming, construction, delivery, filmВӯmaking,
etc. Furthermore, UAVs are being used for indoor and outdoor inspection purposes in
insurance, chemical, power generation, and other industries.
Currently, UAV capabilities have extended to fully autonomous indoor and outdoor flights.
Trajectory tracking in such applications involving UAVs has increasingly demanded faster
and more accurate motion control in order to improve efficiency while also avoiding colliВӯ
sions with the environment and follow the planned path.
1.1
Motivation
UAVs, especially quadcopters are being employed increasingly in robotics research such
as design, analysis, and implementation of flight control techniques. The applications
arising with the use of UAVs involve working in environments that have varied obstacles
so it is essential for the quadrotor to be able to follow a predefined trajectory efficiently in
the presence of disturbances as is the case in real scenarios. Automated formation flight
scenarios, for instance, can take advantage of high performance and robust trajectory
tracking to maintain the formation without collisions and the planned path.
Due to nonВӯlinearities of the dynamics, influences from sensor noise, disturbances, and
parametric uncertainties, autonomous control can be challenging. Many different flight
control strategies have been studied and proposed for this task. Conventional solutions
such as the Proportional, Integral, and Derivative (PID) controller, for instance, can be
useful for very simple tracking but can have large errors when used in complex tasks
[Chaib, Netto, and Mammar 2004]. Also, the interpretation and selection of PID gains in
relation to the actual system and most importantly, PID schemes do not support putting
explicit constraints on the system.
Another common control strategy that can be employed is the Linear Quadratic Regulator
(LQR) approach. In this approach, the control problem is formulated in terms of an objecВӯ
tive function that minimizes an associated performance cost. This formulation provides a
much better interpretation of the gains that are obtained by tuning weights for each state
and input. While this can be effective, it is a form of offline unconstrained optimal control
so there is still no option to explicitly put constraints on the system.
Among the different milestones to be achieved in the development of autonomous multiВӯ
rotor systems finding a suitable control strategy that addresses all the requirements still
remains a challenge, and an active area of research.
1.2
Problem Statement
Applications relating to quadrotors usually involve operating in varying conditions of obВӯ
stacles and disturbances. In such environments, it is essential to understand and take into
account the effect of wind impacting the system as it affects the position and the trajectory

tracking the performance of the quadrotor. When working in outdoor environments this
effect is quite apparent on the system but indoor applications can also benefit from this
modeling. When using formation control with multiple drones indoors or operating close
to the wall and floor, similar wind effects are generated from the thrusters impacting the
performance of the system.
Addressing these challenges requires sound representation and modeling of disturbances,
design, and implementation of robust control strategies for safe operation as well as it is
essential to ensure stability and satisfaction of performance criteria in the presence of
model uncertainties. There are also constraints on the system which need to be satisfied
such as energy minimization due to battery and actuator limitations etc. A common way to
deal with constraints relates to constrained control strategies, specifically predictive conВӯ
trol schemes such as Model Predictive Control (MPC). It is a form of constrained control
that uses an explicit system model to predict future states and inputs by solving an online
optimization problem. Also, the MPC problem can be designed to explicitly guarantee
feasibility and stability of the system. Furthermore, to deal with disturbances and model
uncertainties Robust MPC formulations have been studied and developed.
To this end, this thesis tries to analyze available strategies and decide on an implemenВӯ
tation of a control architecture for trajectory tracking of quadrotor systems under disturВӯ
bance.
1.3
Scope of the project
In this project, the final goals are as follows:
1. Review of the main theory, stability, and feasibility of nominal and Robust Linear
Model Predictive Control (MPC) approach.
2. Select suitable Robust MPC strategy for motion control of quadrotor.
3. State the main assumptions on disturbance, uncertainties, and their modeling.
4. Implement and evaluate the selected Robust MPC approach in MATLAB simulator
to validate the model.
5. Implement and evaluate the selected Robust MPC approach in high fidelity Webots
simulator and RobotOS(ROS) framework to reduce the realityВӯgap.
6. Perform a comparative study with the nominal MPC architecture.

Solving the problem addressed in this thesis can be done using different control techВӯ

2.1.1
Nominal MPC
Initially developed to cater to the specific control needs of power plants and petroleum
refineries[GarcГӯa, Prett, and Morari 1989], MPC can now be found in many other fields
of application. MPC in the nominal sense is a type of computer control algorithm which
predicts the future response of the plant using an explicit process model. MPC algorithm
tries to calculate the optimal future sequence of inputs based on the model and constraints
on the system. At time t, the first optimal input in the predicted sequence is then applied
to the plant and the remaining inputs are discarded. The whole sequence of inputs is
calculated again at time t+1 by solving the optimal control problem again. The basic
structure of MPC is shown in section 2.1.1.
Figure 2.1: Receding horizon control scheme as used in the MPC formulation. The control
problem with objectives, system model and constraints along with the desired reference
is fed to the optimizer. The horizon plan is generated and first input is sent to the plant at
each time step.
The advantages of the design and implementation of MPC and a comparative study in
regards to the linearВӯquadratic control are also pointed out by [GarcГӯa, Prett, and Morari
1989].Commercially available MPC technology in the linear and nonВӯlinear sense is reВӯ
ported by [Qin and Badgwell 2003].
In relation to UAVs, [Kamel, Burri, and Siegwart 2017] implemented Linear and NonВӯlinear
MPC for tracking. They reported tests on real hardware and compared the two controller

2.1.2
Robust MPC
The nominal MPC formulation takes into account the state input and output constraints
and uses a prediction model to decide on the current actuation. In realВӯlife scenarios, disВӯ
turbances and model inaccuracies generally leading to reduced performance and more
importantly loss of feasibility when using nominal MPC formulation neglecting the disturВӯ
bance[Scokaert and Mayne 1998]. This is where Robust MPC schemes can be used
for addressing this issue. Robustness to model uncertainties and noise is an elementary
question about MPC. A robust system maintains its stability and meets some performance
specifications based on specified model uncertainty and type of noise signal[Bemporad
and Morari 2007]. Any purposeful discussion about the robustness of MPC must refer to
a specific range of uncertainty and specific performance criteria.
In realВӯlife situations, uncertainties can take the form of additive unknown but bounded
disturbance or multiplicative uncertainty due to imprecise knowledge of model parameter
or their timeВӯvarying nature [Kouvaritakis and Cannon 2015]. Especially for linear sysВӯ
tems, a good way to model uncertainties is in the form of additive uncertainty. This can
cover most of the nonВӯlinearities of the system.
The broad categorization of Robust MPC based on the existing literature can be done in
the following two ways: minВӯmax formulation and tubeВӯMPC approach [Singh, Pavone,
and Slotine 2016]. MinВӯmax formulation solves the MPC problem for optimizing the openВӯ
loop performance by minimizing the worstВӯcase cost. This is obtained considering the
maximum possible disturbances and uncertainties sampled from a bounded set[Raimondo
et al. 2009]. The original minВӯmax approach for systems with additive uncertainty was
adapted by [Scokaert and Mayne 1998]. In the tubeВӯbased MPC approach, an auxiliary
feedback controller is implemented that compensates for the deviation of states to bound
them in a region or вҖқtubesвҖқ around the nominal or desired trajectory [Singh, Pavone, and
Slotine 2016]. The concept of tubes around the nominal trajectory is depicted in secВӯ
tion 2.1.2
The motivation for this comes from the fact that the future predicted disturbances up to
a certain time will be known to the controller. To exploit this knowledge, optimizing over
a control policy is needed rather than input trajectories. The computation demand of
such an approach increases exponentially depending on the control horizon. To have an
applicable solution of Robust MPC some consideration needs to be given to the choice
of control policies. [Kouvaritakis and Cannon 2015]. Further, the computation can be
reduced by confining the states in sets that are considered to be a crossВӯsection of tubes.
This allows to optimize over predicted trajectories rather than control policies which lead
this form of Robust MPC to be used in industry for over 20 years.

Figure 4.1: Cascaded control structure

4.2
Nominal Linear MPC
The Linear MPC is based on the linearized model of the MAV whereas the more complex
NonВӯlinear MPC (NMPC) takes the full system dynamics into account. NMPC can have
better disturbance rejection capabilities but since agile trajectories are not considered
for this project and Linear MPC has been significantly more exploited in the literature.
Therefore, Linear MPC is considered to be a good starting point for the scope of this
thesis.
MPC can be thought of as an extension of the Linear Quadratic Regulator (LQR) conВӯ
troller. LQR is a regulator which is formulated as an unconstrained optimization problem
that is solved for an infinite time horizon for a linear dynamical system. The gains are
precomputed offline and inputs are translated to states using these fixed gains (constant
control law). Extending this formulation by adding constraints to the inputs and states of
the system can lead to a better formulation as real physical systems have limits. This conВӯ
verts the problem formulation to a constrained optimization problem which unfortunately
does not have a closedВӯform solution. Shortening the prediction horizon to a fixedВӯlength

5.1.2
Solver description
CVXGEN was selected as the solver of choice among the available solvers because it
provides flexibility in formulating the objective function and constraints. This is mainly
useful for adding slack variables to the problem definition which is important to deal with
infeasibility arising from nonВӯcritical constraint violations. Furthermore, no parameters are
fixed during the solver generation, only the size needs to be specified and the parameters
are passed at runВӯtime.
The solvers were generated using the online interface for each subsystem online and the
downloaded .mex files for MATLAB were compiled and separated by each subsystem. As
discussed earlier, CVXGEN is limited to mediumВӯsized optimization problems, the division
into subsystems helps to actually generate solvers. It is not possible to generate a solver
for the complete system as the constraints imposed by the problem are too large for the
generator to handle. The main solver file each subsystem is called at each time step and
the optimum solution is obtained.

Conclusion and Future Work
The problem of trajectory tracking in quadrotors under wind disturbance using robust flight
control scheme was investigated. This problem is of great interest for scenarios such as
filmmaking, autonomous outdoor flight, formation control etc. In order to address it, we
start by studying the theoritical aspects of optimal and constrained control strategies in
chapter 2. Considering the conclusions the robust control strategy namely, Tube based
Robust MPC has shown satisfactory performance.
In the design phase for the controller, a study of model dynamics of quadrotors was conВӯ
ducted and a dynamics model suitable for state prediction by the MPC was selected. A
specific objective function to cater to the specific needs of the problem was implemented.
Different constraints on the system were identified and added to the optimization probВӯ
lem formulation. Finally, the CVXGEN solver was selected after reviewing other available
options based on its flexibility in defining the problem using a simple online interface.
During the implementation of the tube MPC algorithm several practical issues were faced.
First, given the nature of the MPC problem, numerical problem were encountered specifВӯ
ically in the calculation of set differences for minimum invariant set calculation. The PonВӯ
tryagin difference, which is essentially a projection operation on sets can be very computaВӯ
tionally expensive depending on the size of sets. This calculation for a three dimensional
state space in Pitch and Roll subsystems, given the processing power of a laptop comВӯ
puter, could not be carried out to full invariance. This can be a cause of conservativeness
introduced in the system.
The main results from Matlab simulation show there was performance improvement using
TubeВӯMPC in terms of the RMSE position errors. While in the Webots simulation environВӯ
ment, the reality gap is narrowed down and the performance increase is not significant
compared to the nominal MPC. Perhaps this could be improved with further fine tuning of
the weights. Also a more accurate calculation of the noise bounds by conducting experiВӯ
ments on the real hardware can help in better modeling and results.
During the end of the project some experiments on the real hardware using the Nominal
MPC were conducted but could not be completed due to time constraints. In the prelimiВӯ
nary tests the perturbations due to model mismatch were very much apparent. Achieving
even stable takeВӯoff scenario was also quite challenging to achieve without employing
additional mechanisms.
Nonetheless, this formulation has shown to yield some good results compared to the nomВӯ
inal MPC formulation in terms of its inherent disturbance rejection capabilities. The linear
model used, while simple, is a good approximation and also reduces the online compuВӯ
tational cost. For the tube MPC algorithm while the offline computation of set differences
can be very high it still remains very practical for fast applications as online cost is the
same as the nominal system, given its formulation.
There are, however, several points that can be pointed out for future improvement of this
technique. Firstly, comprenhensive tests could not be conducted for the RMPC algorithm
on the real hardware due to time constraints in the scope of this thesis. This could be
the first step in the future to validate the performance of the designed technique. FurВӯ
ther, to reduce the conservativeness of the algorithm, supecomputer clusters could be
employed for the offline calculation of invariant sets. Finally, full nonВӯlinear dynamics for
Robust Model Predictive Control for Trajectory Tracking of MAV Under Wind Disturbance

tube based MPC could be investigated which is a a direction not quite exploited in the
current literature.
Finally, this thesis contributes in the investigation of disturbance handling in trajectory
tracking of UAVs by means of Robust MPC scheme with low online computation, yielding
reasonable results at the expense of higher offline computation.
