Abstract
LiDAR technology and 3D modelling are on the spotlight the last years, being used in more
commercial devices, such as mobile phones. SurveyôÙgrade LiDAR scanning instruments
will be compared with a consumerôÙgrade laserôÙscanner from an iPad and photogrammeôÙ
try from a handôÙheld camera. The results from the 3D point clouds and 3D meshes are
compared in order to find out the advantages and disadvantages of each method. SevôÙ
eral objects and environments are scanned and tested, analysing the accuracy and the
precision of the different methods. Showing that the LiDAR camera from the iPhone can
compete quite well the highly accurate, expensive, surveying instruments is one of the
main object of this thesis. The 3D point clouds and meshes are created within seconds,
by using a mobile application, taking over the other scanners that demand time consumôÙ
ing processes for the extraction of the data. Photogrammetry data seems to follow the
trend and create satisfying 3D point clouds, lacking scale without using known ground
points. The software used for processing is Agisoft Metadata and Cloud Compare and
the 3D scanning App was used for the Apple devices. The 3D point clouds are georefôÙ
erenced, based on the National Danish 3D point cloud (DHM), testing the accuracy and
the methods that can be achieved. The results shows that the models have an accuracy
approximately 30ôÙ50 cm. Augmented Reality (AR) in the iPhone is tested, showing imôÙ
pressive results, offering to the users several tools that are instantly applicable. Finally,
the problems and the difficulties are discussed, followed by advises for improvements in
order to produce and georeference more accurate 3D point clouds.

3D representation of the real world has gained popularity over the last years, not without a
reason. Nowadays, it is used in many different scientific fields, from medicine by modelling
human organs to engineering by scanning entire buildings and constructions. In a world
that is evolving rapidly, high technology becomes commercial and people are getting more
involved. Mobile phones and tables compete high quality, expensive, but most importantly

But what is the quality of the created 3D models? How accurate are they? Can those
devices compete very expensive, high accurate LiDAR scanners? All those questions
and many more will be answered in this project.
Apart from the 3D scanning technology on consumer devices, another field of technology

from research laboratories to widespread availability on consumer devices, AR AppliôÙ

traditional consumer fields as fashion, food, customer service, and gaming. The democôÙ

more about connecting experiences with the realôÙworld. Thorough research about AR is

the rapid development and usage of AR in many different fields. For example in article

LiDAR Technology
Light Detection and Ranging (LiDAR) is a remote sensing method that uses light as a
pulsed laser in order to measure distancesôÙranges. A LiDAR instrument principally conôÙ
sists of a laser (photon emitter) and a receiver. American Geosciences Institute n.d. The
laser scanner transmits a short laser pulse or beam and it measures the time that takes
for the beam to hit the object or surface and reflect back to the laser scanner. The range
R, is calculated from the following equation 2.1:
R = cT

Discussion
Interesting results came out of this project, as well as many concerns and issues that
were dealt with successfully or notôÙ. In this chapter, important outcomes and different
approaches of the project are presented.
To begin with the equipment and its advantages and disadvantages are commented.
Starting from the creation of 3D point clouds from photogrammetry, an expensive program
(Agisoft Metadata) is needed that costs around 3.500 USD and a lot of time to process
the data. Powerful computers can save time and money, although for each step of the
process, the user has to wait for several minutes or hours. The results, although, were
unexpectedly good, as the 3D model of the pipe and the surrounding area was formed
almost identically to the real one. The video that takes less time than the photos, seems
to be the best method for the creation of the model. The amount of images that will be
processed is chosen by the user and furthermore, the user is reassured that photos will
be overlapped.
Regarding the Leica TLS, apart from being expensive, it is need to be set up on the field
in order for the user to take measurements. That means that more time needs to be spent
on the field. The BLK2GO as well as the iPad scanner demand walking through the area,
which sometimes might not be doable if the area is under constructions. The number of
photos showed that it can easily affect the result of the photogrammetric processes.
The LiDAR technology in commercial devices has brought pioneering changes in engiôÙ
neering. The 3D models from the iPhone, having high resolution or no, can definitely give
an overview of the construction in a field. Long travel distances for site visits can be reôÙ
duced as by scanning an area, instantaneously a 3D model is created. That can be used
as a daily report, to keep up with the progress on the field, to improve and study possible
changes in the construction or even to measure distances and the accuracy from the field.
The outcome of this project is that a mobile or a table are capable of doing all those and
many more. In figure 5.1, a timeôÙlapse of the main study area with constructions of this
project is presented. By scanning the area the changes in the scenery at DTU campus
can be followed up with.
Furthermore, the creation of 3D models can reduce the construction rework costs. SimuôÙ
lations of the building projects by using laser scanning of AR can help the building teams
to identify any potential problems that might arise during construction. Detecting problems
during the early stages of a project, can help reduce drastically the cost of the reworks
and prevent timeline delays. As many different scanners were compared in this project,
any of them can be used for that purpose, ranging from very expensive high accurate
scanners to cheap commercial devices or cameras.

First of all, the equipment that was used in this project was limited and therefore, more

Photogrammetry and LiDAR sensors was used for the creation of the 3D meshes and point

used for the creation of 3D models. Radar technology uses radio waves to determine the
distance (range), angle, or velocity of objects and the latest years is on the spotlight as

tions, could have provided more information about how Appleãs LiDAR sensor especially
