Abstract
Over the last couple of decades have the green energy industry taken more
shape and created more awareness especially the wind industry is progressing.
To make the energy prices produced from wind turbines competitive to the
corresponding from oil and gas, the oshore wind farms must be cost ecient.
This can be done by obtaining knowledge of how the extreme waves which will
aect the monopile of the wind turbine, thereby can the force be depreciated
and the materials used for the wind turbine can be reduced.
In this study it is investigated how wave kinematics change under inuence of
a sloping bed. Additionally if it is possible to obtain the particle acceleration
of extreme waves by measuring their surface elevations. The relation between
the extreme surface elevations and acceleration was formulated as du

velocity measurements was carried out for the same wave series and du

Introduction
1.1
Intro
The wind power industry has origin in Denmark and can be traced back to
1979. The green energy industry is a industry in growth and especially the wind
power industry are subject to a fast globalization. It is expensive to construction
oshore wind farms and the prices for the energy gather from these wind farms
are accordingly. To be able to compete with other energy sectors like oil and
gas the production and construction of wind farms much be cheaper. There is
a wide research area investigating methods to make the construction of oshore
wind farms cheaper.
A way to lower the cost of a wind farm is to reduced
the materials used in the construction phase, if a better knowledge of how the
monopile is aected by extreme waves could be obtained a reduction in the steel
can possibly be achieved. Wind farms are build in water depths from 10-65 m
here will the seabed geometry change, this change in seabed will inuence the
waves kinematics.
In this study is the inuence of a changing seabed investigated, two slopes with
angle of respectively angle 1:10 and 1:25 is installed in a wave ume and extreme
wave conditions are simulated in the form of a storm with a return period of
10 years, the experiments are carried out in model scale 1:50.
Additionally
experiments are carried out to investigate if it is possible to measure the particle
acceleration in waves only by obtaining the surface elevation measured with a
wave gauge. If it is proved possible, this can be transacted into measuring the
accelerations in a sea state with a wave buoy, a wave buoy is shown in Fig. 1.1

forces which will aect a monopile can be calculated. These in-line forces can
be used as a rst approximation of forces caused by the sea state and hopefully

Additionally is the waves breaking for studied because the way a wave break

1.2
Project description
Problem motivation
When wind turbines are situated o-shore, it is essential to understand the
wave kinematics in order to determine the forces acting on the lower part of the
tower. When the wave forces on the tower are better understood, it will become
possible to build cheaper wind turbines and thereby the overall price on wind
energy will decrease. This is of course essential if wind energy is to become
competitive on price.
Extreme waves are strongly inuenced by the slope of the seabed. It is therefore
of interest to investigate the waves in themselves, even without the presence of
a monopile, but with varying seabed.
The project
In this project I will investigate how a sloping seabed inuence the wave kine-
matics. Additionally it is desired to test if the particle acceleration can be obtain
by measuring the surface elevation. The experimental work will be carried out
in a two steps
• Generate irregular series of interesting waves. Change bed slope. In this
part of the study I will use wave gauges to measure the surface elevation.

2.3
Shoaling
When waves propagate up a sloping bed, the wave heights increase, wave lengths
decrease, wave periods remain constant and the celerities decrease. This phe-
nomenon is known as shoaling.
Figure 2.2: The eect of shoaling is illustrated, the waves height increases and
the waves length decrease.
Fig. 2.2 shows how the waves deform as they are inuenced by the sloping bed.
The wave heights increase until the energy in the waves are high enough to
cause them to break. Additionally "wave breaks when the horizontal particle
velocity at the wave crest is equal or greater than the wave celerity, or when
a vertical tangent prole occurs at the free surface" [CL15]. Shoaling can be
used to determine the height of the waves at a given water depth. Shoaling
can therefore be used to calculate the deep water wave height, which is used
to calculate the surf similarity parameter. Assuming energy conservation the
bottom friction and wave breaking can then be neglected and the energy ux
between two sections will be constant. The deep water wave heights H0 can

Spilling waves occur when the surf similarity parameter is below 0.5. These
waves will become steeper until the crest collapses.
The spilling waves are
slowly dissipating energy as they approach the shoreline. Due to the slow energy
dissipation these waves break over longer time compared to other types of wave
breaking and are therefore mild forms of breaking.
The plunging breakers have a steeper crest compared to the spilling breaker,
which becomes more vertical and then breaks over. Here most of the energy in
the plunging breakers are released upon impact. The plunging breaker is the
breaking type containing the most energy. Therefore, are the plunging breaker
often the breaker type which is used as design parameter.
Collapsing breakers are an intermediate type between the plunging breaker and
the surging breaker
The surging breaker occurs when the waves have low steepness and the breach
prole is steep. The waves nearly break, because of the high slope steepness.
The energy swiftly disparates.

1:50, Hs≈0.11 m and Tp≈1.3 −1.7 s, which is why these wave characteristics
are selected.

Future Studies
DONG Energy is in the planning phase for one of the biggest oshore wind farms
ever build, the Hornsea project. The wind farm is planned to have capacity of
approximately 4 GW. Hornsea is located outside the east coast of England in
the North Sea. The seabeds bathymetry at the English east coast has an incli-
nation of less than one degree [NC06]. Therefore, would it be ingenious to carry
out experiment with a corresponding bed slope in the wave ume. A bed slope
with angle 1:50 and 1:100 is suggested to get a more precise understanding of
the inuence of the seabed bathymetry.
Additional to simulate the wave conditions better a wider range of wave char-
acteristics is proposed. This means more wave heights are desired to be inves-
tigated and perhaps more wave periods. To obtain more realistic sea states,
experiments with wind and current conditions is suggested applied to the test
matrix. This will increase the accuracy of the waves acceleration and thereby
the in-line force compared to real life sea states.
The method of measuring the waves' heights worked well, however to obtain a
more precise in-line the method of measuring the velocity should be reassessed.
It is suggested to use Particle Image Velocimetry (PIV) techniques to obtain
the full velocity prole. PIV measurements was carried out by Cowen, Sou and
Liu in 2003 [CMLR03] to make two-dimensional velocity measurements in surf
zone. If the full velocity prole could be measured the entire in-line force could
the be calculated instead of in a single point as done in this thesis. It is recom-
mended to install a model sized mono-pile with force gauges in the wave ume,
so the in-line force measured from the force gauges can be compared with the
calculated in-line force and further substantiate the relation

In regards to the videos which was recorded at water depth 0.15 m an extended
research should be performed to possible revise Cyril Galvin wave breaking

be investigated in space as well in time.
A Students T-test should be carried out on the histograms to get a better
understanding of the development in the waves as the propagate upstream the
