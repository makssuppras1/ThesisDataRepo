FerƟlizers are a main source of nutrients used by plants, nutrients such as Nitrogen, phosphorus and

China is currently the largest user of ammonia derived ferƟlizers with 27,3 Mt followed by India with 17.4

Chemical
Ammonia is the second most produced chemical aŌer sulfuric acid, and it is one of the seven basic

Emissions
Depending on how the CO₂ emissions are accounted, and how do the fossil fuel emissions used to 
produce ammonia are allocated, global esƟmates for ammonia carbon food print locate it between 1.2% 
and 1.8%, this esƟmates also converts other greenhouse emissions (Ammonia, Methane) to CO₂ 
equivalents. [Ref 1, 70].
Current CO₂ emissions per ton of produced ammonia vary signiﬁcantly depending on the used feedstock 
and method as well as other factors such as logisƟcs that can have a limited impact, the most commonly 
producƟon method is SMR (steam methane reforming) and has an average of 2.3 tons of CO₂ per ton of 
ammonia, obtaining hydrogen using coal as a feedstock can elevate emissions up to 4.5 tons of CO₂ per 
ton of ammonia, on the other hand renewable electrolysis average 0.5 tons of CO₂ per ton of ammonia 
and is expected to reduce to 0.1 by 2050. [Ref 1, 70] [Ref 2, 10]
Independently form the CO₂ emissions, ammonia by itself  is a hazardous toxic gas at ambient condiƟons, 
to increase storage density ammonia is oŌen sort in it liquid form (-33°C), sƟll ammonia can react 
violently and be suscepƟble to explosion, specialized handling is necessary in all its forms.

Ammonia prices
Mexican producƟon of Ammonia has reduced constantly since 2014, increasing the dependency on 
imported ammonia which has led to increases in prices, the spike on natural gas prices on the last year 
has competed halted local ammonia producƟon which is managed by the stare owned company PEMEX. 
[Ref 8, 3]
A 235% increase on ammonia prices was seen in 2021 alone in the span of only 12 months, with prices 
reaching over $1,500.00 USD Mt of ammonia at the beginning of 2022. Mexican authoriƟes have 
proposed a series of acƟons and reliefs with liƩle eﬀect on the price of ammonia which is currently 
depended on importaƟons. [Ref 8, 3]
Link to Henry Hub, Regional NG price maker
Henry hub is the hydrocarbon logisƟcal center of the Gulf of Mexico, located in the state of Louisiana 
USA, the hub handles the trade of Natural Gas, Hydrocarbons and derived products of the enƟre south 
United States, Caribbean and Mexico, naturally it has become the price maker node for the enƟre region, 
any producƟon or trade of hydrocarbons or their derivates is heavily linked to the hub´s market.
Most of Mexican natural gas importaƟons come from south USA with their prices set at the Henry Hub, as 
a result the natural gas prices in Mexico tend to mimic Henry Hub natural gas market prices. Ammonia 
producƟon in the Gulf of Mexico region is mostly done through steam reforming of natural gas, since 
natural gas is the main raw material needed for tradiƟonal ammonia producƟon, the market price of 
ammonia constantly mirrors the trends of natural gas prices.

MulƟple factors have also inﬂuenced the worldwide shortage of natural gas, such as the Russian-

that can disrupt the producƟon line of green ammonia, but this is an issue that can be solved with the 
correct design of buﬀer elements such as storage systems.
The Ammonia producƟon line requires the use of several products/services that could also potenƟally

Current Ammonia Cost CompeƟƟveness
Natural gas, being the main source of hydrogen and electricity needed for the ammonia synthesis, is by 
far the main driver of current ammonia prices worldwide, ammonia prices tend to follow closely the 
prices of natural gas and oil, with prices ranging signiﬁcantly depending on the region and availability of 
such feedstocks. [Ref 4, 1]
For the last decade, 2010 to 2020, Ammonia prices averaged the € 300 per ton with temporal and 
regional price spikes and regional variaƟons ranging from the € 120 to € 400 per ton, current 
internaƟonal aﬀairs such as COVID-19 and the Russian-Ukrainian conﬂict have spiked the natural gas 
prices and in consequence the ammonia prices currently sit above € 600 per ton worldwide, is diﬃcult to 
determine if the trends are to conƟnue of when and in which range will the average ammonia prices will 
stabilize again, it is only clear that the prices will closely follow the natural gas and other hydrocarbons 
market price trends.
Excluding the last two years due to natural gas market price volaƟlity, brown ammonia prices range 
between € 150 to € 350 per ton, while blue ammonia price range between the € 200 and € 380 per ton. 
[Ref 2, 17]
Contrary to brown and blue ammonia that are linked to natural gas market prices, green ammonia is 
dependent of mulƟple factors that all contribute to its ﬁnal producƟon cost, the availability of renewable 
resources such as wind and solar being the main driver, but land, infrastructure and policies are also 
quite signiﬁcant. When it comes to renewable ammonia regionality will come much more into play than 
brown or blue ammonia that follow a relaƟvely uniﬁed worldwide natural gas market.  In contrast to a 
global market, solar radiaƟon and average wind speed tare quite speciﬁc to each site and the correct 
balance of both can produce aƩracƟve green ammonia projects.
Scenario descripƟon
On a market level ammonia has two main diﬀerenƟators, price and related carbon emissions, other than 
that, any ammonia product should have the exact same chemical requirements regardless of its origin. 
[Ref 28, 3] Three diﬀerent scenarios are compared;
The ﬁrst scenario is a business as usual, using imported ammonia produced from methane steam 
reforming, Brown ammonia.
The second scenario uses electrolysis with most of its electricity provided by renewable energy, however 
downƟme gaps are ﬁlled with electricity form the grid, (Grid Connected), Blue/turquoise ammonia.
The last scenario uses 100% of renewable energy and the downƟme gaps are ﬁlled using a baƩery 
storage system (Island mode), Green Ammonia. This last scenario also serve as the base case scenarios 
where most results will derive and sensiƟvity analysis will be conducted.
Grey and Blue Ammonia price ranges are quite ﬁxed 
and relaƟvely small compared to green ammonia range 
that is esƟmated between € 350 and € 1,300 per ton.

Green ammonia cost drivers
Two factors have a fundamental weight on the LCOA above anything else, the ﬁrst is the natural 
resources of the region, and the second is the technology conﬁguraƟon which is mostly inﬂuenced by the 
ﬁrst factor. On a second level ﬁnancial, social, poliƟcal, and environmental factors have lesser impact but 
can sƟll deﬁne is a project is viable or not.
Resources
Wind, radiaƟon, water and land are the main ingredients and the combinaƟon of these will aﬀect how 
the enƟre project is designed, the ﬁrst two are the main drivers of the enƟre design and will mandate the 
region and operaƟon.
 
Wind 
Wind resource is probably the most sensible one as high annual wind speeds resource is diﬃcult to come 
by especially onshore, due to it high-capacity factor wind power will provide the blunt of the energy 
needed.
Solar
Solar resource is easier to predict and ﬁnd in subtropical regions, solar power will mostly complement 
wind producƟon which has the tendency to increase during mornings and aŌernoons, while solar 
irradiance peaks with daylight.
Although possible to rely in only one type of resource, wind or solar, is the combinaƟon of both wind and 
solar resource what increases the capacity factor of the enƟre system, storage systems would need to 
increase to compensate the loss in producƟon, escalaƟng the CAPEX prices of the project. 
A high NCF (Net Capacity Factor) is essenƟal for a compeƟƟve project, ideally a combined NCF for the 
generaƟng assets and PTX facility, with higher NCF factor PTX standby or idle Ɵmes are reduced, as well as 
conƟnuous producƟon ramping up or down that lead low performance operaƟon levels and degradaƟon 
of components such as synthesizer catalyst , all of which signiﬁcantly reduce the eﬃciency of the system 
and increase costs. [Ref 14, 102]
Water
Access to water is needed for the electrolysis as well as operaƟon of other components, around 1,620 
liters of clean water are needed to produce a tone of ammonia, water also needs a high level of purity to 
be adequate for electrolysis, even if a fresh water source is used, the water ﬁrst need to go under 
treatment, worst case scenario desalinaƟon of sea water is needed, rising the overall costs as well as 
increasing the socio-environmental impacts of the project. [Ref 14, 297]
Land
Large scale PTX project require vast amount of land, mainly for the wind producƟon, wind farms are 
parƟcularly extensive in their use of land, however less 5% of the enƟre land is permanently used for 
energy producƟon meaning that the remaining 95% can sƟll be used for other acƟviƟes or leŌ 
untouched, land use by the solar farm and the other PTX components is the opposite since most of the 
land is permanently used.

A large scale PTX project require hundreds of hectares with high wind and solar resource, it is possible to 
use the land in between the WTG´s for both the solar farm and other components, however speciﬁc 
cases require that certain components are located next to essenƟal resources or infrastructure such as a 
water source, a port or a transmission corridor. [Ref 22, 99], [Ref 25, 17]
Technology
Highly dependent on the available resources is the technology conﬁguraƟon, which seeks to opƟmize 
the resources to obtain the most compeƟƟve prices, large scale PTX projects must consider the enƟre 
supply chain of ammonia from the capture of the resource to the shipment of the ﬁnal product, reducing 
any possible waste (Electricity, heat, water, oxygen). The main technological components are usually 
divided into three diﬀerent groups; GeneraƟng Assets, PTX and oŏake faciliƟes.
GeneraƟng assets
The generaƟng assets refers to all the infrastructure need to generate renewable energy, the generaƟng 
assets are usually divided in two, the generaƟon which in the case of wind refers to the Wind Turbine 
Generators (WTG) and in the case of solar it refers to the solar panels, structure and inverters, and the 
Balance of Plant (BoP) which is all the complementary technology needed to recollect, transform and 
transmit the energy, collector lines, substaƟon, transformers, switch gear, transmission lines, SCADA, 
etc.
Power to X
The power to X assets refers to all the chemical producƟon and reﬁnement components needed to 
produce the desired product, such as electrolyser, Haber-Bosch synthesizer, air separator unit, 
desalinaƟon and/or water treatment plant, buﬀer tanks, compressors, heat exchangers among others. 
Oﬀ taking faciliƟes
Finally, the oﬀ taking faciliƟes are or the logisƟcal components needed to ship the product towards a 
market, these assets vary depending on the locaƟon of the projects towards the market and might use 
exisƟng infrastructure, most used assets are ports, storage tanks, pipelines, and gas terminals among 
others.
Project size
Economies of scale play a fundamental role on the ﬁnal price of ammonia, in fact most CAPEX cost 
outlooks consider project size rather than technological improvements as the fundamental factor in the 
cost reducƟon. Two reasons can be aƩributed, ﬁrst is the speculated reducƟon of producƟon costs of 
components such as electolysers, a very similar price reducƟon curve is expected like it happened to 
wind turbine technology or solar panels a few years ago. The second reason is that large volume faciliƟes 
tend to hive higher performance construcƟon and logisƟcal eﬃciencies.[Ref 17, 295]
Diﬀerent sources agree that green ammonia PtX developments need to have a projected size of 1,000 
Tons Per Day (TPD) to take advantage of economies of scale and projects over 2,000 TPD beneﬁt from 
having full advantage of economies of scale, aŌer the 3,000 TPD projects only have minor decreases in 
CAPEX. As Figure 9 demonstrates smaller scale projects below the 500 TPD don't really beneﬁt from 
economies of scale and the prices quickly increase with smaller projects [Ref 7, 32], [Ref 4, 3], [Ref 1, 45]

once a stable producƟon of electrolyzers is established, so that smaller niche projects could be 
developed without sacriﬁcing their prices.
Over 45 green ammonia projects have been publicly announced, most projects expected to enter

Methodology
To determine how diﬀerent technical and economical factors inﬂuence the ammonia price, is important 
to model mulƟple scenarios and simulate a series of sensiƟviƟes that represent the eﬀect of external 
factors over the green ammonia price.
The model is split in two main areas, a technical and operaƟonal model, and a ﬁnancial model. The main 
role of the technical model is to take the diﬀerent design inputs as well as the site speciﬁc data and 
simulates over a Ɵme series how a group of assets would work together, esƟmaƟng the producƟon 
values of each hour, this mass and energy balance is calculated by a series of predetermined 
performance values and producƟon formulas, the resulƟng volumes are the sum up through the year 
giving a series of producƟon results for the diﬀerent assets, some of these results such as total ammonia 
producƟon, hydrogen producƟon and operaƟng hours of the reactors are criƟcal to calculate economic 
values, other results are less important but provide useful informaƟon as to the performance of the 
system, such as capacity factors or percentage of energy curtailed. [Ref 28 , 11]
The resulƟng volumes are the necessary data to create a ﬁnancial analysis, addiƟonally several costs 
need to be calculated both for iniƟal investment and for annual operaƟons, these costs can be obtained 
through public sources that conƟnuously publish prices and forecasts for the unitary values of mulƟple 
assets. Using such data is simple to calculate the CAPEX and OPEX of the system, by mulƟplying the 
unitary costs and the design parameters that were used for the operaƟonal modeling.
Financial analysis
By having CAPEX, OPEX and producƟon volumes is possible to create ﬁnancial analysis using a set of 
predetermined ﬁnancial set up that consider a series of user determined values such as WACC, inﬂaƟon, 
Tax rates, life span, interest rates, and others, the base case scenario ﬁnancial set up can be appreciated 
in Table 1. The model then calculates a breakeven ammonia price by ﬁnding esƟmaƟng the Net Present 
Value that equals to cero for the selected WACC, to obtain this NPV of cero is necessary to use a solver 
tool that iterates a deﬁned parameter, in this case the price of Ammonia, that directly aﬀects the 
revenue. 
Other parameters can also aﬀect the revenue such as the revenue of selling excess hydrogen or 
electricity, for simplicity in every case Hydrogen is always sold at € 3 per Kg, which is a low price for green 
hydrogen, however such price was selected considering that no oŏaker for green hydrogen is found and 
the excess hydrogen is sold to the regular market.
