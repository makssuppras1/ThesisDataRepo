Abstract
This Project deals with resonant damping of vibrations in monopolie structures on Oﬀ-
shore Wind Turbines (OWTs).The purpose of the project was improving dynamic be-
havior of monopile oﬀshore wind turbines under resonance condition. Because of simple
design, monopile support is the common conﬁguration for OWTs. Although Oﬀshore
areas have high potential for producing electricity, Bad environmental conditions due to
high wave and wind forces on OWTs, create unpleasant vibration on the tower of the
OWTs. Is is needed to use mechanical techniques to reduce destructive vibrations on the
tower of the OWTs, especially on the top to the tower where blades are located. Since
the tower of monopile OWTs are tall and slender in compare of whole OWT structure,
a simpliﬁed model of shear frame set-up can well present the vibration behavior of the
tower, So this thesis is based on experimental and theoretical simulations of ﬁve storey
shear frame set-up and tries to minimize the amplitude of the vibration of top storey
under ﬁrst mode resonant frequency of the shear frame.
Based on tunned-mass-damper (TMD) technique, a pendulum absorber connected with
electromagnetic damper (EMD) is utilized to mitigate the vibrations of the shear frame.
By applying Newton laws of motion to the the shear frame, equations of motion have
been derived. In order to obtain the Rayleigh damping coeﬃcients of shear frame struc-
ture, output signal from free decay tests have been studied. By hamming the structure,
modal parameters have been extracted. The length of pendulum calculated by Mat-
lab simulation. Choosing and purchasing a proper EMD, made based on the designing
requirements. Shunt circuit contains pure resistors connected to EMD has been used
to regulate the damping coeﬃcient of EMD. Performance of pendulum-EMD system
investigated by changing Diﬀerent pendulum masses together with diﬀerent connected
resistors on the shunt circuits.

Motivation
Today’s main problem of human life is the energy. Currently the consumption of energy
is increasing dramatically.
So it is becoming the main concern of human society to
produce the energy that harm the planet less than the other sources. Among the kinds
of energy we are using now, electricity is playing the main rule.
So the problem of
producing the less harmful energy turns into production of less harmful electricity. There
are various ways to produce electricity. Fossil fuel based electricity production is the
major portion among the basis on which electricity can be produced. Today, it becomes
obvious for everyone that fossil fuel is not anymore the trustworthy source of electricity
production. The greenhouse eﬀects and being a lasting resource for the fossil fuel are
the main reasons that make it crystal clear to move to another source.
Renewable resources of electricity production seems to be replacing the fossil fuels and
the other resources. Wind is one of the renewable sources that can be utilized in order to
turn the human dream of green energy into action. Wind is a natural phenomena that
is produced because of natural eﬀects of air movement. It is undeniable that warming of
earth plays an important rule in wind production but the beneﬁts of wind is still heavier
than the drawbacks. Even though, wind is produced because of temperature diﬀerences
in the layers of air and global warming can be reason of wind production, it can help
cooling of earth too. In the other word, earth produces wind to make itself cooler and

can cool the earth in another way.
Wind Turbines are designed to convert the kinetic energy of the wind to electricity
utilizing mechanical mechanism and electromagnetic. To make it simple, wind turns
the blades of the wind turbine and by turning the blades and rotor connected to it, an
electromagnetic ﬁled runs electricity through the coils. So the location of wind turbine
and wind characteristics play an important rule in the eﬃciency of this method. To
make it short, the place of installment of wind turbines are one of the main factors in
the eﬃciency of the method. Current researches shows that, oﬀshore zones are the most
eﬃcient locations in the installment of wind turbine as long as wind proﬁle is not very
turbulent.
In the point of view of electricity production form wind, the best type of wind is the type
that is laminar and has high velocity. Oﬀshore zone can guarantee the speed and laminar
requirement of the wind proﬁle as long as the zone is located in a ﬂat surrounding and
the turbulence will be suppressed in the zone. To have a comparison between Onshore
Wind Turbines and Oﬀshore Wind Turbines (OWT), the OWTs have more capacity
factors [3]. Although the OWTs have more capacity factor in the electricity production,
but they have a more complex procedure in design and installation as well as operation
as long as they are located in the oﬀshore zone. The design and operation requirements
forced OWTs to become larger and installed in the deeper zones. In order to have an
impression on the problems that designers are faced in the design stage of OWTs, we
can list the followings:
• Deeper zone installation
• Stronger wind experienced by blades
• high speed wind that rotates that blades much more faster
• Bigger size in case of structural stiﬀness
• String wave loads and compensation problems
• Hurricane
• Earthquake

1.2
Introduction
Concerning the discussion above, wind turbines are drastically growing in the world
market for renewable energy. The technology grows, helps the growing of wind turbines
too. Wind turbine farms are developed to get the best eﬃciency for the energy produc-
tion. The phrase wind turbine farm, means the installation and conﬁguration of wind
turbines such that they are placed in an area with speciﬁc conformation that creates
a power grid, as it is illustrated in Fig. 1.1. The idea of having wind turbine farms
increased the eﬃciency of wind turbines but at the same time, it increased the risk of
failure in the whole power grid in case of failure of a single turbine by means on drastic
physical damages. Placement of wind turbine farms in the oﬀshore zones, helps designer
to have an open hand in terms of size of the wind turbines.
Figure 1.1: Wind Farm in Copenhagen
As long as designers have the upper hand in design of wind turbines with odd sizes in
the oﬀshore zones, there is a huge intention in increasing the size of wind turbines. The
upper hand in choosing the right size for the designers has the beneﬁt of harvesting
more wind energy. The higher the tower goes, the more energy is harvested. In Fig. 1.2
the size grows of wind turbines is shown with the notation on the energy production of
them. It is noteworthy that the size of the wind turbine plays an important rule in the
harvested energy, but the technologies levels that are utilized in the design of the wind
turbines should be taken to the account.
Other than the size of the wind turbines, the structure of the tower has been under
studies. The simplicity of installation, maintenance and design are the important fac-
tors that pushed designers into using mono-pile support for wind turbines ??. To have an

In this research, we are interested in a spring-mass damper that can be utilized in OWTs
for compensating vibration caused by external determined harmonic forces. As it was
mentioned before, one of the main problems in design of OWTs in the vibration caused
by various sources such as wind, earthquake, waves ,etc in which can be modeled as a
deterministic forces exerted to the base of tower of OWT. In order to have a matching
model of OWT, we have proposed Shear Frame (SF) to model behavior of mono-pile.

enforce by truss structure.
The main idea is that the vibration mode that we are
interested in while the structure is under external deterministic force that represents
earthquake, looks like the vibration mode of SF under the same force.
We have modelled source of vibrations as a deterministic force exerted to the base of
the SF. The research problem here is to design a spring-mass damper based on the
structural speciﬁcations of SF that can damp the vibration exerted by the external
force. The method of damping is the passive damping control. We will then, verify the
theoretical design with the experiments.
Chapter 2 illustrates the governing equation of motion of the SF and the EMD used

Chapter 3
Tuned-Mass-Damper
In vibration theory, a structure starts vibrating when an external force applies to it. Any
structure can be modelled as a spring-mass-damper. It means that, for each structure,
two speciﬁcations are deﬁned which are stiﬀness, k, and damping coeﬃcient, c. The art
of designing structures is to design a structure in which stiﬀness and damping coeﬃcient
are meaningful concerning the forces applied to it.
In design sequence, designing a
structure with small stiﬀness and damping coeﬃcient is very dangerous in case that
the vibration of the structure may become very large in terms of amplitude. This will
happen if the force exerted to the structure has a frequency that is near to the neutral
frequency of the system, we can say resonance happened. There are some cases in which
it is not possible to design a structure that can damp vibrations caused by all the forces
that may apply to the system. We can see the problem in another way in which there
are a wide variety of force that it may not be possible to support the vibration of any
kind which are caused by the forces. In such cases, we can have additional actuators
that can be implemented while the forces are beyond the envelope we have designed the
structure for. These actuators can be modelled as a spring-mass damper that can be
designed to cover the vibration caused by the forces which can not be covered by the
structure itself. In other word, we are interested to design a tuned mass damper for
speciﬁc vibrations caused by speciﬁc external forces.

3.3
Electromagnetic Damper
Eddy current dampers and electromagnetic systems have been used as generators for
producing electricity or as force actuators. This kind of devices can be used in dual
operational state: they can be used to deal with disturbance by using electrical energy
as an input or in the other hand they can be used to absorb energy from disturbance and
convert it to the storage device. The ﬁrst case called active actuators and can be used in
active control systems when the second case called passive actuators and it is suitable for
passive control loops. Both systems have advantages and disadvantages. In the design
tree based on design requirements one of them will be used. Normally electro-mechanical
machines used as force actuators in civil structures. Passive electromagnetic force ac-
tuators also called regenerative electromagnetic dampers, primary studied for vehicle
suspension systems. This research focused on the passive electromagnetic damper. A
passive Electromagnetic Damper (EMD) is an electromagnetic machine that supplying
a force in opposite direction of the movement without using external electric energy.
The absorbed energy can be converted to electric energy and transferred to an external
circuit. In the following section linear EMD will be analyzed.
3.3.1
Linear voice coil Motor

and a static part which is called stator. Linear voice coil actuator belong to the per-

coil actuator (VCA) consist of two separate parts; the magnetic housing and the coil.

Chapter 6
Conclusion
6.1
Conclusion
The novel PTMD is proposed in this study.Several tests performed to extract the modal
characteristics of the structure. A theoretical model of PTMD is presented and validated
by experimental tests. The damping coeﬃcient of the EMD is more sensitive in the range
of 0-450 [Ω]. The optimal resistor for the shunted circuit is equal to 450 [Ω] and the
optimal pendulum mass is equal to 478 [g]. This value is almost %18 of the mass of each
ﬂoor.
6.2
Recommendation For Future Works
The numerical model can be improved and the inertia eﬀect can be considered to the
equations. Instead of the pure resistor, an external circuit consist of R-L-C can be used.
By proper shunted circuit , it is possible to extract the destructive vibrations energy to
the electricity. Due to this fact that the storke of EMD is one of the main constrains for
the base excitation, The proper EMD can be designed to fully covers all requirements.
The external lumped masses can be added to the top ﬂoor, and its can represents the
simulated mass of the nacelle and the blades of wind turbines. By adding noise to the
SF excitation, a set of new analysis needed to fully determined the modal characteristics
of the SF. PTMD location can be changed and the performance of the new model can
be studied.
