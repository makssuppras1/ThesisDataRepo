Summary (English)
The danish healthcare sector is challenged by the increasing elderly population
as more people are being diagnosed with chronic heart failure. Meanwhile, the
readmission rates of heart failure patients is high, which increases the pressure
on heart clinics in Denmark.
One of the most promising solutions to solve
these problems is telemedicine. However, many telemedicine projects initiated
in Denmark does not get past the pilot stage due to lack of scientic evidence.
This thesis have looked beyond the traditional telemedcine solutions by intro-
ducing a new data-driven system, which utilizes mobile apps, smart health
devices and rule-based algorithms. The data-driven system may oer several
benets compared to the existing telemedicine solutions on the market, as it is
much more exible and dynamic. By combining continuous collection of data
from the patient, no matter where he or she is, with an algorithm that auto-
matically detects anomalies and suggest when to up-titrate medication, we hope
the treatment time and readmission percentage can be reduced signicantly.
An experiment have not been conducted at this stage of the project, and there-
fore no scientic results exist. However, this project has started the development
of a rst version functional prototype of the data-driven system, which could be
used in a practical experiment.

Chapter 1
Introduction
Today there are approximately 60,000 to 100,000 people in Denmark that have
heart failure, which is roughly 1.5% of the Danish population [8]. There are
11,000 admissions a year for heart failure in Denmark.
The average age of
patients with heart failure is 72 years and the occurrence increases with age.
Approximately 5% of people over 75 have heart failure, while the gure rises to
over 10% when one reaches over 85 years. With the rising life expectancy in the
world today, the occurrence of heart failure will keep increasing.
Heart failure is a chronic disease, which means the patients will have to live
with it for the rest of their life. The treatment of heart failure is very compre-
hensive and the readmissions rates are high. A study from Sundhedsstyrelsen in
2008, showed that 18.2% of every heart failure patients that have just nished
treatment was readmitted to the hospital after 30 days [9].
Meanwhile, the subsidies of medicine in the public sector in Denmark are in-
creasing, as the medicine is getting more expensive and people are consuming
more medicine than previous. A forecast from Danske Regioner show that the
cost of medication prescribed at hospitals will increase with 10% annually until
2020 [10].
Heart failure is therefore very expensive in a socio-economic aspect and will not
disappear any time soon. To address this problem, Denmark has begun building

1.1
Heart Failure
Heart failure is a chronic disease, where the heart's pumping power is weaker
than normal [12]. The blood moves through the heart and body more slowly
than normal, which increases the pressure in the heart. Due to this, the heart
cannot pump enough oxygen and nutrients to full the body's needs.
The
chambers of the heart may respond by becoming sti and thickened or stretching
to hold more blood to pump through the body. This will help keep the blood
moving, but eventually the heart muscle walls may weaken and thereby not be
able to pump as ecient. Due to this, the kidneys will react by causing the
body to retain uid (water) and salt. If the uid keeps increasing in the ankles,
legs, feets, lungs, arms or other organs, the body will get congested. Congestive
heart failure is the notion used to describe this condition.
1.2
Telemedicine
In recent years many pilot projects in the area of telemedicine have risen in
Denmark [13]. According to evaluations from Region Hovedstaden and Region
Midtjylland, telemedicine have promising prospects both in terms of nanical
gains and patient care [14][15].
The concept of telemedicine covers dierent
types of digitally supported healthcare. With the help of the patients own health
information, which is collected through video, questionnaires and measurements,
such as blood pressure, weight and heart rate, nurses and doctors can diagnose,
treat and advise on diseases while the patient is staying in their own home.
Patients are given tools to follow their own health and may better master their
own illness, just as they do not have to enter the hospital.
Telemedicine have promising aspects, but interestingly, many of the Danish
telemedicine projects do not go past the pilot project due to lack of scientic
evidence [16]. When evaluating the recent telemedicine projcets in Denmark it

1.3
Mobile apps, smart health devices and algo-
rithms
This thesis will look beyond the normal telemedicine solutions and show new
ideas on how the future healthcare system could work.
With the increasing
amount of advanced smart health devices with Food and Drug Administration
(FDA) clearance, there lies a huge unrealized potential. Most of these smart
health devices can be connected to your smartphone. Meanwhile mobile apps
are getting increasing attention in the healthcare sector.
The big technology companies such as Apple and Google have recently provided
frameworks that lets you manage and store health data in one place [17]. By
having the possibility of collecting vast amount of health data from a patient
in real time, it is interesting to apply both rule-based and machine learning
algorithms. Algorithms can detect signs of worsening of a heart failure patient
at an early stage and thereby possibly prevent readmission.
1.4
Research Objectives
The main objective of this thesis is to develop a data-driven system that can
support the treatment of heart failure patients. The data-driven system will
consist of,
1. Continuous collection of data from state of the art smart health devices
2. Rule-based algorithms
3. Mobile apps with great user interface
In order to achieve this objective, the study has been conducted by using both
agile and lean approaches.

Clinical decision support systems
One way the HIT project could have utilized the collected data from a patient,
would be to implement a Clinical Decision Support System (CDSS) [18].
A
clinical decision support system is designed to support nurses and doctors with
clinical decisions. In other words, it is a system that links health observations
with health knowledge to inuence health choices made by clinicians to improve
healthcare. The nurses and doctors will interact with the CDSS to help them
analyse the collected data from a patient and give treatment recommendations.
It is important to clarify, that the CDSS should not make decision for the nurse
and doctors, but solely act as an assistant. Two dierent types of CDSS system
exists, knowledge-based and non-knowledge-based.
2.2.1
Knowledge-based CDSS
A knowledge-based CDSS can be divided into three parts.
• The knowledge base
• An inference engine
• Communication mechanism
The knowledge base contains the rules and associations of data in the form of
IF-THEN rules. For example, if a system should determine drug interactions,
then the rules could be IF drug X is taken and drug Y is also taken, THEN a
nurse should be alerted.
The inference engine combines the collected data, ie. the real-time data that
the patient is generating, with the knowledge base.
The communication mechanism makes it possible to show the results of the
inference engine and knowledge base to the user, and to input new data into the
system.
2.2.2
Non-knowledge-based CDSS
A non-knowledge-based CDSS does not use a knowledge base in the form of IF-
THEN rules. Instead it uses machine learning, which utilize algorithms that can
==PAGE:29==
2.2 Clinical decision support systems

Figure 2.2: INTENSE-HF knowledge-based algorithm [3]
learn from past experience, and nd patterns in data. This eliminates the need
for any IF-THEN rules, as the algorithms instead will build a model that can
predict when a nurse should be alerted. An advantage of non-knowledge is that
the model will keep learning as more data is generated. This can potentially
lead to new discoveries that further improves the support system compared
to a knowledge based system. However, there are still limitations when using
machine learning, as the system is "black-box", meaning the system cannot
explain its reasons for their predictions. The machine learning algorithms used
in CDSS are often based on deep learning, support vector machines, generic
algorithms. Deep learning is a popular branch of machine learning that is getting
a lot of traction, due to its recent advancements [19].

In this section, the whole course of treatment will be discussed in detail.
In Figure 3.1 is shown the course of treatment of a heart failure patient. The
gure shows that the course of treatment is divided into the following three
steps [23].
1. Diagnose patient with heart failure and put patient into heart failure clinic
2. Treatment of patient by prescribing medicine at clinic and achieve optimal
medicine dose
3. Rehabilitation of the patient and focus on lifestyle changes so the patient
can get back to a normal life
The rst step is diagnose, where the patient might experience symptoms of
heart failure. The patient, in most cases, will go to the general practitioner and
if there is a reasonable suspicion, the patient will be referred to a heart failure
clinic. At the heart failure clinic, the patient will go through examinations, to
determine if the patient has heart failure. The most important measurements
to determine heart failure is echocardiography and blood sample tests [5]. If the
patient is diagnosed with heart failure, the patient will go to the next step, the
treatment.
In the treatment step, the patient will go through a treatment, where the main

will start with a low level of medication, as the body needs to adjust to the med-

is achieved, the treatment is done. However, as heart failure is a chronic disease,
the patient will need to take the medication the rest of his/her life. The main
objective of the treatment is to reach the optimal dose of medication as fast as

The last step, the patient will go through a tailored rehabilitation program. The
main objective of the program is to provide guidance for lifestyle changes, which
will reduce symptoms of the chronic disease. Typical lifestyle recommendation

3.3
What is the problem?
The main objective of this thesis, was to nd ways that could support, optimize
or improve the treatment of heart failure patients. Based on the analyse above,
the following comes to mind.
1. Heart failure treatment is currently very rigid/static
2. Heart failure treatment is currently "one size t all", hence it lacks per-
sonalization
3. The treatment time is very long and some patients uses unnecessary time
at the hospital
4. No metrics are used outside of the heart clinic to determine health condi-
tion of the patient
5. Some measurements are not measured today, which could be crucial for a
heart failure patient
3.3.1
Treatment is rigid/static
The treatment today is not really centered around the patient. The consulta-
tions are very static/rigid, meaning that the heart failure clinic decides when
the patient should come for a follow up consultation, and are often scheduled
in an interval of three to four weeks. However, the patients condition might
deteriorate in that period, and therefore not receiving the optimal treatment.
3.3.2
Lacks personalized treatment
The current treatment is "one size t all", meaning patients get the same treat-
ment, and same interval between consultations. This means that patients that
are doing well, are sometimes wasting their time at follow up consultations, and
it is therefore not necessary, while other patients would benet from frequent
==PAGE:43==
3.3 What is the problem?
25
consultations. So in general, the whole course of treatment lacks personalized
treatment.
3.3.3
Long treatment time
The general treatment time is long. If the treatment would be more personalized
to the specic patient, the treatment time might decrease. Patients which are
classied as NYHA 1, might be able to up-titrate a lot faster than today, if
patient can be reviewed more frequent. While at the same time, patients in
NYHA 3/4 might want the nurse to more regularly observe the patient to avoid
setbacks in their up-titration progress.
3.3.4
No metrics outside heart clinic
Currently, no metrics are used outside the heart clinic. This means that daily
measurements of weight and blood pressure to ensure that the patients stay
within the normal range is not utilized. Many people also have the so called
white coat syndrome, which is a phenomenon in which patients have a blood
pressure level higher than normal. Telemedicine projects are already addressing
this problem, but are still very limited in how they utilize the daily measure-
ments.
3.3.5
Missing important measurements
Resting heart rate is another important parameter to look for, as there are
a correlation between high resting heart rate, and mortality of heart failure
patients [28]. From our understanding, after several sessions with the nurses,
this is not addressed at all today. They get a single measurement at the clinic,
when a blood pressure measurement is taken, however it is not really a resting
heart rate measurements. There is a uncertainty if only a single measurement
is taken, and the best time to measure the resting heart rate, is right before you
get out of the bed after a night sleep [29].

Scoping the solution
To full all the needs, would require more resources and time than we have

We therefore needed to focus on what we thought were the most

1. Increase patient engagement in the treatment
2. Reduce the up-titration treatment period
3. Reduce readmissions during the treatment at the clinic
Based on our analysis, the best way to improve the treatment of heart failure

this, is to move the treatment more towards the patient, as patients will feel
their involvement with the treatment is important. One way to do this for heart
failure patients, is to give them the chance to collect data themselves, hence self
measurements and self reported symptoms and medication.

4.3.1
Weight
The weight is important for the treatment, due to water retention in the patients
body. The weight should be measured one time each day in the morning, right
after waking up and urination. The data-driven system should be able to auto-
matically detect critical weight/water retention values. The data-driven system
shall notify the medical sta when a critical values/thresholds are reached.
4.3.2
Blood Pressure
The blood pressure is another important factor for the treatment, as it will de-
termine the eect of the medication (ACE-inhibitor) on the patient. Like the
weight, the blood pressure should be measured once a day in the morning. The
data-driven system should be able to automatically detect critical blood pres-
sure values, to prevent hypotension and hypertension, especially hypotension as
this could indicate too high medication dosage. Regular blood pressure mea-
surements will also be one of the variables that shall be used to suggest when a
patient is ready for up-titration.
4.3.3
Heart Rate
Heart rate is important for the treatment, as it will determine the eect of
medication (Beta-blocker) on the patient. There is a correlation between high
resting heart rate and high mortality rates, hence it is important to measure
regularly [28]. Both resting heart rate and active heart rate should be measured
to be able to dierentiate when the patient is active and passive respectively.
Resting heart rate should be measured in the morning, right before the patient
wakes up. The active heart rate should be measured during the day, when the
patient is active. The data-driven system should be able to detect critical resting
heart rates and notify the patient accordingly.
4.3.4
Symptoms
The patient should be able to report various symptoms in the app, hence the
symptoms listed in 3.2.3.1. If critical weight and blood pressure measurements is
detected, the patient should be able to report symptoms related to the specic
measurement.
The patient should also be able to report systems, when the
following occurs,

Medicine Intake
Medication is the core thing of the treatment, as it lowers the blood pressure,
heart rate and removes water retention. The amount, number and frequency of
medicine is based on NYHA classication and the progress in the up-titration

Activities
Activities should be tracked to support the self reported symptoms and heart

the patient is passive and active. Activities should be group based on the type
of activity, hence if the patient is walking, running, cycling or sitting/resting.
The activity should be measured continuous by either using the smartphone or

6.2.1
Database
For storage it was decided to use a MongoDB, which is a NoSQL document
database [36]. The reason for choosing a NoSQL database is due to its simple
design and its horizontal scalability [37]. The MongoDB store the data as JSON-
like documents with dynamic schemas, instead of the traditional table-based
relational database structure. According to the MongoDB team, their database
is good for storing time series data, which match our data [38]. In Figure 6.4 the
database design is shown. For the rst version of the system, three collections
are used. One collection (User) to store all the dierent patients and nurses
which uses the app, and two collections (Weight and BloodPressure) to store all
the data that is pulled from HealthKit. As shown on the gure, for every weight
or blood pressure that is saved to the database, a username eld will also be
saved with it. This is done in order to dierentiate the measurements for each
user.

7.1
Privacy and security
An important area, which have not been discussed in this project is privacy and
security. This is an important area as health data is very sensitive, hence it is
important that it is kept secure. It is therefore important that the solution is
build on the latest technology in IT and data security. For example, the vertical
prototype that has been developed in this thesis do not use HTTPS, which is a
must, if an experiment should be conducted. This will make sure that the data
that is exchange between the client and server is encrypted and thereby cannot
be intercepted.
Another important aspect is, who owns the data that is generated? Is it the
patient or the clinic? A solution to this problem must be addressed in the early
stage of development. The good news is that 75% of the Danes are willing to
share their personal health data if it is used for health research [45].

7.2
Data collection
One measurement that is crucially for the treatment of heart failure patient
is various values from a blood sample test. There is currently no smart device
available where you can take a blood sample test yourself. Therefore the patient
still needs to visit the clinic every three to four weeks to get a blood sample
test. This is also a problem related to the CDSS suggested in this thesis, as it
requires that a blood sample test is taken often, in order to decide if a patient
can up-titrate medication or not. However, there are many companies that are
trying to x this problem, such as Spot On Science [46]. They have created
a device for self-sampling and robust/storage of blood samples, so it can be
shipped to a heart clinic, where it can be analysed. In this way, the patient will
not need to schedule an physical appointment at the heart clinic to get a blood
sample test.
When collecting data from the patient, there are still things which need to be
decided. Currently, if a patient takes more than one measurement a day it takes
the average of all the measurements that have been taken that day. This means,
if the patients takes one weight measurement in the morning and one weight
measurement in the afternoon, the CDSS will use the average for that day. This
could potential lead to misleading values, as the patients weight will be aected
by water and food intake during the day.
Therefore it might be a good idea to introduce a time window for when the
patient can take the measurement. Optimally, the weight measurement is tak-
ing in the morning after urination. Therefore the time window for a weight
measurement should be in the morning between seven to nine, or the patient
could decide it depending on their circadian rhythm. The data collection should
also be robust to outliers. For example if a patient suddenly would experience
a weight increase of more than 2 kg, the system should ask the patient to take
another measurement, to test whether the rst measurement was correct, or an
outlier.

7.4
Preventive treatment
In this thesis, the focus have been on the treatment when the patient have
already been diagnosed with heart failure. It might also be interesting to look
into preventive treatment as this could have enormous benet for the society.
There are already many guidelines on how to avoid heart failure, but they are
not very personalized. This have already started with the use of wearables, such
as Jawbone where you have a so called smart coach to help you live better [47].
Providing seamless integration of wearables with the hospital IT systems, could
be a way to use preventive treatment.
7.5
Domain knowledge
Domain knowledge is a very important in these type of solutions. Currently,
we have only had access to a cardiac nurses, which could explain the whole
course of treatment, the purpose of the treatment, what measurements that are
important etc.
From my perception, the nurses are phenomenal at using the traditional treat-
ment method. However, they are not up to date with the latest research and
technology, that could potentially benet the treatment. That is why cardiolo-
gist should be more involved in the project, as they know what is possible and
what is not. This is especially important in respect of the CDSS, as cardiologist
needs to validate and verify it. As discussed in the Related Work chapter,
it is a challenge to transform heart failure guidelines into rules, which can be
processed by a CDSS. Therefore more research should be conducted in this area
to make sure the right rules are used in the CDSS.

Chapter 8
Conclusion
This thesis have looks beyond the traditional telemedcine solutions by introduc-
ing a new data-driven system, which utilizes mobile apps, smart health devices
and rule-based algorithms.
Prior to developing the data-driven system, the current treatment have been
extensively studied by analysing the course of treatment at Herlev Hospital.
The analysis gave important domain knowledge about heart failure treatment,
but also illustrated several problems with the current treatment.
This resulted in the development of a new way of treating a heart failure pa-
tient. The new treatment consist of a mobile app that integrates with various
smart health devices, to continuous collect data from the patient. The mobile
app integrates with a Clinical Decision Support System, which analyse the col-
lected data in real-time. The CDSS will utilize a ruled based algorithm, which
automatically detects signs of worsening of the patient. The CDSS will provide
instant feedback to the medical sta via a web or mobile app.
The data-driven system may oer several benets compared to the existing
telemedicine solutions on the market, as it is more exible and dynamic. By
combining continuous collection of data from the patient, no matter where he
is, with an algorithm that automatically detects anomalies and suggest when to
up-titrate medication, we hope the treatment time and readmission percentage
