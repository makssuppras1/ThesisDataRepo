Abstract 
In recent years, bike sharing has expanded rapidly all over the world. Especially in 
China, the bike sharing system booms overnight and grows steadily. The appearance of 
bike sharing brings about the impacts upon the change of traffic behavior, the 
environment, the economy effects, public health and society. This sustainable transport 
mode has attracted much social attention in China. The positive impacts lie in the 
substitution of transport vehicles including metro, bus, private car and taxi, on the 
contrary, the negative impacts are mainly from the substitution of walking. The study 
area in this report we choose is Beijing, the capital of China, because of the flourishment 
of bike sharing and its corresponding representativeness in China. This study focuses 
on the evaluation of environment performance of bike sharing including the 
environment impacts assessment of bike sharing in Beijing and the identification of 
factors affecting the environment performance of bike sharing. The questionnaire 
surveys are performed to provide an overview of bike sharing in Beijing and the original 
data in environment impact assessment and factors’ identification. For the environment 
impacts assessment, the factors identified are economy impacts, environment impacts 
and human health impacts, named supplement effects, substitution effects and health 
effects, respectively. The supplement effects, based on the relation between leisure time 
and economy growth and the method of life cycle assessment (LCA), is performed for 
the estimation of substitution effects and health effects. The final results show that bike 
sharing has massive positive impacts upon economy, environment and human health. 
For the identification of factors, the binary logistic model is applied to identify 
significant factors based on the data from questionnaire surveys. According to the 
estimation from modeling, significant factors that affect the environment performance 
of bike sharing are: gender, monthly income, the reason of charge cheap, the 
supplement location of bike sharing including bus station, metro station and resident 
quarter, the perception for the frequency increase by bike sharing, barrier of damaged 
bikes during usage and the optimistic attitude for bike sharing’s future. This study 
provides the empirical evidence for the management of bike sharing and the policy 
making from administrative department. 
 
Key words: bike sharing, Beijing, environment impact assessment, identification of 
factors, binary logistic model, questionnaire surveys.

Part 1 Introduction 
1. Research framing 
1.1 Research motivation 
Nowadays, the movement towards sustainable mobility in transport has attracted the 
worldwide attention due to traffic congestion and consequent environment issues 
(Pojani, 2015). Bicycle sharing has been the new sustainable means of transportation 
form, growing dramatically worldwide in recent years. More than 500 bike sharing 
programs has been launched in roughly 1000 cities in nearly 50 countries by the end of 
2016 (Wikipedia, 2018). Among these countries China leads the development with the 
number of 1.9 million bikes, in its steady growth (Felix, 2018). The prosperity of shared 
bike overnight in China is mainly due to a great relief form over-reliance on vehicles, 
which results the serious traffic crowding and severe air pollution. The solution to the 
“last mile” problem is also a factor. The characteristic of bike sharing includes the 
operation in environment-friendly and cost-effective economically way, the measure to 
increase mobility and keep healthy (Zhang, 2015). The benefits to traffic and 
environment are significant, however, some corresponding issues still impede the 
progress of the effective urban management and bike sharing development in China, 
which include but not limited bike damage and haphazard parking due to users’ 
improper disposal and bicycle theft. Therefore, for the analysis of bikes sharing, China 
is the ideal country to be the object of this study because of the bike sharing’s prosperity 
and development in representativeness. Among the cities which bike sharing has been 
launched in China, Beijing, with the high-dense population and mass market launching 
in China, is in the mature process for the development of bike sharing, which can be 
regarded as the ideal objective city in this study. 
 
Based on literature review, the bike sharing system have the impacts on transportation 
sustainability, traffic congestion, economic growth, environment protection and human 
health etc. Many studies focus on these impacts qualitatively or evaluate some factors 
for the usage from transport perspective, however, few studies attempt to quantitatively 
evaluate the performance of bike sharing from environment perspective. The 
environment performance of bike sharing can either be positive or negative due to

different transport mode substitution. In order to evaluate the environment performance 
of bike sharing in Beijing, environment impact assessment is needed. In this study, 
limited by data collection, we only attempt to quantify the environment impacts of bike 
sharing in economy, environment and human health and explore the factors affecting 
environment performance of bike sharing. The findings should be helpful for the 
transportation policies and management in Beijing.

In this paper, the environmental evaluation of bike sharing is divided into two parts: 
one is the quantitative evaluation of environment impacts for bike sharing, the other is

are fully or partly based on the data collection of questionnaire. For the environment

2. Theoretical Background 
2.1 Life cycle assessment (LCA) 
Life cycle assessment (LCA) is defined as “a tool to assess the potential environmental 
impacts and resources used throughout a product’s life cycle, i.e. from raw material 
acquisition, via production and use stages, to waste management” (Finnveden, 2009). 
There are four phases composing the whole LCA as Figure 2.1: 
 
 
Figure 2.1. The framework for LCA 
The goal and scope definition is the start of an LCA and determines the purpose of the 
==PAGE:14==
4 
 
study in detail. For goal definition, six aspects are addressed and documented: (1) 
intended applications of the results; (2) limitations due to methodological choices; (3) 
decision context and reasons for carrying out the study; (4) target audience; (5) 
comparative studies to be disclosed to the public; (6) commissioner of the study and 
other influential actors (Hauschild etc., 2018). The scope definition is to determine what 
product systems are to be assessed and how this assessment should take place (Michael 
Z. etc., 2018). The function unit and system boundary are determined in this step. The 
function unit is defined as “a quantitative description of the function or service of the 
function or service for which the assessment is performed, and the basis of determining 
the reference flow of product that scales the data collection in the next LCA phase, the 
inventory analysis” (Hauschild etc., 2018). The system boundary determines which 
processes are included in the product system to be studied. 
 
The inventory analysis is the phase to quantify the elementary flows from all the process 
in the studies system by data collection and calculations. The inventory flows include 
the input data such as materials, energy, resources and the output data such as emissions 
and waste (Hauschild etc., 2018).  
 
The impact assessment is analysis process when the inventory data is transferred into 
impact categories based on the inventory. The aim of this phase is to evaluate the 
potential environment impacts according to the results of different impact categories. 
 
The interpretation is the last phase of LCA, which analyzes the results of life inventory 
analysis and impact assessment to check the uncertainty and test hypothesis during 
these phases. The goal of the study can be answered after interpretation phase.

3. Research background  
3.1 History of bike sharing 
There have been four generations of bike sharing system over the past 50 years 
==PAGE:16==
6 
 
(DeMaio, 2004). The first generation of bike sharing programs named Witte Fietsen or 
White Bikes appeared on July 28, 1965, in Amsterdam (Zee, 2018). Regular bikes 
painted into white were introduced by a group of activists to provide the transport 
service for public use. These bikes were left unlocked for anyone to use and the one 
who finished their ride just left behind for the next user. The problem of steal and 
damage emerged with the flourish of bike sharing, leading to the quick collapse of this 
program. Although White Bikes was short-lived, regarded as the massive failure, it still 
became the pioneer symbol of bike sharing system. 
 
Another attempt for the large-scale public bike program occurred in 1995 in 
Copenhagen as Copenhagen’s Bycyklen or City Bikes (DeMaio, 2009). This is 
regarded as the second generation of bike sharing system which allowed users to pick 
up or return shared bicycles at the specific locations throughout the city with a coin 
deposit. Despite clear improvements with stations to operate over Amsterdam’s White 
Bikes, the second generation still got into the issues of thefts and vandalism.  
 
The failure of second generations of bike sharing system attracted the attention on the 
track of users’ behavior to avoid theft. Therefore, after one year, in 1996, Bikeabout at 
Portsmouth University in England appeared, which was regarded as the breed of third 
generations of bike sharing system (DeMaio, 2009). The users had to swipe an 
individual magnetic stripe card to borrow the bike, in this situation, the bikes could be 
tracked when they weren’t returned. The technology of magnetic stripe card which 
Bikeabout first use facilitated the expand of bike sharing. Based on this technology, the 
following third generations of bike sharing were in a variety of technological 
improvements such as electronically-locking racks or bike locks, telecommunication 
systems, smartcards and fobs and mobile phone access (DeMaio, 2009). In 1998, Vélo 
à la Carte, as a city-scale bike sharing program, were launched in Rennes, France. It 
was with smart card access and free of charge in 30 minutes and 200 bikes were 
distributed in 25 fixed dock stations. The use of the smart card can help the operators 
collect the real-time information and assist in rebalancing the bikes in different stations. 
 
The bike sharing system grew slowly until 2005. In 2005, Vélo’v was launched in Lyon, 
France with the largest number of 1500 bikes in that time. The bikes were equipped 
with electronic components for station identification, track of traveling distance, 
conditions of bikes and information collection of bike usage. The impacts of Vélo’v 
==PAGE:17==
7 
 
inspired the neighbor, Paris. In 2007, Paris launched the program of Vélib in the total 
number of 6000 bikes which expanded into 18000 in 2015 (DeMaio, 2009). These can 
be seen as the third generation “plus” with the characteristic of real-time availability 
and GPS tracking. The booming development and success captured the attention around 
the world, the worldwide movement of bike sharing was in the explosive booming. The 
program of 3rd generation bike sharing system has been launched in 1175 cities and 63 
countries such as Spain, Italy, Germany, Brazil, China, New Zealand, South Korea and 
the U.S. since 2008. The total number of global sharing bikes were estimated in 
2294600 in 2016 (Felix, 2018). China has become the leader in the development of bike 
sharing with the largest number. The worldwide bike sharing map in 2017 is as shown 
in Figure 3.1. 
 
 
Figure 3.1. The bike sharing world map (Google map,2017) 
With the rapid development of Internet, the fourth generations of bike sharing system 
emerged in the right time. Similarly to the third generation plus, the bikes in fourth 
generations are also customized, equipped with GPS for tracking and electronic devices 
such as smart lock. The salient feature for fourth generations is that the dockless bikes 
replacing the docking bikes. Today dockless bikes are designed that a user doesn't need 
to return the bike in the docking station since the next user can find it by installing App 
in the mobile phone with GPS (Colin, 2018). The fourth generations of bike sharing 
system such as Ofo and Mobike has been launched in China with millions of bikes over 
100 cities.

3.2 Models of provision 
The models of provision are various since the third generations of bike sharing is in the 
massive success. The providers range from government to quasi-governmental 
transport agencies, advertising companies, non-profits, and for-profits (Fishman, 2013). 
The revenue sources have included but not limited advertising, self-funding, user fees, 
municipalities, and public–private partnerships (Mátrai,2016). The overview of bike 
sharing providers and funding sources is illustrated in Figure 3.2. 
 
 
Figure 3.2. Different bike sharing models of provision 
In the government models, the local government takes full responsibility for the bike 
sharing program running, including direct design and operation for the well-being of 
cities or the purchase the bike sharing service provided from others. The funding of this 
model ranges from municipality funding to membership usage fees. Most bike sharing 
program has been launched in this model worldwide such as City bikes in Denmark, 
Nubija in South Korea (DeMaio, 2009).  
 
The bike sharing service in quasi-government transport agencies models is guided by a 
public authority to enhance the public transportation. The government subsides and fees 
from membership usage are the main funding sources in this model. The programs like 
Hangzhou public bicycle in China and Call a bike in Germany adopt transport agencies 
model (DeMaio, 2009). 
 
==PAGE:19==
9 
 
The operation of bike sharing system in advertising company is based on the exchange 
with advertising company to advertise on city street furniture and billboards. Obviously, 
the revenue sources are from the advertising funding in city street furniture, billboards, 
bikes and bike stations and the membership usage fees. For instance, SmartBike in 
United States and Cyclocity in France are both in this model (DeMaio, 2009). 
 
The objective of model in for-profit is to provide the bike sharing service with minimum 
government involvement. The membership usage fees and the ads on bikes and bike 
sharing stations provide the main revenue for the operation of this model. This model 
has been adopted in most cities in China, especially in Beijing, because of the expansion 
of Ofo and Mobike. 
 
In opposite, the models in non-profits are supported from public agencies or councils, 
therefore, their funding is mainly from public-private partnership funding, bank loans 
and local funding. This model also has existed in some countries such as BIXI in 
Canada, Hourbike in United States (DeMaio, 2009). 
3.3 Development of bike sharing system in China 
China has ever been the kingdom of bicycles in 1980s since the private cars were 
seldom seen on the streets, which were considered as the luxury products because of 
the high price. It is reported that there were less than 20,000 private vehicles on a 
national scale in 1985 (NBCS,2004). Bicycles, with the characteristics of cheap price 
and convenient mobility, were accepted by most citizens as the main transport mode. 
Along with the economic development and the improvement of life quality, private cars 
replace bicycles to be the major transport tool in daily life gradually. In 2015, the 
number of private cars in China has increased sharply to 125 million 
(Chinadaily.com.cn,2016). Although private cars bring convenience and less travel time 
to citizens, the environmental and traffic issues also appear like air pollution, noise 
pollution, traffic congestion and the pressure of parking space provision. The serious 
issues force people to seek more environment-friendly and less energy consuming 
transport modes, in this situation, bike sharing system attracts social attention and 
booms logically. 
 
The first bike sharing program was launched in 2008 in Hangzhou with 2800 bikes 
(Hangzhou annual report, 2009). Hangzhou public bike sharing system was the third 
==PAGE:20==
10 
 
generations which has been used around the world. Uses ride the bicycle by smart card 
in fixed bicycle docking station. The inception of bike sharing in Hangzhou ignited 
other cities’ interest in China, by the end of 2014, the operation of bike sharing 
programs has existed in 235 cities in China, with the total number of 747400 bikes in 
28000 fixed docking stations (Meddin, 2014). 
However, the operations of these programs were not in a success due to the complicated 
access for smart card and lack availability of docking stations. Users must register to 
receive smart card at specific location fist and the fixed docking stations bring the 
inconvenience since users cannot find the suitable location to get or return the bicycles 
in certain circumstances. The failure in the universality of bike sharing third generations 
stimulates the development of fourth generations bike sharing programs in China. 
These bike sharing programs are in a distinctive feature of dockless, invading most of 
cities overnight. Compared with the previous public bicycle system, all the procedures 
can be achieved by mobile phone including register, deposit, unlock and payment. 
Customers can locate nearby bicycles via the mobile app in their smartphones since 
each bicycle installs GPS satellite positioning and scan the QR code with their phones 
to unlock it. When they finish their usage, they can leave the bike anywhere and lock 
it, the payment is within the app, with their credit cards. 
 
Different built environment factors contribute to the inevitability of flourish in fourth 
generations bike sharing system in China. From the perspective of economy, in recent 
years, the economic aggregate is in an increasing trend in China, where GDP of China 
rises to 74.4 trillion RMB in 2016 (NBSC, 2017). With growth of economy, the 
ownership of mobile phone is also increasing steadily that the penetration rate of mobile 
phone is up to 75.7% (Iresearch, 2017), mobile phone has been the necessary daily 
products in citizen’s life, at the same time, the habit of mobile payment is gradually 
formed based on the rapid expand of Internet. The persistent development of Internet 
and the universality of mobile phone lay the foundation of the penetration of bike 
sharing system. From the perspective of policy, the national and local government hold 
the positive attitude for the development of bike sharing and promulgate some related 
policy to enhance the bike sharing development. From the social angle, citizens have 
struggled with the transport issues of “last kilometer distance” and traffic congestion 
and the environment pollution like air pollution for many years, the occurrence of 
dockless bike sharing system wins the social favor.

5.1 Data collection 
The questionnaire surveys were designed based on the extensive review of the literature 
and carried out by online surveys and field investigation randomly. All the respondents 
were in voluntary and in anonymity to answer the questionnaire, the period of issued

to administer questionnaire in the field investigation range from bus/metro stations, 
shopping mall to crowded streets. In the surveys, the settings of questionnaire involve 
the information: (a) usage experience to compare the proportion of users and non-users;

consumption compared with other transport modes, except the pollution and resources 
consumption existing in the process of bicycle manufacturing and bicycle 
transportation. The replacement of other transport modes with heavy pollution brings 
out less environment pollution and less resources consumption, we refer this 
environment effects as “substitution effects” for quantitatively decreased environment 
impacts analysis. The source of improvement health impacts is also from the transport 
mode substitution since bike sharing producing less pollution will improve the public 
health, in terms of health, we call the effect as “health effect”.

2010). In this study, the supplement effect is from the time-saving of bike sharing, in 
which the time saved can be regarded as alternative time for the potential economic 
increase. Therefore, the steps of supplement effects’ estimation strategy are as follows:

calculation is achieved by calculating the reduced travel time in the commute period,

6.2.2 Estimation strategy for substitution effects and health effects 
As we know, bike sharing brings advantages to environment and human health. 
However, the question “how the bike sharing benefits environment and human health 
quantitatively” still needs to explore. For the estimation strategy of substitution and 
health effects in this study, life cycle assessment (LCA) provides the way to evaluate. 
LCA is the technique to assess the environmental aspects and potential impacts 
associated with a product, process or service (Nigri, 2014). Based on LCA, the complete 
life cycle of different transport modes is considered in this study, including the process 
of production, maintenance, operation and waste. SimaPro, as the effective software in 
==PAGE:34==
24 
 
LCA, is used to collect and analyze the data to evaluate the environment performance 
of different transport modes.  
 
In order to apply LCA in this study, different scenarios of transport modes are 
determined as increased bike sharing by replacing walking and substituted metro, bus, 
private car and taxi. The goal of LCA is to obtain the quantitative reduced environment 
impacts for substituted transport modes because of bike sharing and the quantitative 
increased environment impacts of bike sharing with the replacement of walking. The 
function unit is person·kilometer/day of different substituted transport modes and the 
increased bicylces. The database in SimaPro provides the data for different scenarios, 
therefore, in the process of life cycle inventory (LCI), the model of life cycle is 
established on the given data from SimaPro database since the data collection for the 
whole life cycle of transport modes in Beijing is so difficult to achieve that we assume 
it as data of different transport modes in Beijing. In addition, the data in SimaPro only 
represents 1person kilometer for different scenarios, the total data in person kilometers 
for different scenarios is necessary to collect. The results of impact assessment for 
different scenarios can be obtained after LCI analysis with different methods. Finally, 
the results of substitution effects and health effects are based on the calculation that 
reduced environment impacts of substituted transport modes minus the increased 
environment impacts of increased bike sharing instead of walking. The estimation 
strategy in LCA is shown as Figure 6.1.

Besides, the behavior that they get bicycles in metro and bus station also partly implies 
they choose bike sharing instead of bus and metro, which decrease the environment 
effects. Thus, the location of supplement is the necessary and significant factor affecting 
the environment performance of bike sharing. 
 
The users holding the view that bike sharing help increase the travel frequency are more 
72.6% likely to behave in the positive environment performance than those with the 
view of no frequency increase. The reasonable explanation is that increased frequency 
provides more possibilities for the bike sharing to operate in positive environment 
performance, for instance, more travel frequencies result in more reduced environment 
impacts under the same conditions of transport mode substitution and ride distance.

positive environment performance. As illustrated in Table 8.6, users facing with 
damaged bikes are found to be less 53.5% likely to exert positive environment impacts

modes such as vehicles, this will exert negative effects to environment.

more in the positive environment performance than those with pessimistic attitudes. 
The optimistic attitude will lead people to use the bike sharing, providing the 
opportunities of positive environment performance.

Recommendations for improvement 
Although bike sharing brings massive benefits to environment, some management 
issues still exist to impede the development of bike sharing. Based on the questionnaire 
surveys, the obstacles for the development involve:  
 
a) The improper parking of bicycles leading to the occupation of urban public space. 
The characteristic of dockless bike sharing is that the users can park bicycles 
anywhere after the finish of usage, resulting in the random parking disorderly. In 
daily life, the bicycles are always put randomly in the sides of roads, greenland in 
the park and so on. This behavior not only has the negative effects to the traffic 
order, but also occupy the public urban space, aggravating the pressure of urban 
and roads resources. 
b) Man-made destroy and private occupancy is serious in the development of bike 
sharing. Some people possess bicycles themselves by damaging the lock or hiding 
bicycles in private space. Besides, during the usage of bike sharing, some users 
don’t have the awareness of protection resulting in the failure of bicycles. In 
addition, some intended damage also exists. 
c) The fail to guarantee the safety of users. It is difficult to monitor whether users 
abide by the traffic rules during their usage and there is no explicit stipulation for 
the responsibility or punishment of users when traffic accidents happen. There are 
no measures to hinder the children using bike sharing, the traffic accidents caused 
by the misconduct of children not only put themselves in danger, but also disorder 
the traffic rules. Besides, the safety of deposit is essential since users have no access 
to clear the whereabouts of their deposit, if the failure of operation happens to bike 
sharing company, the deposit is at risk not to be returned.  
d) The company of bike sharing has difficulty in making profits. The main profit 
source of the company is from the deposit, but the maintenance of bike sharing cost 
a lot, which makes the profits lower. The exploration of sustainable profit model 
still has a long way to go. 
 
From the views of sustainable development of bike sharing, though some issues exist 
in the development, we must admire the change of travel behavior and the positive 
effect towards environment are the most important. Therefore, we need to hold the 
==PAGE:57==
47 
 
encouraging attitude to solve the issues to promote the sustainable development of bike 
sharing. For the improvement of bike sharing, the government should be involved in 
the management and provide the support of monitoring.  
a) The public should be educated to have the adequate awareness of bike sharing to 
avoid the damage towards bicycles and private occupancy. The public should be 
clear that they also have the responsibility for the maintenance of bike sharing. 
Some punishment can be carried out for the damage and occupancy from 
government and the company can take advantages of GPS device to provide the 
users’ information for disordered behavior or establish the record of usage credit so 
that users with low credits cannot be allowed to use bike sharing again. 
b) The measures for bike sharing management should be formulated and regulated. 
Government should introduce a set of policies to guide citizens’ behavior to 
promote the usage of bike sharing orderly, administrative rules for the planning of 
proper parking of bicycles and traffic regulations for bike sharing to decrease traffic 
violations. 
c) Explore the diversified profit business model to promote sustainable development. 
The main funding source of deposit cannot support the development of company so 
that it is necessary for companies to explore sustainable profit models. The deposit 
can be used for the investment to gain more benefits and advertisement in bicycle 
and application is also the effective way for the profit. 
d) The supervision for bike sharing system should be strengthened from the 
government, company and society. The supervision from government should focus 
on the operation of company, the safety of deposit, the proper parking and 
compliance with traffic rules. The company should first monitor themselves to seek 
and solve the problems in time during the operation and maintenance period of bike 
sharing. Then they should be companied with the supervision of government in the 
aspects of proper parking and users’ behavior. For the social supervision, the public 
should check on the disordered behavior of bike sharing in time and report it to the 
administrative department in necessity.

11. 
Conclusions and uncertainty 
11.1 
Conclusions 
Bike sharing has expanded rapidly all over the world especially in China. The 
substitution of metro, bus, private car and taxi from bike sharing will reduce the 
negative environment effects, but the substitution of walking will result in some 
negative effects. Several studies have researched on the qualitative environment 
impacts of bike sharing and the identifications of factors for the bike sharing usage. 
Based on the literature review, this study is mainly to explore the environment impacts 
quantitatively and the factors affecting the environment performance of bike sharing in 
Beijing. The questionnaire surveys are necessary for data collection for the overview 
of bike sharing development in Beijing, environment impact assessment and factors 
identification. The environment impacts assessment is identified for the economic 
impacts, environmental impacts and health impacts, in which the economy impacts are 
estimated at the relation between the leisure time and economy growth, estimation of 
the environmental impacts and health impacts is achieved with the method of life cycle 
assessment. In the part of estimation of environment impacts, economic, environmental 
and health impacts are identified as supplement effects, substitution effects and health 
effects, respectively. The results of questionnaire surveys provide the fundamental data 
for the statistic model to factors identification that affect the environment performance 
of bike sharing. A total of 578 valid samples are used to develop the statistic model 
which is chosen as Binary logistic model in this study.  
 
In the part of environment impact assessment, this study finds that the bike sharing 
programs have the positive impacts in the aspects of economy, environment and human 
health. According to this study, for the supplement effects, the bike sharing helps save 
the time in 1.1 hours per day which can be regarded as leisure time. Based on the 
coefficient between leisure time and economy growth, the reasonable estimation results 
of the supplement effects in Beijing are obtained as the increase of per capita GDP in 
$132.3 (893.9 RMB), which is the significant increase. For the substitution effects, with 
utilizing LCA, the results show that the launch of bike sharing programs has the positive 
effects in all impacts related to environment protection and resources saving such as the 
reduction of global warming is 1398091.27 kg CO2 eq. Among the different 
substitutions of transport modes, the substituted bus donates the most contribution to 
==PAGE:59==
49 
 
the reduction of negative environment impacts. The results of health effects in this study 
is the reduction of human health damage in 2.52 daily in LCA. Same as the substitution 
effects, the substituted bus contributes most for the health damage reduction. 
 
In the identification of factors affecting the environment performance of bike sharing, 
the dependent variable is environment performance which is calculated with the data of 
travel distance, travel frequency in questionnaire surveys and the data of substituted 
impacts in LCA. The independent variables are divided into five groups: individual 
characteristic, household characteristic, travel patterns, built environment and 
perception of bike sharing. The estimated binary logistic model shows several 
important independent variables. The eight variables are regarded as the significant 
factors: gender, monthly income, the reason of cheapness to use bike sharing, the 
locations of bike sharing supplement in bus station, metro station and resident quarter, 
the perception of frequency increase by bike sharing, the barrier of some damaged bikes 
during usage and the optimistic attitude for the bike sharing future. 
 
In summary, the results from this study helps us have a better understanding for the 
impacts and environment performance of bike sharing. The overall effects of bike 
sharing effects are significantly positive. Besides, the findings of this study can provide 
empirical evidence in the process of policy decision, the planning and management of 
bike sharing programs for the administrative department and companies.  
11.2 
Uncertainty  
Limited by the capacity and time, the samples from questionnaire surveys used for the 
binary logistic model are not enough for the modeling, the more samples we receive, 
the more degree of reliability. Besides, due to the literature review limits, some essential 
factors may absent in the questionnaire surveys which also affect the effectiveness of 
modeling.  
 
Limited by the data collection, the estimation for the supplement effects only focus on 
the effects based on the users as workers, the other residents and students are excluded. 
Besides, for the estimation of saved time, this study doesn’t take the traffic congestion 
into consideration. Due to these reasons, the results of supplement effects estimation 
are less reliable. With the method of LCA, the assumptions for the data to represent 
different scenarios are based on the database in the LCA software, SimaPro, not the 
==PAGE:60==
50 
 
authentic data in Beijing. The data collected in the inventory may have the difference 
in the standard of different transport vehicles such as the standard for materials, 
emissions and the environment background conditions such as air pollution. The 
assumptions will lead to uncertainty for the final results of substitution effects and 
health effects.  
 
Although these uncertainties exist in the process of estimation, the estimation results 
still provide available evidence with the comprehensive understanding of bike sharing’s 
effects in related environment aspects. Based on this study, further research can be 
performed with more valuable data.
