Abstract 
This Master of Science thesis focuses on research and prototype-development of a Patient Monitoring Service, 
which has the main purpose to reduce the medical expenditures in hospitals by performing an indoor localization 
monitoring on a targeted patient. It starts with a research field based on two investigations, where the first is 
targeting wireless communication technologies, which is required for PMS purpose especially for indoor 
localization purposes. The determination of the proper technology will be based on its support for smart devices 
as well as its cost. The second investigation is targeting indoor location estimation algorithms, which are required 
to estimate a coordinate of a targeted patient in an indoor case. The thesis then proposes a design and 
implementation of a possible prototype of PMS, based on previously preferred technologies and mathematical 
algorithms. Testing of the prototype is performed afterwards, where the testing of localization accuracy will be 
emphasized. The thesis ends up with a discussion of testing results and further improvements of PMS.

1 Introduction 
 
 Motivations 
Medical costs have grown tremendously in the past several decades and have become a phenomenon by being 
a subject of discussions among politicians, administrators and academics in various countries [1]. The significance 
of this issue resulted in global interests and international comparisons of health ex[penditure. Figure 1 depicts 
the medical cost growth in the US in 30 years’ interval from 1980 to 2010.  
 
Figure 1: Medical cost growth in various countries from 1980 to 2010 [2] 
Thus, the market trend moved directly towards IT- and wireless communication solutions in order to reduce the 
growth. This thesis proposes a Patient Monitoring Service (PMS) that is a wireless communication as well as an 
IT-service, which has the main goal to become an important trend in the medical sector with a purpose of 
reducing the enormous aforementioned medical expenditures. The reduction will be achieved by saving human 
resources in every hospitals as w[ell as nursing homes. Monitoring of patients in those sectors requires a high 
==PAGE:14==
 
Page 2 of 140 
 
number of human resources, since every patient needs emergency in case of an accident. Thus, they all need to 
be monitored by benefiting from a service that allows all patients to be monitored from a single central.  
 Technology overview 
Video surveillance is nowadays the most preferred monitoring system for monitoring purpose, since every room 
includes a recording camera, which performs surveillance on the targeted patient. Each camera transmits its 
video data to a central, which includes numerous screens where each displays a room that is under surveillance.  
Although this technology is the most preferable, it still has following drawbacks that might cause the service to 
be less efficient:  
 
The service requires high expenditures, since every room must be equipped with a video camera 
 
At least one responsible must always be located in the central room in order to monitor the conditions 
of all patients   
 
PMS overcomes those drawbacks by benefiting from a notification technique, which directly notifies a manager 
once the target patient needs an emergency in case of a fall. A procedure presenting PMS is shown in Figure 2. 
The map consists of two rooms as well as a corridor between them. The idea is that, once a patient falls down as 
shown in the figure, the service automatically notifies the manager who immediately handles the emergency.   
 
Beside its notification technique, PMS also includes an indoor location 
estimation service, since the notification must include the position of 
the targeted patient in order to indicate the manager of where the 
patient lies down.  Once a notification is received by the manager, it 
also has to transmit the received notification to an Administration 
Center, which gathers all information from several hospitals in order to 
make statistical analysis. Figure 3 shows a metropolitan area with three 
hospitals named Hospital North, Hospital East and Hospital West and 
an administration center. Whenever the manager detects an 
emergency in any of those hospitals, the information is directly 
transmitted to a specific server at the cloud. This server needs to be 
deployed on a public cloud, and authenticated in a way that it only can 
be accessed by a restricted number of users. The administrators at the 
administration center need to request for information, which the 
server includes upon authentication process. The server then 
responses with all included information and the administration center 
has now fully access to all information. The security of the cloud server 
is an essential and required task, but since this thesis proposes a 
prototype of a possible PMS, server implementation will be out of the 
scope for this thesis. Hence, security concerns for the server will not be 
presented.

 Scope 
The targeting field in this thesis is indoor localization techniques and indoor positioning systems using wireless 
communication technologies. Indoor localization is a technique that can estimate a position of an object in a 
limited space. Various technologies have been researched and developed for localization purpose in the last 
few decades. The most widely used technology is the well-known Global Positioning System (GPS). Although 
this technology is the preferred one for location estimation, it cannot be used for indoor localization purposes, 
since the signals from GPS satellites are attenuated by walls as well as various obstacles in an indoor case. 
Thus, the accuracy of GPS device might vary around 10-20 meters for an indoor case [3]. This offset leads to an 
insufficient solution, since an accurate value is required. Indoor GPS systems were considered as an approach 
to overcome aforementioned drawbacks. This solution is able to increase the signal strength of attenuated RF 
waves with local equipment [3], but the cost of GPS devices has to be considered as well, since they require a 
higher investment compared to other wireless RF protocols [3]. Due to the limitations with GPS, researchers 
had begun investigating the performance of RF-technologies that measure the strength of the received signal. 
This is obtained by measuring a Received Signal Strength Indication (RSSI) value of the signal. The idea behind 
==PAGE:16==
 
Page 4 of 140 
 
this approach is to receive a set of RSSI-values that is collected from various broadcasters, thus estimating a 
final location point using a mathematical algorithm from collected RSSI values. Figure 4 shows this approach.  
In addition to indoor localization techniques, this thesis emphasizes a research field based on various wireless 
communication technologies. Considering the requirements 
as well as the procedure of PMS, a wireless communication 
interface is required, so the broadcasters will be able to 
broadcast with RF-signals (Figure 4) and a communication 
interface needs to be established between the patient and 
manager in order to inform a detection of fall. Determination 
of the most sufficient wireless technology for PMS purpose 
will be obtained according to power consumption. Since 
reducing medical expenditures by overcoming already 
existing monitoring technologies is the purpose of PMS, the 
cost of its implementation and sustainability are of high 
importance. Thus, the cost of its sustainability can be 
decreased by choosing the most proper technology, which is 
still capable to serve all requirements defined by PMS.      
 Technical Challenges 
Signal strength in wireless communication systems results 
in fluctuations that are not predictable especially in an indoor environment. In this case, a signal path is 
obviously blocked by obstacles and humans, which outcomes as an insufficient position estimation of the 
target, since the signal experiences either reflection or diffraction, which lead to a signal attenuation. Indoor 
signal strength fluctuations become the most complicated challenge that will be presented in this thesis, since 
the signal strength variation issue cannot be handled but instead limited or decreased by performing some 
specific operations. As mention in previous section, indoor position estimation is the targeting field in this 
thesis and thus the main goal of PMS is based on high-accuracy position estimation of the targeted patient.  
The research field consists of position techniques and algorithms, which will be presented as well as discussed 
in this thesis.  
In addition to obstacles, a presence of human bodies will lead to an additional source of inaccuracy, since our 
bodies contain around 60% of water [4]. Thus the parts of RF-waves that are transmitted by transmitter will be 
attenuated in a case a human body is located nearby. Considering this case with PMS, the patient needs to 
wear a device that receives RSSI-values in order to be localized as depicted in Figure 4. In this case, the 
patient’s body absorbs the RF-waves broadcasted by the broadcasters. Hence, the outcome may result in 
inaccuracy of a position estimation.

2 Project structure 
This section presents a model that will be used as a software management process, whereas all software 
design in this thesis will be structured according to the model. 
Waterfall model is one of the most used software design model as well as the most classical [5]. It is known for 
going step-by-step from one phase to another. Hence, it requires that a specific phase is started only when the 
deliverables of the previous phase have been completed [6]. Figure 5 presents all phases in waterfall model in a 
step-by-step order.  
Requirements
Design
Verification
Implementation
Maintenance
 
Figure 5: Phases in Waterfall model 
The rest of this section presents a brief description of every phase included in waterfall model.  
 Requirements 
Requirements is the first phase as well as the most important, since it first identifies the needs of customers 
and presents both system requirements (expectation for components to build the system) and software 
requirements (expectations for software functionalities). Customer needs are documented on a high 
abstraction level [7] 
System requirements are the first part to consider, since it includes the hardware requirements, software tools 
and other components that are necessary [5].  
Software requirements are decided upon system requirement. Those requirements identify which system 
requirements the software affects, such as performance requirements, user interaction requirements etc.  
==PAGE:18==
 
Page 6 of 140 
 
 Design 
Design phase can be separated in following two minor parts [5]: 
 
Architectural design 
 
Detailed design  
Architectural design is represented by a system architecture. The architecture has the purpose to describe the 
whole system in terms of structure as well as satisfy the business and service requirements defined by the 
customer. The architecture includes a system topology, which defines software/hardware components such as 
server nodes, physical connections in the network, determination of application servers etc [8].  
Detailed design is identified by a “detailed” description of each hardware/software component presented in 
architectural design. It includes a specification of how each component is implemented (based on technology). 
 Implementation 
Implementation phase specifies to the realization of aforementioned design specifications into an executable 
program, application, website obtained by programming and deployment[9]. All programming are done in this 
part and thereafter compiled into an application. Implementation are shortly identified by converting the 
whole requirements into a production environment [9].  
 Verification  
This process is responsible for testing as well as validating in order to conclude whether the implemented 
system meets the requirements and specifications. The system is evaluated to determine whether the final 
product of a given development phase actually satisfies the conditions that were imposed at the beginning of 
the corresponding phase[9]. In software language, the verification phase is identified by debugging, unit 
testing, testing of each use case etc. 
 Maintenance 
This last phase is identified by modifying a system solution after delivery and deployment in order to correct 
error and improve performance as well as quality.

3 Technological Research 
This section describes the most widely used technologies for localization purpose nowadays. It starts 
presenting each technology, goes further with an underlying description and finally concludes by summing up 
the benefits and drawbacks of each. The focus will then continue on position estimation techniques, which 
present mathematical algorithms in order to localize a target object. The section finally concludes by 
determining the technology that will be used for PMS based on the benefits and drawbacks of each.      
 Global Positioning System 
Global Positioning System (GPS) is developed by United States Department of Defense (DoD) and is the most 
widely used technology for outdoor position estimation nowadays [10]. It is based on constellation of 27 earth-
satellites, whereas 24 of them are in operation and the others are required for resilience purpose [11]. 
Benefiting from the operated satellites, a person or object can estimate its two-dimensional position in terms 
of latitude and longitude. All satellites orbit the earth with an altitude around 20 kilometers and perform two 
rotations every day in their own specific path as shown in Figure 6.  
Considering this operation, a random GPS receiver 
located on the earth is able to estimate its position 
using any of four visible satellites. Although this 
technology is effectively used in many navigational 
applications, it has some limitations such as 
insufficient location estimation in indoor 
environments due to signal attenuation caused by 
walls.    
Two main information are required to obtain a 
localization process via GPS:  
 
The distance among GPS receiver and four 
specific satellites it receives signal from  
 
The position of each four satellites in terms 
of latitude, longitude and altitude.  
The GPS receiver collects those two information and 
processes high-frequency, low-power radio signals 
that are received from the satellites.   
3.1.1 Distance measurement in Global Positioning System  
As usually, it is assumed that the clocks of both the GPS receiver and a GPS-satellite are perfectly synchronized. 
GPS benefits from TOA-estimation (explained further in section 3.10.1) and the timeframe between the signal 
transmitted by the satellite and the same signal received by the receiver is used to determine the distance. 
Each satellite are tracked using their own unique identification code, which is a pseudo-random number that 
maximizes the signal-to-noise ratio of a given satellite [12]. A GPS-satellite continuously broadcasts with its 
pseudo-random number that includes information regarding the exact time, the signal was transmitted from

 Inertial Navigation System 
Inertial Navigation System (INS) is a position estimating technology that uses accelerometers as well as 
gyroscopes in order to track a position of a person or an object. A gyroscope is a motion estimation sensor that 
measures the rotation rate (or angular velocity) of an object, whereas an accelerometer measures the linear 
acceleration[11]. INS requires that its initial position, velocity and orientation is known beforehand. Hence, it 
will be able to continuously update the velocity and orientation of the object. Thus, the position of the object 
can continually be tracked without any external information from other reference sources. INS measures the 
linear acceleration and angular velocity in space (x, y and z axis, where each axis is orthogonal to others). 
Mathematical integration of measured acceleration values 𝑎(𝑡) yields to the velocity 𝑣(𝑡) of the object. If the 
velocity is then integrated, the distance from the starting point 𝑑(𝑡) will be obtained. The orientation 𝜑(𝑡) of 
the object is the second required parameter[11]. This parameter can be obtained by integrating the angular 
velocity 𝜔(𝑡) values that are continuously measured by the gyroscope. INS performs all calculations 
periodically, which is required in order obtain a more accurate position. The more often calculations are 
performed, the more accurate position will be achieved. The main drawback of this technology is errors caused 
by both the accelerometer and gyroscope sensors, since wrong measurements can be performed, which will 
directly lead to a wrong position estimation. INS technology is mainly used by military services to track 
submarines, warships, vehicles and missiles.   
 
 
 Radio Frequency Identification  
Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) is a wireless technology that includes RFID tags as well as RFID readers. 
The tag consists of an integrated microprocessor chip with a memory and includes an identity value that can be 
broadcasted to an RFID reader, which operates at an equal frequency [13].  
RFID systems are generally divided into following two categories: 
 
RFID systems with passive tags  
 
RFID systems with active tags  
Passive tags are identified by not requiring a power source and thus are used for application for short-range 
communication that is maximally several tens of meters [14]. Since they do not have a power source, they are 
supplied by the reader, which transmits electro-magnetic waves that induce a current in its antenna [14]. 
Unlike passive tags, an active tag is supplied by a battery and is identified as a fully transceiver system. 
Considering the RFID-procedure, the reader broadcasts with electro-magnetic waves, which are received by the 
tag. The received wave is then modulated by adding the tag identification information and finally transmitted 
back to the reader.  Once the reader receives the wave, it converts it to digital form in order to read the tag 
identity.  
This technology can be useful for position estimation purpose. An active RFID-tag can be placed on a target 
object, which transmits a signal to three or four RFID-readers. Using trilateration algorithm along with RSSI-
technique, will result in a final position estimation of the target device.

target objects and receives it back. While processing signal strength of reflected waves at multiple receives, the

 Bluetooth Low Energy 
Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) is a wireless PAN technology, which is known for its relatively low power 
consumption compared to the classical Bluetooth protocol (Bluetooth 3.0). The technology is a part of Bluetooth 
4.0 (Bluetooth Smart) and Bluetooth 4.1 specification.  
It has the ability to substantially reduce the peak, average and idle mode power consumption with energy 
efficiencies up to 20 times higher than classic Bluetooth[19]. This energy efficiency allows BLE radios to sustain 
with a single coin cell battery for a year or more.  
The incredibly low power consumption of BLE is enabled by its link layer, which is designed for quick connections. 
BLE chips spend most of their time asleep and they only wake up to send data. This process has a short duration, 
which only takes only few milliseconds, compared to 100 milliseconds of the classic Bluetooth protocol. Figure

3.10.2 Time-Difference of Arrival  
Estimation of Time-Difference of Arrival (TDOA) is based on measurement of the difference in time between 
signals arriving from two base nodes. Considering the three base nodes in Figure 25, the base node that first 
receives a signal from the target node is referred as the reference base station. If trilateration is used with 
three base nodes, there is a need of two TDOA measurements in order to localize the target node, and the 
measurements are performed with the reference base station. TDOA overcomes the synchronization drawback 
in TOA by not requiring any synchronization of the target node clock with base node clocks.  
3.10.3 Direction of Arrival  
Direction of Arrival (DOA) is a positioning algorithm that is defined by the angle between the propagation 
direction of an incident wave and reference stations[32]. Hence, it is required that reference stations are 
equipped with antenna arrays, whereas each antenna array must be equipped with RF front-end-components. 
Similar to TOA and TDOA, it is required that the position of reference stations is known beforehand. This 
technique has an advantage that only two reference stations are required for object localization estimation, 
whereas at least three reference stations are required for both TOA and TDOA. Figure 28 shows a case using 
DOA estimation. The position of the target is calculated by combining the angle of the signals from both 
reference stations.

3.10.4 RSSI  
RSSI is defined by measured power of a received radio signal, which is widely used in 802.11 standards[33]. It 
performs location estimation by benefiting from the trilateration algorithm (section 3.10.1.1) similar to TOA. 
Unlike TOA, the location estimation is performed using received signal strength. Thus, the received signal 
strength that is received by a base node indicates the distance. RSSI is represented as 8-bit signed integer (-127 
to 127), which denotes whether the value is either above or below the Golden Receiver Power Range (GRPR) 
[34]. GRPR is defined as the ideal signal strength range. A positive value of RSSI (in dB) denotes that the received 
signal strength is either above or below GRPR, whereas a zero value means that it is within GRPR.  
 Conclusion  
In addition to accuracy, the power consumption of each technology is the main factor to consider. Technologies 
such as GPS, RADAR, and RFID are intended for outdoor localization for longer distances (WWAN or WMAN) and 
thus the outcome results in high power consumption. Hence, the most predictable technologies are ZigBee and 
BLE, since they both consume much lower power and thus are intended for shorter distances (WLAN or WPAN) 
as it is required in indoor cases. Although Wi-Fi is can perform a corresponding operation even with stronger 
signals as well as higher data rate, it is not intended for indoor localization due to its high power consumption 
and thus are not suitable for coin cell operation. Figure 29 shows a comparison among popular wireless standards 
defined by IEEE. The vertical axis defines the area size of the network, whereas the horizontal axis defines the 
data transmission rate. Considering that RSSI-values and battery level percentage are the only information to 
transmit to the manager over wireless interface, there is no need to invest in a technology with a high data-rate, 
since it is small pieces of data.   
==PAGE:40==

Figure 29: Comparison among wireless standards defined by IEEE [54] 
Hence Wi-Fi can be eliminated as a possible approach. Bluetooth was integrated in older cell phones for more 
than a decade and includes an “out of box” compatibility with most smart devices nowadays such as PCs, 
tablets and smartphones. Bluetooth and BLE both exists in single chips called dual mode Bluetooth 4.0 chips. 
Those dual mode chips were integrated with a strategy of making it quite easy for smart device manufactures 
to replace the old Bluetooth chips with the new dual mode chips[35]. Unlike BLE, ZigBee is not support by any 
smart device and thus ensures slower acceptance from the market compared to BLE.

6.2.4.1 
OSAL 
OSAL acts more like a control loop rather than an operating system and is responsible for controlling the whole 
BLE stack and corresponding applications[21]. It allows the TI software to setup execution of events. It is 
required that a task identifier is created and a task initialization routine have to be defined to OSAL 
initialization for each software layer that requires execution of events. Beside its task management ability, it 
also provides additional services such as message passing, memory management as well as timer 
functionalities.   
6.2.4.2 
HAL 
HAL provides an interface of abstraction between the protocol stack (software) and physical hardware of the 
kit. This layer deals with lower level hardware configuration and components such as LED, LCD, ADC, 
Accelerometer etc.   
6.2.4.3 
Application 
Application represents the highest layer that interacts with other layers in order to implement the application 
logic as well as software algorithms.

6.3.1 General considerations 
6.3.1.1 
Asynchronous programming 
Asynchronous programming is an essential task for activities that for instance are blocking the main thread. If an 
application has to access several web resources synchronously, it performs the operation step-by-step, thus 
blocking the application’s main thread. In better words, the application is blocked and any further operations 
cannot be executed until it gets a response from an accessed web resource. In an asynchronous case, the 
application can continue performing other executions that does not depend on the web resource, so the main 
thread is not blocked in this case.  
Asynchronous programming is essential especially in application with a UI-thread, since all UI-related activities 
share a common main thread. Since a smartphone application is of this case, there is a need for asynchronous 
tasks. As mentioned in section 5.5.2, the application has to get RSSI-values from each service accessed in the 
target device. RSSI-values from each broadcaster has its own BLE data characteristic, so it is not possible to obtain 
RSSI-values from all four broadcasters by perform a single BLE-operation with the target device. Hence, it is 
required to perform four operations and this is where asynchronous programming becomes essential. Figure 75 
shows a synchronous case, where four BLE-operations are performed to access all four data characteristics (RSSI 
1,RSSI 2,RSSI 3,RSSI 4) from the target device. At this case, the application starts requesting for RSSI 1-values and 
waits for response. The main thread are blocked meanwhile until a response containing RSSI-data is received 
from the target device. If it succeeds, it then follows the same procedure for the second broadcaster and waits 
for response as well, while blocking main thread.

6.3.1.2 
Asynchronous programming in Android 
Asynchronous operations are obtained by using AsyncTasks in Android. Following three types are used by an 
AsyncTask[45]:  
 
Params  
o The type of the parameter that is sent to the task after execution  
 
Progress 
o The type of the progress that is published during background computation  
 
Result 
o The type of the result from a background computation 
Following four steps are essential for an AsyncTask in Android and will be mainly used in application 
implementation: 
onPreExecute() 
This method is invoked on the main UI thread before the async task is executed. This option is mostly used in a 
setup task in order to inform the user that an async task will be executed.  
doInBackground(Params...) 
doInBackground() is invoked just after onPreExecute() has executed, and is used to perform background 
operation that might take a while. Parameters of the async task are directly passed to this method and the 
computation result must be returned by this method as well.  
onProgressUpdate(Progress…) 
Since the duration of a background operation does not have a timeframe and can take a while, a function is 
required to display any form of the progress in the user interface, while the operation is still running. 
OnProgressUpdate() method is used for that purpose.  
onPostExecute(Result…) 
As its name indicates, the method is invoked in the main UI thread after a background operation finishes in 
doInBackground() method. This method can be used to perform any other operation in UI thread upon an 
async task operation.  
6.3.1.3 
Service Class 
Service classes are used for longer background operation for android applications, while not interacting with 
user objects such as buttons, textboxes etc[46]. This is important for BLE connection purpose, since a standard 
Android activity class will be destroyed once the smartphone goes to standby state. Thus, the smartphone will 
automatically stop scanning for BLE devices, which leads to an insufficient service. The idea is to maintain and 
keep the scanning operation even though the user does not interact with the smartphone. This approach can 
be achieved by benefiting from a service class that performs all BLE operations in the background.

6.3.2.5 
Transmitting fall-detection information to the cloud server 
Cloud computing technologies have been discussed in section 5.4.3, and since AWS offers 1-year free trial, its 
web services will be used as a cloud server. The cloud server must include a database service, since there is a 
need for storing fall-detection information in the cloud server. AWS offers two database services, which is RDS 
or DynamoDB (as mentioned in 5.5.3). RDS is not an independent service, since it allows various SQL-servers 
from different platforms such as Microsoft, Oracle etc to be deployed on it. Thus, it results in a much more 
expensive service compared to DynamoDB, which is independent and does not interact with other platforms. 
In addition to this advantage, DynamoDB also supports an API for Android OS, which results in an easier as well 
as faster approach for the smartphone application to store the data in the cloud.  
The implementation of the DynamoDB service is done by manually creating a DynamoDB service in AWS 
Management Console [48]. The table has been created by considering its design that were presented in Table 8 
in section 5.5.3. The primary key of the table is defined Hash and Range Type primary key. Thus, DynamoDB 
builds an unordered hash index on the hash attribute and builds an ordered(sorted) index on the range 
attribute [49]. Each item in the table are thus uniquely identified by a combination of hash and range key 
values.  
After this step, the service will be implemented in the application by using Android DynamoDB API. It is 
significant to perform DynamoDB queries asynchronously as well, since the application interacts with an 
external AWS server for data storage. Thus, the query operation will not block the main application thread 
while it is executed.   
The application first generates a hospital name (returns either “Hospital East”, Hospital North” or “Hospital 
“West) and calculates the unix time. The hash key attribute is defined as the hospital name, whereas the range 
key attribute is defined as the unix time of the detected fall. Hence, the table will automatically sort each fall 
information according to the unix time.  
The dtu.ble.aws package in PatientMonitoring Android project (Figure 77) is responsible for all AWS operation. 
It includes a “DynamoDBManager” class that includes method in order to perform DynamoDB queries. All 
==PAGE:98==

required methods are directly called from the main ServicesActivity class asynchronously upon position 
estimation of the target. Hence, the insertData() method in DynamoDBManger class is called with all required 
information regarding the detected fall. This method is responsible for performing a “save” query, which saves 
all received information in a given DynamoDB table. It returns a bool, which indicates whether the query has

thesis. Hence, 12 tests have been performed for localization purpose (section 7.1.3) in order to conclude the

7.1.3.2), the accuracy estimation is sufficient in a case the target is located at any coordinate, which is marked

Considering test 1-8 in section 7.1.3.2, the target device were located at any coordinate that is marked with 
blue. As a result, the application managed to estimate the target coordinate or in worst case, it estimated a 
coordinate that were next to the target. This outcome is concluded as sufficient, since the functional 
requirement 2.5.1 in section 4 announces that any offset within 0.5 meters can be accepted. Thus, if the 
estimated target coordinate is next to the ordinary coordinate of the target, it is still located 0.5 meters within 
the ordinary coordinate, since each coordinate is a square with a size of 0.5 meters.  
Regarding the other cases, the target device were located at any coordinate marked with yellow (Figure 129) in 
test 9 and test 10. Hence, the position estimation was insufficient by the application, since the estimated 
coordinates in both tests were more than 0.5 meters away from the ordinary coordinate of the target. 
Reflection (section 3.9.2) is the main cause of the issue, since the coordinates are close to the edges or corners 
in the room and thus the signals transmitted by the broadcasters can experience reflections from the walls.  
From the latter case, it can be concluded that PMS does not meet the requirements for position estimation and 
thus cannot currently be served to customers due to this issue.  
It is clear from the test results in section 7.1.3.3 that the broadcasted signals from the broadcasters are 
sensitive to obstacles. Even a chair blocking the signal path for a single broadcaster can finally lead to a wrong 
position estimation as shown in test 1. Hence, it is required that all furniture in the patient’s room must be 
located according to broadcasters, so they are not blocking the signal path. 
A possible approach to overcome this obstacle issue is placing the broadcasters close to the roof as shown in 
Figure 130. Thus, the furniture can be located anywhere in the room without blocking the signal path. Despite 
having this huge advantage, this solution still has following drawback

while trilateration is performed and thus the position accuracy might be lowered

 Further improvements of PMS 
 
8.2.1 Monitor all rooms in the hospital 
Since this thesis presents a prototype of the PMS, the PMS Manager is only able to monitor a single target in a 
specific room. This is required to be improved in a way that the manager will be able to monitor all rooms in a 
given hospital. Thus, the smartphone application for PMS Manager must include a whole map of the hospital 
rather than a single room.  
8.2.2 Monitor more patients with auto connection establishment algorithm 
The PMS Manager application is only able to monitor a single patient but needs to be improved in a way that 
all patients can be monitored simultaneously in a hospital. Considering this improvement, it could be possible 
that more patients fall down simultaneously and thus, there is a need for an algorithm, which leads the 
application to establish a connection with the closest target.  
Figure 131 shows the aforementioned case, when two patients fall down simultaneously. The PMS Manager 
device discovers both targets and thus becomes able to measure the RSSI-value of each target. In this case, it 
establishes a connection to Patient 1 since this target broadcasts with a stronger signals and might be located 
closer to the manager.

8.2.3 Increase device discovery performance of the target device 
CC2541 from TI is used for PMS target device purpose in this thesis. It presents a drawback regarding its device 
discovery functionality, since it is only able to discover one device at a time as mentioned in section 6.2.5.1. 
Considering that it should be able to measure RSSI-values from four broadcasters, which broadcast 
simultaneously, it outcomes as an inefficient and slow operation if it is only capable of gathering one RSSI-value 
from a single broadcaster at a time. Hence, it updates the RSSI-values slowly and this may lead to inaccuracy in 
final position estimation if the target patient quickly moves around. From the testing procedure in section 
(7.1.3.2) it took CC2541 15 seconds to measure and store 20 RSSI-values from all four broadcasters in its 
memory, which is a slow process.  
An approach to overcome this issue could be investing in another device that is able to discover all four 
broadcasters asynchronously and thus updating the RSSI-values four times faster. In addition to improve the 
accuracy, it could also be an option to define two byte arrays for each broadcaster, so the device gathers 40 
RSSI-values instead. Hence, the PMS Manager device will perform a BLE-read operation twice for each 
characteristics and thus will be able to gather twice as many RSSI-values. This approach will improve the 
position accuracy as well. This solution is not intended for CC2541, since it does not update the values fast 
enough.  
8.2.4 Using a buzzer as BLE-role indication rather than the LED 
The target device (CC2541) consumes much more power than it is supposed to consume. Hence, the integrated 
buzzer in CC2541 can be an opportunity to overcome this issue. Thus, the buzzer will start beeping once the 
BLE-role of the target device turns to peripheral and stop beeping while it is in observer role.   
8.2.5 Obtaining more RSSI-values from broadcasters 
Since RSSI-values have the main influence for position accuracy, it will only be beneficial to obtain more RSSI-
values from each broadcaster. Considering test results for position estimation (section 7.1.3.2), in many test 
results, the manager application detected a target coordinate, which were located next to the ordinary one. 
==PAGE:142==
 
Page 130 of 140 
 
The outcome of those tests could be more accurate if the target device could manage to store more than 20 
RSSI-values for each broadcaster.    
8.2.6 Deploying information in their own server  
Development of new server and a corresponding web service is out of the scope of this thesis. Thus, an already 
developed cloud computing based service has been used for web service purpose. This solution is not efficient 
for PMS purpose, since the owner of the service (Amazon in this case) has fully access to all stored information. 
Hence, a new server with a corresponding web service only for PMS purpose needs to be developed if PMS 
needs to be served to customers.     
 Drawbacks of PMS  
The equipment expenditures of PMS are much lower than other services applied for monitoring purpose (video 
surveillance etc.) since the price of low power RF-devices are generally low. Despite having those above-
mentioned significant advantages, PMS also includes some drawbacks considering the expenditures. 
Monitoring all rooms is one of the most significant improvement for this service as mention in section 8.2.1. 
Hence, it is required that a whole hospital map is drawn in PMS Manager application. Such an improvement 
requires a high workload, since the application needs to be developed according to the size of each room, and 
each room needs to be separated in coordinates as well.  
Large rooms might cause an inaccurate position estimation, since the target device will get further away from 
the broadcasters and thus the distance estimation will get less accurate. If for instance the target device is 20 
meter far away from a broadcaster, the outcome of distance estimation will result in an error of +/- 10 
meters[53]. Investing in broadcaster with a better antenna quality is an approach to overcome this issue. Thus, 
the broadcaster will be able to broadcast with stronger signals, which can be discovered from longer distances.  
9 Conclusion 
Instructions as well as recommendations are presented in this thesis with a purpose of providing an efficient 
PMS. Different wireless communication technologies have been researched in the first part, where BLE has been 
decided as an appropriate wireless communication technology for PMS purpose due to its low-cost as well as its 
compatibility with widely used smart devices such as PCs, tablets and smartphones. Various localization 
techniques were then presented and the distance-based trilateration algorithm was the only approach for 
position estimation purpose for PMS, since there is no opportunity to measure any kind of angles based on RSSI-
values. A possible prototype of PMS has been designed and implemented according to decided technologies and 
implementation of position estimation were emphasized. The prototype has been tested in three parts and the 
first part was based on its position accuracy, the second part presented an acceptance testing of Use Cases and 
finally, an acceptance testing has been performed for functional requirements as well. From the testing results, 
RSSI-fluctuations became the main influence for position inaccuracy and thus resulted as a main challenge for 
PMS as expected. Further improvements for PMS have been perceived and considered in order to increase the 
efficiency of the service, since those improvements are the approach to convert PMS from a prototype 
(presented in this thesis) to a real product, which can be served to customers. Reflection caused by walls resulted 
as the main issue presented in this thesis, since estimation of coordinates close to the edge or corner of a room 
is insufficient and thus PMS cannot be merchandised to customers due to this issue.

public static double mean(int[] values) { 
        double sum = 0; 
        for (int i = 0; i < values.length; i++) { 
            sum += values[i]; 
        } 
        return sum / values.length; 
} 
11.1.2.2 C-2 
public static double getRange(int txPower, double rssi) {       
          if (rssi == 0) { 
            return -1.0; // if we cannot determine accuracy, return -1. 
          }        
          double distance = 1 * Math.exp((txPower-rssi)/(10*1.6)); 
          return distance;  
}
