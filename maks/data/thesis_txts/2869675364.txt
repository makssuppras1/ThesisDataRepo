1
Abstract
This thesis will cover the design and manufacturing of a 3D scanner which can mount
materials in a wide range of sizes for the purpose of measuring them with the use of
Terahertz Cross-Correlation Spectroscopy (THz-CCS), which is a non destructive,
non contact way to extract information about the surface and sub-surface conditions
of materials. The working focal length, and spot size of the THZ-CCS sytem is
investigated. The conductivity of 45 sintered Heraeus Prexonics Silver Ink samples
is modeled using the Drude model from the measured THz-CCS signal, and the p2p
amplitude is compared the their measured end to end resistances.
2
Introduction
Ultrafast optics, including THz has been studied academically for decades. Terahertz
Time Domain Spectroscopy(THz-TDS), is a coherent measurement technique which
allows to measure amplitude and phase of the electric field in a wide spectral range.
However this method has a drawback in that it requires the use of ultrafast lasers,
which is an expensive component. Terahertz Cross-Correlation Spectroscopy (THz-
CCS) however, can be used with an incoherent light source, such as an LD, which
is significantly cheaper. [4]. THz-CCS is non-destructive, non-contact and can be
used to investigate both the surface and sub-surface of materials, which makes THz-
CCS an excellent choice for applications in industry. In this thesis I design a 3D
scanner in the program Solidworks. This system is integrated with the CARMEN
system which is made by GLAZE, allowing for probing of materials by a THz-
CCS in 3D space. I show various ways the data gathered by this system can be
visualized in order to help interpret the data. By using of Knife Edge Scans, I find
the probe size of the THz beam. I investigate the working focal length of the THz
beam by making several z-scans, and in the process optimize for the best settings
in the CARMEN system in order to achieve better data quality. From analysing
these z-scans, slight modifications to the THz-CCS system is discovered further
improving data quality. 45 sintered Heraeus Prexonics Silver Ink samples is then
probed scanned in 2 dimensions with the THz-CCS system, the spectrum of these
scans are modelled in two different ways using the Drude Model with the intent to
find their conductivities. Finally the p2p amplitude across the samples are analysed
and compared to their measured resistances.

Design of 3D Terahertz scanner
I wanted the 3D Terahertz scanner to be versatile in the shapes and sizes of samples
it can scan.
As a starting point, I had two rails that each can move 30cm in one dimension,
placed on top of each other at a 90 degrees in the horizontal plane which in this
text are denoted as; when viewed from the front, the x axis being the left-right
direction and the y axis being the front-back direction. These rails being controlled
by a controller unit I had no part in designing. To allow scanning in the vertical
direction, the z axis, my design has the THz-CCS system move vertically instead of
the sample, with the THz-CCS system being connected to the same type of rail.
I used the software Solidworks to design the 3D Terahertz scanner.

Measurements and Calibrations
To make a measurement with this setup, the GLAZE application is used on the PC
connected to the setup, several parameters can be varied. The number of scanning
points per pulse measurements defaults to 500 but can be varied in the range 500 −
1200. The number of pulses measured at each point can be varied, for the data
analysed in this document I had 1 pulse measurement at each point. When taking
a measurement the x, y and z position can be specified, and two of these can be
intervals, allowing to make 2D scans over any two of the axes, 1D scans over one
axes or just a scan of a single point. The step-size which is the between each scan
also has to be specified, which is the same along all axes. When taking scans over
an interval reference scans can also be included periodically during measurements,
these would typically be a scan over a highly reflective surface, in the case for my
measurements I used the aluminium top plate as my reference scans. The time to
take a full measurement over several points is proportional to the total number of
points scanned, and the number of pulses scanned at each point. A scan of a single
pulse, measuring 1200 points, i found that it takes roughly 0.6 seconds per scan

7.1
Pulse Fitting using GLAZE module
The GLAZE API for python has modules used to model the optical properties of
materials based on experimental data from the reflection of a THz pulse off of a
medium to some reference.
The sample and the reference needs to be modelled as a composite medium with
k layers. For the case of reflection, the simplest case would be k = 2 layers, which
would just model a single reflection. Each medium can be modeled in various ways,
but the only two I use is ”SimpleMedium” and ”DrudeMetal”. SimpleMedium is a
medium of refractive index that is invariant with frequency, and needs a specified
value or allowed ranges for three parameters to be defined; the real refractive index
n, and the extinction coefficient κ and the thickness of the medium d. DrudeMetal
models the material as a Drude metal, and needs a specified value or the allowed
ranges for the four parameters to be defined; The permittivity at infinite frequency
ϵ∞, the DC conductivity σDC, the damping time τ and the thickness of the medium
d. For the first and last medium, the thickness is irrelevant, as after transmission
from medium 2 to medium 1, there is no more reflections, and similarly the last

Conclusion
I designed a 3D THz scanner which after some modifications by Martin Nielsen was
manufactured. This 3D THz scanner is capable of moving 219mm in both directions
in the horizontal by using of rails, and is capable of moving an even greater distance
in the vertical direction, to accommodate materials with varying shapes, different
top plate can be mounted to fit the object being measured. When paired with the
CARMEN system and a THz-CCS scanner this resulted in a system which when
connected to a PC with the GLAZE program installed, allowed for probing materials
of various shapes with THz-CSS. I characterized the beam width, and found that it
was not symmetrical, varying between 5.7 mm at its lowest and of 7.0 mm. With
use of z-scans i calibrated for the ideal height to set the OAP to be at for different
positions in their holder, varying in heigh by up to 0.3 mm. Based on these z-scans,
modifications on the OAP were made to improve data quality, reducing noise my
removing the cover on the OAP, and placing a metal plate between the transmitter
and receiver. I characterized the the polyamide foil on which 45 different samples
of Heraeus Prexonics silver ink sintered on it, with a thickness of 0.21 mm and a
refractive index of 1.49 and an extinction coefficient of 0.03. I attempted to model
the conductivity of the sintered samples, but was unsuccessful in those attempts. I
compared the p2p of all of the square samples, and while i found that the sample
with the highest resistance had a lower p2p than the others, most of the samples
had similar p2p despite their various print parameters.
9
Discussion
Some of my initial inclusions of my design for the 3D Terahertz scanner was cut, but
there was a good justification for their removal. I am satisfied in the functionality
of the 3D Terahertz scanner. I envision a future version of the 3D Terahertz scanner
having fewer exposed bits. The OAP being opened to improve signal quality, could
perhaps be replaced with an anti-reflective coating if economically viable. I believe
that quality control in manufacturing is a great future use case for this technology,
being able to detect a higher fraction of faulty outputs from a factory could be a
great cost saver for a company along with a better reputation for consistent quality
manufacturing amongst their customer base.
When I did the Knife edge scans, I had not done the z-scans, I manually adjusted
the height of the OAP in z until I got the highest p2p I could, so I might not have
placed the OAP such that the top plate is in the focal plane of the pulse. Had I
considered this, I would have done the z-scans first as these scans are not dependent
of any information gained from the Knife edge scans. I later realized that I had
