Abstract
The project deals with a construction that consists of 5 circular towers that is partly linked
together. The highest tower is 61 m above the ground surface. Only the highest tower is con-
sidered.
This report is conducted to investigate the high-rise of Axelborg’s response to static and
dynamic/impulsive loads. The building is considered to have a complex arragement of structural
members and its response towards load combinations in SLS and ULS should be investigated.
The high-rise diﬀers from the traditional Danish buildings, as all the structural members is
casted in-situ. The investigation will include localization of structural members with highest
vulnerability and analyzation of the structural response towards loads deﬁned by norms and
standards.
The structural element will be designed in relation to the analysis made by
calculations, numerical analysis and ﬁnite element analysis.
Additionally, the building will be analyzed in relation to a predetermined blast load. It should
be noted that the knowledge and application of blast loading in Danish building design is close
to non-existence. As the late professor K. W. Johansen, once stated in his publication ≫What
do we engineers really do?≪: "The clever engineer knows that the construction might very well
collapse, even though the calculations fulﬁll all the norms and the authorities’ requirement. This
is the oﬃcial calculation. He, therefore, do his own calculation to ensure that the structure holds.”
A research will be performed in relation to knowledge retrieved from my stay in Singapore.
The research will test the structural response of a predetermined blast load for a structure that
has only been designed to undertake loads deﬁned by the norms and standards. Furthermore, a
potential proposal is provided to eliminate some of these eﬀects if necessary. From this research,
an indication of level of protection for a modern high-rise will be obtained and discussed when
subjected to a blast load, while the structure in contrary is only designed to withstand loadings
deﬁned by Eurocodes.

1.1. DESCRIPTION OF STATIC SYSTEM
1.1
Description of Static System
1.1.1
Deck
The deck in all levels have a constant depth of 280mm, made from in-situ concrete. Even though
the deck is casted in-situ, air ﬁlled plastic balls have been inserted between the reinforcements
before casting to match the property of hollow core slabs. The slab is supported directly to the
walls and columns. There are no beams transferring the surface load into line load, in which
is the traditional technic for load accumulation. Instead it is transferred to a point load at the
columns.
1.1.2
Walls
All the walls in this tower are used to the stair cases of elevator pit. The thickness very from
300mm to 200mm. A core can consist of an inner and outer wall, which is connected by the
stairs.
1.1.3
Column
The outer end of the deck is supported by columns. There exists two columns near the center
of the tower. These two columns are assumed to be placed to prevent a long deck span. It may
also contribute to load accumulation from the bridges that links to the other towers, which will
not be considered in this report.
Figure 1.1-2: Column positions
The columns surrounding the facade have varying diameters. The diameters vary from 450mm
at the bottom and gradually decline to 350mm when reaching the top. Table 1.1-2 shows the
division of ﬂoor levels based on the diameter of the columns near the facade. Since the load
from the levels passes down as axial load, which increases toward the bottom, it is unnecessary

Load Accumulation for Vertical Loads
The vertical load that is applied to the towers, includes the self weight of the structure, imposed
and snow load. Figure 1.2-3 described the vertical load accumulating though the building. The
load on the slabs is transformed through the bubble deck by shear.
The concrete slab are
reinforced in both axes. This gives an advantage comparing to hollow core slab elements, as
they usually are reinforced along the length of the deck element.
Figure 1.2-3: Load accumulation of vertical load
The deck then transfers the load on to the columns and walls by compression. The columns
support the deck directly, which will result in a transformation from surface load from the deck
to a point load by the columns. The walls and columns will accumulate load from story levels
along with their self weight through the tower till the reach of foundation.

1.3. LOAD ACCUMULATION FOR HORIZONTAL LOADS
1.3
Load Accumulation for Horizontal Loads
Horizontal loads that must be indicated in the design veriﬁcation and includes wind or horizontal
mass load. The façade will distribute the wind load, which acts as a surface load, into a linear
axial force though the deck. The loads received by the slabs will be passed on to the shear walls,
which is the three cores, and will lead the forces down to the foundation. Since the cores have
diﬀerent shape, the resistance of each core depends on its location.
Figure 1.3-4: Load accumulation of horizontal load
The slabs transfer the load to the columns. In the leeward side of the building the columns
are subjected to compression, whereas the columns in the windward side of the building are
subjected to tension. The same principle applies for the shear walls.
The behavior of the tower under the impact of horizontal load is illustrated in ﬁgure 1.3-5.
The displacement in ﬁgure 1.3-5a is the result of the of wind load acting on a the façade by
creating a ﬁctional cover in Strusoft. In the ﬁgure, the horizontal load is the only load that have
an impact on the structure. The stabilizing wall deﬂect in the same direction as the wind to
resist the horizontal load.
By not considering the vertical load, including the self weight of the structure, the columns
in the windward side will be in tension, as oppose to the column the leeward side. Columns that
are in the middle of the windward and leeward side, is subjected from little to no axial forces,
when only horizontal load is applied. These expected response from horizontal load accumula-
tion for columns is shown in ﬁgure 1.3-5b generated by Strusoft. Later, when vertical loads are
applied together with horizontal loads; the columns that were in tension, when horizontal load
is only applied, will then be less compressed than the columns in the windward side.
The stabilizing walls resists the horizontal loads.
Each core will resist the horizontal forces
depending on their location. The core nearest to the leeward side have the greatest compres-
sive axial load. For each core, the largest axial stress distribution in compression occurs at the
bottom wall perpendicular to the wind direction in the leeward side, as oppose to the largest
tension that occurs on the bottom wall perpendicular to the wind direction in the windward
side.

1.4
Robustness
A construction is robust when it has the ability to resist unintended eﬀects and their following
defects. The rule and deﬁnitions to robustness are deﬁned by [11] and [5]. Robustness is a prop-
erty where accidental loads may be used for to verify it. Robustness can also be veriﬁed by the
ductility of materials, unpredicted breakdowns or imperfections. A construction is certiﬁed as
robust, when there is no comprehensive collapse when a local carrying member is collapsed.The
safety critical part of the construction should be less sensitive toward unintended eﬀects and
their following defects. Fire, explosions, impact or consequences of human error are given ex-
amples by [5].
The requirement for robustness need to be documented for constructions that have a high conse-
quence class – CC3. Robustness will be veriﬁed by considering accidental load situations deﬁned
in chapter 2. A predetermined blast load on a wall and the eﬀect of collaps of a column will
among other things be considered. By deletion of a carrying element, the deck failure is consid-
ered acceptable if the deck area that collapses are less than 15% at each of the two superjacent
levels.

General
The high-rise is located in a highly populated area in the heart of Copenhagen, which means that
the consequence of loss of human life, economic, social and environmental if failure occurs are
tremendous. The consequences class for this building is CC3, which is high. The life expectancy

3.3. DECK
3.3
Deck
BubbleDeck is a biaxial concrete slab where plastic balls are inserted between the longitudinal
reinforcement, resulting in a lighter slab with minimum sacriﬁce of the strength. According to [1]
the advantages of BubbleDeck is apparent when it come to deformation and bending strength.
The reduction of load caused by the free vacuum that reduces the concrete area in cross-section,
results in multiple advantages for the individual static resistance.
The BubbleDeck is casted in-situ with conventional formwork. BoubbleDeck can also be pre-
stressed and can be used for all concrete quality and density, similar to a solid slab.
Since
Bobbledeck is a biaxial slab and can be directly supported by columns, the supporting beam by
edge element will become redundant. Therefore, the total thickness of ﬂoor separation becomes
shorter, and can result to additional levels for tall buildings. Furthermore, the installations in
the suspended ceiling becomes more practical, as there are no obstacle caused by transverse
beams.
Ball diameter of 225mm is recommended for a minimum slab thickness of 280mm. Using Bob-
bleDeck that consists of a balls that have a diameter of 225mm will result in a reduction of
2,39kN/m2. A surface load of 2,39kN/m2 is applied as an uplift to all slabs in Strusoft, as it
considers the slab as solid.
The shear strength is proportional to the amount of concrete, since all the concrete in the
cross-section is active. Hence, the shear capacity of BobbleDeck will be reduced by 65% com-
pared to a solid slab. Under the calculation of the shear capacity, the factor of 0,6 is used, which
includes the safety factor.
The slabs in Axelborg tower are made of BobbleDeck, as seen in Appendix B. It is informed
that BobbleDeck haven’t been used for all slabs. Since the ﬁnal drawings aren’t obtained, all
slabs for tower E is assumed to be made by BobbleDeck, as there are advantages concerning to
the resistance of blast loading.

3.3.3
Accidential actions
The impact of an accidental action in term of failure of a structural element is analyzed for the
slabs. The eﬀect of the slabs is analyzed for when column 0C.9.1 is subjected by an accidental
action that results in a collapse of the load bearing element. After the occurrence of the deﬁned
incident of the accidental action, the structural system is left with a larger span with a huge
point load at the middle caused by the overlying column. The adjacent elements need to resist
the eﬀect of the modiﬁed structural system in order to prevent further failure of the rest of
the structural system. By ensuring the local damage doesn’t spread throughout the structure,
progressive collapse can be prevented.
The column 0C.9.1 is one of the most critical column to remove, as it is a support for a deck with
a large span. When the load accumulates to the bottom of the tower, column 0C.9.1 will have
to resist an axial force larger than any other nearby columns. By a detaching the column, the
overlaying slab has to resist the accumulative load from the superjacent column, where before
was led down to the foundation by the now detached column.
The moment distribution along the slabs diﬀers from the previous structural system, as the
support conditions is changed by the detachment of column 0C.9.1. Large positive moment will
occur at the slab were the column is missing. Negative moment will occur at the slab near the
adjacent support.

3.5. STABILITY SYSTEM
3.5
Stability System
The building is considered stabile when it can withstand the horizontal forces applied by ei-
ther wind load or horizontal mass load. The stabilizing system should prevent the tower from
overturning, sliding, slipping and have acceptable stresses. Even thought, the availability of
computer-based programs that provides sophisticated analysis capability of stability system,
there still is a need for approximated analytical calculations for the structure.
The used analysis technique for the stabilizing system is an accordance with [8]. The build-
ing is analyzed by the use of cantilever method. The stabilizing shear walls are analyzed as a
cantilever standing on end ﬁxed at the ground level. The columns are assumed to resist the
overturning moment by axial compression in the leeward side and tension in the windward side.
The support conditions for the columns in superstructure can’t be considered fully ﬁxed. The
support conditions of the columns are chosen to be hinged in Strusoft FEM-Design model.
Since the stabilizing cores are not symmetrically positioned, the total deﬂection of the building
will be caused by both bending and warping. Warping caused torsional eﬀects on the building
are enhanced when the center of the twist is eccentric from the center of gravity for horizontal
mass load, or from the shear center for wind loading.

StruSoft
FEM-Design from Strusoft is a powerful and advanced tool for analysis and design of a model. It
can provide a ﬁnite element analysis and design of load bearing elements in reinforced concrete,
steel and timber with accordance with the selected Eurocode. Materials, properties and support
conditions can be deﬁned, where the load combinations can then be applied. After generation
of a suitable mesh, the design forces can be calculated. The design of the structural elements
can be executed based on the design forces. The concrete cross-section is not changed when
verifying the bearing capacity of a structural element; only additional reinforcement necessary
to sustain the deﬁned load combinations is added.
It is possible to import the 3D model from Revit Structure to Strusoft. However, the struc-
ture has been modelled all over again due to complexity and modiﬁcation of the structure. The
diameter of the cylindrical column is reduced through the height of the structure. An uplift
of 2,39kN/m2 is added to the deck to imitate BubbleDeck as much a possible. The applied
reinforcement under the design of slabs is only used for guidance and to compare with the cross
section of BobbleDeck that withstands the design loads calculated under section 3.3. However,
the walls and columns have been completely designed by Strusoft FEM-Design.
Throughout the report, the calculations done by Strusoft FEM-Design are veriﬁed, both in
matter of behavior of the structure from response of diﬀerent loads and the values obtained.
Additionally, few simple test load have been applied, which matches reaction calculated by
hand. Strusoft does provide automatic checks of the equilibrium of the analysis calculation, and
warns whether the structure is unstable. It compares the sum of all reactions with the sum of
applied loads. If the reactions diﬀer from the applied loads, an error will appear in percentage,
which gives an indication if the error should be investigated or is acceptable. The structure is
in equilibrium when the error is 0%.

Conclusion
It is concluded that propose of the report has been fulﬁlled. The bearing capacity for structural
members can resist the loads to which the building is subjected to. Strusoft have been used to
analyze the load accumulation of the tower, which consists of a complex arrangement of load
bearing members. The calculation done by the FEM program have been veriﬁed throughout the
report, both in matter of realistic behavior and force values. With the concrete dimensions given
by the architects and the reinforcement chosen based on analyzation of load combination in SLS
and ULS, the structural members can resist the applied load and have a stable structural system.
The thickness of the load bearing walls is constant through the building height, as opposed
to the columns, in which the diameter is reduced along the top. All the slab elements consist
of biaxial BobbleDeck in which yields advantages in term of a lighter slab with minimal sac-
riﬁce of the strength, and resistance of blast load as it allows the application of a ﬂat slab system.
The quantity of the explosive charge is determined by the use of a categorization based of
25.000 record throughout the world. The structure is investigated by an internal blast scenario
at ﬂoor level, which limits the distance were the charge can be released. The predetermined
blast load is set of to a charge of 20kg TNT 5,0m from a wall.
The blast load is subjected to a near by wall, which have cross-sectional properties that satisﬁes
the load combinations deﬁned by Eurocode. The bearing capacity of the wall can not suﬃciently
resist the predetermined blast load. The level of protection against blast load can’t be assumed
satisﬁed when considering the norms and standards only. An investigation for the structural
response to blast loading shall be preformed for the structural system to conclude the protection
level, even though the desired protection category is, to achieve category 2.
Modiﬁcations of the cross-sectional properties have been analyzed in relation to the blast load-
ing response. The impact of various amount of reinforcement and wall thicknesses have been
investigated and a suﬃcient design is obtained hereby. The change of the dimensions will have
an eﬀect of the wall properties, which have been analyzed to ensure various aspect is suitable
for the chosen design. The aspects that have been considered is displacement, ductility, stiﬀness
and resistance.
Huge improving can be achieved, to the structural design against blast loading, when increasing
the wall thickness. The ﬁnal design does not include a thicker wall as the architect’s desire the
supplied concrete dimensions to remain unchanged. Hence, the reinforcement ratio is chosen to
be increased to sustain the deﬁned blast scenario. For obtaining the ﬂexural limit to sustain
protection category 2 the reinforcement bars in both sides of the wall is increased from 10 to
14mm, which the resulting impact in various aspects are discussed. There is no need of stirrups
to obtain a suﬃcient shear capacity. However, an 8mm bar is needed to sustain the ultimate
shear at support. The global response from dynamic load at support created by the blast, re-
sults in breach of the bearing capacity of column C7.0.1. The bearing capacity is optimized to
suﬃciently resist the load by increasing the longitudinal reinforcement from 16 to 20mm.
