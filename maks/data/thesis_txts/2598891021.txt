Abstract 
 
The problem cause by municipal solid waste in China has raise many attentions of 
government, resident and scholar. As the development of municipal waste 
management system, the disposal of MSW has become more and more efficiency and 
intentionality. Meanwhile, China is experiencing a transformation of energy structure 
for 21th century and the node of 13th and 14th five-years-plan. So, utilizing waste for 
energy as a renewable energy development is a creative topic. An LCA model run by 
EASETECH is done to assess the GWP of MSWMS and energy production. Seven 
scenarios are constructed to find the optimized combination of alternative 
technologies.  
The energy production of the ideal scenario (E2) only occupied 4% of renewable 
energy production (2000Mtce) in 2050 as the expected by China Energy Research 
Institution. Marginal electricity with coal-based electricity, hydro-based electricity 
and wind-based electricity was conducted in the model. And when renewable energy 
is marginal electricity, CO2 eq saving will decrease. Which means, energy from waste 
have more GWP or environmental impact than other renewable energy.

1 Introduction 
(General idea of the thesis) 
The thesis is aimed to analyze the utilizing of Chinese waste for energy under 
alternative technologies based on the waste management system using life cycle 
assessment (LCA). Waste management focuses on MSW collection, transportation and 
treatments and waste will eventually provide a small part of energy that can replace the 
marginal electricity (replace coal-based energy as a target)The paper also set a scenario 
(High renewable energy penetration scenario，the stated policy scenario and the below 
2℃ scenario) that the renewable energy will be well developed and electricity rate will 
account for 60% of the end-use energy demand of the whole energy system.  
 
1.1 Energy status of China and by 2050 
China has a unique resource composition that lack of domestic crude oil and nature gas 
but rich in coal. Until 2017, compared to the average level of the world energy structure, 
China still have lager part of coal and less share of natural gas and oil while the 
percentage of renewable energy is slightly higher than the average level. Figure 1 and 
2 show the composition of the total primary energy supply of world and China by source.

1.2 MSW as renewable energy 
1.2.1 MSW status quo in China 
China had become the world’s largest waste generator since 2004 and as the progress 
of urbanization the amount of MSW will gradually increase toward a higher but steady 
level. (Bank, 2005; Energy Research Institute, 2015) 
Compared to industrialized countries, high organic and moisture content are typical 
Chinese MSW due to around 60% of kitchen waste in urban solid waste. (D. Q. Zhang, 
Tan, & Gersberg, 2010) This situation is partially attributed to the diet and lifestyle of 
Chinese which means the composition of MSW will have barely change even as the 
well-developed of the industry and the living standard as well as waste management 
system in the future. 
Domestically, around 226 million tons waste was collected and disposed annually, 
Figure 1 shows that in 2019 52% of MSW will go to landfilling and 45% of MSW will 
go incineration 45%. Other disposal methods including biotreatments and recycling are

1.2.2 MSW management system 
MSW in China is generally defined as three groups of materials: organic waste (kitchen 
waste, garden waste, etc.), non-recyclable inorganic waste (coal ash, cinder, dust, etc.), 
and recyclable waste (paper, plastics, glass, metal, etc.). (D. Q. Zhang et al., 2010) The 
principle of the waste disposal alternative is from 2019, due to the sustainable 
development strategies from the Chinese government, some municipal governments 
have already required the citizens to process mandatory waste sorting by laws but still 
the residents in other cities or suburban sort their household waste by volunteer 
principle. The recyclable materials with high value are often collected by scavengers 
and other informal collectors and the residents will be paid while they give their waste 
to door-to-door collectors or to some commercial collection points. Lastly, the rest of 
waste will be treated by the city sanitation bureau collected from the collection points 
or collection stations.  
As Figure 2 shows, household waste was partially collected by private collectors and 
other recycle companies which is hard to scrutinize. So, all the composition data of the 
household waste refer to the situation in collection point which will then be taken over 
by the city sanitation bureau. Institutional and commercial waste will be also sent to 
transfer station on their own responsibility and join the MSWM system. However, only 
household waste is considered in this study.

1.2.3 Utilization of MSW for energy 
Energy is irreplaceable to run the waste management system and meanwhile the waste 
management system may generate energy itself in terms of electricity, heat, gas or 
(Christensen, 2011) 
China has put the waste management and MSW recycling and sorting on the table and 
tried to develop waste-to-energy concept. Chinese government has intensified waste 
management by stated some policies to enhance MSW incineration treatment and 
promote MSW safety disposal as a specific plan of 13th Five-year plan.  (2016[28]) 
Also, an implementation plan for MSW sorting has launched in 2017 that 46 cities are 
required to carry out mandatory garbage sorting by the end of 2020 and some relevant 
laws are under the revise process in 2020 to promote the reduction and the sorting of 
MSW.   
Therefore, the utilizing of waste as a renewable energy to generate electricity, heat as 
well as fuel will become more feasible and efficient in the future. 37.5% of MSW in 
China goes to incineration and the total installed power-generation capacity is 5234 
MW which is around one-third of install capacity of biomass energy. (from presentation 
waste-to-energy in China) (still the information about production of heat or electricity 
is missing) 
According to CREO 2018, the capacity of biomass power grows by a factor of three to 
four times by 2025, but still only contributes with just 2.3% of the total power capacity 
in 2025. Biomass capacity growth is a main mean to realize target in the North China 
Clean Heating Plan, and the pace of biomass will be hard to be deployed as the scenario 
prediction.

2 Materials and Methods 
2.1 LCA modeling 
2.1.1 LCA 
As standardized by ISO 14040:2006 Environmental management — Life cycle 
assessment — Principles and framework, LCA is a tool that can assist in identifying 
opportunities or performance indicators to improve the environmental performance, 
supporting decision-makers to find an ideal solution for the MSWMS. It is also known 
as cradle-to-grave analysis and the backbone of the optimization of waste management 
in EU.(Christensen, 2011) According to the framework and the principle of LCA, 
several steps will be included in the study. Goal and system scope will be defined and 
an inventory of relevant inputs and outputs will be compiled, the potential impacts of 
environment associated with the inputs and output will be evaluated and all the result 
and inventory in the study will be interpreted.  
 
Goal definition 
This study intends to find out energy production and environmental impact (only GWP) 
from the MSWMS in China with alternative waste disposal technologies at present and 
in the near future in 2050. The result may produce information for decision-maker 
considering upgrading the energy structure in China in the recent published 14th five-
year plan or other energy and environment development strategy.   
 
Scope definition 
This study modelled the existent and the optimization MSWMS with alternative waste 
disposal technologies composition in China and all the selected technologies were 
introduced in this chapter. Landfill, incineration with energy recovery, anerobic 
digestion, biodiesel and bioethanol production technologies were taken into 
consideration. The energy flow and energy production or net energy output from the 
MSWMS will be revealed.  
 
Functional unit 
A generation and management of 1000 ton household waste per day in a city in China 
is set as functional unit in the study.  
 
System boundaries 
The model focus on the utilizing waste for renewable energy generation, including WtE, 
AD with biogas production, biodiesel and bioethanol production technologies. Only 
household waste which was terminally collected by the government will be sent to the

MSWMS in this study, excluding recycling waste. Energy input are electricity and fuel 
that was used in running the facilities and transportation. Energy output are heat and 
electricity production and bio-fuel production (biogas, biomethane, bioethanol and 
biodiesel). Finally, net energy output of MSWMS was accessed. 
In scenarios constructing, electricity production will have substitution of coal-based 
electricity production, hydro-based electricity production and wind-based electricity 
production in China from Ecoinvent data based.  
 
Lifecycle 
In the study, the lifecycle of the system starts at the generation of household waste as 
the sources and ends at the disposal, emission to the air or WWTP and the production 
of energy in the relevant facilities. 
 
Approach 
The system was modeled and assessed by the consequential approach as the aim of the 
study is in assessing the consequences of the change of the composition alternative 
technologies. The marginal electricity production in the system would be WtE and the 
biofuel will be assumed to replace the production and combustion of conventional 
petrol in car. 
 
Assessment Criteria 
The ‘ILCD recommended – 2013 Prosuit Global NR without LT’ life cycle impact 
assessment method was selected as the tool to assess the environmental impact.(JRC, 
2010) 
The impact categories investigated in this study include the following: GWP 100 years, 
midpoint; GWP100; IPPC2007.

2.4 Technologies 
2.4.1 Incineration (Waste to Energy) 
Incineration has become the main method of MSW disposal in China because of the 
huge demand of capacity of MSW treatment and the deprecation of landfilling. 
Compared to landfilling’s high processing ability, incineration need high investment 
and running cost as well as professional technologies support and operation. Unlike the 
landfilling that is incapable to conduct a comprehensive and efficiency energy or 
material recovery, the production of heat and electricity from waste-to-energy plants 
have increasing market and industrialization prospect in the future.(Li, Zhao, Li, & Li, 
2015) Incineration plant is an essential method to build the projection of China’s new 
energy structure as MSW is regarded as a source of renewable energy. Also, as a 
sustainable waste management technology, incineration can achieve above 90% volume 
reduction in waste and no further decomposition needed. What is more, series of 
incineration facilities have low requirement on geography and weather conditions and 
have low impact on land. In the future, despite of metropolitan areas, suburban areas 
may also benefit from the construction of incineration with lower shipping cost of MSW 
and the production of energy. (Dongliang Zhang, Huang, Xu, & Gong, 2015) 
Chinese incinerations started to develop around 2000 and started from importing 
technologies and equipment from developed countries which is expensive, so the 
majority of the construction of WTE plants in China are allocated in metropolitan areas. 
(Zheng et al., 2014) Moreover in China, the local government are still the main funding 
sources which means local economic and policies matter and constrains from local 
resident opposition because of the potential air pollution. But as the urbanization and 
technologies promotion, WTE will have a bright future.  
Common type of incineration in China are grated firing incinerations (GFI) (80%) and 
fluidized bed incinerations (FBI) (Chen & Christensen, 2010), and China also 
introduced retrofitting cement rotary kilns as MSWIs method in addition to have more 
use of the capacity of existed cement kilns but only deal with mixed MSW with 
industrial wastes. (Liu, Liu, & Li, 2006) GFIs is predominant as the advantage of no 
requirement of waste pretreatment but FBI now is more preferred as additional coal can 
be added to increase the quality of combustion and reduce air pollution (Dioxin) with 
low opportunity to have problem on operation and low maintaining fee in the condition 
of high water content in Chinese MSW. However, as the ban of auxiliary coal in China 
and the improvement of waste sorting efficiency, GFIs will be more dominating in the 
future. 
Air pollution control: Flue gas cleaning using semi-dry scrubber with slaked lime slurry 
injection to remove acid gases, activated carbon injection for absorbing dioxin followed 
by bag filter to collect APC residues including fly ash, reacted and unreactive lime and 
activate carbon commonly used technologies

2.4.3 Landfilling 
Landfilling is one of the ultimate disposal technologies which is irreplaceable in 
MSWM system with advanced alternatives even in industrial nations including 
European Union countries and the USA. And Denmark have already minimized the 
utility of landfill and no household waste is sent directly to landfill since 1980s. China 
still sends around 52% of waste to landfill in 2018, compared to Denmark. 
However, the bulk of waste that go to landfill in developing countries like China are 
organic waste, which will generate a mass of LFG and leachate. The GHG emission 
from landfills is considered a significant contributor to global warming, especially in 
landfill site with poor efficiency or no LFG collection system.  And the methane 
fraction of the LFG is the main resource, which is around 25 times GWP (100 years 
horizon) higher than carbon dioxide. Degradation of waste and generation rate of LFG 
can be found in Table 6 in the Appendix. 
Due to the increasing international concern on climate change, China now have strict 
landfilling management regulation to prevent further GHG emission and strong 
limitation of new landfill construction. So, despite of the first two scenario, landfilling 
was eliminated from the system as well as the continuous generating LFG.

2.5 Sensitivity Analysis 
All models are inevitably flaw because there is uncertainty in all our results. LCA 
modeling have assumptions and data from different source in all of the scenarios so it 
is essential to have a useful tool to examine the robustness of the result. There are three 
types of uncertainties in the model (Lloyd & Ries, 2007), a)parameter uncertainties due 
to the insufficient data, measurement errors and so on, b)scenarios uncertainties when 
subjectively constructing the scenario with assumptions and data selection and c) model 
uncertainties due to the mathematical methods involved in the LCA modelling. 
The methods in a study about quantifying uncertainty in LCA of WMS (Clavreul, 
Guyonnet, & Christensen, 2012) was adopted in this model to assess the uncertainty 
situation. 
Perturbation analysis is a useful method to assess the influence of the model attribute 
to parameter uncertainties. (Heijungs & Kleijn, 2001) It aims determine the effect of an 
arbitrary change of one parameter value at a time on the model’s consequence. Two 
type of ratios: the sensitivity coefficient is the ratio between two absolute changes and 
Sensitivity ratio is come from two relative changes. Sensitivity ratio (SR) is introduced 
in the study as it can show the how much of the change of the result with a single 
parameter variation. The calculation equation is showed below:

4 Conclusion 
The aims of the project are to study the utilizing of household waste in China and how 
the waste was transformed into energy with alternative technologies in LCA method 
with EASETECH modeling.  
In big cities in China, the MSWMS can take care of household waste from generation 
to final disposal. And future scenarios are proved to be able to generate more energy as 
well as causing lower GWP. However, the total amount of energy from household waste 
only occupied 4% of all renewable generation in 2050. The government will till provide 
relevant companies strong supporting by subsidy and policies because the MSWMS 
will not only provide energy bur also provide harmless waste disposal and other service 
for sanitation. But energy from waste still a small part of the whole energy system in 
the future even with high efficiency. 
Waste sorting efficiency is one of the key points of the energy recovery, and it was 
proved that the waste sorting efficiency have important effect on the whole system.  
WtE plant is built as a major waste disposal method in China, WtE is still growing its 
capacity and its function. 
Biogas and Biogas upgrading is the conventional biorefinery and reliable. Bioethanol 
and biodiesel will easily influence by other factors and have strong technologies 
requirement. The ideal combination is that all the technologies are used together, but in 
reality, there are still many obstacles to overcome. 
Limitation of data collection has strong influence on the model. Data reliability is very 
important when doing LCA and modeling.
