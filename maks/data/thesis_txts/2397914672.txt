ABSTRACT 
In the future renewable based energy systems, it will be more important to use the flexibility 
from multi-energy systems to handle the increasing share of renewables. One possible solution 
for improving the flexibility of multi-energy systems is to integrate electricity system with dis-
trict heating system through proper coupling components. In order to schedule and operate the 
future renewable based energy system in an economic, secure and reliable manner, it is essential 
to understand the interactions between the electricity network and heat networks. Besides, it is 
necessary to use appropriate mathematical model to describe the characteristics of district heat-
ing system, electricity network and coupling components. Based on that, an efficient tool can be 
developed to solve the energy management problem for the integrated electricity-heat system.  
The master thesis project studies the components where the electricity and heat networks inter-
act. In specific, the CHP unit (Combined Heat and Power) is analyzed and used in this project to 
connect electric and heat network. Secondly, the mathematical models for district heating sys-
tem, electricity system and coupling components are analyzed and developed in this project. 
Next, based on established models, the algorithm to solve the energy management problem of 
the integrated electricity and heating system are developed and discussed. Furthermore, an op-
timal dispatch of a single time unit for the integrated electricity-heating system will be devel-
oped. Case studies will be conducted to test the efficiency of the developed algorithm 
The primary tasks of this master project can be summarized as follow. First of all: develop 
models for the overall integrated electricity-heat system. Secondly, develop an algorithm based 
on Newton Raphson method to solve the energy management of the integrated electricity-heat 
system. Meanwhile, the algorithm is supposed to properly deal with the uncertainty of flow 
direction in pipelines during Newton Raphson iterations and make the correction accordingly. 
Thirdly, develop an optimal dispatch method for the integrated electricity-heating system. The 
optimal dispatch takes the constraints from both electricity network and district heating network 
into consideration and gives the general optimal plan. Finally, carry out case studies and analyze 
the results.

INTRODUCTION 
In this chapter, the background and necessity of analyzing integrated electricity-heat 
energy system will be introduced in section 1.1 ad 1.2. In section 1.3, the problems re-
garding to the modelling and analysis of integrated electricity-heat energy system are

1.5, the literature review will briefly introduce and discuss the relevant theories and 
work contributed by others regarding the analysis of district heating system and electric-

1.1 Background 
Nowadays, the greenhouse gas emission causing global warming primarily comes from 
the consumption of conventional energy, including the burning of coal, natural gas, and 
oil for generating electric power and heat power. To deal with this problem, it is more 
and more obvious that integrating renewable energy sources within the current energy 
system is necessary. Currently, wind energy is the most advanced renewable energy 
source compared with other forms of renewable energy. Considering the characteristics 
of wind energy, however, the unpredictability and uncertainty of wind energy causes 
problem to the current energy system. Especially, with the integration of wind power in 
large scale in the power system, the fluctuation of the wind leads to the instability of the 
power system.  Meanwhile, it is difficult for wind power to precisely follow the chang-
ing pattern of load profile. To compensate the fluctuation caused by wind, it is neces-
sary to develop a flexible energy supply system which is capable of accommodating 
intermittent renewables like wind energy [1].  
The energy supply system normally can be considered as individual sub-system with 
combination of several separate energy vectors. Apart from electricity, heat is another 
primary energy form that gives rise to greenhouse gas emissions. Currently, electricity, 
as the most reliable and economic energy, takes an essential role in the development of 
smart grid. While other energy systems, still have limited consideration due to certain 
reasons. However, one idea for the future smart grid development is that these different

1.2 Ideas of Integrating District Heating System and Electricity 
System 
One possible solution for the integration of different energy systems is to combine dis-
trict heating system with electricity network through certain coupling components. The 
energy is able to be supplied for both heating and electric network if the balancing of 
the system required. These two independent systems are linked together through cou-
pling components such as Combine Heat and Power unit (CHP unit), electric boilers and 
heat pumps [2]. The coupling components allow the coordination of two energy net-
works for energy storage and the utilization of district renewables. The flexibility of the 
combined system for handling fluctuation of renewables can be done by optimizing 
electricity charging and discharging in coupling components. The more coupling com-
ponents deployed in the system, the tighter the interconnection between electric and 
heating network [3].   
The electric network in the study of integrated energy system usually refer to as distri-
bution network, as presented in figure 1.1. Different from transmission network, which 
transfers large amount of power from large generators to load center with high voltage 
level, distribution network normally has much lower voltage level and more branches 
covering the load points spreading among given area. It is usually equipped with step-
down transformers delivering power from high-voltage side (transmission network) to 
low-voltage side (distribution network) or connecting to several sub-circuits, and step-
down transformers delivering power to the final users (electric devices, illuminating, 
etc.). The structure of distribution network can be divided into two type. One is radial 
and another is network. For radial model, each load point is supported by one source 
and the structure of system is more like a tree. While for network model, multiple 
sources deployed and operated in parallel, which increase the security of the system [4].

Figure 1.2 shows the diagram of district heating network and pipelines. As another form 
of energy system, it usually consist of heat source producing heat power, supply pipes 
that carrying heat power in the form of water or steam to the load points, and return 
pipes carrying mass flow from loads return to the source. In details, the water or steam 
flow through supply pipe with temperature of 70 oC to 120 oC while back to the source 
through return pipe with temperature of 30 oC to 70 oC after heat energy extracted from 
load points [5]. At each load point, water or steam will carry the heat energy and trans-
fer it to traditional heating systems inside the building by heat exchanger. Considering 
the development of energy system nowadays however, district heating network is not 
widely applied to the current energy network. The reasons come from many aspect. One 
is the high cost of providing heat energy by district heating network when comparing 
with other approaches. Another reason is that district heating network is in a sense long-
term investment, it will take long time before the network actually gain benefits from its 
operation. Finally, there is no mature mechanism to regulate the market of district heat-
ing system. In general, there are still large room for the development of district heating 
system.

In conclusion, it is necessary to analyze the integration of district heating network and 
electric network as a complete energy system. The integrated system equipped with 
CHP units is able to reduce greenhouse gas emission compared with other conventional 
energy source, increase energy efficiency and the share of renewable energy sources. 
The coupling component between these two individual systems is the key element for 
the implementation of integrated electricity-heating system [6]. It allows energy flows 
through both networks with adequate flexibility and balance the energy from renewa-
bles for improving stability of the system. In the following chapters of this thesis, the 
modeling of district heating network and electric network are described and analyzed. 
By selecting the types of coupling components, the connection of these two network is 
build. Next, throughout Matlab programming, a complete combined heat-electric energy 
network is established with multiple scenarios and the results of steady state energy 
flow for the combined system are calculated and analyzed. Finally, the optimal dispatch 
of integrated system based on established models will be given. 
1.3 Project Tasks 
The objective of this thesis project is to investigate the components where the electricity 
and heat networks interact, develop models of the interacting components and the whole 
integrated systems, and develop an algorithm to solve the energy management problem 
of the integrated electric-heating system. Furthermore, an optimal dispatch method for a 
single time section of the integrated system will be developed. To verify the validity of 
the developed mathematical model and algorithm, a simple district heating network with 
3 nodes, 3 pipelines and 1 heat source will be studied. Next, a complex heating network 
with 32 nodes, 32 pipelines and 3 heat sources will be tested. The steady state heat pow-
er flow, hydraulic and temperature profile of these two heating network will be calculat-
ed. Next an electricity network with 9-buses with be integrated with the previous com-

1.4 Methodology and Scope  
To study the district heating system and electricity system in a proper way, mathemati-
cal modelling is used to describe the energy flow in district heating system, coupling 
components and electricity system. Hydraulic model and thermal model are applied to 
heating system for connecting heat energy with mass flow delivered through pipelines 
and temperature distribution among nodes. Electric power flow model is used to study 
the power flow of electricity network. The algorithm for solving energy flow of inte-
grated system is based on Newton Raphson method. These models and algorithm with 
be tested on a simple network with assumed data. Afterwards, a case study with real 
network data will be conducted through Matlab simulation and the results will be rec-
orded and analysed. Based on established models and algorithm, the objective functions 
and relevant constraints of integrated electricity-heat system are studied. Finally a case 
study with an example system is conducted through Matlab Yalmip for validating the 
optimal dispatch method of the integrated system.  
In the modelling and analysis of electricity-heating system, the steady state energy flow 
is taken into consideration. No transient states involved. It is assumed that both district 
heating system and electricity system have fast response time and reach steady state 
quickly. For district heating system, the hydraulic status in return pipes is considered 
identical to that of supply pipes but with opposite flow direction. There is no water loss 
in the pipe network. The pipelines in heating system can be connected in different ways. 
The algorithm developed in this project addresses one of the typical connections in pipe 
network. But it is adaptable to other forms of pipe connections after modification. 
Meanwhile, this project only studies the pipe network in system level. The indoor pipe-
lines from customer sides are not taken into consideration. For coupling components in 
this project, only CHP unit with gas turbine, steam turbine and reciprocating engine are 
investigated and applied on case studies. The internal mechanism of CHP unit is not

1.5 Literature Review 
Several published papers and books provide theoretical basis of this project. Remarka-
bly, the concepts of modelling district heating system and the solution of integrated 
electricity-heating system are worth mentioning.  
Reference [7] demonstrated the basic principles of analysing pipe network. With known 
physical features of pipe network, including the diameter, length, roughness of pipelines, 
the analysis intends to determine the flow injection or discharge at nodes and pipe pres-
sure drop for designing purpose. Based on the assumptions and focus areas stated in the 
context, reference [7] investigates the physical structure of pipe network.  For the solu-
tion of network analysis problem, reference [7] gives three different systems of equa-
tions according to the focus areas of study. One of the equation system, called Q-
equations is adopted in this thesis. Based on Q-equations, the physical structure of pipe 
network is represented through incidence matrixes. Relevant equations demonstrating 
the relations of mass flow and pipe pressure drop are written.  
Reference [9] illustrates the approaches of solving steady state energy flow of electrici-
ty-heating system. In the modelling of district heating system, reference [10] uses hy-
draulic model and thermal model to obtain mass flow and temperature characteristics of 
heating system. The derivation of thermal model, namely, the expression of supply and 
return temperature at each node, relies on the pre-calculation of hydraulic model. In 
other words, hydraulic model need to be calculated separately before proceeding to 
thermal model in order to obtain the actual flow conditions of heating network. Such 
approach increases the complexity of calculation when the pipe network is in complex 
structure or conducting dynamic analysis. For the integration of electricity and heating 
system, reference [10] demonstrates that the system can be operated at either grid-
connected mode or islanded mode. According to the operation modes, reference [10] 
gives two calculation techniques for the solution of integrated energy flow. One is hy-
draulic-thermal-electricity decomposed method. Another approach is hydraulic-thermal-
electricity integrated method. These methods are implemented on the system of Barry 
island case study.

2.1 Overview of District Heating System Models 
The goal of pipe network in district heating system is to deliver heat energy from heat 
source to the consumers. It includes supply pipes delivering heat power in the form of 
hot water or steam to load points and return pipes carrying mass flow back to heat 
source. Before establishing proper models to analyze the operation of district heating 
network, there are several basic principles of fluid mechanics need to be illustrated. 
They are first: conservation of mass, or the continuity principle, second: the work-
energy principle, third: the relation between fluid friction and energy dissipation [7].

In general, the steady state energy flow of heating system can be obtained by solving 
two models, one is hydraulic model and another is thermal model [9]. The aim of hy-
draulic model is to solve mass flow rates within each pipe and the injection or discharge 
at each node. The process of modeling district heating network is similar to that of elec-
tric network. The hot water, or steam flows through pipes can be considered as electric 
current go through the transmission line. Similarly, the mass flow injected or discharged 
at each node can be seen as current injected or discharged at each busbar. The pressure 
drop along pipes is similar to the voltage drop along transmission lines.  
The analysis of hydraulic model follows three basic rules, which are continuity of flow, 
loop pressure equation and head loss equation. While the thermal model is used to es-
tablish the temperature profile of the overall heating network, including the temperature 
of mass flow delivered to each node or after flow mixture, within supply pipe and return

==PAGE:18==

pipe network. In this model, temperature mix equation applied on nodes and tempera-
ture drop equation along pipe are used.  
For the solution of hydraulic and thermal model, a common approach is that hydraulic 
and thermal model are solved in sequence. In this approach, hydraulic model will be 
solve through Hardy-cross method or Newton Raphson method first and thermal model 
can be solved linearly afterwards [9]. The results obtained by hydraulic model will be 
input data for solving thermal model. Another approach is to integrate hydraulic and 
thermal models together as one complete set of equations and solve the equation set 
iteratively. In this project, Newton Raphson method is selected for these two approaches.  
In the following sections, the procedures of establishing hydraulic model and thermal 
model are introduced. Based on the existed models, a new model for representing tem-
perature characteristics of heating system is developed. Two case studies are presented 
for validating the established network models and solving heat network steady state 
energy flow through Newton Raphson method.

2.3 Thermal Model 
The thermal model is used to obtain the temperature profile of the overall district heat-
ing system. Such profile suggests that the mass flow delivered to the customers must 
maintain proper temperature in order to satisfy the energy demand. After heat energy 
extracted, the mass flow will return to the heat sources and heated again. This process is 
bounded with hydraulic condition demonstrated in section 2.2. Particularly, the quantity 
and direction of mass flow delivered through pipelines will determined how the temper-
ature distributed along pipelines and nodes. The temperature of mass flow will vary due 
to the reasons that: first, part of the heat energy will radiate to the ambient environment 
as energy losses along pipes. Second, multiple incoming pipes connected to a node will 
cause the mixture of mass flow and temperatures. Therefore, it is obvious that the hy-

2.3.2 
Formulation of Thermal Model 
As demonstrated in previous section, the establishment of thermal model is bounded 
with hydraulic characteristics of the network. Remarkably, the mass flow rate direction 
is the basis of forming thermal model through mathematical approach. The hydraulic 
model accepts self-defined flow direction and still valid even if the defined flow direc-
tion against the actual direction. While the formulation of thermal model must be based 
on the actual flow direction in that the temperature mix equation requires the actual in-
flow and outflow.  
Reference [10] [13] demonstrated one approach to formulate thermal model. The idea is 
that the formulation of temperature drop and mix equation are based on the results cal-
culated from hydraulic model. In other words, the hydraulic model will be formed and 
solved in the first place as pre-calculation. Once the mass flow rate and their actual di-
rection obtained, thermal model can be formulated accordingly by defining supply and 
return temperature at each node. Through this approach, the solution of thermal model 
can be seen as linear since all the pipe mass flow rate рқ‘ҡрқ‘ҡ and nodal mass flow rate рқ‘ҷрқ‘ҷ are 
already calculated and the flow directions are obtained.  
If the initial guess on flow direction agree with the actual direction, there is no need to 
correct temperature equations. The initial guess against the actual direction, however, 
the temperature mix equations need to be rewritten accordingly since the inflow and 
outflow at certain nodes are changed. This approach is not efficient when the pipe net-
work is extended to large scale. Because it would take huge efforts to correct the tem-
perature equations once the assumed flow direction against the actual direction.  
Instead of formulating thermal model based on hydraulic pre-calculation, another ap-
proach is to define pipe ending temperatures and solve hydraulic, thermal models to-
gether by Newton Raphson method. Through this approach, it is supposed that the 
thermal model is still valid even if the initial guess is wrong. The models should be able 
to detect the faulty flow direction and make correction accordingly. For each pipe, the 
temperature of flow is defined at both starting point and ending point. It is already de-
fined that in supply pipes, based on the initial guess of flow direction, the flow tempera-
ture at sending end and receiving end of pipe рқ‘—рқ‘— are denoted as рқ‘Үрқ‘Үрқ‘—рқ‘—

grated together as one complete mismatch equation set and solved iteratively. The de-

becomes available and the energy can be transformed in two different networks. The 
steady state energy flow of the integrated system is obtained through Newton Raphson 
method in this project. The integrated heat-electricity systems can be operated either in 
grid-connected mode or island mode. In grid-connected mode, the integrated energy 
system is connected to the external grid. The energy balancing problem of the system 
will be finally resolved by the interaction with the external gird. For island mode, on the 
other hand, the integrated system is isolated to the external sources and the energy flow 
should be balanced within the system.  
Section 3.1 introduces the power flow model in electricity system. Section 3.2 discusses 
the types of coupling components available for the integrated energy system. In section 
3.3, the integration of DH system and electricity system is investigated. Meanwhile, the 
operational modes and algorithms for solving energy flow of the integrated system are 
demonstrated. Finally, case studies are given in section 3.4 presenting the implementa-
tion of the established integrated system models and algorithms. The steady state energy 
flow of electricity-heating system is calculated. 
3.1 Modelling of Electricity System 
3.1.1 
Overview of Electricity Power Flow Model 
Load flow is the basic tool for the analysis of power system. The aim of load flow cal-
culation is to find out the steady state power flow in the given system [4]. In this project, 
the Newton-Raphson method is used for the solution of both district heating network 
49 
==PAGE:50==
Chapter 3 
and electric network calculation. For Newton-Raphson method applied on electric net-
work, the unknown variables in the calculation include the voltage magnitude |рқ‘үрқ‘ү|on 
each busbar, the voltage angle of busbar рқңғрқңғ, the active power рқ‘ғрқ‘ғ and reactive power рқ‘„рқ‘„. 
The active power and reactive power include the power injected or discharged at busbar 
and the power flow along the transmission line. What is more, the modeling of electric 
power network also requires the impedance of the transmission line between two buses. 
After the system model established, the Newton-Raphson method can be applied to 
solve mismatch functions which correspond to unbalance of power in the system.

3.3 Integration and Solution of Electricity-Heat Energy system 
With CHP unit producing both heat power and electric power, the electricity network 
and district heating network can be connected together and the interaction of energy 
flow between these two networks is available. Remarkably, the heat to power ratio of 
these CHP units is considered adjustable and such characteristics increases the flexibil-
ity of the energy systems for implementing the large integration of intermittent renewa-
ble energy.  
Once the electricity network and district heating network are coupled, the integrated 
energy system can be operated under different modes depending on specific circum-
stances. In general, the integrated energy system can be operated under grid-connected 
mode or islanded mode [10]. For grid connected mode, the slack busbar in electricity 
network is connected with an external grid, which is considered as an infinite power 
source to balance the energy flow of integrated system. For islanded mode, on the other 
hand, the integrated system is isolated from the external gird. The energy flow can only 
be balanced within the system itself. These two operation modes require different algo-
rithms to find out the solution of steady state energy flow. The following sections fur-
ther demonstrate these two operation modes and the corresponding solutions.

For islanded mode, on the other hand, the electricity slack bus is coupled with a heat

isolated system. That requires hydraulic-thermal model and electric power flow model 
together, as one complete model, be calculated multiple times until the stop criterion 
satisfied. The energy balancing problem will be resolved by dispatching the heat and 
electricity output among CHP units in the system. Given such characteristics, the algo-
rithm of grid-connected mode can be considered as a special case of islanded mode 
where the energy balancing task is handed by external gird.

4.1 Introduction 
The economic dispatch of power system considers the issue of how to distribute the 
power output among the operating generation units so that the power demand from cus-
tomers can be covered with minimum fuel cost. The classic approach to formulate the 
optimization problem of power system is to proximate the cost of power plants with 
piece-wise linear or quadratic cost function. The balance of power supply and demand, 
power generation limits are set as equality and inequality constraints. The optimum dis-
tribution among generation units follows the principle pf equal marginal cost criterion. 
Namely, the solution of optimal dispatch based on this criterion is to find out the partic-
ular value of the marginal cost such that the corresponding electricity output of power 
plants matches the total demand [27].  
The integration of electricity-heating system combines multiple forms of energy flow. 
The energy demand includes both heat power and electricity power. The co-generation 
units, CHP units in this project, enable the interaction between heat power and electrici-
ty. The generation of either electricity or heat power is dependent to the conditions of 
another energy system. Meanwhile, the energy flow of the system need to be subjected 
to characteristics of both heating system and electricity system. Such mutual dependen-
cy and multi-energy demand introduce more complications to the optimization of inte-
grated electricity-heating system. Equation Chapter 4 Section 1 
In this chapter, the idea of optimal dispatch for integrated energy system is introduced. 
Based on established mathematical models for components in electricity-heating energy 
system, the optimization problem for a single time unit is formulated and discussed. The 
objective function, associating with all necessary constraints are presented. To test the 
availability of the optimum model, a case study is conducted. The results of optimal 
dispatch are recorded and discussed. In the case study, the heating network is formed by 
5 nodes, 5 pipelines. The electricity network consists of 4 buses. These two network are 
supported by two CHP units and an external grid.

ty-heating models into system constraints, and introducing decision variables to model 
flow direction, it is possible to optimize generation cost of CHP units taking the elec-
tricity-heating characteristics into consideration.

CONCLUSION 
5.1 Conclusion  
The purpose of investigating the integration of electricity system and district heating 
system is to achieve high efficiency of energy system. The coupling components, such 
as CHP units, heat pump and electric boiler, are able to create the linkage and improve 
the interaction between two different energy systems. Such integrated energy system 
with higher energy efficiency and flexibility will be able to deal with varying operation 
scenarios and the increasing share of renewable energy sources in the future. The inte-
gration of electricity system and district heating system is based on mathematical mod-
els describing energy flows and coupling component within the system.  
In this project, the energy flow of heating system and electricity system are modelled 
through mathematical approaches. For district heating system, hydraulic model and 
thermal model are built for obtaining mass flow profile and temperature profile, to 
which the heat power flow is related. The electricity power flow model, as a common 
approach to study electricity system, is applied for obtaining power flow of electricity 
system. For coupling components, CHP unit is selected to connect these two energy 
systems together. The heat to power ratio is investigated depending on the types of CHP 
unit. The integrated system can be operated either with the connection of external grid 
or independently. With established system models, the solution of electricity-heating 
system is based on Newton Raphson method. Finally, an optimal dispatch method con-
sidering both electricity system and district heating system is developed.  
5.1.1 
System Models 
Chapter 2 demonstrates hydraulic model and thermal model. These two models are es-
tablished for investigating the performance of district heating system. By apply continu-
ity principle, work-energy principle and energy dissipation, hydraulic model is able to 
describe the overview of mass flow profile of heating system. The mass flow within 
pipelines, discharge or injection at node and pressure drop along pipelines can be ob-
tained. The purpose of thermal model is to establish the overview of temperature profile 
85 
==PAGE:86==
Chapter 5 
of heating system. Such temperature profile includes the flow temperature at sending 
end and receiving end of pipelines, the temperature delivered to the customers, and the 
temperature in return pipes after flow mixture. Newton-Raphson method is applied on 
the solution of hydraulic-thermal model. The calculation is based on the assumption that 
the flow temperature leaves the heat sources are known and constant. Meanwhile, the 
outlet temperature at load and heat power consumption are considered known.  Conven-
tionally, due to the fact that the derivation of thermal model must rely on the actual hy-
draulic condition, namely, the actual flow direction within pipelines. Hydraulic model 
should be calculated first, and then thermal model will be solved separately. In this the-
sis, however, by defining pipe ending temperature and complementary equations, hy-
draulic-thermal model is able to automatically detect the faulty flow direction and make 
the correction accordingly by changing the relevant coefficient value during the itera-
tion process. These two models can be solved at the same time without separation. The 
case studies in chapter 2 and 3 show that even if the initial guess of flow direction is 
wrong, the calculation still be able to yield the correct results. But one thing should be 
noticed is that it might have convergent problem when calculating hydraulic model and 
thermal model together, since pipe ending temperature introduced large number of vari-
ables. Such convergent problem can be resolved by reducing the step of iteration. The 
results are converged finally after 14 iterations. In chapter 3, electricity power flow 
model is illustrated for obtaining the active power and reactive power flow of the net-
work. Through CHP units, the relationship between the generation of electricity power 
and heat power is established.  
5.1.2 
Solution of Electricity-Heating System 
Based on models built for the components of integrated energy system, the electric-heat 
energy flow can be solved depending on the operation modes. The system can be oper-
ated with either grid-connected mode or islanded mode. These two modes require hy-
draulic-thermal model and electricity power flow model be solved in sequence. Namely, 
the hydraulic-thermal model will be solves through Newton Raphson method in the first 
place. The heat power calculated at heat slack node will be transferred to the electricity 
generated on corresponding electric busbar as a known variable, through CHP unit. The 
electricity power flow model will be solved afterwards. The difference between calcula-
tion of grid-connected mode and islanded mode is that hydraulic-thermal model and 
electric power flow model only need to be solved once in grid connected mode. The 
electric power calculated on slack bus will be compensated by the external grid. While 
for islanded mode, the hydraulic-thermal model and electric power flow model, as one 
complete process, need to be solved iteratively until the stop criterion reached. Given 
that condition, the calculation of grid-connected mode can be seen as the special case of 
islanded mode. The case study given in chapter 3 performs the calculation of islanded 
mode for Barry Island electricity-heating system. Since the system is disconnected from 
86 
==PAGE:87==
Chapter 5 
external grid, the case study successfully examines how the heat demand and electricity 
demand in a self-sufficient system are satisfied through CHP units. By performing cal-
culation in the sequence of hydraulic-thermal model and electricity power flow model, 
the results finally converged after 6 iterations. The algorithm for solving steady state 
energy flow of integrated electricity-heating system can be applied on the design and 
operation of energy system. It will be able to increase the efficiency of energy manage-
ment issues and provide path for the integration of intermittent renewable energy 
sources in large scale.  
5.1.3 
Optimal Dispatch 
Chapter 4 discusses the optimal dispatch for integrated energy system based on estab-
lished hydraulic-thermal model and electricity power flow model. The commonly seen 
approach to perform optimal dispatch for electricity system is based on equal incremen-
tal cost criterion and solve Lagrange function. In this thesis, the optimal dispatch of in-
tegrated system is achieved through Matlab Yalmip toolbox. The cost function of CHP 
units and external grid are written. The objective function is to sum the generation cost 
of CHP units and external grid. It is subject to hydraulic-thermal constraints and electric 
power flow constraints. One difficulty in the optimization of electricity-heating system 
is the determination of optimal mass flow direction within pipelines. Newton Raphson 
method allows thermal model to be self-corrected during iterations. While in optimal 
dispatch, the decision variable is introduced to represent the sending end and receiving 
end of pipes, which on the other hands, determines the actual inflow and outflow at each 
node. Through Yalmip toolbox, the optimization is able to yield optimal flow direction 
depending on the system conditions and gives optimal output of CHP units and power 
exchange with external grid.  
5.2 Contribution and Future Work 
In this thesis, the models for describing characteristics of district heating system, elec-
tricity system and coupling components are developed based on existed theories and 
principles. To address the uncertainty of flow direction in heating pipe network, pipe 
ending temperature and complementary equation are defined, which enable hydraulic 
model and thermal model to be calculated at the same time. It is no necessary to specify 
the correct flow direction at the very beginning. With established models, the steady 
state energy flow of electricity-heating system is successfully solved and case studies 
are conducted for verifying the validity of developed algorithm. Next, a method for op-
timizing energy flow of integrated energy system is developed and implemented on an 
example system.  
87 
==PAGE:88==
Chapter 5 
For steady state energy flow analysis, the case studies show that the finally results will 
not be affected by the initial guess of flow direction. However, due to the large number 
of state variables and the interaction between hydraulic model and thermal model, the 
convergent problem of calculation need to be handled carefully. Meanwhile, the hydrau-
lic-thermal model and electric power flow model are solved in sequence and separately. 
It is possible to integrate district heating models and electricity models as one equation
