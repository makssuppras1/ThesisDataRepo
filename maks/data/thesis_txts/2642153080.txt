Abstract
Construction and building industry is one of the most laborious and manual task
intensive industries with a possibility of heavy automation. The redundant manual tasks
done by the workers at the construction site can be automated with mobile robots that
can collaborate with human workers. While many factors contribute to the robustness
of operation of such mobile robots at site, intelligent perception can be said to be a
crucial one of them as it identifies its adversities in a challenging and ever-changing
environment. The computer vision task of Object detection, aided by deep learning
based algorithms, have achieved state of the art performance accuracy and speed. This
project comprise of a subset of development done on Collaborative construction site
robot for assistive logistic tasks (CoCoBot) project lead by Teknologisk Institut,
Robotteknologi, and supported by Danmarks Tekniske Universitet and LED iBond A/S.
In this project, a holistic approach is taken to perform the task of object detection
for a mobile robot which is supposed to collaborate and aid humans at logistic task of
carrying long beams from one place to another. Since the prospective model is a deep
learning algorithm, a fresh dataset is created that is made of sensor images from real
danish construction sites and web images of resembling domain, capturing instances of
objects of interest. With survey, four classes of objects are determined for detection task;
human worker, ladders, wooden pallets and delineators. The images containing the four
classes are is cleaned, annotated ,analysed ,and finally published over an open platform
as a complete dataset.
The state of the art models that are changing the global scene of object detection are
identified; variants of single stage object detectors Yolov3 and Yolov4. The mentioned
model pool is trained on the multiple variation of published dataset and tested for their
ability to handle unknown data. Experimentation done on the models by training it
with different variations of dataset reveals the best performing models and impact of
data on generalization skill of these models. Best models are then implemented in the
ROS software framework of the mobile robot to do real time detection leading to the
development of a self contained object detection system for collaborative robot.

CHAPTER 1
Introduction
With the advent of AI, a great amount of change has been taking place in
multiple walks of life. Construction industry is one of the prime industries of the world
which is essential for any economy. Despite its developmental, economical significance
in the human society, it is still one of the last movers to the contemporary technological
advancement. AI and Robotics are classified as two of the most disrupting technologies
of the future. The role of AI is discussed in almost all walks of life where it can play the
role of a game changer; medical diagnosis, food and health tech, businesses like retail
and services. Similarly Robotics has been at play from past few decades where it is
slowly moving towards making robots which can perform tasks with more robustness,
agility and dexterity. Bringing the collective learning of existing technology and ground
breaking performance of new AI models to the robust mechanical capabilities of modern
robotics can change the face of provincial industry construction and building. Plenty of
tasks still done in the construction industry even today is repetitive and requires manual
work by human workers. These manual work done by humans can lead to sever health
issues for workers and is unsustainable. In a literature survey conducted by Ding Liu
et al 2020 [20], Musculoskeletal disorders (also known as MSDs) can be directly inked
to the workers participating in the repetitive tasks of manual heavy logistic lifting and
maneuvering.
Within the project, an image dataset is made to develop a deep learning based object
detection model for a mobile robot platform. This model has the ability to identify four
basic classes of objects that are found at construction sites. The work discusses multiple
tasks and challenges that are faced in the task of training an object detection model and
how data effects the performance of such model. The acquisition of data to making a
dataset and training model to on that dataset is another task undertaken in this project.
Construction sites are quite diﬀicult to operate due to the operational complexities. Real
time detection in such situation would require a fast detection model with good precision.
The object detection model trained on different variations of the dataset are tested for
their generalization skill and speed. The final implementation of selected models are
done on a software framework and tested for its speed of operation.

Building a basic software framework to work with an image sensor is the the

2.2
Theoretical Background
2.2.1
ROS
Robotic Operating system is an open source meta operating system that is used for
running most of the contemporary robots. It provides facilities like any other operating
system that are essential for an application to work [24]. ROS in itself is not a stand
alone operating system, instead it can be termed as an added functionality application
with additional libraries and packages that help build custom applications for robots.
The installation of ROS depends on the underlying operating system.
Hence, there
are different ROS distributions which are suitable to be integrated with different OS
available in the industry.
For example.
if one uses Ubuntu 18, corresponding ROS
distribution is ROS-Melodic, while if you use Ubuntu 20 or higher, ROS-Noetic is the
most appropriate distribution to use. Basic terminologies of ROS are explained below.
==PAGE:16==
6
2 System configuration on Robot Operating System (ROS)
Figure 2.1: ROS Functionalities in a glance
The basic ROS functionalities are shown in figure 2.1 which was taken from the
online resource [13].
Packages
All ROS sftwares are organized as ROS packages. These packages are main-
tained in a ROS repository and can be installed by apt-get install command on the
command line.
ROS Master
ROS Master is the core program of ROS framework that allows different
independent programs to communicate with each other. The basic functionality of ROS
ensures that different programs run independently (also called nodes). These program or
nodes are managed by ROS Master, where different programs can communicate as per
requirement. the command roscore initalizes a master and starts the communication
channel. roslaunch can start a specific program and initialize a ROS master if no other
master is running.
Nodes
In ROS when a program is run that uses ROS it creates an an instance of
that program which is called a Node.
These nodes can be of different programs or
same program too. As an example one can run the program image_view that starts a
software application that can visualize image data. on running image view an instance
of the application is created. At the same time another ROS node can be created by
running the same application from a different terminal and visualize another image file.
Topics and Messages
In ROS environment, multiple nodes interact with each other
with data called messages. Thes messages are time stamped data with specific data
structures. The messages are organized in several different topics. These topics are
basically structured in a way of data channels. On each topic, messages are streamed or
other nodes can be configured to stream their own messages.
==PAGE:17==
2.3 System architecture
7
When a specific node is streaming a message service containing data over a topic,
it is called a Publishing node. Whereas, when a node is listening to a topic and com-
prehending the data in form of messages, it is called a Subscribing node. The idea of
publishing and subscribing to topics is a vital part of ROS system where developers

3.1
Theoretical Background
In an effort to reinforce our understanding of this chapter a related theoretical back-

3.1.1
Dataset for Machine Learning
Datasets play crucial role in the tasks of Machine Learning as it is the main factors
on which learning of the model depends. A few practical aspects of dataset and its
utilization is shared below.
Image Dataset
For many computer vision task, it is apparent that some data has to
be fed to the machine learning algorithm or the model in order to make the algorithm
learn its feature and make future prediction. Image Datasets are basically indexed images
with labels marking the instances of objects on the images. Image dataset like Microsoft
common object in context dataset (MSCOCO) [19] feature a huge 330K images images
with 80 object categories.
Train, Validation and Test split
In order to train any Machine learning algorithm a
part of the data has to fed to the algorithm and some part of data is held back for testing
==PAGE:20==
10
3 Image Capture And Dataset Creation
the performance of the model. In context of AI Neural networks where algorithms are
basically Neural network models, Training set is used to train the network and fit the
parameters of the network.
Validation set is basically a part of the dataset used to evaluate the performance of
the model trained on the training set. This performance evaluated is used to tune the
hyperparameters of the network and develop the algorithm further. Thus validation set
is also called ”development set” or ”dev set”. Usually the Validation set is already a part
of the original dataset which is kept off during training, in such scenario it is called a
”Hold out validation set”.
When an model is completely trained and all hyper parameters are tuned to an
acceptable extent, it needs to be evaluated in an unbiased manner on a completely new
or unseen data. This is done to see how good is the model to generalize on the new
data. This generalization ability is a true metric of the performance of the model which
showcases the ability of the model to adapt to new or unseen data and still perform as
expected by truly intelligent agent.
When using a dataset of any kind, there has to be a split made in the dataset to get
training set or and a validation set. As a practical matter this stays a decision of the
analyst to perform, as an industry standard a usual split of training:validation ranges
from 80:20 to 95:5 depending on the amount of data present. Also the split is made
random to make the training and evaluation unbiased. It is also seen in industry that
the split is made in three proportion when test data is not available as a separate test
set. then the split proportion is an absolute choice of the analyst based on amount of
data and application.
3.1.2
Image Annotation
Image annotation is a human facilitated task of labeling data for training. The class
instances that occur in the images are marked with suitable annotation techniques. For
classification task it can be just naming it by the label of the class where as in tasks
of object detection, one has to mark the region of the image where the class instance is
present along with the label.
Based on application and objective of Detection, there are different types of anno-
tation techniques available. These techniques are used to label the present objects in
different ways: boundary based, pixel wise segment, 3D vortex encapsulation, etc. Ba-
sic Annotation techniques are described below (The explanation below is inspired by

• Tagging : In image classification tasks, it is required to mark if the object is
present in the images or not, this can be done my just tagging the image with
class label in a text file or similar data file.

3.3
Selection of Classes of Objects Detection
The construction site environment is dynamic and the presence of objects in the scenarios
are very transient. After observing few construction site, multiple objects classes were
found to reoccur in frames. Out of these reoccurring object class objects there were few
objects that were chosen to be the classes for classification. each of these objects have a
specific reason of selection and challenges to the network for detection.
There are in total four classes of objects selected for the project: Worker, Ladder,
Delineator, Wooden Pallet. In the following section we discuss why these objects were
selected and what makes their detection important for the robot as a collaborative agent
at the site.
• Worker : One of the prime concern of the project is the safety and assistance
of the worker at the construction site. Human workers can clearly be said to be
the prime classes that is needed to be identified as they are the facilitators of
work and are the prime class that deals with logistics and services. Workers in the
construction industry are prone to many different kind of dangers and long term
effects mention in the previous sections. Since the early stages of development of
Computer vision task, humans (and any related class like worker or persons) have
been one of the most sort after classes for detection [19] and [pascal].
The workers are one of the most mobile class in the whole construction site scenario
and also can have different postures and outfits leading to multiple variations of
the same class in the environment. Due to the nature of work at construction site
the human worker can be working with multiple tools and facilities at different
positions making it a bit more challenging task for any object detection model to
identify them.
• Delineator : Due to its work as a safety equipment, delineators are an important
object to be detected. their detection would mean identification of caution in the
surrounding. Usually delineators are used to caution workers and authorities of
areas exclusion or hazardous nature. Their detection for the robot can be used to
avoid areas of exception during operations.
Due to their simplistic features as shown in figure they posses an easy chance
of misclassification. its bright orange color and absolute cylindrical design can
resemble to many other objects similar to it. As show in later section we, we see
how other similar objects are classified to be a delineators.

by the construction site workers. In surveys done at different construction site, it

• True positive (TP): When Detection was made and the object is present. Hence
the detection was correct in making the detection.
• False positive (FP): When The detection was made but the object was not present,
Hence the detection was wrong.
• True Negative (TN): When no detection was made on image and no object was
present.
• False Negative (FN): When no detection was made but the object was present in
the image. Detector failed to make a detection of the object present.

in which the object makes a detection is TP and FP. Precision is the ratio of correct
detection to all detection made. Where as Recall is a metric that captures How much of
the ground truth is covered in all detection. It is a ratio of correctly predicted ground
truth to ground truth which were missed. The formula in 4.2.3 explains the calculation
of precision and recall.

Transfer learning

tasks. In computer vision task, transfer learning is a common approach to obtain a new

and then training it again on new data and new class. This training of network on pre-

Occlusion is a general problem in object detection task where some parts of one class

hence the network requires more training time. Generating labels for augmented images
is also a current issue in the community that is to fixed with better and more intelligent
augmentation tools.

5.3.4
Respective Approach To Improve Performance
of Models on Individual Classes
Ladders as a common tool used in the industry, they are found in multiple variations
in design and color. The distinctive visual property of ladder is it solid wooden frame
structure. This means context and background plays vital importance in learning the
feature of the ladder. The addition of images from ASTA with a clean background were
quite helpful in improving the general performance of models on this class. The clear
background was a distinctive factor difference in images from real construction sites and
images from ASTA. And as training from ASTA imporved the model performance to
great extent on this class as seen in table ?? and 4.5, it can be said that features from
ASTA were helpful in training.
Wooden pallets are mostly obscure and the top of it can be covered with other object
or may be left open. When it is in use to hold other objects that are heavy then it’s
bottom is only visible which can be very diﬀicult to predict. This problem of occlusion
is primarily addressed to be solved by augmentation techniques like random erase and
mosaic. Introduction of augmentation and new mock up data were quite resourceful to
combat the challenge to some respect. Class delineator has a unique problem due to its
simple geometric structure and bright color. The universal structure of delineator as a
cylinder adds bias in the model to classify anything that remotely looks cylindrical as
a delineator. This requires adding additional false negative classes of cylindrical objects
that are not delineator. As examples, images of gas cylinders and fire extinguishers are
added to the web image dataset. This is an intuitive way of making a model learn that
though the class has a simple structure, it should learn more intricate feature of the
class. Also the hue augmentation helps change the bright color feature of the delineator
and make the model understand the class in other color context too.
Workers are at many times the first priority in terms of safety of operation. The class
of humans can be found in dramatic variations in the image and in multiple contexts.
The availability of huge amount of data about humans makes the training much easier.

Model Selection
Selected Models looking at the performance described above multiple models can
be stated to be suitable to perform the task of detection in real time.
The choices

and computational expenses. The Model proposed in this section are selected first on
the criteria of performance on mAP and IoU. It can be observed from IOU reference
figure 5.5 that IOU performance of the model are roughly around 50% . Hence more
importance is give to the mAP metric for selection of model.
• Yolov4(SW) (mAP : 71.72%): Model has performed better than any other model
on the test set. It is deep enough to capture intricacies of objects instances and
identify them in the feature matrix. This model is most suitable to be run on heavy
and high end system with high processing power and GPU capabilities. However,
its deep architecture also makes it computationally expensive to run it on regular
hardware, especially if there is no GPU hardware present on the system.
• Yolov4-Tiny(SW) (mAP : 64.2%) : For relatively less powerful hardware setup
which can be embedded or lower end computers. The tiny version of the Yolov4
can be a great option. The network has 9 layers of convolution and still manage to
perform as good as Yolov4. This model can also be implemented on medium scale
hardware setups to get a high frame rate of detection on incoming video feed.
• Yolov4-Tiny-3l(SW) (mAP : 63.35%) This special version of tiny Yolov4 has
one additional layer for detection. This model has been performing relatively well
compared to its other counterparts. This model can be faster for detection because
of its additional yolo layer making it suitable for any embedded deployment on
objects with distinctive features.

7.2
Experimentation With Data
The focus of the project was not only build an object detection system, but also was
to see the impact of data on the system.
The model pool was trained on different
variations of training sets having combination of web and sensor images to study their
difference in performance.
The four basic models of Yolov, Yolov4-tiny, Yolov3 and
Yolov3-tiny showed a negative impact on performance due reduction in data. Evermore,
the reduction in the amount of web images lowered the performance of the model lower
than the models that were trained on the sensor images only. The best performing models
selected were Yolov(SW), Yolov4-Tiny(SW) and Yolov4-Tiny-3l(SW) with mean average
precision respectively 71.72%, 64.2%, 63.35% on the generalization test.
The same
models performed well in IoU metric with Yolov(SW), Yolov4-Tiny(SW) and Yolov4-
Tiny-3l(SW) attaining 59,08%, 58.09%, 49,53% average IoU respectively. for real time
detection an mAP of above 60 was considered as a good metric value for generalization
leading to validation of all three models for real time deployment.
A model which did not heavily under-performed due the data reduction and showed a
appreciable performance retention was Yolov4-Tiny-3l, It’s performance saw a dip when
data was reduced but the model was still performing 8.4% mAP better than models
trained on sensor images only. This result presents a possibility that such architectures
exist where models can be trained on domain specific data along with smaller amount
of outside domain data and have model performance improved. The intuition for the
selection of the outside domain data (in our case, images from outside the construction
site environment) should be based on the knowledge of the target domain. Conclusively,
domain specific knowledge should be leveraged in improving the dataset size and quality.
For real time object detection, the top three models were tested on an experimental
hardware setup described in 6.1. The Yolov4-Tiny(SW) and Yolov4-Tiny-3l(SW) were
found to be xx % and cc % faster than Yolo-v4. It was observed that all three models
can be implemented for real time object detection given the Yolo-v4 model runs on a
low resolution of 416x416. On such low resolution the model Yolov4 achieves a 32.3 fps
which is good for real time detection. Other models are can be operated on 816x816
resolution and still have average of higher than 50 fps.
Hence, empirically both in precision and accuracy the three proposed models are well
suited for real time detection. But, their performance should be tested and improved
on new data as it is always good to have more data validated for ensuring robustness
of the system. On real video data, the networks are seen to detect some false positives
and false negatives and the author was still trying to improve the models to fight miss-
classification and missed detections 5.2 at the time of submission of this report. This
should be kept in mind when deploying the system in a real environment.

7.3
Future works
Moving from proof of concept to scalable solutions:
Inspired from the core
objectives of the CoCoBot project, work done in this master thesis seeks to provide a
proof of concept of an object detection system that can be implemented. Limited number
of object classes were selected to make the deployment easier and more practical in the
initial phases. After gaining the leanings from development of models for these classes,
a good point forward will be to move ahead and work on the following points.
• Increase the dataset with more images from more different construction
sites As civil construction is common in DTU, lyngby campus, the campus ser-
vice can collaborate further to allow access to the sites and supervise more image
capturing programs. This collaboration can work both ways as campus service
can also benefit from the learning as this research can bring a collaborative robot
closer to reality.
• Use of new and innovative augmentation to improve the network. The
use of Mosaic augmentation has really aided the deep learning networks perform
better learning of object’s features. Understanding context and fighting occlusion
still remains a pretty hefty challenge to be fought in computer vision tasks. New
techniques like adversarial training (GAN) based data augmentation and style
transfer can be very helpful. Li et al. [16] showcased how adversarial method
can improve the training process. The use of style transfer has been proposed by
Jackson et. al [12] as a new kind of augmentation which can aid the learning of
network by introducing objects in multiple specialized context.
• Other models and frameworks An important point to consider at the ending of
the project is that this benchmark on the dataset was made by Darknet framework
with Yolov4 models. Interestingly, there is a class of model that is claimed to be a
newer version of Yolov4 called Yolov5. At the start of the project, Yolov5 was not
accepted as the legitimate successor to the Yolov4 because of proof of performance
on benchmark datasets. Hence, Yolov4 was selected as the top tier model to be
used in the project.
Apart from the Darknet family, the other group of network models that are exces-
sively used for object detection is the Detectron2.0. It’s model pool’s best models
like FAST RCNN and MASK RCNN have been used in many industry applications.
The obvious next step can be to use these models and measure their performance
against the Darknet family models. It will also be a test to see how the models
of the Detecron2.0 are affected by the variations of data and augmentation while
trying to achieve high generalization ability.
And as with different models, different annotation techniques can be tried to see
what kind of performance these models are generating. Also it should be found
out if they are suitable for an application like that of CoCoBot project.
==PAGE:87==
7.3 Future works
77
• Interesting classes for detection There are multiple other classes that can be
vital at construction sites and pose challenge in detection. One common object that
was observed at construction site was electrical wires protruding from the ground.
These underground wire outlets can be diﬀicult to distinguish as they are small
and sometime obscure. In initial phases of the construction, when construction is
done from ground up, the terrain can be uneven and have ditches. As the weather
of Denmark is unpredictable and precipitation is common throughout the year,
these ditches may become water puddle. Detecting these can be a challenge as
these can have reflection of surrounding areas and very different features. It can
be interesting to see what can be achieved by using different annotation schemes
like semantic segmentation can help in such cases.
The project concludes with a tremendous amount of learning and insight on data

efforts led to inferences on how to approach an object detection task? how to get data for
the deep learning tasks and how to make a model get better. The scope of improvement
will always remain as computer vision tasks are always compared to human perception
as an ultimate benchmark.
