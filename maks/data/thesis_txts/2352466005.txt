Summary
This thesis proposes to investigate and discuss Hybrid Models in Neural Activity. The
focus is on the resonate-and-’¼üre model with a periodic forcing. In Introduction (Chap-
ter 1), an introduction to electrophysiology of the neuron and the model which will be
analysed are given. A few de’¼ünitions are made and a quick insight into its stability
properties given as well as proposed topics of investigation. Some key concepts and def-
initions in dynamical systems are reviewed and a very useful map will be presented, the
stroboscopic map, and explained. In Spiking Regions (Chapter 2), we will recognise
that the stroboscopic map is discontinuous in the phase space and try to de’¼üne it in a
piecewise continuous manner. We will analyse the phase space and argue that it can be
given as the union of regions, where each region contains points that present trajectories
spiking the same number of instances. It turns out that the stroboscopic map is con-
tinuous within each of these spiking sets. Finally, we will analyse the boundaries that
separate these sets. The analysis of the models stability properties is given in Stability:
Fixed points and periodic solutions (Chapter 3). We will discuss the existence of
solutions, equilibrium points and periodic solutions, and subsequently derive expressions
to obtain them. The stroboscopic map is used to ’¼ünd periodic solutions of the system as
its solutions are analogous the systemsŌĆÖ. Finally, we will give a succinct overview of how
bifurcations can be triggered. The Grazing Bifurcations (Chapter 4) chapter is solely
dedicated to the bifurcations that the system exhibits. We will ’¼ünd out, that there can
be 2 types of grazing bifurcations and discuss them separately. We will present the case
for transversal grazing bifurcations and discuss the phenomena occurring due to this
bifurcation. For trangent grazing bifurcations, expression will be de’¼üned to ’¼ünd such
bifurcations. However, it will turn out that for this model they do not exist and this
will be formally proved. The proof considers a slightly wider range of systems, in which
the resonate-and-’¼üre model (with periodic forcing) is included. In Final Discussion
(Chapter 5), we give a sightly overview of what has been done, what were the results
achieved and minor considerations about them. Some further questions are proposed as
a continuation of the work done in this thesis.

molecules AŌłÆare also abundant [1]. An illustration depicting the distribution of ions in

We will expose a short review of some concepts in dynamical systems that will be useful

Summary
We will analyse the phase space and investigate the system for 1 cycle, i.e. t Ōłł[0, T].
Since the system is periodic, the discussion given here can be extended for n-cycles,

4.2

4.2.1
Summary

5.1
Conclusions
In this thesis, the resonate-and-’¼üre model with periodic forcing was analysed.
The
stroboscopic map was de’¼üned for this system and it was clear that it is discontinuous.
However, it was possible to de’¼üne it piecewise continuously on the spiking sets. We
observed that the boundaries between these sets consist of segments: transversal and
tangent. It was proven that the system does not possess any equilibrium points. This
is, in fact, due to non-linearity. In case the forcing is constant, I(t) = A, ŌłĆt ŌłłR, the
system would possess an equilibrium point, as it would be autonomous. As for periodic
solutions, they do exist and we were able to ’¼ünd them by means of the stroboscopic
map. We performed a bifurcation analysis and found that there are grazing bifurcations:
transversally and tangentially. On the former case, it was possible to observe that the
T-periodic solution of the system grazes transversally and the appearance of higher
periodic solutions. It is also possible to observe the periodic adding phenomenon in this
bifurcation and, consequently, a resemblance to the Farey tree. For a di’¼Ćerent set of
parameters, we observed the coexistence of T-periodic and higher periodic solutions. As
for tangentially grazing bifurcations, it was shown that for this model they do not exist.
However, changing the threshold to be a line of the type w = mv + w0, with slope m
and w0 w-intercept (taken as parameters) would yield such bifurcations [7].
