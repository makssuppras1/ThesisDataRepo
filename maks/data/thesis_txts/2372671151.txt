Abstract
Universal Newborn Hearing Screening is designed to identify hearing loss. This is critical to ensure
children with hearing loss do not experience deﬁcits in language and brain development. Among
the diﬀerent audiological test in this hearing screening, tympanometry and DPOAEs constitute 2
common standards to evaluate quickly and eﬃciently the degree and the type of hearing loss. A
modern test procedure that has been developed recently, Wideband Tympanometry brings much
more information about the middle ear conditions and increases the accuracy of tympanometry.
DPOAEs is a cochlear feedback test which by deﬁnition involves the transmission of sound
through the middle ear. It is well known that middle ear problems drastically reduces the level
or leads to a complete absence of the otoacoustic emission [26].
Wideband Tympanometry stands as a new potential solution to avoid losing too much from any
middle ear complication. Its capacity to generate a frequency pressure mapping of the eardrum
sound transmission allows to analyze in detail the middle ear. Examination of the WBT could
potentially lead to a new method to maximize DPOAEs in cases where a middle ear problem
might prevent a successful OAE test. Resulting in a new procedure that could have an important
signiﬁcance for the clinical world. The purpose of this research was to ﬁgure out if, based on
the evaluation of the middle ear energy transmission measured with Wideband Tympanometry
(WBT), frequency speciﬁc pressurization diﬀerent from ambient pressure could generate larger
distortion product otoacoustic emissions level (DPOAEs).
WBT and DPOAEs were measured in 15 normal hearing subject. WBT measured the absorbance
across -300 daPa to + 200 daPa (relative to the ambient pressure) and over the frequency ranged
from 226 Hz to 8 kHz. DPOAEs were obtained with a classical ratio of 1.22 and a 10 dB diﬀerent
between primaries level (L1 = 65 dB L2 = 55 dB) for 17 pressure conditions ranging from -200
to + 200 daPa in 25 daPa/step. DPOAEs dependency upon pressurization was observed with a
maximal dB level emissions rarely reached by the 0 daPa ambient conditions. In few subjects,
the improvement can be up to 10-15 dB for a singular frequency which would be an consequent
improvement for clinical applications. Frequency speciﬁc examination reveals diﬀerent optimal
pressure values for each subject. The generalization of this subject speciﬁc path pressure has
been performed by absorbance analysis for frequencies involved in the DPOAEs (f1, f2, 2f1 −f2)
as well as computational approach with neural networks machine learning.
Despite the diﬀerent analysis method used, we do not know the mathematical model involved
behind a potential DPOAEs beneﬁt from WBT. Therefore, we cannot conclude of a higher OAE
level estimation based on WBT absorbance analysis on normal hearing subjects.

Introduction
Behavioural measurement constitutes a quick and eﬃcient way to estimate a hearing loss,
however, it requires an active participation from the test person. Physiological measurement
can be performed regardless the subject’s conditions (age, mobility, etc....) and can provide
speciﬁc diagnostic information about the case of a given hearing loss. The impossibility to assess
any behavioral hearing test for infants pushes the development of better and objective tool for
pediatric applications due to the importance of evaluating the status of the auditory system.
Among early hearing problem, a newborn’s middle ear is frequently aﬀected by ear infections
or ossicular discontinuity which can be easily recovered by proper medical treatment or minor
surgery. However, middle ear complication obstructs early inner ear evaluation, particularly
during a OAEs recordings. Among diﬀerent OAEs procedure, Distortion product otoacoustic
test remains one of the possible neonatal hearing screening options and diﬃcult to test subject
evaluation. Tympanogram measurements do not require a high cooperation from the patient as
well.
Wideband tympanometry is an extension of tympanogram to a broad frequency range.
It
constitutes a recent and modern measurement of the middle ear mechanic across frequency and
air pressure. This new approach of estimating gives more accuracy for detecting pathology
compared to the classical approach for tympanogram based on one single frequency of 226 Hz for
adults and 1 kHz for infants [11]. Additionally, Otoacoustic emissions are small sounds recorded
in the ear canal typically in response to an acoustic stimulation and are one of the most important
features of a normal inner ear. Before reaching the cochlear, sounds are conditioned by the
middle ear, meaning that the tympanometric conﬁguration and eardrum mobility modulate the
amplitude estimation of Distortion product otoacoustics emissions (DPOAEs). As a consequence
middle ear condition can lead to a false conclusion about the absence or attenuated level of the
recorded OAE.
Classical DPOAEs are recorded at ambient pressure, it might be possible that this 0 daPa
ambient atmospheric condition actually does not make the sound transmission optimal. Previous
studies have found out the beneﬁt of inducing positive pressure in the ear canal to compensate
the negative middle ear pressure due to infections [24]. This method generates a higher DPOAE
response and in some circumstances can help the clinician to rule out the miss-diagnosis of a
sensorineural hearing loss, particularly in the case of middle ear infection with eﬀusion.

1. Introduction
So far the research has been focusing on the extreme case with negative pressure around -150
daPa. The eﬀect of moderate negative pressure, as well as positive pressure, has never been
investigated yet in details. This is why this thesis attempts to establish a relationship between
WBT and DPOAEs level as a function of frequency and pressure in order to ﬁnd a new method
to improve clinical DPOAEs measurements.
Is it possible to pick an optimal pressurization strategy based on the wideband tympanometry
evaluation for maximizing DPOAE 2f1-f2 response magnitudes?
The study has been carried out on 15 normal hearing subjects, recording both WBT and
pressurized DPOAEs. All recordings were performed at the facilities of the Technical University
of Denmark. This report articulates around 4 parts: First, a descriptive background of theory
and knowledge related to the topic, as well as recent research on it. Secondly, the acquisition of
the ear canal static pressure for WBT and DPOAEs, preliminary results are investigated and
then a ﬁrst attempt to link these two is performed by considering frequencies WBT absorbance
lines across pressure as potential predictors of DPOAEs response. Followed, by a computational
part about how machine learning is used as a tool to ﬁgure out a generalized model. Finally,
results are interpreted with a general discussion.

can only record the sounds reverse propagation from the Tympanic membrane (TM). Reﬂectance
constitutes then the ratio between the complex amplitude of the incident and reﬂected waves.

For a uniform cross-sectional area, the magnitude of the pressure reﬂectance does not vary with
distance, |R(f, x)| = |RT (f)|. Considering the ear canal as the wave guide during propagation
and the TM as the rigid tube ending, the reﬂectance at the termination (x=0) of an air cavity is
estimated by the termination source impedance ZT and the characteristic impedance of the tube
ZL. The physical meaning of the impedance is the capacity of a mass (an object) to resist to an
energy transmission or ﬂow. Calculation of the impedance tube load is based on the equivalent

Classical Tympanogram
Classical middle ear measures can be separated in 2 clinical technique applications. Depending

The tympanogram quantiﬁes the eardrums mobility as a function of air pressure for a ﬁxed
frequency, 226 Hz for adults and 1000 Hz for infants. The air pump probe delivers a mechanically
varying pressure from +200 daPa to −200 daPa (1 daPa = 100 Pa) and records the amount of
energy reﬂected, which is directly linked to the tympanic membrane and middle ear (ME) bones
compliance. Compliance represents the ease of energy transmission and thus by deﬁnition is
opposite to the stiﬀness.

The equivalent cavity volume (EQV) of the system is determined from the starting pressure

the importance of tympanometry. Otitis media decreases the pressure in the middle ear cavity
and boosts the stiﬀness, resulting in a huge admittance peak toward negative value know in the
clinical literature as a type C tympanogram or a centered ﬂat curve due to the poor transmission
of the low frequencies, referring to a class B tympanogram. Usual classiﬁcation analyzes the
presence of a peak pressure, the compliance value and the pressure localization of the static
admittance, resulting in various pathology diagnostics such as Otosclerosis, TM perforation and
disarticulation.

Wideband tympanometry
Based on acoustic absorbance estimation, Wideband tympanometry brings a spectral analysis
across pressure of the tympanic and middle-ear transfer function. This time ; the mobility of the
tympanic membrane is also analyzed across frequencies, providing much more information of the
energy transmission which is frequency dependent for the auditory system.
The transient stimulus is impulse which has a wide frequency content (acoustic click), due
to his short duration in a short time. The stimulus has been designed to deliver a constant
magnitude spectrum, see Figure 2.6. In the clinical device used in this thesis, the Titan from

pressure is generated as a sweep from -300 to +200 daPa. The pressure sweep can be ascending

WBT output is represented in a 3D format, x axis for the pressure, y axis for the frequency and
ﬁnally z for the absorbance, forming a mapping of the energy transmission as a joint function
of air pressure and frequency. As in classical tympanometry, higher absorbance values imply a
better ME performance.

2.3
DPOAEs : mechanism & applications
The motion and behaviour of hair cells in the cochlear generates a physiological activity called
otoacoustic emissions. This movement creates an acoustic signal, ranged from -20 to 30 dB SPL,
loud enough to be evaluated with the help of an adapted stimulus and the proper recording setup.
The interest for OAE is due to its capacity to reﬂect the status of the cochlear, it diagnoses
all functionality of it, any dysfunctional characteristic (Basilar membrane, the structure of
corti, hairs cells) can be directly linked with an OAE recording. Thus, this cochlear evaluation
constitutes a diﬀerential diagnosis for hearing loss. Moreover, one advantage of OAEs is that the
response mirrors with the input stimulus frequency, making it easy to target some frequency
regions if needed.
Diﬀerent types of OAEs are used in clinical practice, the most common are are transient
evoked otoacoustic emissions (TEOAEs) and distortion product otoacoustic emissions (DPOAEs),
typically used to examine sensorineural hearing loss. These two emissions can only be generated
by an acoustic input, hence their categorization as Evoked otoacoustic emissions (EOAEs).
This research focuses on the DPOAE, theses are generated within the cochlea when two pure
tones close enough in term of frequency f1 and f2 (f1<f2) are simultaneously presented with a
respective level L1 and L2.
Figure 2.9. Spectral representation of the recorded cubic distortion product 2f1–f2 recorded with f1 =
1000 Hz , f2 = 1200 Hz and a 10 dB primaries level diﬀerence [10].
Such a frequency close input generates an inter-modulation distortion produced by the nonlinear
processing of the basilar membrane. The fundamental concept behind this distortion is that
energy incorporated in the input signal (f1,f2) of the non-linear system is transformed into a
set of diﬀerent and new frequencies, where the two primary frequencies are still the highest but
additional new frequencies components are generated. Among those components, the distortion
product (DP) 2f1 −f2 remain the most analyzed in the clinic due to his higher amplitude. In a
DP-gram the level of the distortion product is displayed as a function of frequency. This recorded
level can be mixed with other noises from diﬀerent sources (ﬂoor noise, body noise, artifacts),
however some algorithms are generally implemented to perform a well estimation of the overall
noise in order to improve the signal to noise ratio.
DPOAEs response are believed to be originated from 2 diﬀerent mechanisms of the cochlear.
Nonlinear distortion mechanisms are the consequence of the overlapping eﬀect due to the
narrow frequency space of primaries. Secondly, the coherent-reﬂection mechanism operates in

another way ; the cochlear partition varies irregularly in position, resulting in micro-mechanical
irregularities causing small perturbations, so-called "backscattering" of forward travelling waves.
These particular waves reach the characteristic frequency place 2f1–f2 on the basilar membrane

Maximal response of DPOAE has been obtained with the choice of an optimal primaries ratio,

still in debate with on going research about interaction that might take place among f2/f1, L1
and L2 parameters choice.
DPOAEs can only be roughly interpreted as a Present/Absent test for the whole auditory system.
However, properties of the ME directly inﬂuence OAEs characteristics. Before reaching the
cochlear the stimulus is transmitted through the middle ear and can already be altered. Moreover,
the backward waves from the distortion product must pass across the ME again. As illustrated
with Figure 2.10, the recorded DP product is relatively low in amplitude, as a consequence any
transmission loss induced by middle ear condition (eﬀusion, disarticulation ... ) reduces seriously
the OAE emissions to a hardly detectable level and even sometimes cancels the entire response

2.4
Environmental parameters on WBT and DPOAEs.
It has been observed that environmental conditions aﬀect signiﬁcantly middle ear measurements.
The body position and postural have an immediate signiﬁcant and reversible eﬀect upon the
pressure of the ME. The positional ME pressure change is principally between a sitting and lying
position. From long-term monitoring of ME [7], it has been shown that supine positioning led
to an instant and signiﬁcant ME pressure increment of 19 daPa on average within the ﬁrst 30
seconds.
Repeating measurements of WBT tympanometry might trigger an eﬀect as well. On 8 consecutive
WBT measurements, done in 30 seconds intervals, without the resealing of the probe Laina [6]
observed a kind of «tympanometric pre-conditioning » (NSubject = 32). Consecutive repeated
measurements of WBT suggests a slight alteration in the visco-elastic properties of the eardrum
due to repeated pressure loading on the eardrum and demonstrates that compliance of the middle
ear can be preconditioned. The amount of absorbance change is around 20 % at 1kHz, 14 % at
500 Hz and 9 % for 226 Hz.
Figure 2.11. Amplitude change in Energy Absorbance for consecutive recordings [6].
Tideholm conducted continuous and direct 24-hour ME pressure measurements [4]. They reported
a gradual pressure elevation occurring during sleep and suggested that an excessive amount of
CO2 was transported from blood vessels into the ME lumen. On the other hand, continuous ME
recordings in upright patients have revealed a decrease in ME pressure in those who have fallen
asleep while being studied. The mean pressure rise is 36.4 daPa during sleep. One plausible
explanation is the distinction in blood gas composition in the resting and sleeping condition
(Exchange O2/CO2). During sleep, CO2 is transported more rapidly and cause the net increase
of ME during sleep. The hypothesis of auditory sensitivity to pressure is supported by several
experimental studies performed while under conditions other than resting and sleeping. Another
environmental parameter, the inter-cranial pressure change (ICP), very sensitive to posture alter
the intracochlear pressure as well as the middle ear stiﬀness [25].
The relationship between admittance at ambient pressure, more precisely the Wideband
Reﬂectance, and DPOAE levels in normal hearing adults ages have already been explored
[21]. The hypothesis was that the amount of energy admitted into the middle ear at f1 and f2
DPOAEs frequencies should aﬀect how much energy is transferred into the cochlear. According
to this research, WBT and DPOAEs is negatively correlated at 1000 Hz, positively correlated at
4000 Hz, no statistical signiﬁcance for 2000 hz.

3.1.2
Pressure stability and acquisition
The pressure seal of the Titan probe in the ear canal was tested with a pressure stability
evaluation. A ﬁxed pressure of +200 daPa was induced in the hearing canal and visually tracked
on the software and the manometers. The probe ﬁtting was considered appropriate if the pressure
does not drop under 170-180 daPa after 20 seconds. Otherwise, sealing was attempted again by
changing the probe tip size or the position of the inserted probe.
WBT constitutes a very eﬃcient but short time recording with the chosen pump speed at 200
daPa/s, absorbance acquisition is performed in less than few seconds, questioning the accuracy
of the sampled pressure. It turns out that the pressure acquisition is not strictly linear and
bounded. A deviation up to ±30 daPa exists, meaning that, even if the WBT is supposed to
start at 200 daPa, the data acquisition begins at 170 daPa.
During DPOAEs, long term acquisition creates a deviation due to the impossibility of holding
the pressure at a high and constant value over 20 seconds. It is technically expected that the
diﬀerence will be higher as it diverges from 0 daPa and get close to extreme pressures. This
deviation has been evaluated as a pressure test stability among 5 subjects, the point was to
estimate how much try drops out of the +/- 10 daPa sensibility for each pressure values. Extreme
pressures does not ﬁt with this +/- 10 daPa sensibility and are less reliable.

4.1
Neural Network model
A neural network consists of a biologically-inspired computational model for machine learning
where the information and calculus are inspired from the human brain processing, by
interconnections of processing elements (neurons) in parallel. Neural network constitutes an
self-organized adaptive learning model able to learn from a given data and generates his own
representation of it. The strength of neural networks is his capacity to recognize mathematical
model behind a complex pattern.

4.2
Pattern recognition processing
A neural network is used as a tool in this study to determine from the data which pressure
class maximizes the DPOAEs level. In technical words, the operation is a supervised pattern
recognition processing.
Given an N number of inputs, the algorithm attempts to assess a
combination of observation that will output two or more likely classes to reach the desired
purpose. This approach can be considered as supervised learning, the values of the expected
output are known. A classiﬁer can then be constructed, with one class per 25 daPa pressure,
bringing a total of 17 classes for the same step used in the experiment recording of DPOAEs.
Figure 4.3. Flowchart of the Artiﬁcial Neural Network. The input consists of the 6 recorded WBT
from each subject resulting in a total of 90 entries. The target refers to each DPOAEs
transformed into a label vector.
The general processing method behind the pattern recognition is depicted by Figure 4.3. As
mentioned before WBT constitutes the features used as input of our learning. Features contain
information about WBT characteristics that will by their variation, produces a diﬀerent response
and thus allow to perform a classiﬁcation and to understand the relationship between inputs
and outputs. This is why this computational approach involves all WBT recording instead of an
averaged version like in the previous section.
The DPOAE data are converted into a one-hot label designating the desired output as a class,
each label is essentially a string of 0s and a single 1 located at the index corresponding to the
pressure step that yields the maximal DPOAEs level for this subject.
WBT inputs need to be pre-processed beforehand. This pre-processing might be assimilated as a
feature extraction stage. Dimensionality of the WBT consists of a 501 by 103 matrix, resulting
in ineﬀective informations per WBT inputs. This matrix has to be reduced in order to avoid
a long computing time, resources and guide the algorithm in the learning process. Numerous
techniques exist to reduce the dimension of a data set, however, in our case we already suspect
from the previous analysis the contribution of absorbance amplitude as the main characteristics
leading to a higher DPOAEs level.

5.1
Future research
WBT constitutes a recent middle ear condition diagnostic test. Knowledge about this new
measurement still relies on a poor base of research. This thesis reﬂects the requirement of more
understanding about middle ear absorbance. As a major ﬁnding of this research, the variability
of the TPP questions the reliance of multiples WBT recordings. So far research has been focus
only on tympanogram (pressure compliance) or ambient admittance. The stability of the WBT
has to be investigated in order to allow future experiment to be fully relevant for multiple
WBT recordings. Moreover, WBT records the squared magnitude of the reﬂectance. Phase
information has been totally ignored and could actually reveal some interesting information for
the comprehension of middle ear characteristics across pressure and frequency.
The research was limited to normal hearing subject. Various pattern from middle ear diseases
absorbance should be investigated for giving more analysis cases. The regular pattern shape
WBT provided by normal hearing subjects does not bring enough to exploit the potential link
between measuring WBT and DPOAEs. For hearing loss case, the WBT is likely to be very
much aﬀected in certain frequency regions, by simply knowing from the patient if the hearing
problem is conductive, sensorineural or a mixed hearing loss the energy transmission can be
evaluated much more eﬃciently and related to the cochlear.
TEOAEs has proved also good results for being candidates to a future small step pressurization
investigation [16]. However, as for the DPOAEs, most research has only been conducted for
extremely large NMEP. TEOAEs comes from a diﬀerent mechanism of the inner ear than
DPOAEs, it is crucial to investigate if any pattern can ﬁgure out for normal hearing as well as
hearing impaired populations.
More precision about middle ear bones could be also investigated. In WBT procedure, the
compliance is estimated at the eardrum, not at the stapes. The mechanical articulation of the
malleus, incus and ﬁnally the stapes might not be subject to the same change with pressurization.
The biological and anatomical investigation could bring more knowledge of how the mechanical
vibrations of middle ears bones are reacting to diﬀerent pressurization.
Finally, nowadays
researchers experiment with new stimulus conditions for DPOAEs. Combining this new approach
with a pressurization of the ear canal for this new type of frequency/amplitude dependent f1
and f2 stimulus could open new possibilities for assessing very precise diagnosis.

6
Conclusion
This study investigated the eﬀect of middle ear absorbance on the inner ear distortion product
otoacoustic emissions. How these two physiological measurements are related is currently not
fully understand and is complex. It was found that a high correlation exists between the WBT
and DPOAEs measurements in the frequency regions of 1-2 kHz and 4-8 kHz giving credit to the
hypothesis of more energy transmitting through the ear bones accomplish a larger distortion
product.
Data analysis approach as well as Neural Networks machine learning method results claim the
non signiﬁcance of using any other pressurization conditions than 0 ambient for the DPOAEs
evaluation on average. However, the data set brings more precision about where the model of
pressurization should be worth to apply or not. Across every participant, the potential beneﬁt of
using another pressure path is subject dependant. Some disclose a signiﬁcant gain up to 15 dB
at critical frequencies when the recorded DPOAEs is relatively low whereas some others already
posses their maximal DPOAEs for the ambient conditions.
This research has exposed a variability of the WBT across subjects which could be one reason of
non promising results from the experiment. The model behind WBT and DPOAEs appears to
be very complex but very beneﬁcial in some case, based on this research, more knowledge has
been added for considering a hypothetical relationship.
Very promising results for certain subjects support the remarkable beneﬁt of utilizing DPOAEs
for clinical evaluation and thus justify the need to process to further investigation. Nevertheless,
the rule leading to DPOAE beneﬁt has not been found out and thus we cannot conclude to expect
any improvement of DPOAEs based on the evaluation of WBT for normal hearing subjects.
