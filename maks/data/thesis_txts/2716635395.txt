Abstract
Robots are an ever­increasing profession and interest for many companies. A company
by the name Tellus Remarketing is seeking a customised solution for one of their op­
erations, being cleaning keyboards. An automated solution for this operation is key for
Tellus Remarketing since workers dislike the task of cleaning keyboards and also often
ruin the product by breaking of key caps. This thesis tries to solve these challenges by
implementing a robot arm which can correctly operate the task by an attempt on design­
ing impedance control and thereby try to resolve the defect ratio of the operational task
regarding keyboard cleaning. A camera system is used in order to guide the controller
for each variation of keyboards. That will ultimately decide the effect from such a con­
troller and finally test weather this attempt is successful or other methods are required.
The thesis finds that though it is possible to implement a direct control system, the reality
shows that the variation in keyboard designs makes it difficult to implement. However,
with more implementations, experiments, and adjustments to the controller parameters it
will be possible.

Introduction
In the modern robotic world, the use of force­ and vision based control (touch, force, dis­
tance, visual feedback) has become very popular due to its possibility to integrate robots in
unidentified environments. An important benefit the force­ and vision based control is the
collision handling. By these means, nearby obstacles are detected early can be avoided.
Some examples on tasks which gets possible to automate by force or/and vision control
are.
So in consideration of why force control became so popular instead of traditional con­
trol, known as precise programmed position control, is simply to press down the risk of
unplanned change collisions that leads to damage of surrounding objects and itself. An­
other reason can be staged as the cost. For example if the job task changes, then a new
implementation is needed of a software along with the surroundings design. The cost of
those changes can be huge, due to the precision demands. The benefit of a force control
can thereby be, compared to precision position control.
Regular used force control methods consists of stiffness control, impedance control, ad­
mittance control, hybrid position/force control, hybrid impedance control etc[1].
1.1
Project Scope and Company
Tellus remarketing is a company based in Denmark, Allerød. Their facility receives IT
equipment, mostly keyboards, from various manufactures and producers. They imprint
the language, along with any possible special features, on the keyboard, this process
is mostly done by humans aided by simple machines. The keyboard is prepared and
cleaned by hand, which due to its excess requirement for manual labor, makes it difficult
for the employees.
The cleaning task consists of 200­500 keyboards that needs to be cleaned. The process
of cleaning takes around 1­3 hours depending on the amount of the keyboards received
and has following steps:
1. Find a sponge
2. Add the necessary chemical
3. Wipe off any particles, dust or hair
4. Continue the work n­times (n = amount of keyboard)
Tellus remarketing is facing the ”unhappy employee” problem, due to the cleaning task.
First of all the happiness of the employees, which typically is the reason of termination if
its not there. The happiness of an employee is an important factor to achieve success for
the company. The most important benefits of an happy employee are as following:
• Stays employed
• Increase in productivity
The cleaning task is physically hard, which is also seen in the defect ratio, that goes from
4% (around 200 keyboards) to 17% (around 500 keyboards).

State of the art
This Thesis project use a 6­DoF robot manipulator as an attempt to a solution for the chal­
lenged layed out by Tellus remarketing. The main approach and method are to delve into
a closed control system by implementing an impedance controller to properly feedback
the system as to apply correct forces onto the keyboards.
For this chapter there is no direct existing solutions to compare to but in similar challenges
with a different control design approach.
Most solutions and work committed in this field of control was found to navigate a space
with working tools and hereby use the force controller to correctly assess positions and
force needed to screw of put the bolts into desired targets[28].
However, a newer surge of interest for impedance control is for the robot system to be­
come cobots, in regard to detecting a human collision then to act swiftly by moving in­
vertedly away from the human, and rapidly reposition itself at the starting position before
disturbance by external factors.
research approach with a LQR observer and filter in Roveda et al. (2015).
Found a slight improvement in the accuracy of the testing robot manipulator which was
showcased by a Franka EMIKA panda robot arm.
Through the individual joint torques and a control design encompassing an Extended
kalman filter(EKF). The main challenges discussed by this paper are the scalability of
their solution. The EKF and state dependant ricati equation. Have to be very finely tuned
by a control engineer to probably meet these systems intended function in custom envi­
ronments[28].
A research project by Fares J. Abu­Dakka and Matteo Saveriano published 21 December
2020, at Intelligent Robotics Group, Department of Electrical Engineering and Automation
(EEA), Aalto University, Espoo, Finland.
Had their impedance control adaptable by implementing a learning algorithm, with this
it was possible for the paper to reach a certain state for the robot system that by the
force measurements it was trained to know how to apply proper force since it underwent
a training session from a specific operational task. Hereby, when it finished training the
different operations task. At this project it was tested out in hospitals, museums and
logistics operations[22].
A research project by Yang Zhou, Xiang Li, Linzhu Yue, Linhai Gui, Guangli Sun, Xin
Jiang and Yun­Hui Liu. Published on 28 January 2019 at the 2019 IEEE/RSJ International
Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS).
This research team created a model to regulate the dynamic relationship between in­
teraction force and robot motion for this system impedance control, where the motion is
measured by high­precision vision feedback Jacobian matrix as a dynamic control feed­
back[25].

3.1
Introduction to Robot Operating System ­ ROS
ROS is the abbreviation for Robot Operating System and an open source framework de­
velopment kit for applications about robotics. It provides libraries and tools so that the user
can build, obtain, write and run code. The operating system is not a real­time framework,
but the possibility for integration of ROS with real­time code is still there.
The philosophy of ROS is to ”stop reinvent the wheel”[5]. By that the obtained goals of
ROS is to provide a standard for robotic software that developers can use and re­use in
any robot easily (when the understanding is there of ROS).
So a research has been made about middlewares to find out which one should be worked
on. A table has been made with some criteria and shown below:

3.4
Nodes
Nodes are pieces of software, an action which performs calculations and can be written
in python, C++, so an executable program. The communication of nodes is by using ROS
actions, topics, etc.
A Node is either a ”publisher” or a ”subscriber”, other general word is ”listener”. Publisher
node feeds the topic with information, while a subscriber node is being fed by it, whenever
it is possible. But for that to be possible a ROS Master is needed, which provides tracking
of publishers and subscribers to topics and enables the communication by letting them
know about their existence. The communication is a peer­to­peer.
The benefits of nodes are firstly less complex. better overview, easy to fix if fault appear.
Nodes are similar to functions/methods that you separate into classes, in this case pack­
ages. The advantage of those ”functions/methods” are that they can be written in different
languages, which means that every node does not have to be in the same programming
language.
The communication is alone over ROS, not a direct link between and by that it is known
if a fault appears on one of the nodes, it wont affect other nodes.
For the overview and clarification of the understanding, an simple example is shown.

Industrial Manipulator ­ UR5
This chapter is created with the purpose of understanding the design, which consists of
kinematics and dynamics of industrial manipulator used in this thesis, namely UR5. This
chapter takes care of and describes in more detail the UR5, kinematic, DH Table/conven­
tion, forward kinematic and inverse kinematic.
4.1
Introduction to an industrial manipulator
Industrial manipulators are mechanical robot arms which are controlled/automated by ei­
ther with a program for the purpose of the given task made by the user or manually by a
human operator. It is used in many industries as motor, construction, chemical, electronic
etc. Among other things the handling of different kind of tasks as cutting, pick and pack,
pick from belt, paint, palletizing etc. An example is the purpose of this project, that is to
automate the undesired cleaning task for Tellus Remarketing.
This thesis includes the Universal Robot 5 that consists of six revolute/rotary joints that
gives a total of six degrees of freedom(DoF). Each degree of freedom is known as a joint
that consists of a motor and an encoder, which means the bigger DoF, the more complexity
to the control algorithm. Rigid links are connected by joints and by that a kinematic chain
is obtained.
4.2
Kinematics ­ UR5
Kinematics of robot is the math that makes it possible to study the movement of a kine­
matic chain as in UR5. Links of a robot is designed as rigid bodies and the joints provides
translation (the motion along a straight line) or rotation. The math includes the position,
velocity and acceleration of each links in order to calculate and plan the movement and
to measure forces and torques for the actuator.
Known and important keywords in kinematics are DH table/convention, forward kinematic
and inverse kinematics. The process of forward kinematic is to determine the position
and orientation of the end effector in Cartesian space by the known joint angles. On the
other hand, the inverse kinematic has the process of calculating the angles with position
and orientation of the end effector. In the figure an illustration of the relationship is shown:
Table 4.1: Relationship of forward­ and inverse kinematics[24]
So the process of obtaining the kinematics of the manipulator is as following:
• DH convention
• Forward kinematics
• Inverse kinematics

4.2.1
Denavit­Hartenberg convention
In order to calculate the kinematics and dynamics of the UR robot, the DH parameters
are needed.
Denavit­Hartenberg parameters is the motion definition of actuators that is connected by
rigid links. In 1955 Jacques Denavit and Richard Hartenberg introduced the DH con­
vention. DH parameters are the four obtained parameters with this approach and known
as:
• d offset along previous z to common normal (link offset)
• θ angle (rotation) about previous z, from old x to new x (joint angle)
• a radius of new origin about previous z. length of common normal (link length)
• α angle (rotation) about common normal(new x), from old z axis to new z axis (link
twist)

8.2
Different Keyboards
Tellus remarketing does not only clean one type of keyboard, but multiple types. These
keyboards differ in the amount of pressure needed to clean them, pressure that would
clean one keyboard, could possibly break the other kind. As such not only does keyboards
needs to be identified in the image, but the type of keyboard. Most object detection algo­
rithms allow for the identification of different objects. However, with multiple types at play,
any object might have multiple types above the threshold, this would create a conflict of
identification. The normal way to solve this identification issue, is to simple pick the type
with highest confidence. Should this solution however pick a wrong type, the keyboard
might be broken. Another solution is to give every type a pressure value, when types
are pass the threshold, the one with the smallest pressure value will be selected. This
solution decreases the average quality of cleaning, but drastically decreases to possibility
of destroying a keyboard.
However, at the time writing, all this is theoretical since the data required would increase
astronomically.

Discussion
All in all, the system behaved well for such a custom automation system which where de­
signed for the cleaning operational purpose. However, much can be improved from the
control standpoint for the system. And several implementation of more features would
likely ensure that Tellus remarketing would be content with the functionality of the system.
9.0.1
wear and tear speculation
Many experiments that where conducted was retake on the same keyboard, from this it
might be highly likely that wear and tear on the plastic holding the key caps, would increase
by each attempt. This factor has not been a consideration for the experimented results
thus the conclusion of experiments on several of the failing attempts, especially on the
keyboard models that are made with weaker plastic, might be wrong, in the regards that if
only single cleaning attempts on a recent keyboard. would yield better results. However,
ensuring and aiming for a robot control that would be stable enough to not break key caps,
this consideration has not been taking into account for the analysis and experimentation.
9.0.2
Discussion of experimentation
Apart from the tuning of the parameters. The analysis it is shown that increase in applied
desired force brought a slight increase in stability of the system, the changes of fluctu­
ation The experimentation’s showcase that the durable Lenovo keyboard models which
tolerate applied force of 17 N, had a very little chance of breaking a key cap when the
system was operating through these. The more sturdy material ensures that the cleaning
mechanism not only resulted in the most satisfactory stable applied force, but even when
the reference desired Fz was increased the key caps did not break. Since the system
does not encompass a quality control mechanism, the experiments with 8.5 N and 9.5 N,
often required several more attempts before the dust and hair was completely removed.
Therefore, it is very uncertain for the system to operate with these models in regards to
more attempts needed and the sensitive keyboards would often break.
The experimentation’s are incomplete in the sense that many more areas of the controller
could ideally have been monitored. While the thesis found conclusion on the optimal
parameters for the controller, all of which turned out to be the highest setting and more
experimentation might have shown improved results by increasing the parameters, or
this might have concluded that a further increase would not have a further optimization.
Because of this more experimentation and alteration of the control design, would likely
improve the system considerably.
Testing out a python script using image processing tools ended up not being a solid
method for recognition of a specific keyboard model. It was not possible to find a di­
rect research in this field that have public results regarding this challenge. The most
direct solution method when classifying a similar object was letter identification systems
which have become a very popular tool in today’s world. Though many letter identifica­
tion programs are relying on neural network, Roboflow was a quick and easy solution to
use. However, very costly in time from the training samples needing to consist of at least a
1000 images to reliably work for classifying a single keyboard model. A different approach
and more testing in regards to using the image processing tools would have been a better
alternative in the sense that setting up a new keyboard model class for the ordered force
vector array require much less effort. This is important since Tellus remarketing handles a
large variation of keyboard models and very often inquire a new model in their operations.

Conclusion
As illustrated in the project scope the ability of cleaning keyboard by different models
by an autonomous system is doable. Tuning the parameters discussed and showcased
through the report resulted in a significant increase in stability of force application on the
keyboards. results have shown that the controller allow the robot manipulator to suc­
cessfully stabilize the forces applied to the keyboard surfaces in terms of position and
orientation, and changes in parameters sufficiently optimize the control of the operation.
However, for optimal purpose the thesis highly recommend is that the more durable key­
board models are the one to be used only if designing an autonomous system such as the
one proposed by this project. Most of the models refurbished at Tellus remarketing are
of a weaker plastic material. From the experimentation nearly none of the experiments
resulted in key cap breaks. Hereby, further testing and treating the mushroom as a mass­
spring damper is a lack of this project, because of this a more encompassing nonlinear
control design of the physical system is required to reach a more sufficient goal for a real
life implementation.
To be able to use the system for the intended operation. It is a requirement to spend con­
siderable effort to create thee fixtures that need to be specific for every single variation of
keyboard model that are refurbished. By this fact the company required a system which
is more flexible and able to successfully clean each variation of all the keyboard models
they operate.

a team on full time payment for several months to finish an encompassing training set
and classification list to achieve this. Then a more easy and accessible approach to this
challenges is a necessity for further work.

11.0.1
Quality control
Tellus Remarketing outlined criteria for the system and through this project some of these

aspect. The company expressed that this feature is an absolute necessity. This challenge

Therefor, to satisfy all the needs from the company would require implementing an after
operation which highlight to an operator that the cleaning operation was successful or if
the system needed another turn.

Sponge in contrast to what was expected. Thereby, modelling the Sponge as a mass
spring damper and implementing such a dynamical model in the non­linear control of the
system would be an improvement to the system operation.
