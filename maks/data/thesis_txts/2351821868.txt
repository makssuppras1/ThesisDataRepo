INTRODUCTION 
In the last decade there has been a steep increase of several infective diseases in the 
whole world. After the outbreaks of Malaria and Ebola, the attention now focuses on 
Dengue and Zika.  
The World Health Organization launched an emergency to find reliable and adequate 
solutions to limit these epidemics. The key fact is that those diseases are mainly spread in 
tropical area that are in developing countries where healthcare is deficient. For this 
reason, the solutions available on the market to limit the expansion of those viruses create 
a bottleneck.  
At the moment the key element that restrains the spread of those diseases is the 
diagnostics. Being able to identify infected people is an enormous step ahead because 
it is possible to adopt preventive measures. In most of the cases, when the diseases are 
not monitored, it can easily to turn into the severe stages that leads to harmful 
consequences as well as death.  
Most of the available diagnostics techniques are not adequate to act in such 
environments. Several companies worldwide developed diagnostics tools (based on 
Polymerase Chain Reaction, ELISA and Lateral Flow technology) but due to cost and 
accessibility for some and reliability for others those solutions are not addressable for 
developing countries’ context. 
Blusense Diagnostics, a small Danish startup, developed a Point-Of-Care device able to 
make diagnostics with high quantitative results, at a moderate price and in short time.  
After several donations and funds received, among which there is the recent 1 Million USD 
received for having won the U.S Zika Challenge, Blusense is proceeding closer and closer 
to be ready to be on the market.  
The first part of the thesis aimed to analyze the value proposition and market position of 
the start-up.

RQ1: “How Blusense Diagnostics position itself in the market?” 
Understanding the differences among the different technologies on the market is a must 
to be able to critically evaluate the market and understand how the company can enter 
the sector of infective diseases’ diagnostics.

CHAPTER 1 – BACKGROUND  
1.1 Introduction  
The spread of mosquitoes-borne diseases represents an emerging problem that is 
affecting the entire world. Nowadays, Flavivirus is the genus of viruses that is surging and 
proliferating in several countries and that every year causes several million deaths (World 
Health Organization, n.d.). It is responsible for various diseases that cause encephalitis and 
other harmful consequences. The most relevant to mention are yellow fever, Japanese 
encephalitis, dengue, Zika and west Nile fever.  
In the first place tropical countries, through their warm and humid environment, are the 
main abode for mosquitoes to thrive. Latin America, Africa and South-East Asia are the 
first spots with the highest number of endemic areas but the virus is spreading further.  
Factors  that contribute to the spread of the vector are, for example, climate change, 
intensive farming, dams and international travels (World Health Organization, 2014a). 
The global warming is having a big impact on the diffusion of mosquito-borne infections. 
Basically, when the temperature increases, there is going to be a rise of mosquitoes-born 
infections due to the elevated number of vectors (Peredo-videa, 2001)(see Appendix 
Climate Change and Vector-Borne Disease).  
USA and also Europe are in emergency due to the spread of those sort of diseases that 
before were rarely been diagnosed there (Sabrina Tavernise, 2016)(Ecoscienza, 2012).  
The two major diseases that are surging in the last decades are Zika and Dengue. The 
World Health Organization is taking care to the enormous damages caused by those two 
viruses and also launched the need to find a solution to limit their proliferation.  
1.2 Virus and the diseases 
Dengue and Zika are two of the infective diseases caused by the Flavivirus transmitted to 
humans by the female mosquito of Aedes aegypti. 
Those diseases are endemic in tropical and subtropical countries as Africa, Central and 
South America and Southeast Asia but due to international trades and travelers the viruses 
were imported in other territories (Calza, 2012). 
Those viruses exist in several serotypes. While the dengue serotypes are all spread in the 
world, the Zika virus (ZIKV) has two strains according to the geographical location: the 
American and Asian type (Bosco, 2016a).

1.3 Treatments and Vaccinations 
Until now, unfortunately, no specific treatment has been developed against dengue and 
Zika. Palliative medicines, as analgesics and fluids, are usually the only relief to be given 
to patients (CDC, 2014b).  According to the statistic the survival rate was over 80% but 
due the rise of complications as hemorrhagic severe dengue or birth defects the 
damages are expanding. 
Regarding vaccines, it has been recently released Dengvaxia® (CYD-TDV) the first 
vaccination against Dengue. Dengvaxia®, developed by Sanofi Pasteur, started to be 
marketed in 2015 in some endemic countries such as Mexico, Brazil, El Salvador and the 
Philippines. The use of the vaccine has been licensed for use for people between 9 to 45 
years old. The vaccination consists of 3 shots every 6 months (WHO, 2016).

Although the release of the vaccine is a considerable step ahead, there are still some 
issues. The downside is that the vaccine has different efficacies to the different serotypes

Furthermore, being the vaccination a process that includes 3 shots in one-year time frame,

For these reasons, many authorities of endemic countries are still reluctant to the usage of

In addition to this, the tetravalent vaccine costs around 100 USD for all three shots that is

In conclusion the ratio between efficacy and cost is still insufficient to justify a significant 
investment for the usage of Dengue vaccine. 
Regarding Zika, no vaccine has been developed yet. This is caused by the low impact the 
disease had until few years ago. Developing a vaccine costs millions of dollars and the 
investment is justified if there are many people affected by that specific virus. According 
to Sanofi Pasteur, the developer of dengue vaccine, More than half a billion US dollars is 
necessary to develop a vaccine and the average time is roughly 12 years. Even if the first 
stages of clinical development are 2-3 years long, the tough stage to go through is the 
quality control (see Appendix Sanofi Pasteur) (Pasteur, n.d.).  
But recently things changed and Zika has exploded through many outbreaks in the 
Americas. Moreover, it has been proven the correlation between the disease and fetus 
malformations.

1.4 The emerging problems 
The dominant problem is that both dengue and Zika are spreading and proliferating 
unexpectedly fast. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the measures to 
control those vector-borne diseases are still not completely effective and there is the urge 
to find new methods (World Health Organization, 2014a). 
As mentioned above, the global warming is notably affecting the propagation of the 
diseases. “The direct effects of temperature increase are an increase in immature 
mosquito development, virus development and mosquito biting rates, which increase 
contact rates (biting) with humans” reports Maria Diuk-Wasser at the Yale School of Public 
Health (Diuk-Wasser, 2015). 
Furthermore, a considerable matter is that the vectors mainly hit people that are left 
behind by development. Nearly all the countries that belong to the above mentioned 
continents are undeveloped: housing conditions are poor, water is unsafe, the 
environment is filled with filth and the sanitary structures are insufficient.  
 
 
The main concern is the spread this disease in the whole world. Even if there is not yet a 
valid cure to it, developed countries still have more medical equipment to make 
diagnostics and reduce the spread of the viruses. On the other hand, developing 
countries lack resources as well as adequate infrastructures. Because of this, the

Pregnancy and birth defects  
Both Zika and Dengue have disastrous outcomes on pregnancy. 
In regards to Dengue, if the virus affects a pregnant woman, the disease is automatically 
transferred to the fetus. The main risks that occur are premature birth and higher exposure

and damages the peripheral nervous system2. There is not a specific medical screening 
to diagnose this syndrome and if it is not caught on time the consequences can be life 
threatening. In which case the damages keep spreading, resulting in dangerous 
breathing problems and paralysis occur (National Institute of Neurological Disorders and 
Stroke, 2016).

Blood transfusion and organs transplant 
Another issue that is increasing due to dengue and Zika diffusion is the spread of the 
infection through transfusions and organ transplants. According to the American 
Association of Blood Bank, dengue is categorized as 1 of the 3 priority emerging problem 
(Stramer SL, Hollinger FB, Katz LM, 2009).  
This problem arises because those diseases, in some half cases, are asymptomatic and a 
person, therefore, can be unaware of having one of them (CDC, 2014a). Furthermore, 
being considered until now “neglected diseases”, there has been a lack of diagnostics 
for them.   
As a precaution, the Canadian blood and blood products department proposed to 
postpone donations from donors who have been outside Canada and Europe (Botelho, 
2016).  
Life threatening consequences  
The major problem of dengue is the possibility of further reinfections.  
Having the virus various serotypes, a person that is affected by one of those becomes 
immune only to that specific serotype and not to the others. In other words, a person could 
be infected four times by the four different serotypes of dengue.  
 This is the key issue that leads to harmful complications and in severe cases death. The 
basic principal behind it is that in case of secondary infection there is the highest risk of 
getting severe dengue (previously called dengue hemorrhagic fever).  The probability of 
reaching this stage, showed by Table 2, follows an unusual trend that it is still unexplained

CHAPTER 2 – DIAGNOSTICS PRODUCTS  
Zika and Dengue are two viruses that are affecting million people every year and their 
spread, according to the statistics, is hiking (World Health Organization, 2009). The 
newness of those wide-spread viruses and the absence of treatments to cure them 
restrains the approaches to limit their diffusion.  
At the present time, diagnostics is the most used tool to keep Flavivirus under control. 
Through good diagnostic it is possible to avoid and reduce both the diffusion and 
outbreaks with unpleasant consequences.  
The salient key point is comprehending various detection technologies available on the 
market and the different platforms they belong to.  
To be able to introduce the position of Blusense Diagnostics within the market, it is essential 
to first present those concepts. This point is crucial to understand the value proposition of 
the company.  
2.1 Detection technologies 
The thee detection technologies, available on the market for diagnostics, are PCR 
(Polymerase Chain Reaction), ELISA method and rapid tests. Those techniques belong to 
the two major categories of diagnostics: molecular and immunoassay tests as Figure 7 
shows.

Point-Of-Care diagnostics  
Diagnostics, so far, has always been a tradeoff between accuracy, cost and time. This 
becomes a limit for situations where it is necessary to have accurate results in a short time 
that does not require high investments in laboratory machine.  
“Point of Care Testing (POCT) is defined as medical diagnostics testing performed outside 
the clinical laboratory in close proximity to where the patient is receiving care.”(C. Health, 
2015) 
Dengue and Zika, for example, are spread in zones where there is a lack of sanitary 
infrastructures and hospitals are located mainly in city centers. People who live in rural 
areas do not have access to medical facilities and banal illnesses can turn out to be fatal.  
Measuring disease-specific biomarkers quickly, accurately and at the point-of-care would 
promote early diagnosis and appropriate access to medical care. WHO estimates it could 
help reducing fatality rate to below 1% (WHO, 2012). Precise quantification will then be 
not only desirable, but it’s likely to become required, as widely reported by global health 
organizations.  
POC solutions mean low operational costs, extremely user-friendly format and long term 
stability.  
In regards to the infective diseases market, there is no POC device able to combine the 
accuracy of laboratories analysis and the rapidity of rapid tests.  
Blusense Diagnostics developed a new portable device with unique characteristics able 
to overpass the tradeoffs previously mentioned. Through a new technology, the company 
produced a POC device able to provide high quality results, in extremely short time with 
moderate costs.

CHAPTER 3 – OVERVIEW OF BLUSENSE DIAGNOSTICS 
3.1 History 
Blusense Diagnostics is a biotech spin-out from the Technical University of Denmark (DTU) 
born in 2014. The business is built around a patented novel immunoassay 7detection 
technology through the use of magnetic nanoparticle physics, microfluidics and Blu-Ray 
optics.  
In other words, Blusense enables diagnosis of several diseases just through a single drop of 
blood (BluSense Diagnostics).

Blusense has been working on developing and designing its POCT solution specifically 
targeting its use in sub-tropical regions. Through multiple visits and interviews with potential

Blusense technology leverages on mass-produced electronics components from Blu-Ray 
industry, in partnership with one of the largest hardware producer of the world, Quanta 
Group in Taiwan, who also acted as major investor in the first equity round. The reader 
does not need active components such as pumps, and keep a very simple and solid 
engineering design. 3G/Wi-Fi-assisted automated data reporting features ensure that 
every single device is constantly monitored, allowing Blusense service assistance to 
identify, real-time, when one device is malfunctioning. Embedded connectivity functions 
will furthermore allow geo-timed reporting of serological results and confirmed positive 
cases, providing governments and NGOs with statistical data to address issues such as 
positive cases mis-reporting, and outbreaks counter-measures policies implementation. 
Furthermore, R&D activities have shown the potential of the opto-magnetic readout 
method to perform Zika RNA molecular detection via isothermal LAMP amplifications, 
paving the way towards the implementation of a “total-tracking-tool” for infectious 
diseases, able to run quantitative measurements of viral load, virus antigen, and 
serological titers. 
Blusense Diagnostics developed an innovative, simple and scalable blood testing 
technology. It is the first one-drop-of-blood quantitative dengue and Zika POCT tests.

CHAPTER 4 – GLOBAL DISTRIBUTION  
“Born global (BG) firms are recognized for their early internationalization and fast growth, 
which markedly sets them apart from the other types of new ventures” (Knight & Cavusgil, 
2004). 
This upstream trend presents various obstacles because in general new ventures are 
characterized by resources shortages and going global since the beginning requires 
assets. But it is proven that despite difficulties, many start-ups made it to gain competitive 
advantage.   
 
Which is the secret to overstep those obstacles? 
 
Even if it is a new trend starting from abroad and it presents obstacles, it can work out if 
the market that the company is addressing is, for instance, a niche market where the 
competition is limited and there is room to gain market share.  
The nanotech sector is in expansion but within the diagnostics, especially in the field of 
dengue and Zika, is still a niche market and the technologies available on the market do 
not fulfil the market’s needs. The combination of niece markets in the specific field of 
dengue and Zika within developing countries is a good combination that gives Blusense 
a good chance to enter the business.  
 
Furthermore, networks are dynamic source of resources, present at every stage of the 
firm’s development, that supports the BG’s growth and internalization. Entrepreneurs, for 
instance, would not go further without the backing of stakeholders. Having connections is 
essential to acquire information that otherwise could not be obtained anywhere else.  
 
How many times, did you get an information from someone that otherwise it would have 
not been accessible? 
 
Most of the guidelines come from direct communication with people and unwritten 
information emerge as well. Networking then is a super powerful mean to overstep the 
resources limitations because through it often appears new solutions to apparent 
problems (Globals, Sepulveda, & Gabrielsson, 2013). 
In business it works in the same way as in common life. The more network you grow, the 
more information you get that gives you unique opportunities. Expanding a network

Introduction 
Malaysia, as other emerging markets, has its healthcare system divided in public and 
private structures. Roughly 65% of the Malaysian health facilities are run by the private 
system, with the remaining 35% belonging to the National healthcare system. The entire 
healthcare system is centrally administered by MOH (Ministry of Health).  
The public sector works pretty much like Brazil: every citizen has free access to public 
facilities and practitioners. The small difference here is that even if public healthcare is 
free, patients are charged for a fee-medical visit, that is roughly around (0.30-1.20 US$). 
But many patients do not pay it, probably due to lack of money and this contributes to 
the inefficiency of the service.  
 
As the Brazilian public sector the issue that arises in Malaysia as well is the long waiting time 
to see a doctor. For this reason, people are indirectly pushed to access private 
healthcare. In Malaysia there are two possible scenarios to access the private sector: 
either a patient is covered by a private healthcare insurance or pays individually each 
treatment/consultation. 
The public sector is supported by government funds plus citizens´ taxes while the private 
is financed by the private healthcare insurance or OOP (Out-Of-Pocket) payments.

Secondary healthcare centers (District Centers) 
District Centers are often small-to-medium size hospitals or clinics located in suburban 
areas of the country. These facilities have more limited diagnostics capabilities than 
reference centers, but usually have still availability of ELISA-based kits, which are used as 
confirmation of dengue cases when rapid tests give a positive response. Both serology 
(IgG/IgM) and NS1 are available, giving District Centers a quasi-complete dengue 
diagnostics capability. But they do not have access to virus identification, limiting the 
ability to understand the viral strain of the infection. Usually equipped with qualified 
doctors, these Centers have capacity to diagnose and treat dengue, and are often used 
as reference point by the Primary Health Centers.  
 
Reference Centers (National) 
Reference Centers are the largest clinical diagnostics facilities in the country. Often are 
large public or private hospitals with centralized laboratories, teams of epidemiologists, 
availability of blood banks, large volume of samples, and access to complete laboratory 
tools for multiple diagnostics operations. These Centers are usually located in central areas 
of large cities, and are often used as reference point for the national clinical diagnostics. 
These facilities are used by pharma companies and vaccine makers to implement their 
clinical trials in cooperation with doctors, as it’s here that there is the largest turnover of 
patients and samples. These laboratories have complete dengue diagnostics capabilities, 
usually including virus identification tools such as PCR and virus culture facilities. Rapid tests 
are available and often used, but always supported by other methods as the ELISA-based 
technology for confirmation of positive cases.

mainly to private facilities where the turnover of patients is higher and more tests can be

5.2 Supply chain considerations  
Through the analysis of the distributive channels, it emerged that it is fundamental to make 
some considerations in regards to the supply chain.  
By definition, the supply chain is the entire network that directly or indirectly provides input 
for the creation and sale of a product or service (Business Dictionary, 2016b). It comprises 
the supply of raw materials, machineries for the production, warehouses to store products 
as well as distributions centers that bring the product to the end costumers.  
The analysis of the supply chain is a paramount for every company because it underlies 
value-chain: each stakeholder within the chain adds value to the product. In most of the 
cases, the producer without the engagement to several actors has no ability to give the 
customers what they want, at the right time, at the right place and at the right price.  
Through the optimization of the supply chain, it is possible to out-compete and be 
outstanding in the market. Various elements, as price for example, are important factors 
that affect the accomplishment.

a matter of defining which markets to enter and which distributive method to use, but also 
how to deal with the stakeholders in the chain.

5.3 Pricing strategy  
An important aspect to define for Blusense is the strategy to approach the market. It is 
important to investigate how the competitors do it and figure out the best method to 
enter the diagnostics market.  
As already mentioned the POC market within the Zika and dengue sector is completely 
empty because the competitors are proposing products with different features. Elisa or 
CPR methods are the most accurate but also more expensive and time consuming to 
make whereas rapid tests have the advantage to be portable, quick but on the other 
hand are not that accurate.  
Blusense then is in the middle between those two categories but directly competes with 
the rapid test industry because it has the most features in common. Since it is well defined 
who Blusense has to compete with, it is vital to inspect in depth the various details of the 
business model that competitors adopt to be able to choose wisely a strategy to 
outcompete.  
 
Pricing strategies play an important role in the business because it defines right away the 
kind of product you are offering. Seen from the customers’ point of view, the price defines 
the quality of an item and as consequence its value.  
 
Every company then has to choose a target (quality range) and adapt its price according 
to it but also according to the competitors. Only in the case a company enters a totally 
new market and is the first one that can have the freedom to choose its price otherwise 
a big analysis has to be done. 
For Blusense the target is the same as rapid tests and it is fundamental to keep them as a 
reference point. The price of the competitors has then to be a benchmark to make sure 
to not over price the product.  
The main difference with the competitors is that they just sell tests that are mono use 
(consumables) and their price range is between 10-20 US $ per test (Bosco, Filippo & 
Samuilova, 2016).

It has been investigated a way to “hide” or make it less evident the price of the reader to

The option that showed to be appealing is the razorblade model: “A business model which 
involves selling an item at a low price or for free, sometimes even at a loss, in order to sell 
complementary or consumable products later” (Business Dictionary, 2016a). 
This business model was first originated by Gillette, the most famous brand of razors. It 
started selling the head of the razor at a moderate price without getting any profit (or 
extremely low) and selling the blades, the consumables, at a profitable price and making 
money out of it.  
The concept is extremely simple: 
1-time purchase of the durable item + multiple purchases of the consumables 
This concept has been spread to other companies as Nespresso: they sell the coffee-
machine at a moderate price and they charge for the coffee pods.  
Blusense then should charge for the cartridges and should sell the machine at at a low 
price. But the lowest price of the machine would still be high for the market segment 
Blusense aims at.  
How to “trick” the price of the reader then? 
A satisfying solution would be to split the price of the machine under a subscription to 
camouflage the entire sum.

6.1 Discussion 
After having deeply evaluated the supply chain, dug in the distributive channels and 
having done monetary evaluation of the various market opportunities it is time to make 
the point of the situation.  
Blusense during the next year is going to be ready to enter the market and in the coming 
months is going to take decisions. Through this work, the company has a support in the 
decision-making process that has been analyzed.  
Through the network acquired during the last year, Blusense gained information about 
those markets. For young start-ups that are willing to be Born Global it is crucial to exploit 
the connections that have been gathered from the market environment. Born Globals 
are companies that go directly international instead of expanding first locally and then 
abroad. Going global from the beginning is extremely challenging especially for start-ups 
for a lack resources and knowledge, but a good usage of the network can overcome the 
obstacles to reach out the market.  
 
Since Blusense has a good network both in Malaysia and Brazil, should it approach both 
markets? 
Through the analysis that has been done, it is quite risky to choose to enter just one country 
to get the business started. The more the risk is spread among various options, the less 
extensive the impact is going to be.  
 
Choosing a multiple entry strategy instead of a single one, has showed to be beneficial to 
limit the risk of uncertainty that markets have especially when the know-how is scarce (Li 
& Dimitratos, 2014).  
Since the Malaysian market by itself offers a narrow business as well as a reduced income 
it is imperative to get Brazil on board.   
 
The Malaysian opportunity offers a small-scale market that is certainly a good start but by 
itself is not sustainable. Entering the Brazilian market would significantly expand the market 
possibilities. Brazil rather than being a broad market, also embraces both dengue and Zika 
tests.  
In other words, if the business in one country does not take off, there is still the other one 
that can offer good opportunities.

The Elisa Method  
The ELISA method runs the diagnostics with serum, a portion of the blood contained in the 
plasma. 
Once a sample with venous blood is taken, the 
serum can be obtained. The container with the 
whole blood has to be insert in the centrifuge for 
about 20 minutes to let the plasma separate from the 
rest, as  
Figure 45 illustrates (Lo, 2015a). Subsequently by 
removing clotting factors 10  from the plasma, the 
serum is obtained.  
 
 
Figure 45: Blood composition (Lo, 2015b) 
 
Even if there are four variants of the ELISA method (direct, indirect, sandwich and 
competitive), the basic steps can be summarized by those steps (N. I. of Health, 2015) :  
1. The specific antigen is added in the walls of the 96-well plate 
2. The patient´s serum is insert and if the primary antibody (IgM) against that specific 
antigen is present, they are going to bind together.  
3. A wash is done to eliminate antibodies and other particles that did not bind.  
4. A secondary antibody, with a colorimetric tag attached, is added and it is going 
to stick to the previous antibody IgM.  
5. A second wash is done to remove unbounded elements. 
6. A substrate is added and secondary antibody with colorimetric tag will cause a 
color change.  
In other words, if the target element is present, it is going to create some bindings and a 
change in color is going to show the positive result.  
The previous steps explained how to use the ELISA method to detect an antigen. But as 
mentioned before, this technique can also be used to identify an antibody. Figure 46

Technology and Patent  
The key technological innovation at the core of Blusense is based on the immuno-
magnetic agglutination format and opto-magnetic readout which is part of Blusense 
proprietary IP. Commercial superparamagnetic nanoparticles (size below 200 nm) are 
coated with coupled anti-zika NS-1 antibodies (for NS-1 detection) or ZIKV envelope 
proteins (in case of ZIKV IgG/IgM detection) capable to form sandwich agglutination in 
presence of the target analyte. An AC magnetic field is then used to force nano-clusters 
rotation, which causes a temporal scattering cross-section variation which is optically 
measured using a low-cost and high-tech Blu-Ray laser unit, and a photodetector. The 
phase difference between the applied field and the modulated transmitted light through 
the nanoparticles precisely related with the amount of target analyte (ZIKV antigen or 
specific antibodies). The readout system is implemented on a plastic microfluidic cartridge 
based on centrifugal microfluidics, which allow fast blood plasma separation, metering, 
mixing and resuspension of dry nanoparticles without the need of external pumps nor any 
user sample-preparation. 
The technology is protected by two patent application. It is aimed to file at least 3 new 
patents applications related to the biochemistry and microfluidic parts:
