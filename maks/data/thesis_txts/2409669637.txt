I 
ABSTRACT 
 
 
 
With the increase of renewable energy targets set by governments to contribute to a greener world, 
the applications and the development practices of wind energy development have become more 
critical. At this point, to ensure mutually convenient processes, activities and deliveries during wind 
power installation projects, both developers and local communities play critical role for achieving 
success. Pleasing the local public takes one step forward, especially for the onshore wind energy 
development activities, due to the high interaction and observance possibility of the wind turbines 
with the local inhabitants. According to Peter A.Strachan, one of the most critical aspects, which can 
be used to attempt for explaining the community acceptance in a specific wind energy development 
in UK, are sorted as scale of the project, type of the devleoper and level of community 
involvement/ownership engagement. Even though the model, designed by Strachan, focuses on UK, 
it was also used with broader focus to elaborate the community accceptance concept by Geraint Elllis 
and Gianluca Feraro’s JRC Science for Policity report on social acceptance of wind energy. 
 
In this thesis, three cases which challenges the Strachan’s model were  studied. The subject, 
community acceptance and its sub-objections/aspects, which were extracted from Rolf 
Wüstehnhagens’s work on Social acceptence of renewable energy innovation, will be studied 
toghether with TR-AC Paradigm and Stakeholder power/interst matrix to carlify how the additional 
local acceptance aspects can contribute to the attempt of Strachan’s model on explaining the 
community acceptance of a specific wind energy project.  
 
Results showed the deficiency for the attempts of Strachan’s Model to explain the community 
acceptance levels for the studied cases in this thesis. To advance the model and increase the relaible 
applicapility for broader range of projects, aspects such as; preuse of the project area, developers’ 
initial approach with locals, the created trust and information level, landscape characteristics, 
perception of community development, wind energy experience of local municipality and normative 
impacts of local individuals, were suggested to be joint to the model. This does not guarantee the 
absolute success of the explanation attempts of the model but undoubtly increases it`s capability.

1   INTRODUCTION 
 
 
 
1.1 
Focus of the thesis  
 
 
The focus of the thesis will be to analyse the reliability of Strachan’s scale-engagement trade-offs 
model with studying three completed wind energy projects in Denmark and to propose improvement 
areas for the Strachans’s method by suggesting to include additional community acceptance factors 
or situations, in case the current included aspects of the model will be found as lacking. 
 
Since the model provides the examination method for community acceptance level of specific wind 
energy projects, developer’s unique practices and observed unique situations/factors during the cases 
will be tried to be found to understand related factors that can have direct or indirect impact of 
community acceptance. These mentioned practices and situations are believed that they create value 
for tangible and intangible factors that are concerned by the local context, which allows both parties 
in the projects to achieve the high satisfaction. 
 
Overall, the thesis will therefore focus on clarifying the match between characteristics of community 
acceptance and the provided values from the developers or unique situations, which will provide 
supplementary data to be used for contributing the Strachan’s model, which attempts to explain 
community acceptance of a specific wind project based on following factors; size of project 
developer, scale of the project, local ownership engagement. 
 
After the data gathered for the mentioned cases, Strachan’s theory trade-offs model in community 
energy will be studied and the reliability of the model will be discussed. The final outcome is intent 
to advance the Strachan’ model to provide a better understanding and recommendations to wind 
energy developers for achieving the best fit between the project development and the local context, 
for the forthcoming wind power development activities as well as to contribute the literature by 
examining the renewable energy focused project management theories’ reliability and applicability 
for other wind energy contexts.

1.2 
Definition of the problem and scope of the thesis  
 
 
Social acceptance is one of the key factors during wind power development and researches indicate 
that it could block the use of wind power potential thus, has a big impact on reaching the green 
electricity production targets set by different countries (Geraint & Ferraro 2016). It is also known that 
the level of social acceptance is very dependent on wide range of factors including, perception of 
wind energy, perception of benefit distribution, project characteristics, degree of public involvement, 
impact on biodiversity etc. All of these shows that the social acceptance of wind energy is a complex 
matter, which cannot be addressed through simple suggestions/practices such as more consultation, 
more distributed cost benefits or just with more community involvement. Therefore, the needed 
practices or needed combined acts for providing higher social acceptance thus, higher and healthier 
engagement between wind energy systems with communities and citizens (community acceptance), 
needs to be clarified and designed successfully (Geraint & Ferraro 2016).  
 
Why the social acceptance theory in renewable energy is needed to asses the Strachan’s trade-offs 
model ? 
 
According to the literature, scale-engagement trade-offs in community energy by Peter A. Strachan, 
the projects that are developed by multi-national power companies are expected as less likely be 
successful, comparing the local cooperative, locally owned, national cooperative and part-local 
ownership based projects. However, considering the two successfully completed projects namely, 
Klim, Østrup, which are not developed by local society or cooperatives, but achieved to provide high 
local acceptance, brings the questions of how the developers succeeded it ? At the same time, the 
third project Hvide Sande, which is a project developed by local foundation, is considered in another 
unique category and this project also suitable for being studied to assess the reliability of the 
Strachan’s model, since the ownership is not directly belongs to the local community but the created 
local acceptance is high. Therefore, the thesis will be an empirical study, that focuses on these three 
already completed wind power projects and will try to find out the practised unique approaches by 
the developers and unique situations, which made impact on local acceptance levels of these 
individual wind power projects. Since, the social acceptance of energy, which will be defined in the 
following pages, is very detailed concept thus, require way more time than what is given for a thesis 
project, its sub factor “community acceptance” will be the main focus area, in which the three cases 
will be associated and studied.

1.3 
Research question  
 
 
The research question of the thesis has been re-shaped several times since the beginning of the project 
in order to contribute the most value for the current literature of community acceptance of wind 
energy. The intention of the thesis is to study the reliability of the Strachan’s model, scale-
engagement trade-offs in community energy, and its detailed relationship with the community level 
of social acceptance theory. In Strachan’s model, it is indicated that for developing the soft energy 
paths (such as renewables), the level of local acceptance decreases if the scale of the project and the 
size of the developer increase along with community based development, in other words, community 
focused ownership/involvement engagement decrease. Therefore, the aim of this thesis is to observe 
the insight of different completed projects and to study the Strachan’s trade-offs model along with 
community level social acceptance theory, to examine the reliability and applicability of the 
Strachan’s model. This will be made by studying the gathered knowledge from data sources who 
were involved whether with the development processes or with research activities to the studied 
projects. To conclude, the Main Research Question (MRQ) of this thesis can be examined from 
following lines; 
 
 
MRQ 
 
How can additional aspects contribute to Strachans trade-offs model in the attempt to explain 
community acceptance?

1.5.1 Wind2050 multidisciplinary study on local acceptance and wind 
power development  
 
 
With the increase of worldwide wind power development targets to replace the conventional energy 
production systems with the renewables, the importance of practicing more effective project 
development approaches as well as to understand the expectations of local citizens became more 
essential to complete the upcoming projects successfully. At that point, it should be aware that it is 
very compelling process to please all the stakeholders within a wind power development project. 
Therefore, the momentous factor, to achieve the best fit for different stakeholders/actors, is needed, 
meaning that the acts, practises, regulations and other related factors should be aligned to provide 
maximum benefit and satisfaction for all of the participants in a wind power development project. 
(Borch 2014a). The assertive targets that Danish Government set to increase country’s electricity 
production ratios from renewables by 2020, 2030 and 2050, requires faster, more efficient project 
development practices as well as need high acceptance from local citizens. However, even though 
Denmark has a strong Wind Power development reputation, there are still lack of knowledge 
regarding the factors and decision that shape local acceptance. The reason of oppositions changes for 
different regions and it can be even observed as municipality level such as Esbjerg Municipality, who 
cancels all onshore wind power development as of March 2017 (Ray W 2017). At this point, the 
question comes to minds as what are the reasons behind those decisions and what values should have 
supposed to be delivered to the local decision makers and local society, who shows resistance for 
such project developments. Therefore, to find out the essential values to deliver during different wind 
energy projects are vital and needs to be studied with different local context. Additionally, it is also 
mostly unknown how public decision, project planning and policy measures can be used to reduce 
conflicts during wind power development, unlike the times when these factors block the project

2.1.1 Types of objects 
 
 
Within the social acceptance of wind power, it is important to underline the different objects, which 
are most of the time considered as same thus, may cause misunderstanding during the assessments. 
A good example can be given with the low wind energy development ratio of the countries, where 
the high public support for the wind energy development is observed according to the conducted 
opinion surveys (Wolsink 2012). This controversy can be explained with the 2 misconceptions. The 
first one is, people are generally having positive opinion about wind power, but their decision changes 
when they are confronted with a proposal. This is also so-called individual behaviour-attitude gap 
and can be exemplified with many observations even from the daily life such as: most of the people 
are very positive about eating healthy but when it comes to practicing the idea, the number decreases 
dramatically. As like the smokers, who are aware that the smoking is unhealthy and dangerous, but 
when it comes to stop smoking, the ratio of quit is not that high (OPTUM 2014). Moreover, a similar 
example can be given with the intention of starting to work-out is mostly ends with siting home and 
watching TV. These are the essential realities that may prove that the intention to change is not 
enough (OPTUM 2014).

2.1.1.1 
Social gap and “Not In My Backyard” (NIMBY) 
phenomenon 
 
 
The assumed Gap is described due to the fact that the observed general acceptance of wind energy 
development, but still a low ratio of positive decision to wind farm development (Wolsink 2012). To 
tackle the gap, the concept is defined with two different level of gaps namely, social and individual 
gap. The mentioned social gap concept indicates the high level of public support for wind energy in 
public opinion surveys, yet low success rate in planning application. Whereas the individual gap is 
described with the positive perception of individuals, local residents to wind power as a concept but 
opposing the specific wind farm development projects, which is mostly tried to be explained with not 
in my backyard (NIMBY) objection, but the absolute reliability of the relation between NIMBY and 
individual gap is still not hundred percent clear (Wolsink 2012).  
 
Since, some of the reason statements, which NIMBY concept includes, are selfishness, conservatism, 
unwillingness to cooperate or ignorance of environmental issues. The appropriateness of the usage of 
this objection during the wind energy development activities are considered very negatively since the 
mentioned statements can create more resistance within the society and may cause uncontrollable 
local opposition movements. Moreover, besides the reliability of this label has been criticized since 
1980’s, not having many studies with the observation of an individual who opposed for a backyard 
installation but not to practice any opposition act for developments in somewhere else, decreases the 
reliability of the NIMBY Label significantly (Wolsink 2012). 
 
When it comes to some of the observed reasons of social acceptance, it can be described with two 
arguments; the reliability of  general measurability of  social acceptance and lack of knowledge usage 
from the conducted local acceptance researches (Zaunbrecher & Ziefle 2016). Even though, several 
models and roadmaps have been provided in the literature to tackle with the mentioned gap of social 
acceptance, it is very early for talking about the reliability and the success ratios of the delivered 
outputs.

2.3 
Dimension of social acceptance of energy innovations 
 
 
Since, the importance of social acceptance within a STS was explained in the previous pages, it is 
therefore very significant to clarify the term, social acceptanc, and its current branches within the 
literature.  In one of the latest reports, specifically focus on social acceptance on wind power projects, 
the term, social acceptance is quoted and then defined as following (Geraint & Ferraro 2016), 
  
“a favourable or positive response (including attitude, intention, behaviour and - where 
appropriate - use) relating to proposed or in situ technology or social technical system 
by members of a given social unit (country or region, community or town and 
house hold, organisation) (Upham et al. 2015)“ 
 
The term, social acceptance, in energy projects and its implication analysis were first tried to be 
delivered during the projects of nuclear power infrastructure, waste facilities and hydroelectric 
centrals. With the start of massive development of renewables in the last 1990s, it was also started to 
be considered as a critical factors, thus the implication studies were carried out also during the wind 
energy development projects (Geraint & Ferraro 2016). 
  
The way of conducting related analysis and considering its implications during the wind energy 
development projects to understand the social acceptance context, has been improved and evolved by 
years. However, there are still quiet amount of debates and discussions on the reliability of the way 
of understanding social acceptance within the concept of Socio Technical System (Geraint & Ferraro 
2016)  
 
Additionally, even though it is known that the current social acceptance analysis models for energy 
project lacking on or create confusion of very wide range of social subjects, the models are still 
preferred to be used in order to understand and approach the current social subjects (Geraint & Ferraro 
2016).

namely; community acceptance, socio-political acceptance and market acceptance.  
 
The first sub-branch, socio political acceptance covers the upper level of acceptance for example the 
state incentive or regulations, the nation's opinion about the renewable energy and its development 
etc. The second one, market acceptance, reflects to the market adoption of an innovation, product, 
solution or technology. This branch also includes the topics, which are related with willingness of 
financial institutes on investing or lending against these mentioned technology, manufacturing 
opportunities of the technology. Moreover, for consumers’ engagement in the created market, driven 
by the mentioned technologies, are also considered as a subject in this sub-branch. The last sub-

energy development. At the same time, since the productivity of the turbines is continuously 
improved, the higher and longer bladed turbines are proposed to be used for saving the space or 
replacing with the ones shorter and older technology. To both increase the visual impact and the 
aerodynamic success, the longitude and the shapes of the turbine blades are also in continuous change.

The term stakeholder, is defined by project management institute (PMI) as “an individual, group, or

may be positively or negatively affected by the performance or completion of the project” (Rose

two highly respected organisations, namely; Project Management Institute and The International 
Project Management Association (IPMA), indicates that the success ratio and high achievement 
during the projects have direct relationship with detailed stakeholder analysis and understanding their 
impacts (Aragonés-Beltrán et al. 2017).  
 
Why the stakeholder power/interest matrix is usefull to observe the project ecosystem and how thıs

2.3.3    Tech-Reg and Appropriate Colloboration Paradigm  
 
Before concluding the literature review, it is very essential to elaborate the need of combining the 
appropriate collaboration with local people with technical and regulatory based facts since wind 
power development activities includes high level of need for technical inputs, regulation/legislation 
based decisions and processes as well as high acceptance rate to achieve the overall success (Walker 
et al. 2015). Technical-regulatory, shortly tech-reg (TR) trajectory, indicates the two types of main 
needs during the management of environmental concerns, ecosystems, natural resources, etc.  
 
The first needed norm, tech, accommodates the needed technical solution for the problem and the 
second norm, reg, stands for the perceived need for regulations to implement the mentioned technical 
solution feasibly (Walker et al. 2015). As an example, the process of maintaining the water quality in 
a certain area mainly requires implementing suitable technical solution and ensuring to fulfil related 
regulations to implement this technical solution. Whereas, when there is a project, requires less TR 
approach, the appropriate collaboration (AC) with the municipality as well as the local public plays 
a momentous role to complete the intended project or process with high success. At this point, the 
importance of both TR and AC shows itself, since the wind power development activities include 
both of these mentioned factors and requires unifying TR and AC approaches. The main reason of 
this need can be explained with the characteristics of the wind power projects, which are classified 
as socio-Technical Systems, where social and technical norms are required to be merged as high level, 
to ensure the successful implementation and high achievement (Walker et al. 2015). The relation 
between appropriate collaborative approach and technical regulatory approach can be examined from 
the figure 10 below.

3   METHOD 
 
 
 
In this chapter, the conducted research approach for this thesis is described with the explanation of 
the question why this method was chosen. Additionally, the research design is explained and 
presented with visuals to provide clear understanding. The introduction of research approach is 
followed by research design, data collection and the method criticism. 
 
 
 
3.1 
Research approach  
 
 
The research approach of this thesis has been chosen as “a case study research”, where the details of 
individual wind energy projects have been questioned and studied to answer the research question 
and to give further recommendations.  As it can be known, the case study research approach aims to 
study on complex phenomenon, with the help of studying several events, its participants and the 
relationship between all these participants. Thus, it may lead for having more experience on the 
mentioned phenomena or increase the reliability of the previously made statements and arguments on 
this phenomena during previous researches (Yin 2009).  
 
The reason why case study approach was selected is because of the intention of the thesis is to test 
and refine the Strachans’s trafe-off model with the help of community acceptance theory based on 
the gathered data from the studied cases.  It is critical here to mention that, since the gathered data 
during the case study approach often classified as subjective due to fact that the gathered data are 
dominantly qualitative. Therefore, it is critical to process the gathered data and the variables inside 
as clear as possible, to be more reliable with the conclusions and arguments at the end (Yin 2009). In 
order to ensure the reliability of this study, three case studies are studied by conducting  six interviews. 
Additionally, to test the gathered data from the mentioned interviewees, official documents from the 
responsible municipalities were also gathered, analysed and compared.

3.1.1 Case selection  
 
 
The studied cases in this thesis were chosen by considering some of the criterion, which is believed 
that they increase the reliability of the research and to allow found conclusions to be used for 
relatively wider literature.  
 
Since, first aim was to investigate local acceptance during wind energy development activities, and 
the Strachan’s model was decided to be used as the backbone of the thesis, the focus aspect of the 
model was considered in the first step to select the studied cases. 
 
Scale of the project, size of the developer, local ownership engagement and the local acceptance were

of the Strachan’s model, the cases were chosen as different as possible from each other’s in terms of 
mentioned knowable aspects of Strachan’s model.  
 
Choosing cases as different as possible from each other was the first intention, since to do so, 
increases the reliability of the empirical study as well as the possibility to contextualize the findings.  
 
Hvide Sande Wind Park, which is a small scale, not developed by big company and the western part 
of the Denmark was chosen. The other cases were chosen from the northern part of the Denmark. For

3.4 
Method critisizm & limitations  
 
 
As all the other research methods, naturally, case study approach also has some limitations. First of 
all, generalizing the findings at the end of this thesis, would not be proper, since the number of found

more knowledge regarding the community acceptance of the studied projects to increase the 
reliability of the findings, it would be naturally more healthy and reliable to use much more sources

Case Study 3 -  Hvide Sande Wind Park 
 
 
Hvide Sande is a small fishing town, located in the western part of Jylland with approximately 3100 
inhabitants.  The area was firstly used as a water canal by sailors to reach the Rinkøbing Fjord. Due 
to the changing water depth of both sides of the canal during the year, the Danish Government in the 
beginning of 1930s, was installed a sluice and a lock. The drainage slice has been used to regulate 
the water level as well as the salt intensity in Rinkøbing fjord and the lock has been used for two-
sided passage activates between the sea and the Rinkøbing Fjord (Forlaget VITA 2014). 
 
The area became more attractive for fishermen after the installation of the drainage sluice and the 
lock. Even though, the Danish government at that time was intent to move the fishermen out from the 
area, the tried approaches were not successful and the government was decided to start collecting 
taxes from the fishermen, who located in the area at that time (Davidsen 2017) This was the short 
history about the small fisherman town, which became one of the biggest harbour in the municipality 
of Rinkøbing and Skjern. To develop the harbour for new needs such as more water depth and more 
suitable shipworms for the usage of bigger and newer ships and yachts, an investment capital was 
needed. After the decision was made about to develop a wind park, which will support the evolution 
of the harbour, the 3 Vestas V112 Trubines with 3.3 MW power output were installed to the beach of 
Hvide Sande.

Obtained results and data analysis for the case 3 – Hvide

The results of the award winning project, Hvide Sande, underlines the importance of the place 
attachment and its positive impact on local community’s attitude to wind energy development in the 
area. Since, the locals’ owsnership feeling and place attachment for the town have them feel very 
positive about all types of development in the area if the intent of the development is to benefit the 
town, especially the heart of the town, the harbour.  
 
Unlike the neutral manner of the letters, interviewees underlined the place attachment, normative, 
beliefes and emotions of locals as prominent subjects, which had very positive impacts of the 
development of Hvide Sande Wind Park. 
 
The population of the town is approximately 3100 people thus, even though the submitted 36 letters 
talks mainly negative concerns about the project, the number of the complaint is not even 1%. 
Therefore, the project is considered as it achieved high local acceptance.  
 
The mentioned submitted complaint letters mainly talks about the negative impacts of the project on 
biodiversity, health, noise, and local landscape.  
 
The essential characteristic of the project is its development approaches. A local group who leaded 
the proejct created a foundation to develop the project, have the project ownership, which is marketed 
to the locals as the project is locally developed  but in reality, only the 20% obligatory shares belong 
to the local community. The rest of the ownership belongs to the mentioned local foundation. The 
members of the mentioned foundation and the local former mayor of Ringkobing-Skjern Municipality 
are considered as they had big normative impact on locals. It is also significant here to mention that, 
the project area is very close to where Vestas was established. Therefore, the local people may have 
the feeling of contributing to the local wind turbine developer with developing a wind energy project. 
Some of the letters critisize the tourism very strongly, where as there is another opinion talks about 
the wind turbines as they became the symbol of the town and the beach thus, attracts more tourists.

6   DISCUSSION 
 
 
 
In this chapter, the obtained results from the gathered and interpreted data in the previous chapter, 
will be discussed to answer the research question of the thesis. 
 
Before discussing the findings, it is very important to mention about the terms, social acceptability 
and social acceptance since these terms are rarely used as how they are supposed to be used. 
Additionally, connecting the community acceptance with the mentioned terms, it is necessery to draw 
the outline of this thesis one more time. To start with social acceptability of wind projects, it is defined 
by Fournis and Fortin as the processes that focus to bring togheter and construct various variables 
that are related with the three levels of phenomena, social acceptability namely; meso-political, which 
investigates the observed processes under the multi level governance of wind energy projects, macro-
economic; the relationship between national models of wind enegy and globalisation processes and 
the last one, micro-social, investigates the processes related with wind technology and the social 
dimension. Whereas the social acceptance can be defined as the results of the examined processes’ 
particular evaluation for a specific wind energy project (Fournis & Fortin 2017).  
 
At this point Rolf Wüstenhagen’s concept, which was introduced previously in this thesis step in. 
According to his approach, to study the social acceptance of a specific wind energy development, the 
three dimensional approach of the social acceptance of wind energy, in which the dimensions; socio-
political, market acceptance and community acceptance, are used. In this thesis the analysis were 
conducted focusing on the community acceptance level of social acceptance of wind energy 
(Wüstenhagen et al. 2007). 
 
The other model on community acceptance, the Strachan’s model, which also attempts to explain the 
community acceptance in wind energy projects with considering slightly different aspects, has been 
applied for three completed wind power projects in Denmark and the additional aspects for 
community acceptance were studied to observe if the Strachan’s model is reliable enough to make 
the mentioned attempt for the wind power projects and how the other excluded aspects of local 
acceptance in the literature can be benefited by Strachan’s model for its further improvements, were 
examined. 
==PAGE:96==
 
 
84
As it was described previously, prior studies have noted the importance of local acceptance in wind 
energy development and the critical role of moving to sustainable energy to generate electricity to 
reduce the current CO2 emission values worldwide. The importance of the community acceptance 
during wind energy development was consolidated with the findings, in which it is again and again 
underlined by all of the interviewees that the community acceptance aspects have big influence on 
local public and it is considered by the industry that its one of the biggest barrier during wind energy 
development. 
 
The attemp for defining the local acceptance level for the case studies in this thesis were carried out 
with the application of Strachan´s model and besides the relatively more succesfull explanation for 
Hvide Sande case, the local acceptance level in realtiy did not match with what was found with using 
the model. This, simply indicates that, the aspects in the model, do not cover all the factors that needs 
to be examined to make more realiable statements about the local acceptance levels of a specific wind 
energy project. Therefore, further investigations with social acceptance theory was decided to be 
carried out to find out what are the reasons, which made Strachan’s model insufficient for the studied 
cases in this thesis. 
 
Four out of five intervewees mentioned about the negative impact of big developer on locals, because 
of the percieved capitalistic devlopment approaches by locals. Additionally, it was also found that, 
the big size of the developer creates the perception of high-technical capability and success factors 
on local community. Since, some of the locals have concerns about the possible financial loss, which 
is believed as it can be caused by the failure of the project and they prefer that the project is intent to 
be built by big developers who have big wind energy project portfolio. All of these, indicates that the 
size of the developer, at least in Denmark has big impact of locals’ opinion to local wind energy 
development.  
 
The observations regarding the other community involvement aspects show that besides the positive 
impact of financial benefit distribution and public participation, locals also expect from the project to 
develop the local community. This, can be translated as; even though the community is invited for 
the public meetings, their opinions are asked and considered, the information level is kept high and 
the public opinion surveys are conducted, their addtitional expectation from the developer is in-direct 
==PAGE:97==
 
 
85
benefits, which means even though they do not have any shares in the project, they want to see the 
local area to be developed and become more attractive for both locals and non-locals. 
 
About the other significant finding, impact of project scale, results indicated that the scale of the 
project as not important as the overall height of the turbines. Especially, the findings from Klim 
project was very surprising, since the most of the complains were not about the number of the 
turbines, but more on the hub-heigts and their observance in even far distances. Therefore, it can be 
said that focusing on the height of the turbines are also very significiant besides the scale (the 
production output or number of turbines) of the project.  
 
Pre-use of project area, normative impacts of local individuals or foundations and initital approach 
with landowner was found as determinant factor of local acceptance for Klim project, which are 
different than the aspects of Strachan’s model. 
 
For the Østrup project, the findings indicates that the developer´s attitude and interaction with locals 
allowed project to be succeeded even though the locals do not even have 20% of the ownerhsip and 
the project has been developed by a small private company, located in the northern part of Jylland. 
 
For Hvide Sande Wind park, the most critical determinant factor for the local acceptance was found 
as the perception of community developement and benefit to the community, where the harbor of the 
small town was developed with the obtained revenue from the project.  
 
The current study also found that defining the project ecosystem helps critically to understand the 
relationships between the participants and their importance during the project. This results may be 
explained by the fact that the observation regarding the impact of local municipality during Hvide 
Sande project, where the municipality has the ownership of the project land and has raplaced the role 
of local landowner, which eased the initiation of the project but allowed the developer to be more 
carefull for their relationship with the local municipality. Therfore, using the matrix helped to observe 
the expected relationship between the develeper and the other actors and allowed to observe the projet 
ecosystem with wider perspective.

The current study also revealed several inputs to asses the TR-AC paradigm. The paradigm helped to

criterion for the classification of the projects to locate them into the TR-AC paradigm table can be 
improved with defining more applicaple criterion, which can be chosen to obtain better matches with 
various characteristics of the wind energy projects and the trajectores of the paradigm. For example, 
the scale of the project can be included to the suggested criterion, since the incerasing scale of the 
project requires more technical approach. Moreover, additional criterion about the project location 
may help to define the need of regulatory approach. As it was described in the previous analysis, the 
project area of  Hvide Sande is basicaly located in the beach, very close to the town, which makes the 
project more complicated than Østrup wind park, in terms of land based regulation related processes. 
Therefore, by considering this deficiency, and other possible definicies, it can be suggested for a 
simple revision for the sugggested criterion of the paradigm.

public and the provided perception of having them feel involved, had big impact on succeded local 
acceptance, thus the aspect, “perception of involvement”may be added into the Strachan’s model.

7   CONCLUSION 
 
 
 
The key target of this thesis is to identify the contribution of additional aspects to the attempt of 
Strachan’s trade-offs model to explain the community acceptance level of a specific wind power 
project. 
 
To study the Strachan’s model in this empirical study, qualitiative data for three completed wind 
energy project were collected by conducting inrterviews with five different data soruces by following 
two steps namely; generic talk and direct questions/answers for the impact classification of the 
community acceptance aspects. Additionally official documents were collected from the local 
municipalities to conduct analysis on the complaint aspects of community acceptance and also to 
increase the realiability of the findings from the gathered qualitiataive data. During the work, TR-AC 
paradigm, community acceptance theory in renewable enery projects and stakeholder power/interest 
matrix were applied to the cases along with the Strachan’s trade-offs model.  
 
It was found with studying Klim project that pre-use of the land, the very first approach to convince 
the local landwoners, wind power expereince of local municipality and normative impacts of the local 
individuals played key role on achieving the high local acceptance. This findings falsified the 
Strachan’s attempt on explaining community acceptance level for Klim Wind Park. Additionally, the 
expected negative impact of big size project, multinational big size developer and low community 
involvement/ownership engagement could not be observed.  
 
Studying Østrup Wind Park provided the same output as Klim Wind Park about the positive impact 
of local municiaplity with strong previous wind power expereince. The developer´s unique, cordial, 
first approach with entire local community and the high information sharing with the locals thus, 
ensured high information level were examined as the key factors, which switched up the community 
acceptance level. Based on the reults of Østrup project, the included aspects in Strachan’s model was 
found one more time as not enough to explain the community acceptance of a specific wind project 
with high accuracy rate.

92
The last case, Hvide Sande, demonstrated the power of the aspects, perception of community 
development, to increase the community acceptance level in a wind power project, which is even 
located in the middle of the beach of the small town. Being a fully indtustrialized area, where almost 
all non-residential structure has industrial architectural characteristics, was examined as another key 
factor on community acceptance in Hvide Sande project, where the acceptance of the local public 
was examined very high even though the turbines were located very close to the residential area. 
Additionally, the expected impacts of project scale and the developer type on community aceptance 
level acording to the Strachan`s model was observed relatively more accurate than the findings for 
the other studied cases in this thesis. However, the expected impact for the other aspect of Strachan’s 
model, community involvement/ownership engagement on community acceptance fell through.  
 
To conlude, since the explanation of the unexpected results can only be given for the studied cases in 
this thesis, it would not be dependable to make generalisation regarding the findings on observed 
impacts of additional community acceptane factors. Therefore, the model is suggested to be advanced 
with implementing additional aspects in it, to become more suitable and reliable to be applied for the 
broader range of wind energy projects.

7.1 
Limitations of the study 
 
 
Before concluding on this thesis, the observed limitations should be pointed out. Limited amount of 
time for a very deep field of study, community acceptance in wind energy development, was one of 
the limitations of the study since the Strachan’s model could have been assessed with using more 
theories and also more community acceptance aspects could have been included to observe their 
contributions to the model. Furthermore, only one project for each developer studied in this thesis 
due to the limited time, which restricts the trustworthniess of suggesting developer related findings 
to improve the reliablity of the Strachan’s model. 
 
Additionally, the limited budget can also be considered as another limitation of the sudy. Field trips 
were conducted with the purpose of data gethering and observation as well as to meet with the local 
municipalities, planners and developers in different regions of Jylland. However, due to the limited 
amount of budget thus shorter peroid of acoomodating in the local areas, interviews with other local 
inhabitants such as; farmers, shop owners, local workers, could not be conducted. 
 
Moreover, the used logic of summing up the gathering impact classification results to fill out the 
overall results column of the local acceptance aspects tables were conducted based on a created logic 
table, which rounded off sum of the results from the differnet sources, even though opposite opinions 
were observed about the studied aspect i.e. summing up two neutral results with one negative was 
found as zero (“0” + “0” + “-“ = 0).  
 
Furthermore, the languge can partly be considered also as a limiting factor. Since, all the interviews 
were conducted in English but most of the participants were not native English speaker, which might 
caused some small misunderstanding regarding the details of the projects and also might limit the 
extensity of the given descriptions by the interviewees. 
 
The last observed limitation is also related with languge. Since external help from a native Danish 
individual was used to make translation for the Danish written letters and documents, which were 
collected from the local municiaplitites thus, small amount of data might be lost during the translation 
processes.

7.2 
Future research recommendations 
 
 
To improve the results in the further studies, some recommendations can be given; 
 
- 
Since all of the studied cases in this thesis were challenging the Strachan’s trade-offs model, 
the further research should be conducted with the cases, which support the Strachan’s models’ 
attempt on explaining the local accetapnce level for a specific wind power project. This will 
increase both the relaibility of the model, and also the obtained findings of future researches 
can be compared with this thesis to asses the made advancement suggestions for Strachan’s 
model based on the obtained results for Klim, Østrup and Hvide Sande projects.  
 
- 
Additionally, to asses the findings of this thesis as well as to increase especially the given 
developer related suggestions for Strachan`s model, similar local acceptance aspects can be 
studied on other projects completed by same developers.
