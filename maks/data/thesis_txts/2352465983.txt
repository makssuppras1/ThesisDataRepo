Abstract 
In the present project, an overall method to create better control of the heating systems in existing 
buildings and thereby lowering the return temperature, was investigated.  
The project was based on a case study of an existing building in the municipality of Frederiksberg 
and the investigation consists of pre and post measurements of the implementation of improvements 
to the heating system. As part of the investigation, improvements of the heating system, in the form 
of new electronic thermostats from Danfoss, was implemented to create a better cooling of the 
district heating water. Furthermore, a theoretical simulation-model of the case-building was made, 
and used to investigate the dimensions of the current radiators compared to the heat demand in the 
building. 
Pre and post measurements were obtained from several different meters, such as the heat cost 
allocators, additional implemented return and room temperature loggers as well as data from the 
newly installed thermostats, which was used for continuous monitoring of errors or mismanagement 
of the heating system.  
In addition, an analysis of the economy related to the consumer and the utility company by lowering 
the return temperature of the district heating water was conducted. Based on this analysis, a new 
tariff structure was proposed to increase the incentive of the consumer to invest in improvements of 
the heating system. 
Results showed, that the return temperature from the case-building was lowered, however due to 
operational control of the new thermostats, some radiators had an increase in return temperature. 
Therefore, concluding that the system should be monitored until all errors are corrected. 
Furthermore, it was possible to use the already existing energy meter and heat cost allocators to 
indicate issues related to errors in control of the heating system in the case-building. Thus, this 
approach seems relevant to apply.  
The overall method for better control, through lowering of the return temperature of the heating 
systems, in existing buildings, can be useful. Furthermore, the method is also supported by the 
beneficial economy of the utility company. However, the consumer economy, showed a high 
payback time, thus a new tariff structure would be needed to create a higher incentive for the 
consumer.

Resume 
I nærværende projekt, vil en overordnet metode skabe en bedre kontrol af varmeanlæg i 
eksisterende bygninger og derved sænke returtemperaturen, blevet undersøgt.  
 
Projektet er baseret på et praktisk forsøg af en eksisterende bygning i Frederiksberg Kommune og 
undersøgelsen består af før og efter målinger i forbindelse med gennemførelsen af forbedringer i 
varmeanlægget. Som en del af undersøgelsen, blev varmeanlægget forbedret i form af nye 
elektroniske termostater fra Danfoss, for at kunne skabe en bedre afkøling af fjernvarmevandet. 
Endvidere blev en teoretisk simulation-model af forsøgsbygningen konstrueret for at kunne 
undersøge de nuværende radiatorer i forhold til varmebehovet i bygningen. 
Før og efter målingerne blev indhentet fra flere forskellige målere, såsom varmefordelingsmålere, 
yderligere implementeret rum- og returtemperatur målere, samt data fra de nyligt installerede 
termostater, som blev brugt til løbende overvågning af fejl eller fejlbetjening af varmeanlægget. 
 
Desuden er en analyse af økonomien, baseret på sænkning af returtemperaturen i fjernvarmenettet, 
blevet udarbejdet. Analysen er foretaget for både forbrugeren og Forsyningsselskabet. Baseret på 
denne analyse blev en ny tarif struktur foreslået, for at øge incitamentet for forbrugeren til at 
investere i forbedringer af varmeanlægget. 
Resultaterne viste, at returtemperaturen i forsøgsbygningen blev sænket, men på grund af 
fejlbetjening af de nye termostater, er få radiatorer steget i returtemperatur igen. Derfor kan det 
konkluderes, at systemet bør overvåges, indtil alle fejl er rettet.  
Desuden var det muligt at bruge de allerede eksisterende energi målere og varmefordelingsmålere 
til at angive indikationer på fejl i kontrol af varmesystemet. Derved fremstår denne fremgangsmåde 
yderst relevant at anvende. 
Den overordnede metode for at kunne sænke returtemperaturen af varmeanlæg i eksisterende 
bygninger, ved bedre kontrol af varmesystemet, er brugbar. Endvidere er metoden også understøttet 
af de økonomiske fordele for forsyningsselskabet. Forbrugerøkonomien viste dog en høj 
tilbagebetalingstid, hvorfor en ny tarif struktur vil være nødvendig at implementere, for at skabe et 
højere incitament for forbrugeren.

1 Introduction 
1.1 Background 
The 20th century has been characterized by the access and consumption of unrestrained amounts of 
coal, oil and gas. In the 21st century, a large focus is to diminish the dependency of fossil fuels and 
global warming. Therefore, other ways of meeting the worldly energy demand must be investigated, 
with a focus on renewable energy sources. 
As a part of the new energy strategy from the Danish Government (Danish government 2011), 
Denmark is aiming towards having a 100% fossil-free heating sector by 2050. Furthermore 
Copenhagen is also aiming towards being the world's first CO2 neutral capital by 2025 (CTR et al. 
2014). In order to accommodate the energy policies, the energy sector in Denmark is undergoing a 
conversion from fossil fuels to biomass fuels, which can be observed as the first step towards a 
more sustainable and fossil-free energy production. Furthermore, due to Denmark’s highly 
developed district heating grids, there is a large focus on research and further improvements of 
energy systems technologies to become more energy efficient, in order to maintain one of the 
leading positions in the field of district heating. 
The district heating network has a significant role in the transition from phasing out fossil fuels to 
introducing renewable energy sources into the heating sector. By using district heating grids for 
supplying the heating demands, large amounts of renewable energy sources can be integrated.  
By lowering the supply and return temperatures in the district heating network, it is possible to 
incorporate heat and surplus heat of greater amount from variable energy sources, which ultimately 
results in a more energy efficient system and provides benefits on a financial and environmental 
level. Additionally, the new development in terms of biomass based power plants, leads towards a 
higher exploitation of utilizing the flue gas condensation.  Therefore, a possible economic benefit 
can also be obtained if a lower return temperature can be realized. 
Low temperature district heating is a great opportunity to convert the energy supply into a 
sustainable solution, with a less costly investment, than other types of technologies. In order to 
obtain lower temperatures in the district heating grid, firstly the return temperature must be lowered 
by obtaining a better cooling of the district heating water in the buildings. Once the return 
temperature has been lowered sufficiently, it is possible to lower the supply temperature as well. If 
low temperature district heating was implemented, the temperatures would be lowered the majority 
==PAGE:11==
Page 11 of 95 
 
of the year and an increase of the temperatures would occur, at times where the demand would 
require it (Brand & Svendsen 2013). 
Still, different barriers are related to lowering the district heating temperatures. The primary barrier 
is the implementation of an improved control of heating systems in buildings in order to realise the 
possibility of having a low return temperature most of the year and still ensure thermal comfort in 
buildings. Several studies (Olsen et al. 2014; Olsen et al. 2011; Kaarup Olsen 2014) have already 
been conducted with a focus on implementing a 'complete' low temperature district heating network 
(both lowering the supply and return temperature) on single family houses, but very little research 
has been conducted regarding only lowering the return temperature in the district heating in existing 
buildings in city area. 
Recent research (Østergaard & Svendsen 2015) has found that malfunctions in the operation of the 
heating systems in the buildings are a main barrier for lowering the temperatures in the district 
heating network. Therefore, there is a need for research on how the heating systems in the buildings 
can be improved to reduce the district heating return temperature and ultimately enable the use of 
low-temperature district heating. 
The main focus for project is to investigate the possibility of lowering the return temperature from 
existing multi-storey buildings typical in city areas. Once this is achieved, implementation of low 
temperature district heating (also lowering the supply temperature) will be possible for the district 
heating grid. The primary aim of this project is to improve the operation of the existing heating 
systems to obtain a lower return temperature.  
The project will provided important knowledge on which actions firstly must be taken to lower the 
return temperature without compromising thermal comfort and eventually to implement low-
temperature district heating in existing buildings in city areas. Furthermore, the project will also 
provided knowledge on how the consumers can be motivated into actively optimizing the control of 
their heating systems. Such knowledge is crucial to help implement the already mature technology 
of low-temperature district heating in the society.  
The project was performed in co-operation with Frederiksberg Forsyning. The utility company does 
not produce the heat, but is supplied with district heating from Centralkommunernes 
Transmissionsselskab (CTR), which is an inter-municipal partnership whose purpose is to provide 
heat through their transmission network to five municipalities (Frederiksberg, Gladsaxe, Gentofte, 
Tårnby and Copenhagen). CTR purchases the heat from different sources in the area of greater 
==PAGE:12==
Page 12 of 95 
 
Copenhagen. The heat can originate from different plants, such as waste incineration, geothermal 
plants, peak or combined heat and power plants. With the current conversion from coal to biomass 
of the power plant, which supply heat to CTR, there is now greater benefits in flue gas 
condensation, which is highly dependent on the return temperature of the district heating water. 
Thus, a cheaper district heating price can be obtained if the return temperature could be lowered. 
Furthermore, an increase in transport capacity in the district heating grid can also be obtained, since 
the temperature difference is increased with reduced return temperature. 
When the biomass is to be phased out and renewable energy source, e.g. geothermal and solar and 
waste heat are integrated, there will be a need to lower the supply temperature of the district heating 
water.  
A lowering of the supply temperature in the district heating grid, can be realised by carried out 
energy savings in buildings, e.g. new windows and ventilation with heat recovery. However, what 
makes Frederiksberg Forsyning an interesting case, is the challenges which are associated with the 
limitations of the preservation of the existing facades in numerous buildings throughout the 
municipality. Therefore, if the energy savings cannot diminish the required heating load, larger 
radiator must be installed. But firstly, the possibility of lowering the return temperatures of existing 
buildings, thus establishing a better cooling of the district heating water. will be investigated. 
Furthermore, from a commercial aspect, a review of the current tariff structure of Frederiksberg 
Forsyning have been conducted and a new structure proposed, which considers the individual 
consumers and the degree of cooling of the return temperature in buildings. By creating a new tariff 
structure, it is possible to reward the consumers who actively lowers the return temperature of the 
district heating water, which would increase the efficiency in the heat production. In addition, an 
evaluation of the economic benefits has also been conducted. 
1.2 Aims 
The project is to investigated which method should be applied to create a better control of the 
heating systems in buildings and thereby lowering the return temperature. This will help 
Frederiksberg Forsyning to determine which actions must be taken in order to implement low-
temperature district heating. The basis of the investigation is conducted on a case-building and 
consists of prior and post measurements of implementing the minor improvements or adjustments to 
the heating system and a theoretical simulation model of the case-building. Only the heating system 
==PAGE:13==
Page 13 of 95 
 
of the case-building will be examined in this project. The analysis of the return temperature of the 
domestic hot water system has been conducted by PhD student Dorte Østergaard and the results will 
be presented in this report, but no further analysis will be conducted in this project. 
Furthermore, an analysis of the consumer and Frederiksberg Forsyning’s economy and an 
evaluation of the economic and environmental benefits will be conducted based on the case-
building. Lastly, the data associated with the tariff structure of Frederiksberg Forsyning will be 
assessed and a new tariff structure will be proposed.  
Based on this, the following research questions has been addressed during the project: 
• 
How can inadequate components or errors in the heating systems of existing buildings be 
identified? 
• Which process and tools can be applied to correct potential errors in control and errors in 
components of heating system in existing buildings, to ensure the possibility of lowering the 
supply and return temperatures of the district heating network?  
• Which economic and environmental benefits can be achieved by lowering the return 
temperature in the district heating network in the municipality of Frederiksberg? 
• How would a proposed new tariff structure affect the end-consumers economically?

2 State of the art 
In Denmark, ~63.2 % of the total heat demand is provided by district heating (Danish Energy 
Agency 2015) with an increasing growth according to Dyreland et al. (2008). Since, the heating 
method is so imbedded in the Danish society, research has been conducted in order to determine, 
which actions must be taken, if renewable energy sources are to becomes the primary energy 
sources in the Danish energy sector.  
As has been investigated in Ommen et al. (2016), district heating systems can have a significant 
reduction in primary energy consumption. Accordingly, a larger share of fluctuating sustainable 
energy sources, e.g. wind and solar energy are incorporated into the energy section. The study has 
considered the optimum district heating supply temperatures, in order to reconsider the current 
supply temperatures. An optimization model was applied and production technologies and network 
characteristics for Greater Copenhagen was taken into account. The results showed, that the primary 
fuel consumption was reduced 5-7% at district heating (DH) supply temperatures of 60-70 °C. This 
indicates, that there it could be worthwhile to reconsider the current DH temperatures in the grid, 
which could lead to a decrease in fuel consumption on the utility plants, but also benefit the 
consumer in terms of lower heat cost.  
In order to decrease the supply temperature, it is essential to ensure, that the buildings throughout a 
district heating grid utilize the energy at a maximum and the thermal comfort remains unchanged. 
To obtain this high efficiency it is also important that the heating systems in the buildings are 
operated with both low supply and return temperatures. 
Different studies have been conducted to determine the impact that low-temperature district heating 
(LTDH) would have on the thermal comfort of the existing buildings. According to Haarestrup et 
al. (2013), the decrease in energy consumption of existing buildings, when implementing low-
temperature district heating changed the heating profiles of the buildings, thereby diminishing 
radiator performance. The study included two case-building blocks, which was located within a 
typical urban area and represented a large share and age (1910 and 1906) of existing building types 
in Denmark. Preliminary the existing state of the buildings was determined and analysed. The study 
investigated three different stages of energy renovation, in order to determine the importance of 
renovation level in connection with providing LTDH. As a result of the investigation, indication 
show, that it is possible to apply LTDH the majority of the year without compromising the thermal 
comfort, though energy savings in buildings also needs to be implemented. 
==PAGE:15==
Page 15 of 95 
 
Earlier studies (Schmidt et al. 2013; Olsen 2013), investigate the possibility of using LTDH for 
heating of existing single family houses. The ultralow temperatures tested in these projects 
however, require the use of electricity to boost the temperatures inside the houses. This can be 
avoided if district heating supply temperatures are lowered until around 50-55°C (Yang et al. 2015). 
Such solution is already mature for the market today and technical solutions for this type of low-
temperature district heating are developed and tested in a number of Danish single-family houses. 
The projects have shown that LDTH with a supply temperature of 50-55°C is adequate for heating 
in new low energy single-family houses and single-family houses supplied by floor heating. The 
projects have also shown that the district heating temperatures in areas with existing single-family 
houses from the 1970’ies can be greatly reduced if heating installations are optimized and properly 
controlled (Olsen et al. 2011; Olsen et al. 2014). 
The recent study by Østergaard & Svendsen (2015) have shown that there is a great potential in 
lowering the DH temperatures for providing heat to single-family house. The study has examined 
four existing single-family house from 1930’s, where a dynamic simulation (IDA-ICE) for a year 
was conducted to evaluate the potential of lowering the district heating temperatures based on the 
actual radiator sizes. The research identified nine critical radiators in two of the four houses, which 
had difficulties upholding the thermal comfort. If the return temperatures are to be lowered to 
approximately 30°C, a replacement is required before a conversion into LTDH can be conducted. If, 
however, the replacement was performed, it would be possible to lower the average heating system 
temperatures to 50°C/27°C in all four houses.  
A similar study (Wollerstrand & Lauenburg 2009) to this project, has also investigated the 
possibility of optimizing the control of a radiator system to provide the lowest possible district 
heating return temperature. Though, the controlling of the space heating system was conducted in 
the substation and not on the actual radiators throughout the building.  The study was performed as 
a case study based on a software algorithm, which created a control curve for the radiator system, 
which continuously provided the optimum cooling of the return temperature. Additionally, existing 
components of control equipment in district heating substations were modified and enhanced. The 
study showed that it was possible to achieve the largest cooling in each operating point in a 
substation when the radiator circuit’s temperature and circulation flow varied. Moreover, the 
preliminary results obtained from the studies indicates that a lowering of the return temperature can 
be obtained by securing a better control of the space heating system.   
==PAGE:16==
Page 16 of 95 
 
Other studies (Tunzi et al. 2016; Dalla Rosa & Christensen 2011; Ommen et al. 2016) have also 
shown that there are socioeconomic and environmental benefits by implementing LTDH for both 
the end user, the utility company and the transmission company. According to Tunzi et al. (2016), 
there is a possible discount of 14% for the end-users' energy bills (based on an assumption of cost 
reduction of 1% for each 1°C reduction of return temperature). Moreover, a possible discount of 
16% could be achieved through the average supply and return temperatures, by more efficient 
operation of the thermostatic radiators valves.   
Based on the abovementioned studies, lowering the return district heating temperature in existing 
buildings can be achieved by ensuring a better control and functioning of the heating system as well 
as the components.  
Conducting this project will help expose the specific problems that cause inefficient heating and 
barriers to the lowering of the DH return temperature in city areas. The project is a unique case 
study due to the unexplored field of multi-storey family apartment buildings in city areas. Thereby, 
the project will provide answers for the current questions to whether it is necessary to improve the 
control and the components of the current heating systems to use efficient low-temperature district 
heating as a heating source in the buildings. 
 
3 Method 
The following section presents the methods which will be applied throughout this study. Moreover, 
the case-building will be described. Furthermore, the simulation program and the applied equations, 
which the program is based on will be presented. Then, a thorough assessment of the data, which 
has been collected in the practical part of the project. Furthermore, a review of the conditions and 
process, which has been considered regarding the residents in the case-building. 
Lastly, a presentation of the calculation applied for calculating the consumer and Frederiksberg 
Forsyning economy. 
3.1 Method for conducting case study 
The method for conducting the case study will be describes in the following. Initially, a case-
building, which could have a potential for lowering the return temperature, was chosen in 
collaboration with Frederiksberg Forsyning. As the building was chosen, the initially 
==PAGE:17==
Page 17 of 95 
 
communication was established with the resident’s representative. A description of the case study 
and letter of consent was distributed among the residents and was later collected. The objective of 
the case study, was to lower the return temperature to around 35°C. 
Screenings were conducted for each apartment, where the return and room temperatures loggers 
was implemented, securing pre-measurements before the changes to the heating system were 
applied. The screenings were necessary, so that an exact number of thermostats, incompatible 
valves and other issues which needed to be correct, could be determined.  
Once the screenings were conducted, the new equipment was ordered and an implementation day 
was coordinated with all the residents. Frederiksberg Forsyning’s service department corrected and 
replaced any faulty components and the new thermostats and related Link panels was implemented 
by Dorte Østergaard and the undersigned. 
After implementation, a continuous monitoring of the measurements and a correcting of detected 
errors was conducted. 
Based on this progress and method, the results are presented and analysed in section 9.4. 
3.2 Overall approach for implementing better controlling of heating system 
The following steps describes a general simple method for improving the control of the heating 
system to achieve a return temperature close to room temperature the majority of the operating time. 
It is intended as a proposal of method for those who perform work related to improvements of 
heating systems in buildings and could be performed on all buildings. The method will be analysed 
in the project, though a more detailed and in-depth analysis, in order to verify the proposed method.  
• The data for the heating consumption and cooling of the district heating water in and out of 
the heating season will be obtained and analysed. The data will be obtained from the district 
heating utility company. The data is used to assess the potential for lowering the return 
temperature divided on the space heating system and domestic hot water system.  
• The heat allocators from the given radiators in the building, provides data for radiator 
heating power, annual and short-term values for heating consumption. These values are 
evaluated to assess the space heating consumption, possible return temperatures and 
determine well or poorly controlled radiators.    
• A thorough review of the building's heating substation, piping for space heating and 
domestic hot water systems as well as control of radiator sizes and thermostatic valves and 
==PAGE:18==
Page 18 of 95 
 
floor heating systems will be conducted. This forms the basis for the assessment of needs for 
improvements in the space heating system, which could be due to lack of heating capacity 
and poor management. 
• This will result in a resolution on implementation and correction of the errors in the systems, 
which will be based on economic benefits and willingness of the residents. If there is a 
consent from the residents, the corrections and improvements will be conducted. 
Furthermore, guidance of the applications and new improvements of the systems will be 
mediated to the residents/users. 
• An ongoing operational control based on measurements of district heat meters from the 
utility company and heat cost allocators will ensure an ongoing debugging.  
• Documentation of improvements achieved and statement of financial savings for the 
distribution between contractors and users as well as assessment of the district heating utility 
company efficiency improvement will be presented. 
All of the abovementioned steps will related to the following case-building. Furthermore, a detailed 
simulation model in IDA-ICE of the case-building have been constructed. The simulation is not a 
part of the general method, but have been constructed to provide verification, understanding and

3.3 Simulation model 
The commercially available dynamic building simulation program IDA-ICE (version 4.7.1) have 
been applied in this project, to conduct simulations of the case-building’s energy consumption and 
radiator dimensions. 
In order to conduct the simulation of the case-building, different theories and equations are taken 
into account. The program applies a predefined water based radiator unit, which simulates the heat 
emissions. It is therefore, imperative to understand the theory behind the equations of heat 
emissions, which is applied in the project. The equations and theory related to radiator performance 
and heat emissions, which the simulation program is based on, will be presented and described in 
the following subsections.

6 Uncertainties 
In the following section, the uncertainties related to the project will be described.  
There is an inaccuracy of measurements, which can be caused by the mounting of the temperature 
probe at the specific location. Some of the probes were been attached with tape due to difficulties of 
the radiator type, therefore some uncertainty is related to the location and mounting. 
Furthermore, the probes were not insulated, hence increasing the influence of the ambient 
temperature. However, as the intent of the return temperature loggers was to give an indication to 
whether the return temperature was high or low and not measure an exact value. 
Also, the location of, particularly, the room temperature loggers has a great influence on the 
measurements. It was not possible in all the cases to place the loggers at the most optimum place 
and therefore alternative placements have been used. However, it was still possible to see 
tendencies in the room temperature. 
Some of the analysis are supported by the information which was given from the residents in the 
individual apartments. An uncertainty is also related to the accuracy of the given information, which 
also must be considered. Additionally, a large uncertainty and complication of the case study has 
been the influence of human behaviour, which is uncontrollable.  
The data, which have been logged from the Danfoss Link panels also showed some inconsistencies 
in the logging period. This has an influential uncertainty due to the absent data. However, this issue 
was only detected in some of the rooms for two apartments. Additionally, it could not be detected 
until the data was sent to Danfoss to be decrypted.   
==PAGE:29==
Page 29 of 95 
 
There are also some uncertainties related to the values and calculations of the heat cost allocators. 
As the heat cost allocators are dependent on the location of the radiator, this has an uncertainty 
related to it. If the heat cost allocator is not placed correctly, this would influence the temperature 
measurements, which is then used for calculating the return temperature. This could result in 
deviating results.  
Furthermore, there is also uncertainty related to the specified radiator output at 90/70/20°C from 
Brunata, as it can be difficult to clearly determine with old radiators. Brunata also applies the 
radiator exponent to be a standard value of 1.33 for all the radiators, however this would typically 
vary. 
7 Improving of the heating system 
To improve the heating system, the case study will improve the control of each radiator in the case-
building. By improving the control of the radiators in each apartment, the most optimal operation of 
the heating system can be conducted. 
7.1 Danfoss Smart heating  
In order to ensure a better control of the radiators in the case-building, a Danfoss Link system 
equipped with Danfoss Living Connect thermostat was installed in each apartment. By 
implementing this system, it is possible to control all thermostats on one touch panel, where it is 
also possible to control the different indoor temperature set-points, which is shown as °C. This also 
enhances the understanding from the consumers’ perspective, how high they desire the room 
temperature to be in the different rooms. Furthermore, multiple radiators within one room can be 
connected to each other and collaborate in supplying the required heat to the room in the most 
optimal way.  
In the cases where radiators were partially or fully hidden, an external room temperature sensor was 
connected to the thermostat, so that the measured room temperature could control the thermostat, 
thus ensuring the optimal operation of the radiator.

Fig. 5 - Illustration of Danfoss Smart heating system (Danfoss 2016) 
The electronic thermostat adjusts the required heat, with a more precise PID controlling, by 
reference to the room temperature. The settings on the thermostat is displayed in degree Celsius 
instead of the former 0-5 range. Once the thermostat is mounted on the valve, it will begin a process 
of getting to know the valve and the opening- and closing positions. Within 4-5 days, the thermostat 
will open and close to find the opening point of the valve, hence the thermostat can be observed 
closing, even though the set point has not been reached. After the adjustment period, the new 
thermostats will have adapted to the heating system (Danfoss 2013; Lund 2016). Some of the 
features that make the thermostat useful is the weekly moving of the valve pin and the 
autodetections of an open window.   
Every Thursday at 11:00, the thermostat will automatically move the valve pin, which ensures that 
the radiators are fully functional all the time. Another feature of the thermostat, is the autodetection 
of open windows. Once the thermostat detects a large temperature difference in room temperature, 
it automatically shuts the radiator down for 30 minutes and afterwards opens the valve again.

10.3 Review of the case-building’s heating system 
The following section consist of a description of the heating system of the case-building.  
Firstly, a presentation of the heating substation, which is located within the basement of the 
building, will be conducted. 
 
Fig. 15 - Overview of heating substation of case-building 
As can be shown in Fig. 15, the district heating supply are divided into two systems, domestic hot 
water system and the space heating system.  The domestic hot water system consists of two serial 
==PAGE:46==
Page 46 of 95 
 
connected storage tanks. It can be observed, that the cold-water supply enters the first storage tank 
and is then heated in both tanks, before it is distributed out into the building. 
A domestic hot water analysis conducted by Dorte Østergaard shows, that in the measuring period 
from 24th of November 2016 to 12th of December 2016, the heating of the domestic hot water was 
only 14% of the total heating consumption in the case-building. Therefore, no supplementary 
analysis of the domestic hot water has been conducted, since the contribution to the overall return 
temperature of the case-building during the heating season is minor. However, during the summer 
period, the percentage can be higher, thus increasing the influence of return temperature from the 
domestic hot water system.   
The space heating is distributed throughout the building through sixteen double risers, which can be 
observed in Fig. 16. The risers are not insulated throughout the building, only the main distribution 
pipes from the heating substation are insulated in the basement before branching out to the risers. 
 
Fig. 16 - Overview of case-building risers and radiators (Rósen & Bach 2017) 
When conducting the review of the space heating, a short circuit of one of the risers was detected. 
The short circuit in the risers remained undetected until a thorough inspection of the riser was 
conducted, which was located behind a couch. The short circuit was due to homemade installations 
in the radiator system on the 3rd floor. As the supply riser was ended, this was then led directly into 
the return riser.  
==PAGE:47==
Page 47 of 95 
 
After the correction of the short circuit was conducted on the 17th of January, a lower return 
temperature for the overall space heating system was expected. However, the measurements still 
showed a higher return temperature in Fig. 17. After investigating, why the return temperature 
remained higher, a radiator on the ground floor with high return temperatures was detected. 
As can be seen in the Fig. 17, the return temperature has been around 35°C until the 10th of January, 
after which the return temperature increased significantly. This indicates, that there is an error in the 
controlling of the radiator.  
 
Fig. 17 - Return temperatures after correcting the short circuit 
The reason for the high return temperature is caused by sensitivity of the improper operation of the 
new thermostats, which have been implemented. The thermostats can easily get dismounted, by 
pressing to long on the buttons. This causes the thermostat to release the valve pin, thus having full 
flow through the radiator. Furthermore, this cannot easily be detected by the consumer, therefore 
making it difficult to correct such errors. The residents and error has not been contacted for the 
purpose of correcting the error, at the time of writing. Additionally, other radiators are also 
investigated for high return temperatures and possible solutions are considered. However, this 
supports the necessity of a continuous control of the operation of all the radiators in order to 
maintain a low return temperature.

10.7.1 Socioeconomic and environmental evaluation 
There are a numerous of socioeconomic benefits by lowering the return temperature of the district 
heating grids. Some of the objectives, which should be considered are employment, energy prices, 
reduce social inequality, economic growth and environmental benefits.  
As there is an increasing share of wind power, thus a decrement in heat production and more 
electricity production, there is a need for alternative energy sources with lower flow temperature to 
utilize the existing district heating network as much as possible. Therefore, it is necessary to make 
the current district heating grid more compatible and this can be done by lowering the temperatures. 
Lower temperatures in the grid, also lowers the heat loss in the district heating grid both in the 
transmission and distribution grid. Therefore, a smaller amount of fuel is required, which is both 
beneficial in regards to energy prices and the environment. Additionally, there would be an 
improvement of effect of heating plants. Moreover, other types of energy sources, such as waste 
heat or renewable energy sources can be implemented in the energy sector. 
Furthermore, by adapting the district heating grid for the future energy sources also secures the high 
security of supply, which is in Denmark, and diminishing the social inequality. 
Due to the new technology solutions regarding low temperature district heating, whether it is new 
types of district heating pipes or thermostats with return temperature control, this will help creating 
jobs and knowledge in Denmark, which can be used as an export commodity in the future. This also 
benefits the society, by provided employment and economic growth. Furthermore, the no large 
subsidies are provided to the district heating sector.

10.8 Tariff structure  
The following section will propose a new tariff structure, which creates an incentive for the 
consumer to actively improve the heating system and thereby lowering the return temperature. 
The proposed tariff structure is based on the newly introduced tariff structure of Høje Taastrup 
Fjernvarme (HTF).  The lower temperatures in both supply and return will ensure HTF an 
opportunity to integrate new and cheaper production types such as heat pumps, solar panels, 
geothermal, than with higher district heating temperatures. Furthermore, HFT is interested in 
optimising and utilize their distribution grid better (Høje Taastrup Fjernvarme 2016), thus 
attempting to realize it. 
==PAGE:81==
Page 81 of 95 
 
The proposed tariff is formed in such a way, that consumers with higher return temperatures than 
46°C in year one of the tariff will be penalized with 10 DKK/MWh/°C. A discount on the heat cost 
is provided for the consumers, who lowers the return temperature below 46°C by 10 
DKK/MWh/°C. Further constraints will continuously be proposed until a return temperature of 
41°C is achieved.

11 Discussion 
11.1 Improving the heating system 
Form an overall perspective based on the presented results, the heating system obtained a lower 
return temperature by implementing the Danfoss Living Connect system. However, some radiators 
did not decrease in return temperature. This was caused by several factors, such as the location of 
the radiator, the controlling variables and functions of Danfoss system and the influence of human 
interference.  
The thermostat could be improved further, to accommodate for human influence. Through the case 
study, the thermostats have shown a sensitivity to faulty operation, and some functions of the 
thermostats needs to finetuned, if they are to be used for lowering the return temperature of the 
radiators. The most optimum solution, would be to incorporate an additional control variables 
besides the room temperature. If the thermostat also was controlled by at return temperature sensor, 
it would be possible to lower the return temperature by eliminating situations, where the influence 
of users effects the return temperature.  
Even though, a vast amount of measurements was obtained through the case study, the short circuit 
in the risers was detected after the improvements of the heating system have been implemented. 
Unfortunately, after the correction of the short circuit, a decrease in the overall return temperature 
of the heating system was not observed. However, a few radiators have, in the meantime, increased, 
which indicates, that return temperature varies according to the control of the radiators. Therefore, 
is seems, that only a few radiators need correcting and some residents needs to be lectured in proper 
controlling of the new heating control system, again. This supports the concept of a continuous 
monitoring of the system is necessary until all error have been corrected.  
The preliminary results from using the proposed overall method, have shown, that indication of 
troubling radiators can be observed in the calculated on the values obtained from the heat cost 
allocators. Overall, the measurements showed the same tendencies as the calculated return 
temperature, however, in a few cases, there was minor deviations, which indicates, that the 
calculations of the return temperatures must be considered as indications and not accurate results. 
This could maybe be resolved by conducting an optimized calculation in a software solution.  
==PAGE:83==
Page 83 of 95 
 
A large amount of uncertainties was related to the simulation of the case-building. Some of the 
uncertainty was the internal gains from equipment, occupants. Furthermore, the model was highly 
depended on the input, such as the set point, when the model was validated. The simulation model 
could be used for comparing measurements, with the simulation, however there was a large 
difference between the model and measurements. Thus, making it more reasonable to analyse that 
actual measurements instead of creating a simulation model. A more detailed validation of the 
model could perhaps increase the accuracy of the model. 
11.2 Economic benefits by lowering the return temperature 
Form a socioeconomic and environmental perspective, the case study supports the benefits, which 
can be achieved if the return temperature is lowered.  Such objectives as employment, energy 
prices, economic growth and environmental benefits might all be realised if the temperatures in the 
grid is lowered. The lower temperatures would also benefit the environment by saving fuel, as 
smaller amounts are needed, when decreasing the temperatures. Furthermore, other types of energy 
sources, such as waste heat or renewable energy sources would also be able to be utilized in the 
energy sector. However, a more specific socioeconomic analysis needs to be conducted in order to 
conclude anything definitely.  
Based on the results Frederiksberg Forsyning will obtained 28 million DKK in energy saving each 
year, though it is under the assumption that all of the consumers within Frederiksberg has a return 
temperature of 35°C. The majority of the savings is due to escaping of the penalty from CTR, which 
can be avoided if the return temperature is 35°C.  
However, under the current tariff structure and the current heat cost the consumer has very little 
incentive to conduct any improvements, as the simple payback time is 8.5 years. Therefore, it is 
essential to introduce a new tariff structure, to create this incentive. 
By implemented the new proposed tariff structure, which is based on the tariff structure from HFT, 
a large incentive is created to encourage the consumer to prioritise to improve their heating system. 
If the present tariff structure was replaced with the new proposed, a simple payback time of 1.4 
years would be obtained. 
When conducting the simplified economic analysis for Frederiksberg Forsyning, all the data was 
based on values from 2014, which would have been more accurate if newer data was applied. 
However, this was not possible to obtain.

Page 84 of 95 
 
12 Conclusion 
In order to answer the research question, “How can inadequate components or errors in the heating 
system of existing buildings be identified?” a method has been proposed. The overall method is 
using the already available data for cooling and consumption for a given building within the 
municipality of Frederiksberg and the data from the heat cost allocators, which is already mounted 
on the radiator. Based on linear expressions it is possible to calculate an estimate for the return 
temperature of the radiator, thus a high return temperature would indicate any potential issues.  The 
method also hinted that a continuous monitoring of the heating system should be conducted, so that 
emerging issues could be corrected. The method was tested on a case-building over a short period, 
however it was verified through extra measurement, which showed the same tendencies as the 
calculated values. Based on the preliminary results from the case-building, this overall method 
indicate that it is possible to identify inadequate components or errors in the energy meters and the 
heat cost allocators. 
 
In order to answer the research question, “Which process and tools can be applied to correct 
potential errors in control and errors in components of heating systems in existing buildings, to 
ensure the possibility of lowering the return temperature of the district heating grid?” a case study 
was conducted. Thus, a new control system for the space heating was implemented to investigate 
the possibility of lowering the return temperature. Specifically, a Danfoss Living Connect system 
was implemented in each of the apartments in the case-building. Furthermore, additional 
temperature meters were implemented, so that calculated values from the heat cost allocators could 
be compared to the measured values. An overall decrease of the return temperature from the 
radiators could be observed. However, there were some challenges with the new thermostats from 
the Danfoss Living Connect system. The thermostats were sensitive to faulty operation, and the 
function of the thermostats did not always work well with human influence. 
Furthermore, a IDA-ICE simulation model was constructed based on the case-building. Since the 
simulation program has a lot of uncertainties related to it, no direct conclusion could be drawn by 
the simulation. However, it raised awareness of potential issues, which was then investigated 
further, by actual measurements, thus showing no problems. Therefore, it can also be concluded that 
results are more accurate when obtained through measurements and not the simulation.  
 
==PAGE:85==
Page 85 of 95 
 
In order to answer the research question, “Which economic benefits can be achieved by lowering 
the return temperature in the district heating network in the municipality of Frederiksberg?”. In 
regards to the economic perspective of the study, it can be concluded that there is an incentive for 
Frederiksberg Forsyning and in turn the general public in Frederiksberg municipality to strive after 
a lower return temperature. This is due to a yearly saving of 45 million DKK that could be obtained 
if the return temperature was lowered. However, with the current tariff structure, there is no 
incentive for the consumer to invest in improving the heating system. Therefore, it is necessary to 
implement a new tariff structure, that will encourage the consumers to actively invest in 
improvements for their heating system. Due to the very simplified utility economic analysis, a more 
detailed one should be conducted, to provide more accurate results. Furthermore, an analysis of the 
socioeconomic and environmental aspect should also be conducted, in order to thoroughly 
investigate the advantages and disadvantages of lower return temperature in the district heating grid. 
 
In order to answer the research question, “How would a proposed new tariff structure affect the 
end-consumers economically?”. A new tariff structure, would create a high incentive for the 
consumer to start paying attention to how their heating system is operating. By implementing a new 
tariff structure, which have a focus on lowering the return temperature, it is possible to reward those 
consumers, that contribute to the overall system by ensuring that they have a low return 
temperature.  
 
Collectively, it can be concluded that the overall method can be applied. Furthermore, the method is 
associated with beneficial economic perspectives.

ANVISNINGER TIL STYRING AF TERMOSTATER 
 
Gennem forsøget er det vigtigt at følgende anvisninger overholdes, således at vi får et retvisende resultat. 
GENERELT 
- 
Udluftninger: De nye termostater registrer, hvis I åbner vinduer. Derfor skal I IKKE slukke for termostaterne, 
når I lufter ud. Vi anbefaler, at I lufter ud i korte perioder (max. 30 minutter) fremfor lange perioder, da 
dette sikrer den nødvendige ventilation uden at øge varmeforbruget. 
 
- 
Såfremt, at der er flere radiatorer i rummet, vil disse være koblet sammen. Dette betyder, at hvis 
temperaturen øges på en af de koblede radiatorer, vil de andre også indstilles sig efter dette.  Dette sikrer en 
mere energieffektiv drift. 
 
- 
Brug alle lejlighedens radiatorer, da fjernvarmen udnyttes bedre og der ikke er besparelser i at skrue op og 
ned for radiatorerne. 
 
- 
Hvis I ønsker et koldt soveværelse, kan I overveje at stille termostaten lavere i rummet, men det er dog 
vigtigt at døren forbliver lukket til rummet, således de tilstødende rum/radiatorer ikke forsøger at varme 
rummet op.  
 
- 
Det er ikke fordelagtigt at anvende natsænkning i boliger i fjernvarmeområder. Dette skyldes, at der ikke er 
store besparelser at hente ved temperatursænkning i kortere perioder. Danfoss termostaterne er derfor 
heller ikke indstillet med natsænkning. 
 
DANFOSS LIVING SYSTEMET 
 
I må gerne selv tilpasse den ønsket inde temperatur i de forskellige rum. Dette kan gøres på enten 
termostaten eller Link panelet, men hvis I justerer på termostaten er det vigtigt, at I ikke holder nogen 
knapper inde flere sekunder, da det afkobler termostaten. 
 
Batterierne i termostaterne skal skiftes én gang om året. Udskiftning kan foretages uden afkobling af 
termostaten og der må ikke anvendes genopladelig batterier. 
 
De første fire dage, vil termostaten finjustere sine indstillinger og derfor kan der forekomme mindre 
temperatursvingninger. I bedes derfor vente med at lave eventuelle tilpasninger til efter fjerde dagen. 
 
Termostaten foretager selv vedligehold af ventilstiften, hvilket øger temperaturen kortvarigt. Dette gøres 
automatisk ugentligt torsdag kl. 11:00.  
 
 
Det er MEGET vigtigt at USB-stikkene forbliver i Link panelet gennem måleperioden, da vi logger alt data 
herpå. 
 
Vi appellerer derfor til at I følger disse anvisninger, så længe målingerne foretages.  
På forhånd tak!
