Abstract 
Alginate is a linear polysaccharide abundant in brown algae, it works as viscosifier and stabilizer in 
fields like biomaterials and pharmacy. Alginate can be degraded by alginate lyases, which are 
divided into endolytic alginate lyases and exolytic alginate lyases. The synergy of endo-acting and 
exo-acting alginate lyases can result in degradation of alginate with higher efficiency and obtain 
more monosaccharides. The alginate lyase BoPL17 characterized in this project was from the novel 
Bacteroidetes ovatus (non-annotated) isolated from human fecal feces and belongs to the PL17 
family. It is an exolytic alginate lyase and showed much higher substrate affinity on the M-blocks 
than G-blocks basing on the previous study that the enzyme had no activity on polyG. BoPL17 
exhibited maximum activity at 47℃, but lost 45% activity after 1 hour incubation under this 
condition. The enzyme activity of BoPL17 is greatly influenced by NaCl and pH which is similar to 
other alginate lyases from PL17, all having a narrow range of optimal NaCl concentration and pH. 
The main degradation products of BoPL17 were monosaccharides, disaccharides, and 
trisaccharides, especially mono- and trisaccharides. This is normal for alginate lyases from PL17, 
most of them were reported to generate oligosaccharides with the DP ranging from 1 to 3. Two 
amino acids Y260 and Y453 in BoPL17 WT were individually mutated to identify the catalytic active 
site of the protein. The two sites are in opposite position at the same side of carbohydrate ligand 
and are suggested to work as the proton accepter and donator respectively, during the alginate 
degradation. The enzyme activity of two mutants Y260F and Y453F on CSPM were measured very 
low. The result showed that Y260F had undetectable activity on degrading CSPM, while Y453F had 
a little activity, which means that Y260 is suggested to be the catalytic active site of BoPL17 wild 
type protein. 
Furthermore, two synergy experiments were designed to find the potential in improving 
degradation efficiency. Synergies were analyzed of BoPL38 and BoPL17, and of BoPL38 and BoPL6, 
for comparison. BoPL38 is an endolytic alginate lyase, BoPL17 and BoPL6 are exolytic alginate lyases. 
Results for both synergies showed much higher enzyme activity (1.64 in synergy BoPL38 and 
BoPL17, 6.84 in synergy BoPL38 and BoPL6) than the theoretical combined enzyme activity, which 
means higher alginate degradation efficiency could be obtained by the cooperation of the 
endolytic and exolytic alginate lyases.

1. Introduction 
1.1 Alginate 
Alginate is the main structural component of the cell walls of brown algae and accounts for around 
40% of their dry weight.1 It is a linear polysaccharide consisting of two monomeric units, 𝛽-D-
mannuronate (M) and its C-5 epimer 𝛼-L-guluronate (G), which are arranged in three different 
blocks: the homopolymeric M blocks (PolyM), the homopolymeric G blocks (PolyG), and the 
heteropolymeric blocks of alternating M and G residues (PolyMG).2 They are linked by 1→4 
glycosidic bonds.3  
Commercial alginates are commonly extracted from brown seaweeds, such as Macrocystis pyrifera, 
Laminaria hyperborea, Saccharina japonica, Lessonia nigrescens, Lessonia trabeculata, Ecklonia 
radiata, and Ascophyllum nodosum. Algae can grow in seawater or industrial wastewater which 
reduces land consumption.4 Alginate can be used as a viscosifier and stabilizer in fields like food, 
printing, and biomaterials for the property of viscosity.5 Alginate is a renewable alternative to fossil 
transport fuel, which means it is involved in carbon fixing in marine ecosystems.6  
Alginate oligosaccharides (AOSs) are the degradation products of alginate. The properties like low 
molecular weight and wide-ranging biological activities make AOSs receive increasing research 
attention. AOSs have a wide range of applications in fields of agriculture and pharmacology, 
because of the biological activities they show in anti-inflammatory,7 anti-oxidant,8 and anti-tumor9 
action.

1.2 Alginate lyases 
Alginate lyases are polysaccharide lyases (PLs) that can depolymerize alginate by 𝛽-elimination of 
the glycosidic 1-4 O-linkages between sugar monomers, producing unsaturated oligosaccharides 
containing a 4-deoxy-l-erythro-hex-4-enopyranosyluronic acid motif (∆) as the new nonreducing 
terminus.11 They have been identified in a range of organisms, including a variety of bacteria, fungi, 
viruses, animals, marine algae, marine mollusks, and echinoderms.12 Marine bacteria are the most 
widely studied sources of alginate lyases and account for the largest variety of alginate lyases.13 
Based on amino acid sequence similarities, alginate lyases are classified into 14 PL families in the 
Carbohydrate-Active Enzyme (CAZy)14 database http://www.cazy.org/ (PL5, PL6, PL7, PL8, PL14, 
PL15, PL17, PL18, PL31, PL32, PL34, PL36, PL39, and PL41). Based on substrate specificity, alginate 
lyases can be further classified into three types: PolyM specific lyases (EC 4.2.2.3), mannuronate-
specific lyases that cleave M-M and M-G bonds; PolyG specific lyases (EC 4.2.2.11), guluronate-
specific lyases that cleave G-G and G-M bonds; and bifunctional lyases (EC 4.2.2.26), alginate lyases 
that can degrade poly(4-deoxy-α-L-erythro-hexopyranuronoside) oligosaccharides with 4-deoxy-α-
L-erythro-hex-4-enopyranuronosyl groups at their non-reducing ends into 4-deoxy-α-L-erythro-
hex-4-enopyranuronate monosaccharides and short oligosaccharides. The structures are shown in 
Fig.1A-C. Most endolytic alginate lyases are found in families PL5 and PL7,15 while most exolytic 
==PAGE:10==
 
 
9 
alginate lyases are found in families PL6, PL7, PL14, PL15, and PL17. Both endo- and exolytic 
alginate lyases are necessary for achieving full saccharification of alginate, which means that the 
synergy of endolytic alginate lyase and exolytic alginate lyase has the potential to improve the 
degradation efficiency.16 In the process, endolytic lyases degrade the alginate by cleaving glycosidic 
bonds interior in the chain of the alginate substrate, and produce unsaturated oligosaccharides 
with different degree polymerization (DP), while exolytic lyases can generate monomers or dimers 
from these oligomers.  
Most of the reported alginate lyases are endolytic lyases, and exolytic alginate lyases are fewer.17 
The alginate lyase characterized in this project is from a noval Bacteroidetes ovatus isolated from 
human fecal feces and belongs to the PL17 family. Currently the CAZy14 database contains 17 
characterized enzymes in family PL17, most of them have substrate specificity on polyM. Two 3-D 
structures of proteins from PL17 family have been solved, as shown in Fig.2 C and D. The PL17 
family has two subfamilies,18 the subfamily 1 contains endolytic lyases, while the subfamily 2 
contains exolytic oligosaccharide lyases including the BoPL17 WT which is investigated in this 
project. 
1.3 3-D structure of BoPL17 WT 
BoPL17 WT comprises two domains, an N-terminal (𝛼/𝛼)6 toroid and a C-terminal 𝛽-sheet domain 
as shown in Fig2A, which is similar to other proteins from family PL17. To determine the homology 
and conserved domain of proteins from PL17, structure alignments of BoPL17 WT with Alg17c19 
and AlyA310 were analyzed by PyMOL respectively, as shown in Fig.2 B, C. The PDB files were 
downloaded from CAZy14 database with PDB 4nei and PDB 7bjt respectively. The values of RMSD 
(root-mean-square deviation) are applied to identify the similarity of protein structures. In Fig.2 C, 
the structure of BoPL17 WT is in green while the structure of Alg17c is in blue. They show a whole 
structure RMSD of 1.808 and Cα RMSD of 1.693, which mean they share similarity. That’s the same 
with the alignment between BoPL17 WT and AlyA3 shown in Fig.2 D, they show a whole structure 
RMSD of 1.944 and Cα RMSD of 1.877. 
Alg17c is isolated from the marine bacterium Saccharophagus degradans, it is the first structural 
and biochemical characterization of an alginate lyase from PL17. AlyA3 is from the marine 
flavobacterium Zobellia galactanivorans DsijT.10 It is an exolytic alginate lyase and is the first 
structure of a PL17 presented in the Bacteroidetes phylum. AlyA3 has no activity on PolyG or

PolyMG, it is a PolyM specific alginate lyase and shows the highest activity on oligosaccharides with 
polymerization degree ranging from DP3 to DP6. To determine the catalytic activity region of this 
AlyA3, three amino acids were selected in conserved region to be mutated individually after 
alignment with other alginate lyases. The variants Y274A, Y466A, and H200A were obtained. 
Compared to AlyA3 wildtype, Y274A and Y466A lost their alginate lyase activity having only 0.2%

Compared to Alg17c, AlyA3 has globally a similar crystal structure with Alg17c. AlyA3 has the 
difference that it contains an inserted flexible loop between helices α10 and α11, which is located 
at the entrance to the catalytic groove, likely responsible for substrate recognition, processivity and 
turn over. In sequence analysis, AlyA3 shares 42% sequence identify with Alg17c, they have 
conserved catalytic site residues, substrate recognition residues, ion binding residues, and charge 
neutralization residues. 
Basing on research of proteins from PL17 family10 and protein Blast analysis of BoPL17 WT, two 
possible catalytic residues, Tyr260 and Tyr453 were predicted, which are shown in Fig.2. They are 
in a domain that is conserved in proteins from the PL17 family.20 It is obvious from Fig.2 A, in the 
middle of the active region, that Tyr260 is in an 𝛼-helix structure while Tyr453 is located in a 𝛽-
sheet structure. They are involved in the catalytic reaction and in an opposite position in one side 
of the carbohydrate ligand as shown in Fig.2 B. Substrates will bind with the help of other amino 
acid residues that play the role of substrate interaction sites in the conserved domain. The alginate 
degradation will be able to happen following the 𝛽-elimination, which is explained in mechanism 
part 1.4.

1.4 Hypothetical degradation mechanism of BoPL17 WT towards polyM  
The lyase mechanism of the alginate lyases are 𝛽-elimination involving cleavage of the 4-O-
glycosidic bonds between C-4 and C-5 to produce oligosaccharides with 4-deoxy-L-erythro-hex-4-
enopyranosyluronic acid at the unsaturated non-reducing end.21  
The mechanism of 𝛽-elimination can be divided into two different types based on the differences 
in the neutralization of the C5 proton. His (or Tyr)/Tyr residues elimination and metal-ion-assisted 
elimination. In this study, the mechanism of BoPL17 of Tyr/Tyr elimination as shown in Fig.4. 
In this project, the hypothetical degradation process is as follow. Firstly, the carboxyl group on the 
substrate is neutralized by establishing a salt bridge. Secondly, the H5 proton will be removed by a 
base residue, which is expected to be Tyr453 in BoPL17 WT. An acid residue Tyr260 in BoPL17 WT 
will serve as a proton donor and a double bond will be formed. Generally, the proton on C5 is 
abstracted by a tyrosine residue (Y453 in BoPL17 WT) and the 4-O-glycosidic bond will be cleaved 
==PAGE:14==
 
 
13 
because of the electron transfer, then forming a double bond between C4 and C5. After the 
elimination of 4-O-glycosidic bond by alginate lyases, the unsaturated alginate oligosaccharide 
(oligosaccharides containing 4-deoxy-L-erythro-hex-4-enopyranosyluronic acid as the unsaturated 
non-reducing terminal, the motif ∆) will be produced. Here the alginate lyase activity can be 
determined by monitoring the formation of the 4,5-unsaturated bonds at the nonreducing end by 
spectrophotometrically following the absorbance at 235nm at regular time intervals. 
According to the configuration analysis of the C4 and C5, the lyase products can be divided into 
two different configurations, syn-configuration and anti-configuration. In syn-configuration, the 
proton abstraction and protonation are finished by protein residues from the same side, which 
means the abstracted proton is on the same side with C-4 bridging oxygen. The anti-configuration 
means the opposite position. It always shows syn-configuration in M residues, and anti-
configuration in G residues.6

1.5 Synergy of endolytic and exolytic alginate lyases 
Alginate lyases can be divided into endolytic and exolytic alginate lyases based on their mode of 
action. Endolytic lyases will degrade long alginate chains by cleaving internal bonds in the polymer, 
while exolytic lyases can generate monomers from alginate oligomers as shown in Fig.5. It is 
valuable to study the synergy reaction of endolytic and exolytic alginate lyases since both are 
necessary to achieve full saccharification of alginate. In this project, the mixture of BoPL38 and 
BoPL17, the mixture of BoPL38 and BoPL6 are studied respectively. The BoPL38 is endolytic alginate 
==PAGE:15==
 
 
14 
lyase, while BoPL17 and BoPL6 are both exolytic alginate lyase. They can be compared because 
these three alginate lyases are all located in Bacteroidetes ovatus PUL as shown in Fig.6, and they 
share similar optimal reaction conditions. Moreover, the previous study finished by Rønne et.al in 
prep. showed that BoPL6 is a polyG specific exolytic alginate lyase, while BoPL38 has specificity on 
all alginate substrates.  
Synergies of endo-acting and exo-acting alginate lyases have been studied in recent years. For 
example, the synergy of endolytic alginate lyase PsAlg7A from PL7 and exolytic alginate lyase 
PsMan8A from PL8 was investigated by combining loadings of the two proteins to a total of 0.2 
µM enzyme towards 1.5 g/l polyM or alginate.22 Both PsAlg7A and PsMan8A were from the marine 
fungus Paradendryphiella salina, they share optimal pH, immediate genetic location, and could be 
co-secreted by Paradendryphiella salina under specific conditions.23 The synergy was analyzed 
basing on the degree of synergy (DS), it was defined as the ratio between the specific activity and 
the theoretical specific activity. The DSmax was the optimal ratio between two proteins where the 
degradation shows the highest efficiency and largest difference between the specific activity and 
the theoretical specific activity. In this study, they reached the DSmax 3.2 in setup with the ratio of 
30% PsAlg7A and 70% PsMan8A on alginate. It revealed that the endolytic alginate lyase PsAlg7A 
provided attack sites to the endolytic alginate lyase PsMan8A on alginate, the cooperation catalytic 
reaction improved the degradation degree of alginate and obtained a higher degradation efficiency 
than the enzyme worked individually.

1.6 Aim of the project 
In this project, we aim to analyze the substrate specificity and mode of action of selected alginate 
lyases from polysaccharide lyase family 17. Synergy of endolytic and exolytic alginate lyases will be 
analyzed to obtain higher degradation efficiency. We will also focus on mutants to analyze the 
potential catalytic residues to validate degradation mechanism and the effect on stability. 
Furthermore, we will analyze the correlation between the protein structure and the catalytic 
activity.

2.2 Recombinant protein production 
2.2.1 Transformation and sequence analysis 
The gene encoding the alginate lyase BoPL17 of polysaccharide lyases family 17 was identified from 
==PAGE:18==
 
 
17 
the bacterium Bacteroidetes ovatus by the supervisors. The protein sequence of BoPL17 WT was 
aligned with the sequences of other alginate lyases from the PL17 family by SnapGene. The fasta 
files of proteins were downloaded from NCBI database (https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/). Two 
amino acids residues (Y260 and Y453) in a conserved region were identified and predicted by the 
alignment to have high possibility in affecting the catalytic activity of BoPL17 WT. The variants were 
decided to Y260F and Y453F because phenylalanine has similar structure with tyrosine, just one 
hydroxy group difference. The BoPL17 Wt and the two mutated genes were synthesized by 
Genscript into the pET28a+ vector by NheI and BamHI. Two individual mutated genes were 
transformed into E. coli BL21 (DE3) and DH5𝛼 competent cell. 
2.2.2 Culture and production of protein 
To produce the protein wild type and mutants, the mutated genes in pET28+ vectors were 
transformed into E. coli BL21. The fermentation conditions for BoPL17 WT and mutants were the 
same. The single growing colony of transformed BL21 on a LB agar plate was used to inoculate a 
preculture, growing overnight at 37℃ in 10 mL LB medium containing 100 µg/ml kanamyacin, 
160 rpm. Fermentation was performed in 800 mL LB medium containing 100 µg/ml kanamyacin 
at 37℃, 160 rpm. Adding 100 µg/ml IPTG (isopropyl-β-D-thiogalactopyranoside) to each culture 
when the OD600 value reached 0.6, then down shift the temperature to 16℃ and culture for 20 h. 
Cells were harvested by centrifugation at 4℃, 5000 rpm for 20 min. Pellets were stored at -20℃.  
2.3 Recombinant protein purification 
To purify protein, firstly resuspend 4-5 g cells in 20 mL cold buffer A containing 1 µL DNase. Destroy 
the cell wall by pressure cell homogenizer at 1 bar, and remove the cell debris by centrifugation at 
4℃, 20000 x g, 20 min. Then purify protein from the supernatant liquid by his-trap purification and 
size exclusion chromatography. 
2.3.1 His-trap purification 
Add 2 mL of HisPur Ni-NTA resin into a column and equilibrate it with 20 mL of cold buffer A, then 
add the supernatant to the resin and mix gently. Incubate the protein-resin mixture at 4℃ and mix 
gently for 30 min. Transfer the mixture to the column and collect the run-through. Wash the 
column with 10 mL buffer A followed by buffer B to eliminate non-specific binding impurities, 
collect wash1 and 2. Finally add 5 mL buffer C to eluate the protein and the eluate was stored at 
==PAGE:19==
 
 
18 
4℃ until further purification by size exclusion chromatography. The resin can be inactive by buffer 
D for storage to be reused. Wash with water and store the inactive resin in 20% ethanol in 4℃ 
fridge for next use. 
2.3.2 Size exclusion chromatography 
Collect the sample from his-trap purification and inject it on a Superdex200 16/60 column using

3.1 Bioinformation of BoPL17 WT 
An exolytic alginate lyase BoPL17 was prepared as identified in Bacteroidetes ovatus. The gene 
encoded a putative alginate lyase of 738 amino acid residues with a calculated molecular weight 
of 82593.14 Da. According to the conserved domain analysis in CAZy,14 BoPL17 WT contained an 
N-terminal alginate lyase superfamily domain (Gly95-Phe309) and a C-terminal heparinase II/III-
like superfamily domain (Arg381-Glu546). Aligning the sequence of BoPL17 WT with six other 
genes encoding alginate lyases from PL17 are shown in Fig.7. BoPL17 WT shared highest identity

from Pseudomonas sp., 42.01% with AGM38186.2 from Stenotrophomonas maltophilia, 42.26% 
with ABD82539.1 from Saccharophagus degradans 2-40, 46.46% with AHW45238.1 from 
Shewanella sp. Kz7, and 40.93% with AEM45874.1 from Sphingomonas sp. MJ-3. Based on 
structures analysis and sequence alignments of BoPL17 WT, two amino acid residues Y260 and 
Y453 were chosen for mutation. Mutant proteins Y260F and Y453F were built because tyrosine has 
a structure similar to phenylalanine, the only difference is that tyrosine has a hydroxyl group which 
phenylalanine does not. The hydroxyl group can help alginate degradation by removing the H-5

3.8 Synergy  
3.8.1 Synergy of BoPL38 and BoPL17 
The synergy analysis of the endolytic lyase BoPL38 and exolytic lyase BoPL17 WT mixed with the 
substrate alginate was designed to identify the relationship and roles of BoPL38 and BoPL17 WT 
enzymes. As shown in Fig.23, the relative enzyme activity reached the highest value 1.64 in the 
setup with 90 % BoPL38 and 10 % BoPL17. The theoretical relative enzyme meant the activity of 
BoPL38 without adding BoPL17. The enzyme mixture showed higher activity than the BoPL38 
alone proving that the synergy of endolytic lyase and exolytic lyase can degrade alginate with 
higher efficiency. It is obvious that the endolytic lyase BoPL38 provides attack sites to the exolytic 
lyase BoPL17, then the two enzymes work together to degrade alginate further.

3.8.2 Synergy of BoPL38 and BoPL6 
The synergy of endolytic lyase BoPL38 and exolytic lyase BoPL6 is shown in Fig.24, the relative 
enzyme activity reached the highest value 6.84 in the setup with ratio 60 % BoPL38 and 40 % 
BoPL6. No enzyme activity was found when the ratio was 0% BoPL38 and 100% BoPL6, which 
meant that the exolytic lyase BoPL6 was entirely dependent on the endolytic lyase BoPL38 to 
provide attack sites on alginic acid. It can be explained that in the absence of endolytic BoPL38, 
the polyM ends would be unavailable for the exolytic BoPL6 to work on since it attacks the 
nonreducing ends containing unsaturated polyM. Moreover, the synergy of BoPL38 and BoPL6 
increased the catalytic efficiency on alginic acid significantly since the relative enzyme activity 
was much higher than the theoretical relative enzyme activity, especially in the range from 40% 
to 80% BoPL38. Compared to the synergy of BoPL38 and BoPL17, the synergetic action of BoPL38 
and BoPL6 resulted in higher enzyme activity.

4 Discussion  
4.1 Identification of catalytic residues at active site 
In this project, the exolytic alginate lyase BoPL17 WT identified in Bacteroidetes ovatus, was 
recombinantly produced, purified and characterized. The protein has a calculated molecular 
weight of 82593.14 Da and belongs to polysaccharide lyase family 17. It shows higher catalytic 
efficiency on the alginate substrate polyM compared to the substrate CSPM, CSPG, and AlgM. The 
main degradation products generated by BoPL17 WT are monosaccharides and trisaccharides. 
These oligosaccharides have a wide range of applications in fields like agriculture and 
pharmacology. 
The crystal structure of BoPL17 WT has high similarity with other PL17 family members as shown 
in Fig.2 B, C. They share the same fold and quaternary arrangement, and have low RMSD in 
structure alignment. BoPL17 WT shows a whole structure RMSD of 1.808 and C𝛼 RMSD of 1.693 
with Alg17c, which is from the marine bacterium Saccharophagus degradans. A whole structure 
RMSD of 1.944 and C-alpha RMSD of 1.877 was obtained with a protein named AlyA3 from the 
marine flavobacteria Zobellia galactanivorans DsijT.  
Tyr260 and Tyr453 were selected for mutation basing on published reports and sequence 
alignment of proteins from PL17 family in specious of putative catalytic residues at the active 
sites. Both tyrosines were mutated to phenylalanine in single site mutants because the only 
difference in structure between phenylalanine and tyrosine is the lack of a hydroxyl group, which 
work as the proton donator or acceptor during the degradation reaction. The result is shown in 
Fig.20, mutant Y260F showed no activity detected at 10000 nM enzyme concentration towards 
0.5 mg/ml CSPM, Y453F had a little activity detected but much lower than the activity of BoPL17 
WT on CSPM. This result revealed the undetectable enzyme activity in the mutant Tyr260, which 
means that Tyr260 is the residue at the active site of the exolytic lyase BoPL17 WT. 
 
==PAGE:46==
 
 
45 
 
Fig.25 Close up view into the active site of BoPL17 WT. 
4.2 Biochemical characterization of BoPL17 WT 
BoPL17 WT showed higher specificity for PolyM than PolyG, which is similar to most alginate lyases 
from PL17. Aly23 from marine bacterium Pseudoalteromonas sp. ASY5 exhibited a preference for 
PolyM and displayed minor activity toward PolyG.11 Moreover, alginate lyase AlgSH17 from AlgL17 
from Microbulbifer sp. ALW1,36 Microbulbifer sp. SH-1,37 and OalC from Vibrio splendidus 12B0138 
all showed much higher specificity on PolyM than PolyG. Alginate lyases from PL17 always have 
high efficiency on degrading PolyM, only a few might have similar specificity for both PolyM and 
PolyG. For example, OalY2 from Halomonas sp. QY114,39 showed almost the same specific on the 
two substrates of 6.90 U/mg and 6.96 U/mg respectively. 
The optimal pH range for BoPL17 WT is pH 6.25-6.50, the enzyme activity of BoPL17 WT dropped 
significantly outside the range, which means BoPL17 WT is greatly affected by the environmental 
pH since the optimal range is very narrow. The optimal temperature for BoPL17 WT is 47℃, but 
the enzyme is unstable under this condition since the activity dropped 45% after 1 h at 47℃. The 
result from thermal stability revealed that experiments for BoPL17 WT and mutants can be done 
under 50℃. Comparing alginate lyases from PL17, it is easy to find that most of them show narrow 
optimal range of pH and temperature. For example, Oal17A from Vibrio sp. W13,40 showed highest 
activity in pH 7 at 30℃ which dropped quickly with the decreasing of pH. 
The activity of BoPL17 WT was reduced by 60% after dialyzed in 10 mM EDTA for 10 h and a similar 
result was obtained for another alginate lyase from PL17 named AlyA3.34 The 10 mM EDTA treated 
AlyA3 lost 40% activity compared to the untreated. Cations including Ca2+, Mg2+, Mn2+, Ni2+, Zn2+, 
and Cu2+ were added into EDTA treated enzyme and substrate respectively to determine the metal 
ion dependence of BoPL17 WT. The effects of Zn2+ and Cu2+ were not detectable because of the 
formation of sediment. The existence of Ca2+, Mg2+, Mn2+, and Ni2+ in reaction all showed an 
==PAGE:47==
 
 
46 
increase in enzyme activity compared to the original one treated by EDTA without other cation 
added. Especially the enzyme individually incubated by Mg2+ and Ni2+, showed an increase to 
around 200% activity. However, this result is not reliable because the EDTA untreated BoPL17 WT 
had higher enzyme activity than all the EDTA treated enzymes. it is hard to conclude whether 
BoPL17 WT is affected by Ca2+, Mg2+, Mn2+, or Ni2+, but the activity of BoPL17 WT is greatly 
influenced by the concentration of Na+. Fig.14 revealed the optimal NaCl concentration for BoPL17 
WT is 150 mM and the activity will drop by 20% in 100 mM or 200 mM NaCl. Both absence and 
excess of NaCl will inactive the enzyme, which means that BoPL17 WT is a salt-activated alginate 
lyase depending on Na+. 
Among reported alginate lyases, lots of them are salt-activated, and usually show increasing 
activity in the presence of Na+, K+, Mn2+, and Mg2+. For example, the alginate lyase Alg2951,which 
is from a novel marine bacterium Alteromonas portus HB161718T, showed 21.6 and 19.1 times 
increase of enzyme activity in 0.2 mM NaCl or KCl respectively.41 Moreover, AlgA from the marine 
bacterium Bacillus sp. Alg0742 showed considerably enhanced activity in 200 mM Na+, together 
with 2 mM Ca2+ or 2 mM Mg2+.  
The alginate degradation products were analyzed by ESI-MS. The result in Fig.18A and B showed 
the main products of BoPL17 WT towards CSPM are monosaccharides and trisaccharides, which is 
the same with some other alginate lyases from PL17, including HyAly-I from Hydrogenopahaga sp. 
UMI-18.43 The high intensity of unsaturated DP1 proves BoPL17 WT is an exolytic alginate lyase. 
The relative low intensity of unsaturated DP2, together with high intensity of saturated DP2 reveals 
the conversion between the motif (∆) and DHF. The high intensity of unsaturated DP3 is responsible 
for the high amount of saturated DP2 since BoPL17 WT cleaves DP3 into unsaturated DP1 and 
saturated DP2, which convert to DEH and DHF, respectively. 
4.3 Kinetic parameters 
The kinetic parameters of BoPL17 WT on polyM in Table 3 shows high kcat (203.7±2.0 s-1), low Km 
(0.08±0.02mg·ml-1) and high kcat/Km (2546.3 ml·mg-1·s-1). BoPL17 WT shows much lower activity 
on CSPG with 156.1±5 s-1 in kcat, 1.12±0.09 mg·ml-1 in Km, and 139.0 ml·mg-1·s-1 in kcat/Km. It is 
normal for most of alginate lyases from PL17 since they always have higher efficiency in 
degrading PolyM as illustrated in 4.2. However, the alginate substrates used for degradation are 
==PAGE:48==
 
 
47 
difficult to obtain of 100% purity. For example, the PolyM used in most part of this project is from 
Carbosynth and contains around 15% G motifs, which explains the lower catalytic efficiency than 
polyM of 100% purity. The possible reason for odd M-M fitting of mutant Y453F in 3.6.2 is that 
the substrate was easier to bind to the inactive site region of the mutated enzyme with increasing 
substrate concentration. The concentration of Y453F (1000 nM) was too high, which increased 
the difficulty of correct binding of substrate. 
4.4 Synergy 
The synergy of the endo-acting lyase BoPL38 and exo-acting lyase BoPL17 showed the highest 
enzyme activity with 1.64 by 90 % BoPL38 and 10 % BoPL17. Meanwhile, the synergy of endo-
acting lyase BoPL38 and exo-acting lyase BoPL6 showed the highest activity with 6.84 at 60 % 
BoPL38 and 40 % BoPL6. The two synergy studies had much higher enzyme activity than theoretical 
relative enzyme activity respectively, which means that the cooperation of endolytic alginate lyase 
BoPL38 and the exolytic lyases are able to further degrade alginate oligosaccharides and can greatly 
improve the efficiency in alginate degradation. The substrate used in synergy is alginic acid, it 
contains many G-blocks that cannot be degraded by BoPL17, while BoPL6 is a G specific alginate 
lyase. The higher activity shown in synergy for BoPL38 and BoPL6 than for BoPL38 and BoPL17 
reveals the better adaptation and potential of synergy BoPL38 and BoPL6 in monosaccharide 
production towards alginic acid.

5 Conclusion  
The alginate lyase BoPL17 characterized in this project belongs to polysaccharide lyase family 17, 
it is from a noval Bacteroides ovatus isolated from human fecal feces. BoPL17 wild type is an 
exolytic alginate lyase and has high specificity on M-blocks. It degrades alginate substrate polyM 
by 𝛽-elimination, mainly releasing unsaturated monosaccharides and trisaccharides. Similar to 
other alginate lyases from PL family 17, the degradation activity of BoPL17 WT is largely influenced 
by NaCl concentration, pH, and temperature since the optimal ranges are narrow. The metal ions 
also affect the activity of BoPL17 WT, EDTA treated enzyme showed lower activity towards CSPM 
than the untreated one, which is similar to another alginate lyase from PL family 17. EDTA treated 
enzyme incubated in Ca2+, Mg2+, Mn2+, and Ni2+ respectively showed an increase in activity 
compared to the EDTA treated enzyme without other cation added. Moreover, BoPL17 WT has the 
highest affinity for the substrate polyM (100% purity) compared to CSPM, CSPG, and AlgM. It 
showed a kcat at 203.7±2.0 s-1 and kcat/ Km at 2546.3 ml·mg-1·s-1 when degrading polyM, while a kcat 
at 3.1±0.4 s-1 and kcat/ Km at 1.2 ml·mg-1·s-1 when degrading AlgM. 
The synergy of endolytic alginate lyase BoPL38 and exolytic alginate lyase BoPL17 or BoPL6 showed 
the cooperation of endolytic and exolytic alginate lyases will highly improve the degradation 
efficiency towards the alginate substrate alginic acid, and the synergy of BoPL38 and BoPL6 showed 
a higher maximum activity with 6.84. 
Two predicted catalytic active sites of BoPL17 WT, Y260 and Y453 were mutated and the mutant 
Y260F showed undetectable activity towards CSPM, it revealed that Tyr260 is the alginate 
degradation active site of BoPL17 WT.

6 Future research  
6.1 The verification of BoPL17 WT degradation mechanism 
The 𝛽-elimination mechanism shown in Fig.3 is a hypothetical mechanism inspired by other 
alginate lyase from PL family 17. More experiments and analysis should be done to verify the 
degradation mechanism of BoPL17 WT. 
6.2 The structures of two mutants 
To further understand the role of catalytic active sites, the 3-D structures of two mutants BoPL17 
Y260F and BoPL17 Y453F need to be built.
