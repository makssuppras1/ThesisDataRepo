Abstract
Wind Energy Conversion Technology is undoubtedly leading the trend in integration of Re-
newable Energy Sources (RES) especially in the developed countries which are distant from
the equatorial belt.
The European Union has already positioned itself in pioneering the
growth of RES and minimizing the carbon emission. The scarcity of wind and inconsistency
in power production from onshore wind turbines can be signiﬁcantly replaced by the oﬀshore
wind conversion technology with its highest load factors of about 39%. Oﬀshore wind en-
ergy conversion technology is identiﬁed as the key solution for current and future EU energy
system to meet the 2030 energy challenge.
With the growing concern for RES across the globe, the signiﬁcance of Grid-connected con-
verter is rising as a major component in electrical network. Power electronics, an integral
part of wind farm connected to the grid, provides eﬃcient control and regulated ﬂow of
power under variable resources and power generating capacities but at the cost of additional
harmonic contamination in the converter output voltage and current waveforms. In case
of oﬀshore wind farm, the extent of harmonics injected by the wind turbines into the grid
depends on the submarine transmission system and power electronic interfaces. To eliminate
wide range of harmonics, the size of the passive ﬁlters increases and lead to additional instal-
lation cost. Moreover, the passive ﬁlters are not adaptive to the change of generation-load
conditions and existing harmonic proﬁle. Therefore, the functionality and control of active
ﬁlters are proposed. Active ﬁlters are not only adaptive to the existing harmonic proﬁle but
also provide ﬂexible control mode without any additional installation requirement.
In general, active ﬁlter is popular as power electronic controlled ﬁlter connected in shunt
position in addition to the passive ﬁlter components. Whereas, the motivation of this study is
to explore the possibility to embed the active ﬁlter in the Grid-side inverter without inserting
it as additional shunt component across the transmission system. This research is dedicated
to identify the development of harmonics in long distance transmission network and measure
the harmonic content in the voltage and current waveforms at the Point of Coupling.
The propagation of harmonics is investigated in multiple case studies for diﬀerent medium
voltage submarine transmission lengths. Therefore, a justiﬁed active ﬁltering solution is pro-
posed by using analog low pass and band stop ﬁlters in the closed-loop controller of the
grid side inverter. Additionally, a self-learning smart active ﬁltering technique is also recom-
mended in this study. Finally, an impedance-based stability analysis of the grid connected
inverter system is identiﬁed. Thereafter, the converter side and grid side impedance are an-
alyzed on wide band frequency spectrum and linked with the active ﬁltering solution. The
entire study is done in MATLAB/Simulink.

Introduction
To promote the future energy security, it is essential to enhance the integration of energy
from Renewable Energy Sources (RES) into the exiting power system network.
Among
multiple RES, Wind Energy Conversion Technology (WECT) is undoubtedly leading the
trend, especially in the developed countries which are distant from the equatorial belt. Oﬀ-
shore Wind Energy Conversion Technology (Oﬀ-WECT) is the ﬂagship technology to drive
the growth of RES across the globe. It provides the highest load hours among all RES with
more than 8000 operation hours, which is equivalent to 340 days of production [1]. The
scarcity and lack of constancy of power production in On-shore Wind Energy Conversion
Technology (On-WECT) can be signiﬁcantly replaced by Oﬀ-WECT. The European Union
(EU) has already positioned itself in pioneering the growth of RES and minimizing the carbon
emission. Under this circumstance, Oﬀ-WECT is the key solution for current and future EU
energy market to meet the 2030 energy challenge [2].
Figure 1.1: Load Factors of diﬀerent Renewable Energy Sources [1]
Over the last decade, the size and power generation capacity of the Oﬀshore Wind Turbines
(Oﬀ-WT) are increasing rapidly. Increasing levels of wind power penetrations has led the
wind farms eventually replacing the conventional power plants (based on oil, coal and gas).
Therefore, in the revised grid codes the wind farms are demanded to fulﬁll a wide range
of auxiliary services in order to be integrated to the existing electrical network [3][4]. The
auxiliary services that the wind turbines need to provide are [4] i) Power production regulation
during normal operation condition, ii) Fault ride-through capability (FRT), iii) Enhanced
reactive power control and voltage support, iv) Frequency-active power control.

Power electronics has become an integral part of wind farm connected to centralized grid.
It provides eﬃcient and regulated ﬂow of power under variable wind resources and power

Grid-connected Converter Technology
With the growing concern for RES across the world, the signiﬁcance of Grid-connected Con-

into the utility grid in a sustainable manner but at the cost of its stochastic generation char-
acteristics. Therefore, they are not ﬁt for direct coupling with the utility grid operating at a
nominal frequency (50 Hz or 60 Hz). To connected the RES to the grid, GCC plays the key
interfacing role and transform the RES as an ideal match for the grid conditions [15].
Figure 2.1: Integration of RES into the Grid through GCC
The power generating capacity of each unit of RES (PV panel or Wind Turbine) is much
lower than the conventional power generating unit. To collect suﬃcient amount of energy,
hundreds and thousands of RES are installed in a farm. Therefore, the task of GCC in

maintaining the power ﬂow in a large farm comes at the price of stringent control challenges
[16][17][18]. Some of the major and identical control challenges faced by GCC both in case
of PV and Wind farm are described below,
• Maximum Power Point Tracking (MPPT): The MPPT method is popular in PV
and wind farm for extraction of maximum power for speciﬁed resource (solar irradiance,

of PV, the voltage of the solar array is regulated by continuous computation method to

• Decoupling Generation and Load:

areas to the load center through multiple AC-DC conversion stages.
• Hybrid RES Control: The coexistence of diﬀerent RES connected to the grid through
the same POC is a common occurrence in recent time. The GCC plays the key role
in accumulating energy from diﬀerent RES, depending on their individual generating
characteristics. In several cases, large-scale energy storage system also exists in parallel
to the RES at the POC. Besides the power ﬂow regulations, GCC also maintains the
steady operation of RES within their margin of stability.
To conclude the beneﬁt of GCC in RES integrations into utility grid the following can be
summarized from [19][20][21],
• Extraction of Maximum Power
• Control of Active-Reactive power ﬂow
• Synchronization of the RES with grid voltage
• Enhancement of the quality of power injected into the grid from RES

Operation and Control in Large Wind Farm
Voltage Source converter (VSC) is suitable for distant oﬀshore wind power integration into
the AC grid due to its reactive power control and grid voltage support capability, small size
ﬁlter and black-start capability [27]. In case of VSC connected multi-terminal HVDC link,
there are two diﬀerent converters connected at the ends of the HVDC line, Wind Farm-side
Voltage Source Converter (WFVSC) and Grid-side Voltage Source Converter (GSVSC) [28].
Due to the added advantages of long distance HVDC transmission, two converters are used
to convert power respectively from AC/DC and DC/AC. The GSVSC plays the major role
in controlling the Active and Reactive Power ﬂowing to the Grid.
Figure 2.3: Multiterminal HVDC link connecting Oﬀshore Wind Farms to the Grid
The operation and control of the WFVSC and GSVSC are described below [29][30][31][32],
• WFVSC: To extract the added beneﬁt of power control from the variable-speed wind
turbines,the WFVSC is directly connected to the wind turbine generator. The pur-
pose of the converter is to extract maximum power from the wind turbine following
the MPPT algorithm. It changes the active power reference as per the direction of
the GSVSC under speciﬁc grid operating condition and requirement. The active and
reactive power ﬂow is controlled by dq-axes vector control method. The d-axis is used
for active power ﬂow and q-axis is used for reactive power ﬂow control. In general, the
q-axis reactive power set point is kept zero for unity power factor operation.

• GSVSC: The grid side converter is operated in synchronization with the grid. The
grid synchronization signal is taken from Phase Locked Loop (PLL). The GSVSC is
also controlled by dq-axes vector control method, where the d-axis controls the DC link
voltage and the q-axis controls the reactive power injection to the grid. The control of
the GSVSC can be developed based on optimal power ﬂow scheme.
Primarily, there are two control scheme exists for these converters [28], a) Constant DC
voltage control (master-slave) and b) DC voltage droop control.
In case of master-slave
control, one of the converters is assigned to compensate the power losses which is similar
to the functionality of the slack bus in any AC system. Whereas, in case of DC voltage
droop control, the task of power balance is divided between two or more GSVSC terminals
according to the individual droop characteristics. In both the control strategies, the active
and reactive power references control the power ﬂow through the networks. Moreover, GSVSC
must be controlled so that the existing grid code is strictly maintained. In addition to the
stability enhancement of the grid, the voltage proﬁle (amplitude, frequency, Total Harmonic
Distortion) needs also to be maintained within the acceptable limit.
2.2
Active Filtering Method
The problems associated with the non-sinusoidal waveforms in electrical system was ﬁrst stud-
ied and described in 1890’s in [33]. In recent days, widespread use of power electronics and
high frequency switching devices, the harmonic problem has become signiﬁcantly complex.
Moreover, the long distance transmission line injects additional harmonic contamination into
the power system due to the presence of distributed shunt capacitance and inductance [34].
Due to the presence of high capacitance in submarine cable in case of oﬀshore wind farm
transmission system, a series of resonance points are developed in between oﬀshore and on-
shore grid which cause system harmonic instability by contaminating system voltage-current
waveforms [35]. In the last decade, harmonic propagation in the electrical system is receiving
signiﬁcant attention from grid operators and researchers.
To protect the power system from harmonics generated by the power electronic interfaces,
typically LC ﬁlters are connected with the converters [36][37][38]. In LCL ﬁlters the capacitor
is used to provide the high frequency components a low impedance path to the ground. The
inductors in LCL ﬁlters attenuate the current harmonics passing through it [39]. L, LC,
LCL passive ﬁlers eliminate the high order harmonics but also decrease the over all system
eﬃciency. In addition to that the passive ﬁlters are not adaptive and cannot change itself as
per the system harmonic proﬁle and ﬁltering requirement. Moreover, the equipment cost is
proportional to the size of the LCL ﬁlter, therefore it incurs additional installation cost [40].

The traditional active ﬁlers mitigate the harmonics with better accuracy [41] in comparison to
the passive ﬁlters. It is connected in the shunt position and operated through high frequency
switching devices. The active ﬁlters can be based on Current Source Inverter (CSI) or Voltage
Source Inverter (VSI) principle. In case of CSI and VSI, inductor and capacitor act as energy
storage device respectively [42]. Due to the presence of additional power electronic devices,
active ﬁlter is relatively expensive than passive ﬁlters. To reduce the rating and operate it for
a wide range of harmonic ﬁltering, the design of hybrid ﬁlter is also proposed by researchers

shunt active ﬁlter, it beneﬁts at the additional shunt installations. Therefore, it is challenging
to explore the possibility of developing the active ﬁlter inside the grid side inverter to reduce
the harmonics content in the voltage and current waveforms as in [46][47]. In this research,

3.2
Inverter Design
The DC/AC inverter is the key element in the Grid-Connected Converter (GCC) system. In
general, the DC voltage source can be sourced from an energy storage system or the output
of the controlled rectiﬁer. In this case, since the wind power is ﬁrst converted to DC power
using (generator-side converter) rectiﬁer and fed into the DC link, the DC voltage can be
considered as fairly constant.
There are two types of inverters, Current Source Inverter (CSI) and Voltage Source Inverter
(VSI). The input current and input voltage are kept constant in CSI and VSI respectively.
While both CSI and VSI only require unidirectional current capable switches, VSI requires
unidirectional voltage blocking and CSI requires bidirectional voltage blocking [56]. A sim-
pliﬁed two-level three phase VSI is shown Figure 3.2.
Figure 3.2: Two-level three phase Voltage Source Inverter
The VSC consists of six switches; S1-S4, S3-S6, S5-S2 in three legs with the free-wheeling
diodes connected across each IGBT switch in anti-parallel position. The inverter can be
fed with two diﬀerent voltage sources with VDC/2 voltage for each voltage source. The two

3.2.1
Sinusoidal Pulse Width Modulation
The switches in a VSI are turned on and oﬀto form the output voltage waveforms. In each
cycle the upper and lower switches of a leg are turned on with a tun on delay time to avoid
undesired short circuiting the source [58][59].
In general, the output voltage is achieved
by comparing the desired waveform (modulation signal) with a high frequency triangular
waveform (carrier waveform). Based on the comparison of the two signals, the modulation
signal is larger or smaller than the carrier signal, the positive or negative bus voltage is
applied at the output.
There are three types of carrier signals commonly used in constant frequency PWM. 1) Saw-
tooth Carrier, 2) Inverted Sawtooth Carrier, 3) Traiangle Carrier. The modulation method
for three diﬀerent carrier waveforms are referred as constant frequency trailing-edge mod-
ulation, leading-edge modulation and double-edge modulation respectively. Among them,
triangular double-edge modulation is the most popular due to its inherent harmonic elim-
ination beneﬁt [60]. The triangular carrier signal is the optimal selection for three phase
converter, since it provides minimum switching state during control. In every commutation,
the converter output line voltage changes which is not the case in saw-tooth carrier waveform.
Figure 3.3: Three types of Carrier Waveforms used for PWM technique

techniques for control of the multilevel converters. In this modulation technique, the width
of all pulses are not kept same. Instead of that, the width of each pulse is varied propor-
tional to the amplitude of a sine-wave evaluated at the centre of the same pulse. The main
advantages of the SPWM is its simplicity and good dynamic response in inverter operation.

The most widely used synchronization solution to a time-varying signal is Phase Locked Loop
(PLL) [71]. It is a nonlinear feedback system which tracks the phase of the input signal. The
basic PLL architecture contains, 1) Phase Detector (PD), 2) Time-invariant linear loop ﬁlter
and 3) Voltage-controlled oscillator (VCO). The basic architecture of PLL is shown below,
Figure 4.4: Close-loop synchronization structure
In the above conﬁguration, PD compares the frequency of the input signal and the re-
sulting output signal. The output of the PD is a DC signal and therefore is referred as error
signal. The error signal is fed to the low pass loop ﬁlter, which removes the high frequency
noise. Loop ﬁlter also determines the dynamics of the PLL. The resulting DC noise free signal
is fed to VCO which generates a sinusoidal signal. There are diﬀerent types of PLL based
on the synchronization mechanism [71], e.g. Synchronous Frame PLL (SF-PLL), Instanta-
neous real and imaginary power theory PLL (PQ-PLL), Double Synchronous Frame PLL
(DSF-PLL), Sinusoidal Signal Integrator PLL (SSI-PLL), Double second order generalized
integrator PLL (DSOGIPLL), Enhanced phase-locked loop (EPLL), Three-phase magnitude
phase locked loop (3MPLL) etc. In this case, the SF-PLL is considered.

angle θ is detected by synchronizing the rotating reference frame of the PLL with the provided
input voltage vector Vabc. The PI controller is used to set one of the dq (direct-quadrature)
transformed voltage (Vd or Vq) as zero, due to which the PLL reference frame is locked to
the voltage vector phase angle. The voltage magnitude Vm and frequency f are also obtained
as byproducts. In presence of high order harmonic distortion in the supply voltage, the SF-
PLL with high bandwidth can provide fast and precise phase and amplitude of the supply
voltage waveform. The amplitude, phase and frequency values provided by the PLL are not
individual-phase value but average value for all three phases.

Design and Implementation of Current Controller
4.4
Design and Implementation of Current Controller
The current controller in a three phase VSC plays an important role in controlling the ac-
tive and reactive power injection to the grid. The overall dynamics and performance of the
inverter depend on the performance of the controller.
There are multiple converter con-
trol techniques available for GCC system [75], 1) Hysteresis controller, 2) Model Predictive
Controller (MPC), 3) Linear Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) Controller.
In hysteresis controller, a hysteresis band is set around the reference current with width h.
If the line current is higher (lower) than the upper limit (lower limit) of the band, the receiving
end voltage is set as higher (lower) than the sending end voltage and the current reduces
(increases). Variable switching frequency is a major limitation in this type of controller [75].
MPC strategy is widely applied for Modular Multi-level Converter.
The principle of
operation of MPC lies in making use of a discrete time model of the system to predict the
future value of the control state for variable control inputs. In this technique, an on-line
optimization control algorithm is developed [76].
The limitation of this controller is the
diﬃculty in the formulation of the model which requires high level of expertise to include to
implement it for widespread utilization [77].
The PID controller is the most widely used form of feedback controller. More than, 95%
control loops in industry are based on PID control technique [78]. It is often combined with
logic, sequential functions, selectors, simple function blocks to developed the complicated
automation system.
Therefore, this controller is termed as “bread and butter of control
engineering” because of its simplicity and widespread applicability. In this study, PID (D =
0) controller is implemented.

Active Filtering
The active ﬁltering is desired by implementing a suitable control technique inside the con-
troller of the grid-side converter to reduce the harmonic content in the voltage and current
harmonics at the POC.
Based on the observation and discussion developed in Chapter 5, it is clear that the active
ﬁltering technique can be derived on the fact that harmonic contamination in measured and
control signals MUST be be separated from each other. Instead of using large passive LCL
ﬁlter in combination with or without the shunt active ﬁlter, the use and implementation
of analog ﬁlter can eﬀectively reduce the added the installation burden for an wide range
of harmonic elimination. Therefore the use of analog ﬁlter in the closed-loop controller is
recommended in this study to embed the Active Filtering technique in the inverter.
7.1
Proposed Solution
To implement the Active Filtering in the controller, the voltage waveforms are measured
from the system (at the LCL-ﬁlter output terminal) and passed through the analog lowpass
ﬁlter. Buttherworth, Chebyshev, Bessel, Elliptical Filters are the commonly used analog
ﬁlter. In this study, to eliminate the harmonics from the voltage and current waveforms,
lowpass buttherworth and bandstop butterworth ﬁlters are implemented. The complexity of
the ﬁlter is determined by the ﬁlter order.

within the design.

7.3
Smart Active Filtering
To achieve the Active Filtering technique inside the grid-side converter, the implementation of
lowpass and bandstop butterworth ﬁlter is explained in previous section. Since, the purpose
of the lowpass ﬁlter is to pass eﬀectively the DC component (Ed and Eq) of the measured
voltages (Eabc) the pass band edge frequency should be close enough to zero.
Whereas,
the BSF edge frequencies (fL and fH) are decided by analyzing the impedance-frequency
spectrum to eliminate the harmonic contamination from the modulation waveforms (mabc).
Therefore, it requires additional interactive control adjustment. Moreover, in a multi-WTs
integration the impedance spectrum is more complicated (as previously shown in Figure
7.16). Therefore, it is diﬃcult to predict the major resonance point and develop an error-free
Active Filtering scheme.
An improved Active Filtering technique is developed by allowing the system to decide the
BSF edges, following the FFT analysis of the measured voltage signal. This Active Filtering
technique with additional smartness involved is termed as Smart Active Filtering.

Conclusions
This thesis performs analysis and study of active ﬁltering technique in a large-scale oﬀshore
wind farm connected to the grid through long distance MV and HV transmission system.
The main conclusions of this thesis are listed below,
• The oﬀshore farm is radially distributed around the PCC and includes power electronic
interfaces, transformers, long distance submarine cables.
• Grid-side converter of a wind turbine can be used to eﬀectively control the active and
reactive power ﬂow to the grid.
• A suitable control technique of the grid-side converter can accurately extract the beneﬁt
from the wind turbines to support the grid operation under variable steady/faulty
operating conditions.
• LCL-ﬁlter plays a key role in limiting the harmonics injected by the high frequency
switching converters into the grid.
• The interaction between the converters and submarine cable capacitance-inductance,
transformer inductance can tune one or multiple resonance points which primarily con-
taminate the POC voltage and current waveforms.
• It is possible to embed the Active Filtering technique inside the grid-side converter
by separating the harmonic contamination of the transmission system and the closed-
loop current controller from each other, by suitably using the Notch Filter (Band Stop
analog ﬁlters).
• Impedance-based stability analysis provides an unique opportunity to understand the
qualitative and quantitative proportion of harmonic content in POC voltage and current
waveforms in a large, complex transmission network.

Alternative Active Filtering technique
An alternative active ﬁltering technique is described here based on the intuitive knowl-
edge developed by the author during this study. It is true that any harmonic component can
be expressed as a trigonometric summation of lower order harmonic components by suitably
adjusting the harmonic order, phase angle and amplitude of the individual lower order har-
monic components. The modulation waveform carries the harmonic component injected by
the measured voltage and current harmonics, the ﬁltering of the modulation waveform by
using notch ﬁlter is recommended in this study. But the elimination can also be achieved by
injecting suitable lower order harmonic comments and nullify the eﬀect of existing harmonic
component. For example a sinusoidal waveform carries 11th harmonic
V (ωt) = B1sin(ωt + φ1) + B2sin(11ωt + φ2)
(68)
Now B2sin(11ωt + φ2) can be expressed as H(ωt) where,
H(ωt) = A1sin(3ωt + φ3) + A2sin(5ωt + φ4)
(69)
Therefore, small proportion of 3rd and 5th harmonics can cancel out the existing 11th harmonic
component from the modulation waveform.
This method requires detailed mathematical derivation with dynamic phase angle detec-
tion.
