1 | Introduction
Denmark has always been a country that has adopted an active energy policy for a long time.
This was a response to the ﬁrst oil crisis in 1973. Over the past few decades, the Danish govern-
ment has reached a broad consensus to promote the transformation of the Danish energy system
to reduce energy. Consumption, decentralize energy production and increase the use of renewable
energy.Through a clear political direction, the Danish experience shows that it is possible to main-
tain signiﬁcant economic growth and reduce dependence on fossil fuels protecting the climate and
environment through sustained and active energy policies that focus on improving energy eﬃciency
and ambitious use of renewable energy [1] .
In order to establish a more integrated energy system, on the one hand, it is necessary to improve
eﬃciency of energy use and strengthen the coupling and cooperation of multi-energy. On the other
hand, more eﬃcient market mechanism and developed dispatch scheme should be proposed.
1.1
Background and motivation
Denmark is a country with relatively scarce natural resources. Before the oil crisis in the 1970s,
the energy self-suﬃciency rate of Denmark was less than 2%, and more than 90% of energy supply
was depend on the oil that imported from other countries [2]. In 1973, the ﬁrst oil crisis broke
out. Because of the single structure of energy supply, the Danish economy was severely shocked.
The Danish government realized the importance of energy self-suﬃciency and initialized the ﬁrst
transition of energy structure to ensure the safety of energy supply. By the early 90s, with giving
a huge push to develop the oil and gas ﬁeld in North sea and increasing use of coal [3], the
energy self-suﬃciency rate of Denmark had exceeded 100% and its energy structure had been more
diversiﬁed.
Entering the 21st century, as the serious issues of greenhouse gas emissions and climate change had
became more severe, the Danish government started the second energy transformation with the
goal of achieving green sustainable development. During this period, the Danish government took
measures to increase investment and infrastructure in renewable energy, improve energy eﬃciency
and reduce energy consumption. As the ﬁgure 1.1 shows, from 2000 to 2017, the share of renewable
energy in the energy consumption of Denmark had rose rapidly, meanwhile, the share of oil, coal
and natural gas had decreased each year.

Since the start of the second energy transition, the proportion of renewable energy in the energy
consumption of Denmark has been steadily increasing. As a country with the leading technology
in wind energy, nowadays, wind power takes a large proportion of renewable energy in Denmark.
Together with biomass energy, it has become the two pillars of the Danish renewable energy system.
What is more, the proportions of biogas, heat pumps and solar energy are also rising steadily. The
energy system of Denmark trends to become diversiﬁcation as whole.
By 2050, the Danish government has set the goal to realize a 100% renewable based energy system,
of which development target will be implemented in a number of phases. Eventually, all the electric-
ity, heat, transport and industry sectors will be covered by renewable energy sources domestically.
So that, the Danish low-carbon transition energy system not only requires further energy eﬃciency
improvement and new low-carbon energy, but also further energy cooperation with Denmark’s
neighboring countries to reduce costs and ensure the stability and security of safe energy supply.
In order to secure operation of the cost-eﬃcient low-carbon energy power system, optimisations
and tests for diﬀerent scenarios should be conducted. In this thesis project, the focus have been
on real time closed loop test of the model predictive control (MPC) based dynamic dispatch of
the integrated 2050 Danish energy system. The MPC based dynamic dispatch scheme has been
developed to deal with the uncertainty from renewable production and the changing electricity
demand during real-time dispatch.
1.2
Power system and future perspectives
Since 2006, countries around world have begun to make eﬀorts in the development of renewable
energy. As the country which has the largest wind power utilization rate in the world, the success-
ful decision-making planning, technical solutions and developing process in renewable energy of
Denmark have always attracted attentions. As the renewable industry becomes more mature, the
eﬃciency, safety and stability of renewable energy in the operation of power system are becoming
more and more important. How to actively and eﬀectively consume renewable energy and overcome
its intermittent and instability has been the key to further promote the construction of fossil-free
energy system.

Compared with conventional energy sources, the construction costs of solar and wind power are
still high, however, since 2008, the Danish Renewable Energy Law has clariﬁed a series of subsidy
schemes for wind energy, reducing its market prices and making it the priority to be accepted by
the market. As the coverage rate of wind power increases each year, In 2017, the average annual
coverage rate of wind power in Denmark reached 43% [5] of electricity consumption, conventional
large-scale power plants have been shut down or upgraded to only provide necessary system aux-
iliary services for grid stability. With the promotion and improvement of wind energy subsidy
policies these years, conventional coal and oil power plants have been retiring from the stage of
history. On September 2nd, 2015, the Danish Power System made an attempt for the ﬁrst time to
supply electricity to the whole country without any large conventional power plant during a daily
operation [6], in this case, the electricity consumption demand was mainly met by wind power,
solar energy, combined heat and power and the interconnection with neighboring countries.
’The world is ushering in the third industrial revolution of the coordinated development of re-
newable energy and internet information technology. In the future, millions of people in society
will realize self-production of green and renewable energy in their homes, oﬃce area and factories.
Any energy producer can share the produced energy with others through a smart grid’ [7], such
the scene is pictured in the, American economic and social theorist, Jeremy Rifkin’s book, The
Third industrial Revolution. As a leader in the development and utilization of renewable energy
in the world, Denmark actively uses modern information technology to integrate energy networks
and strives to build a smart energy system which can realize the production, storage, transmission
and sharing of renewable energy. The smart energy system is composed of new technologies and
infrastructure, which have brought ﬂexibility to the system that are diﬀerent from traditional en-
ergy system. The smart energy system combines the electricity, heating and transportation sectors
to make up for the inﬂexibility of renewable resources by using the ﬂexibility across these three
diﬀerent ﬁelds. In the plan of Danish government, the smart energy system will be built around
three networks: smart electricity grids, smart thermal grids and smart gas grids [8]
The establishment of Danish smart energy system will mainly focus on the following directions:
heat pumps and district heating, user-side demand response, data communications, electricity
transmission infrastructure and regional power market based on the interconnection of neighboring
counties.

1.3
Problem statement and research objectives
With increasing wind energy penetration rate year by year, there are challenges in achieving better
cost saving and increased the revenue. Although wind forecasting technique becomes more and
more accurate, there are still have errors between the prediction and actual power production,
especially during the periods of medium and fast speed of winds. Due to the deviations of forecast,
there will be a demand for power balance, which could be adjusted from two aspects: supply and
demand. On the supply side, biomass, biogas plants and other fast-adjustable units, such as energy
storage, could be play an important role in complementing renewable energy. On the demand side,
electric boiler or heat pumps, large industrial consumers and other ﬂexible consumer units are well
suited to provide the balanced services. what is more, the grid coupling between regions and the
integration with the energy system of neighboring countries can also contribute to the balance of
the system.
when it comes to the system balance, the electricity market has always acted a pivotal part in
balancing the supply and consumption of electricity.
In the electricity market, Denmark has
alternative support schemes for wind energy, the most common way is to pay a premium for wind
power on top of the wholesale electricity market price. If most of the electricity based on wind
power is sold at a low or negative price, not only it is proﬁtless to wind power suppliers but also a
discouragement of investment in the development of wind power. In order to ensure the eﬀective
consumption of wind power, the power system of Denmark should be extremely ﬂexible. During
the period of high wind, electricity could be exported to neighboring countries and promote the
consumption by economic means, at the mean time, reduce the production of ﬂexible power plant
in dispatch. While, during the period of low wind, suﬃcient and fast power generation capacity
(biomass and biogas power plants) and imports from other countries are required to ensure the
electricity supply.
In the project, the integrated dispatch will be calculated through optimal MPC, and in order to test
its eﬀectiveness on top of the oﬄine simulation, the real-time closed loop test will be conducted.
There are objectives of this project which are stated below:
• A closed loop test platform will be established with the real timed digital simulator (RTDS)
and MATLAB optimal MPC based dispatch scheme.
• The 2050 Danish electrical power system will be modeled in the RTDS and interact with the
optimal MPC based dispatch scheme.
• Typical operation scenarios will be used to test the scheme.

2 | Closed-loop Test
2.1
Introduction
The modern power system has developed into a large interconnected system which is composed of
high-eﬃciency, low-pollution, low-energy-consumption generation equipment, large-capacity, long-
distance transmission network and dynamic loads. Because of the constraints of environmental
protection, land use and energy market competition mechanism, there is a urgent need to expand
on the basis of current existing transmission network to supply more electricity power to cater the
future power demand. Minimize the operation costs as well as improve the operating eﬃciency
and stability of the power system. Accurately determine the power transmission capacity between
regions of the power system and its inﬂuencing factors, the power system can balance the production
and consumption of all regions under the conditions of meeting the safety and reliability limits.
Operation and planning of power systems further lead to many kinds of optimization problems.
Questions like ’What power plants should be generating how much power at any given time?’
require the solution of some optimization problem, where the set of feasible solutions is determined
by power ﬂow problems [9]. A model predictive control (MPC) based dynamic dispatch of the
2050 Danish energy system have been developed to further minimize the costs of integrated energy
system operation. Meanwhile, in order to test the Practicality of the MPC in real time, a closed-
loop system have been established.

3 | Model Predictive Control
3.1
Introduction
Model Predictive control (MPC) is a feedback control algorithm which uses a model to make
predictions about future outputs of the process. Since there are several advantages in MPC, it
is very widely implemented within diﬀerent industries. First, MPC is a multi-variable controller
that controls the outputs simultaneously by taking all the interactions between system variables
into account; second, MPC can not only optimize the current time slot, but also take future time
slots into consideration, which can easily incorporate future reference information into the control
problem to improve the performance of the controller.
In a control problem, the objective of a controller is to calculate the inputs to the system such that
the system outputs follows a desired reference. The strategy of MPC to compute the inputs is to
predict the future. MPC utilizes the model of a system to predict its future behavior and its solves
an online optimization problem to select the best control action that drives the predicted outputs
to the reference.
Figure 3.1: A discrete MPC scheme.[11]
As the ﬁg 3.1 shows, the vertical axis reﬂects the current time, anything on the left reﬂects the
past, and values to the right of it indicate the future. The red line is the reference. At the current
time, the MPC controller uses the system model to simulate the system behavior in the next p
time steps. p is a measure of how far ahead MPC looks into the future and is referred to as the
prediction horizon, which is often represented by the length of time into the future or the number
of future time steps.
The MPC controller needs to ﬁnd the best predicted path that is the closest to the reference.
By solving the optimization problem, the MPC controller tries to minimize the error between the
reference and predicted output of the system. It also tries to predigest the control behavior from
one time step to the next. what is more, MPC makes sure that the control behavior and system
output stay within prescribed limits which are referred to as constraints.
At current time step, the MPC controller is solving the optimization problem over the prediction
horizon while satisfying the constraints and applies only the ﬁrst step of the optimal sequence to
10

the system and disregards the rest. Based on the applied control behavior, the output of the system
will get closer to the reference. At the next time step, the controller gets a new measurement of
output position, which might be slightly diﬀerent than what the MPC controller has predicted
before. This could be due to some unmeasured disturbance acting on the system. After the ﬁrst
control action, the prediction horizon shifts forward by one time step and the controller repeats
the same cycle of calculations to compute the optimal control for the next time.

The MPC is based on the DC optimal power ﬂow dispatch scheme. By considering the power
injections distributed along the power lines and the nominal capacity of transmission lines are
not exceeded by power ﬂow, through linearizing the non-linear factors, the DC-OPF solve the
opimization problem to minimize the cost of the system operation. In order to provide a optimal
electricity dispatch, all the supply and demand should be taken into consideration as well, on the
supply side, the onshore and oﬀshore wind farm and import from other countries are treated as
regular generation variables while the output of CHP, biomass plant will be the ﬂexible energy to
balancing the system. In terms of consumption, the basic demand for electricity and the export
of electricity to other countries are regarded as regular loads, while the demand for ﬂexible loads,
P2G, electric boilers and heat pumps will be all modeled as ﬂexible load.

4 | Real time digital simulator
4.1
Introduction
Figure 4.1: The hardware of Real time digital simulator [14]
RTDS (Real Time Digital Simulators) is a real-time all-digital power system electromagnetic tran-
sient simulation device developed by Canada’s RTDS company, contained with sophisticated hard-
ware and software integrated computer system.
RTDS adopts parallel hardware structure and high-speed digital signal processing (DSP) chips. To
be able to calculate the amount models and constraints in real time, the concept of mathematically
separable subsystems have been used to share computing tasks among various computing chips or
chipsets. The connection between each subsystem uses a transmission line model or a transformer
model.
It is an important inspection tool for research and analysis of power system characteristics and
closed-loop testing of automatic control and protection equipment. Because the physical dynamic
model simulation system of similar principles retains the physical characteristics of the actual
system, the simulation results of RTDS are widely recognized. As the RTDS Technologies continues
to make improvements and update its products, the real-time digital simulator has became the
world standard and benchmark tool for real-time digital power system simulation.[15]

4.2
Hardware
Since the high-speed Digital Signal Processing (DSP) chips and Reduced Instruction Set Computer
(RISC) are employed in RTDS to achieve real-time computational speeds, the Calculation of the
solution for each time step requires no more than one time step. what is more, the system is
modular, allowing users to increase computing capability as required.
4.2.1
RACK
Figure 4.2: The RTDS rack
The RTDS rack is a unit of the hardware which consisting of a number of parallel processing cards,
including one to six RISC processor cards (GPC), one Giga Transceiver Workstation InterFace card
(GTWIF) and maximum capacity 72 main network nodes plus auxiliary components etc. Each
rack has an independent power supply to ensure the safety of operation .

GTWIF card

As the ﬁgure 4.2 and 4.3 shows, the Giga Transceiver Workstation InterFace card (GTWIF) has
always been installed on the right side of an RTDS rack which provides the communication interface
between the Local Area Network (LAN) and the RTDS simulator. Each GTWIF is assigned a
unique IP address so that it may communicate with any computer or device accessible on the
LAN. It has several functions:[16]
• The GTWIF provides communication which takes place to load, start, and stop simulation
cases; to retrieve results from the simulation while it is running; and to permit the user to
interact with the simulation case while it is running.
• The GTWIF provides the time-step clock to all processors, synchronizing their calculations,
and coordinates the communication between cards. In multi-rack simulations, one GTWIF is
designed to provide the master time-step clock which is passed to other racks via the Globe
Bus Hub (GBH).
• Each GTWIF has six high-speed Inter-Rack Communication (IRC) channels. Each IRC chan-
nel is bi-directional and capable of transferring one gigabit of data per second.
• The data that must be exchanged between racks will automatically determine by software.
Only essential data is exchanged in order to reduce the amount of time required for commu-
nication. As well, data exchange between various racks is done in parallel.

In this project, some component models in the power system library of RSCAD are used, which

Dynamic PQ Source
Figure 5.3: The model of dynamic source (PQ injection) in RSCAD
Both single and three phase dynamic PQ source are provided in the library of Draft module. The
three phase dynamic source is presented in the ﬁgure 5.3 , which provides a balanced three phase
current injection for a given P, Q, or Power Factor reference, so that it could been seen as a
constant stable power source. In the system model, it is assume that the supply of onshore and
oﬀshore wind farm along with import from neighboring countries are constant at each step, so, it
is reasonable to use dynamic PQ source to represent this three type of supply. The power set point
at each step could be controlled by sliders and switch in the RunTime through the ’CC’ option in
the ’Current Control Parameters’ conﬁguration menu. Once selecting the ’CC’ mode, the signal
must be deﬁned in the Draft module or the dynamic source cannot be control in the RunTime.

Synchronous Generator and Controls
Figure 5.4: The model of synchronous generator and controls in RSCAD
Because the biomass-based combined heat and power plants are used as auxiliary units to balancing
the electricity system, the CHP must be able to make up for the lack of supply in the current power
dispatch in real time, for this reason, the synchronous generator model, the power output of which
is continually changing base on the condition of the electricity system, have been used to represent
the biomass supply.
Transformer
Figure 5.5: The model of transformer in RSCAD
Several types of transformers are provided in the RSCAD. because the eﬀect of transformers in the
power system must be take into account, in the system model, the typical three phase two winding
transformer conponent model has been used. There are three type of connection in the transformer,
delta, wye and wye-grounded which can be selected based on the porpuse of users in the transformer
conﬁguration menu. Additionally, three options could be chose to set the operation condition of
the transformer, ideal, linear or saturation. Tap changer is also available in the transformer model.

5.3.1.0
Transmission Line
Figure 5.6: The model of transmission line in RSCAD
Travelling wave transmission line models and PI section models are both available to represent
transmission line on the RTDS. Travelling wave models are distributed parameter representations
of transmission lines and are much more accurate for modelling long lines.
Since some of the
transmission line’s length are over 90km, the travelling wave model are preferred in the system
model. Additionally, travelling wave line models provide a means for separating the power system
into mathematically isolated subsystems which can be run on separate RTDS racks [24]. In RSCAD,
the Tline module is used to deﬁne the line geometry, conﬁguration and other relevant parameters.
The Tline ﬁle (x.tlo), which must be in the same simulation case folder and must be consistent
with the name of transmission line model in draft, is used by the Draft module upon compilation
of a case containing the line.
Dynamic Load
Figure 5.7: The model of dynamic load in RSCAD
Dynamic three phase load model is provided in the libaray of RSCAD to serve diﬀerent needs of
loads in the power system. The dynamic load model maintains real and reactive power set points
by dynamically varying the conductance and can be arranged in delta or wye connection. In the

neighboring countries, the other represents the ﬂexible load, electric boiler, heat pump and P2G.

of communication protocol. And the RTDS oﬀers IEC61850-SV protocol capability which enables
RTDS stream out a set of analog simulation values (voltage, current and power etc) to an external
device with a ﬁxed sample rate. The sample rate of the protocol implementation in RTDS is either
80 samples or 256 samples per cycle. In other words, the communication speed is either 4.8 KHz or
15.36 KHz in the case of a 60Hz system [25]. In the system model, commands to the simulation are
sent out from the external application MATLAB and meantime retrieving the corresponding simu-
lation results from RTDS, forming the closed loop. The RSCAD/RunTime oﬀers a script function
for automating the operation of the RTDS simulator, which means there is no need to manually
control the simulation by changing the sliders, pushing buttons or turn switches in the RunTime.
Using script ﬁle to operate the simulation could not only avoid errors caused by manual interfere
but give an option to run simulations that may lasting hours or days by using loop functions. Since
the MPC have been encoded in MATLAB, it is important to transmit the data between MATLAB
and RSCAD which could be accomplished by using the TCP/IP protocol. As the most widely used
network communication protocol, TCP/IP uses the client-server model of communication in which
a user or machine (a client) is provided a service by another computer (a server) in the network.
Once the communication channel is established, the RSCAD/RunTime becomes a TCP server and
MATLAB becomes a client, a TCP stream commences between RSCAD/Runtime and MATLAB.
Steps that how to operate closed-loop test platform with real timed digital simulator and matlab
optimal MPC based dispatch scheme are brieﬂy introduced below:
1. Open and compile RTDS draft ﬁle (x.dft)
2. Load the Runtime ﬁle which correspond to the compiled ﬁle (x.sib)
3. At RunTime of the compiled ﬁle, go to ‘Script/Open’ menu and run the script ﬁle (Lis-
tenOnPort.scr) by selecting the ‘Run Script’ button in the script toolbar. Now, the RunTime
becomes a TCP server and listens to the port number given by the script command.

5.4.1
4. Run Matlab script (contains MPC algorithm) which connects to RunTime through opened
communication port
5. Wait for Matlab to connect
6. MPC provide the optimal electricity dispatch power set points for all the generators and
loads at each step in RTDS
7. Run simulation in RTDS and retrieve the data of CHP units’ output from RTDS to MATLAB
8. Stop the simulation in RTDS, wait for MPC provide the optimal dispatch solution of next

is reasonable and could be neglected at some points. Therefore, the MPC based dispatch scheme
are operationally feasible in actual power system, while the adjustment is necessary to develop the
dispatch scheme for more complicated operation of electric power system.

7 | Discussion
7.1
Concern to build a more integrated system
In the modern power system, the loss of operation just could not be avoided, so manage to reduce
the lost of energy in power system could be one of the challenges to save energy and establish a
more integrated energy system for Denmark. Some of the measures for reduce the lost are pre-
sented below.
• Based on the current electricity demand and prediction, reasonably Increase transmission
capacity by increasing the cross-sectional area of conductors or the number of split conduc-
tors per phase, or use high temperature resistant wires and optimize utilization of power
generation resources.
• Reasonably carry out reactive power compensation and improve the power factor of the grid.
The system model in RSCAD has hardly used any compensation units. However, in actual
power system, on supply side, reactive power compensation devices should be installed to re-
duce the reactive power delivered by the high-voltage nodes in the power grid, so that reduce
the current of the transmission line thereby reducing the network loss; on the consumption
side, since most of the electrical equipment used by power users has low power factor, large
power users should be required to install power capacitors, and use reactive power compen-
sation automatic input devices to switch reactive power compensation capacitors in a timely
and reasonable manner to reduce power loss.
• Replacing the energy-cost equipment in power system. The resistance and reactance of the
wire are inversely proportional to its cross-sectional area. Therefore, the resistance and re-
actance of the line with a small cross-sectional area are large, and its active and reactive
power losses are considerably large under the same capacity load. Therefore, it is necessary
to strengthen the grid structure and plan the transition of electric devices, replacing the old
lines, small cross-section lines and high-energy-consuming transformers in the distribution
network.
• In order to establish the smart energy system, the smart electricity grids, smart thermal
grids and smart gas grids should be planned, dispatched, and operated in a uniﬁed manner
to avoid energy loss caused by some human factors.

7.2
The MPC based dispatch scheme
The traditional Economic dispatch in electricity market have not give any thought on the electricity
power ﬂow. however, because of the complexity of the power system operation, and much of the
complexity comes from the limitations of the transmission systems and the fact that the balance
state of supply and consumption must be maintained at all times, the economic dispatch can
no longer serve the purpose in this perspective.
The dispatch scheme applied in the MPC is
DC optimal power ﬂow scheme, which have taken the power injections and nominal capacity of
the transmission lines into consideration. In practice, the AC optimal power ﬂow scheme should
be better in interpreter the non-linear power system, however it takes too much computational
work solving the optimal problem. In order to balance the calculation cost and accuracy of the
results, DC optimal power ﬂow scheme has been applied, which exclusively focused on real power
constraints in linearized the power ﬂow equation [27].
In the closed-loop test, the dispatch scheme apply in MPC have satisﬁed the need of providing
optimal power dispatch, lay the foundation of establishing the integrated energy system for the
future.
7.3
Future work
The testing on closed-loop platform with the real timed digital simulator and MATLAB optimal
MPC based dispatch scheme is still in the early stages. There are still more supplement and ad-
justment could be done in the system model.
• Scenarios
More and diﬀerent scenarios should be used to test the integrated energy dispatch scheme,
and some of the extreme conditions should also be taken into consideration.
• Power system model
In the system model, only the 400kv power system has been represented, more sophisticated
transmission and distribution network should be implemented like adding diﬀerent voltage
level nodes in the future to simulate the power system better and more accuracy.
• Dispatch scheme
In order to match the future power system model to provide optimal solutions of the op-
timization problems, the dispatch scheme should be made some adjustment to serve the
purpose.
