Summary  
Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFASs) is a group of more than 4700 synthetically produced 
fluorine substances that have been used in the industry since the early 1950s. PFASs are highly 
persistent in the environment and PFAS exposure has been associated with several health outcomes. 
Studies have shown that the European population is exposed to high levels of PFAS, and analyses 
have revealed that many areas in Denmark are subjected to point source contamination related to high 
levels of PFAS concentration in ground water and local food production. 
The disease burden on a population level can be estimated with burden of disease (BoD) studies, 
which provides quantitative evidence on the public health impact of diseases, risk factors, or injuries.  
BoD studies can be used to compare the magnitude of health loss related to different diseases, risk 
factors or injuries, which are otherwise noncomparable, and to prioritize resources within health care 
and research. The disease burden can be quantified with the health metric disability adjusted life year 
(DALY), which quantifies the years of healthy life lost due to morbidity and mortality. One DALY 
represents the loss of one healthy life year.  
The overall aim of the thesis is to estimate the burden of disease caused by PFAS in Denmark in 
terms of DALY. The BoD is estimated by combining exposure assessment of PFAS exposure in the 
general Danish population with quantification of association between outcome and risk (PFAS) in a 
comparative risk assessment (CRA). The CRA is divided into five steps: definition of the exposure, 
exposure assessment, identification of health outcomes, quantification of the association between 
outcome and risk, and calculation of the attributable BoD. In this approach, the years of healthy life 
lost attributable to a PFAS in Denmark, are estimated. In other words, the years of healthy life that 
could potentially be saved by reducing the PFAS exposure to zero.  
The identified health outcomes are kidney cancer, small for gestational age (SGA) related to 
intellectual disability, and lower respiratory tract infections (LRTI) in children. Testicular cancer and 
hypertensive heart disease (HHD) are included as extra health outcomes in an extended model 
including health outcomes with less certain epidemiological evidence of the association with PFAS 
exposure. A population attributable fraction (PAF) is calculated for each of the health outcomes, and 
it is interpreted as the percentage of the BoD for the specific health outcome that can be attributed to 
PFAS exposure. The BoD for kidney cancer that can be attributed to PFAS exposure is approximately 
4%, for SGA 34% of the BoD can be attributed to PFAS exposure, 47% of the BoD of LRTI in 
==PAGE:3==
Page 3 of 86 
 
children can be attributed to PFAS, 1% of the BoD of testicular cancer can be attributed to PFAS, 
while 9% of the BoD of HHD can be attributed to PFAS exposure.  
The results in this thesis indicate that between 19 and 45 deaths, mainly related to kidney cancer and 
HHD, in Denmark every year, can be attributed to PFAS exposure. And between 777 and 1101 
DALYs are lost every year in Denmark due to PFAS exposure. The health outcomes that mostly 
contribute to the DALY are kidney cancer and HHD. Approximately, 350 DALYs related to kidney 
cancer and 320 DALYs related to HHD are attributable to PFAS exposure. And approximately 30 
cases of kidney cancer, 2130 cases of SGA, 7150 cases of LRTI in children, 2 cases of testicular 
cancer and 30 cases of HHD are attributed to PFAS exposure every year in Denmark.  
An analysis of the exposure pathways related to PFAS exposure indicates that approximately 69% of 
all perfluorooctanoic acid (PFOA) exposure and 78% of all perfluorooctane sulfonate (PFOS) 
exposure are attributable to dietary exposure. It is estimated that between 542 and 765 DALYs are 
lost every year in Denmark due to dietary exposure of PFASs. Compared to other studies that have 
investigated the BoD attributable to foodborne hazards in Denmark, the size of the BoD associated 
with dietary exposure of PFAS is similar to the size of the BoD associated with dietary exposure to 
lead (between 383 and 1261 DALYs per year) and methylmercury (592 DALYs per year). Compared 
to PFAS, campylobacter is the only foodborne hazard with a significantly higher BoD (1709 DALYs 
per year) related to dietary exposure.

Sammendrag  
Per- og polyfluoroalkyl stoffer (PFAS) er en gruppe på mere end 4700 syntetisk producerede 
fluorstoffer, som har været anvendt i industrien siden starten af 1950erne. PFAS ophobes i naturen 
og PFAS-eksponering er associeret med flere helbredseffekter. Studier har vist at den Europæiske 
befolkning er eksponeret for høje niveauer af PFAS, og analyser har afsløret at mange områder i 
Danmark er udsat for punktkildeforurening med høje niveauer af PFAS-koncentrationer i grundvand 
og lokale fødevareproduktioner.   
Sygdomsbyrden kan estimeres på populationsniveau med sygdomsbyrdestudier der fremlægger 
kvantitativ evidens vedrørende indvirkningen af sygdomme, risikofaktorer og fysiske skader på 
folkesundheden. Sygdomsbyrdestudier kan bruges til at sammenligne størrelsen af helbredstab 
relateret til forskellige sygdomme, risikofaktorer og fysiske skader, som ellers er usammenlignelige, 
og at prioritere resurser indenfor sundhedspleje og forskning. Sygdomsbyrden kan kvantificeres med 
”disability adjusted life year” (DALY) som kvantificerer antallet af sunde leveår tabt på grund af 
morbiditet og dødelighed. Én DALY repræsenterer tabet af et sundt leveår.  
Det overordnede mål med denne afhandling er at estimere sygdomsbyrden forårsaget af PFAS i 
Danmark udtrykt i DALYs. Sygdomsbyrden er estimeret ved at kombinere eksponeringsvurdering af 
PFAS-eksponering i den generelle Danske befolkning med kvantificering af sammenhængen mellem 
helbredseffekt og risiko (PFAS) i en komparativ risikovurdering. Risikovurderingen er inddelt i fem 
trin: definition af eksponering, eksponeringsvurdering, identifikation af helbredseffekter, 
kvantificering af sammenhængen mellem helbredseffekt og risiko, og beregning af den 
sygdomsbyrde som kan tilskrives PFAS eksponering. Ved denne tilgang bliver antallet af sunde 
leveår som går tabt hvert år i Danmark på grund af PFAS-eksponering estimeret. Med andre ord, 
antallet af sunde leveår som potentielt kunne blive reddet ved at reducere PFAS-eksponeringen til 
nul.    
De identificerede helbredseffekter er nyrekræft, lav fødselsvægt relateret til intellektuel 
funktionsnedsættelse, samt nedre luftvejsinfektioner hos børn. Testikelkræft og hypertensiv 
hjertesygdom er inkluderet som ekstra helbredseffekter i en udvidet model som inkluderer 
helbredseffekter med mindre sikre epidemiologiske beviser for sammenhængen med PFAS-
eksponering. En ”population attributable fraction” (PAF) er udregnet for hver af helbredseffekterne, 
og fortolkes som procentdelen af sygdomsbyrden for den specifikke helbredseffekt som kan tilskrives 
==PAGE:5==
Page 5 of 86 
 
PFAS-eksponering. Sygdomsbyrden for nyrekræft som kan tilskrives PFAS-eksponering er ca. 4%, 
for lav fødselsvægt kan 34% af sygdomsbyrden tilskrives PFAS-eksponering, 47% af sygdomsbyrden 
for nedre luftvejsinfektioner hos børn kan tilskrives PFAS-eksponering, 1% af sygdomsbyrden for 
testikelkræft kan tilskrives PFAS-eksponering, mens 9% af sygdomsbyrden for hypertensiv 
hjertesygdom kan tilskrives PFAS-eksponering.  
Resultaterne i denne afhandling indikerer at mellem 19 og 45 dødsfald, hovedsageligt relateret til 
nyrekræft og hypertensiv hjertesygdom, hvert år i Danmark, kan tilskrives PFAS-eksponering. 
Mellem 777 og 1101 DALYs går tabt hvert år i Danmark på grund af PFAS-eksponering. Nyrekræft 
og hypertensiv hjertesygdom er de helbredseffekter som bidrager mest til DALY. Ca. 350 DALY for 
nyrekræft og 320 DALY for hypertensiv hjertesygdom kan tilskrives PFAS-eksponering. Ca. 30 
nyrekræft tilfælde, 2130 tilfælde af lav fødselsvægt, 7150 tilfælde af nedre luftvejsinfektioner hos 
børn, 2 tilfælde af testikelkræft, og 30 tilfælde af hypertensiv hjertesygdom kan tilskrives PFAS-
eksponering hvert år i Danmark.  
En analyse af eksponeringsvejen for PFAS-eksponering indikerer at ca. 69% af eksponeringen af 
perfluorooctansyre (PFOA) og 78% af eksponeringen af perfluorooctansulfonsyre (PFOS) kan 
tilskrives fødevareindtag. Det estimeres at mellem 542 og 765 DALYs går tabt hvert år i Denmark på 
grund af PFAS-eksponering via fødevarer. Sammenlignet med andre studier som har undersøgt 
sygdomsbyrden forårsaget af fødevarebårne sundhedsfarer i Danmark, er sygdomsbyrden for PFAS-
eksponering fra fødevarer tilsvarende sygdomsbyrden for bly-eksponering (mellem 383 og 1261 
DALY per år) og methylkviksølv-eksponering (592 DALY per år) fra fødevarer. Sammenlignet med 
PFAS er campylobacter den eneste fødevarebårne sundhedsfare hvortil en signifikant højere 
sygdomsbyrde (1709 DALY per år) kan tilskrives eksponering fra fødevarer.

1 Introduction  
Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFASs) have recently gained a lot of attention in the Danish 
media because analyses of ground water have revealed PFAS concentrations above the quality 
criterion in different regions of Denmark (Miljøstyrelsen 2022). The analyses were initiated after high 
concentrations of a specific PFAS, perfluorooctane sulfonate (PFOS), were found in meat from cattle 
from Korsør in March of 2021. It was revealed that the PFOS contamination originated from a fire 
school nearby (Eurofins 2023; Norup and Madsen 2021). PFOS was used in fire extinguishing foam 
before 2011, but was restricted after being listed in the Stockholm Convention (Eurofins 2023). The 
Stockholm Convention aims to protect human health and the environment from persistent organic 
pollutants (POPs) by decreasing their existence in the environment, and three different PFASs are 
currently listed in the Stockholm Convention (UN environment programme - Stockholm Convention 
2022). Some of the most persistent PFASs have been phased out by the industry, but they are very 
stable and will still be present in the environment many years after the industry has stopped using 
them. The industry has utilized other PFASs instead of the most persistent ones, but some of the non-
persistent PFASs are precursors for persistent PFASs which then ends up in the environment (Buck 
et al. 2011).  
PFAS exposure has been associated with many different health outcomes including reduced antibody 
response to vaccination, increased propensity for infection, low birth weight (LBW), and increased 
cholesterol (EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020). The ubiquitous nature of PFAS as well as the associated 
health outcomes have caused concern, and scientists are requesting stricter guidelines globally as well 
as stricter regulation nationally (Green Science Policy Institute 2022; Philip et al. 2022). However, 
stricter guidelines and regulations could lead to high costs associated with for instance testing and 
monitoring PFAS concentration in water and water treatment measures intended to remove PFAS 
from water supplies (Cordner et al. 2021). Therefore, scientific research for determining the extent of 
the problem related to PFAS exposure and recommend relevant guidelines and regulations is crucial.  
Burden of disease (BoD) studies provide quantitative evidence on the public health impact of 
diseases, risk factors, and injuries. The studies can be used to compare the magnitude of health losses 
and prioritize resources within health care and research (Haagsma et al. 2015; Salomon et al. 2015). 
The BoD can be quantified with the health metric disability adjusted life year (DALY), which 
estimates the years of healthy life lost due to morbidity and mortality (Devleesschauwer et al. 2014). 
==PAGE:12==
Page 12 of 86 
 
DALY is the most frequently used health metric to quantify BoD. In the approach, the years of healthy 
life lost in a population attributable to a disease, injury, or risk factor (such as PFAS) are estimated 
(Plass et al. 2022). It is relevant study the disease burden attributable to PFAS in the Danish 
population since it could provide valuable insights regarding the size of the public health impact of 
PFAS exposure in the Danish population, and the size of the BoD could be compared with other 
hazards.   
2 Aim and objectives  
The overall aim of the project was to estimate the BoD caused PFAS in Denmark in terms of DALY. 
The specific objectives were:  
• To estimate PFAS exposure in the Danish population.  
• To identify health outcomes associated with PFAS and collect relevant dose-response 
relationships.  
• To estimate the proportion of the total number of cases for a health outcome that could be 
attributed to PFAS exposure in the population (PAF). 
• To estimate DALYs attributable to PFAS exposure in Denmark.  
• To estimate BoD attributable to dietary PFAS exposure through source attribution.

3 Background 
3.1 Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances  
PFAS is a group of more than 4700 synthetically produced fluorine substances which have been used 
widely in the industry since the early 1950s. PFAS have been used in oil-resistant coatings for food 
contact paper, oil-, water- and stain-resistant coatings for clothing, fire extinguishing foam, carpets, 
paint, leather, personal care products such as cosmetics, and many other products (EFSA CONTAM 
Panel 2020; Sundhedsstyrelsen 2022). 
PFASs are aliphatic substances with one or more carbon atoms on which at least one of the hydrogen 
atoms, which is present in the nonfluorinated analogues that the PFASs are derived from, is replaced 
by a fluorine atom (Buck et al. 2011). PFASs consists of a hydrophobic and a hydrophilic component 
giving them both water- and oil-repellant properties. The hydrophobic component is the carbon chain 
in which the hydrogen atoms are entirely or partly replaced by fluorine atoms (KEMI - Swedish 
Chemicals Agency 2022). Perfluoroalkyl substances are aliphatic substances with a fully fluorinated 
carbon chain, where all the hydrogen atoms, which is attached to the carbon atoms in the 
nonfluorinated analogues, have been replaced by fluorine atoms (except for hydrogen atoms whose 
substitution would modify the nature of any functional groups present). Polyfluoroalkyl substances 
are aliphatic substances with a partially fluorinated carbon chain, meaning that all hydrogen atoms 
attached to at least one (but not all) of the carbon atoms, have been replaced by fluorine atoms (Buck 
et al. 2011). PFASs can be differentiated by their functional groups, which are the hydrophilic 
component of the substance (KEMI - Swedish Chemicals Agency 2022). Most research has focused 
on perfluoroalkyl carboxylic acids (PFCAs) and perfluoroalkyl sulfonic acids (PFSAs) with between 
four and sixteen carbon atoms such as perfluorooctanoic acid (PFOA) and perfluorooctane sulfonate 
(PFOS). The structural formula of PFOA is illustrated in figure 1, where it is seen that PFOA consists 
of a fully fluorinated C8 chain with a carboxyl group. Figure 2 illustrates the structural formula of 
PFOS which consists of a fully fluorinated C8 chain with a sulfonyl hydroxide group (EFSA 
CONTAM Panel 2020).

PFASs are very stable due to the strength of the Carbon-Fluorine-bond. The chemical and thermal 
stability of PFASs together with its hydrophobic and lipophobic nature, lead to a very high persistency 
in the environment. Long chain PFASs have been shown to bioaccumulate more than their short-
chain analogues, and the production of some long chain PFASs such as PFOA, PFOS, 
perfluorohexane sulfonate (PFHxS), perfluorodecanoic acid (PFDA) and their precursors was phased 
out by the major manufacturer (3M) in 2002. Perfluoroalkyl products with shorter chain length have 
been introduced as alternatives to the previously used PFAS products (Buck et al. 2011). The 
definition of long chain PFASs is PFCAs with seven or more perfluorinated carbons and PFSAs with 
six or more perfluorinated carbons (Buck et al. 2011; Sunderland et al. 2019). PFOA is a long chain 
PFCA, while PFOS is a long chain PFSA (OECD 2023).  
Not all PFASs are persistent in the environment but some of them are precursors for other persistent 
PFASs. Polyfluoroalkyl substances have the potential to be transformed into perfluoroalkyl 
substances, and fluorotelomer alcohol (FTOH) is for instance biotransformed into several metabolites 
including PFOA (Buck et al. 2011). Precursors are often not measured in PFAS analysis, which can 
lead to an underestimation in quantification of human PFAS exposure (Sunderland et al. 2019). 
Another issue in the quantification of PFAS human PFAS exposure is the presence of a mixture of 
both linear and branched isomers. One of the PFAS production methods is electrochemical 
fluorination, which were the primary PFOA and PFOS production method from the 1950s until the 
early 2000s (CDC 2022). Electrochemical fluorination generates approximately 70-80% linear and 
20-30% branched isomers in the case of synthesis of PFOA and PFOS (Buck et al. 2011). 
==PAGE:15==
Page 15 of 86 
 
Fluorotelomerization, which produce almost exclusively linear compounds, became the primary 
production method in the 2000s (CDC 2022).  
3.1.1 Absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion  
The main exposure route of PFAS is through food and water intake and the absorption is thereby oral. 
Absorption through the gastrointestinal tract is above 95% for PFCAs and above 90% for PFSAs 
(EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020). PFAS is widely distributed in the body with highest concentrations 
found in blood, where it is bound to albumin, as well as kidney and liver. PFAS is bound to liver fatty 
acid binding protein (L-FABP) which is a lipid-binding protein expressed mainly in the liver, but also 
in the intestine and kidney. PFAS is mostly accumulated in the blood and liver. Maternal transfer to 
offspring occurs both prenatally (through placental transfer) and postnatally (through breastfeeding) 
(EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020).  
PFSAs and PFCAs are not metabolized, but precursors such as FTOHs and polyfluoroalkyl phosphate 
esters (PAPs) are biotransformed into metabolites such as PFCAs. PFASs are eliminated in urine and 
feces. PFASs with shorter carbon chains are primarily excreted through urine, while PFASs with 
longer carbon chains are primarily eliminated in the bile where they undergo extensive intestinal 
reabsorption, and afterwards are excreted through feces (EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020). 
In humans, the half-lives of short chain PFASs range from a few days for e.g., perfluorobutanoic acid 
(PFBA) to approximately 1 month for e.g., perfluorobutane sulfonic acid (PFBS) and 
perfluorohexanoic acid (PFHxA). Long chain PFASs, such as PFOA, PFOS, PFHxS, PFDA and 
perfluorononanoic acid (PFNA), have half-lives of several years. The established half-lives for long 
chain PFASs vary a lot between studies, but most of the estimations are between 2 and 6 years 
depending on the specific PFAS (EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020). 
3.1.2 Exposure  
PFASs are ubiquitous in the environment due to the extensive use in the industry since the 1950s, 
which has led to release of the chemicals into the environment during production, use of products and 
disposal of products. The high concentrations of PFAS that have been dispersed in the environment 
through many years and the persistency of the substances has led to PFASs being widely spread in 
the environment. Several exposure pathways contribute to the total PFAS exposure, and figure 3 
==PAGE:16==
Page 16 of 86 
 
illustrates an overview of the exposure pathways of PFAS besides from occupational exposure 
(Sunderland et al. 2019).

Food and water intake are the main PFAS exposure pathways (Sunderland et al. 2019). The use of 
aqueous film forming foams used for firefighting is a major source of drinking water contamination. 
The high water-solubility of some PFASs has led to accumulation in groundwater, rivers, and the 
ocean, leading to contamination of drinking water and aquatic food sources (Sunderland et al. 2019). 
Fish and other seafood are the most important sources of dietary PFAS exposure in adults, followed 
by eggs, meat, and fruit. Vegetables and drinking water are also significant sources of PFOA exposure 
(EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020). PFAS contamination of foods occurs through bioaccumulation in 
food chains, transfer from contact materials used in food packaging and food processing. Products 
that are in contact with foods and might contain PFAS are for instance popcorn bags, cookware and 
grease-resistant packaging used for fast food. Cooking has been shown to reduce PFOS 
concentrations in foods (Sunderland et al. 2019).  
PFAS exposure through indoor environment, which consists of air and house dust, where PFAS leaks 
from consumer products into the dust, can also be significant. Dermal exposure is a small contributor 
to the total PFAS exposure and is caused by a wide range of consumer products that humans are in 
touch with such as clothing and personal care products including cosmetics. For some groups of the 
==PAGE:17==
Page 17 of 86 
 
population, for instance firefighters and fluorochemical production workers, occupational exposure 
is significant (EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020; Sunderland et al. 2019).  
According to European biomonitoring data, the most prominent PFASs in Europe are PFOS, PFOA, 
PFHxS, PFNA, PFDA, perfluoroundecanoic acid (PFUnDA) and perfluoroheptane sulfonate 
(PFHpS). The relative contribution of these seven PFASs is approximately 95% compared to the rest 
of the PFASs. PFOS and PFOA are found in the highest serum concentrations. PFOS is responsible 
for 64% of the total exposure in adults and 35% in children, while PFOA is responsible for 16% of 
the total exposure in adults and 36.6% in children (EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020). 
3.1.3 Health outcomes 
Scientific studies have investigated many different potential health outcomes related to PFAS 
exposure, but the studies have focused on a few PFASs, and most of the information on PFAS is 
therefore based on PFOA and PFOS (EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020). The health outcomes related to 
PFAS exposure have been evaluated in the scientific opinion from 2020 published by the European 
Food Safety Authority (EFSA). According to the scientific opinion, the strongest epidemiological 
evidence exists for the health outcomes of reduced antibody response to vaccination, increased 
propensity for infection, low birth weight, increased serum levels of total and LDL cholesterol, and 
increased serum levels of liver enzyme alanine transferase. Table 1 presents the association between 
PFAS exposure and different health outcomes according to the EFSA scientific opinion. Only health 
outcomes where epidemiological evidence exists are included in the table, and the body of 
epidemiological evidence (according to the EFSA scientific opinion) is stated in the table. The critical 
effect related to PFAS is reduced antibody response to vaccination, and the dose-response relationship 
for the association between PFAS exposure and reduced antibody response to vaccination has been 
used to derive the current tolerable weekly intake (TWI) guidance value (EFSA CONTAM Panel 
2020).

3.1.4 Mechanism   
The mechanisms behind PFAS toxicity in humans are currently unknown, but scientific studies have 
suggested different mechanisms. Some of the health outcomes related to PFAS exposure seem to be 
associated with binding of PFAS to proliferator-activated receptor-α (PPAR-α) but it is unclear to 
what extent the mechanism can explain the health outcomes. In rodents, PFASs seem to cause liver 
hyperplasia via activation of PPAR-α receptor with different potencies depending on the specific 
==PAGE:19==
Page 19 of 86 
 
PFAS (EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020). PPAR-α functions as a transcription factor and is important 
for different processes such as lipid metabolism, lipoprotein metabolism, gluconeogenesis, and bile 
acid metabolism. PPAR-α regulates the expression of genes involved in for instance fatty acid beta-
oxidation and energy homeostasis (EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020; Raalte et al. 2004). However, 
humans seem to be less affected by activation of the PPAR-α receptor than rodents as these receptors 
are less frequent in humans (Steenland, Barry, and Savitz 2018). There is some evidence for oxidative 
stress induced by both PFOS and PFOA, which can be a mechanism by which PFAS cause cancer in 
humans. No evidence suggests direct genotoxicity (EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020).  
Oxidative stress can also be a mechanism by which PFAS cause hypertension (Ceriello 2008). 
Otherwise, hypertension might be caused by transcriptional induction of the CYP11B2 gene, due to 
PFOA or PFOS exposure, which leads to aldosterone production. Aldosterone is important for the 
regulation of blood pressure since it ensures the sodium uptake in the kidney, and the dysregulation 
of aldosterone can cause cardiovascular and renal disease (Buekers, Cops, and Rodriguez 2022; Kang, 
Choi, and Park 2016).  
Different mechanisms behind immunotoxicity of PFAS have been suggested. The health outcome of 
increased respiratory tract infections in children might be caused by reduced levels of the antibody 
immunoglobulin G (IgG) induced by PFAS exposure. PFOS exposure has been shown to cause an 
imbalance in immunomodulatory genes (TH1/TH2-type cytokines), which might be the mechanism 
leading to decreased antibody concentrations (Neagu et al. 2021).  
The mechanism behind small for gestational age (SGA) might be related to the peroxisome 
proliferator-activated receptor-γ (PPAR-γ), which is a regulatory mechanism of placental metabolism 
and controls the amount of maternal nutrients passing across placenta to the fetus. The placenta 
requires a constant and abundant supply of energy for growth, and insufficient essential fatty acids 
supply can lead to low birth weight (Buekers et al. 2022; Xu et al. 2007). PFOA are able to bind to 
PPAR-γ (Yamamoto et al. 2015), and since PPAR-γ is important for normal growth of the fetus, the 
fetal growth might be disrupted (Buekers et al. 2022; Xu et al. 2007).  
3.1.5 Tolerable weekly intake  
In 2020, EFSA established a TWI of 4.4 ng/kg bodyweight (bw) per week for the sum of PFOA, 
PFOS, PFNA and PFHxS. The TWI was reduced significantly from the previous TWI of 13 ng/kg 
==PAGE:20==
Page 20 of 86 
 
bw per week for PFOS and 6 ng/kg bw per week for PFOA established by EFSA in 2018 (EFSA 
CONTAM Panel 2020).   
The TWI of 4.4 ng/kg bw per week for PFOA, PFOS, PFNA and PFHxS was based on effects on the 
immune system which were consistently observed for several PFASs and at the lowest serum levels, 
compared to other health effects, in both animals and humans. Two studies, that investigated the 
association between PFASs and reduced antibody response to vaccination, were selected as critical 
studies (Abraham et al. 2020; Grandjean et al. 2012). The dose-response relationship from Abraham 
et al. 2020 led to the lowest tolerable intake, and was therefore used to derive the TWI (EFSA 
CONTAM Panel 2020). Abraham et al. 2020 investigated associations between serum concentration 
of PFASs and antibody concentrations of haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib), diphtheria and tetanus 
in one year old breastfed children. The measured PFASs were PFOA, PFOS, PFNA and PFHxS, and 
higher serum concentrations of PFOA or total PFAS (total concentration of PFOA, PFOS, PFNA and 
PFHxS) were associated with lower antibody concentrations (Abraham et al. 2020).  
The dose-response relationship established in Abraham et al. 2020 was used to derive a benchmark 
dose lower confidence limit (BMDL). Based on physiologically based pharmacokinetic (PBPK) 
modelling, and assuming 12 months of breastfeeding, a BMDL10 of 17.5 ng/ml was derived. A 
BMDL10 of 17.5 ng/ml corresponds to a serum level of 6.9 ng/ml in the mother at 35 years of age 
and an intake of 0.63 ng/kg bw per day for the mother. The daily intake is multiplied with seven to 
get a weekly intake because PFAS bioaccumulates. The TWI is established as the intake that a women 
should stay below to avoid the risk of her one-year-old breastfeeding child developing health effects 
due to PFAS exposure. No uncertainty factors were added since the calculation takes the most 
sensitive population (one year old children) into account (EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020).   
The TWI was based on exposure through foods, where the mean concentration value of PFOA, PFOS, 
PFNA, and PFHxS in food categories were obtained, and the sum were equal to the PFAS 
concentration within that food category. Consumption data were combined with concentration data

3.1.6 Legislation  
Three different PFASs are currently listed in the Stockholm Convention which is a legally binding 
international instrument which aims to protect human health and the environment from persistent 
organic pollutants (POPs) by gradually decreasing their existence in the environment. The chemicals 
in the Stockholm Convention are divided into chemicals that should be eliminated (annex A) and 
restricted (annex B). PFOS has been listed in annex B since 2009, PFOA has been listed in annex A 
since 2019, and PFHxS was listed in annex A in 2022 (UN environment programme - Stockholm 
Convention 2022). 
Restrictions regarding PFOA and PFOS are described in (EU) 2019/1021 from the 20th of June 2019. 
The use of PFOS is restricted and it is allowed in substances or mixtures in concentrations below 10 
mg/kg. It is generally not allowed to produce, market, or use PFOA, but there are exceptions in which 
very small concentrations are allowed. PFOA or any of its salts is allowed to be present in substances, 
mixtures, or articles in concentrations below 0.025mg/kg. PFOA-related compounds or a combination 
of PFOA-related compounds is allowed to be present in substances, mixtures, or articles in 
concentrations below 1 mg/kg. PFOA-related substances are allowed in concentrations below 
20mg/kg in substances used as transported isolated intermediate fulfilling strictly controlled 
conditions. PFOA and its salts is allowed in polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) micropower in 
concentrations below 1 mg/kg. Firefighting foam with PFOA or PFOA-related substances which is 
produced before 4th of July 2020 is allowed to be used until 4th of July 2025 under certain conditions 
==PAGE:22==
Page 22 of 86 
 
(EUR-Lex 2020). A global ban of PFHxS is expected to enter into force at the end of 2023 (ECHA 
2022). In Denmark, the addition of fluorinated substances has been banned in paper and cardboard 
food contact materials since the 1st of July 2020 (Sundhedsstyrelsen 2022). The Danish Environment 
Agency has established quality criteria for the sum of 22 PFAS-compounds in groundwater (0.1 µg/l) 
and in earth (0.4 mg/kg) (Miljøstyrelsen 2021). The tap water quality criterion is 0.002 µg/l for the 
sum of four PFASs (PFOA, PFOS, PFNA and PFHxS), and 0.1 µg/l for the sum of 12 different PFASs 
(Miljøstyrelsen 2022). 
3.2 Burden of Disease  
BoD studies provide quantitative evidence on the public health impact of diseases, risk factors, and 
injuries. They are also useful to measure the potential benefit of proposed interventions to reduce 
diseases or exposure to health risks. BoD studies can be used to compare the magnitude of health 
losses associated with different diseases, injuries, and risk factors, and also prioritize resources within 
health care and research (Haagsma et al. 2015; Salomon et al. 2015).  
The Global burden of diseases, injuries, and risk factors study (GBD) is a standardized and 
comprehensive study, that provides a tool to quantify health loss from different diseases, injuries, and 
risk factors (Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation 2022a). GBD was initially commissioned by 
the World Bank to provide a comprehensive assessment of the BoD in 1990. The first GBD study 
included 10 risk factors, and more than 100 diseases and injuries. Between 1998 and 2004, the World 
Health Organization (WHO) undertook a new assessment of the global BoD from 1999 to 2002. The 
assessment was based on extensive analysis of mortality data for all regions of the world and 
systematic reviews of epidemiological studies and population health surveys. WHO also incorporated 
methodological improvements, and a major research program, the Comparative Risk Assessment 
(CRA) project, was undertaken to quantify global and regional attributable disease burden for 26 risk 
factors. Since 2007, the Institution of Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME) has led the GBD study 
(Mathers 2017). The last published GBD study is from 2019, and has gathered available evidence on 
health outcomes that capture premature death and disability from more than 350 diseases and injuries 
in 195 countries (Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation 2022a).

a lower life expectancy. In this way, the DALY is comparable across different populations 
(Devleesschauwer et al. 2014).  
DALYs can be expressed as an absolute number, giving the total population burden, which is relevant 
in national BoD studies, where the impact on a specific population is relevant. DALYs can also be 
expressed relative to the population (e.g., DALYs per 100,000 population), allowing comparisons of 
BoD across different populations. Lastly, DALYs can be expressed as DALYs per case which allows 
assessments of disease impact at individual patient-level (Devleesschauwer et al. 2014). 
The total population level BoD in DALYs due to a specific disease, injury, or risk factor, is described 
as the DALY envelope, and has been estimated both nationally and globally in the GBD study. For 
instance, the total BoD (in DALYs) caused by kidney cancer in Denmark is described as the national 
DALY envelope for kidney cancer.  
3.3 Comparative Risk Assessment  
CRA is commonly used to estimate the share of disease burden attributable to specific risk factors. 
CRA is the methodology applied in GBD to estimate the burden attributable to risk factors. In the 
CRA approach, the current harmful risk factor exposure level in the population is compared to an 
alternative exposure level where the risk factor is reduced to a theoretical minimum risk exposure 
level (TMREL). The TMREL could for instance be zero smoking or sufficiently high levels of whole 
grain consumption (Plass et al. 2022). 
 
Figure 6: Five steps in the comparative risk assessment 
==PAGE:26==
Page 26 of 86 
 
As illustrated in figure 6, the CRA can be divided into five steps (Plass et al. 2022): 
1. Definition of the exposure: Define what kind of exposure should be covered and how it should 
be quantified. Define the TMREL.  
2. Exposure assessment: Measure or model the exposure of a risk factor towards the population. 
3. Identification of risk-outcome-pairs: Identify which health outcomes that are causally related 
to the risk factor. 
4. Quantification of the association between outcome and risk: Quantify how the risk increases 
with increasing levels of exposure to a risk factor.  
5. Calculation of the attributable BoD: The attributable BoD is calculated from the Population 
Attributable Fraction (PAF) and the total population level BoD. 
In the exposure assessment, the aim is to estimate exposure at population level, and the basis of such 
estimates is often epidemiological studies. Depending on the risk factor, the exposure can be 
measured by for instance testing bodily fluids, using questionnaires, or models to approximate 
personal exposure (Plass et al. 2022).   
When identifying risk-outcome-pairs, it is important to find scientific epidemiological studies that 
quantify the increased risk of a health outcome related to the risk factor. Causality is often established 
based on the strength of evidence which should be ‘convincing or probable’. The Bradford-Hill 
criteria is often used to establish causality (Plass et al. 2022). The Bradford-Hill criteria consists of 
nine criteria for assessing whether epidemiological associations between environmental exposures 
and disease are likely to be causal. The criteria are strength, consistency, specificity, temporality, 
biological gradient, plausibility, coherence, experimental evidence, and analogy. According to 
Bradford-Hill, uncertainty does not give reason to ignore the current knowledge or to postpone the 
action that it appears to demand. Therefore, the criteria should not be used as a checklist before 
concluding that a causal relationship exists but rather a list of issues to consider before making a 
scientific judgement based on the weight of evidence (Bartell and Vieira 2021; Hill 1965; Obsekov, 
Kahn, and Trasande 2022).  
A key step of the CRA is the quantification of the association between outcome and risk which has a 
great impact on the result of the BoD. The association can be extracted from relevant epidemiological 
studies quantifying the relation between exposure of the risk and the health outcome through a dose-
response relationship. The quantification should be expressed as relative risk (RR) or hazard ratio

4 Methods  
In this thesis, I estimated the BoD attributable to PFAS exposure in Denmark with CRA i.e., 
combined exposure assessment and quantification of the association between risk and outcome to 
calculate attributable BoD. I included different health outcomes, related to either PFOA, or PFOA 
and PFOS, based on evaluation of the available evidence. I based the exposure assessment on 
available data from epidemiological studies investigating PFAS serum concentration in Denmark. 
When it was not possible to get PFAS serum concentration data from Denmark, I included data from 
other Scandinavian countries. I calculated the BoD attributable to PFAS exposure from the PAF and 
the DALY envelope. I applied source attribution to estimate the proportion of PFAS exposure from 
food intake as well as disease burden caused by PFAS exposure from food intake. The methodology 
applied is described in detail in the following.  
4.1 Identification of health outcomes 
I identified health outcomes associated with PFAS exposure and included them in the BoD model 
based on existence of sufficient epidemiological evidence establishing association. I selected health 
outcomes based on the scientific opinion on PFAS from the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) 
(EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020), two published PFAS BoD models (Buekers et al. 2022; Obsekov et 
al. 2022) and the evaluation of carcinogenic risks to humans published by IARC (IARC 2017). Table 
8 illustrates the health outcomes associated with PFAS exposure according to EFSA, IARC, and the 
two published PFAS BoD models (Buekers et al. 2022; EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020; IARC 2017; 
Obsekov et al. 2022).

According to EFSA (2020) and IARC (2017), limited evidence indicates association between PFAS 
exposure and cancer (EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020; IARC 2017), and a positive tendency has been 
observed for both kidney- and testicular cancer (IARC 2017). In 2021, a meta-analysis which 
established association between PFOA and kidney- and testicular cancer was published, and I 
included kidney- and testicular cancer based on this meta-analysis (Bartell and Vieira 2021). 
EFSA (2020), Buekers, Cops and Rodriguez (2022), and Obsekov, Kahn and Trasande (2022), agree 
that epidemiological evidence supports the association between PFOA and SGA exists. According to 
EFSA and Buekers, Cops and Rodriguez 2022, epidemiological evidence for the association between 
PFOS and increased propensity for infection in children also exists. Therefore, I included SGA and 
increased propensity for infection (Buekers et al. 2022; EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020; Obsekov et al. 
2022).  
I included hypertension (high blood pressure) as an extra health outcome based on uncertainty related 
to association and high relevance in the Danish population. Limited epidemiological evidence 
establishing association exists (Buekers et al. 2022; Min et al. 2012), and different mechanisms, such 
as oxidative stress and increased aldosterone production, for which PFAS cause hypertension have 
been suggested (Buekers et al. 2022; Ceriello 2008; Kang et al. 2016). Buekers, Cops and Rodriguez 
(2022) included hypertension in their BoD model based on the available evidence. Hypertension is 
very relevant in a BoD model, because it is a risk factor for cardiovascular disease, which is a very 
big health issue in Denmark. Approximately 524,000 people in the Danish population suffers from 
cardiovascular diseases and 56,400 people get a cardiovascular disease every year. 12,000 people 
dies from a cardiovascular disease every year (Hjerteforeningen 2022). Hypertension is a risk factor 
for diseases, and it is not related to morbidity or mortality by itself. Long-term hypertension is 
described as hypertensive heart disease (HHD), which is related to morbidity and mortality, and 
therefore has a BoD from which the attributable disease burden related to PFAS exposure can be 
estimated. Therefore, HHD is included in the model. HHD is described as a long-term condition that 
develops over many years in people with high blood pressure. HHD is a group of heart conditions, 
caused by high blood pressure, such as heart failure and conduction arrhythmia (Institute for Health 
Metrics and Evaluation 2022b).

Quantification of association between outcome and risk  
I based the kidney cancer model on the quantification of association between PFOA exposure and 
risk of developing kidney cancer presented in the meta-analysis by Bartell and Vieira (2021). The 
meta-analysis was based on four different peer-reviewed epidemiological studies. The overall RR of 
the meta-analysis was 1.16 per 10 ng/ml increase in serum PFOA with a 95% CI of 1.03 to 1.30. The 
meta-analysis concluded that there was sufficient evidence to indicate that PFOA most likely was a 
cause of kidney cancer in humans (Bartell and Vieira 2021).

4.2.4 Intellectual disability related to Small for Gestational Age       
Exposure definition and assessment  
SGA was associated with prenatal exposure of PFOA, and therefore PFOA serum concentrations in 
pregnant women is the relevant exposed population group for the health outcome. All PFAS exposure 
above zero was expected to increase the risk of developing SGA, and a TMREL of zero was 
established. The exposure assessment for this health outcome was the same as for LRTI which is seen 
in table 11.  
Quantification of association between outcome and risk  
I based the model on the quantification of association between PFOA exposure and risk of developing 
SGA presented on Govarts et al (2018), which was a pooled analysis of five European cohorts that 
investigated the association between endocrine disruptive chemicals (including PFOS and PFOA) 
and SGA. The chemicals were measured in maternal, cord blood or breast milk samples of mother-
child pairs. SGA infants were those with birth weight below the 10th percentile defined by gestational 
age, country, and sex of the infant. The risk of SGA related to PFOA exposure were described by an 
odds ratio (OR) of 1.64 per interquartile range (IQR = 300ng/l to 1200ng/l), and the 95% CI was 
0.971 to 2.761. The interpretation of the OR was that a maternal PFOA exposure of 1200ng/l 
increased the risk of SGA with 64% compared to a maternal PFOA exposure of 300ng/l (Govarts et 
al. 2018).  
In this report, I calculated the BoD for intellectual disability attributable to PFOA because association 
between SGA and intellectual disability was established in an epidemiological study investigated 
SGA and cognitive performance from infancy to adulthood and established an association between 
SGA and intellectual disability (expressed in IQ points). According to the study, SGA was associated 
with -8 IQ points per SGA case for children (with 95% CI of -13.66 to -0.64 or -13.75 to -1.98 
depending on the model applied). However, the difference was reduced to -3 IQ points during 
adulthood (Eves et al. 2020). When weighted by age, there was a loss of approximately 4.25 IQ points 
per SGA case (Buekers et al. 2022; Eves et al. 2020).

4.3 Source attribution  
After calculating the BoD attributable to PFAS exposure in Denmark from all sources, I used source 
attribution to estimate the proportion of PFAS exposure related to the diet as well as disease burden 
caused by dietary PFAS exposure. Source attribution was based on data from a review of pathways 
of human PFAS exposure (Sunderland et al. 2019). The review was published recently and included 
data from eight different studies where the relative contribution of different PFAS exposure pathways 
were estimated. In the studies, PFAS concentration data were combined with information about 
consumption of foods, ingestion of dust, and inhalation of air. Some of the studies collected evidence 
on concentration and intake from the literature and calculated the relative contribution of different 
sources to the total PFAS exposure (Egeghy and Lorber 2011; Gebbink, Berger, and Cousins 2014; 
Lorber and Egeghy 2011; Trudel et al. 2008). One study compared four scenarios for relative 
contribution of different PFAS exposure pathways by collecting estimates of relative contribution 
from published studies (Vestergren and Cousins 2009). In one of the studies, PFAS concentrations in 
foods, dust, and tap water samples were measured, while consumption data were collected from the 
literature (Shan et al. 2016). In another study, PFAS exposure from dust, air and foods was 
investigated in 41 Norwegian women. Samples of house dust, indoor air, and serum concentration 
were collected, and the women answered a questionnaire covering demographic information, lifestyle 
factors and dietary habits. Consumption data for drinking water, inhalation of air, and ingestion of 
dust were collected from the literature (Haug et al. 2011). Another study measured PFAS 
concentrations in indoor dust samples collected in Korea, and data on PFAS intake from house dust 
was collected from the literature. The result was compared with intake from inhalation, diet, and 
drinking water collected from other scientific studies (Tian et al. 2016). Data from the studies are 
seen in table 18 and 19 for PFOA and PFOS, respectively.

6 Discussion 
6.1 Overall results  
The results of this thesis indicated that between 777 and 1101 DALYs were lost due to PFAS exposure 
every year in Denmark. According to the main model, which included the health outcomes of kidney 
cancer, SGA related to intellectual disability, and LRTI, 777 DALYs were lost. The extended model, 
which included two additional health outcomes (testicular cancer and HHD) indicated that 1101 
DALYs were lost due to PFAS exposure every year in Denmark. The extended model thereby 
indicated a BoD of approximately 325 DALYs more than the main model, which was almost 
exclusively due to the BoD related to HHD. The BoD caused by kidney cancer and HHD were 
highest, while the BoD for testicular cancer were lowest. The BoD for testicular cancer did not 
contribute much to the total BoD, which was mainly because the PAF for testicular cancer was very 
low. Only 0.76% of the BoD related to testicular cancer could be attributed to PFOA exposure, but 
the DALY envelope for testicular cancer in Denmark was also not that high compared to the DALY 
envelope for the other health outcomes. The PAF for kidney cancer was 3.77% and for HHD it was 
8.51%, while the PAF for SGA was 34.46% and for LRTI in children it was 46.98%. The PAFs for 
SGA and LRTI seemed very high, and it does not seem realistic that 34.46% of all SGA cases could 
be attributed to PFOA exposure and that 46.98% of all LRTI in children (at the age of 0 to 4 years 
old) could be attributed to PFOA and PFOS exposure. Then, if PFOA and PFOS exposure were 
decreased to zero in Denmark, almost half as many LRTI cases in children at the ages of 0 to 4 years 
old would occur. The PAF is directly related to the RR collected from epidemiological studies, and 
the RR for LRTI was collected from an epidemiological study. A meta-analysis of the association 
between PFOA and LRTI including more epidemiological studies would provide a RR with more 
certainty about the estimation.    
The highest incidence attributable to PFAS exposure was related to LRTI where 7,155 cases in 
Denmark every year were attributable to PFOA and PFOS exposure. This estimate is also directly 
related to the PAF calculated for LRTI in children. The highest number of deaths attributable to PFAS 
exposure was related to HHD since 26 deaths were attributed to PFOA exposure in Denmark every 
year. The total number of deaths attributable to PFAS exposure in Denmark were 19 according to the 
main model and 45 according to the extended model. The number of deaths in the main model was 
mainly due to kidney cancer (17 deaths due to kidney cancer related to PFOA exposure every year in 
Denmark).  
==PAGE:63==
Page 63 of 86 
 
According to the source attribution, food intake was the main contributor to PFOA and PFOS 
exposure. Approximately 69% of the PFOA exposure was related to dietary intake, while 78% of the 
PFOS exposure was related to dietary intake. The BoD attributable to dietary PFAS exposure was 
between 542 (main model) and 765 (extended model) DALYs every year in Denmark.  
6.2 Comparison with other studies 
In Buekers, Cops and Rodriguez (2022), the BoD attributable to PFAS exposure in the Flemish 
population was 3776 DALYs per year. The main contributor to the overall DALY were the DALY 
for SGA (associated with intellectual disability) which were 3027 DALYs per year in the Flemish 
population (Buekers et al. 2022). The largest difference between the results presented in this thesis 
and the results in Buekers, Cops and Rodriguez (2022) was the PAF for SGA which was much lower 
in Buekers, Cops and Rodriguez (2022). The estimation of the PAF were higher in this thesis because 
the exposure data was higher, and the RR calculations were different. Buekers, Cops and Rodriguez 
(2022) assumed a linear association between RR and PFAS serum concentration, while a log-linear 
association was assumed, according to the method described by Barendregt and Veerman (2010), in 
this thesis. The difference in the results illustrates the importance in the assumption regarding the 
trend of the RR with increasing exposures.  
In Obsekov, Kahn and Trasande (2022) the BoD attributable to PFAS exposure in the US was 
estimated. According to the results, 3.17% of all SGA cases, 0.34% of all kidney cancer cases, and 
0.076% of all testicular cancer cases could be attributed to PFAS exposure in the US. The attributable 
cases per 100,000 population per year were 3.08 for SGA, 0.04 for kidney cancer, and 0.002 for 
testicular cancer. Compared to the results in this thesis, the PAF results and attributable cases in 
Obsekov, Kahn and Trasande (2022) were lower. A difference between calculations for all the health 
outcomes were that Obsekov, Kahn and Trasande (2022) assumed a threshold effect where exposures 
below 0.1ng/ml did not result in increased risk for the three health outcomes, and that the 10% of the 
population with the lowest exposure had an exposure of zero. Whereas in this thesis, the 10% of the 
population with the lowest exposure had an exposure of approximately 0.7ng/ml. The exposure data 
applied in this report were also generally higher, than the exposure data applied in the calculations 
for the three health outcomes in Obsekov, Kahn and Trasande (2022). Lastly, the choice of dose-
response relationship for the association between PFOA and SGA were different than the dose-
response relationship applied in this thesis.

Thomsen et al (2019) established DALY attributable to dietary exposure from four different chemical

Denmark, lead caused between 383 and 1261 DALYs per year, inorganic arsenic caused 5 DALYs

6.3 On the uncertainty in the exposure assessment  
The exposure assessment was based on biomonitoring data of PFOA and PFOS serum concentrations 
in the Scandinavian population. The exposure data that were applied in the models, were collected 
from two different epidemiological studies. Serum concentration data from Bjermo et al (2013) were 
applied in the kidney cancer, HHD, and testicular cancer models, while serum concentration data 
from the Odense child cohort (HBM4EU 2022; Kyhl et al. 2015) were applied in the BoD models for 
LRTI and SGA related to intellectual disability. 
Data from Bjermo et al (2013) were chosen, because the study had the broadest study group with 
adult men and women in all ages (18-80 years old), and because the PFOA serum concentrations were 
in the middle (it was neither the highest nor the lowest serum concentrations) compared to the other 
available data. However, the study group was very small and consisted of only 270 participants, and 
the data was collected in the Swedish population. Data from a larger study group in the Danish 
population would represent the PFOA exposure in Denmark better. PFOA serum concentration data 
from two of the studies (HBM4EU 2022; Mørck et al. 2015; Poothong et al. 2017) were lower than 
the data from Bjermo et al (2013), with P50 values up to 0.66ng/ml lower. PFOA serum concentration 
data from two of the studies (Joensen et al. 2013; Stubleski et al. 2016) were higher than the data 
from Bjermo et al (2013), with P50 values up to 0.77ng/ml higher. Data from Bjermo et al (2013) 
thereby represented a middle point between the available data, and if data from the other studies 
would have been applied in a BoD model, the BoD would be lower or higher depending on the study 
applied.  
For the health outcome of testicular cancer, serum concentration data from Bjermo et al (2013) was 
also applied although the study population consisted of both men and women. These data were chosen 
because the available data consisting of a study population of men (Joensen et al. 2013) were older 
and based on a narrow population of young men. The serum concentrations from men and women 
might be different, and therefore it would have been better to use data from a study population 
consisting only of men. The serum concentration data from Joensen et al (2013) were approximately 
1ng/ml higher for each percentile (Bjermo et al. 2013; Joensen et al. 2013), and BoD calculations 
including these data instead would most likely have resulted in a higher BoD.  
Data from the Odense Birth Cohort (HBM4EU 2022; Kyhl et al. 2015) was applied in the models of 
BoD caused by LRTI and SGA related to intellectual disability, because it was the newest collected 
==PAGE:66==
Page 66 of 86 
 
data (2010-2013) with the most complete dataset available. The study consisted of PFAS serum 
concentration data from 639 pregnant women in Denmark. Data had been collected from two 
additional studies (Bach et al. 2016; Bjerregaard-Olesen et al. 2016; Vorkamp et al. 2014), and the 
data from all three studies were very similar. P50 PFOA serum concentrations from the other two 
studies were 0.15-0.35ng/ml higher than the data applied in the BoD model, and P50 PFOS serum 
concentrations were 0.25-0.35ng/ml higher. However, the two other studies did not publish as many 
percentiles as in the data from the Odense Birth Cohort, and the BoD could be calculated with 
exposure data from P5 to P95, instead of P25 to P75. If the BoD were calculated with the other serum 
concentration data, the BoD would most likely have been slightly higher, but similar because the 
serum concentrations were very close to each other.  
The studies did not explicitly specify if the PFOA serum concentrations consisted of total PFOA, 
branched PFOA or linear PFOA. If the studies did not measure the total PFOA serum concentrations, 
the exposure data would most likely underestimate the actual exposure and the BoD. Some of the 
studies (Mørck et al. 2015; Stubleski et al. 2016) indicate linear and branched PFOS concentrations, 
but these studies report adult PFOS concentrations, and in this report, only PFOS serum 
concentrations for pregnant women were applied. Mørck et al (2015) investigated PFAS serum 
concentrations in women and children, and the median serum concentrations in women were 
2.11ng/ml for branched PFOS, 5.30ng/ml for linear PFOS, and 7.57ng/ml for total PFOS (Mørck et 
al. 2015). Stubleski et al (2016) indicated linear PFOS serum concentrations, and the median (for 
participants at the age of 80 years old) was 7.6ng/ml (Stubleski et al. 2016). The median PFOS serum 
concentration in the data applied for pregnant women in this model was 8.05ng/ml (HBM4EU 2022; 
Kyhl et al. 2015), which is most similar to the values for total PFOS in Mørck et al (2015). However, 
if it was an estimation of only branched or linear PFOS serum concentration, the PFOS exposure 
would be underestimated.  
The year of data collection is related to uncertainties in the CRA method. According to this method, 
the exposure data is collected almost at the same time as the outcome data (DALY envelope), whereas 
in reality, outcomes develop during a long timeframe for chronic effects such as kidney cancer or 
HHD. In this report, the exposure data were measured approximately 10 years earlier than the DALY 
envelope data, and the exposure- and outcome data therefore represents a scenario closer to reality 
than if newer data would have been used. However, this causes uncertainty because guidelines and 
regulation for PFAS have been restricted several times throughout the 2000s, and studies have 
==PAGE:67==
Page 67 of 86 
 
indicated a decreasing trend within this period for PFOA and PFOS (EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020). 
Other PFASs have been used increasingly in the 2000s as replacement of PFOA and PFOS, and the 
time trends for other PFASs do not follow the same decreasing tendencies as for PFOA and PFOS 
(EFSA CONTAM Panel 2020). 
The applied data is an appropriate approximation of exposure in the general adult population and the 
exposure in pregnant women. However, PFAS exposure in specific subpopulations in Denmark might 
be very high, e.g., in the case of the contaminated meat in Korsør where very high PFOS serum levels 
were measured in some people (Norup and Madsen 2021). The exposure assessment performed in 
this thesis is not representative of point source contamination or occupational exposure, which would 
be significantly higher.  
The dietary PFAS exposure were estimated in the source attribution, which were based on a review 
of different source attribution studies investigating exposure pathways in countries such as the US, 
and European countries. The results from the individual source attribution studies were very different 
(ranging from 47% to 85% of the PFOA exposure attributed to the diet and 66% to 96% of the PFOS 
exposure attributed to the diet) (Sunderland et al. 2019). The differences were related to the difference 
in population groups, time, and methods. Estimations of exposure pathways related to PFAS exposure 
in Europe and the US could be used as a proxy for the exposure pathways in Denmark, but it would 
be more precise if exposure pathway studies from Denmark were used. According to the source 
contribution data from the review in tables 18 and 19, the source contributions in the studies were 
very different, and therefore the results from Danish studies might be significantly different as well. 
Studies investigating source contributions in the general Danish population, as well as subpopulations 
(age groups, regions in Denmark, areas of point source contamination) should be investigated to gain 
more detailed information on the PFAS exposure pathways in Denmark.  
6.4 On the uncertainty in the identification of health outcomes  
The health outcomes included in this thesis, where included based on existence of sufficient 
epidemiological evidence associating the health outcome with a specific PFAS. Increased cholesterol, 
increased serum levels of liver enzyme alanine transferase and reduced antibody response to 
vaccination were excluded even though epidemiological evidence indicates association. The health 
outcomes are not diseases for which a disease burden can be calculated, since they are not directly 
related to morbidity or mortality, and they could therefore not be included in a BoD model by 
==PAGE:68==
Page 68 of 86 
 
themselves. Reduced antibody response to vaccination is a risk factor of disease which is most likely 
associated with the risk of developing LRTI, and it is thereby indirectly included in the BoD model. 
Increased levels of liver enzyme alanine transferase indicates liver damage and might be related to 
the health outcome of kidney cancer which is already included in the model. Increased cholesterol is 
a risk factor of cardiovascular disease and could have been included in the model by relating increased 
cholesterol to cardiovascular disease.  
Other health outcomes could also be included in an extended model based on limited epidemiological 
evidence supported by evidence in experimental animals. Many different health outcomes have been 
observed in experimental animals, such as increased kidney and liver weight, decreased levels of 
thyroid hormones, impaired development of mammary glands, and increased fetal and/or neonatal 
mortality. Some of the health outcomes could be included in a BoD model e.g., decreased levels of 
thyroid hormones which is associated with hypothyroidism. Obsekov, Kahn and Trasande (2022) did 
include hypothyroidism in their BoD model, and the health effect could be included based on limited 
epidemiological evidence (providing quantification between outcome and risk), supported by 
evidence observed in experimental animals. Some health outcomes measured in experimental animals 
could not be included in a BoD model, such as increased liver and kidney weight, but these health 
outcomes might indicate which areas to focus on in future research since some health outcomes 
associated with PFAS exposure seem to be related to e.g., the liver and kidney. 
Hypertension (related to HHD) was included in the extended model based on limited epidemiological 
evidence as well as relevance in the population. It would be an advantage to develop a worst-case 
model including all health outcomes that are very important in the population and where limited 
evidence exists. Insufficient epidemiological evidence for the association between PFAS exposure 
and cardiovascular disease, as well as diabetes exist. But these two health outcomes are very relevant 
in the Danish population, and if PFAS were related to them in a BoD model it might increase the BoD 
significantly. A worst-case model could have been developed, where uncertainty about association 
with these health effects could lead to inclusion in the model.  
Published epidemiological evidence investigating association between PFAS and different health 
outcomes, have mainly focused on PFOA and PFOS. But the group of PFASs consists of many other 
PFASs and the association between other PFASs and different health outcomes should be 
investigated. If the associations are quantified, the BoD model could be extended to include other 
==PAGE:69==
Page 69 of 86 
 
PFASs. Besides from PFOA and PFOS, different health outcomes have been associated with e.g., 
PFNA and PFHxS. However, more research is required to establish the associations between other 
PFASs and health outcomes.  
6.5 On the uncertainty in the quantification of associations between risk and outcome  
According to the CRA approach, the quantification of the association between outcome and risk 
(given as a RR) should be based on a systematic review and meta-analysis of recent studies (Plass et 
al. 2022).  The RR has a great impact on the results since the PAF is calculated directly from the it. 
The RR applied in the kidney- and testicular cancer calculations were collected from a very recent 
meta-analysis. The RR for the association between PFOA exposure and kidney cancer was 1.16 per 
10 ng/ml increase in serum PFOA with a 95% CI of 1.03 to 1.30. The RR for the association between 
PFOA exposure and testicular cancer was 1.03 per 10 ng/ml increase in serum PFOA  with a 95% CI 
of 1.02 to 1.04 (Bartell and Vieira 2021). None of the 95% CIs overlapped 1.0, which was used as a 
proxy of statistical significance. However, the RR for testicular cancer was only based on two 
epidemiological studies (Bartell and Vieira 2021), and more epidemiological studies should be 
published, as well as a new meta-analysis including more studies, to increase the certainty in the 
estimation.   
Different RR-values were collected for the association between PFOA exposure and SGA. The 
considered studies were Govarts et al (2018), Steenland, Barry and Savitz (2018), and Meng et al 
(2018). The first study was a pooled analysis of five European cohorts which established an 
association between PFOA exposure and SGA (Govarts et al. 2018). The second study was a meta-
analysis which found an association between PFOA exposure and low birth weight (Steenland et al. 
2018). The last study was a Danish prospective cohort study which established an association between 
PFOS exposure and SGA. The first exposure quartile of 22.9 ng/ml was used as a reference in the 
study (Meng et al. 2018) and this exposure was much higher than the PFOS exposure data collected 
in this thesis (P95:15.48 ng/ml), therefore the association collected from this study was not 
appropriate to apply in the BoD calculations. The RR-values collected from both Govarts et al (2018) 
and Steenland, Barry and Savitz (2018) were appropriate to use in the BoD calculations for SGA 
related to intellectual disability. If more time had been available, the calculations could have been 
done with RR-values collected from both studies to investigate the impact on the results.  
==PAGE:70==
Page 70 of 86 
 
In the hypertension (related to HHD) calculations, the OR for the association between PFOA and 
hypertension established in an epidemiological study were applied (Min et al. 2012). It would be 
preferrable to apply an OR based on a meta-analysis including several studies. The association 
between PFOA and hypertension is generally uncertain due to the lack of epidemiological studies 
establishing association. More epidemiological studies should be performed, as well as a meta-
analysis including several studies, to gain more certainty about the association between PFOA 
exposure and the health effect, and to achieve more certainty related to the size of the OR.  
The BoD related to LRTI in children associated with PFOA exposure were calculated with two 
different RR-values. In the final model, the BoD were calculated based on the RR in Dalsager et al 
(2021), while the alternative BoD model included a RR from Impinen et al (2019). Dalsager et al 
(2021) investigated the association between maternal PFAS serum concentrations during pregnancy 
and the child’s rate of hospitalization due to LRTI between birth and 4 years of age in Denmark. 
Impinen et al (2019) investigated maternal levels of PFAS during pregnancy and pneumonia cases in 
children between birth and 3 years of age. In both studies, RR-values were established for the 
association with both PFOA and PFOS exposure. The RR from Dalsager et al (2021) was applied in 
the final model because it was a Danish study and because it investigated LRTI, whereas Impinen et 
al (2019) investigated pneumonia. The results from Dalsager et al (2021) were more comparable with 
the DALY envelope, which only existed for LRTI in children at the age of 0 to 4 years old. 
The alternative LRTI model was useful in terms of comparing the results of BoD models based on 
different RR-values. The PAF for the alternative LRTI was 82%, while the PAF in the included LRTI 
model was 47%. Both PAF values were very high, and the difference between them were also very 
significant, which illustrated the importance of the RR in PAF calculations. A PAF of 82% seemed 
like an extremely high estimation of LRTI disease burden attributable to PFOA and PFOS exposure. 
The DALYs attributable to PFOA and PFOS exposure were 155.49 and 272.93 for the included and 
the alternative model respectively.  
6.6 Comparison between the exposure assessment and the tolerable weekly intake  
The TWI of 4.4 ng/kg bw per week established by EFSA in 2020, corresponds to a serum level of 
6.9ng/ml (for PFOA, PFOS, PFNA and PFHxS) in a 35 year old woman who breastfeeds (EFSA 
CONTAM Panel 2020). The exposure assessment in this thesis indicates that the total P50 serum 
concentrations in adults (men and women at the ages of 18 to 80 years old) and pregnant women are 
==PAGE:71==
Page 71 of 86 
 
approximately 10ng/ml for PFOA and PFOS, and that 69-78% of this exposure can be attributed to 
dietary PFAS exposure. If the serum concentrations related to dietary PFOA and PFOS exposure in 
50% of the general Danish population is approximately 7.5ng/ml or higher, it suggests that a 
significant part of the general population might be exceeding the serum levels corresponding to the 
TWI without taking PFNA and PFHxS serum concentrations into account. In the EFSA scientific 
opinion, the dietary exposure assessment indicated that most of the European population were 
exceeding the TWI based on dietary exposure for the four PFASs. In this thesis, 22-31% of the PFOA 
and PFOS exposure were attributed to other sources than the diet, indicating a very concerning PFAS 
exposure level in the European population. More studies investigating the exposure of the Danish 
population should be performed, to investigate if the exposure of PFASs in Denmark are decreasing 
as the guidelines and regulations regarding PFAS are getting stricter.

7 Conclusions  
The overall aim of this thesis was to estimate the disease burden caused by PFAS in Denmark in 
terms of DALY. The BoD was estimated by applying the method of CRA, where exposure assessment 
and quantification of association between outcome and risk were combined to establish the BoD 
attributable to PFAS exposure in Denmark. The relevant health outcomes related to PFAS exposure 
in Denmark were identified based on sufficient epidemiological evidence, and included kidney 
cancer, SGA related to intellectual disability and LRTI in children. Testicular cancer and HHD were 
included as extra health outcomes in an extended model including health outcomes with less certain 
epidemiological evidence of the association. The relevant dose-response relationships were collected, 
and the PAFs were calculated. The PAFs were approximately 4% for kidney cancer, 35% for SGA, 
47% for LRTI, 1% for testicular cancer and 9% for HHD. The results established that between 777 
and 1101 DALYs were lost every year in Denmark due to PFAS exposure. The health outcomes that 
mostly contributed to the DALY were kidney cancer (353 DALYs) and HHD (320 DALYs).  
The estimated BoD is related to a lot of uncertainties, and more studies collecting data on exposure 
in Denmark and quantifying the association between health outcomes and PFAS exposure should be 
performed. The PFAS serum concentrations in the current general Danish population as well as 
subpopulations subjected to very high exposure should be investigated to achieve a better exposure 
assessment. The association between different PFASs and health outcomes should be investigated to 
establish how many of the PFASs that are associated with health outcomes, and more health outcomes 
should be investigated to establish association with PFAS exposure. If more time had been available, 
the calculations could have been performed with quantifications of associations between outcome 
and risk established in different studies. In this thesis, it was only done with LRTI, and the results 
illustrated that the quantifications have a great impact on the result. It would have been informative 
to test the differences in results for more of the health outcomes where several quantifications were 
available. The BoD model could have included more health effects, such as the health effect of 
increased cholesterol related to cardiovascular disease, as well as associations between different 
PFASs and the included health outcomes.  
The extended model could have been developed further to include more health effects and represent 
a “worst-case” scenario. The model could have included health outcomes based on limited 
epidemiological evidence together with high relevance in the population (as in the case of HHD), and 
==PAGE:73==
Page 73 of 86 
 
health outcomes where epidemiological evidence was more uncertain but was supported by evidence 
from experimental animals. The “worst-case” scenario could for instance have included diabetes and 
decreased levels of thyroid hormones related to hypothyroidism.  
Source attribution was performed to establish BoD attributable to dietary PFAS exposure. 
Approximately, 69% of all perfluorooctanoic acid (PFOA) exposure and 78% of all perfluorooctane 
sulfonate (PFOS) exposure were attributable to dietary exposure. It was estimated that between 542 
and 765 DALYs were lost every year in Denmark due to dietary exposure of PFASs. Compared to 
other studies that have investigated the BoD attributable to foodborne hazards in Denmark, the size 
of the BoD associated with dietary exposure of PFAS was similar to the size of the BoD associated 
with dietary exposure to lead and methylmercury. Campylobacter was the only risk factor with a 
significantly higher BoD related to dietary exposure than PFAS.
