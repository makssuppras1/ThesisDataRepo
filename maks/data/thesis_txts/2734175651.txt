Abstract
The Effective Length Method (ELM) is currently provided as the stateВӯofВӯtheВӯart approach
in EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007) for the stability design of beamВӯcolumns subjected to combined
compression and bending. The literature has indicated that the ELM is inconsistent and
potentially yields unconservative results. To ensure accurate design of slender timber
beamВӯcolumns under combined loading, the Nonlinear Method (NLM) was introduced in
prEN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (draft version) (2023).
A comprehensive parameter study comparing both methods was conducted in this theВӯ
sis, aiming to investigate the scenarios in which the ELM tends to produce conservative
or unconservative results. The parameter study revealed errors in the ELM when applied
to slender elements. The load bearing capacity of beamВӯcolumns was consistently underВӯ
estimated in longВӯterm, mediumВӯterm, and shortВӯterm scenarios. Conversely, it tended to
overestimate the load bearing capacity in instantaneous and fire cases.
The comparison between ELM and NLM led to the conclusion that buckling behavior is
not accurately considered for slender sections. Additionally, cases have been identified
where the ELM is conservative or unconservative. As a result, this research provides
clear recommendations for the limited use of the ELM.
Furthermore, this research assessed the applicability of the NLM for boundary conditions
other then simply supported by validating the nonlinear deflections of beamВӯcolumns, obВӯ
tained through the NLM, with Finite Element Analysis. The results obtained from the
parametric Finite Element Analysis showed that the nonlinear deflections predicted by
the NLM for various boundary conditions are consistently conservative.
Based on the conservative results obtained from the comparison between the NLM and
the Finite Element Analysis, a modified version of the NLM to design timber beamВӯcolumns
for pinВӯfix and fixВӯfix boundary conditions is presented in this research. The introduced
modification results in a more efficient design for both pinВӯfix and fixВӯfix cases, allowing
for the use of more slender sections.

Introduction
Since the construction of larger timber buildings gained popularity over the last years, the
development of existing and new design methods is still evolving. Increasing size of timber
buildings requires a sensible and coherent method for the design of timber beamВӯcolumns.
Additionally, it is important to provide slender sections while still ensuring structural safety.
The behavior of these members under loads can vary based on their geometry and mateВӯ
rial properties. For instance, nonВӯslender timber elements predominantly fail upon reachВӯ
ing their section capacity. In contrast, slender elements often present stability challenges,
leading to buckling.
EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007) provides two different approaches to design beamВӯcolumns subjected
to stability:
вҖў Effective length method
вҖў 2nd order analysis
The primary recommendation in EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007) is to employ the Effective Length
Method which is an empirical method, based on the Euler buckling length. The option to
use a 2nd order analysis is also presented, though not elaborated upon in detail. However,
prEN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (draft version) (2023) gives detailed guidance on how to perform a 2nd
order analysis based on an analytical approach.
In general for timber beamВӯcolumns there are two stability failure modes:
вҖў Flexural buckling
вҖў Lateral torsional buckling (LTB)

In EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007) these failure modes are addressed with the Effective Length Method

This research investigates the differences between the stateВӯofВӯtheВӯart Effective Length
Method (ELM) stated in EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007) and the recently developed Nonlinear Method
(NLM) proposed in prEN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (draft version) (2023) in order to determine the conВӯ
tinued applicability of the existing verification method. Furthermore, a parameter study
is being conducted to explore the applicability of the recently developed NLM for various
boundary conditions.
The scope of this study revolves around the 2nd order analysis proposed for prEN 1995Вӯ
1Вӯ1 (draft version) (2023) and the ELM which is the current stateВӯofВӯtheВӯart design method
stated in EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007). The primary material in this research is glued laminated
timber due to its predominant use in beamВӯcolumn constructions. A comprehensive comВӯ
parison of the performance of both methods will be conducted across various load duraВӯ
tions, crossВӯsectional geometries and initial imperfections, covering a wide spectrum of
slenderness levels.
HГ¶rsting, O. (2008), Abeysekera (2019) and TГ¶pler and Kuhlmann (2022a) have shown
that assumptions for initial imperfections in the ELM are below the recommended limit of
l/500 for glulam, explained in section 10 of EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007). Furthermore, beams and
columns, when subjected to both bending and axial loads are expected to yield identical
results as they are mechanically equivalent. However, this is not the case in practice.
Abeysekera (2019) indicates that the duration of the load significantly affects whether
the ELM is conservative or unconservative. There is a gap in the literature regarding a
detailed parameter study that identifies scenarios where discrepancies within the methods
become apparent.
The main questions investigated in this research are as follows:
вҖў In which scenarios does the ELM lead to conservative or unconservative design?
вҖў Can the NLM, originally derived for simply supported beamВӯcolumns, be extended
and effectively applied as a general solution for beamВӯcolumns with boundary conВӯ
ditions other then simply supported?
This research seeks to compare the ELM with the NLM by conducting a parameter study.
The aim is to discern scenarios where the ELM might either be overly conservative or unВӯ
conservative in order to enhance the efficiency of design. A variety of examples, demonВӯ
strating the inaccuracies of ELM, is required by the industry in order to assess its suitability
for the design of timber beamВӯcolumns.
The subsequent part of this research aims to investigate the feasibility of applying the
NLM for other boundary conditions since the methodвҖҷs derivation is based on a simply
supported beam. Moreover, the option to implement a correction factor for the NLM is
investigated which might allow for more efficient design. Finite Element Analysis is carried
out to determine the elastic nonlinear deflection of glulam timber beamВӯcolumns under
various support conditions. The NLM is the recommended method in the draft of the
forthcoming Eurocode prEN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (draft version) (2023). However, no information on
the applicability under other boundary conditions is provided.

Material Nonlinearity
Buchanan (1984) investigated the interaction between combined bending and axial loadВӯ

utilization term. This is a conservative approach, used to factor in the observed increase
in bending strength when an axial load is applied.
Figure 2.6 illustrates the combined loading of axial load and moment for varying perВӯ
centiles of short timber columns. The 95th percentile curve appears nearly linear, indiВӯ
cating nearly perfect columns that fail due to compression. Conversely, the weaker 5th
percentile samples show a tension failure which can be attributed to defects in the tenВӯ
sion zone, as noted by Buchanan (1984). At the peak moment, the elements reach both
tension and compression limits. This concept parallels that of reinforced concrete.

2.9
Theoretical Synthesis
Hassan (2019) focused on the analysis of flexural buckling for simply supported braced
beamВӯcolumns subjected to axial load and uniaxial bending. A parameter study which
investigates the influence of slenderness was conducted. Theiler (2014) Theiler employed
a strainВӯbased calculation model for simply supported columns subjected to axial loads.
HГ¶rsting (2008) contributed by investigating analytical and finite element methods for the
design of timber columns subjected axial load, major axis bending and a combination
of both. Based on TimoshenkoвҖҷs research, Abeysekera (2019) developed an analytical
approach for designing slender timber columns subjected to combined axial load and
biaxial bending.
The literature review indicated that the indirectly assumed imperfections for the existing
verification methods in EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007) are not consistent with the ones recommended
for 2nd order analysis. Moreover, only bow imperfections are provided and twist imperfecВӯ
tions are not considered. Current measurement methods indicate that initial imperfection
assumption can be decreased. prEN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (draft version) (2023) reflects the recent
research and implements their recommendations regarding imperfections.
The kcВӯ and km methods are empirically based methods which add difficulties to underВӯ
standing their mechanical background. For example, imperfections are not an input which
makes it difficult to use recently updated limits for imperfections which might lead to more
efficient design. EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007) provides different formulas for beams and columns
even though mechanically they are the same. This indicates further inconsistencies within
the ELM.
Existing research indicates deficiencies of the ELM, however few parameters are used to
compare the ELM to the 2nd order methods. For example, some researchers investigate
multiple slenderness values but neglect the duration of load cases or consider different
durations of load but a limited amount of slenderness ratios. An in depth comparison of
existing verification methods to the nonlinear method taking into account load scenarios
is nonexistent. Especially lateral torsional buckling cases have not been researched in
depth. Therefore, no general statement about the scenarios in which the ELM is conserВӯ
vative or unconservative can be conducted.
While there has been published a strainВӯbased model for buckling by Theiler (2014), as
well as analytical models for flexural buckling and lateral torsional buckling by HГ¶rsting
(2008) and Abeysekera (2019), the latter which has been developed on the basis of TimВӯ
oshenko and Gere (1961c) is investigated more in depth and used as a baseline for the
parameter study. AbeysekeraвҖҷs method already provides codified checks including biВӯ
axial cases. Moreover, it is included in the prEN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (draft version) (2023) as the
main method to check buckling of beamВӯcolumns whereas the ELM is transformed into a
simplified method.
Buchanan (1984), Steiger and Fontana (2005) have presented conflicting viewpoints reВӯ
garding the nonlinear interaction of timber elements subjected to axial Вӯand bending loads.
The tension failure due to bending is suppressed by moderate axial compression forces
which is a well known phenomenon for reinforced concrete columns. Specimens with
lower strength tend to fail due to tension, whereas the more robust specimens fail beВӯ
cause of compression, indicating a statistical phenomenon. Therefore, plastification of
the compression zone can be neglected for the design of timber columns.
Creep significantly impacts the loadВӯbearing capacity of timber columns as it magnifies
longВӯterm deflection, consequently resulting in increased 2nd order moments. EN 1995Вӯ
Parametric Study of a Mechanical Based Method for Timber Member Buckling
17
==PAGE:26==
1Вӯ1 (2007) considers the duration of load using the kmod factor and the longВӯterm deforВӯ
mation using kdef. However, the latter is only implemented for serviceability limit states
of elements subjected to bending and therefore not applicable to use for 2nd order analВӯ
ysis of elements subjected to compression. As the ELM is empirically determined it is
not certain to which degree creep is considered. Creep is not further investigated in this
research, nevertheless, the literature review underscores the importance of having the
flexibility to incorporate creep considerations in the design.
This research compares existing verification methods of EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007) to the meВӯ
chanical based method of Abeysekera (2019). A parameter study is conducted to compreВӯ
hensively investigate and identify the scenarios in which the existing verification methods
may lead to conservative or unconservative column designs, resulting in overestimation
or underestimation of the actual structural requirements. By identifying such situations,
a comprehensive understanding of the limitations and potential risks associated with the
existing verification methods according to EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007) will be provided, helping
readers avoid certain scenarios when using these methods.
Mechanical based methods like HГ¶rsting (2008) and Abeysekera (2019) are derived for a
simply supported beam. Closed form solutions for analytical methods only exist for simply
supported or cantilevered support conditions because of mathematical complexity. The
effect of different boundary conditions on the load bearing capacity of timber columns is
not discussed in the literature.
The ELM employed in EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007) as well as analytical methods previously deВӯ
scribed above are all based on simply supported boundary conditions. This research
project aims to investigate the applicability of the closed form NLM to support conditions
other then simply supported. The focus lies on understanding how different support conВӯ
figurations might affect the critical buckling load of structural members. By examining
various support conditions and evaluating their effects on the critical buckling load, this
study seeks to determine whether the closedВӯform NLM can be considered as a general
solution applicable to various support conditions.

Evaluation of the Parameter Study I
The parametric study was selected due to its ability to explore a vast array of scenarios
efficiently, which would be impractical with experimental testing. Moreover, implementing
the duration of loading and experimental tests for fire would take a lot of effort.

Discussion of the Comparison between
ELM and NLM
In the previous parameter study, significant inconsistencies between the stateВӯofВӯtheВӯart
Effective Length Method (ELM) and the recently developed Nonlinear Method (NLM) proВӯ
posed in prEN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (draft version) (2023) were identified. This chapter aims to deВӯ
termine in which scenarios the ELM is applicable and in which scenarios it is not recomВӯ
mended based on results obtained in the parameter study.
The NLM is assumed to be accurate and serves as a baseline for evaluating the ELM.
This assumption will be further investigated in chapter 6.1.
5.1
Flexural Buckling
Figure 4.2 illustrates that all curves exhibit relatively flat behavior for low N/Ncr values,
increasing or decreasing as N/Ncr values rise. This phenomenon appears due to the
effect of an increased axial force acting on the imperfect shape, leading to the developВӯ
ment of nonlinear moments. The high degree of nonlinearity is resembled by high N/Ncr
values. NonВӯslender elements are less susceptible to buckling and therefore show modВӯ
erate differences in utilization ratios across various design cases. This analysis supports
the theory that the ELM may not accurately consider buckling behavior.
While the ELM tends to overestimate the reduction in load capacity due to buckling in longВӯ
term to shortВӯterm cases, it underestimates this effect in instantaneous and fire scenarios.
This observation aligns with findings reported by Abeysekera (2019).
Results indicate that variations in widths and heights have negligible influence on the
utilization ratios. Across different widths and heights, identical utilization ratios were obВӯ
served for corresponding N/Ncr values, resulting in overlapping lines in plots 4.3 and 4.4.
Therefore, utilization ratios are only dependent on the slenderness but not on the cross
section geometry. This is valid for all duration of loads as shown in Appendix A.
The disparities observed for different widths, illustrated in Table 4.1 compared to constant
values for height in Table 4.2 can be explained by the method of plotting the utilization
ratio of N/Ncr,z. The latter is notably more influenced by varying widths.
Previous research carried out by Hassan (2019) investigates the influence of beam diВӯ
mensions on the utilization of the ELM compared to the 2nd order method.

sections (about 10%), possibly due to the effect of secondВӯorder moment, which is not fully
considered by the effective length method.вҖқ

results ranging from 3% to 8% for nonВӯslender elements.

term amplifies the reduction in axial utilization, resulting in exponential utilization curves.
However, simultaneously acting axis force and moment is likely to reduce this effect as
the moment utilization term is not squared. This characteristic leads to more linear LoadВӯ

The fact that multiplying a value less than one by itself results in a smaller number underВӯ

below one. If moment utilization is part of the interaction formula, the axial utilization must
always be below one. Consequently, the reduction of the axial utilization is amplified by
the squared term. This explains why the combination of axial load and weak axis moment
generally leads to a decrease in utilization ratios compared to axial load only.

to determine the suitability of the modified NLM for predicting nonlinear deflection and

8.1
Verification of the Nonlinear Method
Generally, a low deviation is observed between the NLM and the FEA which aligns with
the expectations. The NLM is an analytically derived method with an exact solution for a
simply supported beamВӯcolumn.
Research carried out by HГ¶rsting (2008) demonstrated a good fit between his analytical
method and FEA. Moreover, a deviation from the Effective Length Method is evident. Even
though the results agree with the results in Chapter 7.1, they cannot be directly compared
as different ranges for slenderness and material parameters were investigated.
As previously shown in Figure 7.3, the deviation between the kmВӯmethod and the NLM is
smaller than for the other failure modes. This is mainly due to the crossВӯsection geometry
and length of the beamВӯcolumn which does not show large nonlinear effects. NevertheВӯ
less, a clear indication is given which serves to verify the NLM for lateral torsional buckling.
The difference in utilization between the Effective Length Method and the NLM aligns
with the parameter study, in terms of small deviations for major axis moment only and
increased deviations if axial load is applied additionally.

8.2.1
BeamВӯcolumns subjected to compression
Overall beamВӯcolumns subjected solely to axial load show a small deviation between FEA
and NLM. These results are expected as equation 2.5 is valid for pinВӯfix and fixВӯfix beamВӯ
columns as well according to Timoshenko and Gere (1961b).
The deviations rise with higher N/Ncr ratios as the effects are amplified by increased
loads. The discrepancies increase for shorter length beamВӯcolumns which indicate that
the around 4% higher deflection in the Finite Element Model arises due to shear deflection.
As illustrated in Figure 7.6 , the introduction of weak axis moment increases the disparity
between FEA and NLM when applied in combination with axial load. The interaction
between axial load and weak axis moment results in a deflection pattern that deviates
from a pure sinusoidal shape because the weak axis moment does not necessarily cause
52
Parametric Study of a Mechanical Based Method for Timber Member Buckling
==PAGE:61==
a sinusoidal shaped nonlinear deflection. This deviation is influenced by the shapes of the
moments. A moment caused by a uniformly distributed load tends to produce a deflection
closer to sinusoidal compared to a constant moment. To account for this, the weak axis
moment is scaled by the Dischinger factor. This scaling factor is 0.032 for a moment which
results from a uniformly distributed load and 0.273 for a constant moment.
The scaling of the moment by the Dischinger factor, however takes place with the asВӯ
sumption of deflecting into a halfВӯsine wave shape. The NLM therefore does not match
the buckled shape of a beamВӯcolumn with the pinВӯfix configuration.
That is why the elastic nonlinear displacements by the NLM are larger and therefore lead
to conservative results if the weak axis moment Вӯ axial load ratio increases.
8.2.2

Figure 7.7 illustrates that the deflection disparity for pinВӯpin configurations is of a simiВӯ
lar magnitude as the pinВӯfix and fixВӯfix configurations. This observation is unexpected,
considering that the NLM provides an exact solution for the simply supported case.
In the case of simply supported beamВӯcolumns subjected to major axis moment, there is
a median difference of 17% and a mean difference of 18%. Figure 7.3 and 7.4 indicate
that this deviation from the baseline has small practical implications as the utilization is
less influenced by the deviation of weak axis deflections. However, this error persists in
combined load cases and affects the correction factor introduced in Chapter 7.3. The
GSA Software team is currently investigating whether this error is caused by the finite
element software.
To confirm the sinusoidal behavior of the buckled mode shape in the FEA model for simply
supported beamВӯcolumns subjected to a major axis moment, the weak axis deflection is
plotted alongside the nonlinear deflection for a half sine wave along the element length in
Figure 8.1.
As the buckled mode shape exhibits sinusoidal behavior, the assumptions align with the
NLM. Therefore there should be minimal disparities in weak axis deflection. The incorpoВӯ

Conclusion and Future Work
9.1
Conclusion and Final Remarks
This research aimed to evaluate the stateВӯofВӯtheВӯart Effective Length Method (ELM), emВӯ
ployed in EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007), for the design of timber beamВӯcolumns subjected to buckВӯ
ling.
Furthermore, this study also aimed to explore the applicability of the Nonlinear
Method (NLM), proposed in prEN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (draft version) (2023), for different boundary
conditions.
The central questions for this research were as follows:
вҖў In which scenarios does the ELM lead to conservative or unconservative design?
вҖў Can the NLM, originally derived for simply supported beamВӯcolumns, be extended
and effectively applied as a general solution for beamВӯcolumns with boundary conВӯ
ditions other then simply supported?
A parameter study was conducted to explore a broad spectrum of design scenarios in
the field of application for the ELM. The NLM served as a benchmark to compare the
utilization ratio of these methods across a range of N/Ncr values. To ensure the validity
of the benchmark, the NLM was subsequently verified through Finite Element Analysis
(FEA).
Results from the parameter study indicate a limitation of the ELM in accurately considering
buckling behaviour, particularly in slender elements. The ELM is generally conservative
in longВӯterm, mediumВӯterm, and shortВӯterm situations. However, it tends to be unconserВӯ
vative in instantaneous and fire scenarios. Flexural buckling is particularly sensitive to
imperfections. The analysis indicates that exceeding the imperfection limit of l/1100, as
assumed in the kcВӯmethod according to TГ¶pler and Kuhlmann (2022b), can result in signifВӯ
icant overutilization. This is especially true in instantaneous and fire scenarios. Combined
axial load and biВӯaxial bending led to significant overutilization of the ELM, as the current
formulation of the ELM does not account for this specific load condition.
To assess the applicability of the NLM under different boundary conditions, parametric
FEA was carried out across various parameters. The nonlinear deflection was then comВӯ
pared for several load settings between the two methods.
The deviation in utilization between the NLM and the FEA was minimal for simply supВӯ
ported beamВӯcolumns. This aligns with the expectation as the NLM provides an explicit
solution to that problem. Based on the comparison of weak axis deflection between the
Finite Element Analysis and the NLM it can be concluded that the latter is conservative
across all boundary conditions and can be applied as a general solution by adjusting the
critical buckling load to the corresponding boundary condition.
The largest deviation between NLM and FEA was observed in cases where combined
axial load and biВӯaxial moments were investigated. Buckled shapes of the FEA and the
NLM do not match for boundary conditions other than simply supported. Moments interact
nonlinear on these different buckled shapes which causes different deflection profiles and
cannot be approximated with the first term of a Fourier series expansion for a square wave
anymore. The deviation between FEA and NLM can be approximated by a polynomial
function. The predicted correction factor shows robust results for different geometries.

The currently recommended ELM by EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007) and the recently introduced

9.2
Future Work
The parameter study establishes a solid foundation for evaluating the existing stateВӯofВӯ
theВӯart method outlined in EN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (2007). However, an extension of the scope to
cover the modified ELM proposed in prEN 1995Вӯ1Вӯ1 (draft version) (2023) would provide
valuable insights regarding the applicability and effectiveness of the modified ELM.
As connections in timber structures are rarely entirely fixed, investigating the applicability
of the NLM to spring supports would be of practical use. Design recommendations based
on spring stiffness input would allow for a more flexible Вӯand accurate design.
The work by Abeysekera (2019) introduced an approach to account for creep in beamВӯ
column design, which is important given the susceptibility of timber beamВӯcolumns to
creep. To investigate the Nonlinear MethodвҖҷs applicability to various boundary conditions
including the effects of creep would be highly relevant.
The option of applying a correction factor to reduce the error of the NLM for different
boundary conditions was investigated for beamВӯcolumns subjected to major axis bending.
However, an extension of the scope to beamВӯcolumns subjected to combined axial load
and biВӯaxial bending would be beneficial, given that these scenarios exhibits the most
significant deviations.
