Abstract
Allergies pose a significant healthcare challenge worldwide, necessitating development
of improved treatments. Although allergenยญspecific immunotherapy vaccines generally
are effective, several patients discontinue treatment due to side effects or lack of efficacy.
In this study, a novel crossยญreactive allergy vaccine based on a consensus allergen was
investigated for its use in allergenยญspecific immunotherapy. The consensus allergen was
designed to represent the allergen family of nonยญspecific lipid transfer proteins (nsLTP).
BALB/c mice were immunized subcutaneously with three doses of a mRNAยญlipid nanoparยญ
ticle, plasmid DNA, or recombinant protein formulation of the consensus allergen. In addiยญ
tion to the consensus allergen, mRNA and DNA vaccines encoded a lysosomalยญtargeting
sequence for elevate MHCยญII presentation, while the protein vaccine was expressed withยญ
out this sequence. The antibody response was assessed by ELISA, and T helperยญcell
responses were analysed by flow cytometry. It was found that immunization of mice with
the consensus allergen resulted in induction of crossยญreactive antibodies capable of bindยญ
ing both to the designed allergen and to the peach nsLTP, Pru p 3. Both antiยญPru p 3 IgG1,
IgG2a, IgA, and IgM antibodies were detected in mice immunized with protein and mRNA,
whereas no antibody response was observed in DNAยญimmunized mice. We found a slight
TH1ยญbias in mice immunized with mRNA and a slight TH2ยญbias in protein immunized mice,
as indicated by IgG1/IgG2a/IgE and TH1/TH2 ratios. The inhibition of IgE binding was
evaluated in vitro using serum from patient with proven nsLTP allergies. It was found that
antibodies in serum from mRNA or protein immunized mice were able to block the bindยญ
ing of human IgE antibodies to Pru p 3 by 90% and 65%, respectively. These findings
suggest that consensus allergens potentially could be used AIT, possibly being used for
treatment of several related allergies simultaneously.

Introduction
Allergies have emerged as a significant global health concern, affecting up to 30ยญ40% of
people in specific populations [1]. Over the past decades, prevalence of allergies has
been dramatically increasing in developed and developing countries. The increased inยญ
cidence of allergy is in particular pronounced in children [2]. Although the precise underยญ
lying mechanisms driving this rise remain unclear, it has been associated with improved
hygiene standards and reduced exposure to natrual environments, bacteria, and dirt [3].
The number of people suffering from allergies are therefore anticipated to continue rising
[2, 3].
Untreated allergies impose major consequences on individuals, associated with reduced
quality of life, family economics, and work attendance. In addition, allergies impose a
substantial economic burden on society, resulting from medical expenses, and indirect
costs from absenteeism, and reduced productivity. In the United States, direct medical
costs are estimated to approximately $25 billion annually, while the European Union faces
indirect costs between โฌ55 and โฌ151 billion per year due to untreated allergies, equivalent
to approximately โฌ2405 per untreated patient per year [4, 5]. Appropriate and effective
treatment options may consequently not only improve patientsโ lives but also yield a posยญ
itive socioeconomic impact, underscoring the need for improved treatments and allergy
vaccines.
1.1
Sensitization and the allergic reaction
Allergies are caused by an exaggerated immune response, classified as type I hypersenยญ
sitivity. Allergies arise due sensitization of the immune system against otherwise harmless
substances in the environment known as allergens. Once sensitized to a specific allerยญ
gen, subsequent encounters with it can trigger a reaction, which results in a wide set of
symptoms that may vary from mild reactions like itching and sneezing to severe and poยญ

of allergy is complex and is generally (but not exclusively) mediated by immunoglobulin
E (IgE) antibodies. In nonยญallergic individuals, contact with an allergen mounts a normal
IgG response that does not lead to allergic inflammation [7]. Whether a healthy or hyยญ
persensitive response is induced depends on a wide range of factors, including genetic
predisposition, allergen exposure in early life, and adjuvant factors [8]. Still, much remains
unknown about the underlying mechanisms of allergic sensitization.
In respiratory allergies, allergen exposure occurs at mucosal membranes of the eyes,
nose, or airways, where is the allergens are captured by dendritic cells, or other profesยญ
sional antigen presenting cells (APC). For food allergies, allergen capture occurs durยญ

are recognized by the naรฏve CD4+ T cells in the presence of a high relative concentration
of interleukin 4 (ILยญ4) cytokines, this leads to induction of the T helper (TH)ยญ2 response.

1.2.1
Allergy treatment and impact
Currently, most protocols for management of allergies are based on avoidance of the
source, especially for food allergy [26]. While avoidance is effective in some cases, it may
not be feasible for airborne allergens due to seasonal exposure. Appropriate allergy treatยญ
ments are therefore essential. Several different types of treatment are available, which
generally can be classified into antiยญinflammatory symptomatic treatments and curative
treatments. Symptomatic treatments can be administered as a preventive treatment to
preยญsensitized patients, leading to an inhibition at effector stage. This includes oral antiยญ
histamines and intranasal corticosteroids sprays, in addition to different biologics which
neutralise allergenยญspecific IgE [17]. Although these treatments effectively alleaviate acยญ
cute symptoms and are cheap, they often require daily doses, and fail to prevent disease
progression towards asthma [27].
Antihistamines are the most commonly applied treatment for management of allergies,
and function by inhibiting the effect of histamines. Histamines are chemical messengers
released during degranulation of mast cells and basophiles, which function by binding
to H1 receptors on a variety of cells including airway and vascular smooth muscle cells,
endothelial cells, epithelial cells, eosinophils, and neutrophils. While binding of histamines
stabilises the activated conformation of the receptor, antihistamines competitively bind to
the same receptors and stabilizes its inactive conformation, thereby inhibiting downstream
signalling pathways [28].
Corticosteroids are another class of medication, commonly used as treatment for many
different allergic and inflammatory disorders, including asthma, allergic rhinitis, and atopic
eczema [29]. Corticosteroids are synthetic hormones that bind to glucocorticoid recepยญ
tors, present in most cells of the body. Glucocorticoid receptors belong to an evolutionarily
conserved receptor superfamily and act as transcription factors that respond to glucocorยญ
ticoids in a ligandยญdependent manner, enabling a diverse range of actions. Once cortiยญ
costeroids bind to these receptors, the complex is translocated to the cell nucleus, where

1.3
Induction of immune tolerance using AIT
The primary objective of AIT is to restore peripheral immune tolerance to allergens and
to inhibit allergic reactions. Induction of tolerance is a complicated process that involves
the interplay between adaptive and innate immune cells, and operates in two phases:
an early phase with diminished mast cell responsiveness and an increase in TH2 cells
and IgE levels, and a late phase that takes 2ยญ3 years of treatment involving regulatory T
cells (Tregs), secretion of suppressor cytokines and synthesis of allergenยญspecific IgG4
antibodies [9, 36]. The exact mechanisms are still being studied and much remains to be
fully understood.
As allergies are characterised by a TH2ยญdominated immune response, the efficacy of AIT
is considered to be dependent on shift from a TH2 response to a TH1 response and/or
on induction of Tregs. In a healthy immune system, the balance between tolerance and
intolerance must be carefully tuned. Treg cells are a class of lymphocytes that orchestrate
the immune response, and these are essential in induction and maintenance of peripheral
tolerance by suppressing excessive immune reactions [9]. During AIT, continuous expoยญ

weeks of AIT, allergenยญpresenting tolerogenic dendritic further induce Tregs subsets [27].

expression of chemokine receptors CCR2, CCR4 and CCR8 that inhibit migration of effecยญ

protein 4 (CTLAยญ4) and Programmed cell death protein 1 (PDยญ1), involved in induction

ILยญ35 and transforming growth factor beta (TGFยญฮฒ) [9]. These suppressor cytokines are
essential in downยญregulating the inflammatory response to the allergen and selectively
promoting isotype specific antibody secretion. Secretion of ILยญ10 and TGFยญฮฒ by T regs, in
addition to cellยญcell interaction between T regs and B cells, have been shown to induces
isotype class switching of B cells favouring synthesis of allergenยญspecific IgG4 [13, 9]. In
addition, secretion of TGFยญฮฒ has been shown to induce preferential isotype switching to
IgA1/IgA2 by B cells [16]. Thus, the specific set of cytokines and their relative concentraยญ

tion, IgG4 does not have a binding domain for complement component C1q, rendering
it unable to induce complimentยญrelated inflammation [13]. Interestingly, IgG4 has a low
affinity for the activating Fc receptor for IgG (FcฮณR), while maintaining high affinity for the
inhibitory FcฮณRIIb [48]. Binding of IgG4 to the FcฮณRIIb receptor on mast cells counters the
effects of IgE binding to FcฮตRI. This results in reduced synthesis of cytokines, histamines,
and prostaglandins and inhibit IgE signalling pathways, leading to increased thresholds
for release of the inflammatory mediators [16, 9]. In IgEยญsecreting B cells, it has been
suggested that engagement of IgG4 with FcฮณRIIb, and subsequent crossยญlinking, signals
to reduce IgE synthesis, thereby reducing number of soluble IgE antibodies capable of
binding to the allergen [43].
Another antibody isotype that has been suggested to be important for successful AIT is
IgA. IgA is predominantly found in mucosal membranes and compromises around 80%
antibodies in the human gastrointestinal system [50]. Hence, induction of allergenยญspecific
IgA may play a protective role in food allergies, although this has been disputed [51].
However, cross linking in vitro of IgA on monocytes induced ILยญ10 synthesis supporting
its role in AIT [52]. Interestingly, both maternal IgG and IgA antibodies have been shown to
be transferred over the placenta and are thought to be important for prevention of allergy in
the offspring [16]. These findings highlight IgAโs relevance to AIT in desensitizing patients.
Induction of allergenยญspecific IgA and IgG isotypes prevent an allergic reaction in different
ways. Upon contact with the allergen, IgG4 and IgA (and other isotypes) compete with
IgE for allergen binding. These serves and โblocking antibodiesโ that cover the allergen,
thereby preventing formation of allergenยญIgE complexes and crossยญlinking of FcฮตRIยญbound
IgEs on mast cells and basophils, and the subsequent immediate allergic inflammation
[27, 9]. Beside from preventing crosslinking of IgEยญFcฮตRI, allergenยญspecific blocking antiยญ
bodies induced by AIT also interfere with IgEยญfacilitated allergen presentation to T cells,
which reduces their activation, leading to less allergic inflammation and likely also reduced
eosinophil activation [16, 43]. Thus, the main outcome of successful AIT is induction of
a tolerogenic micromilieu, differentiation and expansion of regulatory cell subsets, and
synthesis of allergenยญspecific blocking antibodies, altogether aiding in evading the effects
of IgE.

Discussion
Allergies are a significant healthcare challenge that affects people worldwide, creating an
urgent need for effective and better treatment. Although vaccines for AIT have proven to
be a successful treatment strategy for certain allergies, many patients discontinue treatยญ
ment due to side effects or due to lack of efficacy. Reduced clinical efficacy has been asยญ
sociated with use of natural allergen extracts, and vaccination with recombinant allergens
has been suggested as a better alternative. In order to tackle challenges and improve
the efficacy of AIT, especially for patients suffering from crossยญreactivities, we designed
a novel crossยญreactive allergy vaccine. Previous research has indicated that vaccination
with natural crossยญreactive allergens may induce tolerance to related allergens through
epitope conservation, thereby effectively treating multiple allergies simultaneously [73].
To further build upon this principle, we designed a novel nsLTPยญbased consensus allerยญ
gen that represented a broad set of allergens in a single amino acid sequence. It was
hypothesized that vaccination with this consensus allergen would induce crossยญreactive
โblockingโ antibodies with improved binding properties to multiple different nsLTP allerยญ
gens. As individuals who undergo AIT are often sensitized to multiple related allergens,
this vaccine strategy might potentially improve the outcome of therapy.
In the experiments conducted in this thesis, we found that vaccination of naรฏve mice with
this consensus allergen indeed induced production of crossยญreactive antibodies capable
of binding both to natural allergens (Pru p 3) and the designed allergen (CA1) (Figure 3.12
and 3.15). The ability of the vaccine to generate antibodies capable of binding to natural
allergens, and not only the immunogen, is crucial for its efficacy as these are the ones
triggering an allergic reaction. Although it was not tested, it is plausible that antibodies
raised against the consensus allergens are able to bind to other nsLTPs than Pru p 3,
given the high conservation within the nsLTP allergen family. The precise extend to which
these antibodies are able to recognize different natural allergens, is yet to be determined
in future studies.
More interestingly, we also found that antibodies exhibited the ability to block the binding
of IgE in serum from severely allergic patients to natural allergens. These findings demonยญ
strate that the antibodies generated in response to the vaccine specifically targeted the
relevant epitopes, physically hindering the access of IgEs. As the clinical outcome of AIT
has been shown to be dependent on induction of blocking antibodies [74], these findยญ
ings highlight the potential desensitizing effects that can be achieved through vaccination
with consensus allergens, although further investigations are needed to fully understand
the therapeutic implications. The use of consensusยญlike allergens for treatment of crossยญ
reactivity allergies was similarly examined in study on Bet v 1ยญmediated birch allergy.
Here, crossยญreactive blocking antibodies capable of binding to different related allergens
were also induced, and these were in a similar manner able to block the binding of human
serum IgE to Bet v 1 [75]. As Bet v 1 belongs to the PRยญ10 allergen family, the use of
consensusยญlike allergens may be a broadly applicable strategy for AIT.
In this study, we investigated the use of mRNA, DNA, and proteinยญbased vaccines for
AIT by examining their respective ability to elicit antibody responses against the designed
consensus allergen. We included mRNA and DNA vaccines due to their promising potenยญ
tial for use in AIT, while the protein formulation was included as resembles traditional AIT
approaches. More recently, nucleotideยญbased vaccines for AIT have attained increasing

To evaluate the potential impact of our study in human patients, certain differences in the
immune response between humans and mice must be taken into considerations. Comยญ
monly, the objective of AIT is to shift the response from a IgE/TH2 dominated response to
a TH1 response, and potentially induce Tregs to promote tolerance maintenance. In most
human studies, ratios of IgE and IgG4 generally servers as biomarkers for efficacy of AIT.
Notably, a major difference in humoral responses between mice and human patients is
the absence of the IgG4 heavy chain gene in mice. As an alternative, several studies
have applied the ratio of IgG1 and IgG2a as biomarkers [60]. A TH1ยญbiased serologiยญ
cal profile is characterized by low IgG1/IgG2a ratios and no functional IgEs. In contrast,
TH2ยญbiased serological profiles are characterized by functional IgEs, high IgG1 levels and
complete absence of IgG2a, being indicative for allergic sensitization [60, 72]. Notably,
biological functions of IgG1 and IgG2a differs remarkably from IgG4, as both isotypes
are potent activators of the compliment system whereas IgG4 is a neutralizing antibody.
Therefore, elevated IgG1 and IgG2a levels may potentially contribute to inflammation. In
our study, we did not find any major differences between vaccine formulations with reยญ
spect to IgG1/IgG2a ratios (Figure 3.13), although a slight skewing towards IgG2a was
found for the highยญdose mRNA vaccine, while a slight skewing towards IgG1 was found for
the proteinยญbased vaccine. This may indicate a potential TH1ยญbias of the mRNA vaccine
and a TH2ยญbias of the protein vaccine. From that perspective, the mRNA vaccine seems
favourable relative to the protein injections.
Notably, though IgG1 and IgG2a has been correlated with a shift in the TH1/TH2 response,
other isotypes are also of interest with respect to their blocking potential. IgA, in particยญ
ular, may be relevant for food allergy, as these allergens are encountered on mucosal
surfaces of the gastrointestinal tract. As IgA is the most abundant antibody isotype at muยญ
cosal surfaces, it may block allergen epitopes before they reach the blood stream, thereby
inhibiting IgEs from binding and the resulting degranulation [16, 79]. However, the extent
to which serum samples can be used to indicate presence of allergenยญspecific IgAs at
mucosal surfaces is questionable. IgM was characterized as these as well as IgG and
IgA have been suggested to aid in viral clearance for the covidยญ19 mRNA vaccine [80].
Therefore, it was considered that IgM in a likewise manner could be relevant for allergen
binding, potentially blocking relevant epitopes. Like for IgA, we observed allergenยญspecific
IgM antibodies at high titers. Thus, as both IgG1, IgG2a, IgA, and IgM, have been found

generally raises the concern of adverse reactions, as anaphylaxis is observed in up 5%

Generally, with respect to inducing the production of allergenยญspecific antibodies, the proยญ
tein formulation was more successful across replicates, while the mRNA vaccinated mice
had high variability in the highยญdose group, and no response in nether medium nor low

population (n=3). However, other factors may also help explain these results. Initially,
variability may relate to the practical administration of the vaccines. It was planned that
50ฮผL of the different vaccine formulations should be injected into each mouse but due to
unanticipated dead volume in the syringe, only 30uL could be injected. Consequently,
mice may have been immunized with slightly deviating doses of the vaccines, which could
affect their response. Nonetheless, this variability would be expected to be accounted for
in later booster doses. In addition, the efficacy of transfection as well as the organ being
transfected could also explain the high variability. Ideally, dendritic cells or other APC
are taking up the LNPs via endocytosis for presentation on MHCยญII to Tยญcells. However,
tissueยญresident cells are also likely to be transfected, and the overall response depends on
which cell types are being transfected. If the tissue around the site of injection is dense, the
LNPs are less likely to distribute out of the organ, causing transfection of tissueยญresident

LNPs will be distributed in the whole body, effectively causing transfection in the liver and
spleen. If many lymph vessels are present in the organ, LNPs are rapidly transported to
lymph nodes [82]. Dependent on the cell types at the site of injection and the distribution
of the LNPs, this may affect the induction of allergenยญspecific antibodies. Moreover, the
strategy for injection also affects accumulation of LNPs. If injection is done intra venously
(IV), the particles accumulate in the liver causing transfection of these cells. For intramusยญ

To increasing the safety of the therapy and mitigating unwanted side effects from conยญ

was done in an attempt to limit the risk of free allergens being released from the cell
to the extracellular micromilieu. Hence, resulting allergic reactions or anaphylaxis was
expectedly reduced substantially. Similar approaches have been applied in other studยญ

sequence of the consensus allergen to a Lysosomeยญassociated membrane glycoprotein

this approach has been found to enhance immunogenicity of the target antigens signifiยญ

pathway, which usually are associated with desensitization in conventional AIT. Likewise,
Tan et al. compared DNA vaccines with and without LAMPยญ1 sequences in a study on dust
mite allergy. They found that transcriptional fusion of a LAMPยญ1 sequences to the coding

allergen sequence without a LAMPยญ1 domain yielded a TH2ยญbiased response [84]. Addiยญ

Thus, addition of a LAMPยญ1 domain to the consensus allergen appears to be highly favorยญ

observations are in accordance with our observations on strong antibody responses and
slight TH1 skewing (Figure 3.15 and 3.17).
Generally, the use of consensus allergens for AIT represents a novel approach that only
faintly has been explored in other studies, though it represents a promising approach
to treatment of patients suffering from crossยญreactive allergies. Moreover, the principle
of consensus proteins has also been successfully applied in other research areas e.g.,
for development of broadly neutralizing antibodies against different toxins and viruses
[86, 87]. While allergen consensus sequences for AIT may have for several advantages,
there are certain drawbacks. To induce tolerance to certain allergen sources, it is imporยญ
tant that the consensus allergen represent all clinically relevant allergens corresponding
to the patients sensitization pattern. In southern Europe, nsLTPยญmediated allergies are
common and these might effectively be treated with a nsLTP consensus allergen like CA1.
However, patients that are reactive to different allergen families in the same source may
not benefit from vaccination with a consensus nsLTP allergen. E.g., for apple allergies,

northern Europe, the consensus design could prioritize either of these regions to reduce

Conclusion and future perspectives
In this thesis, the effects of immunizing with a consensus allergen using mRNAยญLNP, plasยญ
mid DNA, and protein formulations has been studied to evaluate its potential use for AIT.
It was demonstrated that vaccination with both mRNA and protein formulations induced
crossยญreactive antibodies capable of binding to both the consensus allergen and natural
allergens. This suggests that vaccination with the consensus allergen could potentially
provide broad protection against multiple related allergies caused by nsLTP allergens.
Furthermore, it was demonstrated that antibodies generated in response to vaccination
with the consensus allergen effectively inhibited binding of IgEs in serum from severely
allergic patients to natural allergens, indicating a potential desensitizing effect.
Although the findings of this study are a first step towards a new type of allergy treatment,
much still remains to be understood. One key area for future exploration is the extent to
which antibodies produced after immunization with the consensus allergen can bind to
a more diverse range of nsLTP allergens, and whether these antibodies exhibit heightยญ
ened crossยญreactivity compared to those generated following immunization with natural
allergens like Pru p 3. In addition, to validate the clinical utility of these vaccines, further
research is needed. Therefore, an exciting next step will be the vaccination of allergic
models with the consensus allergen to evaluate its potential in protecting against allergic
reactions.
In summary, consensus allergens have the potential to transform allergy treatment by proยญ
viding a more targeted and personalized approach to desensitization, leading to improved
patient outcomes and quality of life for individuals suffering from allergic diseases.
