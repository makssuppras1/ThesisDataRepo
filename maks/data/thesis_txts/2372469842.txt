Abstract
This thesis seek to investigate the possibilities of conducting robot localisation
based on electromagnetic fields. Localisation with electromagnetic field could
be useful in a underwater environment, where standard localisation methods
like GPS and vision are limited. In order to investigate this there have been
developed a proof of concept system, which consisted of a transmitter system
and a receiver system. The development of the proof of concept system ensured
the possibility for testing localisation based on electromagnetic fields and was
able to provide an estimation of the distance and angle between the transmitter
and the receiver. The transmitter ensure that there are generated the larges
possible electromagnetic field by inducing a coil mounted on a ferrite magnet.
The receiver capture and process this electromagnetic field and uses the ampli-
tude and the phase of the signal to provide a localisation estimate. In order
to process and use the data the receiver perform a FIR filtering, which ensures
that the localisation is based on the electromagnetic signal generated by the
transmitter. To ensure a reliable phase detection there have been implemented
a CUSUM fault detection algorithm, which ensures that faulty measurements
are neglected.
The system have been thoroughly tested and were able to obtain a maxi-
mum range of 3 m with a deviation of 2.6 cm. In terms of the accuracy of the
system it was able to perform localisation with an angle ranging from 0-180◦.
The accuracy of the system were tested at a 1 m distance for various angles
in the ranging span, where it was possible to achieve a deviation ranging from
0.25-3.1 cm. The obtained results have been assessed in terms of usability in-
cluding possible use cases and in terms of the performance metrics.
Furthermore the attenuation constants for freshwater and seawater have been
presented and there have been proposed suggestions for further development
of the system in order of being able to operate in an underwater environment.

CHAPTER 1
Introduction
This master thesis is conducted as an investigation of the possibilities for mak-
ing robot localisation based on electromagnetic fields. The advantages of mak-
ing an electromagnetic based localisation are that it can be used for underwater
robots. The Technical University of Denmark (DTU) is currently running a
research project called REMORA, which investigate the possibilities for au-
tonomous underwater vehicles (AUV). The REMORA robot is shown in figure
1.1.
Figure 1.1: The Remora AUV robot.
According to Christensen et al. 2015 the REMORA project is intended to
conduct autonomous inspection of offshore underwater structures, however it is
certainly not limited to this. The advantages of an AUV system for inspection
of underwater structures is numerous, but for the industry the most significant
and important one is cost reduction. According to Lund 2015 DONG energy
had to allocate 2 billion DKK in order to do offshore repair work on their Siri
offshore oil platform due to cracks in an oil tank on the seabed. The idea behind
the REMORA project and AUV inspection is, that if continuously having
AUV’s examining sensitive areas of an offshore platform or similar structures, it
would be possible to detect problems and perform the necessary repairs, before
a problem reach a critical state, that could force a shutdown in production,
which is a very costly affair. In order to successfully conduct these inspections
robot localisation will be crucial.
==PAGE:18==
2
1 Introduction
The normal methods for robot localisation (GPS, vision etc.) encounter
severe problems when used underwater due to the fact, that water is greatly
attenuation. This makes the use of GPS very limited. Similarly the lack of
light under water makes vision based localisation impractical. A solution to
localisation in a problematic environment is presented in Sogade 1999 in which
there is conducted electromagnetic cave-to-surface mapping. Even though the
article focuses on a different environment the principles are the same since it
is conducted under the same prerequisites including lack of vision and GPS
signals.
The method used in the article is based on a receiver/transmitter
system with a low frequency signal which is similar to the localisation method
that is presented in this thesis.
Another use case for electromagnetic field based localisation is the possi-
bility to make autonomous docking of AUVs. In Mintchev et al. 2015 several
docking methods for AUVs is evaluated and in the following the three main
steps of the docking procedure is presented:
1. The Homing Phase is when the AUV is guided towards the docking
station. This phase is focused on providing the AUV with knowledge
regarding direction and distance to the docking system, so the AUV is
able to find its way close to the docking station.
2. The Fine Alignment Phase is related to when the AUV is relatively
close to the docking station and making the final adjustments to ensure
the position of the AUV is perfectly aligned with the position of docking
station.
3. The Connection Phase is the last phase where the AUV is physically
connected to the docking station.
A system capable of making a complete autonomous docking should be able
to operate through all three phases. In relation to AUV docking, Feezor et al.
1997 created a system for the AUV homing phase with electromagnetic fields.
The homing system were able to obtain a range of 20-25 m when used to dock
the robot, with the range of the system primarily limited by the attenuation
of the electromagnetic fields caused by water.
In Farid et al. 2013 several localisation methods are presented and eval-
uated according to the performance metrics. The performance metrics is a
manner of classifying different areas of localisation systems and evaluate them.
The metrics is as follows:
• Accuracy is the precision of a localisation system which is defacto the
difference between the estimated position and the actual position.
• Responsiveness is an expression for how fast the localisation is updated.

• Coverage is related to the area in which the localisation system is func-
tional.
• Adaptiveness describes the systems ability to adapt to environmental

Electromagnetic localisation
In this section, previous investigations regarding electromagnetic localisation
will be presented and shortly explained. This will include two overall methods.
In order to achieve a functional localisation in a underwater environment 3
dimensions is needed (x,y,z). There have been proposed several methods to
succeed in this. In Paperno et al. 2001 it is proposed to use two orthogonal
excitation coils in order to get a rotating magnetic field, which is measured
by a sensor. This can be used in order to determine the sensor’s position and
orientation based on the minimum and maximum of the ac part of the squared
field H2
ac.
In Song et al. 2013 an electromagnetic localisation method based on a
rotating magnetic dipole is presented. It is based on a 2-axis generator creating
the electromagnetic field and a 3-axis sensor as shown in figure 1.2 (a).

Figure 1.2 (b) shows the experimental plane used to test the obtainable
localisation. This approach is based on the same principles as the previously
presented method with the sensor replaced by three sensing coils. According
to the author this method is less computationally demanding and thereby the
localisation can be completed faster which is crucial since localisation have to

sufficient for robot localisation, due to the fact that magnetic fields are greatly
attenuated over distance.
This method forms the basis for the investigation conducted in this thesis.
In order to enlarge the generated electromagnetic fields the coils are mounted
around a ferrite core.
1.2
Objectives
The objective of this thesis is to investigate the possibilities for conducting
robot localisation based on electromagnetic fields. The intention of performing
this investigation is to get an understanding about the possibilities for robot
localisation with electromagnetic fields and assess the applicability for AUV´s.
In order to complete the objective of this thesis some focus points is defined as
follows:
• Create a system for transmitting and measuring an electromagnetic field.
• Estimate a position based on a electromagnetic field.
• Evaluate the position that can be obtained with the constructed system.
• Evaluate the possibilities for using electromagnetic fields for AUV locali-
sation or similar use cases.
• Evaluate the system in perspective of the performance metrics.
The completion of these focus points will be assessed at the end of the thesis
and subsequently contribute to the conclusion of whether electromagnetic fields
can be used for localisation.

Structure of the thesis
In this section, the structure of the thesis will be shortly presented. The thesis
consist of 7 chapters.

cludes a proposition to the further work that could be conducted in relation to
this investigation.
Chapter 7 Conclusion is the final chapter of the thesis, which conclude on
the objective and focus points of the investigation presented in the introduction.
Furthermore there is concluded on the work conducted with this thesis and the
obtained results.

CHAPTER 2
Analysis
This chapter elaborates on the analysis of how to perform robot localisation
based on electromagnetic fields.
First a description of the basics of the relevant electromagnetic field theory
including wave- propagation and attenuation is presented. Thereafter follows
a theoretical comparison of the attenuation of electrical fields in freshwater and
seawater. Conclusively a developed proof of concepts solution and the relevant
tasks are presented.
2.1
Electromagnetic Wave Propagation
This section explains electromagnetic wave propagation along with the atten-
uation caused by water.
The relevant theory presented in this section are
deducted from Ulaby et al. 2010 and Cheng 2014.
The connection between electricity and magnetism origins in the discov-
eries made by the danish physicist Hans Christian Ørsted. He proved that an
electric current in a wire causes a movement of electrons, which creates a mag-
netic and electric field. A discovery made in 1820. Later in history Michael
Faraday and Joseph Henry conducted experiments that lead to the discovery
that the converse effect is in fact also true, hence magnetic fields can establish
an electric current. A fact that is crucial regarding the perspectives of this
thesis.
These discoveries along with other breakthroughs in physics lead to the
famous ”Maxwell equations”, which describes the dynamics of electromagnet-
ics and is fundamental in calculating electromagnetic wave propagation and
attenuation:

where H is magnetic field intensity, E is electric field instensity, J is the current

The absolute permittivity is given by ε = ε0εr with ε0 being permittivity
of free space and εr the relative permittivity. The absolute permeability is
similar given by µ = µ0µr, where µ0 is permeability of free space and µr is the

Equation 2.1 is also known as Faraday’s law and states that the voltage in-
duced in a circuit is equivalent to the change in magnetic flux.
Equation 2.2 is known as Ampére’s circuital law, which similary to equation

this is ensured if the sampling frequency Fs is chosen with respect to equation

Fs ≤2Fmax

right side of equation 3.2 is more commonly known as the Nyquist rate.
The frequency of the applied electromagnetic signal was explained in sec-

The obtainable frequency for the filtering is Fs = 37 kHz which means that the
filtering is unaffected by aliasing and the whole analog signal is converted to a
digital signal for further processing.
The Nyquist frequency (also known as the folding frequency) is the maxi-
mum frequency that can be detected at a sample rate. The Nyquist frequency

Equation 3.9 states that the FIR filter runs M number of times. The pre-

3.3
FIR Filter Implementation
This section relates to the implementation and testing of the FIR filter.
Since the signal is a 9 kHz sine wave the FIR filter was designed to be a
bandpass filter, which ensured that frequency outside the signal was attenuated.
The coefficients was found using the Matlab fir1 function. The fir1 function
==PAGE:46==
30
3 ADC and Filtering
uses a Hamming window to generate the filter coefficients. The function needs
three inputs:
• Order of the filter
• The minimum and maximum boundary of the passband range
• The filter type
In order to decide these input variables in a reasonable way, the following
considerations have been made:
The filter order is basically proportional to the precision of the filter mean-
ing that a higher order ensures a more precise filter. However simultaneously a
high filter order costs more computational power and since a localisation have
to be computed in real time to be functional, the computational time have
to be kept at a minimum. If the computational time is increased, the sample
time is similarly decreased, which will result in the sample time not fulfilling
the Nyquist rate in equation 3.2. This results in the loss of data from the
input signal. Therefore the filter order were chosen to be 32 since this gave an
acceptable number of computations, giving sufficient high sampling time along
with a decent filtering of the signal.
The boundaries of the filter were chosen to range from 8.5-9.5 kHz ensuring
that the filtering was around the 9 kHz transmitter signal.
The filter type was as the boundaries predict a bandpass filter. As mentioned
earlier a bandpass filter was chosen for the system. This was the correct choice
for the system since it was only interesting to process the transmitted 9 kHz
signal and the bandpass filter ensured that signals outside the bandpass range
were greatly attenuated cf. figure 3.6.

Performance Metrics
In order to evaluate the proof of concept solution presented in this thesis,
the system is assessed in relation to the performance metrics presented in the
introduction.

with the distance 0.25-3.1 cm at angles ranging from 0-180◦. The assessment

the future.

This section elaborates on the assessment of localisation based on electromag-

The proof of concept solution developed in relation to this thesis was
proved to be a feasible method in order of investigating robot localisation with
electromagnetic fields. The solution gave encouraging results in terms of using
electromagnetic fields for localisation. The system were able to conduct robot
localisation with a maximum deviation of 3.1 cm from the actual position.

to 3 m. This means that it should be possible to use electromagnetic fields
for robot localisation in a full scale system, when suggested improvements is
conducted. A full scale implementation of the system would involve multiple
coils in both the receiver and the transmitter, which would expand the range
capabilities from 0-180◦to 0-360◦. Furthermore it should be possible to improve
the filtering method and possibly conducting noise filtering.

6.3
Use cases
This section address the possible use cases for the system.
A possible use case for this localisation method could be for the docking
procedure as described in the introduction consisting of three phase: the hom-
ing phase, the fine alignment phase and the connection phase. Based on the
results presented in this thesis, a possible use case for the localisation method
could be for the homing phase of the docking procedure. In order to complete
the homing phase the robot have to be guided towards the relevant docking
station and therefore knowledge regarding direction and distance would be es-
sential in this phase. This phase do not require an accuracy as high as the
fine alignment phase, hence even the proof of concept solution could in fact
be used. In regards of the fine alignment phase, which is related to the final
adjustments needed to ensure perfect alignment with the docking station, the
method could possibly be used. Since this thesis do not include testing the
system on distances shorter than 1 m, it is not possible to conclude on the
subject. However the precision obtained at 1 m is expected to be improved at
shorter distances, since the electromagnetic fields would be larger at shorter
distances. This could indicate that localisation based on electromagnetic fields
could also be used for the fine alignment phase.
Another use case, which could be possible for the method is related to the
REMORA project presented in the introduction of this thesis. The REMORA
robot operate in an underwater environment, hence the localisation possibilities
with this method would sustain range limitations due to the attenuation caused
by water. Nevertheless if the REMORA robot were to be used for inspection

would not be crucial if the operating area complied with the ranging capabilities.
In this case localisation based on electromagnetic fields could prove more useful

6.4
Future Work
This section is related to the future work that could be conducted in relation
to electromagnetic field based localisation.
The future work with the system would initially require a solution to the
problem related to the critical angle presented in chapter 4, where perhaps the
solution proposed in section 6.2 with a round of ferrite magnet could work. If
the suggested solution does not work, then it would be crucial to find another
solution to the problem, since the quick fix solution chosen in this thesis would
not be sustainable in a complete system.
The further development of the proof of concept solution could include
an expansion of the ranging capabilities as shortly described in section 6.2.
This should be done by expanding the receiver with an additional sensing coil
placed perpendicular to the two coils, which would enable the possibilities for
conducting localisation in 3 dimensions with (x,y,z) coordinates. In order for
this to work the transmitter should similarly be expanded with at least one coil
resulting in a design that are similar to the current receiver. Hence making it
possible to determine the angular position with a 360◦range.
These improvements would also make it possible to test the system in an
underwater environment, which could be interesting in relation to an investiga-
tion of under water localisation possibilities. In relation to testing the system
in an underwater environment a waterproof casing for both the receiver and
the transmitter should be developed.
Another possible task that should be investigated in the future work is
an improvement of the noise filtering. If an higher amount of the noise could
be filtered, then the range of the system would be increased. The outcome of
this would probably be a replacement for the Teensy processor, which would
provide more computational power and thereby higher a sampling time.

CHAPTER 7
Conclusion
In this thesis there have been conducted an investigation of a localisation
method based on electromagnetic fields. The investigation included the de-
velopment of a proof of concept system, which were used in order to test and
validate the obtainable results. One of the motivations for this investigation is
the difficulties that AUV localisation experiences in water. Therefore the at-
tenuation constants for freshwater and seawater were found, in order to give an
understanding of the limitations that emerges in an underwater environment.
The choices and decision related the system developed in this thesis were all
made in regards of a future usability in a underwater environment.
The developed proof of concept solution consisted of two main parts: A
transmitter and a receiver. The transmitter was used to generate a electro-
magnetic field, which was captured and processed by the receiver in order to
interpret the data in terms of the distance and angle between the transmitter
and the receiver. The design of the transmitter and the receiver originated
from previously conducted investigations and have been modified in relation
to this project, in order to obtain the best possible results. In order to estimate
the distance and angle between the transmitter and the receiver the phase and
amplitude of the electromagnetic signal was used. The system was able to
provide a valid estimation of the distance and the corresponding angle.
The processing of the electromagnetic signal included a successful FIR
filtering and a fault detection algorithm, which ensured the reliability of the
estimated distance and angle. Furthermore there were conducted a calibration
of the system in order to convert ADC-values into the corresponding distances.
The results obtainable by the proof of concept solution was a maximum
distance range of 3 m with a deviation of 2.6 cm. The system were able to
perform localisation with a angle ranging from 0-180◦, in which it was possible
to conduct robot localisation with an accuracy of 0.25-3.1 cm at a 1 m distance.
In regards of the focus points and the objective presented in section 1.2
the following can be concluded:
• There have successfully been created a system capable of transmitting
and measuring an electromagnetic field.
• The system was able to estimate a position based on the electromagnetic

• The system have been evaluated in perspective of the performance met-
