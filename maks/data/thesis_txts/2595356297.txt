Abstract 
 
This is test report for bolted gusset plate connection marked as part 1.  
• Part 1 contain a theoretical background used for calculations and 
discussion of results with authors comments.  
• Part 2 contain a test results with information about tests itself 
(preparation, utilization, used tools and procedures).  
Part 1 and part 2 form a full report for L-type cut out plate block tearing.  
Design of bolted connection demands vast amount of time. It is dictated by 
complexity of many components taking part in this kind of connections. Currently 
applicable theoretical approach is taken from Eurocode EN1993-1-8. Unfortunately 
Eurocode approach is not sufficient in the situations that are more complex. Complex 
load situations involves a combinations of shear force combined with moment force. 
Due to this situation different path of conduct has been proposed by Jeppe Jönsson 
(Jönsson, 2015).  
New approch is based on von Mises criterium with an interaction between 
verdical force, horizontal force, moment force, yield lines, bolts capacity and different 
states of stresses. Summing it up it is hard to propose a consistent, universal theory 
which would always act sufficiently.  
This part of report is focusing on theoretical approach from Eurocode and new 
theory proposed by Jeppe Jönsson. It contain description of two approaches and 
proposition of improvement. Improvement part is focusing on “L-type” cut-out of block 
tearing, becouse corresponding experiments has been made for such set. 
Both parts theory and test reports should serve as help for future investigation 
of the topic.

Part 1 - Theoretical report 
1.1. Introduction to Theory 
Theory for this report has been described based on knowledge get during process 
of studying and few publications that were necessary for proper understanding. In 
calculations von Mises yield criteria has been used. Main reason of that is Jeppe 
Jönsson in his proposed theory has also used same criteria. Due to his approach all 
students whom work on block tearing theory has also used same capacity theory. To 
remain the constancy of results von Mises theory has been used.  
For bolts capacities the Eurocode calculations has been used. This part has been 
described to make sure that the weakest part of connection is block itself. Reason of 
this approach is - capacity of connection is as strong as it’s weakest part. To make 
sure that designed test provide data need for investigation it’s weakest part needs to 
be plate, not bolts. For critical bolt identification Eurocode procedure also has been 
used. 
Small investigation of elastic rotation center has also been made based on theory 
described by (Gath, 2009). It has been achieved by usage polar moment of inertia, 
however calculations made for elastic rotation center has been assumed as not 
sufficient enough due to much more complex stress situation during test. Position of 
rotation center used in calculations has been obtained empirically, which even with 
high measure error has been assumed as more correct. 
Investigation of Eurocode approach for block tearing problem has also been 
described. After obtaining test results and compared values  with tests ones, the 
statement from abstract about not sufficient calculations has been proved. 
Jeppe Jönsson’s theory for block tearing constitutes the main part of this thesis. 
Initially capacity calculations presented in (Jönsson, 2015) has been checked. Next an 
attempt of improvement of formulas has been made by modifying an assumptions in 
theory. Finally implementation of friction capacity of connection has also been made. 
All values obtained in tests and calculations has been compared in summary 
paragraph of this report. Summary contains also some observations and future 
investigation suggestions.

1.6.4. Friction effect 
Additional improvement of formulas by implementation of extra moment capacity 
due to friction effect of bolts has been made. Calculations has been made based on 
other student previous thesis (Winterskov-Andersen, 2014), whom made it based on 
(Aksel Albertsen, 1997) and Eurocode calculations (The European Union Per 
Regulation 305/2011, 2005). It has been investigated and stated that in case of 
tightening of bolts capacity of the connection is enlarged, even in the situation that 
applied force  overcome initial friction.  
First the assumption that failure line of connection has been establish as outer edge 
of bolts in the connection shown on Figure 1.16. This assumption is consistent with 
observation of plates after tests.  
 
Figure 1.16 – Relative sliding in set with marked failure line  
Friction happened during test on both sides of set between plates type B and plates 
type A. However usage of full area will influence capacity of whole connection to a 
significant extent, which has been assumed as not valid. Instead small area around 
each bolt close to failure line will be used. This area will be represented by washers, 
thanks to that assumption, calculated capacity of the connection can be assumed to 
be on safe side. Parts of washers that exceed failure line on relative sliding has been 
assumed as friction area of the connection. Described area has been presented on 
Figure 1.17.

1.9. Conclusions and future research recommendation 
Based on summary presented in paragraph 1.8 few conclusions can be deduct: 
1) Proposed theory by Jeppe Jönsson is a good starting point, but still demand 
some development. In this report focus areas were in friction contribution of 
connection and modification of state of stress. This alternation has effect by 
some improvement (values of expected yield force get closer to values obtained 
in tests), but there is still field for more modifications. There could be a 
proposition of some modification factor that would be based on significant 
amount of tests.  
2) The most important thing for testing methodology is the constancy of results. 
Method used and described in test report is fulfilling that condition, however it is 
really important in future repeating experiments to follow the procedure, 
because small mistakes during preparation part could give corrupted results. It 
can be seen at C4 type tests, yield force obtained for this samples type has 
been calculated with an error due to significant initial slip. Initial slip took place, 
because of not applying extra dynamic force on sample during preparation time. 
3) For future research there is a recommendation for design a different set for 
another stress state. By that kind of approach the universalism of proposed 
theory can be checked. The equation for an different than in experiments state 
of stress has been derived in point 1.6.2, it could serv for future research. 
4) During experiments there took place buckling of specific side of plate (look in 
part 2 - test report). Explanation of that behavior could be purely due to state of 
stresses that happened in the sample. However bolts that has been used in 
experiment were not long enough. Most part of force was transferred by shaft 
part of bolt, but threaded part of bolt also take part in it. That unnecessarily 
complicated transfer of stresses in experiment. There is a recommendation for 
usage a longer bolts, so only shaft part would be used in force transferring. 
5) In needs to be mention that material of test samples used for block tearing could 
not live up to the demand of minimum capacity. One of cooperative student 
Kenneth Dan Olsen - s133743 obtained results of capacity in dog bone tests. 
After data analysis the problem has been identified and noticed. For more 
information look for Kenneth Dan Olsen - Tensile test report - Dog-bone test of 
steel material. However to avoid errors, in block tearing calculations capacity 
data from Kenneth Dan Olsen has been used.
