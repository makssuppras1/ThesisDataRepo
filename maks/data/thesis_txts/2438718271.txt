Summary
The multisensory modeling and Ventriloquism Effect have been well known for a long
time, but how multimodal perceptual take place in the brain is quite a new area of
the cognitive and neuroscience field.
Technical University of Denmark (DTU) performed an experiment by using a
large room with established sound system to create auditory stimuli and using Virtual
Reality(VR) to create visual stimuli; the data collected from that experiment gave the
opportunity to investigate modeling of audiovisual perceptual integration in VR and
audiovisual multisensry analysis for azimuth and distance discrepancy perception.
We used 8637 data points collected from the DTU project (9 participants x 20
sample x (6 congruent + 30 incongruent + 12 unimodal) - 3 lost data points) for our
analysis.
Four statistical modeling methods (Fusion, Confusion, Robust Integrated and
Gaussian) applied on DTU experiment data and evaluated by AIC/BIC methods.
The results indicated that the Robust Integration Model was the best model.
The weights of the Azimuth and Distance data produced by Confusion and Robust
Integration model was also analyzed and the Ventriloquism Effect observed in both
modalities. The audio weights calculated by the confusion model increased as the
azimuth between the two stimuli increased, which is in agreement with the previous
researches.

CHAPTER 1
Introduction
Our brain benefits from multiple sensory information to construct and enhance the
perception of an external event to create a robust and coherent perception from the
external world. The brain needs to integrate that information efficiently. We are
usually surrounded by different signals (sight, sound, olfaction, haptic) and the brain
responsibility is to find the signals related to common cause and integrate those.[SB12]
How multisensory information integration happens in the brain is the focus area of
the cognitive and neuroscience fields.
The multisensory integration can occur in two cases: time inconsistency and dis-
tance inconsistency. Neurosiecnce separate these into two tasks: temporal numerosity
judgment and spatial localization.[FS11] It is already known that when the stimuli
are incongruent and the perceptual expectation is congruent, sometimes an illusion is
created. As an example of spatial inconsistency, the ventriloquist illusion (when the
auditory stimulus perception shifts toward the location of the visual stimulus)[FS11]
is scientifically known and has been discussed for a long time, and in many articles;
this phenomenon is called Ventriloquism Effect. We experience Ventriloquism Effect
every time we watch a video on TV or computer: screen and speaker are separated,
but our perception is make a relation between coherent events.
To understand the brain’s perception and behavior, theoretical neuroscience uses
mathematical modeling. Many studies focus on the integration process([SJB05],[Kus+10])
in the brain and on finding a mathematical model for multisensory perception which
may help us to explain the brain integration model, but we need to be aware that this
explanation does not necessarily mean we know how the brain’s perceptual system
really works. [CS12] [SB12]
The aim of this project is to evaluate different statistical models on multisensory
data collected from a previous DTU (Technology University of Denmark) experiment
and to analyze the perception of the distance and angles modalities information in
congruent and incongruent conditions (Ventriloquism Effect).
Many previous studies have already demonstrated the existence of the Ventrilo-
quism Effect in incongruent audiovisual azimuth perception.[Eti+15] We have this
opportunity to investigate the probability of this phenomenon in incongruent dis-
tance.

1.1
Theoretical Background
The multisensory integration and segregation is not a new idea in psychology, but
the ability to apply mathematical modeling to data collected from the experiments
by computer has only recently become available.
Most spatial localization researches indicate that visual modality dominates inte-
grated perception. Two hypotheses proposed for this dominance:
Howard and Templeton (1966) proposed the hypothesis that attention weight is
higher for the sense that is more precise, which they called ”Modality Specificity
Hypothesis”. The Modality Specificity Hypothesis or in another word the Modality
Precision Hypothesis, implies that the modality with higher precision for the discrim-
ination will dominate the percept of the discrimination, and Welch inferred that the
observer’s attentional weight places on the more precise modality.[Eti+15] [Wel99]
O’Connor & Hermelin (1972) proposed a hypothesis about relative appropriate-
ness or usefulness of that modality for the task in question, called ”modality appro-
priateness(usefulness) hypothesis.” This hypothesis implies that modality which is
more appropriate and useful in a particular condition has dominance for the task of
localization, and vision is more trustworthy than audition.[Wel99]
Following these hypotheses, a research project called ”Humans integrate visual
and haptic information in a statistically optimal fashion” [Ban02] by Ernst and Banks
investigated the visual weight vs. haptic and proposed that ”general principle, which
minimizes variance in the final estimate, determines the degree to which vision or
haptic dominates”[Kus+10]. Ernst and Banks’s experiment demonstrates that, by
using noise to change the visual unimodal perception variance, the modality by higher
variance was the one that dominated the perception.
However, what if a prior knowledge evolved in the process? Marc O. Ernst and
Hannah B. Helbig undertook another research project to investigate this question
and research title implies their result: ”prior konowledge about a common source can
promote visual-haptic integration”.[HE07]
Before going through similar and related projects, a summary of the theory behind
the multisensory modeling would be helpful.

CHAPTER 2
Method
The data for our project was collected by the previous DTU (Technical University of
Denmark) project in audiovisual experiment in Virtual Reality (VR) environment. A
brief about how experiment applied and data collected from DTU VR project:
2.0.1
Room
The experiment performed in a 7.50m x 4.72 x 2.75 listening room.(IEC 268-13 stan-
dard). To avoid front-back confusion interfering in the audio localization, task the
subject is seated at a position offset from the center of the room, to eliminate sym-
metric reverberation REF. The virtual room in Virtual Reality system created to be
identical with physical room and calibrated to be matched.
2.0.2
Virtual Reality
The subjects exposed to AV events at six different locations in the virtual space. The
task of the subject is to point at and indicate with a VR controller the position
where the audio is perceived. All information about the audio and visual stimuli
and perceived audio from participants saved. HTC Vive VR system used for VR
application, including head-mounted stereoscopic displays (HMD) and VR controllers.
A Unity3D application developed for this experiment.
2.0.3
Conditions
Each participant is exposed to and prompted to indicate the position of an event
for four different AV conditions: Incongruent AV, Congruent AV, Audio-only and
Visual-only. 6 different positions are included in the experiment for both modalities
independently, amounting to a total of 48 AV combinations in the overall conditions
- 30 incongruent, 6 congruent, 6 audio-only and 6 visual-only. 20 repetitions included
for each combination, resulting in a total of 960 trials per subject.

2.0.4
Participants
Nine subjects with no hearing or vision impairment participated in the experiment -
6 male and 3 female, mean age of subjects: 27.3 years, standard deviation: 3.6 years.
2.0.5
Stimuli
To make the experiment more realistic, the visual stimulus is a virtual ball dropping to
the ground, impacting with the floor and bouncing back up. The subjects exposed to
the corresponding audio of the impact. The AV events are presented in a controlled
shuffled order, ensuring that the same AV combination does not occur more than
three successive times, which is intended to minimize a familiarity bias.

Six speakers positioned as illustrated in Figure 2.1. Audio stimulus recorded by a Rde
NTG-1 microphone with the anechoic recording of a ball impacting with a carpeted

Visual
The virtual ball/floor collisions of the Unity3D application is identified and a Max

MSP application will control the output channel and playback audio from either of
the 6 speakers, in accordance with the current condition. Visually, the events consist
of the animation of a 14.5 cm diameter ball, matching the size of the ball in the audio
recording. The ball is colored red, to stand out visually from the virtual surroundings.

Protocol
The VR experiment takes on average 1 hour per subject.
Before the experiment,
the subject is instructed to adjust the HMD to ensure comfort and for the binocular
viewports to match the individual’s inter-pupillary distance to minimize eye strain
and maximize clarity of the binocular viewing. As the subject enters the listening

until the subject is wearing the HMD, and thereby blind to the physical world.
2.0.9
Training
Initially, the subject is to complete a training session. This session is intended to
familiarize the subject with the localization tasks and the interactions with the virtual
reality equipment. In the training session, the subjects are presented with visual-only
and audio-only events. They are prompted to point with the VR controller, which in
the virtual Space appears as a laser pointer, at the location on the ground where they
perceive the audio of the ball impact. To minimize noise introduced by button presses,
a second VR controller is used to log the laser pointer position. Before each event, the
subject is prompted to steady the viewing direction by centering a view crosshair on
a presented green square. This green square appears at zero degrees reference angle.
The next event can only be triggered when viewing direction is centered.

2.1.5
AIC/BIC
The Akaike information criterion (AIC) is a statistical model estimator for a given
set of data for comparison between models and aic value calculated by:
AIC = 2k −2ln( ˆL)
K is the amount of the parameters and ˆL is the maximum likelihood of the model
by given data. The smaller AIC value demonstrates the better model. The equation
of AIC implies that method penalizes the models by the amount of the parameters.
The Bayesian information criterion (BIC) essentially estimates the models like the
AIC does, and just penalizes models differently:
BIC = ln(n)k −2ln( ˆL)
The number of samples n involves in the estimation, which is typically penalized
with a higher ratio for parameters than AIC considered.
During the project we used Matlab aicbic function to calculate this values.

CHAPTER 4
Discussion
In this project, we have investigated audiovisual perceptual integration models and
ventriloquism effect in congruent and incongruent azimuth and distance conditions in
Virtual Reality(VR). Four probabilistic models - namely Fusion (MLE), Confusion,
Robust Integration and Gaussian - were applied to a Virtual Reality experiment data
collected from a previous DTU project.
The data preparation including analyzing data outliers, human outliers and nor-
malization test applied. The number of the outliers was ineligible which means that
the experiment data were valid. One human atypical found which his/her data ex-
cluded. Kolmogorov–Smirnov test have been applied and data were normally dis-
tributed. The azimuth and distance data were used during all analysis.
We demonstrated scatter plot of 1 participant unimodal auditory percept in a 2D
cartesian plane and the same for an unimoal visual percept. This visualization is not
so mathematically useful but just gave us a good imagination of the condition.(Figure
3.1)
Fusion model concept indicates that audiovisual variance is lower than both uni-
modal variance in a multimodal perception process. On the other hand visual modal-
ity variances were lower than auditory modality variances (Boxplots(Figure 3.2)),
thus if Fusion model could be a good model for our data, the audiovisual perception
variances vs. visual variances should be able to demonstrate that. For figure 3.14
created to illustrate this assumption. The scatter plot demonstrate that in most cases
the audiovisual variances were higher than visual variances, which implies that Fusion
model probably is not the best model for our experiment.
The parameters of our models calculated by applying models to data.

The models and data have been demonstrated in figure 3.10 which the bar charts
are actual data.
The bar charts of the audiovisual data (red) in the most cases
specially incongruent conditions are not normally distributed. Fusion model has this
assumption that multimodal perception is Gaussian with higher reliability which is
in contrast with our data, and it indicated again that Fusion model is not a suitable
model for our data.
Two evidences have already demonstrated that the Fusion model is not qualified
model for our experiment, and it is the time to look at the AIC/BIC analysis(Figure

AIC/BIC results illustrated that the Robust Integration model performed

The difference between AIC and BIC values is not so significant because the

and this is not much higher than 2, which is the AIC coefficient for penalizing.
The weight distance graph of AIC demonstrated that as the distance increased
the Fusion model AIC growth more than the other models, which is in agreement
with the theoretical idea of the fusion model and all other analysis.
In the congruent conditions, the Fusion model performed better than it did in
incongruent conditions but it was still not able to reach the Rubost Integration and
Confusion model. The RMSE in AIC/BIC analysis for congruent conditions revealed
that in some cases Fusion model performed better. I assume that prior knowledge
could be the reason for the confusion in perception in DTU experiment. People usually
fuse the multi cues to create a coherent perception, but when they knew about the
possibility that the cues could be incongruent then the probability of confusing could
be increased, and this could be the reason that in the congruent conditions of the
VR experiment, the Confusion model performance was better than that of the Fusion
model. This assumption can be evaluated in future experiments.[HE07]
Based on the AIC/BIC plots, we can see that a Gaussian model, which usually
has higher accuracy in prediction, is not a suitable model because it penalized for the
amount of the parameters as we expected.
The Confusion-Azimuth weights plot (Figure 3.20) was a good demonstration way
to confirm the finding of previous studies by illustrating the audio stimuli weights
enhancement during azimuth increment, and I can say it is the best results achieved
during this project. The weights bars increased from the left and right side to the
middle, and from the middle to the sides and create an illustration of the idea. On the
other hand minimum weights located at the auditory stimuli which is representative
of the visual dominancy in the congruent conditions.
However, we are not able to estimate the significance of the degree for this dis-
crepancy as the other researches achieved[Eti+15], due to the limited azimuth (just
3 azimuths) available from our experiment.

higher weight, especially in the 5-meter distance. When the audio stimuli is located
at 5 meters 15-degree, this pattern is not followed, and thus it would be extreme to
draw any definitive conclusion.

CHAPTER 5
Conclusion
To sum up, we applied four different perceptual audiovisual integration models to
data collected by a DTU Virtual Reality experiment and evaluated the outcome.
The Robust Integration model, which is a mixture of the Confusion and Fusion
model, demonstrated better performance based on the AIC/BIC test and audiovisual-
visual variances distribution.
The auditory weights from the Confusion and Robust Integrated models for az-
imuth and distance data has also been analyzed.
The auditory weights of the Confusion model for azimuth analysis is in agreement
with previous researches studies and increased as the distance between two modalities
increased.
The auditory weights of the Confusion model for distance, rise up some questions
that needed further research.
