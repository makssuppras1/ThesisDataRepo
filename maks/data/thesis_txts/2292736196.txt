Summary 
This project is about economical optimal energy renovations of single family houses from the 1960s 
and 1970s. In these years the houses needs renovations because the materials are reaching their 
expected lifetime. The houses also need energy renovations because demands for energy 
consumptions has minimized a lot since the houses were built. It is new that there are strategies and 
demands for energy renovations, and the introduction of renovation class 1 and 2 in the building 
regulation and also the governmental vision of a fossil free society in 2050 is described in the 
beginning of this thesis. 
Different approaches can be used to plan an energy renovation process: the single problem based, the 
combined problem based and renovation or demolishing. The current best solution in Denmark is the 
BedreBolig solution, which combine different energy saving initiatives and other improvements. The 
optimal solution for the house owner and the house will always be individual, so standardized 
solutions cannot be used, but a standardized method can advantageously be used. The house owners 
do not just start an energy renovation, but if their house have a problem, they will renovate. It is 
therefore important that the energy renovation is related to renovation, and that the whole house is 
assessed holistic to make the optimal energy renovation plan taking into account all different kinds 
of added value.

1 Introduction 
It had become common knowledge that it is important to economize on the use of energy to act 
environmental friendly, and technologies are developed to help people use less energy. The big vision 
is that Denmark should be fossil free in 2050 and all energy should instead come from renewable 
energy sources. Since 40 % of the energy used in Denmark is used in buildings, it is important for the 
vision of a fossil free society that the energy use in buildings is improved considerable. In every 
building regulation the energy frame has been tighten and the development of new buildings has 
therefore lead to nearly zero-energy buildings. This is a very good development, but it leave no 
solutions for all the existing single family houses that would still exist in 2050 and it is therefore 
about time, that solutions are developed for energy renovations of single family houses to improve 
the energy consumption. 
In Denmark there is about 2.5 million housings, where around 1 million are single family houses and 
out of this number approximately 450,000 houses is from the 1960s and 1970s. The many houses was 
built within a short period and there was not many demands to the energy consumption. The single 
family house is one of the preferred housing types, and the price per square meter is lower than for 
both older and younger houses [Bolius: Fordele og ulemper], so many house buyers start with a house 
of this type, but the requirements and wishes for the home has changed since the houses were built. 
Houses are seen as something constant and it has been a tradition that existing houses are renovated 
instead of being demolished and replaced, even that it had become a more widespread approach in 
recent years. Most of the single family houses from the 1960s and 1970s was built as type houses 
after the same concept, and therefore there is also basis for a standard solution for renovation, even 
that some houses is maintained better than others. 
The following chapter 2 is state of the art, where the current vision and requirements for energy 
renovation is presented followed by the recent research of different aspects of energy renovation. 
Chapter 3 concern the typical economical and human issues that would affect the decision making 
process about energy renovation. In chapter 4 and 5 different approaches of energy renovations will 
be described and analyzed, first the short term single problem based planning and then the more long 
term combined planning including the new BedreBolig solution. Chapter 6 be about the more drastic 
solution whether to renovate the existing house or if it is more optimal to demolish the existing house 
and replace it with a new type house, which easier would have lower energy consumption. In the end 
chapter 7 is about a new method for optimal planning of energy renovations of single family houses, 
and in chapter 8 the method will be used on an example. The report will end by discussion and 
conclusion consider the objective. 
1.1 Objective 
This project focusing on studies and evaluations of existing approaches of economically justifiable 
energy renovations of single family houses, both scientific methods and practical used methods will 
be described. In the end a proposal for economically acceptable energy renovation of single family 
houses in Denmark will be presented, and it will be used on a case.

2 State of the art 
This chapter coves the latest knowledge about planning of energy renovations of single family houses 
in Denmark, and how important the economy is to consider in the decision making process, to be sure 
that the knowledge is widespread. First the current requirements from the Danish building regulation 
2010 and the coming building regulation 2015 will be described. Then the political objective from 
the Danish government and the newest research is presented. 
2.1 Current requirements 
The current Danish building regulation 2010 do not only have demands for new buildings, also 
existing building has to fulfill demands due to energy consumptions when they are rebuild or 
renovated. In the latest building regulation new rules for energy improvements during renovation is 
introduced. These must be read carefully, in order to determine if a renovation must fulfill the 
demands no matter what, or if the improvements only has to be made if they are cost-effective. This 
section describes when and what demands existing buildings has to fulfill when they are being 
renovated. 
2.1.1 BR10 
Chapter 7.4 in the Danish building regulation from 2010 is about conversion and other alterations to 
the building, and it is here rules for renovation of single family houses are found [BR10]. 
If a replacement is done on a building, the new building component has to fulfill the requirements in 
chapter 7.4.2 and in chapter 8. It means for example if the roof construction is replaced, insulation 
must be added in order for the roof to achieve a U-value of at least 0.15 W/m2K. 
If the change on the building instead just is maintenance and rebuilding of the house, the thickness of 
the insulation only have to be improved to fulfill the U-value if it is cost-effective. It means for 
example that if the roofing is changed, an improvement of the insulation thickness is may needed to 
lower the U-value of the roof construction, but only if it is cost-effective to insulate. 
An improvement is considered cost-effective if the investment is paid back within 75 % of the 
expected lifetime. The expected lifetime of energy-efficient arrangements is found in appendix 6 table 
2 to the building regulation. 
An example from Bolius (The home owner’s information center) show that exterior insulation of 260 
mm on a single family house from 1971 including consequential work cost around 210,000 DKK. 
The result is a cost reduction for heating of DKK 5,500 per year, making the simple payback time 
about 38 years [Bolius: Isoler udefra]. From appendix 6 the expected lifetime for re-insulation of 
building components is found to be 40 year. In order for the insulation to be cost-effective according 
to the building regulation the payback time should not exceed 30 years, so there are no demands for 
the investment to be done. In this case and cases where it is not constructional possible with exterior 
insulation, it could instead be cost-effective to insulate the cavity in the wall, and then it is the 
arrangement to be accomplished, even though the U-values requirement will not be fulfilled. 
Appendix 6 also contain a list of often cost-effective arrangements in connection with renovation 
[BR10: Rentabilitet]. The list is with reservations, so the cost-effective calculation should always be 
made for each building respectively.

2.1.2 BR15 
This year a new building regulation, BR2015, will replace the existing regulation. According to plan, 
it will be released for hearing February 2015, and be valid simultaneous with BR10 from 1st of July 
2015. It will completely replace the existing building regulation on 1st of January 2016. The changes 
mostly concern chapter 7 and 8, and it is expected primarily to include the changes from the strategy 
for energy renovation from the government. The following text is based on the building regulation 
given for hearing in February 2015 [Digitaliseringsstyrelsen, BR15]. 
Chapter 7 in the coming building regulation is extended with a new part 7.4.3 about energy 
performance for existing buildings. The new energy frames is an alternative to the demands for 
building components and can be used to look more holistic on a building renovation project. To 
demonstrate the buildings compliance of the energy frames the SBi-specification 213 (calculation 
program Be10) used for new buildings should be used to make an overall energy performance 
calculation. At the same time it is part of implementing the Buildings Directive, which is an EU 
directive about increasing the energy effectiveness for buildings [Energistyrelsen: EU's 
Bygningsdirektiv]. 
To fulfill the renovation classes the need for energy should be lowered by at least 30 kWh/m2 per 
year and part of the total energy supply should come from renewable energy. A building can be 
classified as renovation class 2 if the total need for supplied energy is less than (110+3200/A) kWh/m2 
per year, where A is the heated floor area in square meter. Class 1 is when the total need for energy 
is less than (52.5+1650/A) kWh/m2 per year and the demands to indoor climate from chapter 6 is 
fulfilled. 
There are not yet any official announcements if renovation class 1 and 2 are expected to be standard 
in the next building code like it was the case with low energy class 2015 and building class 2020. 
Depending on how the building industry adapts to the new renovation classes, it could be a step into 
looking holistic at energy consumption for existing buildings like when designing new buildings. 
2.2 Political incentive 
The Danish government have ambitious plans for the Danish building stock, which they published 
May 2014 [Regeringen, 2014]. The goal for the existing single family houses and the political 
contribution to meet the goal will be described in this section. 
2.2.1 Vision of the Danish Government 
The Danish government has a vision that in 2050 the energy supply system should be based on 100 
% renewable energy. Buildings often have a lifetime of hundred years or more, and few buildings are 
demolished, so most of the houses in use today will also be in use in 2050. An important focus point 
is therefore energy effectiveness of existing buildings. 35 % of the total energy consumption in 
Denmark takes place in buildings, and if the vision of renewable energy should be realized in a cost-
effective way the need for energy should be lowered. It would be possible to build a lot of renewable 
energy producing systems, but it would be expensive, so to have a cost-effective solution it is always 
best first to minimize the need and then cover the need. The strategy for energy renovation should 
secure that the change to an independence from fossil fuel is cost-effective in order to invest as little 
as possible. 
==PAGE:13==
Page 13 of 73 
 
Since the oil crisis in the 1970s the demands for energy consumption of new buildings has been 
tightened in every new building regulation, so the potential for more strict rules for new buildings is 
very limited.  Instead the focus is put on demands for existing buildings, because the saving of energy 
is done best and most cost-effective as a part of the buildings common maintenance and renovation 
process. More than 80 % of the total area of single family houses in Denmark is built before 1979, 
which is before the Danish building regulation had introduced requirements for energy consumptions 
in new buildings, so it makes sense also to introduce rules to improve the existing building stock. As 
an outcome of the energy renovation the indoor climate will also be improved, so the use-value and 
soundness make it better for the residents to live in the building. The energy renovation is not just 
about saving money, the renovation would improve the functionality of the building, where the indoor 
climate is a central element. 
In Denmark a considerable effort over many years in the field of renewable energy and energy savings 
has resulted in a development of competences and larger firms. In accordance with the EU energy 
efficiency improvements directive this may cause new sales chances for Danish firms to foreign 
countries. The effective Danish firms would take advantage of the growing international market and 
expand to a new export markets. 
If the saving of energy should be achieved in a cost-effective way, it is critical that energy renovations 
are made every time the building is renovated or building components are replaced. It is important to 
think energy renovation in a broad environmental sense. Partial solutions like small amounts of 
insulation could look like an easy start, but in the end it could end up being more expensive than a 
well-planned renovation process. Also partial improvements may make it technical impossible to 
realize the full energy saving potential of the house later. 
The largest potential for saving of energy in the Danish building stock is in single family houses 
because more than half of the total energy for heating of buildings is used in single family houses. 
An advantage connected to energy renovation of single family houses is that the owner and the 
resident is almost always the same person, and then the condition of the building and the indoor 
climate is important to the owner because it is the frame for his life. A drawback is that in the 
beginning the owner do not have knowledge or competences to plan an energy renovation project, 
which seems complicated for untrained people. 
2.2.2 Initiatives from the Danish Government 
In 2014 the BedreBolig (better housing) scheme was launched and promoted as one joint entry to 
energy renovation for building owners. Figure 1 show the vision from the government of a reduction 
of 35 % of the energy consumption in buildings in 2050 compared to 2011 and six initiatives, how to 
achieve the goal. The following text will explain more deeply the initiatives from the government to 
reach the vision [Regeringen, 2014].

The building owner has to take the final decision whether to start an energy renovation, but it is the

 Assess if there is a need for further training within energy efficient and renewable energy in

 Development of teaching material and tools for further training of bank advisers to enable

If the craftsman has a broad knowledge, he can suggest a holistic solution, when he is asked to 
renovate a building. It is important that the banks have a good basis to decide whether an energy 
renovation budget is profitable, because they often have to finance the projects if it should be realized, 
so the building owner do not have to save up all the money for the investment beforehand. When the 
sales personnel inform about energy efficient solutions for energy renovation and gives advises where 
==PAGE:15==
Page 15 of 73 
 
to get more knowledge, it seems easier for the building owner and he is forced in the right direction 
from the beginning. 
The energy demands for building components is both decided on an energy assessment and from an 
overall economy assessment, so it would not be an extra economic burden for the building owner. 
For existing buildings there are demands for building components that always has to be fulfilled, and 
then there are demands for building components that only has to be fulfilled if the energy saving 
improvement is cost-effective. Therefore the calculation of cost-effectiveness must always be done 
before the work is started, but studies had shown that this rule is not always followed. The demand 
for building components that always should be followed is simple to understand, and is done to a 
great extent. On the other side the calculation of cost-effectiveness is often not the basis for the 
replacement as the building regulation demand, but still the energy saving initiative is often done, just 
on a different basis. Therefore three different initiatives will be implemented: 
 Change of the building regulation, so it becomes easier to understand. 
 Clout the information effort about the building regulation i.a. by example collection at the 
homepage of Danish Energy Agency. 
 Follow up on compliance of the energy demands for existing building in the building 
regulation and study the compliance every second year. [Regeringen, 2014] 
Especially the simplification of the building regulation and the follow up process is important 
initiatives for the building regulation to be followed more precisely in the future. Another coming 
change is the introduction of optional energy classes for existing building as an alternative to demands 
for building components, see chapter 2.1.2 BR15. A replacement of the windows can change the 
daylight conditions and thereby lower the need for electric lighting. To help the building owner’s 
decisions concerning daylight and sound issues in the process of choosing new windows, new 
information material for the building owner is under preparation. 
One major thought about the BedreBolig scheme is that building owners should just have to go to one 
place to get a complete package for the realization of an energy renovation. Anyway, if the number 
of energy renovations really should be increased and promoted it requires product development and 
cheapening of energy efficient solutions and technologies with industrialized and systems-oriented 
renovation technologies. 
The information and communication efforts on energy renovations and energy efficiency in 
construction should be clout by new goal-oriented information tools and by more support of the 
municipalities. The utility companies should also be involved in discussions about the feasibility of 
a target for the proportion of their energy savings to be achieved in existing buildings. More analysis 
of energy labellings and which effect the labels has on the sales price of a building because the cost-
effective improvements should maintain the value and the energy labels to be used more goal-
orientated. As a last initiative the government support the establishment of the community partnership 
Innovatorium til bygningsrenovering i verdensklasse (Innovatorium for world class building 
renovation). 
2.3 Recent research 
This section present the latest findings from research of the economy of energy renovations in 
Denmark. When investing in energy renovation it is important that it is the right investment for the 
==PAGE:16==
Page 16 of 73 
 
house in the longer run both for the objective of the government and for the economic dependence 
for the family making the improvement. 
2.3.1 Typical incitements 
What is most motivating for house owners and influence them to start energy renovation of their 
property is noticeable savings on the heating and electricity bill. Things that are more often mentioned 
in the media is what to do to avoid global warming and minimize the dependence of Denmark for 
energy from other countries, but that is less significant to the house owners [Knudsen and Jensen, 
2013]. 
As mentioned lower bills is very motivation for the house owner, because he is living in the house 
himself, but the house is also seen as an investment. Several other typical motivation factors could be 
outlined: long-term savings, saving of energy, better temperature conditions, good indoor climate in 
general and higher market value of the house [Knudsen and Jensen, 2013]. When the higher market 
value is mentioned as an incitement, it may be because it is a legal requirement that the energy label 
should be part of the sales presentation, and research had shown that energy consumption had become 
more crucial for the sales prices from 2006 to 2012 [Hansen et al, 2013]. 
Even though the benefits sound attractive for most house owners, technical, architectural and 
economical barriers limit the part of the single family houses that are actually improved [Kragh, 
2009]. From a questionnaire survey in Furesø municipality among owners of single family houses, 
one third answered that their reason for not renovating with regard to energy was doubt about what 
the right solution would be. Other reasons were about economy where 19 % did not know, if they 
have the possibility to finance an energy renovation while also 19 % did not think energy renovation 
is cost-effective [Knudsen and Jensen, 2013]. A way of making the economic aspects less frightening 
for the house owners, is by government supported subsidy schemes for energy renovation. It works 
well according to the intention since it help realize energy renovations that else would not had been 
made. On the other hand some say they would not have made energy improvements if it was not for 
the subsidy schemes [Dansk Byggeri, 2013]. This statement could be a sign that the subsidy schemes 
unfortunately had spoiled the building owners, so if the subsidy is removed it can be expected that 
the amount of energy renovations will drop immediately. 
2.3.2 Typical improvements 
Windows is a building component that house owners have accepted to be a component that has to be 
changed frequently because the lifetime is not as good as for other parts of the building. Furthermore, 
windows is a product, which has undergone a lot of development during the last decades. Even though 
the windows in the single family house had been changed years ago from the original windows, it 
could be an easy and energy-efficient improvement to do again. For windows it is possible to change 
the glass and keep the old frame. This is not to prefer, since the frames are improved considerable 
together with the glass and the old frame would be a limitation for what kind of glass to choose. As 
mentioned window replacements are done from other point of views than energy renovation, but a 
window replacement could start a process of several energy renovation improvements [Kragh, 2009]. 
A decision to take concurrent with the window replacement is exterior insulation of the facade. 
Exterior insulation will often bring a relocation of the windows closer to the new façade so the 
window is placed in the insulation layer due to minimization of thermal bridges, it is cheaper, faster 
and less complicated to improve windows and facade at once. 
==PAGE:17==
Page 17 of 73 
 
Both development of insulation and windows are ongoing and the products are improved all the time, 
and better insulation make it possible to improve U-values in narrow spaces too. The drawback for 
the most efficient insulation is, that it is much more expensive than the standard insulation. Most 
single family houses would benefit from a cost-effective re-insulation of the attic. A help to for house 
owners could therefore be a package solution combining new windows and re-insulation of the attic 
with more customized improvements [Kragh, 2009]. A package solution would standardize the 
building renovation industry and result in more uniform solutions not depending on the knowledge 
and opinion of the actual advisor. 
2.3.3 Calculation of cost-effectiveness  
As mentioned in chapter 2.1.1 BR10 the Danish building regulation have requirements for cost-
effective investments during renovation. This may be the reason why the gap between requirements 
and cost optimal solution in major renovations is very small [Aggerholm, 2013]. Even though it is 
not always the calculation of cost-effectiveness that is the reason for choosing a specific 
improvement, the products and solutions are developed to fulfill the requirements, and therefore the 
demanded become standard to choose. 
Energy renovations had been a focus point in Denmark for couple of years, but the saving caused by 
renovation is still theoretical because general knowledge and experience is missing. However 
demonstration projects show, that the saving is less than expected, because of changed user behavior 
[Wittchen and Kragh, 2013]. It is important to find examples where the expected and the actual saving 
is the same. Else energy renovation gain a reputation for not meeting the expectations, and then people 
could start saying that energy renovation is waste of money and do not want to start renovation their 
own house. 
2.3.4 Financing of energy renovations for single family houses 
A project from 2009 [Kragh, 2009] showed that lots of house owners can finance the cost of an energy 
renovation by a mortgage loan because the mortgage lenders have the possibility to mortgage up to 
80 % of the current market value of the building. That mean that 75 % of the Danish single family 
houses have an average equity of DKK 100,000-200,000 and in the Capital Region the number is up 
to DKK 300,000-400,000. The numbers show, that there is potential for significant energy 
renovations financed by a mortgage loan. Most loans have a term of 30 years, which is a shorter 
period than the lifetime of most re-insulation initiatives, and in addition the indoor climate will 
improve [Kragh, 2009]. As mentioned in chapter 2.2.2 Initiatives from the Danish Government there 
is an initiative from the government working to educate bank advisers, in order to make financing of 
reasonable energy improvements easier to get in the future. It has already been shown, that a high 
ranking energy label make it possible to borrow more money in the bank, which is another incitement 
to buy a house with a good energy label [Hansen et al, 2013] or it is an argument for the house owner 
to use in the bank, if he want to borrow money for an energy renovation.

3 Introduction to fundamental economical and human issues regarding 
renovations 
The coming chapters describe and evaluate different approaches to plan energy renovation of single 
family houses in an economically optimal way. If possible cases would be drawn in to show 
advantages and disadvantages of using the method in real life, and focus will all the time be on 
economic influence. 
This chapter begin with the fundamental issue how to define expenses for energy renovation when 
energy saving improvements are done together with other renovation projects, and when the comfort 
is also increased by the improvements. Different often used formulas will be explained and it is 
assessed what is the best way to use them. The second part will discuss how the decision making 
process is slowed down and how different house owners have different requisite. 
3.1 Determination of expenses for energy renovation 
When people buy a house or a car it is not rational thinking, but when they are asked to invest money 
in energy renovation of their home, the economy become very important and the thoughts are about 
payback time and the increase in value of the house. After the renovation of a house the comfort is 
often improved significant, but how to divide the cost for the renovation into cost for renovation, 
energy renovation and other improvements is still not streamlined. Also the division of the cost for a 
house totally renovated into improvements and energy improvements is difficult, but it is important 
to be able to calculate if a specific improvement should be done according to the calculation of cost-
effectiveness in the Danish building regulation. 
3.1.1 Energy investment 
To say if an energy improvement is cost-effective, it is needed to know the cost of the improvement 
before you can calculate the cost-effectiveness. For new windows or re-insulation the total cost is put 
down to be an energy renovation cost [Energihjem: Tilskud], even that it also result in other 
improvements like thermal comfort and aesthetics. Few single family houses from the 1960s and 
1970s have original ventilation systems, but it could be a consideration to put in a ventilation system 
with a high efficient heat recovery during an extensive energy renovation to fulfill requirements for 
a good indoor climate as is the demand to the new renovation class 1 of the building regulation 2015. 
In a bigger renovation project the total cost will be lower if the needed renovation and the energy 
renovation is done at once, instead of begin spread over years. It is cheaper only to have to establish 
construction site with electricity and facilities for the craftsmen once. It is also cheaper to have the 
craftsmen to do several things at the same engagement than to engage craftsmen for every task. This 
is a cost reduction that is hard to divide into renovation and energy renovation, but it could be tried 
to be divided into two equal parts. To easier convince the house owner to do energy renovation 
together with needed renovation, it could be said, that the cost reduction be assigned to the energy 
renovation. In this case the additional cost for energy renovation connected to the needed renovation 
is the actual amount of money the house owner have to invest more here and now. This marginal 
additional costs can be visualized by asking the craftsman to make an offer without and with extra 
insulation for instance. It could maybe be an encouraging surprise and it will be very clear, that it 
mean lower total investment and less total rebuilding period if the house can be renovated both for 
technical and energy issues at the same time.

3.2 Issues for the building owner in the decision making process 
The house owner analysis 2013 from Bolius show that 3 out of 4 want to renovate their house, but 
some external factors discourage them from doing it [Bolius: Boligejerne energirenoverer ikke]. This 
section will look at the drawbacks in connection to energy renovation seen from the house owner’s 
point of view. For instance exterior insulation increase the gross area of the house and that cause a 
higher taxable value, but the Danish building regulation chapter 1.6 paragraph 1, 1 allow 250 mm 
exterior insulation without counting as an extension of the heated living area [BR10]. It is cheaper 
and easier to do several initiatives at once, but the house owner may not have the money, and maybe 
the family then cannot live in their house for a period. 
3.2.1 Changes in the house 
Insulation is the first and most well-known energy renovation procedure, but it is also a wide scale 
improvement that would affect the daily life of the residents in the house. Insulation on an unutilized 
attic or cavity wall insulation is the less affecting. Interior insulation can be done in one room at a 
time, but it will cause big influence on the different rooms, and the usable area will decrease. Interior 
insulation can be done by the house owner to save money, but he has to be very much aware of the 
moisture barrier. Exterior insulation is often best for the house due to moisture, and it can be done 
with little effect on the residents. By exterior insulation the architectural expression of the house will 
be changed, and therefore it is not always possible to do. Standard single family houses is often of 
lower architectural quality and therefore it is not really a drawback but more an advantage that the 
house will become looking like a new house. Also insulation of the floor could be a possibility and it 
would be easiest to do without residents in the house [Bolius: Planlæg]. It could save money for 
craftsmen to let them have room to maneuver in the house, so they can do their work in their work 
==PAGE:22==
Page 22 of 73 
 
pace with no limitations from which rooms the residents are using. On the other hand it will be an 
additional cost to find somewhere else to live during the renovation period, and it could make the 
daily life for a family complicated if the transportation time is increased for daily activities. 
The energy label provide information about the energy consumption of the building, when the 
building is for sale, but the energy label also serves another purpose. It gives an overview of cost 
effective energy improvements of the building with information about their objective, implementation 
cost and savings on energy bills [Energistyrelsen: Energy labelling]. As mentioned in chapter 2.3.5 
Impact on assessment from energy renovation research show, that the energy labelling has impact on 
the sales price of the house and the length of the sales period, so potential house buyers are using the 
information the energy label provide to decide which house to be their future home. The second 
intention about the energy labelling is not documented to have an influence. It is a good tool for the 
house owner to get inspiration about different improvements and their costs, so the knowledge about 
it will hopefully become more widespread in the future. 
Some house owners have clear expectations to what a change of their house should contain, and the 
expectations are different from person to person. Changes could therefore be divided into need to do 
or nice to do, where for instance a broken roof is in the first group while a new kitchen is in the second 
group. When the house owner has decided to do some changes of the house it could sound boring for 
the house owner that should do cavity wall insulation and insulation of the attic, while another do 
new windows and solar panels on the roof. The wishes for improvements can be different, but it 
should always be the improvements that is best for the house, that are realized, and then aesthetics 
should be secondary. Some house owners prefer solar panels on the roof because it is visible for the 
surroundings. It has become a signal of environmental concerns and a clear signal that here lives a 
family that take responsibility, maybe in spite of two big cars are placed in the driveway. 
Energy renovations can change the use of the space in the single family house and make it feel bigger, 
because the space is used in a new way. For instance Realdania byg in the project Energiparcel 
renovated the single family house on Mejløvænget 9 from 1973. The renovation among other things 
was about changing the floor to ceiling windows and two other windows in the living room. The new 
windows result in less heat loss and therefore less need for heating from the wood stove, for what 
reason it was removed. When the wood stove do not longer take space more square meters are 
available. The new much better windows made it less uncomfortable to stay near the windows, why 
it also feel like the living room has become bigger. A last benefit to mention is the increased air 
quality, both for the family in the house and for the neighbors because the chimney is no longer 
leading smoke to the outside [Realdania byg: Energiparcel]. 
3.2.2 Bad publicity of energy renovations 
It is hard to take into consideration individuals in the planning of the theoretical saving, because the 
preferred comfort is different. Often the energy renovation increase the comfort in the building and 
people want warmer temperatures than before or want to have higher ventilation rate than before 
renovation, so they end up by saving less than expected. People have different expectations to 
building of different state, so they are more likely to accept for instance a colder temperature in an 
old building than in a newly renovated building. When it is published that people do not save as much 
as expected and the investment therefore has a longer payback time, people become more reserved to 
energy renovations, because maybe the budget and expectations for their house will fail too. It is 
==PAGE:23==
Page 23 of 73 
 
always bad publicity when the expectations are not fulfilled, and many good histories are needed to 
counterbalance, but it is not often you hear that the savings are larger than expected. One way to 
reduce the number of cases not ending up as expected, is by basing the calculation more on the 
comfort and habits of the specific family than standards. This would take longer time in the planning 
phase, but it will end up by expectations closer to the reality, if the family is honest to themselves and 
do not has a too ideally conception they cannot hold. 
3.2.3 Too expensive to energy renovate 
New developed solutions tend to be more expensive than widely used standard solutions, and once 
the new solution become standard it become cheaper. This could refrain people from choosing the 
newest and best technologies and instead chose a standard solution, because it is too expensive or it 
could imply that people wait to do a specific investment because they are waiting for the price to 
drop. House owners want to get most value for the money and do not want to pay full price for 
something, if they expect it to be cheaper very shortly. 
House owners are a broad group of people with different economic requisites, and it is therefore 
difficult to lump them all together. Instead house owners can roughly be divided into three groups. 
 High equity house owners (elderly) 
 Technically insolvent house owners and first time buyers (young) 
 Average house owners 
The first group has high equity in the house. It is typical elderly people, maybe some original owners, 
which do not owe anything in the house. The good thing about this group is, that they typical have 
the money to renovate or they can borrow based on the equity. The challenge is, that they are used to 
the house and getting the best out of the given conditions, and also they know, that they will not be 
living in the house for another forty year, so they do not feel kindly towards investments with long 
payback time [Miljøministeriet: Målgruppens identitet]. The next group is contrary, because they do 
not have the money and cannot borrow money. It is typical first time buyers, who borrowed what 
they could afford to buy the house, or technically insolvent house owners, that brought their house 
when the prices was on the highest and therefore now live in a house where they owe more than the 
house is worth. The last group is something in between both in typical age and in the availability to 
money for renovation, since they do have some money saved up in the bank and they are able to 
borrow more money. Their house do not necessary need an immediately renovation but the owner is 
interested in saving energy and increase comfort through renovation. 
Economy is a big topic in the house owner analysis 2014 from Bolius, and it is mentioned as both the 
biggest barrier for not starting to rebuilding, and as the biggest motivation factor to start energy 
renovating. House owners are afraid that it will cost more than they are able to pay, but at the same 
time they want to save money on the energy bills. Bolius find that the behavior can be changed by 
regular energy bills instead of one per year [Boligejeranalyse 2014, 2013]. People can check their 
energy use on the homepage of the utility company, but few use this possibility because it is nerdy 
and time consuming. Energy bills more than once a year could increase the focus because it is 
reminding the house owner about energy use more than nowadays, but it is still just a bill that has to 
be paid. Bills does not give the overview that is really interesting, but it would create little more 
awareness.

Another impact that could make it hard to decide when it is economically optimal to make energy 
renovation of the house is the different subsidy schemes the politicians implement and remove. Here 
an energy advisor could help with guidance. An advisor could seems like an extra expense, but on 
the total budget it will often be a little item, and it may save expenses for incidental expenses 
[Boligejer: Fase 2]. 
Energy renovation cause savings on the heating bill, increase the comfort and is good for the 
environment, which is all very good. However, if the house owner do not have the money to invest 
in energy renovation, the cost of a bank loan should be included in the budget, because maybe he 
cannot afford to incur debts [Bolius: Energirenovering godt i gang]. This could be neglected by 
special loans with low interest rate for energy renovations, which may be a result of the government’s 
vision of more education of the personnel in banks. Especially young first time buyers in the second 
group of house owners would benefit from new types of loans because it could be smart to make 
energy improvements before they really start living in the house. In general when the interest rate is 
low and the energy price is high, it is beneficial to take a loan for energy renovation instead of paying 
high increasing energy bills [Nielsen et al, 2012]. Instead of a big loan it could seems tempting to 
start by small investments, and if the best plan for energy renovation of the house make it possible to 
begin with small investments, it is fine to do. Often this is not the case because bigger investments 
will lead to bigger savings, so the payback time will be shorter for bigger investments, said in other 
word you have to spend money to make money. 
Today several banks offer energy loans typical with an interest rate of 4-6 %, with a shorter loan 
period like 10 years. This is a relatively high interest rate today, but for people that do not have to 
possibility to borrow in the equity it is a good alternative. The banks offer this types of loans because 
there is a general public interest in energy renovations, and the houses tend to increase in value after 
an energy renovation [Energihjem: Finansering]. The bank does not decide which energy 
improvement to be realized, but for some loans, the loan period should be shorter or equal to the 
lifetime of the improvement [Bolius: Energilån]. One reason for the relatively high rates can be that 
the banks does not have any collateral for the loans, while some banks offer lower rates if there is 
collateral for the loan. When the banks borrow money to the house owner without any idea if it is the 
best investment for the house long term, and it could unfortunately end up being a bad investment. If 
a requirement for the energy loans was to invest the money in improvements recommended by an 
impartial advisor, it would help the house owner doing the right for his house and it would be the best 
for the society. 
A problem mostly occurring between the first type of house owners, where the cost of the investment 
is not really the problem, is if investments with a long payback time is planned, and the house owner 
expect to move before that period. He could feel that he only has the expenses but will not experience 
the profit. This is true, but on the other hand a newly renovated house is more attractive on the market, 
and therefore the house owner can expect a higher sales price for a house in better state and probably 
with a better energy label [Boligejer: Fase 1]. Savings on the energy bills and higher sales prices are 
strong arguments why always to do energy renovations even that it seems expensive. Another 
problem typical for this group is the strong connection to the house, because they have lived in the 
house for so many years that it is filled with memories. If the best solution due to energy is to demolish 
the house and build a new one, it can be hard to realize because they look subjective at the house, and 
maybe they feel too old for a new house building process, maybe for the second time of their life.

The average house owners have some money saved up, or they can borrow cheaply in the equity of 
the house, but still it is maybe not enough to cover the costs for a total improvement of the house here

right order. When a house owner has decided that an energy renovation of the house should begin, it

3.2.4 The house owner basis for decision of energy renovation 
It is alone the house owner of the single family house that could take the decision to energy renovate 
the house, and therefore is the house owner a very important participant in the Danish governments 
vision for lower energy use in building [Nielsen et al, 2012]. 
When a house owner begin to consider the possibilities for energy renovation of his house, he is first 
discussing it with his acquaintances [Miljøministeriet: Målgruppens identitet]. It can be hard to 
believe that advisors are totally neutral to the products they recommend, like there has been examples 
where doctors write perceptions to medicine from special companies. When the house owners instead 
start asking family, friends and colleges for experience, he expect to get more honest information, 
and inspiration for which advisor to contact. It is very important, that the house owner do contact 
professional experience in the decision making process, because no wrong choice should be taken 
based on ignorance, since it can be hard to correct later. 
When the house owner has decided to energy renovate the house and is in the process of deciding 
which improvements to realize it seems tempting to renovate the whole house for the available 
money, so the house owner can live in the renovated house, and do not need to go through another 
renovation process. This is not the best possible solution for the house. Instead it is better to do the 
best possible as long as the money goes, and then wait with the rest. For instance it is better to replace 
the windows in the Southern and Eastern facade to the best windows on the market instead of 
choosing okay windows for replacement of all windows in all facades [Realdania byg: Energiparcel]. 
Today standard calculations for how much the house owner will save and the length of the payback 
time is used. To help the house owner relating to his own everyday life, the calculation could be done 
more personal, because it has to be made individually for each house anyway. The information to the 
house owner should be very clear what the conditions in the house is now and how is the expected 
conditions after the energy renovation. Also what are the house use in energy today, and what would 
it use after the energy renovation. 
If the long term plan for the house is to be demolished the investment until then should be kept at a 
minimum for the necessary things. This may not seems like the best solution for the house owner here 
and now, but it is the best for the society. Even that it can be hard to accept continual dissatisfactory 
conditions, it is easier to tolerate the waiting time with temporary solutions while saving money up 
for a very good future house. The necessary improvements should be solved in the cheapest way to 
save up most money possible for the new energy efficient house. It is maybe not technical issues but 
comfort or aesthetics for the house owner. Maybe it is a daily source of irritation that the kitchen 
cabinet door fall off, and then the cabinet doors can be fixed up by screws and paint. It could also be

a source of irritation that the house from outside appears to be in poor condition. This was the case 
on House A [Grøn, 2015], where they thought that the roof could last for some years more, so the

4 Single problem based planning of energy renovation 
This chapter is about the case based planning of energy renovation, where a solution is planned when 
a problem occur. The typical reason for renovation is that something at the house does not function 
as intended or that the house owner just brought a house, where the home condition report outline 
some issues that should be taken into consideration to be repaired immediately or within a few years. 
When a problem occur and have to be solved, other minor maintenance tasks are maybe done 
simultaneous, but one problem is not seen as an occasion to comprehensive improvements of non-
problematic building components for the typical house owner. The chapter also describes a present 
subsidy scheme for single energy improvements given by the utility companies as part of an 
agreement with the government. 
An analysis from 2013 among Danish house owners reveal that the three things the house owners are 
most aware of are general maintenance, damages on the building and energy and energy renovation. 
The house owners see the general maintenance of the building as an ongoing process consisting of 
small repairs, and scarcely every fourth house owner start the maintenance when the problem is 
noticeable, especially people 70+ years old and people with low income choose this approach. Within 
the recent year 78 % of the house owners’ answer, that they had repaired smaller damages and 55 % 
has spent below DKK 10,000, while only 31 % has done bigger maintenance and in average they 
spent DKK 70,000. The three most motivation factors for the house owners to do general maintenance 
is also related to the ongoing renovation of the house: It is a necessity because it will become more 
expensive to repair if they wait, repairing of existing damages owing to abrasion, and increased 
delight of the home created by specific expectations to specific improvements [Boligejeranalyse 
2014]. For the house owners to start energy efficient renovations or house improvements more 
generally two 'trigger points' are discovered. The first one is salient events, which for instance could 
be a boiler breakdown, so the house is hit by a problem that needs to be fixed immediately for the 
house to be comfortable. The second is periods of transition in the household lifecycle, for instance 
having a child, retiring or moving, so the demand and wishes change because of new demands from 
the owners [Wilson et al, 2015]. New house buyers demand their coming house to be energy 
renovated, and if the house is not, then it is used as a reason for price reduction, and then the new 
owners do the energy renovation as soon as possible to benefit from the improvements economical, 
and by better indoor climate and increased comfort [Bolius: Energirenovering glade ejere]. For the 
first time house buyers it is important to remember, that in houses with bad indoor climate an energy 
renovation is not synonymous to a healthier indoor climate [Byggeplads.dk: Råd advarer] but a well-
planned energy renovation can result in better indoor climate. 
Basically the house owner can finance an energy renovation by saving up the money beforehand or 
by borrowing in the bank. It is always a good idea to save money up for incidental expenses, but 
problems occur indecently on how much money the house owner has saved up. If there is a cheap 
good solution the house owner can afford and a more expensive very good solution, where he have 
to borrow the extra money, the first solution may sounds best from an economic point of view. The 
less money available for the house owner, the more short-term he has to invest, even that it can be 
expensive on the long run. It is expensive to be poor and it end up being like peering in your pants to 
keep warm [Nøjsomme løsninger: Skift fokus]. Many single family houses are in a condition where 
the roof and the windows are about to be changed, so it is easy and important to insulate and choose 
good solutions, cause else the opportunity will not occur before in 30-50 years, when the building 
==PAGE:28==
Page 28 of 73 
 
component again have to be changed. The extra cost for energy efficient building materials is modest, 
so it is a shame both for the house owner and the environment, if energy optimization is not done 
during renovation. The political goals will never become reality if the private house owners are not 
involved [Dansk Byggeri: Husejere lukker øjnene]. One way the house owners are forced in the 
direction of choosing good solutions is by the building code and the recently introduced demands for 
building components changed or maintenance. When the buildings are energy optimized, the top five 
over most implemented initiatives show, that it is easy and typical known worn building components 
that are changed: Energy windows (17 %), door (13 %), replacing of old thermostats on radiators (11 
%), insulated roof (10 %) and replacing of boiler (9 %). Among the Danish house owners three out 
of four is interested in energy improvements, but for many people it remain an interest and is never 
realized, mainly because of the cost [Bolius: Ikke uden tilskud]. 
4.1 Short-term campaigns and subsidy for single initiatives 
Short-duration national campaigns with changing focus has over the last years forced people to use 
less energy in their homes. For instance the Go'Energi campaign in 2011, that made people aware of 
measuring the energy, on/off sockets to avoid standby power consumption, low energy light bulbs 
after the incandescent bulb was made illegal and also the possibility for energy renovations. Also the 
green property tax is a way for the government to force people to behave more environmental friendly, 
since the tax is paid according to the use of electricity, water and heat. The artisan deduction was a 
briefly arrangement from the government for among others to have more jobs in the building industry, 
so if the house owner hired professionals for a renovation on his house, he got a tax deduction. 
Common for all initiatives is, that it is up to the house owner to decide what part of the house he want 
to renovate or seek further professional guidance somewhere. The improvements are based on savings 
here and now, with no comprehensive professional opinion, so it is tempting to make the biggest 
saving for the smallest effort, and a low payback time also sounds attractive and like a good argument 
for the investment in the bank. A current subsidy scheme is an agreement between the government 
and the utility companies, where the companies are committed to give subsidy for energy saving 
improvement, and this will be described further. 
4.1.1 Subsidy scheme from the utility companies for energy saving initiatives 
An agreement between the government and the energy companies obligate the energy companies to 
participate actively in the reduction of the Danish CO2 emission level, and that can result in economic 
benefits for the house owners. The energy companies should enter energy savings in their accounts 
to the public authorities, so they are interested in buying energy savings caused by energy renovations. 
The companies set their own subsidy tariffs, but the house owners can choose freely which company 
to apply at [Nybolig: Hent ekstra tilskud]. The scheme was agreed the 13th of November 2012 and so 
far it will stand until 2020, and it include both subsidy for private and for businesses 
[Energisparesiden: Hjælp til energibesparelser]. The subsidy can vary from company to company but 
also depend on the type of energy improvement. Common for all companies is, that they calculate the 
subsidy depending on the saved kWh after the first year. It is not the actual saving in each case 
individually but a standard saving, which is calculated for a lot of different kinds of improvements 
and can be found in a list [Standardværdikatalog for energibesparelser]. It is only allowed to apply 
for subsidy for one energy renovation at one company, but the house owner can chose whatever 
company he find best and most beneficial. The energy companies notify the subsidy to the Danish 
Energy Agency, so no subsidy is paid out in a wrong basis [ProjectZero: Tilskud]. 
==PAGE:29==
Page 29 of 73 
 
For the house owner to be qualified for subsidy he have to follow a specific procedure. The house 
owner start by invite offers from craftsmen on the renovation. Before the owner chose the craftsman 
and accept the offer, he should apply for subsidy from the chosen company online. When the company 
has approved the application, the house owner can accept the craftsman and the renovation can begin. 
In the end the invoice from the craftsman should be uploaded to the energy company, and if 
everything is in order, the money will be transferred to the NemKomto of the house owner 
[ProjectZero: Tilskud]. It is very important that the house owner get the approval from the energy 
company before the work on the house has begun, because the subsidy cannot be paid as additional 
grant. There exist websites that list the different companies’ different subsidies, so the house owner 
can compare. Typical the subsidy is around 30 øre per saved kWh per year [Energihjem: Liste over 
energiselskaber]. 
In 2014 26 % of the savings reported by the Danish utility companies was from households, but 
compared to the energy consumption the households is actually the end-user group with the smallest 
energy savings. The reason is, that the utility companies find it easier and more cost-effective to find 
energy savings in the production businesses, for instance because the saving potential of each 
improvement is often bigger than in the households. The trend is, that the companies realize most 
savings within their own type of energy, and more than half of the energy savings is realized by 
improvements in the building envelope, boilers and process equipment [Energistyrelsen: Status 
energispareindsats, 2015]. The expenses the utility companies has used for the energy saving effort 
show, that they so far has chosen the type of projects with the lowest expenses, and it indicate that 
savings in the households is still too expensive. The end-users, who had been involved in energy 
saving projects, is in general very pleased. However the scheme is still not totally marketed, and it 
appear opaque for many end-users, so there is not a broad knowledge about it among the end-users 
[Evaluering energispareindsats, 2015]. 
4.2 Evaluation of single problem planned energy renovation 
To reach the goal about Denmark being fossil free in 2050 it is important that the existing buildings 
are optimized. On a general level it seems immaterial which energy improvements are done, as long 
as something is done it is a step in the right direction and good for the environment. However, to do 
the optimal solution in the energy renovation process it is not immaterial in which order and on which 
basis the decision is taken. The single problem based approach to energy renovation has its pros and 
cons, and it will here be evaluated. 
When components are not replaced before they breaks the full lifetime is made use of, and 
components are not discarded when they still work as intended, so there is less waste of materials. It 
is expensive for the environment to dispose old materials and create new materials, so it is important 
not to discard well-function components, but instead do little ongoing maintenance so components 
will last longer and then become recycled. 
The intention of using the building components until they break and not replace components there is 
nothing wrong with can be problematic if maintenance check and maintenance are not done. For 
instance when part of the building envelope break, there is a risk that moisture will penetrate and 
damage the construction or insulation. Then the renovation will become more expensive because it is 
both the broken building part and the consequential damaged building part that has to be replaced. 
==PAGE:30==
Page 30 of 73 
 
Knowledge about energy optimization become better and so do the materials, and as the time goes on 
the good solutions become cheaper, because they become standard. The newest and often most 
effective solution will typical be more expensive than the acceptable solution. The house owner has 
to take the choice if it should be the acceptable or the best solution he is using to improve his house 
and his economic situation will often have impact on the choice. The extra cost for 350 mm insulation 
instead of 200 mm is maybe DKK 10,000 here and now, but is will result in larger energy savings, 
and therefore the money will be earned back [Rockwool: Energirenovering der betaler sig]. The best 
thing to do is always to choose the best solution but in an immediate situation the savings of the house 
owner is maybe not enough to cover the cost, and then it should be a strong argument in the bank that 
the loan is used to improve the house by the best solution, and it would be the best in the long run. 
4.2.1 Improvements of the approach 
Without totally changing the approach of single problems based planning of energy renovations 
smaller initiatives could make big changes. When a problem occur on a building component it should 
be weight up if it is just a problem at that spot or if it is the whole building component that is worn 
up. The easiest and cheapest here and now would be to repair for instance the roof at the specific spot 
where there is a problem, but if it is an old roof the next breakage would maybe occur in half a year, 
so it would be time and cost-saving to do the replacement. 
In the single problem based energy renovation a counselor is not often asked to give an opinion on 
the house, and therefore the solution remain focusing only on the broken building component and not 
seeing it as an opportunity to have a holistic view on the building. A handyman would maybe chose 
the approach because it then is possible for himself to do most of the work, and then professionals 
rarely consult the house. When a craftsman is hired for a task, he can tell the house owner if there are 
other things that have to be done or would be a good idea to do soon. This could be thought of as the 
craftsman trying to earn more money, but it would be good for the house, and maybe the owner can 
save some money and difficulty by doing more tasks at once. 
When a building component is replaced it is not allowed to replace it by a similar solution because 
the building code have demands so the new building component should be more energy effective. 
Then renovation become energy renovation even that it was not the focus point of the house owner 
when he had to change a problematic component. It is a way to force private house owners to help 
reach the goal of 2050, but it is very important that everyone is aware of the rules and that the 
craftsmen as the professionals stop the house owner from planning something not following the rules. 
4.2.2 Comments on the subsidy scheme 
From the point of view of the house owner the ideas behind the subsidy scheme is good. The house 
owner can decide the content of the project, he can chose what he think is the best offer, and he know 
the value of the subsidy before he pay the bills. From a social point of view the scheme is not that 
considered since there is no demands for professional guidance, so the house owner can decide 
different projects for his house, that is not necessary considered and the best solution long-term. 
The whole process of getting subsidy may sounds comprehensive for private house owners, but this 
is not the only reason why the subsidy scheme is not used widespread. The households only know a 
little about the subsidy scheme from the utility companies, and the scheme is not really promoted for 
owners of single family houses. Many house owners are therefore not aware, that they can get subsidy 
when they realize an energy saving initiative, even though the scheme has operated for years. 
==PAGE:31==
Page 31 of 73 
 
Compared to the energy saving, it is expensive for the utility companies to guide households instead 
of businesses, because the businesses are bigger than the households, and therefore the saving 
potential is bigger, so it is obvious that the companies chose the biggest result for the smallest effort. 
However, there is a national initiative in form of a website energisparesiden.dk to inform the 
consumers, but it is not really known by the end-users, and compared to other consumer information 
websites the number of visitors is relatively low. Since all utility companies did not reach the latest 
goals, it was maybe worth to make an effort and relaunch the website by a national campaign, so the 
population is made more aware of the information on the website, and the companies can share the 
costs of the campaign. 
An initiative that maybe would expand the knowledge of the scheme is the BedreBolig solution, 
which will be described further in chapter 5. The subsidy is taken into account in the budget 
[BedreBolig plan: Fuglebjerg], so the house owner having the BedreBolig project done become aware 
of the subsidy. Then he can mention the scheme, when people from his acquaintances talk about 
energy saving initiatives, because people find information most reliable, when they hear good 
experiences from people they know. 
The whole idea about the subsidy scheme from the utility companies is to realize the most economic 
energy savings, but it is not the optimal procedure to reach the extensive goal of a fossil free society. 
The scheme support short term solutions, where the initiative with the biggest saving and subsidy is 
chosen, but at a time single initiatives will no longer have good economy, and then the improvements 
will stop. To reach an extensive goal it is therefore necessary to look more long time and combine 
initiatives, where the economy is not the only essential.

5 Combined planned energy renovation 
This chapter is about how energy improvements can be planned to be implemented in a combined 
energy renovation, so the home would increase in value for the house owner and it is an attractive 
process to start renovation. The combined planned energy renovation will often look holistic and 
long-term at the building and improvements and economy will be considered before it is started. 
Together with the plan from the Danish government launched in May 2014, a new arrangement was 
introduced with the BedreBolig (Better Housing) solution. It started to be tested in nine municipalities 
and is now spread nationally, and this chapter will describe and evaluate the concept of BedreBolig. 
5.1 Long-term planning 
One simple way of starting long-term planning is if the house owner consider which changes and 
improvements he would like on the house in the coming years. The he can hire a professional to 
examine the house to find out if something is critical and has need to be done as the first thing, but if 
the house owner just brought the house, he can just look in the home condition report. A more overall 
long-term plan is the heat supply solution renewable energy plan 2035, which will be described 
further. 
Brick houses build in 1960-1980 could need the brickwork to be repointed, and it is a very time 
consuming work for a bricklayer. Also the insulation thickness is very limited compared to today's 
standards, so exterior insulation would be a good initiative to reduce the heating demand of the house. 
Instead of using time and money to seal the old brickwork, it could be a standard solution to put up 
insulation and build a new exterior facing wall. Lifetime is a central issue in long-term planning, for 
instance in case of exterior insulation. Exterior insulation with a new membrane only lasts for 20-40 
years while a brick facade lasts for centuries. At the same time it is energy consuming to produce 
building materials, so in a lifecycle assessment the environmental benefit become negative if the 
lifetime is short. The demand for airtightness of buildings was introduced in the building code in the 
1960s, so in the 1970s and 1980s carpenters used membranes in wooden buildings. Twenty years 
later the same houses needed repair for rot damages, so it was a good business for the building 
industry but not for the environment [Realdania GI: Kortsigtet energirenovering er dårlig].The 
mindset to overcome is the conflict between the short-term profit by a low investment, and the long-
term profit by investment in lowering the operation expenses [Nøjsomme løsninger: Skift fokus]. If 
the heating source in a single family house is an old oil-fired boiler, it could seem like the best 
investment to install or connect to a new heating source, and that would result in substantial savings 
immediately. Instead of finding a more effective heating source the heating demand could be lowered 
beforehand, so the heating source be dimensioned to the good conditions of the house, and result in 
further savings. 
When a building renovation is of high technical, economic and aesthetic quality the building become 
prettier, the lifetime extends and the running and maintenance costs for reduces, so the building 
become more desired. At the same time it is important to remember, that the value of the house can 
decrease if a renovation is done without thoughts about what will happen for the house on longer term 
and without considerations about the existing qualities of the house [#renoverdk: Bygningsmassen 
fortjener bedre]. 
==PAGE:33==
Page 33 of 73 
 
Long-term schemes running for years have a more positive effect than short-term schemes [SBi: Ny 
viden]. That knowledge is important for the politicians to remember when they establish and abolish 
energy renovation schemes, because long-lived schemes will be the best for the society. 
5.1.1 Heat supply solution renewable energy plan 2035 
The long-term plan for the Danish energy supply is, that it should be 100 % based on renewable 
energy in 2050 [Energistyrelsen: Energiaftalen], and milestones are planned to help and measure the 
development. Some of the milestones affect the single family houses directly and others indirectly. 
The first one in 2020 does not affect the house owners since it is the goal of the government, that half 
of the traditional electricity use is covered by wind power. The next milestone in 2030 cannot be 
fulfilled without an effort from the private house owners. Coal should be phased out from the Danish 
power plants and all oil-fired boilers should be replaced by renewable sorts of heating. The last 
milestone in 2035 is about the electricity and heat supply, which should be covered by renewable 
energy. The house owners are not affected directly, because it is a responsibility of the utility 
companies that they deliver electricity and heat based on renewable energy to the customers 
[Energistyrelsen: Vores energi]. 
5.2 BedreBolig solution 
The BedreBolig solution is a political incentive to educate counselors, who can give a standardized 
overview of the current and coming need for renovation of a specific house, both to improve the 
energy use of the house, and to increase comfort for the residents. The development of the solution 
started in the spring 2013 [Energistyrelsen: Udvikling]. To support the development and secure that 
the building industry feel ownership of the BedreBolig solution, it is based on existing initiatives and 
experience of market operators. 
The Danish solution is inspired from the Green Deal scheme in the United Kingdom, which was 
launched in October 2012 by the UK Government. The hope is to reduce the carbon emission in the 
UK by 80 % be 2050 compared to the level in 1990 [UK Power: What is Green Deal]. The Green 
Deal is helping the owner to do energy-saving improvements in his house and to find the best way to 
finance the improvements. Companies must be authorized to offer Green Deal. The typical 
improvements can include insulation, draught-proofing and renewable energy generation. One 
initiative that is not included in the Danish scheme is governmental economic support, in UK called 
The Green Deal Home Improvement Fund, which has different amount of grant depending on which 
improvement and when the improvement is made [UK Government: Green Deal]. 
The solution have two phases, the first one is called One stop shop and means that all kind of 
information about energy renovations should be found through one well-known entrance. Then the 
house owners are never unsure where to start research no matter what kind of renovation they want 
to do. The second phase is about ensuring financing of energy renovation projects, so the cost of the 
renovation compared to the savings of the house owner is not the crucial [Energistyrelsen: 
BedreBolig]. Besides the two phases the important thing about the BedreBolig solution is, that it is a 
total package, where the consultant follow the house owner from the first ideas to the completed 
renovation project and the guidance include technical issues, financing and practical issues in the 
construction process [Energistyrelsen: BedreBolig]. 
For the house owner, the BedreBolig process is divided into five steps [Energistyrelsen: BedreBolig 
Boligejer]: 
==PAGE:34==
Page 34 of 73 
 
1. Find a counselor: On the website BedreBolig.dk there is a list with contact information to 
counselors authorized by the Danish Energy Agency, who can be contacted for a no-obligation 
proposition. 
2. Preliminary dialogue: Conversation about the house owners plans and wishes for the house, 
and the chosen counselor tell relevant information for the owner about the BedreBolig solution 
including the cost of the service. 
3. Preparation of plan: The counselor outline the house and suggest how to renovate in an energy 
efficient way. The house owner and the counselor prioritize and chose the initiatives that best 
fulfill the wishes and economic situation of the owner. Then the plan for the renovation is 
gathered to get the full overview of background, advantages and economy for each initiative. 
4. Help for construction management: The counselor help project, invite tenders and manage the 
construction process. 
5. End of the renovation: The counselor check the house to see if the work is accomplished as 
agreed. As an acquisition the counselor can follow-up on the renovation after one year, to see 
if the renovation is satisfying for the house owner. 
As part of step 3 the counselor make a budget for the house owner to bring to the bank [Bolius: 
BedreBolig], so the bank advisor has profound information to base the decision about a loan for 
renovation to the house owner. 
The BedreBolig solution is market dependent, so the price for the service is set by each counselor and 
agreed by the house owner before the renovation plan is composed [Bolius: BedreBolig]. 
5.2.1 Testing of the BedreBolig concept 
Before spreading the concept of BedreBolig nationally nine municipalities was chosen to test the 
concept in the first half of 2014 [Energistyrelsen: BedreBolig Kommuner], and to evaluate telephone 
interviews were made in week 19 and 22 2014. The main conclusions from the municipalities was, 
that the sparring to the Danish Energy Agency was very valuable, and that they see the solution as 
the best on the market [Larsen and Baltzer-Andersen, 2014]. However the interviews was made when 
the municipalities was still trying to implement the solution, so the real value of the solution is still 
not to conclude. 
Some municipalities saw the nonexistent economic support as a challenge and do not think all citizens 
are willing to pay for the renovation plan from authorized counselor. They see the willingness to pay 
together with the importance of creating interest as the biggest challenges for the BedreBolig solution 
to become a success [Larsen and Baltzer-Andersen, 2014]. 
In the Green Deal it is possible to finance the energy saving improvements by a loan that follow the 
house and not the owner by being paid back over the electricity bill. The possibility had not caused 
much interest, and only few house owners has made use of it, but it has result in expenses for the 
government. Copenhagen Economics has analyzed the scheme and conclude that it would have low 
effect to implement it in Denmark, but the Danish utilities by the energy saving obligations already 
fulfill some of the same goals. The difference is, that the standard of buildings and the loan terns for 
many house owners is better in Denmark than in the UK [Energistyrelsen: Analyse af engelsk 
ordning]. 
==PAGE:35==
Page 35 of 73 
 
Based on the evaluation some advises was made for the national roll-out, where sharing of knowledge 
is the most important. The test municipalities already have some good and bad experience, which 
could be inspiration for others and help them not making the same mistakes. The sharing could be 
through official templates for presentations, invitations and agendas, and examples of business cases 
targeted for different target groups, so it is clear what is in it for them [Larsen and Baltzer-Andersen, 
2014].

The national campaign to launch the new renovation concept 
was started 13th October 2014 [Energistyrelsen: BedreBolig 
lancering] by a 15 million advertising campaign [Larsen and 
Baltzer-Andersen, 2014]. It is the intention that the 
campaign will run until 2016, and it covers commercials on 
TV and internet and the new webpage BedreBolig.dk

point in the marketing is, that it is targeted renovation ready house owners, and that the starting point

way [Energistyrelsen: BedreBolig markedsføring]. To be easy to recognize, a logo and a slogan is

5.2.3 Demands for BedreBolig counselors 
With the new solution a new education was introduced to certificate counselors. Before craftsmen 
could become energy advisors by three days of teaching in building installations or building envelope, 
depending on their existing education [Teknologisk Institut: Energivejleder], but it was not an 
authorized education. The new education apply to professionals e.g. architects, engineers, 
construction managers or craftsmen, and it is the company that get the authorization, when at least 
one employee have fulfilled the education. The list of authorized counselors contain 238 different 
companies and most are smaller companies with one counselor employed [Energistyrelsen: 
BedreBolig Find rådgiver]. It is different companies from architects to carpenters, so the whole target 
group is reached as intended. Craftsmen stand out with their vocational education so it is a special 
requirement, that they have some relevant continuing education e.g. the energy advisor [VEB: 
Godkendelsesordning]. 
The goal about the education is to be able to give house owners holistic counseling about energy 
efficiency improvements in connection with renovation based on the wishes of the house owner. The 
counselor should listen to the house owner, and the suggested solution should be decided together 
with the family, where the counselor supplement with his knowledge about constructional issues and 
demands in the Danish building code. The concept is for energy renovation ready house owners, so 
the typical client will be families with surplus or families that just brought a derelict house, so what 
they search for in a counselor is qualified guidance [Mortensen and Ditlefsen, 13.04.2015].

From the beginning of the introduction of the concept the education has been for free, but the last

So far the education has been divided into two phases: the planning and the project, where the 
counselor could chose to accomplish the first or both phases. It there is no counselors in a company

two, else the company cannot be authorized. The first phase contain the following parts: 
1. Introduction course (7.5 hours): Introduction to BedreBolig rules and the calculation program

2. Three independent courses in building envelope, installations and renewable energy systems

3. Practical course (3.5 hours): Registration of a building including using the software Energy10. 
4. Practical test (0.75 hours): Simulating of a real work situation with meeting, registration and 
planning of a renovation of a test building. 
After passing the first phase the firm of the counselor can get authorization, and the counselor is 
allowed to plan renovation projects using the BedreBolig name and method. If the counselor should 
also be allowed to help the house owner realize the project, the second phase has to be passed. It 
consist of an access course, an optional course by physical presence or online and it end up with a 
prescribed online test to pass. 
The education that is accomplished until now has reached the broad target group and the education is 
broad constructed because the different professions have different basis. Anyway, the drop-out rate 
has been huge, with 448 signed up for the education but only 246 has graduated the BedreBolig 
counselor education. Reasons for the drop-out rate is referred to be  general busyness in the industry, 
so some participants did not have time to complete the education and the non-existing user fee, maybe 
result in less commitment [Munkholm, 27.04.2015]. 
The education has shown to be mostly for engineers and constructors because the counselor needs to 
be holistic and know a lot of constructional issues to give the best guidance. One front where 
craftsmen has an advantage is in relation the house owner, where craftsmen normally has lot of 
experience in listen and fulfilling the needs of the individual house owner, but where the energy 
labelling consultant is used to walk around alone in the house because the owner is going to move 
[Mortensen and Ditlefsen, 13.04.2015]. 
To make the guidance there is demands from the Danish Energy Agency about an approved and 
certified quality system so the counseling is standardized and it always fulfill quality demands 
[Energistyrelsen: BedreBolig Rådgiver]. For doing the information conversation with the family and 
the screening of the house there are some checklists. For making the prioritized plan of the renovation 
of the house a special calculation program called Energy10 is used. It is the same program that is used 
as calculation program for energy efficiency label scheme. The program has built in guesstimate for

improvements except from kitchens, where a sum from a specific offer should be typed into the 
program. Energy10 contain a database of almost all district heating plants in Denmark, and the 
database is updated every year. The sum calculated as subsidy from energy companies is set to be 
DKK 0.25/saved kWh, which is a conservative estimate. The program is helping the counselor by 
help text, self-regulation and validation, so the plan for renovation is guaranteed in good quality 
[Brugermanual Energy10]. Energy10 is introduced already at the first course, and it is recommended

the behavioral guidance, that would help getting the best comfort and economy after the renovation 
[Mortensen and Ditlefsen, 13.04.2015]. If necessary the plan the counselor make, should be taken to 
the bank as basis for financing of the renovation. Therefore, it is important that the plan is prepared 
thoroughly and appear as the result of a professional and competent guidance, because it is the 
collateral for the bank that the technical assessments are done by Danish Energy Agency certified 
counselors [BedreBolig-rådgivning]. 
5.3 BedreBolig cases 
The solution has not been on the market for long, but here two cases will be described from September 
2014, where authorized counselors made plans for the renovations and guided the house owner 
through the process. It is both single family houses, the first on in Fuglebjerg is built in 1954, while 
the second one in Virum is from 1972, but it is very different initiatives and budgets. The written 
advises from the counselors to the house owners are 11-15 pages, and in easily read language, so the 
advices is communicated clear to the house owner.

5.4 Evaluation of the BedreBolig solution 
The solution is the first of its kind in Denmark, and it make it easier for the house owner to start an 
energy renovation of the house, because information is found at one place, so it should no longer be 
a reason for not renovate, that the house owner do not know where to start seeking help. According 
to the two phases it seems like the first One stop shop about one well-known entrance for energy 
renovations is becoming well-known after the TV commercials which very kept simple. Also the logo 
is easy to recognize. The second phase is an important part of the written advises from the counselors, 
where it for each improvement is pointed out the cost and the expected yearly saving of energy. 
5.4.1 Huge drop-out rate on the free education 
It is a pity that there on the past free education was a huge drop-out rate of 45 %, because it means, 
that there is not as many authorized counselors as expected. The courses was filled up very fast, so 
the persons who dropped-out has probably taken places from other interested in the education. It is 
planned that the next version of the education will have a fee, so the drop-out rate will probably be 
lower, but on the other hand, it may prevent smaller companies from participating because of the cost. 
5.4.2 The role of the counselor 
The new education to become counselor is more thorough than the earlier energy advisors, which 
enable the counselors to guide the house owners wider, and therefore result in better energy 
renovation projects. The authorized counselors can be found in a list on the webpage BedreBolig.dk, 
which is a precaution for the house owner, so he can trust, that he get the right guidance. Compared 
to the energy labelling scheme, which also suggest coming maintenance, it is an advantage that the 
counselor follow the renovation from planning to realization, and the counselor is involved with the 
residents. 
The counselor can be hired for different tasks depending on the wishes of house owner, needs and 
budget, which at first can seems like a good thing. It can be found disappointing, that the house owner 
can decide not to have the whole house examined by the counselor, since best decisions for a house 
is done when knowing the house, and important things can be neglected because the house owner is 
not aware of it, and the counselor is asked only to look at other specific parts of the house. 
5.4.3 The BedreBolig plan 
Every house should be analyzed individually from the wishes of the owner. This is an advantage 
because the energy renovation project become optimized for the current situation. It is a dis-advantage 
because benefits of standardized processes and solutions, which would save time and money, is not 
really possible to use widespread. The prioritized plan of the renovation should be made together with 
the house owner, which is not necessary the best guidance. It could be an improvement, that the 
counselor listen to the wishes of the house owner, then examine the whole house and come up with 
the best budget for improvements with basis in energy optimization of the house. 
5.4.4 Disappointing dissemination of the solution so far 
In the beginning of April 2015 there was made around 300 BedreBolig plans, and most of them was 
made since the national campaign started in mid-October. It is too early to say how big a percentage

improvements there is only a cost to show. According to the case of the house in Virum, it was very 
important for the house owner that the cost of the renovation resulted in an increase in the market

of energy improvements of the house.

6 Replacement or Extensive renovation 
This chapter is about when it is economical optimal to renovate an elderly single family house or how 
to decide if it would be cheaper to demolish the existing house and build a new house that would 
easier use less energy, which is an increasing trend. In the end of this chapter a case will be described, 
where a house was renovated for a budget slightly less than a replacement would have cost, and the 
process about replacement or extensive renovation will be evaluated. 
Replacement or extensive renovation is more obvious to consider in elderly houses where only the 
most important maintenance has been done than in houses were lots of ongoing maintenance has 
improved the house. It is expensive to maintain but it is more expensive not to, and maintenance 
backlog has large economic consequences [Dansk Byggeri: Renovering eller nybyg]. It is typically 
when the house need expensive renovation like roof, kitchens and bathrooms, that the thought about 
new build occur [Finans: Populært at rive ned og bygge nyt]. 
There is a tradition that buildings as a rule should be preserved, because buildings lasts for many 
years, even that they are no longer up-to-date. Every year it cost billions for the society that the 
maintenance or energy savings are put off, and that is just the visible costs. The invisible costs is that 
the buildings over the years become inexpedient, because demands and needs are changing [Dansk 
Byggeri: Renovering eller nybyg], and over the years the difference between the existing house and 
a new house become bigger. 
To take the optimal decision about replacement or renovation the overall economy should be 
considered for both cases, so the decision is made on an informed basis, taken into account both the 
short-term and long-term costs. It is necessary to look at each building and guide the house owner 
independently, and it is not just about how much money to spend but also how they are spend. Short-
term budgets can be the reason why it cannot be afford to build a new house even that this solution 
would be the best long-term. The analysis of the overall economy should secure the most beneficial 
solution within the lifetime of the building, but it will take time to implement the consideration in the 
decision making process because habits have to be changed. Since the 1th of July 2013 it has been 
law for the municipalities that overall economy should be considered for new buildings and 
renovations project above DKK 20,000,000 [Dansk Byggeri: Renovering eller nybyg], so it is one 
way be start using the analysis and change habits. 
There are no doubt that a new house is cheaper to live in, because it need less maintenance and use 
less energy, but to buy a totally renovated house is often cheaper than to buy an old house, demolish 
it and build a new house. If the house owner can do some of the work himself, it can instead be 
cheaper to buy a derelict property and renovate. It is not just the economy that is important, also the 
history, architecture and aesthetics play a role in the decision making process [Bolius: Købe gammelt 
hus eller bygge nyt?]. 
Another consideration is if the money used on the renovation will be equal to an increased value of 
the house. In the Greater Copenhagen area there is a big chance that the value of the house will 
increase by a million if there is spend a million on the renovation, but it will be a much smaller 
increase, if the house is located for instance on Lolland. On the other hand, a newly built house will 
always be sold to a price above the average of the area [Bolius: Købe gammelt hus eller bygge nyt?], 
but it is still not definite that the cost of building a new house will pay off by a sale.

6.1 Replacement 
In 2010 1,111 Danish single family houses was demolished, it is the same as three houses demolished 
every single day all year. Most of the houses was from the 1960s but there were also a great deal from 
the 1950s. Some of the houses was sacrificed for the state demolition fund for peripheral areas, but it 
was mostly in the metropolitan areas houses were demolished. It had become so widespread that 
every third house the type house company HusCompaniet, which is one of the largest type house 
companies in Denmark, build on Zealand replace a demolished house. In many cases it is less 
expensive to build a new low energy house than to renovate the existing house and try to up-date it 
to the demands of today. In many cases the house owner can save DKK 2,000-3,000 per month on 
heating, and on the same time he should not use time and money on renovation.  
There is two types of house owners that often chose the replacement solution, the first one is house 
buyers, and the second is high equity house owners [Ingeniøren: Villaer ofres]. The low interest rate 
means, that it is not related to large additional costs to lend an extra million to build a new house, 
compared to the cost of a total renovation, and the energy saving can pay for the higher costs of the 
loan [Finans: Populært at rive ned og bygge nyt]. 
6.2 Realdania Energiparcel Project the Prototype house case 
The single family house from 1974 of 176 m2 was renovated for a budget of DKK 2,000,000, which 
were set to be the sum slightly less than it would cost to demolish the existing house and build a new 
house. This section will be about how far it was possible to improve the old house, and if it would be 
better to add a little extra money to get a brand new house build for today's demands. 
The aim of the project was to show, how much a single family house from the 1970s could be 
improved, when going close to the limit of cost-effectiveness. Lower energy costs, better health and 
higher quality of life were also aims of the project the house should end up fulfilling. The reason why 
the Realdania Byg project Energiparcel, as the house on Langøvænget 8 is a part of, is about houses 
from the 1970s is because houses were built quickly in the period, without many thoughts for energy 
savings and the houses were rather uniform in design. Since there is also many of the type of houses, 
they are obvious for an example-based energy saving project. To have comparable and realistic data 
of the house before and after the renovation, a test family lived in the house for a three-year period 
[Realdania Byg: Summary in English]. The energy renovation of the house was so comprehensive 
that the family could not live in the house, but to follow the process closely and not have to find 
temporary accommodation, they untraditionally lived in a trailer in the garden [Realea A/S, 2010]. 
The project merge energy and architectural thoughts. This was important because the houses in the 
neighborhood are very similar, and therefore the form and the expression of the house was important 
to preserve, so the house was not alienated from the other houses in the neighborhood. One example 
of the merge of energy and architecture it that the existing front wall was removed. The front wall did 
not carry loads or insulate considerable, so for the house not to have very thick walls, it was more 
optimal to make a new wall system with effective insulation. 350 mm insulation was added under the 
roof, so the trusses was made higher, and seen from an architectural point of view this was important 
because roof and the walls increase uniformly, so compared to the neighborhood the house keep the 
form and expression. The facade cladding is kept in the same material, but with an updated expression 
in form of thin screen bricks. Other distinctive changes in the layout of the house is the relocation of 
the stair. Now the stair is like a room divider in the living room, where part of the ceiling is removed, 
==PAGE:44==
Page 44 of 73 
 
so the living room is a double height room. Because the stair is moved the hall became bigger and a 
shower was added to the extra toilet [Realea A/S, 2010], so the house now have two bathrooms like 
many families wants. 
Airtightness is important in new houses to keep the heat loss low. The airtightness was also important 
in the work with the new facades, so tape was used almost as much as traditional tools. To have the 
work done precisely, the new elements for the facade wall was prefabricated in the carpenter's 
workshop, and because of the weight of the elements the insulation was placed after montage [Realea 
A/S, 2010]. 
Daylight was also important to consider in the renovation process, and basis for several changes of 
the house. The new three layer super low energy windows are placed optimal for the passive solar 
heating in spring and autumn, and the balcony in the Southern gable provide shade from the sun in 
the summer. To have better daylight conditions skylights are added to the roof [Realea A/S, 2010]. 
Since the energy renovated house is more airtight, new demands for air change occur, and an 
automatic ventilation system with heat recovery is installed. The ventilation system provide a stable 
air change, but the heat will stay in the house and therefore the heating demand is decreased. When 
the outdoor temperature is right, the exhaust device of the ventilation system turn off, and instead the 
skylight in the double height living room opens, and fresh air is secured without pumps. The new 
pumps and automatic operation increase the electricity use, but new photovoltaic on the roof produce 
electricity [Realea A/S, 2010]. 
The total building envelope was energy renovated, but not against the ground because of shortage of 
money, so the second best solution was chosen by insulation of the footing. [Realea A/S, 2010]. 
The house was only measured for half a year before the renovation began, so the energy of the house 
taken into consideration the behavior of the actual family has to be assumed to have data for one 
whole year. The energy consumptions was also calculated in the program BE06. Before the energy 
renovation it was calculated to be 197.2 kWh/m2 per year, and after the renovation it was calculated 
to be 40.9 kWh/m2 per year [Realdania Byg: Summary in English]. This is equal to a calculated 
energy saving of 78 % and DKK 16,000 per year [Realdania Byg: Langøvænget 8]. The calculated 
energy consumption meet the low energy class 1 from the building regulation 2008, which was the 
valid building regulation and a goal of the project [Realea A/S]. In addition there is significant side 
benefits in form of better indoor climate, larger usable floor area and higher everyday comfort level 
[Realdania Byg: Summary in English]. 
6.3 Evaluation of replacement or extensive renovation 
The reason why replacement versus extensive renovation should be considered when looking at single 
family houses from the period 1960-1980 is because they are not ascribed to any important value to 
preserve. Many houses were built within a short period so aesthetics and materials were not always 
the best, and therefore the houses are not categories worth preserving like older or special houses 
could be. 
6.3.1 Type house companies giving advises 
It is important to assess the message and the consignor behind, especially when there are large costs 
related to follow the advice or not. Some Danish type house companies are advocates of demolish the 
existing house and build a new house. They promote the approach of demolishing the old house and 
==PAGE:45==
Page 45 of 73 
 
build a new one for being cost-effective, because the house owner save time and money on 
maintenance and energy bills. One company also tells that the process can be within five months 
[HusCompaniet: Riv ned og byg nyt], and it is not long time compared to an extensive renovation 
project. However since building new houses is the type house companies business and no other 
solution will benefit them, they are clearly not impartial advisors, so their given advises can be right, 
but it has to be assessed by an impartial professional, because the decision should be taken for each 
house individually. 
6.3.2 Fear of more dependency on large loans 
Large loans for building a new house can scare the house owners because the loan bind the house 
owners to interest rate and repayments for a long period, and maybe their life situation change so it 
will become hard to pay. If it is elderly house owners that have to take the decision, their adult children 
is maybe also a consideration, because they do not want to debts to the children, but not either a house 
that is impossible to sell. For house owners with equity they can use it to finance an energy renovation, 
and it is cheaper to have a mortgage loan. If the bank advisers were better educated to assess house 
investments, it would be possible to provide cheaper loans for well-considered projects, because the 
bank had assessed the investment, so it will be with low risk. 
6.3.3 Different types of house owners 
House owner who had lived in the house for a long time will be more inclined to renovate their house 
because the house contain lots of memories, and they do not look as critical on the functions of the 
house, because they are used to it. House owners that just brought the house look more objective on 
the house and their decision is therefore taken more impartial based on realities. Also if the house 
does not function any longer for the family, they can consider to build a new house. Then they can 
stay in the neighborhood they like and close to the people they know. 
6.3.4 The Prototype house 
The project has shown, that it is possible to achieve very large energy savings, when you have a large 
amount of money. It is possible to improve the house a lot, and the many changes made it look and 
feel like a new house, even that the house do not advertise with big changes. However, even with a 
budget of two millions it was not possible to choose the best solutions for all improvements, so there 
in not added any insulation against the ground and the second best solution by insulation of the 
footings was chosen instead. Since this was an experiment of how much a house could be improved 
by two millions, it would probably be chosen to insulate to the ground if it was a project of a private 
house owner, because so much was done on the house, that it was not livable for the renovation period 
anyway. 
The energy renovation of the house made the house much more livable. Two bathrooms is a thing 
many families appreciate. The new living room with double height and lots of daylight will also be 
liked by many, but some square meters on first floor is sacrificed, so it is only possible because the 
house is large.  
The energy consumption is lowered a lot, and the calculated energy consumption fulfill the demands 
of building regulation 2008 low energy class 1, but the measured energy consumption do not fulfill 
the frame, and the low energy class 2015 from the building regulation 2010 is not fulfilled either. The 
project therefore show, that it is possible to renovate a lot for a budget slightly lower than the cost of

a new house has to, and as new houses today easily be better than.

7 Recommended method for economically optimal energy renovation of 
single family houses 
Based on the different methods and examples described in the chapters until now, this chapter will 
propose a method for systematic energy renovation of single family houses in an economically 
optimal way. The method will be presented and substantiated and then it will be demonstrated on a 
case in chapter 8. 
7.1 Background for the new method 
For many years there has been regulations to lower the energy use in new buildings, but since old 
buildings are not just replaced, they instead have to be renovated when they are rundown. To reach 
the goals of the government of a fossil free society, a national approach for energy renovations of the 
existing single family houses is needed. This is what the BedreBolig scheme is meant to be, but 
offhand it has not shown to fulfill the need. This could be because the scheme has not been running 
for so long, but there are no similar solutions to fulfill the need of energy renovations, so the basis for 
the scheme to be popular is present. 
The lacks about the scheme is a consistent systematic examination of the whole house and a 
recommendation for the house owners taking into account different kinds of value to have a holistic 
solution for the energy renovation, so this is what the new method is trying to implement. 
7.2 The method 
BedreBolig is the holistic energy renovation method in Denmark, so the new method will be based 
on BedreBolig, but it will focus more on the interaction with normal renovation and different kinds 
off added value besides energy savings. Energy renovation is the combination of renovation and 
energy savings with normal renovation as starting point. The method consist of several steps to give 
the best and most considered solution for a well-planned energy renovation. It is important that the 
method does not become too professional, because the house owner is the most important decision 
maker, and he does probably not have any noticeable experience with energy renovation. 
The method should take into account, that the majority of house owners do not just choose an energy 
renovation of their house, if they do not have any problems with the house. If they on the other hand 
have a problem, for instance puncture windows, they do renovate. Energy renovations should 
therefore be made in combination with renovation projects, but it will be driven by solving the 
problem of the house, and then the energy saving initiative will be of secondary importance. The 
holistic approach to energy renovation means, that the reason to renovation is solved, so the energy 
consumption of the house will be lowered too. The renovation problem should optimal become a start 
of an extensive energy renovation of the house. If this is not possible, it is important, that the plan for 
energy renovation of the whole house at least is worked out. When the plan is done, it is possible to 
plan phases of the energy renovation process, so initiatives is combined in suitable groups, where 
later initiatives do not influence earlier done work negatively.

7.2.1 Involving the house owner 
Single family houses is often owned and occupied by the same private person, and it is alone the 
house owner, who can decide to start an energy renovation, so the house owner is very important to 
involve in the process. The house owner is probably only doing one energy renovation in his life, so 
he have no experience like the rest of the advisors and craftsmen involved, but since he is the decision 
maker, the communication among the stakeholders is important. 
First of all, for the house owner to take the decision about starting an energy renovation, he need to 
know the possibility and what it contain. Since it should be a national scheme, commercials in national 
television and relevant websites for instance Bolius.dk could be good places to start making the 
population aware and interested in energy renovations. The social circle is very important for the 
house owner in the decision making process, since information then come from someone the house 
owner already trust and the information then seems more reliable. To be sure that energy renovation 
is a topic people discuss, the commercials needs to be remarkable and they have to appear were people 
are gathered, for instance in canteens and public transport. 
Sometimes people are not aware that the information is relevant for them and therefore they do not 
really care about big campaigns. To reach this kind of house owners, nearness is important. Single 
family houses from the 1960s and 1970s is often build as neighborhoods with lots of similar houses. 
The nearness can therefore come from a flyer distributed to every house in the neighborhood with 
description of an energy renovation made in the neighborhood or in a similar neighborhood. It is 
important that the flyer is plain but still inform about all relevant issues like budget, rebuilding 
discomfort, increased value and advisors/craftsmen. 
The idea from BedreBolig about one single entrance to information about the energy renovation 
concept is good, because the house owner easily can get an overview, when the information is 
gathered. Like the flyer, it is important that the website is plain and grouped into clear subjects, so 
the house owner easily can find what he is interested in, and so house owners that is slightly interested 
in energy renovations do not give up, because it seems too complicated. It is important that the website 
has a remarkable name, which is mentioned in all correlations. Then the house owner easily can find 
the website, since there is also lots of websites about energy renovations from companies. The website 
should appear competent, so there is no doubt that it is a website for an independent national scheme 
to expand energy renovations and not just a company that want to earn money. 
7.2.2 Education of advisors 
The advisors should be able to plan and lead the execution of the energy renovation. Since the energy 
renovation both consist of renovation and energy savings it is important that the advisor have 
knowledge and experience with both. The education should be about extensive building review, 
==PAGE:50==
Page 50 of 73 
 
renovation, energy calculation, energy optimization, budgeting and good communication to different 
target groups, to present a holistic solution fitting both the house and the house owner. 
To guide the house owner in a positive and reliable way, it is important to have experience in the 
building industry before becoming an authorized energy renovation advisor. Many things can be 
learned theoretically and by cases on courses, but it will never be as complex as the reality, so the 
education should supply the experience of the advisor. Also the house owner will believe more in the 
advices, when he know, that the advisor already have practical experience. Some professions in the 
building industry are more evident to undergo further training to become energy renovation advisors, 
simply because they in their original education are taught holistic so they already know the way of 
thinking. Craftsmen are more specialized in their education than engineers and construction 
managers, so there is a risk, that they look narrower to find solutions and that they will base the 
solution on their original education. After the education the advisor get an authorization, so the house 
owner has certainty, that he gets the guidance he expect. 
With experience from realizing projects, the advisor have some knowledge of planning budgets. The 
difficult thing here could be, that the budget should be professional enough for the bank to base a 
loan on, while it at the same time should be simple enough for the house owner to know exactly what 
he gets for his investment. The budget should be a standard budget made in the software, so the costs 
are standard costs built into and automatically updated in the software. Before the final plan for energy 
renovation is made, Table 3 give an overview of the cost of the different initiatives, which the house 
owner and the advisor could use to choose the initiatives for the final plan. 
At the moment there are different possibilities for economically support when the house owner do an 
energy renovation of his house. It is important that the advisor keep himself updated on this and 
coming schemes, so he can guide the house owner, and if possible apply or at least prepare the 
application. 
7.2.3 Initial meeting 
The initial meeting is between the house owner and the chosen advisor, and maybe the house owner 
has to have more initial meetings with different advisors to be sure that he feel, that he gets the best 
guidance for the energy renovation of his house. At the initial meeting the house owner present his 
situation like budget and plans for the house in the future. For instance if children are planned to be 
born or leave home, or if he is going to retire, so he will spend much more time in the house. Also 
the current satisfaction of the house is important, because in a big energy renovation, it is much easier 
to change the layout of the house, than few years after everything is finished. 
The initial meeting should also be used to match expectations. It is important that the house owner 
and the advisor trust each other and feel, that they are working on the same project, and also that they 
are clear about their different roles in the energy renovation process. 
When the house owner have the initial meeting with an advisor, he has already decided that he want 
an energy renovation of his house, and he should be ready to present his thoughts to the advisor. The 
advisor should be prepared by searching in the property, and if possible find former energy renovation 
projects on similar houses, so he has something visual to show the house owner.

7.2.4 Building review 
If the house is just or almost just sold without the new owners has done anything significant to the 
house, the energy label report and the home condition reports can be good places to start finding 
problems and solutions. 
Another place to find inspiration should be from an example bank. The house owners are individual 
and each solution therefore will be more or less different, but the houses are build similar, and 
therefore they will have some of the same problems, and there is no reason to reinvent the wheel 
every time. Also the visualization and communication between the house owner and the advisor 
would be easier, if they both can share ideas by examples. The example bank should be public, but 
some house owners may not like information about themselves public, and then it could be a censored 
version that is public, and only the company that made the project have the access to all inputs, but 
they are not allowed to share it with coming clients. 
It is important, that all the preparation does not make the advisor too prejudiced, because the advisor 
should always review the whole building himself. 
When the advisor is reviewing the house, he should use the checklists for all building components, to 
be sure that everything is examined. The advisor should examine the house together with the house 
owner, because the house owner can tell, if specific building components are reason to discomfort 
and reason for contacting the advisor in the first place. When the house owner and the advisor goes 
around and talk about the house, they maybe skip from one topic to another, so the checklists is a 
good tool, to be sure that nothing is missed in the end. 
As an optimization the home condition report, the energy label report and the energy renovation plan 
should not be three very different documents. If there are no valid documents on the house, the 
building review should examine and judge the house on the parameters as both the home condition 
report and the energy label report is normally based on. 
7.2.5 Suggested solutions for energy renovations 
Based on the different aspects, the method itself can be summed up to six steps, which the advisor 
should use in the process from mapping the needs to the final energy renovation plan: 
1. Need for renovation 
2. Need for extensive energy savings 
3. Possible initiatives 
4. Economy 
5. Renovation or demolishing and new build 
6. Renovation plan 
The new building regulation 2015 introduce more rules for energy optimization included in 
renovations, so the difference between renovation and energy renovation is reduced. The rules can be 
difficult to understand for inexperienced house owners, so it is important, that the advisor are aware 
of the minimum demands but also can suggest better solutions. In order to determine the degree of an 
energy renovation, the new building regulation 2015 can also help, because of the new renovation 
classes, but more ambitious goals can also be set. 
==PAGE:52==
Page 52 of 73 
 
The initial conversation and the building review should map the needs for renovation and energy 
savings. Based on the needs, all relevant improvements should be calculated, so the economy can be 
determined, and the internal balance of what to realize can be found. Then cost of the improvements 
should be compared to the cost of a new built house, and if improving the house is the optimal 
solution, the plan to realize the energy renovation should be made. The plan should depend on, if the 
process must be one extensive project or smaller phases. 
The improvements should be calculated both alone and in combinations with each other where it 
make sense, because it could be an advantage in relation to cost and difficulty, when certain things 
are done together. In an extensive energy renovation, there are different ways of assessing the 
improvements. For the energy renovation initiatives the CCE can be used to compare small and big 
and more or less expensive initiatives. The CCE can be the base to choose the most effective 
initiatives, but to make the budget, it is more relevant to use the simple payback time for the energy 
saving initiatives. For the non-energy saving initiatives there are no payback time, but it will add 
value to the house. It can be hard to locate exactly how much value the different initiatives add, and 
a sum cannot be putted on everything. The added value to the house caused by the suggested 
initiatives can be assessed by a real estate agent or the bank, based on the house before and after the 
realized improvements. The use value cannot be made up in money, but if the layout of the house is 
optimized, so for instance the square meters are used in a better way, the growing family can remain 
living in the house, and save the costs related to relocation. Also the increased comfort and the 
satisfaction about living in an extensive energy renovated house provide value in the house owner’s 
daily life, but it does not give him more money in the bank. 
If it is tried to lower the energy consumption as much as possible, the last kWh will be much more 
expensive to save than the first one, and therefore it is necessary to look at the project as a whole. 
When the optimal plan for energy renovation is ready, it is important also to assess, if it would be 
more optimal to demolish the existing house and build a new house. There should be a balance 
between the cost and the calculated energy consumption of the extensive energy renovated house 
compared to the cost of a new house and the assumed energy consumption of the new house. If these 
numbers are even, the new house will be the best solution. In a new house, all materials will be new, 
so there is an expected long lifetime and less maintenance. In a renovated house, the maintenance 
will also be low, but some building components will still be the original, for instance embedded pipes, 
so there is larger risks related with living in an old house. If the comparison between the energy 
consumption and the cost is in favor of the energy renovation, two more thing has to be considered 
before the final decision. The first one is, which house will be cheapest to live in. In this consideration 
total costs should be taken into account, covering interest expenses, energy costs and costs for 
maintenance. This is all taken into account in NPV. The second consideration is, that a new house is 
typical worth more and have higher sales prices. For instance a search on Boligsiden.dk show a clear 
difference in square meter price, in the area of Kongens Lyngby, see Table 4.

7.2.6 Financing 
To reach a broad group of house owners, it is important, that all three groups of house owners 
described in chapter 3.2.3 Too expensive to energy renovate is able to finance the energy renovation, 
because else it will take too long time before the single family houses are updated. For the first group 
of house owners, the high equity house owners, financing is not really a problem and it is more a 
matter of being willing to have the hassle of a renovation. The second group is the average house 
owners, and they have the possibility to finance part of the renovation by themselves, and the rest 
they have to borrow in the bank, maybe based on equity. The third group is the technically insolvent 
house owners and first time buyers, and the banks do not kindly lend them more money. Anyway, 
they are an important group, which should not be neglected in the energy renovation process. For the 
banks to have most possible collateral for the loan, it is important that the budget of the suggested 
solution is professional and prepared thoroughly. 
It is beneficial for the society, that the house owners make energy renovations of their single family 
houses. The banks needs to understand and take responsibility, that it would benefit the society, that 
more people have the possibility to borrow money for energy renovations. Since it is in the interest 
of the society, the energy renovation loan should be low cost, and in return the state can provide some 
sort of guarantee, when the bank on normal conditions do not want to take the risk of a loan to the 
house owner. 
It should be possible to finance the whole optimal energy renovation at one time. It is the least trouble 
for the house owner with just one rebuilding, it result in less maintenance, and while the house owner 
live for longer time in a new renovated house, the energy consumption is lower and the comfort is 
better. 
7.2.7 Realization 
When both the plan and the financing is place, it is time to start the rebuilding to realize the energy 
renovation project. For the house owner to have confidence in the process, it is important that it is 
still the same advisor that have the main communication to the house owner. Of course the advisor 
==PAGE:54==
Page 54 of 73 
 
cannot be present in the house every day, but he should introduce the daily manager of the building 
site, and regularly inform the house owner, when the project go into a new stage. 
Depending on how extensive the energy renovation is, the house owner may have to live in another 
place during the rebuilding, and this is also a cost, that should have been taken into account. 
When the project is finished, the house owner has got a different house to live in. It is important, that 
there are simple user manuals to new systems, so the house owner do not use them in a wrong way 
and waste energy. It is also important, that there is a plan for maintenance or control, so new building 
components fulfill the expected lifetime. The advisor should make a handover to the house owner, 
and he should be available if the house owner is in doubt about anything. After one year the advisor 
should make a new building review to be sure, that new systems are suitable for the house and the 
owner all year long. 
7.2.8 Sharing of good experience 
During this description of a suggested solution for energy renovations, some knowledge sharing 
initiatives had been mentioned. There is no big tradition to share knowledge across, but every energy 
renovation is good for the society, and therefore it is also good for the society, that each energy 
renovation is based on best practice, which again is based on lots of experience. 
A problem of promoting energy renovations is that the theoretical saving is too big compared to the 
actual saving, so people become disappointed about the result, and do not feel, that the energy 
renovation fulfill the goal. It has become a common opinion, that an energy renovation is very 
effective, if the payback time is short, and therefore initiatives with a short payback time is seen as a 
good choice. To avoid this, less attention should be paid to the payback time and instead the project 
and the added value as a whole should be in focus.

Ground deck: The existing ground deck only has 50 mm insulation, and there are no other possibilities 
for more insulation than casting of a new ground deck. Since the heat distribution system is outdated 
it is interesting with a new ground deck with water based floor heating. There are no calculations of 
a new ground deck with floor heating, so it is assumed to be a combination of the ground deck with

high energy saving, but when a new ground deck is casted, it seems ridiculous not to install floor

current façade is sand lime bricks and the type houses typical have brick facades, so thin types of tiles 
can be chosen.

8.5 Renovation or demolition and new build 
The total cost of the energy renovation of House D is found to be DKK 1,274,600, while the cost for

8.6 Renovation plan 
Some initiatives should be done before others in order not to lose value in later improvement phases. 
It would be most optimal, if the whole energy renovation is made at ones, but if this does not fit the 
house owner, the plan should be divided into considered phases. The building envelope should always 
be improved before the systems, and improvements should be done in an order, so later improvement 
do not have an inconvenient impact on earlier done improvements. For instance it is inconvenient that 
there has been installed a solar heating system on the roof, which now induce extra work, because it 
has to be removed and reinstalled when the roofing is replaced. If the work is done in phases, it is 
still good to have a holistic long term energy renovation plan. Then the house owner can start with 
the first phases, and then house buyers already have a plan for the house, when they buy a not totally 
energy renovated house. 
If this energy renovation process should be divided into phases, the initiatives should be done in the 
following order: 
1. New roof cladding and attic insulation 
2. Exterior insulation of outer walls and base 
3. New windows and doors 
4. New casted ground deck with floor heating 
5. New bathroom and kitchen 
6. Ventilation system 
The roof cladding and the attic insulation is the first because there are problems with the current roof 
cladding, and it is much easier to place insulation, when there are no roof. Then the outer walls and 
the base should be insulated before the windows and doors are replaced, because then the windows 
and doors can be placed with minimum thermal bridges. The last part of the building envelope is the 
ground deck, before the new bathroom and kitchen can be installed. The last initiative is the 
ventilation because then the building envelope has been sealed.

9 Discussion 
In this chapter, the main issues from the previous chapters will be discussed, and then the main 
conclusions will be outlined in the next chapter. 
9.1 The development of energy renovation 
Since the first awareness of energy saving initiatives, there has been a development from smaller 
simple here and now improvements to extensive energy renovations. The simple here and now 
improvements could be attic insulation, low energy windows or smaller improvements the handy 
house owner can do without guidance from professional. Then the energy saving initiatives become 
more organized in the single problem based approach. Here professionals are involved, so there is 
certainty that the realized improvement fulfill the regulations in a good way. This approach is induced 
by the energy saving agreement between the government and the utility companies. The problem 
about the subsidy scheme is, that it is still the easiest and cheapest improvements for the biggest 
saving that are encouraged, so it is only suboptimization. Newest is the combined planned BedreBolig 
solution, which combine different energy saving initiatives and other improvements. BedreBolig 
introduce the beginning to a mainstream scheme for extensive energy renovations. The method 
proposed in chapter 7 try to improve the BedreBolig solution, by implementation of more extensive 
energy renovation thoughts like mandatory building review and more emphasis on the holistic 
solution, where the energy renovation is driven by a renovation need. 
9.2 Energy renovation based on the house owners need 
To reach the goal of a fossil free society in 2050, where buildings are supplied by energy from 
renewable energy sources, it is important to improve the existing buildings. The special issue about 
single family houses is, that it is the private house owner, which has to pay and take the final decision. 
The government can encourage energy renovation by introducing rules in the building regulation and 
by introducing subsidy schemes, but it is still up to the house owner, whether he want to do an 
extensive energy renovation or just renovate regularly. 
It is important, that energy saving initiatives is easy to incorporate in normal renovation or rebuilding 
projects. To have widespread energy renovations among private house owners, energy saving 
initiatives must not be related to lots of extra work and expenses. Energy renovations should be 
promoted in addition to replacement or renovation, because if energy saving initiatives and 
commercials become too idealistic or complicated, many house owners feel, that it does not concern 
them. Another reason is, that the largest part of the cost is related to the renovation initiative, and only 
a smaller extra cost is related to the energy saving initiative. For the house owner to think that energy 
renovation is the obvious choice and he also find it important to seek for guidance, the advisor should 
have a good reputation. When the house owner first has contact to an advisor, the holistic plan for the 
house can be made, and then the process of energy renovation has begun. 
9.3 Considerations of renovations or new built 
There are no tradition of demolishing the existing buildings, but as the last thing, when the energy 
saving initiatives are found, and before the energy renovation project is began, it should be 
considered, whether it would be worth more to build a brand new house. It would typical be newly 
house owners, that buy a house with the intention of demolishing it, because they like the location, 
but cannot find a house, that would fulfill their wishes, and then the choice is not based on energy 
reasons. Most people maybe consider new built before they moving into the house and/or begin to 
==PAGE:60==
Page 60 of 73 
 
renovate, but the obvious situation is, if no remarkable changes has been made since the house was 
built. Then the building components is about to have exhausted the lifetime, and they therefore do 
not represent any value, which will be lost, if the house is demolished. To have a rational base to 
decide, if the house should be renovated or demolished, it is necessary to assess the cost and the 
advantages of an energy renovation compared to the demolishing and new built. From an energy 
consumption point of view, all approaches end up with houses using less energy than before, and that 
is good for the environment. On the other hand, it could be a waste of materials if houses are just 
demolished because the house owner wants something different, and that is not good for the 
environment. From an economical point of view it should always be calculated, what would be the 
cheapest solution, and maybe it is waiting five years, because more building components then has 
exhausted their lifetime, and an extensive renovation then become more obvious. 
9.4 The optimal method for energy renovation 
Lots of the houses built in the 1960s and 1970s is type houses, built in similar layout, sizes and 
materials, and therefore it could be a solution to develop type energy renovations, so house owners 
could just choose between standard initiatives. This is not seen as a good possible solution because 
the old type houses has been treated very different over the years and the house owners have different 
wishes and situations. In the 1960s it was a big change and improvement, that normal people could 
afford a single family house with garden, so the design of the house itself was less important. 
However, the mindset has changed over the years to become more individualistic, so people has 
personalized their houses, and what is the optimal solution for one house will probably not be the 
optimal solution for another house. As advisor it is therefore important to keep an open and individual 
approach to each project, instead of thinking in very standardized solutions. Anyway, the method to 
find the optimal solution seems obvious to standardize like it is tried in the BedreBolig scheme. With 
a standardized method for energy renovation, the house owner get certainty, the advisors have a 
template to follow for each project and all resources can be gathered to promote one method. 
9.4.1 Combination of reports 
As it works today, the home condition report, the energy label report and the BedreBolig report is 
three different systems, but it would be an optimization, if these worked more together. The current 
home condition report and energy label report should be base for the building review, which is base 
for the energy renovation plan, so it is one process in order to lower the energy consumption in Danish 
houses. If the energy renovation plan was mandatory in a sales situation, the buyer would get a very 
detailed overview of the problems and the potentials of the house, and also the economy in the 
improvement process of the house, which would be a very strong base for a big decision like buying 
a house. The house buyer will then easily have access to an advisor, which already know the house, 
and easy can help the new house owner choosing the best plan for energy renovation. Besides in sales 
situations, the energy renovation plan could also be mandatory for houses forty years old and older, 
because then it is about time, where renovation is much needed. 
9.4.2 Added value 
A renovation will add value to the house in a sales situation, because the house buyer then do not 
have to start paying for roof replacement for example. 
Energy saving initiatives will add value both for the current and the coming house owners, because 
the energy consumption will be lowered, and therefore the energy bills will be cheaper than before 
==PAGE:61==
Page 61 of 73 
 
the project was realized. Anyway, it is important to remember, that if it is just the building envelope 
that has been improved by energy saving initiatives, it does not automatically mean, that the energy 
use will be lowered. After the improvements, the heat supply also has to be changed, because else it 
could end in a situation, where the house is still supplied by the same amount of heat, which would 
be too much for the residents, and if they cannot change thermostats suitable, they will open the 
windows, and then the heat is wasted. To avoid most unnecessary waste of energy, the systems like 
heat supply and light should be automatically, so it is self-regulating, and the behavior of the residents 
has minimum influence on the energy use. 
The renovation and the energy renovation should both be made, so the aesthetics of the house is 
improved. This would also have influence on the value of the house, both in money in a sales situation 
and on daily basis in satisfaction for the house owner, because there is status in living in a good 
looking house.

10 Conclusion 
Lots of type houses was built in the 1960s and the 1970s, when the demands to energy consumption 
was almost non-existing. Today there are strict demands in the building regulation to new houses, 
and as a new object, BR15 also introduce renovation class 1 and 2 for energy renovation of buildings. 
The governmental goal of a fossil free society means, that all buildings should be supplied by energy 
from renewable sources in 2050, and that require an energy optimization effort in existing houses. 
Depending on the condition of the house and the wishes and situation of the house owner, it will be 
different which solution will be optimal in the specific project. It is therefore important not to think 
in standard solutions, but it is an advantage to have a standardized method like the BedreBolig 
solution. The BedreBolig solution is the first national scheme that combine different energy saving 
initiatives and other improvements, but the use of the scheme has been disappointing, and possible 
improvements can be outlined to improve the method. For economically optimal energy renovation 
of single family houses to become widespread, it is important that the advisor is holistic in the 
conversation with the house owner and in the building review. If new built or one extensive energy 
renovation is not the optimal solution, the energy renovation plan should be made long term, so the 
realization happens when it fit the house owner and the building components have low remaining 
lifetime. But most important to remember is, that house owners do not just start an extensive energy 
renovation, if they do not see any problems of their house. In an energy renovation the cost of the 
renovation initiative is much larger than the extra cost of the energy saving initiative, and the energy 
renovation is too expensive, if it should be paid back by energy savings. Therefore the energy 
renovation should be driven by the renovation need, and all kinds of value like added value, energy 
savings, better comfort and higher satisfaction should be appreciated.
