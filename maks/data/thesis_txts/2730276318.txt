Abstract
This master’s thesis investigates that how a software using the Unity game engine can
be created to enable remote meeting participation through virtual reality. Game develop­
ment and virtual reality are popular in the fields of computer science, game engines found
applications in several industry. This research project aimed to develop a initial version
of this software and validate the project’s potency through testing.
Despite of considering building a real­time virtual environment, the completed program
simply uses a 360° video, to provide an immerse experience. The testing showed that
most people would use a similar program.

Introduction
As many research papers state, working from home became the norm in 2020 during the
COVID­19 pandemic. Most of these papers investigate the effectiveness of this way of
working. Communication plays a key role when we talk about the effectiveness of work;
messages and calls are usual in the daily life of a company, and there are more official
methods of communication in the form of presentations or meetings. My thesis provides a
way to create a more immersive experience for an individual working from home, allowing
them to participate in their company’s meetings and feel that they are also a part of the
meeting.
1.1
Background
Virtual reality (VR) is an advanced technology that immerses users in interactive 3D en­
vironments through specialized headsets or screens. It replaces the physical world, en­
abling users to interact with and explore virtual environments. Virtual reality has uses in
gaming, education, and healthcare. Despite its benefits, VR has challenges such as VR
sickness and expenses. The technology has evolved significantly over the years, with
high­resolution screens, motion tracking, and immersive audio systems.
Game engines have gained popularity because they are designed to simplify develop­
ment, even for non­programmers. Unity is one of the most popular of these game engines,
used for developing 3D and 2D graphical interactive applications, not limited to games.
It also serves as a platform for VR development. Unity is a constantly evolving platform
that ensures user satisfaction.
Remote meetings, conducted online using audio, video, and internet connectivity, have
become essential in the digital age. They facilitate international cooperation, cutting down
on travel requirements and contributing to environmental sustainability. These meetings
became increasingly popular during the pandemic, providing a safe mode of communica­
tion while working from home. Technological improvements have provided solutions for
technical issues such as dependable internet connectivity and hardware needs. Remote
meetings offer tools for screen sharing and document collaboration.

1.2
Relevance
Combining remote meetings with VR technology provides engaging and interactive com­
munication experiences. Participants can join meetings from different locations and col­
laborate in real­time within virtual environments. During remote meetings, VR technology
enhances the sense of presence and engagement.
VR meeting software is relevant because it transforms how people collaborate, engage,
and communicate, offering a more immersive, efficient, and inclusive alternative to tra­
ditional online meetings. Its potential to revolutionize various industries and enhance
communication makes it a significant area of development and investment.
1.3
Problem / Methodology
The research problem I investigate is the potential of VR to enhance online communi­
cation, specifically in the context of meetings. This would be achieved by creating an
immersive experience that connects people’s senses more deeply with the meeting room
in the online space, thus, increasing the focus and engagement.
The first task was to get to exploration of the existing field of VR technology and its appli­
cations in online communication, as well as its limitations, for understanding the current
capabilities of VR.
The next step in my research process was the design and development of a VR application
for online meetings with user­friendly interface. It was important to find the middle ground
between complexity and realistic implementation.
For validating the decisions made in the steps above I designed and conducted a pilot
test. The primary objective of this test is to gather feedback from individuals unfamiliar
with the program, aiming to evaluate the software’s usability and direction. Furthermore,
to examine the program’s impact on users, focusing on any discomfort or motion sickness
experienced during testing.
Through these insights discussion on the future of an app like this, my research aims to
contribute valuable insights to the evolving field of VR technology and its impact on human
communication and collaboration.

2.1
Virtual Reality
In this study, it is crucial to provide a comprehensive understanding of virtual reality (VR)
by defining its key aspects, establishing its relevance, and discussing both its benefits
and drawbacks.
According to a study [34] from 1998, that has been widely cited, “Virtual Reality is an
advanced, human­computer interface that simulates a realistic environment”. Through
customized headset or screen, it is a technology that immerses the user in an interactive
3­dimensional environment. VR seeks to generate a sensation of presence by blocking
the physical world and replacing it with a virtual one that enables users to interact with and
explore the simulated actual or imagined world as if it were real. This technology creates
a sense of presence and interaction, enabling users to engage with and explore virtual
worlds.
Additionally, the paper mentioned before [34] also discusses the complexity of the area
and how many different branches of science it derives from, including mechanical engi­
neering, real­time and distributed systems as well as computer graphics. At that point of
the technology VR products have reached a certain level of technological maturity. For in­
stance, the user could make minor alterations to the virtual object. The field has advanced
significantly over the past 25 years.
2.2
Head­Mounted Display
As I mentioned before VR requires a headset or screen to help to close off reality and
display the simulated environment. This device is called Head­Mounted Display or HMD.
The first HMD was patented in 1960 by Morton L. Heilig [13] This device was able to
create the feeling of a 3­dimensional view produced by independently supplying the right
and left eyes with images captured by separate cameras.
The Oculus Rift project also can be considered a breakthrough. The goal of this Kick­
starter project in 2012 was to make a high­quality, affordable HMD available to the general
public. This project reached its funding target in less than 24 hours. [2] After this year
more companies started to produce affordable devices and VR began to spread more
rapidly, hence making this field even more attractive.

Over time HMDs have developed significantly.

At the first stages of development scientist try to use gloves for controlling VR, like moving

2.2.2
Application and Impact
People realized the opportunities in VR technology a long time ago and use it in various
applications. We must mention perhaps the best known use of VR: gaming. It offers a new
level of immersion and interactivity in gaming experiences as well as the mentioned sense
of presence because of the realistic game environment. It can bring changes in several
areas of life, in education with training simulation or in healthcare, especially therapy.
VR has the potential to revolutionize education and training [33] It enables interactive and
hands­on learning experiences, allowing users to practice skills in a safe and controlled
environment. VR can be used for medical training and flight simulations providing realistic
scenarios that enhance learning outcomes. Besides, in many cases it also reduces the
cost.

2.2.3
Drawbacks
VR sickness, Cybersickness
From the perspective of the thesis, it is important to talk about VR sickness that is an
unpleasant feeling after using an HMD. The sickness itself is similar to motion sickness
that may occur while driving. This can negatively affect the quality of experience while
using VR.
As the authors of the paper [6] describe through a variety of sensory organs, people can
perceive their direction and movement. We can easily and precisely perceive our position
and movement in space because all the sensory data is processed in synchrony with
one another. The sensory mismatch between what the eyes perceive and what the body
senses can trigger motion sickness, VR sickness or cybersickness.
The symptoms are discomfort that can be physical or mental, drowsiness and fatigue from
tiredess, dry or watery eyes, blurred vision, eye redness, or a general feeling of eye fatigue
and soreness, and it can also include nausea or disorientation. [25] The intensity of the
illness depends on many components. Human factors, individual characteristics like age,
gender, overall health, and level of fatigue. The sufficiency and efficiency of the hardware
like the delay between a user’s movement and the corresponding visual updates in the
virtual environment, potential misalignment in tracking head movements. The physical
position of the user, like using the HMD seated or standing. The measurement of VR
sickness is frequently done with a method called SSQ (Simulator Sickness Questionnaire)
[22] that was made to measure motion sickness in simulators.
Expenses
Although after 2012 VR technology became more affordable, with the introduction of
consumer­grade VR headsets like the Oculus Rift and HTC Vive, despite the decrease in
cost, VR did not achieve widespread adoption. Systems can require powerful computers
to run smoothly, which added to the overall cost. [24] Refining VR glasses takes expen­
sive research and prototyping. It is worth mentioning that the tech giant, also Apple plans
to launch VR headset called Vision Pro next year for $3,499 the specialty of this device is
that it will be able to process commands with your voice, eyes, and hands. [15]
2.3
Remote Meeting
A remote meeting can refer to an online session where people can communicate with each
other. In a remote meeting, participants join from different locations using audio, video,
and internet connectivity to communicate in real­time. These forms of meetings have
become increasingly popular and essential in this modern digital age, enabling individuals
and teams to connect and collaborate regardless of their physical location. Most of the
virtual meeting applications also provide tools for screen sharing, document or whiteboard
collaboration.

The frequency of remote meetings significantly increased during the pandemic started
in 2020 and become an integral part of modern work culture, since this provided a safe,
isolated way of communication while it was recommended to work from home. The benefit
of online meeting was great during this time, but it also has advantages that are always
relevant.
One benefit of everything happening in a virtual environment is that it can easily be
recorded, most of the meeting applications also have the tools for recording, making the
documentation of the event easier.
Participants can join the meeting from all over the world allowing collaboration for indi­
viduals from different countries or even continents. This eliminates the need for travel
that reduces expenses as well as it saves time for the participants, this in a wider aspect
also contributes to environmental sustainability by reducing carbon emission. [19] Re­
mote meetings also create equal opportunity for people with physical disabilities, mobility
challenges whether temporary or permanent.
2.3.1
Challenges
Meetings in all forms can be a serious source of stress [16] during the pandemic with re­
mote meetings quickly becoming widespread a new type appeared called “Zoom fatigue”.
The mentioned paper describes it as an emotional exhaustion, there are more factors in­
fluencing the feeling of this fatigue, like the frequeny of breaks or the need for multitasking
(focusing on a variety of information sources at once, such as our own and other’s audio,
video or shared documents) [5]
It also has some technical challenges, but these nowadays seem to disappear. On the
level of hardware people need a webcam, microphone and speakers or headphones. [8]
Today it is expected for these to be built into a new laptop, although in some cases, as
companies want to keep the prices of their PC low, and in the case of a desktop com­
puter it is recommended to have newer, more advanced devices. This can affect the
meeting experience positively [14] A webcam is used to share their image, that enables
people to have a conversation with non­verbal communication techniques regardless of
the distance, thus increase the sense of presence. A microphone captures the voice and
allows one to communicate with others during the meeting. Speakers or headphones are
necessary to be able to hear other participants.
Reliable internet connection became available for most households. The internet connec­
tion is stable and consistent, without frequent slowdowns or interruptions. An engaging
meeting requires suitable speed and bandwidth for both uploading and downloading data.
Low latency is also crucial to immerse in a conversion, this means that the time it takes
for data to travel should be as short as possible. These days internet service providers
are trying to offer this, as it has become necessary in the current interconnected world.

3.1
Theoretical approach
The basic concept of the software was to develop a program for use with VR glasses that
allows a single person to join in a physical meeting, regardless of capturing or displaying
the remote participant. The aim was to create an immerse experience that would make
the remote member feel as if he is present in the room where the meeting takes place,
making it easier to focus and keep mentally connected to the meeting.
3.1.1
First idea
A paper from 2020 [3] recommended by my co­supervisor gave an excellent basis for the
development of software suitable for VR meetings. The paper describes a methodology
of creating a layered representation of a stereo 360° imagery for six degree­of­freedom
rendering. They claim to do so by estimating depth and distributing the scene based on
the result, thus creating a more natural experience for the user. This would eliminate or
at least delay VR sickness. Unfortunately, the mentioned project has not been updated
since October 2021, however it promised releasing the real­time Unity application on the
GitHub repository of the project, [4] so I could not examine its exact operation.
3.1.2
The completed program
The completed program turned out much simpler than how the first idea would be imple­
mented. In the initial stages of this thesis, I had limited experience using the Unity Game
Engine, and this unfortunately had a major impact on the finished project. In a nutshell the
program’s main part is an inverted sphere with unlit material and uses that as a canvas to
display a video. To implement this, I needed to use software other than Unity, for example
Blender.
When I started to get to know Unity, I used a tutorial provided by Unity’s learning platform,
the “Create with VR” course. Later not only the knowledge I acquired was useful but some
of the provided assets also, like the VR device emulator.
To present the reasons for changing the first idea to a much easier first I would like to
describe the software I used. Thus the next section will be about the software and in the
section after that I will present my reasons of implementing a much easier software.

3.2

3.2.1
Unity Game Engine
General Information
Unity is one of the most widely used game engines today. The program’s aim is to make
the development of 3­dimensional and 2­dimensional graphical, interactive applications
easier. As their name suggests game engines like Unity are mainly used to develop
games, but they also began to appear in industry, for example in the construction industry.
One of its advantages is that prior knowledge is not sufficient to develop with its help so
that a person without a programming background can work with it. The developer of Unity,
called Unity Technologies, is also aimed to help their users. There are many tutorials
available online provided by the company on their learning platform Unity Learn. [29]
Another big benefit of using Unity is that it has a big community of supportive users and
a forum where one can ask questions, Unity Forum. [28] These websites play a key role
ensuring the expansion of their user base, besides the platform is constantly changing
and evolving. Unity often has even 4­5 new releases of improved software in a month
with long term support.
Wide Applicability
With the help of the Unity game engine, it is possible to develop applications for multiple
computer platforms, this means that it is cross­platform software. It provides developers
with several features that help the development process significantly. Additionally, Unity
also includes an editor which is not only provides its users with several built­in tools, but
also the possibility to extend it in various ways. Anything that can be used in the project
is called an asset, this includes any files or resources, regardless of whether it is linked
to game development, like 3­dimension models, materials, textures, or not like videos,
images, sounds. To make the game development even more comfortable Unity provides
an asset store, [27] where these resources can be bought and sold. The asset store also
offers some of it for free. After downloading the user can easily import assets in the game
engine.
Structure of the Unity applications
All applications that can be created using the Unity game engine consist of at least one
scene. These scenes can be used to separate the different parts of a program. For small
and simple applications, it is unnecessary to use more than one, but for a complex game,
for example, it may be worthwhile to have the menu and the different tracks in separate
scenes. This does not only increase the maintainability of the application, but it also
reduces memory usage, as only the active scene space is loaded.
The scenes contains objects, in object­oriented programming we are used to objects be­
ing a so­called parent class, that is used as a reference to every single element of the
program. In our case this is called Unity GameObject. This is not fully consistent with the

just mentioned parent class, only in the sense that this is a container for everything in the

There are many kind of components, these can perform different tasks, such as visuali­

nent, which executes C# code. Conventional programming in Unity is almost exclusively
done in this way.
Scripting
As mentioned, there are several kind of components in Unity, but we can state that the
most important of them is the script components as almost the entire logic of the game
can be implemented with them. Unity primarily supports C# as the language of scripts.
With these scripts we want to communicate with the components of Unity and the program
itself, to accomplish this there is a pre­implemented class called MonoBehaviour. This
class has to be the parent of every script component. Thus these classes can inherit
the properties of this class through which the services of Unity are available. This for
example used to create components that can be connected to components within Unity,
thus checking or updating their status.
The most important of these is probably that this way we can use Unity’s event handler
functions, like Start() or Update(). The Start() function is called when the application starts
so any initialization can and recommended to be placed here. The Update() function is
called in every frame or timestamp of the applications, so actions triggered by user input,
movement or time should be handled here.
Unity Editor
The editor that Unity offers is easy to use, since this is a key factor in being able to write
game software almost without programming. The structure can be seen on the Figure
3.1. The window is generally divided to four main sections, excluding the toolbar, under
a regular menu bar, on the top. On the left side of the window there is the ”Hierarchy”
section, that contains the scene and the tree structure of the GameObjects, that can be
easily chosen this way. To the left of that, in the middle of the window, there is the ”Scene”
section, that displays the object on our scene and simplifies to move, rotate and scale

Modelling in Blender
The scenes in my application contain inverted spheres, what I use as a canvas, so it is
important for it to be smooth. In the virtual world every polygon is made of triangles, that
is why I used a modelling application called Blender. As the program is very popular there
are a lot of tutorials on the internet, to create a sphere that suits as a canvas I watched and
reproduced “[2.8] Blender Tutorial: How to Make A Perfect Sphere Without Lines” video
from YouTube [30], it was not made with the same version of Blender that I used, but
using this modelling software was still convenient. This method showed how to smooth
a cube to a sphere with dividing it to more polygons. After that I just needed the normal
vector of the sphere to face towards the center of the sphere, Blender gave an option to
invert these normal, that, in this case, meant the same.
3.4.2
Unity Scene

system, I also adjusted their size and orientation to display properly.

Discussion
4.2.1
Comparative Analysis
This concept is not very common. Most of the VR meeting applications require everyone to
wear VR headsets and represent all participants in the online meeting space, for example,
Meta Horizon Workrooms. [20] These software platforms are completely different and
have distinct challenges to solve, so comparing them would not be appropriate.
4.2.2
Limitations
Unity is used to develop a wide variety of applications, but primarily a game engine, de­
signed for creating and optimizing games. While it offered significant assistance in devel­
oping the application, I found that it lacked many features crucial for my software, which
could be generally useful in various industries.
To ensure the developed software functions fully, a solution is needed to display the person
wearing the VR headset at the meeting in the real world. The simplest approach would
be to use a monitor with a video stream of the user. However, VR glasses typically cover
most of the wearer’s head, making a simple video display of the person not suitable for
the task.
A software like this has to compete not only with VR meeting applications but also with
video meetings, which are highly developed by now and offer numerous features to facil­
itate online collaboration.
4.2.3
Potential of the software
This field of science is developing rapidly, including the production of HMDs, VR develop­
ment, and meeting culture. However, for an application like this to become widespread,
it is also necessary for it to become common in workplaces, for example.
Although an application like this may not ever have the same features as a video meeting,
or it is harder to envision. For example, I am thinking of collaboration tools like a white­
board. With proper planning, I am sure that collaboration can be ensured. In the case
mentioned, for example, one solution could be that every physical participant should use
their own PC with an application for this purpose connected to the VR application of the
remote participant. Another solution could be that a physical smart whiteboard, which is
in the meeting room, communicates with a virtual one in the VR environment.
The testing revealed, along with other findings, that the majority of people would welcome
a program like this.

Conclusion
5.1
Status
The app shifted focus from complex, real­time scene modeling to prioritizing immersive
video playback and user interaction. Although the application in its current form is not
capable of displaying meetings in real­time, it was suitable to validate the idea.
5.2
Conclusion
After the attempts to integrate Python and TensorFlow with Unity for machine learning
failed due to technical limitations and time constraints, the study shifted focus to creating
a VR application connecting remote users with the help of video. Unity was used to create
the environment of the video, and a a user­friendly menu system controlled by event
handlers and scripts. That enhance the users overall interaction. Although the application
is definitely not yet complete, it has the potential to open a new era in online meetings,
bridging the gap between physical and remote meetings.
The findings of the test revealed that the participants were highly satisfied with the sound
quality but had mixed feelings about the scene and the naturalness of the people they
saw, with average ratings. Participants found the software easy to use and understand.
No participants experienced motion sickness. They suggested the need for adjustable
settings, this part also implied that the app should demonstrate the functions of the VR
headset and controllers. Four out of five participants expressed a willingness to use the
program. Besides that one participant provided specific feedback about the comfort of the
VR headset, although he noted visible pixels in the video. In contrast, another participant
described the experience as excellent.
5.3
Future work
Although this is not its primary function, it is probably worth creating a 360° video player
to promote and normalize the idea. For this to happen functions, like the ability to select
videos, rewind, pause, and play them must be implemented.
Another aspect is that the development of VR and 3D applications have made significant
progress in the last decade and offers broad applicability, at the same time there is also still
a lot of room for improvement in this field. So hopefully, when Unity will support machine
learning, the presented idea based on AI technology will be easier to implement.
