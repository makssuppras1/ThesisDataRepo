Abstract
The terahertz and high millimeter-wave bands are rapidly developing frequency bands due to
their numerous applications in multiple ﬁelds like spectroscopy [2], Imaging Radar, medical
imaging [3] etc. So, there is a requirement for developing the high performing solid-state power
ampliﬁers (PA) for these applications.
This master thesis reports the design and analysis of 300 GHz integrated power ampliﬁer.
Transferred substrate InP heterojuntion bipolar technology is used. Optimized feedback cas-
code topology was implemented for designing the unit PA cell, which has more output power
capabilities than a cascode topology. Comparison among di↵erent topologies were performed.
A tree architecture was used for making di↵erent stages of PA using equal split Wilkinson
power combiners. ADS and HFSS software are used for analyzing and designing the PA. The
PA has a output power of 13.3 dBm and gain of 17.1 dB at 1-dB compression point. For
stability calculation, K-∆and pole-zero identiﬁcation analysis are performed.

CHAPTER 1
Introduction
The terahertz and high millimeter-wave bands are rapidly developing frequency bands due to
their numerous applications in multiple ﬁelds like spectroscopy [2], Imaging Radar, medical
imaging [3] etc. This is mainly due to their large bandwidth capabilities and high speed data
transfer, 10 Gb/s or higher [4],[5]. The terahertz regime is very attractive for medical imaging
because they can penetrate the living tissues and cells without harming them or the DNA [3].
To implement these applications, there is a requirement for developing the high performing
solid-state power ampliﬁers (PA). It is challenging because the PAs are operated closer to the
maximum frequency of oscillation (fmax) (due to high frequency of operation) and substrate
losses are higher. Selection of transistor technology is very important in designing the PA. High
electron mobility transistors, Metamorphic HEMTs and heterojuntion bipolar (HBT) are some
of the best technologies present for operating at these frequencies [6]. Many of these technologies
uses indium based semiconductor material, as it helps to achieve the highest fmax to date [1].
This project implements the transferred-substrate InP double heterostructure bipolar transistor
at 300 GHz frequency.

CHAPTER 1. INTRODUCTION
Methodology
The unit PA cell (PA1), the basic block of the PA, is designed initially. The PA is selected to be a
Class A ampliﬁer for achieving maximum gain. The cascode topology was implemented for PA1
by taking [7] as reference. But cascode design was not satisfactory in regards to the output
power. To further improve the output power, an optimized feedback cascode topology was
implemented as shown in ﬁgure 1.1. This technique improves the maximum available gain (Gma)
higher than the unilateral gain (U), which is better than neutralization or unilateralization
techniques [8].
This technique is also used in [9] and [10] for designing 0.325-THz CMOS
ampliﬁer and 260 GHz CMOS ampliﬁer respectively.

2.1
Transistor Technology
The transistor technology used for design PA is transferred substrate InP heterojuntion bipolar
technology . This technology has maximum cuto↵frequency (fmax) of 530 GHz and maximum
unilateral gain cut-o↵frequency (ft) of 350 GHz. It has collector break down voltage (BVCEO)
more than 4 V [1].
The schematic cross section drawing of transferred substrate DHBT and traditional triple-
mesa are shown in ﬁgure 2.1. The problem in traditional approach is that there is a parasitic
capacitor e↵ect between the base and collector of transistor. To improve this, the collector
region was place above the base and emitter as shown in ﬁgure 2.1, where the collector contact
area with base is reduced and volume is increased. This setup leads to signiﬁcant reduction of
this parasitic capacitance between collector and base [1].

4.1
Introduction
Ampliﬁer is a two terminal device, used to amplify the source signal by boosting either it’s
voltage, current or power. The major focus of power ampliﬁer (PA) is to get maximum output
power possible with stable output at required frequency range. To achieve this, the PA has to
be operated under large signal operating conditions. This introduces non-linearity in the PA
system, which has to be carefully analyzed and designed as it is responsible for degrading the
system performance. The PA also becomes strongly non-linear if it is operated at 1-dB gain
compression or more [11].
4.1.1
Nonlinearity
Non-linearity is present in all the practical electronic circuit components. The intensity of non-
linearity can vary form very weakly nonlinear to strongly nonlinear. Some of the properties of
nonlinear circuits or systems are: They do not follow the superposition principle and generate
new frequencies apart from the frequencies present in the input signal [12].
These nonlinear systems can be analyzed by using di↵erent approaches. The most com-
monly used ones are time-domain (Transient) analysis or frequency-domain analysis.
The time-domain analysis uses the nonlinear di↵erential equations to solve numerically. It
is straightforward and simple. But it is not eﬃcient for higher frequencies and its very time
consuming for steady state analysis [13]. It cannot implement the frequency-domain quantities
(like S-parameters) and for analyzing multi-tone circuits. The harmonic balance method (which
is a frequency-domain analysis method) is better than the time-domain analysis [13],[12]. These
are some of the reasons which make the frequency domain analysis preferable and hence is used
in many CAD software including ADS from keysight.
Most popular frequency-domain methods are Volterra-series and harmonic-balance (HB).
The Volterra-series is extension and improvement of the power series, which also includes the
phase e↵ects. It is best used for weakly nonlinear circuit analysis.
15

The harmonic balance is used for strongly or weakly nonlinear circuit [12]. It directly solves

ampliﬁer with large signal input. In this project the HB is used for analyzing the PA circuits

Small Signal Analysis
The small signal analysis is performed initially on the PA unit cell to analyze and obtain some
useful information for designing. This can be used to ﬁnd various parameters like two-port
S-parameters and Y-parameters. These parameters can be used for optimizing the PA cell,
calculating the stability of the PA and to ﬁnd di↵erent values like gain, maximum stable gain
(MSG), bandwidth and frequency response of the circuit.
Large Signal Analysis
Large signal analysis has to be performed to include the nonlinear e↵ects of the PA. As dis-
cussed in section 4.1.1, HB analysis is used for nonlinear analysis. It is used to ﬁnd the gain
compression, power added eﬃciency and output power at the required frequency. To ﬁnd the
optimum matching conditions, load pull analysis is used along with HB. All these techniques
are implemented with the help of the ADS software.
Large signal analysis is performed at the circuit schematic level to get an idea for designing
the circuit at the layout. After the layout is designed, it is EM simulated either using ADS
momentum or HFSS. The simulation layout is again analyzed by the large signal analysis to
ﬁnd the results.
Load-Pull Analysis
Load-pull analysis is used to ﬁnd the maximum power gain of PA cell, under large signal
condition, by optimizing the load impedance. The load impedance is varied to produce contours
of constant output power or PAE. These contours can be used to improve the power gain, ﬁnd
the optimum matching network for PA and to understand the trade-o↵of di↵erent parameters
like PAE, gain and output power.

Pole-Zero Identiﬁcation Stability Test
The stability of PA can be veriﬁed by using the K-∆as shown in 4.1.4. The PA is stable with
very high K value and ∆less than 1. But there are some limitations in this test. It cannot
detect the instabilities generated internally, due to odd mode instabilities, spurious oscillations
from feedback loops [23]. This test also performs small signal analysis for ﬁnding the stability.
To overcome this problem pole-zero identiﬁcation analysis can be performed to ﬁnd internal
stability by AMCAD Engineering [23]. It uses the frequency response of a circuit H(jw), either
simulated under large signal or small signal conditions, to ﬁt a rational polynomial transfer
function ( ˆH(s)) and ﬁnd the poles and zeros [24], [25]. To calculate this response, a small
current source is applied at the edge of the ampliﬁer near the dc bias point as shown in ﬁgure
5.16. This source act as small disturbance in the network and is used to study the stability
of circuit under test. If the poles and zeros are on the left half of the complex plane then
the system is stable and if poles are present in the right half of the plane then the system is
unstable.

CHAPTER 6
Conclusion
In this project a class A integrated power ampliﬁer at 300 GHz was designed and analyzed.
Transferred substrate InP heterojuntion bipolar technology is used.
First, the unit cell of
PA was designed. Di↵erent topologies were analyzed, which would provide maximum output
power with good enough gain at 1-dB compression point. Optimized feedback cascode design
was implemented. This technique improves the maximum available gain (Gma) higher than the
unilateral gain (U), which is better than neutralization or unilateralization techniques [8].
The unit cell is termed as PA1. PA2 was designed with two PA1 blocks with matching
network. Matching network was designed to have broader bandwidth. This PA2 was used to
design the tree architecture of di↵erent stage of PA with the help of equal split Wilkinson power
combiner. Unequal split Wilkinson power divider was analyzed and compared with equal split
Wilkinson power divider.
For designing and analyzing the project, Ansys High Frequency Structure Simulator (HFSS)and
Keysight Advanced Design System (ADS) software are used. For stability analysis, K-∆and
pole-zero identiﬁcation analysis were performed. PA is stable at all the operating frequencies.
Final results of PA layout has a output power of 13.3 dBm and gain of 17.1 dB at 1-dB
compression point. The objective of the project is achieved as the output power of PA is more
than 10 dBm.
