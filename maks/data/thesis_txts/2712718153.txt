Abstract
At offshore wind farms inter-array cables (IAC) are protected by a cable protection system
(CPS) to ensure durability against dynamic and static array cable failure and for the IAC
to survive the expected lifetime of the offshore wind farms. However in recent years it has
been discovered that the CPS has proved not to be sufﬁcient and has suffered damages.
Due to the optimization of the design of the scour protection now only having one layer
of stone material, the CPS is now laying on top of the scour protection instead of being
embedded in between the stone layers. In the area where the cable leaves through the
outer wall to be resting on top of the scour protection, the CPS will therefore be exposed
to hydrodynamic loads challenging the design and durability of the CPS.
The aim of this thesis is to investigate the hydrodynamic loads along the CPS in the
area where the cable is exposed through a numerical study. This study will be carried
out by doing numerical simulations using OpenFOAM, which is a free open source CFD
software. The numerical simulations will be performed for a 3D model setup with the
presence of oscillatory ﬂow i.e. regular waves, where results of force time-series and force
coefﬁcients for different ﬂow regimes i.e. Keulegan-Carpter numbers, KC = 10−40, will
be investigated. A validation of the numerical model has been conducted by simulating
different 2D cases of circular cylinders. For the validation, both force time-series and
force coefﬁcients were compared to existing literature and have showed good agreements
between CFD results and existing work. The exposure of the CPS to hydrodynamic loads
have proved to be a more complex case since there are different effects, which also are
being mixed together, affecting the forces and force coefﬁcients CD, CM and CLrms of
the CPS. The investigation showed that the force coefﬁcients are inﬂuenced by effect of
wall proximity both in the area close to the scour protection and close to the monopile.
Furthermore the blockage effect of the monopile and CPS ampliﬁes the ﬂow velocity,
which showed clear inﬂuence on the force coefﬁcients along the CPS in comparison with
2D results, where also the geometry of the CPS is deemed to be of inﬂuence.

Resumé
Ved havvindmølleparker er inter-array kabler (IAC) beskyttet af et kabel beskyttelses sys-
tem (CPS) for at sikre holdbarheden mod dynamiske og statiske array-kabel svigt og for at
IAC’en kan overleve havvindmølleparkernes forventede levetid. I de senere år er det dog
blevet opdaget, at designet af CPS ikke har vist sig at være tilstrækkelig og har lidt skade.
På grund af designoptimeringen af erosionsbeskyttelsen, der nu kun består af et lag sten,
ligger CPS’en nu oven på erosionsbeskyttelsen i stedet for at være indlejret mellem stenla-
gene. I det område hvor kablet går gennem ydervæggen til at den hviler oven på erosions-
beskyttelsen, vil CPS’en derfor blive udsat for hydrodynamiske laster, hvilket udfordrer
CPS’ens design og holdbarhed. Formålet med dette speciale er at undersøge de hydro-
dynamiske laster langs CPS’en i det område hvor kablet er udsat igennem en numerisk
undersøgelse. Denne undersøgelse vil blive udført ved at lave numeriske simuleringer ved
hjælp af OpenFOAM, som er en gratis open source CFD software. De numeriske simu-
leringer vil blive udført for et 3D model setup med tilstedeværelsen af oscillerende ﬂow
i.e. regulære bølger, hvor resultater af kraft tidsserier og kraftkoefﬁcienter for forskellige
ﬂow regimer, dvs. Keulegan-Carpter tal, KC = 10 −40, vil blive undersøgt. En valid-
ering af den numeriske model er blevet udført, som simulerer forskellige 2D tilfælde af
cirkulære cylindre. Til valideringen blev både krafttidsserier og kraftkoefﬁcienter sam-
menlignet med eksisterende litteratur og har vist gode overensstemmelser mellem CFD
resultater og eksisterende litteratur. Eksponeringen af CPS for hydrodynamiske laster har
vist sig at være et mere komplekst tilfælde, da der er forskellige effekter, som også bliver
blandet sammen, som påvirker kræfterne og kraftkoefﬁcienterne CD, CM og CLrms af
CPS. Undersøgelsen har vist, at kraftkoefﬁcienterne er påvirket af vægnærhedseffekter
både i området tæt på erosionsbeskyttelsen og tæt på monopælen. Desuden forstærker
blokeringseffekten af monopælen og CPS strømningshastigheden, som har vist tydelig
indﬂydelse på kraftkoefﬁcienterne langs CPS’en i sammenligning med 2D resultater, hvor
også CPS’ens geometri vurderes at have indﬂydelse.

CHAPTER 1
Introduction
Wind energy is currently and have over the years been an important renewable energy
solution for the transformations of energy systems to become independent of fossil fuels
and with the demand for renewable energy still increasing, wind energy continues to be
on the rise. For instant in Denmark more than 40 % of the electricity production comes
from wind turbines, (Energistyrelsen, 2022).
Wind turbines have both been placed onshore and offshore, though offshore wind farms
have especially proved effective, since offshore the wind speed tend to be faster and stead-
ier than on land, generating more renewable energy, (American Geosciences Institute,
2022). Several offshore wind farms have been constructed over the years with more
projects on its way. However offshore wind farms can also be expensive and complicated
to build, which is also why methods and techniques have been developed to reduce cost
and make production more efﬁcient.
For offshore wind farms, where wind turbines are constructed on monopiles, scour pro-
tection will be placed around the monopile foundation. It has been possible to optimize
the design of the scour protection only having one layer of stone material, instead of
the traditional design of two layers. Furthermore from the monopile an inter-array cable
(IAC) penetrates the outer wall through a special designed angled aperture, where the IAC
continues to a transformer station.
This has simpliﬁed the monopile design, removing need for additional work, for instants
not having to do a more costly approach of installation of J-Tubes. The IAC is protected
with a cable protection system (CPS), to ensure durability against dynamic and static array
cable failure and for IAC to survive the expected lifetime of the offshore wind farms. An
example of a offshore wind turbine on a monopile with penetrating cable, is shown in Fig.
1.1.

CHAPTER 1. INTRODUCTION
Figure 1.1: Example of offshore wind turbine with CPS emerging from monopile and laying on top of the
scour protection, (Balmoral, 2022).
The new design efforts of only having one layer of material for the scour protection, have
meant that the cable is laying on top of the scour protection instead of being embedded in
between the stone layers. The cable now laying on top the scour protection, will therefore
be exposed to hydrodynamic loads, challenging the design and durability of the CPS.
Hydrodynamic loads are forces being imposed on a structure due to the impact of ﬂuid
ﬂow as a steady current or/and waves ﬂowing against and around the structure. Though
if not considered properly into design of the structure, the hydrodynamic loads can cause
damage and problems, which for instant is now the case for cables at offshore wind farms.
Discovered in recent years, the current design of the CPS around the IAC has proven not
to be sufﬁcient in the area where the cable leaves through the outer wall to be resting
on top of the scour protection. For this area, the CPS have suffered being subjected to
increased hydrodynamic forcing and motion against the rocks of scour protection causing
failure and damage to the CPS. This problem has for instance been reported by Ørstad
having the possibility of ten wind farms being affected by this problem and in worst case

Vortex shedding regimes
Having a KC number larger than 7 causes vortex shedding, which will happen during
each half period of oscillatory motion. With increasing KC number different vortex
shedding regimes will occur in different intervals of KC. The regime in the range of 7
< KC < 13 is called the transverse-vortex-street regime, which is also in the range of the
single-pair regime, being 7 < KC < 15. In an oscillatory ﬂow one pair of vortexes will

For the numerical calculations and meshing of model the open source program Open-

discussed, starting with the numerical model.

CHAPTER 6
Conclusion
The aim of this thesis is to contribute and broaden the insight and knowledge of the hy-
drodynamic loads affecting the exposed part of the cable protection system (CPS) for
offshore wind farms. The conducted investigation has been carried out as a numerical
study and through numerical simulations of a 3D model exposed to oscillatory ﬂow ob-
taining results of forces and force coefﬁcients in variation with KC numbers of 10 to 40.
Furthermore also results of force coefﬁcients for a 2D model have been determined for
investigation of the relation between 3D and 2D results. Though ﬁrst a validation of the
setup of the numerical model was conducted.
The validation of the numerical model, simulating different 2D cases of a circular cylin-
der, has showed good agreements with compared literature. For the validation both force
time-series and force coefﬁcients were compared to existing literature. Regarding the lift
coefﬁcients it appeared that it has been more of a challenge for the numerical model to
simulate a good estimation of the lift coefﬁcients related to the vortex environment, both
for the case of a free cylinder and for a cylinder close to the wall. The overall performance
of the numerical model has proven it self to be satisfactory. It further gives conﬁdence
with given validation that the numerical model has the abilities to accurately enough pro-
duce and simulate the environment and effects around the CPS to which it is exposed.
For the results of force coefﬁcients along the CPS, have showed that with increasing KC
numbers that both drag and lift coefﬁcients are decreasing and the inertia coefﬁcients are
increasing. The overall behavior of CD, CM and CLrms have showed similar develop-
ment compared to literature concerning proximity of a wall. Though also showed by the
investigation for all KC cases, are a large variation of the force coefﬁcients depending
on which part is being considered. The CPS have been divided into ten equal sized parts,
part 1 being close to the scour protection and part 10 being close to the monopile.

The investigation of the CPS has shown to be a more complex case since there are dif-
ferent effects, which are also being mixed together affecting the forces and the associated
force coefﬁcients of the CPS. Furthermore the analysis has shown that the effects magni-
tude of inﬂuence will vary along the cable, since the exposed part of the CPS is stretching
a longer distance and therefore experience a larger variation of the results.
The ﬁrst effect was reﬂected in the investigation of the variation of the ﬂow velocity. It
was found that the presence of the monopile creates a blockage effect, which ampliﬁes
the ﬂow around it. The ampliﬁed ﬂow velocities measured increases going from part 1
to part 10 where last mentioned experience the largest ampliﬁed ﬂow velocity. The mea-
sured maximum velocities were compared with determined potential ﬂow, which showed
a good agreement, being in the same order of magnitude. Besides the monopile, the pres-
ence of the CPS itself also creates a blockage effect further amplifying the ﬂow velocity.
The effect of the ampliﬁed velocity were also found to change the behavior of the lift
force, reﬂecting a different vortex environment around the CPS, than what was expected
at a certain KC number.
Furthermore for the investigation of forces and the related force coefﬁcients in variation
with KC, showed to be affected by the effect of wall proximity. Part 1 to 6 reﬂect in
greater magnitude to affected by wall proximity of the wall. For the ﬁrst three parts of
the CPS the largest magnitude of drag, inertia and lift coefﬁcients are showed. Here after
the force coefﬁcients in general decreases with increasing gap ratio. For part 7 and 8 the
force coefﬁcients are found to have a more stable behavior, where effects of wall proxim-
ity would reﬂect to be of smaller signiﬁcance. Last part 9 and 10 reﬂect to be inﬂuenced
by the proximity of the monopile. The drag and lift coefﬁcients have showed to decrease
being close to the monopile, where as the inertia coefﬁcients slightly increases. For part
10 the lowest magnitude of drag and lift coefﬁcients have been found.
Lastly the force coefﬁcients of the 3D model have been compared to force coefﬁcients
obtained with a 2D model. The 2D model has the same numerical setup and gap ratios
representing to be at the middle of each part to strive for the same effect of wall proximity.
Though for the 2D model there is no presence of the monopile and the geometry is that
of a horizontal cylinder. The comparison of CD, CM and CLrms showed differences for
all three relations of force coefﬁcients. The relation showed, that for both models having
small gap ratios, being affected by wall proximity, the difference between the two models
was found to smaller in magnitude. Though reﬂected for all three force coefﬁcients, the
results of the 3D model are showed to be inﬂuenced by the ampliﬁed ﬂow velocities.

6.1
Further work
In the following section suggestions are made to which could be interesting for further
investigations of hydrodynamic loads on cable protection system for offshore wind farms.
The work done in this thesis have been carried out as a numerical study investigating the
exposed part of the CPS. Though no previous research have been found for comparison
and to support the numerical ﬁndings. For further work it could therefore be of interest if
physical experiments were to be performed on the same or a similar model setup, to com-
pare and support the numerical ﬁndings of this numerical study, but also for any future
numerical investigations.
For the setup of the numerical 3D model, the scour protection has been modelled as
a ﬁxed wall, where the surface roughness could be setup with the nutkRoughWall
Function. Though in reality the scour protection would not behave as a ﬁxed wall.
For further work it could therefore be of interest if the scour protection were modelled
as a porous media to investigate how this would inﬂuence the forces to which the CPS is
exposed.
In this thesis the numerical setup of a 3D model were analysed as a static problem, having
no movement of the CPS. However with the hydrodynamic forcing to which the CPS is
exposed, may cause movement of the CPS. For further work it could be interesting to
investigate the 3D model with a dynamic setup for examining motion of the CPS and how
this would inﬂuence the forces acting on the CPS.

APPENDIX E
Validation - Missing lift force
For the validation case of e/D = 0 a gap in the mesh will be present as is seen in Fig. E.1.
This will create a problem for the Force function used in OpenFOAM when determining
the total lift force, missing a surface at the bottom. In the top of Fig. E.2 an example of
the lift force time-series is shown when the lift force at the bottom is missing.
Figure E.1: Gap in mesh for the validation case of e/D = 0, with marking in pink illustrating probe
