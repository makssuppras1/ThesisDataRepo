Abstract
Abstrakt
Preliminary dimensioning of the mechanical system is ﬁrst carried out on the basis
of similar manipulators used in the industry. Afterwards, kinematic modelling of the
multi-body system is formulated, position,velocities and accelerations are numerically
computed. The results from kinematic analysis are used to compute by kinematic in-
version the forces exerted on to the links of the mechanical system. The results from
the dynamics of the mechanical system are used to select the hydraulic actuators, and
the rest of the components of the hydraulic system. A preliminary estimate, of the eﬃ-
ciency of the system is computed. After having selected the rest of the components, the
hydraulic system is modelled and simulated in a matlab code. A very simple position
proportional controller is also added to the hydraulic model. Finally the mechanical and
hydraulic models are coupled in order to simulate also the eﬀects of the inertial loads
on to the actuators. Results for diﬀerent operating conditions are presented.

Introduction
Hydraulic powered machineries are becoming ever increasingly used in the industry. As
the use of such machines have started to spread to new sectors of the industry than the
classic ones such as agriculture and building yards, with the use of excavators,tractors,
loader cranes and a endless variety of hydraulic powered machines and tools.
Such
machines usually require specialized personnel with certiﬁed experience to be operated.
While on the other hand in many industrial activities a complete automation level has
been achieved. The most of these industrial manipulators, present in manufacturing
lines, are robots, actuated by means of electric motors. While it is still not so common
to ﬁnd hydraulic actuated manipulators.
Nonetheless the use of hydraulic manipulators, especially for heavy-duty purposes, in
some manufacturing activities like forging or casting of large parts, for mass production
or for very precise manufacturing robots, is sporadically found .
In this study will be proposed a kinematic synthesis and analysis of an industrial manip-
ulator, for heavy-duty purposes. The analysis of the multi-body system will be carried
out with the use of constrain equations,[1] that will be solved by means of numerical
methods with the use of a computer program. Afterwards by means of kinematic inver-
sion will be determined the forces acting onto the kinematic links, that will be used as
input for the design of the hydraulic circuit. This will allow to put on a model of the
hydraulic system whose response can be modeled, including a friction force acting on
the hydraulic cylinders. Friction force plays an important role, because by means of the
lubricating action exerted by the oil, it converts the dry friction into viscous damping.
A certain compressibility of the hydraulic oil, will also be taken into account, in the
modeling of the system, by including the bulk modulus.

1.2.1
Modeling of the Multi-Body Mechanical System
To model a mechanical system, it needs to know, the number of coordinates necessary
to describe the motion of the system. Such number is also called, degree of freedom or
(DOF) . For a mechanical system with n(d,o,f), are needed n independent coordinates.
Such coordinates can have a length or can be dimensionless, like angles or direction
cosines.
A number of independent coordinates which is in the same number as the
(DOF) of the system, is called a set of independent coordinates. If the mechanical system
has also dependent coordinates these last ones can be found, by solving the constraint
equations as a function of the independent ones[1]. The (DOF) is an important piece
of information, since it corresponds to the number of actuators or motors required to
move the system. Or equivalently it says how many driving constraints equations are
needed. In order to determine the nd,o,f of a mechanical system, generally the following
two equations are used.

Conclusions
Conclusions
In the ﬁrst part after having deﬁned the requirements and main dimensions of the me-
chanical system it was created a multi-body model in order to calculate the kinematics
of the system. By deﬁning the constraint equations, was ﬁrst determined the Jacobian
matrix of the system. Than it was found the right hand side of the velocity vector, by
diﬀerentiating the constraint equations with respect to time. Afterwards, diﬀerentiating
twice the constraint equations with respect to time, it was found the gamma vector. At
this point by means of the Newton-Raphson algorithm, it was calculated the position,
speed and accelerations of the system. The results obtained in the kinematic analysis,
were now used to carry out the inverse kinematics. Consisting, in determining the forces,
that cause a particular mechanical system to execute a speciﬁc or desired trajectory or
path. By means of inverse kinematics were found the forces acting on to the actuators,
these were used to select the proper size of the pistons and the cylinders. Afterwards
the rest of the hydraulic system was deﬁned and a ﬁrst estimate of the eﬃciency was
computed. A modelling of the hydraulic system was also made and the coupling between
the mechanical system and hydraulic-system was carried out. The simulations of the
coupled system, highlighted the need to ﬁnd the correct settings of the initial conditions
vector of the hydraulic system and the mechanical system. As in some occasions the
mismatch between the initial conditions in the two systems brought to abrupt the sim-
ulation. Moreover was also highlighted, the eﬀect of the tuning of the crack pressure
and spring stiﬀness of the pressure relief valve. The simulations also revealed the eﬀect
of the pressure drop settings in the pressure control valves. Finally a basic position
controller with proportional control was deﬁned. This provided an information of the
error between the desired position and the actual position, executed by the actuator.
It also shows that in order to have, position accuracy, a more advanced PID controller
would be better suited.
