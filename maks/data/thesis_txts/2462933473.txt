Abstract:  
In this project, Chitosan was opted as a potential material for drug delivery application due to its numerous 
applications like non-toxic, biocompatibility, and various antibacterial effects. Chitosan- based drug 
loaded nano fiber mats are successfully spun by using electrospinning technique. Electrospinning has been 
used extensively to synthesize nanofibers for various applications because it allows the precise control 
over the composition, structure, and mechanical properties of the resulting biomaterial. Chitosan fibers 
are spun with poly-ethylene oxide (PEO) and pullulan blends with various ratios for resulting stable and 
uniform fibers. Aqueous acetic acid 90% was chosen as a solvent and for stable fibers collection various 
parameters like polymer solution concentration, temperature, flow rate, distance between tip and collector, 
shear thinning, needle gauge and voltage are optimized. Initially experiments were performed in optimum 
process parameters that could form uniform fibers. These as-spun mats were investigated by 
morphological studies like optical microscopy and scanning electron microscope (SEM). The 
morphological studies showed the differentiation of fibers and nano particles, alignment of spun fibers 
and fiber diameter. This study also focused much on fiber collection to collector or electrode and spun 
mat detachment for further studies. Stable fiber formation, increased surface area, uniform alignment and 
separation of mat from collector are seen more in temperature thinning controlled parameter. The better 
chitosan combination is opted and further used for drug encapsulation of amoxicillin. This drug loaded 
spun mats are cross linked by glutaraldehyde for increasing mechanical strength of fibers. Fast degrading 
test is also carried out with various fiber mats for finding strength of mats. The study was further extended 
to invitro drug release studies through µdissolution. The results could be explained due to presented 
defects such as characterization procedures and undetachable fiber mats from collector. 
 
Keywords: Electrospinning, Chitosan Nanofibers, Amoxicillin, drug delivery, Encapsulation, µdiss, Drug 
release studies and Data Analysis.

1.0 Introduction:  
In recent few decades, nano sized materials are becoming more popular in the field of advanced drug 
delivery system because of their excellent properties and wide variety of potential applications. Various 
polymer nano size fibers or particles are showing interesting features such as higher volumes with higher 
surface areas, convenient processing and robustness .Therefore it can be easy to use in vast range of 
applications like tissue engineering, drug delivery, biomedical products, wound dressing materials, 
artificial tissues and organs etc. (1)  
These synthetic and natural polymer-based fibers are fabricated by using electrospinning techniques at 
various micro/nano diameter range. This is simplest and least expensive prominent technique that use 
electrostatic forces for spinning the fibers from polymer solution. When polymer solution is run under 
high voltage a charged jet is formed with small bubble (Taylor cone) and continues towards charge 
collector with evaporation and solidification which forms thin micro scale fibers. (2) 
Chitosan is derived from chitin as N-deacetylated product. Chitin is second most abundant product found 
in various living organisms like shrimps, crabs, and insects.  chitosan is a linear polysaccharide composed 
of β-(1-4)-linked D-glucosamine and N-acetyl-D-glucosamine units. Basically D-glucosamine units plays 
a vital role to form chitosan when Degree of deacetylation (DDA) is greater than 50%. Chitosan is the 
only natural polymer with positively charged polysaccharide with unique properties like biocompatibility, 
biodegradability and nonantigenic. Chitosan can be used for various formulations like liquid gels, 
powders, beads, films, tablets, capsules, microspheres, sponges, 3D scaffold materials, and nanofibers. (3) 
In recent years, the number of reports and publication are significantly increase on chitosan nanofibers. 
Due to its cost effectiveness, simplicity, high efficiency and reliability chitosan nanofiber research market 
has been growing to obtain better fibers with high range of applications. The research on chitosan 
nanofibers with different co polymer, combinations, parameters and conditions are investigating to its 
excellent features on various applications. (1)

1.1 Electrospinning: 
Electrospinning was first observed in 1897 and patented in the year 1934 by Formhals. It has been used for 
more than a century yet is under research and development in the fabrication of continuous Micro/Nano 
fibers. The term “electrospinning” derived from “electrostatic spinning”. Electrospinning is a process which 
uses an electrical charge to draw a several micro or Nano scale fibers from a polymer solution. It is a 
technique for electrostatic fiber formation which utilizes electrical forces to produce polymer fibers with 
diameters ranging from 2 nm to several micrometers by using polymer solutions of both natural and 
synthetic polymers. Electrospinning shares characteristics of both electro-spraying and conventional 
solution dry spinning of fibers. This is the major process particularly suited to the production of fibers 
using large and complex molecules. From the past 2006 and recent years, the electrospinning process has 
enormous attention. The number of scientific publications about electrospinning are increased from 2006 as 
shown in figure 1. (4,5)

growth in reported work for key words like “chitosan” and “electrospinning” in Science Direct.

1.1.1 Setup:    
The basic electrospinning setup has 3 major components as shown in figure 2.  
1) Spinneret: A metallic needle or capillary tube connected to high voltage supplier. It is necessary to use 
needle for obtaining nano fibers. There are various sizes of needles that are used for spinning. (6) 
2) High voltage supplier: It is very often DC direct current voltage is used from range of (1-30 kV) to 
generate electro spun fibers. Alternative current is also used widely, mostly these voltage suppliers are 
with controllable in flow of current towards liquid. (6) 
3) Ground collector: This is metallic plate or electrode that is connect to neutral for collecting fibers. There 
are various kind of collector like metallic plate or screens, rotatory drum, disc collector, and conductive 
bars separated by a non-conductive gap etc. For achieving desirable pattern of fibers like random or 
aligned, collecting devices plays vital role in specific arrangement of accumulated fibers. (7) 
 
Figure 2: Schematic of a typical electrospinning apparatus, including: (1) spinneret, (2) voltage 
supply, and (3) ground collector. (13)

1.1.2 Fundamental process and principle:  
When a high voltage is applied to a liquid droplet, the body of the liquid becomes charged, and electrostatic 
repulsion counteracts the surface tension and droplet is stretched, at a critical point a stream of liquid 
erupts from the surface. This point of eruption is known as the Taylor cone. If the molecular cohesion of 
the liquid is sufficiently high, stream breakup does not occur (if it does, droplets are electro sprayed) and 
a charged liquid jet is formed. As the jet dries in flight, the mode of current flow changes from ohmic to 
convective as the charge migrates to the surface of the fiber. The jet is then elongated by a whipping 
process caused by electrostatic repulsion initiated at small bends in the fiber, until it is finally deposited 
on the grounded collector as shown in figure 3. The elongation and thinning of the fiber resulting from 
this bending instability leads to the formation of uniform fibers with nanometer-scale diameters. (4) 
 
Figure 3: Image showing formation of Taylor cone. Reproduced by permission from Han et al., Polymer 
49, 1651 (2008). Copyright 2008 by Elsevier Ltd. 
1.1.3 Electrospinning parameters:  
Nanofibers are influenced by different parameters and all these parameters are divided into 3 sub-groups. 
Those are: 
Solution parameters: Polymer concentration, viscosity, surface tension, conductivity, dielectric constant, 
solvent volatility. 
==PAGE:10==
10 
 
                                                                  
 
Process parameters: Solution feed rate, type of collector, nozzle diameter, orifice diameter, voltage, 
collecting time and distance between tip and collector. 
Environmental parameters:  Temperature, humidity, atmospheric pressure. (7) 
Controllable parameters with possible problems in electrospinning: 
There are various problems that appears in electrospinning of nanofibers. Too high polymer concentration 
will not allow to form fibers due to its high viscosity and polymer properties like type of solvent used to 
form spinning solution, ratio of various polymers also differ in fiber formation. If voltage rate is high leads 
to arching of fiber formation, too low will not form fibers. Meanwhile flow rate also forms globules or 
beads if it is high and inconsistent fiber formation is seen when it is low. Distance between needle to 
collector and needle diameter also effects fiber formation. Each of these parameters can be controlled by 
various suitable techniques to form uniform fibers. (8)  
There are various potential defects that may be observed in the electrospun nanofibers some of them are 
beads that are formed due to parameters like increased viscosity of fluid see figure 4, pores from bubbled 
solutions, heterogenous fiber diameters, and unstable wet fibers that are undetachable from the collector. 
Various parameter that are optimized such as solution viscosity, applied electric field, solution feed rate, 
inner diameter of the needle, temperature etc. to obtain homogenous fibers. (9)

1.1.4 Applications of Nanofibers: 
Electro spun nanofibers are produced in lab scale and industrial scale with huge interest due to its 
interesting features like small diameter with higher surfaces, volume rate is high in units and easy to 
handle. The process of making nanofiber from electrospinning is attractive due to cost effectiveness, 
attractive with various applications and most of the parameter are controllable for obtaining stable form 
of end products. Due to all these applications nanofibers are growing in various fields and market with 
advanced benefits.(10, 11, 12) 
This electro spun nanofiber are used in most advanced fields like tissue engineering, drug delivery, 
cosmetics, wound dressing, filters in both micro & nano, medical prostheses, coating films, medicated 
films, protective cloths and various other fields like textile industry and industrial based applications as

1.2.2 Polysaccharides: These are derived from biopolymers with numerous applications in medical and

chitosan electrospinning that are used in this project for producing electrospun nanofibers. (15, 16) 
1.3 Chitin and Chitosan: Chitin is a second most abundant polysaccharide found in living organism 
such as shrimps, insects, crabs, lobster and marine worms etc. after cellulose. Chitin is poly-N-acetyl-d-
glucosamine with lesser amount of glucosamine units linked by bonds. Chitin is derived as three 
polymorphs, α, β, and γ forms, based on source of origin. From these α- chitin is most abundantly found 
in living organisms such as fungal and yeast cell walls, crabs, shells, shrimps, lobster and insects. β-chitin 
are found less abundantly like in marine worms. γ form is variant of α- chitin was found occasionally. (17) 
Chitosan is derivative of N-deacetylated of chitin. Structurally, chitosan is a linear polysaccharide 
composed of β-(1-4)-linked D-glucosamine residues with a variable number of randomly located N-
acetylglucosamine units. (see figure 5). Most of the bio polymers are depended on the D-glucosamine 
degree, commonly known as degree of deacetylation (DD). (DD) is calculated on ratio of glucosamine to 
sum of glucosamine and N-acetylglucosamine. When (DD) is greater than 50% usually referred as

1.3.1 Electrospinning of chitosan: 
Electrospinning of pure chitosan has huge limitation due to its polycationic nature, intermolecular bonds, 
and rigid polysaccharide chain. The strong hydrogen bonding between chitosan polymeric chain leads to 
high viscosity and break the jet flow of the solution which leads to breakage of fibers. Additionally, 
various effects like repulsive forces between iconic group on the polymer backbone results polymeric 
beads in fiber. However, there are few papers reported on electrospinning of pure chitosan at lower 
molecular weights and lower DD see table 1. At higher DD and molecular weight, some paper reported 
successful spinning of chitosan fibers, but they used harmful, toxic, and corrosive solvents trifluoroacetic 
acid (TFA) and dichloro methanol (DCM) see table 1. (13, 17)

There are many co-polymers such as polyvinyl alcohol (PVA), poly-ethylene oxide (PEO), poly-lactic 
acid (PLA), nylon6, collagen, polyethylene terephthalate (PET) and pullulan etc. that are blended with 
chitosan to form nanofibers. These co-polymers help chitosan to disrupting the self- association of heavy 
polysaccharide chain and hydrogen bond formation between chitosan to hydroxy (OH) groups and water 
molecules. Commonly, PEO is seen as better co-polymer with chitosan at higher concentrations and it 
reduces viscosity of chitosan.  Z. Qin et al. (2019) successfully fabricated Chitosan- pullulan based fast 
dissolving film. Similarly, Park et al (2004) produced nanofibers by combining with natural polymers 
such as nylon6, gelatin, collagen, silk fibroin etc. These studies proved chitosan can produce stable 
nanofibers when blended with other polymers compared to pure chitosan. 
In chitosan electrospinning, solvent plays a vital role in fabrication of nano fibers. Highly concentrated 
acetic acid (10-90%) are most frequently used solvent due to its ability to decrease surface tension and 
concurrently increase the jet charge density without effecting solution viscosity. Similarly, TFA and 
combination like DCM break the salts with the amino groups of pure chitosan which leads to destruction 
of rigid interactions. Range of solvent is very important to obtain chitosan based nano fibers and some of 
the solvents are mentioned in table 1. ( 20, 21) 
For homogenous bead-free fibers from chitosan, electrospinning needs to be carried out at various 
conditional parameter based on their fiber morphology. Some of the basic conditions are voltage, distance 
between tip of syringe and collector, flow rate, molecular weight and temperature. Most of chitosan fibers 
are spun at different voltages like 15-20 kV, the range of current used in chitosan spinning is 1-30 kV. 
Distance between tip and collector would varies from 7-20 cm and flow rate 0.1 – 2 mL/hr. Temperature 
varies from solution preparation and spinning room, mostly temperature is maintained at room 
temperature to 45oC. In general chitosan electrospinning is very sensitive to handle and all these 
parameters need to be optimized to find best fit for stable spinning combination. Few of papers like 
‘Sangsanoh and Supaphol’, ‘Li and Hsieh’ faced problems in electrospinning of chitosan with acetic acid 
solution. These are claimed, due inappropriate conditions and molecular weights of chitosan. (13, 20, 7, 
4)

successful with copolymer like PEO, PVA, collagen and nylon-6 etc. at appropriate conditions. the

1.4 Drug Encapsulation of nanofibers:  
From various publication studies drug loaded nano fiber mats prefer direct electrospinning of polymer and 
drug suspension blends see figure 6 as drug encapsulation of nanofibers. Most of them use this polymer 
and drug suspension blend due its vast benefits like easy, scalable technique and possible to achieve 
sustain drug release as compared to post spinning drug loaded constructs like adsorptive drug loading. It 
is acceptable that blending polymer solutions with drugs exert some affect to properties of electro spun 
fibers. In A.M. Piras studies dissolution of diclofenac sodium in a polymer solution of n-butyl hemi ester 
of poly (maleic anhydride-alt-2-methoxyethylvinylether) in acetic acid as solvent changed the electrical 
conductivity of the solution. But this also helped in the decrease diameter of electrospun fibers and due to 
higher elongation forces leads to increase in charge density on surface of electrospun   spun jet. (71, 72)

1.5 Cross-linking techniques of nanofibers: 
The cross-linkers with free amine groups present in their polymer backbone are errand to cross-link with 
chitosan chains. These are some of cross-linkers that are used in previous studies like resimene, 
epichlorohydrin, genipin, hexamethylene 1,6-di (carboxysulfonate) and Glutaraldehyde (GA) etc. Based 
on the structural integrity of chitosan or chitosan blend polymers cross-linking can be done both pre and 
post fabrications. Cross-linking nanofibers has various advantage like increase in mechanical strength and 
biodegradable rate in aqueous state that are used in drug release studies. Glutaraldehyde (GA) vapor are 
used in post fabrications process for crosslinking chitosan and poly blends. Cross-linking with GA tends 
to increase in diameter and brittleness of nanofibers. (13, 33)

The main aim of this project is to form amoxicillin encapsulated chitosan nano fibers.

3.2.1 Shear and temperature thinning effect:  
All the powder containing solution CS/PEO, CS/PULL and their blend solutions are prepared under shear 
and temperature thinning effects. A magnetic stirring hot plate (IKA RCT basic) was used for dissolving 
all the polymer powder solutions and blend solutions at different temperatures and speed see figure 8. The 
stir bar had a rotational speed of (0-1500 RPM) and temperature up to 120oC. Each solution was mixed 
under magnetic stir bar where the stir bar gives shear force to the inside viscous liquid and reduce the 
thickness of solution by thinning effect. Concurrently increase in temperature also helps in uniform mixing 
and thinning effect (see temperature parameter in table 2 & annex 1).

3.3 Electrospinning setup and process parameters:  
The electrospinning setup was made in the closed room platform model, provided with thermostat for 
temperature control inside the room. Inside the spinning room a ground wire connected metallic screen 
collector is placed with support of plastic plate and behind temperature sensor is placed. This ground 
collector can wrap with aluminum foil for every new batch. The spinning solution was filled in plastic 
syringe (1-10 mL) and metallic tip was connected with various needle gauge (like 20, 21, 22). Then this 
syringe is placed in pump (Aladdin-1000 from World Precision instrument USA) and the needle tip is 
connected to high voltage power supplier (Glassman High voltage INC Series FJ). This syringe pump has 
an option to control the flow rate of liquid by mL/hr. ratio. The whole setup is show in figure 9. 
Once the polymer solution is prepared for spinning, the spinning solution can be loaded in the syringe. 
Then liquid can be adjusted manually by pushing until the solution reach the needle tip and this will allow 
the air bubbles to settle in another end of syringe. This loaded syringe is placed in the pump with lock 
which hold the syringe when pump started working 
==PAGE:28==
28 
 
                                                                  
 
Figure 9: Electrospinning setup with various other parts in laboratory 
   
 The electrospinning parameters are different from each blend combination to obtain defect free fibers. 
All the spinning combinations are mentioned in table 2 & annex 1 with various parameters. Then pump 
needs to be started for spinning where the fibers get collected on aluminum sheet.  
The spun fibers will get collected on aluminum foil present in the collector. All these fiber mats need to 
be separated from foil sheet and subsequently done characterizations. But spun fibers are sticky or very 
thin to collect it from foil paper. It is impossible to separate the spun sheet without damaging them. For 
microscopic characterization fibers are collected freshly on glass slide and used for preliminary 
characterization on compound microscope. By tuning various process parameters fibers are collected and 
characterized for stable fiber where the mats can be detachable from foil sheet (see annex 1 detachable 
fiber mats refers to positive electro spinnability).  
In order to produce spun mats with suitable thickness and mechanical resistible to handle, each batch must 
spin for 1hr at least 1mL polymer solution. The mats were spun carefully with stable parameters and 
further used for characterization studies. The positive electro spinnability spun mat combinations were 
further used for drug encapsulation.

3.4 Drug Encapsulation and electrospinning of Polymer/drug blends: 
Amoxicillin Trihydrate was drug of choice available as powder for readily dissolvable in water. A 1% wt., 
2.6% wt. and 5% wt. solution were prepared by dissolving amoxicillin powder in the distilled water for 6 
h on magnetic stir plate with stir bar at room temperature see figure10. This drug suspension is mixed with 
co-polymer pullulan at equal ratios (v/v) for 6 h on magnetic stir plate at room temperature as co-polymer 
and drug blend see annex 2. Then co-polymer drug blend is mixed with chitosan solution at equal ratio 
(v/v) and this ensure 50% polymer and 25% pullulan co-polymer with 25% amoxicillin in total 100% 
spinning solution. Polymer and co-polymer drug blends (see annex2) are used for electrospinning, nano 
fiber mats are collected for characterization and drug release studies. Some of these are followed for post 
electrospinning treatments like crosslinking.

3.6 Characterization and release studies: 
The collection of nanofibers samples are done at two different ways. The first way is produced nanofibers 
are collected on microscopic glass slide as samples to find optimum parameters and good alignment. The 
second way is spun fibers are collected on aluminum sheet or silver foil with larger areas and sheets 
contains continues electrospun fibers mats. These sample are Characterized by using optical microscope, 
scanning electron microscope (SEM), differential scanning calorimetry (DSC), Fourier-transformed 
infrared spectroscopy (FTIR) to understand fiber alignment, morphological studies and thermal properties. 
The stability studies and biodegradability of crosslinked and non-crosslinked in aqueous phase like PBS 
buffer solution. In addition, drug release studies are performed by µ dissolution apparatus. The 
characterizing instruments used in this study are presented below with sample preparations. 
3.6.1 Optical Microscope (OM):  
In this project, the optical microscope was used to observe the fiber alignment, beads in spun fibers and 
droplets in the fibers to optimize the process parameters in the initial stage. Chitosan and blend fibers are 
collected on the microscopic glass holder for 1–2 minutes and used for microscopic examination. The 
collected nano fibers samples were observed on Carl-Zeiss Axion scope A1 and capture pictures at 20 X 
and 50 X used for further studies (see annex 3).

tape see figure 12. These sample holders are placed on the specimen holder and inserted in the chamber 
for observations. Images can be observed in SE secondary electron (SE) detector, where possibility to get

for very low angle pictures. In this two-acceleration voltage of 5kV and 15 kV are used for generating 
signals which helps in magnification of samples in three variation low, medium, high magnifications. By

The drug release studies are carried by Pion µDiss profiler in rotating disk intrinsic dissolution rate (IDR)

by filling PBS medium see figure 14. This magnetic disk is used to agitate the suspension at chosen speed. 
Then dissolution test is performed with support of software and results are used for release studies by data 
analysis.

4.3.2 FTIR results: 
FTIR spectra of chitosan powder, pullulan powder and chitosan pullulan nanofiber films were obtained 
spectra’s to confirm the interaction between chitosan and pullulan see (figure 20). In the chitosan powder 
spectra, a broad peak located around 3230 cm−1 was seen to the combination of O-H and N-H stretching 
vibration, this would be affected by hydrogen bonds. The peak around 2776 cm−1 indicated C-H 
stretching vibration. Small characteristic bands of CS were observed at 1565 cm−1 and 1487 cm−1 due 
to C-O stretching vibration and N-H bending stretching vibration. Two peaks at 1317 cm−1 and 1273 
cm−1 reflected CH2 and O-H bending stretching. The region from1100 cm−1 to 800 cm−1was the 
characteristic region for carbohydrates. Peaks at 1053 cm−1, 1050 cm−1 and 1019 cm−1 were refers to 
C-O stretching vibrations. For pullulan powder, the peak at 1140 cm−1was attributed to -OCO- and a 
broader peak around 3100 cm−1 corresponded to -OH. The other peaks at 901 cm−1, 805 cm−1 and 714 
cm−1 were characteristics of α-(1,6) glyosidic bond, α-glucopyranoside unit and α-(1,4) glyosidic bond. 
By comparing the FTIR spectra with fiber samples, intensity of absorption peaks of NH in chitosan 
pullulan blend nanofiber films was stronger than that in pullulan powder state, which proved that hydrogen 
bonds were formed in between polymer chains during electrospinning process. As for peaks around 3400 
cm−1 in Chitosan pullulan blend nanofiber films, a shift to lower wavenumber was observed with the 
addition of chitosan. For a while, OH peak was observed at 3476 cm−1 of nanofibers. This proves that 
hydrogen bonds between OH groups from pullulan and NH groups from chitosan affected the shift of 
band peaks bonding between OH groups of pullulans and NH2 groups of chitosan.

5.0 Conclusion: 
The aim of this project was to produce Chitosan based amoxicillin drug encapsulated nanofiber mats by 
electrospinning process and study drug release effects. To achieve this chitosan/PEO combination was 
chosen as proposal 1 and chitosan/pullulan as proposal2. At first the chitosan/PEO blends are used for 
producing nanofibers using various process parameters. Then proposal 2 was also used for producing 
nanofibers. The best nano fibers combination is used for drug encapsulation. These fibers are analyzed by 
optical microscopy, scanning electron microscopy, visual and morphological studies were carried out. 
Thermal analysis is done by DSC test and crosslinking stability test are performed in aqueous phase. At 
last drug release studies are done by Pion µDiss profiler. All these are performed by optimizing various 
parameters to achieve uniform, stable and bead free fibers. The conclusion for this research study is listed 
below point wise.  
• From above results temperature shifting plays a vital role in fiber spun formation and alignment. 
When spinning solution is prepared at high temperature, solution viscosity controlled & uniform 
compared to room temperature prepared solution. Due to Uniform viscosity of fiber solution, 
acceleration of solution is constant in the syringe, where bubble sprinkles are seen very few in 
compared to room temperature spinning. The increased temperature in solution mix and spinning, 
resulted in fiber elongations & thinning of fiber in different direction from Taylor cone 
continuously and spread in larger surface of mat. 
• By using temperature parameter chitosan 5% has better spinnability at polyblend with PEO and 
Pullulan at (50:50 v/v) with 18-20 kV, 10 cm distance between tip and collector, 35oC at solution 
mixing & spinning, and flow rate 1mL/h.  
• Electrospun nano fiber are formed with Chitosan 5% and pullulan 20% blend by using above 
parameters resulted with bead free, stable and uniform fibers that are detachable from collector 
and possible for encapsulation studies   
• Amoxicillin drug encapsulation was successful with above combination by using drug 
suspension spinning  
• Encapsulated amoxicillin fibers are successfully crosslinked with glutaraldehyde and stability 
studies are successful at aqueous phase.  
• Drug release studies are positive in µDiss profiler by drug absorption curve vs time.
