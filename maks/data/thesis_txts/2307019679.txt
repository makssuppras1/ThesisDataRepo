Abstract
A still rising concern today is the CO2-emission, and the production of cement is responsible for 4.8% of the
global CO2-emission yearly. Furthermore, it is estimated, that the need for cement will be doubled by 2050.
To accommodate this problem coal y ash, produced as a by-product from coal red plants, today is used as a
partial replacement of cement.
However, the Danish government has required a phase-out of all coal red plant by 2030. This results in an
ongoing conversion of the power plants from the use of coal as the main fuel to using other alternative materials
as the main fuel. Alternative materials could e.g. be greenwood, which moreover is considered to be CO2-
neutral, leaving no carbon footprint. But the use of greenwood has some disadvantages. Greenwood tends to
contain large amounts of alkalis, which can lead to corrosion of the kettle at the power plant and thus a reduction
of the electrical eciency of the power plant. To prevent the alkalis from corroding the kettle, injection of coal
y ash could be a possibility, thus leading to a formation of less problematic compounds in the y ash. This
results in an alternative y ash; co-combustion ash origination from wood and coal.
In this thesis three dierent types of ashes were investigated; co-combustion ash originating from wood and coal
from Advedøreværket, traditional coal y ash from Advedøreværket and wood y ash from Herningværket. The
aim was to determine whether the co-combustion ash resemblance the traditional coal y ash, the pure wood y
ash or neither. An assessment of the three types of ashes was conducted and the usage of the co-combustion ash
as a replacement for cement was investigated based on four material parameters; workability, setting process,
pozzolanic activity, and compressive strength. These parameters were also tested for specimens with a cement
replacement with traditional coal y ash and wood y ash for comparison.
From the conducted experiments it could be concluded, that the co-combustion ash behaves as a dierent type
of ash compared to the coal y ash and wood y ash, but with a resemblance to the traditional coal y ash
investigated. Both the co-combustion and the coal y ash were seen to contain numerous of dierent potential
strengthening minerals, whereas the wood y ash was seen to contain a very limited amount of potential
strengthening minerals. Co-combustion ash was seen to have a slightly higher reactivity than the coal y ash,
either because of the small ash particles or the content of organic material. It was thus expected, that both
the co-combustion ash and the coal y ash would have pozzolanic properties. Further, both the co-combustion
ash and the coal y ash complied with the ller limit leading to a lling of the intergranular voids between the
cement particles and thus an increase in the compressive strength.
From the material testing an improvement of the workability was seen when replacing cement with co-combustion
ash compared to a control. Wood y ash is a water reducing admixture, and since it was chosen to keep the
added amount of water constant, the mortar with a replacement of cement with wood y ash obtained a poorer
workability. No signicant dierence was seen in the setting process for mortar with a replacement of cement
with either co-combustion ash or coal y ash compared to a control mixture. The results obtained from a
mortar with a replacement of cement with wood y ash was a bit more ambiguous, which could indicate a
contribution to the compressive strength through the surface chemical level of the ller eect. Mortar with a
cement replacement with co-combustion ash complied with both limits set to determine the pozzolanic activity
of the ashes. Mortar with a cement replacement with coal y ash was argued to comply with both limits as
well and mortar with a cement replacement with wood y ash did not comply with any of the limits. From
testing the compressive strength it was seen, that the mortar with a cement replacement with co-combustion
ash exceeded both mortars with a cement replacement with coal y ash and wood y ash after 28 days of curing

where the pozzolanic reactions take place. Mortar with a cement replacement with co-combustion ash even
exceeded the measured value for the compressive strength of a control mortar at 90 days of curing.
In conclusion it was seen, that the co-combustion ash behaves as a dierent type of ash compared the coal y
ash and the wood y ash, which shows very benecial properties when mixed into a mortar. Previous studies
regarding replacement of cement with co-combustion ash originating from coal and wood did not show such
benecial results. Therefore it is necessary to study this particular co-combustion ash further in order to decide,
what determines these benecial properties e.g. combustion materials, combustion method, and temperature.
This knowledge could subsequently be used to optimise other types of y ashes and this might result in a
more applicable by-product, implementing a more sustainable, economical and holistic perspective on the entire
combustion process.

Sammenfatning
CO2-udledningen giver i dag anlednign til en stadig stigende bekymring. Produktion af cement anses for at
være ansvarlig for 4.8% af den årlige CO2-udledning. Derudover estimeres det, at behovet for cement vil være
fordoblet, når vi når til år 2050. For at imødekomme dette problem bruges kulyveaske, som er et bi-produkt
produceret på kulkraftværkerne, i dag som en delvis erstatning for cement.
Imidlertid har den danske regering krævet en udfasning af alle kulfyrede kraftværker i perioden frem mod år
2030. Dette har resulteret i en igangværende omlægning af kraftværkerne fra at fyre med kul til at fyre med
andre alternative materialer.
Alternative materialer kan fx være greenwood, som desuden antages at være
CO2-neutralt. Men brug af greenwood har også sine ulemper. Greenwood har tendens til at indeholde en
høj koncentration af alkalier, hvilket kan føre til korrosion af kedlen på kraftværket og dermed reducere den
elektriske eektivitet af kraftværket. Korrosion af kedlen på grund af alkali kan blandt andet forbygges ved
at injicere kulyveaske, hvilket vil skabe mindre problematiske forbindelser i yveasken. Dette resulterer i en
alternativ yveaske; sammenfyringsaske stammende fra træ og kul.
I dette speciale er der undersøgt tre forskellige asker; sammenfyringsaske stammende fra træ og kul fra
Advedøreværket, traditionel kulyveaske fra Advedøreværket og træyveaske fra Herningværket. Formålet er at
bestemme, hvorvidt sammenfyringsasken ligner den traditionelle kulyveaske, træyveasken eller ingen af dem.
Der er udført en askekarakteristik for alle tre typer af asker og brug af sammenfyringsasken, som erstatning for
cement, er undersøgt ud fra re materialeparametre; bearbejdelighed, afbindingsforløb, pozzolansk aktivitet og
trykstyrke. Disse materialeparametre er også undersøgt for prøver med en cementerstatning med kulyveaske
og træyveaske for at kunne sammenholde resultaterne.
Ud fra de udførte forsøg kunne det konkluderes, at sammenfyringsasken ikke opfører sig som kulyveasken eller
træyveasken, men som en anden type aske, som dog har ligheder med kulyveasken. Både sammenfyringsasken
og kulyveasken indeholder en række forskellige potentielle styrkeudviklende mineraler, hvorimod træyveasken
har et relativt lavt indhold af potentielle styrkeudviklende mineraler. Sammenfyringsasken havde en smule
højere reaktivitet end kulyveasken, enten på grund af dens små partikler eller indholdet af organisk materiale.
Det forventes derfor, at både sammenfyringsasken og kulyveasken har pozzolanske egenskaber. Desuden over-
holder både sammenfyringsasken og kulyveasken ller grænsen. Dette resulterende i, at de interkrystallinsk
hulrum mellem cementpartiklerne vil blive fyldt, hvilket medfører en forøgelse af trykstyrken.
Fra materialetestene sås det, at bearbejdeligheden blev bedre, når dele af cementen blev erstattet af sammen-
fyringsaske.
Da træyveaske er et vandreducerende materiale, og da det er valgt at holde vandindholdet i
mørtlen konstant, resulterer dette i en dårlig bearbejdelighed for mørtel med en delvis cementerstatning med
træyveaske. Der ses igen signikant forskel på afbindingsforløbet for mørtel med sammenfyringsaske og kuly-
veaske i forhold til en kontrol mørtel. Resultaterne fra afbindingsforløbet for mørtel med træyveaske er en
smule tvetydige, hvilket kunne indikere at træyveasken bidrager til overade kemiske niveau af ller eekten.
Mørtel med sammenfyringsaske overholder begge grænser fastlagt for at bestemme pozzolansk aktivitet. Mørtel
med kulyveaske er argumenteret til at overholde begge grænser, og mørtel med træyveaske overholder ingen
af grænserne. Det ses, at mørtel med sammenfyringsaske opnår en højere trykstyrke end både mørtel med
kulyveaske og træyveaske efter 28 hærdedøgn, hvor de pozzolanske reaktioner begynder. Trykstyrken for
mørtel med sammenfyringsaske overstiger sågar trykstyrken for en ren kontrol mørtel efter 90 hærdedøgn.

Det er blevet vist, at sammenfyringsaske opfører sig som en anden type aske, sammenlignet med kulyveaske
og træaske, med meget gavnlige egenskaber, når den blandes i mørtel. Tidligere studier udført for sammen-
fyringsaske stammende fra kul og træ viste ikke samme gavnlige egenskaber.
Derfor er det nødvendigt at
studere denne sammenfyringsaske yderligere for at kunne fastlægge, hvilke faktorer som bestemmer udviklingen
af disse gavnlige egenskaber. Dette kan f.eks. være materialer brugt ved forbrænding, forbrændingsmetode
og forbrændingstemperaturer. Denne viden vil senere kunne bruges til at optimere andre typer af yveasker,
hvilket kunne resultere i et mere anvendeligt biprodukt og implementere et mere bæredygtigt, økonomisk og
holistisk perspektiv på den totale forbrændingsproces.

Introduction
Today the production of cement is responsible for 4.8% of the global CO2-emission (Olivier et al., 2014) and the
need for cement and concrete is estimated to be doubled in 2050, compared to 2010 (Teknologisk Institut, 2016).
Therefore a solution which de-attaches the CO2-emission from the rising demand for cement and concrete is
needed. One of the solutions is to use alternative commodities in the production such as y ash.
In Denmark, the tradition of using y ash from coal red plants as an alternative commodity to cement in con-
crete, is huge. However, the Danish government requires a phase-out of all coal red plants by 2030 (Rothenborg,
2011) and it is crucial to nd suitable, alternative combustion material for use at the power plants. A solution
is to use biomass e.g. wood instead of coal as a combustion material at the power plants. Furthermore, biomass
is considered to be CO2-neutral and therefore leaves no carbon footprint (Rambøll, 2016). However, using
biomass, such as wood, as fuel has some disadvantages. E.g. wood tends to contain a large content of alkalis
will cause corrosion of the kettle if precautions are not made (Holm, 2016). This use of alternative fuel at
the power plants leads to alternative types of y ashes, which needs to be assessed before use in the concrete
production as an alternative commodity to traditional coal y ash.
The largest power plant in Denmark is Advedøreværket (Rothenborg, 2011), with two blocks fueled by coal and
wood respectively. Low-alkali coal y ash from Asnæsværket are added to the block fueled by wood to avoid the
problematic alkali compounds leading to corrosion of the kettle (Holm, 2016). This results in a co-combustion
y ash originating from both wood and coal.
The purpose of this theses is to investigate co-combustion ash originating from wood and coal from Advedørevær-
ket, together with a traditional coal y ash from Advedøreværket and a wood y ash from Herningværket for
comparisons. An assessment of the three types of ashes and selected properties of mortar containing the three
types ashes will be investigated. The main scope is determining whether co-combustion ash originating from
wood and coal resemblance a traditional coal y ash, a pure wood y ash or neither and an investigation of
whether this type of co-combustion ash originating from coal and wood are suitable for use in concrete.
This report is aimed partly at civil engineering students from the fourth semester onwards and partly at the
ZeroWaste Group at DTU. A certain amount of theory is therefore included, it is however expected that the
reader knows the most essential basic theory regarding for instance concrete, mortar, admixtures and tests
conducted with those materials.
The report are divided into two parts; assessment of the three types ashes and selected properties of mortar
with a 10% replacement of cement with the three types of ashes. An overview of abbreviations used throughout
the thesis is to be found on the following page. The abbreviations will be introduced at their rst appearance.

Theory
2.1
Mortar and Concrete
The main components of cement paste are cement and water, see gure 2.1. Cement is a dry, nely pulverised
material, which is not by itself a binder, but when water is added the cement develops binding properties as
a result of hydration (Mehta, 2006). The cement is called hydraulic when the cement sets and hardens by
chemical reaction with water and is capable of doing so under water according to ASTM C125-15a (2015). The
most commonly used hydraulic cement is Portland Cement (Mehta, 2006).
In this thesis Portland-limestone cement CEM II/A-LL 52.5N (LA) from Aalborg Portland (BASIS AALBORG
Cement) will be used and thus the following theory addresses Portland cement from Aalborg Portland. CEM
II/A-LL describes the cement as a limestone cement containing maximum 20% limestone. 52.5N describes the
minimum strength of the concrete to be at least 20MPa after 2 days of curing and 52.5MPa after 28 days of
curing. (LA) describe the alkali content to be approximate 0.6% (Aalborg Portland, 2012). Portland-limestone
cement CEM II/A-LL 52.5N (LA) will from this point onwards be addressed as CEM II. The strength of concrete
or mortar refers to the compressive strength.
Mortar consists of cement, water and sand, see gure 2.1, creating a composite material. Sand is dened as
ne aggregates, smaller than 4.75mm and larger than 75µm, which derives from either natural weathering or is
produced by the crushing of rocks and stones (ASTM C125-15a, 2015).
Concrete derives when gravel is added to the components of mortar during mixing, see gure 2.1. Gravel is
a coarse aggregate, larger than 4.75mm, deriving from natural disintegrating by weathering of rocks (ASTM
C125-15a, 2015).

2.1.1
Portland Cement
Portland Cement is a cementitious material mainly consisting of calcium silicates originating from the raw
materials used in the manufacturing process. Natural occurring calcium carbonate (CaCO3) can be found in
materials such as limestone, chalk, marl, and sea-shells. Silica (SiO2) originates from clay, sand or shale. Clay
minerals further contains additional impurities such as alumina (Al2O3), iron oxide (FeO3) and alkalies (Na+,
K+). These impurities facilitate the formation of calcium silicate at much lower temperatures than otherwise
possible (Mehta, 2006).
The production of cement can be done by wet process, dry process and semi-dry process. The dry process is
used at Aalborg Portland. When using the dry method all the raw materials are crushed, mixed and fed to a
cement kiln. The raw materials are heated to about 1450°C as it moves through the kiln and carbon dioxide
(CO2) is liberated from calcium carbonate (CaCO3), which forms calcium oxide (CaO). Calcium oxide blends
with the other raw materials included in the mix and forms clinker minerals (Mehta, 2006), see table 2.1.
After heating the clinker minerals are cooled down and pulverised into an average particle size of 10-15 µm.

clinker minerals in order to control the early setting and curing behaviour of the cement (Mehta, 2006). This
will be discussed in section '2.2 Hydration of Portland Cement'.

(ASTM C618-15, 2015).

3.1
Three Types of Ashes
In this thesis tree dierent types of ashes will be studied and compared:
- Coal Fly Ash from Advedøreværket, block 1 (CFA)
- Wood Fly Ash from Herningværket (WFA)
- Co-Combustion Ash from Advedøreværket, block 2 (CCA)
3.1.1
Coal Fly Ash and Co-Combustion Fly Ash from Advedøreværket
Avedøreværket is located on Avedøre Holme in Copenhagen, Denmark. Avedøreværket is the largest power
plant owned by Dong Energy (Rothenborg, 2011) and has a power capacity of 849 MW and a district heating
capacity of 895 MJ/s (Gyldendals, 2016b).
Advedøreværket has two blocks; block 1 and block 2.
Coal Fly Ash from block 1
Block 1 is coal red and produces traditional coal y ash, produced as described in section '2.6 Traditional Fly
Ash'. The coal is burned at approximate 1500°C and block 1 has a furnace outlet temperature of approximate
1150-1200°C. The produced coal y ash is already approved and used in concrete production (Holm, 2016).
Co-Combustion Fly from block 2
Block 2 uses wood pellets. The wood pellets are burned at approximate 1400°C and block 2 has a furnace
outlet temperature of approximate 1275°C (Holm, 2016). The wood pellets contain a high amount of alkalis,
primary potassium, which will lead to corrosion of the kettle if precautions are not made. Corrosion of the kettle
would make it impossible to achieve the maximum steam data (pressure and temperature), thus the electrical
eciency could be reduced as high as 44% which is undesirable (Miljøministeriet, 2010). To prevent acceleration
of corrosion of the kettle a low-alkali coal y ash from Asnæsværket are injected. The mineral content in the
coal y ash reacts with the alkali salts from the wood pellet and forms less problematic alkali compounds with
a higher melting point. This results in a large reduction of the risk of melting the formed alkali compounds,
which would otherwise result in a plastered, melted coating of the inside of the kettle leading to corrosion (Holm,
2016).
Approximate 4% by mass of coal y ash is injected and the wood pellets are estimated to have an ash content
of 1%, which results in 20% by dry mass derived from the co-combustion material wood pellets, categorised as
green wood (Holm, 2016), complying with DS/EN 450-1 (2012). It should further be emphasised that DS/EN
450-1 (2012) states co-combustion ash to originate from ring coal and at least one co-combustion material
simultaneously. This is not the case for the ash produced at Advedøreværket, block 2, but since no specications
are established for this specic type of ash, the ash will be analysed, and referred to, as a co-combustion ash as
described in DS/EN 450-1 (2012).

Wood Fly Ash from Herningværket
Herningværket is located at Herning in the central-western part of Jutland, Denmark. Herningværket is owned

Pure wood y ash is normally not used directly in the concrete production. This is due to a number of dierent
factors, e.g. a high loss of ignition which leads to a low compressive strength (Ottosen et al., 2016).
3.2
Replacement of Cement with Ashes
Determination of the optimal replacement of cement is not easily determined since the optimal replacement
varies with factors such as type of ash used and application. No studies, investigation the optimal replacement
of cement with co-combustion ash originating from coal and wood, could be found, thus the replacement of
cement is based on knowledge from studying the optimal replacement of cement with coal y ash and with
wood y ash.
Replacements of cement with traditional coal y ash is typically around 10-25% relative to the cement-weight,
but it is possible to replace up to 50% of the cement with coal y ash (Mathiesen and Kaasgaard, 2014).
Determination of the optimal replacement of cement by wood y ash is more complicated since the use of wood
y ash as a substitution for cement has not been studied and implemented at the same level as traditional coal
y ash. This is due to the high alkali content and the diculties in predicting how the wood y ash reacts
with the calcium hydroxide produced by the hydration of cement (Siddique, 2012), but some studies have been
conducted and conclusions are described in the following.
Siddique (2012) describes compressive testing of specimens with replacements of 5%, 8% and 12% of the of
cement with wood y ash and with a curing age from 28 to 365 days. The control mixture achieved strength
of 34 MPa at 28 days and 44 MPa at 365 days and strength of concrete mixtures containing wood y ash
ranged from 33 MPa at 28 days to between 42-46 MPa at 365 days. The specimen containing 8% wood y ash
obtained the lowest value of compressive strength after 28 days, but the highest value after 365 days indicating
the highest contribution of wood y ash to pozzolanic activity, is found at an 8% wood y ash replacement.
Abdullahi (2006) tested replacements of 10%, 20%, 30% and 40% of the cement with wood y ash at curing
age 28 and 60 days, and found the replacement of 20% to be most benecial both at 28 and 60 days, compared
to a control specimen. Furthermore, at the curing age of 60 days, the compressive strength of the specimen
containing 20% wood y ash increased considerably from the curing age of 28 days, indicating that greater
strength could be obtained at later ages. According to this study, a replacement of 20% is optimal.

3.3
The Use of Mortar Specimens Instead of Concrete Specimens
According to DS/EN 196-1 (2005) the compressive strength, alongside ow, setting time and strength activity
index, should be measured on mortar specimens, not concrete specimens.
Normally aggregates are considered to be several times stronger than the matrix and the interfacial transition
zone in concrete and thus the inuence of aggregates on concrete strength is not generally taken into consider-
ation. However, as described in section '2.4.2 Aggregate', the strength of concrete is inuenced, among other
characteristics, by the size of aggregates. Larger aggregates tend to weaken the interfacial transition zone. All
though it is not the aggregates themselves that fails, the addition of larger aggregates lead to a reduction of the
compressive strength (Mehta, 2006). When the compressive strength of mortar specimens are tested, according
to DS/EN 196-1 (2005), the strength of the mortar is in general used as a quality control measure of the cement
itself and only gives an indication of what the strength of concrete with the same cement and any admixtures
would obtain.
In this thesis the focus is amid towards investigating how the dierent types of ashes interfere with the compo-
sition of cement and the added water, more than testing the interfering with the aggregates, which is considered
to be limited, making testing of mortar and mortar specimens ideal.

Hydrated cement exists in a state of equilibrium between the solid phase, consisting of relatively insoluble
hydrates, such as Ca(OH)2 and C3S2H3, and a high-pH pore uid. Depending on the concentration of Na+,
K+ and OH−the pH-value ranges from 12.5 to 13.5 (Mehta, 2006). If the pH-value decreases to less than 11.5,
it would destroy the passivity of the environment around any reinforcement and start the corrosion process
(Mehta, 2006). Furthermore, a high pH-value promotes the dissolution of the glass particles in the ash, which
alters into clay-like minerals with same advantageous properties as clay benecial for e.g. the stability of a
concrete structure (Zevenbergen et al., 1999).
A reduction of the pH-value can be caused by e.g. a reduction of the Ca(OH)2-content (Mehta, 2006). By
replacing the some of the cement with CFA, WFA or CCA less Ca(OH)2 will be formed, as a pozzolanic reaction
is expected to take place (Mehta, 2006). This can cause a lowering of the pH-value. A high pH-value of the ashes
itself are therefore desirable as it will contribute to the passivity of the environment around any reinforcement.
From the results displayed in table 4.4 WFA is seen to have the highest pH-value, which was to be expected
for a y ash originating from wood, (Sigvardsen and Bonnerup, 2014), (Østergaard Hansen, 2015). Both CFA
and CCA is seen to have a pH-value below 12.5, but still above 11.5, which makes it safe to assume that the
addition of either CFA or CCA will have no or only limited eect on the environment around any reinforcement,
based on the pH-values. CCA is seen to have a higher pH-value than CFA, which makes it more desirable to
use in concrete. This is supported by Christiansen and Øster (2014), where a likewise high pH-value of 13.1 for
co-combustion ash was obtained. The pH-value for CFA corresponds to results displayed in Zevenbergen et al.
(1999) obtaining a pH-value between 11.7 - 12.4.

disadvantage since this may induce a serious of unknown, uncontrollable reactions in the ash.

could correspond to a higher amount of organic material. According to Liu et al. (2004) and Davison et al.
(1974) smaller particles are more reactive, which could explain, why CCA is more reactive than CFA. This
could also be explained by a higher content of organic material in CCA compared to CFA, thus not as high as
the organic content of WFA.

4.1.16
Summary of Assessment of Ashes
An assessment of the three types of ashes, CFA, WFA, and CCA, has been carried out to determine, whether
CCA resemblance CFA, WFA or neither and if the ashes could be used in concrete.
From the SEM-analysis CCA looked like a mix of CFA and WFA, but with a smaller particle size, which was
supported by the particle size distribution. From the particle size distribution it was furthermore established
that both CFA and CCA complied with category N (DS/EN 450-1, 2012) and the ller limit, thus can be
treated as a ller.
The particle density showed a likeness between CFA and CCA, with a smaller particle
density than CEM II, which was to be expected. CFA and CCA showed a pH above 12, which is preferable
when used in concrete. The buer capacity disclosed a reactive WFA, which was supported by the conductivity
and volumetric content of carbonate disclosing a content of organic material. The CFA and CCA showed a
lower buer capacity, but also a low conductivity and a low volumetric content of carbonate disclosing lower
reactivity for CFA and CCA. CCA, in general, obtained evidence of a slightly higher reactivity compared to
CFA, which can be explained by the content of organic material and the small particles. The water content for
CFA and CCA were low, which makes accounting for it regarding w/c-ratio unnecessary. A low loss of ignition,
complying with DS/EN 450-1 (2012), and a low water solubility was determined for CFA and CCA. The amount
of soluble anions complied with DEPA (2015) for CFA and for CCA, but for all three types of ashes comparison
between water soluble anions and cations revealed an unknown amount of water soluble anions in the ashes.

The content of chemical elements did not comply with the limits set by DEPA (2015), category 1, for neither
of the ashes, but since CFA already is allowed for use in concrete, this is not considered an obstacle. CFA

4.4.1
Comparison of Test Methods to Asses Pozzolanic Activity
When assessing the pozzolanic activity of a material, it is important to take into account the method used.
A variety of dierent methods for assessing the pozzolanic activity of a given pozzolan are described in the
literature, categorised as either direct or indirect methods:
 Direct method: The direct method measures the presence of Ca(OH)2 and its subsequent reduction in
accordance with time as the pozzolanic reaction proceeds in the mixture (Donatello et al., 2010). This can
57
DTU Civil Engineering
==PAGE:74==
CHAPTER 4. RESULTS AND DISCUSSION
be measured e.g. by chemical titration according to the Frattini test described in DS/EN 196-5 (2005).
According to the Frattini test (DS/EN 196-5, 2005) chemical titration is used to determine the amount of
dissolved Ca2+ and OH−in a solution containing CEM I (regular Portland Cement (Aalborg Portland,
2012)) and a pozzolan (Donatello et al., 2010), (DS/EN 196-5, 2005). This test has been used to test e.g.
the pozzolanic activity of y ash (Rahhal and Talero, 2004).
 Indirect method: By using the indirect method, physical properties, such as the compressive strength,
are measured and used as an indication of pozzolanic activity (Donatello et al., 2010), (DS/EN 450-1,
2012). According to DS/EN 450-1 (2012), the pozzolanic activity should be tested by the indirect method
measuring the compressive strength of a specimen containing a 25% replacement of cement with the
pozzolan. This test can also be used to test the pozzolanic activity of a y ash (DS/EN 450-1, 2012).
The direct method is often used to support the indirect method in conrming, that pozzolanic reactions are oc-
curring. Donatello et al. (2010) investigated the correlation between dierent methods for testing the pozzolanic
activity of dierent materials and found a signicant correlation (R2 = 0.86) between results obtained by the
use of the direct Frattini test and the indirect method measuring the compressive strength according to DS/EN
450-1 (2012). When testing for pozzolanic activity by the indirect method, testing the compressive strength,
the ller eect is not taken into account. This could be an explanation, why the correlation found by Donatello
et al. (2010) is not R2 = 1. Since the correlation is signicant anyhow, it was chosen to use the indirect method,
testing the compressive strength, for testing the pozzolanic activity of the three ashes as prescribed in DS/EN
450-1 (2012).

CHAPTER 4. RESULTS AND DISCUSSION
4.6
Summary of Material Properties of Mortar Containing CFA,
WFA and CCA
The inuence of using CFA, WFA and CCA on selected material properties were investigated. The selected
material properties are workability, setting process, pozzolanicity and compressive strength.
The workability was examined through the average ow value, describing the consistency of the mortar mixtures.
In accordance with the theory, the (eq)-mixtures obtained lower average ow values than the (r)-mixtures.
Mixtures containing WFA were seen to obtain the lowest average ow values, indicating WFA to be water
reducing, corresponding with the content of aky particle structures in the WFA. CCA is seen to obtain the
highest average ow value, thus being the least water reducing of the three ashes. A low water reducing ash is
benecial to use in concrete structures, making it possible to maintain or sometimes even lowering the water
content.
No signicant dierence or retardation of the setting process are seen for mixtures containing CFA and CCA,
in conjunction with the described theory. This also leads to the conclusion, that neither CFA or CCA acts as
nucleation sites as described for the surface chemical level of the ller eect, thus does not contribute to the
strength through this level. The setting process for the mixtures containing WFA is more inconclusive since
WFA (r) shortens the setting process and WFA (eq) prolongs the setting process, which was not expected
according to the theory.
This could be explained by the possibility, that a higher amount of WFA reacts
with the cement, when more cement is added, preventing the cement to gure in the hydration process, thus
prolonging the setting process. The inconclusive results obtained from the addition of WFA could indicate a
contribution to the surface chemical level of the ller eect, even though WFA does not comply with the ller
limit, contribution to the compressive strength of mortar.
The indirect method of using the compressive strength to ashes the pozzolanic activity of the ashes, according to
DS/EN 450-1 (2012) was used. Both CCA and CFA were argued to comply with both limits for the compressive
strength at 28 days and of 90 days of curing for at specimen containing 25% y ash. WFA does not comply
with any of the limits. The indirect method of testing the compressive strength does not take into account the
ller eect, which is a disadvantage of using the indirect method compared to a direct method, even though
this is the method to be used according to DS/EN 450-1 (2012).
From the development of the compressive strength CCA (eq) was seen to exceed the compressive strength of
a Control specimen at 90 days of curing. CFA (eq) obtained a slightly lower value than Control and WFA
(eq) obtained the lowest compressive strength, which was to be expected based on the theory and previous
results. Theoretical values for the compressive strength calculated with Bolomeys formula and results obtained
for the compressive strength of CFA (r), CFA (eq), WFA (r), WFA (eq), CCA (r), CCA (eq) and Control were
compared, and both values obtained for CFA and CCA exceeded the theoretical values. An empirical coecient
of activity was calculated based on the measured compressive strength at 28 days of curing. The empirical
coecient of activities calculated for CFA and CCA were higher than the limits set by Herholdt et al. (1985)
and DS/EN 206-1 (2002) and the empirical coecient of activity for WFA was lower. When calculating with
literature a great variation of the coecient of activity was noted and stresses the caution in which Bolomeys
formula should be used, at least in relation to alternative types of y ashes.
By comparing the obtained results from selected properties of mortar specimens containing CFA, WFA or CCA,
it has been noted that CCA behaves as a dierent type of ash, with some resemblance to CFA, stressing the
conclusion made in the assessment of the ashes. CCA has shown better results in all test, compared to CFA
and WFA, making CCA a better admixture to concrete than CFA.

4.7
Suggestions for Future Research
The investigations performed in this thesis have provided a lot of results. This has lead to interesting conclusions,
but also triggered a series of new research elds, that future experiments could seek to explore further.
More additional testing could be conducted according to establish interaction between the ller eect and the
pozzolanic activity of the three types of ashes, and their individual contribution to the compressive strength.
Mortar specimens containing an inert ller would be a good reference to establish how much contribution the
ller eect and thus the pozzolanic activity each contributes to the compressive strength. Furthermore, the
direct Frattini test (Donatello et al., 2010) could be used to determine the pozzolanic activity of the ashes
separately from the inuence of the ller eect. Heat of hydration could be used as an alternative indirect
method since pozzolanic activity is known to inuence the temperature during hydration (Tseng et al., 2005).
The added amount of water to the mixtures could be reassessed according to the high water content of WFA and
it could be pursued to obtain the same average ow value for all mixtures. By repeating the tests the inuence
of the adapted water content would inuence the mixtures signicantly resulting in e.g. a better compressive
strength of the cured specimens.
From the assessment of ashes and material testing, CCA has shown the same qualities or better compared to
CFA, thus making CCA ideal for replacement of cement. Replacements of more than 10% should be tested in
order to investigate the possibility of replacing more than 10% of the cement when using CCA. This would be
favourable since it could lead to a further reduction of the cement production, thus a further reduction of the
CO2-emissions in its connection.
The investigated CCA holds properties, which makes it much more favourable to use as a replacement for cement
than previously studied co-combustion ashes originating from wood and coal (Christiansen and Øster, 2014).
These properties need to be investigated thoroughly. This could e.g. be done by investigating the mineral
composition further through additional XRD-analysis, determining the exact inuence of the materials used for
ignition, type of combustion and the inuence of the combustion temperatures, on the mineral composition.
This knowledge could be used widely for optimisation of the by-product y ash origination from co-combustion
materials or other types of combustion materials, implementing a more sustainable and economical perspective
on the process. This could result in a more applicable by-product, maybe even more applicable than coal y
ash, which is used today.

Conclusion
In this thesis co-combustion ash originating from wood and coal from Advedøreværket (CCA) has been investi-
gated, alongside a traditional coal y ash from Advedøreværket (CFA) and a wood y ash from Herningværket
(WFA) for comparisons.
An assessment of the three types of ashes has been conducted. From the assessment it was concluded, that
CCA behaves as a dierent type of ash compared to CFA and WFA. CCA and CFA are alike, but CCA is in
general seen to be slightly more reactive either due to the small particle size of CCA, compared to CFA and
WFA, or due to the content of organic material. Both CFA and CCA contains a high amount of potential
strengthening minerals, compared to WFA, and it is expected, that both CFA and CCA will have pozzolanic
properties. Further, both CFA and CCA complies with the ller limit set by Herholdt et al. (1985). Thus both
CFA and CCA can be used directly in concrete and mortar without the need for milling, and the particles are
expected to ll the intergranular voids between the cement particles consequently contributing to the strength
of the mortar for both cases. Only according to the content of chemical elements CCA was seen to have a small
problem. The amount of arsenic, cadmium and nickel detected in CCA exceeds the limits set by DEPA (2015),
but since CFA, which is already approved for use in concrete, also exceeds the limit for arsenic and cadmium,
this is not considered to be a signicant obstacle. Further, from the investigation of leaching, it is seen, that
neither of the mentioned chemical elements leaches in alkaline environments.
Four dierent material properties were tested for mortar with a cement replacement with the three types of
ashes investigated: workability, setting process, pozzolanic activity, and compressive strength. The workability
of a mortar with CCA improved, compared to a mortar with CFA and WFA, due to the small particle size. This
makes it possible to lower the amount of water needed for mixing, thus improving the compressive strength.
When analysing the setting process no signicant dierence was seen for CFA or CCA compared to a control.
WFA showed slight inconsistencies, which could indicate a contribution to the compressive strength at the
surface chemical level of the ller eect. CCA complies with both limits for determination of the pozzolanic
activity, exceeding both CFA and in particular WFA. CFA is argued just to be categorised as possessing poz-
zolanic activity. This is consistent with the observations made in the assessment of the ashes. The compressive
strength was tested with mortar specimens with a 10% cement replacement with ash. Again the specimen con-
taining CCA obtained the highest compressive strength at 28 days of curing and beyond, where the pozzolanic
reactions progress, even exceeding the compressive strength of the control specimen at 90 days of curing.
In conclusion it is established that CCA behaves as a dierent type of ash, which is highly applicable as a re-
placement for cement and with a resemblance to CFA. Furthermore, the investigated type of co-combustion ash
showed better properties for use as a cement replacement compared to co-combustion ashes examined in previ-
ous studies (Christiansen and Øster, 2014). These properties of CFA needs to be studied further and compared
to e.g. type of materials used for combustion, type of combustion and burning temperatures. This would make
optimisation of various types of y ashes possible, resulting in a more applicable by-product, implementing a
more sustainable, economical and holistic perspective on the entire combustion process.

Heavy Metals pH-desorption (only acid) 
 
 
A 
Principle 
 
Depending on the type of soil and pollution an acidic or basic effects of the soil 
will cause the change in bonding of heavy metals in the soil. Most heavy metals 
(eg. Cu, Cr, Pb, Zn, Cd) will in most cases be in cationic form, and an acid effect 
will therefore increase the concentration of heavy metals dissolved in the soil 
water - desorbing the heavy metals from the soil. For some metals (eg. As and Cr 
in some cases) will be present on the anionic form, so a basic influence has the 
effect desorption of the heavy metals from the soil. By acting on the ground with 
various concentrations of acid and base, may be examined for how loose or fixed 
heavy metals are bound.
