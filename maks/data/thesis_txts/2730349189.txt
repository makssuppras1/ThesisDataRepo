ABSTRACT
The transition to renewable energy sources and the electrification of key sectors are
necessary steps to achieve sustainable and decarbonised energy systems. In Denmark,
the energy sector is undergoing significant development, including the electrification of
heating, transport and industry. At the same time, the installed capacity of renewable
energy sources, especially solar and wind, is expected to increase, in line with the Danish
government’s target of 100 per cent renewable energy by 2050. As a result, there is a
growing need to increase the flexibility of the electricity grid, which will be crucial to
optimise the use of zero emission energy sources and to effectively manage the inherent
variability of renewable energy.
This thesis analyses the impact of renewable energy integration and electrification in the
region of Funen. The high-voltage electricity network from 400 kV to 150 kV operated
by Energinet is studied. In order to model the effects on the grid, information on the
topology and capacity of the grid is required. Access to publicly available databases and
their subsequent analysis and verification are key steps in modelling the region’s energy
system. Python-PandaPower software has been used to model and simulate the Funen
grid.
The development of future energy consumption and production scenarios in 2040 is also
studied. Load flow simulations are carried out to assess the load conditions that the
network will experience in these predicted scenarios.
Finally, this study aims to identify the resources and mechanisms required to ensure
sufficient network flexibility to accommodate the loads and generation levels expected by
2040.

RESUM
La transició a fonts d’energia renovables i l’electrificació de sectors clau són passos necessaris
per assolir sistemes energètics sostenibles i descarbonitzats. A Dinamarca, el sector energètic
experimenta un desenvolupament significatiu que inclou l’electrificació de la calefacció, el
transport i la indústria. Alhora, s’espera que augmente la capacitat instal.lada de fonts
d’energia renovable, sobretot solar i eòlica. al.linant-se així amb els objectius del govern
danès d’acoseguir un 100% de generació renovable per al 2050. Com a resultat, hi ha una
creixent necessitat d’augmentar la flexibilitat de la xarxa elèctrica, la qual serà crucial per
a optimitzar la utilització de fonts d’energia de zero emissions i gestionar eficaçment la
variabilitat inherent de les renovables.
Aquest Treball Final de Màster analitza l’impacte de que tindran la integració de les
energies renovables i l’electrificació a la regió de Fiònia. S’investiga la la xarxa elèctrica
d’alta tensió, de 400 kV a 150 kV, que és operada per Energinet. Per modelar l’impacte
a la xarxa elèctrica, es requerix informació sobre la topologia i la capacitat de la xarxa.
L’accés a bases de dades que són disponibles públicament, així com també el seu posterior
analisi i verificació són passos clau per a modelar el sistema energètic de la regió. Per a
portar a terme el modelatge i simulació de la xarxa a Fionia s’ha utilitzat el programari
Python-PandaPower.
També s’investiga el desenvolupament d’escenaris futurs de consum i producció d’energia
al 2040. Es fan simulacions de flux de càrrega per avaluar les condicions de càrrega que
experimetarà la xarxa en aquests escenaris previstos.
Finalment, aquest estudi pretén identificar els recursos i mecanismes necessaris per garantir
una flexibilitat de xarxa suficient capaç d’acomodar les càrregues i els nivells de generació
previstos per a l’any 2040.

INTRODUCTION
1.1
Background
European Union (EU) governments are actively pursuing various strategies to align with
the decarbonisation targets set by the Paris Agreement [1]. Electricity generation from
wind and PV is at the heart of the European Union’s energy policy [2]. Denmark, a key
player in this transition, already boasts a net power generation share of 62% from wind
and PV [3].
This commitment to renewables is underscored by Denmark’s ambitious targets. The
nation anticipates a significant increase in both net electricity consumption and renewable
production capacity. Net electricity consumption is projected to surge from 37.4 TWh in
2022 to a substantial 156 TWh by 2040. This growth is concurrent with a remarkable

2.1
Python-PandaPower
In this MSc thesis, Pandapower plays a central role in the study of the Funen power grid in
Denmark. Pandapower is a Python software tool for modelling, analysing and optimising
power systems.
Pandapower is based on two main libraries: pandas and PYPOWER. It effectively
combines these libraries to manage data about power system elements such as buses,
lines, transformers, loads and generators. It also performs complex calculations such as
power flow, optimal power flow, state estimation and short circuit analysis [9]. More
information and documentation can be found on the www.pandapower.org website.
When using pandapower to model the transmission network and perform the time series
simulations, the following features were used:
1. Data and elements: A Pandapower network consists of an element table for each
electrical element in the network. Each element table consists of one column for each
parameter and one row for each element. The following functions have been used to
create the network elements:
• create_empty_network: This function initialises the pandapower data struc-
ture. Creates net, a pandapower attrdict with empty tables.
• create_bus: Adds a bus to the table net[’bus’]. Busses are the nodes of the
network to which all other elements connect.
• create_line_from_parameters: Creates a line element in net[’line’] from
line parameters.
• create_load: Adds a load to the table net[’load’]. All loads are modelled in
the consumer system, meaning that load is positive and generation is negative
active power.
5
==PAGE:24==
2. Methodology
• create_sgen:Adds a static generator to table net[’sgen’]. Static generators
are modelled as positive and constant PQ power.
• create_ext_grid: Creates an external network connection. External networks
represent the higher level network connection and are modelled as a slack bus
in the power flow calculation.
• create_transformer_from_parameters: Creates a two-winding trans-
former in table net[’trafo’]. The transformer parameters are defined using the
standard type library.
2. Time Series Simulation: The Time Series Module is designed to simulate time
based operations and is linked to the Control Module. Time series simulation requires
the following inputs:
• pandapower net. The transmission network in Funen, which was previously
created.
• Time series for loads and generators (in a pandas DataFrame). The methodology
used to create these time series is explained in next sections.
The specific imports required for the time series simulation are as follows:
• ConstControl: This ’constant controller’ is made for use with the time series
module to read data from a DataSource and write it to the network. Two const
controller are added to the grid to update the P and Q values of loads and
static generators. The values for P in MW and Q in MVar for each time step
are stored in a DataSource.
• DFData: The Dataframe DataSource contains the time series to be calculated.
It is the format required by the controllers.
• OutputWriter: Creates the output writer used to store and format specific
output from the time series calculation.
• run_timeseries: Main time series function. Runs multiple PANDAPOWER
AC power flows based on time series stored in a DataSource within Controllers.

External grids
The primary objective of this study is to gain a comprehensive understanding of the
operational dynamics of the Funen electricity network. However, it is important to note
that the load flow analysis does not explicitly model the power flows between Funen and
its neighbouring regions, including Jutland and Zealand. Instead, all connection points
outside the defined boundaries of this study are treated as external networks.
The purpose of including these external networks in the model is to ensure an accurate
representation of power flows and to take into account interactions with neighbouring
networks. In essence, this study focuses on the internal intricacies and behaviour of the
network within Funen, while external connections are taken into account in a way that
facilitates meaningful analysis and evaluation.
For the integration of Funen into the wider Danish transmission network, there are
three interconnection points with Jutland within DK2: Ryttergården, Landerupgård and
Sønderborg. In addition, Funen is connected to DK1 via the Storebælt HVDC link to
Herslev. This information is visually represented in the figure 2.2 above.
It should be noted that the external networks are designated as slack nodes with their
voltage set to 1 per unit.
HVDC line
The Storebælt HVDC system is a 600 MW Line Commutated Converter (LCC) HVDC
system operating at 400 kV. The system consists of the Fraugde converter station on
Funen, which is connected to an existing 400 kV substation, and the recently built Herslev
converter station on Zealand, which is connected to an existing 400 kV overhead line.[11].
It is important to note that Denmark has two separate transmission systems. The eastern
system, DK1, is synchronised with the Nordic grid, while the western system, DK2, is
synchronised with the Continental Europe grid. These two systems have asynchronous
phases and can only be interconnected by HVDC links due to the phase mismatch.
As mentioned above, it was assumed that the HVDC line would be connected to bus 221,
as it has been designated as the slack bus for the system. This decision was made because
this particular bus seems to play a key role within the Funen network, making it a strategic
choice as a slack bus.

3.1
Model validation in 2022
The primary objective of this analysis is to evaluate the loading conditions of the systems
during the year 2022. With that purpose, time series analysis is performed. Time series
analysis is a dynamic tool in power system engineering that examines how electrical
networks evolve over time. Its main purpose is to model system responses to fluctuating
loads, renewable energy generation, and contingencies.
In load flow simulations, it is essential to assess the load on both transmission lines and
transformers. This assessment ensures the reliability, efficiency, and overall health of the
power system. Overloaded lines or transformers can lead to voltage instability, equipment
damage, increased energy losses, and even blackouts.
The data used for this analysis contains hourly values for consumption and production in
2022. The processed data discussed in Section 2.3, categorised as Consumption in each
load bus and Production in each generation bus, are used.
To achieve this goal, the loading percentage of the transmission system lines and
transformers in 2022 has been presented using Load Duration Curves. These curves
provide valuable insights into the loading patterns of the electrical lines and transformers
within the system. On these graphs, the y-axis represents the magnitude of the load,
ranging from lower to higher levels, while the x-axis quantifies the cumulative hours during
which these loads are sustained. These curves effectively illustrate the frequency and
duration of different levels of stress. A steeper slope within the curve indicates that
higher loads are sustained for shorter periods, while a flatter slope indicates that loads are
maintained for longer periods.
When analysing the transmission lines, most have stable loading patterns, with little
variation between their maximum and minimum loading percentages, indicating consistent
and reliable performance as it can be seen in Figure 3.1. However, lines L193-304, L220-299,
and L221-232 have steeper load duration curves, indicating notable variations in line loading
depending on system conditions. Line L192-304 experiences the highest loading in this

Power Losses
In the process of transporting electricity from the point of generation to the end user,
there are always power losses in the various components of the electricity network. Losses
caused by current flow that are converted into thermal energy due to the resistance of the
network elements.
The main method for determining the losses in the transmission entails is to assess the power
difference between the sending and receiving ends of the transmission line. In a transmission
network, the majority of the losses occur on the lines and they are approximately three to
four times greater than the losses from transformers [28].
Transmission line losses are primarily influenced by the resistivity of the conductor, known as
Joule Losses (about 85% of the total losses [28]). These losses depend directly on the current
flowing through the conductors and the length of the transmission lines. Consequently, the
total transmission losses can amount to about 2-3% of the total transmitted power.
Figure 3.4 visually represents the aggregated active power losses for individual lines,
measured in gigawatt-hours. The cumulative active power loss across all lines is to 12.93
GWh. This loss is compared to the total active power transmitted by the lines, which is
4382.74 GWh.
These losses represent about 0.3% of the total active power. This positive result underlines
the efficiency of transmission lines equipped with low-resistivity conductors and advanced
insulation, both of which play a key role in reducing energy losses.

3.2.4
Flexibility in the Transmission Grid
While the electric grid has always possessed some degree of flexibility to address variable
electricity demand, the increasing variability and ramping demands associated with a
56
==PAGE:75==
3. Results
cleaner and more modern power system are making grid flexibility increasingly valuable.
This trend increases the uncertainty and variability of electricity production. Historically,
the power system has relied on flexibility primarily from the generation side to maintain
a balance between energy consumption and production. However, in recent years, there
has been a growing adoption of consumption-based flexibility, known as demand response
(DR).
Demand response refers to balancing the demand on power grids incentivizing customers
to shift their electricity consumption to times of higher availability or lower demand, often
through pricing or monetary rewards. Together with smart grids and energy storage,
demand response plays a crucial role in providing flexibility to address the challenges posed
by variable renewables and the increasing electricity demand, ensuring the stability and
reliability of electricity grids.[29]
The growing integration of distributed generation, along with the continuous electrification
of heating and transportation, will place additional stress on the network. To avoid
costly retrofits and infrastructure upgrades, residential flexibility could be used to reduce
equipment loading during peak consumption or generation periods. [30]
Electric vehicles and thermal loads such as electric heat pumps and boilers are an important
source of flexibility at a residential costumer level. Nevertheless, assessing the combined
flexibility of these thermal loads can be challenging due to the variability in demand
response, which is influenced by factors like ambient temperature, room temperature,
weather conditions, season, and the day of the week, among others.
Therefore, the
potential flexibility from heat pumps and boilers is not investigated in this thesis.
Conversely, EV owners may be motivated to align their electricity consumption with periods
of lower electricity prices. Anticipating a rise in electric vehicle adoption in the future,
this adjustment could potentially alter the electricity consumption pattern, smoothing out
peak demand and alleviating stress on the grid infrastructure.
As illustrated in Figure 3.40, electricity spot prices exhibit daily fluctuations due to a range
of influencing factors like supply and demand dynamics, the composition of the generation
mix, weather conditions, and fuel prices, among others. The intricate interplay of these
elements contributes to the variability observed in electricity spot prices.
Figure 3.40. Elspot Price Curve in DK1 on January 1st in 2022.
57
==PAGE:76==
3. Results
Hence, in this study it is evaluated how the integration of EVs into the grid influences
the loading conditions of the transmission system. In the modeled Weighted Increase in
the Consumption Profile, the demand arising from the electrification of transportation
is integrated into this curve. The simulation specifically entails relocating 30% of the
total consumption attributed to electric cars and vans in that profile to off-peak hours of
demand, while the remaining 70% follows the pattern defined by the modeled Weighted
Increase in the Consumption Profile.
The reason for choosing the 30 % target is that it is a promising and achievable goal.
Higher percentages currently appear to be far to be reached in residential uses due to
existing consumer habits and monetary incentives that are not attractive enough to shift
the loads [31].
Various alterations in consumption patterns have been explored, each involving a distinct
distribution of shifted consumption across different time frames. The examined EV charging
patterns include the following, and their implementation yields the consumption profiles
depicted in Figure 3.41.
1. EVs flexibility: 00-03h. This strategy examines the impact of concentrating the
charging of the 30% of the total consumption attributed to electric cars during 3
hours from midnight.
2. EVs flexibility: 00-05h. This approach extends the charging duration, allowing for
more gradual and distributed EV charging during the early morning off-peak period.
3. EVs flexibility: 00-12h. In this consuption pattern, EVs have a longer charging
window to charge that 30% of the EV demand, further spreading the load over the
off-peak hours.

LIMITATIONS AND FUTURE RESEARCH
The primary aim of this study is to comprehensively understand the operational dynamics
of Funen’s electrical network, emphasizing the development of a transmission grid model
and the analysis of system loading on lines and transformers under various future scenarios,
incorporating increased integration of renewable energy sources and electrification.
The study shows that the existing transmission system is operating within safe loading
conditions in 2022. However, the N-1 contingency analysis, a standard practice among
TSOs to assess system design and operation, is not considered in this study. The N-1
analysis examines the impact of a sudden failure or outage of a system component on the
safety and reliability of the electricity system. Consequently, the absence of this assessment
may affect the accuracy of the safe load condition findings.
The analysis considered the possibility of components being overloaded by 100%.
Nevertheless, some specific components may have different capacity limits, meaning that
congestion problems could occur even if they were loaded to only 70%.
In order to improve the accuracy of the results, it is worth investigating the power flow
dynamics between Funen and its neighbouring regions. At present, this study has not
taken into account the power generation in these neighbouring areas, which may have
a significant impact on the power flow. In view of the ongoing construction of floating
energy islands in the North Sea and on Bornholm, as well as power-to-x projects in the
country, future research should investigate how these developments could affect the power
flow between Funen and neighbouring regions.
Another critical issue affecting the accuracy of the results is the quality of the consumption
and production data. Primarily, hourly consumption and production values were obtained
from the Danish TSO, Energinet. These values were given at the municipal level, as
Funen consists of ten municipalities. However, a notable limitation is the lack of specific
information on the allocation of production and consumption to each node within the
transmission system.
For production, the assumption that load distribution within municipalities is proportional
to the size of the local population, while practical, may not capture the nuances introduced
by large industrial or commercial facilities. Future research should aim to refine the load
distribution model by considering the influence of such industrial or commercial facilities
on local energy demand.
In terms of production data, extensive research was carried out to compile information on
the power plants in the region. This involved identifying the coordinates of each power
plant and linking them to the nearest busbar on the transmission network. Possible future
research could include contacting the municipalities and DSOs in Funen to further refine

With regard to the projected scenarios for 2040, estimates of the increase in production in
Funen have been used, based on data from the Danish Energy Agency. These estimates
are generic for the whole of Denmark, including the two separate Danish transmission
systems, DK1 and DK2. In order to adapt these estimates to the conditions in Funen,
appropriate adjustments have been made to the production profiles, taking into account
the expected increase in wind and solar energy by 11.1 and 1.76, respectively. However, it
should be emphasised that detailed information on future power plants that are expected
to be built and commissioned in the future is currently not available.
The assumptions made in this study have made it possible to assess the impact of the
increase in consumption and production on Funen on the transmission network. In addition,
an analysis has been made of the scenarios that put the greatest strain on the lines and
transformers, with particular emphasis on the L220-299 line between Svendborg and
Fraugde, which has the most critical load conditions. It should be noted that these results
depend on the assumptions made and that real conditions may result in a lower load. As
can be seen in the case of the 50% increase in solar power, the capacity load on this line
decreases significantly. For future research, a more complete examination of the load and
generation assignments to node 299 is warranted.
Integrating flexibility strategies involving both EVs into the algorithm is a crucial step in
assessing how these strategies can effectively address critical load conditions and improve
overall power system efficiency and reliability. This extension has the potential to improve
grid performance by adapting consumption patterns and optimising energy use in real
time.
However, this extension presents a dual challenge. First, it requires a comprehensive
understanding of the existing EV infrastructure and its impact on the grid. Second, it
requires the development and fine-tuning of the algorithm to accommodate the dynamic
and often unpredictable nature of these flexible loads. Achieving this goal requires a deeper
understanding of load modelling and the development of intelligent charging strategies.
Future Research
Further assessments and investigations could be carried out to analyse more deeply the
influence of increasing the integration of renewable energy sources and electrification of
key sectors in 2040 for the transmission grid in Funen:
• Explore the power flow dynamics from neighboring regions, especially in relation to
ongoing projects like floating energy islands and Power-to-X initiatives.
• Conduct a comprehensive N-1 contingency analysis to assess the safety and reliability
in the event of a sudden failure or outage of a system component.
• Engaging with local authorities such as Funen municipalities and DSOs to gather
detailed information about generators and loads connected to each substation in the
transmission system.
• Perform a detailed analysis of reactive power within the system to ensure proper
management and optimization.

CONCLUSION
This thesis has been dedicated to a comprehensive examination of the evolving landscape in
the Funen’s energy sector. This transformation is characterised by the rapid expansion of
renewable energy capacity and the increasing electrification of key sectors, such as heating
and transportation. The primary focus of this study was the Funen transmission grid, and
the approach included several key phases.
The project started with an extensive investigation to collect valid and publicly available
data necessary for modeling the current electricity network, as well as the electricity
demand and generation in Funen.
The system modelling was then implemented using Python-Pandapower. The code involved
modelling the transmission network, assigning loads and generation to their respective
substations, and running time series simulations with the associated controllers for the
loads and generators.
Next, the model of the existing transmission network was built and then verified. Future
scenarios were then developed for the year 2040, taking into account the electrification of
key sectors and the expansion of renewable energy capacity through wind and photovoltaic
power. Load flow simulations were carried out to assess the load conditions on the network.
Finally, this research included an investigation of the resources and mechanisms needed
to ensure the presence of sufficient network flexibility to handle the expected loads and
generation in 2040. This investigation included the development of different consumption
profiles, with a particular focus on increasing flexibility through the integration of electric
vehicles.
Finally, the research questions are concluded in this chapter:
1. How to use the available data sources to study the current state of the
transmission system?
At present, there is plenty of information available online, which can make it difficult
to find relevant data from official sources.
In this case, the first step involved
an extensive search for data on the existing transmission network and planned
investments in the network for 2040. Understanding the data was challenging as
the available datasets included information for the entire Danish system and used
acronyms. It was also important to understand how python-pandapower works and
what data was needed to model the power system and run time series simulations.
The next step was to gather data sets for production and consumption profiles in
Funen, as well as loads and generation units in the system. The data was then
tailored to meet pandapower’s requirements, specifically assigning production and
64
==PAGE:83==
5. Conclusion
consumption in Funen’s municipalities to the loads and generation units connected
to the substations in the transmission system.
2. What will the future system look like under different scenarios involving
renewable energy sources, electric vehicles, and other factors?
Is the
expansion of the transmission network necessary to accommodate these
changes?
As the integration of new renewable energy plants and the electrification of key
sectors increase towards future scenarios, this study has shown that demand and
production in Funen will increase by 2040 due to a number of factors.
On the demand side, the increase in electricity consumption for electric transport is
the most remarkable, as it is expected to be 20 times higher than in 2022. Heating
technologies such as individual and large heat pumps and electric boilers will also
increase their electricity demand. Overall, electricity demand is expected to be twice
as high in 2040 as it is today. On the other hand, energy production is also expected
to increase significantly by 2040. In this study, approximations and estimates have
led to a doubling of total annual production in 2040. With an increase in solar
production of 11.1 times and an increase of 76% for wind production.
It is also important to note that Energinet has plans to upgrade two lines on Funen,
and the load flow analysis has taken these expansions into account.
In this way, it has been shown that the transmission lines and transformers that make
up the electricity system will be able to cope with the predicted changes for most of
the time. However, a number of cases have been identified where it is possible that
the grid could become unsafe. For example, situations where there is a high level of
energy production that is mainly allocated to one substation can lead to overloading
of the components. It is therefore important to carry out optimal energy planning,
taking into account the variability and uncertainty of renewable energy sources.
3. Can the flexibility of the system address operational challenges? If not,
what enhancements or reinforcements could be required?
This study focused on exploring the flexibility of electric vehicles. A demand response
strategy was developed with the aim of shifting 30% of the total energy needed for
charging private cars and vans to off-peak hours.
The analysis shows that in the projected energy scenario for Funen in 2040, this
strategy sometimes helps to reduce the loading on certain components during
peak demand periods. However, the implementation of this strategy during peak
production times makes the situation worse for elements with high loads. Therefore,
it might be interesting to explore additional flexibility strategies. In general, the
network shows good and reliable conditions, despite occasional critical hours that
require potential reinforcement, which could be provided by alternative technologies
or flexibility methods.
