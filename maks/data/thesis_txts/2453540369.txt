Preface
This thesis is submitted at the DTU Electrical Engineering department and Centre for
Electric Power Energy (CEE) in partial fulfillment of the requirement for Master of Sci-
ence in Electrical Engineering. The work is conducted at Converter lab of PowerLabDK
and is in collaboration with Siemens Gamesa Renewable Energy. The work is super-
vised by Prof. Tonny Rasmussen from DTU Elektro and Emerson Guest from Siemens
Gamesa Renewable Energy.
This work contains the analysis of factors affecting the harmonics spectrum of 3-level
inverter used in type-IV Wind Turbines. The analysis is carried out by theoretical for-
mulation, simulation and laboratory results. The content of the thesis is divided into
seven chapters and 5 appendices.

Abstract
There is significant growth in the sustainable energy industry in the past few decades.
Among the renewable energy sources, wind technology has matured to a great extent.
Within the wind industry, offshore wind farms are becoming more and more popular
due to its advantage over onshore wind farms. Offshore wind energy technology has
advanced with more and more offshore farms being developed. Even so, there are still
many challenges within this industry. One of the challenges is resonance in wind farm
between its power electronics and passive components. The harmonics generated due to
converter gets amplified when in resonance with capacitances and inductances of cables
and transformers. Thus, it is pivotal to know the frequency response of wind farm com-
ponents and devices. Besides, the size of wind turbines is likely to reach double-digit
in near future. This has opened research to explore other converter topologies than
traditionally used 2-level converter.
This thesis focuses on analysis of the harmonics spectrum of the 3-level, full load con-
verter. Effect of factors such as deadtime, semiconductor voltage drop and DC-midpoint
deviation are analyzed. The investigation is carried out using theoretical analysis, simu-
lation model and laboratory experiments. Different sampling and modulation techniques
for converter control are studied and double Fourier analysis is carried out to scrutinize
the harmonic spectrum. One of the main contributions from this thesis is the derivation
of expressions for the Fourier coefficients with deadtime and asymmetrical DC midpoint
cases. MATLAB Simulink is used for the converter modeling, its control and simulation.
Hardware design and development for the control board is carried out for the experi-
mental purpose.
The results obtained using all the methods match quite closely. It is observed that odd
ratio provides superior harmonics spectrum for Phase Disposition (PD) type of modula-
tion whereas even ratio is better for APOD (Alternative Phase Disposition)/POD (Phase
Opposition Disposition). PD, APOD and POD are different type of carrier waves based
on the phase difference. Regular sampling introduces odd harmonics in the baseband.
It is also noted that deadtime causes both odd and even harmonics along with the
DC component. These harmonics are of significant magnitude near center frequencies.
They can be shifted to higher-order by increasing the carrier frequency. The deviation
in DC-midpoint evokes notable lower order even harmonics and DC component due to
asymmetry produced in the output voltage. This effect is independent of the frequency
and ratio. It can only be reduced by minimizing the DC asymmetry.

1.2.1
Overview of advancement in Wind Turbine
Converter
As stated in the previous Section 1.1, there has been a tremendous advancement in the
wind industry. This is the driving force for the research in the areas of technologies used
in wind farms such as power electronics. Nowadays, a popular choice for wind power
plants (WPPs) is type-IV wind turbines. Traditionally, a 2-level converter is used in tur-
bines. But with the increase in the size of wind turbines, there is a demand for increase
==PAGE:25==
1.2 Literature Review

in the size of the power converter used in the turbines. But an increment in size of power
converter at the same voltage level would however result in increased losses due to the
higher current requirement. Consequently, increasing the voltage using a higher level
converter is a suitable option. Also, there is a requirement to improve power quality for
renewable energy systems [2]. All these factors, steered the research in the direction of
multilevel converters w.r.t performance, modelling, control etc. [3–9]
Multilevel inverters provide the output closer to sinusoidal wave resulting in better
power quality. Moreover, it operates on higher frequencies thereby shifting the harmon-
ics produced by it to higher orders. But the downside of multilevel converters is the
increased number of components. The recommendation from these research work is for
NPC 3-level converter due to various factors such as reliability as compared to cascaded
H-bridge converter [10]. It provides the advantage of high power with reduced stress
on its semiconductor components, reduces harmonics [11]. One main disadvantage with
a 3-level NPC converter is the imbalance of DC mid-point voltage which has an effect
on the output voltage and may cause shoot through in switching devices [11]. However,
extensive research has been carried out to mitigate the imbalance through converter
control [11][12].

1.3.1
Motivation
As seen in Section 1.2.1, the increasing size of WT requires an increment in the converter
size. The increase in current is not preferred as it would raises the losses. Therefore,
increasing voltage level is a better option. Increase in the voltage level can be easily
achieved by increasing the level of the converter. Moreover as stated previously, it is
important to take care of the harmonic content of any wind farm to avoid resonances.
Turbine converters are one of the sources of harmonics. Therefore, it is important to
study the harmonic behavior of any converter used in a wind farm and factors affecting
it. The focus of this thesis is to investigate into this area in collaboration with Siemens
Gamesa Renewable Energy. The information about behavior of harmonic spectrum is
then can be used for accurate design of filters or other mitigation techniques for wind
farms.

investigates these factors and their effect on the harmonic spectrum. This project targets
to scrutinize and contribute in this area. The objective of this thesis are:
• Design control board for 3-level NPC converter

2.1
Inverter Configuration
A multilevel Neutral Point Clamped (NPC) inverter employs clamping diodes between
the lower and upper part of the inverter legs and DC-link is cascaded into (L-1) levels
using capacitors, where L is the converter level. As discussed in Section 1.2.1, this report
focuses on 3-level NPC inverter.
Figure 2.1 shows the circuit diagram of a NPC 3-level 3-phase inverter used in this
project. The discussions and explanations are limited to only one leg, phase-A. The rest
two phases have identical configuration and working, due to symmetry, as can be seen
from Figure 2.1.
Leg A has four switches A1 to A4 with respective anti-parallel diodes D1 to D4. The
DC side is divided into two capacitors C1 and C2. The voltage across each capacitor is
represented by V_dc1 and V_dc2. Under ideal conditions, each of these voltages are
equal and thereby providing a DC-midpoint, n. Under operating conditions, due to var-
ious factors, stated below, these voltages tend to become unequal and thereby shifting
the DC-midpoint. Its effect on the frequency spectrum is also analyzed in Chapter 6.
The total voltage across the DC-link is taken as 2V_dc and thus under ideal conditions,
V_dc1 = V_dc2 = V_dc. Two clamping diodes D1_c and D2_c are connected between
the switches and the neutral point.
Factors affecting the deviation of DC-midpoint:
• Asymmetry in the load current due to fault in the grid or any other type of
unbalanced operation.
==PAGE:32==
10 of 175
2 Three Level NPC Converter - Working and Operation
• The size of the converter and its losses. The high power converter has fewer losses
as compared to the converter rating and the effect of DC-midpoint is high. On the
other hand, low power converter has a higher percentage of losses as compared to
its rating and thus the effect of DC-midpoint variation is less evident.[22]
• The manufacturing tolerances of the components such as capacitors or switching
devices can also cause unbalance.
Currently, there are several control schemes in place to minimize the deviation. Nonethe-
less, some deviation still exists in practice.

2.4
Modulation Techniques
In order to generate the desired switching pulses for a 3-level NPC inverter there are
various well-established modulation schemes, such as SVM and SPWM. This report fo-
cuses on the SPWM modulation technique. In this technique, a low frequency sinusoidal
reference wave is compared with a high frequency triangular wave. Usually, the reference
wave is chosen of the same nature as the targeted output voltage or current [20]. In this
analysis, the desired output voltage and current are 50Hz sinusoidal wave and thus the
reference wave is taken of the same nature. Moreover, the carrier wave can be chosen as
either a triangular or saw-tooth wave. Here, the triangular wave is chosen as it provides
an improved harmonic spectrum as compared to saw-tooth. [20]
The switching pulse is created by comparison of carrier and reference wave.
If the
reference is greater than the carrier wave, the pulse is kept high and low otherwise.
There are three different types of ways in which the reference wave can be sampled and
compared with the carrier wave. Each of it is explained as follow:
(a) Natural Sampling

• Regular Symmetrical Sampling: In this technique, the reference wave, in blue,

the reference wave is sampled once in a carrier period, as shown in Figure 2.5b

• Regular Asymmetrical Sampling: In this case, the reference wave is sampled
twice in a carrier period, once at the positive peak and next again at the negative
peak. The latest sample obtained is used to compare with the carrier wave, Fig-
ure 2.5c. This technique is a compromise between the above two techniques in the
sense that it requires more digital processing than symmetrical sampling but less
than natural. However, it gives harmonic spectrum very close to natural sampling,
making it more preferred in the industry.

CHAPTER 4
Simulation Model Setup
This chapter contains information about the implementation of the simulation model to
realize different modulation techniques as well as the NPC three-level converter behavior.
The model is built in steps and divided into sections. MATLAB/Simulink is used for the
development. Moreover, various parameters for some block, especially semiconductor de-
vices, cant be set as zero and if an extremely low value is set then the internal solution
matrix becomes sparse. This affects the results obtained from simulations. Thus, two
different types of converter models are developed. The first model is realized by directly
using the semiconductor devices available in Simulink whereas, in the second model, the
mathematical algorithm is derived and implemented using logical blocks.
The following sections provide a detailed description of each step.
Please note that
the explanation is done for only one phase (Phase A) and then identical models are used
to build a 3-phase system. The only parameter to take care while integrating the model
as 3-phase is to set reference waves 120° phase shifted.

CHAPTER 7
Future Work
This project is a first attempt in analyzing the harmonic spectrum of 3 level converter.
As seen throughout the report, there are various factors which affect the spectrum. Due
to the number of influencing factors and the time duration of the project, not all the
cases and aspects are covered in the report. Nonetheless, an attempt is made to get
a holistic view of the factors to support future work. Following are the recommended
potential areas in which future work can be carried on:
• Phases of harmonics: It is observed in Section 6.1.2 that some of the harmonics
cancel out due to the opposite phase. An investigation into the phases can provide
with potential combinations for harmonics reduction or complete cancellation.
• Effect of Modulation Index: It is well known that the modulation index influ-
ences the spectrum. This factor is completely left out in the report and can be
explored in future.
• Mitigation of the harmonics: The analysis carried out in the project can be
used to further develop harmonics mitigation methods.
• Analysis at WPP level: In a wind farm different turbines can be operated such
that harmonics produced are out of phase. In order to achieve this, the analysis
of a group of wind turbine converter is required.
At last, there is scope for improvement in measurement methods used in laboratory
experiments and also for the simulation model to achieve more accurate results.

CHAPTER 8
Conclusion
The scope of the project, described in Chapter 1, is to analyze the effect of deadtime,
SCVD and DC midpoint deviation on the harmonic spectrum for a 3-level inverter. The
analysis is carried out using the theoretical formulation of double Fourier coefficients in
Chapter 3. In the beginning, two kinds of sampling techniques are used, namely natural
and regular asymmetrical sampling. Fourier coefficients are obtained including deadtime
for both cases. During the analysis, it is concluded that regular asymmetrical sampling
is the preferred method due to its lower digital processing requirement with comparable
harmonic spectrum. Hence, this technique is used for further analysis. For a three-level
converter, depending on the phases of the carrier waves two types of modulation are
discussed and Fourier coefficients are formulated.
Furthermore, expression including
asymmetrical DC-midpoint is formulated as well for all the cases. All the theoretical
formulations, except natural sampling without deadtime, are the contribution of this
report to this research area. The simulation model is also developed in Chapter 4. The
converter control board is designed to achieve regular asymmetrical sampling for both
PD and APOD/POD types of modulation, Chapter 5. The results obtained from each
of these methods match quite well which implies that the theoretical formulation and
MATLAB modeling is implemented correctly. Also, the design and development of the
control board is accurate.
The theoretical analysis helped to investigate the source and nature of the harmonic
components in the spectrum. For PD modulation, it is observed that harmonics at even
multiples of carrier frequency are absent in the ideal case. For carrier group harmon-
ics, the even carrier groups comprise of only odd harmonics. The decrease in their the
magnitude with harmonic order is reasonably high. On the other hand, the odd carrier
group nature is found to be dependent on the ratio with slower rate of decrease in the
magnitude and thus extended till lower order harmonics. In the APOD/POD type of
modulation, it is discovered that there are no center frequency components.
During the analysis, it is observed that regular asymmetrical sampling causes odd har-
monics in the baseband compared to natural sampling. However, with the odd ratio it
produces the same results in the spectrum as natural sampling, Figure 3.15. Hence, odd
ratio is preferred for PD type of modulation. On the other hand, for APOD/POD type
the even ratio proved to be superior. It is also noted that the sidebands of APOD/POD
reduce at a higher rate compared to PD type. Additionally, it is discovered that various
factors, discussed in the project, have the same effect on the harmonic spectrum of both
types of modulation techniques.
==PAGE:126==
104 of 175
8 Conclusion
The analysis of DC-midpoint deviation on the harmonic spectrum shows that it intro-
duces lower order even harmonics as well as DC component. The magnitude of the
second harmonic is quite significant with other lower order even harmonics decreasing
with higher order.
It is observed that deadtime causes both odd and even harmonics in the spectrum.
The major contribution due to it is near the center frequencies. It also extended the
sidebands even further as compared to without deadtime case. The center frequencies
which were zero in case of no deadtime appeared due to the introduction of deadtime.
The effect of the higher carrier frequency is also investigated during the project. The
higher frequency shifted the carrier group harmonics to the higher order. This reduced
the magnitude of sidebands harmonics extended till lower order. But, the magnitude of
even harmonics increased with increase in the frequency. On the other hand, no effect
was seen on the harmonics contribution due to DC-midpoint. The magnitude and nature
of even harmonics remained unchanged even with the increase in the frequency.
Hence, it is concluded that various factors exhibit different effect on the spectrum. This
information is useful while designing the converter control scheme and harmonic filters.
