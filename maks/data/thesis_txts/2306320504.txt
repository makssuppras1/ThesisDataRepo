Summary (English)
The purpose of this thesis is to create a system that is able to detect a content
and a style of a speech wave signal. The content is what has been said, it can
be any sentence. The style is the speciﬁc voice characteristics of the person who
pronounced this sentence. Moreover, this system should be able to adjust the
style component so it corresponds to the the style of another person. In other
words, the content is preserved but the style is changed so the result is the same
sentence (content) but pronounced by another person.
A major aﬀord was put into investigation of deep neural network capabilities for
analyzing speech from its spectrograms. The two tasks were solved simultane-
ously: the phoneme classiﬁcation and the speaker classiﬁcation. In particularly,
the VGGNet was used for this research because it has clear structure and a lot of
features. Several VGGNet layers were considered to ﬁnd the best source for two
softmax classiﬁers. Finally, the outputs from these two softmax classiﬁers were
used to make a loss function for gradient descent algorithm which transforms
the style of a speech utterance and preserves the content.

Chapter 1
Introduction
There are several ways for people to perceive information: visually, auditory
and somatosensory. There is a lot of information produced nowadays and big
part of it is presented as a visual and audio information. People communicate
using speech which makes it vital and important for their lives. Development
of systems which can understand human speech and transform it will beneﬁt
human nation in the future. Touch interfaces could be replaced with speech
interfaces which will be more convenient to use.
1.1
Motivation
The motivation for this work comes from a desire to improve understanding and
manipulation of human speech. This work searches a way to synthesize speech
utterances of a concrete human by identifying his speech style and adjusting
utterances that have been said by another person. There are many ways how
this technology can be applied. The ﬁrst one that comes to mind is to synthesize
someone’s voice for diﬀerent bad purposes like tamper with evidence or speak
on behalf of somebody on the phone. However, there are much more useful
applications that could be built. Firstly, it could be used to synthesize speeches
of famous dead people. Film translation is another ﬁeld for application of the
technique described above. An original voice of an actor can be used during
==PAGE:12==
2
Introduction
content translation. So the ﬁnal translation has the content of diﬀerent language
spoken by the original actor voices. Finally, this technology will give better
intuition in how to work with sound showing that the content and the style of
a speech are separable which is a step forward in the analysis of speech. The
content and the style features can be used separately, simultaneously or in some
combination to improve the performance of systems for speech recognition tasks.
1.2
Related work
The inspiration for this work was taken from the results described in [GEB15a].
The basic idea of "A neural algorithm of Artistic Style" is to take an artistic
style and apply it to any picture. The idea is to discover some features of images
using the 19 layer VGG-Network [SZ14], namely a content feature vector and a
style feature vector, and then transform a white noise image according to the
captured parameters.
1.3
Goal
This work seeks a technique that could apply to a speech the idea described
above.
Namely, this technique captures voice characteristics of one speech
recording and content (sentence or phrase) from another speech recording and
transforms the second recording (or random white noise) so that the sentence
that has been said on the ﬁrst recording is preserved but sounds like being pro-
nounced by the person from the second recording. Therefore, it simulates speech
of a person using adjustment of an already pronounced utterance by someone
else in the direction of the style of the person.

Chapter 2
Theory
This chapter is dedicated to the description and explanation of the background
required for understanding the method which was implemented to achieve the
goal of this thesis. This chapter starts with explanation of representation of
sound and the way of storing and dealing with it. Next, the basic theory of neu-
ral networks is presented with explanation of loss function for classiﬁcation tasks,
diﬀerent ways of regularization and other ways of preventing overﬁtting, com-

schemes of parameter updates.
Lastly, this chapter covers the convolutional
neural networks describing the operation of diﬀerent layers and most common
architectures. It also provides a speciﬁc example of CNN with VGGNet.
2.1
Continuous nature of sound
The nature of sound is a continuous wave signal and it is impossible to store all
continuous values. Therefore any sound is reduced from a continuous signal to
discrete signal using sampling technique, and interpolation algorithms are used
for the backward sequence, namely, for reconstruction of a continuous signal
from a discrete one. Sampling is performed every T seconds, it is called the
sampling interval. The sampling frequency is deﬁned as 1/T and it shows the
==PAGE:16==
6
Theory
average number of samples obtained in one second period. Human hearing range
is from 20 Hz to 20000 Hz and it follows from Nyquist theorem that audio data
of this range should be sampled at double-rate, for example 44.1 kHz. However,
much lower rate could be used for a sampling of a pure human speech. The
energy is concentrated in 5 Hz - 4 kHz for most phonemes allowing sampling
with the rate of 8kHz.[Tho05]
Another important parameter is the bit depth. It is basically an amount of bits
taken to store one sample. It represents an amount of diﬀerent levels of energy
(amplitude values) that could be distinguishable. For example, for 8-bits there
are 28 = 256 diﬀerent levels, 16-bits 216 = 65536. It could also be seen as a
precision of sampling.[Tho05]
2.2
Neural Networks
Neural Network (NN) is a collection of neurons organized into layers, neurons
from diﬀerent layers are connected in feed- forward acyclic manner, so the out-
put from a previous layer becomes an input for a current layer. Usually neurons
from two adjacent layers are fully-connected meaning that they are fully pairwise
connected but there are no connections between neurons of a single layer. The
very ﬁrst layer of NN is called input layer and the very last is output. Between
input and output layers there are several hidden layers, the number of them
depends on tasks that one tries to solve, because the number of hidden layers
inﬂuences the capacity of the network. Neural Networks with more neurons
can express more complicated functions. However, it might cause overﬁtting of
data if the data is simple and the huge NN is used. Overﬁtting occurs when a
model describes random noise instead of the underlying relationship. Although,
complex NN with a lot of neurons might overﬁt data, they are usually used in
conjunction with other techniques that prevent overﬁtting. They are described
in section 2.4. The tendency nowadays goes to increasing of number of hidden
layer in the network. For example, the network that has won the ILSVRC 1 com-
petition in 2015 is ResNET that has more than 1000 layers [HZRS15a]. Below
Figure 2.1 presents a NN architecture with 2 fully-connected hidden layers.

SVM vs Softmax
The SVM loss is satisﬁed when the diﬀerence between the correct and incorrect

or decreases but stays greater than the margin ∆, SVM remains the same but
softmax function expresses the preference for any change in the scores.
2.4
Techniques for prevention of overﬁtting
There are several ways of preventing overﬁtting during training: the most com-
monly used is regularization and recently discovered dropout which is very ef-
fective.
2.4.1
Regularization
The regularization term is added to total loss function as follows:
L = L + λR(W)
(2.7)
where λ is a hyperparameter and R(W) is a regularization function of the
weights W. The regularization prevents overﬁtting of the parameters W. The
==PAGE:20==
10
Theory
aim is not only to minimize the loss function but also to keep W parameters
nice so they can deal with more general examples instead of perfectly ﬁtting
the training set. Therefore, by introducing regularization one can get greater
loss on a training data set but it usually performs better on a test set. λ is a
hyperparameter that is usually found by trying diﬀerent values and choosing the
one that performs best. Most commonly, crossvalidation is used for λ search.
There are several choices of R(W) function:
• L2 regularization is R(W) = ⌃k⌃lW 2
k,l. It penalizes the squared mag-
nitude of all parameters directly in the objective, particularly, it heavily
penalizes neurons with high magnitude. The L2 regularization prefers to
use a little bit of all inputs instead of considerably using only some of the
input due to multiplicative interactions between weights and inputs.
• L1 regularization R(W) = ⌃k⌃l|Wk,l|. In contrast with L2 regularization,
L1 regularization ends up using only the most important neurons and is
almost invariant to noisy input.
2.4.2
Dropout
Another technique that prevents overﬁtting is dropout which turned out to be
very eﬀective and simple. During training a neuron is used only with a certain
probability p which is a hyperparameter, otherwise it is put to zero.[SHK+14].
Therefore, during every iteration of training at every layer of a network only
some neurons are chosen for this iteration.
2.5
Activation functions
An activation function is a non-linear function which is implemented at every
single node of a neural network. There are several activation functions that are
most widely used:
2.5.1
Sigmoid
The sigmoid function has the following form σ(x) = 1/(1+✏−x) and is presented
in Figure 2.2. It takes the values from (−1, 1) and maps it into the range (0, 1).
It maps large positive values to 1 that fully activate neuron and negative values

to 0 which do not ﬁre neuron at all. The sigmoid function is barely used now
because it has several disadvantages. The main problem is that the sigmoid
nonlinearity asymptotically goes to 1 for positive values and to 0 for negative
ones thereby the slope at these places is almost horizontal and this means that
the gradient will be close to 0. Therefore, the sigmoid function can eliminate
the gradients from following layers.
2.5.2
Tanh
The Tanh non-linearity squashes number into the range (−1, 1) and is shown
in Figure 2.3. Like a sigmoid function tanh kills the gradient that comes from
neurons above but its output is zero centered which gives a better dynamic in
comparison with sigmoid in the gradient updates.
2.5.3
ReLU
The ReLU (The Rectiﬁed Linear Unit) is computed as f(x) = max(0, x) and
presented in Figure 2.4. Stochastic gradient descent converges 6 times faster
using ReLU in comparison with sigmoid or tanh because it does not saturate on
positive values [KSH12]. The implementation of ReLU function is more eﬃcient
and requires less resources for calculation.

2.6
Optimization
Optimization is the process of ﬁnding the way to optimize parameters W so the
loss function goes to 0
• Random Search
The naive solution is to randomly choose weights W several times and
pick the one with the lowest loss.
• Gradient Descent
Is the process of updating the weights with respect to the gradient:
W = W −↵5 F(W, x)
(2.8)
Where ↵is a step size or learning rate. When the gradient is calculated
the weights are updated in the way of decreasing the slope to achieve the
local minimum. The gradient is usually calculated using backpropagation
and it is described in details in the section 2.7. There are several strategies
for parameter updates. Section 2.8 contains explanation of the diﬀerent
ways of updating the weights.
• Mini-batch Gradient Descent
The idea is exactly the same as the Gradient Descent but it evaluates
the gradient on a small portion of the training data set. It ends up in
noisy estimation of the loss function which goes down over time allowing
to make more steps. Mini-batch Gradient Descent is more eﬃcient than
the normal Gradient Descent and gives better results on practice.

Convolutional Neural Networks
Convolutional Neural Networks are very similar to Neural Networks as they also
have diﬀerent layers with learnable weights and biases. Every neuron performs
dot-product on its input and optionally applies an activation function on the
result of dot-product. The feed-forward path is diﬀerentiable. Like usual neural
networks convolutional neural networks have fully connected layer at the end
and a loss function (SVM/softmax). Although there are a lot of similarities
between CNN and NN the main diﬀerence is how neurons are wired between
each other. First of all, CNN are usually used for analysis of images and takes

3-dimentional data as an input, more precisely if an image is an input then
neuron deals with width, height and depth (number of channels). For example,
224x224x3 is especially used as an input for VGGNet where 224x224 is an image
resolution and 3 goes for RGB channels. Every neuron is only connected to a
small region of the previous layer which is described in section 2.9.1.1
2.9.1
Layers in CNN
Unlike Neural networks that mainly use only fully-connected layers, convolu-
tional neural networks consist of diﬀerent layers. There are three types of layers
that are mainly used: Convolutional layer, Pooling layer and Fully-Connected
layers.
[KSH12] These layers are stacked one after another to form a CNN
architecture. For instance, a convolutional network can have an architecture:
[INPUT - CONV - CONV - RELU - POOL - CONV - CONV - RELU - POOL
- FC].
2.9.1.1
Convolutional layer
The convolutional layer computes the output of neurons that are connected to
a local regions of previous layer. The set of parameters of a convolutional layer
can be understood as a set of learnable ﬁlters.[KSH12] The width and height
of a ﬁlter vary but the ﬁlter always goes through full depth. It was empirically
proved that small ﬁlters are the most eﬀective and require less computations.
For instance, the ﬁlters of size 3x3 are used in VGGNet. The depth of a ﬁlter
varies from layer to layer. Every ﬁlter is slided across width and height and
computes dot product between the entries of the ﬁlter and the input at any
position. As ﬁlter is slided over width and height a 2-dimentional, activation
map will be produced and will contain responses of that ﬁlter at every position.
A ﬁlter is slided across width and height with several steps: one for width and
one for height. Steps or strides are usually equal. Let’s consider the following
example: given an image of size 224x224x3 at very ﬁrst layer the ﬁlters of size
3x3x3 are used (3x3 is an architectural choice and the last 3 is because an input
image has depth of 3). The stride was chosen to be 1 both in width and height,
which means that the ﬁlter is moved by one pixel at a time. It is a common
practice to add some zero-padding to save the original image width and height
just for convenience. A padding depends on a ﬁlter size, for example if ﬁlters of
size 3x3 are used then a margin of 1 is added to the input image and therefore
the image of size 225x225x3 is used. Let’s assume that there are 16 ﬁlters at
ﬁrst convolutional layer therefore the output of the convolutional layer will be
224x224x16 where 224x224 is preserved because steps were chosen to be 1 and

2.9.1.2
Pooling layer
It is common to insert a pooling layer after several convolutional layers. It pro-
gressively reduces the size of the input for the following layer therefore it reduces
the amount of parameters for further layers and computational complexity of
a network.[KSH12] A pooling ﬁlter has the size of Fw ⇥Fh and operates inde-
pendently on every depth level and resizes it using one of the max, average or
L2 −norm. The most commonly used ﬁlters are of size 2 ⇥2 with stride S = 2
along both width and height which downsamples input by 2 along with both
width and height and preserves the depth. The summary [Kar] of pooling layer
is following:
• Accepts a volume of size Iw ⇥Ih ⇥Id
• Requires two hyperparameters:
The ﬁlters size Fw ⇥Fh ⇥1,
The stride S along width and height (usually it is the same for both
dimentions),
• Produces a volume of size Ow ⇥Oh ⇥Od where:
Ow = (Iw −Fw)/S + 1
Oh = (Ih −Fh)/S + 1
Od = Id
• Introduces zero parameters since it computes a ﬁxed function of the input
• Note that it is not common to use zero-padding for Pooling layers

Convolution Networks architecture
As it was mentioned above in section 2.9.1 a convolutional neural network con-

has not been mentioned yet is a RELU layer. It takes an input and applies an
elementwise activation function max(0, x) so that the size of input is preserved.
Sometimes RELU layers are embedded in convolutional layers. The most com-
mon architectures have several stacks of CONV -> RELU layers following by
POOL layer and this pattern is repeated several times until an input image be-
comes small in size. After this pattern there are several fully-connected layers,
where the last fully-connected layer holds scores for classes.[KSH12] The full
pattern can be described as follows [Kar]:
INPUT−> [[CONV −> RELU] ⇤N−> POOL?] ⇤M−> [FC−>
RELU] ⇤K−> FC
Where the ⇤indicates repetition, and the POOL? indicates an optional pooling
layer. Moreover, N ≥0 (and usually N 3), M ≥0, K ≥0 (and usually
K < 3).

The main idea of the paper [GEB15a] is to capture the texture information so-

that will possess both captured content and style. Capturing of both content
and style is done using Convolutional Neural Network, namely VGGNet. Input
image is transformed into layers’ representations along the processing hierarchy
of the network which increasingly cares about content of the image.

The Fourier transform decomposes a periodic function (signal) of time into fre-

which are presented in Figure 3.2

The Fourier transform is a complex-valued function of frequency, its absolute
value corresponds to amplitude and complex argument to phase oﬀset. A result
of the FT can be visualized as a so called spectrogram where axes correspond
to frequencies, and time and color of each element is amplitude. An example
of a spectrogram is presented in Figure 3.3. The VGGNet takes and image as

wave signal from only real part of FT produces the sound that is very close to
the original. At least it was not possible to diﬀerentiate them by human. In
case of 50% overlap the content and speaker are both easily recognizable but the
reconstructed sound has additional noise in it. Therefore, the decision was made

the step size is 64. One frame corresponds to one power spectrum. Each speech

chosen at each iteration. The ﬁrst 50 samples were chosen to be a validation set.

the style ps1 is how the speaker s1 pronounces it. Let deﬁne second speaker as

4.5
Back and forth transformation of sound into
images
To be sure that transforming sound into set of spectrogram chunks and recon-
structing a sound wave from the set of spectrogram chunks do not introduce
any kind of noise of sound distortion the following experiment was made. The
utterance u was taken and transformed into set of spectrogram chunks C as de-
scribed in section 3.4. The transformation regarding the style is not performed
so the spectrogram is just cut into chunks. The set of chunks C was taken and
reconstructed back into sound using procedure presented in section 3.7. The
original and reconstructed sounds were compared. Although, the reconstructed
sound introduces some error of noise, it is not possible to ﬁnd the diﬀerence
between these two for a human if spectrogram was sampled with window of size
256 with step 64 which means that two nearest power spectrums have 75% of
overlap. The experiment with 50% overlap was made as well which would give
an advantage during processing of chunks because the amount of them would
be lower. In this case the original and the reconstructed speeches can be eas-
ily understood by a human with respect to the content and the speaker. In
other words, any human could identify that these two recordings are made by
one speaker and the content is the same but there is an additional noise in the
reconstructed one. Therefore, to have higher accuracy in transformation the
spectrograms were created using 75% overlap and there is no loss during this
forward and backward transformation.

The pipeline using POOL4 layer
It can be seen from the results presented in Figure 4.10 that the transformation
using feature vector from layer POOL4 can almost reconstruct pixel values.
Therefore, the decision was made to train two new softmax classiﬁers which will
work with output from POOL4 layer. The time for preparation of the data set is
reduced because it was needed to make calculations only till the POOL4 layer.
However, the size of the data set exploded because every feature vector now has
size of 112 ⇥112 ⇥64 = 802816 instead of 4096 from the layer FC21. A very
small dataset was used to check the theory with POOL4 layer due to hardware
limitations. The dataset consists of feature vectors from POOL4 of 3-4 seconds
of speech from 2 speakers a male and a female. The total size of the training
set is 1045 vectors and 448 for the test set. The phoneme softmax classiﬁer
now has 802816⇥60 weights and 60 biases, so the total number of parameters is
802816⇤60+60 = 48169020. The speaker classiﬁer has 802816⇤2+2 = 1605634
total number of parameters because only two speakers were used. Although, the
training set is too small to train such big number of parameters, an attempt was
made. It ended up in a very low accuracy for both classiﬁers as it was expected.
This is due to very limited training set which for some phonemes has only one
example and it is not enough for training of such a big number of parameters.

Chapter 5
Conclusion
This thesis contains a lot of analysis of sound, namely speech, using deep neural
networks. Despite the fact of uncertain result this master thesis leads to further
research. The further work needs to ﬁnd a correct layer that can identify both
the content and the style suﬃcient enough for transforming spectrograms. The
provided loss function for obtaining both the style and the content should be
checked at the correct layer when the one will be found.
This work shows that the algorithm described in paper "A Neural Algorithm of
Artistic Style" [GEB15a] was mastered and applied to the other domain, namely
sound domain.
The major ﬁnding of this work is the pipeline that takes sound as the input
and allows to convert it into a set of spectrogram images. The spectrogram
images are transformed using some feature vectors in deep neural network. The
transformed spectrogram images are used for construction of a sound from them.
In other words this pipeline allows to construct a sound from correctly ordered
feature vectors from a layer in the deep neural network.
It has been shown that a content and a style of a speech utterance are separable
and that both of them can be captured from the utterance spectrogram. This
ﬁnding can be used for creating speech and speaker recognition systems which
might lead to improvement in performance.

Chapter 6
Further Work
First of all, the idea of usage of the POOL4 layer as the input for softmax
classiﬁers should be checked which requires more powerful hardware. There is
an improvement in pipeline that can be made to decrease space for storing the
dataset. There are two stages in softmaxes training: the ﬁrst one is to obtain the
dataset and the second one is to train using obtained dataset. In can be changed
in a way that when a mini-batch is obtained then the one iteration of gradient
descent is executed.
Therefore, the space for storing only one mini-batch is
required.
Secondly, the reconstructing power of layers POOL7, POOL11 and POOL15
should be checked. If it is suﬃcient enough then the usage of these layers will
simplify the classiﬁer training process because they have a smaller number of
output features.
Next, the total loss function can be deﬁned as the sum of every chunk loss func-
tion which will cover the whole utterance at once but will lead to the increased
amount of calculations required for every step of gradient descent.
Finally, the idea of sound decomposition into content and style can be elaborated
even more and lead to speech decomposition into content, style and emotion.
Now emotion is one of the features of style.
