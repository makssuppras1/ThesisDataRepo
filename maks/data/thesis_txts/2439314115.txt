Abstract
Oп¬Җshore platforms are subjected to random and repetitive force, such as wave and
wind load, making them prone to fatigue damage.
Fatigue is assessed in the design
process of platforms by assigning the platforms with a design lifetime on the basis of an
approximate load history. Several oil platforms are approaching the end of their design
lifetime, and large losses are associated with either abandoning an oil п¬Ғeld or constructing
a new platform. By obtaining information regarding the actual health of the structure
it is possible to extend its life time.
A way of acquiring such information is through
vibration based structural health monitoring. Sadly, there are several conditions that
complicate the monitoring. Non-linear connectors between platforms causing the response
of the structure to be non-linear, changes in platforms structural properties due to marine
growth and ingress of water are some of the issues that complicates the monitoring of the
structure. This thesis investigates a method of reducing the uncertainties associated withe
the actual structural health of the structure. The method considered takes advantage of
the one step updating approach[14] to reduce the uncertainties on the modal properties.
The updated modal properties are then utilized in a full п¬Ғeld strain estimation by use of
modal expansion to reduce the uncertainties on the stress time histories.
The investigation was carried out by creating two п¬Ғnite element models that were slightly
diп¬Җerent from each other, where one represented the experimental structure and the other
represented the numerical model of the experimental structure. Three diп¬Җerent responses
were then simulated for the experimental structure. One case where the response was
linear, and two non-linear cases.
The non-linearity was achieved by adding a friction
element in form of bilinear spring where the friction of the second part was zero. In the
п¬Ғrst friction case the spring had a low stiп¬Җness causing both the damping and stiп¬Җness of
the structure to vary with the amplitude of the response, in the second case the spring
was stiп¬Җand thus only the damping changed with the amplitude of the response. In each
simulation an operational modal analysis were performed based on the response at six
measurement points at the top side of the platform to obtain the modal properties of
the experimental model. The modal properties of the numerical model was then updated
based on the frequencies and mode shapes identiп¬Ғed by operational modal analysis. Next
the responses at the six measurement points of the top side was expanded to estimate
strains at two points perpendicular to each other 100mm above the base of the platform.
The expansion was carried out by using the mode shapes of the numerical model both
before and after updating. In both cases the precision of the strain estimates were assessed
by comparing the estimates to the strains measured by virtual strain gages.
The п¬Ғnding of the thesis implicates that using the proposed method one are able to reduce
the uncertainties on the modal properties and increase the precision of the strain estimate
for both linear and non-linear responses.

1.1
Literature Review
Oп¬Җshore oil platforms are costly structures and can have several people working and living
on them. Over the years numerous oil platforms have collapsed causing large economical
losses and the loss of many lives[1]. There are several damage scenarios that can cause these
collapses. One of them is the accumulation of fatigue damage, caused by random and repet-
itive forces such as wind and wave load. In the design process of oп¬Җshore platforms fatigue
is accounted for by a design lifetime[3].
However, there are uncertainties associated with
fatigue design in terms of the fatigue material characterization and the real hot spot time
series[2]. There are two main reasons for the uncertainties on the stress time histories. The
п¬Ғrst is introduced as a result of inaccuracies in the analytical or numerical model, as the mass,
stiп¬Җness and damping are only approximate estimate of the true properties. The second is
due to use of simpliп¬Ғed load models that does not represent the true loads with the needed
accuracy, regarding their variable amplitude, random nature, frequency bandwidth, sequence
eп¬Җect and so on.
Vibration based structural health monitoring might help reduce the uncertainties mentioned
above [28]. However, monitoring the structure in-situ can however be challenging, as most
of the structure is covered by water. Below-water-line measurements are expensive and can
put human life at risk if divers are used to place the equipment[1]. Oп¬Җshore platforms face
several sources that can create variability in structural properties, which causes the structural
response to become non-stationary. This complicates the structural health monitoring. Ex-
amples of phenomenas that changes structural properties are marine growth that alters the
mass, water ingress in structural elements which alters the mass and energy dissipation, and
connectors between platforms that induces non-linearities in the structure. In addition this
can make the numerical models of the real life structures to imprecise.
There have been develop several techniques to update the п¬Ғnite element models modal prop-
erties to reduce the diп¬Җerence between the numerical model and the real life structure[14], for
instance [35, 36, 37]. Among the most popular techniques are the sensitivity based methods
where the optimization is achieved iteratively[38, 39]. In [14] developed an approach where

the updating is achieved through one single step utilizing the local corresponds principal[13].
To reduce the uncertainties on the stress time histories a full п¬Ғeld dynamic strain responses
can be estimated based on measurements above sea level. The full п¬Ғeld strains estimates can

1.2
Scope of Thesis
An oп¬Җshore platform is designed with an expected life time. During the platforms lifetime
it would be of interest to gather information regarding its actual structural integrity. By
obtaining this knowledge a signiп¬Ғcant proп¬Ғt could be achieved if the life time of the structure
can be extended, or severe accidents can be avoided if it is detected that the structure is in a
worse state than expected. One way of obtaining the information is to follow the procedure
shown in Fig.1.1. First the platforms response under operating conditions is monitored only
above water in order to avoid the diп¬ғculties of placing sensors below the water line. Then an
operational modal analysis is performed to determine the modal parameters of the system,
damping ratio, frequency and mode shapes.
Further, the experimentally obtained mode
shapes are expanded by FE mode shapes from a model with large resemblance to the real life
platform. The expanded mode shapes can then be used to estimate the real time stress history
in the unmeasured points, which in turn gives information regarding the actual structural
integrity of the platform.

2.2
Operational Modal Analysis
Operational modal analysis is a method of obtaining the modal properties of a elastic system
by use of the systems measured response to an ambient loading, such as wind, waves or
traп¬ғc. The loading should have approximate white noise characteristic, which means that
it has energy distributed over a wide range of frequencies that covers the frequency range of
the structures modal characteristics[5]. This is important as it justiп¬Ғes ignoring the input
and only focusing on the output for the modal analysis as the loading will not hold any
7
==PAGE:22==
speciп¬Ғc information, it also ensures that all modes are adequately excited which enables their
identiп¬Ғcation. However, it is of minor signiп¬Ғcance if the loads impacting the structure does
not have exact white noise characteristics, provided that the modes of interest are suп¬ғciently
excited so that their contributions to the response can be measured. Sensors are used in order
to capture the response. The sensors are strategically placed such that the modes of interest
can be identiп¬Ғed from the response at the discrete points. Both frequency and time domain
can be used to process the measured time signals. In the time domain correlation functions
are used to perform the analysis, while spectral density functions are used in in the frequency
domain.

2.3.2
Friction Dampers
Friction dampers have at any time two possible motion states, stick or slip state[7]. The
damper motion switches between these two states. It is only in its slip state that the damper
dissipates energy. The damper is said to be in its stick state if there is no relative motion
occurring on the friction interface. In the stick state the friction force will be equal to the the
loading force[7]. Whereas the friction force in the slip state is equal to the constant slip force.
10
==PAGE:25==
At any given time the state of the friction damper is interactively determined by the response
of the structural system on which it is attached, and by its own properties. As the force and
kinematic condition in the two states are diп¬Җerent, the response of a structure becomes highly
non-linear. This causes analytical solutions of such systems to be too complicated, hence
the damper force is determined by iterating the non-linear damper force from the attached
friction element within each time step[7][3].

Conclusion
In the preceding sections the precision of strain estimates before and after updating has been
investigated both for linear and non-linear cases. The investigation was carried out though
simulations. This was done by creating two FE-models of an oп¬Җshore platform where one was
a slightly perturbed version of the other. The perturbed model represented the real life struc-
ture and the other FE-model the numerical representation of the real life structure. Linear
and non-linear responses of the experimental model to random loading was then simulated
using a state space formulation. The non-linearities were introduced by adding a friction ele-
ment that was behaving as a bilinear spring to one of the degrees of freedom of the platforms
top side. The simulations were carried out with both low spring stiп¬Җness causing both the
damping and stiп¬Җness of the platform to vary with amplitude of the response and one with
high stiп¬Җness were only the damping varied with the amplitude of the response. An opera-
tional modal analysis were then used to obtain the modal properties for each of the simulation
cases. The frequencies and mode shapes found from the modal identiп¬Ғcation was then used
to update the п¬Ғnite element model, creating three updated models one for the linear cases
and one for each of the non-linear cases. The strains were then estimated at point 100mm
above the base of platforms pile. To emulate a real life scenario were the sensors would have
been placed above the water line, the stress 100mm above the base was found by expanding
the response of the six active DOFs of the platforms top side. The expansion was don by
using the mode shapes of FE-model before and after updating for each simulation case. To
evaluate the precision of the stress estimates, they were compared to the strains measured by
virtual strain gages.
The investigation indicates that uncertainties with regards to the modal properties of the
FE-model can be reduced by using the one step updating approach both for linear and non-
linear structures. Further the results implies that through the method described above one
are able to estimate the strains with high precision both for the linear and non-linear cases.
The strain estimates for the non-linear cases is of similar quality to estimates for the linear
case only the fourth signiп¬Ғcant digit changes.
One of the weakness of the investigation carried out was that the experimental model and
the FE-model were to similar initially. There was not much gain in terms of precision on
the strain estimates by updating the model, when they are as precise as shown in TAB.4.1
to begin with. Thus, it would be interesting to see if similar results would be obtained after
updating if there was a larger discrepancy between the original models. The investigation has
58
==PAGE:73==
not provided any information regarding the tendency and trends of the estimation method, as
only one simulation were performed for each case. Hence, more simulations should be carried
out to get a better understanding of the precision of the method for both linear and non linear
stress estimations. In addition to this the method should be test using a true experimental
model, to make it closer to reality.
