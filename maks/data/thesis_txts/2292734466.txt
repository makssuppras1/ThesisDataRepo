Abstract:
In this thesis, the optimal maximum angle of pelvic yaw rotation was determined
for a model of the humanoid robot DRC-Hubo+ developed by Hubolab, KAIST. In
order to improve the energy usage and joint motor wear of the robot, in the form of
minimising the squared joint velocity and the position variation respectively, and
the work space of the robot. An equation for the pelvic yaw rotation was proposed
and utilised in the analysis of the optimal angle. The walking path patterns used
in the analysis are based on ZMP-based preview control and Orbital Energy. The
robot kinematic model is constructed based on the denavit-hartenberg convention
for forward and inverse kinematic. The dynamic robot model is determined numer-
ically, and based on the Euler-Lagrange dynamic equation. The results obtained
include a relation between the optimal angle and the step length, for the minimum
squared velocity- and for the minimum position variation analyses.
Key words: "DRC-Hubo+" "Pelvic rotation" "Improvement" "Optimal" "Step length"

Chapter 1
Introduction
In this chapter, an introduction of the project, its background and layout of the
following chapters are given. The project was proposed by prof. Oh, and has been
completed at HuboLab, KAIST.
1.1
Thesis Background
The focus of this project is the analysis of the humanoid robot DRC-Hubo+ with
regards to the use of pelvic yaw rotation. The DRC-Hubo+, see ﬁg.1.1, is a hu-
manoid general purpose robot developed speciﬁc for the DRC competitions, and is
the latest model in the Hubo robot series. The DRC-Hubo+ model was used by
HuboLab to win the DRC ﬁnals competition in June 2015. The Hubo robot series
is a series of humanoid robots, ﬁrst developed in 2002, which Hubo lab continues
to develop and improve with each new model.
In the development of the Hubo robot series, HuboLab has applied diﬀerent biped
walking concept in their research, including the pelvic rotation scheme. The pelvic
rotation used in HuboLab’s research has been used to obtain larger steps length,
but further eﬀects on the robot has yet to be researched. Among the eﬀects of
beneﬁts, that are of interest for possible improvement; are the energy consumption
of the robot’s joints and the wear on the robot’s joint motors.
1.2
Problem Formulation
The focus of the thesis; the analysis of the use of pelvic rotation for DRC-Hubo+
mentioned above, is about the analyses of the possible beneﬁts obtained by the use
of pelvic yaw rotation. The beneﬁts of the usage of pelvic yaw rotation, that will be
focused on in this thesis include a joint energy consumption estimate in regards to
squared joint angular velocity of the robot, and the joint motor wear of the robot
in regards to joint movement, both in order to determine the optimal pelvic yaw

1.2.1
Hypothesis
The analysis of the beneﬁts in the thesis, is based on the hypothesis that the usage
of pelvic rotation will result in the following improvements in the robots:
• Reduced total energy consumption in the joints of the robots.
• Reduced wear on the joint motors.
• increase in maximum walking height - COM height placement
• increased step length
The last hypothesis about step length has been proven priory, but is included in
the analysis to determine the amount of angle needed to increase the step length.

1.3
Thesis limitation
Due to the focus of this thesis, being about the rotation of the pelvic of the robot,
the movement of the upper body of the robot is of no interest to the analysis.
Therefore the following assumptions is made about the robots upper body:

2.1
Objectives of the Project
The main objectives of the analysis of DRC-Hubo+ in this project is to determined
whether the usage of walking pattern with pelvic yaw rotation, provides any further
beneﬁts than longer step sizes. For the case that the beneﬁts of interest do improve
with the usage of pelvic rotation, the optimal angle of the pelvic rotation is desired
to be determined. As mentioned in the chapter 1, the beneﬁts of interest in this
project, is the total energy consumption in the joints with regards to the squared
angular velocity, the wear on joints of the robot with respect to movement wear,
and the limits on the height of COM and step length.
The reason for the choice of beneﬁts of interest, is as follows:
Total energy:
By minimizing the total energy consumption, the cost of energy can be lowered,
this would make the robot more economical eﬃcient and also improve the operation
time of the robot, making longer distances possible. If the distance is of lesser
importance, it also allows for using batteries of same capacity density with lesser
volume, resulting in reducing the weight of the robot. The energy consumption of
each joint is estimated/deﬁned by the assumption that the joint’s kinetic energy is
only a function of joint angular velocity ˙θ.
joint Wear:
The more a joint motor is rotating or moving, the more the mechanical parts of the
joint motor is worn out. Therefore by minimizing the joint motor movement, the
expected operational life time of the joint motor can be improved with regards to
mechanical failure. The movement of the joint can be evaluated by its variance of
angular position during a test walking.
COM height:
By increasing the walking height of the COM/torso, a broader and more ﬂexible

4.2
Forward Kinematic
The Forward Kinematic of the robot is determined, by using the denavit-Hartenberg
convention in order to describe the geometric relation of each link of the the robot,
with regards to the links position and orientation. The Denavit-Hartenberg con-
vention describes position and orientation of link i with regards to link i −1 with
only four parameters, θ, α, d and a. The meaning of the four parameters are:
• θ: the angle between the x-axis’ of each frame, given as rotation amount
about the z-axis of the frame of link i −1.
• α: the angle between the z-axis’ of each frame, given as rotation amount
about the x-axis of the frame of link i.
• d: The oﬀset between the two frames of link i and i−1, given as displacement
along the z-axis of frame i −1.
• a: is the distance between the z-axis of each frame, given as a displacement
along the x-axis of frame i.
The value of the four parameter described above, for each link of a leg is given in
table 4.5, where the joint variable is marked with a *. The only diﬀerence between
the parameters of each leg is the sign of a1, it is positive for the left leg and negative
for the right leg. Some of the link names in table includes a _ this is a place holder
for either an R or L, depending whether one is interested in the right or left leg

6.1
Testing Procedure
The analysis of the results of the simulation is based on several simulations of
walking patterns with multiple varying parameters. The Procedure for each test
simulation is; First the desired walking path pattern is generated with the respect to
the simulations parameter. Then the walking pattern is transformed from feet and
torso position history to joint history by the use of the inverse kinematic, derived in
chapter 4. By using the obtained joint history and the numerical dynamics derived
in chapter 5, the joint torques of the robot are calculated, using the generated
walking pattern as the input.

Conclusion
In this project the beneﬁts of the usage of pelvic yaw rotation was examined for the
humanoid robot DRC-Hubo+, with regards to the approximated energy consump-
tion in form of squared angular joint velocity and joint motor wear with respect to
joint movement, as position variance, in addition to the eﬀect on the work space
of the robot. To do this the kinematic and dynamic models of the robot was con-
structed. Two designs of walking patterns was used for generating the desired test
paths for the analysis. A pelvic yaw rotation equation was proposed as:

It was used for generating the orientation of the pelvic for the walking patterns,

The proposed equation succeed in producing continuous rotations for position, ve-
locity and acceleration for constant step periods, but not continuous acceleration
for varying step periods. Based on the results obtained in the analysis, that the
usage of pelvic yaw rotation has no indicating eﬀect on the workspace of the robot
with respect to its walking height. The opposite results was achieved for the eﬀect
on the workspace with respect to the step length, therefore supporting the previ-
ous research that pelvic rotation can increase step length. The eﬀect of the pelvic
rotation was found to increase the maximum step length with a 10 cm increase,
by usage of approximately 30◦maximum pelvic yaw rotation or more, with the
cost of an increase in the needed minimum velocity. But it was also found that
the usage of pelvic rotation lowered the minimum velocity for a given step length.
With regards to the analyses of the optimal pelvic angle for the minimization of
position variation and squared velocity, it can be theorized that:
The optimal maximum pelvic yaw rotation angle for minimizing either the variation
of the joint angular position or the sum of the angular joint squared velocities, has
respectively a linear and exponential relations to the increase of step length, and is
independent of the velocity of the robots COM, and with the slope of the relation
being dependent on the robots walking height.

7.1
future work
For the future of the work of this thesis, the following topics and points could be
works of considerations:
1. For the further analysis of the beneﬁts of the use of pelvic rotation, the torque
change rates(steepness, velocity or accelerations) could be of interest to see
if the change of torque load on the motors can be improved, this relates to
decreasing the wear on the motors.
2. For more realistic humanoid walking pattern simulations to base the results
on, walking patterns with dual support phase of the feet can be considered in
stead of instant change of support leg.
3. Further work on improving the pelvic yaw rotation equation either in a
trigonometric form or a pure polynomial form for achieving continues ac-
celeration for time-varying step periods.
4. Improving the dynamics of the robot either by including the upper body
which was simpliﬁed in this project, or determine a more precise dynamic
of the robot by determine the exact eﬀect the joint torque has on the torso
frame. Which can be expanded to also include external disturbances on the
robots.
5. A repeat of the analyses either with a ﬁner interval on walking parameters,
especially the pelvic angle interval, or a mathematical approach like optimal
control theory in order to test the theory stated above, and if not falsiﬁed;
determine a mathematical description of the relation between the optimal
angle and the increase in step length, given above.

Summary: Danish
Formålet med dette kandidat speciale er at bestemme fordelene ved brugen af hofte yaw rotation 
for den humanoide robot DRC-Hubo+, udviklet ved Hubolab, Kaist, og i den sammenhæng 
bestemme den optimale rotations vinkle for  de givne fordele.  I rapporten er de interessante 
fordele, som analysen of hofte rotation vinklen er baseret på; robottens energi forbrug estimeret 
som summen af kvadrerede led hastigheder,  slidtagen af robottens led motorer med hensyn til 
led bevægelse, evaluerede som led position variansen, samt virkningen på størrelsen af 
robottens handleområde med hensyn til skidt længde og gang højde. 
En lignings definition of hofte yaw rotations vinklen er foreslået, hvorved en hofte rotation i 
fase med svingbenets bevægelse, så maksimum vinkel er opnået i det øjeblik svingbenet rammer 
jorden. 
I denne rapport bliver en simplificeret version af den reelle robot brugt, med den antagelse at 
robottens overkrop kan repræsenteres med et enkelt massepunkt. Robottens ønskede bevægelse 
brugt i analysen af fordelene er baseret på to forskellige udgangspunkter; orbital energi tilgang 
og ZMP-baseret preview kontrol tilgang. 
Modellen for robotten i projektet, indeholder både en kinematisk model og en dynamisk model 
af robotten. Den kinematiske model er basseret på Denavit-Hartenberg konventionen for at 
bestemme den simplificerede robots forward og inverse kinematik . Robottens dynamik er 
baseret på en numerisk tilgang til Euler-Lagrange bevægelsesligningen. 
Blandt de fundne resultater fra analysen af fordelene, blev det fundet at brugen af hofte rotation 
ikke havde nogen synlig betydning på handleområdet for robotten, med hensyn til ganghøjden. 
For skridt længden blev det fundet at det maksimale skridt længde kunne forøges ved brugen af 
hofte rotation, med en forøgelse på 10 cm ved en brug af 30 grader maksimum rotations vinkle 
eller mere. 
Andre resultater inkludere at relationen mellem den optimale hofte vinkle og skridt længden er 
lineær og eksponentiel relation, for minimeringen af henholdsvis position variansen og kvadrede 
led hastighed.
