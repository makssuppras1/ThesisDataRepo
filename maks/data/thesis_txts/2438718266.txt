Abstract
The leading edge of turbine blades erosion became one of big problem for wind turbine manufactures. To
investigate with the rain droplet impinge to the leading edge surface becomes one of solution. It will be
the solution for future leading edge design. Rain droplet impingement simulations could probably solve the
problem by analysis the force distribution and material study.
In this project, The model is based on ABAQUS simulations where the conditions for rain droplet impinge
to the leading edge surface due to velocity changes and material changes. The number of droplets can be
regarded as accumulating fatigue loads. And surface erosion is describing as potential growing deletion on
the surface and continually damage from surface to further comp-site substrate. The longer period last for
the surface will potentially minimize the crack growing in material,and it will also be able to lower damage
depths. The erosion rate can be calculated from performance of internal energy and impacting velocity. The
potential crack growing is correlated with the velocity of water droplet and matter of strikes object thickness.
The material characterized by fracture toughness where is also referring back to the Young’s modulus of
material. The method is creating impacts by various velocities and compare with experimental measurements
strain/internal energy rates. By doing the force distribution analysis, the results will be captured by force wave
propagating in diﬀerent velocities.
The 2D axi-symmetrix models were created to see force distribution from the static/quasi status. However,the
3D model is more functional to investigate with dynamics object conditions. With diﬀerent velocities im-
pacted on surface the material strain energy is changing. The project is about using rain droplet and rubber
ball for testing out the impacting on coating surface material. These two diﬀerent experiences are showing
rain droplet for realistic performance test and the other one is using rubber ball to compare with the experi-
mental results. For example the rubber ball shoots on the leading edge test.
The purpose of impingement simulation with two diﬀerent material is to get closer with the realistic cases
and to predict the cracking procedure. The the report will be also describing leading edge section where the
cracks exists in manufacture process. With developing the rain droplet simulation models into more complex
forms, it may be value-able for future designs.

Introduction
Leading edge erosion is one of big problem for wind turbine companies. Since the energy production declined
due to to the leading edge roughness, which is also lower the wind turbine reliability and lifetime. Generally,
the leading edge performance is directly inﬂuenced the power outputs. The signiﬁcantly damage will reduces
annual energy production (AEP) and increase the replacement or maintain budgets.
Researching in DTU has put in more eﬀorts to reduce the damage and to improve the surface coating. The
project includes to test the high speed rubber ball striking the leading edge prototype and to see how the
number of cycles eﬀect to the damage. However, the Finite element simulation is part of project and the
computational model is able the to capture the performances of surface’s erosion.
The motion of object can be characterized in to 3D performance and capture some of computational analysis
data. which can be used as improvement of material construction. The model can be used as a prediction of
impacting boundary layers transition. A better lifetime prediction will be able to mitigate edge erosion. Thus
there are some coating method or tape to protect the leading edge, but still the damage is not being mitigate
or properly solved. Nevertheless, the coating method is not good enough to solve for the long lifetime of
blades. Since the blade is checking in period of time and to make sure the leading edge in good condition.
Since the rain droplet is one highly eﬀecting to the wind turbine leading edge. In order to understand the
eﬀects and results it should be starting with the basic theories. By applying the theories and doing simulations
we will be able to optimize the surface treatments. In this master thesis, the data results are using ABAQUS
explicit function to simulate the dynamics movements and damages. In this thesis we will study about the
material characteristics, which is one of most important factor to understand. The material designs surface
hardness, performance, strength and ﬂexibility. A ﬁne coating material is able ti last for years by protecting
and slowing the surface being erosion by rain acid or strike.
By focusing on the structure itself where we will understand the how the droplet impacting to the surface.
A better design surface structure will be able to mitigate hard rebound and absorb the energy directly from
impingement to structure. In the relative study, we will go for striking wave theory/wave distribution theory.
The force distribution has ﬁve basic load case, longitudinal tension, longitudinal compression, transverse
tension, transverse compression and shear. By studying the most damage aﬀects we can lead to a better
material structure design.
To have a inside view, the stress waves traveled through elastic and plastic material with diﬀerent velocities.
And the longitudinal compression and transverse tension are main contribution of stress waves. In the pres-
sure where it involved with Poisson’s ratio and the direction of material particle movements and the stress
distribution also will be inﬂuenced. In this project, the propagation of force distribution is needed to be
calibrated.
In the thermal-dynamics point of view, the impact will generated the heat where the area have been hitting
with. The higher speed of object hitting is, the bigger heat generated will be.

Background of Wind Turbine developing
Since the ﬁrst windmill called Charles Brush’s which had been used for generating electricity in 1888. The
wind power has been used for long time in diﬀerent ways. For example the wind power has been used as sail-
ing tool for pushing the boat moving and used wind mill machine as pumper to do the agriculture. However,
the most rapidly developed wind power as household in early 20th century.
with the development of society, the fossil fuel and coal resource has been considered huge carbon emission
concern and the green energy became the replaceable energy in future. Today wind powered generated in big
size of turbines which will provide more power as national electrical net work for a country.
The wind energy price always a big concerns and to compare with oil price, but the oil price is ﬂuctuated
from year to year. Therefore, to develop a technology which can extracting power form nature became a
trend. The new technology will introduce with governments. For example, In the USA a series of prototype
turbine designs were created, starting with the 38m diameter 100kW Mod-0 in 1975 and ending with the
97.5m diameter 2.5MW Mod-5B in 1987 [23].
Moreover, for the Danish wind turbine development there is a signiﬁcant changed in 1987, where the ﬁrst
multi-megawatt wind turbine was constructed and many technologies has been used in modern wind turbines.
There were some companies like Vestas, Siemens and others to divide the design part for each company,
where they can get the part they need. The initial capacity of wind turbine was being made for 2MW. With
developing wind turbine history, these wind turbine still looks very identical to the modern windmills. Fol-
lowing these developing, they have public a standard for design and upscale for all wind turbines, for example
the rotation direction was counter-clockwise in the old day. After the blade supplier økær make a decision
to change the rotating direction. Those company, VESTAS,SIMENTS,ENERCON and NORDEX public
demands for the wind turbine to rotate in same way. The end the turbine is made in clockwise rotating as
standard.

The scale of wind turbines have grow rapidly for commercial design in 1980’s. By increase the size of blade
and the material has need to be changed in order to avoid some of problems. For example the over weight
of blade and blade structure. The sizes also matter with the tip speed ratio, when the tip speed potentially

lection, for example the rain droplet, hailstones hitting and birds striking they all damage the turbine leading
edge very much. When the rain droplet and hailstone highly speed around 80 m/s to hit with the leading
edge, it will turn out to be huge impact on surface protection. There Figure 2 is showing how the leading
edge impact by the rain droplet during 10 years.
According to the industry design, the blade serve life should be last for 15 years. During the time blade is not
only exposed to all kind of damages, but also the constant loading fatigue from the incoming winds. For the
case, the blade serve life will be dramatically decreasing by the problem of leading edge erosion. Moreover,
it needs to be maintaining in periodic time, which is also added up with total cost of wind turbine (LCOE).
Therefore, to make the wind turbine more durable, the surface treatment will be important for engineering to
investigate or research.

Blade design is considered as complex process. There are some of step have to fulﬁll in order to have good
design. The following points show how the consist design category need to be done:
• Aerodynamics shape for blade–The blade design have to fulﬁll with aerodynamics performance. Specif-
ically, the parameter like, airfoil type, chord length, twist and blade thickness, all the features should
vary along with the blade.
• Material–the material using for blade are mainly from composite material, which is reduce the weight
and prices in mega huge turbine. The material should be able to take a lot strength and load for ﬁerce
environmental conditions.

4.3
Background of blade design
For the years back when the size of wind turbine much more smaller than now. The material of blade was
designed to be alloys or any other stainless steel. However the problem of structural became to be more
impart to design a new blade. The performances due to many problems, for example the fatigue, ﬂexibility
and weight. In few years later, the composite material has been introduced as new material to do the big
scale turbine blades. Moreover, the most common composite material for design a wind turbine is glass ﬁber,
but glass ﬁber is not perfect, of course it gives very good performance for generating the power and it is
lighter than other material,but it is weak for internal shear stress. Although, the glass ﬁber is cheaper than
wood, alloy and carbon ﬁbers, but the glass ﬁber is not performance good when it be exposed with water
and any other liquids. Since the glass ﬁber will be damaged the inner structural and the weight of blades.
Due to the diﬀerent inertial weight the blade will not be last for longer than expected. Overall, the protection
methodology become a hot topic to do and investigate.
On the other hand, the glass ﬁber has strong strength to against with high speed wind and natural frequency
which is due to fatigue and bending moment for most cases. To choose a material which has higher performers
and light weight is material engineer most concern value for new material.

Conclusion
The purpose of this project was to investigate the damage evolution in coating wind turbine blades due to the
leading edge erosion. This project was accomplished by simulating 3D model by ABAQUS where the explicit
dynamics is implemented on. By doing the simulations on a model to obtain the impacted data. However,
there are some material selections and property insertions are according to some literature’s, therefore the
failure criteria is choosing as carbon ﬁber for lamina. The lamina material for carbon ﬁber is having some
property like tensile stress, compressive failure stress, shear strength, and coeﬃcient of stiﬀness characteris-
tics. By applying all the criteria into a model, and run the model to get the failures criteria on the surface.
On the other hand, the glass ﬁber failure criteria is not available to obtain from the literature’s and the carbon
ﬁber may be the one close to the case where it used for composite material for wind turbine blades. There are
two diﬀerent simulations which is about two diﬀerent objects strikes on the leading edge surface. After that,
it was showing the failure criteria may be reacting diﬀerently by speed objects and thickness. Furthermore,
the cracks reaction force is also being demonstrated by Appendix A where the case has showing the diﬀerent
shear force by object incoming loading force.
However, the force distribution is not linearly propagating in lamina structure, for the case rubber ball strikes
on isotropic lamina material has created deformation on the ﬁrst layer with very high speed. The deformation
is correlated with geometry of plane where it maybe more complex on the leading edge structure. Since a
rubber has reacted very intensive force compare to rain droplet, and the rain droplet force distribution is
creating a force ring and spreading from a small ring to a bigger ring. Moreover, a rain droplet impacting
stress is very mall as it showing on the results, but for the case where the rain droplets are continually hitting
on the surface for hours, which maybe cause more damage by accumulating the energy inputs.
Generally, the coating layer with ductile feature is able to mitigate high speed impacts on the surface and
reduce surface’s damage. The surface deletion is not obviously to observe by high speed rain droplet impacts.
The analysis was creating from axisymmetric 2D model to 3D explicit model to observe wave propagation
and distribution. The counter plot is showing energy distribution on the section view of material. The results
of simulation showed that energy outputs are changing proportionally with velocities.
The elastic property and coeﬃcient of stiﬀness are using for simulating the damping and tensile failure stress
and compressive failure stress. Those material properties are showing diﬀerences impacts results. Since the
rain droplets was too small to create a failure on the surface, therefore it is necessary to compare with rubber
ball impacting simulations.
In ABAQUS, there is limitation for doing explicit dynamics model in 2D, therefore the force distribution is
not going to be symmetry. The explicit dynamics model is able to capture the impacting counter plots and
material deformation. However, A 3D model is well presenting a rain droplet contacting with surface and
reformed it after strikes. The energy counter plot is showing the strain energy form a small ring to bigger
ring. Moreover, the simulation is challenging for CPU time and accuracy of the explicitly solver and time.
In this project, the goal was to create compressive force force from layer to layer and to test out functional
ductile property coating reacted after the dynamics motions. Since the diﬀerent material are not compile well
in this model,it was not able to demonstrate coating layer with carbon ﬁber reaction in high speed of object
