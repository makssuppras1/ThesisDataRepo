ABSTRACT
By
Shokufeh Shahin
Due to the increasing demand for renewable energy worldwide, performance, reliability and
quality issues of photovoltaic systems are becoming increasingly important. This work is a research
project to study the installation and analyse the operating characteristics and performance of a
3 kW grid-connected Photovoltaic-Battery system installed at DTU Ris√Є Campus. The пђБrst step
is implementation and commissioning of the Photovoltaic-Battery system to supply alternative
energy both to consumer and utility grid, with possibility to store the excess power into a battery
storage system. For this purpose, simulation of the studied grid-connected photovoltaic system
is performed using PVsyst software. Finally, the operational behaviour and performance of the
whole system are analysed comparing the experimental results with the predicted and theoretical
results for individual component. This thesis provides diпђАerent approaches to analyse performance
of a grid-connected Photovoltaic Battery system, which is beneпђБcial to comprehend and improve
the operational behaviour and energy yield of grid-connected Photovoltaic Battery systems.

1.1
Background and Motivation
Nowadays, with increasing electric energy consumption worldwide and concerns about reducing
greenhouse gas emissions, including carbon dioxide (CO2), adopting renewable energy sources to
produce clean electricity on the various ways is stressed1. Among the renewable energy sources,
wind power and solar Photovoltaics (PV) are the two most interesting due to their high accessibility
and potential everywhere in the world2. However, the power produced by these energy sources is
dependent on climate and weather, and пђВuctuates with meteorological conditions3. Therefore, the
power generation is non-predictable in the long term and can not be scheduled. In this regard,
integration of a Battery Energy Storage System (BESS) is one of the potential solutions to provide
a reliable and eпђГcient power generation, by enabling optimal use of renewable electricity sources.
BESS gives the possibility for energy security4 during non-production hours or when, for some
reason, the grid is unstable. In this respect, validating the operating performance of the grid-
connected PV-Battery system, is a key requirement to deпђБne the ability of the system.
1.2
Project Objectives
Based on the considerations above, concerning the quest for innovative renewable energy solutions,
this thesis presents a study of one of these proposed solutions. The main objectives of the project
can be summarized as follows:
вАҐ Installation and commissioning of a 3 kW grid-connected PV-Battery system.
вАҐ Analysing the performance of the system by validating the operating characteristics of the
system main components.

Design of the Photovoltaics-Battery System
2.1
System Overview
A solar photovoltaic power system, commonly referred to as PV system, is designed to convert
sunlight directly into electricity by employing diпђАerent components.
The power system in the
present study is a grid-connected PV-Battery system as demonstrated in Figure 1.
The main
advantage of this system is that has propensity to utilize the PV power generation by minimizing
the energy consumed from the grid.
The system is composed of a PV array generating renewable energy, a Battery Energy Storage
System (BESS) connected to the inverter to store electrical energy, a hybrid inverter, which engages
with both PV array and battery through Direct Current (DC) coupling, and interfaces with a
consumer and utility electric grid by Alternating Current (AC) coupling.
The electrical schematic diagram of the proposed grid-connected PV-Battery system is displayed
in Figure 1.
Figure 1: An electrical schema of system design illustrating the main components
The hybrid inverter allows the DC power generated by the PV array to supply the consumer
connected to the AC side of the inverter and sends the excess power to either the grid or BESS,
once the generated power is higher than the power required by the consumer. However, electricity
can be imported from the grid not only during the night time or in absence of the sun, but also
during the daytime, if the demand is greater than the PV power production.
Adding a battery storage could be a potential solutions to gain the power system пђВexibility and
eпђГciency, by enabling optimal use of PV electricity. The battery storage can give the possibility
for energy security5 during non-production hours or when for some reason, the grid is unstable. A
DC-coupled6 energy storage system consisting of a high voltage battery (to match PV voltage) is
designed for this purpose.
The imported electricity power to the system (either from PV panels or grid) will supply the
consumer or charge the battery. A percent of this power will be used for the components self-
consumption, or dissipated in the form of heat in the components. On a summer typical sunny
day, there is a considerable PV production by middle of the day. Around this time, the electric-
ity demand is at its minimum level compared with the rest of the year. Therefore, a signiпђБcant
part of the PV power will be delivered to the grid, then, the electricity imported from the grid is low.

2.2
Overall Measurement System
A PV system performance and accordingly its power generation are dependent on both the weather
conditions and the system quality, so it is essential to have a clear understanding of the system
being tested. An optimum power production is dependent on high irradiance, low module/ambient
temperature and the least possible losses. In this regard, monitoring and analysing some related
parameters such as the modules temperature, solar irradiance, produced current, voltage and power
could be beneпђБcial to characterising the ability of the PV-Battery system to generate electricity.
Irradiance -
Since solar radiation is the input power for a solar energy generation system, and
due to dependency of the PV modules short circuit current (Isc) and open circuit voltage (Voc) on
the solar irradiance, monitoring this parameter is a key component to characterise the PV-Battery
system by determining the performance of the PV array.

PV modules are usually designed to have a lifespan of about 25 years, but the modulesвАЩ protec-

There are many techniques to achieve these purposes, but Electroluminescence Imaging and I-V

a number of causes for disturbances in the PV modules, which reduce its performance and safety.
One of the advantages of this technique is the ability of EL imaging a PV cell or module in a
relatively short time frame. The light output increases with the local voltage so that areas with

3.1.2
Current-Voltage Curve Characterisation
Since the conversion eпђГciency of a photovoltaic module is a ratio of the produced PV power (W) to
the total received input energy from light irradiance (W/m2), the module performance depends on
some parameters such as module temperature, irradiation level, PV voltage and PV current. But
the related parameters determined by manufacturers are measured in STC, which rarely occurs in
пђБeld. Furthermore, manufacturing processes often leads to slight diпђАerences in modulesвАЩ electrical
characteristics, even within the same PV cell type. Therefore, it is nearly impossible to пђБnd two
identical PV modules.
In view of the above considerations, the experimental measurement of the Current-Voltage (I-V)
curve makes it possible to gain an awareness of the precision in the electrical characteristics and
determines the performance of a solar module.
The I-V curve of a PV module is characterised by three main parameters. Two of them are open
circuit voltage Voc (@ Ipv = 0) and short circuit current Isc (@ Vpv = 0). Hence, it is obvious
that the maximum voltage available from a module will be measured at open circuit, whereas the
maximum current will be measured at short circuit. However, in neither of these two situations,
the PV module will generate any power. The third remarkable parameter placed on вАЩkneeвАЩ of the
I-V curve is maximum power point. At this point, the solar module has its ideal operation and
generates maximum power.
A typical I-V curve starts at the short circuit current and ends at the open circuit voltage. Since
power is the product of voltage and current, so Power-Voltage (P-V) curve can be generated from
these measured parameters. The point on a P-V curve that has the highest value, presents the
maximum produced power by the module. This maximum power is corresponding to maximum
power point located at the I-V curve.

6
Conclusions and Future Work
A detailed characteristics and performance analysis of the grid-connected PV-Battery system has
been performed.
In the пђБrst instance, a description of the studied system design, selected components and the
measurement principles is given.
Simultaneously, a PVsyst simulation has been provided, and
supported by on-site weather data, to predict and assess the function of the studied system. Then,
to determine the capability of the system operation, diпђАerent experimental measurements were
performed. All the main components have been investigated in terms of their characteristics and
behavior under real operating conditions. The performance and eпђГciency of each component was
evaluated by validating the collected experimental data by comparing them with the predicted
results and theoretical values and calculations based on the datasheets.
The analysis results from solar panels conпђБrmed the strong dependency of the modules performance
on the solar irradiance level, as by increasing the irradiance from 100 W/m2 to 1000 W/m2, a 90.3 %
growth occurs in PV power generation, Pmpp (see Table 7 and Table 9). From the eпђГciency curve
of the hybrid inverter illustrated in Figure 28 and by considering Table 22, this growth will cause
an increasing of 93 % (from 212 W to 3000 W) in AC power generation. Therefore, the irradiance
information of the PV site is one of the main considerations, when designing and sizing PV power
systems. Referring to the previous argument and as experienced (see Figure 23), since the PV
modules are highly dependent on the environmental conditions, namely the solar irradiance and
the temperature, they often operate below their nominal DC output power. Consequently, the
rated DC power of the PV array is lower than nominal AC output power of the inverter (3 kW).
It is referred to as an oversized inverter, which is waste of capital, considering the dependency of
the inverter price on its nominal capacity.
6.1
Future Work
Further research could be carried out in diпђАerent areas. In order to increase the solar system
capacity and produce the maximum amount of energy - in the most cost-eпђАective way - it could
be suggested to oversize the PV array by adding more PV modules to the system. By doing so,
on the bright sunny days, the DC power produced by PV array can exceed inverter capacity. In
this scenario, the inverter will вАЩclipвАЩ the excess power above its rated capacity. However, it allows
to generate more power from solar energy harvesting, and make better use of the inverter AC
output capacity, which leads to a reduction in the cost per kW. Furthermore, a full charge of the
battery from PV array will occur in a relatively shorter time duration. However, this suggestion
is dependent on the available installation area on the rooftop.
Additionally, since seasonal variation and dust accumulation are major drawbacks for power gener-
ation in PV systems, it can be suggested to investigate the performance of the PV-Battery system
under a long-term (at least, one year) analysis, in future work.
As electricity usage has been rapidly increasing, and accordingly, the energy prices are rising, also
due to 2030 Climate Target Plan40, decreasing the upfront expenses of PV systems is one way to
make solar energy more accessible. In this regards, including a cost-beneпђБt analysis in future work
could be worthwhile.
The work presented in this thesis provides an overview of the installation process and operating
