Summary
In this thesis, a cryogenic 1H and 13C volume coil for small animal MRI at 3 Tesla
was designed. It consists of a dual tuned birdcage low pass coil , two 90 ◦hybrid
coupler and a cryogenic system.
The dual tuned low pass birdcage coil composed of 16 legs separated by 22.5◦and
two end rings , 8 legs tuned to Larmor frequency of hydrogen 1H 127.79 MHz and
the remaining 8 legs tuned to Larmor frequency of carbon 13C 32.13 MHz, and they
shared same two end rings.
A trap circuit was connected at each carbon leg to
minimize the coupling between hydrogen legs and carbon legs.
The dual tuned birdcage coil was driven by quadrature excitation sources with
a phase difference 90◦and geometrically 90◦apart. A 90◦hybrid coupler was used to
design the quadrature excitation source of carbon 13C and hydrogen1H. The hydrogen
90◦hybrid coupler was constructed from a quarter wavelengths transmission line while
the carbon 90◦hybrid coupler was constructed from lumped component.
The cryogenic system is used to cool the birdcage coil with liquid nitrogen to
improve the Q-factor for both channels . The cryogenic system consisted of a birdcage
coil fixed inside a box which is composed from poly-carbonate and filled with liquid
nitrogen.
Keysight’s Advanced Design System , Momentum and CST studio suite were used
to design and simulate all the mentioned components. All the parts were fabricated
and tested. The final results fulfill the design requirements.

CHAPTER 1
Theory
Human body contains 70% of water as their major constituent . The water molecule
is composed of two hydrogen atoms and one atom of oxygen bonded together. One
hydrogen atom consists of a central nucleus with proton positive charge and an or-
biting electron turning around with a negative charge. The proton rotates physically
around its own axis, it is referred as Spin. Due to this spinning, a magnetic moment
is produced in the direction of the spinning axis, so each proton acts as a micro bar
magnet with two poles (N and S).
The magnetic moments of the protons in the human body are randomly oriented
and not produce net magnetization (M).
1.1
Precession and Larmor frequency
If these protons exposed to a strong magnetic field B0, the magnetic moments of
these protons will align with the direction of the field or opposite. The magnetic
moments with low energy state will align with the direction of the field and the
magnetic moments with high energy will align ant-parallel to the field.

1 Theory
The ratio of the magnetic moment (magneto) to it is spin (gyro) called gyro-
magnetic ratio γ and it has a unit of radians per second per Tesla. The gyro-magnetic
ratio γ will have a positive value if the magnetic moments are in the same direction
of the spin and vice verse.
The number of hydrogen protons which are parallel to the field more than the hy-
drogen proton which is anti-parallel to the field by a small percentage, so the net
magnetization is not zero figure (1.1).
Figure 1.1: The effect of exposing a strong magnetic field (1 Tesla)
The difference between two energy state for the parallel and anti-parallel proton
are proportional to the strength of the magnetic field B0 [Lan11]
∆E = γ.h.B0
(1.1)
Where h is Planck’s constant which has a value approximately 6.626x10−34Joule-sec
.
By increasing the exposed magnetic field B0, the number of anti-parallel hydrogen
protons will change their orientation to parallel figure (1.2).
Figure 1.2: The effect of increasing the magnetic field B0 (1.5 Tesla)

tipped from their alignment and wobbling in a circular path about the direction of the
magnetic field.This kind of motion called precession. The difference between spinning
and precession, spinning the protons rotates around its own axis (white arrow) while

Superconducting magnet :
Most of the MRI system uses a superconducting magnet, it is a large magnet takes

The superconducting magnet consists of a large coil with many winding wires. When

coil close to ZERO ohms and the current will continue passing through the magnetic
coil and create magnetic field called B0 perpendicular to the human body inside the
scanner as long as the coil wires resistance is zero.

Birdcage Coil Design
Birdcage coils are standard for the volume coil in MR (Magnetic Resonance), they
provide a good homogeneous field B1 and signal to noise ratio. In dual-tuned birdcage
coils, the main challenge is to achieve a good homogeneous field B1 and circular
polarized field at two-desired frequency by controlling the distribution of the current
passing through the legs and end rings of the birdcage . The required design is dual-
tuned birdcage coil for hydrogen 1H and carbon 13C at 3 Tesla for a small animal like
a mouse.

designs of single birdcage coil(High pass-Low pass) were proposed to study the field
homogeneity and complexity of each design at the desired frequencies. The specifica-

4.3
Cryogenic system for the dual-tuned birdcage coil
The quality factor of the coil can be improved by decreasing the coil resistance
Rcoil. The temperature coefficient of resistance of copper at room temperature 20◦is
+0.394◦/C◦[Del], so by decreasing the coil temperature the Rcoil coil resistance will
decrease and Q-factor will increase. The coil cooling can be done by immersing the
birdcage coil in the liquid nitrogen (-196 C◦). The cooling can be accomplished by
designing a cryogenic system for the birdcage coil. The cryogenic system consisted
of ploy-carbonate box without lid which is placed inside a Styrofoam box. The coil
was fixed inside the box to make sure that the whole coil is immersed in the liquid
nitrogen .
The box constructed from a poly-carbonate material, the poly-carbonate was selected
because it has a thermal expansion coefficient close to the fiberglass tube. The co-
efficient of thermal expansion (CTE) describes the dimensional change in a material
as a response to a change in temperature and is defined as a percentage change in
length per unit temperature. For poly-carbonate the CTE is (65 to 70)x10−6 m/K
and for the fiberglass tube CTE is 39x10−6 m/K [Eng]. The Stycast epoxy glue is
used to fix the birdcage coil and all box parts. The CTE of the Stycast epoxy glue
is (35 to 98)x10−6 m/K. The gluing done inside the fume hood to limit exposure to
hazardous or toxic fumes.
The poly-carbonate box has no isolation, but it was placed inside the Styrofoam box,
this ensures a small thermal gradient across the glue joins.

In this thesis, a Cryogenic 1H and 13C volume coil for small animal MRI at 3 Tesla
was designed.
The birdcage coils are widely used in magnetic resonance imaging
system because they provide a high homogeneous field and it can produce a circular
polarized filed by using quadrature excitation. Dual-tuned low pass birdcage coil was
designed to be suitable for a small animal like a mouse. The low pass birdcage coil
configuration provides a good homogeneous field and easier to design than high pass
configuration because it requires only half the number of trap circuit to implement.
The dual tuned birdcage coil consisted of 16 legs of two virtual coils, the even legs
(8 legs) allocated for a first virtual coil at a desired frequency, and the odd legs allo-
cated for the second virtual coil at another desired frequency; all the 16 legs shared
same two end rings. To minimize the coupling between hydrogen legs and carbon
legs, the trap circuit is connected to each carbon legs.
CST studio suit software used to design and simulate the birdcage coil performance,
S-parameters, B1+, B1- and the magnitude of H field used to investigate the RF
homogeneity, circular polarized fled, matching and decoupling between the two vir-
tual coils. Then the birdcage coil fabricated on substrate roger 5880 and tested with
comparison to the simulation results.
For the quadrature excitation sources, two 90◦hybrid coupler were designed. For
carbon quadrature excitation sources, the 90◦hybrid coupler designed with a lumped
component to operate at larmor frequency of carbon 32.13 MHz. ADS software is
used to design, layout and simulate the couplers. The carbon coupler was fabricated
in the lab and the results met the design requirements. At 32.13 MHz the power split
equally (45.5%) between port 2 and port 3, with phase difference 89.43◦and a good
isolation -19 dB.
For hydrogen quadrature excitation sources, the 90◦hybrid coupler designed with a
compact transmission line to operate at larmor frequency of hydrogen 127.79 MHz.
The hydrogen coupler is fabricated in FR4 PCB and the results met the design re-
quirements. At 127.79 MHZ the power split equally (45.5%) between port 2 and port
3, with phase difference 87.65◦and a good isolation -24.6 dB.
To verify the coil performance, Q-factor was calculated for both channels. Q-factor
of carbon channel and hydrogen channel was 29.8 and 43 respectively, there is no

5.1.1
90◦hybrid coupler
The FR4 is a lossy substrate, the loss of power at output ports and the phase shift is
proportional to the loss of FR4. The performance can be improved by changing the
substrate with a less lossy substrate like Roger series. Moreover the size of hydrogen
90◦hybrid coupler can be reduced with more curves and use of the free space inside

5.1.2
Dual-tuned birdcage coil
Imaging experiments using MRI scanner can be performed to verify the performance
(signal to noise ration SNR and field maps) of the designed dual-tuned birdcage coil.
5.1.3
Cryogenic system
Another design method and techniques can be implemented to cool the coil. In this
design the birdcage coil elements continued to work without any cracks in elements
or soldering points but some joints of the poly-carbonate box which is glued together
by epoxy glue have been cracked. Therefore a new design is recommend to avoid this
kind of breakdown.
