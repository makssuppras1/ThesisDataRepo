Abstract
Security associated threats are often increased for online media during a pan-
demic, such as COVID-19, along with changes in work environment. For exam-
ple, employees in many companies and organizations have started to work from
home due to the COVID-19 pandemic. This working environment has increased
remote activities and further reliance on email for communication, thus creating
an ideal condition for email fraud schemes. In this context, the major of this
project was to evaluate the privacy policy of online social media and to identify
potential security associated problems. In this work, the risk analysis of online
social media, namely Facebook, Twitter and LinkedIn was performed by using a
STRIDE model to ﬁnd threats and vulnerabilities in the online social media. In
this analysis, the phishing attack was found to be a main threat in online social
media, which is a social engineering attack, where users are convinced through
some fake messages or emails to extract their personal credentials. The next
step, that was mainly focused in this project, was to design a phishing attack
and provide a solution from protection. In this project, a phishing attacked
model was developed to demonstrate how the developed phishing attacked can
succeed to collect users credentials. In particular, the URL was designed by
using HiddenEye tool in Kali Linux and succeed to collect users credentials by
convincing to login fake LinkedIn page. The designed URL in Kali Linux us-
ing HiddenEye tool can only be used for phishing within a local network. To
overcome this limitation, real phishing attack was designed using real Apache
server and collected victim user’s credentials in Mariadb database. And user
awareness anti-phishing solution is provided for user by analyzing a features of
designed URL and phishing email. For protection, this project focuses on user
awareness to protect and prevent against phishing attack and provides some
awareness tools for users.

Social networking, in the recent years, is

It is better to choose social media sites that can be relevant to individual users.

Attacks on Facebook
In most of the cases, attackers make use of the Facebook infrastructure to gather

In doing so
the attackers are able to make them go to malicious links, advertisements by

Some of such common attacks in Facebook are as
follows [31]:
• Compromised Account Attacks
A compromised account is the condition of an account in which legitimate
users lose complete or partial control over the login credentials of their
account [16]. Accounts can be compromised in diﬀerent ways, via. by
using phishing scam to gather user login credentials, by utilizing cross-site
scripting, adopting bots to harvest the login credentials. Compromised
accounts are very powerful means to spread out the malicious contents
that can deteriorate the relationship established by the legitimate user in
the past, to communicate the malicious contents rapidly and eﬀectively.
• Sybil Attacks
Malicious users create Several fake identities, called Sybil, for inﬂuencing
their identity within a target network [32]. After that, such malicious users
send friend request to rest of the users of that network. When one accepts
the friend request, the malicious users will forward the malware and spam.
Normally, Sybil attacks are found to be of two ways on Facebook. Attacker
generate several fake identities to create legitimate accounts to spread
malware and spam to friends in their friend list or form more social links
to distribute malware and spam.
• Socware Attacks
In this type of attack, contenders create malware, known as socware, in the
form of events, applications or pages capable of having links to malicious
contents. False gift vouchers, coupons, and gifts work as stimulants to
attract victims, which will make them install or accept the malware [20].
Soon the malware is installed in the system, the attackers easily gather
personal information stored by the users. On top of that, the malware is
posted on the user’s wall, which will spread on their friend’s proﬁle too.
• Identity Clone Attacks
Malicious users, sometimes, create similar proﬁles pretending to be the
victims and outspread malicious content in their network.
To make it

• Creepers Attacks

Phishing Attack
Phishing is a social engineering attack, where users are convinced through some
fake messages or emails, to extract their personal credentials. In phishing at-
tacks, the intruder or phisher uses some social engineering techniques to convince
the victim to expose their personal credentials by using fake email, fake website,
fake links, IP spooﬁng etc,. The fake email used by phisher to convince victim
in two ways legitimate email and embedded hyperlink email [1]. The fake email

response, intruders also use instant messaging by using Viber and WhatsApp.

Chapter 6
Conclusion and Future
Work
The main purpose of the thesis is to give an overview of common attacks in online
social media, way of designing a phishing attack and its anti-phishing solutions
by awareness to the user about phishing attack. Nowadays, cyber criminals are
using social media such as Facebook, Twitter and LinkedIn for their phishing
attack. Cyber criminals are taking advantage of social media to collect personal
information for their attack because users make a lot of conﬁdential informa-
tion public such as location, job, email, contact number and more. To design
an attack this project was focused in security policy and risk analysis of online
social media. By using risk analysis, I found that phishing attack has a high
probability of risk in online social media or using an online social media tools
for phishing attack as compared to other potential attacks. During the research,
I found that criminals are taking advantage of the COVID-19 pandemic. So in
the designed phishing attack, the information about COVID-19 is included for
convincing the user to click on designed URL.
There is a limitation on designed URL by using HiddenEye tool that works
only within a local network.
To overcome this limitation, I designed a real
phishing URL that can catch victim’s real username and password outside of
the network. I tested real phishing for DTU student in my DTU student email.
DTU server could not stop the phishing even it was not secured URL and I
succeed to collect username and password. These attacks cannot be prevented

but they can be mitigated. Companies should train their employees in regards
to email use and detecting phishing attacks. This training should be done at on-
boarding for new employees and everyone should get a periodic refresher course.
Companies should also review what information they make public and carefully
consider what information should be made public and what should not. From
the real phishing test in my gmail account and DTU student email account by
using designed URL in hiddeneye tool ( bit.ly/3eGnm9d) and real phishing URL

training programs, user should be followed up with sending fake emails to test

The research will help users understand the attacks and diﬀerent techniques

and prevention awareness to the users, awareness education should be connect
to phishing simulator module for automatically educate organizations, employ-
ees and common private users against cyberattacks making them the ﬁrst line
of defense against today’s advance attack. For the future work, I would like
