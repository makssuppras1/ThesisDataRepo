Introduction
Large Language Models (LLMs) have been steadily improving in a wide range of tasks,
demonstrating enhanced performance with each new release across various benchmarks
that demand complex reasoning capabilities. This evolution began with the introduction
of the Transformer architecture [1], which significantly boosted the accuracy of token┬¡
generation models in diverse applications. Most notably, foundational models like BERT[2]
and GPT[3] showcased the initial emergence of reasoning abilities. As these models
scaled in sizeÔÇöwith GPT┬¡2[4] featuring 1.5 billion parameters and GPT┬¡3[5] scaling up by
approximately 100 times to 175 billion parametersÔÇöthey excelled in System 1 thinking,
which according to Cognitive Sciences[6] is the fast, automatic, and intuitive way of think┬¡
ing, enabling quick decisions with minimal reasoning effort. However, they struggled with
System 2 thinking, which is the heavy┬¡thinking that relies on deliberate logical reasoning
and systematic problem┬¡solving.
Around this period, Chain of Thought (CoT)[7] prompting was introduced as a break┬¡
through technique.
By incorporating few┬¡shot examples of step┬¡by┬¡step reasoning in
prompts, CoT unlocked latent reasoning capabilities in foundation models.
This was
further simplified with Zero┬¡Shot CoT[8], where merely appending ÔÇØThink step by stepÔÇØ
to the prompt could elicit System 2┬¡like thinking. Building on this foundation, several
other test┬¡time inference methods emerged to refine and enhance CoT, including Tree of
Thoughts[9] (which explores multiple reasoning paths in a tree structure), Self┬¡Consistency
[10] (which samples multiple reasoning chains and selects the most consistent answer),
and Self┬¡Refine[11] (which iteratively improves outputs through self┬¡feedback). These ap┬¡
proaches yielded incremental improvements over standard CoT. One of the most recent,
sophisticated, and high┬¡performing variants is Adapted Graph of Thoughts (AGoT)[12],
which leverages dynamic decomposition via directed acyclic graphs (DAGs) to break
down complex tasks into interconnected sub┬¡problems, allowing for more structured and
parallel reasoning, achieving even higher reuslts that itÔÇÖs predecessors.
Drwaing inspiration from the test┬¡time inference methods, another major advancement in
achieving System 2 thinking in LLMs came with the release of models like OpenAIÔÇÖs o1[13]
and DeepSeek┬¡v1[14]. These models exhibit explicit reasoning traces before generat┬¡
ing final answers, trained using supervised fine┬¡tuning (SFT) and reinforcement learning
(RL) on vast corpora of System 2┬¡style reasoning examples. This approach, often termed
Long Chain of Thought[15], incorporates three key characteristics: Deep Reasoning (thor┬¡
ough step┬¡by┬¡step analysis), Extensive Exploration (considering multiple angles and al┬¡
ternatives), and Reflection (self┬¡evaluation to correct errors). Although these reasoning┬¡
focused models generally outperform traditional post┬¡inference prompting techniques,
they demand substantial computational resources and data during training, and they
remain susceptible to overthinking (unnecessary elaboration) and hallucination [16][17]
(generating plausible but incorrect information). Nevertheless, state┬¡of┬¡the┬¡art models
such o3[18], DeepSeek┬¡v3[19], and Grok┬¡4[20] leverage this kind of training and reason┬¡
ing paradigm to achieve state┬¡of┬¡the┬¡art results.
In this thesis, I introduce Immersive Chain of Thought (ICoT), a post┬¡inference prompting
technique that draws inspiration from the problem fragmentation in AGoT, the simplicity
of CoT, and the dual┬¡process logic of System 1 and System 2 thinking. ICoT capital┬¡
izes on foundation modelsÔÇÖ tendency to default to System 1 thinkingÔÇöbypassing deeper
reasoningÔÇöby guiding the model to generate a step┬¡by┬¡step reasoning process until a

2.1
Inspiration
Drawing inspiration from the simplicity of Zero┬¡Shot Chain of Thought (CoT), which effec┬¡
tively triggers System 2 thinking, as well as the interplay between System 1 and System 2
thinking, and addressing the limitations of existing post┬¡inference prompting techniques,
we propose Immersive Chain of Thought (ICoT). Transformer┬¡based large language mod┬¡
els (LLMs) are typically trained on vast corpora of text rich in factual knowledge, which
predisposes them to produce quick, intuitive System 1 responses that often lack deeper
reasoning. The introduction of CoT marked a significant milestone by unlocking advanced
reasoning capabilities in these models through structured, step┬¡by┬¡step reasoning pro┬¡
cess. Subsequent techniques have built upon CoT, achieving incremental improvements.
For instance, methods like Tree of Thoughts generate multiple reasoning paths to explore
diverse solutions, while Self┬¡Refine iteratively refines a single reasoning chain until an
optimal result is achieved. Unfortunately these techniques seem like a hit or miss and
they do not take into consideration the continuous advancement of the LLM landscape.
Moreover, most of these techniques do not account for the fact that transformer┬¡based
LLMs are highly sensitive to previous text and intermediate results[21], due to their at┬¡
tention mechanisms and this can distract the model and cause it to go off track, lead┬¡
ing to suboptimal reasoning outcomes. Other innovative approaches, such as Adapted
Graph of Thoughts (AGoT), leverage fragmentation to decompose complex tasks into
sub┬¡problems. However, AGoTÔÇÖs complexity requires models to manage intricate graph
structures and nodes, distracting them of the main problem, which otherwise, can lead to
inefficiencies or unintended loops.
Recognizing these challenges, ICoT is designed to balance sophistication and simplic┬¡
ity. While it is a complex technique in its ability to handle intricate reasoning tasks, it
remains straightforward for the model to execute, as it does not require tracking multiple
operations or maintaining complex structures. Instead, ICoT guides the model to focus
on a recursive, step┬¡by┬¡step reasoning process, updating the main objective to the new
sub┬¡problem without lolsing any iformation or being distracted from previous analyses by
leveraging a dynamic ÔÇØAnalysis JournalÔÇØ to store summarized intermediate findings. By
recursively addressing complex sub┬¡problems as they arise and aggregating insights to
resolve the original query, ICoT enables robust System 2 thinking while mitigating the
structural complexities and drawbacks of other methods.
2.2
The method
Given a problem q, the model is prompted to follow a step┬¡by┬¡step reasoning process to
derive a solution. For each step, the model generates three components: a title describ┬¡
ing the step, an analysis for that step, and a complexity flag indicating whether the step
is complex (requiring further decomposition) or not. Throughout the process, a global
ÔÇØAnalysis JournalÔÇØ is maintained, which stores insights, findings, and intermediate results
from all steps across all depths. The model is instructed to consult the Analysis Journal
at each step to inform its current analysis, avoid redundant work, and maintain focus by
leveraging prior context.
When a complex step is encountered, the reasoning process pauses. At this point, an
independent model, generates summarized insights and updates the Analysis Journal

problem) and either no complex steps is encountered or max_iterations is reached. At
this point, the model synthesizes all insights from the Analysis Journal and provides a
final step by step analysis with a final answer to the original problem q. An Algorithmic

2.5
Discussion
In this section, we discuss our interpretation of the results and propose hypotheses about
the evolving landscape of large language models (LLMs) and their future. The observed
performance degradation of AGoT and CoT on more advanced models is intriguing and,
we believe, stems from distinct underlying causes. CoT, despite its foundational role in
enhancing reasoning, appears to fall short with newer models, likely due to the challeng┬¡
ing nature of the GPQA dataset. Originally designed to help models like gpt┬¡3.5 solve
middle┬¡ and high┬¡school┬¡level problems, CoT struggles with the complex, postgraduate┬¡
level questions in GPQA, which demand deep analytical reasoning. While later models
(e.g., gpt┬¡4o┬¡mini and gpt┬¡4.1┬¡mini) are inherently more capable of such analysis, impos┬¡
ing a CoT structure forces them to unnecessarily elongate their reasoning into multiple
explicit steps, even when a more concise, direct approach might suffice. This stretched
reasoning, while akin to the deep reasoning seen in Long Chain┬¡of┬¡Thought models like
Immersive Chain┬¡Of┬¡Though

==PAGE:11==
o1┬¡mini and o4┬¡mini, often overcomplicates the process, flooding the modelÔÇÖs context win┬¡
dow with verbose and complicated distracting information and analyses that eventually
harm the final answer.
For AGoT, the decline in performance from gpt┬¡4o┬¡mini to gpt┬¡4.1┬¡mini suggests a dif┬¡
ferent issue. AGoTÔÇÖs graph┬¡based structure, which involves tracking multiple nodes and
connections, is effective in earlier models like gpt┬¡4o┬¡mini but becomes counterproductive
in gpt┬¡4.1┬¡mini. We hypothesize that more advanced models, with improved adherence
to instructed methods, overly focus on the procedural complexity, prioritizing the mainte┬¡
nance of its intricate graph structure over the essence of problem┬¡solving. This leads to a
loss of context, as the model becomes victim of managing the methodÔÇÖs complexity rather
than addressing the question directly.
In contrast, our ICoT method mitigates these drawbacks. By emphasizing concise sum┬¡
marization of findings, maintaining simplicity, and fragmenting reasoning into clear, man┬¡
ageable steps rather than a complex structure, ICoT aligns more organically with the intrin┬¡
sic reasoning capabilities of advanced models. This compatibility enables ICoT to scale
effectively, as evidenced by its performance gains from gpt┬¡4o┬¡mini (0.46) to gpt┬¡4.1┬¡mini
(0.54), approaching that of o1┬¡mini (0.58). Looking ahead, we hypothesize that as LLMs
continue to advance, traditional post┬¡inference techniques like CoT and others may in┬¡
creasingly underperform, as their rigid frameworks conflict with modelsÔÇÖ growing ability to
reason natively. Even though ICoT demonstrated good results, it still underperformed o1┬¡
mini and we think that as, the rise of specialized Long Chain┬¡of┬¡Thought models, such as
o1┬¡mini and o4┬¡mini, it suggests a future where internal reasoning mechanisms may dom┬¡
inate, potentially reducing the need for external post┬¡inference techniques altogether.

3
Long Chain of Thought
As we saw earlier, models capable of Long Chain of Thought (Long CoT) heavily out┬¡
perform traditional input┬¡output (IO) models, even when those are enhanced with post┬¡
inference techniques. For instance, o1┬¡mini, released earlier than 4.1┬¡mini, still outper┬¡
formed it significantly in tasks requiring extended reasoning. More specifically, Long CoT
involves deeper reasoning, reflective analysis, and a broader exploration of logical struc┬¡
tures. It enables reasoning across a wider range of logical steps, effectively addressing
both known and unknown elements of a problem. There are three main components that
characterize it: deep reasoning, exploration, and reflection. These components play dis┬¡
tinct yet complementary roles in enhancing cognitive processes. In this chapter, we will
investigate how ICoT can be enhanced to demonstrate such characteristics, making it
eligible as a Long CoT technique.
3.1
Deep Reasoning
Deep reasoning ensures each logical step is executed rigorously, even within complex
structures, fostering robust logic across intricate relationships. To achieve this, a straight┬¡
forward approach is to instruct the model to generate step┬¡by┬¡step reasoning until reach┬¡
ing ground truths or fundamental principles. This way, the problem is rigorously ana┬¡
lyzed down to its core elements. ICoT already heavily leverages fragmentationÔÇöbreaking
down problems into smaller, manageable stepsÔÇöbut the number and detail of these steps
depend on the modelÔÇÖs interpretation.
By setting the parameters max_iterations and
max_depth to very high values, and explicitly instructing the model to create as many
simple steps as possible, we believe we can achieve something similar to deep reason┬¡
ing. This encourages exhaustive decomposition, ensuring no logical gap is overlooked
and promoting thoroughness in complex problem┬¡solving.
3.2
Exploration
Exploration encourages the identification of new pathways, revealing potential avenues
that may not be immediately obvious. Currently, ICoT follows a linear reasoning process
where each step leads directly to the next, eventually converging on a specific pathway.
If no satisfactory result is found, subsequent iterations may introduce new steps informed
by the Analysis Journal, but the context window can become polluted by initial steps, af┬¡
fecting attention and limiting divergence in later ones. To overcome this, we suggest an
approach similar to Tree of Thoughts (ToT), where multiple ICoT processes are generated
and run asynchronously. By setting a high temperature on the foundation model, this re┬¡
sults in varied initial steps and, eventually, diverse pathways, allowing broader exploration
of possibilities without being constrained by a single starting trajectory.
3.3
Reflection
Reflection enables iterative analysis and reassessment of conclusions, allowing reason┬¡
ing to evolve throughout problem┬¡solving. This can be achieved as an extension of the
exploration capability, by having the multiple processes or agentsÔÇöwith their different
pathwaysÔÇöshare a common Analysis Journal. At each step, they can discuss how to up┬¡
date the journal, resolve discrepancies, and solve conflicts. This fosters a self┬¡reflection
mechanism that ensures the overall process solidly advances toward the solution, refining
ideas through collective critique and adaptation.

4
Conclusion
In conclusion, we introduced Immersive Chain┬¡of┬¡Thought (ICoT) as a simple yet effective
post┬¡inference prompting technique that enhances LLM reasoning by recursively decom┬¡
posing complex problems while maintaining focus through an Analysis Journal. Empirical
evaluations on the GPQA benchmark demonstrate that ICoT scales well with advanc┬¡
ing models, outperforming traditional methods like CoT and more sophisticated ones like
AGoT in reasoning┬¡intensive tasks and approaching the capabilities of specialized Long
Chain┬¡of┬¡Thought models. We discussed how, as models evolve, post┬¡inference tech┬¡
niques start to fall short, and how ICoT can keep up with these advancements by adapt┬¡
ing to incorporate deep reasoning, exploration, and reflectionÔÇöthe main characteristics
of Long Chain┬¡of┬¡Thought modelsÔÇöto bridge the gap between prompting and fine┬¡tuning
approaches.
