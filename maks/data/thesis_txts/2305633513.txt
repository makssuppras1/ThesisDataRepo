Abstract
Reduction of unwanted vibration is important in the design of structure. A new method has been
suggested to reduce the vibration of structure by reduction of the wave reﬂection from the edge of which
using acoustic black hole. When the thickness of structure decreases gradually, a ﬂexural wave propagating
to the edge slows down and does not reach the end of structure. Then, the wave energy is concentrated near
the edge without reﬂection and this is called acoustic black hole (ABH) effect. In practice, it is not easy to
make structure which has zero thickness and the truncated edge gives a high value of reﬂection coefﬁcient.
By covering the tapered wedge with small amounts of damping material, this high reﬂection coefﬁcient can
be reduced dramatically. Then, the ABH structure becomes very efﬁcient vibration dampers for ﬂexural
vibrations. However, not many research has been conducted to give a guide to design effective acoustic black
hole plate in practice. Then, the goal of this study is to design an effective ABH plate structure to attenuate
vibration energy in the interested frequency range. To achieve this goal, the parameters which affect the
acoustic black hole effect was studied theoretically and numerically using ﬁnite element method, and also be
attempted to optimize by surrogate model using limited number of numerical results. In this work, the effect
of ABH has been evaluated not only using the magnitude of resonance peaks of direct point mobility but
also using mean-sqaure spatial average velocity in the interested frequency bands. To maximize acoustic
black hole effect, optimization was largely divided into two parts. First, optimization of a tapered wedge was
conducted. It is noticed that two thing are important to design optimized wedge proﬁle. One is a smoothness
of wedge proﬁle at the point where the wedge meets the homogeneous plate. It can be expressed using a
normalized wavenumber variation. The other one is the length of constant minimum thickness. Then, it is
concluded that the optimized wedge proﬁle of ABH plate has the normalized wavenumber from 0.45 to 0.55
as far as it has constant length of minimum thickness larger than 1/10 of length of wedge. It is obtained
that the ABH plate which consist of optimized wedge proﬁle has smaller vibration energy than one which
consist of initial wedge proﬁle. Second, optimization of a damping layer on the optimized tapered wedge
was conducted. It is noticed that to dissipate the vibration energy concentrated at the end of tapered wedge
the mass of damping layer is important. Then, optimized damping layer is when the mass of damping layer
is between 10 g to 20 g. When ABH plate which consist of optimized wedge proﬁle and optimized damping
layer is compared with the ABH plate which consist of initial wedge proﬁle and optimized damping layer
for the initial wedge, it is concluded that optimized ABH plate has large attenuation of vibration energy in
comparison to the initial ABH plate and simple plate. Therefore, ABH plate becomes much more efﬁcient
vibration dampers for ﬂexural vibrations as far as it satisfy these design conditions which are the value of
normalized wave number variation, length of minimum thickness in wedge, and mass of damping layer.
Keywords: Acoustic black hole, reduction of vibration, optimization of ABH plate

CHAPTER 1
Introduction
1.1
Background
Reduction of unwanted vibration of structure is important to design the structure. Largely, there are
two methods to reduce the vibration of structure. One is a passive damping treatments and the other is an
active technique. Usually the passive technique is more stable, needs less energy in comparison to the active
technique, and is easy to implement. This study only focused on the passive technique.
The passive technique consists of moving natural frequencies of system out of the range of operating
frequencies, reducing the vibration at key frequencies, and attenuating vibrations at certain frequency range.
Generally, two methods are used. First one is increasing energy dissipation by adding damping material
which has highly damping loss factor to the surface of structure. However, this method is hard to implement
without impacting the other characteristics of the structure. Second one is reducing the resonance peak of
vibrations by decreasing the wave reﬂection from the edges of structure. In 1996, the attenuation of waves in
plates and bars using a graded impedance interface at edges is proposed by Vemula. This result shows that
the lowered reﬂectivity is caused by energy dissipation within the composite material at the free end (1).
The other new approach to reduce vibration by reduction of wave reﬂections from the edge has been
proposed by Mironov (2). If the thickness of structure decreases gradually by power-law proﬁle, a ﬂexural
wave propagating to the edge of which slows down and does not reach the end of plate. It seems like that all
of the wave energy are absorbed near the edge without any reﬂection. This is called ‘Acoustic black hole
effect’. The main advantage of acoustic black hole effect is that attenuating vibration energy using very
small amount of added damping materials (3). Then, this study focused on the acoustic black hole effect to
reduce the vibration for plate structure.
1.2
Review on the acoustic black hole effect
As mentioned before, the acoustic black hole effect was introduced by Mironov in 1988 (2). The main
idea of this Mironov is based on the gradually decreased thickness of plate or beam, and the thickness proﬁle
introduced in this study is thickness decreased by higher order of power-law proﬁle. As speed of propagation
wave is proportional to square root of thickness of plate or beam, if the thickness is decreased, the speed of
propagation wave is also decreased. Then, if the thickness is decreased by the higher order of power-law
proﬁle which is larger than 2, the wave speed goes zero and the wave never reach the end of edges. It results
in zero reﬂection coefﬁcient. It means that the energy does not transit to the heat or the other form of energy
but is concentrated in the end of plate or beam which has zero thickness. Mironov suggested to call this
effect as ‘Vibration black hole’ in terms of an effective vibration absorber.

However, in practice it is impossible to make structure which has zero thickness at the edge and the
truncated edge gives a high value of reﬂection coefﬁcients. Therefore, this approach is hard to implement
because of the limitation of manufacture. Then, for ﬂexural waves Krylov has suggested to cover the surfaces
of near the sharp edges of beam or plate by small amounts of damping material (4). According to the theory,
this can be very efﬁcient damping system in comparison to the plate or beam which has tapered edge without
damping material. It is shown that plate or beam with damping layer at the tapered edge can reduce the the
reﬂection coefﬁcient dramatically. Therefore, it was seen that the acoustic black hole effect can be an very
efﬁcient vibration dampers for ﬂexural vibrations. In this paper, even if it is related to the bending waves
in a elastic plate or beam, Krylov has mentioned the terms of ‘Acoustic black hole’ instead of ’Vibration
black hole’. After that, all the studies related to the reduction of bending waves in a plate or beam using this
principal start to use the term ‘Acoustic black hole’. Then, this study will use the term ‘Acoustic black hole’
in order to maintain consistency with the previous papers.
As an extension of the study, Krylov also showed the results of the experimental investigation of a
damping system comprising a steel wedge of quadratic shape covered on one side by a strip of absorbing
layer located at the sharp edge (5). He has conﬁrmed the acoustic black hole effect in experimentally and
demonstrated the possibility of its use in practice.
In 2010, O’boy has developed a numerical model for a ﬁnite plate with wedge attachments (6). It is
also conﬁrmed by the experimental measurement. It is hard to say this numerical model is precise because
of difference between experimental and numerical results. However, the similar trend can be seen in each
results. It has been shown that the addition of damping layer is effective in middle frequency range. He
has shown that thicker damping material to a wedge will not result in large decreases in the amplitude of
resonances of plates. Then, he mentioned the possibility of the optimization of the thickness or stiffness of
damping layer to maximise damping performance.
Georgiev has also developed a new alternative approach to the description of damping structural vibrations
in beams with acoustic black hole (7; 8). He also conﬁrmed numerically and experimentally that the
importance of an absorbing layer in the design of acoustic black hole. So, the parametric study of absorbing
layer has been also conducted. The larger the damping loss factor results in the smaller the reﬂection
coefﬁcient.
However, it is also costly to manufacture the elastic wedge with a very thin edge due to need of high
precision machining. Then, Bayod has suggested to attach modiﬁed wedge which made by conventional
tools and the end of thin edge extended with constant thickness for some length with covering by damping
layer. It is shown that the modiﬁed low cost wedge also can achieve important damping level (9). From
this result, it can be seen that the possibility to ﬁnd the other optimal thickness proﬁle of wedge instead of
power-law proﬁle.
The other approach to show the effects of geometrical and material imperfections elastic wedge has been
studied experimentally (10) by Bowyer. He showed that even if there is the mechanical damage resulting
from the use of cutting tools to wedge tips or early truncation, this imperfect wedge still provide a very
efﬁcient damping. He also showed that the effects of welded and glued bonding of wedge attachment to
basic rectangular plates does not give worse results in comparison to a homogeneous sample containing the
same wedge at its edge. He also studied about the case of rectangular plates which has rectangular slots of
power law proﬁle inside of it (11). Different conﬁguration of slots has studied experimentally and compared
with each others.
There are also study about acoustic black holes which is implemented by the plate with axisymmetric pit
which thickness gradually decreases to zero toward its centre by power law proﬁle. The ﬁrst study of it has
done by Krylov (12). This pits can be placed at any point of plate. Like acoustic black hole implemented by

The application of acoustic black hole also has been studied. In (5), Krylov proposed to apply acoustic
black hole effect of tapered wedge to the turbofan blades. Then, experimental study into the damping of

been conducted (15). It has been proved that the turbofan blades which has tapered trailing edges can be
an efﬁcient in reduction of air-ﬂow vibrations of the fan blades with covering damping layer at the end of
tapered edges.
Recently, another approach to use acoustic black hole as a energy harvesting system has suggested by
Zhao (16). Because acoustic black hole features has extreme levels of energy density at the tapered part, the
experimental results conﬁrm that the acoustic black hole produce a drastic increase in the harvested energy
independently from the nature of the excitation point.
As seen in the number of papers related to the acoustic black hole effect, the interests in it have constantly
risen. A lot of study has conducted about acoustic black hole as theoretically and experimentally. However,
not many research has been conducted to give a guide to design effective acoustic black hole plate in practice.
In order to use the acoustic black hole effect in practice, the parameters which affect the acoustic black hole
effect are still needed to study for the ﬁnite ABH plate. Moreover, these parameters can be optimized in
order to has maximum reduction of vibration energy in the interested frequency range. Lastly, in the previous
study the effect of acoustic black holes is only evaluated by reduction of amplitudes of resonance peak or
their average from the driving point mobility. Even though the driving point has large energy in comparison
to the other points, it has a limited response at the selected point. Then, acoustic black hole also has to be
evaluated in terms of energy for whole system.
1.3
Objectives and scope of research
This research is focused on the acoustic black hole effect by tapered wedge at the end of plate as a
method to attenuate vibration energy in the plate structure, not by tapered pits inside of plate.
Then, the aim of this study is design an effective ABH plate structure for the reduction of plate vibration
in the interested frequency ranges. In order to achieve this goal, the possibility of the optimization of ABH
plate has been studied based on the parametric study and numerical analysis. In addition, the effect of ABH
has been evaluated using the other index which representative vibration energy in the interested frequency
band as well as magnitude of resonance peak of the driving point mobility.
Then, this thesis is organized as follows. In Chap.2, the theoretical background for acoustic black hole
for ﬂexural waves by tapered wedge at the end of plate is studied based on the acoustic black hole model of

Parametric study of acoustic black hole effect
In order to design ABH plate based on the theory of acoustic black hole, design parameters which affect
the reduction of reﬂection coefﬁcient have been studied. The design parameters are largely divided into
two case: One is related to the geometric proﬁle of wedge and the other is related to the damping layer on
the tapered wedge. Fig.2.7 shows the design parameters of ABH plate, and the property and dimension of
tapered part is written in Tab.2.1. Then, the study of some important parameters is introduced in this section.

Geometric proﬁle of wedge
Minimum thickness, h0

Numerical simulation
The reﬂection coefﬁcient calculated from Chap.2 is based on the semi-inﬁnite ABH plate which is
assumed that the bending waves propagate only in x direction. However, the ﬁnite ABH plate has bending
waves which propagates not only in x direction, but also in y direction. Then, the response of the ﬁnite ABH
plate is hard to solve in analytically. Therefore, the ﬁnite ABH plate has been solved numerically using ﬁnite
element method (FEM). When the plate is driven by a unit harmonic external force at angular frequency ω
the governing Eq.(2.4) of thin plate with varying thickness along x can be rewritten as a matrix form. Then,
the discretized equation can be obtained as
([K] −ω2[M]) {W} = {f} ,
(3.1)
where K is the complex stiffness matrix, M is the mass matrix of the structure, f is the force vector, and W
is the displacement matrix.
In this study, the ﬁnite element method is performed using a commercial ﬁnite element code ABAQUS
in order to simulate and analysis characteristic of the ﬁnite ABH plate model. Before simulating the ﬁnite
ABH plate, some basic study is conducted to validate the accuracy of ﬁnite element model.
First, ABAQUS contains a large number of ﬁnite elements which can be used to build complex structures.
In this study the three dimensional ﬁnite elements is chosen in order to model the plate with varying thickness.
The three dimensional ﬁnite element has over stiffness in bending application and modal analysis for linear
hexahedral elements (23). This numerical problem is called shear locking. Then, in order to avoid shear
locking and increase the accuracy of results with coarse mesh, the quadratic isoparametric hexahedral
elements is used. In order to avoid another shear locking from fully integrations, at least three layers of
elements through the thickness is chosen (23).
Second, the size of mesh is decided by the wavelength of propagation wave. Commonly, the six elements
per wavelength is used for quadratic elements (24),

3.2
Computational result
When the reﬂection coefﬁcient from the edge is decreased, it results in the attenuation of vibration energy
of plate. The attenuation of vibration energy can be observed by reduction of amplitude of resonance peak
of system in simple way. Therefore, in order to see the effect of decreasing reﬂection coefﬁcient of the
ﬁnite ABH plate, the amplitude of resonance peak of driving point mobilities will be analysed in this part.
However, there is limitation of using amplitude of resonance peak as an index of attenuation of vibration
energy. Then, the other objective to evaluate the vibration energy of system has been discussed and acoustic
black hole effect are also evaluated using this objective.

In order to ﬁnd the design variables which give minimum objective function, the optimization has been
conducted using surrogate model (response surface model). The surrogate model is a method used when
all outcome of the design variables can not be easily measured. To evaluate the design objective of design
variables, the simulation results of design variables has to be obtained from the numerical analysis. However,
it is impossible to simulate all conﬁgurations of design variables. Therefore, the approximation model known
as surrogate model is constructed using the limited number of sample data. Then, it is possible to get the
approximated result at the points which are not simulated. In this study surrogate model was conducted
using Kriging method. This Kriging method is based on the interpolation between sample points, then it is
well known to have good approximation result in non-linear system as well as linear system. In order to
obtain surrogate model using Kriging method, the Matlab Kriging tool Box ‘DACE’ is used in this study
(26). Before constructing surrogate model, the initial sampling has to be selected ﬁrst. It is need to select
effective sampling because the initial sampling also affects the accuracy of the approximation model. In
this study, the initial sampling are selected using Latin Hypercube Design based on the random sampling.
After the surrogate model is constructed from initial sampling, the accuracy of this model is evaluated by
cross-validation. In this study Leave-One-Out Cross-Validation (LOOCV) was used to evaluate the accuracy
of surrogate model. This optimization procedure is displayed in Fig.4.1 and has been explained in detail in
Appendix B.
Before optimizing the ABH plate using this method, the design variables are selected from the parametric
study of ABH plate by theoretically and numerically. Four design variables are selected to obtain the
minimum objective function: power law order (m), length of constant thickness of wedge proﬁle (l0),
thickness of damping layer (h2), and length of damping layer (ld). Minimum thickness of wedge (h0) and
the constant thickness of simple plate (h1), and property of damping layer are ﬁxed in practical reason. It is

The constraint of thickness of damping layer is deﬁned based on the two conditions. One is the
effectiveness of damping layer and the other is to ignore the mass effect of damping layer. First, the
additional damping layer is effective when the thickness of damping layer is larger than one ten of thickness
of base structure, and the combination damping loss factor of plate covered by additional damping layer is
not increased after the thickness of damping layer is larger than 10 times of thickness of base structure (22).
Second, in order to ignore the mass effect of damping layer the mass of damping layer should be smaller
than the mass of tapered wedge per length. Then, the constraint of thickness of damping layer is deﬁned
based on these two conditions. In this study, the mass of damping layer in given length is limited 80% of
wedge proﬁle in given length.
For length of damping layer, the damping layer is assumed to be placed from the end of the wedge
because the combination damping loss factor between tapered wedge and damping layer has the largest
value at the minimum thickness region. Therefore, the constraint of design variables is set as follow:

CHAPTER 5
Conclusions
The aim of this study is to design the acoustic black hole plate which gives a large attenuation of vibration
energy in the interested frequency range. In order to achieve this goal the design parameters which affect the
acoustic black hole effect has been studied theoretically for semi-inﬁnite ABH plate and numerically for
ﬁnite ABH plate using ﬁnite element method. It has been concluded that the thickness proﬁle of tapered
part and damping layer affect the acoustic black hole effect and necessary to be optimized. Then, thickness
proﬁle of tapered wedge and the damping layer on the wedge are optimized.
In order to ﬁnd the optimized tapered wedge proﬁle, the power law order (m) and the length of constant
minimum thickness (l0) have been optimized using surrogate model based on Kriging method. From the
result of optimization of the power law order and the length of constant minimum thickness, it is concluded
that the reﬂection coefﬁcient from theory is valid when the normalized wave variation is smaller than 0.55.
Then, the optimum region is when m and l0 satisfy the normalized wavenumber variation from 0.45 to
0.55, and also l0 has the quantity larger than one ten of length of wedge. It means that the two things are
important to have optimum ABH effect. One is smoothness of wedge proﬁle at the point where the wedge
meet the homogeneous plate. It can be expressed using normalized wavenumber variation. The smaller
value of normalized wavenumber variation means that the smoother of wedge in that point, and a lot of
conﬁgurations of m and l0 has same normalized wavenumber variation. Moreover, when the normalized
wavenumber is in the range from 0.45 to 0.55, the shape of wedge is really similar. This is the reason why it
is hard to decide the exact optimum point in this range. The second one which is important to have optimum
tapered wedge is the length of constant minimum thickness. Even if m and l0 has same gradient at the
point where the wedge meet constant thickness plate, when l0 is larger than 1/10 of length of wedge gives
more attenuation of vibration energy. Therefore, it can be concluded that in order to have smaller vibration
energy in the frequency range from 4400 Hz to 7070 Hz the wedge has to be designed by m and l0 which
has the normalized wavenumber variation from 0.45 to 0.55 and l0 which is larger than 1/10 of length of
wedge. Then, based on the randomly selected wedge proﬁle designed by m and l0 in the optimum region,
the optimization of damping layer on it is conducted.
In order to ﬁnd the optimized damping layer, the length of damping layer (ld) and the thickness of
damping layer (h2) have been optimized also using surrogate model based on Kriging method. From the
result of optimization of the ld and h2, it is concluded that mass of damping layer is critical to get large
reduction of vibration energy as far as both parameters satisfy the constraint condition of mass of damping
layer. There are two optimum region of damping layer. One is when the mass of damping layer is between
10 g to 20 g and the other one is when the damping layer cover the whole tapered wedge with largest damping
layer in the given range which has value of mass larger than 50 g. Because using small mass of damping
layer is better, the damping layer which has mass between 10 g to 20 g is selected as optimized damping layer.

CHAPTER 5. CONCLUSIONS
Then, it can be concluded that in order to dissipate same amount of vibration energy concentrated in the
end of the wedge, the mass of damping layer is important. This is also can be explained using combination
damping loss factor. Because the thicker damping layer has larger combination damping loss factor at the
region where the tapered wedge has constant minimum thickness of wedge, thinner damping layer need
longer damping layer to dissipate same amount of energy. Therefore there a lot of optimum conﬁguration
of thickness and length of damping layer as long as the mass of damping layer is between 10 g to 20 g and
smaller than the mass of wedge.
By comparing ABH plate which has optimized wedge proﬁle with two simple plates and the ABH plate
with initial wedge proﬁle, it is concluded that the optimized wedge proﬁle has smaller vibration energy
in interested frequency range from 4400 Hz to 7070 Hz. Also, when ABH plate with optimized wedge
proﬁle and optimized damping layer is compared with two simple plates with optimized damping layer
for each simple plate models, it can be seen that the optimized wedge proﬁle has smaller vibration energy
in comparison to two simple plate models using much smaller mass of damping layer. Therefore, it is
concluded that by combination of optimized wedge proﬁle and optimized damping layer for that wedge
proﬁle, the ABH plate has large attenuation of vibration energy in comparison to the initial design of ABH
plate ans also the two simple plates. Moreover, it is seen that the ABH effect is larger when tapered wedge is
attached to the simple plate.

CHAPTER 6
Further suggestion
In this study, the wedge proﬁle and damping layer of ABH are optimized to maximize attenuation of
vibration energy in interested frequency range from 4400 Hz to 7070 Hz. However, there are some further
works that should be conducted. First the optimum ABH design from this study is only conducted by
numerical simulation. Then, it is need to be veriﬁed by experiment.
Second is about the objective function. In this study, in order to ﬁnd the optimum design of ABH plate
the mean square spatially averaged velocity which quantity proportional to the vibration energy is used as
objective function. However, this objective function can be used only in high frequency where the system
has has at least ﬁve resonance peak in given frequency band. Then, the other objective function which also
can be representative the vibration energy of system in the other frequency range can be used to optimize the
ABH plate in the other frequency range.
Last suggestion is about the type of force. In this study, the response of ABH plate only has been studied
for the steady-state force. However, the transient force is also important in practice. Also, when considering
force, in this study it is only studied for the case when force is applied to one random point which is not on
the nodal line. But, in order to obtain more general optimized ABH plate, the response of ABH plate can
be also studied for the rain fall force. Then, the mean square velocity averaged over all possible excitation
position can be used as objective function.
