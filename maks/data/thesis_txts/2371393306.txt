Abstract 
 
 
This Master thesis includes three reports on three RF components which are Birdcage Coil for MRI 
System and Low Noise Amplifier (LNA) and Quadrature hybrid coupler. The goal of the thesis 
project work is designing and developing these RF components. The Birdcage coil and the 
Quadrature hybrid coupler are components for MRI scanner with operation frequency 300.34 MHz 
while the low noise amplifier is for another MRI scanner with operation frequency 32 MHz.  
The physical model of birdcage coil is redesigned from 200 MHz to 300.34 MHz and the 
Quadrature hybrid coupler is designed and fabricated on PCB - FR4. The Quadrature hybrid 
coupler is designed to work at the same operation frequency 300.34 MHz of the redesigned 
Birdcage coil. The output ports of the Quadrature hybrid coupler are connected to the redesigned 
Birdcage coil for providing two RF source signals with 90 degree phase shift between the signals. 
The low noise amplifier is designed and developed to achieve a main objective which is 
improvement of SNR that will increase the sensitivity of the existing Magnetic Resonance Imaging 
(MRI) systems. 
The thesis working on all these three RF components are successfully conducted and the goal of 
the thesis project is completely achieved.

 Abstract                                                                      I 
 
MRI is one of the major imaging techniques in the daily clinical routine. MRI is a medical imaging technique 
to visualize the inside body structure. In order to perform scans, MRI systems require the use of radio 
frequency (RF) resonator. In our MRI system, the resonator known as Birdcage coil and it is used to obtain 
images for small animals (Like a mouse). 
The first aim of the thesis project is to redesign the physical model of Birdcage coil for the MRI system. The 
physical model of Birdcage coil operates already at 200 MHz and it will be redesigned to work at 300.34 
MHz. 
In order to achieve redesigning of Birdcage coil at 300.34 MHz, a study is carried out firstly on Design of the 
physical model of Birdcage coil at 200 MHz by using the computer program Birdcage Builder (developed by 
the Penn State Hershey collage of Medicine). Secondly, the results obtained with this simulator are 
introduced to the CST Microwave Studio, creating a 3D view model and generating a simulation for the 
physical model of Birdcage coil. Then the design parameters of the physical model of Birdcage coil at 200 
MHz are readjusted at the computer program Birdcage Builder to obtain redesigning at the desired Larmor 
frequency 300.34 MHz. The 3D view model and matching network and simulation of the redesigned 
Birdcage coil are done in CST to ensure homogenous magnetic field inside the coil at 300.34 MHz. 
The capacitors components on the physical model of Birdcage coil at 200 MHz are replaced with the new 
calculated components from the redesigning process after the CST simulation is completed. The 
measurements are conducted by Using VNA on the redesigned Birdcage coil and it achieved homogeneous 
magnetic field at 300.34 MHz with matching at port 1, |S11|= -14.8 dB and port 2 ,|S22| = -14.6 dB. 
The goal of redesigning of the physical model of Birdcage coil to the desired operation frequency 300.34 
MHz is successfully achieved.

2. Birdcage Coil 
 
2.1 MRI Coil 
 
The use of RF MRI coils look for two objectives, the first is to generate RF pulses at the Larmor frequency 
that is referred to B1 (uniform magnetic field) to excite the nuclei in the sample that to be imaged, in this 
case is an RF transmission coil. The second, is to pick up the RF signals emitted by the nuclei at the same 
frequency, in this case is an RF receive coil. The RF reception coil measures the signal emitted from excited 
tissue and it must be tuned to receive maximum signal at the Larmor frequency. 
In MRI system, volume coils are preferred to use for brain analyses in contrast to surface coils, because, 
volume coils have a bigger field, are able to produce a homogeneous B1 field and have a high penetration 
depth. These are important characteristics for this application. It has to be mention that the physical model 
of Birdcage coil 200 MHz is a volume coil and it is received RF coil.

2.2 Structure and analysis and operating of Birdcage coil 
 
2.2.1 Birdcage coil structure and analysis 
 
The birdcage coils physically consist of multiple parallel conductivity segments equally spaced that are 
parallel to z axis. These parallel conductive segments are called legs or rungs. And two circular end rings to 
denote the end loop. The birdcage coil has a high homogeneity and improves the homogeneity over saddle 
coil and improving the approximation to the theoretical ideal of a sinusoidal distribution of current around 
the circumference. 
Figure 2.1 The Birdcage coil structure 
Depending on the position of the elements there are 3 birdcage coils types: High pass, Low pass and hybrid 
as shown in figure 2.2. The number of legs effects directly the field homogeneity, more legs the birdcage 
coil has, more homogeneous the field is. The more legs, the design is more expensive and complicate to 
produce. For this reason the number of legs represents a compromise between B1 homogeneity and 
complexity of realization. 
Figure 2.2 In the number 1 is represented a Low pass birdcage coil, in number 2 is represented a High pass 
coil and in number 3 is represented a hybrid coil

When Birdcage cage coil is driven at any one point by an oscillating voltage, the birdcage will resonate 
producing a linear oscillating inside as shown in figure 2.3. The benefit of the birdcage design is its ability to 
be driven in quadrature or at two points 90 degrees apart with a phase difference of 90 degrees. By driving 
the coil in this manner, it will generate a circularly polarized B1 field of the form which is shown in figure 2.4 
 
 
Figure 2.3 Top view of a birdcage coil being driven at two different points producing linear oscillating 
magnetic fields in the x- and y-directions. 
 
 
Figure 2.4 Top view of birdcage coil being driven in quadrature by combining two orthogonal linear 
birdcage coils 
 
Driving a birdcage coil in quadrature has numerous advantages over a single drive point. By creating a 
circularly polarized field within the volume of the coil, the magnitude of a circularly polarized field B1 is 
constant with respect to time. A circularly polarized field however, is continuously at a maximum 
magnitude while a linear field will oscillate between its maximum magnitude and zero, only interact some 
of the time with a sample to be imaged.

2.2.2 Birdcage Coil Operating  
 
Most of the human body is made up of water molecules, which consist of hydrogen and oxygen atoms. At 
the centre of each hydrogen atom is an even smaller particle, called a proton. Protons are like tiny magnets 
and are very sensitive to magnetic fields. 
When you lie under the powerful scanner magnets, the protons in your body line up in the same direction, 
in the same way that a magnet can pull the needle of a compass. 
Short bursts of radio waves are then sent to certain areas of the body, knocking the protons out of 
alignment. When the radio waves are turned off, the protons realign. This sends out radio signals, which 
are picked up by receivers. 
These signals provide information about the exact location of the protons in the body. They also help to 
distinguish between the various types of tissue in the body, because the protons in different types of tissue 
realign at different speeds and produce distinct signals.  
In the same way that millions of pixels on a computer screen can create complex pictures, the signals from 
the millions of protons in the body are combined to create a detailed image of the inside of the body.

3.2 Calculation of the Legs-Capacitor in Birdcage coil 
 
Birdcage coils have been utilized to generate homogeneous B1 fields. The desired current pattern in the coil 
legs should generate a desired B1 field distribution inside the birdcage coil. To create a homogeneous B1 
field inside cylindrical birdcage coils, the current pattern on a conducting surface (or discrete legs) should 
have a sinusoidal as shown in figure 3.1. The calculation of the capacitor to achieve the resonance 
frequency 200 MHz is conducted by using Penne State Birdcage Builder computer program. The rest of the 
Birdcage coil specifications are taken from table 3.1.and are inserted in the computer program.

Abstract                                                                       I 
 
This thesis project works on designing and developing of an Ultra-Low Noise Amplifier which should 
potentially increase the sensitivity of the existing Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) systems. 
This will improve the diagnostic accuracy of MRI which is vitally important in medical applications. The LNA 
should be designed to interface low impedance sources. The Design of the LNA should also be fabricated 
and characterized including matching, balancing, and input high power protection circuits. Then estimate 
improvement of SNR of the LNA in comparison to room temperature operation is taken. 
The researching of exciting amplifier circuit topologies and suitable semiconductor technologies is 
conducted in this thesis project. The performing of circuit topologies design are conducted through 
advance design system (ADS) simulation and the fabrication of the chosen topology is constructed on PCB 
FR4- board. The Cascode amplifier topology is chosen to be investigated for our Low Noise amplifier design 
and fabrications. The main characteristic of Cascode amplifier it has advantage of higher input-output 
isolation. This means there is no direct coupling from the output to input and then this eliminates the Miller 
effect and thus contributes to a much higher bandwidth. 
The fabricated PCB layout of the Cascode LNA is tested by using measurement instruments Spectrum 
Analyzer and Vector Network analyzer and the test is passed successfully. At the measurements, the 
Cascode LNA had the following performance, the operation frequency is 32 MHz, the noise figure is 0.45 dB 
at room temperature, the source impedance is 50 Ω, the gain is 11.6 dB, the output return loss is 21.1 dB, 
and the input return loss 0.12 dB and it is unconditionally stable for up to 6 GHz band. 
The goal of designing and developing of an Ultra-Low Noise Amplifier is successfully achieved. The Cascode 
LNA had improvement of SNR that will increase the sensitivity of the existing Magnetic Resonance Imaging 
(MRI) systems.

1. Introduction 
 
An LNA combines a low noise figure, reasonable gain, linearity and stability without oscillation over entire 
useful frequency range. The Low Noise Amplifier (LNA) operates in Class A. Class A is characterized by a bias 
point more or less at the center of maximum current and voltage capability of the device used, and by RF 
current and voltages that are sufficiently small relative to the bias point that the bias point does not shift. 
The LNA function, play an important role in the receiver designs. Its main function is to amplify extremely 
low signals without adding noise, thus preserving the required Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) of the system at 
extremely low power levels. Additionally, for large signal levels, the LNA amplifies the received signal 
without introducing any distortions, which eliminates channel interference. 
Our LNA is designed for MRI scanner and since the MRI signal is very low, it needs to be amplified to a level 
that is sufficient for transmission along long cables and further processing. Noise is inevitably added to the 
signal during this amplification so the signal-to-noise ratio (=SNR) of the MR signal is decreased. The noise 
contribution during the amplification is defined by noise factor F, being the ratio of input SNR to output 
SNR. According to the Friis formula, if the preamplifier (amplifier in the first stage) has gain, the noise from 
the following next amplifiers can be neglected and the preamplifier basically dominates the total noise 
factor of the whole receiving chain. Therefore, low noise factor and high gain are the basic requirements 
for MRI preamplifiers. Typical state-of-the-art MRI preamplifiers have noise figure <1 dB. 
One of the most important factors to achieve low SNR at LNA is choosing the right transistor to be built as 
LNA. PHEMT ATF54143 transistor is chosen for our Cascode LNA design which it contributes very low noise 
figure at its optimal source impedance Zop and it is suitable semiconductor technology for low noise 
cryogenic circuit realization. 
The input and output matching circuit are one of the important requirements for achieving the desired 
specifications of the LNA. In LNA, the input matching circuit means presenting optimal source impedance 
Zopt to the input of the transistor for achieving lowest contribution of noise figure at transistor. The output 
matching circuit has different role in LNA, it can increase gain of LNA by reducing the loss at LNA (Reducing 
the reflected wave at output of LNA). 
Other requirement that it has be taken into account, the loss of components and the loss at RF paths 
(Traces that connect the components on PCB). These losses contribute to increasing of noise figure factor at 
the LNA. Therefore it has to design the LNA circuit on PCB which has specific components and short traces 
at the input of the LNA which offer minimum losses. 
The LNA should be designed to have protection at input high power during the transmitting period of MR 
system. Therefore it used a technique of using 2 shunt Pin diodes at the input of the LNA to limit the input 
high RF pulse power. The LNA should also be designed to interface low impedance sources. 
This report presents work by first introducing the relevant theatrical background on desired specifications 
requirements for LNA and then on Cascode topology and Design in ADS and construction of the LNA 
prototype and RF components and results from the prototype measurements.

3. Cascode Topology 
 
There are two types of amplifier topology used for LNA, it is cascading and cascoding technique. The 
Cascoded topology is much popular since this technique can introduce higher gain, due to the increase in 
the output impedance, as well as better isolation between the input and output ports. To solve the 
problems of obtaining high gain and low noise encountered in MRI RF receiver, it chosen a single stage 
Cascoded LNA amplifier of a PHEMT transistor ATF54143. 
A Cascode amplifier consists of a common-emitter/source stage loaded by the emitter/source of a 
common-base/gate stage. Figure 3.1 shows an example of a Cascode amplifier with a common source 
amplifier as the input stage driven by a signal source, Vin. This input stage drives a common gate amplifier 
as the output stage, with output signal Vout. As the lower FET is conducting, by providing a gate voltage, the 
upper FET conducts due to the potential difference now appearing between its gate and source. The major 
advantage of this circuit arrangement stems from the placement of the upper field-effect transistor (FET) as 
the load of the input (lower) FET's output terminal (drain). Because at operating frequencies the upper 
FET's gate is effectively grounded, the upper FET's source voltage (and therefore the input transistor's 
drain) is held at nearly constant voltage during operation. In other words, the upper FET exhibits a low

5.3.2 Design of stability circuit

phenomena: internal feedback of the transistor, external feedback around the transistor caused by external 
circuit, or excess gain at frequencies outside of the band of operation. In order to achieve unconditionally 
stability state on the Cascode LNA, some of the stability techniques are applied in the LNA Design as shown

5.6 Linearity of the Designed LNA 
 
In an amplifier that exhibits linearity, the output-versus-input signal power graph appears as a straight line. 
Thus, the Cascode LNA will be linear if its gain is constant over varies input signal power.   
There are also characteristics that are related with the linearity of an amplifier which are a third order 
intermodulation distortion and 1 dB compression point (P1dB). The P1dB point is the input power that 
causes the gain to decrease 1 dB from the normal expected linear gain plot. It is the point where the 
amplifier goes into compression and becomes non-linear. Therefore operation of the Cascode LNA should 
occur below this point in the linear region. It is important to know at what point compression begins to 
occur so the input signal power levels can be restricted to prevent distortion in the Cascode LNA. 
For a third order intermodulation distortion, when an amplifier or other circuit becomes non-linear, it will 
begin to produce harmonics of the amplified inputs. The second, third, and higher harmonics are usually 
outside of the amplifier bandwidth, so they are usually easy to filter out if they are a problem. However, 
non-linearity will also produce a mixing effect of two or more signals. 
If the signals are close together in frequency, some of the sum and difference frequencies called 
intermodulation products produced can occur within the bandwidth of the amplifier. These cannot be 
filtered out, so they will ultimately become interfering signals to the main signals to be amplified. The 
linearity conditions of an amplifier are illustrated in figure 5.21 below. 
 
 
 
Figure 5.21 Plot of Linear gain, P1dB and a third order intercept at an amplifier 
==PAGE:73==
                                                                                                                Department of Electrical Engineering  
Low Noise Amplifier (LNA) 
 
 
 
31 
The 1-dB compression point is important since it shows you the input power point where compression 
begins and distortion will occur. Amplifiers should be operated below the compression point. 
Third-order products are the most troublesome of the intermodulation effects caused by non-linear 
operation. The IP3 value is an imaginary point that indicates when the amplitude of the third-order 
products equals the input signals. This point is never reached, as the amplifier will saturate before this 
condition can occur. Nevertheless, it is a good indicator of amplifier linearity. 
The higher the output power at the intercept point, the better the linearity of amplifier and the lower 
distortion. The IP3 value can be read with reference to the input or the output. If you read the value from 
the output axis, it is called OIP3. Reading the value from the input axis, the value is IIP3. The IP3 point is 
typically about 10 dB above the 1-dB compression point.

8. Conclusion 
 
The thesis project of designing and developing of an Ultra-Low Noise Amplifier could achieve the goal of the 
research. The fabricated PCB layout of the Cascode LNA had extremely good desired performance at the 
measurements. The estimate noise figure 0.45 dB at source impedance 50 Ω is very low and this will 
improve the diagnostic accuracy of MRI system which is vitally important in medical applications. The 
Cascode LNA could achieve stability for up to 6 GHz band and it could interface low impedance sources. The 
stability is tested with open, short and 50 Ω impedance sources and it could achieve unconditionally stable 
robust LNA at the operation. The main objectives of the LNA design are obtained in the Cascode LNA 
performance. 
Furthermore, at the measurements, it could achieve moderate gain 11.6 dB and the matched Cascode LNA 
at the output with magnitude of S-parameter |S22| = -21.1 dB. This matching has benefit to prevent the 
power of the output reflected wave propagating through the MRI scanner. 
The measured input-output isolation is high at the Cascode LNA and it could achieve it about 63.8 dB. The 
measured input return loss 0.12 dB at the Cascode LNA is acceptable. The input return loss could be 
improved by using input matching network but at the expense of the noise figure. 
It is also succeed to use protection circuit to the input of the Cascode LNA. The protection technique is used 
2 shunt Pin diodes to ground at the input of the Cascode LNA to limit the high input RF pulse power. 
The measured Drain current 15 mA is less than the simulated Drain current 18 mA in the EM- simulation 
due to the components are slightly adjusted in the fabricated PCB layout. 
The measurements of linearity of the Cascode LNA showed Gain compression P1dB = -18 dBm with respect 
to the input and third order intercept IP3 = 3.1 dBm with reference to output. The Cascode LNA had 
approximately linear gain.  
It can improve the Cascode LNA to achieve higher than the measured Gain compression P1dB by 
redesigning the Cascode LNA with DC Supply Voltage 10 volts. This redesigning will prevent the transistors 
to be beyond the linear region for input power higher than -18 dBm level but the redesigning leads to more 
noise at the Cascode LNA because it will be higher power dissipation in the transistors. 
The choice of making the PCB layout design of the Cascode LNA on microstrip board was pretty good and 
important to achieve the desired performance. The bottom (flat conductor) layer of the board provided 
solid ground plane under the circuit and it allowed connecting the ground nodes over via holes without 
increasing the parasitic inductance of the RF paths in the layout design.   
The using of EM/Circuit Co-Simulation capability in ADS allowed verifying the layout design of the Cascode 
LNA. The careful attention to the layout and the EM-simulation results supported the design process and 
provided a design cost savings by avoiding multiple PCB designs. 
Finally the Cascode amplifier topology with Low Noise PHEMT transistor-ATF54143 could be successfully 
designed as an Ultra- Stable Low Noise Amplifier.

 Abstract                                                                      I 
 
This thesis project includes working on designing and developing of a Quadrature Hybrid Coupler (90 
degree hybrid coupler). The MRI coil needs a 90 degree hybrid coupler to be connected with it at receiving 
or transmitting because the MRI coil works by two RF signals with 90 degree phase shift between these RF 
signals as it showed in report of the birdcage coil for MRI system. A 90 degree hybrid coupler can provide 
two output signals with 90 degree phase shift between its output ports.  
The operation frequency of the redesigned birdcage coil is 300 MHz. Therefore the quadrature hybrid will 
be designed to work on 300 MHz. The researching of exciting technology for suitable design of quadrature 
hybrid is conducted in this thesis project. There are technologies that they can make design of quadrature 
hybrid like Branchline coupler and coupled line coupler. Branchline coupler design is chosen to be made in 
this thesis project because it can be made by a simple technology. 
The desired operation frequency 300 MHz of the quadrature hybrid has wavelength 1 meter and then the 
physical quadrature hybrid will be too big. Therefore it used bends in designing of the quadrature hybrid 
coupler to achieve a compact design. The type of the quadrature hybrid coupler is made in microstrip line 
and it is fabricated on PCB-FR4 board. 
The achieved results at measurements of the fabricated quadrature hybrid coupler at frequency 300 MHz 
are the phase shift between two output ports is 90° ±0.3° and the magnitude of the signal at output port 
with 0 degree phase is -3.2 dB while the magnitude of the signal at output port with 90 degree phase shift 
is -3.5 dB. The Isolation between input port and isolated port is 22.7 dB. 
The goal of the quadrature hybrid coupler design is achieved as it can be seen from the results of the 
measurements, it achieved desired phase shift between the output ports and the power at input port is 
spilt to about -3 dB at the output ports. The quadrature hybrid coupler had good isolation. The compact 
design of the quadrature hybrid coupler could successfully reduce the physical size of the fabricated 
quadrature hybrid coupler board to be mounted in the desired house by using bends in the design.

1. Introduction 
 
The first step taken in this project was to understand the physical performance of the 90- degree hybrid 
coupler. Throughout this process journals and several other reputable articles and books have been studied 
to gain a better understanding of this device. Additionally, different topologies and currently implemented 
solutions have been investigated to understand and choose the best solution for our design of 90-degree 
hybrid coupler. The goal of this project is to design and fabricate of 90 degree hybrid coupler at the 
redesigned Birdcage coil operating frequency 300 MHz and also developing the design by decreasing its 
physical size. This report presents work by first introducing the relevant theatrical background on 90 degree 
hybrid coupler and the desired specifications for the design. The design process of the 90 degree hybrid 
coupler will be conducted in Advanced Design system (ADS) software and then the design will be 
transferred to a layout design in ADS momentum. The Layout design will be verified by EM-simulation in 
ADS and then it will be fabricated on FR4 Microstrip board. Finally the fabricated 90 degree hybrid coupler 
is measured by using 4-ports Vector network Analyzer.  
2. Quadrature Hybrid Coupler Analysis and the desired 
Specifications 
 
Hybrid couplers are four-port devices that split the incident power signal into two output ports. The signals 
at the outputs are attenuated by three decibels (3dB) and have a 90 degree phase difference with respect 
to each other. In addition, reflections due to mismatches are sent to the isolation port preventing any 
power from reflecting back to the input port. In addition to splitting a signal they can also be used to 
combine power signals with a high degree of isolation between the ports. The designed 90 degree hybrid

7. Conclusion 
 
The performance requirements of the Quadrature hybrid coupler are successfully achieved. The results of 
the measurements at the desired operation frequency 300 MHz showed the phase shift is 90° between the 
output ports with phase unbalance equal to ± 0.3°. It also showed the power at the input port is spilt to 
about -3 dB at the output ports with magnitude unbalance equal to 0.31 dB. Furthermore the Quadrature 
hybrid coupler had good isolation with 22.7 dB. Finally, it could reduce the physical size of the fabricated 
Quadrature hybrid coupler board and it could be mounted in the desired house. 
The magnitude unbalance and phase unbalance are proportional to the loss of PCB. It could improve the 
design of the Quadrature hybrid coupler by changing the substrate from FR4 to less lousy substrate like 
Roger Ro3000 series with keeping thickness of PCB to 1.5 mm. 
The goal of designing and developing Quadrature hybrid coupler at the desired operation frequency 300 
MHz is completely achieved with excellent performance and the fabricated Quadrature hybrid coupler is 
tested with the redesigned Birdcage coil at 300 MHz and it worked very well.
