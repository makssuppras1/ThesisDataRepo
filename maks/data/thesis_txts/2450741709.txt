Abstract 
The thesis aims at purified sediment from industrial port and eutrophicate lake with microbial fuel 
cell (MFC). The evaluation of MFC performance is mainly based on turn-over and the pollutant 
concentration in the sediment, DO, pH and other parameter is used to monitoring the process. The 
project consists of two parts, pollutant degradation experiment using Esbjerg sediment and 
temperature experiment using both Esbjerg sediment and Lake Søllerød sediment. 
 
MFC is built with AISI 316 stainless steel and 1L bottles (Duran GLS80). Without adding nutrient or 
bacteria, the MFC generate current around 60 μA with Esbjerg sediment. A single MFC extracts 5 C 
electrons per day from the sediment. The nitrite, phosphate, ammonia concentration is also measured 
during the pollutant degradation experiment. High intra-group deviation is found in all parameters 
and caused difficulties in analysis. There is no evidence shows MFC or MFC with potentiostat, 
which sets anode potential at +197 mV vs SHE (pMFC), promote the release of phosphate. 
 
Temperature experiment starts around 45 days after pollutant degradation experiment. Based on the 
experience from pollutant degradation experiment, design of electrode is improved. The intra-group 
deviation is lower comparing with pollutant degradation experiment. It is MFC generate more power 
on 23 ⁰C than 12 ⁰C. At low temperature, the power generation do not increase for the first month, 
and then follows by a slow increase. Arrhenius Temperature correction cannot explain the result very 
well. Phosphate, nitrite, and ammonia concentration in two kinds of sediment and 3 temperature 
returns similar result comparing with pollutant degradation experiment: there is no evidence showing 
MFC promote the releasing of phosphate and nitrite. 
 
Potential of electrode is measured since March. By studying the distribution of electrode potential 
and combining it with other result, it shows these potentials might help finding the limiting factor.

1.Introduction 
1.1 introduction of MFC 
Microbial fuel cell (MFC) is considered to be a clean and renewable technology of treating 
wastewater and providing energy. It utilizes organic waste to generate renewable energy and treated 
pollutants at the same time via electrochemical reactions involving biochemical pathways (Franks, 
A.E. and Nevin, K.P., 2010). Its performance and efficiency are usually measured in terms of 
electronic parameters (volumetric power density, current, potential difference, cell internal 
resistance) and biological treatment efficiency (COD removal) (Logan 2012). The power density is 
usually normalizing to anode surface area. By using pure culture of Shewanella oneidensis as 
biocatalysts and lactate as fuel, its power density reaches 3 mW*m-2. Using modified two-chamber 
MFC and cassava mill wastewater as fuel, a power density of 1771 mW*m-2 is achieved 
(Kaewkannetra et al. 2011).  
 
Comparing with conventional fuel cells, it has lower cost in theory and be able to treat utilize 
feedstock that fuel cells would not be able to i.e. waste streams. Stainless steel and carbon are 
common electrode material. Besides, it works under normal temperature and atmospheric pressure 
(Du et al. 2007). However, it is not commercially used on power generation yet because of its low 
efficiency. Conventional fuel cells which produces 103–105 fold higher current than MFC (Kim 
2009). 
 
Besides power generation, there are some research using MFC as remediation tools for sediment. 
Sediment MFC (sMFC) is a kind of MFC. It lacks a membrane and its anode is buried in sediment. A 
range of biodegradable substrates like glucose, glutamic acid, river water, cysteine, acetate, and 
starch can be the fuel (Abbas et al 2017). By using electrode and wires, anaerobic sediment is 
connected with aerobic water column. Electrochemically active bacteria widely exist in the 
environment. The MFCs have the distinct advantage that, they promote the degradation of intractable 
petroleum hydrocarbons, like diesel (compounds ranging from C8 to C25), which takes more time to 
be naturally remediated under anaerobic conditions with limited nutrients at low temperatures (0 to 
20 °C), especially in marine sediments. (Mohanakrishna et al., 2018) 
==PAGE:6==
 
Temperature is likely to be an important environment factor on MFC performance. e.g.: Kadlec 2019 
found in wetland, the effect of temperature is much greater at the lower end of the temperature scale 
(< 15 °C) than at the optimal range (20 to 35 °C)( Kadlec, R.H. and Reddy, K.R., 2001.). 
Temperatures inside laboratories are usually higher than 20 °C (Alipanahi, R. and Rahimnejad, M., 
2018). Thus, effects of temperature needed to be studied before on-site experiment. Temperature also 
affects the growth of bacteria. 
 
1.2 Sediment Used in this experiment. 
Sediment used in the study comes from Lake Søllerød and the port of Esbjerg. Lake Søllerød is 
situated in Rudersdal Municipality, North of Copenhagen in Denmark. The area of the lake is 13.4 
ha, the average depth is 5.8m and the maximum depth 8.5m (Naturstyrelsen, 2012) During the 
1950s, the lake was highly polluted and suffered many algal blooms (Jensen, 2014). Since 
wastewater ceased to be led into the lake in 1975 (Naturstyrelsen, 2012), conditions have improved, 
but it still suffers from eutrophication. The sediment here is black and smelly. Dead leaves and waste 
can be easily found. 
The port of Esbjerg has included a variety of industries. Industries like the oil and gas production in 
the North Sea emerged up through the 1970s and Esbjerg became, and still is, a center for the 
offshore industries. It also handles approximately 4.5 million tons of cargo each year. It is also the 
leading port in terms of car imports. The sediment here is polluted and dark. 
In eutrophicated lake and other polluted surface water, pollutants accumulate in sediment. For 
example, Sediment as an internal phosphate sources cause difficulty on long-term remediation 
(Søndergaard, M. et al 2007). Comparing with sediment dredging, insert in-site MFC might

3.Methods and material 
3.1 experimental set-up 
3.11The pollutant degradation experiment 
The reactors are constructed from 1L bottles (Duran GLS80). The bottles are 6 cm in diameter and is 
filled with 400 mL sediment and 600mL seawater from Esbjerg harbor. The sediment is stored in 4 
⁰C and dark environment within one month before the experiment. Before injection, the sediment is 
stirred for around 5 minus. The anode is inserted in the sediment 2 cm below the sediment-water 
interface. Reactors were operated at approximately 25⁰C. A subset of reactors were operated in a 
different lab at 26⁰C. 
 
They are 5 groups of reactors, consisting of MFC with potentiostat (pMFC), MFC, 2 disconnected 
groups and control.  In total there are 15 reactors. Figure 1 shows MFC design and the MFC used in 
this experiment. The pMFC has a reference electrode inside the sediment but without resistor 
outside. Instead, the wires attached to the 3 electrodes are all connected to a potentiostat (model and 
manufacturer). The potentiostat was configured to control the potential of anode at the same potential 
of reference electrode (+0.197 mv verse SHE).  It is almost the potential where Fe2+ is oxidized to 
Fe3+ under standard situation(Nicolaisen, 2018). Disconnected group should not have a resistor to 
connect cathode and anode, however, in the first 2 week, the cathode and anode are connected 
together by mistake. Because of this mistake, a new disconnect group is built in the middle of 
January.  Control group only has sediment and water inside. Aluminum paper is used to prevent 
light from shining the sediment.

During the first 3 month, 220 ±10 Ω resistor is used. Treatment with potentiostat started on day 23

week. Measuring of all parameter happens at least 24 hours after refilled deionized water. 
 
3.2 Temperature experiment  
The temperature is set at 4, 13, 25 ⁰C. The 4 ⁰C reactors are inside a fridge. A thermometer is used to 
monitor the temperature inside the fridge. It is found that the temperature inside the fridge is not 
stable. The temperature in reactors reaches-2 ⁰C to 8 C occasionally. The 13 and 25 ⁰C room is also 
==PAGE:9==
used. Due to evaporation, the true temperature in the reactors range is 11.5 ⁰C to 12⁰C and 22.2 ⁰C to 
22.8 ⁰C separately. 
Before the MFC were connected, all reactors were placed in their temperature for 2 days. Then OCP 
was measured to find possible crack to the anode, small shack or other movement might lead to a gap 
near the wires or tube. This gap leads the surface water and oxygen to the anode and interfere the 
performance of MFC. Other steps of building reactors are the same as the pollutant degradation 
experiment. 
 
Because nutrient release from sediment is crucial to lake remediation, control group is set up for 
rectors containing Søllerød lake sediment. There are 9 groups of reactors in this experiment: Esbjerg 
MFC at 4, 12 ,23 ⁰C (E4, E12, E23), Søllerød MFC at 4, 12 ,23 ⁰C (S4, S12, S23), control at 4, 12 ,23 ⁰C 
(C4, C12, C23). 
 
  3.3 Electrode preparation  
The electrodes are made of stainless steel AISI 316. This material is highly resistant to corrosion, 
thus widely used in marine environments. Stainless steel wire mesh with square holes of 10x10 mm 
was used for making electrodes. Wire gauge is 1mm. Stainless steel wire mesh was cut and bent as 
seen in Figure 2, each electrode contains 4 layers. It is 4 centimeter-high and has a footprint of 21 
cm2. This design aims at affecting more sediment compared with 1-layer electrode. 
 
In the pollutant degradation experiment, the electrode was connected with electric wires and 
conductive epoxy. They were then covered with silicone-tube. Silicone is used for avoiding epoxy 
releasing Nano-silver particles. This particle is high toxic to bacteria (Choi, O. and Hu, Z., 2008.). After 
making the cathode and anode, the resistance between the electric wire attached on the electrode and 
the bottom of the electrode was measured. Maximum resistance of a single electrode is 1.2 Ωwhile 
the resistance of anode plus cathode on average was approximately1 Ω. 
(from 0.8 to 1.8 Ω).

3.4 sampling and measurement 
Working potential(WP), Current (C), Open circuit potential (OCP)were measured with digital 
multimeter Victor VC 890C+ and VC130-1. WP is the potential difference across external resistor 
when the reactor is working. C is the current through the external resistor, which reflects the turn-
over. After measuring C and WP, the system is disconnected for more than 15 minutes, before OCP 
is measured. 
 
Pc (potential of cathode) and Pa (potential of anode). By measuring the potential difference of a 
silver-chloride reference electrode (+0.197 mv vs SHE) and anode or cathode, the potential of 
cathode and anode can be calculated. It is measured when the reactors are working and 30 minus 
after disconnection. 
 
==PAGE:11==
Electrical conductivity (EC) meter Cond 340i uses and automatically line correction with 
temperature. It is used to measure the electronic conductivity around cathode. At the first month, EC 
is also measured near the sediment, however, no EC gradient is found in the reactor, thus no more 
measurement near the sediment 
 
Both pH and ORP of water near cathode (Rw) are measured with pH 3110 meter. This meter returns 
the potential difference between sampling place and reference electrode. Initially, it is also measured 
near anode, however, considering the following reasons: 
  1. the probe is 1 cm diameter. Measurement digs a hole at the sediment and brings the oxygen into 
deep layer. 
 2. The result is not precise. The difference inside one reactor can varies 100 mv. 
 
Dissolved Oxygen (DO) is measured near the cathode and reflects the ORP condition of cathode. 
The sample for phosphate and nitrogen are collected at 2 cm above the sediment. Then it is filtered 
with 0.2 µm filter. After that, it is sent to spectrophotometric analysis (SKALAR (no date)). Iron is 
measured with LCK 321 tool box once.  
 
At the last day of experiment, “switching cathode test” is done. The test uses a new cathode to switch 
the old ones and then WP, Pa, Pc, & C are recorded. The electric resistance of old electrodes is also 
measured with and without the rough layer on the surface can be seen in Figure 1and Figure 11. With 
rough layer means measured the resistance between the web rough layer and the wires on the other 
side. After that, rough layer is removed, and the stainless steel is exposed. The resistance of the fresh 
area and the wire is also measured. The When with the rough layer, the electrode is wet, the EC of 
the liquid is around 40 mS/cm.  
 
After “switching cathode test”, the anode was taken out while the sediment attached on the anode 
was scratched back into bottle. Then the sediment was poured into a plastic bag and mixture for 5 
mins. After mixing, around 100 ml sediment was stored at -20 ⁰C. Around one week later, they were 
taken out and freezing dried and sent for analysing total petroleum hydrocarbon(TPH).

5. Discussion  
5.1 interpretation of results  
5.1.1 Biocathode or pure chemical reaction 
While it is clear that bacteria domains the anode reaction, the reduction of oxygen is purely chemical 
reaction or depends on biofilm growth on the cathode is still unknow. The electrode switching 
==PAGE:27==
experiment happens on the last day of pollutant degradation experiment. A new made cathode is 
used, in some reactors, it shows a higher ability of power producing while in other reactors, the 
current remains similar or decrease. If the cathode reaction relies on biofilm, the new cathode should 
support little current. So that hypothesis is not absolutely right. By comparing the result with the 
resistance (Table 1) measurement that day, the new cathode does not generally support more current 
even with much lower resistance. The reaction is also not a purely chemical reaction. This is not 
likely to be cause by pollution and other reaction with metal. The switching cathode experiment only 
uses 1 cathode, and it sometimes generate more than 100 μA and sometimes below 20 μA. If that is 
caused by pollutants, it should have a more stable behavior or behavior associate with time. 
 
A possible explain is, although the reduction of oxygen can happen without biofilm growth on the 
cathode, biofilm can promote the reduction. Meanwhile, bacteria growth and fouling result in a high 
over potential and inhibit reduction of oxygen.

5.1.3 The potentiostat and pMFC 
The aim of setting pMFC in this experiment is setting the potential of anode to a certain value and 
then influence the sediment around anode. Because potentiostat influence metal part of anode, we 
assuming the potential of anode is highly relevant to the sediment around it. However, it is found that 
in the first month, Pac reaches even +400 mV vs SHE and gradually falls to -100 mV vs SHE.Pad 
might reflect the r redox potential of sediment near anode, but Pac do not. As discussed before, it is 
the result of electronic process. Setting the +197 mV might not oxidized Fe(II) around the anode. 
 
Instead of focus on Pac, focus on how much charge is removed from sediment might be a more 
reasonable choice (Touch, N.).

  5.2 comparison of other results  
A DO biosensor based on sediment MFC is developed in a eutrophicate lake recently. (Song, N. et 
al) With 5% of organic compound addition, they gain a good linear relationship between voltage and 
DO. This lake is high eutrophicate and DO concentration is found decrease to zero under 30 cm 
depth. Their in-site experiment lasts for 2 months and still get a R-square between voltage and DO of 
0.87. In this case, their turn-over is limited by the cathode.  
 
In this project, DO and Rw shows different trend, In the last month of pollutant degradation 
experiment, although the DO concentration keeps increasing, the WP(Figure 4) and OCP(Figure 5) 
remains. From the potential of electrode (Figure 6)and the discussion, although the cathode reactions 
limited the turn-over at that time, DO does not shows a clear linear relationship with WP or OCP. 
That might because of fouling. 
 
There is another way to use electrokinetic treatments (EKT) for remediation sediment. They use 
potentiostat to withdraw a 100 C, 200 C and 300 C charge from different reactors. With shows a 
significant difference on sediment ORP. It also reduces H2S releasing from sediment(Touch, N. et 
al). This method not only makes a better result but also make more sense in theory. Another 
interesting factor in their experiment is that although the EKT significantly affects the ORP in 
sediment. ORP trends to return to previous level. It is likely that the electrode has a very limited 
effect range. Once it stops, the diffusion will transport eletrons nearby to the oxidized zone. 
 
Fixing the potential of electrode is usually used in pre-treatment of biocathode and bioanode. By 
giving a constant potential, growth of bacteria is promoted. (B. Erable 2013).

  5.3 limitation and constraints 
In temperature experiment, the cathode does not resist corruption enough, especially the connecting 
part. Broken of cathode happens in the stainless steel should be well buried in the silicon also shows 
using silicon to resist the corruption cannot fit the demand. 
 
Measuring ORP in sediment is important. ORP is an important parameter to illustrate how well the 
anode works, due to the limitation mentioned in material and method, it is stopped. There are also 
needle like probe for ORP.  With it, and small hole on the side of the bottle, it is possible to measure 
it. 
  
Comparing with climate room, the temperature inside a fridge is not stable enough, temperature 
gradient from back to front is also not ideal.  
 
Too much group variation is found in this experiment, especially the pollutant degradation 
experiment. Comparing with temperature experiment, the pollutant one immediately started after 
injecting sediment. In the temperature settings, it stabilized for two days and then than works. 
The duration of this experiment only last for months, however, the fouling effect is already found on 
cathode. 
 
Tubificids might also affect the performance of MFC when lake sediment is used (Figure 23). They 
have significant effect on the ORP and pH near the sediment-water interface (Davis, R.B., 1974.). In 
his study, 1800 worms per squared meter was introduced and maintained. That density equals to 3 
worms in one of my reactors. It is found in 9 reactors in the temperature experiment. At least half of 
the lake sediment contains worms. Figure 23 is taken in Reactor 11, which is an MFC using lake 
sediment at 4 ⁰C. The nest of worms in this reactor reaches the bottom of sediment while in other 
reactors, it is mainly within 5 cm below the sediment-water interface. This reactor is the middle one 
in MFC Figure 16. The high potential of anode when it is disconnected is likely caused by worms’ 
nest provide a high way of oxygen gets into the sediment.

  5.4 outlook and improvement 
Intra-group deviation needs to be reduced if we want to achieve better result. It is found that new 
design of electrode and check OCP before connection helps. Tubificids and depletion of oxygen 
threaten the performance. Heating the sediment to 60 ⁰C ( Davis, R.B., 1974.) might be helpful with 
killing worms while maintain other characteristic. Using small air pump to ensure sufficient oxygen 
is also important when using lake sediment. 
 
As temperature has a strong non-linear effect on MFC performance, setting temperature based on the 
real situation is important for up-scaling. 
 
The pMFC settings should also be improved. One solution is given a fixed number of electrons, or 
extract electrons from sediment at a constant rate.

6. Conclusion 
The MFC is able to operate with both Esbjerg and Søllerød for at least two months without adding 
nutrient or preparing biofilm. In Esbjerg sediment, there is no problem with depleting of oxygen, and 
the current maintains around 60 µA. By using Faraday constant and assuming acetate (C2H4O2) or 
glucose (C6H12O6), a single electrode oxidizes 0.4 mg organic matter (acetate) per day.  
 
Due to variation inside group, it is hard to find if the MFC degrade TPH. There is also no evidence 
showing if MFC promote the release of phosphate.  
 
The cathode efficiency is threatened by two factors in this experiment. In pollutants degradation 
experiment, it is found that the corrosion is underestimate.  
 
The cathode reaction can happen without biofilm. Even biofilm has a large resistance, it still has 
similar ability of consuming oxygen as the new cathode did. 
 
Potential of electrode is a tool of finding the inner resistance ratio around cathode and anode. 
According to this and result from switching electrode test, it is suggested that EAB need a few week 
to grow.
