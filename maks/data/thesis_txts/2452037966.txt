ABSTRACT
The shapes of arches and domes have been used for centuries, but in recent decades, the shell structure
has lost its popularity due to high costs related to labor work and formwork. The construction of concrete
shells is very labor intensive and cumbersome to make, making them unattractive to developers. This
master thesis is a design project which investigates two different types of Hyperbolic Paraboloid shell
shapes that can be repeated creating a larger spatial structure. The two shapes considered are the Saddle
shell and Single module shell. In the design process, it has been desirable to use prefabricated concrete
elements to construct the two shell structures, avoiding difямБculties related to construction. To efямБcient
the use of materials, the technology of Super-light concrete structure is used. The idea of Super-light
Structure is to create a skeleton from strong material and ямБll-in with light material.
The shape of doubly curved shell structures gain signiямБcant strength due to their shape and not neces-
sarily material properties. They can convert vertical loads to normal forces instead of bending moments.
This trait of shape gives them signiямБcant strength and reduces the amount of material. The prefabricated
elements are designed as rib elements with shear plate distributed to secondary moments and normal
forces. The ribs are the skeleton of the structure while the shear plate is the ямБll-in element. For a more
sufямБcient utilization of the compression property of concrete, it is desirable to pre-stress the materials
where tensile stresses occur due to moments. Furthermore, The pre-stressing is placed in the ribs and
functions as a connection.
The design process has been an iterative process targeting a design with a minimum amount of connec-
tions, the number of elements and unique elements. The iterative process needs to consider the element
size and the shape of the curvature of the two shells. For the Single module shell, a curved prefabricated
element is chosen which reduces the connections by half compared to a triangular shape. The element
of the Saddle shell is designed as a trapezoidal rib element. The ямБnal dimension of the Saddle shell
is a 14x12x6 meter divided into four segments, which only requires two unique elements to construct.
The ямБnal dimensions for the Single module shell is a 10x10x5 meter with a 4x4 generation line division
with a total of 16 elements and require only three unique elements. The ямБnal design of the Saddle shell
element is a rib frame with an arch-shaped shear plate. The material for the linear shear plate for the
Single module shell is normal concrete and rib framing.
The ямБnal designs and construction processes can reduce both manufacturing costs due to low unique
elements and repetition of elements in the structure. Also, the pre-stressing contributes to an easy con-
nection solution. By assembling segments of the shells and lifting them to place, the amount of temporary
supports is reduced and work in the height related to connections is avoided. The shells can be repeated
creating a bigger spatial structure, where the assembling of the construction is systematically repeated
reducing the construction costs.

INTRODUCTION
1.1
Background
Shapes of arches and domes have been used since prehistoric times as far back by the ancient Egyptians,
Babylonians and Greeks. In earlier times, the shapes were mostly used for supporting small structures. It
was not until the Romans modernized the shape by using concrete, which was a much stronger material
at the time, it was introduced for bigger constructions. This inspired other architects as it was possible to
support a signiямБcantly higher amount of weight. Historic buildings like the Pantheon in Rome, Chartres
in France and the Majesty of Taj Mahal in India are built using arches and domes. [1]

Although concrete and shell structures in the form of arches, cupolas, and vaults were invented centuries
ago, the epoch of concrete shell started at the beginning of the 20th centuries when the development
of reinforced concrete accelerated. The ямБrst revolutionary thin concrete shell was Carl ZeissтАЩ spherical

The тАЩ50s and тАЩ60s was the period when atomic experimentation and space travel were making headlines

material costs, these labor costs are considered prohibitive. Surfaces such as cylinders and hyperbolic
paraboloids can be formed with nearly ямВat panels supported by straight members. But shapes like domes
are difямБcult to extrude using traditional construction methods. Shell shapes also require highly skilled
labor and tight tolerances on the formwork. Thin shell structures are very sensitive to variations in the ge-
ometry regarding stiffness and internal forces. this makes shell structures, especially thin shell structures
less competitive. [10] Another reason for its loss of popularity was the changes in fashion. Architectural
fashion saw the return of Cartesian geometry and straight lines.
In recent years shell structures are attracting interest amongst the new generation of architects and en-

1.2
Scope
This thesis aims to investigate and design hyperbolic paraboloid shell structures. The project will focus
on design and construction in the preliminary design. It is desirable to reduce the labor expenses related
to formwork which is one of the main reasons why shell structures lost its popularity. It is therefore
desirable to build the shell structure with prefabricated elements that can easily be constructed on the

create a bigger spatial structure. The structure could for instance function as a roof or large hallways

bolic paraboloid concept; the Saddle shape and the Single module shape. The project aims to hopefully
increase the interest of developers, engineers and architects to create shell structures.
1.3
Arches and shell structures
The shape of the hyperbolic paraboloid, abbreviated HP, can be related to the behavior of arches. Arches
employ the principle when uniformly weight is applied the forces resolve into axial compressive stresses
and thrust at the base bearing points instead of bending moments. Horizontal reactions appear even if
the structure is only distributed to vertical loads. This phenomenon is known as the arch action. The
height of the arch is related to the outward thrust; as the height decreases the thrust force increases and
maintaining the arch action, preventing the arch from collapsing. If the thrust force is too high, internal
ties or external bracing must be employed. Fixed arches are statically intermediate structures to the third
degree. These types of arches are the most rigid but are sensitive to relative settlement at the supports
and additional forces that creates changes in temperature. Two hinged arches are ямБrst degree statically
intermediate and also sensitive to relative settlement at the supports. The three hinged arches are stat-
ically determinate and not affected by settlement or additional temperature changes. A strong tension
between members connected between the springing level reduces the amount of any external bracing
requirements. [11]

Chapter 1. INTRODUCTION
A shell is a structural element that is characterized by its geometry being a three-dimensional solid whose
thickness is very small compared with other dimensions of the element. The relation between a plate and
a shell is similar to a beam and an arch. [12] The curved shapes can use its curvature to resist a load
perpendicular to its axis or surface by compression or tensions parallel to the surface. A shell might be
curved in two directions, like a dome, or cylindrical with a one direction curve. [12] A shell has an inямБ-
nite number of plane curves at any point p on the surface, meaning that every point has a local curvature
and a corresponding radius of the curvature at that point. Out of the inямБnitely many plane curves, a curve
having a maximum value (K1) and another having a minimum value (K2) will exist. Gaussian curvature
can be used to make a classiямБcation of the surfaces depending if K = K1 тИЧK2 is negative, positive
or zero. The surfaces are deямБned as synclastic, anticlastic or surface with zero Gaussian curvature, re-
spectively. The surfaces can also be deямБned as developable or nondevelopable. Developable surfaces are
surfaces that can be developed to a plane without stretching or cutting the surface. Surfaces with double
curvature are usually nondevelopable meaning that the shells need more energy to be stretched out or to
collapse into a plane form. [5]
1.3.1
Hyperbolic paraboloid
A hyperbolic paraboloid is a doubly-curved surface and has a convex form along one axis and a concave
form along the other, meaning that every point on its surface lies on two straight lines across the surface.
This makes them easier to construct using a series of straight structural members and are used to construct
thin shell roofs compared to other doubly-curved shells. The shell forms achieve strength through their
shape rather than gaining strength from the mass and reduce their tendency to buckle in compression.
[13] Felix Candela (1910-1997) was a Spanish-born architect, designer of reinforced-concrete structures
and was a great inямВuence of the development of the hyperbolic paraboloid thin shell element which was a
standout feature of many of his projects. [14] Hyperbolic paraboloid shell structures come in a variety of
conямБgurations such as the single module shell, the hip-roofed form and the inverted umbrella exempliямБed
in Figure 1.5.
Figure 1.5: Typical hyperbolic paraboloid shapes [5]
The shell design is structurally efямБcient and has many constructional and aesthetic advantages. They
are used to cover large spans, vast roofed areas and a variety of other roofed spaces and can be simply
prefabricated. [5]

1.4.1
Pearl-chain reinforcement
One challenge with SLS is creating curved shaped compression zones and a variable cross-section cast
into light aggregate concrete. A concrete arch might break before it is embedded in aggregate concrete
caused by tensile forces from the self-weight or unexpected load is applied to the ямБnal structure. Another
problem is that prestressing is not a good idea when considering a mixture of normal and light concrete
in one element causing creep and unloaded prestressing forces. This increases deformation and overall
large deямВection problems may occur. These problems can be avoided by using pearl-chain reinforcement.
Figure 1.7: Pearl-chain element [7]
Pearl-chain reinforcement is a method of producing compression zones in an element in a sufямБcient way.
The shape can be curved like a spine of a skeleton designed as a chain of prefabricated segments of a
strong material assembled by prestressing wires. This makes the building processes more efямБcient and
reduces the prices by utilizing the properties of the material. By using the skeleton concept it becomes
affordable to provide structures with shapes that are usually avoided due to difямБcult labor. By prestress-
ing the pearl-chain with a sufямБcient compression force, the chain can resist tensile forces entirely as
unloaded compression. [7] This allows more resource-economical designs because it removes practical
hindrances of shapes.
Pearl-chain is several prefabricated segments of a material of sufямБcient strength that is cast or extruded.
Each segment has one or more holes which prestressing elements such as wires or lines can be passed
through. When the wires are anchored at the ends, the segment is prestressed where the pearl chain is
unaffected by the path of the chain. The prestressing wire follows the same path, meaning that many
different shapes can be made. Even though prestressing is a known technology it has not previously
been applied for the construction of strong or tension zones of prestressed segments to be cast into light
structures. Mortar can be used to ямБll out holes in the adjacent planes to create shear locks in the curved
shapes. Since the segments are independently prestressed, the segments distributed to compression zones
can be avoided of prestressing and rather apply compression from the self-weight of the light concrete.

Structural system
The structural system refers to the load-resisting sub-system of an object, where the two shells transfer
loads through the interconnected elements. The elements distribute the applied loads to the highest stiff-
ness (the ribs). The shape of both structures can reduce the arise of bending moments by creating normal
forces. The strength of the structures is related to the ratio between height and width of the structures,
where the height of the structures increases the strength.
The Saddle shell system transfers the surface loads from the shear plate as a line load to the shortest span
in the ribs. The line load is transferred as punctual loads in the arches as illustrated in Figure 3.1a. The
system for the Single module shell distributes the loads as pure shear, creating normal forces that increase
linearly downwards to the supports. This results in the elements on the ground level being exposed to the
largest forces. The loads are distributed as vertical and horizontal forces (thrust forces) at the support.

Conclusion
This projects ambition is set out to investigate and design two shell structures with the shape of Single
module shell and Saddle shell with the use of prefabricated elements. It strives to reduce the complexity
related to construction and manufacturing related to the shell structures to reduce costs and design hin-
drances related to the shapes.
The solution resulted in a Saddle shell structure with a width of 14 meters and a length of 12 meters. The
peak height of the structure is 6 meters. The Single module shell has a width and length of 10 meters and
a peak height of 5 meters. Both structures are of doubly curved, making them strong and stable struc-
tures. The shapes are mostly distributed to normal forces and bending moments. The overall geometry
of the structures allows for large spans without any additional supports. The Saddle shell does not need
any additional supports as it transfers the loads directly to the ямВoor, while the Single module shell only
needs thin columns to prevent instability and deformation.
In the preliminary design, it was considered that the main acting forces on the structures would be nor-
mal forces due to the double curvature of the structures. As a result of the conservative approach in the
hand-calculations and construction methods, bending moments needed to be considered in the design
process. For the Saddle shell arch, smaller moments were occurring in the FEM model compared with
the hand-calculations of the circular arch. It was concluded that the form of the curvature of the arch
modeled in Autodesk Robot is in between the deямБnition of a circular arch and a parabolic arch where no
moments would occur. Single module shell is also distributed to bending moments during construction.
But the biggest bending moments are found during load case with snow on half the roof surface. In the
hand calculations, 2D sections of the ribs are considered when calculating the loads on the beams which
does not consider the curvature of the structure. Even though the calculations on the shells are considered
conservative which may result in bigger cross sections, both reinforcement, cable, stirrups and concrete
cover needs to ямБt in the ribs, so the overall dimensions of the sections are of sufямБcient size.
The ribs in the elements create a skeleton in the structure, as the loads are distributed to the highest
stiffness. The shear plates contribute as a ямБll-in and are exposed to shear distribution. In the earlier in-
vestigation, it was found that the arch-shaped plate for the Saddle shell is a much more sufямБcient shape as
the secondary bending moments are signiямБcantly reduced. A linear shape would require a much thicker
section, and besides, the thickness of the ribs would need to be higher to ямБt with the ямБll-in shear plate.
The Single module shell transfers vertical loads as pure shear, creating high normal forces. This has been

one of the main challenges for designing the shear plates. An analysis of normal concrete and aggregate
concrete materials was performed and concluded that normal concrete gives the best result considering
the amount of material and weight.
An arch segment in the Saddle shell can be built by one repeated unique element in two different lo-
cations, while one unique element in the Single module shell is applied in several different locations.
The structures can also be repeated creating a bigger spatial structure, without increasing the number of
unique elements. The systematic repetition of elements and modules means that the same manufacturing
and construction process can be repeated and as a result reduce construction costs. The simplicity of
joints and structural elements ensures a further reduction in manufacturing costs and construction costs.
The rib solution creates an effective way of constructing the elements together and assembling the el-
ements before lifting them to place minimizing complex work in height. The overall element size is
sufямБcient regarding transportation and total connections for completing the construction. Furthermore,
the simplicity of the connections and the low number of unique elements reduces the risk of mistakes
during construction. The use of prefabricated elements eliminates cumbersome formwork, which is one
of the main reasons why shell structures lost their popularity.
