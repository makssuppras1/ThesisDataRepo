Abstract
This project is based on a particular problem concerning terrorist attack with the use of
explosives and maybe in some cases accidents with high amount of released energy. It is well
known that cause of the most injuries happened in a ballistic attack is due, to ﬂying glass
parts. For that reason this requires special design to obtain the safest case possible, considering
aesthetic and economic factors. However the main focus of the thesis is no the window type glass
element with all the edges supported, but balustrade type, with only one edge that is retained
completely or partially. Glazing element used in this subject is the laminated glass, that oﬀers
necessary stiﬀness and safe failure behavior compared to monolithic annealed glass.
In ﬁrst part of this project, few things were studied.
First, diﬀerent mechanism used in
explosion type connection or even seismic events, were analyzed, those devices are describe in
some references. Because material proprieties of the glass and PVB are of a great importance,
for this aspect a special attention was oﬀered. First of all in such an special event, loading
rate is very high, meaning that typical proprieties of the glass and polyvinyl butyral are no
longer valid, an another rate-dependent proprieties are required for realistic simulation. While
glass proprieties for diﬀerent strain rates could be found and implemented, rate-dependent PVB
proprieties were still unknown. It is important to mention that not only mechanical parameters
for material behavior is required, but also an failure criteria, without which it would be not
possible to understand what is the safe design. Besides that, considering the amount of energy
and magnitude of forces generated in such a scenario, elastic proprieties without considering
plastic and rupture energy dissipation would result in a conservative and highly expensive
solution. For that reason an entire chapter was dedicated for determination of rate-dependent
material for interlayer, with an failure criteria, and based on experimental results that were done
by some authors.
Lastly but most important few connection mechanism were designed. The principle of how
those mechanism dissipates energy, is based mainly on friction and plastic works.
Diﬀerent
conﬁguration were studied, and the results in terms of dissipated energy, failure of the glass
panel and trajectory of the blowing part were determined and discussed. Results shows that
some of the proposed devices are able to absorb a part of kinetic energy generated by the blast
wave, and actually control the way glass panel is failing which is an as important criteria as the
amount of dissipated energy. However studied cases were based on 10 kg TNT and 1 m stand-oﬀ
distance load setup, which is extremely strong and a scenario, where glass or even PVB dose not
failing, is not possible, unless either diﬀerent gazing element is used and not necessary stiﬀer one
but one that can deform more before tearing, or by assuming a softer explosive scenario with a
lower ballistic parameters.

Introduction 1
1.1
Problem statement
In last few decades with the growing globalization and world wide relation between
countries,more misunderstandings and disputes based on economical, social or religious reasons
were born. Some of those conﬂicts grow in a aggressive form at certain point, and further those
leads to terrorist attack. Most of this attacks implies an explosive charge. Statistic from National
Consortium for the Study of Terrorism provide data that shows that most preferred weapon used
by terrorist is bombing Figure 1.1. The reason for this high percentage is the eﬃciency of this
method and also the message that such an attack will give to the population, being a symbol of
power and chaos. Usually because of the lack of prim materials and professional machines and
laboratories, this explosive bombs are hand made, which in the end provide and unpredictable
damage.
There are few books (Watson 2004) that discuss and oﬀer some recommendations
how to cover or hide main structural elements or design entrances in a such way that would
diminish damage to key element as well will decrease radius of action. One of the main challenges
regarding blast loads, is the safety of the people in the building. If construction can be designed to
withstand even if a structural element is eliminated, by making it robust and develop alternative
loading paths, then glazing elements are more eﬀected by the explosive. Architectural trends
in past years relay on usage of more and more glass facades and balustrades, even structural
elements such as ﬂoors, beams, meaning that a big proportion of the surrounding materials are
glazing elements. It is well known that highest threat during an explosion for people inside the
building are glass parts that blow away with high speed and cause most of the injuries. Therefore
designing those glass elements in a way that they would not break with large and sharp parts as
well as the velocity of those fragments is small enough so they do not spread to far from its initial
position, to cause sever damage to people, is the main challenge for an engineer. There are few
studies on window type and facade glass elements with blast mitigating proprieties. However
scenarios where a balustrade glass element is exposed to a blast wave is rarely researched and to
ﬁnd any references that discuss this topic is diﬃcult.

1.2
Scope of the project
Aim of the project is a detailed investigation of a balustrade glazing element. And speciﬁcally it
is the connection of the glass part with the rigid part of the support structure. This investigation
can be divided in several parts.
Whole problem will be analyzed with the use of commercial FEM software Abaqus. First part
was parametric study, where key aspects regarding blast wave propagation, reaction forces, and
behavior under ballistic pressure were investigated. Second, material proprieties for high load
rated should be estimated for interlayer and Glass.
And after the dissipating proprieties of
the connection should be analyzed and an as eﬃcient as possible design should be obtained.
Modeling is major process that requires a very comprehensive and complex approach. First,
laminated glass element is modeled with corresponding glass proprieties for high strain rates
as well material for interlayer. Information for this speciﬁc topic is very limited and only few
scientiﬁc papers can be found regarding mechanical proprieties of polyvinyl butyral and silica
ﬂoat glass at high strain rate, hence a major part of time is dedicated to this subject.
With an adequate laminated glass model, few energy dissipating mechanism are proposed and
discussed.
Where capacity of those device should be estimated in terms of kinetic energy,
dissipated energy, and reaction force curves.

2.1
Blast load
Explosions are fast chemical reactions that increase locally air pressure, which can be called
as blast wave. Those are often used as an weapon tool, due its colossal destructive power, and
terror that they generate, being on of most used weapon by terrorist groups.
Figure 2.1: Alfred P. Murrah Federal Building Oklahoma City USA after explosion
In literature blast loads can be divided in two major types, in respect to wave shape. One
is when the charge explode from the ground level, known as hemispherical wave.
And the
second one, is when the explosion occurs in air, which is named spherical wave. Distinctions
between those 2 types are based on the shape of wave-front, and how those are propagated from
the explosion source to the surroundings. Most important characteristic are stand-oﬀdistance,
explosive material, size, charge mass and environment conditions that can propagate or absorb
energy released by explosions.
2.1.1
Stand-oﬀdistance
Stand-oﬀdistance is the length measured between the explosion source to the building or
object of interest. It is one of the fundamental parameters when an blast eﬀect is studied. As
this parameter increases the peak over-pressure registered on the building facade signiﬁcantly
decreases. Meaning that having a structural element as far as possible from the bomb charge,
will make it safer and with minimum load pressure. However in the big cities possibility to close
or secure an area around the building so the stand-oﬀdistance is long enough is quite diﬃcult.
Moreover estimating this parameters usually is not straight-forward. Since predicting the place
and distance and position of the bomb inside or outside of the building is not possible. However
there are some techniques and requirements that can mitigate and somehow "control" where the
bomb will be positioned. In most cases choice of stand-oﬀdistance for a design is a matter of cost.
Figure 2.2 from (Watson 2004) explains very well how explosion distance should be estimated.
Either you increases directly stand-oﬀradius by securing surroundings which increases with the

2.1.3
TNT equivalent and explosive weight
Generally there are 3 type of explosive: Nuclear, Mechanical, Chemical. Nuclear is explosion
where a rapid nuclear reaction occurs instantly with enormous amount of energy, mechanical
is related to the those explosion where a cavity is under a high pressure and sudden release by
failure of the boundary conditions will cause an rapid release of energy by balancing external and
internal pressure. And third type which is one that is used the most is chemical type. Chemical
explosive are divided in two 1) detonating and 2) deﬂagrating. Detonating explosion such as
dynamite or TNT are characterized by fast decomposition with releases of high pressure.
In literature (Vasilis KARLOS 2013) are shown means value of TNT capacity per type of
carrier. For example a truck can carry a explosive weight of 5000 kg. While medium size car
200kg. If the explosion occurs inside the building it may be fair to assume that explosive will
be carried in a back-pack or suitcase, with capacity of 10 kg of TNT. In this way depending on
what is design preferences and what are possible scenarios, an approximate blast charge can be
assumed.
Most of the publication related to the blast eﬀects are using TNT as reference explosion
measure. First of all because TNT is explosive, with largest available experimental data and
second its behaviour is most well known.

2.1.5
Structural response
When a structure is aﬀected by an time-dependent load such as earth-quake, sea waves, wind
, and blast load for that matter, response in terms of displacements and forces on structure can
not be described by static relations. Instead equation of motion (2.6) must be used. Where M, K
and C are Mass matrix, Stiﬀness and Damping matrix for multidegree of freedom. If a simpliﬁed
model is used with one degree of freedom SDOF then those represents single values.
M · ¨x + C · ˙x + K · x = P(t)
(2.6)
In addition structural response is a matter of the load characteristic, in (Watson 2004) 3 diﬀerent
loading regime are characterized. First one is when load has a long period td compared to the
natural period of the structure T. This happens when explosion occurs from long distance, so
element has enough time to respond to the load, and resistance follows approximately applied
load and it is known as Quasi-static loading see Figure 2.5 (a). When explosion occurs at a
shorter distance with high amount of charge, loading regime is impulsive, meaning that it has a
much shorter duration compared to T, so in result, load decays ends while structural response
just started, see Figure 2.5 (b) . Third type is known as Dynamic loading Figure 2.5 (c) it occurs
when natural period of the structure and loading period are approximately the same. Structural
response can be describe by solving equation of motion.

2.2
Structural connection for glazing elements
Energy dissipation is concerning two main parts. First is the glazing balustrade or the safety
glass, where a part of energy is absorbed by the cracking of the glass plies, and strain deformation
of the interlayer until rupture. And the second major part is taken by the connection between
laminated glass and rigid element. If connection is designed in a rigid manner, then a higher
amount of load will be stored in both structural element, laminated wall and connection parts.
And with such design a very expensive and under-optimal solution will result.
In order to
avoid that, suggested connection must be provided with an dissipating mechanism, and allowed
movement in directions of reaction forces, however respective translation should not be free but it
should have some sort of energy absorption due to viscous,internal or friction work. Unfortunately
information regarding blast connection for glazing elements is limited . And it is even more
diﬃcult to ﬁnd any literature regarding balustrade connection type, which is the main focus of
this project.
However as basis of the initial design a typical non blast connection can serve well such as detail
presented by (Department 2018).

Edge retention
Edge retention must be taken into consideration especially for explosion scenarios. In many
experiments some of the specimens failed due to fact that laminated glass was pulled out of the
frame. Therefore a preferably solution is that pulling forces of the windows is higher than tearing
strength of the interlayer. In (Watson 2004) few suggestion are oﬀered for selection of laminated
glass retention in the connection. Some of them are suited better for blast action then other,
where a small description can be found bellow.
1. Bolt-ﬁxed glass Panes are ﬁxed to the rest of connection through bolts, at some spacing
along the width of the glass. This requires some holes to be made which lead to concentrate
stress around that area. Therfore glass must be toughened and laminated. Overall the blast
resistant is lower than other type of ﬁxation as it mentioned in (Watson 2004), for the same
reason of making holes, that will create crack when the pane is loaded with shock wave.
This solution requires more additionally research.
2. Elastomeric gaskets Elastomeric gaskets is a rubber-like material meant to keep in place
glass element. It is relatively quicker and cheaper than other solution, although its strength
capacity is lower compared to the structural silicon. Whereas the pull-out forces will exceed
gaskets capacity and in results the glass wall will failure by being removed from the support.
Which is not preferred especially during the explosion where entire capacity of the glass
ply and interlayer should be used.
3. Structural silicone Most eﬃcient restrain is considered with structural silicone. This
sealant has a high performance and can be design so the PVB inter-layer will tear rather
than whole glazing element will be pulled of from the supports.

2.3
Laminated glass
On of the most crucial factors regarding the safety of the people, when the structures is
subjected to blast load are windows and facade. Normally many of them are build using annealed
glass which when fail, it fractures in big slices that are thrown away and cause most of the injuries
during the explosions for example after bombing of the Alfred P. Murrah Federal Building in
Oklahoma City in 1995, more than 75 % were cause by the glass parts (Remennikov, Chipperﬁeld,
and Mcinerney n.d.). In order to avoid such a destructive behavior of the facade and windows it
is recommended to rely on the use of laminated glass . Since it is a composite element, made of
glass part and a interlayer. This type of materials are described more detailed in the following
sections.
2.3.1
Glass
Glass is one of the most preferable material used by architects, which provide natural light
into enclosure, and an aesthetic look to the construction. Before it was seen just as a envelope
material, while in past years it was used as a part of the structure. Starting from annealed glass
with low strength proprieties continue to strengthen one, it was widely applied in many solution.
But the weakest point of glass material remains the same, is its brittleness and failure without
notiﬁcation. Fortunately there are few possibilities to combine properties of glass with another

and one interlayer, where in some cases more layers can be added. Production of sheets of glass
is thermal process where raw materials silica, lime, soda, alumina are melted at a temperature
1500 °C. Further the melted liquid is poured into area were its panel shapes are formed. The
thickness oﬀglass is controlled by the the pouring speed, meantime the temperature is lowered
to 600 °C. Next step is annealing oven, where a slow process of cooling occurs, in order to avoid

integration. First of all by looking at the (2.25), the only inverted matrix is the mass matrix of
the elements. So by using "lumped" mass matrix, which contains values only on the diagonal,
calculation becomes less expensive. Second, equations that need to be solved are uncoupled
meaning that, several processors can be used to lower the computational time. Another important
aspect of the dynamic explicit is the geometrical and force nonliniearities including contact, which
are stored in the internal force vector, thing that again improves calculation since no inversion of
that vector is required. And ﬁnally no convergence checking is needed in explicit method since
values are calculated explicitly and not iterated as in implicit method.

Summarize
Two methods to solve and dynamic event were brieﬂy introduced. One is based on Central
Diﬀerence Method and second on Newmark numerical integration.
By analyzing both
procedures it can be concluded that Implicit method is very stable and highly accurate for
linear problems where load rate and displacements are relatively small, while Explicit is more
suited for highly dynamic problems that includes contact. From this reason for problems such
as blast load Explicit FEM solver is best choice, in terms of computational cost and results,
moreover Conwep interaction is available exclusively in Abaqus/Explict. However output results
from an Explicit analysis must be seen with criticism and several conditions must be fulﬁlled
in order to consider that those results have an realistic meaning. With that being said in the
following sections only features regarding Abaqus/Explicit method will be introduced. For more
information regarding explicit and implicit method follow references should be checked (Bathe
2016) book for many other numerical integration and FEM implementation, (Mashayekhi 2015)
summary of both methods, (Hibbitt Karlsson and Sorensen 1992) and (SIMULIA n.d.) regarding
Abaqus theory and formulations.

First model will be based on the brittle cracking failure criteria. Normally this is not well suited
or characteristic for a material like polyvinyl butyral, but because main focus is on blast loading
which is highly fast dynamic load, it will be fair to assume that interlayer fails in brittle way.
Analysis started by using initial input parameters for the PVB material. In order to deﬁne Brittle

mandatory for Abaqus explicit analysis. This proprieties are deﬁned in various literature’s, for

no use in this particular case, because those material proprieties are meant for simulation with
smaller deformations and strain-rates. Publication (FORS 2014) recommend a value for PVB
between 0.45-0.49, therefore it was decided for a value of 0.47. Density is used as 1070 kg
m3 .

Conclusion
Proposed material for the PVB shows decent results compared to experimental data, not
only for one speciﬁc load rate but also for diﬀerent load velocity, which clearly shows an rate-
dependent behaviour, that is an important aspect in the blast loading simulations. Two diﬀerent
damage formulation were proposed in order to include failure of the interlayer. Moreover an mesh
inﬂuence study was performed, from which it can be concluded that using a mesh coarser than
5 mm will results in accuracy lose. However for ﬁnnier simulation will take longer calculation
time, therefore 5 mm seem fair and optimal. As for which damage criteria should be use in this
work, both show pros and cons, for example lack of possibility to provide accurate results for
load rate of 0.5 m/s and also some deviation for failure stress and strain for other loads. One

of both ends, would help to have an more pure axial traction rather then various actions on the
connection element.

Conclusion 6
In this project were studied possibilities to diminish the eﬀect of an explosion on a glazing
element type balustrade. For that several aspects were needed in order to obtain an detailed and
fairly accurate model, required for FEM analyses.
Considering that an blast wave applied to a structure will results in a highly dynamic, impulsive
loading, rate-dependent material properties are needed. Hence chapter 3 was dedicated for this
aspect only.
First based on the knowledge provided by other authors regarding high-strain
rate properties of the glass, and most suited material model was found. That bein Johnson
Holmquist constitutive model, whose properties were found in few reference regarding this topic.
Implementation was done with the use the built-in material of Abaqus, where all corresponding
parameters were written in the keyword option of the model. While rate-dependent properties
of the glass could be found in some publication, interlayer constitutive model was still unknown.
From that reason in the same chapter PVB, representation was developed based on the uni-axial
tests data from external references and implementation through the Johnson Cook constitutive
model. The diﬃculty was not only to describe well stress-strain curve at diﬀerent loading-rates.
But at the same time to model tearing of the PVB that would correspond to speciﬁc strain.
Two failure criteria were used, and each of them presented some pros and cons. In the end most
optimal to use for complete 3D model including energy dissipating mechanism was based on the
Ductile Damage failure criteria. It should be stressed out that no ideal matching between test
results and FEM simulation was reached. Moreover proposed material is only suited for high-
loading rates as it was meant for that purpose. Therefore it can not describe PVB behaviour in
a static or even quasi-static load case, or especially the unloading phase (hysteresis behaviour)
when no damage occurred of that interlayer.
Before starting to look into connection for the glazing structure, and preliminary parametric
studied was performed on diﬀerent blast and boundary conduction conﬁguration. It was found
that stand-oﬀdistance and charge mass are both have an exponential variation in energy release
by increasing explosive mass or decreasing stand-oﬀdistance. Hence a design scenario may be
estimated with care to this bot parameters. Further the inﬂuence of how glass panel is connected
was studied. Expected results were found, that for case with rigid boundary connection higher
reactions and stresses in the panel are generated especially in the ﬁrst phase when blast wave
just hits the panel. Another smaller analysis was performed on the use of spring elements as
supports. Where it showed that failure or movement of the structure during the explosion can
be manipulated in the way that will ﬁt safety requirements. As well with a lower stiﬀness of
the spring elements, smaller forces are transferred meaning that the force over time load have
smaller peaks but at the same time average of all values will be the same. It is important to
mention that parametric study accomplished in that chapter are done in order to understand some
phenomena, and therefore only simpliﬁed FEM model was used, with normal elastic properties
for the materials and shell elements for the glazing element.

==PAGE:78==
Finally few mechanism designs were introduced meant for energy dissipation.
Two main
principle were applied for those devices, friction work and elasto-plastic deformation.
Blast
load in contrast to earth-quake have just one period positive phase and usually smaller negative
phase, as well those are very short which lasts few milliseconds. From that consideration a very
high amount of energy can’t be dissipated since there is only one movement and not a cycling
vibration as for earthquakes. Results for several mechanism in terms of converted energy from
kinetic to internal and frictional, showed that most useful device is the one with combined sliding
cylindrical parts and compression of the multiple layers of crushing material. It dissipates almost
2 times more energy which is 8 kJ compared to 4 kJ from model without mechanism, while total
external work from blast pressure, on the 20 cm wide glass panel is around 20 kJ1. Few criteria
where taken in consideration on whether one connection type is better than other. One of that
being the trajectory of the part that tore from the rest of the structure. It basically show what
is the vector of the blown part, based on the velocity resultant of the element. Results shows
clearly correlation between amount of the dissipated energy and calculated slope. However it is
important to point out that a major factor is that there is a hinge connection, between mechanism
and the proﬁle which is retaining the laminated glass panel. So basically by allowing a movement
when the blast hits it delays the time when the glass element would fall, time where panel will
begin to rotate and take the vector which would lead to a closer landing distance. Here comes on
of the assumptions which is that whole structure is freely to rotate, which would not be the case
for a real balustrade where it has to be kept in place, however it can be designed with an bolt
element that would fail and further allow that rotation. In any case inﬂuence of that ﬁxation
needs to be studied additionally.
Even though connection can be designed to convert a part of kinetic energy into friction work,
or plastic dissipation, its capacity is limited by the glazing element.
Meaning that it is not
possible to increase the applied pre-stressing force that would result in a sliding force so it can
dissipate all the transferred force from the panel.Therefore dissipation capacity of the connection
is a matter of both laminated glass and used mechanism. In other words if design requires to
dissipate an higher amount of incoming blast energy, it is not enough just to increase the crushing
material stiﬀness or friction, but also to use an glazing element that would able to transfer that
amount of force to the connection. It should be noted that increasing stiﬀens of the glass may
not necessary have an positive eﬀect, from what was observed, more suited would be to use an
glazing element that would deform and deﬂect a lot before failing or resisting all external work.
With that being said, introduced connections improve the resistance of the glazing structure.
However results from this study shall be taken with care, because of the several assumptions
that were made.
First oﬀall, both glass and interlayer constitutive models are describing
approximately the real material behaviour.
Moreover delamination between layers is not
included, which can lead to some major changes in the output data. As for energy dissipating
mechanism, proposed design shall give similar results regardless of the material properties of the
glazing elements, certainly in case if those main parameters are adjusted accordingly. But to be
more conﬁdent in the properties and capacity of those devices it would be deﬁnitely necessary
to validated through experimental tests.

6. Conclusion
6.1
Further work
The multitude of aspects and diﬀerent topics required to look into for this project is quite
large. Some of them were covered accurately and detailed while some were not included and
hence some assumptions were introduced. From that reason many subject that required an more
long-term study were born.
More eﬃcient rate-dependent material for interlayer and glass, including and
failure criteria for FEM analysis
First oﬀall problem concerning blast loading on the glass facade can’t be studied eﬃciently
without an realistic material formulation.
Hence one of the future project could be focused
mainly on this subject.
Where either rate-depended glass or PVB or another interlayer are
studied and accurate material model is set, by performing a variety of tensile of bending test.
Based on large amount of test data an FEM material could be developed and tested, including
many section regarding inﬂuence of the mesh and other factors. At this topic can be added as
well the failure criteria. For that would be necessary to ﬁnd through test fracture energy of the
material.
Detailed and parametric study on one or more mechanism, determination of upper
and lower capacity with respect to the blast load. Optimization
In this thesis few mechanism were presented but only one case of blast loading were discussed.
Therefore one of the topics could be a parametric study in terms of scaled stand-oﬀdistance on
the model with energy dissipating device. Further upper and lower limits of the capacity of this
mechanism can be identiﬁed, also regarding both glass panel and connection itself. And ﬁnally
an optimization of the mechanism dimension and material used, perhaps also other parameter
could be covered regarding friction coeﬃcient, method to use surfaces with diﬀerent friction
properties etc. In addition global eﬀect by using an wide glazing balustrade on the results can
be analyzed , since in this thesis only 20 cm wide panel were considered in the study.
Inﬂuence of laminated glass layers thickness on the forces transfer and dynamic
response of the structure
A further work can be done focusing more on glass panel. Where such aspects as inﬂuence
of the thickness of the glass on the transferred forces to the connection. Or what would be the
most optimal way to design a glazing element including discussion about how many interlayers
should be used, what would be thickness and so on. Maybe additional method to reinforce the
glass panel to obtain a stiﬀer model that can withstand longer and transfer longer forces to the
connection.
Delamination eﬀect and shear strength between glass and interlayer. Inﬂuence on
the results.
One important aspect that was not included in the FEM from this project is delamination
eﬀect. It is not known well whether including this eﬀect would have a positive or negative eﬀect.
Therefore it would be interesting to identify the shear strength between interlayer and glass, and
further to research global eﬀect of it on blast wave simulation. As well retaining capacity of the
silicon sealant in the glass proﬁle can be studied here as well, since both topic are similar in a
way.
