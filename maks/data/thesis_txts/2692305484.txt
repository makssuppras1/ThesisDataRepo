Abstract 
We have to build more sustainable and more energy efficient, with a focus on optimal indoor 
climate. There are now a number of publications that prescribe the environment benefits of building 
wood intensive.  
 
This report looks at the energy and indoor environment performance of a building project “det 
Narturlige Hus”. “Det Naturlige Hus” is built with hygroscopic materials such as clay, wood and wood 
fiber insulation. The ventilation and energy concept of “det Naturlige Hus” is an exhaust ventilation 
heat pump in combination with air supply windows.  
 
The investigations in the report show that it is possible to built sustainable wood intensive houses 
with both low energy consumption and a high level of the indoor environment.  
 
The energy study in the report show that the building concept meets the requirements for low 
energy buildings in the building regulation BR18. The result show that the concept has a 50 % 
consumption as if the building had natural ventilation, normal windows and was heated with district 
heating. The energy concept can compete with building with mechanical ventilation with efficient 
heat recovery and heated with a heat pump.  
 
The predictions in the simulations of the indoor environment, based on standard values for people's 
heating load and moisture and CO2-production, are shown to be different from measurements made 
under real conditions. CO2 levels in simulations match measured values well. Overtemperatures 
appear higher in simulations compared to real measurements. Moisture levels appear lower in 
simulations compared to real measurements. 
 
Measurements of the ventilation rate show that open hygroscopic constructions can be built with an 
air tightness that meets the requirement in the building regulation BR18. The ventilation rate 
measurements also show that the combination with air supply windows and the exhaust heat pump 
can create a good ventilation rate level. Although some window sections are not openable due to the 
function of the air supply windows, it is possible in the building to create good venting via the 
windows that are openable as well as the skylights.  
 
Results of Particle and TVOC measurements show acceptable levels. The spread of particles from 
cooking turns out to be low. TVOC level is medium low. Both measurements bear the mark that the 
building was not completely finished when the measurements were done.  
 
Measurements of temperature and moisture levels in the building envelope show that it is possible 
to control moisture levels in hygroscopic building structures, but they also show that towards the 
outer layers of the building envelope must be cautious to eliminate all possibilities of moisture 
accumulation.

Målinger af temperatur og fugtniveauer i klimaskærmen viser at det er muligt at kontrollere

1. Introduction and background 
For many years now there have been continuous tightening of regulatory requirements for lower 
energy consumption of new buildings and for refurbishments. As our buildings have become more 
and more insulated, the buildings have become more and more dense and the construction trend 
has switched to more lightweight interior structures and large windows. Moisture problems, 
overheating, inadequate ventilation and generally poor indoor climate have become a major issue 
that has to be tackled.  
 
Buildings must be protected against harmful accumulation of moisture as a result of moisture 
transport from the indoor air. In the Danish building culture, this has for many years meant that 
moisture transport is ensured by installing vapor barrier and that one must have a mechanical 
ventilation system with heat recovery to provide the necessary ventilation with a low energy use. The 
required ventilation rate for residential buildings has mainly been to remove moisture and is 
inadequate to control overheating and maybe other indoor pollutants. 
 
In the last revision of the building regulations only single-family houses are now allowed to use 
natural ventilation. All other buildings must have mechanical ventilation with heat recovery. 
 
Natural ventilation is difficult to control, and it does not have any heat recovery. So, until now the 
most effective method to both keep energy consumption down and at the same time have the 
necessary air change, has been to install mechanical ventilation with a high degree of heat recovery 
and low electric energy consumption (SFP value). 
 
However, most existing single-family houses still have natural ventilation. But it can be both difficult 
and costly to install mechanical ventilation. 
 
A very old ventilation concept has been given new life in recent years: ventilation windows, supply 
air windows or Russian windows. From the exterior, the air can be drawn through an airspace 
between some windowpanes and be preheated partly from the sun and partly from the heat loss 
from within the building. The ventilation window's driven force is obtained from wind exposure and 
from the temperature gradient from intake to return. 
 
In recent decades, there has been a more focus on sustainability and indoor climate. We need to 
build more in wood because it is good for the environment that CO2 becomes embedded in buildings. 
[1] There is a big potential for building more wood intensive in Denmark. Today only about 8% of the 
buildings in Denmark are made of wood. In Sweden this number is 92 % [2] A single family house 
with a wood share of 78% has a carbon load of 40 % compared with a single family house made of 
conventional materials. [2].  
 
But until now there has been a fear against the use of natural organic building materials in building 
construction for reasons of fear of mold accumulation. 
 
Det Naturlige Hus (The Natural House) is a single-family building project under construction on 
Kejserkåbevej 11, 4300 Holbæk. It is expected to be completed once in 2021. However, the family 
moved in in spring 2018, when only a few rooms were finished. The construction project attempts to 
build as healthy and sustainably as possible in order to have a better basis for creating the best 
possible indoor environment for the residents with the lowest possible carbon footprint.

In the construction project, a number of actions are planned to contribute to a healthy indoor 
environment, low energy consumption and sustainable solutions. The structures are built from 
natural and hygroscopic building materials such as lime, clay and wood. The insulation is with wood 
fiber insulation. The building envelope is built up with airtight vapor retarder and not a vapor barrier. 
This in the form of the interior clay-plastered walls. 
The building is hybrid ventilated with air supply windows in combination with an air to water heat 
pump that exhaust air from kitchen bathrooms and utility room. 
 
 
1.1 Aim and scope 
The overall aim of the project is to evaluate the energy and indoor environmental performance with 
the chosen building concept in “det Naturlige Hus” (dNH).  
 
In “det Naturlige Hus” an air to water heat pump extracts air from the bathrooms, kitchen and utility. 
The supply air comes into the building through air supply windows located in bedrooms, living room 
and in the kitchen. There is also a skylight in the living room that can supplement the natural 
ventilation on warm summer days. 
The extraction from the cooking takes place by room extraction in the ceiling above the boiling niche. 
 
In this project I would like to document how effective the system is and also how the correlation is 
between simulations and practical measured values are. 
In the project I will seek to answer questions: 
- 
What are the typical air change rates? 
- 
How well does the building perform in terms of indoor environment, like levels of CO2, moisture, 
temperature and other pollutants? 
- 
 How well does the hygroscopic design of the building assembly work in terms of buffering moisture 
and other pollutants?  
- 
How well does the building perform energy wise, and does it meet the energy standards of today?  
- 
What are the energy savings towards building in a more traditional way.

2. Description of the case study 
2.1 Det Naturlige Hus 
Det Naturlige Hus has 187 m2 gross living space, for which 48 m2 unheated garage and 27 m2 
unheated basement. The building appears, with the black tiled roof and white plastered façades, as a 
typical single-family house in Denmark. The building is located in a residential parcel district on the 
eastern side of Holbæk.  
 
The building has a well-insulated building envelope that is elevated above terrain with a ventilated 
crawlspace underneath. The building is designed to be naturally ventilated via ventilation windows in 
combination with an air/water heat pump. This exhaust air from the bathrooms, the utility room and 
the kitchen produce hot water and central heating for an underfloor heating system.

2.1.2 Windows 
Windows in the building are mainly ventilation windows (air supply windows) from Horn Windows. 
Skylights are 2 900 x 1600 mm Velux with external sunscreen and manual electronic control as it is 
installed (ability for automatic control upgrade). 
 
The ventilation windows consist of an external 2-layer energy pane with argon filling and warm edge 
distance profile, 80 mm cavity and a 6 mm interior glass. Outdoor air is fed into an opening in the 
base frame to the cavity. Here it can be preheated by the sun's radiation as well as the heat loss 
through the 1-layer pane towards the room. In the top frame there are two valves. A heating/cooling 
valve and a bypass valve. 
 
Research has shown that you can reduce the effective U-value by 30 – 40 % [3] [4] taking the 
preheating and cooling of supply air into account. The total energy demand can be reduced with 
approx. 20 % compared with closed cavity windows [5]

2.1.3 Ventilation system design 
 
The ventilation and energy principle of the house is contiguous, as the air/water heating pump 
exhaust in the kitchen, utility room and bathrooms. The heat pump, a Comfortzone EX50L with a 
210-liter hot-water tank, is located in the basement room under the utility room and bedroom 1. 
 
Fresh air supply comes through the air supply windows located in all bedrooms, living room and 
kitchen. The heat pump extracts heat from the exhaust air and produces hot water and room heating 
via underfloor heating. 
 
The ventilation can be supplemented by window openings in all windows other than air supply 
sections and a skylight in the dining room.

2.1.4 Energy system design 
The design of the energy system for dNH is that an air/water heat pump is extracting heat from the 
exhaust ventilation from the building. The heat from the exhaust air is transferred via heat exchanger 
to a refrigerant. The temperature of the refrigerant is raised  via a cooling system and compression 
(heat pump) to be exchange via another heat exchanger to either central heating in an underfloor 
heating system or domestic hot water.  
The heat pump from IKM is a Comfortzone EX50L with a 210-liter domestic hot-water tank. The 
nominal efficiency, COP is approx. 3 to 4 for either DHW or central heating mode. Documentation 
from the manufacturer is located in appendix A.2 Be18 dokumentation

A number of different measurements have been made in the house including the following.  
 
• 
Data logging of indoor environmental parameters temperature, relative humidity, CO2, radon,  
particle mass and  TVOC. 
• 
Data logging of temperature and relative humidity in selected areas of the building envelope.  
• 
Tracer gas measurement of selected rooms (room 2, 3, 4 and living room) 
• 
Handheld measurement of ultrafine particles (particle count) 
• 
Handheld control measurements of air and indoor environment air velocity m/s, lux, CO2, %RH 
and °C

• 
2 pcs. HOBO MX 1102 dataloggere fra Onset (Temp, RH and CO2), 
•

• 
1 electronic Corentium Home radon meter (Day and week average Radon) 
• 
1 pcs. FOOBOT FBT 2100 datalogger (Temp, RH, PM2.5, TVOC) 
• 
Enervisual datalogging system with sensors in walls, ceiling and floor (Temp, RH)

absorption, multizone natural ventilation, energy consumption and daylight performance on an

calendar year 2010 in compliance with the Danish Building regulation, BR18.

4.2.4 Total volatile organic compounds, TVOC. 
It must be stated that the TVOC measurements is only done with the simple meter FOOBOT and 
therefor must be taken with a bit of uncertainty.  
 
 
Figure 37: TVOC measurements FOOBOT 15.08 -  21.08.2018. Average = 300 ppb, Min. = 125 ppb, Max. = 646 ppb. 
   
In the website https://ieqdatabase.herokuapp.com/ Here its stats that SEPA State Environmental 
Protection Agency recommend an 8 hour average exposure not to exceed 300 ppb and IEE Institute 
of Environmental Epidemiology recommends TVOC ceiling exposure not to exceed 3000 ppb.  
 
The TVOC measurement is the total amount of volatile organic compounds. Volatile organic 
compounds are different compounds with a high vapour pressure and a low water solubility. They 
are emitted from building materials, furniture, paints, cleaning products etc. Exposure to VOC´s can 
have health effects on humans and in high amounts even fatal health effects.  
 
In the sustainable build program DGNB a building with a TVOC concentration over 3000 µg/m3 
(approx. 1500 ppb) and Formaldehyde concentration over 100 µg/m3 (approx. 80 ppb) is a knockout 
criterion and can therefore not be certified. A building gets the best score in DGNB if the 
concentrations are less than 300 µg/m3 (150 ppb) towards TVOC and less than 50 µg/m3 (40 ppb) for 
Formaldehyde.  
These are measurements to be done when the building is finished.  
 
Towards the TVOC measurement in Figure 37 the building is way under the TVOC ceiling and 
knockout criteria. With an average of 299,8 ppb (600 µg/m3) the building could not meet the highest 
score in DGNB, but can must likely meet the next best score of 250 ppb when the building is 
completely finished. By this it can be said that the building has an ok level of TVOC´s.   
 
Comparing with results of “Sunde boliger” in Holstebro [16] with three different building approaches 
No-tech, Now-tech and Yes-tech. These results showed 2000 – 4000 µg/m3 dropping to 400 – 1000 
µg/m3, 200 days after the finished buildings.

5. Conclusion 
This report confirms that it is possible to build with a low energy consumption and high quality of 
indoor environment using the building and energy concept of dNH.  
 
 The result of the energy performance and the indoor environmental examinations show positive 
numbers in regard to both the energy system and the system for maintaining the indoor 
environment.  
 
This combination of air supply windows and an exhaust ventilation heat pump both reduces the 
energy consumption and enhances the performance and ventilation rate through the air supply 
windows and in the building.  
 
By taking inlet air through the air cavity of the 2 +1-layer air supply windows it is possible to both 
reduce the heat loss from the windows and to preheat the inlet air.  
 
5.1 Energy performance 
Estimating the energy gain from the air supply windows it a very complex task and there is a lot of 
uncertainties towards the real performances, which is very sensitive to wind exposure.  
 
The overall energy performance of the current building meets the low energy standard in the 
building regulation of under 27 kWh/m2. The method I used comply somewhat with the result using 
the values from the AAU-model [5]. My result is a little bit lower than the AAU-model.  
 
A solution with mechanical ventilation and an exhaust heat pump also shows a result that meets the 
low energy standard.  
 
The performance of the solution with air supply windows in combination with the exhaust heat 
pump is a little uncertain because of the sensibility to wind exposure. So the solution is maybe not as 
robust as the solution with mechanical ventilation, but on the other hand it also have the possibility 
to enhance the energy savings given the right conditions.  
 
The method produced in this report for estimating the performance of the air supply windows 
showed realistic results. Results that is comparable with results from the AAU-model the window 
supplier is using for calculating energy gain. The values produced in this report  
 
5.2 Indoor environment 
The results of the indoor measurements show that the building has a good indoor environment with 
temperature and CO2 levels which can comply with best indoor environmental class I.  
 
There is a big difference between measured and simulated values even when taken the different 
external temperatures into account. The measurements show that the build house is more robust to 
high external temperatures and solar gain, than what is simulated. 
 
The simulations show that there are still possibilities of overheating in the rooms even though the 
simple tool for residential buildings Be18 can document to comply with the building regulation BR18.  
==PAGE:54==
 
 
Det Naturlige Hus 
54 
The measurement shows low values of temperature even though the period was a quite warm 
summer with outside temperature over 30 °C.  
 
Towards the indoor humidity the measurements show higher values than the simulated ones. The 
measured values are still within the acceptable level between 30 and 70 % RH with single peak up to 
80 %. In the simulations there is not much difference on the indoor humidity level between solution 
with a vapour barrier and solutions without a vapour barrier.  
 
Tracer gas measurements have been made to evaluate the ventilation rate. The results show that 
finished areas of the building can obtain a good air tightness. The ventilation rate when converted to 
the air tightness at under/over pressure of 50 Pa is 0,86 l/s m2. This comply with the demands 
towards tightness in the building regulation BR18 at 1,0 l/s m2.  
 
In relations to the air supply windows the tracer gas measurements show that the function towards 
ventilation rate of these is limited if the doors are closed. No change in the ventilation rate can be 
observed between open and closed valves when the doors are closed. It is thereby important to have 
focus on and to maintain the driven force of the window. Under normal circumstances approx. 7 l/s 
is coming through an air supply window with open doors and the heat pump running. If the doors are 
closed it is only approx. 0,9 l/s.  
 
The building has a ventilation rate of approx. 1,3 h-1 under the measured conditions. The house was a 
bit leaky though, because of a big part of the building was unfinished. The exhaust heat pump has a 
capacity of 0,56-1, so it must be assumed that the ventilation rate will be a bit smaller if the house 
was completely finished. There is a good ventilation rate higher than the demanded ventilation rate 
in the building regulation BR18 of 0,5 h-1. 
 
Measurement of ultra-fine particles UFP particles during cocking showed a particle count up to 
65.000 in the cocking area it spread to the nearest rooms up to a particle count of 18.000 in the 
bedrooms up to 9.300. This with a background particle count of approx. 5.000. The cocking niche did 
not have a cocking hood only exhaust ventilation in the ceiling over the cocking hood. The kitchen 
arrangement thereby works in reducing the spread but could work even better if an effective cocking 
hood was installed. PM2.5 Measurement in the kitchen showed up to 40 µg/m3 while cocking 
dropping to background level at 13 µg/m3 in approx. 1 hour. confirming the same process measure in 
particle count.  
 
TVOC measurement showed an average of approx. 300 ppb in the measured period of 7 days. This is 
well under recommended ceiling of 3.000 ppb and DGNB knockout criteria of 1.500 ppb. It has 
maybe problems to meet the recommended 8-hour average of 300 ppb in certain periods. It must be 
expected to drop further when the building is completely finished.  
 
In the measurements done in the building constructions the completion of the building envelope and 
heating system can be followed. In the start of 2018 high levels of moisture can be seen. Water 
content of higher than 20 % in the wooden components. But these levels drop to under 20 % for both 
the interior side and the exterior side. The exterior side still has single spikes over 20 % when 
exposed to wind and rain, but again they drop to under 20 % fairly fast within days.  
 
Two exterior walls with sensors VS02 and VS03 stood with unprotected wood fiber boards to the 
exterior side for approx. 1,5 year before getting the finishing exterior wood cladding. Wall with VS02 
unvented exterior wood cladding and wall with VS03 with wood cladding with a vented air cavity. 
==PAGE:55==
 
 
Det Naturlige Hus 
55 
Moisture content drops after the finishing cladding but clear difference between two solution cannot 
be confirmed.  
 
Looking at the mold index calculations most constructions have a low risk of mold growth and no 
construction exceed a mold index of 3.0 according to Ashrae 160 [11]. But especially two sensor 
points distance them selves from the others. Wallsensor VS01 on the SW corner of the building and 
Floor sensor GS02 over the vented crawl space have an increasing tendency.  
 
By wallsensor VS01 it is not completely certain why this tendency appears. It could be that the 
exterior mineral cement-based plaster is not tight enough or an unfinished rainwater downpipe is 
exposing the wall to extra rainwater. The plaster should be examined and is recommended to finish 
up the rainwater downpipe. 
 
In the crawl space under GS02 warm humid air in the summertime get in and condensates on the 
surfaces of the crawl space moisturizing the building components. It is suspected the ventilation rate 
in the crawl space is inadequate.  The crawl space should be inspected and cross ventilation is 
recommended established.  
 
5.3 Future work  
To estimate a more reliable performance of the air supply window it would be good to look at what 
the windows experience towards pressure differences under real conditions and how the driven 
force can be upheld.  
 
Given the uncertainties of the air supply windows and the sensibility to wind exposure it could be a 
gain to the robustness to establish electronic control of the valves. Then the windows could adjust to 
the wind pressure. Of course, this would also mean that the regulation of the air supply windows 
would get more complex and properly also more costly.    
 
Towards building sustainable like the concept of det Narturlige Hus the airtightness of the building is 
an important factor to account for. In fact, I did a blowerdoor test of a single room during the thesis, 
unfortunately it turned out to faulty so I could not use the results.  
 
When buildings are built with a ventilated crawl space it is important to ensure the balance between 
heat transfer and moisture transfer. It would be interesting to measure the ventilations rate of the 
crawl space and to analyze how to make the crawl space resilient. There are some positive qualities 
in building with a ventilated crawl space. Of course, as showed in the results of this report, there are 
also some challenges. On the positive side thermal bridges can be reduced to a minimum.  
 
Building with natural hygroscopic building materials can lower the environmental impact but there is 
a need for more cases of these experimental building cases and measurements in building envelope 
to make guidelines and to ensure robust building concepts.

i huset kan etableres om sommeren.

Gældende for tilbudsgivningen: 
Alle elementer er som udgangspunkt tilbudt i vores standardudførelse, hvorfor specifikationerne kan afvige fra 
udbudsmaterialet. Der tages forbehold for, at antallet af elementer med og uden ventilation er opgjort ud fra det af 
kunden fremsendte skitse- og tegningsmateriale. Vi tager derfor ikke ansvar for, om løsningen opfylder BR eller andre 
krav til bygningen. 
Undgå ærgrelser: Tjek tilbuddet nøje for utilsigtede afvigelser vedrørende mål, antal, noter, profiltype, farve, 
hængsling, glas, flugtning, tilbehør etc. og få dem rettet, så prisen er retvisende. Horn Group er uden ansvar for 
optælling af elementer i tilbud/ordre. 
Opstalttegninger viser det enkelte element udefra. Ruder i ventilationselementer er alle med 6mm enkelt lags floatglas 
indvendigt og 2-lags energiglas og ”varm” afstandsprofil yderst. Standardelementer tilbydes oftest med 3-lags 
energiglas for optimal komfort og bedre energimæssige egenskaber. Undtagelser vil være begrundet i 
konstruktionsmæssige forhold. 
Til fyrretræselementer anvendes fingerskåret træ, der udmærker sig ved, bl.a. at være helt fri for knaster. 
Kulørvariationer, som følge af harpiksudtræk, reduceres betragteligt. Til gengæld må overfladebehandling med 
heldækkende malingfarve anbefales, da stor variation i farve og åretegning præger de sammenlimede træstykker. 
Om elementerne monteret overholder Bygningsreglementet er ikke Horn Group’s ansvar. Vi kan kun opfordre til, at 
man inden ordreafgivelse aktivt forholder sig til krav om f.eks. sikkerhedsglas, redningsåbninger og ventilation. 
Gældende for levering: 
Levering sker med forvogn/hænger med løftebagsmæk, køber skal derfor sikre at der er fast køre underlag, såsom 
fliser, asfalt eller køreplader der muliggør kørsel og aflæsning med almindelig palleløfter. Ekstraomkostninger til 
eventuel kran/truck bæres alene af kunden, men kan undgås, hvis der er tilstrækkelig med hjælp til aflæsningen i 
form af maskiner, mandskab o. lign.  
Gældende for montering: 
Alle leverede elementer skal monteres med karmskruer med hoved, som skrues gennem egnet opklodsning således at 
sidekarme presses mod opklodsning og murværk, for herved at øge karmens styrke. Læs endvidere vor Monterings-og 
Brugervejledning. 
Ved bekræftelse for produktion: 
Efter accept af tilbud bedes kunden fremsende tilfredsstillende sikkerhedsstillelse eller aftale om 50% forudbetaling 
som betales efter faktura (fremsendes særskilt), inden ordrebekræftelse udarbejdes. Endelig leveringstidspunkt kan 
først oplyses efter at alle detaljer på ordrebekræftelsen er godkendt af begge parter. 
Leverancen er eksklusiv montage og leveringsomkostninger, med mindre andet tydeligt fremtår. 
Produkter uden garanti: 
Elementer der er højere eller bredere end maksimummål oplyst i medsendte monterings- og brugervejledning.  
Døre med 1-punkt lås. Døre med el-slutblik. Døre der efterfølgende monteres med dørpumpe/automatisk dørlukke. 
Indadgående døre leveres ligeledes uden garanti for tæthed mod vandindtrængen.  
Hvordan fungere et Horn Ventilationsvindue® (principielt): 
Et Horn Ventilationsvindue tilføre boligen en kontrolleret forvarmet luft/energi, styret af Horn Groups patenterede 
termostatiske reguleringsventil som er integreret i vinduets karm eller ramme. Ude-luft ledes ind nederst i vinduet og 
op mellem den udvendige 2-lags termorude og en indvendig 6 mm forsatsrude. På vej op til vinduets top opvarmes 
ude-luften fra varmetabet indefra/fra solen udefra. I overkarmen leder den termostatiske ventil, den nu forvarmede, 
luft ind i boligen. På rigtigt varme dage drejer ventilen rundt, så ude-luften ledes ud igen, imens en bypass-ventil leder 
tempereret ude-luft direkte ind i boligen.  
Konceptet tilbydes i 3 niveauer: 
1.
Horn Ventilationsvinduer anvendt i eksisterende bolig/lejlighed uden permanent udsug. Denne løsning
giver et rigtig godt indeklima med naturlig ventilation og øget ventilation ved brug af emfang/udsugning i
baderum. Det er en forudsætning at der er undertryk i bygningen for opnåelse af effekt.
2.
Horn Ventilationsvinduer anvendt i eksisterende bolig/lejlighed med permanent udsug fra f.eks.
badeværelse og køkken. Denne løsning kan give et konstant undertryk i beboelsen og sikrer luftskiftet
døgnet rundt og giver et sundt og tørt indeklima med lavt energiforbrug.
3.
Horn Ventilationsvinduer anvendt i nybyggeri/total renovering af boliger/skoler/institutioner mv. sammen
med installation af en varmepumpe. Med denne løsning suger varmepumpen den forvarmede ude-luft fra
ventilationsvinduerne via aftrækskanaler i f.eks køkken, bad og evt. bryggers, således at alle rum
ventileres kontrolleret samtidig med at varmepumpen kan generere energi til opvarmning og/eller til
varmt brugsvand.

Beskrivelse 
EX serien er en komplet varmepumpe unit, komplet 
med automatik og styring beregnet for indendørs 
opstilling til ventilation, opvarmning og varmt 
brugsvand.  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Anvendelse 
Udsugningsvarmepumpen udnytter energien i den 
varme ventilationsluft som suges ud fra vådrummene, 
bad, bryggers køkken mm. 
Ved at tage mere varme ud af udsugningsluften end der 
skal bruges til at genopvarme frisk erstatningsluft, fås 
varme til også at opvarme boligen og til at producere 
varmt brugsvand. 
Varmepumpen sørger derfor både for ventilation, 
opvarmning og varmt brugsvand. 
Opbygning 
EX serien er på alle synlige udvendige flader opbygget 
af hvidlakerede stålplader, der sikrer en livslang 
holdbarhed og et ens blivende udseende. 
Beholderen indeholder 210 liter brugsvand som 
opvarmes af varmepumpen.  
Varmepumpen har indbygget backup varmelegeme 
som anvendes hvis varmepumpens ydelse ikke er 
tilstrækkelig, f.eks. ved stort vandforbrug eller en kold 
vinterdag. Varmelegemet bruges desuden i forbindelse 
med desinfektion af tanken. 
Øverst er placeret selve varmepumpemodulet, som ved 
evt. defekt kan demonteres for reparation. 
Nederst er placeret tilslutninger til centralvarmen, 
ventiler, styring mm. så alt er samlet på et sted. 
Varmepumpemodulet leveres tilslutningsklar komplet 
med trykekspansionsbeholder indbygget. 
  
Styring 
Den indbyggede styring passer er intuitiv og nem at 
betjene med dreje/trykknap. 
Styringen kan indstilles individuelt til mange forskellige 
behov og driftsformer. 
Fjernbetjening 
Som tilbehør fås en app styring Online så der er mulighed 
for at styre og kontrollere varmepumpen fra de fleste 
mobil telefon styresystemer. Kan f.eks. benyttes i 
forbindelse med sommerhus. 
 
 
Behovsstyret kompressor 
Varmepumpen tilpasser sig automatisk til det aktuelle 
behov. Ved f.eks. styring efter udetemperaturen, sørger 
varmepumpen for at levere en temperatur til gulvvarmen 
som er afstemt efter behovet i huset i forhold til hvor koldt 
det er ude.

EX35L/50L/65L 
Beskrivelse 
EX L serien er en komplet varmepumpe unit, komplet 
med automatik og styring beregnet for indendørs 
opstilling til ventilation, opvarmning og varmt 
brugsvand. Denne modelserie har ikke indbygget 
brugsvandstank, men denne opstilles separat. Det giver 
også mulighed for større tanke, samt evt. tilslutning af 
solfanger mm. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Anvendelse 
Udsugningsvarmepumpen udnytter energien i den 
varme ventilationsluft som suges ud fra vådrummene, 
bad, bryggers køkken mm. 
Ved at tage mere varme ud af udsugningsluften end der 
skal bruges til at genopvarme frisk erstatningsluft, fås 
varme til også at opvarme boligen og til at producere 
varmt brugsvand. 
Varmepumpen sørger derfor både for ventilation, 
opvarmning og varmt brugsvand. 
Opbygning 
EX L serien er på alle synlige udvendige flader 
opbygget af hvidlakerede stålplader, der sikrer en 
livslang holdbarhed og et ens blivende udseende. 
Varmepumpen har indbygget backup varmelegeme 
som anvendes hvis varmepumpens ydelse ikke er 
tilstrækkelig, f.eks. ved stort vandforbrug eller en kold 
vinterdag. Varmelegemet bruges desuden i forbindelse 
med desinfektion af tanken. 
Øverst er placeret selve varmepumpemodulet, som ved 
evt. defekt kan demonteres for reparation. 
Nederst er placeret tilslutninger til centralvarmen, 
ventiler, styring mm.  
Vandtanken, f.eks. Tida placeres et andet sted, typisk 
ved siden af. 
Varmepumpemodulet leveres tilslutningsklar komplet 
med trykekspansionsbeholder indbygget. 
  
Styring 
Den indbyggede styring passer er intuitiv og nem at 
betjene med dreje/trykknap. 
Styringen kan indstilles individuelt til mange forskellige 
behov og driftsformer. 
Fjernbetjening 
Som tilbehør fås en app styring Online så der er mulighed 
for at styre og kontrollere varmepumpen fra de fleste 
mobil telefon styresystemer. Kan f.eks. benyttes i 
forbindelse med sommerhus. 
 
 
Behovsstyret kompressor 
Varmepumpen tilpasser sig automatisk til det aktuelle 
behov. Ved f.eks. styring efter udetemperaturen, sørger 
varmepumpen for at levere en temperatur til gulvvarmen 
som er afstemt efter behovet i huset i forhold til hvor koldt 
det er ude.

varmtvandsbeholderen. Styringen vil så bringe

at beholderen er desinficeret.

lavenergi EC ventilator, vandvarmeflade, grov- og

selvlukkende frostbeskyttelses spjæld.

klasse F7 og opvarmes til passende 
indblæsningstemperatur.

tilgængelig bag lågen.

fordele varme fra rum med overskudsvarme, som f.eks.

2  Beregning  
 
Vandprocent 
Målinger i væggen måler temperatur °C og relativ luft fugtighed % RH. For at omsætte at omsætte 
den relative luftfugtighed til vandprocent i træ benyttes sorptionskurver. Sorptionskurver og omreg-
ningsformel 
 
 
Figure 5: wood shaving materialedata fra WUFI. Formel2 beregning ud fra formel. Værdier ved 0 og 50 % RH er sammenfal-
dende 
 
Formel er udviklet ud fra Sorption Isotherms – A catalogue, Kielsgaard Hansen, Kurt, 1986.  
 
U = 44*EKSP((-1/2,2)*LN(1-LN(RH/100)/0,017)) 
  
Mold index 
Mold index beregnes efter metode beskrevet i Ashrae 160. Mold index er mest relevant at beregne 
i konstruktionsdele hvor der er relative høje fugtniveaer. Derfor er mold index kun beregnet for de 
ydre lag i klimaskærmen. For ydervægssensorerne VS01, VS02, VS03 og VS04 er dette sensor i 
yderste træfiberplade. For sensorerne i gulv og loftskonstruktion sidder yderste sensor i træfiber-
isoleringen.  
I Ashrae 160 modellen er skimmelvækt muligt ved relative luft fugtigheder over 80 %, afhængig af 
temperatur og varigheden af forholdene hvor skimmelvækst er muligt. Skimmelvækst topper ved 
temperaturer omkring 20 – 30 °C og går i davle under 5 °C.  
Et mold index på 3 svarer til synligt skimmel på overfladen med et areal på under 10 % heraf.  
Materialer inddeles iht. Ashrae 160 i følsomhedsklasser: very sensitive, sensitive, medium resistant 
og resistant. Ubehandlet træ ligger i klasse sensitive og glas ligger i resistant.  
Materialerne træfiberisolering og træfiber plader er vurderet til at have kategori sensitive.
