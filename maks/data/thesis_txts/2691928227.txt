Abstract
Face age progression was a newly discussed topic in recent years, which can be used in
entertainment purpose photography effect. It can even be contributed to the social service
department such as assist police to simulate the future appearances of a wanted criminal
or help to find lost children. With the success of the deep learningôÙbased method, the
face generated task achieved impressive performance through Generative Adversarial
Networks. Until now, many stateôÙofôÙtheôÙart approaches had successfully produced high
resolution face images with age effects. The contribution of this thesis is the development
of a face age progression model based on an encoderôÙdecoder architecture, training with
a high resolution database with 70000 images. Compared to the baseline model, the
identity preservation, visual fidelity remains as much as possible but improved the age
accuracy extremely.

Introduction
Nowadays, biometric systems play an important role due to the uniqueness of the bioôÙ
metric characteristic and the high convenience and acceptance of the subjects. Face
recognition based technology is already widely used all over the world, like China railway
transportation system[1] and the European EntryôÙExit System[2]. However, the facial atôÙ
tributes will change more or less with age change. Existing face age progression systems
can be fineôÙtuned with artificial age progressed mated samples to be more robust against
intraôÙidentity variations caused by ageing effects. Therefore, face age progression is an
important topic in the biometric field and is frequently used for crossôÙage face recognition
or finding the lost children.
With the development of Deep Learning [3], neural networks are able to reach human
level performance in many fields of computer vision tasks[4][5]. In this context, ConvoluôÙ
tional Neural Networks(CNN) are particularly successful in solving the image recognition
problems. However, traditional CNNs are mainly used for image classification task but
not for image generation. In 2014, [6] proposed Generative Adversarial Nets, by pitting
the generator against the discriminator in order to generate new, synthetic data samples
similar to those included in the training dataset. Therefore, GANôÙbased models are widely
used for imageôÙtoôÙimage translation where the goal is to learn the mapping between an
input image and an output image. For the face age progression topic, although the face
image can be generated by the GAN model, there still existed problems. The age of the
generated face image was out of control so that the age accuracy could not be preserved.
Afterwards, the author of [7] further proposed the Conditional Generative Adversarial Nets
which extends both the generator and discriminator by conditioning them with extra labels
in order to gain control over the generation module. Therefore, this method is widely used
in face age progression including age labels to gain control over the target age of the ageôÙ
synthesis. Antipov et al.[8] was among the first to apply Conditional GANs for face age
progression. In general, face age progression methods focus on achieving three goals
at the same time: the identity preservation preserves the identity information for the
synthesized faces, age accuracy guarantees the generated faces fall into the target age
group, visual fidelity represents the quality of the generated face image.
In the work of Yao et al. [9], an encoderôÙdecoder architecture is presented that creates a
latent space containing the face identity, and a feature modulation layer corresponding to
the age of the individual. Then, these two elements are combined to produce an output
image of an individual with a desired target age. In addition, Xiang Li et al.[10] generalizes
the standard GAN by treating realness as a random variable instead of a single scalar by
feeding the generator with more information so that it can generate more realistic images.
Following up on this idea, this thesis evaluates a new method that improves the FAP
architecture given by Yao et al.[9] by treating realness as a random variable, as introduced
by Xiangli et al.[10] in order to further improve the face age progression performance.
The core idea is to adopt the discriminator from the fakeôÙreal(0,1) mode to a realness
distribution.

2.1
face age progression
Face age progression is the process of aesthetically rendering a given face image to
present the effects of aging. Face aging occurs mainly as the result of skin changes
and changes in the deeper soft tissues of the face. Factors that influence external skin
changes include natural aging, skin type, sun exposure, facial movement, and routine
skin care. To simulate the facial effect automatically by a computer application, it requires
the face age model has the ability to generalize the effects of aging smoothly according
to the natural evolution.

Convolutional neural network(CNN) is a class of artificial neural networks that has become

3.2.1

The convolutional layer is the core building block of a CNN, and it is where the majority of
computation occurs. A convolution is a linear operation that involves the multiplication of a

contains a set of filters whose parameters need to be learned, the height and weight of the
filters are smaller than those of the input tensor. In the convolutional operation, the filter
slides across the width and height of the input, applying an elementwise multiplication.
As a result, it will be summing up the results into a single output pixel in the feature map.

3.3.2
Patch GAN
As described in above sections, the traditional GANãs discriminator is responsible for idenôÙ
tifying whether an generated image is real or fake. Meanwhile, the output of the discrimôÙ
inator should be a single scalar [0,1], indicating whether the image is real or generated.
However, considering the the complexity of information contained in an image, one single
scalar from the discriminator may be not enough to provide the comprehensive guidance
to the generator. Therefore, a new discriminator architecture (PatchGAN) was proposed
by the author of [17]. Compared to the original GAN, PatchGAN effectively models the
image as a Markov random field[18], assuming independence between pixels separated
by more than a patch diameter. Instead of outputting a single probability of the entire
image to be real or fake, the PatchGANãs discriminator will output a N û N array. For each
element Xij in the array, represent for one region in the original image to be real or fake.
Then taking an average of those elements as the overall realness of the whole image.
Through evaluating each local region independently, the discriminator is able to focus on
more local texture details which provided more guidance to the generator. The example
of the architecture is shown in Figure 3.7:

4.1
GANôÙbased method
Since the Deep learning[3] based method made impressive progress on the image proôÙ
cessing field. Specially, Generative Adversarial Network[6] was proposed to deal with
the image generation tasks. By pitting the generator against the discriminator in order to
generate new, synthetic data samples similar to those included in the training data set.
Given a noise vector z which is sampled from a normal distribution or a uniform distribuôÙ
tion pz(z), , the generator maps z to a synthesized image ùs. The discriminator takes either
xù or x from the real data set as input and tries to distinguish whether the ùs is real or not.
The generator is trained to fool the discriminator be unable to discriminate them.
However, the GAN model were suffer from strong instability of the training process. To
facilitate this flaw, Radford et al. [25] proposed a Deep Convolutional GANs (DCGANs)
framework, afterwards, [26][27] successively improve the training stability by optimizing
the objective of GAN. In [7], a Conditional GANs(CGANs) model extends both the genôÙ
erator and discriminator by conditioning them with extra labels in order to gain control
over the generation module, which make it is possible to direct the data generation proôÙ
cess. Furthermore, plenty of advanced methods were proposed, such as CycleGANs
[28], styleGANS [29][30] which impressive results in manipulating facial attributes.
4.2
Discriminator
As aforementioned, discriminator is the core component of GANôÙbased model. In traôÙ
ditional GAN, the discriminator is simply a classifier use the 3 linear layers to output a
single scaler that represent for the real instance or not. Then in [25], instead of using fully
connection layers in the discriminator, applying deep convolutional layers with spectral
normalizations[31] and leakyrelu activation functions, which maps the latent space to a
realness representation. Afterwards, in [32][17], a Markovian discriminator was proposed
that only penalizes structure at the scale of image patches. Meanwhile, the discriminator
would output a NxN vector X, where each Xij in the output signifies whether the patch ij in
the image is real or fake. The discriminator above, a single scalar is constantly adopted to
represent the concept of realness. However, XiangLi et al.[10] provided a new perspecôÙ
tive that expanding the scalar realness score into a distributional form prealness, so that the
discriminator D naturally provides stronger guidance to the generator G. This approach
can be treat as a generalization of the standard formulation from traditional GAN[6] model.
4.3
Style transfer
In the early time, style transfer can be categorized as a image stylization problem, a topic
studied for over two decades within the field of nonôÙphotorealistic rendering. Ashikhmin
[33] presented a competitive method based on the coherent synthesis algorithm he proôÙ
posed earlier. The texture transfer algorithm between images that is up to several orders
of magnitude faster than other stateôÙofôÙtheôÙart techniques at that time. The core idea is
methods based on texture transfer are global in the sense that the user need not deal
with details such as defining and painting individual brush strokes. Afterwards, with the
growth of deep learning[3] based method, some techniques that employ neural networks
to separate and recombine the content and style of arbitrary inputs are appeared. Gatys
et al. [21] first introduced the concept of neural style transfer (NST). He exploited VGGôÙ19
network which was preôÙtrained on ImageNet database, then compute style loss with Gram

4.4
Face age progression
Traditional FAP methods can be categorised into physicalôÙmodel based and prototypeôÙ
based approaches. More precisely, for physicalôÙmodel based methods, Ramanathan et
al. [35] first develop a shape transformation model that is formulated as a physicallyôÙ
based parametric muscle model that captures the subtle deformations facial features unôÙ
dergo with age. Then using an image gradient based texture transformation function that
characterizes facial wrinkles and other skin artifacts often observed during different ages.
For prototypeôÙbased approaches, Rowland and Perret et al.[36] proposed a technique for
defining facial prototypes is described which supports transformations along quantifiable
dimensions inã face spaceã. Age prototypes are generated by averaging faces belonging
to the same age group. Age progression is achieved by adding on a given face image,
differences between age prototypes corresponding to different ages. However, those
traditional methods are suffering the high computational cost and low generalisation caôÙ
pability.
Recent years, with the emergence of deep generative methods, generative adversarial
networks(GAN) led to a new research direction. GANôÙbased approaches can be divided
into three classes: translationôÙbased, sequenceôÙbased, and conditionôÙbased. TranslationôÙ
based methods aims to learn the mapping between an input image and an output image
using a training set of aligned image pairs. CycleôÙGAN[28] was a typical approach that
captures the styleôÙbased characteristics of an image collection and translates these charôÙ
acteristics to another collection of images. Unlike translationôÙbased method, sequenceôÙ
based methods like [37][38] using multiple networks trained separately for the translation
between adjacent age groups. Each of the trained models is then concatenated in a recurôÙ
sive way to form a single FAP framework, where the aged output of the iôÙth model defines
the input of the (i + 1)ôÙ th model. ConditionôÙbased methods are inspired by Condition genôÙ
erative adversarial network(cGAN), which extends both the generator and discriminator
by conditioning them with extra labels inorder to gain control over the generation module.
Afterwards, plenty of methods like [39][40] further preserve the identity information by a
Local Manifold Adaptation approach. Then the author of[24] proposed an autoôÙencoder
conditional GANs which encodes the input image to a manifold and then reconstructs
aged images. And Wang et al. [23] constructed oneôÙhotôÙencoded tensors directly conôÙ
catenated with the input image. Those methods were all achieved high performance on
the FAP task.

To guarantee training stability, R1 Regularization[53] method is applied to penalize the
discriminator from deviating from the Nash Equilibrium via penalizing the gradient on real
data only: when the generator distribution produces the true data distribution and the
discriminator is equal to 0 on the data manifold, the gradient penalty ensures that the
discriminator cannot create a nonôÙzero gradient orthogonal to the data manifold without
suffering a loss in the adversarial optimization. The equation is defined as:

where R1 is simply the norm of the gradients, which indicates how fast the weights are upôÙ

the real distribution and ö° is a hyperparameter. Gradient regularization penalizes large
changes in the output of some neural network layer. Different hyperparameter choices for
ö° were tested in this work, where ö°=10 has proven to achieve a good FAP performance
while eliminating colorful noises in the generated images.

Limitation and Future work
This section briefly introduces the limitation and disadvantage of the proposed method
and discusses future work directions.
8.1
Data augmentation
As aforementioned, the training dataset is based on the FFHQ [29], which contains 70000
unlabeled high resolution face images. And then the age label is created by an age clasôÙ
sifier pretrained on IMDBôÙWIKI[45] dataset. However, the author of [9] observed the that
FFHQ contains much more samples of young faces than of old ones. Meanwhile, during
the training, it generated an older face based on a younger face is happened more freôÙ
quently than generating a younger face. In another word, the age transformer would be
trained to perform aging much more often than deôÙaging, failing to yield satisfying deôÙaging
results. One possible way that the author of [9] did is applying a data augmentation using
StyleGAN[29] to generate a large amount of synthetic images. Then they filled the quaôÙ
siuniform age distribution with those data and finally produced an ageôÙbalanced dataset
containing 47990 images in the range {20...69}. Since the training period for each task is
fairly long(72 hours), there is no enough time to perform this data augmentation method
to improve the training equilibrium. In the future, it will be an interesting and necessary
step to further improve the model performance.
8.2
Potential network modification
8.2.1
Conditional discriminator
There still exist some experiments on the architectures that is worth to test. For instance,
currently the discriminator did not take the age label injected in the middle layer. MeanôÙ
while, the age information is only depends on the age classifier. This leads to a problem
that the aging and deôÙaging effects are obtained solely, which the aging information may
be not enough. As mentioned in previous sections, the age accuracy is not good enough,
although this work already improved the age result than the baseline method. Therefore,
in the future, a projection discriminator[55] that injecting the age label on the features afôÙ
ter the last convolutional layer could be applied. And dividing the ages into several age
groups such as 20ôÙ35, 35ôÙ45, 45ôÙ55, 55ôÙ70. This setting should be able to provided more
age information than relying on a age classifer solely.

Conclusion
This thesis aim to propose a new discriminator combined with the idea of realness disôÙ
tribution and patchGAN, and apply this discriminator on an encodeôÙdecoder based face
age progression method training on the dataset FFHQ. The discriminator is able to proôÙ
vide more guidance than a single scalar version distribution based loss function. The
proposed model is wellôÙfitted to the high resolution face image generation tasks. Then
by adopting the experiments based on different hyperparameter ãÎsettings, it can be
observed that with the increment of the ãÎ, the model is more competitive to generated
a wellôÙperformance image according to the target age. Furthermore, the regularization
technique were introduced to stabilize the GAN training process in order to improve the
local convergence. The colorful artifacts are suppressed through the R1 regularization[53]
which penalize the discriminator from not going further on the real data distribution.
Compared to the baseline model, the proposed model achieved improvement on the age
accuracy factor. The average age of the generated face image is improved from 30.261
to 25.337 at target age 25, from 41.044 to 48.111 at target age 65. Even through the
other factors visual fidelity and identity preservation is dropped down, The analysis of the
performances indicate the overall performance still enhance the drawback of the baseline
method at a high level.
Lastly, the linearôÙbased model was also tested even though the result is not as good as
another proposed model and baseline especially at the age accuracy term.
The last part of the work is to provide a future work perspective that through the data
augmentation to balance the dataset, adding condition label on the discriminator, or even
adopting the parallel discriminators to further improve the performance. Especially point to
the disadvantage which is the identity preservation, an addition faceôÙrecognition module is
suggested in the future. Lastly, a problem existing in this kind of architecture is introduced,
which related to the children face progression. The current styleôÙtransfer based method
is not a proper way to achieve a children based face editing task. In the future, maybe
there existing another way to achieve this kind of task.
All in all, the objective of this work has been achieved, the proposed model is proven to
be highly competitive with the baseline model and even exceed the baseline on the age
accuracy. In the future, it is believable to further improve the performance under more
proper training setting.
