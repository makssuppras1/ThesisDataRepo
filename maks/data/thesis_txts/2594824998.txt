Abstract
The wind turbine noise is considered to be the main barrier in the acceptance of wind as an
alternative source of energy. A large amount of the wind penetration comes from onshore
wind turbines, which makes the acceptance from the major stakeholders such as residents,
farmers, land owners or environmental associations highly important.
Due to the results of previous studies regarding the eﬀect of wind turbine noise on humans,
regulations have been established in order to reduce this negative impact. However, the com-
pliance with these regulations proves to aﬀect the energy production from the wind farms
by limiting the number of turbines allowed to be installed onshore.
This Master’s thesis intends to optimize a wind farm layout, by using wind farm topology
optimization tools. An optimization problem will be formulated where the main goal is to
reduce the noise impact without compromising the energy production. The only way to
improve a major investment on new onshore wind farm projects is to ensure their economic
viability along with their full respect for the safety and good health of the population in-
volved.

1.
INTRODUCTION
1.
Introduction
1.1.
Motivation
Wind power has been increasing in the past years. It is considered to be the most cost-
eﬀective way to produce energy from renewable sources. The total installed capacity of wind
turbines all over the world has reached 650 GW by the end of 2019, of which 59.6 GW were
added only in the year of 2019. This was considerably more than in 2018 when 50.2 GW
have started operating. [26]
The fast growth of wind energy has led to an increase in the number of complaints regarding
this alternative source of energy. The threat to wildlife such as birds and bats, the impact in
ﬂora and fauna and the visual pollution represent some of the consequences of using wind as
an energy source. However, the main complaint lies on the noise generated by wind turbines,
since it aﬀects directly the population. Depending on the wind direction, the turbines may
be heard within a distance of hundreds of meters, which can damage in several ways the
human health. [27]
Based on these facts, the noise pollution generated by wind turbines has become the main
obstacle in the further development of wind farm projects. Therefore, the motivation of this
thesis focuses on mitigating the noise impact in order to make wind power more acceptable
by the public. If this happens, it will be easier to increase the wind penetration into the
electrical grid, reducing therefore the dependence on fossil fuels.
1.2.
Project goal
The aim of this thesis is to optimize a wind farm layout where noise is included as a con-
straint. Noise may be treated as an objective function or as a constraint and it was decided
that case studies would be performed for both situations. The goal is to minimize the noise
and maximize the annual energy production of a wind farm when both parameters are de-
ﬁned as objective functions. If noise is deﬁned as a constraint, it is necessary to select a
threshold, which should be based on the Danish noise regulations. The wind farm is then
optimized in order to maximize the annual energy production, but always respecting the
selected noise limit and other constraints.
Comparing with previous studies on wind farm layout optimization, the innovative part of
this project is the noise model developed to calculate the sound pressure level at each receiver
position emitted by one or multiple sources. After being completed, the new noise model
is incorporated with an already existent layout optimization framework, which is used to
perform all the necessary case studies to achieve the goal of this thesis.

2.
THEORETICAL BACKGROUND
2.
Theoretical background
2.1.
Noise
2.1.1.
Noise pollution and its consequences
Noise pollution can be deﬁned as an usual exposure to highly sound levels that can have
a negative impact in humans and other living organisms. The World Health Organization
states that sound levels inferior to 70 dB are considered to be harmless, no matter how long
is the exposure to the noise. If a living organism is exposed to a constant noise superior to
85 dB for more than 8 hours, then it can be harmful. [1]
Hearing is an essential process for communication and human survival. It is not possible
to turn oﬀthe noise and its exposure may have several eﬀects. Noise can be considered
a psychological and physiological stressor, as it can trigger oﬀa classic stress response by
activating the autonomic nervous system and the endocrine system, conducting to a chain of
eﬀects, which includes increasing in heart rate, blood pressure and levels of stress hormones.
This stress response is understood to be a physiologic adaptation to stress, which may result
in pathophysiologic changes if the exposure is extreme. [2]
Noise pollution may be dangerous to our health in several diﬀerent ways. It is listed below
some of the consequences from the exposition to noise pollution.
• Hypertension: Noise pollution causes high blood pressure levels during an extended
period of time.
• Hearing loss: The exposure to noise pollution is directly related to hearing loss.
• Sleep disturbances: It is usually a consequence of constant air or land traﬃc noise
during night. It can have a serious negative impact on the daily performance and be
the cause of several diseases.
• Child development: Children are usually more responsive to noise pollution. Some
diseases related to noise pollution aﬀect children and it goes from hearing loss to
psychological and physical disorders. [1]
Nowadays, noise pollution also has a signiﬁcant impact in wildlife and marine life. Due to
the large amount of machinery present in the oceans such as oil drills, sonars and seismic
survey devices, marine animals like whales get aﬀected, since hearing is essential for them
to communicate, to feed and orientation. [1]

cardiovascular diseases. This will cost money to the healthcare system, reduces the person’s

Noise pollution from wind turbines
A wind turbine is usually placed at a minimum distance of 300 meters from houses. The
sound pressure level of the turbine at this distance is around 43 decibels. This value will
decrease to 38 decibels at a distance of 500 meters. The background noise usually goes from
40 to 45 decibels in most of the areas. This means that the sound coming from a wind
turbine wouldn’t be noticed within this range of background noise. In more rural areas, the
background noise is around 30 decibels, which means that if a turbine is placed at a distance
around a mile, it won’t be heard [3]. Figure 1 taken from reference [3] shows how the noise
varies with the distance from a wind turbine.

quite characteristic and it is primarily generated by the movement of the blades. A swish-

turbine machinery can also be quite unwelcome due to its tonal character. [4]
There is signiﬁcant evidence to set a relation between the noise from wind turbines and

2.1.2.1.
Inﬂow turbulence noise mechanism
The inﬂow-turbulence noise happens when the natural atmospheric turbulence meets the
blade and induces a broadband noise radiation. The noise is expected to be quite responsive
to the properties of the turbulence. [5]
2.1.2.2.
Trailing edge noise
Trailing edge noise is considered to be the dominant noise source in wind turbines and its
maximum frequency is in a range of 500-1500 Hz. This type of noise source happens when
the turbulent boundary layer meets the airfoil sharp trailing edge. In accordance with the
Oerlemans acoustic ﬁeld measurements, the trailing edge noise is more relevant close to the
tip region where the ﬂow velocity is higher. [5]
2.1.2.3.
Tip noise
The formation of the tip vortex comes from a cross ﬂow induced by the pressure diﬀerence
between the pressure side and the suction side. A tip noise is generated when the tip vortex
meets the tip side and the trailing edge, which is the same noise mechanism used for the
trailing edge noise. Usually, this noise takes place in the high frequency zone and it is con-
sidered to be the dominant source within this range. As humans are more responsive to the
frequency range of 1-4 kHz, the tip noise is considered to be a signiﬁcant contributor to the
discomfort caused by the noise from wind turbines. [5]
2.1.2.4.
Separated/Stalled ﬂow noise
The separated/stalled ﬂow noise occurs from a speciﬁc angle of attack. The blade stalls leads
to a large scale ﬂow separation and the instability of the stalled ﬂow results in a broadband
noise emission. It can be avoided by reducing the stale conditions at the blade. [5]
2.1.2.5.
Blunt-trailing-edge
Blunt-trailing-edge is what causes the Von Karman type vortices. This leads to a tonal noise
emission and it is considered to be the culminate in the usual wind turbine noise spectrum.
There is a dependency of this type of noise on the shape of the trailing edge. Usually, the
blunt trailing edge noise may be deleted with a sharp trailing edge. [5]
2.1.2.6.
Laminar-Boundary-Layer-Vortex-Shedding noise
When the Reynolds number is inferior to around 106, the ﬂow on the sides of the airfoil will
continue laminar up to the trailing edge. In this situation, inconsistencies in the boundary
layer will probably occur and this will lead to elevated tonal noise levels from the blades,

far upstream of the trailing edge is one of the main ways to avoid this kind of noise. [5]
2.1.3.
Wind turbine noise regulations
Noise limits are established for both weak and strong wind speeds. The Statutory Order on
wind turbines is the reference followed and the noise limits should apply to the total noise
coming from all wind turbines. The fact that noise limits are respected, doesn’t mean that
the noise is imperceptible. It simply means that the limits have been set up to make sure
that there is no relevant perturbation. [4]
The section 3 of the Statutory Order on noise from wind turbines states that the noise impact
cannot exceed the limits speciﬁed below:
• Dwellings, summer cottages, etc: 39 dB(A) for a wind speed of 8 m/s and 37 dB(A)
for a wind speed of 6 m/s. [6]
• Dwellings in open country: 44 dB(A) for a wind speed of 8 m/s and 42 dB(A) for a
wind speed of 6 m/s. [6]
The limit for low frequency noise is considered to be 20 dB(A) for the categories mentioned
above. This applies to the indoor noise level at 6 and 8 m/s. [4]
The noise from wind turbines is usually at the low frequency region in areas where the
population is exposed to the noise at their houses. There will be a transmission loss from
outdoor to indoor within the range of low frequencies between 10 and 160 Hz, due to the
soundprooﬁng and sound propagation attenuation. That is why indoor wind turbine noise
is known as low frequency noise.
In accordance with section 8 of the Statutory Order, noise measurements may be carried out
by the owner of the wind turbines. The municipal council can order this in the following
situations:
• When a notiﬁed wind turbine starts operating. [6]
• In accordance with the general statutory control. [6]
• In accordance with complaints about the noise, whenever the municipal council feels
that it is necessary. [6]

Optimization algorithms
Optimization is considered to be a collection of mathematical models which are used to solve
quantitative problems in several areas [14]. An optimization problem is usually composed
by the following three main elements:

• Objective function.
• Collection of variables.
• Set of constraints.
The goal is to maximize or minimize the objective function. The variables are quantities
that can be changed in order to optimize the objective function. The set of constraints are
restrictions on the quantities that the variables can assume. [14]
A wind farm layout optimization aims to optimize the wind turbine positions inside a wind

2.2.1.
Genetic algorithms
The purpose of a genetic algorithm is to solve both constrained and non-constrained op-
timization problems.
It is based on a natural selection process that simulates biological
development. The algorithm changes iteratively a group of individual solutions. At each
phase, it randomly chooses individuals from the present population and these are used to
create the next generation. Through the successive generations, the population goes towards
an optimal solution. [15]
A genetic algorithm is composed by several elements. This is considered to be a strength
since the standard elements may be re-used in diﬀerent genetic algorithms with an easier
implementation. The main elements of a genetic algorithm are stated below: [16]
• Chromosome encoding.
• Fitness function.
• Selection.
• Recombination.
• Evolution scheme.
2.2.1.1.
Chromosome encoding
Populations of chromosomes are manipulated by genetic algorithms. These represent solu-
tions to a speciﬁc problem, while a chromosome is considered to be biological DNA that can
be represented with a string of letters from the alphabet. In order to encode solutions, a
bit-string is used by the standard genetic algorithm. A bit-string representation is a string
8
==PAGE:22==
2.
THEORETICAL BACKGROUND
of genes whose allele values are letters from the alphabet referring to a speciﬁc point of the
chromosome. [16]
Usually, genetic algorithms are applied to problems where its solutions are ﬁnite. However,
these solutions are so large that their assessment is not feasible to compute. The interpre-
tation of the strings is fully problem dependent. One of the strengths of genetic algorithms
are the common representations that may be used for multiple problems, which allows the
deployment of mutual routines and operators. This strength, makes it faster and easier to
apply genetic algorithms to new cases. However, the fact that the chromosome encoding will
hold limited information is a disadvantage. [16]
2.2.1.2.
Fitness function
The ﬁtness function assesses the capacity of the chromosome as a solution to a speciﬁc
problem. The translation process may be of high complexity. A chromosome is translated
into a timetable of scheduled operations. The computation will measure the success of the
timetable in what concerns the several criteria and objectives. [16]
2.2.1.3.
Selection
As already explained before, the genetic algorithm uses the ﬁtness function to discriminate
the quality of the solutions represented by the chromosomes. The selection part of the genetic
algorithm is projected to use the ﬁtness function to orientate the development of the chro-
mosomes by selective pressure. The chromosomes with higher ﬁtness have a better chance of
selection than the ones with lower ﬁtness. Highly ﬁt chromosomes may be chosen more than
once or recombined with themselves. Roulette Wheel is the standard selection process used,
where the selection probability of each chromosome is proportional to its relative ﬁtness. [16]
There are several selection methods. The Random Stochastic Selection chooses each chro-
mosome a number of times equal to its expectation of being selected according to the ﬁtness
proportional method. The Tournament Selection ﬁrstly chooses two chromosomes with uni-
form probability and selects after the chromosome with the highest ﬁtness. The Truncation
Selection randomly chooses from the population of chromosomes eliminating ﬁrst a ﬁxed
number of the chromosomes that are less adequate. [16]
2.2.1.4.
Recombination
Recombination is considered to be the process where the chosen chromosomes from a source
population are recombined to constitute members of a successor population. The goal is to
simulate the combination of genetic material that can happen when organisms reproduce.
[16]
The two major elements of recombination are the genetics operator crossover and mutation.
A crossover operator shows the combination of genetic material from two chosen parent
chromosomes to generate one or two child chromosomes.
A random number within the
interval [0,1] is created with uniform probability and compared to a pre-deﬁned cross-over

rate after choosing the two parent chromosomes. There isn’t any occurrence of crossover
when the random number is bigger than the cross-over. If the opposite happens, then the
cross-over operator is applied. [16]
2.2.1.5.
Evolution scheme
The resultant chromosomes from recombination pass to the successor population. The selec-
tion and recombination processes are iterated until a ﬁnal successor population is created.
The genetic algorithm iterates through several generations until the most suitable criteria is
achieved. Several evolutionary plans may be applied depending on which chromosomes from
the source population can pass without any changes to the successor population. [16]
Choosing the evolutionary scheme is dependent on the essence of the solution. ”Replacement
with elitism” is a largely applied scheme that is almost totally replacement, apart from the
fact that the best individuals from the source population remain in the successor population.
This plan avoids high relative ﬁtness solutions to be lost over the next generation due to the
non-deterministic process. [16]
Estimation of distribution algorithms have been recently considered. In this case, a prob-
abilistic model of the allele values distribution in high ﬁtness score chromosomes is built
based on the source population. The model to produce chromosomes for the next generation
is sampled by the algorithm, substituting the standard recombination element. [16]

Molecular relaxation
In physics and chemistry, relaxation is considered to be the return of a perturbed system to

Maximizing the annual energy production is usually the objective function used in a wind
farm optimization problem, as it is the main goal of developing a wind farm. However, other
objective functions may be deﬁned such as maximizing the proﬁt or minimizing the cost of
energy. The distance between turbines and the boundary of the wind farm are commonly
deﬁned as inequality constraint functions. [21]
When multi-objective functions are being used in an optimization problem, a group of solu-
tions known as Pareto optimal solutions or Pareto frontier is usually found. If none of the
objective functions correspondent to a solution can keep being improved without disregard-
ing the rest of the objective functions, then the solution can be considered Pareto optimal.
A solution that meets all the deﬁned constraints and bounds is considered to be a feasible
solution. [21]

3.3.4.
Multi-objective random search algorithm (MORSA)
The multi-objective random search is the algorithm used to perform all the simulations. It is
capable of optimizing a wind farm layout by formulating the turbine locations as continuous
variables. The algorithm is also able to optimize the number of turbines and their locations
at the same time. [21]
As it is explained in 2.2.1., genetic algorithms are considered to be population based search
methods, while the multi-objective random search is a single solution search algorithm. A
new feasible solution is created at each iteration of the algorithm by adding, removing or
randomly move one turbine to the prior solution. When this new feasible solution is created
based on the present solution, all the constraints and bound requirements are respected.
The new feasible solution is compared with the running Pareto optimal solutions for a non-
dominance veriﬁcation and the set of solutions is updated. [21]

5.
Conclusion
5.1.
Discussion
The noise model explained in 3.1. was successfully implemented into the layout optimization
framework. The results obtained in the optimization problem are reasonable, which means
that the noise databases were correctly applied to the model.
The diﬀerent case studies performed in chapter 4. show how the optimization process varies
with the number of evaluations, the number of receptors and their diﬀerent positions. It
was possible to conclude that the higher the number of evaluations, the better the results
obtained as it is shown in 4.1.1.. In 4.2., the receptor’s position was changed. Despite of
being at the same distance from the center of the wind farm boundary compared to 4.1., the
outdoor/indoor sound pressure level increases for this location due to the change of coordi-
nates explained in 4.2.1.. The receptors were located at the same distance from the center
of the wind farm boundary precisely to assess possible variations related to the coordinate
change. More receptors were added in sections 4.3. and 4.4. to see how the optimization
layout changes with this increasing. In the previous scenarios it was possible to see a clear
tendency of the turbines in moving to a speciﬁc area of the wind farm in order to be as far
as possible from the receptor. When diﬀerent receptors are placed in opposite directions,
the turbines tend to stay in a mid-term position in order to balance the noise felt at each
receptor. This was explained in 4.3.1..
In this study, noise was treated both as an objective function and as a constraint in 4.1..
When comparing both scenarios, it becomes clear that a higher annual energy production
is reached with noise deﬁned as a constraint function, since the optimizer is only concerned
in maximizing the AEP objective function. However, it is only possible to treat noise as a
constraint function when the initial layout respects the established threshold. The decision of
treating noise as an objective function or as a constraint should be based on the optimization
goals of the wind farm developer. If the goal is to maximize as much as possible the annual
energy production only respecting the noise limit stated in the regulations, then deﬁning
noise as a constraint function is the best option. But, if the project owner is also concerned
in minimizing as much as possible the noise emission for the population living nearby the
wind farm, it is recommended to deﬁne both AEP and SPL as objective functions.
5.2.
Future work/Final considerations
The overall goals deﬁned for this thesis were achieved. As already referred, the noise model
was successfully developed and applied to the performed case studies. Although the obtained
results are suﬃcient to show that it is possible to optimize a wind farm layout including noise
emission, there is further work that can be done within this theme. Due to the time taken by
each simulation to be executed, the number of evaluations was limited to 30000. However,
as shown in 4.1.1., the results improve with a higher number of evaluations, which means
that further simulations could be performed with numbers greater than 30000. The same
happens with the number of receptors that can also be increased in order to see how the
