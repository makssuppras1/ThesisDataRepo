Abstract
This study set out to examine leadership development practices in Danish engineering companies. It
did so by studying leadership development theory and interview 5 Danish companies. The theory
and empirical data was then analyzed separately to ﬁnd trends that could be helpful in general.
After the initial analysis the report combined the theoretical knowledge and the empirical data to
create 7 principles for good leadership development that could be implemented depending on the
leadership model of a company.
The 7 principles was
• Self development
• Understanding people
• Developing coworkers
• Leader group meetings
• Clear advancement steps
• An active goal
• Evaluation
The paper then moved on to explain the implementation of these principles, describing which should
be integrated in the leadership model, which the leaders should take responsibility for and which one
the HR should focus on.
The thesis then discussed challenges for integrating the leadership principles in the company and how
to handle them. It argued that it was important for the company to have a leadership model that
could handle the principles and a mature leadership culture.
Lastly the the thesis discusses potential sources of error and topics to look further into.
The study concludes that Danish leadership development practices are good but still has potential to
be better. It suggests to use the 7 principles found in the study but to measure the results carefully
since not all of the principles have been scientiﬁcally veriﬁed yet.

Introduction
"Can the blind lead the blind?
Will they not both fall into a pit?
The student is not above the teacher,
but everyone who is fully trained will be like their teacher "
Luke 6, 39-40
Can a person without leadership experience develop another leader?
Will they not both end up
causing problems for the company and end in a ﬁgurative pit with the blame? To be a leadership
developer it is important to ﬁrst be a leader. The leadership developer should then strive to develop
the leaders to be at least as good as they are.
Leadership has always been a crucial part throughout history. It does not matter whether it is in
military of civil positions, a successful country or organization has always been dependent on a good
leader. The best argument against anarchy is that even anarchy groups need a leader.
These examples clearly shows how important leadership is and have lead to many studies about lead-
ership and its inﬂuence on companies. This has lead to a greater focus on leadership in most ﬁrms no
matter which area they operate in. This has also lead to a huge increase in leadership development
books and articles.
There is now so much material on leadership and leadership development that it can be diﬃcult to
ﬁnd out what good leadership actually is. There are countless leadership models and new ones seem
to appear quicker than the scientiﬁc institutions can validate them. This does, however, not change
the fact that leadership development is very important. A study on 1100 Danish companies by T.
Eriksson and V. Smith (2007) showed that the most important growth factors for a company is the
leadership development. The study did not say what kind of leadership development is employed by
the companies just that it is important for growth.
Knowing tha tleadership development is important but not knowing what kind of leadership devel-
opment is being used has led me to my reasearch question:
What are Danish engineering companies doing to develop their leaders?
To clarify the problem I have added some sub questions:
• What are the common practices for leadership development?
• What are the common challenges
• Which of the common practices has had a positive impact on leadership?
• What are potential beneﬁcial practices that can help with the common challenges?
• How can the positive common practices and the potentially beneﬁcial practices be combined
with theory to give a recommendation to improve leadership development?
The purpose of this study is then to understand and describe the common practices and challenges
of Danish engineering companies, to create principles that can be applied to improve leadership
development.
The study will include leadership theories that have not yet been widely researched by the academics.
These theories includes John Maxwell’s 5 levels of leadership and Jim Collins’ level 5 leadership.
Through the study of these relatively new theories I hope to bring new perspectives to leadership
development by combining them with the ﬁndings in the Danish companies. The report will try to
describe leadership principles that are important for any leadership development model and thereby
make leadership development more simple.

Method
In this study I examined Five companies were one of them where in Jutland and the rest were in the
Copenhagen area. I conducted a total of (15) interviews with HR people, leaders and a few experts
outside the companies. The study mainly followed the phenomenological approach as described by
Jill Collis and Roger Hussey (2003) in the book Business Research.
The Method section is split up in a more theoretical part looking at the methodology of the thesis
and a more practical part which describes the process and methods used to get and analyze the
data.
2.1
Methodology
As outlined in by Collis and Hussey (2003) the two main study paradigms are the positivistic and
phenomenological paradigm. In a positivistic study the researcher tries to stay detached from the
subjects being researcher and collect quantitative data. In the phenomenological study the research
and the subjects being researched are more interrelated and the data often more qualitative. This
study was more phenomenological than positivistic. In my research I used qualitative interviews.
This meant that the interrelation between the me, the researcher, and the subject being researched
was impossible to separate. This interrelation was further reinforced by the interviews being semi-
structured. This meant that much of was asked was dependent on what the subject answered and
how they reacted to the questions asked. This meant that I had a direct inﬂuence on the subject
through my questions, the tone of voice, body language, etc.
In a positivistic study I would have tried to minimize the impact on the subject being researched.
This could have been done through questionaires, however, in this case I wanted to understand
the participants point of view and that was done better by using the phenomonological approach.
Furthermore there would not have been enough time for obersvations or for setting up an artiﬁcial
environment that would be used in the positivistic study.
The relation between theory and emperics was where I slightly deviated from the phenomenological
study. Often the phenomenological study is more inductive, having focus on the data and then getting
theory from that. In contrast the positivistic study often starts with developing a hypothesis and
then use the empirics to either validate or reject it. In my study I took a more abductive approach.
Accroding to Blomkvist and Hallin in Collis and Hussey(2003) this is an approach where both theory
and empirics interact throughout the study. I used theory to get a basic understanding of the concepts
and then used it in the interviews with the people being leaders or working on leadership development.
They in turn shared shared some of their material with me which lead to more theory for my part
which again helped me in the next interviews and so on. This hermeneutical approach helped me get
a more full picture of the topic.
Most studies will also have some degree of interrelation close to the abductive approach rather than a
clearly deductive or inductive. In my study, however, I neither started with formulating a hypothesis
for a deductive study nor did I do a inductive study with pure emperical data for theory. I judged
that the abductive method would give the best picture since it would give an understanding of the
topic through the theory however it would still leave room for the data to give the results and support
the theory. The abductive framework did not lock my study to a single hypothesis yet it still gave
me a frame to analyse the data from.
According to Collis and Hussey (2003) there are four main types of study. The types are deﬁned by
the keywords explore, describe, analyse and predict. These types shows the purpose of the research.
In this study I chose describe as my key word. This was done since there is an abundance of leadership
literature and theories. All this is, however, very unstructured and very diﬀerent. It seemed like every
company had a diﬀerent approach and all the literature also made other sugestions than what was
seen. I wanted to see if there were some general patterns in leadership development in Denmark and
then describe these to give some clarity to a huge and very messy subject.

I decided to cover the subject as a case study. I chose to have the process of leadership development as
my case rather than having a single company as my case. This was chosen since there have been other
studies doing that but I found relatively few studies that actually compared leadership development
in diﬀerent companies and I therefore judged that this would be the area where my study could have
the biggest impact. By comparing several companies I would be able to ﬁnd some general trends that
probably would have gone unnoticed if I did a case study of a single company.
2.1.1
Qualitative interviews
The research question was "what are Danish engineering companies doing to develop leaders." To
answer this question I found that the quantitative method was not the most useful. Leadership is
still a very broad term and it would be very diﬃcult to make questionnaires precise enough for there
to be no misunderstandings. I also have very limited time since this is just a master thesis. Finding
many companies and getting as many of their leaders to ﬁll out a questionnaire as possible is very
diﬃcult for a master thesis project. To get the numbers needed in the short time available, it would
almost be necessary to be a renown leadership researcher. The reality of any project, which involves
the help from outside companies, is that it would be a slow process and many companies might not
be interested. As a leadership guru it might still be possible to get the commitment, however, as a
masters student getting the contacts and commitment was hard work.
Questionnaires focus much more on the number of answers and often cannot be used to explain why
the answers are as they are. Because I also wanted to understand why and because of the diﬃculty
with using questionnaires I decided to do a qualitative study. In a qualitative study less interviews
are needed to form the basis of a good empirical foundation. In my study I was also more focused
on understanding the people involved in leadership development rather than getting a big amount of
answers to questionnaires. The qualitative interviews are better suited for this. The qualitative in-
terviews also allowed me to dig deeper and understand how the people perceived and used leadership
development.
I decided to make the qualitative interviews semi-structured. Using semi-structured interviews made
it easier to ask for clariﬁcation when I was unsure about something and generally avoid misinterpreta-
tion and misunderstandings. This gave the data a very high validity since it was rich and subjective.
This also, however, made the reliability of the data low, since the samples would be harder to recreate
and generalize from.
I decided to have at try and get at least two interviews with each company and if possible more. This
would allow me to get an overview of the the leadership practices and how they would be applied.
To get full data satiety I would have preferred 4 interviews with each company over a long period of
time. The 15 interviews were, however, enough to get a good ﬁrst glance and understanding of the
leadership development. The data from most of the companies are very good to create the basis for
a master thesis.

2.2.2
Changing focus
Starting the study I wanted to look at companies that produced a physical product and where
engineers played a vital part of the production. I set out to ﬁnd these companies but found only very
few. The only companies I found that really did this were companies like Novo Nordisk where their
product were very important and delicate. Therefore it needed experts to produce it.
In almost all other companies engineers did project work. Taking into consideration the high salary
this makes sense. It would not be beneﬁcial to put a person with such a high hourly wage to do the
generally more menial tasks that is required in most production.

Theory
4.1
What is leadership
The ﬁrst question that needs to be answered before going into leadership development is "what is
leadership?".
The entire theory of leadership development hinges on the deﬁnition of leadership.
Getting a wrong deﬁnition will lead to a wrong kind of leadership development. If leadership for
instance was categorized as getting people to do the job for you, then the slave drivers in Ancient
Egypt would be a great example of good leadership, since they got the pyramids built. In this case
good leadership development could be training the slave drivers the best ways to whip the workers,
to get maximal work out of them. This is hardly an approach suitable for leadership development
today. It is therefore very important to get the deﬁnition of leadership right so we do not get wrong
leadership development.
4.1.1
Leadership theories
Through the ages there have been many diﬀerent deﬁnitions on what leadership is and what makes
a leader.
A deﬁnition of nine leadership theories have been summarized by Amenchukwu et.
al
(2015).
The great man theory
This theory says that leaders are born not made. It states that some people are destined to become
leaders and will become it no matter what. Leadership cannot be learned it is inherent in the person.
It was called "the great man" theory because it was developed at a time when leadership was seen as
a primarily male quality.
Trait theory
Trait theory, as the name suggests, focuses on certain traits or qualities that make people more suited
for leadership. These traits are often personality or behavioral characteristics. Similar to the great
man theories, the trait theory believes that leaders are born with these traits and that they are not
learned. This theory has been criticized for having inconsistencies this has made people leave it. It
was pointed out that there was rarely a correlation between leadership eﬀectiveness and the deﬁned
traits. Another problem was the people who had the traits but were not leaders.
Contingency theory
Contingency theory looks at variables related to the environment and tries to ﬁnd the best leader
under those circumstances. The contingency theory involves the coherence of the group, the structure
of the assignment, and the power distance of the leader according to Fiedler (1977). The model
developed by ﬁedler can be seen on ﬁgure Figure 4.1 below.
This theory says that no leadership style is appropriate to all situations. according to Fiedler (1977)
The success of leadership hinges on getting the right kind of leader whose talent matches the need
of the situation.
He also believes that leaders cannot change their styles between the task and
relationship motivated leadership styles and that is why he calls it the leadership game about matching
the man and the situation.

Situational theory
In situational leadership was developed by Hershey and Blanchard and is based on the idea that
there is no best way to inﬂuence people according to Ardichvili (2008). The theory is based on the
leader follower relationship and says that the leader should take on diﬀerent roles depending on the
commitments and compentences to a given task. One of the diﬀerence between the situational and
the contingency theory is the belief on what can be changed. The contingency theory focuses on
changing the environment to ﬁt the leader the situational leadership focuses on changing the leader
to ﬁt the environment. In the contingency model the company had to choose the best leader for the
situation in situational leadership the leader has to choose the appropriate style.
Behavioral theory
This theory is based on the idea that leaders are made not born in contrary to some of the earlier
mentioned theories. According to the behavioral model leadership is deﬁned by actions and not by
internal qualities. This model stresses that everyone can be a leader through observation and training.
Naylor was quoted by Amenchukwu et. al (2015) for saying that the interest in behavior has been
stimulated by a comparison of the autocratic and democratic leadership styles. It has been observed
that groups under these types of leadership styles perform diﬀerently. In the autocratic style the
group is very eﬃcient but when the leader disappears the work stops. There also tends to be some
degree of enmity between the leader and the followers. The democratic style can work nearly as well
as the autocratic however the work continues if the leader is gone. The group members are also more
positive towards the leader.
Participative theory
The pariticipative theory suggests that optimal leadership comes from taking input from others
and involve them in the decision making process. A participative leader will therefore encourage
participation and contribution. Through this process they aim at making the task important for the
coworkers and make them committed to the decision making process.
Transactional/management theory
The transactional or management theory focus on the exchange that takes place between leaders and
followers. It emphasizes supervision. organization and group performance. According to Charry,
referenced by Amenchukwu et. al (2015), these leadership systems base their leadership on reward

and punishment. Lamb also referrancesd in Amenchukwu et. al (2015), says that it is important
to make it clear what is expected of the group and also what results/consequences there will be for
success or failure to meet the expectations.
Transformational/relationship theory
The transformational or relationship theory focus on the connections formed between the leader and
the follower. In this theory leadership is based on making a connection with the follower which will
lead to increased motivation and morality for both. The transformational theory says that leaders
motivate and inspire people by helping them see the bigger picture of the assignment. The leader is
focused on the performance of the group and also how the individuals best fulﬁll their potential.
Skills theory
The skill theory says that good leadership comes from learned skills, a developed style and acquiring
of knowledge. This does not mean that it ignores things like inherited traits but just that eﬀective
leadership comes through hard work. In this theory leadership is also made not born. Where the
behavioral theory looks more at certain behaviors of the leader, like autocratic and democratic, the
skills theory looks at what tools the leader can use.
While Amenchukwu et. al has described many of the classical leadership theories, Ardichvili and
Manderscheid have described some of the more contemporary leadership theories. They also cover
the situational and transformational leadership styles in their paper because these are still seen
implemented in business to today. They do however also add leader-member exchange theory (LMX),
servant leadership, authentic leadership and complexity theory.
Leader-member exchange theory
The LMX theory is a relationship-based theory versus a leader- or follower-based theory according
to Uhl-Bien in Ardichvili et al (2008). This theory describes leaders making an in-group and an
out-group of the followers, according to Dansrou in Ardichvili et al (2008). Whether the followers get
in the in-group or the out-group depends on how well they work with the leader and how well the
leader works with them according to Nothouse in Ardichvili et al (2008).
Servant leadership theory
Servant leadership as a way of leading that distributes power to the followers. In this leadership theory
the leaders themselves as servants for those they lead for the purpose of increasing the capacity of
others (Ardichvili et al 2008). The servant leader will provide a good working environment to allow
the followers to perform at their maximum potential.
Authentic leadership theory
Authentic leadership is being deﬁned as working from a combination of leadership, ethics and posi-
tive psychology & organizational scholarship according to Cameron, Dutten, Quinn and Luthans in
Ardichvili (2008). The core of the authentic leadership theory is authenticity and self-knowledge. This
leads to the leader being self-aware, relational transparent, have an internalized moral perspective
and a balanced processing ability.
Complexity theory
Complexity theory does not focus on the leadership follower relation but focuses more on the dynamic
environment around the leader such as the evolving behavior patterns and the complex interaction
of organizational players. Leadership from the complexity theory focuses more on disrupting exist-
ing patterns to promote innovation through novel ideas and non-linear thinking. It seeks to inter-
pret change for the followers instead of making it happen according to Plowman in Ardichvili et al
(2008)

4.1.2
Leadership deﬁnitions
Finding a leadership deﬁnition can be diﬃcult with so many diﬀerent leadership theories.
The
leadership deﬁnition should be something that, to some extend, covers most of the diﬀerent theories
and therefore has to be broad yet speciﬁc enough to clearly state what leadership is.
The ﬁrst place to ﬁnd a deﬁnition is often the dictionary. The Oxford dictionary describes leadership
as the action of leading a group of people or an organization. Well, that was not very helpful. Let
us check "to lead" next. To lead is deﬁned as "be in charge or command of" it also describes it as
organize and direct. This deﬁnition is a bit better but it still a bit to broad.
In the book Leder udvikling by Sørensen (2011) Lars Ib adds a deﬁnition of leadership in the foreword.
He describes leadership as, creating results through other people by:
• Being present in the situation
• Find a solution
• Make it happen
This deﬁnition is a bit more speciﬁc than the ones we ﬁnd in the dictionary and it is also better suited
for leadership in business where the one in the dictionary seem to be referring more to a command
structure as seen in the military.
Maxwell (2011) describes leadership simply as inﬂuence. This is a very simple and broad deﬁnition
that covers most of the leadership theories above. It is however still a little to broad.
Eriksson and Smith (2007) argues that leadership will be deﬁned diﬀerently depending on who you
ask, especially in a organizational context. For the owners leadership will be, securing the company’s
long term goal with a minimum of burdens for themselves. They then argue that for the coworkers
leadership would be described more as, creating a stable and safe setting where family and work life
are hang together.
These are just a snapshot of all the deﬁnitions of leadership there are. Farr et. al (1997) describes how
Rost analyzed 221 leadership deﬁnitions and found that most describe leadership as an interpersonal
inﬂuence process that is goal directed and purposive. For engineering managers they chose a deﬁnition
made by Roach and Behling in 1984 which deﬁnes leadership as, "the process of inﬂuencing an
organized group toward accomplishing goals".
Ardichivili and manderscheid (2008)introduce another deﬁnition that comes very close the one above.
The deﬁnition is made by Northouse after he too had analyzed many diﬀerent leadership deﬁnitions.
He found that there where four main components in the leadership deﬁnitions:
• Leadership is a process
• It involves inﬂuence
• It occurs within a group context
• It involves goal attainment
This led to him describing leadership as a process whereby an individual inﬂuences a group of indi-
viduals to achieve a common goal.
Out of all of these deﬁnitions the one that is best suited for the purpose seems to be the last one
mentioned. It is important to note though that this deﬁnition omits the idea of self leadership. The
leader often has to learn how to lead themselves before they can lead others. This is, however, not
a leadership process that is often seen from the outside and tweaking the deﬁnition to also accom-
modate this part of leadership might make the deﬁnition more cryptic and harder to understand.
Another consideration is the kind of goal pursued. Often the leader sets a goal that is bigger than
any individual. This is why the leader is needed to reach it because no individual would be able

All of the leadership theories in the section before, have something to do with inﬂuencing people to

4.2
Leadership development
From the deﬁnition, that leadership is is a process whereby an individual inﬂuences a group of
individuals to achieve a common goal, is now possible to deﬁne leadership development. The measure
of leadership in this deﬁnition is reaching the goal through inﬂuence.
To develop a leader they
will then have to learn how to gain inﬂuence with people to better reach the goal. anything that
accomplishes this will then be leadership development.
In this section I will look into some tools and then three models of leadership development. The tools
are some practical ways to develop leaders whereas the models provide a frame to for the leadership
development. The ﬁrst is the leadership pipeline that gives tools to the leader to understand their
situation and then know how to work with people to reach the goals from there. The second is the
ﬁve levels of leadership by Maxwell which is concerned with where the leader gets their inﬂuence
from. The last model is level 5 leadership by Collins that looks at how some highly successful leaders
have reached their goal through others.
4.2.1
55 tools for leadership development
The book "Leder udvikling 55 værktøjer til ledere og konsulenter" by Sørensen (2011) describes
diﬀerent tools for developing leadership. It has four main categories for these tools which are analysis,
on the job, for managers and workshops.
The ﬁrst tools the book introduces are the 16 analytical tools. These tools are designed to help
the leadership developer to identify the need for development and also the area in which it will be
the most eﬀective. The ﬁrst 11 tools are speciﬁcally designed for this purpose and presented in a
suggested order for use if there is no prior knowledge. The last 5 tools are then supplementary to the
ﬁrst 11.
The next is on the job leadership development. There are 9 tools for development on the job. The
ﬁrst two are for making a plan for and ﬁnd the potential for the leader.
The next two tools are for leadership during the daily job of the leader. The ﬁrst of these are for the
leader do get better at doing what he/she does. To do this it is possible to pair them with someone
who already does it very well and also get the leader to make a development plan. The second thing
that can be done on the job is to expand the task the leader is doing. This is often giving the leader
new tasks with greater complexity.
The next two tools are job rotation. In this the leader can be given a new job on the same level but in
a diﬀerent unit. This will challenge their leadership capabilities and give them a better understanding
of the company. The other rotation is upwards, where the leader tries to take responsibility for some
of the tasks their boss is doing. This is testing whether they are ready to advance upwards but also
helping them to do so.
The last thing that can be done on the job is projects. Here it can either be an individual or a group
project. Individual projects can be tailored to ﬁt the needs of the leader so they are challenged to
grow but also be made in a was so they get to understand the company better and through that get
better equipped for greater responsibility. Group work is an even better way to give the leader a
better understanding of the company and the employees working in it, however, it is not always the
17

Master thesis
intended leader that takes the leadership responsibility. In those cases this method shows that they
might not be ready to move up according to Sørensen (2011).
The third part describes what managers can do to help the leaders under them grow. This time the
tools are more on a spectrum going from instructions to involvement. The tools the managers should
use depends a lot on where the leader is currently in the job and what capabilities the manager has.
The ﬁrst is instructions. These should be given when the leader is new to the job and requires the
manager to have a good understanding of the tasks the leader has to perform. If this goes well the
leader can then move on to sparring which is two way communication about how to do the tasks.
When the leader then has a greater mastery of the tasks the manager can move on to coaching.
Coaching takes more time and is designed to let the leader solve the problems themselves. The role
of the coach is to ask the right questions to make the leader think. The next step is delegation. Here
the tasks are given to the leader and they handle it themselves. This is more eﬀective for the manager
since they do not have to spent so much time with the leader and can trust them to do a good job.
The last level is involving the leader in the managers tasks. This is done when the leader is ready to
move to the next level and get more responsibility.
Throughout the above process it can be helpful to give feedback. It is a method that can be used by
everyone from the leadership developer to the people being lead by the leader. It is a tool that is not
bound to any competency level.
Lastly it is important for not just the leader but also the leadership developer to have a plan for the
continued growth of the leader and themselves. This can help the leadership developer ﬁnd out who
would be best suited to handle each kind of leadership development mentioned above and when to
do it.
The fourth part is concerned with how to make and host a workshop. First it is important to choose
the type of workshop that is needed. After that follows a list of three physical tools that can be used
to convey the message these are ﬂip-overs, powerpoint presentations and having a personal course
folder. It then moves on to ﬁve activities that can be used in the workshop which are case studies,
group discussions, games, training exercises and role playing.
The next four tools are concerned
with structuring the workshop which mainly includes making a plan and structuring the physical
environment. The next seven tools are for the facilitation of the workshop which are concerned with
the preparation before and the delivery on the workshop. Lastly there are two tools for evaluation.
These are all tools that can be used individually but often are better to use in combination. I expect
to see some of these when visiting the companies and interviewing them. These tools do however
not provide a framework for leadership development in themselves. What follow after this are three
models for leadrship development where these tools can be used when the leadership developer thinks
they ﬁt the process and the person.

The leadership pipeline Noel et. al (2013) is a leadership development model based on where in the
organization the leader is. It breaks down the positions in the company into 7 diﬀerent levels and
goes through what leadership skills and competences are needed on each level. It then goes on to
describe the turns in the pipelines and how to navigate them. These turns are the ones that are
shown as passages in ﬁgure Figure 4.2. It is often at the turns the problems occur. The greater part
of the book is dedicated to navigating through the turns in the pipeline. The model can be seen on
ﬁgure Figure 4.2.
The following is a short description of each level.
• Level 1 is managing self. This is where the person is a specialist and has to lead themselves.
This means motivating themselves and understanding their work and how it contributes.
• Level 2 is managing others. Here the leader has to manage other people which are normally
specialists. It is often people that used to be on the same level as the leader before they got
the leadership position.
• Level 3 is managing managers. At this level the leader will have to become pure managers.
• Level 4 is the functional manager. Here the leader becomes the head of a function within the
business. This could be Human resources, business or the specialist area they came from.
• Level 5 is business manager. Here the leader has to lead an entire business in one area. This
could for instance be the Danish market.
• Level 6 is the group manager. On the level the leader is managing a portfolio of several businesses
at the same time.

• Level 7 is the enterprise manager. At this level the leader is managing the entire enterprise as
one unit and not just a portfolio of diﬀerent businesses.
Developing leaders in the leadership pipeline model is mainly navigating the turns in the pipeline.
That is also why Noel et. al (2013) focuses mainly on these transition points throughout the book.
The ﬁrst step is going from level 1 to level 2. The shift here is from doing the work to doing work
for others. The most diﬃcult change is starting to value managing work rather than just tolerating
it. Many of the high performers often want to do the same thing so they might not be the best
candidates for this promotion, which has been the classical approach. The person who likes being
productive who still wants to take this step must learn to enjoy making others productive through
planning work, ﬁlling jobs, motivating and delegating.
The second turn is from level 2 to 3. At the previous level the leaders would still have some operation
work but at this level the leaders have to become pure managers. This means that the leader has to
assign management to others, help people going from A to B, think beyond the function and start
thinking strategy. It is important to be able to distinguish between the people who are good at
working and the people who can lead.
The transition from level 3 to 4 is a bit harder than it seems. Now the leader has to penetrate
two layers of management to talk to the individual and must start to manage areas outside of their
expertise area. At this level it is important that the leader thinks of other functions than just the
one they came from. They must be a team player and a competent strategist. It is also on this level
that there begins to be more competition for the resources.
Transitioning from level 4 to level 5, from functional manager to business manger, gives signiﬁcantly
more autonomy. At level 5 there is a clear link between the eﬀorts and the marketplace results. This
turn is, however, also a major shift in skills. At this level it is important to integrate functions and
not just work with them. A common mistake on this level is using the support functions wrong. It is
also important to both think of long term and short term goals when looking at plans. The leader has
to be able to see if the company will make money following the plan and if it is long term sustainable.
It is therefore important that the leader has time to reﬂect and analyze at this level.
The second last turn from level 5 to level 6 is running not just one but more businesses. At this
level it is important to value the success of other people’s businesses and not just their own. It is
necessary to think broader and have a more holistic leadership approach. The leader must ask them
self whether they have the right business, the right core capabilities and what resources they have.
The leader cannot have a specialist mentality at this level but has to invest where there is the biggest
return and cannot have favorites.
The last leadership turn is from level 6 to 7.
This is where the leader goes from being a group
manager to being an enterprise manager. Here the leader should have a greater focus on values than
skills. They have to reinvent themselves to be a long term visionary thinker. The leaders at this level
need the ability to understand and sense external factors and must be more proactive than reactive.
Success at this level often hinges on 3-4 mission critical points every year. The leader has to be able
to let go of the pieces and see the whole of the organization as one. To do that the leader needs a
strong team to help them.
One of the reasons this model is called the leadership pipeline is because they want to encourage a
steady ﬂow of leaders through the pipeline at all times. This will make the company more long term
sustainable since leaders should be looking for their successors and be able to move freely when able.
If a leader fails to make a turn it can cause clogging in the system and that can damage the company.
That is also why there is such a big focus on the turns since this is where the clogging most often
happen.
This leadership development model focuses much on what skills are needed at what level. To develop
good leaders they have to be taught the necessary skills. This makes this model lean towards the skill
theory. Looking at the deﬁnition of leadership develppment this model mainly focuses on what is the
goal on each level and how to reach it. It is through the tools that the inﬂuence of other individuals
comes into play in the model.

4.2.3
The ﬁve levels of leadership John Maxwell
John Maxwell introduces another kind of leadership development that is not dependent on where in
the leadership pipeline the leader is. This model is based of his deﬁnition that leadership as inﬂuence.
The model then focuses on how the leader gets or has inﬂuence with the people who they lead. This
inﬂuence comes in 5 levels according to him. The brief overview of this model can be seen on ﬁgure
Figure 4.3. The model starts with level 1. This level is the position level. The key-word here is rights.
Figure 4.3: Maxwell’s 5 levels of leadership
Here people follow the leader because they have to. This is where the leader has gotten a leadership
position and has power over the subordinates. the leader can give them a raise or cut in their salary.
The leader can also ﬁre them. At this level the leadership should utilize their formal power to get
the job done.
This level is nice to visit but you wouldn’t want to live there as Maxwell (2011) puts it. This is the
level were the leader gets the least out of their coworkers. The coworkers are only following because
they have to and not because they want to. The only inﬂuence the leader has is the title and the
power that comes with it.
It is important to master this level because it is not possible to move up to the levels before fully
mastering the level before. Mastering this level is about learning how and when to use power to

motivate the employees.
The second level is the permission level. The keyword here is relationships. On this level people
follow the leader because they want to follow him/her. The leader has already established that they
are the leader and can now begin to create relationships with the followers.
On the second level the leader is getting to know the people and working to create good relationships.
It is through these relationships that the followers begin to want to follow the leader and are willing
to give more than just the bare minimum as is the case on level 1.
It is important to master this level because leaders can only inﬂuence people that like them. Should
a leader fail to master this level they can always fall back to their position. A common trend is that
all the leadership levels that have been mastered before will work as a safety net for the leader if they
fail to move up to the next level. In this regards it is dangerous to only be on level 1 since it has not
safety net.
On level 3 the leader ﬁnally starts to produce. This is why this level is called production. The
keyword here is results. On this level people follow because of what the leader has done for the
organization. According to Maxwell very few people make it all the way to level three.
On the third level the leader starts to really help the bottom line of the organization. This is also
where the leader starts to get their credibility as a leader. On this level it is important to go in front
and be a role model because people do what people see according ti Maxwell (2011). He uses an
illustration were he says it is important to be a tour guide and be able to take people to the exciting
places and not be a travel agent that just sends people of to places they have never been themselves.
It is also on level 3 that the leader starts to get momentum. Momentum is crucial because if it ﬁrst
starts it is easy to maintain. Maxwell describes momentum as a huge mill wheel, it is diﬃcult to get
it started but when it is started it is very easy to keep it going. Momentum is also one of the keys to
solve many of the problems, since most of the problems becomes insigniﬁcant when compared to the
progress being made.
When a leader has mastered this level the followers are not just individuals anymore, they have been
organized into teams where the strengths are being utilized and the weaknesses are insigniﬁcant be-
cause the team members support each other.
Level 3 is the place where the leadership pipeline can be used. The leadership pipeline shows what
competences and skills are necessary to be eﬀective depending on the level of the organization. It is
at level 3 the leader has to understand and use these tools to be as eﬀective as possible.
A danger on level 3 is to forget or ignore level 2 and just focus on getting the results. This can lead
to the leader falling back to level 1 which is the least eﬀective level. Some leaders might be tempted
to try to skip level 2 and jump directly to level 3 but this has costs. This jump means that the leader
will have to spend considerable time later getting to know the team and get their permission. The
time spent on this might break momentum. The reason it is crucial not to forget level 2 is because
it is very important for moving on to level 4.
Level 4 is the people development level. The keyword on this level is reproduction. Here people follow
you because of what you have done for them. It is at this level that it becomes clear that people are
the most appreciable asset of the company.
This level is all about growing people. According to Maxwell (2011) you grow the company by grow-
ing the people because this increases the capacity of the company. At this level the leader wants to
train the followers to become leaders themselves.
A very important thing on level 4 is getting the right people. According to Maxwell 80 % of the
solution is hiring the right people.
It is important to ﬁnd out what qualities and characteristics
are needed. After getting the people, positioning becomes equally important. Here Maxwell quotes
Collins (2001) on the importance of getting people on the right places. According to Maxwell suc-
cessful people position themselves well, successful leaders, however, position others well.
The last level is level 5 the pinnacle. On this level the keyword is respect. Here people follow because
of who the leader is and what they stands for. The leader has now developed leaders for so long and
done it so well that these leaders have become advocates for him/her. Normally a leader has to start

from level 1 each time they get a new employee but when they have reached the pinnacle they can
often start on level 4 immediately.
Getting to level 5 is a long process it can often take a full lifetime. If the leader reaches this level
they have to remember that they have not arrived and that leadership is still a growing process even
at this level. Being at the pinnacle is not something permanent either. The leader is often there for
a short time and have to make the most out of the time there.
A leader at the pinnacle is evaluated not on his own leadership but on the quality of leaders he/she

Maxwell’s model provides a way for all leaders to grow, it does not matter what level of the orga-
nization they are on. This model also explains why some of the top managers in a company can be
bad leaders and how some leaders at the bottom can be really good leaders. It gives some principles
for growth that are universal.
The leadership development model touches on a lot of the diﬀerent leadership theories.
At level
one it is clearly a transactional leadership. It is about give and take. Going to level 2 we ﬁnd the
more relational theories like the participative theory and to some extend also the transformational
leadership theory. On level 3 it could be argued that we are more in the situational theory where
the leaders would use what works in the circumstance to get the job done. Going to level 4 we once
again ﬁnd some transformational leadership and to some degree also the servant leadership. At the
pinnacle there is some authenticity theory. There are also some of the theories that do not ﬁt in this
leadership development model like complexity theory and the great man theory.
While this is in many ways a good model there is still another model that could be very beneﬁcial to
study. This is also a model with ﬁve levels of leadership and was published earlier than this model
by Jim Collins.
4.2.4
Level 5 leadership Jim Collins
Jim Collins have describes what he calls "level 5 leadership" in his book From good to great (2001).
This kind of leadership is crucial for a company if they want to go from being an ordinary company
to a great one.
Since this is level 5 there are other levels before it. Level 1 is being a capable individual, level 2 is
being a good team member, level 3 is being a competent team manager, level 4 is being an eﬀective
leader and then there is level 5. Level 5 is hard to categorize in just one sentence. The research team
for the study was talking about titles like "servant leader" or "humble leader" but they did not think
that it would work. They felt it gave a picture of the leader being weak or meek but that was not
the case for the leaders they had studied.
That leads to the ﬁfth level. The ﬁrst thing to notice for the Collins level 5 leader is a duality. The
leader is modest yet willful and humble yet fearless. Often these characteristics are not found in the
same person. A modest and humble person is often thought of as standing in the back and supporting
while the willful and fearless leader is the one standing in the front. The interesting thing about the
level 5 leader is that he/she does exactly that, both.
The level 5 leader does not want to promote themselves but have a passion for their company. They
are ready to make the hard decisions and face much hardship for the sake of the company. They do
not do this to get any fame themselves, when they succeed they prefer to stand in the back and enjoy
the success of the company and of the people in it.
This mentality has lead to what Collins and his team described as the window and the mirror. For a
level 5 leader it is natural to go to the window when they have success and point out to the people of
the company and give them credit for the success. The people on the other hand points right back at
the leader and says that it is because of him. They are both right, but a level 5 leader would never
acknowledge that. On the other hand, if things goes wrong the level 5 leader goes to the mirror and
asks themselves why they messed up. They take the blame for the failure. This is an interesting
pattern since many "larger than life" leaders do the exact opposite. When they have success they

For Jim Collins a level one leader is a capable individual. Being capable is often enough to get you

The third level also correlates well. Here Collins says it is an eﬀective manager. Exactly what is

to become the level 5 leader in his model too.
Going back to the leadership deﬁnition the Collins level 5 leader has gotten his/her inﬂuence from
who they are as people. This means that people follow them for who they are. This again is the
exact same sentence that describes Maxwell’s level 5 leader.

5.3.1
The brain
In the ﬁrst section about the brain they talk about there being two diﬀerent kinds of worlds. There
is the real world which is concrete and intuitive and then there is the parallel world which is logical,
systematic and strategic. In the brain this duality is shown in two kinds of operating systems called
system one and system 2.
System 1 is the fast and unconscious part of our brain.
We don’t have control over it and it is

emotionally driven. The way we learn in this system is through experience. This system’s role is to
asses the situation, deliver updates and then act on them.
System 2 on the other hand is the slow and conscious system. This is where we have our self awareness,
our logical thinking and our conscious awareness. In this system we learn through attention. The
role of this system is to seek new or missing information and then make a conscious decision based

They describe how this works in our mind as an elephant and a rider. Our system 2 is the rider who
knows where he wants to go and is trying to direct the elephant. System 1 is the elephant of which
the rider only have limited control. If the rider wants to go one way but the elephant wants to go
another, most often the elephant wins. To make sure that the elephant goes the way the rider wants
it is best to make that way easy or the other roads hard since the elephant often takes the easiest
route.
In a leadership context this teaches the new leaders that if there is something they do not want the
coworkers to do they will have to make it diﬃcult to do it or add friction in another way. It can also
be used to motivate workers to do the right things by making those things the easy ones to do.
5.3.2
The Ofman model
Figure 5.4: The ofman model, as used in the course in Company C
In the psychological part they use a model created by Daniel Ofman. This model is designed for people
to get to know their own strengths and weaknesses. The model can be seen on ﬁgure Figure 5.4.
The model works by ﬁrst ﬁnding a core strength within yourself that you put into the Core qualities
box. Then the user takes this core quality to the extreme to ﬁnd the potential downside in their core
quality. This downside is then called the pitfall of the person. After discovering the pitfall the person
has to ﬁnd the opposite positive of the pitfall. This is often the thing people has kept telling them for
the most of their life. This then becomes the challenge for that person. They then take the challenge
and take it to the extreme to ﬁnd the negative outcome of that. this then becomes the allergy of that
person which is most often something the person cannot stand.
Person C1 gave me an example of this which is quite helpful to understand this model. First the
core quality was being optimistic. If you are to optimistic you can quickly become unrealistic. This
is the pitfall. Most often people with this core quality is always told to be more realistic which is
the positive opposite of being unrealistic and becomes the challenge. Lastly being to realistic to an
extreme degree makes a person pessimistic which is the allergy of an optimist.

5.3.3
The existentials
The last important part of the toolbox is the existentials. These are four statements that they believe
are relevant and important for all human beings to have fulﬁlled. The four existentials are:
• Everything a person does is meaningful
• Everyone always does their best (under the circumstances)
• Everybody needs to be seen
• Everyone develops from where they are
These four are used as tools to help the new leaders understand the coworkers for whom they have
become leaders. Some of these are easier accepted than others for the new managers.
The ﬁrst, everything a person does is meaningful, does make sense on the outset, however, sometimes
a person will start shouting or maybe just leave a meeting. In these cases it can seem very strange
and hard to reconcile that everything has meaning. Accepting this ﬁrst statement then lets the leader
explore why the person did a seemingly meaningless act and ﬁnd out what was behind it instead of
just scolding the coworker. This curiosity helps the new leaders understand their coworkers better and
allow them to handle the real problems that hides underneath the symptoms. Often symptoms can be
almost impossible to deal with, like a coworker leaving meetings. This existential helps people realize
that there is probably another root cause for the behavior and then allows them to do something
about it. This is also very important for giving feedback to employees. If an employee is in system
2 then they know that negative feedback is a gift that can help them grow however if they are in
system 1 they are almost certain to be unable to recieve negative feedback as a gift. Knowing this it is
very important that the leader can read what system the coworker is operating on and give feedback
accordingly.
The second existential is often the hardest one to accept for the new leaders, everyone always does
their best. It put the parenthesis there because sometimes the give the softer and more classifying
version where immediately show that it is under the circumstances. It was interesting at the leader

used to motivate others but the humanistic-encouraging tends to be more up building and focusing
on strength whereas the oppositional focuses on weaknesses and can easily be interpreted as hostile.
Another example is the competative on the left and approval on the right. A person motivated by
approval seeks the aﬃrmation of others while a person motivated by competition seeks to beat the
others in their speciﬁc area.

5.5.1
Leadership and self deception
The book "leadership and self deception, getting out of the box" is not a classical leadership develop-
ment book. It is not a compilation of diﬀerent techniques to improve leaders nor a leadership model
that teaches you how to be a leader and how to advance certain steps. It is a story about a new
manager in a company that has to learn the ways of the company. It is build up around this mans
work situation and family and as readers we follow how the main character becomes aware of his own
shortcomings and in turn becomes a better person who is considered to his family and coworkers.
The premise of the book is about getting out of the box. Often when people use the expression "get-
ting out of the box" they are referring to thinking diﬀerent than normal and try to break the norms
you have created for yourself. It is often being brought up in cases where creativity and innovation
is needed to ﬁx a problem. The box being talked about in the book is slightly diﬀerent though.
Being in the box often has something to do with being selﬁsh, self-centered and blaming everyone
and everything around you for the problems. Getting out of the box means acknowledging you own
shortcomings and then meeting people where they are with humility.
In their workshop company E presents 4 boxes that people can be in. There is the "better than" box,
the "I deserve" box, the "others should see me as" box and the "less worth than" box. It then goes on
to ask the participants for what the name of their box is. This allows the leaders to reﬂect on their
own personal shortcomings and allows them to work with them.
People get in the box because of lacking self-knowledge and defense mechanisms. This leads us to
betray our true motives of doing what is best for others according to the book. When we then are
caught in the box we become self-righteous and start typecasting people. We then use others short-
comings to justify our own faults. After being in the box for a long time we forget what got us there
and start carrying it around all the time. This in turns lead to us taking the responsibility from our
coworkers and leads to low moral or even hostility.
When being in the box people are self centered and use energy on defending oneself. No matter how
many leadership tools you know or how well you remember the right way to say things you will not
be eﬀective as a leader in the box because people can see that you do not have a real interest in
them nor respect for them. To get out of the box, and stay out, the leader must stop working against
others, lower the defenses and start taking responsibility for own actions and mistakes.
All this is presented in the book in the form of a story. The story is taking the ideas to the extreme
and has a good ending. It makes it all a bit cliche but it also gets the point through quite clearly. As
E2 puts it
"Since it is written about the wife and the child, as it mainly is, you get another relation to it,
because the child cannot be an idiot ... and you have chosen your wife yourself ... so to put that
angle on it is genius."
It is clear that this book has made an eﬀect on the organization. All three leaders I interviewed men-
tioned getting out of the box before I mentioned anything about the box in the interview. Both E2 and
E3 also mentioned that the knowledge from the book has had a positive inﬂuence on their personal life.

game breaking nature and should be real as the coworkers will see through false ones and fatal ones

The second is about reading between the lines. It is important to understand what is going on and

Data analysis
In the previous section I described what each company did and gave a brief overview of their leadership
development models and philosophies. In this part I will look at what each of these ﬁve models have
in common and what common challenges they face.
6.1
Things in common
There are many things that all the diﬀerent companies do. To simplify it I have made three major
categories where there are some common practices. The categories are people, culture and general
leadership development practices.
6.1.1
People
All the companies without exception had a huge focus on people and the soft values. I did expect to
ﬁnd this to some degree but I got overwhelmed with how explicit this focus is. This should not be
to surprising since both Farr et. al (1997) describe how the engineers need a bigger focus on the soft
values for becoming good leaders. This was also echoed in the interviews since all the HR people I
interviewed agreed that this was the area that needed most focus since most of the leaders used to
be very talented specialists as one would expect from an engineering company.
Throughout the project there seemed to be a pattern that emerged as I interviewed the companies.
It seems like leadership development starts with some degree of self insight and then it moves on to
understanding people.
In all the companies, with company B as a possible exception, the leadership development always
start with some kind of self insight. In Company A it is what the ﬁrst module is about, Company
C has dedicated almost all of leadership one to this topic, Company D’s model has a huge focus on
this and Company E has it as the most central part in the form of getting out of the box. Company
B also have a big focus on this topic as it is the ﬁrst topic in the book I do not, however know if this
is where they start their leadership development but I would assume it.
This begs the question of why start there.
E1 describes it quite well when he in the interview
said:
"Without developing yourself you cannot develop others"
This also leads back to the quote in the very beginning that says the blind cannot lead the blind.
If someone wants to work with people they have to understand people. Therefore it is incredibly
important that the leader understands themselves since the way they are will have an impact on
others.
Self understanding also has a huge impact on people development. It is important that the people
who wants to lead others know how to help people grow in the right way. Company E’s leadership
development uses a quote from Kirkegaard the Danish philosopher that puts it like this:
In order truly to help someone else, I must understand more than he–but certainly ﬁrst and foremost
understand what he understands.
I believe this is why company A and E use personality tests. These tests helps the leader understand
themselves but also helps them understand others and that in turn allows them to help others.
When the leader then understands themselves and understand people to a certain degree conﬂict
handling becomes much easier. This is also something all the companies have described either direct
or indirect as a reason for the focus on people in the leadership development.
When a leader understands someone it is much easier to communicate with them in a way that
avoids misunderstandings. Many interpersonal problems are based on simple misunderstandings. If
the leader is aware of how the coworker thinks and reacts they can convey the message to them in a

A concrete example of this is seen in Company C’s leadership development in the existentials. If

6.1.2
Culture
When talking about leadership culture all the companies also had some common goals that they
wanted to achieve through their leadership development courses. These goals would be expressed in
diﬀerent ways but the princples were the same.
First and most importantly the companies wanted a behavioral change from the leaders after the
courses. This would look diﬀerent depending the company since they have diﬀerent leadership mod-
els but they all wanted the leaders to have some form of behavioral changed. It is not enough with
just a thought process change. The leaders need to change their approach.
This change is one of the most diﬃcult to achieve for the companies since it is very easy for the leader
to fall back on what they are used to when they return to their job. The way to make sure this does
not happen is to develop a leadership culture that reminds people of the things they learned on the
course.
One of the ﬁrst ways to notice the cultural change is through the language. The leaders who have
been on the leadership development program will have the same termonology when discussing certain
leadership aspects. In company E this is shown by "the box" and in Company D it is "dolphin",
"snail" or "hippo" behavior. When people speak the same leadership language it becomes a constant
reminder of the leadership model and helps the leaders remember the models, values and intended
behavioral changes.
The courses combined with the common language also helps the company to get a shared purpose
and shared values. These will, again, be expressed in the language. If the language becomes everyday
use then the desired leadership culture will be be much easier to reach.
While the leadership culture that each company develops might be unique there are still some aspects
that all wants to share. Of course there are the people oriented focus as described above but there
is also another very important thing and that is delegating or giving the leadership development to
the leaders and not to HR.
In all the companies HR wanted the immediate manager above the leader to be the person with the
main responsibility for developing the new leaders. HR would then focus on supporting the leaders
doing the training. This supporting could be training the leaders to train leaders and do or ﬁnd
coaching when the leader needs to practice a skill their immediate leader does not know.
This model of HR is also described by Sørensen (2011) as the most ideal way for HR to work. This
allows the immediate leader who knows the less experienced leader best to help them grow. The
experienced leader will ﬁnd it easier to challenge the less experienced leader in ways that makes them
grow. It also helps the experienced leader to grow themselves.

7.3.1
The leadership model
It is crucial that the leadership model is designed to accommodate growth of the leaders. This is
easiest done by having a goal for what the company judges to be the best kind of leader for them.
The goal should be ambitions so it is diﬃcult to reach and it requires a longer development process
to get there. A goal that is easily reached will likely end up not producing as strong leaders.
It is however, also important that the goals are reachable. It should not be so diﬃcult that the leaders
do not want to try reaching it. The goal should be deﬁned in such a way that the leaders understand
why it is beneﬁcial to be there and make them want to get there.
If a leader then manages to get to the goal it is very important that the goal is deﬁned in such a way
that the leader keeps growing despite having reached the pinnacle designed by the company. This is
the reason I call it an active goal.
To make the goal active there could be implemented a certain amount of self development into the
goal. An example could be a leader at the top goes through at least two leadership books a year.
Another way to make the goal active is to include some teaching. An example on this could be that
the leader has to mentor or train at least one new leader each year or help at least two leaders advance
one leadership step. There are many ways this could be implemented it is just important that the
goal does not become a pillow for the leader and they do not get the mentality of having arrived.

The steps I mentioned as an example above refers to the second important thing that has to be
implemented in the leadership model. After designing a goal it is important to have a clear path
to that goal. That can be done by designing clear leadership steps that the leaders have to master
before they can reach the leadership goal.
With clear advancement steps it will become clear for the leader where they stand and how far they

step they can start seeking knowledge about it and train the necessary competences. An example

level, how to master them and what pitfalls to avoid there.
The clear steps also provide a good basis for evaluation, which is an important part in any leadership
model. The steps will make it possible to design questions that can point to what step the leader is
on. These evaluations should optimally be done 360 degrees with both employees, leaders at their
own level and leaders above them. This is not a big change for most of the companies since they are

an active leadership goal with clear steps, after they have accomplished this it would be relatively
easy to create questions that will examine the level the leader is on.
From the ﬁndings in the study it was very clear that understanding people should be a central part
of the leadership model. It is most often through understanding people that the leaders had greater
success as leaders. This ﬁnding is not surprising since leadership is about people after all.
The last thing that is important to incorporate in the model is time to develop people. Develop-
ing people secures the future of the company and expands the capacity of the coworkers which in
turn makes them more productive. This is also another reason why it is important to have people
understanding in the leadership model since it is almost impossible to develop people if you do not
understand them.
The reason I have put develop coworkers into the leadership model area is that it has to be expected
of the leaders. If it is not expected that they set time aside to develop people the ﬁndings show that
most leaders will not get it done. This is because it is not an immediate threat compared to the next
deadline. To make sure that the company actually develops its employees it has to prioritized in the
model.
A practical way to prioritize development of coworker would be to be having a certain amount of
hours that the leader has to spend developing their coworkers. By having a concrete number it be-
comes easy to ask the leader whether they have done it and it gives the leader a reason to prioritize
it.
Having development of coworkers as a requirement is also a good way to make sure that the goal of
the leadership development is active. There should be an element of this on every step so the leaders
get used to doing this as part of the job. Having something active on each step will allow the leaders
to understand that no matter where they are they cannot just stagnate but have to keep learning
and improving.

7.4
Risks associated with the 7 principles
It is important to note that while these principles are based on a mixture of theory and empirical
data, this model has not been tested and may not yield the results hoped for. It is therefore very
important to measure the results of the leadership development if the companies want to implement
these principles in their leadership development model.
Ideally a study will be made to test the eﬀectiveness of the principles.
This could be done by
comparing the leadership level and maturity of a company that uses many of the principles in their
leadership model with a company that uses few or none at all. Finding these companies is likely
very diﬃcult and this study might therefore be diﬃcult. Another study that could show the eﬀects
is implementing the principles in a company that does not have them and then observe how the
company changes. This will, however, be a very long study which makes it diﬃcult.
There is no proof that implementing the 7 principles in the leadership development model will bring
better leadership. The principles are however based on what the leaders of the companies have found
useful in their experience as leaders and have been designed to accommodate common challenges and
lead to the desired leadership culture that all the companies wanted.
A very important aspect to mention of the principles is that they are very dependent on the leadership
model the company uses.
If the company uses a model that is unsuited for making leadership
development steps, the steps made can actually harm the company’s leadership development instead
of helping it. If a company ﬁnds themselves at a place where the principles are impossible to implement
in a constructive way they should take a look at the model and ask these questions:
• Is our company already accomplishing what it wants through its leadership development pro-
gram?
• How would this/these principles support the company’s values and goals?
• Could the leadership development program become better by implementing this/these princi-
ples?
• Is it necessary to expand out leadership development model with new theory to incorporate a
principle we want?
• Should the company change leadership development model?
Hopefully these questions would help a company to judge whether these principles are useful for the
company and how to adapt them to ﬁt into the company culture.
If a company judges that these principles would be beneﬁcial for them there might still be reasons

7.5.1
Selection of Companies
When I selected potential companies I used the book "pejling". Since it is a guide book for a job
conference north of Copenhagen, most of the companies are from the Copenhagen area. This was also
something I decided to prioritize since I am located in the Copenhagen area and that would make it
easier for me to collect the data. Four out of my Five companies where located in the Copenhagen
area and one was located in Jutland.
The location is important because diﬀerent locations can mean diﬀerent cultures. Denmark is, how-
ever, so small that this does not pose a big threat. There are diﬀerences in the local culture between
Zealand and Jutland but the diﬀerences are so small that they can be neglected.
Another concern regarding choice of companies is the type of company. There are many diﬀerent
kinds of engineering companies so it is important to get a wide range of diﬀerent kinds of companies.
In my study I did not have two companies that did the same thing so under the circumstances I do
not believe that this could have been done better. To improve on the variety I would have needed
more companies. I would preferred to have an IT company too but none of the ones I asked could
participate in the project.

Conclusion
In this study I set out to examine what Danish engineering companies do to develop their leaders.
To answer this question I started looking at leadership theory to get background information on the
topic and understand useful leadership development models.
At the same time I studied the theory I also conducted interviews with 5 diﬀerent companies to
understand what leadership development looked like in practices. The data suggested that the main
focus of leadership development lies on the soft values, in particular how to understand oneself and
how to understand people. It was also these values in particular the leaders pointed out as being the
most valuable for their own leadership.
Through the interviews the companies described some common challenges that the leadership devel-
opment faced. The main challenges were concerning evaluation, getting the leaders integrate and use
what they learned on the courses and reach a leadership culture where the leaders are the ones doing
the development.
I then combined the knowledge from the theory, the good practices and challenges to make 7 principles
for good leadership development. These principles are designed to build on the strengths discovered
in the companies, mitigate or remove the challenges and to make the company able to reach the
desired leadership culture. The principles are:
• Self development
• Understanding people
• Developing coworkers
• Leader group meetings
• Clear advancement steps
• An active goal
• Evaluation
After presenting the 7 principles I discussed where they should be applied and who had the respon-
sibility for them. The principles that should be worked directly into the leadership model of the
companies are all but leader group meetings and self development. Having designed a leadership
model with these principles should create clarity for leaders on how to grow and motivate them to
do so. It should also make it easier for HR to monitor and motivate the leaders.
The leaders have responsibility for the four principles, self development, understanding people, de-
velop coworkers and Leader group meetings. If a person wants to be a better leader they should
be proactive in these areas since most of them have been proven to help leaders in the companies
interviewed and it will help create the leadership culture that the companies desire.
If the model is well designed and the leaders take responsibility for their own growth the HR should
then work as a catalyst for the growth. They should support the leaders by arranging the leadership
meetings and make sure the leaders ﬁnd time to develop their coworkers. They are also responsible
for evaluating the leaders and follow-up on the results.
It is judged that these principles have the potential to make good leadership development if imple-
mented correctly. The implementation is, however, very dependent on the organization. For good
implementation the leadership model the organization uses has to be able to accommodate for these
principles. Implementing the 7 principles also requires a mature leadership culture where the leaders
are ready to commit to the changes. It is important to note that all of the companies interviewed,
have implemented at least three of the 7 principles already
The report concludes that Danish leadership development practices are good when compared to other
countries, based on Maxwell’s 5 levels of leadership. There is however still room for improvement.
This report suggest that it can come about by including the 7 principles but companies doing this
