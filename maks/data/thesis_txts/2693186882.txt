Abstract 
 
This master thesis focuses on risk management and post-market surveillance which play 
key role in proper functioning of the organization and helps the organization in producing 
high quality products and services. Medical devices and medical technology are one of the 
delicate industries, so risk management and post-market surveillance has more importance. 
However, With the EU MDR regulations coming to the picture from 26th May 2021, in 
medical devices and medical technology, post-market surveillance will be widely used. And 
this new regulation mandates all the companies to perform risk management and post-
market surveillance. Nevertheless, only compliance reasons, are not enough for a company 
to perform proper risk management and post-market surveillance, to create interest and 
motivation it is also important to see the business opportunities in performing risk 
management and post-market surveillance. So, this thesis aims to investigate to show the 
role of risk management and post-market surveillance, how  new regulations are going to 
affect the existing processes, and how performing these activities creates business 
opportunities. 
 
Keywords: Risk Management, Post-Market Surveillance, EU MDR regulations, 
Requirements Management.

1.1 Motivation 
 
There is a personal motivation behind writing this thesis. It started back when I was 
previously working as a consultant in a consulting firm, and my client then was one of the 
leading medical device & MedTech companies in the world. I was working for their Quality 
Control and Quality Assurance department. I was performing the risk management and 
post-market surveillance activities. We were trained for performing these activities and we 
were educated on the points like “adverse event”, “reportability” and also about the “high 
penalty to the company in cases of adverse event’s late reportability” during the knowledge 
transfer phase. Then  I understood how important it is to perform risk management and 
post-market surveillance activities in medical devices and the MedTech sectors. 
Furthermore, how carefully these activities need to be performed, and how a small mistake 
in performing these activities can lead to adverse events and considerable penalties to the 
company. All this I got to know from my personal experience. This created an interest in 
me to delve more into the risk management and post-market surveillance field. So, I have 
chosen to write my master thesis in this forte.

4. Literature Review 
 
4.1 Risk Management 
 
If we don’t know what risk is then we can’t do risk management [5].  
So, what is risk?  
Risk : “Risk is measurable uncertainty; uncertainty is unmeasurable risk” [6]. Risk has 
many definitions from different sources. According to ISO 31000 (which is a standard for 
risk management), “risk is the effect of uncertainty on objectives” [7]. Whereas PMBOK, 
2000 edition defines risk as “an uncertain event or condition, that if it occurs, has 
positive or negative effect on a project objective” [8]. From my research what I have 
understood about risk is that, “risk is an event that impacts an organization”  in layman’s 
terms. Basically, there are three components of risk; 
1. Risk, itself. 
2. Likelihood of the risk to happen. 
3. Consequence of the risk if it happens . 
To put these three components in an equation;  
Risk = Likelihood × Consequence [9] 
As mentioned earlier, risk has both positive impacts like creating opportunities that brings 
benefit to the project or organization [10] and negative impacts like damaging the outcome 
of the project or spoiling the goodwill of the organization. If it the earlier one then it’s well 
and good but the problem arise when it is the latter one. Also, risk is always a foreseen 
==PAGE:37==
28 
 
event that might or might not happen. So, to tackle the consequences of the risk, it needs 
to be assessed and managed properly and this is where risk assessment and risk 
management comes into the picture. 
 
How to manage risk?  
Risk management : A systematic process of identifying, analyzing, and responding to 
risk is known as risk management, according to PMBOK [8] and risk assessment is a 
part of risk management. Marquette University Risk Unit states that risk management is a 
continuing process to identify, analyze, evaluate, treat and monitor [11]. And risk 
assessment  is the processes and technologies used for identification, evaluation and 
reporting risk [11]. In ISO 31000, risk management is referred to a coordinated set of 
activities and methods that is used to direct an organization and to control the many 
risks that can affect its ability to achieve objectives [7]. It is also mentioned in the ISO 
31000 that risk management is a programme that is composed of processes, framework, 
and principles. 
 
Risk management process: According to PMBOK [8], risk management process consists 
of 6 steps: 
1. Planning risk management. 
2. Identification of risk. 
3. Qualitative analysis of risk. 
4. Quantitative analysis of risk. 
5. Mitigating risk. 
6. Monitoring and controlling the risk. 
==PAGE:38==
29 
 
The initial step in risk management process is to plan to identify the different risk 
management approaches. The step 2 is to identify the actual risks and it’s sources. Step 3 
is qualitative assessment where risk matrix and other qualitative techniques are used to 
prioritize risk. Step 4 is quantitative assessment which is more model based techniques 
used to prioritize risk. Step 3 and step 4 can be combined together for ranking risk [12]. 
Then comes step 5 risk mitigation which is basically planning response actions towards 
risk. And finally step 6 monitoring and controlling risk which includes activities like follow-
ups and reporting of risk [8], [13]. 
 
Risk management framework : Risk management processes consists of all the steps, 
techniques, and tools to carry out risk management. But an organization also needs a high-
level overview and methods which can explained through a framework [14]. Risk 
management consist of 5 key elements [15]: 
1. Risk identification. 
2. Measuring risk. 
3. Risk mitigation. 
4. Risk monitoring and reporting. 
5. Risk governance. 
It might seem like risk management process and framework is the same because there are 
both interlinked. Where the framework provides an overview of the system, processes 
include the actual working model of the framework. 
 
==PAGE:39==
30 
 
Risk management approach : There are also different risk responses approaches and 
strategies which are implemented to response to the identified risks. They are as follows 
[16];  
1. Avoid. 
2. Reduce. 
3. Share. 
4. Retain.  
 
➢ Avoid: In most cases the risk can be avoided by forfeiting the activities that lead to 
the risk. But doing so there is good chance of loosing an opportunity as it never sure 
if that risk is going to happen. 
➢ Reduce: There are some cases where it not possible to avoid the risk but the effect 
or weight of the risk can be reduced by manipulated some activities. 
➢ Share: One organization or department baring the whole loss due to risk is too 
much, so in some cases the loss or the consequences happen due to risk is shared 
among different organizations involved in that activity or process or it is shared 
between different departments within the organization. 
➢ Retain: In this case the outcome of the risk is calculated, and the organization 
compares the lose and gain from that risk. If the gain is more compared to the loss 
happens due to the risk, then the organization tends to accept and retain the risk.  
 
Risk management principles : Till now we have covered the risk management processes, 
framework and strategies. Now come the risk management principles which is also very 
important while delving more into risk management. There are  11 risk management 
principles as stated in ISO 31000 [17]. They are as follows; 
1. It should create and protect the value.

3. It should be an integral part of the organizational processes. 
4. It should be tailored. 
5. It should eb part of decision-making. 
6. It should always take cultural factors and human factors into consideration. 
7. It should address uncertainties clearly. 
8. It should be completely transparent and be very much comprehensive. 
9. It should be structured, timely and systematic. 
10. It should assist in continuous improvement of the organization. 
11. It should responsive, iterative and dynamic to change.

Risk assessment methods : To perform risk management, risk assessment is the initial 
step and there are different methods of risk assessment. Some of the major risk assessment 
methods are as follow [18]; 
• What-if analysis 
• Fault Tree Analysis (FTA) 
• Failure Mode Event Analysis (FMEA) 
• Hazard Operability Analysis (HAZOP) 
• Incident BowTie 
• Event tree analysis 
 
==PAGE:41==
32 
 
What-If analysis  
This method involves brainstorming and examination to determine possible deviation or 
what can go wrong in the actual process. 
 
Fault Tree Analysis (FTA) 
It is a top-down vertical graphic model to show all the events and combination of events 
that can lead to a risk. In this method the top level is represents the whole system and from 
there it goes backward in time while going down.  
 
Failure Mode Event Analysis (FMEA) 
It is a step-by-step approach for identifying all the possible events that can lead to risk in 
a process. It is one of the widely used methods to prevent failures. 
 
Hazard operability analysis (HAZOP) 
It is a structure and systematic technique used for identifying both potential hazard and 
operable issues. 
 
Incident BowTie 
It is a combination of two analysis methods; BowTie analysis and Tripod incident analysis. 
It is used to get the best out of both the methods, where the inputs from BowTie analysis 
is used as input for incident analysis to cover all the possible scenario and the input from 
Tripod incident analysis is used to make the BowTie analysis more realistic [18]. 
 
==PAGE:42==
33 
 
Event Tree analysis 
It is the opposite of fault tree analysis. Where fault tree analysis is a top-down vertical 
graphic model method, event tree analysis is a bottom-up horizontal graphic method.

Risk management is important in every organization because it helps in defining the

4.2 Post-Market Surveillance  
 
In earlier section we have seen how risk management is important in all the organizations. 
And we have also seen the process, framework, and approaches. Now let’s delve into one 
of the important steps of risk management i.e., monitoring. 
In risk monitoring, all the identified risks are tracked and then evaluated to check the level 
of risk in an organization and in turn improves the risk management process [21].  
Risk monitoring can be divided into two phase, phase 1 before the product, device or 
software enters into the market (pre-market) and phase 2 once the product, device or 
software is out in the market and the customers or consumers are using it (post-market).    
==PAGE:43==
34 
 
Because this thesis concentrates on medical devices, medtech and pharmaceutical (life 
science), so this section focuses on risk monitoring more in these sectors.  
 
Pre-Market analysis : Pre-Market analysis is the process of evaluating and review the 
product before launching it into the market to identify all the possible failures. This is done 
based on a number of trials and tests and based on the results of this analysis the 
organization or the notified bodies decides on launching the product [22], so it also aids 
in decision making. Pre-Market analysis places a vital role in reducing the weight of risk 
before the customer or consumer is affected because of the product. It adds input to the 
design and development of the product. 
 
Post-Market Surveillance or monitoring : It is the process of continuously monitoring the 
product which is launched into the market and being used by the customers or consumers 
[22]. In this process, the failures are not identified instead the adverse events, failures, 
non-conformities are recorded, and this data is used for improving the product in the future. 
And this process also helps in reporting the non-conformities to the notified bodies for 
example: FDA, etc. The definition of post-market surveillance changes with the standards 
the organization or company follows. FDA defines post-market surveillance as “the active 
data collection, analysis, interpretation of a marketed device” [23].  
 
 
Post-Market Surveillance plan : Just like the definition, the process of performing post-
market surveillance also changes based on the company and the jurisdiction in which it is. 
==PAGE:44==
35 
 
Following the PMS plan is the normal accepted structure [24]. And the post-market 
surveillance plan is as follows [25]: 
• Specifying all the activities. 
• Specify the sources from where the information is collected. 
• Specify which information is collected. 
• How the collected information is evaluated. 
• And this information is transferred. 
• What actions need to be taken.  
 
Importance of Post-Market Surveillance :  Post-Market surveillance is important in every 
medical device company as the data collected from this is used for many activities. They 
are  [26] : 
• For updating of benefit-risk determination and to improve the risk management 
process. 
• For updating of product design and manufacturing information and this information 
is used for labelling the product. 
• For updating clinical evaluations. 
• For updating clinical and safety performance. 
• To  identify corrective and preventive actions (CAPA). 
• To identify the different ways of improving the performance, safety and usability of 
the product. 
• The data from the post-market surveillance of one device can be used for other 
devices if relevant. 
• To identify the trends  and report it . 
All this is listed in the EU MDR regulation, chapter 7, section 1, arcticle  83 (3). 
==PAGE:45==
36 
 
In addition to the above-mentioned points, post-market surveillance also plays an important 
role in medical device companies during the approvals from notified bodies, i.e., getting CE 
mark on their devices etc [27]. 
 
Post-Market Surveillance approach : Medical device companies uses a number of post-
market surveillance approaches for monitoring and reporting purposes like; spontaneous 
reporting database, prescription-event reporting, electronic health records, patient’s registry, 
etc [28]. 
Finally post-market surveillance creates a loop in risk management process as it collects 
data from the customers or consumers and this data is used as an input for risk 
management planning. 
 
This states how important and necessary it is to perform post-market surveillance and how 
it is performed. And this answers the research question 1.2 and second part of research

4.3 Quality Management 
 
Quality management : Quality management is the systematic process of looking into and 
maintaining all the activities in an organization to maintain the performance of that 
organization [29]. Sometimes It is also considered as Total Quality Management (TQM).

Quality management system (QMS) : Quality management system is defined as system 
that is used for documentation of all the processes, procedures, responsibilities that are

9000:2015 defines all the requirements for quality management system. And it states that 
QMS should always be able to meet the customer needs and demands [31]. 
 
Principles of quality management : ISO 9001:2015, one of the world’s widely used 
standard states the following 7 principles of quality management system [32]. 
1. Customer focus. 
2. Leadership. 
3. Engagement of people. 
4. Process approach. 
5. Continuous improvement. 
6. Evidence-based decision making.  
7. Relationship management. 
 
Benefits of quality management : Implementing a well define quality management system

• It ensures that the customer requirements are meet and improves customer 
satisfaction. 
• It ensures that the organizations objectives are fulfilled and business effectively in 
the market. 
• It helps in continuous improvement of the organization.

4.4 Requirements Management 
 
Requirement : Every organization aims to sell their product or service to the customer or 
consumer and this this process involves a number of business process and each business 
process have it its own individual needs which is known as requirement [35].   
 
Requirements Management : Requirements management is simply the process of proper 
management of these requirements. In other word requirements management can be 
defined as the set of activities carried out to manage the requirements throughout the 
lifecycle of the product or service in order to meet the quality of the product or service and 
also meet the customer needs and customer satisfaction [35]. IBM states that, 
“requirements management is the set of techniques to document requirements, analyze 
them, prioritize and agree upon those requirements” [36].   
 
Requirements management plan (RMP) : As mentioned earlier, requirement management 
takes place throughout the lifecycle of the product or service, so there should be a concrete 
plan and process to carry out requirements management. This risk management plan helps 
the organization to understand how and from where the initial information is received, how 
==PAGE:50==
41 
 
to analyze the information and document it [36]. Requirements management plan consist 
of requirement management process, roles and responsibilities, types of requirements, etc. 
 
Requirements management process : Requirements management process consist of the 
following 9 steps. 
1. Identifying the stakeholders. 
2. Gathering initial requirements. 
3. Analyzing the gathered requirements. 
4. Record the requirements 
5. Verify, validate, and prioritize requirements. 
6. Communicating the requirements. 
7. Tracing the requirements. 
8. Controlling the requirements. 
9. Report all the requirements. 
It is important to document this whole process because it helps in making the process of 
requirements management smooth and also to make sure that the same process can be 
used for all the projects within the organization. And this also creates transparency among 
stakeholders, departments and projects.  
These steps can be further simplified into 5 phases: requirements gathering, analyzing 
requirements, defining requirements, prioritizing these requirements, and finally validating 
and maintaining the requirements [37].  
 
Types of requirements : The requirements are categories into 3 types based on the focus, 
source of the requirement and outcome or the requirements. They are : 
==PAGE:51==
42 
 
➢ Business requirements 
Business requirement are the needs to satisfy the internal and external customers, they are 
not something that is related to the product or service itself. But in some cases these 
business requirements might coincide with the products. These requirements are stated by 
the top management, business analyst, etc. [37]. These requirements are more business 
focused and the outcome of the requirements helps in business expansion. 
Examples of business requirements: how to establish multi-cultural discipline in the 
organization, identify different markets.  
 
➢ User or stakeholder requirements 
These requirements are stated by the user or stakeholder. It deals with what the user or 
customer needs to interact with the product and how the customer interacts with the product. 
It focusses both on the business and technical side [37]. 
Example of user requirements: Installing a register in the system as the customer would 
like to log all the entries. 
 
➢ System or solution requirements  
System requirements are completely technical. These requirements emerge from the 
product or service, like what technologies needs to be used, etc. These requirement aid in 
fulfilling the needs of the product and also to improve them [37]. 
Example of system requirement: updating to software to eliminate bugs.

4.5 Medical devices & MedTech 
 
As this thesis is more focused on medical sectors i.e., medical devices and medical 
technologies, so a brief description of them is provided in this section. 
Medical devices : FDA define medical device as “an instrument, apparatus, implant, 
implement, machine, in vitro reagent, contrivance, or similar or related article, including 
a component part or accessory which is recognized in United States pharmacopoeia, 
official national formulary or any supplement to them” [38]. These products or devices 
are used for examination, diagnostics and treating diseases, injuries, and disabilities [39]. 
These devices can be used on both humans and animals. 
 
Types of medical devices : There are over 500,000 different types of medical devices 
ranging from a small surgical suture to pacemakers. There are all classified into 4 classes 
based on risk [40]: 
 
1. Class I  
They are low risk medical devices which don’t require any special approvals. For example: 
thermometer, etc. 
2. Class IIa  
They are medium risk medical devices and need a 510K For example: hearing aid, etc. 
3. Class IIb 
==PAGE:54==
45 
 
They are medium to high-risk devices which also require 510K. For example: X-ray 
machine, etc. 
4. Class III  
These are very high-risk devices, and they need premarket approval. For example: 
pacemaker, etc. 
In most case the medical devices are physical products. 
 
Medical Technology : There is misconception that both medical device and medical 
technology is the same. They are not completely same but similar. Medical technology is a 
combination of medical device and technology [41]. In other words it can be defined as a 
application of technology to develop the medical device, that collects data and converts that 
data into knowledge [42]. Medical technologies comprises of both physical devices and 
also the software and both need CE mark for entering into the market. 
 
4.6 Standards & Regulations 
 
This section presents all the standards and regulations involved in medical devices. 
 
ISO 9001:2015 
ISO 9001:2015 specifies requirements for a quality management system (QMS). It applies 
to all the organizations irrespective of the size of the organization that means it applies to 
medical devices as well. This standard helps the organization in organizing processes, 
==PAGE:55==
46 
 
improving the efficiency of the processes, continuously improves the processes and also 
saves cost to the organization [43]. 
 
ISO 13485:2016 
ISO 13485:2016 specifies both regulatory requirements and customer requirements for a 
quality management system of an organization. This standard is applicable to all the medical 
device companies irrespective of their size [44].  
 
ISO 14971:2019 
ISO 14971 specifies the requirements of best practices of risk management throughout the 
life cycle of a medical device. There are 3 versions of ISO 14971; ISO 14971:2007, ISO 
14971:2012, and ISO 14971:2019. Both ISO 13485 and ISO 14971 are interrelated, as 
ISO 14971 is the more detailed in risk management requirements, ISO 13485 takes 
guidance from it [3].  
 
FDA (Food and Drug Administration) 
FDA is a United States federal agency that is a part of Department of Health and Human 
Services. It is one of the top notified bodies in the world whose responsibility is to protect 
public health by making sure the medical devices are safe and secure for humans and the 
food supply and cosmetics are safe for human consumption and use [45].   
All the class III medical devices, food items and cosmetics needs to be approved by FDA 
before pre-marketing. All the manufacturers must report when an incident is occurred 
because of their product or device with and this reporting should be done with 30 calender 
==PAGE:56==
47 
 
days [46].  Even if the device is class I and lead to a failure or adverse event it should be 
reported to the FDA. 
 
MDD (Medical Device Directives) & MDR (Medical Device Regulation) 
A directive puts certain results that needs to be obtained by each member state, but they 
are free to decide how to switch these directives into national laws. 
Whereas a regulation is more binding, it has legal forces on all the member states and 
enter into force in all member states on set date [47]. 
 
In European Union. All the medical devices need to follow some guidelines in order to get 
approved for EU market and receive the CE marking which allow the companies to market 
their products. These guidelines includes  MDD and MDR. 
Medical Device Directive (MDD) applies to all the medical devices that doesn’t fall under 
active implantable medical device directive and In vitro diagnostic medical device directive 
[48]. MDD was first published in June 1993 and again revised in March 2010 [49]. Its 
been almost close to 30 years so MDD is outdated and also because it is a directive it the 
member states are free to choose how to scope it. 
So to make this MDD more strict MDR (medical device regulation) is adopted on 26th may 
2017. MDR is stricter version of MDD. It has all the requirements same as that of MDD 
with some additional requirements like in MDD active implantable medical devices are not 
included but in MDR they are included. The transition from MDD to MDR is started on 26th 
May 2017 and finished on 26th May 2021 and by 26th May 2024 all the medical devices 
needs to be MDR confirmed [49], [50].

6.1 Research question 1 
“What role does both Risk Management and Post-Market Surveillance play in Quality 
management of medical device and med-tech industries?” 
As this research question and the sub questions are more theoretical based so to answer 
this particular research question, I have taken the data from literature review which 
comprises the information collected from various articles, journals and paper.  
 
• RQ 1.1: What is Risk management, and what are the types of risk 
management activities? 
During the research process I have identified that every human performs risk management 
in his life voluntarily or involuntarily, for example: planning monthly expenses, buying a new 
and expensive product, or investing in stocks, etc. In all these actives a person does some 
sort of risk management as it helps in decision making. Just like risk management, knowing 
what is risk that you are going to face is equally important as without knowing the risk you 
cannot perform risk management.  
==PAGE:77==
68 
 
In the section 4.1 of literature review we have seen risk management is one of the important 
step or process that every organization need to perform. Risk management is a key factor 
for delivering quality product or service. We have also seen in its benefits that risk 
management not only helps in identifying the failures but and prepares the organization to 
mitigate the risk but also helps in reducing the impact of the risk.  
In all the cases, the basic risk management activities always remain the same, i.e. you 
need to identify all the risks, then analysis the all those risks to categorize them in order to 
know which risks are more critical and which needs immediate attention, the plan on how 
to tackle the risks, whether to face accept the risk or avoid it, then you track those risks 
long the process of the risk management to monitor its deviation and also continuous 
tracking will help in anticipating the outcome of that risk and finally controlling it so that it 
does not create any more incidents. 
 
• RQ 1.2: What is Post-Market Surveillance, and how is it performed? 
It is a good thing that all the organizations perform risk management to manage and control 
the risk before delivering the product so the product or service that is being delivered is of 
high quality but what if the failure or incident happens after the product is out in the market 
and some event happens while the customer or consumer is using the product. It is the 
responsibility of the company to make sure the product works as intended throughout of the 
lifetime of that product. The same is mentioned in section 4.2, which states that the tracking 
and controlling of risks after the product leaves for the customer. 
The companies employ various methods to collect the data on the usage of the products 
by the customers like feedback forms, rating system, etc. This information is again used 
back by the organization for improving the quality of future products. Post-Market 
surveillance acts a link between customer and R&D department. 
==PAGE:78==
69 
 
 
 
• RQ 1.3: How Risk Management and Post-Market Surveillance are related to 
quality management? 
In section 4.3, we can see that only performing risk management and post-market 
surveillance is not enough. There should be a proper system to record and document all 
these processes. There should be an integrated system which binds all the processes and 
that is what a quality management system does. It connects risk management, post-market 
surveillance and other process for the organization to understand the processes and 
changes easily. 
We have seen that to provide the customer or consumer with the best quality product, the 
organization need to maintain the quality of product in both pre-market and post-market. 
So in order to do that all the processes needs to be in coordination with each other and 
QMS does that work for an organization.  
  
• RQ 1.4: How Risk Management and Post-Market Surveillance are performed in 
medical devices and med-tech industries?  
From sections 4.1 and 4.2 we have seen that irrespective of the type or size of the industry 
risk management and post-market surveillance are performed the same. So in a medical 
device or med-tech industry the process of identify, analyzing, planning, tracking and 
controlling of the risk is the same and also the monitoring of the product when it is released 
into the market and using the feedback information back into the product remains the same. 
But the only thing that I would like to mention here based on the research and my dialogs 
with the subject matter expects is that, medical sector (medical devices and med-tech) is 
==PAGE:79==
70 
 
one of the delicate industry compared to others and the products of these industry are likely 
to have more contact with the human and have high chances of effecting human’s life. So 
there are many standard and regulations that are imposed on these two industries on how 
carefully and mandatorily medical device and med-tech industries need to perform risk 
management and post-market surveillance. 
 
6.2 Research question 2 
 
Research question 2 (RQ 2): “How the Risk Management and Post-Market Surveillance 
will be affected by the new EU MDR?” 
To answer this research question, I have taken the data from literature review, interviews 
and questionnaire to present my implications. 
 
• 
RQ 2.1: What is MDR, and what are the new EU MDR regulations? 
As mentioned earlier, medical devices are one of delicate products in the market (delicate 
based on the risk impact). So normal rules and regulations are not enough to control the    
quality of the medical devices. They need more strict regulations. That is why we have seen 
in section 4.6 that medical device directive is transitioned to medical device regulation, 
which is imposes more rock-solid regulation on the medical devices. And the new 
regulations also states that the medical devices cannot enter the market unless they receive 
a CE marking. 
 
==PAGE:80==
71 
 
• 
RQ 2.2: How is MDR connected to Risk Management and Post-Market 
Surveillance? 
During my interviews and data from the questionnaires, the interviewees have mentioned 
that in some companies, risk management and post-market surveillance are only checkmark 
activities. So, these activities are just done once and they are never revisited. And 
sometimes even these activities are not performed perfectly which results in adverse events 
and penalties to the companies. With MDR into picture medical devices companies need to 
perform risk management and post-market surveillance in more serious fashion. This new 
regulations improves the quality of risk management and post-market surveillance and also 
keeps track of these two.  
 
6.3 Research question 3 
Research question 3 (RQ3): “How can Risk Management and Post-Market Surveillance 
be a good business opportunity in any industry, especially in medical devices and med-
tech industries?” 
To answer this research question, I have used the information form interviews and 
questionnaire and I have divided this answer into 2 parts, one focusing on what is the 
monetary loss of not performing risk management and post-market surveillance and the 
other focuses on creating a requirements management for developing a tool.  
As stated earlier they are few companies that just consider risk management and post 
market surveillance as checkmark tasks so either they are performed incorrectly or they are 
not performed completely. And this might not be a big issue in then but during audits this 
will be a nightmare. And the new MDR pours nitros into the fire because if risk management 
and post-market surveillance are not performed properly, the medical device does not

customer base initially which leads to monetary loss. 
In an era of digitalization, documents with paper and pen takes too much time and energy.
