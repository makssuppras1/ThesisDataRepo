Abstract 
 
 
This report concerns with the design of 61 meters long roadbridge 
over the new Frederikssund Motorway M12. 
The project is a static calculations and to prove bearing capacity of 
the bridge in accordance with the current Eurocode. The target is to 
produce a report, which proves the bearing capacity of all structural 
members, by using a new finite element analysis program ( LUSAS ). 
 
The project includes a static system and robustness description. A 
speciﬁcation of the load distribution for vertical and horizontal loads 
is made. Subsequently, an establishment of the assumptions for 
static calculations takes place. Norms are chosen and materiel 
parameters are deﬁned. The bearing capacity of the bridge will be 
veriﬁed for load combinations for both service limit state and 
ultimate limit state, and the bearing capacity is veriﬁed for bridge 
deck, walls and columns. 
 
Finally, the bridge deck will be designed in the new and 
revolutionizing concrete technology (light concert), A new and 
revolutionizing concrete technology, in which the principles from 
such a simple thing as a pearl chain is used as the basis of a whole 
new way of constructing highway bridges. This technology makes it 
possible to assemble the bridge on site and then be lifted into place. 
This does not only mean lower building expenses, but also the fact 
that the existing traﬃc will not be aﬀected by the ongoing 
construction to same extend as if it was a traditional bridge 
construction. Moreover these bridges do notrequire the same 
maintenance attention and are more durable than regular bridges, 
and last but not least these bridges require less materials and 
thereby they are emitting less CO2.

1 Introduction 
 
1.1  Location 
 
The bridge is an overpass of the municipality road Ledøjetoften in Smørum. It crosses the M12 
Frederikssund Motorway.  
 
 
1.2  Structure type 
 
The structure has been designed as post-tensioned concrete slab supported on three intermediate 
wall columns made of reinforced concrete. The deck is connected to the walls by means of concrete 
hinges. At the ends the deck is supported with three columns on each side with elastomeric 
bearings. The model is presented in Figure 1-1 
 
 
 
 
Figure1-1: AutoCAD visualization of the structure

presented in Figure 1-2

1.3 Finite Element Analysis System (LUSAS)  
 
LUSAS Bridge is a world-leading finite element analysis software application for the analysis, design 
and assessment of all types of bridge structures, and its can solve all types of linear and nonlinear 
stress, dynamics, composite and thermal engineering analysis problem. The two main components 
of the system are: 
 
 
LUSAS Modeller – a fully interactive graphical user interface for model building and viewing 
of results from an analysis. 
 
LUSAS Solver - a powerful finite element analysis engine that carries out the analysis of the 
problem defined in LUSAS Modeller.

In order to handle torsional moments, the torsional constant J has been reduced to 1.0% of their 
nominal values. This approach forces the model to spread the torsional moments into both 
orthogonal directions as additional components of bending moments in both directions. This allows 
the torsional moment to be handled however, this causes a more conservative distribution of loads 
in the transverse direction. For this reason a local slight exceeding (up to 5%) of utilization under 
characteristic and frequent combinations of actions has not been recognized as an error and has 
been disregarded. 
 
 
 
2.3.4 Walls 
 
There are three walls in the model, the thickness of the walls has been applied as 0.600 m. Between 
the wall columns and the deck there are concrete hinges fully released at rotation about the 
transverse axis. This connection has been realized by means of joints with no rotational stiffness. 
The end supports (three on each side) have been applied with spring stifnesses corresponding to the 
elastomeric bearings behavior.

2.6 Loads 
2.6.1 Permanent 
 
The bridge has been designed according to consequences class CC3. 
The bridge must be designed based on a working life of 100 years. The first 25 years without major 
repair being necessary. Not all parts of a bridge can be designed for a working life of 100 years; these 
parts shall be designed so they easily can be replaced. Significant construction parts must be 
designed so they can be inspected.

2.6.1.1 Self weight 
 
The concrete volumetric weight has been considered as 25 kN/m3, and multiplied by the 
corresponding element thickness.

Pavement 
 
Thickness of the carriageway: 0.115 m. 
Volumetric weight: 23 kN/m3. 
 
2.6.1.3 
Safety barriers 
 
Linear intensity: 1.0 kN/m.  
 
 
2.6.1.4 
Settlement 
 
Value of settlements: 0.01 m between adjacent foundations. 
The effects of the settlements have been considered with the concrete elastic modulus as for the 
long term.

2.6.3.5 
Braking and acceleration forces 
 
A braking force Qlk has been taken as a longitudinal force acting at the surfacing level of the 
carriageway. The characteristic value of Qlk, limited to 900 kN for the total width of the bridge, has 
been calculated as a fraction of the total maximum vertical loads corresponding to the LM1 likely to 
be applied on Lane Number 1, as follows: 
 
 
Qlk = 0.6Q1(2Q1k) + 0.10q1q1kw1L 
 
Q1 : Justeringsfaktor      = 1.00 
Q1k  : Aksellast                = 300 kN 
q1  : Justeringsfaktor      = 0.67 
q1k   : Bevægeliglast        = 9.00 kN/m2 
w1   : Width of the lane  = 3.00 m 
L     : langh of the bridg  = 62.94 m 
 
Qlk = 0.6x1.00x(2x300) + 0.10x0.67x9.00x3.00x62.94 = 360 + 114 = 474 kN 
Qlk =474 kN ∈ <180∙αQ1 (kN) ≤ Qlk ≤ 900 (kN)> 
 
 
As this type of load is relevant mainly to the substructure, the braking and acceleration force have 
been considered as acting across the whole carriageway with the following intensity: 
 
qlk = Qlk/(wtotL) = 474/16.70 = 28.4 kN/m.

2.6.5.3 
Seismic load 
A horizontal mass load is to be applies to evaluate the structure for the seismic design situation. 
 
The horizontal mass load is a load to ensure the structures strength and stability from minor ground 
motions.  
Any vertical load is assumed to be capable of giving rise to a horizontal mass load. A horizontal mass 
load is assumed to be a fixed action, and it assumed to occur together with the associated vertical 
load only. It act at the center of gravity of the associated vertical loads and are assumed to act in any 
horizontal direction. This direction is the same for all of the horizontal mass loads occurring 
simultaneously. 
 
==PAGE:27==
 
27
The seismic effects have been included at the level of substructure only. The intensity has been 
considered as 1.5% of the total dead load. 
 
 
2.6.6 
Prestressing load  
 
When determine the effects of the post tensioning tendons, creep of the concrete and relaxation of 
the prestressing steel must be taken into account. 
 
 
2.6.6.1 
Immediate losses

table contains the following data:





2.6.6.3 
Initial prestressing force considered in the design 
 
The force of 3550 kN has been considered assuming that it is after long term losses. The real initial 
force needs to be greater at least 316.9/3550 = 0.09 and therefore 10% has been considered. For the 
strength analysis, the short term effects of the prestressing force have been increased using a factor 
of 1.10. For example, if Lusas model returns a value of 100 kN then for short term effects a force of 
1.1x100 = 110 kN has been used. The value of 100 kN has been interpreted as that appeared in a 
long term period.

2.7 Load combinations 
2.7.1 Design situations 
There are two design situation considered, i.e. short term and long term. The first is considered for 
the structure being loaded at the age of 30 days and the second refers to 36500 days (100 years). 
This distinction results in different shrinkage and creep effect and consequently different concrete 
elastic modulus as well as prestress losses. Moreover, it has been assumed that in the first stage no 
settlements occur while it is fully developed in the final stage at the age of 100 years. The reason of 
not including settlements in the initial phase is that the nature of settlements has been identified as 
a long term effect, gradually appearing during the service. Therefore, all static and strength analyses 
have been carried out for these two phases and their results reflect this.

deck 4) as shown below. 
 
Figure 3-1 Division of the deck into four parts 
In order to identify particular cross section an additional local coordinate  has been introduced. This 
number represents a current location measured from the support axis divided by span length. This 
results in a range between =0.00 and =1.00. Each span has been therefore divided into 10 zones. 
This resulted in 11 points of analysis per span. 
 
3.2 Reference lines 
As the deck is wide, its thickness is variable and there is significantly uneven force distribution in the 
cross section, an additional measure has been introduced in order to identify particular point of the 
deck under consideration. The figure below shows 11 reference lines projected along the deck's 
length. The 1st reference line refers to the edge beams and the 11th represent the deck in the middle. 
In this way couples of lines (1-1, 2-2, 3-3 etc.) have been introduced and in term of the internal 
forces the results on the right side equal to those on the left side. Only the post-processing is 
affected by the side of the deck chosen to design. In such case, always that side (left or right) which 
represents the lesser thickness of the deck is considered. For example, the reference line 06 
represents the thickness of 609 mm (on the left) and 639 mm (on the right). In the design the 
representative thickness will be therefore 609 mm (on the left).

5 Design of the concrete hinges 
 
Regarding the concrete hinges, a rotation about the transverse axis and vertical force is relevant only.  
 
Figure 5-1 Modification of the support conditions regarding foundations of the concrete hinges

due to several points located in lines results in support free to rotate in the transverse direction and 
fixed in the longitudinal one.

model and are combined in SLS combinations.

7 Design of the light concert deck 
7.1 Introduction 
7.1.1 Super Light Structures 
 
Super-light structure is a new patented building technology for optimize the use of normal concrete and then 
support it with light concrete. By applying this new method you can build houses, bridges, tunnels, and ships 
with longer spans, less material-, and energy consumption, without scaﬀolding, and making the use of arches, 
vaults, domes, ribbed shells, and new architectural shapes aﬀordable and robust for ﬁre, explosion, and earth-
quake. 
 
By using this technology it’s possible to create very light concrete structures. This technology is resource saving 
in consumption of raw materials and energy. One of the important advantages of this technology is that the cost 
is often less than half that of similar structures in concrete and steel. They are heat-insulating, ﬁre-resistant and 
they 
open up the possibility for large spans and the creation of advanced shapes. 
7.1.2 Super Light Deck Element 
Blocks of light aggregate concrete are placed in a mould, and then the prestressed wires are placed, followed by 
purring the ”normal” concrete over them and thereby casting it together. It’s how the super light deck elements 
(SLD) are made. The light blocks have a shape resembling a half cylinder standing on a low box shape.See ﬁgure 
7-1. The weight of each block is 600
  
  , and the outer box dimension of 500x400x185mm. The dimensions of the 
light block can be seen in ﬁgure 7-2. 
 
 
 
 
Figure 7-1: Light Aggregate Block 
The blocks are placed in parallel rows in the slab casting mould. In this project the decks were made by 6 rows of 
400 mm making the total width 2526 mm wide. This width includes 1 time 12 mm slack reinforcement between 
the blocks, 1 wire on the outside of the end blocks, making it a total of 12 wires. In the middle of the element a 
65 mm post tensioned duct is placed.See ﬁgure 7-2. 
==PAGE:87==
 
87
 
Figure 7-2: Cross Section of SLD -Element 
 
 
Finally the mould is ﬁlled with normal concrete. Figure 7-3 shows the SLD before and after the normal concrete 
has been casted. The advantages of this method, is that the normal concrete at the top is used as compression 
zones as well as the prestressed wires in between the blocks. So the remaining areas of the element, which adds 
nothing to the overall strength, are ﬁtted with light concrete blocks. This means a decrease in weight of the 
deck. The remaining 200 mm of each block ends arto the prestressed wires. 
The light concrete unit is only visible from underneath and if only 1 wire is used, these wires are never closer 
than 60 mm from the bottom of the deck. The light concrete works extremely well as an insulator, which makes 
it very good as a ﬁre resistance.

7.1.3 Super Light Slab–arch 
 
The combination of super light PCR systems provides the optimum solution for many types of 
constructions. The objective of this project has been to design an arch bridge using SLD. These 
elements, which have already been tested and used in several projects. 
 
The bridge has a length of 60,62 meter, as supported on three intermediate wall columns. The max 
long span of the SL-deck is less than 30 meter, the SL-deck will be designed with about 12 meter 
long.  
The Slab-arch element will be build by using 6 super light deck and 2 end-blocks in each span of 
supported wall.
