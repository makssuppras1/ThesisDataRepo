PROJECT EVALUATION  
 
This project has been a long journey, where I have had to deal with a lot of challenges and practical 
problems, but at the end of the day, I think I have learned a lot of things. The original project plan was a 
little too optimistic about what I wanted to do, and as time progressed I realized that I had to make some 
serious changes in the plan.  
Looking back, I think that there is a lot of things I could have done better, especially with regard to the 
planning process.  
In the beginning phase of the project, the focus was on collecting literature and reading about the subject. 
My knowledge about PV systems was very limited, so there was a lot of things I had to read and 
understand, however I found it a little difficult to find good literature about the subject.   
My original project plan included simulation and modelling of the system with PV and battery, because I 
thought it would be a good idea to make some simulations to see if the simulated results matched up with 
the experimental results. However it soon became clear that I didn’t have a clear overview about what I 
wanted to simulate, and how to do it the right way, and therefore I spend a lot of unnecessary  time, trying 
to create simulation models of the system in Simulink, which I ended up not using at all.  After talking with 
my supervisor Per Nørgård, I decided that the simulations of the system actually wasn’t necessary and 
therefore I focused my energy on the practical part of the project, which at first was, to establish a 
connection to SYSLAB so that I could get to work with battery and PV systems. However the establishment 
of a connection to SYSLAB soon proved to be a bit of a practical challenge, which was something that I did 
not expect, and therefore did not account for in my time schedule.   
In order to control the battery and get measurement from SYSLAB, I had to use some files which served as 
an interface between MatLab and SYSLAB. The first files I got hands on, proved to be out of date, which 
took me several weeks to realize. So I had to find the right person, and he gave me the correct files.  
Other practical problems included, SYSLAB servers that went down, and SYSLAB connection problems as a 
consequence. Problems with the battery, which sometimes just went down, and then trouble shooting and 
fixing of the problem.   
The original project plan also included a case study of a PV system made by the company Balslev. But I have 
had to discard this part of the project because of time pressure. 
During the work with this project I have learned that good planning is very important, and that it is a good 
idea to be more systematic in my approach to a problem.  
Another thing I have learned is that real world systems tends to create some time consuming practical 
problems, that has to be fixed before the real work can begin.
