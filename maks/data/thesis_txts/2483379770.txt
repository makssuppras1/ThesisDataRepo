Abstract
Solar energy is characterized as a clean and environmentally friendly technology,
and also known as one of the cheapest forms of electricity generation. Both beneﬁts make
it such an attractive and promising energy supply solution in many countries.
The signiﬁcant development of solar power results that more and more potential
users, especially residents, have willingness to install their own solar system.
Therefore, this project aims to come up with a comprehensive economic assessment
of investing solar PV system with storage system in Denmark. There has been consider-
able research progress in industrial domain, but relatively rare reference for small-scale
users, the assessment in this report will emphasize household scale.
The work can be mainly divided into three parts: analyzing Levelised Cost of Elec-
tricity(LCOE) of a solar photovoltaics system, introducing a novel efﬁcient method based
on unsupervised machine learning to operate storage system, and analyzing environmen-
tal and social beneﬁts of investing solar power.
More speciﬁcally, the calculating of LCOE, in Chapter 2, is combined with some
real expenditures, so that the result is more practical. The third chapter predicts potential
cost reduction of such a solar system. Simultaneously, the simulation results show that
using machine learning algorithms can reduce the computation signiﬁcantly. In the Fourth
Chapter, some beneﬁts and impacts for environment are analyzed and discussed from a
macro perspective.

Solar Energy
With the development of global society, the energy demand has been dramatically in-
creased. The society has been more and more heavily reliant on limited fossil fuels, such
as mine, petrol, and gas. Concerns about energy crisis has been raised. Moreover, the
overuse of fossil energy has been proved to be one of the main reasons that cause serious
environment pollution and climate change. For all of the above, sustainable clean energy
technologies are highly desirable by modern society. Massive of research are undertaken.
The research includes not only increasing the efﬁciency of utilizing the available fossil
fuel, but also exploring the alternative renewable energy, such as solar, wind, and hydro
energy. Renewable energy has been established globally as a mainstream source of elec-
tricity generation for several years [1].
As International Energy Agency (IEA) reported, renewables increased by 4% in
2018, accounting for almost one-quarter of global energy demand growth. The power
sector led the gains, with renewables-based electricity generation increasing at its fastest
pace this decade. Solar PV, hydropower, and wind each accounted for about a third of
the growth, with bioenergy accounting for most of the rest. Renewables covered almost
45% of the world’s electricity generation growth, now accounting for over 25% of global

2.1
Levelized Cost of Electricity (LCOE)
2.1.1
Why LCOE?
As stated in the ﬁrst Chapter, there is great demand for investors to know a quanti-
tative and accurate economic value of a solar system when considering investment. Grid
parity is a most commonly used term when discussing renewable energy sources, es-
pecially solar and wind power. Grid parity occurs when alternative energy source can
generate power at a cost that is less than or at least equal to the price of power from the
electricity grid [10]. Reaching grid parity is considered to be the point at which an energy
source becomes a contender for widespread development without subsidies or govern-
ment support. It is believed, when reaching grid parity, a wholesale shift in generation to
these alternative energy will take place.
Compared to the conventional electricity generation system, PV systems are largely
maintenance-free since there is no fuel consumption when operating, the cost is domi-
nated almost entirely by the capital cost of the system. A method is in demand to fairly
compare energy costs produced by distinct ways.
The levelized cost of energy (LCOE) is a measure of a power source that commonly
used. It is an economic assessment of the average total cost to build and operate a power-

tant information that investors need to refer to when considering investment of an asset or
system.

Chapter 3
A Novel Operational Method
Due to the high cost of storage system, it is very essential to estimate the ﬁnancial
returns. A straightforward idea is that to charge the battery when electricity price is low
or PV production is surplus and discharge when power consumption is high. From the
imbalance in electricity price and PV production at different hour throughout a day, it is
possible to reduce the electricity cost signiﬁcantly.
This appears to be a optimization problem that to predict and schedule the operational
strategy of storage system. Yet the optimization problem has big amount of computation.
Therefore, a novel method based on unsupervised machine learning will be intro-
duced. The optimization result is used as prior information as well as benchmark to
estimate the performance of the proposed method. The method aims to reduce the cost of
electricity as optimization when operating while have a better computing efﬁciency.
This Chapter is organized by four sections. Section 1 is about formulating the opti-
mization problem. In section 2, the novel machine learning based method is introduced.
The simulation results are discussed in section 3. The advantages and drawbacks will be
discussed in last section.

Why Machine Learning?
The optimization problem that stated above can be well-solved with the help of opti-
mization solver now. The result of optimization is acceptable yet the computation is quite
intensive and the process is time-consuming.
On the other hand, machine learning, has been a hot spot in recent years. As a branch
of intelligence system, machine learning is a method of data analysis. Machine learning
aims to build an analytical model that makes the system can learn from data, identify dif-
ferent patterns and make decisions automatically.
The impressive development of machine learning for the last few decades gives a
hint, that some algorithms could be applicable in this case. If a massive of historical data

3.2.3
Machine Learning Based Approach
As stated, in this case, there are 365 days of data in 2018 that can be used as training
data. When considering about applying classiﬁcation on input parameter, there are some
rules that can be summarized beforehand. The solar radiation always has same feature
throughout a day, for example, it reaches the peak value around noon most of the day and
will be zero during the night time. The total amount of PV production varies far different
from summer to winter. Otherwise, the energy consumption also shows a typical proﬁle
within a day, that people usually use more electricity during meal time. The pattern of
activities could be very similar from day to day, that the the peak consumption appears at
a certain time. But the amount of electricity consumption might be different.
For reasons above, it is reasonable to use sum of PV generation per day as the basis
of classiﬁcation, since the features within a day can still be kept. Same approach is ap-
plied on load information.
It is possible to abstract some typical patterns of strategies for battery device. How-
ever, it is difﬁcult to tell what exactly these patterns would be and how they distribute
in a year. Because of the uncertainty, it is assumed that unsupervised machine learning
algorithms would be good choice.

rithms. It is a method of vector quantization that derived from signal processing. A polish

dard algorithm was ﬁrst formulated by Stuart Lloyd in 1957 as a technique for pulse-code
modulation and it is published by Bell Labs in 1982 [24]. Following equations show the
standard form of K-means clustering.

Algorithm 1: Machine Learning Based Training
DATA
Hourly historical data of n days:
PV production, load proﬁle and electricity price.
OPTIMIZATION
Perform the optimization process, obtain the optimal strategy for operating.
TRAINING
1: Calculate the sum of PV production and load for each day.
2: Apply K-means clustering on each day sum of PV as N categories and on load
as M categories.
3: Obtain the N cluster centers of PV and M centers of load.
4: Divide n days sample into N*M categories.
4: Calculate the mean value of charging and discharging for each hour of N*M
categories.
5: Extract N*M patterns of strategies.
Algorithm 2 shows the steps to predicting new operational strategy by model that
built previously. The input data should be 24 hours ahead forecast of PV production and
load proﬁle.
Algorithm 2: Machine Learning Based Predicting
1: Obtain 24 hours ahead forecast data of PV production and load.
2: Calculate the sum of PV and load.
3: Match with N cluster centers of PV and M centers of load.
4: Find the corresponding pattern among N*M strategies.
5: Operate the battery device as the selected pattern.

3.4
Discussion
Even though the optimization gives the best strategy and best result that can be ob-
tained theoretically, the aim of the proposed method is to replace it with a less computa-
tional way but still have a good performance. A brief comparison of these two method is
discussed as follow.
The proposed method has some outstanding advantages from the optimization:
1. Less computation. The operating time is signiﬁcantly lower when using the pro-
posed method. In this case, the operating time of optimization is about two hours,
while the machine learning method only cost around one minute.
2. Reduction of the dependence on prediction accuracy. The optimization is supposed
to be processed based on a highly accurate prediction. But in reality, it is impossible
to obtain a precise prediction on PV production and the load proﬁle. Hence, the
optimal result will never be reached in real. A major difference when applying the
proposed method is that it only uses the summary of a day to match the patterns
but the hourly detail will be leave alone. There is always a margin when matching,
which makes the predictive accuracy per hour become not that crucial to the result.
Meanwhile, there could be two drawbacks of the proposed method:
1. Pre-processing and numerous historical data are necessary. The performance of the
method is sensitive about how the machine learning model is trained. That means
the more historical data is collected the better. These data vary from place to place,
and it would also changed over time. Thus, massive work of data processing need
doing if the method is supposed to be universally applicable.
2. There is no learning ability from the coming data. The training data was collected
before the model is built. However, there will be new data everyday after start
operating the system that can be used to improvement the model. The method
might perform better if the data accumulated over time can be use to updating the
model.

Environmental/Social Beneﬁts
Based on the content above, the value of investment and operational strategy have
been discussed. Except for economic beneﬁts, there are also massive of environment
and social beneﬁts to invest solar technology, including reduction of carbon emissions,
relieving the water stress and creating jobs positions.
4.1
Reduction of CO2 Emissions
Solar system is very different from traditional power system, such as coal system.
For coal system, the operational processes, including coal preparation and coal combus-
tion, always generate over 98% of the life cycle. However, the carbon emissions mainly
occur in upstream processes, for example, raw material extraction, materials production,
module manufacture and so on. The upstream processes generate 60% to 70% carbon
dioxide of the whole life cycle. The operational processes of solar systems, since op-
eration and maintenance is fuel-free, only has about 20% carbon emissions of the total
amount.
Due to the difference, it is not reasonable to use only fuel consumption to estimate the
CO2 emissions of different system. Carbon footprint is introduced to avoid the problem.
A carbon footprint measures the total greenhouse gas, usually carbon dioxide, caused
directly and indirectly by a person, organisation, event or product. Carbon footprint con-
siders almost all processes of a energy system. In this report, it can be used as a proper

4.2
Reduction of Water Consumption
In addition to the carbon emissions, solar energy has great impact on water consump-
tion as well. Since this is an increasingly water-stressed world, it is necessary to take into
account the minimization of water consumption in power production when developing a
power technology. Water consumption could be one of the most criteria for a technology
selection when there are more alternative solutions available.
For conventional power plants, water is used to condense portion of the thermody-
namic cycle. Coal power plants also use water to clean and process fuel. These process
consume a large amount of water up to millions liters per day. Thus, an alternative energy
that can reduce the water consumption will be a relief.
Figure 4.2 [29] shows the water consumption related to electricity generation of four
renewable energy. Even though renewable energy has a diverse water consumption, wind
and solar power use no water. The zero consumption of solar energy makes it perfect
choice of electricity supply solution.

Employment Opportunities and Job positions
In addition to environmental beneﬁts, there are also social beneﬁt of solar power.
The job creation potential is one of the most important metrics when considering the
economic impacts of new technologies. There are a lot of studies about job creation, while
they use different types of jobs as metrics. So it is necessary to clarify the deﬁnitions of
different job positions related to renewable energy. The International Renewable Energy
Agency (IRENA) provides a understanding deﬁnitions of them [30].
The deﬁnitions include direct jobs, indirect jobs and induced jobs. Direct jobs means
that related to core activities, such as manufacturing/installation and operation and main-
tenance, which are relatively easy to measure. Indirect jobs related to the supply and
support of solar energy industry, such as extraction and processing of raw materials. In-
duced job refer to those economic activities of direct and indirect employees, government
and shareholders. It is better to consider more types of job positions when measures.
A review article of Lachlan Cameron et al. [31] has gathered results from 27 publi-
cations about direct employment that created from three common renewable energy tech-
nologies, including PV energy. They also introduce a metric called employment factor

Conclusion
The objectives of the project that stated at the start have been achieved through the
completion of the report. A comprehensive assessment of solar PV and storage system has
been given in three sides, the calculation of Levelized Cost of Electricity (LCOE) before

The assessment revealed that such a solar system has great potential for investment
because of the lower cost of all components as the technology develops in the future.
As predicted, the LCOE will drop continuously in the few decades. The main problem
regrading PV LCOE is that there is not enough information for a certain district. So it
is valuable to apply the LCOE calculation based on local cases. The calculation in this
report combined real price information in DTU, Denmark, and also assumptions based on
European situations. The sensitivity analysis considers a range of uncertainty of LCOE,
which makes it more adaptable in Denmark.
Besides, the cost reduction of electricity when operating such a system can also be
a economic advantage of solar power. The conventional optimization progress and the
novel machine learning based method are both introduced in this report. The cost reduc-
tion from these method is up to 54%-58% annually.
