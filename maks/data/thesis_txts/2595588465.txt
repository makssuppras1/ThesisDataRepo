I
Abstract
There is a growing part of the population that is concert about security and pri-
vacy online. This has the after eﬀect that when people are using methods to hide
themselves online and being anonymous the providers can’t regulate the traﬃc in
the same manner they are used to. In this thesis we will look into diﬀerentiating
VPN and non-VPN traﬃc from a web server. The thesis will use a machine learning
based traﬃc analysis method and apply it in two diﬀerent environments. The ﬁrst
is going to be a virtual environment and later the method is going to be validated
in a real world environment. The result of the thesis is that traﬃc analysis can be
use to diﬀerentiate between VPN and non-VPN traﬃc on a web server but further
study is recommended before implementing the method as the primary method of
detecting VPN.

Introduction
The internet is a big part of the modern society and is being used for communi-
cation between diﬀerent parties all over the world. People uses the internet for
nearly anything from entertainment and work to accessing news and communica-
tion across the world.
The internet is not a "free" place because a lot of diﬀerent organisations keep track
of nearly every one that is using the internet. These organisations could be keeping
track of what you are browsing and searching for on the internet. They can also
keep track of where you located either by GPS data or tracking the IP address you
are using to access the internet, depending on what type of devices that you are
using.
Some of the organisations that are keeping track of you are the authorities and
governments in diﬀerent countries. Some governments even restrict the freedom
citizens have on the internet and the level of freedom varies a lot across countries.
One can look to China that is restricting the freedom of the internet in a way that
makes it diﬃcult for the citizens to experience the freedom of the internet and the
vast knowledge and information that is contains.[1]
If the internet has a lot of organisations that are tracking or restricting the users of
the internet then how can people uses the services that are being tracked without
exposing them self?
They can use the tool called VPN that stands for Virtual Private Network. The idea
behind VPN is to make it look like the client is in another network than the client
is. The client is in this case referring to a device such as a computer.
So what are people using VPN for? Looking at the statistics of the usage of VPN a
list can be made of the top reason for using it. The list is taken from GO-globe.com
and is sorted by the most used reasons in the top of the list.[2]
• Access restricted entertainment content
• Access social networks or news services
• Anonymity while browsing
• Access sites/services at work
• Access restricted sites
• Hide web browsing from government
• Access to browser

Looking at the list a picture can be drawn. The usage of VPN is mostly focused on
accessing services and content that are otherwise restricted. Using VPN to bypass
the restrictions and the surveillance of governments and organisations is a com-
monly known reason and helps a lot of people getting news that are not regulated
or communicate with other people without having to fear who is listening to the
conversation.
The issue with VPN is when looking from a service providers perspective. Most of
the things people do with VPN is accessing restricted content from diﬀerent service
providers. The restrictions are there for a reason. It could be because of licensing or
other reason but the main thing is that when people are bypassing the restrictions
the service provider has less control over the people using their services and that
can have many diﬀerent consequences. In a lot of cases the consequence could be
that the company loose money one way or the other.
The companies then might want to block or restrict the access knowing that users
are accessing their content with VPN connection. There are already some methods
that are used to block VPN traﬃc but most of them imply blocking known VPN
services IP addresses. This is a brute way of blocking the VPN traﬃc because not
every VPN connection or service would necessarily be listed in a way so they can
block them.
1.1
Contribution
In this thesis a machine learning based traﬃc analysis method for detecting VPN
traﬃc is proposed. The method is meant to be a more dynamic way of detecting
VPN traﬃc from a end-point perspective. The machine learning method is then
tested in a test-bed environment of virtual machines. The method is then further
validated with a real world experiment.

Literature review
Some research has been done in VPN technologies and how it is possible to detect
them. In the following, we are going to look a ﬁve diﬀerent papers that look at VPN
and VPN detection in a number of diﬀerent ways. the papers give an overview of
the research ﬁndings on VPN and VPN detection. The scope is limited to papers
that is looking at detection of VPN or papers related to that subject.
The paper by Draper-Gil G., Lashkari A. H., Mamun M. S. I. and Ghorban A. A.
from 2016 and is about how to characterize encrypted and VPN traﬃc using only
time-related features.[3] They are categorizing the data into non-VPN and VPN
and then into seven sub categories so there overall are 14 categories of network
traﬃc. The paper uses machine learning to ﬁnd the pattern in the features that are
extracted from the network traﬃc. The network traﬃc that they are looking at is
the traﬃc generated in the local network. The machine learning algorithm they
are using are KNN and C4.5 Decision Tree. The features they are using are among
other the duration of a ﬂow, ﬂow bytes per second, ﬂow packets per second and
diﬀerent time base features based on the time between two data packets in diﬀerent
scenarios. They manages to predict if the network traﬃc were VPN traﬃc with a
precision that is 0.89 and 0.906 for the network traﬃc that is not VPN.[3]
The second paper that we are going to look at is written by Panchenko A.,Niessen
L., Zinnen A. and Engel T. from 2011.[4] They are trying to show that onion routing
based anonymization networks such as Tor and JAP isn’t as strong as thought. They
are using website ﬁngerprinting that is a method of ﬁnding the unique pattern
that a website has even when the traﬃc is encrypted. Then they are trying to
diﬀerentiate between diﬀerent websites so even if they can’t break the encryption
they can ﬁgure out if the client is visiting websites that it is not suppose to. Here
they are making the ﬁngerprint features such as size markers, HTML markers, total
size of transmission, number of packets and occurring packets sizes. There are more
features but this was some of them that were used. The data is collected locally such
as in between the client and the ﬁrst anonymization node. They are using three
diﬀerent data set where the ﬁrst one is gathered in a closed world environment, the
next one is gathered in a open world environment and the last one is a data set that
is generated when detection countermeasures were used. Other than using features
they are also using the machine learning algorithm Naive Bayes to recognize the
pattern in the traﬃc. The paper manages to successfully diﬀerentiate around 55%
of traﬃc form Tor in the close world data set and 80% with JAP. The accuracy falls
when they are testing it on the open world data set but the true positive rate is on
73%. They then test their method on the data set that is made when they are using
countermeasure techniques. Here the accuracy falls to under 5% for both the Tor
and JAP and makes is easy to see that if Tor and JAP needs better security then
they should implement such countermeasure.[4]

In this project some methods and technologies are used and the two most impor-

3.1
VPN
VPN is as mentioned in the Section 1 a way to transport data over the internet
without having to fear that the data is compromised, either by having the integrity
of the data compromised or by having the conﬁdentiality of the data compromised.
But how does the VPN work?
There is multiple types of VPN and VPN protocols to ensure that the data is secure
and private.
There are two types of VPN
• Site to Site VPN
• Remote Access VPN
Site to Site VPN is a method that allows networks to function as one network. This
is done by the router and not by the client as the Remote Access VPN does. Site
to Site VPN is used by companies to connect diﬀerent networks together, these
networks could be diﬀerent oﬃces that needs to access each others networks.
Remote Access VPN is a type of VPN that allows users to access services internally
on a network from other networks, and allows users to hide their IP address by
routing the data through another network.
The VPN type that is in focus in this thesis is the Remote Access VPN where clients
have installed a VPN that connects them to a VPN server. In the Figure 3.1 an
example of how the Remote Access VPN looks like can be seen.

Figure 3.1: VPN
Figure 3.1 shows that a tunnel is made from the client in the left sub network to the
VPN server in the right sub network. All the data that is send through the tunnel
is secure and private, and no one from the internet is able to see or alter the data.
The tunnel and the setup of the tunnel is the main part of VPN but there are multiple
protocols to set up a VPN tunnel. A list of the diﬀerent protocols can be seen
below.[12]
• Internet Protocol Security (IPSec)
• Layer 2 Tunneling Protocol (L2TP)
• Point-to-Point Tunneling Protocol (PPTP)
• Secure Sockets Layer(SSL) and Transport Layer Security (TLS)
• OpenVPN
• Secure Shell (SSH)
Of the six diﬀerent protocols, the protocol that is used in this project is the Open-
VPN protocol.
OpenVPN is a open-source solution that uses SSL and TLS together with other
technologies to secure the data. One of the reasons for choosing OpenVPN is that
it is highly conﬁgurable which means that it can use a number of diﬀerent ports
and supports a number of diﬀerent ciphers.
OpenVPN is also considered secure as long as the conﬁguration of OpenVPN is
implemented correctly. This is because OpenVPN can conﬁgure in a lot of diﬀerent
ways and therefore if the conﬁguration and implementation of OpenVPN is isn’t

implemented correctly, security vulnerabilities could be made in the VPN connec-
tion.
In OpenVPN there are to main parts that needs to be secure before the VPN tunnel
is considered secure. The data channel encryption and the control channel en-
cryption. The data channel encryption secures the data in the transmission and
the control channel encryption secures the connection between the client and the
sever. To give a picture of it, when looking at a tunnel there are two ends of the
tunnel. The security of these to ends are secured with the control channel encryp-
tion so it’s only the correct client and the correct server can use the tunnel. The
data channel encrypts the data itself and makes sure that even when a data packet
is picked up by a third party, the third party has no way to get hold of the informa-
tion in the data packet. It is important that both channels have a high encryption
because a VPN connection is only as strong as its weakest link.[13]
The setup of each channel is diﬀerent because each channels function is diﬀerent.
The two channels consists of diﬀerent parts:
• Control channel
– Cipher
– Handshake encryption
– Hash authentication
• Data channel
– Cipher
– Hash authentication
As seen above there are two diﬀerent parts that each needs a diﬀerent method of
encryption. Cipher is the data encryption that is needed in both channels, and
here there are three symmetric-key ciphers that are typically used, Blowﬁsh, AES
and Camellia where the AES is the most commonly used symmetric-key cipher.
The strength of the cipher is often decided by the length of the key. In AES the
standard is a 256 bit size key.[13]
The handshake encryption is a way to establish the connection with the other party.
In the handshake encryption, the two parties exchange messages to acknowledge
each other, verify each other, choose a encryption algorithm to use such as AES
and then agree on session keys. The often used methods here is the RSA where
devices has a public and private key or Diﬃe-Hellman that uses prime numbers to
calculate asymmetric keys. In the handshake and in the part where the two parties
verify each other, certiﬁcates are often used as a way of verifying the other party.
Certiﬁcates is issued by a certiﬁcate authority as a trusted third party.[13]

The last part of both channels is the Hash authentication, which is a way of authen-
ticating data. A hash function is a one way function where the same input always
gives the same output but the input can’t be found if just the output is known. The
hash function that is used most of the time is SHA(Secure Hash Algorithm). The
most commonly SHA algorithm is SHA-2 that can be implemented with diﬀerent
levels of security. [13]
The combination of the two channels, the diﬀerent parts and methods that ensure
that the channels are secure makes up a VPN tunnel. A VPN connection has other
parts such as client software and server software that implements these things but
the essence of a VPN connection lies in a secure and private connection between
two parties, where the two parties have been veriﬁed.
3.2
Machine learning
Machine learning is as the name says machines that learns. This is of course in the
scope of the algorithm that is used to make the machine learn and what data it is
using to learn.
The idea is that when the machine is placed in a situation where is has to diﬀeren-
tiate between the data it is giving it is looking for patterns in the data. The data set
that is given to the machine learning algorithm is split up into diﬀerent instances
and diﬀerent features. If for example we are looking at data on trees, the goal is
to diﬀerentiate between diﬀerent kinds of trees and each tree would be a instance
in the data set and the collected data for each tree such as height, wide and cir-
cumference would be features that each tree would have. The goal for the machine
learning algorithm would then be to ﬁnd a pattern in the features that would make
it possible to diﬀerentiate between diﬀerent types of trees.
When looking at patterns that can be seen from a combination of diﬀerent features
an example is given in Figure 3.2 where x and y represents to diﬀerent features
and the blue points is the data instances that are in the data set. When looking at
Figure 3.2 all the points are blue but two clear clusters can be seen. This would
most likely mean that the two diﬀerent clusters represents two diﬀerent types of
trees or two diﬀerent types of things that are collected in the data set.

Figure 3.2: Machine learning
There are diﬀerent kinds of machine learning that is normally used.
• Supervised machine learning
• Unsupervised machine learning
• Reinforcement machine learning
The diﬀerences between the two ﬁrst kinds of machine learning is that the data
set that is given to the machine learning algorithm is either labeled or not labeled.
This means that with a label data set the machine learning algorithm already knows
which of the points belong to the diﬀerent groups of instances that is in the data
set. This is used if there are already collected data where the result is known. A
model can then be trained on the data set and then be used on data that are not
label. The unsupervised machine learning doesn’t have label data so it can only try
to ﬁnd patterns in the data that it is given. The Figure 3.2 example is unsupervised
machine learning because all the points are blue and there are no known diﬀer-
ence between each point other than the diﬀerence in the features. The last type
of machine learning is where the machine learning interacts with the environment
and the result of the machine learning algorithm is judge by a third party and the
machine learning algorithm can then learn when it makes mistakes.[14]
KNN is a supervised machine learning algorithm that uses labeled data sets to train
a model. It then uses the model on other data sets that aren’t labeled. KNN stands
for k-nearest neighbors and is an algorithm that looks at each feature and compares
it to features of other instances. The comparisons is done by looking at the closes
neighbors when taking all the features into account. The instance that is looked at
is then labels accordingly to how the neighbors are labeled. That means that when
two or more instances looks similar then they are labeled in the same way.[15]

Design
As described before in Section 3.1 a VPN connection is where the network traﬃc is
going through another network. This makes it seems like the source of the network
traﬃc originates from the network that the traﬃc is routed through.
The idea with the project is to ﬁgure out a way to diﬀerentiate between VPN and
non-VPN network traﬃc from the end point perspective. If looking at the two
ﬁgures ﬁg. 4.1 and ﬁg. 4.2 then the end point would be the Web server in the right
side of the ﬁgures. The detection software would then be ran on the Web server.
When looking at the real world there are diﬀerent scenarios for how the network
traﬃc is going to get to the end point.
The ﬁrst example is for a centralized VPN solution where the client routes the
network traﬃc through a VPN server that is placed somewhere on the internet.
This scenario can be seen in ﬁg. 4.1, where the client sends that network traﬃc
via a VPN tunnel past the router in the local network and to the VPN server. The
VPN server then forwards the network traﬃc to, in this case a Web server that lies
behind a router. It can also be seen that in the big cloud that represents the internet
other servers can be seen. This is to illustrate that the big cloud is the internet.
This example is the most common scenario in the real world when taken into ac-
count that centralized VPN is the most common VPN service that are being used
until now.

The other scenario that represents the decentralized VPN service can be seen in

PCA analysis

module sklearn. The data was ﬁrst extracted from the chosen pcap ﬁle and then

feature. The weight for each feature for each of these components was then placed
in a table and the total weight for each feature was then calculated. The weight
here takes into consideration how much each component meant for the accuracy.
The result of this was that each feature had a weight that couldn’t be left out of the
implementation.

in the implementation can’t be removed because it inﬂuences the accuracy of the

the implementation is rather low. Or in other words, the model isn’t overﬁtted and

Conclusion
9.1
Summary
This project started by looking into the diﬀerent literature on the subject of detect-
ing VPN or classifying network traﬃc. Traﬃc analysis is here a method that can
be used to diﬀerentiate VPN traﬃc from non-VPN traﬃc. A method that has been
looked into in this project. Then diﬀerent features were selected and the experi-
ment was designed to imitate the real world in a virtual environment. The virtual
environment that was set up had a client, VPN server, router and a web server. The
client and VPN server were on one network and the VPN server on another one
with the router connecting the two networks. All the virtual machines were set up
with Linux and the VPN server used OpenVPN as the VPN method. The web server
had a simple index page with four pictures and each picture could be accessed indi-
vidually. Data was then generated with a shell script from the client. The script had
a certain randomization, so the traﬃc would be more realistic. Features are then
extracted by a python script and the data is given to a KNN machine learning algo-
rithm. There are two machine learning scripts that have been implemented. One
script is to train a KNN model, and the second script is used to apply the model
made in the ﬁrst script. The model is made over a whole pcap ﬁle and is applied
in the same way. This also makes it possible to use the same model on diﬀerent
pcap ﬁles. The results from the machine learning script, that applies the model, are
the accuracy and the number of true positive, true negative, false positive and true
negative. When having this information for each pcap ﬁle that the model is applied
to, makes it possible to give not only the accuracy but also the precision, recall, true
negative and the F1 score. Looking at the results from the virtual experiment it can
be seen that the model and methods work well in that environment.
Some optimisation was done in order to ﬁnd the best number of neighbors in the
machine learning algorithm. The accuracy got a bit higher, but the diﬀerence was
about 2%, so the need for optimizing the number of neighbors wasn’t that high.
Other than the number of neighbors, a PCA analysis was made but with poor results
because every feature that was uses was needed.
The ﬁrst experiment was a place in a virtual environment, but the results needed
to be validated in a real world environment, where several other factors could in-
ﬂuence the network traﬃc. The real world experiment was made with a real web
server that was set up with cloned web pages from other web servers. The VPN that
was used was one of the most used VPN services, NordVPN. NordVPN has multiple
servers in Denmark and when generating the traﬃc a number of these servers was
used. This added more realism to the experiment because the distance and delay for
each server wouldn’t be the same. The data was generated by a python script and
then the same method was applied, as used in the virtual experiment. The model
was based of the larges pcap ﬁle collected in the real world experiment and the

9.2
Discussion
In this project two diﬀerent experiments have been done with two diﬀerent setups.
The ﬁrst experiment is the experiment done in a virtual environment and the sec-
ond in a real world setting. If we look at the ﬁrst experiment and its setup, we can
see that the setup was made in a way so the traﬃc generated would be very clean.
What is meant by clean, is that there were not a lot of factors that could make noise
or inﬂuence that traﬃc pattern in a negative way. This meant that when using ma-
chine learning to predict the outcome, the prediction would be more accurate. That
was also what happened when looking at the results that we got from the data in
the ﬁrst experiment.
When looking at the second experiment in the real world environment, the setup
itself had a lot of factors that would inﬂuence the traﬃc generated. Some of the
factors would be that nearly ever VPN connection were made to a diﬀerent VPN
server, the traﬃc went through the internet and an unknown number of nodes that
could have inﬂuenced the traﬃc. The thing with the internet is that it is not known
how much traﬃc each node would have at the time the traﬃc that we have gen-
erated would pass that node. That would make the delay for that node longer and
therefore make the prediction harder. So there are a lot of external factors that we
can’t control in a real world experiment. This is also what makes the experiment
good, because if these methods would be implemented in the real world, the exter-
nal factors would be a problem that would make it harder to predict what traﬃc
that is VPN traﬃc and what is not VPN traﬃc. The diﬃculties can also be seen
when we look at the results from the real world experiment where the accuracy
fell a lot when compared to the results from the virtual experiment.
When looking at the results and the implementation itself, it can be seen that im-
provement can be made in the implementation and in the methods. This should
be done it these methods should be implemented for the real world to make a dif-
ference for a company or others that needs to make sure that the service they are
providing isn’t given to people that uses a VPN. There should be other VPN detec-
tion or VPN IP address range blocking implemented as well, but the methods used
in this project would be a useful add-on to detect VPN traﬃc.

The methods used in this project can in diﬀerent ways be improved. Moreover,
some parts need to be further explored.
The ﬁrst thing is the machine learning algorithm. In this project the machine learn-
