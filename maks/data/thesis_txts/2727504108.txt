Abstract
This master thesis investigates the frequency response of a coupled electrodynamic shaker-
piezoelectric accelerometer system via numerical study and experimental measurement.
The electrodynamic shaker is described by a three degree-of-freedom vibration system
and characterized by isolation mode, suspension mode and coil mode using classical
transfer matrix method. The analytical model is easily to be built up, however, is difficult to
present the detailed performance and intuitive mode shape. In the project, a finite element
model-COMSOL Multiphysics model is developed to characterize the mechanical reso-
nances of the coupled shaker-accelerometer system and the corresponding mode shapes
are illustrated intuitively. The coil mode described in analytical modelling is characterized
as mounting effect of accelerometer and working standard. The factors influence this
mounting effect are investigated both by numerical simulation and experiments.
In addition, the difference of the accelerometer resonances when simulated with bottom
mechanically clamped, when simulated with accelerometer axis aligned placed on the
working standard, and experimental measurement, is investigated and discussed.
Last, the effect of mounting location, or misalignment mounting of accelerometer is inves-
tigated both from measurement and from numerical simulation compared with the axis-
aligned mounting. The corresponding mechanical mode is presented by numerical study
results.

Introduction
1.1
Background and motivation
Shaker and accelerometer are one of the most universal equipment to conduct struc-
tural vibration measurements. Except some specific types of shakers are designed using
piezoelectric actuation for use of ultrasonic structural excitation, most shakers employ
electromagnetic force or more specifically, the Lorentz force, converting electrical signal
to mechanical force which introduces acceleration on the measured object. From the
measurement acceleration response curve, the corresponding resonances and mechan-
ical modes of the tested object or system can be characterized and analysed.
(a) Shaker HBK 4809.
(b) Accelerometer HBK 4371.
Figure 1.1: The real figure of electrodynamic shaker and piezoelectric accelerometer used
as research objects cite from [1] and [2] respectively.
Apart from the excitation source, the accelerometers are used to detect the acceleration
on the test object or system by using piezoelectric or capacitive transduction in order to
convert the mechanical vibrations quantity such as strain or stress into electrical charge
or voltage variation. However, capacitive transduction type is mostly seen and used on
MEMS accelerometer with size advantages and low power consumption, which is univer-
sally applied in consumer electronics. In the contrast, it shows lower measurement fidelity
than traditional piezoelectric transduction accelerometer when exposed to a relatively high
accelerometer force in structural vibration measurements due to its light weight. There-
fore, piezoelectric transduction accelerometers are universally used in structural vibration
measurement.
Understanding the working performance of an independent shaker or accelerometer is
helpful to evaluate the measurement quality. As for the electrodynamic shaker, the in-
jected current coil is exposed in a static magnetic field formed by permanent magnet
and soft iron, which generates Lorentz force to drive the moving mass element inside the
shaker vibrating and bring acceleration. Such physics topology can be simulated by using
lumped element method under certain assumptions, for example, the element is consid-
ered as a homogeneous spatially performance object, only the first order mode of object

is included. G.F.Lang uses lumped element and transfer matrix method to describe the
behaviour of a Data Physics model S-100 shaker in [3]. The model shows a good match
at low frequencies regarding to the isolation and suspension mode compared to measure-
ment results. However, the resonance discovered in high frequencies region explained
by coil mode is not convincing due to the model limitation. J.Martino describes the be-
haviour of HBK shaker 4809 with two port modelling in [4]. By optimizing the parameters,
the simulation results shows a good prediction of the shaker response across frequencies.
However, the resonance discovered at 6313 Hz is explained by mass load is not intrinsic.
Sebastien develops a virtual shaker simulator [5] based on measurement results, which
shows a good illustration on electromechanical coupling at low frequencies. However,
the mentioned coil mode happened in 2160 Hz is not identified by modal analysis and is
limited by the impossibility to place the accelerometer on coil part.
Moreover, as for the piezoelectric accelerometer, its natural resonance frequency accord-
ing to value on [2] is far beyond the range that lumped elements or transfer matrix can
describe. Therefore, a new study approach should be introduced to simulate the physical
behaviour.
As is mentioned above, the shaker and accelerometer engages not only single physics
domain but convert electrical or mechanical signal to another form of signal respectively.
Regarding to the transducers, COMSOL Multiphysics provides a strong finite element
approach to study the detailed performance numerically.
In addition, the accelerometer is typically used as a mounting object on the shaker or the
other vibration excitation source. However, this mounting attachment will introduce extra
effects from the study of Gustavo P. [6], it can be confirmed that the dispersion of a single-
ended accelerometer on calibration at high frequencies differs significantly depending on
using different vibration exciters. There might be reasons including mounting stiffness
condition and modal deformations on the armature of vibration exciters, which impact the
final calibration results. Besides, when the manufacturer designs the accelerometer using
numerical simulation, the bottom surface of the accelerometer is mechanically clamped
to stabilize the computation. However, such artificially fixation will make the accelerom-
eter stiffer than real physics condtion, which leads to an overestimation on resonance
frequency.
Last but not least, even though the mounting method does have an impact on both of the
accelerometer calibration results and test object’s vibration characteristics, the mounting
location also exerts an influence on the measurement results. Choosing a location which
provides a short and rigid vibration transmission path to the excitation source to avoid
any complaint and damped elements is quite important to the measurement quality [7].
Slight mounting location difference, in other words, a little misalignment will cause large
deviation at high frequencies compared to the axis aligned with desired measurement
location. As a result, the effect of misalignment mounting introduced by the redundant
vibration transmission path is worthwhile being investigated.
Based on these backgrounds and motivation, the goal of this project includes:
• To build a multiphysics model which includes essential part of shaker and an ac-
celerometer to analyse the mounting effect by numerical study.
• To evaluate the difference between a developed accelerometer model and a bottom-
mechanically clamped accelerometer model.
• To investigate and characterize the impact of misalignment mounting impact of ac-

1.2
Outline
In order to realize the objectives listed above and to research the questions step by step,
the main content will be divided into six chapters below:
• In the overview, the fundamental working principles of the electrodynamic shaker
and piezoelectric accelerometer will be detailedly described combining with derived
equations and analytical modelling across different physics domain.
• In the 2D investigation chapter, a 2D axis-symmetric geometry is extracted from the
original shaker geometry, which can be utilized to determine the essential geometri-
cal domains included in different physics of an electrodynamic shaker. This chapter
can be regarded to a pre-investigation of 3D numerical study.
• In the 3D investigation chapter, based on the previous 2D investigation conclusion,
the results of 3D electrodynamic shaker simulation will be presented. Meanwhile,
the facing challenges introduced by high computational load will also be discussed.
• In the accelerometer simulation chapter, the numerical study of the piezoelectric
accelerometer will be conducted using the developed COMSOL Multiphysics model
supplied by HBK. The frequency response and the corresponding mechanical modes
will be presented and analysed.
• In the coupling experiment and simulation chapter, a measurement setup to inves-
tigated the frequency response of coupled shaker-accelerometer system will be de-
signed and conducted to find the experimental results of the corresponding mounting
performance. Meanwhile, the repeatability and reproducibility of the measurement
will be examined as well. In addition, the measurement results will be compared and
discussed to the corresponding numerical study results in order to characterize the
impact that the mounting of accelerometer brings and the difference of accelerome-
ter natural resonances compared with the bottom mechanically-clamped simulation
model.
• In the misalignment chapter, the measurement setup of misalignment mounting of
coupled shaker-accelerometer system will be designed and conducted. The ob-
tained experiment result will be compared with the axis alignment mounting in order
to characterize the impact of misalignment. Besides, the corresponding numerical
study results will explain the reason why such misalignment influence is resulted.

When the external factors make the H-field stronger, such as the current through the coil
increases, until point f, B-field starts to increase. However, as H-field becomes stronger
and stronger, B-field starts to saturate until point a. If the current decreases at this point,

process repeats in the opposite direction forming the entire magnetic hysteresis loop.
Thus, in the numerical study, the magnetization model of soft iron is set to be B-H curve in
order to express this kind of hysteresis behaviour. However, as for the permenent magnet,
because it is hard to be magnetized or demagnetized and remains a constant magnetic

Magnetic boundary condition
From the geometry supplied by HBK in figure 3.1a, the
given configuration shows all the solid part. However, when doing the numerical simula-
tion of magnetic field, the air domain is essentially needed to conduct the magnetic flux
density at least beside the coil since there are gaps. As a result, how much air domain
should be included in the magnetic field physics and what kind of magnetic boundary
condition should be applied become a core question in this part of investigation.
In COMSOL Multiphysics, magnetic field physics has a default boundary condition called
magnetic insulation expressed as 3.1:
n × A = 0
(3.1)
where n is the normal vector of a surface or boundary, A represents the magnetic potential
vector potential. Equation 3.1 shows a zero tangential component of the magnetic poten-
tial on the boundary or surface, which also means zero normal component of magnetic
flux density on that boundary or surface. Meanwhile, there is another boundary condition
in magnetic field physics called perfect magnetic conductor:
n × H = 0
(3.2)
where n is the normal vector of a surface or boundary, H represents the magnetic field
strength. Equation 3.2 shows a zero tangential magnetic flux density on that surface or
boundary.

without any stray field outside the shell in an ideal circumstance. Nevertheless, it only
exists in the artificial simulation. According to the data sheet of shaker 4809 [1], a 0.02 T
magnetic flux density is measured at the table surface. Considering that the shaker is
surrounded by a huge air sphere, it can be concluded that the real magnetic boundary
condition of the outer surface of that sphere should be between the magnetic insulation
and perfect magnetic conductor. The radius of this sphere becomes bigger, more accurate

Conclusion and Future work
8.1
Conclusion
This thesis project focuses on investigating and characterizing the frequency response of
a coupled electrodynamic shaker HBK 4809-piezoelectric accelerometer HBK 4371 sys-
tem both from numerical study in COMSOL Multiphysics and experimental measurement.
Firstly, in the overview, the inner structure and basic work principle of an electrodynamic
shaker is introduced in terms of magnetic domain and mechanical domain. Secondly, the
piezoelectric transduction principle of accelerometer is presented. Combining with the
Delta Shear assembling method and inner structure of accelerometer, the corresponding
analytical mechanical model is proposed, thus, the resonance frequency of accelerometer
can be characterized.
Stepping into the numerical investigation, firstly, a 2D axis-symmetric geometry is ex-
tracted from the original 3D shaker geometry and is used to characterize the essential
domain needed to be included in magnetic field physics and solid mechanics physics re-
spectively. It’s worth noting that the skin effect of magnetic field induced by the current
through the coil wire is investigated and characterized across frequencies by boundary
layer mesh. However, when extending this 2D axis-symmetric model to original 3D re-
search, more detailed and adaptive mesh optimization should be figured out in order to
reduce the computational load and make the frequency domain perturbation study work.
Then, the experimental setup is designed and installed in order to measure the frequency
response of a coupled shaker-accelerometer system. The repeatability and reproducibil-
ity of the measurement is examined as well. Moreover, the change of contact condition
between shaker and working standard and tightening torque of the mounting object are
demonstrated to influence the so-called coil mode illustrated in analytical modelling. From
numerical study, the suspension mode and coil mode shape are captured by using me-
chanical body load on the coil in frequency domain study, even though the damping and
amplitude are unmatched due to limitation of excluding magnetic field and bottom struc-
ture of shaker such as the compliant studs.
Apart from the shaker resonances, the accelerometer resonances are also captured in the
measurement and simulation both with bottom mechanically clamped and with bottom
contact to the working standard. An obvious frequency downwards trend is observed
in the simulation with bottom contact to the working standard compared to the bottom
mechanically fixed simulation. However, as for the measurement, the obtained resonance
frequencies are significantly higher than the other two situations. The reason is speculated
to be a different vibration transmission way.
Last, the measurement setup of misalignment mounting is installed to investigate the influ-
ence of mounting location on the frequency response of the coupled shaker-accelerometer
system. Except the significant and complex influence introduced by the excursion at high
frequencies, two slight resonances observed at low frequencies are worth investigating.
As is shown in the numerical study, the first misalignment mode is resulted by the bi-
ased distribution of mass loaded on the working standard surface. However, the second
misalignment mode can not be captured by the mechanically-symmetric model.

8.2
Further work
From the investigation of this project, most of the research questions are explained, how-
ever, some work can be still perfected and some areas have emerged which have not
been addressed as well in the investigation in order to better analyse the performance of
the coupled shaker-accelerometer system. These areas will be presented as recommen-
dations for future work:
• Even though, the material property is approximated or deduced from investigation,
it still addresses relatively reasonable results. However, if more detailed material
properties are given, such as the magnetic flux density of permenent magnet, the
Young’s modulus of the spring flexures, the results will be more accurate and con-
vincing.
• Even though all the mechanical resonances of shaker and accelerometer are cap-
tured in the numerical simulation without introducing the magnetic field of shaker but
replacing the Lorentz force with mechanical body load on the coil, the magnetic field
should be included with further adaptive mesh optimization regarding to frequency
so that the effect presented by Lenz’s law and real force excitation to the vibrating
part can be simulated resulting more accurate and reliable results.
• When changing the tightening torque between the working and shaker table surface,
the low, medium and high torque degrees are simply perceived by hand, however,
if it can be measured and quantified, the results will be more convincing and can be
compared to the technical data sheet.
• It would be better that a laser vibrational measurement can be conducted to detect
the changes of displacement of the table surface and shaker armature so that the
mode shape presented by numerical simulation can be verified experimentally.
• When building the geometry of simulation of the axis-aligned and off-axis aligned
mounting, the accelerometer is directly placed on the top surface of working stan-
dard, unlike the real situation, where the accelerometer is attached on the working
standard via a scrum. It would be better that the complex geometry of the scrum
can be created and used on the attachment of accelerometer so that the vibration
transmission path can be restored to the full extent of real physics.
• As for the misalignment investigation, the existing mechanically-symmetric model
can not describe the possible motion of second misalignment mode, an entire ge-
ometry model is needed to be developed to capture the second misalignment mode.
