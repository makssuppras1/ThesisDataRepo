Abstract
The use of air conditioning has xx times over the last xx years, and with increasing temôÙ
perature due to climate change it is estimated that the market for HVAC will increase xx
over and become a significant part of buildings energy use. Technological innovation is
therefore paramount. The investigation of novel material such as MetalôÙorganic frameôÙ
works (MOF) for the use in energy technology has been an increasing area of research
due to their unique properties. The use of MOFs in energy technology opens up the posôÙ
sibility for new designs and thermodynamic cycles. One of this usage of MOFs to create
a new cooling mechanism using the phenomenon of desorption of water vapour through
the change of pressure. A simple prototype to create the desired cooling effect.
The thesis consists of 3 parts: 1) The investigation of eight different MOFs to asses their
desorption kinetics through an experimental approach. And based on the results, a MOF
with the best kinetics will be chosen for further use. 2) The investigation of a simple
prototype design where the MOF from part 1) will be used and its cooling effect will be
measured. Based on theory several prototypes will be created in order to come up with
best design. 3) If time the results will be used in a simulation for further investigation.
Unfortunately the thesis was unfinished and final conclusion will be presented in the oral
defence part

1.1
Background

According to xxx the change in temperature due to climate change has lead to an increase
in use of energy technology for airconditioning. This increase in usage represents an
increasing share of overall energy usage for buildings and therefore innovation in new
energy technology is a hot topic. In the later years the use of new materials and new cycle
ddesigns has become prelevant. The Use of MOF (Metal organic framworks) are a new
class of materials that has been investigated. These are novel crystalline structures with
a metallic and organic buildup, creating a large surface area and are thereofre known to
have impressive sorption abilities. These new characteristics opens up the possibility for
designing new cycles for refrigeration. One of these cycles involves the phenomenon of
desorption cooling using MOF, and watervapour as the cooling medium driven by pressure
difference.
As mentioned above there are several thousand MOFs that has been discovered but focus
on their practical usage in technology has not been the main driver for research. This has
led to a situation where research engineers are hoping to discover that some MOFs that
a chemist has discovered will be useful. Instead of continuing this linear approach, and
due to the tunebility of MOFs, this project hopes to establish a feedback loop where the
proof of concept of the cooling machine can serve as the basis for what kind of properties
is needed in MOFs to increase the cooling effect/efficiency.
1.2
Problem Statement
Your problem statement goes here.
Can a pressure driven desorption cooling cycle based on Metal organic frameworks be a

Three forms of water sorption in MOFs
The way that water sorption in MOF happens can generally be described by three driving
mechanism. The first one is chemisorption. in the case of adsorption, a water molecule
from the surroinding air sticks to the open metals site in the MOF. As this is a gasphaseôÙ
toôÙsurface reaction and the chemical species involved is a metal node, heat is released
from the reaction.
The second mechanism is physiosorption, where clusters or multiple layers of water
molecules stick due to Van der walls forces and then form connected lines transporting
the water further into the MOF, and can eventually lead to pore filling [5]. This type of
adsorption is generally regarded as having weaker bonds that then what chemisoprtiond
does.
The third is cappillairy condensation. Since MOFs are mesoporus materials, their pore
diameters support normal condensation and firstôÙorder phase transition. caapialry conôÙ
densation can also happen for microporous MOFs. An equation for criticalpore diameter
for when cappilary condensation takes place has been calculated by Coasne et al [6],
where the critical diameter for room temperature is calculated to be 2nm [2].
This mechanism is driven by surface tensions and cappilary action.
The molecurlar geometry, the metal sites, the activation energy etc all plays a complicated

to begin again. This type of cycle differs from the normal vapour compression refrigerôÙ

5.3.1
Short on DVS and TGA machines
The purpose of the experiment is to measure the rate of heat supplied to the MOF that is
necessary to keep it at a constant temperature for 30 minutes, during desorption.
This category of investigation of sorption effects of a material is a widely investigated
field of science. Normally to do this, one usually either uses a Dynamic vapour sorpôÙ
tion machine (DVS) or a thermoôÙgravimetric analysis (TGA). The first machine uses the
gravimetric method to measure the change in mass of a material, by controlling the vaôÙ
por pressure surrounding the material, such as water vapor. Since the machine operates
isothermally, meaning at constant temperature, one can produce Isotherms for studied
materials.
TGAs works by heating a sample of a saturated MOF under controlled environment and
record the change in weight of the material. For MOFs this means one can study threshôÙ
olds at which temperature desorption of water vapor happens.
There is also another machine that one could argue falls within the same category of
material study, which is differential scanning calorimeter. This machine is measures the
heat capacity of materials, e.g the amount of heat needed to raise its temperature by 1
degree.
This background knowledge is relevant as he instrument created can be described as a
mix between all three.

and which opens up for possible chemical tuning of the MOF in order to optimize it for
heat release.

variable energies of the binding sites found in the different MOFs, which was explained in

regeneration cycle can be argued to have a positive impact on the overall efficiency of the
system.
One of the goals of this papers was to investigate the optimal kinetic properties of the
MOFs and have them tested in a built system. This implies the possibility of a theoretical
investigation into the optimal properties of water + MOF based systems. This question of
the ideal properties of MOFs has already been lightly touched upon by others, but mostly
as investigation into the ideal shape of the isotherm. Bagheri et al did a theoretical inôÙ
vestigation using the ClausiusôÙClapeyron equation to find the optimal isotherm for cooling
applications [27]. Goldsworthy et al created an optimization algorithm and found that as
long as the change in enthalpy does not vary with the uptake level, then a specific shape
matters not much [28].

7.2.2
Three effects of cooling
There are at least three different phenomenon of cooling playing at the same time to create
the total cooling effect for the caseof desorption cooling. The first one is Cooling of the
air when deôÙpressurizing it, also known as adiabatic cooling. The second one is due to
condensed water vapour on surfacesses of the HEX other than the MOF coated ones,
which will evaporate due to lowering the vapour pressure in the box and thereby create a
cooling effect. The third one is cooling due to desorption of watervapour on MOF, and is
the effect that is being investigated in this thesis. The fraction that each effect accounts
for in the total cooling effect is interesting and should be investigated more.

of the material for sorption purposes. But it is also the case that the exact method of
synthesis creates meaningful differences in pore structure, crystalinity, and other aspect
that has an effect on the final isotherm of the material. And so, even though the isotherm
of MilôÙ100(fe) has been thoroughly studied and published several times, it is important for
strengthening the validation of the kinetic and the cooling system experiment. by making
sure that the isotherm are similar or exactly alike previously produced work, it validates
that the material has not degraded or lost other properties that are meaningful for the
two other experiments and the results gained from those. Meaning one can argue with
more weight that the results gained from the two experiments are indeed due to the MOFs
inherent property and not as a consequence/affect of any other reason like degredation,
