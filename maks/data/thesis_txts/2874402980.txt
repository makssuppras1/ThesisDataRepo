Abstract 
The pathogen Bacillus cereus group is resistant in adverse conditions due to the spores forming 
ability. In addition, the produced toxin from the Bacillus cereus group, the emetic cereulide, 
cannot be easily eliminated during the food production, it is crucial to find a fast and reliable 
method for the revelation of the cereulide presence. 
Exploring a new method, known as MALDI-TOF MS, with the purpose to validate it for the 
detection and possible quantification of the emetic cereulide, alone and in food. This master’s 
thesis project analyzed four group of samples: (1) the pure cereulide in known different 
concentrations, (2) the cereulide produced from B.cereus strains (3) inoculated rice with pure 
cereulide with known concentrations and (4) inoculated rice with B.cereus strains BP-1 and 
F4810/72, separately, in order to produce cereulide during incubation at 23±1oC in different time.  
Standard curves have been formed after the detection of the toxin in known concentrations to 
confirm if the method is reliable regarding the intensities in corresponding concentrations. The 
detection limit of the pure cereulide using the MALDI-TOF MS was found to be 0.009 μg/ml 
detecting the potassium ions that the cereulide tends to merge at 1194 mass to charge ratio and 
0.02 μg/ml detecting both potassium and sodium adducts.  
The analyses of the cereulide that was produced from strains of the Bacillus cereus group led to 
the best extraction solvent, which was the ACN≥99.9%. While the Ethanol 75% provided lower 
intensities and TFA 10% has proven as an inefficient solvent. 
 The detection limit in food has been found to be different that the isolated pure cereulide and 
has been found to equals 0.05 μg/g after the detection of the potassium adduct and 1 μg/g after 
the detection of both potassium and sodium at 1194 and 1178 m/z, respectively.  
Lastly, the BP-1 strain has been found to be positive in the cereulide presence after 36 hours of 
incubation, as the F4810 has been found to have potassium adducts after 30 hours of incubation, 
which reveals the presence of cereulide. After 48 hours both potassium and sodium have been 
confirmed.  
Although, the findings of this project were significant for the better understanding of this new 
method analysis on the detection and quantification of the cereulide. Nevertheless, further 
research needs to be conducted, with the aim to expand the knowledge and improve the analyses 
using MALDI-TOF Microflex Mass spectrometer to reduce the uncertainties as much as possible.  
 
Key words: Bacillus cereus group, Cereulide, Emetic toxin, Foodborne disease, MALDI-TOF, Mass 
spectrometry,

1 Introduction 
Bacillus cereus group is known as Bacillus cereus sensu lato derives from the soil and has the 
ability to form spores, which makes it resilient to adverse conditions. For that reason, it can be 
found in diverse food since the removal or even the reduction of the spores is not an easy process. 
(Andersson A, Ronner U, Granum PE. , 1995 ) Based on outbreaks Bacillus cereus group has been 
reported for the causing of illness for many people due to cereulide producing. According to 
European Union, out of 6,657 cases that have been reported, the 352 required hospitalization 
and no fatality case has been reported. (EFSA Panel on Biological Hazards (BIOHAZ), 2016) 
Moreover, outbreaks have shown that this group of bacteria mainly has been confirmed in starchy 
food. For instance, rice, potatoes and pasta. However, it has been found in ready-to eat food, 
dairy products, meat, fish, vegetable, and fruits as well. 
It is important to mention that there are two toxins that can cause negative influence on the 
human system. The diarrheal and the emetic cereulide. The differences between those two toxins 
are in their production time. The first is produced in the host’s system while the second is secreted 
s out of the host’s system. The symptoms of diarrheal and emetic include vomiting and diarrhoea, 
with the last to can be acute and cause even the death. Another very important difference with 
scientific aspect is that the cereulide is secreted by species that have been investigated and 
discovered in their possession the ces genes. (Kranzler, Markus, et al., 2024) As the 
microorganism, cereulide is resilient to chemicals and thermal treatments such as the 121oC for 
90 minutes, which makes it a serious hazard to the food industries threating the public health. 
(Meng, Jing-Nan, et al., 2022) 
There are few other methods that are currently using from industries and laboratories for the 
detection of cereulide such as the liquid chromatography together with mass spectrometry and 
fluorescence.  However, a new method is trying to cover the analyses for the detection and 
quantification of the emetic cereulide in food. The purpose is to contribute to the avoidance of 
more foodborne poisonings from this serious toxin that has affected many humans. Matrix-
assisted laser desorption time-of-flight mass spectrometer (MALDI-TOF) belongs to mass 
spectrometer technology, and it is considered as a time saving method with less sample 
preparation.  
Currently, the knowledge about the analyses for the detection of cereulide using MALDI-TOF MS 
is that the device can detect the ions of potassium and sodium that are cereulide’s adducts at 
1191 and 1178 m/z, respectively. In addition, it is noteworthy to mention that the difference of 
those to mass to charge ratios is 16 Th if the detector of the device recognizes the presence of 
the cereulide. (Doellinger, Joerg, et al. , 2020) 
Unfortunately, the investigations using the MALDI-TOF MS for analyzing the presence of the 
cereulide in food are few. More studies and evidence grounded in reality are needed to conduct 
to build a solid and complete understanding of this method and its abilities in order to be 
==PAGE:8==
subsume as a daily method of detection and quantification of the emetic cereulide in various food 
in industrial level.  
1.1 Thesis Objectives 
The primary goal of the project is the finding of the detection limit of the emetic cereulide with 
the MALDI-Tof. For that reason, the study involved samples with determinate concentrations of 
pure toxin started from the lowest one to discover the lowest detection limit of the cereulide.  
The next goal of this study is to achieve the detection and quantification of the emetic toxin in 
rice with unknown cereulide concentration. Industrial analyses for the exposure of the cereulide 
in food is quite unidentical with the analyses in known concentrations in the laboratory. Because 
the amount of the toxin can vary depending on the food, the hygiene and handling during and 
after cooking, and the contamination time. For that reason, the detection and quantification of 
the cereulide in contaminated food is crucial, as the formation of standard curves to validate the 
MALDI-TOF MS machine from the findings of pure toxin. 
The study has investigated the cereulide from various origins, pure and produced from bacteria 
cells of different Bacillus cereus strains, and in different concentrations in four sample groups. 
More specifically, the project investigated (1) the pure cereulide in known different 
concentrations, (2) the cereulide produced from 6 positive B.cereus strains in cereulide 
production having a blind sample from a negative strain, (3) inoculated rice with pure cereulide 
in in known concentrations. While results from MALDI-TOF and microbiological analyses from (4) 
inoculated rice with B.cereus strains BP-1 and F4810, separately, in order to produce cereulide 
during incubation at 23±1oC for different time. From the microbiological outcomes growth curves 
have been formed and are given. 
2  Scientific Background 
2.1 Bacillus cereus  
Bacillus cereus is considered as a positive gram bacterium shaped like a rod since it has long and 
cylindrical cells. This microorganism forms spores and has the ability to move. It is widely known 
that it derives from the soil, air, and many foods such as the species belonging to the group known 
as Bacillus cereus sensu lato. (Vidic et al., 2020)

According to investigations, the B.cereus colonies vary depending on the culturing such as the

2.1.1 Growth Conditions of Bacillus cereus 
This bacterium can grow under the presence of air or without. (Messelhäußer, Ute, and Monika 
Ehling-Schulz, 2018) The temperature that the microorganism is possible to multiply is between 
10oC to 42oC, with an optimum range the 30oC to 37oC, according to Pexara A. and Govaris A. 
(Pexara, Α., & Govaris, Α., 2010) Based on Patino-Navarrete et al. the B.cereus divided into 
psychotropic and mesophilic species with range temperature of growth 7oC to 40oC for the first 
category and 10oC to 45oC for the second category. However, there are mesophilic species that 
their growth starts from 15oC to 45oC. (Patiño-Navarrete, R., & Sanchis, V. , 2017) Another study 
supports the bacteria can grow in a range of temperature from 4oC to 48oC, with optimum range 
the 28oC to 35oC. They can grow in 4.9-9.3 pH and maximum NaCl concentration 7.5%. (Batt, 
2000) Contrary to the pH, EFSA says that the minimum, optimal and maximum pH for the B.cereus 
growth is 5, 6-7 and 8.8, respectively. In addition, the minimum aw is 0.93. (EFSA Panel on 
Biological Hazards (BIOHAZ), 2016) 
During the sporulation the B.cereus cells are inactive but after germination the cells return to 
their initial function as active vegetative cells. High temperatures in the range of 65oC to 80oC 
provoke the germination (Vidic et al., 2020) and last 30 minutes but depend on the species. (Batt, 
2000) 
The spores are extremely resistant to adverse conditions such as pH in the range of 1 to 5.2 or in 
high temperatures such as 95oC for 2 minutes. (Vidic et al., 2020) The spores D values for 100oC 
has a range of 2.5 to 5.4 minutes, nevertheless it depends on the species. (Batt, 2000) Soaps that 
contain alcohol are not capable to inactivate the spores of B.cereus since they appear tolerance 
in Ethanol. (Drobniewski FA., 1993) 
Bacillus cereus has many favorable culture media such as Mannitol-Egg Yolk-Polymyxin (MYP), 
Brain Heart Infusion (BHI) and Tryptone Soya Broth (TSB). The optimum incubation is with one of 
the last two in 48 hours based on Drobniewski FA. (Drobniewski FA., 1993)  
1.1.1 Pathogenesis of Bacillus cereus group 
Bacillus cereus is known as a pathogen, especially from foodborne illnesses which means that 
caused health issues even the death to people due to the consumption of contaminated with-it 
food. The reason for the numerous outbreaks this bacterium incited is the intoxication arises from 
its toxin well known as cereulide. The production of the cereulide is encrypted on bacterium’s 
plasmids as in different cases with other toxins the ability of their production appears in genes 
that are carried by the chromosomes of the bacterium. (Ehling-Schulz M.Lereclus D.Koehler TM., 
2019) 
==PAGE:12==
Therefore, there are positive and negative B.cereus strains in the emetic cereulide production. 
The distinction between the strains that do not produce the emetic toxin and those who produce 
it is the presence of the ces genes in their plasmid. These genes synthesize the toxin. (EFSA Panel 
on Biological Hazards (BIOHAZ), 2016) (Stenfors Arnesen, Lotte P., Annette Fagerlund, and Per 
Einar Granum. , 2008) In greater detail, the production of the emetic toxin occurs from the 
CesNRPS in the megaplasmid pCer270. The encoding of the emetic toxin is done by the 
contribution of the ces genes. (Kranzler, Markus, et al., 2024) The emetic positive strains belong 
to the minority, and for that reason the evaluation of their ability to produce or not the emetic 
cereulide cannot be valid by the counts after microbiological analyses. (Jääskeläinen, Elina L., et 
al., 2003)  
2.2 Epidemiology  
The diarrheal or the gastrointestinal (GI) first symptoms onset within 6 to 12 hours base on 
Robinson et al. (Robinson, Richard K., 2014) (Stenfors Arnesen, Lotte P., Annette Fagerlund, and 
Per Einar Granum. , 2008) Some of the developed symptoms may be the watery diarrhea, strange 
ache and in some cases nausea and gastrointestinal expulsion. It can last approximately 12 to 24 
hours, even for some days. However, it is considered as the mild disease that B.cereus groups can 
cause and in rare cases more serious episodes reported. (Stenfors Arnesen, Lotte P., Annette 
Fagerlund, and Per Einar Granum. , 2008) Otherwise, the emesis or emetic cereulide represents 
the non-gastrointestinal but gastroenteritis form (Johler, Sophia, et al. , 2018) and symptoms 
occur within the time period of 1 to 5 hours after the toxin insertion in the human system through 
the contaminated food. (Robinson, Richard K., 2014) According to EFSA, the emetic toxin appears 
within 15 minutes to 6 hours after the infected food to be consumed.  (EFSA Panel on Biological 
Hazards (BIOHAZ), 2016) Symptoms that emetic cases reported are the same with those in 
diarrheal along with more severe episodes or even the death. (Stenfors Arnesen, Lotte P., Annette 
Fagerlund, and Per Einar Granum. , 2008) 
The mild type of toxin is linked with the Nhe, Hbl and CytK enterotoxins, which are unstable in 
thermal processing, while the emetic cereulide is coded by the ces genes based on Johler et al. 
(Johler, Sophia, et al. , 2018) In addition, there are seven ces genes, cesA, cesB, cesC, cesD, cesH, 
cesP, and cesT. The first two are in charge to build the sequence of the cereulide, since cesA 
provides the D-O-Leu and D-Ala, while cesB provides the L-O-Val and L-Val. (Rouzeau-Szynalski, 
Katia, et al., 2020) 
Regarding the required dose to cause illness, it is approximately the same for both diarrheal and 
emetic cereulide. It has been investigated that diarrheal requires more adult cells than spores 
with concentration 105 to 108 cfu (in total) and above. (Stenfors Arnesen, Lotte P., Annette 
Fagerlund, and Per Einar Granum. , 2008)Then the spores will be grown in the small intestine’ 
host and the vegetative cells will secrete the mild type of toxin. (Rouzeau-Szynalski, Katia, et al., 
2020) The reason is that diarrheal causes the disease since the toxin has been produced in the 
small intestine. On the other hand, the emesis requires 105 to 108 cells cfu/g to incite the serious 
==PAGE:13==
13 
 
illness by the intoxication of the already secreted cereulide out of the host’s system. (Stenfors 
Arnesen, Lotte P., Annette Fagerlund, and Per Einar Granum. , 2008) 
 The mild type diseases have been found in many foods such as vegetables and animal products 
like meat and milk. While the acute illness has been found in starchy food such as pasta and rice. 
(Stenfors Arnesen, Lotte P., Annette Fagerlund, and Per Einar Granum. , 2008) In this project the 
focus has been given to the emetic cereulide. 
Initially, the emetic toxin produced during the late log phase as the highest concentration has 
been observed in the stationary phase. (Dommel, Monica K., et al., 2011) The temperature that 
the cereulide can be secreted depends on the species of B.cereus group. Based on studies, a 
psychrophilic species produced the emetic toxin at 8oC. (Guérin, Alizée, et al. , 2017) However, 
investigations have shown that the optimum temperature for the secretion of the emetic toxin is 
between 20oC to 37oC and it pauses production at 40oC. (Kranzler, Markus, et al. , 2016)The are 
many studies that show the concentrations of the emetic cereulide was in the highest level at 5.8 
pH (sauce) than 7.0 pH (rice) or at 5.9 pH (baked food) rather 5.1 pH (quark dessert). Based on 
Rouzeau-Szynalski et al., the pH influences the emetic toxin secretion, nevertheless there are 
many more factors that the concentrations of the toxin in various food vary. Generally, emetic 
toxin has been reported in food items around neutral pH. (Rouzeau-Szynalski, Katia, et al., 2020)

2.2.2 Legislation 
The modified Regulation (EC) 1441/2007 (previous Regulation was the (EC) 2073/2005) 
established a new microbiological standard regarding the possible presence of the Bacillus cereus 
at the final products. The referred to foods are dried baby formula and dried nutritional food for 
medical usages for babies under six months old. The Process Hygiene Criterion is that only one in 
five samples is allowed to have concentrations between 50 to 500 CFU/g of B.cereus. (EFSA Panel 
on Biological Hazards (BIOHAZ), 2016) 
According to the EFSA, the food industries must ensure that the concentrations of the Bacillus 
cereus would not increase on the consumption day. (Frederiksen K et al., 2006) 
EFSA suggests efficient ways to manage the Bacillus cereus growth, for instance the foods and 
leftover’s storage at least ≤7o C, but ideally at ≤4oC. Moreover, some other options to control the 
bacterium could be thermal processing, high hydrostatic pressure, UV radiation and chemical 
disinfectants. Unfortunately, these methods are highly capable of managing the cells, still the 
spores are uncontrollable with these common methods due to their resistivity. However, high 
pressure and thermal processing in high ranges at the same time could contribute to the 
management of them.  (EFSA Panel on Biological Hazards (BIOHAZ), 2016) 
2.3 MALDI-TOF Microflex Mass spectrometer  
MALDTI-TOF MS or Matrix-assisted laser desorption time-of-flight mass spectrometer was first 
discovered in 1987 according to Karas et al. (Karas, Michael, et al., 1987) In the next year, 1988, it 
has been found contributing to general experiments. (Tanaka K, Waki H, Ido Y, Akita S, Yoshida Y, 
Yoshida T, Matsuo T., 1988) While in 2002 gained a Nobel-Prize. (Fenselau C, Demirev PA. , 2001) 
Although it is considered as a recent method for microbiological analyses. (Biswas, Silpak, and 
Jean-Marc Rolain, 2013) As a mass spectrometer has the possibility for molecular identification 
and quantification of ions by the mass-to-charge ratio (m/z). (Hou, Tsung-Yun, Chuan Chiang-Ni, 
and Shih-Hua Teng. , 2019) MALDI-TOF consists of the ion source, the mass analyzer, and the 
detector of m/z. (Biswas, Silpak, and Jean-Marc Rolain, 2013)

2.3.1 Functionality of MALDI-TOF MS 
Initially, the preparation and the putting of the samples on stainless-steel target plate takes place. 
After, it continues with the addition of the saturated matrix solution, which is a liquid that covers, 
the sample and forms crystal with it. It uses for the laser absorption therefore, it can influence 
the outcomes of the m/z and the intensities that the device records. (Biswas, Silpak, and Jean-
Marc Rolain, 2013) The composition of the HCCA matrix solution is Acetonitrile 50% , water 47,5% 
and TFA 2,5% according to Doellinger et al. (Doellinger, Joerg, et al. , 2020) 
==PAGE:16==
 
Figure 5: Pulsed laser rays on a sample during the analysis with MALDI-TOF.  (Bruker) 
While the target plate is inside of the device the analysis of the sample starts by the exposure of 
the crystal to the laser rays as it is shown in figure 5 for brief period in order to prevent the sample 
to be destroyed or degraded (Clark, Andrew E., et al. , 2013),  Consequently, the pulsed UV laser 
that the sample receives are set (Biswas, Silpak, and Jean-Marc Rolain, 2013) and the matrix 
solution is removed from the sample’s molecules due to the vaporization. (Beavis, Ronald C., and 
Brian T. Chait. , 1991) The peptides and proteins of the sample are ionized. (Biswas, Silpak, and 
Jean-Marc Rolain, 2013) The electrostatic field that is created after the ionization contributes to 
the reabsorption of the gaseous ions through a plume of neutrals (Vestal, Marvin L., 2009) as it is 
illustrated in figure 6.  
The ions flight separately towards the detector of mass/charge (m/z), but each one of them 
reaches a different time. The time is unrelated to the velocity and the position that each ion 
started (Vestal, Marvin L., 2009), since according to Beavis et al., the speed is almost identical for 
each ion as a mean of 750 m/s. It is important to mention that the speed of the matrix solution’s 
molecules is significantly higher than the peptides and proteins. (Beavis, Ronald C., and Brian T. 
Chait. , 1991) Regarding the position that each ion started depends on the dimension of the 
formed sample-matrix crystal, which most of the time is approximately 10μm based on studies. 
(Vestal, Marvin L., 2009) When the all the ions reach the detector, the outcome is presented 
automatically as a graph showing the spectrums that have been reported from each ion in one 
sample. (Biswas, Silpak, and Jean-Marc Rolain, 2013) 
The beam attenuator’s role is to reduce or increase the radiation that hits on the sample. It is 
fixed by the company that provides the device, although there is the possibility to be customized 
on the experiments demands.  (Clark, Andrew E., et al. , 2013)

2.3.2 Analyses with MALDI-TOF  
In the microbiological field, MALDI-TOF has been applied as a method that concerns the 
identification of bacteria, proteins, or the categorization of the bacteria through their biochemical 
features.  (Biswas, Silpak, and Jean-Marc Rolain, 2013)

2.3.3 MALDI-TOF as emetic cereulide detector  
There are limited investigations regarding the detection of emetic cereulide. According to Meng 
et al., this method has the ability to identify the bacteria cells and the toxin at the same time. 
(Meng, Jing-Nan, et al. , 2022) The cereulide is detected in mass-to-charge (m/z) 1191 for the 
detection of potassium adduct and 1175 for the detection of the sodium adduct (Doellinger, 
Joerg, et al. , 2020) (Ducrest, Percevent J., et al., 2019), with a difference between those two m/z 
equals to 16 Th. More specifically, the study detected the toxin at 1191,6 and 1175,6. The 
investigations shows that superior intensities have been found cultivating the B.cereus strains on 
Casein-Soy-Peptone (Caso) Agar. On the other hand, the reduced intensities have been recorded 
using Luria-Bertani (LB) Agar. The investigations shows that superior intensities have been found 
cultivating the B.cereus strains on Casein-Soy-Peptone (Caso) Agar. On the other hand, the 
reduced intensities have been recorded using Luria-Bertani (LB) Agar. The highest intensities have 
been received after the extraction with ACN and Ethanol, compare to acetone which shaping 
bigger crystals between the sample and the matrix, it is considered inappropriate for use with 
this method. The highest intensities have been received after the extraction with ACN and 
Ethanol, compare to acetone which shaping bigger crystals between the sample and the matrix, 
it is considered inappropriate for use with this method. Based on Doellinger et al., the limit 
detection of the emetic cereulide analysing with the MALDI-TOF is 0,25 ng per spot. (Doellinger, 
Joerg, et al. , 2020) 
The analysis with the MALDI-TOF has the advantage that for the preparation of the samples the 
needed time is limited. Moreover, the whole analysis is very fast and gives the results directly, 
which is considered as one of the fastest methods, especially for analysis microbiological samples. 
(Biswas, Silpak, and Jean-Marc Rolain, 2013) The fact that can recognize a wide protein spectrum, 
it is competent for the recognition between species that belong to the same group or have 
similarities. (Murray, 2010) In addition, analyses with MALDI-TOF it is possible the saving of the 
data that occur for future analyses, since a protein or peptide molecule can identify even the 
subspecies. (Biswas, Silpak, and Jean-Marc Rolain, 2013) However, the cost is high for the 
purchase of the device, its installation and the training for the personnel that are going to conduct 
the experiments using the MALDI-TOF technology. Another negative point for the MALDI-TOF 
could be the fact that there is limited knowledge regarding real outcomes from practical 
experiments utilizing this method for the detection of the emetic toxin. Considering these two 
reasons, the establishment of the MALDI-TOF is deemed a slow process.  (Meng, Jing-Nan, et al. , 
2022)

3.5.2 Cereulide produced from liquid cultivation from different strains of the Bacillus cereus 
The second group of samples involves the emetic toxin which was produced from the bacteria 
cells of liquid cultivations of the Bacillus cereus strains. The main step of the process includes the 
incubation of the strains in nutritional broth, the centrifuge of them, the sampling of the 
supernatant for analysis, the re-centrifuge of the samples and the extraction of the cereulide from 
the pellet using 3 different methods ACN ≥99.9%, TFA 10% and Ethanol 75%. (Doellinger, Joerg, 
et al. , 2020) 
After the 24 hours of incubation of the Bacillus cereus strains at 30°C, a small quantity of the 
bacteria cells from each strain was transferred separately by dipping an individual inoculation 
loop into the growth plate containing the respective strain and releasing each one of the strains 
into a different tube containing 1ml of Brain Heart Infusion Broth (BHI). Afterwards, the inoculated 
tubes are incubated again at 30°C for 24 hours. The same procedure was repeated using tubes 
containing 1ml of Tryptone Soya Broth (casein soya bean digest broth) (TSB). 
When the incubation was completed, the tubes were centrifuged at 10000g at room temperature 
for two minutes. 1 μl from the supernatant was collected (only from the TSB cultivations), prior 
to discarding, for the analysis of the potential cereulide present in it. The next steps include the 
washing of the remaining pellet with 1 ml of double distilled water, centrifuging the samples again 
under identical conditions, 10000g at room temperature for two minutes, and discarding the 
supernatant. Subsequently, the cereulide was extracted from the pellet by the addition of ethanol 
75%, ACN ≥99.9%, and TFA 10% separately. The three extraction methods are mentioned 
elaborately in Section 3.6 (Extraction method).

In the last experiment, the cereulide produced by B.cereus strains in BHI broth, the procedure 
was executed identically to the previous times, except for an additional step that was conducted

3.6 Extraction methods 
The second group of samples with cereulide produced from liquid cultivation from different 
strains of the Bacillus cereus has been extracted with ACN ≥99.9%, TFA 10% and HPLC grade 
Ethanol 75% (Doellinger, Joerg, et al. , 2020) separately as it was the first sample that extraction 
was needed. Each tube contains a pellet with bacteria cells and a solvent. The total volume of 
each tube’s solvent was 600 μl since centrifuge was following and identical volume was essential. 
For the extraction of the unknown concentration of the toxin produced from the Bacillus cereus 
strains, 600 μl ACN ≥99.9% concentration was utilized and added to the tube containing the pellet 
with the bacteria cells. Following 1 minute vortex and centrifuged at 14000rpm for 3 minutes. 1 
μl was taken from the supernatant and analyzed with MALDI-TOF for any possible existence of 
the cereulide. The initiate TFA’s concentration is 100%. However, TFA was diluted on a scale of 
1:10 with double distilled water. Thus, the final concentration of TFA that has been used was 10%.  
For the extraction of the cereulide from the bacteria cells was added 600 μl of TFA 10% in the 
tube, following 1 minute vortex and centrifuged at 14000rpm for 3 minutes. 1 μl was taken from 
the supernatant and analyzed with MALDI-TOF for any possible existence of the cereulide. The 
initiate concentration of HPLC grade Ethanol was 100%. For the cereulide extraction from the 
strains 150 μl of HPLC water and 450 μl of absolute HPLC grade Ethanol were mixed (in total 600 
μl in the tube). Therefore, the final concentration of the Ethanol that has been used for extraction 
was 75%. Following 1 minute vortex and centrifuged at 14000rpm for 3 minutes. 1 μl was taken 
from the supernatant and analyzed with MALDI-TOF for any possible existence of the cereulide. 
(Doellinger, Joerg, et al. , 2020) 
The experimental results showed that the ACN ≥99.9% was the best method out of the three for 
the extraction of the cereulide since it appeared consistent across the replication of the 
experiments. While the TFA caused damage to the analyzed samples and ethanol was not so 
reliable as it appeared inconsistent. For that reason, ACN ≥99.9% was the only solvent that was 
used for the next samples since it appeared more consistent regarding the results from the 
experiments. 
For the samples with pure toxin of cereulide in food in different concentrations, the extraction 
method of the toxin was ACN ≥99.9%. More specifically, 1000 μl of ACN ≥99.9% was mixed with 
1g of rice. Following 1 minute vortex and centrifuged at 14000rpm for 3 minutes.  1 μl was taken 
from the supernatant and analyzed with MALDI-TOF for any possible existence of the cereulide. 
For the last group of experiments with cereulide produced from BP-1 and F4810 of Bacillus 
cereus in food in different collection time, the extraction of the cereulide from the rice was done 
using ACN ≥99.9%. The ACN ≥99.9% used in different percentages such as 50, 60, 80, or 100% 
depending on the weight of each sample. Explanation is given under Tables 20 and 21 in Section 
4.4 in Results. Following 1 minute vortex and centrifuged at 14000rpm for 3 minutes.  1 μl was 
taken from the supernatant and analyzed with MALDI-TOF for any possible existence of the 
cereulide.

3.7 MALDI-TOF Analysis 
The analyses by the MALDI-TOF Microflex Mass spectrometer were conducted with the usage of 
the target plate, which consists of 8 rows with 12 cyclical spots each one of them, where the 
samples were put on and analyzed.  
After the extraction and centrifugation 1 μl was taken from each of the sample’s supernatants 
and was put on one spot of the target plate. In total, 6 spots were used for each sample to ensure 
the consistency of the findings. Waiting for the 1 μl to get dry and thereafter 1 μl of saturated 
Matrix solution was placed on top of each spot to protect the samples from the laser shots and 
let it get dry. Lastly, the samples must be fully dry before starting the analysis with the MALDI-
TOF. (Doellinger, Joerg, et al. , 2020)

The findings and the discussion from each experiment are presented in this section divided into

4.1 Pure cereulide in different concentrations  
In the first two experiments belong to the special course, nevertheless the rest of the 
experimental work was part of the master’s thesis. The positive findings contributed to the 
understanding of the detection of cereulide since the 1191 (±3) m/z and 1171 (±7-8) m/z have 
been recorded. The pure toxin in the concentrations 0.005, 0.05, 0.5, 5 μg/ml that have been 
analysed in the first experiment and were detected successfully with MALDI-TOF since the device 
detected the K+ at 1194 m/z and Na+ at 1178 m/z. The results obtained after the Flex Analyses 
program and after the calculations (in excel) the standard deviation (STDEV.S) and the average 
of the intensities for both 1194 m/z and 1178 m/z to check the reliability and consistency of the 
findings. The concentration of 5 μg/ml was the highest in this experiment and all the 4 used spots 
on the target plate have been detected as the concentration 0.5 μg/ml. The amounts of 0.005 
and 0.05 μg/ml have three successful trials out of the four spots on the target plate. This 
observation helped to comprehend that the detection limit of the cereulide must be lower than 
0.005 μg/ml since MALDI-TOF did not struggle to detect the toxin. However, the gaps between 
the concentrations in this experiment were huge and due to that in the next experiment the 
concentrations were closer to each other.

5 Conclusions  
This master’s thesis had a purpose to validate the MALDI-TOF Microflex Mass spectrometer for 
the detection and quantification of the emetic toxin-cereulide in forms of pure and produced by 
the responsible bacteria, emetic Bacillus cereus and in food. 
According to the findings of this investigation, the MALDI-TOF Microflex Mass spectrometer can 
detect the emetic cereulide, pure and produced from various emetic strains and in rice but not in 
a consistent way. In greater detail, the lowest amount that the pure emetic toxin has been 
detected was 0.009 μg/ml. However, only at 1194 m/z was recorded four out of six used spots on 
the target plate, while the sodium adducts at 1178 m/z were not detected at all. This leads to the 
conclusion that the detection limit of the emetic cereulide using the MALDI-TOF MS method is 
0.009 μg/ml for the detection of potassium adduct. It is noteworthy to mention that the lowest 
concentration that both potassium and sodium ions have been recorded at the same time (same 
sample) was at 0.02 μg/ml of pure emetic cereulide. 
On the other hand, the produced emetic cereulide by different strains of B.cereus has been 
detected after the extraction with ACN ≥99.9%, and Ethanol 75%, while the TFA 10% as an 
extraction method has not proven effective. Based on this study the detection of the pure emetic 
toxin in rice has been found in the lowest concentration of 0.05 μg/g at 1194 m/z, and 1 μg/g at 
both 1194 and 1178 m/z. Lastly, the produced emetic cereulide from Bacillus cereus strains BP-1 
and F4810 has been proved at both m/z after 36 and 48 hours of the inoculation at 23±1oC, 
respectively. However, using the F4810 the first detection observed at 1194 m/z after 30 hours of 
incubation. Based on this study, the quantification of the emetic cereulide using the MALDI-TOF 
Microflex Mass spectrometer was not feasible. Upon repeated experiments the intensities for 
each pure toxin concentration did not demonstrate consistency, resulting in inaccurate outcomes.   
The microbiological analyses provided valuable insights and contributed to the growth curve’s 
formation regarding the mesophilic, B.cereus and B.cereus spore’s total counts in the last 
experiment after the incubation at 23±1oC in various collective points (time) with the B.cereus 
strains in rice. The findings revealed that in both experiments BP-1 and F4810 the total mesophilic 
bacteria and B.cereus had the same growing path relatively. In the inoculated rice sample with 
the BP-1 strain it has been observed that the growth of both total mesophilic bacteria and 
B.cereus had significant growth. After 21 hours the growth was at 7.40 log cfu/g and 7.44 log 
cfu/g, ending after 44 hours at 8.11 log cfu/g and 7.81 log cfu/g, respectively. Nevertheless, the 
B.cereus spore’s total counts were from 2 log cfu/g after 21 hours ending to 7.42 log cfu/g after 
44 hours, but their growth in the meantime was gradual. In the F4810 samples in rice for the 
same incubation periods the total mesophilic bacteria, B.cereus and B.cereus spores were from 
7.01 log cfu/g, 6.91 log cfu/g and 2 log cfu/g (21 hours), to 8.21 log cfu/g, 8.00 log cfu/g and 6.63 
log cfu/g (44 hours). The difference in the results between the two strains was approximately 0.5 
log cfu/g, corresponding which is not remarkable to conclude that there was actual reduction but 
may be due uncertainties.  
==PAGE:55==
55 
 
Undeniably, this investigation conveys important knowledge regarding this specific method of 
emetic cereulide detection. However, further research needs to be conducted in order to expand 
the insights and improve the analyses using MALDI-TOF Microflex Mass spectrometer. For 
instance, future investigations could focus on the preparation of the samples, using pure emetic 
cereulide from only one supplier every time and using more than 6 spots on the target plate since 
this can increase the likelihood of detection. In addition, they could investigate and after each 
calibration of the device if the receiving results show consistency.  
Concluding, the analyses using MALDI-TOF Microflex Mass spectrometer for the detection of the 
emetic toxin can save time and contribute to significant discoveries. For instance, the presence of 
the toxin in food, which a company with this knowledge can act fast ensuring the public health. 
However, for the quantification of the emetic cereulide its reliability is questionable. In addition, 
its price for purchase and installation of the machine and the trained personnel that it needs 
depends on the industry ‘s ambition.
