Abstract
The paper is based on developing a website prototype for type 2 diabetes patients by
personalizing the structure of the content in much simpler way to guide the user from scratch.
The user can find information by sorting their requirements. The data is available in text
and video format. So that the user can easily choose to watch a video or read the text to
get to know the information they are looking for. Here, The user is a person with type 2
diabetes mellitus. The data is in the form of educational content packages.
Problem statement: The main problem is a lack of structured information for the
newly diagnosed person or living with type 2 diabetes.
Objective: The project aims to design a website prototype for person with type 2
diabetes. To develop a desirable prototype, we met type 2 diabetes patients.
Methods: Initially designed by literature review and developed by double diamond
method by taking interviews at slagelse hospitals.
Results: Designed high fidelity website prototype by considering type 2 diabetes pa-
tient’s needs during two interviews
Conclusions: Our results show that type 2 diabetic patients need a high-fidelity pro-
totype with personalized content, which includes knowing the information and guiding in-
dividually to learn insights. One solution has been developed in this project, a customized
content prototype from scratch for the prevention and treatment of type 2 diabetes. .
Index Terms:
Website prototype, educational content, type 2 diabetes,
Chronic illness, Interviews.

Introduction
Diabetes is a chronic illness where blood sugar or blood glucose level is higher than
average. Blood glucose is the primary source of energy that comes from the food we take
daily. Some reasons for getting infected with diabetes include family genetics, health issues,
and ethnic background [1]. Diabetes divided into three types. Which are:
1. Type 1 diabetes
2. Type 2 diabetes
3. Pre diabetes
4. Gestational diabetes
Type 1 diabetes is called insulin-dependent diabetes. It is because when Pancreas
doesn’t produce enough insulin, the person will be affected with type 1 diabetes. Insulin
is a hormone located behind the stomach. Insulin induces cells called beta cells to absorb
glucose and produce energy[2].
Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus is a disease. It is also called adult-onset diabetes. Type 2
diabetes occurs when your blood glucose or blood sugar level is very high, or your pancreas
doesn’t use insulin properly. Usually, middle-aged people can affect type2 diabetes[2].
Pre diabetes The blood sugar or blood glucose level is higher than the regular level
but not enough to consider type 2 diabetes, and then it is called pre diabetes. Adults and
children have more pre-diabetic symptoms, which leads to developing type 2 diabetes. The
treatment and care are the same as type 2 diabetes[3].
Gestational diabetes It only develops during pregnancy. It is because of hormonal
imbalance. If the person ignores it during pregnancy, it affects the baby and raises the risk
of breathing problems. In addition, it is a sign of developing type 2 diabetes later in life.[2]

we cannot see any difference in our blood sugar levels. So, every element plays a crucial role
in reducing the risk of type 2 diabetes.
2.1
Diet
Diet is one of the important segments in reducing the risk of type 2 diabetes. There are
three popular diets to manage diabetes 2. The low-carbohydrate or ketogenic, vegan diet,
and the Mediterranean diet. These help to improve glycaemic control and weight loss and
long-term manageability plays an essential role in each diet [8].
The low-carbohydrate or ketogenic diet is high in fat and low in carbohydrates
which is less than 50-g carbohydrate per day. This diet helps to lose weight. The dietary
carbohydrate is the primary macro-nutrient that raises blood glucose levels. Tracking carbo-
hydrate consumption and blood glucose level is essential. Typically, the American Diabetes
Association recommends that about 45 percent of a person’s calories should come from car-
bohydrates, which is about 30 to 60 g per meal and about 15 to 30 g for snacks. Person
should individualize the diet and calorie needs. Some foods which includes in this diet are
whole eggs, avocado, dark chocolate, cheese, nuts and greens [8].
Vegan diet The vegan diet consists of a diet without meat, which includes no added
animal fats when cooking. A low-fat plant-based diet influences nutrient intake and body
composition in several ways that may, in turn, affect insulin sensitivity. First, such diets are
low in fat and high in fiber. The vegan diet patients saw more significant improvements in

2.2
Exercises
Exercises are considered the cornerstone for the reduction of type 2 diabetes. In addi-
tion, regular activities can help build muscle and avoid obesity. Thirty minutes workouts
per day keep most of the diseases away, including type 2 diabetes.
Physical activity prevents and manages type 2 diabetes. In addition, it has several benefits,
such as weight loss and weight maintenance and improving the insulin/glucose profile for
people with prediabetes, glycemic control in individuals with type 2 diabetes, and quality
of life for all individuals. Of particular importance for people with diabetes or at risk is a
reduction of cardiovascular risk factors. Its role as a vital component of a lifestyle interven-
tion that includes dietary modification and weight loss has been well-documented.[9]
Before increasing the usual patterns of the exercise program, a person with type 2 dia-
betes Mellitus must undergo a detailed medical evaluation with an appropriate diagno-
sis.Individualized exercise prescription can minimize the risk to patients. so doctor’s advice
and exercise prescription are essential to start workoutsâsimple workouts such as walking,
swimming, and cycling. Aerobic exercises and resistance training are the two most important
factors to prevent type 2 diabetes.[10]
2.3
Medication management
There are several ways to minimize blood sugar or blood glucose level. Firstly, medica-
tion varies from person to person. Some may need one prescription, and others may need
combinations. Primarily type 2 diabetes can be managed by diet and exercise only. Some
people use oral medication, and some use insulin.[11]

2.4.1
Blood sugar level
The blood sugar level tests whether the person has diabetes or not. First, the blood
sugar level is measured using a glucometer.
The measurement is 70-100 milligrams per
deciliter (mg/dL) before the meal and less than 140 mg/dL 2 hours after the meal, then
the person is diagnosed with a normal blood sugar level (No diabetes). The measurement is
between 100-125 milligrams per deciliter (mg/dL) before the meal and 140-199 (mg/dL) 2
hours after the meal, and the person is diagnosed with prediabetes. Finally, if The measure
is greater than 126 milligrams per deciliter (mg/dL) before the meal and greater than 200
(mg/dL) 2 hours after the meal, the person is diagnosed with type 2 diabetes [13]
mmol/L is millimoles per litre and mole is scientific unit. mmol/L and mg/dL are both
metrics. We can use either of them. For example, people use mmol/L in some areas and
mg/dL. Both are used to measure blood glucose levels.
A1C is the diagnostic test, also called the HbA1c test, which indicates an average
blood sugar level over three months. In addition, it measures the percentage of blood sugar
in hemoglobin. If the A1C test results in 6 percent or less, the person is diagnosed with a
normal blood sugar level. If the A1C test results are 6-6.4 percent, the person is diagnosed
with prediabetes. If the A1C test results in 6.5 percent or more, then the person diagnosed
with type 2 diabetes.[14]

Methods
Two interviews were conducted with type 2 diabetes patients at slagelse hospitals to
design a prototype by considering patients’ needs through iterations. The final stage is a
validated digital prototype by Diabetes nurses at Herlev hospital, with the personal doctor,
family and friends. .
Figure 5: Project Overview
3.1
Interviews design process
The interview structure was based on double diamond method.[15]. The model has 4
phases. 1. discovery, 2. definition, 3. development and 4.Delivery.
Discovery phase: Exploration of the problem statement and discuss the needs of type
2 diabetes patients to be addressed.
Definition phase: The needs gathered from the discovery phase can help to the re-
finement of problem.
Development phase: Creation of the solution.
Delivery phase: Testing user needs and evaluation of solution.
The interviews main focus was understanding the needs of type 2 diabetes patients.
Interviews was carried out by the persons are newly diagnosed with type 2 diabetes and
persons living with type 2 diabetes for a while. Initially, explored the problem and discussed
the needs and refined the problem. Created prototype designs, tested with type 2 diabetes
