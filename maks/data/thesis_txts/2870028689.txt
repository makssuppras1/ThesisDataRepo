Abstract
Obesity was estimated in 1 in every 8 people in 2022.
It is known to be a risk facยญ
tor for diseases such as cardiovascular diseases, cancer and type two diabetes. It is
thought to cause systemic chronic lowยญgrade inflammation, causing metabolic dysfuncยญ
tion. Metabolic syndrome is a syndrome defined by a cluster of syndrome, such as hyperยญ
tension, insulin resistance and dyslipidemia. The lowยญgrade inflammation seen in obese
patients is thought to be a possible link to MetS, which is often seen in obese patients.
Throughout this study, flow cytometry was used to study Tยญcell populations and cytokine
secretion in Ossabaw pigs. Ossabaw pigs have been considered a good model for obeยญ
sity due to their small size, similar immunology, metabolism and physiology to humans.
The project had two aim: designing and optimizing an intracellular staining panel for flow
cytometry and to use the panel to investigate Tยญcell subsets and cytokine secretion in lean
and obese Ossabaw pigs. Initially, the effects of cryopreservation on cytokine secretion
was investigated as well as ageยญdependency changes in Tยญcell subsets and cytokine seยญ
cretion in Ossabaw pigs. Cryopreservation experiment confirmed that cryopreservation
did no affect cytokine secretion. It also showed the development and changes of the imยญ
mune system with age in Ossabaw pigs. A few different stimulators were compared on
their ability to induce cytokine secretion in PBMCs from Ossabaw pigs.
A new intracellular staining panel was developed and optimized, containing an antibody
panel for CD3, ฮณฮด, CD8, IFNฮณ, TNF and ILยญ17 markers. Tยญcell subsets and cytokine seยญ
cretion from two obesity experiments: shortยญterm and longยญterm, were compared to lean
control pigs. Results showed a significant decrease of PBLs but no significant differences
in cytokine secretion or Tยญcell subsets in longยญterm induced obese pigs compared to lean
control pigs. Results from shortยญterm inducing obesity showed a decrease in CD8+ popยญ
ulation, and CD8+ IFNฮณ secretion in the obese pigs compared to lean control pigs.
A new intracellular cytokine staining panel for flow cytometry to investigate immune reยญ
sponse in PBMCs from Ossabaw pigs was successfully designed. While differences in
CD8+ Tยญcell populations and CD8+ IFNฮณ secretion in shortยญterm obesity induced pigs was
observed, it cannot be concluded whether these changes are solely due to obesity or influยญ

after the two different obesity induced experiments could be due to adaption to obesity or
difference in diet.
Further studies with larger sample sizes and more samples over time are needed to clarยญ
ify the impact obesity has on the immune system. The project has provided a valuable
framework for using flow cytometry to investigate immunological function in Ossabaw pigs,
which is valuable tool for obesityยญrelated studies.

Introduction
Obesity is one of the major health concerns in the modern world. World Health Organisaยญ
tion estimated that around 16% of adults were obese in the year 2022 [1], which can be
associated with diseases such as type two diabetes and cardiovascular diseases [2]. It
can also lead to chronic lowยญgrade inflammation, which can become systemic. Metabolic
syndrome (MetS) is a syndrome caused by a cluster of syndrome leading to a higher risk
of developing diseases such as type two diabetes (T2D), fat liver, cardiovascular diseases
and more. It is often seen in patients that are obese and this lowยญgrade inflammation is
thought to be a possible link between MetS and obesity [3]. Although many studies have
been performed to further understand how obesity and MetS are connected, it is still not
fully known. The effects it can have on peoples health are also not fully known, such
as the effect it can have on the immune system. It is known that this chronic lowยญgrade
inflammation can lead to changes in both the adaptive and immune systems, e.g., imbalยญ
ance in Tยญcell subsets and increased proยญinflammatory cytokine secretion [4]. Moreover,
obesity and MetS have been correlated to more severe infections and vaccine failure [5โ
8], which highlights the need for a better understanding of the disease.
Pig animal models have been used for biomedical research for many years as they reยญ
semble humans anatomically, physiologically, and metabolically [9]. Ossabaw pigs are a
breed of miniature pigs that express a specific genotype, making them prone to becoming
obese and developing metabolic diseases when given a highยญcalorie diet. For that reason,
they are an excellent animal model for obesity and MetS research [10, 11].
Flow cytometry is a wellยญestablished technology often used in immunology research [12].
However, its use with pig cells has some challenges, such as the availability of purified
antibodies and fluorophoreยญconjugated antibodies.
In this project, the effect of obesity on the adaptive and innate immune system was invesยญ
tigated using flow cytometry by examining intracellular cytokine secretion from Ossabaw
PBMCs. An intracellular cytokine staining panel was designed and optimized, building
on a surface staining protocol developed before in the Innate Immunology group. Furยญ
thermore, antigenยญspecific stimulation of PBMCs from Ossabaw pigs was explored using
two antigens: AP12 and ApXIV. Their ability to stimulate IFNฮณ and TNF secretion was
examined with an ELISA analysis.
The hypothesis is that obesity in Ossabaw pigs causes systemic inflammation, which
would be seen by elevated levels of IFNฮณ, TNF and ILยญ17 secretion. This has been reยญ
ported before in various studies on human patients with obesity and/or MetS.

2.1.1
The Immune system
The immune system is a complex system composed of circulating and scattered cells as
well as a collection of lymphoid organs. The immune system has two different types of
responses to protect against invading microbes: innate response and adaptive response
[13]. The innate response is the first line of defense against pathogens. It is unspecific
and, therefore, gives broad protection. It consists of physiological and chemical barriers,
innate immune cells, components of humoral immunity, and cytokines. The innate imยญ
mune cells are phagocytic cells, such as granulocytes, monocytes, macrophages, natural
killer (NK) cells, and dendritic cells (DC). Innate immune cells recognize molecular strucยญ
tures, called pathogenยญassociated molecular patterns (PAMPs) or damageยญassociated
molecular patterns (DAMPs), by pattern recognition receptors (PRR). That activates the
innate effector functions, such as phagocytosis and cytotoxicity [14]. Lastly, the innate
immune system is an activator of the adaptive immune system through the synthesis of
cytokines and antigen presentation to T and B cells [15].
The adaptive immune response is an acquired immune response that eliminates pathogens
and creates memory against known antigens, therefore having the ability to respond more
vigorously to repeated exposure. It is divided into two types: humoral and cellยญmediated
immune response. Humoral immunity is mediated by antibodies that are secreted by
plasma cells. Cellยญmediated immunity is mediated by antigenยญspecific T lymphocytes,
triggered by the recognition of antigens by Tยญcell receptors (TCR). Tยญcells can eliminate
pathogens, regulate immune responses, and provide a longยญlived memory T lymphocyte
response that rapidly reacts against repeated exposure [13, 16].
2.1.2
Tยญcell development and function
Tยญcell development starts in the bone marrow, where hematopoietic stem cells differentiยญ

cortex of the thymus, where they undergo Tยญcell lineage commitment. These thymocytes
lack Tยญcell receptors (TCR) as well as CD4/CD8 coยญreceptors and are therefore characยญ
terized as double negative (DN). The DN cells undergo TCR rearrangement, giving rise
to either ฮฑฮฒ TCR or ฮณฮด TCR [17, 18]. Cells expressing ฮณฮด TCR become immature ฮณฮด
thymocytes until they become IFNฮณ or ILยญ17A producing ฮณฮด Tยญcells, based on their exยญ

coยญreceptors CD4 and CD8, making them CD4+CD8+ double positive (DP) Tยญcells. Durยญ

and present selfยญpeptides on Major Histocompatibility Complex (MHC) class I and MHC
class II to facilitate either CD8+ or CD4+ single positive (SP), respectively. Only cells
that receive survival signals by engaging in selfยญpeptide MHC complexes survive, while
the other cells die by neglect. From here, SP cells migrate to the medulla of the thymus,
where they undergo negative selection. Medullary thymic epithelial cells (mTECs) genยญ

strongly to these selfยญpeptides die due to apoptosis. This process ultimately results in thyยญ
mocyte differentiation into mature single positive naive CD4+ or CD8+ Tยญcells that move
to the blood vessels where they travel to secondary lymphoid tissues and undergo Tยญcell

Matured T lymphocytes that leave the thymus are considered naive until they have been
exposed to an antigen. Antigen presentation happens in the secondary lymphoid tissues,
such as lymph nodes, spleen, and tonsils [13, 20]. Three signals are required for a proยญ
ductive Tยญcell activation. Antigenยญpresenting cells (APCs) present antigens on their MHC
class to Tยญcell receptors (TCR) of naive Tยญcells. TCR:peptideยญMHC complex provides a
downstream signaling pathway, leading to Tยญcell activation. Other signals that are also reยญ
quired for productive Tยญcell activation are coยญstimulatory ligands and cytokine instructions.
The main coยญstimulatory signal is the binding of CD28 on naive Tยญcells to CD80 or CD86
on DCs. This amplifies the TCR signal and results in Tยญcell proliferation and differentiation
[17, 18].
Antigen presentation is when APCs present a specific antigen to TCRs on naive T lymยญ
phocytes. The antigen is bound to histocompatibility complex (MHC)ยญderived molecules
on APCs, which then interact with TCR. MHC has two major forms, MHC class I and
class II. Mature T lymphocytes express either CD4 or CD8 molecules that interact with
MHC class I or class II, respectively. That leads to the activation of CD4+ T helper (Th)
cells or CD8+ cytotoxic T lymphocytes (CTLs) [20, 21]. Cytokines also enhance Tยญcell
activation and proliferation. The initial source of cytokines is APCs, as well as other inยญ
nate immune cells. Differentiated cells also produce cytokines and can, therefore, make
a positive feedback loop, enhancing the differentiation and response of naive Tยญcells [22].
CD4+ Tยญcells differentiate into Th cells and T regulatory (Treg) cells, while CD8+ Tยญcells
differentiate into CTLs. The cells are further divided into Th1, Th2, Th17, and T follicular
helper (Tfh) cells, depending on the cytokine signal they receive [22]. Th1 differentiation
is initiated by Interleukinยญ12 (ILยญ12) and IFNฮณ. The main effector function of Th1 is proยญ
viding stimulatory signals that lead to CD8+ Tยญcell activation, differentiation, response,
and memory formation [23]. Th2 differentiation is initiated by ILยญ4 and ILยญ2. Th2 cells
provide an immune response to extracellular parasites as well as play a role in the acยญ
tivation of B cells by inducing B cell differentiation, antibody class switching, and affinity
maturation [24]. Th17 differentiation is initiated by ILยญ1, ILยญ6, ILยญ21 and TGFฮฒ. Their main
function is defense against extracellular pathogens [23]. Treg cells are characterized by
their production of TGFยญbeta and ILยญ10. Tregs regulate inflammatory responses by proยญ
ducing proยญinflammatory suppressing cytokines [20, 23]. Tfh cells secrete ILยญ6 and ILยญ21.
Their main function is aiding in the development of antigenยญspecific Bยญcell immunity [22].
CD8+ Tยญcells differentiate into CTLs after ILยญ2ยญmediated signaling. The effector function
of CTLs is killing cells that are a threat to the host, e.g., virally infected cells and tumor
cells. CTLs kill cells directly with cellยญcell contact or indirectly by secreting TNF and IFNฮณ.
Direct killing is mediated by the binding of the Fas ligand on the surface of CTLs to the
Fas receptor on the target Tยญcell, triggering apoptosis. CTLs can also release perforin and
granzymes, highly cytotoxic proteins, into the intracellular space, which leads to apoptosis
of target Tยญcells [21].

Effects of obesity on immune response
The effects that obesity and Mets have on the immune system are complicated and not
yet fully understood. As AT expands in obesity, the tissue grows beyond vascular supยญ

Animal models for obesity and MetS
The use of animal models is essential for many biomedical research fields. It provides
us with a better understanding of many pathological and biological processes and can be
a tool to better understand human biology. However, the use of an appropriate animal
model is essential, and the choice should be based on each individual project. Rodent
models are often used for animal studies as they are cheap and easy to use, and many
knockout and transgenic mice are available. They are similar to humans in anatomy,
physiology, and genetics, which can make them a great animal model for genetic reยญ
search [61]. However, when researching obesity and MetS, there are some important
biochemical distinctions in metabolism, physiology, and immunology between humans
and mice. Instead, the use of nonยญprimate or large animal models would be a better fit,
as they resemble humans more [62].
The use of pigs as large animal models for biomedical research has emerged as beยญ

metabolic profile, and size [9]. They also have some similarities in immune system reยญ
sponses, particularly Tยญcell responses. The characteristics and function of porcine Tยญcells
closely resemble human Tยญcells [63].
In regards to obesity and MetS research, the fact that the immune system of pigs more
closely resembles the human immune system and the fact that their cardiovascular system
is more similar makes them a good animal model. The heart and coronary circulation and
the susceptibility to coronary artery disease are very similar in pigs and humans [64].
When pigs are fed a highยญfatยญcholesterol diet, they develop coronary conditions that are
very similar to the human condition [10, 11]. In order to gain an understanding of obesity
and MetS, and the comorbidities following, such as chronic lowยญgrade inflammation and
cardiovascular diseases, these are great characteristics to look for in animal models [65].

Experimental Background
3.1
Flow Cytometry
Flow cytometry is a technology that uses single or multiple lasers to analyze single cells
or particles rapidly. It can characterize a mixed population simultaneously, making it a
great choice to analyze a population of cells, e.g., immune cells. Each cell is analyzed
for visible light scatter and fluorescence parameters. It uses two visible light scatters:
forward scatter (FSC) and side scatter (SSC). FSC indicates the relative size of the cell,
and SSC measures the internal complexity or granularity of the cell. The cells are marked
with fluorescent material, either with fluorescent proteins, dyes, or fluorescent conjugated
antibodies. Fluorescent emission coming from the material is then used to identify cells
that the proteins, dyes, or antibodies bind to. The cell viability is detected by binding a
dye to amines within the cell to determine whether the cell membrane is intact. [12]
3.1.1
Traditional flow cytometers
Traditional flow cytometers consist of fluidics, optics, and electronics systems. The fluidics
system uses a pressurized sheath fluid to transport the particles in a singleยญfile fashion
to the interrogation point, where they are met with the optical system [81]. The sample
is injected into a flow chamber, where it is met with the sheath fluid, usually phosphateยญ
buffered saline (PBS). The flow cell is under pressure, forcing the cells into a single file
before they pass through the laser beam at the interrogation point [82].
The optical system consists of excitation optics (lasers) and collection optics, which are
mirrors and filters that gather and direct scattered light to optical detectors. At the interroยญ
gation point, a light from the laser beam hits the cells in a forward manner. FSC and SSC
provide information on the physical properties of the cell, thereby allowing it to differentiate
between different Tยญcell types. FSC light scatters around the cell, and the amount of FCS
light is dependent on the size of the cell. The light that passes through the cell membrane
will refract and reflect off of the cytoplasmic organelles or the nucleus of the cellอพ this is
the SSC light. The fluorochromes are excited by an absorption wavelength, emitting light
within a range of wavelengths, called emission wavelength [82]. Dichroic filters allow the
passing of either shorter or longer emission wavelengths but reflect the remaining light at
an angle, steering the fluorescence light to photomultiplier detectors [12].
Emission wavelength is a range, and therefore, there can be spillover between ranges
of different fluorochromes. A fluorochrome can be detected by more than the assigned
detector and thereby be detected as a different fluorochrome. This is called spectral
overlap, and it needs to be corrected with compensation by running compensation controls
of each individual fluorochrome [82].
The electronic system converts the light signals into numerical data. Photodetectors conยญ
vert the light into electronic pulses [81]. A numerical value is generated by the height,
width, and area of voltage pulses, which are then displayed as dots on an analysis plot
[82].

6.1.1
Cryopreservation
Investigating the effects of cryopreservation on PBMCs is essential. While using fresh
samples is preferable, it is not always feasible, and the process of freezing samples for
later use is well established. When comparing differences in cytokine levels between
groups or across studies, it is important to consider and account for any small changes
that cryopreservation may introduce.
In the present study, significant differences were observed between the live, CD3+ (p
= 0,018), CD8+ (p = 0,043), and DN (p = 0,031) cell populations in fresh compared to
frozen samples, where all these populations significantly decreased in the frozen samples,
as seen in figures 5.1 and 5.2. However, since antiยญCD4 staining was not performed in
fresh samples, comparisons of CD4+, DP, and DP IFNฮณ populations between fresh and
frozen samples were not possible. Therefore, the DN population appeared larger in fresh
samples, as CD4+ cells would have been a part of this group.
Research on the effects of cryopreservation on the viability and functionality of pig PBMCs
remains limited, and studies on human PBMCs have reported conflicting results. Li et al.
(2021) reported that IFNฮณ secretion significantly increased in frozen pig PBMCs following
PMA stimulation [100]. In another study, Li et al. (2021) found no significant difference in
IFNฮณ production between fresh and frozen cells after mitogen stimulation, similar to what
was found in the present study. That same study also reported an increase in DN cells
and a decrease in CD8+ cells in frozen compared to fresh unstimulated samples. When
stimulated with PHA, DN cell populations decreased, whereas ConA stimulation led to
reduced proliferation of CD3+, CD4+, CD8+, and DN cells [101].
Despite these differences in cell populations and cytokine secretion, in all the studies and
the present study, cryopreservation did not affect Tยญcell viability. The viability in the fresh
and frozen samples were similar, 89,5% and 91,4%, respectively. The amount of live
cells per ml was much lower for the frozen sample compared to the fresh, 3,08 million
and 10 million, respectively. However, the amount of live cells in the fresh sample is an
estimate made during the PBMC isolation and is therefore not completely comperable
to the frozen sample. Looking at the difference between live populations from the flow
cytometry results, it is clear that the fresh samples are of a higher quality with more live
cells. The reason that there is significant difference between live cells is likely because
the frozen and thawed samples are more sensitive to stimulation and other processes
than the fresh cells [102].
As can also be seen in figure 5.2, the CD4/CD8 populations are not fully separated and
very little CD4+ and CD4+CD8+ DP populations are seen in the frozen sample. LAC
is known to internalize CD4 surface molecules during stimulation [103]. It is clear that
this is happening in these samples, as the populations are separated much clearly in the
unstimulated medium control. In order to further validate this theory, unstimulated PBMCs
were surface stained and run on flow cytometry. They show similar results, where there
is much clearer separation, as can be seen in figure 5.3.

6.1.2
Ageยญdependence
The composition of Tยญcell subpopulation in pigs changes a lot with age. Pietrasina et al.
reported a significant increase in CD3+ and CD4+CD8+ DP Tยญcells but decrease in CD4+
Tยญcells [59]. PomorskaยญMรณl et al. reported similar results, where there was a significant
increase in CD3+, slight decrease of CD4+, significant increase of CD8+ and DP Tยญcells
[57]. In the present study, CD4+ and DN cells significantly decreased (p = 0,01 and p
= 0,002) with age while CD8+ cells significantly increased (p = 0,0005). CD8+ and DP
IFNฮณ cytokine secretion also significantly increased (p = 0,001 and p = 0,03) with age.
This corresponds to what PomorskaยญMรณl et al. and Pietrasina et al. have reported. It
seems that the population of DN Tยญcells is age dependent, where young pigs have a
high percentage of DN and low percentage of DP but in older pigs it is reversed. Young
pigs have a very high proportion of ฮณฮด Tยญcells, around 30ยญ50% at 4 months old [50]. It
could be that this high proportion of CD3+ Tยญcells, that are CD4โCDโDN, are not ฮฑฮฒ Tยญ
cells but rather ฮณฮด Tยญcells. Other changes in subpopulations are likely due to extraยญthymic,
antigenยญdependent changes in CD8 expression on T lymphocytes as well as development
and maturation of subpopulations [60]. Memory Tยญhelper cells in pigs are characterized
as CD4+CD8lo DP Tยญcells. As the pigs age and are exposed to more foreign antigens,
it drives naive Tยญhelper cells to antigenยญspecific DP memory Tยญhelper cells, leading to a
larger DP subpopulation [59].
The problem of antibodies failing to bind to CD4 molecules has been seen before in the
Innate Immunology group. That time, a different antibody clone was used, 74ยญ12ยญ4 which
has been known to not detect CD4+ Tยญcells in some minipig breeds [104]. Therefore,
a different clone was tested this time, b38c6c7. However, the same problem presented
itself in the same two pigs as last time with the new antibody clone, see pig 95 and Angel
in figure 5.5a. Matsubara et al. identified two phenotypes of CD4 alleles in a herd of
microminipigsอพ CD4.A and CD4.B. Amino acid substitutions in CD4.B led to their failure
to bind to certain antiยญpig CD4, while the CD4.A allele bound [105]. Sundt et al. also
identified two alleles of CD4อพ CD4.1 and CD4.2. The CD4.2 positive pigs did not bind
to the 74ยญ12ยญ4 clone [104]. It is therefore likely that the two pigs that do not bind to the
antibody clones (pig 95 and Angel) express the CD4.B/CD4.2 phenotype.
Here, there is also a lot of internalization of CD4 surface molecules, which can be seen
when comparing stimulated and unstimulated samples. Unstimulated samples have a
much clearer CD4/CD8 separation, as can be seen in figure 5.5a. Due to the problems
with staining CD4+ Tยญcells, it was decided to eliminate antiยญCD4 from the antibody panel.

Conclusion
The study aimed to establish an intracellular cytokine secretion antibody panel to invesยญ
tigate cytokine secretion in PBMCs from Ossabaw pigs with flow cytometry and use the
protocol to study the difference in cytokine secretion in obese and lean pigs.
An intracellular cytokine staining protocol for human cells was adapted for use in Ossabaw
pigs with good results. The study successfully established a protocol for measuring Tยญcell
subsets and cytokine secretion in pig PBMCs with flow cytometry. With the protocol, CD3+
Tยญcells were identified as CD8+, ฮณฮด+, DN and DP. Additionally, cytokine secretion of ฮณฮด+
ILยญ17, CD8+ IFNฮณ, CD8+ TNF, ฮณฮดโCD8โILยญ17, ฮณฮดโCD8โIFNฮณ and ฮณฮดโCD8โTNF can
be measured. Unfortunately, staining of NK cells and detection of ILยญ4 cytokine secretion
was not feasible with the available resources.
Cryopreservation was found to have no effect on IFNฮณ secretion, although it resulted in
fewer live cells, less CD3+, and CD8+ population. Ageยญdependent studies were also perยญ
formed to investigate the maturation of Tยญcell subsets and IFNฮณ secretion. These studies
showed that CD4+ and DN subpopulations decrease with age while CD8+ subpopulation
increases. During these studies, polymorphism was likely detected in the CD4 allele in
the Ossabaw pig population used, as some pigs lack the ability to bind to CD4+ antibodยญ
ies. Additionally, the LAC stimulation seems to internalize CD4 molecules as the CD4+
subpopulation is significantly lower in LACยญstimulated samples compared to unstimulated
samples.
The protocol was used to investigate Tยญcell subpopulations and cytokine secretion in
obese and lean pigs. Two different dietยญinduced obesity experiments were performed,
shortยญterm and longยญterm. The shortยญterm obesity experiment showed lower CD8+ popยญ
ulation and CD8+ IFNฮณ secretion. The longยญterm obesity experiment, however, showed
no difference in Tยญcell subsets and cytokine secretion. CD8+ decrease in obese humans
has been reported before. Whether this decrease was due to metabolic state or other
factors is unclear. This surprising find that shortยญterm obesity induced some immunologยญ
ical changes which were then not observed in the longยญterm obesity experiment would
be valuable to investigate further. Whether these changes are some sort of adaption to
obesity or wether these differences are due to the difference in diet used to induce obesity.
Two different antigenยญspecific stimulations were also performed using AP12 and ApXIV
antigens. AP12 antigen was used to stimulate PBMCs from obese and lean cells, and
IFNฮณ and TNF secretion were analyzed with ELISA. Unfortunately, no IFNฮณ secretion
was detected. TNF secretion was significantly higher in the obese pig compared to the
lean pig. The TNF response was likely, however, due to the activation of innate immune
cells rather than adaptive immune cells, which also explains why no IFNฮณ secretion was
detected. ApXIV antigen stimulation could not generate any IFNฮณ and TNF response,
likely due to underestimation of antigen concentration.
During this study an intracellular cytokine secretion protocol for flow cytometry to investiยญ
gate Tยญcell subsets and cytokine secretion in PBMCs from Ossabaw pigs was established.
This protocol is a valuable tool to study immune responses in Ossabaw pig models, which
is relevant for further research on MetS and obesity. Further optimization of the protocol,
such as choice of stimulant and expansion of antibody panel, is possible and should be
explored for further use in immunology studies.

Future Perspectives
Throughout this experiment, intracellular staining of cytokines for flow cytometry from
PBMCs in Ossabaw pigs was investigated. However, this experiment should be repeated
with more samples to have improve statistical power and examine the change in cytokine
secretion over time. Specific study parameters, such as diet composition, age and gender
of pigs, and duration of study should be examined further
Further optimization of the intracellular cytokine staining protocol, such as choice of stimยญ
ulation should be examined, especially to see if other stimulants give the same robust
answer as LAC without CD4 surface molecule internalization.
Other cell markers could be added to the panel to examine more parts of the immune
system. Innate immune cells, such as NK cells and monocytes could be added to the
panel. Th2 immune response could also be added if resources allow. Additionally, B cells
markers could be added, especially if memory response is going to be investigated further.
For example, if antigenยญspecific stimulation is repeated with a new antigen. It could also be
useful for vaccine research, if these other components of the immune system are added.
By adding these markers and further optimizing the antibody protocol and panel, future
research on obesity and the impact it has on the immune system will provide a deeper
understanding of its effects. This could be highly valuable for future vaccine and infection
studies as it allows us to examine the immune system and its components in great detail.
Not only will it enhance our understanding of effects obesity has on immuniy, but also allow
us to further utilize the Ossabaw pig model in immunology research and hopefully the
development of effective treatments for metabolic dysfunction that are related to obesity.
