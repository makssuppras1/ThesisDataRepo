Abstract
With demand of miniaturization in the semiconducting industry. The switching frequency is
power converters and circuits is pushed to higher frequency to attain high power density and
compact material but the sole purpose of this converter is to operate at higher eﬃciency. In
order to attain higher eﬃciency the losses should be very low from the converter. The ma-
jor source of error are the magnetic losses which aﬀect the performance of the whole system.
therefore the importance of ccurate magnetic core modeling is paramount and at higher fre-
quency it becomes really tricky to obtain the exact losses from the system as the core be-
comes highly non linear with respect to the frequency, ac ﬂux density, dc bias, and tempera-
ture. In the high frequency.
The main existing method to measure core losses are the electrical and thermal method but
the electrical methods have lots of uncertainties and becomes inaccurate at higher frequency.
Therefore in this research the main aim is to design the high frequency calorimeter that can
precisely measure the core loss for the given DUT(device under test).However, the calorime-
ter measure the overall power losses of the inductor and therefore, the separation of winding
loss and the measurement time are still open questions. This could be answered by employ-
ing a transient calorimetric approach and isolating the winding for the core well enough. Fur-
thermore, the core temperature measurement point and design of thermal chamber can have
an impact too.
The main aim of the this research will be to test diﬀerent inductors at diﬀerent frequencies
and verify the accuracy of the system based on the measurements. Thus this work wil in-
clude literature review of the calometry and its principles to design the accurate calorimetric
setup for the high frequency core loss measurement.

Introduction
1.1
Motivation
The main focus of the major research in the power electronics industry lies in manufacturing
of the energy eﬃcient converters which involves designing , estimation, analysis , optimiza-
tion and reduction of the power losses through the system. with recent development in semi-
conducting industries like GaN power semiconductors enable very high switching frequency
and improves the eﬃciency of the converter tremendously. In order to achieve higher eﬃ-
ciencies and high power density compact systems the converters now a days are designed to
operate at higher frequencies.
As the power converter is operated at higher frequency it becomes highly important to model
the magnetic core precisely to estimate the losses. Therefore, the accurate loss computation
of the magnetic components is of great importance when the operating range of the converter
is up to few MHz ranges.The most challenging part in the magnetic loss estimation is to esti-
mate the core loss which becomes highly non linear with the respect to the AC ﬂux density,
frequency, temperature and DC bias.The ferrite materials at higher frequencies are of major
interest due to the fact that these ferrite material enables low core losses which makes the it
prone to the measurement errors.The Power electronics converters and devices are the very
important part of the auxiliary system in the power generation facilities. Hence, their eﬃ-
ciency also relies on the eﬃciency of the these power converters. In the SMPS(Switch Mode
Power Supply) the converters have losses from the switches and the magnetic losses from the
transformer including the winding and the core losses. The latest development in the semi-
conductors enables to lower the switching losses at higher frequencies but the magnetic losses
become quite non linear and hard to estimate. Therefore, The main motivation of this re-
search is to focus on the magnetic losses in the power electronics system as they are hard to
estimate at higher frequencies.
1.2
Problem Statement
Generally, the magnetic losses in the power converters attribute to the Hysteresis losses caused
by the core, Eddy current losses due to voltage induced in the coil and the skin depth or the
proximity eﬀect which is loss caused by the winding due to non uniform current distribu-
tion.But, at higher switching frequencies the theoretical and experimental quantiﬁcation of
these losses are very diﬃcult. It has been found from the research that the winding losses can
be reduced tremendously based on the winding conﬁgurations and optimization based on the
diameter of the winding to reduce the skin depth. But, the core losses are major concerns
and they are highly unpredictable at higher frequencies and therefore, It is important to pre-
cisely estimate the core losses for the higher reliability of the system.
The core loss measurement methods are broadly classiﬁed into electrical and the calorimetric
methods. The traditional electrical method measures the power losses of systems by ﬁnd-

essential and the vital part of the circuits in the power electronic systems. Hence, the losses
are observed in the windings and cores of the magnetic components (which constitute the
transformer losses), power semiconductor devices like MOSFET, auxiliary circuit like the
gate drivers and the capacitors which are subjected to the higher ripple currents. Hence the
eﬃciency of even the most accurately designed power converter is aﬀected due to the loss of
the power in the form of the heat during normal switching cycles. For these reasons many
steps are taken in order to improve the amount of the losses in the system like proper design-
ing and selection of the components.The use of the snubber circuits, heat sinks are employed
to improve the power losses. The power loss analysis is done to determine and estimate the
total losses in the system to deﬁne the system eﬃciency. The various losses across the the

Switching losses
Switching losses are the losses across the switch that occurs due to the transition of the switch
from on to oﬀstate and back. The switching losses consist of the transition losses, turn on
and turn oﬀlosses. These losses occur due to the fact that the voltage and the current through
the switch do not fall to zero or rise to the peak instantaneously after the switching cycles as
shown in the ﬁgure below.
Figure 1: Mosfet and the waveform for the turn on and turn oﬀlosses[15]
The total switching losses across the switch can be calculated by the given equation:
Psw = Idon · V DD · (ton + toff) · 0.5 · Fsw
(1)
where the Id(on) is the current ﬂowing across the switch when it is on the VDD is the volt-
age across the drain and (ton+toﬀ) represents the total duration of the switch to turn on and
oﬀand the FSW is the switching frequency.
The power MOSFET also has the losses across the junction and the output capacitance losses
along with the parasitic inductance losses across the power diodes and switches and the re-
verse recovery of p-n junction diode. These losses are signiﬁcantly high when operating in
transient condition which causes high voltage and current ﬂuctuations resulting in the heat-
ing of the MOSFET junction producing losses.
The input capacitance loss or the gate charge loss can be measured by the following equa-
tion:

Conduction Losses
The conduction losses are also known as the static losses. The conduction losses are caused
due to voltage drop across the circuit or the device when the current ﬂows through it. The
conduction losses occur in power semiconductor like MOSFET, diodes, thyristors and BJTs
furthermore, these static losses also occur in the form of heat dissipated in the PCB traces,
Connecting wires, capacitance series equivalent resisters and in the inductors they are often
referred as the copper losses I2R which are dissipated in the form of the heat.
The conduction losses are inversely proportion to the size of the semiconducting device the
larger the size the smaller will be the conduction loss.The conduction loss across the current
carrying conductor is given by

Losses in the Magnetic components

as the core loss and the winding losses together they constitute the larger losses in the entire
power electronics circuit and therefore it is really important to proper design the magnetic
parameters. It includes proper selection of the core, its geometry , ﬁnding appropriate turns
and arrangement of the winding for lower losses. It also include losses from the ﬁlters and ,
coupled inductor, multiwinding and deﬂection coils.All these components are necessary in the
system for performing speciﬁc tasks like ﬁltering , isolation . resonant transition and ampli-
ﬁcation. and therefore it is really important to properly estimate the losses. But, in power
converters core loss and winding losses are the major source of magnetic losses.

the magnetic ﬁeld. Eddy currents are caused due to changing magnetic ﬁelds and they can

Analog measurements
: The analog measurements techniques involve the measurement of the electrical quanti-
ties in DC or low frequencies with devices like voltmeter, ammetters and wattmeters. In

at higher frequencies.

The digital measurement of power losses involve digital equipment like the oscilloscope which
digitally acquires the instantaneous current and voltage values and continuously samples
them and provide the average products to ﬁnd the power values and are highly suitable for
the higher frequencies.

Core Loss Measurement
The losses in the magnetic components are main focal point of the power electronics after
the coming of the fast switching semiconducting devices as they are the major source of the
power loss in the system. The magnetic losses include the winding losses and the core losses.
In this section the core loss measurement techniques are primarily focused.
The core loss measurements are primarily divided into two types :
• Electrical core loss measurement: which includes the classical four wire measurement,
two wire measurement and the resonant measurement techniques which are recently
developed by MIT and Virginia Tech
• Calorimetric measurements
The core loss testing is considered to be very important but they are very tough to measure
because of the non linear behaviour which requires the large signal testing, testing with bias
and understanding or testing the inﬂuence of the waveform shape and furthermore the high
Q( low loss) measurements are very diﬃcult at the higher frequencies and therefore it is very
important to properly determine the losses.

Calorimetry
The underlying principle of calorimetric power loss measurement is called as the calorimetry.
The term Calorimetry is derived from the latin word ”Calor” which means heat therefore,
the heat calorimetry indicate the measurement of quantities of heat.THe calorimeters are tra-
ditionally used in the ﬁeld of chemistry or food sciences. But in recent years the Calorime-
try has gain attention in the ﬁeld of electronics and electrical measurement in order to mea-
sure the power losses of the system and their components. The calorimeter directly measures
losses through the convection , conduction and radiation which enables an indirect measure-
ment of the power losses in the electrical and electronics components[4]. The main advantage
of the calorimetric method is that it removes the traditional inaccuracy of the input-output
method. The another advantage of the Calorimetry is that it is high measurement accuracy
and is completely independent of the power rating , magnetic excitation and the operating
frequencies which reduces the changes of error and increases the reliability of the measure
mental results.
The Calorimetric methods are highly eﬃcient for determining the total power losses of the
system by measuring the total heat dissipated out of the system the calorimetric chamber
with stable thermal conditions.
The calorimeter are basically classiﬁed into two types they are as follows
• Inertia Calorimeters : In this type of calorimeters the heat content and the thermal re-
sistance os the component are used to ﬁnd the losses
• Flow Calorimeter: The most popular type of the calorimeters are the ﬂow calorimeter
in which the ﬂuid ﬂow is used to determine the total loss of the system by keeping the
entire system thermally stable to attain higher accuracy.
There are many calorimetric methods and based on the diﬀerent principles within these two
categories they can be further classiﬁed as the Drop Calorimetry where the DUT or the de-
vice under the test is immersed in the chamber ﬁlled with the cooling ﬂuid or the medium
or the heat ﬂow calorimeters which determine the heat ﬂow based on the Seebeck Eﬀect and
the bomb calorimeters which are profoundly used to determine the caloriﬁc value of the com-
bustible ﬂuid.

In the ﬂow calorimeter the DUT or the device under the test is thermally insulated and placed
in the hermetically sealed environment or chamber called the calorimeter along with ﬂuid or

5.2.1
Accuracy of the ﬂow calorimeters
The accuracy of the ﬂow calorimeter is dependent on the various constructional , measure-
ment and calibration techniques being used which are discusses as follows:
1. Thermal insulation of the test environment or the chamber:
The chamber
should be constructed such the insulation losses should be lower than the losses the the
DUT . The test setup can be calibrated for these unknown losses if the variation of the
insulation resistance is signiﬁcantly low.
2. The temperature Measurement:
For the temperature measurement it is important that the it should be insensitive to
the radiated heat from the device under the test also, the external EMC and the EMI
interference should not aﬀect the temperature measurement of the setup this inter-
ference’s should be avoided as they be hard to screen. The temperature measurement
should be homogeneous over the entire chamber and therefore, temperature measure-
ment should be taken at several diﬀerent point and the average is calculated to attain
higher accuracy of the system. The temperature of the test environment should be sta-
ble and the heat source should be avoided near the testing setup which can be direct or
indirect.
3. Settling time
The settling time should be chosen adequately so the device reach the steady state usu-
ally settling time of 3 to 6 thermal time constant of DUT is chosen for maintaining the
higher accuracy. For the magnetic components under the test the typical settling time
of 10-20 minutes is chosen which result in the settling time of 1-2 hours to the system
to reach steady state furthermore, with increasing frequency the settling time is pro-
longed and it takes much more time for the system to reach to the steady state.
4. Calibration The calibration of the test setup is one of the most crucial part that im-
pact the accuracy of the measurement usually the test setup can be calibrated using
the resistor powered with known power supplied to get the power losses for the setup
for the entire testing range. The diﬀerent powers and the positions have to tested in
the test area.
5. Computer Modelling or data logging Now a days computer modelling or the digi-
tal data loggers are used to acquire all the measurement data from the system to obtain
the measurement results for the whole range of the test cycle.

water as the secondary coolant and air as the primary coolant. In order to ensure the
zero heat leakage the additional thermally insulated chamber is is placed within the
larger chamber separated by the airgap Te to match the temperature inside the cham-
ber inner chamber Ti. The DUT is placed inside the inner chamber. The main advan-
tage of this type of calorimeter is that it eliminates the gradient over the outer and the
inner surface of the walls to improve the eﬃciency of the test measurement. The heat
will only ﬂow when there is change in the temperature and it is very much required
that there is no heat leakage from the wall of the chambers to ensure higher accuracy
of the system. Which will be ensured if all the losses by the device is absorbed by the
coolant which is circulated in the chamber. The airgap temperature control is governed
by the relation |Ti −Te| where Rth is the thermal reistance of the inner wall. This is
gives very high accuracy for the measurement losses.

and the temperature rise is equal to the total loss dissipated by the actual core in the test
system while the test measurement. The winding resistor is heated by the AC currents and

minimize the error for the power loss calcultaions. The energy dissipated by the CUT can be

Future Work
The future work will be to calibrate the system with the coolant and to get the lower oﬀset
value for the temperature.
Also to build separate power ampliﬁcation stage that can provide higher current supply for
the winding to excite so the winding losses could be properly measured by getting the trend
for the copper losses at given RMs current at diﬀerent frequencies so the winding losses can
be predicted from the interpolation.
Furthermore, The temperature calibration can be increased so that the winding losses along
with the total losses can be measured for the same datapoints for the accurate referencing of
the data points and to extract trends from interpolation at the same measurement points.
