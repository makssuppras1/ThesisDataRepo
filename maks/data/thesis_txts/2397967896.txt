Abstract 
In the last few years there has been an increased interest from car manufacturers to produce a 
completely autonomous vehicle. The report describes the process of making an autonomous driving 
system for a robot. The goal is to use a single camera to detect the traversable area in front of the robot, 
in addition to nearby walls and obstacles, should be detected in order to assist the robot localization and 
navigation. The stations that need service by the robot will have a LED marker, this should be detected 
from the camera images too. 
At first, some an image processing methods is tried out with the purpose of extracting information for 
the robot to use when the robot navigates on the road. The method that be used is Adaptive threshold. 
Then using the image to navigate the robot to its destination. To do this, the pinhole method will be 
used to calculate coordinates from the image. So that the robot can drive forwards the coordinates. 
All image segmentation methods and calculations are prepared and tested in matlab. The image 
coordinate system converted to the floor coordinate system, after that converted to the global 
coordinate system. The process is also done with the light. 
Image segmentation is being made on a camera which then sends driving information in the form of 
coordinates to the robot that is being programmed with regbot program.

Resume 
I de sidste år er der en øgende interesse fra bilfabrikanterne om at producere et complet autonomt 
kørselssystem til en robot. Opgaven er ud fra et enkelt kamera at detektere, dels farbart område foran 
robotten, dels placering af vægge og forhindringer for at assistere robottens lokalisering og navigation. 
Missionen for robotten er indendørs industriel transport, og stationer der skal serviceres er markeret 
med en LED markør, disse markører skal ligeledes detekteres ud fra kamerabilleder  
Først vil der bliver afprøvet en billedbehandlingsmetoder Adaptiv threshold, som robotten kan bruge til 
at navigere på området.  
Den næste del af rapporten handler om at navigere robotten ved bruge af billederne. For at gøre dette 
bliver pinhole metoden brugt til at udregne koordinater ud fra billederne. Robotten vil så kunne køre 
mod koordinaterne, så et nyt sæt koordinater bliver forberedt. 
Alle billedsegmenteringsmetoder og beregningen er blevet forberedt og testet i Matlab. 
Billedkoordinatsystemet konverteres til gulvkoordinatsystemet, bagefter det er konverteret til det 
globale koordinatsystem. Processen gøres også med lyset. 
Billedesegmentering bliver lavet på et kamera, som sender køre information i form af koordinater til 
robotten, der bliver programmeret med Regbot programmet.

Introduction 
 
The definition of the term "mobile robot" is not widely accepted, it means that a device that can move 
autonomously from place to place to achieve a set of goals, e.g. moving from one position to another 
position has often been intended for example, vehicle-drive vehicles and AGVs, that have been used in 
many applications, such as health (G.B. of Pharmaceuticals). In 1953, automated driven vehicles or 
automated driven vehicles (FTS) were detected AGV is most commonly used in industrial applications to 
move materials around manufacturing facilities or warehouses. The use of mobile robots and AGVs is 
particularly prominent in the range of robotic applications such as factories, hospitals and office 
buildings. Mobile robots can use a wide range of transfer technologies such as flight, swimming, 
dragging, walking, and rotation.

Image processing segmentation: 
The important step in image analysis, object representation, visualization and other images processing 
tasks can be achieved by dividing an image into significant structures. 
The first step with the autonomous robot drive are interpretation of information from images, or can 
give the ability to identify roads. 
Project I have used one image segmentation processes named Adaptive threshold  
3.1 Adaptive threshold segmentation: 
Thresholding can be used to divide an image to two parts the first part contains all pixels whose 
intensity values are above a threshold named foreground value and the second parts contains all the 
remaining pixels named the background value.  
The adaptive thresholding takes a grayscale or color image as input image and the output is a binary 
image that represents the segmentation of the input image, where the threshold for each pixel 
calculated and segmented it to two parts, if the pixel with value below the threshold is put to the 
background values and set the remaining pixels to a foreground value. There are two way to finding the 
threshold, the first is the Chow and Kaneko, that divide the image into an array of overlapping sub 
images and find the best threshold for each sub images by checking its histogram. By interpolating the 
results of the sub images each pixels threshold is found.  
This methods weakness is that it is very hard to run, and because of this reason it is not fitting for real-
time applications. 
The second approach to finding the local threshold is meant to statistically inspect the intensity-values 
of regional area of each and every pixel. The most appropriate statistic absolutely depends on the input 
image. Some of the fast and simple functions include the median of the regional distribution intensity. 
T means the median value or the mean of the minimum and maximum values  
 
The neighborhoods size needs to be big enough to cover an adequate amount of foreground and 
background pixels, if that isn’t the case, then an inferior threshold will be chosen. However, selecting 
regions that are too big, can breach the assumption of relatively consistent brightness. This method is 
less computationally intensive than the Chow and Kaneko approach, and it causes great results for some 
applications. 
To implement the adaptive threshold I used imbinary function in matlab 
The result, T, is a matrix that is the same size as the result of the adaptive threshold containing 
normalized intensity values in the range [0, 1]. I have used the adaptive threshold method with three 
different images below shows in figure (3.1), figure (3, 2) and figure (3.3).

Post processing 
5.1 Introduction 
The accessed regions that act as the different divided objects can be handled in the way that some of 
the structures have been ascertained inaccurately they will be useless and therefore thrown out. 
Some of the expectations has been made for distinctive of the regions. 
The main expectation is based on the quantity of pixels that are ascertained for every structure. The 
object needs to have a specifically known size, if it is tinier than the recommended size, it will be 
deleted. The Connected Components algorithm can be put into use if we are searching for the varied 
structures of the binary image. The Connected Components algorithm uses neighboring pixels to find 
and set apart pixel groups. Their positions need to be found so that they can be put into a map where 
there is a polygon. Its construction or structure can then be rewritten into the coordinate system of the 
robot, so it can give an account of the robot’s position. This can give a measure of distance to the 
components and finish the map-structure.  
To make the polygon, the edge of the structure can be used as a rough contour of the polygon. The 
disclosure of the edge leaves holes inside the structure behind. The entire edge of an item may include a 
lot of pixels’ witch can make it hard to process and can toughen the process of saving it.  We can lessen 
the amount of pixels and create a reduced polygon by using a reduction algorithm on the edge.

10.1 Localization: 
10.1.1 Introduction: 
Localization is solely about the robot is placed now. Robot centrically, it is like saying “where am I”, 
whilst the answer “here” usually means the point of origin, so that we’ll know the way and that we are 
never lost, if we decide that it doesn’t matter where we are located. Although it is somewhat hard to 
figure out the localization, because it depends on the type of robot that we are referring to. The 
localization techniques might work very well for one robot in one particular environment, however it 
will work crudely or not at all for a different robot in an another environment. Therefore, these 
localization techniques are not the same in all environments. All localization techniques offers 
information about both of these information-topics down below.  
• What is the current location of the robot in an environment? 
• What is the robot's current orientation in that same environment? 
The first topic can be in the form of geographic latitude and longitude or Cartesian or Polar coordinates, 
while the second topic could a mix of roll, pitch and yaw or a compass heading. 
Mobile robots often operate in large facilities and many different approaches have been taken for 
localization and navigation.  They range from methods in which the entire facility is first mapped and 
routes are planned a priori to those in which sensors provide information about traversable areas and 
the vehicles determine their own current positions and plan their paths dynamically based on features 
recognized in the environment.  
If tasks or workspace often change, it is more likely better at planning dynamically. 
Manufacturing/production facilities generally take a middle road. We can place markers in the work 
area.  
These markers are recognized by sensors that are boarded on the vehicles, and supplies precise 
localization through triangulation and hence simplifies the navigation. Different sensors that are on the 
vehicles keep an eye on the path, to exanimate it for any type of obstacles, unexpected objects etc. They 
might be able to work out a plan to get around them in time before returning to their pre-planned 
route. 
It is also important to know the position and direction of the mobile robot, and several methods have 
been developed to prepare this information. Methods commonly used depend on odometer
