Abstract 
The reliability of electronic products under different climatic conditions is one of 
important factors for electronic manufacturers. In the practical applications of 
electronic components, the failures may be caused by the design and materials issues, 
but these failures can also be caused by the environmental conditions during the service 
life of the electrical products, such as temperature, humidity and the presence of 
hygroscopic contaminations on the surface of electronic products. When these factors 
are existing along or in the combination in the actual practical applications, it is very 
likely to form intermittent failures in electronic products. 
In the climatic experiment, a climatic chamber was used to simulate the climate varying 
of different regions from arid to humid climate and high temperature to low. Meanwhile, 
the internal humidity and temperature of chamber and enclosures was recorded with the 
relation of the leakage current on surface insulation resistance – printed circuit board 
(SIR-PCB). The main variables in the experiment include different pitch sizes of comb 
patterns, different pre-contaminated weak organic acids, with and without the presence 
of thermal mass, with and without the presence of enclosures and the hole sizes of 
enclosures.  
The results of this experiment provide a deep understanding of the average failure rate 
of electronic products based on the climate conditions and packaging design of 
electronic products. The fluctuations of temperature and humidity during the 24h 
climatic cycle can cause the formation of condensation water on the electronics, 
especially if the dew point is very close to the air temperature. In addition, the presence 
of hygroscopic contaminations and the thermal delay of the thermal mass can enhance 
the possibility of condensation water to form on the surface of PCB, resulting in the 
corrosion reliability problems and the permanent failures of electronic products. 
Keywords: Climate, Contaminations, Enclosures, Humidity, Leakage current, 
Impedance tests, Thermal mass, Water layer formation, Printed circuit board.

1. Introduction 
After the human society enters twenty-one century, the development of science and 
technology is changing with each passing day, and electronic devices have come into 
everyone’s life. At the same time, these electronic products are expected to integrate 
more and more complex functions [1], taking mobile phones as an example, the basic 
functions include high-definition cameras, fast wireless telecommunication technology 
such as 4G and 5G applications, and global positioning systems, etc. In addition, with 
the price increase of raw materials, the manufacturers of electronic products are 
constantly looking for some cheaper materials to reduce the production costs to make 
the products more competitive, but these products still have to achieve better 
application performance, longer service life and higher reliability in different climatic 
conditions. 
 
Because the climate varies from region to region, the electronic products may work in 
different climatic conditions. For example, the household appliances are used in indoor 
climates, while other products are exposed to harsh conditions like high humidity 
offshore locations, hot engine compartments of vehicle, and atmospheres with corrosive 
gases [2]. But regardless of the working environmental conditions of these electronic 
products, high reliability is the common goal pursued by all electronics manufacturers. 
When the electronic products are used in the harsh environments, permanent or 
intermittent failures often occur on circuit boards, and these types of failure occurs 
usually due to the long-term operation of electronic products at high temperature, high 
humidity, corrosive gas, atmospheric contamination and their combination [2].

The experimental conclusions [4] have reported that the weak organic acids (WOA) 
cause failures on PCBA which resulting in a large amount of leakage current. The 
WOAs have hygroscopic nature, therefore they can absorb water from the humid 
environment to form a water film on the surface of PCBs. In addition, WOAs have two 
factors to affect the reliability of PCB. For one thing, the WOAs can be ionized in the 
water film to increase the conductivity by forming electrolytes [5]. Another reason is 
the solubility of WOA, which can determine the possibility of residues to be dissolved 
in the water at a certain temperature. As the combination of two factors may lead to the 
increased conductivity in the electrolytes and finally cause the serious corrosion to 
occur on the PCB surface.

The reliability improvement of electronic devices is a very complicated process, at 
present, there is no single method to completely control corrosion-related failures in

2. Theoretical basis 
2.1. Different type of corrosion failures mode on PCBA 
Corrosion is a very common phenomenon in the nature, it usually occurs between 
metallic materials. When carefully analyzing the structure of PCBA, it can be found 
that it is composed of different components, materials and structures. Therefore, like 
the conventional corrosion on metallic materials, the corrosion will also exist in the 
PCBA of electrical systems with different types of corrosion failures. There are five 
types of corrosion failures mode that usually occur on PCBA to be summarized as 
follows: Surface insulation resistance and leakage current [1], electrochemical 
migration [9], conductive anodic filament, gas phase corrosion and galvanic corrosion. 
a) Surface insulation resistance (SIR) and leakage current 
The SIR of the dielectric materials between conducting points in PCB is one of the most 
influential parameters in the electronic corrosion mechanism [1]. Because, when two 
conducting points connected, it will cause the current leaked from a conductor and 
travels along different conductive path to the opposite conductor [6]. Under humid 
environment, the water vapor in the air condense forms the water layer on the surface 
of PCB if the temperature of PCB is lower than the dew point. While there are some 
residues and contaminations on the surface of PCB which will be ionized in the water 
layer to form electrolyte. The formation of the electrolyte creates the necessary 
conditions for the current conduction between two conductors, however under the dry 
condition, current leakage will not be an issue for electronic PCB [6], because, the 
resistivity of the surrounding dielectric materials is high enough to avoid the current 
conduction, but the resistivity of the PCB surface can be significantly decreased in the 
humid environment [10]. 
b) Electrochemical migration 
Electrochemical migration (ECM) is a typical corrosion phenomenon in electronic 
==PAGE:12==
7 
 
 
devices, but only few metals like Sn, Pb, Cu, Ag and Au are susceptible to 
electrochemical migration [11]. ECM can form a large amount of corrosion on PCB in 
very short period, which significantly reduces the reliability of these products. This type 
of corrosion usually occurs when two oppositely biased points such as a pair of exposed 
conduction lines, legs of components and closely spaced electrodes are connected by 
condensed aqueous electrolyte [12]. In the ECM reaction, the relatively active metal 
(M) undergoes an oxidation reaction which loses electrons to form Mn+ ions, then these 
positive ions move toward the Cathode electrode and deposit here [1]. Under the 
conductive conditions like humid and wet environment, the deposits will be extended 
from cathode electrode to anode electrode as the form of dendrite as shown in Figure.3. 
In addition, if the condensed water layer is very weak, for example the PCB surface is 
very clean and without any contaminants in the air, the evaporation of the condensed 
water will limit the electrochemical migration process [12].

The dendrite grows from right (cathode) to the left (anode) [1]. 
c) Conductive anodic filament 
The formation of conductive anodic filament (CAF) is a failure which occurs in the 
PCB sub-surface [6]. This phenomenon is an electrochemically induced process, which 
owing to a conductive copper-containing salt growing from the anode to the cathode 
along the interface between epoxy and glass fiber [1]. There are usually two steps to 
the formation of CAF [13]. Firstly, due to the degradation of the epoxy/ glass interface

which creates a path for the migration of the copper-conductive filament. Secondly, the 
water vapor was absorbed on the laminate PCB to be electrolyte to connect the Cu 
conductors and resulting in the electrochemical reaction. There are about five factors 
affecting the CAF reliability related to some studies as showing below [6]: 
 
The substrate materials of PCB packing process.

d) Gas phase corrosion 
In the production of electronic devices, various gases such as gaseous sulfides, ozone, 
ammonia [6] are existed in the surrounding environments. These gases have high 
solubility which can be dissolved easily into the water layer to form conductive 
electrolyte under high humidity environment. As a result, the surface of metal conductor 
lines especially copper and silver lines are corroded severely. Taking silver conductor 
lines as an example, which is corroded with gases sulfide (HS) related to the following 
chemical reaction [6]: 
                 2𝐴𝑔+ 𝐻𝑆−→𝐴𝑔2𝑆+ 𝐻−                     (2.1) 
The sulfide sliver will be formed on the surface of silver conductors as the form of 
dendrites. Presence of very a small number of gaseous sulfides will lead to a serious 
effect on the reliability of electronic device, which will lead to thinning of conductor 
lines and the increasing of line resistance [1]. 
e) Galvanic corrosion  
This type of corrosion is attributed to the connection of metal materials with widely 
varied electrochemistry potential difference such as Au/Al on a PCBA surface in a 
humid environment. In electronic devices, galvanic corrosion usually occurs on the 
connectors and switches. This is because the connectors and switches are usually prone 
to contact with different electrochemistry potential metal materials, and when water 
films will be formed on the surface of PCB, these two materials are connected and

resulting in the galvanic corrosion. The formation of corrosion products will increase 
the resistance in the circuit and affect the reliability of electronic devices.

form ionic components and polar molecules, then they will be dissolved by the water 
to enhance the conductivity of water. In addition, hydrogen bonds in water molecules 
can react with water molecules to form cohesion and can also react with other molecules 
to form adhesion.

2.2.5. Water adsorption by the hygroscopic contaminations on PCB. 
Some water-soluble ionic residues and contaminations on the PCB will affect the 
surface insulation resistance (SIR) after water adsorption. The number of adsorbed 
molecular layers of water increases with the increasing of the RH [17]. When the RH 
in the ambient environment reaches a certain threshold value, these hygroscopic 
substances will deliquesce and form a solution [22]. Deliquescence can be considered 
as a phase transformation from solid phase to saturated solution phase, which is affected 
by the relative humidity that depends on the properties of solid and temperature of the 
climate [23]. When the ambient is higher than the deliquescence RH, the residues and 
contaminations on the PCB tend to exist in the phase of aqueous solution, while below 
the deliquescence RH, they will exist as the phase of solid which surrounded by gaseous 
water [17].

2.3.1. Activators in the flux/ flux residues. 
The no-clean solder flux systems have been widely used in the electronic device 
industry for the assemble process of printed circuit board. At present, majority of 
soldering fluxes are halide-free organic-activator which based on properties of de-
oxidizing metal surfaces and good wettability of the molten solder [24]. Generally, 
these activators which are used in the no-clean flux soldering systems are dicarboxylic 
weak organic acids, these weak organic acids are easily to decompose or evaporate from 
the printed circuit board surface and leaving very less amount of non-corrosive residues 
[24]. Therefore, in theory, under the application of this type of soldering process, no 
additional cleaning process is required to maintain the corrosion reliability of the 
electronic devices. However, in the actual production process, a large amount of residue 
will remain on the printed circuit board surface, which may lead to the corrosion 
reliability issues on contaminated printed circuit boards when these PCBs are exposed 
to a humid environment [27]. In order to have deep understanding of these actual issues,

15 
 
 
some physicochemical properties such as acid’s strength, hygroscopic nature and 
solubility of different WOAs will be expressed below. 
2.3.1.1. 
The physicochemical properties of WOAs 
⚫ Acid’s strength 
From the related viewpoints of the reliability of corrosion, it is known that the weak 
organic acids are the main factors which cause deterioration of PCB performance [16]. 
Different WOAs exhibit different physicochemical properties which can determine the 
susceptibility of PCB towards corrosion [24]. Most of WOAs have the nature of water 
solubility and ionization, the acids’ strength of different WOAs are directly reflected by 
their dissociation/ ionization constant 𝐾𝛼 , the lower value of 𝑝𝐾𝛼 , the higher acid 
strength of WOAs they have and the easier they are to ionize [24]. 
⚫ Hygroscopic nature of WOAs. 
The hygroscopic nature of organic weak acids means that these WOAs are more likely 
to absorb moisture from surrounding air. This is due the existence of polar functional 
groups in their lattice structure [25]. The lattice of these hygroscopic substances form 
stable structure after being absorbed with water molecules and do not easily make these 
WOAs release water molecular even though the external conditions change [24]. 
⚫ Solubility of WOAs. 
The water solubility of weak organic acids is another important factor that affects the 
corrosion reliability [24]. It can determine the possibilities of the amount of residue to 
be dissolved into water at the given temperature. This parameter has a significant effect 
on the corrosion reliability, this is because the water film former under humid 
conditions is very thin, even a very small amount of dissolution of WOAs in water can 
lead to the saturation state, then the ionization reactions occur in the water to increase 
the water conductivity. Finally, leading to more corrosion appears on PCBA surface 
[26]. In addition, the existing of WOAs as a form of residue on PCB surface reduces its 
critical relative humidity for water film formation. This is the reason why moisture 
==PAGE:21==
16 
 
 
absorption of contaminated PCB takes place in a lower humidity environment than 
clean PCB surfaces [28]. According to the study of Adams [29], higher hygroscopic 
species usually exhibit lower critical relative humidity for deliquescence, which will 
lead to an increased conductivity of the water film and the decreasing of surface 
insulation resistance of PCB, resulting in a large amount of leakage current and 
corrosion on the surface of PCB. Hygroscopicity is not the only influencing factor on 
the critical relative humidity for deliquescence, among temperature, crystalline solid 
and different contamination will affect it also [24]. 
2.3.2. Use environment related contaminations. 
The using environment related contaminations are mainly from particulates 
contaminations and corrosive gases. Majority of particulates contaminations are 
existing in the sulphate, chloride, nitrate, acetate, ammonium atmospheres [17]. If the 
electronic devices are working under controlled humidity environment, the deposited 
corrosive particles will not affect electrical functionality of the devices [17]. Therefore, 
these corrosive particles can accumulate on the surface of circuit board and form the 
bridges to connect adjacent conductors over many years, this phenomenon is also called 
dust dendrites [17]. With the formation of ionic contaminations contained dust dendrites, 
conductors will be connected under specific humidity conditions (such as condensation-
forming), resulting in the failure of conductors. 
 
In addition, the corrosive gases are still presenting in the atmosphere, or forming from 
the surroundings of electronic devices during service life. When the electronic devices 
are exposed in the gases like 𝑁𝑂𝑥, 𝑆𝑂𝑥,𝐶𝑂𝑆, and 𝐻2𝑆, corrosions and failures may 
occur at the metal part of devices such as the soldering joint, interconnects and electrical 
contacts [17].

3.3.1. 
Principle of impedance tests 
Impedance (Symbol is Z, the unit is ohm), which is a measurement of the overall 
opposition of a circuit to current. Similarity to the resistance in DC circuit, the 
impedance in AC circuit indicates that how much flow of charge is impeded by the 
circuit. But the difference between these two parameters is that the impedance also 
considers the effects of capacitance and inductance. Therefore, the impedance is more 
complex than resistance since the effects of capacitance and inductance varying with 
the frequency of current, which means the impedance is also varying with frequency of 
current, but the resistance is a constant value regardless of the frequency of current. In 
simple circuit which has no capacitance or inductance. Impedance can be assumed as 
resistance and it can be expressed by the following equation [30] (3.1):

3.4. Climatic testing. 
3.4.1. 
Principle of SIR tests 
Surface insulation resistance (SIR) measurement is a common process quality control 
test in the electronic industries. The specification and method were firstly applied in 
1970s when rosin was the main matrix materials for solder fluxes system [17]. The main 
purpose of the SIR test is to understand the effect of heat flux residues and 
contamination on the performance of electrical devices, especially from the impact of 
conductive and corrosive nature of contaminations. In the test of SIR measurement, the 
obtained data can provide a combined result of leakage current and corrosion. This 
measurement can also be applied to evaluate the deterioration effect on SIR comb 
pattern under the certain climate parameters. 
The current value in the SIR testing between the comb pattern electrodes can be 
converted into the SIR values, which give the resistivity of surface in the unit of ohms 
per square. The comb pattern has the following geometric data variables including: the 
width of the conductive lines, and the spacing between conductive lines and area size 
of comb pattern. For the surface finish of comb pattern, copper finish is the most 
common, while the SIR patterns may have solder finish also such as hot air solder 
leveling surface finishing (HASL) and Tin immersion surface finishing. There are some 
standards for the tests boards with different applications as showing in the follow: IPC-
B-24 (surface insulation resistance), IPC-B-25(A) (multipurpose test board), IPC-B-36 
(cleaning alternatives) and IPC-B-52 (cleanliness and residue evaluation) [17]. 
 
Due to the SIR measurements are affected by various parameters, therefore, it has the 
obvious disadvantage, the SIR measurement has a nonlinear relationship with the 
geometry of the comb patterns and applied voltage for obtaining the SIR data. For 
example, under the high voltage experimental condition, the SIR value tends to be 
higher than low applied voltage. In addition, under the lower humidity environments, 
==PAGE:32==
27 
 
 
no condensation occurs on the PCB comb pattern, which will lead to a higher SIR value. 
While the condensation forms on the comb pattern, the SIR value will be low due to 
the corrosion of conductive lines and the dendrites forming between the electrodes. 
3.4.2. 
Surface insulation resistance (SIR) test boards. 
The surface insulation resistance test boards are used in variety of ways to characterize 
residues or to determine the effect of these residues on the performance of printed 
circuit boards (PCB) or printed circuit boards assemblies (PCBs) [33]. In short, SIR 
testing is a measurement for the leakage current on or in a PCB, usually when the PCBs 
are exposed a relatively long exposure time under the condition of the fluctuated 
temperature and humidity. 
In the leakage current tests, the model number of substrate layer of tested SIR boards 
is PCL-FR-370HR laminate, which is a high performance 180℃  glass transition 
temperature FR-4 system for multilayer printed wiring boards [31]. It has excellent

as shown in the Figure 14. There are four sets of comb patterns with 300𝜇𝑚  line 
width and different pitch sizes on this tested circuit board, which are 300𝜇𝑚, 600𝜇𝑚, 
1000𝜇𝑚  and 2000𝜇𝑚 , and the area of each comb pattern is 325𝑚𝑚2 ,

5. Conclusions 
The main tests included in this report are: 1. The conductivity tests of HASL and Sn 
immersion samples with different pretreatment methods (as received, water clean and 
heat reflow). 2. The impedance tests of HASL and Sn immersion samples with different 
pretreatment methods (as received, water clean and heat reflow), and verify whether 
the water layer thickness has effect on the impedance testing results. 3. Leakage current 
tests of SIR-PCB under different climatic conditions.  
After the above experimental tests, the following experimental results can be obtained: 
⚫ In the conductivity tests, the conductivity values of the solution which dissolved in 
contaminations from the HASL and Sn immersion samples were slightly higher 
than the conductivity values of Millipore water, but there was no obvious difference 
between them. 
⚫ In the impedance tests, the impedance of three sets of Sn immersion samples were 
about 650 ohms for 10kHz current frequency and 100ohms for 68kHz, while after 
pouring 200𝜇𝑚 Millipore water, the impedance values dramatically decreased to 
about 5 ohms. In addition, the impedance of HASL samples under 10kHz and 
68kHz current frequencies were 600ohms and 95ohms, which were lower than the 
impedance values of Sn immersion samples. When the 200 𝜇𝑚 Millipore water 
was pouring on the surface of PCB, the impedance decreased to 10 ohms. 
Furthermore, for the samples with different amount of water pouring, the 
impedance decreased to 5ohms in the end. This indicates that the thickness of water 
layer which poured on the surface of PCB wouldn’t one of the reasons to affect the 
impedance variation. 
⚫ In the climatic tests with the protection of enclosures, the maximum humidity and 
the temperature difference between the surface of PCB and its respective dew point 
were the main climatic parameters to determine the corrosion reliability of PCB. 
Because of, when a large amount of leakage current was observed from the SIR-
==PAGE:63==
58 
 
 
PCB, the internal humidity in the enclosure reached to the maximum value and the 
temperature difference was at its minimum value. 
⚫ 
According to the climatic tests with the protection of enclosures, the enclosures can 
efficiently decrease the maximum internal humidity in the enclosure and 
temperature difference to avoid the condensation to form on the surface of PCB, 
resulting in the improvement of the PCB reliability. In addition, the maximum 
values of leakage current of samples which were tested in 1mm and 3mm hole 
enclosure under Chennai climate were almost same around 4mA, but the difference 
was that the leakage current of samples tested in 3mm hole size enclosure and 
contaminated by glutaric acid with thermal mass last longer time than the sample 
tested in 1mm hole size enclosure. But the hole size of enclosure hasn’t apparently 
affected on the current leaking time of samples contaminated by adipic under both 
Chennai and Matam climates and samples contaminated by glutaric under Matam 
climate. Furthermore, the thermal mass didn’t significantly decrease the surface 
temperature of PCB in the climatic tests, therefore thermal mass has little influence 
on the reliability of electronic products in this experiment.  
⚫ 
Glutaric acid has lower deliquesce RH than the adipic acid, therefore, when do the 
comparison with the samples contaminated by adipic acid, the samples 
contaminated by glutaric acid were more likely to observe dendrites and a large 
amount of leakage current under these two climatic conditions 
⚫ 
The maximum humidity of Chennai is much higher than the maximum humidity 
of Matam, and the glutaric acid contaminated samples were more likely to form 
dendrites and a large amount of leakage current under Chennai climate instead of 
Matam climate, which owing to the glutaric acid was not totally deliquesced in 
Matam climate. 
⚫ For the samples which can observe obviously leakage current phenomenon, such 
as samples contaminated with glutaric under Matam and Chennai climatic 
conditions, higher amount of leakage current was generated in the smaller pitch 
size comb patterns, especially 300𝜇𝑚, there were no obvious leakage current to
