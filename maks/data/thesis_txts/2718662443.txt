Abstract
Global warming is currently a major topic worldwide. In order to address this
problem, countries around the world have formulated corresponding policies, with
energy policy being an important part. Countries led by Denmark have proposed en-
ergy targets for 2050 or 2060, as well as corresponding medium-term plans. Among
these proposed goals, increasing the construction of renewable energy and the pro-
portion of renewable energy in the energy system are often given top priority. While
reducing carbon emissions, this change also poses challenges to the functioning of
energy markets.
The main problem with the increase in renewable energy is the increasing un-
certainty in the energy system. The power generation of renewable energy sources
such as photovoltaics and wind power is extremely unstable, easily affected by the
weather, and prone to short-term sudden changes. This leads to an imbalance be-
tween supply and demand in the energy system, causing instability in frequency.
To solve this problem, it is necessary to accurately forecast the power generation of
renewable energy and make an accurate supply plan based on this forecast informa-
tion when power market transactions occur.
This study uses Bornholm Island as a demo site. The first problem to be solved
is the uncertainty of the output of renewable energy. Based on the deep learning
method, the PV and wind power generation, as well as the power and heat demand,
are predicted. This study first collects, selects, and cleans the data. Then, based
on the LSTM model, the hyperparameter selection and training process of deep
learning are analyzed. Afterward, the reason for choosing the CNN-GRU-Attention
model is systematically analyzed, as well as the mechanism and function of each
part of the model in prediction. Finally, the prediction results of the two models are
shown, and the superiority of the latter is proved through a comparative analysis of
the prediction effects.
The second question addressed by this study is how to use the predicted results
to decrease the potential imbalance between supply and demand in the energy sys-
tem. By analyzing the energy system structure of the demo site, an energy market
optimization control model based on the day-ahead market plan and real-time mar-
ket forecast is constructed, and the functions of each equation of the constructed
mathematical model are explained. Then, how the three important elements of the
MPC strategy are reflected in this study is analyzed. Next, the coupled power and
district heating system is optimized and controlled based on the above mathemati-
cal model through the MPC strategy. Finally, typical dates in summer and winter
are taken as examples to prove the effect of the optimization model. Suggestions
are put forward for the establishment of the future renewable energy system of the
studied demo site based on the optimization results.
keyword: MPC, Power Prediction, Predictive Models, Energy System.

Chapter 1
Introduction
1.1
Motivation
The greenhouse gas emission, mainly including carbon dioxide (CO2), nitrous
oxide(N2O) and methane(CH4) still increasing within these years, A report[1] indi-
cates that the global anthropogenic CO2 emission increasing every years, from 34793
million tonnes in 2012 to 36573 million tonnes in 2018. The Paris Agreement[2] set
an goal that to keep the global temperature increase below 2°and pay the best ef-
forts to keep it to 1.5°, because the IPCC report[3] uses scientific evidence to show
that global temperature rise must be limited to around 1.5°to avoid serious climate
risks. To achieve this goal, global greenhouse gas emissions must be reduced by at
least 40% below 1990 levels by 2030, and reach net zero emissions by 2050, and then
achieve negative emissions.
The EU has committed to be climate-neutral by 2050 which means an net-zero
greenhouse gas emission society according to Paris agreement [4], EU member states
determines their own long-term goals under this green agreement which is depended
by their own situation. For example, Denmark parliament has already set a goal
that the electricity system in Denmark would be completely independent of fossil
fuels in 2030, because there is already 50% electricity in Denmark is supplied by PV
and wind power[5]. These efforts are part of the EU’s broader efforts to transition
to a more sustainable and low-carbon economy.
In order to achieve these goals, the EU has proposed the following policies. In
2009, the EU launched the Renewable Energy Directive with the goal of increasing
the proportion of renewable energy in the EU. By 2020, the proportion of renewable
energy had reached 22%. In 2018, the Directive[6] was amended to set a short-term
goal for 2030 of reaching a 32% proportion of renewable energy, up from the previ-
ous target of 20%. Special measures have been introduced in sectors such as district
heating and cooling to support the transition to renewable energy. The REPowerEU
plan[7], published in 2022, sets an even more ambitious target of 45% renewable en-
ergy by 2030. The difference between each goals is shown in Figure 1.1, these goals
represent both a challenge and an opportunity for the EU as it works to transition
to a more sustainable and low-carbon economy.

Apart from the EU member states, other major world economies have also pre-
sented their own plans for the Paris climate agreement.
At the COP26 climate
meeting in Glasgow, India[9] pledged to achieve carbon neutrality by 2070.
To
achieve this goal, India plans to expand its renewable, hydro, and nuclear capacity
to 500 GW, which means that half of India’s energy consumption will be generated
from renewable sources. The United States[10] also presented its own 2050 plan at
COP26. The plan includes a medium-term nationally determined contribution goal
of reducing greenhouse gas emissions by 50-52% compared to 2005 levels by 2030.
Of this reduction, 4.5 billion tons will come from the energy sector, representing
over 70% of the total reduction in greenhouse gas emissions.
Three East Asian countries, which together contribute more than 30% of the
world’s total emissions, have also declared their own zero-carbon emissions goals.
These countries recognize the need for significant reductions in greenhouse gas emis-
sions to combat climate change and are taking steps to transition to a low-carbon
economy[11]. South Korea pledged to achieve carbon neutrality by 2050, but did
not give a specific route to reach the standard. However, it also proposes to achieve
the goal of carbon neutrality by using more renewable energy, improving energy
efficiency, and researching carbon capture technology. Japan has pledged to reduce
its greenhouse gas emissions by 46% from 2013 levels by 2030, while committing to
becoming carbon neutral by 2050[12]. China also proposed the goal of building a
clean, low-carbon, safe and efficient energy system, in order to reach carbon peak in
2030 and carbon neutral in 2060[13].
Without a doubt, energy is not the only aspect we need to consider when we
talk about carbon neutral, but it is the most important sector in this transition,
Figure 1.2 shows that the energy sector contribute more than two-thirds of total
GHG emissions globally[14]. As discussed above, in order to achieve their emis-
sion reduction and carbon neutrality goals has been committed to by themself, each
country has proposed different solutions. However, these solutions must include the
construction of renewable energy generators to increase the use of renewable energy

in the energy system. As a result, the proportion of renewable energy, led by wind
and PV power, will continue to increase in the energy system.
Figure 1.2: GHG emission by sector[15].
As the proportion of photovoltaic and wind power connected to the grid increases,
we are able to obtain clean and increasingly cheap energy. However, problems such
as the lack of energy density and the unstable power output of these sources can have
a greater and greater impact on the traditional energy system. These challenges will
need to be addressed in order for renewable energy to play a larger role in our energy
mix.[16]. In particular, the sudden changes in the power output of photovoltaic
and wind power can cause serious power safety problems for the traditional energy
market. These changes, which can occur on a short-term basis, can disrupt the
stability of the power grid and make it difficult for traditional energy sources to
respond quickly enough[17, 18]. Therefore the traditional three markets(the day-
ahead market, the intra-day market, real-time market[19, 20]) must be made more
flexible to account for the potential mismatch between production and consumption.
This mismatch can be addressed through accurate supply-side and demand-side
forecasting and the addition of more storage, transmission, and backup capacity in
the energy system[21]. The combination of these strategies is known as the MPC
strategy, which is used in this research[22].

1.2
Background
1.2.1
Energy island Bornholm
As discussed in the previous section, in order to address climate change, major
countries have agreed to achieve the goal of carbon neutrality by 2050 and have
formulated their own short-term goals to achieve this target. As a laboratory for
green energy solutions, Denmark has more experience in this field than other coun-
tries and has therefore designed its own short- and medium-term plans based on
its experiences and situation[5]. Denmark must reduce its greenhouse gas emissions
by 70% by 2030 compared to 1990 levels. This 70% target and the 2050 climate
neutrality target mark the first time Denmark has passed a law setting a target
for the country’s total greenhouse gas emissions. As of 2020, Denmark’s emissions
have been reduced by about 38% compared to 1990, which means that Denmark
still needs to complete an emission reduction equivalent to the past 30 years in the
period between 2020 and 2030.
Calculations by the Danish Council on Climate
Change show that this target is achievable and will ensure that Denmark continues
to be a leader in reducing emissions. In order to achieve these goals, Denmark has
formulated two tracks. The first focuses on proven and widely used green technolo-
gies, such as renewable energy and electric vehicles. The second track focuses on
developing new technology to enable people to eventually realize a green lifestyle[23].
Figure 1.3: Denmark’s monthly power generation ratio of different energy sources in
the past year (Dec, 2021-Nov, 2022), excluding imported and exported electricity,
combined calculation of DK1 and DK2 regions[24].
Figure 1.3 above shows the power generation from different sources in Denmark
from December 2021 to November 2022. The figure does not include the import and
export of electricity, but it is worth noting that Denmark is a net importer of elec-
tricity. In 2021, the import value is expected to be 4.5 TWh, accounting for about
14% of the total annual power generation[25]. The figure shows that power gener-

Time series prediction
Machine learning is a mathematical method that uses data to enable algorithms
to improve and make predictions. The history of machine learning can be traced
back to the early 20th century, and it was officially recognized as a distinct field in
1980. Over the past four decades, machine learning has advanced significantly and
has become an essential tool in many fields, including computer science, finance,
and industry[32]. Machine learning algorithms can be divided into three types: su-
pervised learning, unsupervised learning, and reinforcement learning. Supervised

learning learns a model through training samples and then uses this model for infer-
ence. Unsupervised learning does not have a training process. Given some sample
data, the machine learning algorithm directly analyzes the data to obtain knowledge
about it. Reinforcement learning is a special type of machine learning algorithm.
The algorithm must determine an action to execute based on the current state of
the environment and then transition to the next state. The goal is to maximize the
income obtained[33]. This research focuses on regression problems under supervised
learning.
The convolutional neural network (CNN) used in this article was first proposed
by Fukushima[34] and applied to the field of image recognition. It is still one of
the main network structures in the field of deep learning. In 1997, Hochreiter[35]
proposed the long short-term memory (LSTM) model based on the ordinary re-
current neural network (RNN) with long-term memory. In 2014, Cho[36] modified
the LSTM model and proposed a more simplified model - the gated recurrent unit
(GRU), which greatly increased the calculation speed of the model. This alleviated
the computational burden caused by larger and larger data sets. In the same year,
Chung[37] compared the effects of LSTM and GRU in their paper and demonstrated
that these two RNN models are close in predicting actual problems. They also sys-
tematically explained why this happens from the mechanism of the GRU model.
Also in 2014, Mnih[38] proposed a method called "attention" to address the com-
putational burden caused by the increasing complexity of CNNs and the growing

number of pixels in images. This method allows the network to self-adaptively se-
lect a series of regions from which to extract information, reducing the amount of
computation needed. Since the amount of computation required by a CNN scales
linearly with the number of pixels in an image, attention allows the network to be
more efficient, particularly when dealing with large and complex data sets.
In 2016, the combination model of GRU and Attention began to be widely used
in NLP area, and its effect in time series prediction was widely recognized[39, 40,
41, 42]. In 2020, a CNN-GRU-LSTM model with improved information extraction
capabilities and prediction accuracy was proposed and applied to fields such as au-

prediction methods in the PJM energy system, using the daily mean absolute per-
centage error (MAPE) as the evaluation metric. He found that the prediction error
based on a simple neural network was two times better than the traditional SD
method. In the past two years, the RNN-CNN model based on the attention mech-
anism which has a better performance has been used for smart energy systems, such
as Wei[47] applied the network in 2021 for residential electricity load forecasting,
while Wang[48] applied the network in 2022 to make accurate prediction of wind
power. The application of time series prediction methods from the field of artificial
intelligence to the energy field is likely to become mainstream.
1.2.3
Dynamic optimization of energy system
In general, an Integrated Energy System (IES) is a system of energy produc-
tion, transmission, storage, and distribution that is optimized through coordination
to create a comprehensive solution from energy production to consumption. An
IES is composed of energy production, coupling of multiple energy sources, and a
comprehensive energy terminal system[49]. In a more specific sense, an IES is a com-
prehensive system that organically combines fossil fuel, heating, wind power, PV,
hydrogen, and other energy systems with public services in society, and through
intelligent scheduling of the system, realizes efficient use of energy, multi-energy
complementarity, reduction of clean energy waste, and user energy use classifica-
tion[50].
There are three types of IES structures: inter-regional IES, regional IES, and
park-level IES. Inter-regional IES is mainly composed of long-distance, high-capacity
equipment such as transmission grids and natural gas backbone networks. Regional
IES is mainly composed of medium-voltage distribution networks, gas distribution
networks, and heating networks, with a radiate structure. Park-level IES focuses
on user-side energy management in small areas, and can implement intelligent elec-
tricity systems, distributed heating and water supply, and demand-side manage-
ment[51]. This research focuses on Park-level IES.
8
==PAGE:21==
In an Integrated Energy System (IES), electricity is the core component. By
integrating various energy sources and leveraging innovative technologies such as
cogeneration, energy conversion, and renewable energy generation, the IES can op-
timize energy production, use, and storage[52, 53] . However, the dynamic properties
of different energies (such as electricity, gas, and heat/cold) within the IES can vary
greatly, as can their control characteristics, network characteristics, and response
to scheduling commands. This, combined with the uncertainty of load prediction,
makes system planning difficult. Ultimately, the operation of an IES is a complex
process that involves coordinating multiple systems (electricity, gas, heat), multiple
time intervals (milliseconds, minutes, hours), and multiple operational objectives
(energy, environment, and economic efficiency)[54].
At present, IES is a research hotspot in the field of energy, and there are already
many related projects and research. Yu[55] gives an overview of integrated energy
system and energy internet. Wang[56, 57] designed a model that could accommodate
flexible loads and the fruit fly optimization algorithm is used to solve the economic
dispatch model. Chengshan Wang[58] design and optimize a Chinese Park-level IES
project, significantly reducing chiller startup and shutdown frequency. Taking the
heating system of Jilin Province in China as a model, Wei[59] designed an energy
system with the goal of reducing wind curtailment, which effectively improved the
utilization wind. Shao[60] focuses on taking advantage of customers’ flexible energy
needs, including thermal energy demand and power demand, providing balanced
resources, and easing the difficulty of integrating variable wind power with cogener-
ation.
In addition to the energy research mentioned above, there are a considerable
number of large-scale integrated energy projects underway[26]. Energylab Nord-
havn has studied a coupled system based on electricity, heating and transportation
to achieve higher flexibility and energy efficiency through low-temperature heating
systems [26, 61]. EcoGrid 2.0 studies the coupling of electricity and heating systems
on Bornholm island[26, 61]. EPIMES studies a coupled power, heat and gas system
near Beijing, China to improve wind power utilization[26, 61]. CITIES is a data-
driven smart energy system to improve the utilization of renewable energy[26, 62].
FLEX-TES investigated the effect of heat storage on the flexibility of CHP[26, 61].
HEATflex studies the potential of waste heat in heating systems[26, 63].
Based on the aforementioned IES, the control optimization behavior of the Dan-
ish energy system occurs at three points in time, which are referred to as the Day-
ahead (DA) market, the Intraday market, and the Real-time (RT) market. The DA
market is based on the predicted demand and supply on the day before the trading
day and balances supply and demand, providing the hourly energy system operation
plan for the next day. The Intraday market is a fine-tuning of the DA market’s plan
based on updated data from 24 to 1 hour before the transmission occurs. The RT
market plays a crucial role in maintaining the balance between electricity generation
and demand during operational hours. It provides the last opportunity for balanc-
ing any prediction errors that may have occurred in the power system, occurring in
9
==PAGE:22==
the last hour of energy market trading, and is typically coordinating by adjusting
traditional generation units and coordinate flexible demand[19, 20, 64]. This project
focuses on the DA market and the RT market.
Based on the Danish energy trading market explained above, it can be learned
that the DA market on the day before the actual transaction will have a production
and supply schedule for the second day based on the information of the previous
day, but when the trading time is approaching, more accurate data and information
related to the energy system will be obtained. In this case, the original plan needs to
be adjusted in the RT market. However, in actual real-time market regulation, not
only should distribution be made based on more accurate uncertain parameter pre-
dictions, but also the difference between DA and RT markets should be minimized
to reduce the impact caused by regulation. In order to realize the above-mentioned
optimal adjustment function of the RT market, the MPC algorithm is introduced.
Model predictive control is a family of control algorithms[65] that compute a
sequence of manipulated variable adjustments in order to optimize the future be-
havior of each section of the system, It was first proposed and used in the 1980s at
Shell Oil[66]. MPC generally includes three important elements which are predictive
model, rolling optimization and feedback correction. A predictive model refers to
a type of dynamic model that can explicitly fit the characteristics of the controlled
system. Rolling optimization refers to optimizing the system according to the given
finite time-domain objective function based on the current state of the system and
the prediction model in each sampling period, finding the optimal control sequence,
and using the first sequence as a command sended to the system. Feedback cor-
rection is used to compensate model prediction errors and other disturbances[65,
67]. The application of MPC in the energy field in the past ten years is mostly in
building-related fields and power systems, but less in IES[26]. This research will
use MPC as the optimization algorithm of RT market control system to realize the
rolling optimization control of energy market.

The correlation matrix is a tool commonly used in the field of machine learning

The correlation matrix displays the pairwise correlation between all the parameters
being studied. It is calculated using the formula shown in Equation 2.1, which shows
that the correlation is the covariance of the two parameters divided by their respec-

negative correlation, 0 indicates no correlation, and 1 indicates a perfect positive
correlation. Therefore, the larger the absolute value of the correlation coefficient,
the greater the correlation between the two variables. In this study, since the time
periods of different parameters are different, the calculation of the correlation is not
completed at one time, but performed by selecting the largest time overlapping area
between the two parameters, so as to reflect the true degree of correlation between
different parameters.

Cross validation
The last thing to discuss in this section is cross validation (CV). CV is a method
commonly used in machine learning. Its function is to test and quantify the perfor-
mance of a machine learning model. The correct application of such an evaluation
method is an important prerequisite for analyzing whether a model is effective.
When we have multiple models to compare, how to determine the best effect of a
certain model, and how to reflect the real effect of a model in general situations,
this is what CV solves.
CV is a practical way to statistically cut a data sample into smaller subsets.
After cutting, one subset can be analyzed first, while other subsets can be used for
subsequent confirmation and verification of the analysis. The initial subset is called
the training set. The other subsets are called validation or test sets. The general
cross-validation method needs to satisfy the uniform sampling of the training set
and the test set, that is, to scramble when selecting data points. However, since the
RNN model need the data points. Because the RNN model needs to analyze the
20
==PAGE:33==
relationship of data in time series, so in this research, such a shuffling operation is
not performed when dividing the data.
The training set is used to train the parameters in the model, and after the
model is trained, the evaluation of the model effect should be based on the test set.
The parameter optimization process of a machine learning model can be regarded
as a complex fitting problem, and if the error rate of the training set is used as
the evaluation standard of the model effect, it cannot correctly reflect the general
performance of the model. The reason for this is that when evaluating the effect of
the model, it is based on the data that the model has been used, which leads to the
fact that the model may only be applicable to the situation involved in the current
training set. This phenomenon is called ’Overfitting’[33]. Therefore, putting the
test set data into the model and using the error rate of the test set as the evaluation
method of the model performance can effectively solve this problem.
There are three mainstream CV methods, namely hold out method, K-fold CV
and leave-one-out method, the leave-one-out method is a special K-fold CV, that is,
the data set is divided into subsets equal to the number of samples. In the field of
deep learning, due to the huge amount of data, generally when using K fold CV, the
value of K will not be too large to reduce computing costs. The 2 layers CV method
used to reduce the generalization error would also not be used for the training.

RNNs suffer from short-term memory[35].
If the sequence is long, they will
have difficulty transferring information from earlier time steps to later time steps.
Therefore, if trying to process a piece of text to make a prediction, the RNN may
miss important information at the beginning. RNNs also suffer from the problem of

calculations in Figure 2.8 are an example. Therefore, in a RNNs, layers that get
small gradient updates stop learning. Those are usually the earlier layers. Because

2.3.3
LSTM
In this section, LSTM neurons will be introduced to replace RNN neurons, so
as to achieve the function of absorbing long-term memory, while other parts of the
network in the above Figure 2.9 will not be changed.
(a) Sigmoid
(b) Tanh
Figure 2.10: Schematic diagram of Sigmoid and Tanh, where Sigmoid is on the left
and Tanh is on the right.
LSTM has a similar control flow compared to general RNNs, and the difference
is that LSTM operates differently within a single neuron. The operation in LSTM
neurons is used to allow LSTM to forget or retain information. The core concept is
cell state, and the unit that controls this process is called the gate. The unit state
transfers information in the sequence and can be regarded as a long-term memory.
In theory, the unit state can save the information during the entire sequence pro-
cessing. In this way, information from earlier time steps can be applied to later
time steps, enabling long-term memory transfer. In the process of transmission, in
each unit, information will be deleted or added to the unit state, and it is up to
the gate to decide whether to delete or not, and which information needs to be added.
Before talking about the gate structure of LSTM, it is necessary to introduce
two activation functions, namely Sigmoid and Tanh. The schematic diagrams of
the two are shown in Figure 2.10. In this paragraph, the function of the activation
function is to realize the above-mentioned gate function. The Sigmoid function is
a function in the interval from 0 to 1, and its value rapidly changes from 0 to 1
around 0. Tanh is similar to Sigmoid, except that its range is -1 to 1. The Sigmoid
function is very conducive to the update and forgetting of data, because when any
number is multiplied by 0, the result is 0, thereby achieving the effect of ’forgetting’,
25
==PAGE:38==
and when the information is multiplied by 1, the information is preserved. The role
of Tanh is to scale, which can scale all values between -1 and 1, so as to avoid the
explosive growth of some values due to multiplication, thereby adjusting the output
of the neural network.
Figure 2.11: Mechanism of LSTM model[35].
Figure 2.11 shows how the LSTM model works. The lower side of the figure is
the short-term memory, and the upper side is the long-term memory, and the three
lines connecting the upper side are, from left to right, the forget gate, the input
gate and the output gate. First, the forget gate is introduced, which controls which
information in long-term memory needs to be lost or retained. The hidden state
from the previous neuron is combined with the input of this unit, and the combined
information outputs a value between 0 and 1 through Sigmoid to decide whether to
forget or not. The input gate is composed of two lines to update the information in
the long-term memory. One of the lines passes the previous hidden state and the
current input to the Sigmoid function to decide whether to pass the information to
the long-term memory, and the other line passes the hidden state and the current
input to the Tanh function, compressing them between -1 and 1 to regulate the
network. Then the Tanh output is multiplied by the Sigmoid output and the result
is saved. The final output gate determines the information passed to the next unit
and the output of the current unit. The final output is calculated by passing the
hidden state and the current input to the Sigmoid function, and then passing the
new long-term memory to the Tanh function, and multiplying the results of the two.
2.3.4
Fully connected layers
The fully connected layers mentioned above, in the traditional sense, are gen-
erally divided into input, output and hidden layers, but in essence, it is the most
simplified neural network based on the combination of linear and nonlinear term.
26
==PAGE:39==
The network generally allows data to cycle through linear transformation and non-
linear transformation. Every pass is called a layer. Input and output layers are
special, because each neuron in the input layer only stores input data, and each
neuron in the output layer does not undergo nonlinear transformation, the other
middle layers are called the hidden layer. In fully connected layers, each neuron
in each layer is connected to each neuron in the next layer, that is, the value of
each neuron in the next layer is obtained by linearly connecting all the neurons in
the previous layer, and go through the activation function before sum them together.
As the earliest proposed neural network model, the fully connected layer plays
a pivotal role in the construction of the entire deep learning network. It can ef-
fectively scale the number and dimension of information. The reason this form of
the network is effective is it combines linear and nonlinear terms. The linear term
is used to transfer information from the upper layer to the lower layer, while the
nonlinear term called the activation equation is the most essential difference from
the traditional linear regression model. The mainstream activation equations used
to add nonlinear terms include Sigmoid and Tanh mentioned in the previous section.
These activation function has a lot of great property. They are continuously differ-
entiable which make backpropagation works and have nice nonlinear-like curves at
the same time. Such a network containing linear items and nonlinear items, when
the number of layers is sufficient, can construct a approximate mathematical model
of the research problem to a certain extent.
As shown in Figure 2.9, in this model, the information output from the RNN
network passes through the activation function after a linear layer, and then go
through the other linear layer, the output of second linear layer is the final result
of the entire model. After that the loss of the model is calculated by the output
and the ground turth through the loss function. Then, through the backpropagation
process, an update of the model parameters is completed.

This section will introduce the combination model of CNN-RNN-Attention, which

2.4.1
Difference between GRU and LSTM
In order to solve the computational problem brought by more complex models,
GRU needs to be introduced instead of LSTM. Figure 2.12 systematically shows
30
==PAGE:43==
the difference between the two. As can be seen from the figure, GRU simplifies the
three gates in LSTM to two gates of reset gate and update gate. GRU simplifies
the short-term memory module in the traditional RNN model and merges it into
the long-term memory (cell state) module, thus omitting the module similar to the
output gate in LSTM. In GRU, the long-term memory and the input data of these
neurons are combined to control the reset gate and update gate through the Sigmoid
function. The difference between the two gates is that the reset gate directly uses
input data, while the update gate uses input data that has passed through Tanh,
and the update gate also directly controls the value range of the long-term memory.
Figure 2.12: Comparison between LSTM and GRU.
It can be seen from the structure of GRU that it greatly reduces the amount
of calculation of LSTM, while maintaining the advantages of LSTM - long-term
memory, at the cost of abandoning the short-term memory. Chung[37] proved in his
research that the effect of the two is basically the same. Therefore, when constructing
a complex neural networks and considering the RNNs model that requires long-term
memory, the priority of GRU is higher than LSTM. The amount of model parameters
in this section is much higher than in the previous section, so substitutions are made
in the combined model.
2.4.2
Convolutional neural network
As already introduced in the first chapter, the convolutional neural network(CNN)
was proposed by Fukushima[34]. It is true that the concept of CNN can be traced
back to Hubel’s research on the cat’s visual system in 1962, but the structure based
on convolutional layers and pooling layers designed by Fukushima in his article is the
basic mechanism for constructing CNN networks in current deep learning practice.
31
==PAGE:44==
CNN is essentially a multi-layer perceptron. The key to its success lies in the way it
adopts local connections and shared weights. On the one hand, the reduced number
of weights makes the network easy to optimize, and on the other hand, it reduces
over-fitting risk.
The first function of the CNN network is to simplify complex problems. When
processing information with a large amount of data, it can effectively reduce the di-
mensionality of the information. This function will be explained in the subsequent
calculation process of the CNN layer. The second function is that it can retain
important features. For example, in a period of wind speed change, 24 continuously
time points are randomly sampled, while ensuring that the two partial overlap. In
this sampling method, the positions of the overlapping parts of the two are different,
so in the traditional fully connected layer network, the information provided by the
two will be regarded as very different, but from the perspective of human cognition,
the features contained in this piece of overlapping information will not change be-
cause of its position. CNN is designed to imitate how humans process information,
so when the position of information changes, it can effectively identify that they are
similar information.
Figure 2.13: Calculation of convolutional layer, kernel size is (3,3) and stride is 1.
Figure 2.13 shows how the convolutional layer is computed. The most important
part of the convolution layer is the convolution kernel. When designing CNN, the
size of the convolution kernel is very critical. Generally, it is set to (3,3). In a convo-
lution layer, k convolution kernels will be included. The values of each convolution
kernel are different which is used to extract different information from the input.
Dot product will be performed for the kernel and the input in the sliding window.
The window will iterate all the possible locations. The distance of each movement of
the window is controlled by the parameter ’stride’. After each window in the input
is multiplied by a convolution kernel, the result is a smaller size matrix compared to
the input matrix. The final result is obtained by combining the results of different
kernels. It can be seen from the operation process of the convolutional layer that
the size of data is reduced, but the depth of the data is increased.
32
==PAGE:45==
Figure 2.14: Calculation of max pooling layer, window size is (3,3) and stride is 1.
Pooling layers are simpler than convolutional layers. The pooling layer also needs
to go through the above window selection process, so there are also two parameters of
’window size’ and ’stride’ in the pooling process. There are various ways of pooling,
the mainstream ways are max pooling and mean pooling. The method adopted in
this study is max pooling, that is, the maximum value in each window is taken as
the result, and its operation method is shown in Figure 2.14. The pooling layer can
reduce the parameters that need to be learned and summarizes the features present
in the region of the feature map generated by the convolutional layer. This makes
the model more robust to changes in feature locations in the input data.
2.4.3
Attention layer
The attention mechanism is a problem-solving method proposed by imitating
human attention. Simply put, it is to quickly screen out high-value information
from a large amount of information. It is mainly used to solve the problem that
the final reasonable result is hard to be obtained when the input sequence of the
RNN model is long. The working mechanism of attention combined with RNN is
shown in Figure 2.15, The difference between it and Figure 2.9 is the principle of
the attention mechanism. This mode was first applied to the video processing by
Mnih’s work[38] mentioned above.
When the final result needs to be obtained from multiple vectors, at least one
linear transformation is required, and this process requires a weight for each vector,
and there are two ways to obtain this weight directly and through calculation. The
former is the traditional RNN model, and the latter is the origin of the attention
mechanism. The weight of the information extraction model with attention is not
fixed, because Attention can use the information contained in the vector itself to
calculate the weight of each vector.
More specifically, in Attention model, the weights of multiple vectors output by
the RNN used to calculate the final result are calculated, so the core of the model is
to train the parameters that required for calculating the weights of each vectors. In
the process of dealing with sequence problems, the important features are often not
contained in the information of one or more specific positions of the sequence, but
are constantly changing according to the sequence. Therefore, the key to dealing
with the sequence problems is not to extract the information of each points in the
33
==PAGE:46==
sequence with different weights, but how to judge the importance of each points
automatically. This results in that when the weights of different points are a fixed
value, a better training effect cannot be obtained. For example, when the input of
the model is ’I like it’ and ’I dislike it’, if the weight of each vector is a fixed value,
then the second vector will get a very high weight, but when using ’it is awful’ as
the test set, the weights trained above cannot extract information correctly.

3.1
Motivation
Through the previous chapter, the predicted values of the four changing param-
eters (Wind and PV power generation, load and heat demand) of the demo site
discussed in this research at a specific time point have been obtained. In order to
realize the thermoelectric coupling energy system based on MPC to optimize the
control system, it is also necessary to convert the energy system into a mathematical
model for analysis.
Integration is the technical guarantee for analysing IES. Integrating wind, light,
water, natural gas, coal and other types of energy resources on the supply side, inte-
grating electricity, heat, cooling, gas and other types of energy needs on the demand
side, and breaking down barriers between different energy categories are important
steps for the establishment of IES.
The first chapter analyzes the current methods and difficulties of IES modeling.
The most prominent problem is that it is difficult to solve all sectors of the IES
model uniformly, because different energy sectors have different sources, and the
general IES system will establish different models for multiple energy sources[26,
54]. For example, in power systems, AC flow models[72] and DC flow models[73] are
commonly used. In district heating systems, hydraulic models and thermal models
are generally used[26]. Natural gas systems require pressure models[74]. In cou-
pling modeling, the decomposition method and the unified method of the Newton-
Raphson method are commonly used[54]. To due with these problems, this thesis
focuses on the application of machine learning methods to improve the effect of tra-
ditional IES operation rather than using different models to analyze systems with
different characteristics. Therefore, the energy-based modeling method is adopted
to simplify the model structure and reduce its complexity while maintaining the
accuracy of the model.
This chapter begins by introducing the basic information of the demo site located
in Bornholm in this thesis, including its demand side and supply side units, as well

as the components contained in the energy system, and analyzes its sub-modules.
The next section elaborates on the the Danish energy market system and provides
theoretical support for the subsequent model construction section. Then the com-
plete mathematical model used in this study is presented, after that each of these
equations is explained.
3.2
Mathematical modeling of energy systems
Figure 3.1 shows the energy system transfer process in the area studied in this
research.
The blue lines in the figure represent the transfer of electricity, while
the red lines represent the transfer of heat. It can be seen from the figure that
there are three sources of power supply, namely wind, PV and external power grid.
After the three are connected to the grid, they will be sent to the demand side.
When the electricity generated by wind and PV is larger than demand, the excess
electricity is converted to heat by the electric boiler. The local heat demand is met
by district heating and electric boilers. At the same time, there is a water tank
in the heat network as a heat storage unit to improve the flexibility of the energy
system. Although the energy system is a whole, it can be roughly divided into three
modules, namely the electrical part, the heat storage part and the heat network part.

Heat storage model
The second part of the model is the heat storage model. Under the traditional

transfer model of the medium and the water tank wall. since there will be com-
pletely different mathematical modeling methods with such kind of analysis method

own fluid inlets and outlets position, shapes, wall thicknesses, which can make it
difficult to integrate this module when analyzing energy systems. Therefore, when
dealing with heat storage modules, this study only considers the transfer of energy,
ignoring the hydraulic model and thermal model. Although this behavior reduces
the precise control of the heat storage module, it greatly simplifies the complexity
of this module, thus providing assistance for the analysis of the entire energy system.
The main component module of the heat storage unit in this study is a water

amount of energy entering and exiting has a maximum limit. In this study, the
maximum energy change per hour is 10% of the maximum energy in the water tank,
that is 8 MWh per hour. In addition, since the wall of the heat storage tank is made
of metal, the energy in the storage tank will slowly transfer to the environment over

storage tank. According to the above analysis, the energy loss of the heat transfer
process is simplified as a fixed heat dissipation coefficient, in the above tank, this
value is 0.98.

3.3
Energy market
Before introducing the complete IES optimization model, there is another im-
portant influencing factor that needs to be discussed, which is the energy market
operation model in the area where Bornholm island is located.
The stability of
power transmission is determined by an important indicator, which is frequency.
Within continental Europe, the indication of grid stability is given by the system
frequency of 50 Hz. When the power supply is equal to the power demand, the
value of the frequency will remain at 50 Hz. When the demand is larger than the
supply, the frequency will decrease relatively and vice versa, the instability of the
frequency will cause problems for the energy market. The same problem will also
appear in other sectors of the energy system, so keeping the energy supply equal to
the demand is the core reason for studying the energy market. In the first chapter,
the three sub-markets in the energy market, namely DA market, Intra-day market,
and RT market, have been given a preliminary explanation. This section will con-
tinue analyzing the impact of this energy market operation mode on the design of
the IES optimization model.
45
==PAGE:58==
Figure 3.2 provides a complete picture of how the Nordic energy market works.
It can be seen from the figure that the energy market is actually divided into two
modules (Red and blue part in the figure). These two modules may be operated by
different organizations in different regions of the world, but their essential functions
are the same. In order to discuss how these two modules operate separately in the
energy market, it is necessary to first propose three important participants in the
energy market, namely sellers (Supply), buyers (Demand) and transmission system
operators (Transmission). The first two are the main body of energy production
and consumption, while TSO is mainly responsible for the transmission of energy.
In most countries, TSO also plays the role of energy market operator, that is, hosts
energy market bidding and other activities.
Figure 3.2: Mechanism for energy market operation[19].
Dividing the energy market into three sub-markets is a simple division. The
three sub-markets play different roles, and the subjects of the three sub-markets are
also different. It is not just the difference in the operating time. The fundamental
function of the energy market is to balance supply and demand. Therefore, the
upper part of the figure shows the process of how supply and demand are balanced
by bidding. This process includes two sub-markets, namely DA market and intra-
day market. The working mechanism of these two markets is the same, except that
the intraday market operates closer to the actual energy transmission time, so the
energy price in this market is higher than that in the DA market. The function
of the DA market is to conduct bidding based on the bidding prices provided by
the supply and demand sides, thereby facilitating energy transactions. Before the
transaction occurs, the energy producer sets the amount and price of energy they
could provide in the market, while the energy consumer also sets the amount and
price of energy it needs in the market, after that the operator of the energy market
host the transaction according to the bids of the two. The main body of the markets
is the sellers and buyers. This process will inevitably result in some demand not
being met, and some energy suppliers not being able to start production.
When the time come to an hour before the transaction, the bidding process of
sellers and buyers was over, the final RT market comes. Although through the first
two markets, the operators of the energy market have formulated a relatively com-
plete energy transmission plan, but in the last hour, the supply and demand sides
46
==PAGE:59==
will continue to change. From the supply side, the precise power generation of re-
newable energy represented by wind and PV will become more and more accurate as
time goes by. At the same time, some factory operating problems may also lead to
changes in supply. From the demand side, each energy consumer could only predict
the possible energy consumption in the future according to its own prediction, but
in fact this value would differ from the final consumption.
RT market is to solve the imbalance between supply and demand as much as
possible in the final stage before transmission happened, so the main body of RT
market is TSO. Based on the plan of the day-ahead market, TSO will formulate
the final energy delivery plan according to the change of supply and demand in
the last hour, and apply the corresponding intervention methods to maintain the
frequency to 50 Hz. These methods are divided into three levels, and the distinction
is determined by the response time, the process of using these methods is called
regulation.
Since the imbalance between supply and demand can be positive or
negative, when the supply is greater than the demand, the regulation process is
called down regulation, and when the supply is less than the demand, it is called
up regulation. Since Regulation is not a planned process, the cost of this process
is relatively high. Therefore, the RT market should not only balance supply and
demand, but also minimize the large deviation between the actual energy delivery
and the plan. How to balance the two functions of RT market is also a problem
considered in this study.

4.1
Motivation
Model Predictive Control (MPC) is a control method that uses a model of the
system to make predictions about its future behavior. At each time step, the MPC
algorithm solves an optimization problem to determine the optimal control action
to take based on the current state of the system. This control action is then applied
to the system, and the process is repeated at the next time step. One key aspect
of MPC is that it solves an open-loop optimal control problem at each time step,
meaning that the control action is determined without considering the system’s re-
sponse to that action. This is in contrast to other control algorithms, which may use
pre-computed control laws or feedback from the system to adjust the control action.
Overall, the idea behind MPC is to use a model of the system to make predictions
and optimize control actions to achieve a desired behavior. While the implementa-
tion of MPC may involve a specific model of the system, the fundamental concept
of using prediction and optimization for control is independent of the model used.
As mentioned in first chapter, to realize the MPC algorithm, there are three
necessary elements. Among them, the first element prediction model is used as the
premise of the realization of the MPC algorithm, which have been obtained in the
previous two chapters. In this chapter, the parameters predicted in Chapter 2 and
the mathematical model of the energy system constructed in Chapter 3 would be
combined to design the rolling optimization and feedback correction mechanism of
MPC, in this way, the optimal control strategy of each sector of the energy system
in real-time operation could be obtained.
This chapter includes three main parts. First of all, the frist section sorts out the
working mode of the MPC algorithm under the situation of this research, and make
a systematic exposition of the operation mechanism of the entire MPC optimization
system. The next section specifically expounds the rolling prediction mechanism of
MPC and the simulation tools used in the actual actual operation process. Finally,
the actual results of the MPC control algorithm based on the CNN-GRU-Attention
deep learning prediction model are shown and analyzed.

4.4
Optimization platform and solver
As analyzed in the previous chapter, the problem solved in this study is a
mixed integer linear programming problem (MILP). In the cyclic solution process
described above, an optimization process is performed for each time point advance-
ment. Therefore, this process should be based on an optimization platform, and in
order to obtain the optimal solution, a solver is also necessary. Since both of them
are essential for solving optimization problems, some optimization platforms and
solvers are integrated.
At present, the optimization platforms on the market are mainly divided into
two categories. One is the software specially designed to solve this kind of planning
problem. The representative one is GAMS, which is a traditional planning software.
In addition, there are software such as Julia that can solve the MPC problems in this
study. This type of software is characterized by relatively comprehensive functions,
and generally has a built-in solver, but there are also problems such as mismatching
with other software interfaces. Since the optimization problem in this study needs to
use the prediction results of the deep learning model, such software is not considered.
In order to directly import the prediction results of variables into the optimization
model, the best choice is the second type of optimization platform, that is, the plat-
form attached to mainstream programming software such as python, c, java, etc.
The main function of these platforms is to enable users to convert mathematical
models into code in the form of optimization planning problems. The mainstream
ones include CVXPY, CVXOPT, PuLP, etc. By comprehensively comparing these
platforms, CVXPY has excellent ease of use and is currently the mainstream op-
timization platform choice. It was developed by Steven Boyd’s research group at
Stanford[75]. CVXPY is a pure Python optimization tool, which does not have a
built-in solver, but its advantage is that the current mainstream external solvers
could be flexibly called.
There are also two types of external solvers, namely commercial solvers and
open-source solvers. Famous commercial solvers include GUROBI, MOSEK, etc.
Commercial solvers have great advantages in solving specific problems, but the op-
timization problem in this study is relatively simple, so the use of commercial solvers
is less meaningful. At the same time, a pre-test has been done and shows that the
use of GUROBI for this study does not show a better-measured performance than
the final chosen open-source solver, so commercial solvers are not considered. The
widely used open-source solvers include CPLEX, CBC, and GLPY, all of which
can solve MILP problems, and can be applied to the CVXPY platform through
external interfaces. For the planning problem in this study, the performance of the
three is close, CPLEX is more convenient to call and faster to solve, which is the
final choice for the solver. CPLEX is a linear programming solver developed by
IBM[76] that provides an official python interface. It has two versions, commercial
57
==PAGE:70==
and community, where the community version can be used to solve problems within
1000 variables and constraints, so it is enough to be used to solve the optimization
problem of this research.

5.1
Conclusion
In this research, the real-time optimization problem of a coupled power and dis-
trict heating system in Bornholm is studied. To address the imbalance between
supply and demand caused by the uncertainty of renewable energy, a deep learning-
based forecast for photovoltaic and wind power generation, as well as a forecast
for power and heat demand, is proposed. By combining these four forecasts with
day-ahead market information and analyzing the energy system structure of the
researched location, a real-time optimal control mathematical model of the local
energy market is established. Using this model and the prediction results, control
optimization of the energy system is conducted using the MPC algorithm, the real-
time operation mechanism and improvement plan for the local energy system are
investigated. The following are the specific research work and achievements:
1, This research discusses the impact of new policies in the energy field of vari-
ous countries on the energy system under the background of global climate warming
and the global energy crisis, and analyzes the research significance and process of
energy system control optimization. Additionally, the current status of energy de-
velopment in Denmark, the development of deep learning and its application in the
energy field, as well as the application of MPC in energy management of energy
systems is introduced.
2, The collected energy system related parameter data is screened, cleaned, and
preprocessed, suitable prediction models are analyzed and selected, and the model
hyperparameters and optimizers are selected using cross validation. The CNN-GRU-
Attention model is used to replace the traditional RNN model to extract features
and self-calculate weights. The advantages of rolling prediction are proved through
experiments, and finally, the prediction effect is evaluated according to the selected
evaluation criteria.
3, The energy system of the demo site is analyzed in three modules: the electrical
model, the heat storage model, and the heat network model. The current operation
mode of the energy market is analyzed and how to optimize the energy system is
discussed. Based on the above information, a mathematical model of the energy

MPC algorithm are analyzed and how they are reflected in the problem studied in

of a typical summer and a typical winter 48 hours, the operation mode of the local
