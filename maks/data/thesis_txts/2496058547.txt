Abstract
In IDA ICE there were performed two investigations; the investigation of six
diﬀerent parameters eﬀect on the air ﬂows through the windows, and the inves-
tigation of an advanced modelling of a horizontal top pivot windows eﬀect on
the air ﬂows through the windows. For these investigations there was created a
basic model with three diﬀerent zone combinations.
The six parameters investigated in the basis model were discharge coeﬃcient,
maximum relative width and height of the window, the opening schedule of the
door, wind proﬁles and their associated pressure coeﬃcients, window width and
window height. Furthermore, three diﬀerent versions of an advanced window
opening for a horizontal top pivot window were created. The three versions of
the advanced window were compared to the normal window opening used in
IDA ICE. The three diﬀerent versions were for eﬀective window area ("Area"),
the variable discharge coeﬃcient ("Variable Cd") and the combination of the
eﬀective window area and variable discharge coeﬃcient ("Area and variable dis-
charge coeﬃcient").
Both the area of the window and the discharge coeﬃcient have a high eﬀect on
the air ﬂows through the windows. The change from a closed to an open door
had also eﬀect on the air ﬂows through the windows. The diﬀerent wind proﬁles
and their associated environment had a low eﬀect on the air ﬂows through the
windows.
For the three diﬀerent versions of the advanced window, it can be concluded that
they all have an eﬀect on the air ﬂows through the windows. The version for
the eﬀective area have the highest impact on the air ﬂows through the windows.
For zone combination 3 with "Area and variable Cd" was the eﬀect higher when
the door was opened. Due to numerical errors in IDA ICE, it was not possible
to perform simulations for zone combination 3 with "Area".

Resume
I IDA ICE er der udført to undersøgelser; undersøgelsen af seks forskellige
parametres eﬀekt på luftstrømmen gennem vinduerne, og undersøgelsen af en
avanceret modellering af et tophængt vindue med horisontalt drejningspunkts
eﬀekt på luftstrømmen gennem vinduerne. Til disse undersøgelser var der lavet
en basis model med tre forskellige zone kombinationer.
De seks forskellige parametre, der er undersøgt i basis modellen, er udstrømn-
ingskoeﬃcienten, den maksimale relative bredde og højde af vinduet, dørens
åbning, vindproﬁler og deres tilhørende trykkoeﬃcienter, vinduets bredde og
vinduets højde. Derudover var der lavet tre forskellige versioner af den avanceret
vinduesåbning for et tophængt vindue med horisontalt drejningspunkt. Disse
tre versioner var sammenlignet med den normal vinduesåbning, som bruges i
IDA ICE. De tre forskellige versioner er det eﬀektive vinduesareal ("Area"),
den variable udstrømningskoeﬃcient ("Variable Cd") og kombinationen af det
eﬀektive vinduesareal og variable udstrømningskoeﬃcient ("Area and variable
Cd").
Både arealet af vinduet og udstrømningskoeﬃcienten har en høj eﬀekt på luft-
strømmen gennem vinduerne. Når døren ændres fra lukket til åben har den
også en eﬀekt på luftstrømmen gennem vinduerne. De forskellige vindproﬁler
og deres tilhørende trykkoeﬃcienter har en lille eﬀekt på luftstrømmen gennem
vinduerne.
For de tre forskellige versioner af det avanceret vindue kan det konkluderes, at de
alle har en eﬀekt på luftstrømmen gennem vinduerne. Versionen for det eﬀektive
areal har den højeste eﬀekt på luftstrømmen gennem vinduerne. For zone kom-
bination 3 med "Area and variable Cd" var eﬀekten højere, når døren var åben.
Grundet numeriske fejl i IDA ICE var det ikke muligt at lave simuleringerne for
zone kombination 3 med "Area".

Preface
This master thesis was prepared at the Technical University of Denmark, DTU
BYG in fulﬁllment of the requirements for acquiring a M.Sc. in Architectural
Engineering.
The thesis is produced in collaboration with DTU BYG in the autumn/winter
2020. The study investigate the simulation of air ﬂows through windows in IDA
ICE. Six parameters related to natural ventilation was investigated to examine
their eﬀects on the air ﬂows through windows. Furthermore, it was investigated
if a more advanced modelling of a horizontal top pivot window would eﬀect the
air ﬂows through the windows compared to the normal sliding window used in
IDA ICE.
Lyngby, 26-January-2020
Nikoline K. Walther

Chapter 1
Introduction
We live in a world where there due to global warming is an increased focus
on the environment. We want to reduce our CO2 emission and become more
sustainable. In the construction, use and demolish phases of buildings there
are emitted a huge amount of CO2, and therefore it is important to build more
energy eﬃcient. When creating more energy eﬃcient buildings simulations and
models are important factors. Simulations and models can be used to analyze
and optimize the building in order to become more energy eﬃcient. Simulations
and models of a building provide an imitation of the building. In order for the
imitation to be as realistic as possible, it is necessary to have reliable simulations
and models.
In buildings natural ventilation will have an eﬀect on the energy consumption
in the building. Natural ventilation can be used for cooling in order to prevent
overheating, and thereby reduce the energy consumption [1], [2]. Furthermore,
natural ventilation have both positive and negative eﬀects on the indoor air
quality and thermal comfort [2], [3]. In order to know the eﬀects of the natural
ventilation, it is important to have reliable simulations.
Natural ventilation can occur as single-sided, cross and stack ventilation through
windows. The diﬀerent types of windows will result in diﬀerent types of air
ﬂows, and therefore it is important that the windows are modelled as realistic
as possible [2].

Background
2.1
Natural Ventilation
In a building natural ventilation happens by two driving forces, thermal buoy-
ancy and wind pressure. The air exchange for both driving forces is caused by
the pressure diﬀerence, which is called driving pressure. In the case of thermal
buoyancy and wind pressure acting together are they dominating at low and
high wind velocities, respectively. When thermal buoyancy and wind act to-
gether can the wind pressure, depending on the wind direction and the position
of the openings, enhance or reduce the total driving pressure. If the wind pres-
sure acts opposite to the thermal buoyancy it will result in a small drop in the
total ventilation rate. [6], [7]
For single sided ventilation the thermal buoyancy is the main driving force in
winter, whereas it is the wind pressure in the summer. For single sided ventila-
tion in the winter the air inﬁltrates and exﬁltrates through the bottom and top
part of the window. Typically cross ventilation is caused to wind pressure. [3]

3.1
IDA Indoor Climate and Energy (IDA ICE)
The simulation program IDA ICE calculates the air ﬂows for the natural venti-
lation through a large opening, e.g. windows and doors, by using the component
model "CeLVO" [13]. The type of windows and doors used in the program is
sliding. This means that when the window or door is opened, it slides to one
of the sides. Therefore, a simulation in IDA ICE will be for a sliding window.
This type of simulation will later be referred to as the normal simulation in IDA
ICE.
The mass air ﬂow is calculated for ﬂat and slanted velocity proﬁles [13]. When
there is a density diﬀerence between adjoining zones or a zone and the outside
environment the vertical velocity proﬁle is slanted, and otherwise the proﬁle
is ﬂat with the wind pressure acting on the building facades [13]. In the case
of both ﬂat and slanted proﬁles, where the neutral plane is not located in the
opening, the air ﬂow move in one direction through the opening [13]. If the
proﬁle is slanted and the neutral plane is located in the opening, the ﬂow moves
in two directions through the opening, and it is bi-directional [13]. For the bi-
directional air ﬂow is the air moving in one direction through the top of the
opening and in the opposite direction at the bottom of the opening [13].
From the coding in the component model "CeLVO" it is known, that "CeLVO"
calculates the bi-directional mass air ﬂow through a large vertical opening at
==PAGE:36==
14
Method
any height. "CeLVO" converts the air ﬂow from a mass air ﬂow to a volume air
ﬂow by dividing the calculated mass ﬂow with the air density in the zone the
air is moving from. As the mass air ﬂow is bi-directional the volume air ﬂow is
also bi-directional. This means that the volume air ﬂow is calculated through
the top and bottom part of the opening.
A repeating part in the equations for calculating the air ﬂow is: cd · Width · zt,
where cd is the discharge coeﬃcient, Width is the adjusted/current width of the
window, and zt is the height of the window. In IDA ICE both the height of the
window and the discharge coeﬃcient are assumed to be constant, meanwhile the
width of the window is adjusted based on the control signal. The ﬁrst equation
in the macro is for the calculation of the adjusted/current width: Width =
MAX(leak, Contr) · w, where leak is the minimal leak, Contr is the control
input, and w is the width of the window. This width will later be referred to as
the geometrical width of the window. The control input determine the opening
of the window or door based on the chosen control schedule. The input is equal
to or between 0 and 1, where 0 corresponds to a closed window or door, and 1
corresponds to a fully open window or door. The equation means that the width
of the window is multiplied with either the leak or the control value, depending
on which of them has the largest value. The leak has a constant value of 1·10−8,
while the control input depends on the type of control and schedule used for the
opening of the window.
In IDA ICE there is used the Neutral Model Format (NMF). NMF is modeling
language, which is used for coding [13]. The NMF is explained in the "NMF
Handbook" [14].

There are three directions to choose between when modelling a variable discharge

4.1
Numerical Errors
During the simulations there occurred some numerical errors for four cases. In
the case for zone combination 3 with the use of "Area and variable Cd" and the
door is "never open" there occurred a numerical error. The error was solved
by changing the constant with the small value connected to the MAX element
from 0.001 to 0.01 and the π-value from 3.141593 to 3.14. As mentioned is the
function of the constant connected to the MAX element to ensure that the sine
and tangent functions do not become 0, as it will result in division with 0.
An error also occurred for the same zone combination with the same macro,
but where the door is "always open". The error was solved by changing the
constant with the small value to 0.1, the π-value to 3.14 and the tolerance for
the simulation from 0.02 (the default value) to 0.002. The value of the tolerance
is decreased, because it causes the simulation’s solver be more careful when
calculating the system of equations in the program, and the equations will be
calculated for smaller time steps [15]. When the tolerance is decreased it results
in a longer simulation time [15].
For the case for zone combination 3 with the use of "Area", the error could
not be solved only by making the changes as described above. Therefore, the

For the advanced modelling for the opening of the window in "Area" and "Area
and variable Cd", the opening area of the window is converted from a sliding
to a horizontal top pivoted function. In IDA ICE however the window is still
simulated as a sliding window. This means that the openable part of the top
pivot window will be simulated as it normally does in IDA ICE, where it opens
the window by sliding the pane to one of the sides.
Thereby, the openable
part is not tilted out from the wall construction, which is the case for a real
horizontal top pivoted window. For a sliding window the outdoor air ﬂows from
all directions through the opening, and the air is only blocked by the pane in
the wall construction. For a pivot window the openable part of the window
is blocking some of the air from certain directions, depending on the type of
window. These diﬀerent ways of blocking have an inﬂuence on the temperature,
type and movement of the air ﬂowing through the window, and thereby also
on the indoor comfort and energy used for heating [3]. The temperature, type
and movement of the air ﬂowing into the room through the open window are
aﬀecting the comfort in the room.

window to the axis placement. For at middle pivot window the HA is half of
the geometric height of the window. If the window instead is vertically pivot
the eﬀective area is calculated by swapping the width and height of the window
in the equations for the horizontal pivot window. This can also be used for a
door, if it is vertically pivoting.

or from a graphical script in a macro.

For the diﬀerent simulations the air ﬂows through the windows are given as a
volume ﬂow, which cause the diﬀerence between the air ﬂows through the top
and bottom part of the windows. As mentioned are the air ﬂows through the
windows ﬁrst calculated as the air mass ﬂows through the top and bottom part
of the windows. The diﬀerence between the mass ﬂows through the top and
bottom of the windows are very small. The air mass ﬂow through the windows
are transformed into volume ﬂows by dividing with the air density for the zone
(outdoor and indoor) the air is moving from. As the air density is diﬀerent
between the zones, this diﬀerence will create a diﬀerence in the volume ﬂows.
In the coding in "CeLVO" the geometrical width of the window is changed
with the multiplication of either the opening control signal or the leak value,
depending on which value is highest. When a window is closed a very small
amount of air ﬂows through the cracks and leaks in and around the window
construction, and the leak is set to a constant on 1.0·10−8. The leak is not used
in the macros for the advanced modelling because of its low value, and the fact
that it is used when the window is closed. The leak does not have an inﬂuence
on the air ﬂows when the window is open.
In the programming of the eﬀective area in "Area" and "Area and variable Cd"
there is used the component MAX to ensure that there is not divided by zero.
Instead the component Add can be used. The Add component adds two inputs,
and would in the macros be used to add a constant to the α-value. The MAX
component is used, because inputs below the value of the constant connected
to MAX, are exchanged to the value of this constant. Thus it does not have
any inﬂuence on the input values above the value of the constant. The Add
component is adding the value of a constant to every single input regardless
of the input value. So where the MAX component have an inﬂuence on some
or all of the inputs, depending on the size of the constant, the Add component
aﬀect all the inputs. How much these components aﬀects the output depends on
the value of the constants used. An increase in the constant leads to increasing
errors in the output for both components and the new control, which eventually
leads to an unnecessary air ﬂows through the open windows.
