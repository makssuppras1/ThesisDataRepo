Abstract:
Salmonella enterica is one of the most common foodborne pathogens both in
Denmark and worldwide. Therefore find out it's original source is very necessary for
both food safety and human health. The previously popular MLST approach as well
as the comparative exposure assessment approaches both have some limitations.
The models used in this project are based on whole gene sequencing, and the K-mer,
pan-genome, core genome, accessory genome, and five uniquely found genes are
the input features for the model. The K value from 3 to 13 was investigated for the
model and had the best accuracy at 6, with the codon start with ATG. The model was
constructed with random forest algorithm and the average accuracy was calculate on
the randomly generated training set(70% animal data) with cross-validation. Given an
average accuracy of 96.9% (95%, CI :0.958, 0.979). The performance of the 6mer ATG
only model was estimated on the testing set(30% animal data) and given a valid
accuracy of 99.4%. For the pan-gene relevant features, the pan-genome model has
an average accuracy of 97.6% (95%, CI:0.970, 0.983) with random forest algorithm
and given a valid accuracy of 97.9%. For the five uniquely find gene models, the
Cattle uniquely find gene model has an average accuracy of 96.5% (95%, CI:0.9600,
0.970) with random forest algorithm and given a valid accuracy of 97.9%. For all the 3
best models, they all predicted 100% human cases.
The project still has lots of stuff that needs to be further researched. For K-mer, only
the value 13 was tried because any number higher than 13 will have more than 2
billion variables(that will acquire more than 1.4TB of RAM). For that large matrix size,
even the high performance computer used could not permit using RAM higher than
1.4TB and will encounter errors and model nonconvergence during the first stage
involving feature selection.
So for further research, we need to find the balance point between the
computation time and the overall performance.

1. Introduction:
1.1 Salmonella
The genus Salmonella has two species, Salmonella bongori, and Salmonella enterica,
the main one, Salmonella enterica, has six subspecies, arizonnae, diarizonae, enterica,
houtenae, indica and salamae. The most common of subspecies is Salmonella
enterica subsp. enterica - themselves divided into more than 2000 serovars as
Enteritidis, Derby, Hadar, Infantis, Paratyphi, Typhi, Typhimurium for exemple. The
present study, incompasses exclusively Salmonella enterica subspecies enterica
serovar Thyphimurium and its monophasic variant.[1]
Salmonella enterica enterica is a rod shape Gram-negative bacillus. Most of the
Salmonella has peritrichous flagella to enhance its mobile ability [2]. Salmonella is a
facultative intracellular pathogen. [3]It is an anaerobic bacterium, however it can also
perform aerobic respiration. When oxygen is not available, it can produce energy by
fermentation.[4]its optimum growth temperature is between 35 and 37 C, however
Salmonella has a weak thermal resistance and can be killed while at 60 ° C for 12
minutes[5]. Salmonella can survive for several weeks in dry conditions and several
months in water. They are therefore frequently found in polluted aquatic
environments, contamination by the excrements of carrier animals being very
significant. Since poultry, cattle and sheep are frequently contaminating animals,
salmonella can be found in foods, especially meats, milk or an egg. Eggs, poultry, and
meat products are believed the main media of Salmonella disease.[6] Therefore，
heating up the food sufficiently can effectively eradicate Salmonella.
1.2 Hazard of Salmonella
Salmonella enterica is one of the most common foodborne pathogens in Denmark
and worldwide.[7] It leads to acute gastroenteritis that is characterized by nausea,
vomiting, diarrhea, and fever as the main symptoms. Salmonella is not often
considered as a fatal bacteria; most patients do not need specific treatment after
being infected and will recover in few days. However, it may also bring severe and
life-threatening consequences for children, the elderly, and people with low
immunity. Salmonella is widely distributed in nature, and it is commonly found on
domestic and wild animals. Salmonella can vary from the bottom of the food chain to
the entire food chain. Humans are usually infected by Salmonella after consumption
of contaminated meat in the food chain. Although vegetables can also spread
Salmonella, but the risk is much smaller [8]. According to the annual report on the
incidence of foodborne diseases in Denmark in 2017. There were 1067 human cases
related to Salmonella in Denmark，and the number of related reports to Salmonella
in Denmark has been very stable in the past seven years. In these human-related
cases, the pork produced in Denmark was considered to be the most important food
source of human salmonellosis (87 cases, 8.2% to all cases), followed by imported
pork and imported broiler.[9] The global human health impact of Salmonella is also

1.3 Previous study
In order to prevent Salmonella diseases, it is essential to trace back the source of
Salmonella in each case. In the past, people used two methods to do that. One is a
microbial subtyping, mainly using Multi Locus Sequence Typing method; this
gene-by-gene approach utilizes the allelic combination of a set of genes defined in a
scheme specific to each bacterial species. Another method is a comparative exposure
assessment that is the systematic evaluation of the changes in population health,
which result from modifying the population distribution of exposure to a risk factor,
here Salmonella. Both of these two methods have some significant weaknesses. The
Multi Locus Sequence Typing method cannot tell the different exposure within the
same species, while the comparative exposure assessment method usually has a
large uncertainty, due to the lack data and knowledge among other species of risk
and immunity. Even if we combine these two methods together, the final
performance will not be better than only using the Multi Locus Sequence Typing
method alone. In the past 20 years, the human case of different sources attribution
of Salmonella is based on a Bayesian modeling method, which compares the human
cases caused by different Salmonella subtypes with the same subtypes isolated that
collected from various animal-food sources. For a long time, people have used these
methods for subtype definitions: phage typing, serotyping, Multiple Locus Variable
Tandem Repeat Analysis, and resistance profiling. However, progressively, the
Multiple Locus Variable Tandem Repeat Analysis and serotyping methods were
replaced by the whole genome sequencing method, using all the isolates from
national Salmonella surveillance programs, which collected a huge amount of
isolates from animals, food, and humans[9]. In 2017, Munck et al have developed a
source attribution model for Salmonella Typhimurium. This model is based on
Whole-Genome Sequences and machine learning. The input feature she used is the
core genome multilocus sequence typing. The model was looking for allelic variations
in the core genes and then used these allelic variations to infer where this source was
from. In the model selection part, two machine learning algorithms were compared:
random forest and logit boost, in the core genome multilocus sequence typing case,
the logit boost was more accurate than the random forest, with an average accuracy
of 0.933 (95 % CI: 0.92-0.94) compared to 0.783 (95% CI: 0.77–0.80). And the final
model was based on the logit boost and successfully predicted 81% of the human
case[11]. From the result, the main source of salmonella human cases in Denmark
was Denmark pigs (53%) followed by imported pigs (16%), imported broilers (6%),
imported ducks (2%), Danish produced layers (2%), Danish produced cattle, and

imported cattle (*1%)[11].
1.4 Objectives
The research goal for the project is to develop a whole genome sequences based
source attribution model for Salmonella that estimates links between human
infections to different food sources and animal reservoirs.
To do so, we will use information from the whole genome sequences of Salmonella
enterica by using pan-genome, core genes, accessory genes, uniquely find genes, and
K-mer; we will evaluate the performance of different algorithms for the source
attribution machine learning part; then we will attribute the human case to different
animal-food sources; and eventually we will compare the overall performance of
different input features.
2. Bioinformatics tools
The biological information is contained in DNA sequence. DNA is composed of a
double helix of nucleic acid linked by hydrogen bonds. In each strand, there are four
complementary nucleotides: adenine (A), thymine (T), guanine (G), and cytosine (C).
If an A is on the strand, there is always a T paired with an A on the other strand,
similarly G is always paired with C [12]. Sequence size of Salmonella enterica is
around 5 Mbp. After chemical DNA extraction, this nucleic chain is sequenced using
one of the various sequencing platforms. In this work illumina technology was used
and provided files that encompass the raw data of the sequences, the so-called fastq
files.
Bioinformatics tools will used the latter files to analyze and process the data from
raw data sequencing to matrices that can be used as input for machine learning. The
bioinformatics tools and procedure that were used in this study are the k-mer and
the pangenome analyses.

2.3 K-mer
Usually, the term K-mer refers to the substring of a fixed length K contained in the
entire sequence. The K-mers are the set of all these substrings. For the DNA
sequences, k-mers are extracted at every position from the original sequence.
Figure 1, an example showing a DNA sequence with k-value=6
•For a sequence of the length of L, the number of k-mer within that sequence is:
L-k+1
L: the length of the sequence k: fixed length for the k-mer
•The number of all the possible k-mer combinations: 4K
For example, for the sequence: ATGCGT and the k is 3, the number of all the possible
3mer will be 43=256
As the bacterial DNA are double-stranded, the reverse complement is considered as
the same sequence, thus following the A-T G-C paired rule, when using 2-mer, CC and
GG, they are considered the same. This means we only use half of the number of all
Salmonella k-mers.
In the data processing part, the frequency of each unique k-mer was counted. But
==PAGE:13==
5
even the two samples have the same sequence length, but their K-mer count could
have a significant difference because the k-mer count will vary from species to
species. Identifiable species are often identified using K-mers since sequences with
high similarities share K-mers profils. When dealing with an unknown sample,
different databases are usually used to compare k-mers of unknown sources with
those of references.
There are several advantages of the K-mer method. First, the K-mer method can use
all the biological information in the sequence, especially when compared to other
methods like cgMLST. The cgMLST scheme is unstable if a specific rearrangement
occurs in the genome, which will make it inconsistent in the case of missing data. The
results of k-mer are independent of mutation, and therefore there is no missing data.
Second, the K value in the K-mer method is not fixed, so when dealing with different
species, we can adjust the K value according to the actual situation (which depends
on hardware capabilities, computational time, sequence length, etc.) and finally
compare the overall performance and choose the best K value. This advantage will
make the K-mer method notably more flexible compared to other methods. The
cgMLST method lacks that flexibility and may perform poorly under certain
conditions.
2.4 Pan-genome
A pan-genome includes all the genes present within a clade of organisms.
Pan-genomes is divided into core and accessory genomes. The core genome consists
of all genes present in all individuals. The accessory genome contains genes present
in a portion of the strains. The accessory genome is subdivided in two, the shell and
the cloud: the shell encompasses genes that are often present or present in a
sizeable number of strain, whereas the cloud defines genes that are rarely present,
or present in small number of isolates. Accessory genomes contains 'dispensable'
genes present in a subset of strains, as well as strain-specific genes. [14][15]
2.5 Prokka
Prokka is a software tool for rapidly annotating prokaryotic genomes. The annotating
of a typical 4Mbp genome takes less than twenty minutes.[16]
2.6 Roary
The Roary pipeline estimates a pan genome based on assembled data in GFF3 format
(produced by Prokka (Seemann, 2014)). The method can analyze datasets with
thousands of samples using a regular desktop PC, something computationally
infeasible with existing methods, without compromising the quality of the results. A
single processor and 1 GB of RAM can analyze 128 samples in under an hour. Existing
methods would take weeks and hundreds of GB of RAM to complete this analysis.
Roary is not intended to be used for meta-genomic analysis or for comparing highly
==PAGE:14==
6
diverse sets of genomes[16].
Roary takes GFF3 files as input. The file must end with the nucleotide sequence. In
this project, we used Prokka files as input. It is recommended to generate the input
files from Prokka because all GFF3 files created by Prokka are compatible with Roary.
Using a locus tag to identify where genes originated in each input file (--locustag) will
make it easier for us to identify the source of the genes.
We will create a pan genome without a core alignment and perform set operations
on the pan genome to determine the gene differences between groups of isolates
like core genes, accessory genes and uniquely find genes from the data set.
2.7 BLAST
BLAST, stands for Basic Local Alignment Search Tool, an algorithm that compares the
primary structure of the sequence. By using BLAST, we can discover regions of
similarity between genomic or protein sequences. By comparing nucleotide or
protein sequences to sequence reference genes or proteins databases, the program
identifies the significance of each sequence (for this project, the similarity).
3. Methods
3.1 Data Description and collection
We sequenced 65% of the 325 Salmonella isolates and its monophasic variants from
domestic and imported food as the animal data set for this study.
The human data set included 18% of the 764 Salmonella samples and their
monophasic variants. Sequencing was performed on all isolates using an Illumina
HiSeq, NextSeq, or MiSeq sequencing machine.
The total data set includes 351 samples of Salmonella enterica from the serovar
typhymurium and its monophysic variant from humans and animals collected
between 2013 and 2014 as part of integrated surveillance systems: it includes
domestic and imported animals and food sources. This panel was published in
Denmark's annual reports on zoonoses [18].
3.2 QC check and Assemblies
First of all, the sequenced isolates raw data were screened and assembled using the
Food QC & assembly pipeline.[19] The sequencing adapters from Illumina was
removed from the raw data. Low-quality data were excluded if the length of reads
was smaller than 50 bp and the Phred score per base was inferior to 20.
After these first steps of quality control, a de novo assembly was performed using
SPAdes 3.9[20]. De novo assembly results in a whole-genome continuous sequence,
without any reference sequence, that is then assembled and spliced, thereby
==PAGE:15==
7
providing scaffold genomic sequence in contigs.
Eventually, the quality of the assembly was assessed using the number of contigs,
N50, and the total size of the assembly. Genome assemblies with less than 500
contigs were kept in the data set. N50 is a statistical measure of the length of contigs.
It is the median of the lengths of contigs and the longer it is, the better is the
assembly. Finally, the total size of the assembly was checked to match the expected
size for Salmonella enterica is around 5 Mbp.

3.5 Source attribution model – Machine learning
To predict the source from which a given salmonella-infected human case originated,
we used machine learning algorithms trained on K-mer and pan-genes derived from
domestic and imported animal samples. We applied supervised machine learning
classification models for this project. The classification was supervised since the
classification contains class labels so that the algorithm can learn which animal class
a particular sample of the animal comes from.
In figure 2, the modeling procedure is illustrated and elaborated in the following
sections. The feature selection part was carried out in Python, while the modeling
was carried out in R version 4.0.5 (2021-03-31).

Figure 2, the machine learning approach (Munck et al., 2020)
3.5.1 Feature reduction
In order to reduce the overall amount of variables and save some computational
source thus costs less running time. Two features reduction method was performed.
Firstly, the Near zero variance function. The NearZeroVarience function will remove
the variables, which the values in that column are mostly the same or providing the
model with a limited amount of information. Secondly, the Boruta function. The
Boruta will shuffle the original real features into the shadow features by random
forest, and then it will train on these new features. After that, we will compare the
accuracy of these new feature matrices with those of the original real features. If a
==PAGE:20==
12
specific feature was not included in the shadow features data frame and the model
performance was decreased, these features were considered to be important and
included in the data frame; however, if the model performance remained unchanged,
these features were deemed to be less important and excluded from the data frame.
This procedure will be repeated 100 times in order to obtain the best features for the
later machine learning process.
3.5.2 Sample Imbalance
For this project, in the data set there are five animal species divided into 9 class
depend on their original country, and the composition is extremely uneven. This
leads to the problem that the data set is unbalanced and may cause a problem to the
later modeling process. In order to figure out what these unbalanced datasets may
affect the model, an upsampling function was applied to evaluate that. The Caret
package in R performed this upsampling function, making the sample distribution
uniform among sources corresponding to the source with the most animal species
isolates[22].
Upsampled data were used to select models and construct models. However, the
final model was built based on the original data, without upsampling the data set.
3.5.3 Machine Learning
Once the variables have been selected, the next step is machine learning. In general,
machine learning consists of three parts. The first step is to select the best model.
Two machine learning algorithms are compared for this project, random forest and
logit boost. The best algorithm was chosen after comparing the overall performance
of each algorithm. The algorithm will be used to construct the model in the following
section.
To perform the process mentioned above. The data set was divided into animal data
and human data. Machine learning models were developed using only animal data.
Model selection
We trained our models using a randomly generated training set (70% of the animal
data set) and estimated their performance using a testing set (30% of the animal
data set). In order to estimate which algorithm is better, we need to use the training
set to perform cross-validation. During the cross-validation procedure, ten iterations
were performed. By using cross-validation, the training data were randomly divided
into seven subsets (also called folds): one held-out subset and six subsets were
equally divided. The model will be built using the six subsets, and the average
accuracy will be calculated from the held-out subsets. Following this, the
held-out-subset will be returned to the training set, and the process will be repeated
until all subsets have been used as held-out subsets and get predicted. Once this
process has been repeated ten times, we will calculate the average accuracy, and the
algorithm with the highest average accuracy will be selected as the best algorithm
and then used for the model construction part.
Model construction and evaluation
After selecting the best-performing model, a new model was constructed by
==PAGE:21==
13
following the same modeling procedure as that described in the model selection
section. Three indicators will be used to evaluate the overall performance of the
constructed model: valid accuracy, kappa value, and confusion matrix. Rather than
cross-validation, the models' accuracy was assessed by determining their ability to
predict the sources of isolates in the testing data set. An additional way to calculate
the valid accuracy was through the confusion matrix, which compares observed and
predicted data. From the confusion matrix, the kappa value could also be
calculated[22].
Final model
By applying the algorithm from the model construction to the entire un-upsampled
animal data set, the final model was constructed.
Prediction of Human sample
The final model was used to predict the probability that each human salmonella
infection originated from a specific source. For each source, the sum of the
probabilities equaled the number of human cases attributable to that source. The
human samples for which a prediction could not be made will be classified under the
unknown category.

5 Discussion
As whole genome sequencing develops, the cost of whole genome sequencing is
getting cheaper, faster and more accurate. As a result, we will be able to sequence
more samples and add them to a database more quickly. Thus enabling research and
monitoring to continue over time using this cutting-edge technology. Our project
aims to develop a more accurate source attribution model for salmonella by
comparing three kinds of attribution features. Source attribution models predicted
the probability of human Salmonella infection cases originating from specific sources
of animal food. The original country of each species was also investigated.

5.2 Bioinformatic features
5.2.1 K-mer
The biggest advantage of the K-mer method is that it can use all of the information in
that sequence. As whole gene sequencing develops, the cost of whole gene
sequencing is getting cheaper, the speed is getting faster, and the accuracy is getting
higher. As a result, we will be able to sequence more samples and add them to a
database more quickly. Thus enabling research and monitoring to continue over
time.
==PAGE:31==
23
For the K-mer method, balancing the number of K-values with their total amount of
variables is the key point. The bigger K value will generate more unique K-mer
variables. These unique K-mer variables are good for machine learning. While
bringing some problems at the same time.
First, the higher the K value, the more amount of variables it will generate. With a K
value of 12, the total amount of variables will be 4367212. When we generate the
K-mer matrix, we have already filtered out the variables whose entire columns
contain 0 frequency count. However, the total amount of variables is still too large.
As we already discussed in the method section, Computerome has 1.2TB of memory
per node, so the maximum number of variables should be no more than 180,000
(just a speculation based on the experience of this project), so even if we have a
powerful supercomputer like Computerome, we still need to take some actions to
reduce the total amount of variables. In this project, we used two methods. The first
method is that we only generate K-mer starting with ATG. The reason is that the ATG
is the start codon, so the K-mer starting with ATG will have more biological meaning
than other K-mers. Through figure 3 We can find that the K-mer matrix starting with
ATG has a valid accuracy better or equal to the all K-mer matrix. So we believe that
the ATG only method can still provide a good training effect for the model. At the
same time, we found that the total amount of variables in the K-mer matrix
generated using the ATG only method is greatly reduced, only 95% of the total
amount of variables of all K-mer matrices. So we believe that the ATG only method is
effective. However, we also observed that even we applied the ATG only method
when the number of K values reached 15 or higher, the total amount of variables
became extremely large again. So we introduced the second method, the sum in
column method. Through table 3, we can see that sum in column method have the
same advantages as the K-mer method have. The sum in column K-mer matrix still
can uses all of the biological information in that sequence, it just make the matrix
more "compact". From figure 3, we can see that this method's validity accuracy is
quite good, and it’s quite close to the accuracy of all K-mer methods and the ATG
only method. When the K value is small, for example, when K = 6 and 7, the valid
accuracy obtained by the sum in column method is relatively low when compared to
the all K-mer and the ATG only method. That is because the sum in column method
reduces the variables at a great extend. When the K value is small, the sum in column
will make the total amount of variables too less to use in the variable selection
section. Therefore, affecting machine learning. In the 6mer_sum10 and 7mer_sum10
models, the valid accuracy is quite low. In the meantime, we can manually control
the overall amount of variables. In situations where there are a huge amount of
variables, we can sum 50 or even 100 columns into one. If the computer we use is
not powerful enough or does not have enough memory, this method will be very
useful. So we are able to reduce the total amount of variables to the greatest extent,
and this method is very flexible, very easy to control the total amount of variables,
and resolves the problem that the ATG-only method may encounter when the K
value is too high.
Second, when the K value is large, the newly generated K-mer variable will be very
==PAGE:32==
24
unique, so the number of each unique K-mer variable will be small. The frequency
count of many variables is 0 or 1 or even that entire column is the same value or only
has few non-zero numbers. In order to solve this problem, we used the near-zero
variance method to filter out these kinds of variables. Then we apply the Boruta
method to find all features carrying information that can be used for prediction. By
doing so, model training takes much less time, and the training process is more
efficient. Boruta method includes a parameter: Per. The default setting value for this
parameter is 100. In general, the maximum is too strict. Low per means that more
false positives will be picked as important features, while these less relevant features
will be left out at the same time. In order to compare the 23 models, we set the per
to 99, which means more variables will be considered as important features and used
in machine learning. In this project, we have to compare the overall 23 models
together, and if we use the default per 100, there will be nothing left for some of the
features, even if some features have enough variables. After the Boruta selection,
there will only have few variables left and considered as important features, thus we
set the per to 99 for all the features selection.
5.5.2 Pan-genome
As a species evolves, each individual develops a unique genetic condition due to
mutations and selection forces, and an individual genome does not encompass all
the genetic information of an entire species. For genetic domestication, using only a
single reference genome can result in an absence of much information because the
reference genome contains only a limited number of unique sequences.
Using pan-genome relevant features as inputs for machine learning source
attribution has some advantages. Using the Pan-genome method, a lot of unique
gene functions or gene cluster are identified. By Extracting and combining these
sequences into one FASTA file and blast them against your database, we found
sequences with the highest similarity in the data set, these variables are quite unique
and were ideal for machine learning. The downside is that it consumes too many
computational sources to extract these Pan-genome relevant features. First, we have
to find the unique function or cluster and we have to extract the corresponding
sequence from each sample and combine them into one FASTA file. After finding
these sequences, we need to blast them against the database. Since the query type is
nucleotide and the databse type is protein in our case, we need to translate each
query into a protein first before comparing them. As a result, we spent 167,0000 thin
node CPU hours on Pan-Genome's relevant feature extraction, which in result was
too computationally heavy and costly. Moreover, in order to support a long-term
source attribution research project, a large number and new samples are required,
so whenever new samples are added to the database, we have to look for new gene
functions or gene clusters and repeat the complicated procedure again. Especially we
have to translate the nucleotide into protein before the blast procedure.
Comparatively, K-mer method to not required such effort, as it is a simpler and
user-friendly procedure that do not require high computational capacities, so these
pan-genome relevant features are not an ideal method for long-term research.

5.3 Model evaluation and human case prediction
As we see in table 22, all 15 of these K-mer models have similar Salmonella human
case source attribution prediction results. There is no significant difference in the
human case source attribution prediction result between these 15 K-mer models and
all of the 15 K-mer models had high valid accuracy as all exceeded 95%. Thus, we
believe that the K-mer is a reliable and accurate bioinformatics feature for machine
learning.
According to the result of the 8 pan-genome relevant features models, the Pan (rf),
cattle uniquely find gene(rf), ducks uniquely find gene(rf), have similar trend of results for
human case prediction. When compared to the K-mer results, the distribution trend
of the number of different species is quite similar in these three models. From the
result of the five uniquely found gene models, the distribution trend of the number
of different species is quite different when compare to the K-mer model. This may be
an indication that the true prediction ability of the model is actually quite poor,
which is in accordance with our previous thoughts regarding the weaknesses of our
animal datasets. In these five models, pigs always have the highest number of human
cases attributed, broilers or ducks have the second or third number of human cases
attributed, which is also quite similar to the results of the K-mer model. In the results
of the core and accessory models, the German pigs barely have any case attributed
to it. This outcome indicates that the core genes and accessory genes relevant
features cannot distinguish the difference between Denmark and German pigs.
The machine learning model is restricted to predicting the origin of human
Salmonella cases only if it has been trained on the corresponding animal case before.
Therefore, add more newly find Salmonella from animal samples to the database and
timely update the model is very necessary if we want to use this model to be applied
to investigate outbreaks in the future. We should also focus on the collection of
relevant Salmonella animal samples in addition to further research into machine
learning and new bioinformatics tools.

Conclusion
In this project, source attribution models were developed and investigated in order
to predict the animal source of the danish human salmonellosis cases. To do so we
use genomic sequencing from both animal and human sample and applied machine
learning models. The models developed were supervised classification machine
learning models that focused either on Pan-genome relevant features or on the
frequency count of each unique K-mer.
According to our research, for long-term monitoring research that expects there will
be more new samples added to the existing database in the future, such as the
Danish national Salmonella surveillance program. The K-mer relevant feature is a
better choice. Compared with the Pan-genome relevant features, the extraction of
the K-mer feature is simpler, the computation time needed is shorter, the cost is
lower, and the accuracy of the machine learning model is higher. In the future, there
are still more potentials that can be explored and if the study needs to try with a
higher K value, the ATG only method and the sum in column method could be
investigated.
