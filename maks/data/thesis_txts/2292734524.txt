4     ABSTRACT 
A Wireless Power Transfer (WPT) system is a safe, convenient and smart charging 
solution for Electric Vehicle (EV) users. However, a drawback of WPT systems is the 
reduced efficiency in comparison to conventional wired charging due to lower coupling. 
By increasing the volume of EVs in the market, improving the system efficiency even a 
few percent, beside of being environmentally suitable, will benefit both the consumers 
and distributors.  
According to the previous studies, efficiency improvement by decreasing the vertical 
distance (VD) has been one of the aims of the companies and universities. However, in low 
VD, system performance becomes highly sensitive, due to the magnetic coupling strength 
between transmitter (TX) and receiver (RX) coils.  
The most important aims of this project are, firstly, to study the system (WPT resonance 
tank) efficiency in different 𝑉𝐷 and different associated parameters such as mutual 
inductance, quality factor and reflective impedance. Secondly, to offer new solutions to 
improve the efficiency of WPT, and finally to conclude the results by analyzing the 
presented idea of decreasing the vertical distance between the transmitter and receiver 
coils. 
In this project, the system is operating under the limits to analyze the system behavior 
from efficiency point of view. Therefore, analyses are based on two major methods, 
including the Safe mode and On-Load analysis:  
- 
In the Safe mode analyzing method: all the parameters including coil’s self-inductance, 
capacitor’s capacitance and their internal resistances are measured through the RLC 
meter. These analyses are made in different frequencies and then these values are 
implemented to the related equations to calculate other parameters such as mutual 
inductance. Finally, all results are plotted out in 2D and 3D through Matlab to 
illustrate these values and their differences according to different variable parameters 
such as frequency and vertical distance. 
==PAGE:9==
 
Abstract 
 
Page | 7  
 
- 
In the On-Load analyzing method: the WPT system is supplied by DC power, and 
results are analyzed and captured through the oscilloscope device. 
According to the safe mode analyses on the WPT system resonance tank, it can be seen 
that by decreasing the 𝑉𝐷, the magnetic coupling is significantly increasing, which leads to 
the maximum efficiency of up to 97.36% for the distance variability of 𝑉𝐷= 1 𝑐𝑚 
compared to 88.65% for 𝑉𝐷= 18 𝑐𝑚.  
According to the theoretical calculations based on the real RLC measured values, the 
system expected to operate at resonance for 𝑉𝐷= 4 𝑐𝑚. However, the On-Load 
experimental results shows that the system is interfered by the 3rd harmonic impedance 
“𝑍3𝑟𝑑” for the distances below 𝑉𝐷= 4 𝑐𝑚. One of the reasons is, in lower distances the 
magnetic coupling strength increases significantly, therefore the system will be sensitive 
and hard to tune. 
One of the other solutions is to change the ferrite thickness. In order to investigate the 
solution, several more experiments have been made, such as the setup with half, one 
fourth and no ferrites applied. Results lead to decreasing the mutual inductance 
significantly due to decreasing the ferrite. Therefore, results mitigate the effects of 𝑍3𝑟𝑑 
on the WPT system. However, less ferrites lead to less efficiency, but the goal of those 
analyses are just to investigate the system performance improvement with less magnetic 
coupling strength. For the WPT system applications in low 𝑉𝐷, a new system design have 
to be considered to meet all requirements and achieve more efficiency.

1     INTRODUCTION 
A static WPT system is a stationary system to transfer power wirelessly, also called 
Inductive Power Transfer “IPT” system. In Figure 1, the basic infrastructure of a static 
WPT system connected to an EV [1] is shown. It basically consists of: charging spot that is 
connected to the grid, intelligent charging box that controls the charging traffic 
procedure, the primary or transmitter coil to transfer energy and secondary or receiver 
coil to pick-up the energy wirelessly. 
 
Figure 1: Basic WPT system structure supplying an EV [1] 
One of the most important goals of this project is the efficiency improvement following 
the main idea of decreasing the distance between the transmitter and receiver coil, which 
will be discussed more in the Main Idea chapter. 
After studying different available products and researches and to the best of our 
knowledge, there is no company which has claim about implementing a project like the 
==PAGE:14==
 
Introduction 
 
Page | 12  
 
proposed project. Hence, in this project, the general studies about WPT systems and the 
related analyses in the laboratory have been done with the university supports. 
In general, the idea is including of a smart control unit, controlling the sensors and 
actuators and an expandable lift table. The prototype will decrease the vertical distance 
intelligently, when the sensors detect the parked EV in the right position, the vertical 
distance will be decreased based on the height of the EV. 
The main purpose of the project is to evaluate the WPT system efficiency when the 
vertical distance is decreasing and analyze the effects and side-effects of this procedure. 
Then, by gathering the results, the next improvements will be considered and will be 
discussed.

1.1 
Case Studies 
There are several different static WPT system companies in the market that are 
producing different types of static WPT system with different efficiencies. Some other 
companies, have been trying to find a solution to increase the efficiency by decreasing the 
𝑉𝐷, such as: 
 (IISB) Nose to nose wireless charging 
Researchers in Fraunhofer Institute for Integrated Systems and Device Technology 
(IISB), are developing a product that is able to charge the EV wirelessly with less 
vertical distance. In this approach, transmitter and receiver coil are placed in front 
nose of the EV (nose to nose). As a result researchers have been succeed to transfer 
three kilowatts (3 kW) at an overall efficiency of 95%. [2]  
In Figure 67: Nose-to-nose, Front Nose charg, a photo of the system is added in the 
appendix. 
 Plugless, Level 2 EV Charging System 
In this company that is supplying WPT products for Nissan Leaf, Ford Focus and 
Chevrolet Volt, the receiver is placed in the back bottom of the EV in their new models 
as shown in Figure 68: Plugless Back Bottom Charger. In their old versions the 
receiver placed in the front bottom of EV as shown in Figure 69: Plugless Front 
Bottom Charger. 
Based on the published report from Evatran Group Inc., the maximum efficiency of 
their WPT system at 𝑉𝐷= 10 𝑐𝑚 and 𝐹𝑟. = 19.5 𝑘𝐻𝑧, is about 88.8% [3]. 
 WiTricity 
WiTricity is one of the other companies that produces the WPT products. One of their 
last development kits (WiT-3,300) it is capable of transferring 3.3 kW power over 
𝑉𝐷= 18 𝑐𝑚 𝑎𝑡 𝐹𝑟= 145 𝑘𝐻𝑧 with 90% efficiency. Size of WiT-3,300 transmitter and 
receiver resonators are 50×50 cm [4].  
 University of Auckland  
Auckland university is also developed a 5 kW stationary WPT system with 90% 
efficiency over 𝑉𝐷= 20 𝑐𝑚. The coil design size is 75 cm [5].

 Magne Charge 
Magne Charge is also one of the other products made by Hughes Electronics for

unsuccessful in the market. However, the idea of decreasing the vertical distance was 
successfully lead to 95% efficiency. 
Furthermore, the proposed idea in this project was trying to solve the standardization

2     PROBLEM STATEMENT 
Using WPT technology brings comfortability, ease of use and safety. While the involved 
components are electrically isolated, operating the system in the rainy environment, 
won’t affect the performance.  
By improvement of the technology, the volume of EVs are increasing everyday. This leads 
to increasing the power consumption specially during the rush hours. Therefore, 
improving the system efficiency even for few percent will benefit both the consumers and 
distributers.  
The focus of this project is to discuss the considered matter that is mainly related to 
studying the efficiency of the WPT resonance tank and proposing some improvement 
ideas. In addition, there were some confidentiality limitations of accessing to the 
designed laboratory WPT system, which led the project to analyze the resonance tank of 
the system based on the RLC meter measurements. 
2.1 
Problem definition  
According to the consumer needs, the main problem arise, when the power consumption 
increasing due to the EV popularity.  
Mainly, if the WPT system is assumed to be the convenient solution with about 90% 
efficiency (according to the WPT setup measurements which is available in the following 
chapters), increasing that efficiency five to seven percent, perhaps doesn’t make that 
much difference at low volume of consumers and low power transfer. However, in higher 
volume (future) and higher power, it will save a considerable amount of energy. 
2.2 
Background 
Denmark has a future plan to achieve 40,000 Electric Vehicles in the roads by 2025 [7]. 
Perhaps this volume of EVs, with respect to the people interest and ability of power 
distributers to supply sufficient power, is a little bit far from the reality achievement on

2.3 
Project scope 
Mainly, the main focus of this project is to study the efficiency of the WPT resonance tank 
while the VD is decreasing, which affect the system efficiency. This is why the main 
vertical distance of 𝑉𝐷= 10 𝑐𝑚 at 𝑓= 21.5 𝑘𝐻𝑧, has been chosen as a benchmark 
comparison point. Furthermore, this is the operating resonance frequency at 𝑉𝐷= 10 𝑐𝑚. 
While the system has been designed at this vertical distance, it has been decided to 
choose the relative parameters as the main benchmark, compare to other vertical 
distances. 
Secondly, the main idea is an approach to improve the efficiency by decreasing the 
vertical distance between coils will be discussed. All other effective parameters such as 
mutual inductance, efficiency etc. will be analyzed in different levels; from 𝑉𝐷=
1 𝑐𝑚 to 18 𝑐𝑚, and with the sweeping frequencies from 𝑓= 1 𝑘𝐻𝑧 𝑡𝑜 130 𝑘𝐻𝑧.   
Furthermore, all of the vertical distances assumed to be in five digits and equal to exact 
values e.g. 𝑉𝐷= 1 𝑐𝑚= 1.00000 𝑐𝑚. Therefore, just the first values will be used.

4.4.1 Tight and Loose Coupling 
The best case scenario for an inductive power transfer which provides higher efficiency is 
the time that alternative magnetic field (Flux) achieves the highest level of itself. 
Basically, just a fraction of the magnetic flux will affect the secondary part of the coil and 
contribute to the power transmission. This depends a lot on the distance between two 
coils.  
In the applications where the distance between transmitter and receiver coils are higher, 
the less amount of magnetic flux exist, which leads to less efficiency. These types of 
operation defined as loosely coupled operation, as it is shown in Figure 14(a). The 
solution to solve the low efficiency in such applications, is tuning the system to operate in 
resonance that will be discussed in more details in the following sections.

Skin Effect 
Generally, the skin effect is the tendency of the AC current to distribute within a 
conductor. According to the Eddy current law, it will increase the internal energy that 
leads to the internal temperature. 
The magnitude of the Eddy current is given by Equation (‎4-26). 
As shown in Figure 22, the problem with the skin effect is the tendency of current flows in 
the area between the surface of the cable and a level called skin depth “𝜹”, which is usually 
about 63% of the whole current flow. This also highly depends on the alternating current 
frequency, e.g. by using a copper wire in the 60 Hz frequency, the skin depth would be 
about 8.5 mm, and by increasing the frequency, this amount will decrease and get much 
smaller. 
 
Figure 22: Skin Effect, Skin depth vs. Surface 
Therefore, it is important to consider the effects of the higher frequency to the skin depth 
because it leads to the higher resistance on the cable. 
The solution to this problem in high frequencies is, instead of using a single thick wire, 
several thin wires will be parallel together that can support the same current flow with 
less skin effect. Due to the presented argument the Litz wire have been found as the best

4.10 Resonance Tuning 
In order to optimize the system and achieve more efficiency, it is important to tune and 
keep the system in full resonance. In practical, one of the easiest approaches to tune the 
system is to variate the frequency to achieve the matching phase angle between current 
and voltage in the system. This approach will be done by measuring the phase angle of 
output current or secondary circuit in comparison to output voltage of the bridge 
(𝑉𝑖𝑛= 𝑉𝑃) through an oscilloscope; the measured phase angle have to be ideally about 
90° as shown in Figure 25; in this situation the full resonance will achieve, but tuning the 
system to the exact 90° angle value is hardly possible. Generally, when the system 
resonate, the capacitor and inductor will cancel each other and the system becomes 
resistive. In the   , the primary and secondary impedances of the circuit plotted as a 
domain of frequency for 𝑉𝐷= 10 𝑐𝑚. As it is shown, the circuit is resonating around 
𝑓= 21.5 𝑘𝐻𝑧.  
In order to have an efficient system, it is very important that the primary and secondary 
of the system resonate in the same frequency. However, it is possible to tune one of the 
sides through changing the capacitance value to tune the system.

6     ON-LOAD ANALYSES 
 
    
“EXPERIMENTAL” 
Analyzing the WPT system in the on-load operation is a complex task while the system is 
dependent of several different parameters. This is the main reason that all those analyses 
have been made during the last chapters to study all these parameters and evaluate the 
system behavior as accurate as possible.  
Therefore, implementing the on-load analyses will be necessary despite the fact that the 
RLC meter operation is in low power compared to on-load analyses. On the other hand, 
through the transferred power efficiency analyses, it is expected to have harmonic 
interferences in low vertical distances. 
According to the analyses, the magnetic coupling strength will be very high at 𝑉𝐷= 1 𝑐𝑚 
and operating the system in resonance will be risky. Hence as an analyzing approach to 
prevent from any damages to the system, it has been decided to operate the power supply 
in lower power (0.2 kW).  
In this section, the analyses regarding the on-load will be discussed; different approaches 
to analyze the system will be presented and the system behavior and its interferences 
due to the vertical distance decrement will be analyzed. Finally, an approach to prevent 
the system from interferences will be discussed.

Improvement Approach 
Based on the studies that have been performed in this project, it is clear that the magnetic 
coupling strength is one of the main factors in the system to produce the 3rd harmonic 
impedance interference in low vertical distances. By following the Table 22: Non-Ferrite 
measurements, it can be seen that the coupling strength will significantly decrease since 
no ferrite will concentrate the produced magnetic field.  
According to this, the laboratory setup configured with less ferrite to week the magnetic 
coupling strength. Therefore some new experiments have been done in two different

7      MAIN IDEA 
The proposed idea is based on static wireless power transfer technology and the aim is 
to propose a cost benefit solution to improve the system efficiency.  
According to the experimental analyses on the WPT system, it can be seen that by 
decreasing the vertical distance, the magnetic coupling will significantly increase and the 
system is interfered by the 3rd harmonic impedance in the low distances. Therefore, one 
of the proposed solutions is to change the coil design to a smaller geometrical size that 
leads to several benefits including less prototype expenses, less power losses, less weight 
and etc.  
During the previous chapters, results of changing the vertical distance have been 
discussed, theoretically and experimentally.  
In this chapter, a solution explaining how this vertical distance variability can be achieved 
will be discussed. It can be considered as an easy and cheap solution that can be fit with 
all different types of static WPTs available in the market. 
7.1 
Idea Procedure 
The main idea was to design a parking spot for EVs that has the ability to set the 
transmitter coil automatically with the receiver coil “in the bottom of EV”. When the car 
stops in the parking base, this will make the charging process more comfortable and safe 
as shown in Figure 56. 
==PAGE:81==
 
Main Idea 
 
Page | 79  
 
 
Figure 56: Parking spot with customizable Tx Coil 
The charging base has a smart sensor in order to sense the EV’s position and set the 
inductive transmitter fit to the EV’s center point “Horizontally”. Then, the transmitter 
starts moving to the receiver point under the EV. After it fits itself as close as possible to 
the receiver, it starts moving “Vertically” to get even closer to the receiver point and 
starts charging. 
The idea raised many questions such as: 
- 
Is it cost benefit? 
- 
Is it safe enough to move high voltage cables and inductive transmitter? 
- 
As long as different companies have different standardization for their final 
products and also safety definitions, how the idea can match with all those 
differences. 
According to the mentioned questions, it seems a very problematic idea to continue with, 
and then solution is needed. 
Basically, the first idea came to my mind because that the different companies set their 
charging receiver to different places in the bottom of different EVs. The customizability to 
get as close as possible to the receiver point is also still a good idea with some problems. 
Hence a solution to that problem can be produced in a cheaper price, and installed in an 
easier way, and can be fit with all different Tx productions, as shown in Figure 57. 
==PAGE:82==
 
Main Idea 
 
Page | 80  
 
 
Figure 57: Idea on charging pattern 
The new idea has the lack of moving horizontally but it moves smart in vertical positions 
using few different sensors and an electronic control unit. One of the additional benefits 
of the new idea is that it moves the transmitter plate vertically, a mini table lifter that is 
already produced has been found very cheap with plastic material. 
Finally, following all the above discussions about the idea and its benefits, more study 
needs to be done to analyze the system behavior, when the vertical distance going to 
change. Furthermore, studying the other parameters that can affect the system efficiency 
will help the idea from one side and the system from the other side to achieve better 
results.

8      CONCLUSION 
In this project, a state of the art of WPT systems in general and the specific laboratory 
setup have been discussed. As the main purpose of this project was to study the WPT 
resonance tank, thus the circuit equivalent models have been made from different points 
of analytical process and all the related parameters are discussed and analyzed such as, 
the WPT resonance tank mode, it’s block diagram, coils configuration and it’s 
components, capacitor design, circuit topologies, coupling coefficient, safety guidelines 
and resonance tuning. 
8.1 
Safe Mode 
During the past months, over 175 experiments have been made in various positions and 
frequencies. All the results have been reported in tables and some others have been 
plotted in 2D and 3D figures to demonstrate the system behavior while the vertical 
distance is changing. 
Theoretical analyses done by different approaches in this section. Through these 
analyses, all of the parameters of the circuit including the inductors, capacitors and their 
internal resistances are measured and verified through RLC meter. The mutual 
inductances, have been calculated for different vertical distances and with different 
quality factors. Finally, two methods of efficiency analyses made for the transferred 
power of the WPT circuit, which resulted to maximum efficiency of 𝜂= 95% and for the 
resonance tank, which resulted to maximum efficiency of 𝜂= 97.36%. 
Moreover, the circuit impedance response, transfer function, input and output current, 
power and efficiency calculated. The bandwidth of the system and the minimum value for 
the quality factor, discussed. 
According to the analyses, the efficiency improvement of up to 10% achieved for the 
resonance tank, while the vertical distance decreases from 18 cm to 1 cm. Also according

8.2 
On-Load 
This chapter is made by over 50 experimental analyses to demonstrate the system 
efficiency behavior while the vertical distance is changing. 
Regarding the analyses, two parameters including of 3rd harmonic impedance (𝑍3𝑟𝑑) and 
the quality factor (Q) have been found as the major parameters that influenced through 
decreasing the vertical distance, though increasing the mutual inductance. 
According to the achieved results, it is clear that when the vertical distance decreases, 
tuning the system will become more complex due to the system magnetic coupling 
strength. This is mostly based on the system design, which has been done for the vertical 
distances of 𝑉𝐷= 10 𝑐𝑚 and above. 
Furthermore, based on the analyses, it is clear that the system is highly output dependent 
and that is due to using the simple rectifier in the output part of the system, that leads to 
inability of controlling the related output factors like quality factor. As it is shown in the 
analyses, whenever the primary quality factor (𝑄𝑃), tuned to set above two to achieve the 
non-interfered point, the secondary quality factor (𝑄𝑆), decreased and vice versa. 
In order to sum up the conclusion: 
1. Mutual Inductance: The Mutual Inductance is one of the most effective reasons to 
destroy resonance in low vertical distances. Through the Figure 46: Flowchart of 𝑍3𝑟𝑑 
interference process on 𝐼3𝑟𝑑, it can be seen that decreasing the vertical distance will 
affect the system to operate in non-resonate operation. Therefore, increasing and 
decreasing the circuit parameters will affect the system by third harmonic 
interference (𝐼3𝑟𝑑). 
The improvement suggestion is to reduce the secondary coil geometry to half or less 
size, then the self-inductance of the secondary coil decreases, hence the mutual 
inductance. However, changing the coil geometry will change all the circuit 
parameters and need the new WPT design. 
==PAGE:85==
 
Conclusion 
 
Page | 83  
 
2. Ferrite Affects: One of the studies have been done in this project was the idea of 
ferrite and aluminum effects in the system. Through the analysis and calculations 
presented in Table 22: Non-Ferrite measurements; it is clear how the ferrite 
permeability will affect the system mutual inductance. While one of the major 
problems of decreasing the vertical distance was increasing the mutual inductance 
significantly, then: 
The suggestion is to reduce the ferrite thickness. Therefore, more experiments have 
been done (in the appendix). According to Figure 53: Analyses for 𝑉𝐹𝑒𝑟= 2 𝑚𝑚 at 
𝑉𝐷= 4 𝑐𝑚 and Figure 54: Analyses for 𝑉𝐹𝑒𝑟= 4 𝑚𝑚 at 𝑉𝐷= 4 𝑐𝑚; it can be seen that 
the results are even more promising than full WPT laboratory setup in the same 
vertical distances. However, with a rough calculation through the oscilloscope 
readings, it can be seen that the efficiency will fall down about 5% to 10% due to the 
lack of ferrite to focus the magnetic field. 
3. Output Rectifier: One of the other major influences, which is increasing the system 
tuning complexity, is the output rectifier that made the system dependent to the load 
parameters variation. On the other hand, the available rectifier has no control to the 
state of the charge of the batteries, therefore, it will reduce the life time of the 
batteries. 
A good suggestion is to use the AC/DC converter bridge to support the output part of 
the system instead of the simple rectifier. However, in the cases like WPT system that 
meant to support EV batteries, which are also including a complex system, seems 
necessary. 
Therefore, based on studies the ZVS converter proposed to the future WPT designs, 
which has 15% loss reduction compare to the benchmark converters.  
Finally, based on the experimental and theoretical analysis, the main idea of improving 
the efficiency by decreasing the vertical distance seems to be not efficient with the 
present WPT setup. However, based on the analyses, the solution of decreasing the coil 
geometry to control the mutual inductance strength is promising and it can be counted as 
an achievement of this project through the main idea.

9      FUTURE WORKS 
Following the discussions, in order to improve the efficiency of the system, it is proposed 
to design the WPT system with a smaller geometrical size of the secondary coil. This will 
be one of the aims of the future designs. Basically, using such setups with a bigger 
primary and smaller secondary coils are suitable cases for the Dynamic WPT systems. 
According to the results, in the future design, several factors that have been analyzed in 
this project, have to reconsidered, including: 
1- Smaller secondary coil design; there was no more time to build the new secondary 
coil and implement more experiments 
2- Operating the system in a lower frequency will also be possible due to the minimum 
efficiency  
3- By implementing the system in low distances, it will be possible to operate the system 
in tight coupling and off-resonance, hence, less 
switching frequency 
4- A better capacitor bank design will improve the 
system efficiency and complexity; as shown in 
Figure 58. Using the Litz wire to make a simple 
connection between the capacitor’s layers is 
expensive and will behave as a complete 
uncalculated inductor in the system 
This can be fixed easily by using spacer type of 
screws which can connect each breadboard to 
the other one in the least distance  
5- In the secondary part of the system, need of a AC/DC converter to supply and control 
the output is necessary. By using the converter, it will be possible to control the SoC of 
the system that prevents the batteries from damages and less tuning complexity  
6- Using more accurate facilities to make more accurate measurements. With the 
available facilities, it was not possible to tune the system properly

11.7 Resonance Coupling  
One of the most important benefits of the resonance frequency is to get the higher power 
transfer rate in the magnetic field, when there is a limitation of connectivity.  
In the other word, the resonant circuit brings the lower impedance that leads to the 
higher current, higher power transfer and higher efficiency. 
In order to find the resonance frequency for the system, it is important to ensure that the 
chosen inductors and capacitors are synchronized to the frequency in the way which the 
lowest impedance achieves to transfer the maximum power.

11.7.1 
RLC Impedance 
Basically, in a RLC circuit, the magnetic field produced when the current flow in the 
circuit and energy will be stored in the inductor through the capacitor discharging. On the 
other hand, capacitor will be charged by the produced current through collapsing the 
magnetic field. This procedure will continuously go on and cause the production of 
magnetic field through electrical energy. 
When the reactance of the inductor and capacitor in some points of frequency meeting 
each other, i.e., in some frequencies the reactance of the capacitor and inductor will be 
equal, that it called resonance frequency. This causes the highest amount of current can 
pass through the circuit, which causes also the higher magnetic inductance production. 
The existence of the resistance in the circuit causes the damping effect that, in the period 
of time, will damp the oscillation of the magnetic field in inductor and electric field in 
capacitor. 
In the following sections, more discussion about resonance and its related phenomenon 
will be considered.
