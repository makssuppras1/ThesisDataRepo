Abstract
The goal of this thesis is to validate the sand transport module in MIKE
3 Wave Model FM for edge scour processes under the action of steady
currents. Numerical calculations in MIKE 3 Wave Model FM will be
compared to previously made experimental results in order to help val-
idate the sand transport module in MIKE 3 Wave Model FM by using
hidden features that has not yet been launched in the guided user inter-
face.
The ямВow is resolved in incompressible Reynolds-Averaged Navier-Stokes
equations coupled with a k тИТ╬╡ turbulence model. Extensive analyses of
the horizontal mesh and vertical layers, roughness of the surface and the
speedup factor have been made.
Results showed that when dealing with a rectangular scour protection
the moving sediments were governed by the primary ямВow and the sec-
ondary ямВow that stirred the sediments into the main ямВow and away from
the stone layer into the sand pit, respectively. The secondary current
showed to change direction frequently. The ямВow pattern was mostly pro-
nounced by using a porous zone interpreting the scour protection instead
of changing the bathymetry.
Simulating ямВow around circular scour protections, neglecting the monopile
installed on top of it, showed that edge scour occurred at the transverse
sides of the scour protection. Although some features and sub results
matched well, the majority of the simulated results did not match per-
fectly with the experimental results.
Key words: Edge scour, sand transport, MIKE 3 Wave Model FM,
CFD

Introduction
OямАshore wind farms can produce more (and more stable) wind energy than wind
farms onshore because the wind is steadier and stronger oямАshore rather than on-
shore. OямАshore wind farms do, however, require more eямАort in the construction
of the wind turbines, since this is often done at approximately 60 m water depth.
Asgarpour (2016) states that approximately 90 % of oямАshore wind turbines today
are installed on monopiles used as foundations.
Installing a monopile at the seabed, erosion of sand will happen in the vicinity of
the structure. This phenomena is called scour and must always be taken into con-
sideration. Scour will always occur to some extend. The depth of the scour hole is
a key parameter for a designer to outline the design of the structure.
A potential scour protection will be considered in the design of a structure like this.
A scour protection is a one or two layer structure composed of rocks surrounding
the structure to protect it against sand erosion, and hence decreasing stability. The
size of the rocks is chosen so that the ямВow conditions are not able to wash them
away. The scour protection is shaped like a doughnut when looking at cylindrical
monopiles. Scour will still occur but will move further away from the structure to
the edge of the stone layer leading to the term edge scour.
The long term modelling of edge scour around scour protections is highly valuable
so the eямАects of the scour protections can be assessed.

Three types of sediment transport exist, namely bed load, suspended load and wash

As mentioned is the bed load characterised as the sediments moving in more or

bed. Einstein (1950) did the ямБrst attempt of predicting the bed load transport in

the diameter of the grain. The outcome from this was that if the instantaneous
agitating forces (horizontal drag and lift forces) were greater than the stabilising
forces (weight of grains), the particle would jump or roll downstream until a place
where the stabilising forces is greater than the agitating forces. EinsteinтАЩs method
has showed that assumptions made has not been truly correct as they do not resemble
real situations well. Thus, the methods have been developing since.

4.2.3
Time integration
The numerical solution for the transport equations can either be done in a ямБrst
order, explicit Euler method, or in a second order, explicit Runge-Kutta scheme.
The Runge-Kutta scheme is also referred to as the midpoint method. This method
uses the tangent line at the midpoint instead of the current point to go to the next
point.
The second order scheme is computationally heavier than the ямБrst order
scheme (will double the computation time). The Euler method is more accurate if
the time step size is small as the error on each time step is proportional to the step
size to a power of 0.5 (planetcalc 2021).

Conclusion
The goal of this thesis was to validate MIKE 3 Wave Model FM to model edge scour
processes around scour protections. Conclusions are made based on simulated case
studies. These cases were modelled in case-speciямБc settings making the validation
of MIKE 3 Wave Model FM narrowed to these setups.
Discrepancies were generally shown. Case studies showed diямАerent results in varying

The generation of ripples and the calculation of the friction velocities using the

Several parameters were tested and especially the horizontal mesh grid cells, to-

Approaching the equilibrium scour depth the development in the scour hole was

7. CONCLUSION
Case study 3 showed that by using a porous zone resembling the scour protection
the simulation behaved similar to the experimentally obtained results used for com-
parison. A complex ямВow is developed in this case and corner ямВow and secondary
ямВow patterns were observed in conjunction with Petersen (2014).
The magnitude of the scour hole changed signiямБcantly using diямАerent approaches
for modelling the scour protection. These two test cases did, however, have diямАerent
assumptions and the same output was therefore not expected. The ямВow pattern de-
scribed was mostly pronounced when using the porous zone as the scour protection.
This signiямБcant change might be due to the turbulence model used in the calcula-
tions not capable of modelling the secondary ямВow and the roughness anisotropy.
Case study 4 showed that MIKE 3 Wave Model FM was not able to recreate the
same edge scour process obtained in the experimental results in the same period
of time. Edge scour on the transverse sides of the scour protection did occur to
some extend, but only at a small location on each transverse side. The diямАerence
in the comparison must, however, be kept in mind as the simulation was conducted
without the monopile installed on top of the scour protection.
It is therefore concluded that MIKE 3 Wave Model FM is not (yet) able to simulate
edge scour processes fully around a toe berm or around a cylindrical scour protection
for these types of setups. However, several tendencies and observations did show
relatively good ямБts with experimental results and known theory.

Further work
The work done in this thesis can be used as a starting condition for further testing
and validating of MIKE 3 Wave Model FM. Several parameters (including hidden
features) were tested along with the simulations in the diямАerent cases.
It could be enlightening to see how the eямАect from the back wall changes with in-

For modelling scour protections as a porous zone it could be interesting to test

The monopile was left out of the simulations in case study 4 for simplicity. This
could then be included in the simulation to determine if there is an eямАect and to
what extend the monopile aямАects the edge scour process on the transverse sides of
the scour protection. An improvement of the mesh could be tested further.
