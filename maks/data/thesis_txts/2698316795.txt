Abstract 
TNF is one of the most important and well-studied cytokines. It is currently believed that binding of 
TNF to its primary receptor TNFR1, can cause cell death by apoptosis or necroptosis. The cell 
death is believed to be inflammatory, leading to the occurrence and persistence of inflammatory 
diseases.  
Much of the knowledge on the TNF signaling pathway has been acquired from experiments on 
immortalized cell lines. However, early findings in my supervisors' lab suggested that in at least 
some primary cell types, TNF is unable to induce cell death, contradicting its currently believed role 
in inducing inflammation through cell death. 
The overall aim of the study was to investigate several primary cells for their ability to induce TNF-
mediated cell death. This was done by examining cell survival when the cells were challenged with 
TNF and checkpoint inhibitors by MTT reduction assay and flow cytometry. Furthermore, there was 
an attempt to establish the proteins in the TNF pathway responsible for the observed phenotype 
with western blots. 
Interestingly, the results showed that TNF is not able to induce cell death in the primary cell types 
chosen. Additionally, the western blots showed that most of the important proteins participating in 
the TNF pathway behaved as expected. 
The lack of sensitivity to TNF-induced cell death in primary cells presented in this thesis, contradict 
the currently established models of TNF signaling. If the lack of sensitivity to TNF-induced cell 
death is shown to apply to more primary cell types implicated in inflammatory diseases, the current 
models will have to be modified, marking a major shift in our understanding of these diseases.

Introduction 
Autoimmune diseases and cancer are two of the most serious health problems that we are facing 
in our society today. The National Institutes for Health has estimated that autoimmune diseases 
affect approximately 5% of the U.S. population, while approximately 39.5% of the population in the 
USA will be diagnosed with cancer in their lifetime1 2. In addition to the humanitarian cost of 
reduced life quality and mortality from these diseases, it is estimated that the cost of healthcare 
just for cancer, is 209 billion dollars annually3.  
Dysfunction in the immune system is the cause of autoimmune diseases such as rheumatoid 
arthritis and inflammatory bowel disease, which are disorders where the immune system attacks 
itself. Commonly drugs which specifically bind and inhibit the cytokine TNF, such as the chimeric 
monoclonal antibody Infliximab, are used to alleviate the excessive immune response, 
representing a 25 billion dollars industry4.  
Furthermore, the importance of the immune system in cancer has been gaining increasing 
attention in the last decade, both in the causation and treatment. The tumor microenvironment 
contains several mechanisms by which the tumor cells avoid being recognized and killed by 
immune cells. Additionally, treatment of cancer has seen major breakthroughs in the last decade 
by using the body’s immune system to target cancer cells, examples being therapy with checkpoint 
inhibitors and immunotherapy.  
The detrimental effect of these diseases makes research into the immune system very important. 
However, much knowledge in the field of Immunology is gained by experiments on immortalized in 
vitro cell lines and genetically engineered mice. This thesis proposes that TNF, one of the most 
abundant and extensively studied cytokines, does not behave the same way in these laboratory 
models as in some types of primary cells. 
 
Advantages and disadvantages of immortalized cell lines and primary cells 
Immortalized cell lines are commonly used as a model for human tissue instead of human primary 
cells in scientific research. Immortalized cell lines are chosen as they are easier to work with, often 
having multiple targeted mutations or transgene insertions, adding useful functions to the cells. 
These mutations and transgene insertions could for example immortalize them and make them 
resistant to certain antibiotics, making immortalized cell lines easier to maintain as cell culture than 
human primary cells. Primary cells have not been immortalized which makes them have a limited 
==PAGE:7==
6 
 
life span, as they after a certain amount of cell divisions undergo senescence. Finally, immortalized 
cell lines are cheaper to work with as primary cells have to be obtained from tissue, which then has 
to be further processed to establish a cell culture.  
The immortalized cell lines occasionally originate from other species than humans making inter-
species differences a concern. Furthermore, the mutations introduced can have unforeseen 
consequences in their signaling pathways, and thus immortalized cell lines are not completely 
representative of what happens in vivo. Whereas human primary cells more closely resemble the 
tissue of interest, making human primary cells a better model for what occurs in vivo. 
TNF 
Tumor necrosis factor (TNF) is a very important proinflammatory cytokine that is secreted from a 
wide variety of cells but primarily immune cells such as macrophages, T cells and natural killer 
cells when activated by contact with e.g. pathogens5. TNF exists in two forms, transmembrane 
bound TNF and secreted TNF, the latter is created when membrane-bound TNF is proteolytically 
cleaved. The secreted form of TNF is a small 17 kDa protein, which forms stable homotrimers in 
solution6. 
TNF regulates diverse cell functions including acting as the master regulator in the inflammatory 
response. TNF is a ligand for two receptors TNF receptor 1 (TNFR1) and TNFR2. TNFR1 is 
present in almost all cell types in the body, while TNFR2 is only expressed in a few cells including 
immune cells, endothelial cells and neural cells7. TNFR1-TNF interaction can induce cell survival, 
apoptosis or necroptosis while TNFR2-TNF interaction primarily promotes cell survival through the 
non-canonical NF-κB pathway in addition to aiding in the maturation of immune cells8.

The canonical NF-κB pathway 
 
Figure 1: The canonical NF-κB pathway by TNFR1 stimulation  
Upon stimulation of TNFR1 by TNF RIPK1, TRADD, TRAF2/5 and cIAP 1/2 are recruited into 
complex I. cIAP 1/2 K63 ubiquitinates several subunits enabling recruitment of LUBAC, which 
forms M1-linked ubiquitin chains on the complex. The TAB-TAK and IKK complexes are now 
recruited, TAK1 phosphorylates IKKβ which in turn phosphorylates IκBα. NF-κB translocates to the 
nucleus where it helps the transcription of pro-survival and inflammatory genes. 
 
TNF-TNFR1 results in the formation of large multi-protein signaling complexes. Stimulation of 
TNFR1 can lead to two major outcomes, proinflammatory survival signaling or cell death, 
depending on which signaling complexes are formed. The most common result of TNF-TNFR1 
interaction is activation of the proinflammatory survival canonical NF-κB pathway.  
 
The canonical NF-κB pathway starts when soluble TNF binds to TNFR1, inducing trimerization of 
the receptors, leading to a conformational change in the cytoplasmic side of the receptor. The 
cytoplasmic side of the receptor is now able to recruit tumor necrosis factor receptor type 1-
associated DEATH domain protein (TRADD) and receptor-interacting serine/threonine-protein 
==PAGE:9==
8 
 
kinase 1 (RIPK1), which subsequently bind the proteins TRAF2/TRAF59. Hereafter, the ubiquitin 
ligases inhibitor of apoptosis I and II (cIAP 1 and cIAP2) are recruited leading to K11-, K38- and 
K63-linked ubiquitination of RIPK1 and themselves (figure 1). The K63-ubiquitination of RIPK1 
allows the ubiquitin ligase linear ubiquitin chain assembly complex (LUBAC) to attach to complex 
I10. LUBAC which is composed of the proteins HOIP, HOIL-1 and SHARPIN initiates M1-linked 
(linear) polyubiquitination of several proteins in complex I, in some instances creating K63/M1 
hybrid chains11. The ubiquitination of complex I functions as a scaffold allowing the TAB-TAK and 
IKK complexes to attach. The TAB-TAK complex gets recruited by TAB2/3 binding to K63-linked 
ubiquitin chains followed by recruitment of the kinase Transforming growth factor-β activated 
kinase 1 (TAK1)12. Meanwhile, the adaptor protein NEMO is recruited to the M1-linked ubiquitin 
chains followed by IκB Kinase 1 (IKKα) and IκB Kinase 2 (IKKβ), the three subunits together make 
up the IKK complex13. One study suggests the ubiquitination structure of complex I allows TAK1 to 
be located in close proximity to the IKKβ subunit from the IKK complex enable it to phosphorylate 
and activate it11. The activated IKK complex phosphorylates Inhibitor kappa B-alpha (IκB-α) which 
masks the nuclear localization signal of the transcription factor nuclear factor kappa B (NF-ĸB), 
targeting it for degradation by the proteasome thus unmasking NF-κBs nuclear localization signal 
(figure 1)14. The NF-ĸB primarily in the form of the p50/65 heterodimer then translocates to the 
nucleus where it enhances the transcription of several proinflammatory and pro-survival genes 
(figure 1)15.

Complex II mediated cell death 
 
 
Figure 2: Apoptotic and necroptotic pathway by TNFR1 signaling. 
RIPK1 can dissociate from complex I and bind to FADD and caspase-8 forming complex II. If 
caspase-8 is not inhibited it will activate executor caspases-3 and -7, leading to apoptosis. If 
caspase-8 is inhibited RIPK1 autophosphorylate and binds RIPK3 forming the necrosome. RIPK3 
autophosphorylates and then phosphorylates MLKL, which translocate to the cell membrane and 
induces necroptosis. 
While complex I begins assembly immediately after TNF-TNFR1 interaction, the formation of a 
second complex known as complex II begins several hours later16. In contrast to complex I, 
complex II is not bound to a cell membrane but is a cytosolic complex. Complex II is capable of 
inducing cell death through either apoptosis or necroptosis17. 
The complex II assembly starts with RIPK1 dissociating from complex I and binding to Fas-
associated death domain protein (FADD) and the master regulator of cell death pathways caspase-
8 (figure 2)18. The apoptosis pathway is initiated as caspase-8 binds to Fas-associated death 
domain protein (FADD), which activates it. As caspase-8 is an initiator caspase, the activation 
==PAGE:11==
10 
 
enables it to further activate executor caspases, including caspase-3 and caspase-7, which 
degrade many cellular components leading to cell death by apoptosis (figure 2)19. 
As caspase-8 is activated it cleaves and inactivates RIPK1, which is essentiel for the necroptotic 
pathway, thus inhibition of caspase-8 is needed for necroptosis20 21. Upon inactivation of caspase-
8, the kinase RIPK1 can autophosphorylate its serine residues 14/15, 20, 161 and 16622. The 
autophosphorylation leads to a conformational change, which enables it to associate with RIPK3 
and FADD, forming a complex known as the necrosome23. The necrosome allows RIPK3 to 
autophosphorylate and then further phosphorylate mixed lineage kinase domain like pseudokinase 
(MLKL). The activation of MLKL leads to oligomerization followed by translocation to the cell 
membrane where it induces pore formation resulting in death of the cell by necroptosis24.  
Regulation of signaling by ubiquitination 
Ubiquitin is a small 8.6 kDa protein consisting of 76 amino acid residues, which can be added to a 
substrate protein in a post-translational process known as ubiquitination. Ubiquitination of a protein 
can consist of either a single ubiquitin molecule (monoubiquitination) or several ubiquitin molecules 
(polyubiquitination). Polyubiquitination creates ubiquitin chains where one ubiquitin is conjugated to 
either the N-terminus (M1) of another ubiquitin or one of its seven lysine (K) residues. In total, eight 
types of polyubiquitin chains exist, named after their linkage point, they are known as M1, K6, K11, 
K27, K29, K33, K48 or K63 ubiquitination, respectively25.  
The type of polyubiquitination is important for the effect it has on the targeted protein and 
determines its fate. Ubiquitination is essential for maintaining many important cellular processes 
including apoptotic signaling, DNA transcription and the inflammation response, thus proper 
regulation of this process is essential for maintaining tissue homeostasis26. 
Ubiquitination plays an essential role in regulating the TNF-TNFR1 pathway. The ubiquitin chains 
function as platforms for recruitment of the subunits participating in the pathway. The polyubiquitin 
chains assembled during TNF-TNFR1 stimulation include K11, K48, K63 and M1 linked 
ubiquitination27. The homeostasis in ubiquitination levels is achieved by having E3 ligases adding 
ubiquitin chains while deubiquitinating enzymes (DUBs) are removing them. 
M1-linked ubiquitination 
M1-linked ubiquitination of proteins can occur during activation of several different receptors, 
including TNFR1, nucleotide-binding and oligomerization domain-containing complex (NOD2) and 
toll-like receptor (TLR)28. Regulation of M1-linked ubiquitin chains, also known as linear ubiquitin 
chains, is important in the TNF-TNFR1 pathway for maintaining proper inflammatory response and 
preventing infection29. The attachment of the M1-linked ubiquitin chains is mediated by the E3 
==PAGE:12==
11 
 
ligase LUBAC and the DUB OTULIN30.  LUBAC is composed of two regulatory subunits HOIL-1 
and SHARPIN, in addition to the catalytic subunit HOIP. LUBAC ubiquitinates several of the 
subunits involved in TNF-TNFR1 signaling including RIPK1, NEMO and cFLIP31. The DUB 
OTULIN negatively regulates M1-linked chains and is very specific, exclusively cleaving M1-linked 
chains28. To mediate its DUB function, OTULIN binds to the PUB domain of the LUBAC subunit 
HOIP. OTULIN deubiquitinates the proteins that have been linearly ubiquitinated by LUBAC, of 
particular importance is its ability to deubiquitinate RIPK1. However, interestingly OTULIN also 
promotes rather than diminishes the activity of LUBAC, as it can prevent it from auto-
ubiquitinating32.  
Dysfunction in the regulation of M1-linked ubiquitin signaling causes severe inflammatory disease 
in humans33. Particularly, people lacking OTULIN develop the inflammatory disease OTULIN-
related autoinflammatory syndrome (ORAS)34. As deficiency in OTULIN levels leads to 
accumulation of M1-linked ubiquitin chains on several of the TNFR1 pathways proteins, increasing 
NF-κB activation upon stimulation of TNFR1. 
 
Checkpoints in TNF-TNFR1 signaling 
The TNF-TNFR1 pathway is often targeted by pathogens in an effort to limit the inflammatory 
response and thereby evade detection and killing by the immune system. As a defense 
mechanism, the cells are safeguarded by several checkpoints, which check the integrity of the TNF 
pathway. Tampering with the pathway removes critical survival checkpoints leading to cell death, 
ultimately protecting the host. Meanwhile, normal activation of the TNF-TNFR1 pathway leads to 
proinflammatory and cell survival signaling. 
The checkpoints are tightly regulated and can be divided into three categories, the ubiquitination 
checkpoint, the phosphorylation checkpoint and the transcription checkpoint. 
The ubiquitination checkpoint 
Proper ubiquitination of complex I is important for cell survival upon TNF stimulation. The absence 
of either the linear ubiquitinating protein HOIP or K63 linked ubiquitinating proteins cIAP 1/2 
sensitizes cells to cell death upon TNF stimulation35. Ubiquitination is important for the recruitment 
of TAK1 which is responsible for pro-survival signaling through several pathways36. Additionally, 
RIPK1 is a target for several types of ubiquitination. The ubiquitylation forces RIPK1 to be 
integrated in complex I instead of dissociating and starting the assembly of complex II, which 
ultimately results in cell death36. Thus, the ubiquitin-dependent checkpoint can be activated as a 
result of lacking ubiquitination by cIAP1/2 and LUBAC. 
==PAGE:13==
12 
 
The phosphorylation checkpoint  
The phosphorylation checkpoint is mediated by several kinases with TAK1 being of particular 
importance. TAK1 is responsible for several types of pro-survival signaling, making inhibition of this 
molecule detrimental to the health of the cells if combined with TNF administration37. One pro 
survival function of TAK1, is its ability to activate the kinases IKK and MK238. MK2 in particular is 
able to induce phosphorylation of the serine residue 321 of RIPK1, inhibiting it from binding FADD 
and caspase-8, preventing the formation of complex II and thus the apoptotic and necroptotic 
pathway39. 
The transcription checkpoint 
The predominant response to TNFR1 stimulation is an activation of the canonical NF-κB pathway, 
leading to transcription of proinflammatory and pro-survival genes. Some proteins transcribed due 
to NF-κB activation promote cell survival including FADD-like interleukin-1β–converting enzyme 
(FLICE)-inhibitory protein (cFLIP), which is a central anti-apoptotic regulator40. cFLIP is able to 
form heterodimers with caspase-8 forming an apoptosis inhibitory complex (AIC). The AIC inhibits 
the apoptosis functions of caspase-8 but enables it to cleave CYLD, RIPK1 and RIPK3 preventing 
activation of the caspase cascade and formation of the necrosome41. Thus, if the activation of the 
NF-κB pathway is prevented it leads to the death of the cell, while functional activation promotes 
cell survival 
Induction of cell death in experiments 
In an effort to activate caspases to induce apoptosis in cancer cells, small molecules that bind 
cIAPs and block their inhibition of caspase-3 can be administered42. These compounds are known 
as SMAC mimetics and are based on the natural, endogenous cIAP inhibitor second mitochondria-
derived activator of caspases (SMAC). SMAC is a mitochondrial protein that binds to inhibitor of 
apoptosis proteins (IAPs) and either directly inhibits them or in the case of cIAP1/2 induces k48-
autoubiquitination targeting them for proteasomal degradation43. 
cIAPs promote the pro-survival pathways NF-κB and MAPK. cIAPs have also been shown to bind 
and inhibit caspase-3, which has an executioner role in apoptosis and limit its activity by preventing 
it from fully maturating44. The degradation of the cIAP 1/2 resulting from administering SMAC 
mimetics induces the non-canonical NF-κB pathway, leading to the cell secreting TNF43. The 
secreted TNF can activate the TNFR1 receptor of itself or nearby cells, by autocrine or paracrine 
signaling. Due to the lack of ubiquitination from the degradation of cIAP 1/2, the ubiquitin 
dependent checkpoint is activated upon TNF signaling, leading to formation of complex II and 
apoptotic cell death. 
==PAGE:14==
13 
 
Pan-caspase inhibitors as their name suggest inhibit the functionality of caspases. The pan-
caspase inhibitor Z-VAD-FMK inhibits the human caspases 1-10 with caspases-2 being the 
exemption, by irreversible binding to their active site. Z-VAD-FMKs ability to inhibit caspase-8 
prevents it from cleaving RIPK1 which is essential for TNF-induced necroptosis. Pre-treatment with 
SMAC mimetics and a pan-caspase inhibitor before stimulation with TNF is a commonly used 
protocol to induce TNF-mediated necroptosis in cells45. 
 
Aim of study 
The overall aim of the study was to investigate the ability of TNF to induce cell death in an 
immortalized cell line and several primary cells. An additional aim was to investigate the proteins in 
the TNF signaling pathway and their implications in the observed differences in sensitivity to TNF-
induced death. 
 
Materials and Methods 
Cell culture 
The primary human fibroblasts from the ORAS patient and the control were cultured in Ham's F-10 
Nutrient Mix + GlutaMAX™ (Thermo Ficher scientific), the medium was supplemented with 20% 
Fetal Bovine Serum (FBS) (Biowest) (v/v) and Penicillin-Streptomycin solution (Biowest) at 37°C in 
5% CO2. 
The mouse embryonic fibroblasts (MEF) were cultured in Dulbecco's Modified Eagle Medium 
(DMEM) (Thermo Fischer Scientific) containing 4.5g/L D-Glucose, no glutamine and no phenol 
red. The media was supplemented with 10% FBS (Biowest) (v/v), 2mM glutaMAX™ – I (Life 
Technologies), 1mM sodium pyruvate solution (Sigma-Aldrich) and Penicillin-Streptomycin solution 
(BIOWEST) at 37°C in 5% CO2. 
For the purification of splenocytes protocol, RPMI-medium 1640 (Life Technologies) was used. The 
media was supplemented with 10% FBS (Biowest), 20mM HEPES buffer (Fisher bioreagents), 
50µM 2-mercaptoethanol (Life Technologies), 2mM glutaMAX™ – I (Life Technologies) and 
pencilin-streptomycin solution (Biowest). 
Cell lines 
The immortalized MEF originates from E13.5 Cre-ERT2-Otulin-/flox mice embryos34. The MEFs 
contained a floxed OTULIN gene and were cultured in media containing 4-hydrotamoxifen (100nM, 
Sigma-Aldrich) for 7 days. The 4-hydrotamoxifen treatment caused the OTULIN gene to be deleted 
through Cre-Lox recombination.

Statistical analysis 
The statistical analysis of the data on cell survival in the MTT reduction assay experiments was 
conducted with two-tailed t-tests. The two-tailed t-tests were used over the one-tailed t-test to 
ensure analysis for both the possibility of increased and decreased survival levels between the 
data sets. The significance level was set to p>0.05, meaning if the p-value was less than 0.05 the 
differences in survival was considered statistically significant. The statistical test was performed in 
Microsoft Excel using the T.TEST function.

Results 
The results are composed of two major sections; the first part is dedicated to testing the hypothesis 
that certain primary cells respond differently to TNF compared to immortalized cell lines. The 
second part will attempt to establish the proteins in the TNF mediated pathway responsible for the 
observed lack of TNF sensitivity in primary cells. 
TNF sensitivity in primary cells and immortalized cell lines 
The following section explores the sensitivity to TNF-induced cell death in several types of primary 
cells and an immortalized cell line by examining the survival levels with MTT assays and flow 
cytometry when challenged with TNF and checkpoint inhibitors.

Discussion 
The results show that there are exceptions to the established rules concerning TNF in certain 
primary cells, such as lymphocytes and human fibroblasts. According to my knowledge, the 
difference in TNF sensitivity between primary cells and immortalized cell lines has not been 
described in the literature previously. This knowledge is very interesting as TNF is one of the most 
influential molecules in the immunological response and is targeted with inhibitors for treating 
several severe inflammatory diseases. Much research in TNF signaling is performed in 
immortalized cell lines and currently it is believed that TNF-induced cell death is causing the 
inflammation responsible for several inflammatory diseases. However, if the lack of sensitivity to 
TNF-induced cell death is shown to apply to more primary cell types implicated in inflammatory 
diseases the current models would have to be modified, marking a major shift in our understanding 
of these diseases. 
OTULIN deficiency and its implications in TNF sensitivity  
The lack of sensitivity to TNF induced death in the ORAS patient cells from primary human 
fibroblasts was stunning due to their lack of OTULIN. The subsequent experiment in immortalized 
MEF cells indicated that OTULIN deficiency at least in some immortalized cell lines does lead to 
increased TNF sensitivity. We know that the increased sensitivity to TNF when OTULIN is deficient 
is not isolated to immortalized cell lines as it has been reported that OTULIN deficiency in primary 
myeloid cells leads to increased sensitivity to TNF-induced death46. 
It would be interesting to further explore the reasoning for why the lack of OTULIN and the 
resulting excessive M1-linked ubiquitination does not trigger the ubiquitin dependent checkpoint as 
the current model suggests it should. 
One possibility is that the lack of TNF sensitivity is a result of selection, as the cells that were 
sensitive to TNF induced death died when exposed to the TNF present in the body, while the cells 
that were not sensitive got to proliferate. 
==PAGE:34==
33 
 
However, this explanation does not explain the cellular mechanisms responsible for the lack of 
sensitivity in OTULIN deficient cells. 
B and T cells in some instances do not express TNFR1 
In breast tumor-draining lymph nodes (TDLN) it has been observed that the B and T cell 
populations' expression of TNFR1 receptors is nearly absent while they do express TNFR249.  
The environment of the TDLN is vastly different from the spleen and the lymphocytes may have 
been affected by the tumor microenvironment. However, if TNFR1 is not expressed in the 
lymphocytes from the spleen while TNFR2 is, it would explain their insensitivity to TNF-induced 
death making it worth investigating in the future. 
Validity of experiments 
To assess the difference in TNF response between primary cells and immortalized cell lines we 
compared primary fibroblasts from a human source to an immortalized fibroblast cell line 
originating from mice. As both cell types originate from mammals they share 97.5% of their DNA, 
meaning the genetic distance is close, also the two species immune system is remarkably 
similar50. The similarity makes the comparison performed valid, however it would have been 
optimal to compare either cell lines from humans to primary cells from humans or the same for 
mice. This would ensure the differences observed are due to whether the cells are primary and not 
caused by differences in the TNF pathway as a result of being different species. 
Shortcomings of the experiments performed for this thesis include the limited scope.  Two types of 
primary cells and a single immortalized cell line were investigated for their sensitivity to TNF. On 
this limited data it cannot be established if the lack of sensitivity to TNF observed is representative 
of all primary cell types or not.  
Future perspectives 
The research into the differences between primary cells and immortalized cell lines in the TNF 
mediated signaling pathway had a limited scope and only examined a few of the most important 
proteins in the pathway.  The TNF signaling pathway is very complicated with many proteins 
participating and affecting it. Broadening the scope and investigating the presence or absence of 
more of these proteins in the primary cells would be interesting. Additionally, the experiments 
performed so far have been done to qualitatively determine if the proteins were present or not. In 
future research it would be interesting to quantitatively examine the level of proteins in primary 
cells and compare it to immortalized cell lines to see if any differences could explain the observed 
phenotypes. 
==PAGE:35==
34 
 
Quantitative real-time PCR (qPCR) is a method which allows quantitative determination of the 
levels of a specific protein in cells. In contrast to traditional PCR which only measures the amount 
of protein at the end, qPCR enables monitoring the amount of protein in real-time. Making qPCR a 
viable method for exploring the concentration of important proteins in the TNF pathway. 
Conclusion 
This thesis aimed to investigate whether TNF is able to induce death in certain primary cell types.  
Based on the results presented in this thesis, it can be concluded that there is a lack of sensitivity 
to TNF induced death in some primary cell types. These results are contrary to what is currently 
established in the scientific literature. As TNF is a very important cytokine combined with the fact 
TNF inhibitors are a 25-billion-dollar industry, this discovery could be an important step in better 
understanding of the mechanisms responsible for the occurrence and persistence of inflammatory 
diseases. 
Furthermore, the presence of major proteins in the TNF pathway who might be responsible for the 
observed lack of sensitivity to TNF induced death was investigated. The results showed in one at 
least one primary cell type when challenged with TNF, the major proteins in the TNF pathway 
behaved as expected.  
 
Further research is needed to better understand the implications of these results. This could take 
the form of investigating more primary cell types, to determine whether this is isolated to certain 
types, or it is a more general trait. Finally, it would be interesting to investigate the specific reasons 
what the molecular reasons is for the discrepancy in TNF sensitivity between primary cells and 
immortalized cell lines.
