Abstract 
 
Communication technologies had evolved from the basic telegraph to telephone to the
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
current internet. Evolution of Internet has made communication easier and more
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
effective. The early stages of internet communication was internet of information like
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
sharing information through search engines like Google. Gradually it evolved into
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
communication among people through the social networking platforms like Facebook.
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
And now the latest evolution is Internet of Things(IoT) which means different devices (like
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
sensors, microcontrollers, mobile phones, computers etc) connected using internet to
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
share information and communicate among themselves. Devices of IoT can sense,
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
compute and act intelligently. Therefore IoT can be applied to different sectors like
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Business, Health, Security,Agriculture etc., for smart work. 
 
This Thesis work is about applying IoT to Rice cultivation. Rice fields need to be flooded
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
with 5 to 25 cm deep water during the entire lifecycle of the crop depending on growing
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
conditions in different stages of growth. In the process of flooding the fields , most of the
  
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
times the water is overfilled or wasted by overflowing. To avoid this, a control system is
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
  
developed in this project using IoT which can sense the water level in the fields and
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
inform the user when required level is reached so the user can control the water pump. 
 
The unique feature of this system is that it uses GPRS mobile network for internet
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
connection so that it works even in the places where WiFi or Ethernet is not available
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
since agriculture lands are usually located out of the city area, the infrastructure for the
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
internet may not be always available at the fields. This product is cost effective, easy to
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
use since the user does not require any knowledge of internet. This innovation is a step
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
ahead of existing mobile and web applications. It encourages the other side of
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
application developments based on mobile network.

1. Introduction  
 
Water is the most valuable resource for human life and need to be used very carefully.
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Improper usage leads to wastage of water which may in turn lead to scarcity of water
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
and cause serious problems to the future generations. Apart from the domestic usage,
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
water is also needed for industrial , irrigation , commercial purposes etc. of these, about
  
 
 
 
  
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
70 percent of the freshwater on the planet is used by agriculture. Most of the farming is
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
done by flood irrigation methods where the fields are flooded with water. Rice is one
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
such crop which is flooded with water in all stages from planting to harvesting.Rice
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
cultivation requires the fields to be filled with water to some level everyday. So the
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
farmers usually turn on the water source to fill the field and leave it unattended for some
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
time. In this process, most of the times water is over filled and wasted or some times
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
there may be less water than required. If water overflows, it takes some of the nutrients
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
along which may in turn lead to less productivity. To avoid this problem, a control system
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
is designed in this project using IoT. 
 
This system is designed to control overflowing and wastage of water by automating the
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
watering system so the farmer knows when the required water level is reached and can
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
control the water pumps without being physically present at the location. 
 
 
Figure 1.1 Rice fields 
 
The main idea of this project is to design a simple, easy to use and affordable product
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
for controlling the watering system in agriculture. The main users of this product will be
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
the farmers and since farmers are usually not much educated technically( in most parts
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
of the world), this product is designed to be controlled with the simple mobile phone so

any external Wifi or Ethernet infrastructure.

preparation of land to harvesting, irrigation methods and calculations about nutrient
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
loss in case of over flooding. 
 
Though most of the information is common for rice cultivation anywhere, it is based on
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
conditions in India.

world. It is feeding of world population. Rice is an important food in many parts of the

2.3. Requirements for Rice Cultivation 
Water is the first and most important requirement for cultivating Rice. Around 2,000 -
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
3,000 liters of water is needed to produce one kilo of rice. Warm and humid climate with
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
average temperatures of around 15 -27 degree celsius and surplus rainfall are most
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
suitable for rice growth. Rice fields are flooded with water from the preparation stage to
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
harvesting . Water helps balancing temperatures and avoids growing of weeds. 
  
Rain water, rivers, ponds, canals, and underground water are the different sources of
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
water for Rice cultivation. If the fields are close to rivers,ponds and canals , water is
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
  
directed from the source. For those at a certain distance and those above the sources
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
like rivers, canals or ponds, a water motor is used to pump water. If no such water
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
source is accessible, the underground water from wells or borewells is pumped using
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
water motor pumps.

2.4. Stages of Rice Cultivation  
2.4.1. Preparation 
1. Land preparation 
 
Land preparation is important for the rice fields before planting because it helps to
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
control the weeds, recycle plant nutrients and makes the soil soft and suitable for
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
planting. Preparation starts 3-4 weeks before planting or seeding by clearing the
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
remains of previous crop, plowing, adding manures and fertilizers and keeping the
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
surface covered with water. 
  
 
 
 Figure 2.2  Land flooded for preparation 
 
Land preparation starts with plowing dry land and sometimes it is also done on wet land
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
to make the job easy. The land is plowed least 15 cm(up to 20 cm)deep to make sure it
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
is free from grass, straw and any organisms that can be toxic for seedlings. Plowing
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
mixes up, turns and loosens the soil. Plowing is followed by harrowing . Harrowing
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
breaks the soil clods into smaller mass and levels the soil to prevent uneven irrigation. It
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
is done 3 times. First time, 5 days after plowing, second harrowing is done 7 days after
 
  
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
  
  
 
 
the first harrowing and the third harrowing is done 7 days later. The field is flooded with
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
  
 
 
 
  
 
 
2-3 cm water during the entire process of ploughing and harrowing. And finally levelling
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
is done to make it easy for plantation.The field is now ready for planting. 
 
2. Seed preparation 
 
There are 2 different practices in planting the rice - direct seeding method and
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
transplantation. In the direct seeding method, the pre-germinated seeds are sowed
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
directly in the soil. For transplantation,the seedlings are prepared in nursery. Most of the
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
times it can be in the field itself. For raising seedlings in the field, a small portion of land
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
  
 
 
 
 
is prepared first instead of whole field and pre-germinated seeds are sown(spread) in it.
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
12 
==PAGE:14==
 
This land is flooded with water after 2-3 days and remained flooded until the seedlings
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
are ready (about 20-25 days). 
 
Germination​:  
The seeds are first soaked in water for 24 hours and then incubated for another 24 - 48
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
hours to germinate. 
2.4.2. Planting 
There are two different methods  of planting practiced  
 
1. Transplanting  
 2. Direct seeding 
 
1. Transplantation 
 
Transplantation is the most popular method of planting in India. In this method, the
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
seedlings are planted in the rice field manually or by machines (rice transplanter). The
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
seedlings are pulled from the nursery and 2-3 seedlings are planted into the soil at
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
1.5cm depth with uniform spacing of 20cm X 20cm approximately. 
 
Manual transplanting is suitable for places where surplus labour is available whereas
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
machine transplantation requires less labour and time. Transplantation ensures proper
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
root anchoring since seedlings are directly planted in the soil and avoids weed growth in
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
  
the early stages of crop since the field is flooded with water during entire process. This
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
method requires 35 -65 kilos of seed per hectare.

2. Direct seeding  
 
Direct seeding can be done either by broadcasting(scattering) or sowing using a drum
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
seeder.
Broadcasting
is done in the fields with shallow water by scattering
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
pre-germinated seeds in the field. 
Sowing using drum seeder requires the soil to be smooth, wet and levelled very well.
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Drum seeder is filled with pre-germinated seeds and driven manually in the field so
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
seeds fall in the soil from the holes of the seeder. Using this methods,even spacing
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
between the plants can be obtained. Direct seeding requires less labour and matures
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
earlier than transplanted rice.
However, the seeds are exposed to birds and
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
animals(rats) and weed growth is high. Direct seeding methods require about 80-100
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
kilos of seed per hectare.

2.4.3. Crop management 
 
Rice crop needs regular maintenance like weed management, water management and
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
nutrient management 
 
1. Weed management 
 
14 
==PAGE:16==
 
Weed management is very important for rice. Weeds compete with the rice plants for
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
light,water, nutrients, etc. uncontrolled weeds reduce the quality of grain and increases
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
the production cost ( cost of harvesting,cleaning and drying)  
 
Weed control is done in different stages of rice production. 
 
1. During land preparation: Plowing destroys any existing weeds. Levelling and
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
flooding the field controls weed growth. Herbicides are used before planting to
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
control weeds (this is optional). 
2. During early crop growth: weed control during this stage can be done manually
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
or by using machines. Manual weeding is most efficient but requires high labour
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
and is difficult for large areas. Mechanical weeding is done by passing weeder
  
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
between the plant rows(furrows) . It is appropriate for crops transplanted in
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
straight rows. If  weeder is not used carefully, it may damage the crop. 
 
 
 
 Figure 2.5 Methods of weeding 
2. Water Management 
 
Rice fields are continuously flooded with water from preparation stage until 2-3 weeks
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
before harvesting. Flooding conserves the organic matter of the soil, controls weeds,
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Improves efficiency of all other production inputs such as nutrients, herbicide, pesticide
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
etc. For good water management, the field should be well levelled. Proper levelling
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
avoids
stagnant water in lower parts and drying out the higher parts of the field. Apart
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
from these, it makes all the operations ( planting,weeding,etc.) easy. The water level is
 
  
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
  
maintained at 2-3 cm in the beginning and it is gradually increased to 5-10 cm.
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
Insufficient water leads to reduced yield and quality of product.

There are two popular methods of  irrigating  Rice fields. 
 
1. Continuous Shallow Flooding (CSF)  
 
In this method, the field is continuously flooded with 2-5 cm until 2-3 weeks
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
before harvesting.  
 
2. Alternative Wetting and Drying (AWD) 
 
In this method, the fields are flooded and dried alternatively. Its flooded with
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
water left dry until the water level is dropped upto 15 cm below the surface.  
 
3. Nutrition management 
 
Applying nutrition is optional because the soil supplies required nutrients to the crop.
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
However, it helps to increase yield. Initial nutrition applied helps plants to absorb them
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
quickly and grow at a faster rate. Specially when transplantation method is used, the
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
plants are pulled from one place and planted at another so it takes some time for the
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
root to be established in the soil. In this case, applying nutritions helps faster root
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
establishment because the roots get nutritions directly instead of absorbing from soil. 
 
Nutrients are usually applied 3 times- Just before transplanting, one month after
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
transplanting and one month before flowering.The most needed nutrients for Rice are
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Nitrogen(N), Phosphorus(P) and Potassium(K) . 
 
Nitrogen improves plant growth rate,yield and quality of the grain. It is needed in a large
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
   
 
amount and is very volatile. It gets volatilized by washing out root zone. Nitrogen
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
deficiency limits photosynthesis and therefore leads to lower yield. 
 
Phosphorus is helps root development, tillering, early flowering, and ripening. sandy soils
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
with low organic matter and soils that are very acidic soils and in alkaline soils have
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
phosphorus deficiency and therefore it is more needed for these type of soils.
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Phosphorus deficiency delays maturity, leads to reduced tillering and changes the color
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
of the leaves to blackish green. 
 
Potassium : Helps in root growth and making the plant healthy. Increases resistance of
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
crop to diseases. Potassium deficiency reduces root growth, causes lodging, prone to
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
diseases.

2.4.4. Harvesting  
Harvesting should be done at right time to get maximum yield and grain quality. Early
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
harvesting gives immature grains which break easily. Late harvesting leads to grain loss
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
through shattering and bird attack and decreases the quality of grain.

3.5. Relay 
 
Relay is an electromagnetic switch that operates with a very small current and controls
  
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
a device that uses relatively much larger current. It acts as a bridge between low
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
powered electronic device such as Arduino and the high power devices (eg. motor)that
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
has to be controlled by it. 
 
Arduino operates on a very low current(max 5V) which is not sufficient to control high
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
power output devices. In such case, a relay is connected between Arduino and the high
 
 
 
 
 
  
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
power device that switches from low to high power. It acts as a bridge between the
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
  
 
 
 
arduino and the high power device to control them. 
3.5.1. Working of Relay 
 
A relay consists of an electromagnetic coil that is connected to input current and a
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
  
mechanical switch. Both circuits are isolated from each other. When the input circuit is
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
closed and current flows through the coil, a magnetic field is created around it which
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
  
 
  
 
pulls the mechanical switch towards it to close the output circuit and the current flows
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
through the switch and the high power device is controlled.

a given input, the output is expected to be a certain value, accuracy is related to how
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
  
 
 
  
 
 
 
close the sensor’s output is to this value.  
 
Cost - Along with all the above factors, cost is also an important factor to be considered
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
while choosing a sensor specially when  many sensors are needed.

4.7. Sequence Diagram 
 
The sequence diagram describes the technical background of the message or call flow
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
from one mobile to another.  
 
 
 Figure 4.17 Sequence diagram for the flow of control call / message 
 
Description: 
 
When a message is sent or received by the GSM module from users mobile, it goes
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
through some sequence of steps to reach from the sender to receiver. First mobile
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
equipment(ME) requests Base Station Subsystem(BSS) for a signalling channel
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(Request Access Channel - RACH) to send an SMS to the user on the other side of
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
58 
==PAGE:60==
 
mobile network. The BSS( or Base Station Controller - BSC ) will respond to ME with an
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
immediate channel assignment  Access Grant Channel(AGCH).  
 
With this stand alone dedicated channel, mobile phone sends the service message
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
request ( message management service request ) to the BSS then BSS forwards it to the
  
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
Mobile switching station (MSC) using Direct Transfer Application part (which is another
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
type of BSSAP protocol used for direct communication between MS and MSC/VLR) here
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
BSS acts as transmission device to forward or relay that message directly to the MSC. if
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
the message is not authorised then it should be done at MSC. 
 
ME sends another DTAP message (call control setup message ) to MSC/VLR to setup a
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
  
call along with receivers mobile number in order to check if it is a valid subscriber. So
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
  
 
 
 
ME asks for the permission to the MSC whether to proceed with this call or not. Then
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
now MSC again sends DTAP message ( i.e. call control call proceeding message ). Here
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
MSC agrees and says that the call can be continued on this number.  
 
Now MSC sends a message ( BSSMAP assignment request ) to BSS that says what
 
 
  
  
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
channel should be used and what time slot. Then BSS translates this message and
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
sends to the ME as assignment command message. This message includes the time
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
slot number of the radio traffic channel that BSS wants to assign to ME and the
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
frequency hopping characteristics and offers the transmit powers to the ME. Now ME
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
sends a message ( i.e. assignment complete message ) and says I have received what
  
  
 
 
 
  
 
  
 
 
 
radio traffic channel ME has to use and time slot etc. information.  
 
Now BSS deallocates the stand alone dedicated channel and connect the radio traffic
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
channel to ME to establish a direct path between ME and MSC. BSS sends BSSMAP
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
assignment complete message to the MSC during this, there is some silence at ME. The
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
MSC sends network setup message( include dial digits and details of specifying which
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
trunk use to the call ) to the local BSS of the receiver. Once the local BSS on the

4.8.1. Transmission Control Protocol - TCP 
 
TCP establishes a connection between the server and client to enable communication
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
between them . When the data is transferred using TCP, the client sends request in form
 
  
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
of packets and the server sends response in form of packets. It is a two way
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
communication. The packets sent are numbered to make sure they are received in
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
order. Once the receiver receives data packets, it acknowledges the sender. If no
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
  
 
acknowledgement is received, the sender sends the packets again. And the packets
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
are checked for errors. All the packets can be tracked so there will be no data loss when
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
TCP is used even in case of network interruptions. It is reliable. 
 
User Datagram Protocol - UDP 
 
UDP works similar to TCP but there is no error checking. Once the packets are sent the
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
sender does not check or wait for acknowledgement from the receiver It just continues
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
sending next packets. This increases the speed of communication since there is no
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
tracking and resending of packets. However, there is no guarantee that all packets are
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
received. This protocol can be used when speed is the concern. For example for live
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
 
streaming. 
 
TCP and UDP serve the same purpose of transferring the data between client and
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
server. When reliability is needed TCP is used and UDP is used when the speed is
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
necessary and packet loss does not affect the communication. For this project,
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
reliability is most important so the TCP protocol is used for communication.

plain text, images, hyper text, videos,audios between server and client. TCP on other
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
hand is a transport layer protocol and HTTP works on top of TCP. TCP sets a connection
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
between the client and the server and HTTP uses this layer to transfer the data. TCP is
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
responsible for reliable and in order transfer of data packets but is not concerned about
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
 
 
the data inside the packets.
