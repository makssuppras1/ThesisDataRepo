Abstract
We present various methods bundled as a process to generate insights for businesses that
use Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central. We deﬁne a baseline to only cover insights
generated over ﬂow of capitals by ﬁrst aggregating the values in order to generate a time-
series of important events like sales and expenses. We then perform anomaly detection
to distinguish time intervals which exhibit unusual behaviour in the ecosystem of the
enterprise. The importance of anomaly detection lies in the scope of addressing events that
act abnormal in relation to normal behaviour, and formulating this as an insight will help
the businesses to take business actions with minimum eﬀorts. The process for detecting
anomalies is divided into two stages. First stage covers ﬁnding suitable periods which are
classiﬁed as anomalies in the time-series. We deﬁne these periods as the segment of data
input required for the second phase. The second phase focuses on mapping a relation
between attributes of data tables using graph and generating insights from these relations.
Our contributions include: (a) a process which works as a extension on top of the product.
(b) a method to ﬁnd anomalies in periods in a time-series. (c) a method to construct a
graph ordered by the attributes from the data stored in relational database.

An enterprise or a business can be separated by software systems like Finance, Human
Resource, Production and Operations.This arrangement makes all the components of a
business isolated and minimizes the communication within business components.
ERP
system brings every component of your business together to improve the collaboration
between diﬀerent business processes.The scope of discussing when and why to use ERP

This behaviour of ERP system, where it brings all the processes together is a very important
factor as it allows us to building a generic process for providing insights. This process is
discussed further in this thesis and it will elaborate on required assumptions for the process
to behave normally.

1.3
Anomaly Detection
Insights provided to the customers are highly based on the unusual behaviour on their ﬁ-
nancials where unusual behaviour is termed as an anomaly. On a broader view, an anomaly
in data is a pattern that does not follow the normal expected behaviour as illustrated by the
data. Therefore, anomaly detection is an important part of the process which is responsible
for ﬁnding the anomalies in the data. Anomaly Detection as a complete process for this
problem is divided into two stages, where, initially, execution of ﬁrst stage is mandatory
in-order for second stage to function. The two stages are Time-series based and Graph
based anomaly detection, and they are discussed in the following sections. The notion of
dividing it into two stages and making second stage dependent on ﬁrst is to reduce the
ﬂow of data between the machine learning service and the ERP system.
1.3.1
Time-series Based
Initially the anomaly detection will be based on uni-variate time-series. A time-series is
deﬁned as: “A time series is a series of key-value pairs which are ordered by key in

chronological order and key is a timestamp entry. So, for a time-series y, the value at
timestamp t is denoted by yt.” A time-series can be uni-variate or multi-variate, where
uni-variate time-series has only one value per timestamp and multi-variate time-series will
have more than one value per timestamp.
Anomalies can be categorized under three categories:
• Point Anomalies: In a data, if a single data point is to be classiﬁed as anomaly, it
is refereed to as a point anomaly. There can be multiple data points which can be
anomalies, and labelling them as anomalies individually is also referred to as point
anomaly. Refer Exponential Smoothing 3.1 and Holt-Winters 3.2 for examples.
• Collective Anomalies: When a sequence of contiguous data points collectively looks
unusual, then it is classiﬁed as Collective Anomalies. In the sequence of contiguous
data points, all or any of the points can or cannot be a point anomaly.
Refer
Streaming Least Squares 3.3 and Ensemble Methods 3.4 for examples.
• Contextual Anomalies: It occurs when a point or collective anomaly has been classi-
ﬁed as anomaly with a context behind it. A context here can be any related available
supporting data or features. For an example, high sales on a festive season doesn’t
necessarily classify it as an anomaly and should be considered normal.
This thesis will focus on Point and Collective Anomalies. Anomalies and outliers are can
be used interchangeably throughout the thesis.
1.3.2
Graph Based
Presenting a list of events as a story to the user is a complicated process, and therefore
it becomes important to identify and keep a track of relations between each events. As
the data in ERP systems is already stored in relational database, it is easier to construct
a graph-based data model out of it. Using a graph-based data model will give additional
beneﬁt as it can handle complex relations and is not bound by a strict schema[6]. The
advantage of having a graph model is the ability of graphs to connect nodes with edges,
thus making it possible to construct natural language insight by traversing over the graph
collecting relations and attributes to ﬁll up a handcrafted templates.
As answered to
the question “Why Graphs” in [7], the highlighted points are: Inter-dependent nature
of the data, Powerful representation, Relational nature of problem domains and Robust
machinery.

1.4 Background

Anomaly Detection has been a well researched topic[8] and is widely used in many ﬁelds and
branches such as Security, Medicine, Image and Video Processing, Space and Industrial
systems, and many other domains.
Anomaly detection being a wide ﬁeld and its use
changes between each domain as what seems normal to someone may be abnormal to
other. While there are many techniques developed to ﬁnd anomalies, Hoage and Austin[9]
provide a deep survey on methodologies for outlier detection. While there are supervised
methods like Support Vector machines and decision trees[8] present, we will only cover
unsupervised methods in this thesis.
Time-series
Time series is an insightful way of representing data as it clearly shows how value is
changing with respect to time. Change in stock prices, Heart arrhythmia, sales, web page
hits and many other things are represented by time series.
Determining anomalies or
irregularities in a time series is an important and well studied topic. While there are many
papers on ﬁnding similarity between multiple time series, there is a paper discussing about
ﬁnding the most unusual subsequence(by their deﬁnition) in time series. Finding the most
unusual time series subsequence[10] is very closely related to the method covered in this
thesis. Though, it is closely related to the problem being solved, it adapts a total diﬀerent
approach in solving the problem. The approach they use is to convert a time series to a
Heuristically Ordered Time Series using Symbolic Aggregate ApproXimation[10], in which
they order SAX sequences and ﬁnd subsequences which are anomalies.
Graph-based
Moving from data stored in relational databases to data being stored on graph databases,
there has been a ongoing research in speciﬁc problems that are represented by graph. An
eﬃcient way of ﬁnding anomalies is to observe the relationship between two nodes of a
graph, if the relationships observed is dissimilar to the relationship present, then it is highly
likely to be an anomaly. The method used in this thesis revolves around building relations
between nodes and utilizing them to ﬁnd anomalies.
Graph-based Anomaly Detection
and Description: A survey[11] documents the methods for anomaly detection using graph
modelling in detail.
Most of the research has been in ﬁnding anomalies in data, and limited in explaining the
anomaly[12]. A two pass approach has been discussed in Anomaly Detection and Explana-
tion Discovery on Event Streams[12], as they claim it is hard to ﬁnd and explain anomalies
in one method. There is another approach known as EXstreams [13], which utilizes Logis-
tic regression and decision trees to do feature selection and importance, thereby producing
a predicate form of insight.

2.5
Graph-Based Anomaly Detection
Based on Schreiber’s book[18], a graph G = (V, E) consists of a set of vertices (also called
nodes or points) V and a set of edges (arcs, links) E, where each edge is assigned to two
(not necessarily disjunct) vertices. An edge e connecting the vertices u, v is denoted by {u,
v}, we say u and v are incident with e and adjacent (or neighbors) to each other. Graphs
truly are the best way to represent relations between objects[19], and therefore they are
essential to deﬁne the ﬂow of capital based on the attributes of entities. While there are
many kinds of graphs, few relevant kinds are describes as follows:
• Undirected Graph: Given an edge e connecting two vertices u and v, the order of
the two vertices connected by the edge doesn’t matter, which means {u, v} ⇔{v,
u}.
• Directed Graph: Given an edge e connecting two vertices u and v, the order of the
two vertices connected by the edge matters. This means if there is an edge from
vertex u on v, it doesn’t mean that there is an edge from v on u.
• Edge labeled Graph: In an edge labeled graph, every edge has a label associated
with it. Edge {u, v}, if labeled, can be represented as {u, v, L} where L is additional
information supporting the edge.
• Weighted Graph: When in an edge labeled graph, the edge {u, v, L} has a label
L which has numerical value and is used for comparison when connecting the two
edges is called as weighted graph.
For an example, consider a railway network
with stations as vertices and edges as rails, then the distance between each station
connected by rails is weight. This forms a weighted graph with distance between
each connect station as weight.
The approach using graph-based data model is to reduce the record based entries in a
table to an edge labeled graph.
Before proceeding any further, there are strong assumptions which are stated as follows:
• ERP system being used by a business, the cardinality of products handled by the
business is limited.
• A table in database where events(see 4.1) are recorded will have multiple attributes
and as an insight is designed for easy understanding, the attribute selection is held
responsible to domain expert and doesn’t’ rely on feature selection and importance.
2.5.1
Modelling
The notion is to build a graph level-by-level, where each level of graph will represent a
attribute from a table.
This approach makes helps generating an insight in form of a
text easier because we are ordering the ﬂow of graph by attributes.
This implies that

2.5.2
Securing the graph
Protecting the data has a high signiﬁcance value, and the graph being directly build upon
the customer data possesses a threat to data privacy and protection. It is therefore very
important to secure the essential customer data being used to create the graph-model.
While building the graph-based data models, the keys stored in dictionary at every node is
built up by the distinct values from each attributes. Therefore, if this graph-based data is
acquired by an intruder, it is easy to reconstruct the data and is vulnerable to data leak.
Therefore, while building up the graph, it is important to hash the value of every key with
any suitable hash function before storing it in a dictionary of any node. As hash functions
carry the property of irreversibility, an intruder user who creates illegal access to graph-
based data model will fail to uncover the business information stored by the graph-based
data model.

points in Result B and inner loop goes through all the intervals in Result A. Then for every
point in Result B, if it occurs in any of the interval in Result A, the point lies in that interval.
Exclude all the other points in Result B if they don’t fall in any of the intervals in Result A.
Analysis: Looping through both Result B and Result A will give time-complexity O(MN),
as each point in Result B goes over each interval of Result A.
3.5.2
Balanced Binary Search Tree (BBST)
Deﬁnition 1: A Binary tree is a tree where each node has at-most two child nodes.
Deﬁnition 2: A Binary Search Tree is a Binary tree where the value of left child node
is less than value of parent node, and value of right child node is greater than value of
parent node.
Deﬁnition 3: A Balanced Binary Search Tree is a Binary Tree where the diﬀerence between
the depth of two sub-trees of every node in maximum 1. Where the depth of a node is
the number of edges from the root to the node[Taken From wikipedia].
The problem is solved by creating a Balanced Binary Search Tree from Result A, where
a node of tree has two properties start-date and end-date such that the start-date of left
child node is less than the value of start-date in parent node and start-date of right child
node is greater than the value of start-date in parent node. Now, as the Balanced Binary
Search tree is created, for every point in Result B, traverse down the BBST and ﬁnd the
interval where it belongs. This method excludes the point if it reaches the end of tree and
doesn’t fall in any of the intervals.
The implementation of forming a BBST is shown in algorithm 3.

the results, but we choose to add it as an insight. The knowledge base can very dynamic
and is discussed in future work.

3.6
Graph-based Data Model
Section 2.5 covered the constructing of graph-based data model from a relational database.
This section will cover how graph-based data model is used to generate insights based on
the anomalies.
The following assumptions are important for implementation to ﬁt with the graph-based
data model built:
• The period used to build the graph should be the same while trying to generate
insights.
• The number of attributes used to build the graph should be same, otherwise it is
necessary to rebuild the graph to match the structure.
The main idea of ﬁnding anomalies and generating insights is to traverse the graph based
on a new period and compare the current values to the usual values(mean and variance)
present on the edge required to go from node A to node B. If the current value falls out
of the range(mean±variance), then that value should be classiﬁed as an anomaly.
From the product point-of-view, there are two cases when building the graph model. They
are as follows:
• New User: A new user will have a fresh database with no entries in the tables.
This makes it computationally easy to start building the graph-data model for every
period from the beginning. In this case, ﬁrst few initial periods should only be used
to build and tune the graph. It is biased towards the periods that occur initially and
should have been classiﬁed as an anomaly.
• Existing User: Provided a signiﬁcant amount of data is available, the process is to
perform anomaly detection using Ensemble Method(section 3.4). For every anoma-
lous windows, choose a normal window preceding it to build graph model. After
building the graph model for each normal window, traverse the graph model for
the subsequent anomalous period. By building upon the graph on normal periods
and traversing on anomalous periods iteratively, the process is ordered by time, thus
preserving the change in time-series.

section 2.5, with Country ID, Partner ID, and Product ID. Figure 3.7 shows a path present

What if there are no existing further nodes to traverse? This can be solved by building
the new path while raising it as an anomaly. Building a new path ensures that if this
event is seen next time, the graph already has some information regarding it. This is bad
for one time events, which are very less likely to happen in future and are updated in the
graph. To eliminate this issue, a concept of decay is introduced in the graph. To introduce
the concept of decay, an additional parameter LastUpdated is introduced at every edge.
This parameter is inspired from Time aware Least Recently Used(TLRU)[20] cache policy.
Using the LastUpdated value of an edge, erase the edge if diﬀerence between current time
and LastUpdated is more than a deﬁned threshold(days, weeks or month). Even though
the mean and variance is adaptive, because it is being updated continuously, it will fail to
adapt to the changes of new events that are continuously exceeding the threshold, which
should be the new normal. Erasing the edge will adapt to these new changes, as the new
edges will be formed with values that will match the current trend.
Cases on graph traversal: (a) For any edge during the traversal, which looks normal, is
updated. (b) If an edge in the beginning is normal, it does not imply that there are no
anomalous edge(s) later on, hence, complete path should be traversed.

5.1 Limitations

5.1
Limitations
While the methods used in this thesis are widely conﬁgurable, there remain static once
initiated. From business point of view, there are various periods on which the insights
can be acquired, but current methods will only focus on a deﬁned conﬁguration given a
limited amount of space. The time series constructed is completely based on the value
associated with each individual item in the database which will generate ambiguous results
after aggregation.
The anomaly detection only takes into account the calender events
which are currently fed statically to the knowledge base, this restricts the possible diversity
of context.
5.2
Future Work
There is always room for improvements, therefore this section will outline possible under-
taking that may be followed to undertake future work based on this thesis.
As the seasonality of data is calculated by trying out diﬀerent values and choosing a
value with lowest Mean Absolute Error, it can instead be acted upon by more advanced
methods in terms of result.
Better grasp over the seasonality will improve the overall
result of the model.
Exponential Smoothing is always an option when the seasonality
is too be skipped, but the tightness on the smoothing is lost and therefore it becomes
essential to ﬁnd seasonality of time series when it comes to Holt-Winters. The current
time series anomaly detection is based on uni-variate analysis, it can be further extended to
multivariate analysis to see how the results change if two diﬀerent time series have causal
eﬀect on each other. The anomaly detection mildly captures context of time-series by
depending directly on the external source for knowledge base. even if this approach allows
us to extend the capabilities without aﬀecting the model directly, it still requires additional
work on how to model knowledge base as an external entity to cover various contexts,
for example, syncing the time periods with news application programming interface and
narrowing out the relative sentiment to the time series.
The traversal in case of graph data model can be modiﬁed to support bi-directional search,
this will allow us to perform operation on the graph faster, computationally. Also, this will
improve the time required to remove decayed edges, because the edges in the later levels
will have older LastUpdated value, which will reduce the number of edges traversed as the
search always starts from ﬁrst level. Further studies are need to choose the attributes of
tables instead of relying on the domain experts. The attributes chosen should be informa-
tive enough to generate an understandable insight. The graph data model constructed is
ordered by attributes and thus restricts the movement from one node to another. Further
research is needed to store it as a graph database which can utilize graph query language
giving freedom to generated multiple kinds of insights. Thresholds being very important
in case of anomaly detection in every case as discussed in this thesis, it will remarkable to
have them dynamic and adaptable to the current trend.

Chapter 6
Conclusion
We have developed a process for generating insights for business users by ﬁnding anomalies
in data.
We showed how to convert data to time-series and ﬁnd periods time using
Streaming Least Squares supported by Exponential Smoothing methods. We then used
these periods to get interesting data from the database to traverse over a graph and
comparing the values present over the edges of the graphs. Estimating the deviation from
the values present on the edges, we generated a a sentence deﬁned by a template as an
insight. We also showed that the calculations required to process a new data point can be
done in constant time, hence making it an online algorithm. We believe our process is ﬁrst
attempt to provide insights and is made to closely work with Enterprise Resource Planning
Systems. This process will enhance the ease of use and understanding of the data logged
by the enterprise in their systems, thus making it much easier to catch analytics. While the
development of this process was intended as a standalone product, it is now in transition
to be tightly coupled with the Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central. Therefore, there
are further chances of reinventing the process if the estimated performance is not met.
