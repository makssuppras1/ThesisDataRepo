Thisthesisdescribestheprocessandoutcomeof
developing
a
concept
for
flexible
home
workstations.Theprojecttakesaholisticapproach
to the design process. It investigates the
surroundingfactorsinfluencingemployeesworking
from home, the legislations towards home
workstations, how different types of work
influence the possibility to work from home, a
segmentationoftheusersworkingfromhome,and
the practice of working from home by means of
interviewsandaquestionnaire.

Thethesisisperformedincollaborationwiththe
companyCUBEDesignA/S.Here,theopportunities
for CUBE Designs on the market for home
workstationsandthecurrentcompetitiononthe
marketisanalyzed.

Workingfromhomehasexistedforalongtimeina
historical point of view. Before the industrial
revolution many people, such as craftsmen and
evengovernmentofficialsworkedandlivedunder
thesameroof(VanMeel,2007).Thepractice,asit
existstoday,hasevolvedfromtheearly1970’sasa
consequenceoftheoilcrisis(Christensen,2015).

Up to this point, the home working practice has
decreasedduetotheemergenceoftheindustrial
revolution, with companies growing in size and
officeworkbecomingthedominantwayofworking
(VanMeel,2007).Astheoilcrisisarose,commuting
becameextremelyexpensive,andthepossibilityof
working
from
home
became
appealing.
Simultaneously,thedevelopmentoftechnologies
enabling communication in a more efficient
mannercontributedtoanewpracticeofworking
fromhome.

Workingfromhomehasbecomepossiblewiththe
technological development of laptops, mobile
phones, smartphones, tablets, headsets and the
developmentofbroadbandandincreasinglyfaster
internetconnection(Andreasen&Olesen,2012).
Therefore, the possibility for performing work at
hometodaymostlydependsofthetypeofwork
which is performed, e.g. service jobs such as ITͲ
support, sales or secretary work (Bloom, et al.,
2014).

An extensive research on working from home,
publishedin2014(Bloom,etal.,2014)showsthat
workingfromhomeincreasestheefficiencyofthe
work, but in cases where most of the work is
performed at home it results in a lack of social
interaction with work colleagues. A study
performed by Zapera on 1.674 representative
selectedDanesshows,thatonlyeverytenthDane
believe they are less productive when working
from home (Johansson, 2008). The increased
efficiency is attributable to shorter and fewer
breaks, and that tasks are solved better due to
fewer disturbances by colleagues. (Bloom, et al.,
2014)

In addition to the increased efficiency, another
advantageistheincreasedflexibilityinthebalance
between family and work life. This enables the
employee to organize their day at home
(Johansson, 2008). As a consequence of the
problem with social interaction with the work
community,manyworkasmixedͲworkers(Census,
2010),whichisdefinedasbothworkingathome
andatthecompany.

Theflexibilitythatworkingfromhomeoffersthe
employeeisanimportantassetforthecompanies.
According to Lars Qvortrup, Professor at the
FacultyofSocialStudies,Denmark,theattitudehas
shiftedfrombeingabouthowtheemployeecould
contributetothecompany,towhatthecompany
can offer to the employee (Statistics Denmark,
2002).Therefore,morecompaniestakeadvantage
of letting employees work from home, thus
harvestingthebenefitsofthehigherefficiency,but
also in order to raise the employee satisfaction,
work satisfaction and loyalty to the company
(Statistics Denmark, 2002). This manifests itself
whenlookingatreportsfromStatisticsDenmark,
whichshowthat27%ofDanesworkedathomeat
least once a month in 2014 (Statistics Denmark,
2016).

Even though homework is beneficial for both
company and employees, the workstation and
devicesusedathomemuststillcomplywiththe
sameworkenvironmentlegislationsasdefinedfor


work performed at companies (Arbejdstilsynet,


Severaltypesofofficefurnitureexistwhichsolve
the requirements defined by the Danish working
environmentauthority,i.e.theyhaveappropriate
worksurfaceareaandareergonomicallycorrect,
but these furniture are designed for offices and
thus do not fit into the style of a home. As a
consequence of this, the officeͲlike workstations
arepreferablyplacedinasecludedroom.Formany
peopleworkingathome,thisisnotanoptionas
theyliveonfewsquaremeters(Theimann,2014)
soroomforanofficeathomeislimited.

Thisthesisexaminesthetensionfieldwhichexists
betweenthegoodworkspaceinthehomeandthe
context of the home. The underlying basis is to
analyzethecontextinwhichtheworkstationwill
beplacedandinvestigatethedevicesusedforthe
workprocessesperformedathome.Thefinalgoal
ofthisthesisisthe“Developmentofaconceptfor
flexiblehomeworkstations”whichfavorsworkand
thecontextofthehomeequally.

Thisprojectonlyaddressesthedevelopmentofa
concept for the furniture at which the work at
homeisperformed.Thismeans,thatadistinction
is made between this furniture and the other
elements,suchasthedesignofchairordevices.
Futurereferencetothefurnitureatwhichthework
athomeisperformed,willbetheworkstation.


Toelucidatewhyusersareworkingfromhome,it
is investigated which drivers and blockers are
present when working from home. This is done
fromauserpointofviewandacompanypointof
viewandisinterestingtoinvestigateasthishasa
huge influence on the context of working from
home.


Personal reasons 
Personal incentives for working at home highly
relate to the flexibility that it provides. Flexible
workhasbecomenormalformany.Thisisinmany
aspects advantageous as it enables coherence
betweenworkandprivatelife(StatisticsDenmark,
2016).

This coherence between work and private life is
importantasbothmenandwomenconstitutethe
workforceandfocusequallyontheircareers.The
flexibility that working from home provides
enables coordination of picking up children, or
findingtimeforrecreationalactivitiesasdayscan
beplannedmorefreely.

Ageneraltrendinsocietyisthatpeoplenolonger
makecleardistinctionsbetweenwhere,how,and
whentheywork.Thistrendiscalled‘Bleisure',due
totheblurringof‘business’and‘leisure’(Raymond,
2010).Thetruereasonforthisblurringisfoundin
thelogicsofthesociety.

AccordingtotheCopenhagenInstituteforFutures
studies(2004)thereareseveralcoexistinglogics;
theindustriallogic,thelogicofthedreamsociety
andthelogicofthecreativeman(Table1).Alogic
isaframeworkfordescribingtheinterrelationship
betweenassumptions,beliefsandvaluesbywhich
individualsandorganizationsprovidemeaningto
theirdailyactivities(Understandingsociety,2014).
ThelogicsoverlapandaccordingtotheAmerican
psychologist Clayton Alderfer, motivation is only
achieved by satisfying multiple needs (Sørensen,
2006).

Thelogicsaffectthemotivationsofworkingfrom
homedifferently.Lookingattheindustriallogicin
Table1,thefocusongoodworkingenvironmentis
importanttoaddress.Iftheworkenvironmentat
the company is too noisy this may enhance the
needofworkingfromhome.Ontheotherhand,
theworkenvironmentinthehomemustbegood
to be a realistic option. The Institute of Future
studies (2006) states that the industrial logic
revolvearoundsystematization,whichresultsina
lack of flexibility. This may be problematic in a
workingfromhomeperspective.


to

tacit
knowledge

“Itdoesn’ttakemuchtobuildacommunity,buton

Process dependency and 
communication
Oneofthemostdominantlimitationsforworking
at home is the task dependency, i.e. how
dependentthesubͲprocessesareintaskswhichare
solved by multiple employees. Thompson (1967)
defines the dependency of tasks as a set of
dependency characteristics, being aggregated
dependency,
sequential
dependency
or
codependency.
Aggregated dependency 

Aggregated dependency covers multiple subͲ
processes,whichneedtobeaggregatedinorderto
create a collected output. The singular subͲ
processes are well defined by guidelines and
routines,whichseldomchange.Thustheworkhas
alowdegreeofcomplexityandthedifferentsubͲ
processes can be done individually. Examples of
work types with aggregated dependency are
caseworkandDdministrativework.






Sequential dependency 

Sequential dependency covers a type of subͲ
processes where one subͲprocess depends on
another,meaningthatoneprocesscannotbedone
before
the
other.
An
example
could
be
development teams, where one task must be
finishedbeforetheothercanbeinitiated.Thework
tasks are still defined by guidelines, but require
planning meetings, where the processes are
coordinated.Inthecaseofsequentialdependency
working at home may be possible, if the task at
hand does not interfere with the dependency of
others.Thismayimpedetheopportunitytowork
several days at home, but does not hinder the
possibilitytoworkathomeingeneral.

Codependency

Codependency covers the type of subͲprocesses
whicharecontinuouslycontributingwithinputto
each other. This dependency type has a high
degree of complexity and needs constant
adjustmentbetweenthedifferentsubͲprocesses.
Examplesofworktypesdefinedbythisdegreeof
dependency are idea generation or surgery.
Workingathomeperformingcodependenttasks,
will be cumbersome due to the constant


communication
needed.
As
different
tasks
simultaneouslyprovideinputandthuschangethe

Communication
The
foremost
reason
why
the
increasing
dependencyoftasksimpedesworkingfromhome

athome.

possibleinformationrichness.Sincethismodelwas
introduced in 1984, several other information
mediums have been developed, such as videoͲ
calling.

In order to develop a suitable workstation, it is
important to investigate the regulations for
working at home in Denmark. The regulations
described have been selected based on their
relevance for the design of the workstation.
Elements such as room ventilation are therefore
not included. The research focuses on physical
measurements, material choice, placement, and
functionalities.

The Danish work environment authority has
developedasetofrulesforworkingathome,and
someoftheserulesdealwiththeimportanceofthe
design of the home office. The Danish work
environment authority has specific requirements
tothehomeworkstation,iftheemployeeregularly
work from home and the working hours
correspond to one day per week or more.
(Arbejdstilsynet,2014)

Anotherrequirementisthatthefurnituremustfit
theworkerandthetaskstheworkerwillbedoing.
Iftheworkincludesworkingwithacomputerfor
extendedperiods,therearerequirementsthatthe
screen,keyboard,andmouseshouldbeadjustable
independently from each other. This is also the
case if working on a laptop, which would then
require external keyboard, mouse and screen.
Furthermore, there are special requirements for
workinginfrontofascreen.Therequirementsare;
there should be enough space for appropriate
working positions and movement, and there are
specialrulesfortheworkstation,chair,screen,and
lighting.(Arbejdstilsynet,2014)
Thesurfaceofthetablemustreflectaslittlelight
aspossible,andthereshouldbeenoughspacefor
optimal working conditions. This means that the
workstationshouldbeofacertainsize,andthat
sitting and working heights should fit the
employee. It is not a requirement that the table
must be adjustable, but according to the Danish
work environment authority, this is the best
solution to varying working positions. The
workstation must be wide enough for a flexible
setup of screens, keyboard and documents, and
alsodeepenoughtoplacethescreeninavisually
comfortable distance and room to support the
arms in front of the keyboard. (Arbejdstilsynet,
2009)


Ergonomic measurements 
The workstation should comply with different
types of ergonomic requirements. The following
ergonomicrequirementsarebasedon90%ofthe
European population. This means that the 5%
highestand5%smallestpersonsarenotcoveredby
thesestandardsforworkstations.

between60Ͳ90cmfromthefloor,andforstanding
workbetween96Ͳ122cm.

When a workstation is placed in the home, one
mustacknowledgetheimportanceofthestyleand
designinthehome.Thisisthesetting,orcontext,
inwhichthefinalproductmustfit.Ifitdoesnot,
otherproductswillbeprioritized.Therefore,itis
importanttoinvestigatewhichdesignsandtrends
appeal to the user segment, in order to design
productsthatareappealing.


Trend types 
Differenttypesoftrendsexistsimultaneouslyand
thelengthofwhichatrendispresentalsovaries
dependingonthetypeoftrend.Inthefurniture
industrytrendsplayanimportantrole.Furniture
are products that, opposite e.g. clothing, have a
longusestage,sodesigningtowardsashortͲlived
trendmaynotbethebeststrategy.Therefore,the
understandingoftrendtypesandtrendsurvivalare
importantfactorstoaddress.Differenttypescan
overall be defined in relation to their length
(Kongsholm&Frederiksen,2015)asdescribedin
thefollowing.
Giga-trends
GigaͲtrendsdefinemidͲlengthtrends,thatexist10Ͳ
30 years and which change the framework and
conditionofourlife.GigaͲtrendsareoftenglobal
andprimarilyentailelementsofeconomy,politics,
andtechnology.

Examples of gigaͲtrends are globalization and
communication.Bothofwhichhaveaffectedthe
framework of the life we live. Communication
technologieshaveevolvedextremelyrapidlyover
thepastdecadesandhaveenabledcommunication
across time and space. In the same manner
globalizationhasmadecompetitionacrossborders
possibleandenabledoffshoringofservices.

Mega-trends
MegaͲtrendsaretrendsthatdescribethezeitgeist
ofaspecificperiod.Thesetrendsmostlyrelateto
the development of products, where time to
market is a little longer. These trends are often
associatedwithtypesoflifestyle.MegaͲtrendsvary
between3Ͳ7yearsinlength.

MegaͲtrends often switch between the two
oppositepoles.Thismightbeurbanlifevs.country
life. The varying between poles often relate to
counterͲtrends. When a trend like materialism
emerges,acounterͲtrendoftengraduallyemerges
inanothersubͲculture,whichinthiscasemightbe
somesortoffocusonimmaterialism.

Micro-trends
MicroͲtrends are often associated with fashion
trendsorstyletrends.MicroͲtrendsaretherefore
often very tangible and easily copied by other
producers. They exist between 6 months and 3
years.

ExamplesofmicroͲtrendsarecolortrends,material
trends,orfabrictrends.Intheclothingindustry,it
mightbeacertainsilhouettewhileinthefurniture
businessitmayrelatetotheshapeofsofasorthe
typeofaccessoryonehasasdecor.Ifawareofthe
factors affecting microͲtrends, one can try to
predictthedirectionnewtrendswilltake.


One-hit wonders 
OneͲhitwondersorfadsaretheshortesttypeof
trend.Andtheydisappearalmostasfastasthey
emerge.Theymayexistbetweenafewweeksand
uptoacoupleofmonths.


Trend Curves 
ThelifeͲstageofatrendcanbedescribedbyatrend
curve(Figure9).Trendsspreadandmaturebefore
decreasingslowlyinimpact.

Generally, trend curves are interesting when
assessing the maturity of an identified trend.
Dependingontimetomarket,thetrendmighthave
begun to decline or interest in the trend is not
sufficientanymore.Ontheotherhand,producing
aproductfollowingatrendwhichhasnotmatured
sufficiently,canresultincastawayoftheproduct,
asthesocietyisnotyetreadytoacceptit.

Theacceptanceofnewtrendsdiffersdependingon
thetypeofusers.Trendcreatorsortrendsetters
canbesaidtogenerallybeyouthful,avantͲgarde,
often working in creative industries and living in
metropolis, but one cannot specifically define
them,associalmechanismsplayanimportantrole
intheadaptionoftrends.Ingeneral,itcanbesaid,
that the more a trend has matured, the more
mainstreamitbecomes,andthebroaderaudience
thetrendhas.


The trend groups and counter-
trends
Trendsevolvefromagroupofpeoplewhooften
seektobedifferentandseekthenew.Theinterest
ofthetrendgrowsuntilitbecomesmainstream.
Afterthispoint(SeeFigure10)theimpactofthe
trend slowly declines, while more conservative
consumersmightpickuponthetrend.

Trendsettersandtrendcreatorsareveryopenand
curiouspersons,whoproactivelyseekchangeand
see change as something positive. Thus, trend
settersandtrendfollowersoftencounterͲreactto
mainstream, as this is not new and interesting
anymore.Earlymainstreamersandmainstreamers
acceptthenew.




Mainstreamers resemble the average user, they
neither object to the new, nor are conservative.
Latemainstreamersaremorehesitantandneedto
beconvincedinordertotrysomethingnew,but
generally accept that change is inevitable.
Conservativeconsumerspreferproductsthathave
existedforalongtime,andingeneralpreferthe
itemstheyalreadypossess.

When a trend becomes mainstream, new trends
emerge as a reaction to the mainstream. This is
called a counterͲtrend. Roughly, it can be stated
thatalmostalltrendsaresomehowacountertrend
(Kongsholm&Frederiksen,2015).


Understanding trend and 
context
ThedifferenttrendsaffecteachothersothatgigaͲ
trends affect megaͲtrends, which affect microͲ
trends and so forth. Trends may also affect the
other way around, but mostly they affect each
otherdownwards.

TheunderstandingofgigaͲtrendsandmegaͲtrends
ishighlyusefulforbusinessworkingwithlifestyles
astrendsmaycoͲexist,supporteachother,oreven
oppose each other. The more trends one can
embrace,themorelikelyaproductistosucceed.
Thus, the knowledge and drivers behind these
trends are the Alpha and Omega for launching
successful products. Therefore, it is relevant to
analyzegigaͲ,megaͲandmicroͲtrendsinrelationto
homedecorinordertoidentifywhichtrendscan
beusedwhendesigningaproductforworkingat
home.

Throughouttheanalysis,aseriesofdifferent
methodsareapplied.

Context analysis 
Thecontextanalysisisamethodthatmapsboth
actors, groups of actors, objects, and notions
equally. Connections are drawn where social
aspectsandtechnologiesaffecteachotherinorder
tounderstandhowtheseaffectthedesignsofthe
workstation.

Mindmap
Mindmapping is a method used for structuring
largequantitiesofknowledge.Itentailsallrelevant
knowledge obtained from interviews and thus
helpstoidentifythetradeͲoff’s,breakdownsand
needs.

Personas
Creatingpersonasisamethodusedfordefiningthe
insights
obtained
about
the
users.
The
development of a persona helps define the life,
problems,andotherrelevantfactorsrelatingtothe
focusoftheproject.Inthiscase,thedefinitionof
personasisbasedontheelementsreflectingtheir
useofaworkstationforworkingathome.

Competition canvas 
The competition canvas is a method used for
analyzingtheexistingcompetitionbasedonprior
identifiedproductfeatures.Thisisdoneinorderto
identifyasuitableapproachtothemarket.

Strategy canvas 
ThestrategycanvasisatooldevelopedbyKimand
Mauborgne (2005) and used for visualizing how
competitors or competing products score in
different key competitive factors. It enables an
analysis of the possibilities for differentiating a
productorserviceinthekeycompetitivefactorsin
ordertolowerthelevelofcompetition.

4D trend index 
Trend indexing is a method developed by The
TrendBureauKjaerGlobal(TheTrendBureauKjaer
Global, u.d.). It is used for combining identified
microͲ, megaͲ, and gigaͲtrends in order to
investigatehowtheyareconnected.Thissupports
theunderstandingoftrends.Itmaybescaledtofit
thetypesoftrendsoneisaddressing.

Porter’s five forces
Porter’s five forces is a method developed by
MichaelEugenePorter(Porter,1996).Itisusedfor
understandingthelevelofrivalryinacompetitive
situation. The method analyses the current
competitivepositionofacompanybyanalyzingthe
buyerpower,supplierpower,threatofnewentry
andthreatofsubstitution.
SWOT
TheSWOTanalysisisamethodusedforidentifying
the Strengths and Weaknesses internally in the
company,andforidentifyingtheOpportunitiesand
Threatsexternaltothecompany.

x

week(3Ͳ5daysaweek)
x


3-5 days a week 
Users who work at home more than half of the
week have a really high need for ergonomically
correctfurnitureandhavehigherexpectationsto
their

Inthissituation,theneedsforaworkstationare
thatitisstationaryandthatitisabletoholdthe

thepossibilitytohideawaythedevicesinaneasy
manner,withouthavingtopackthemaway.Itmay
alsobetheopportunitytousetheworkstationfor

1-2 days a week 
Usersthatworkathomeonceortwiceaweekalso
have a need for an ergonomically correct

necessarily prioritize the space for a stationary
solution. Devices will sometimes be put away
between workdays, but in general, a solution
where these are easily stored or hidden may be

especially for users who have not prioritized an

shouldhaveanotherpurposewhennotusedfor
workingathome.

1/4-1/2 day a week 
Whenworkingathomeonlyacoupleoftimesa
monthitseemsthattheconcernaboutergonomic
furnituredecreases.Thefunctionsneededarethat
theworkstationcanbehiddenaway/minimizedor
thatitmaygenerallybeusedforsomethingelse.
Thiswayitcanbeturnedintoaworkstationthefew
timesthisisneeded.Mostlyusersworkingthislittle
timepermonthwillresorttoworkingatthedining
table.

Brainstorming
Brainstormingisacreativitytechniquedeveloped
by Osborn (1963). It aims to create a creative
environment where ideas for solving a defined
problemcanbedeveloped.Severalmethodsexist
forenhancingtheresultofbrainstormning.

Negative brainstorming is a method where ideas
are developed leading to the worst scenarios
imaginable. Subsequently taking the ideas and
turningthemintogoodsolutions.

Association brainstorming uses different artifacts
to enhance the development of ideas associated
withthepresentedartifact.Itisaneffectivewayto
circumventtypicalthinkingandbreakfree,when
theflowofideasisstagnating.

Twobrainstormingsessionshavebeendefinedfor
this project. “Out of the head” brainstorming,
where loose ideas are drawn on paper. This
ensures,thatnofixedideasblocktheemergingof
others. The other type of brainstorming is a
brainstorming on the solution types which have
beenidentifiedinpreviousanalysis.

Weighted objectives method
Theweightedobjectivesmethodisanevaluation
methodusedforcomparingideasorconcepts.A
rangeofcriteriaareweightedandtheperformance
oftheideaorconceptisassessedforeachofthe
criteria. This provides a total value per idea or
concept(Cross,2008).
Functions-means tree 
The functionsͲmeans tree is a method used for
functional decomposition. At the top level main
functions are identified and for each function
different means (i.e. solution elements) are
defined.Beloweachmean,functionsaredefined.
The decomposition is normally performed until
means are found for the most important subͲ
functions(Tjalve,1979).

Morphological chart 
The morphological chart is a method used for
systematic idea generation. It maps a series of
solutionsforidentifiedfunctions.Bycombiningthe
solutions in different ways ideas are generated
(Cross, 2008). The morphological chart can be
basedonthefunctionsͲmeanstree.

SCAMPER
SCAMPER is a creativity method that helps
generating and assessing ideas using seven
heuristics(Daalhuizen,etal.,2013).Theheuristics
are Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to
anotheruse,Eliminate,andReverse.Theheuristics
and their guiding questions have been used
unstructured, but have served as a guideline for
element to consider in the conceptualization
processofassessingtheideas.

Performance Wheels 
Theperformancewheelsarebasedonthestrategy
wheelmethod,whichisarepresentationtoolused
formappingstrengthsofacompany(Daalhuizen,
etal.,2013).However,insteadofrepresentingthe
strengths of the company, it is used to illustrate
performancesofdifferentideasdeveloped.


for the product of interest. The presented cards

Choice of focus 
Thechoiceoffocusisbasedonthedistributionof
“goodqualityideas”,onanassessmentbasedon
thecompetition,andontheneedforasolution.

Thenumberof“goodqualityideas”isacceptably
highforallpersonas,ascanbeseeninFigure40.

However, no final choice should be made
exclusivelybasedonthenumberof“goodquality
ideas”.Therefore,otherfactorsneedtobetaken
intoconsideration.

Fromthesolutionspace,ithasbeenidentifiedthat
thediningtableisabigcompetitoringeneral.The
moredaystheuserworksfromhome,thehigher
workcomfortisvalued.Thisstronglyindicatesthat
it may be difficult accessing the market with a
solutionforusersworkingfromhome¼Ͳ½daysa
week.Therefore,itmaybemorebeneficialtofocus
ontheusersworking1Ͳ2daysaweekfromhome.

Furthermore, a choice between medium housing
andsmallhousingcanbemadebasedontheneed
forasolution.Inmediumhousingtheopportunity
tohavespaceforanofficeismorelikely,reducing
theneedforflexibleworkstationbecausethereis
roomforastationarydesk.

Basedontheaboveargumentationitischosento
focustheongoingideationonuserslivinginsmall
housingsandworking1–2daysathomeperweek.

As a design guideline, it is assumed that the
workstationwillbeplacedinthelivingroomas55%
ofthequestionnairerespondentsworkintheliving
room, while only 21% work in the kitchen, 20%
workinanoffice,and5%workinthebedroom[RAͲ
07].


Theobjectivesforthelatterdesignprocesscanbe
clarifiedasafocusisdecidedonusersworking1Ͳ2
daysathomeperweek,livinginsmallhousings.
Thus,thefindingsfromtheresearchandanalysis
areusedtosettheframeworkfortheproject.

Fromthesegmentation,ithasbeenfoundthatthe
usersworkingmostoftenathomeareattheageof
35to64.Theymostlikelyworkintheinformation
andcommunicationindustryandarehighmiddle
class.Furthermore,theanalysishascontributedto
theknowledgethatusersonaverageuse6devices
forwork.Ingeneral,theanalysisofuserneedshas
resulted in an increased understanding of how
devices, the work processes, number of working
days, and housing size affect the needs when
workingathome.Themostrelevantfindingsthat
affecttheworkstationitselfareimportanttokeep
inmindwhendevelopingthefinaldesign.

Storage and leaving devices 
Storageofdevicesnearbyisimportantfortheusers
whenworkingmoredaysathome.Itrelatestothe
identified tradeͲoff “need of leaving devices vs.
daysworkingfromhome”.

Work surface area 
Differentnumbersofdeviceshavebeentestedon
differentsizesofworksurfaceareasasdefinedby
theDanishworkingenvironmentauthority.From
theanalysisishasbeenfoundthattheworksurface
areashouldbearound600mmx1200mminorder
tocomplytotheaveragenumberofdevices.

Sitting and standing work 
Ergonomics are increasingly important when
working more days from home. Therefore, it is
important that the workstation ensures an
opportunitytoworksittingaswellasstanding.
Placement in living room 
The workstation must be designed so it can be
placedinthelivingroom.Thisisduetothefactthat
userswholiveinsmallhousingsdonothavethe
excess space for an office. Furthermore, the
placement of the workstation should be as
unrestrainedaspossible.Thismakesiteasierfor
theusertoplacetheworkstationinthelivingroom
andalsoensurethattheneedforfacingtheroom
canbemet.

Easy transformation 
Theworkstationmustbeeasytoaccess,meaning
thatitshouldhaveasfewtransformationstepsas
possible. If not sufficiently achieved, there is a
breakdown that users will work from the dining
tableinstead.

Trends at home 
Designing for home trends is important as the
workstationwillbeplacedinthelivingroomandis
morelikelytosucceedinthemarketifitfollowsthe
furnituretrends.


Concept choice discussion 
Firstly,ithasbeenchosentodeselectWallerseven

workshopparticipants.ThisisbecauseWallershas

ocean (Kim & Mauborgne, 2005). This is not
favorableforCUBEDesign.

TableChairandSquaresscoredsimilarpointsinthe
feedback from the workshop participants. From
theworkshopitwasidentified,thatTableChaironly
fitsinoneofthesmallhousingscenarioswherethe
livingroomspaceislarger[ICͲ13].Thismeansthat
TableChaircanonlybeusedbyhalfofthetarget
group. One of TableChairs biggest downsides is,
thatitisbigandcannotbeminimized.Ifchanging
thedesignenablesminimization,TableChairmight
be a good choice. The problem is, that the
stylistically
beautiful
design,
which
favors
TableChairs, may not survive if TableChair is
redesignedsoitcanminimize.

Squares performed well in the feedback from
workshop participants and CUBE Design. The
biggestchallengeistolowerthenumberofstepsin
thetransformationprocess.Despitethisproblem,
the workstation is extremely flexible, which is a
majoradvantagewhenaddressingtheneedsofthe
targetgroup.Furthermore,aproductlikeSquares
hasnotbeenseenonthemarketbefore,whichis
advantageous for CUBE Design as competition is
lessintense.TheproductionofSquaresispossible
forCUBEDesign,asitcanbebuiltofpanels.Also,
SquaresseemstofitwellinCUBEDesignsproduct
portfolio. If the number of transformation steps
can be lowered, Squares is a very promising
concept.

Squaresasaconcept,butdifferentbasicstructures

Data collection 
Adequateanthropologicalliteratureonthepraxis
of working from home has not been obtainable.
The retrievable literature has mainly been about
positiveornegativeaspectsofworkingfromhome
or theoretically based on work processes,
dependencies and communication from an
organizationalpointofview,butfewdescribedthe
needsandproblemsrelatedtoperformingworkat
homefromauserpointofview.Therefore,the
questionnaireandinͲdepthinterviewshavebeen
extremely
valuable
in
substantiating
the
knowledge about the practice of working from
home.

Theknowledgeobtainedfrominterviewshasbeen
comprehensive and the acquired information
aboutthepraxisisassessedasbeingsufficient.The
representativityofthequestionnairehowevercan
be questioned. In total, 47 responses were
collected, which is an acceptable number, but
uncertaintiesarise,ashalfoftherespondentswere
youngerthanthetargetgroup.Itcanbediscussed
if the allocation of age groups may have had
influenceontheresponsesonwhichtheanalysisis
based.

Thetypeofdevicesandaveragenumberofdevices
are dependent on the work and are therefore
deemed trustworthy despite of the age of the
respondents. However, the age of respondents
maytosomedegreehavedistortedthenumberof
days and percentile distribution of the places
wheretheworkisperformed,astheseaspectsare
dependentonthecontextinwhichtheuseris.A
contextwhichmaychangewithage.


Trend analysis 
Itcanbediscussedifthetrendanalysishasbeen
comprehensive enough. Trend forecasting and
trendanalysisarescientificfieldsofstudieswhich

this study as users strongly emphasized that a
workstationshouldfitthestyleofthehome.

Thetrendsidentifiedarebasedonacknowledged
Danishtrenddecormagazinespublishedwithinthe

oftrendsisjudgedappropriatewhencomparedto
the extent of which the identified trends are
appliedintheproduct.Trendshaveanimpacton
theshapeoftheproduct,butcolorͲwisecustomers

The focus of the thesis has been to develop a
concept for a flexible workstation for home use.
Thestartingpointwasfoundedinresearchonthe
overalllimitationsonworkingfromhomeandthe
requirementswhichaffectthedimensionalaspects
oftheworkstation.

The target group was identified through a
segmentation, elaborating on age, industry, and
salary.Furthernarrowingofthetargetgroupwas
performed through the analysis and ideation
screening,whereuserslivinginsmallhousingsand
workingathome1Ͳ2dayswerechosenamongst
fourpossibleusergroups.

Itwasconcludedthatthereisamarketforhome
workstations if a combination of a high level of
work comfort and nonͲoffice design (i.e. homely
design)
is
obtained.
Homely
design
was
substantiatedbymeansoftrendanalysisresulting
inaseriesofidentifiedtrends,wherethemegaͲ
trendsBeing,Naturalmaterials,Mixedmaterials,
Oldquality,Minimalism,andLessismorewerethe
mostpopular.

Thedevelopmentandchoiceofthefinalconcept
was based on extensive ideation, assessment,
testingofthreepossiblecandidatesandafurther
conceptualization of Squares by means of basic
structures.

The establishment of detailing elements laid the
basisforthesequenceofwhichthedetailingwas
performed, with special focus on the elements
ensuringtheflexibilityoftheworkstation.Design
adjustments were performed for CUBE Designs
production and the sales possibilities were
identified.

Allinall,ithasbeenaccomplishedtoincorporate
mostneedstoasatisfactorylevel,ensurethatthe
appearance of the workstation reflected the
identified trends, and comply with the defined
specifications.Thus,itcanbeconcludedthatthe
initialgoalofdeveloping aconceptforaflexible
workstation, which fits the needs of the target
groupisfulfilled.

CONCLUSION




Squareshasmadeitpossibleforuserstohavegood
workingconditionsalthoughnothavingthespace
tohaveanoffice.Thisenablesuserstoworkfrom
homewhichhavenotyethadtheopportunityto
doso.

Squaresisspecificallydesignedtowardsuserswho
liveinsmallhousingsandwork1Ͳ2daysperweek,
butSquarescanalsobeattractiveforotheruser
groups.

Squaressolvestheneedsfortheusergroupwhich
liveinlargerhousingsandthereforehavethespace
for an office. Squares fits well for users living in
mediumhousingandwork1–2daysperweekas
usersprioritizeergonomicsonequaltermswiththe
intendedtargetgroup.InthisgroupSquareswill
compete with normal adjustable desks as users
havethespacetoputtheseitinanoffice.

Forusergroupslivinginmediumhousing,butnot
working from home often, the need for a
workstationcommittedforonlydoingworkisless
profound.Squarescaneasilyfittheneedsofthese
users,butthecompetitionfromthediningtableis
quitehigh.

TheonlyusergroupthatSquaresdoesnotcover,
areuserswhichbothhavelimitedspaceandrarely
workathome.Thereasonforthisisthatergonomic
needsarenotprioritizedandthereforethedining
tableisamoreattractivesolution.

ItisbeneficialforacompanysuchasCUBEDesign
tostriveforothermarkets.Squaresisabigmove
towards a market with another angle than the
other companies presently have. Besides the
market advantages, Squares is also easy to
implement in CUBE Designs production. The
biggestchallengeliesinthemarketingofSquaresif
asignificantentryonthemarketissought.Squares
mayenableCUBEDesigntobefirstͲmoverswithin
this niche, but it may be expected that other
companieswillfollow.

Inawiderperspectivethemarketopportunitiesfor
Squares could be students living in small
apartmentsorindorms.Theproblemhereisthat
thepriceofSquaresistoohigh,eveninthecaseof
possiblepricereduction.Therefore,designchanges
will need to be performed to reduce price even
furtherifSquaresshouldbesoldtothisusergroup.
