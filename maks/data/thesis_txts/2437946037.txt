A B S T R A C T
The objective of this thesis was to investigate the relation between daylight design and glare and to
make some guidelines for reducing duration of glare and to determine what parameters needs to be
considered in general when choosing a fabric screen as the shading method. The thesis investigated
glare in relation to the daylight glare probability scale (DGP) and together with some proposed
glare comfort ranges this was the main method for evaluating a very basic parametric model and
two case studies.
While DGP from previous studies were found to be strong dependent on the vertical eye illuminance
it was investigated in this thesis how the incident angle of the sun affected glare. A vertical displace-
ment angle of the sun compared to the view direction of 20-25o were found to be the combination
that resulted in the highest vertical illuminance and glare perception for a sedentary occupant. The
horizontal displacement angle were found to be 0o for a straight view while for a person sitting
perpendicular to a window a horizontal displacement angle of around 35o from the normal view
direction point of view were found most critical for most of the scenarios investigated.
Based on the angular ﬁndings two matrices were then created for two critical view positions based
on a critical static glare analysis that tested which fabric screens from a sample of 102 modelled
as bidirectional scattering distribution function BSDF that were able to reduce DGP≤0.38 and or
DGP≤0.42 under an assumed critical clear sky with sun sky component. A third matrix were cre-
ated similar which investigated the sample of fabrics but with a intermediate clear sky with sun sky
component where the limits instead were investigated as DGP≤0.35 and or DGP≤0.40.
Some modern cases were tested and evaluated which showed signiﬁcant reduce in glare time when
projecting a proper window solution. One of the matrices were used to propose a fabric screen
that would secure glare control within a modern meeting room, which were seen to control glare
DGP≤0.40 for at least 95% of the occupied time and DGP≤0.42 for the the critical 5% of ofﬁce
time. A simpliﬁed simulation of the screen gave a rough estimation on how daylight and glare was
affected by the proposed screen.
The main ﬁndings of thesis covered how duration of discomfort glare caused by direct sunlight was
affected by room orientation, window layout, LT, occupant position and view direction and what
needed to be considered when choosing a fabric screen as the shading method. Utilising vision for
a fabric screen with a high OF was observed to be very dependent on the LT of a window, sky type,
incident angle and occupant view direction, which were found to effect the upper boundary value
of OF that were able to secure glare control at critical periods. The outcome of the thesis were some
general design strategies that could be used by a turn key contractor company as MT Højgaard
which explained a proposal for discomfort can be evaluated using the DGP and what parameters
that was needed to be considered when controlling glare with fabric screens with a high OF.

I N T R O D U C T I O N
Today more than ever there is a focus on that the buildings that we live in and uses have a good
indoor climate but at the same time the requirement for designing buildings that uses less energy
is also of highest priority. In the end the energy consumption of a building depends on how good
or well the indoor environment is designed to be. The optical environment is one of the four main
indoor parameters apart from thermal, atmospheric and acoustic which needs to be considered and
evaluated when designing and projecting a building project with a good indoor climate.
The new Danish Building Regulation code (BR18) from this year states the following ﬁve points
should be concerned when designing daylight for a building [BR, 2018].
1. Dagslyset udnyttes bedst muligt som lyskilde
2. Unødigt energiforbrug undg˚as
3. Unødig varmetilførsel til rummene undg˚as
4. Gener ved direkte solstr˚aling kan undg˚as
5. Gener ved blænding minimeres
Daylight is utilised as a light source by projecting and placing windows in a certain height and
position within the facade. Sufﬁcient daylight is seen to be documented by having at least 10% glass
to ﬂoor ratio when there are no shades on a window and the window glass has a transmittance
of 75% [BR, 2018]. If the window is shaded by external factors the glass ratio has to be increased
by correction according to some individual factors [TBB, 2018]. As stated in the BR18 sufﬁcient
daylight can also be documented by having at least 300 lux in minimum half of the space, in half of
the daylight hours, when the operation of a solar shading is taken into account[BR, 2018]. Because
the projection of the windows is the most important to consider in daylight design the following
paragraph is also important to consider when projecting a window:
”Vinduer skal udføres, placeres og eventuelt afskærmes, s˚a solindfald gennem dem ikke medfører
overophedning i rummene, og s˚a gener ved direkte solstr˚aling kan undg˚as.” [BR, 2018]
Energy is saved by having a good daylight design that creates an even distribution of daylight within
a room so the need for artiﬁcial lighting throughout the day is limited but also when a solar shading
is in use. It is important that the windows are energy efﬁcient (low U-value) for reducing heat loss in
the winter and additional (low g-value) for reducing heat gain and thereby save energy for cooling
during the summer. Also energy can be saved if the right solar shading is used since sufﬁcient
daylight can still be obtained when a shading is active if it is controlled correct. In Denmark a
requirement of controlling a solar shade is that it should not be active more than 30% of the total
working where the vision is totally blocked, but if the shade is more transparent it is allowed to be
used more than that[Vorre and Mortensen, 2017]. This is recommended due to the fact that a solar
shade disturb the user of the building, both when it changes operation settings but also in general
because some solar shadings decreases the visibility to the exterior more than others.
Direct sunlight genes can be minimised by designing a room after a proper orientation or by use of
4
==PAGE:24==
a solar shade. A shade is either used to reﬂect heat gain or by control the daylight within a room.
The last point that glare genes should be minimised, is still a point that is unclear since glare is a
complex phenomena, which has not yet a standardised method to be evaluated on or are assigned
with design categories. The major issue about the new BR18 is that it only state that direct sunlight
genes should be avoided and that glare should be avoided but not what the limitations are and how
it can be documented that it wont be a problem on a yearly base.
1.1
thesis statement
In most projects that consulting companies advises the window orientations are though already
decided and therefore it is only possible to recommend a special window type, layout and or a
shading strategy for improvement of the visual comfort. Two of the most often used active solar
shadings system by the company MT Højgaard used to control glare and heat gain are venetian
blind systems and roller shades also refereed to as fabric screens. The fabric screens are the least
expensive solution to use but has shown itself to be more difﬁcult to project since the control
of incoming sunlight is very dependent on what fabric screen type is used. The fabric screen is
controlled by lowering the screen to a certain level and thereby reduce incoming sunlight, and is
thereby not as good as redirecting light as a venetian blind but is more relied on its projected optical
parameters.
The problem about projecting a fabric screen for the turnkey contractor MT Højggard is the lack of
information about how the different optical properties of a fabric screen should be considered in
collaboration with the current circumstances within a project. Fabric screens are seen to come in
many different variations, with very different colours resulting in different visual of transmittance
(Tv), solar gain (g-value) and opening factor (OF).
Fabric screens has thought as other shading types been deﬁned with a number from 0 to 4 on
how well they control either daylight utilisation, glare and outdoor visibility, which is based on
the standard EN14501 [MER, 2018]. According to this standard screens with a high OF 10% are
classiﬁed with a 0 on glare control, while they are scoring higher 3-4 in vision control dependent
on their colour. Since fabric screens with a higher OF is classiﬁed as being less disturbent for the
users they are also allowed to be used in more percentage of the occupied hours according as seen
in ﬁgure 1 [SBI, 2016]
Figure 1: Weighting of a solar shade
While a fabric screen with a high OF=10% is seen to create a better vision to the exterior and
is allowed to be used in more hours of the occupied time it is observed that the glare control is
decreased. If a high OF is preferred within a building project it is though important to consider
more than just the vision parameter. While daylight utilisation is common known to be evaluated

on the horizontal illuminance level, a method that can evaluate the glare control performance of a
fabric screen is in general needed.
The turn key contractor MT Højgaard is thereby interested in some design strategies that can be used
to evaluate duration of glare within a building project and what parameters that are needed to be
considered when they chose a proper shading strategy that suits both the indoor climate, the energy
consumption but also the architectural expression of a building. Beside this more information is
also needed on how the choice of OF and Tv are related to control of glare when the less expensive
solution with a fabric screen is used.
1.1.1
Aim of the thesis
The aim of this was set to investigate if there is yet proposed any efﬁcient methods for assessing
glare on a yearly base within buildings and if it could be used to evaluate different scenarios. The
thesis rises the following questions that needs to be answered.
1. What are the most accurate method for assessing glare in buildings and are there seem to be
developed any suggestions for an efﬁcient metric that can be used within building projects?
2. How is the duration and occurrence of glare inﬂuenced by window orientation, window lay-
out, light transmittance, occupant position and view direction?
3. What is the correlation between the sun and the glare perception?
4. How does passive window shading solutions such as MicroShade solar control coated glass
effect the duration of glare?
5. How are the values OF and Tv of fabric screens related to glare control?
6. How can glare be reduced in modern classrooms and meeting rooms.
7. What are the design guidelines when selecting fabric screens as the shading method within a
building project.
Answering the following questions raised for the thesis will be done by ﬁrst making a literature
study about previous research about glare and fabric screen while afterwards a method for the
thesis will be developed. The outcome of the thesis should be some design guidelines that can be
used by a consulting engineering company or a turn key contractor, that describes strategies that
reduce glare and also what should be considered when choosing a fabric screen with a high OF as
the shading method so that daylight, vision and glare are balanced.

L I T T E R AT U R E S T U D Y
This chapter will present some relevant literature about glare studies that had been made in the past
in order to collect theory and tools that could be used for developing a proper method to answer
the questions raised in the thesis statement
2.1
the source of glare
From knowledge about the suns position daylight design for buildings had been improved over
the years, since a lot of research had been made about daylight design and daylight harvesting
[Ruck, 2000]. A good optical environment was deﬁned by the presence of a sufﬁcient amount of
light that allows an occupant to perform his work. Visual discomfort could be caused by either
too low or too high level of light and therefore the use of daylight should be optimised towards by
both having enough light but also from having too much light. The uniformity of light describes
how evenly light was distributed over a task area. With good and even distribution of light visual
stress could be avoided due to less frequent eye adaptations between light and dark spaces, and
thus reduces the risk of visual discomfort also referred to as glare. Glare with the sun visible in
the ﬁeld of view was already found to be an issue back in 1972, where it was found that the most
sensitive region of the sky for glare was at an angle elevation of 20-30o above the horizontal view of
an sedentary occupant [Hopkinson, 1972].
Glare covers a range of different areas but was in general deﬁned as a phenomena that disable the
eyes ability to see clearly. Throughout the years a range of different glare types had been deﬁned.
Disability glare was deﬁned as a person ﬁnding it difﬁcult to see a task clearly and that the glare
then prevents them from doing the task. Discomfort glare on the other hands was when a person
experienced an excessive contrast between illuminated and dark parts of the visual ﬁeld.
Discomfort glare could be caused by either a high illuminance created by the sun or by a strong
artiﬁcial light source. The perception of discomfort glare had been found to be very individual
and dependent on a range of circumstances [Pierson and Bodarta, 2014].
If either the sun or a
light source was directly visible in the ﬁeld of view they could cause direct glare, while some
materials could have either a high reﬂectance or a high specularity which could cause reﬂected glare.
Discomfort glare from windows had though been found to be reduced by having white coloured
surfaces around windows [Hopkinson, 1972]. Since the sun could provides up to 100.000 lux on a
vertical exterior facade it was much brighter than all artiﬁcial lighting equipment and were thereby
the main source of glare. The way of developing glare metrics that described the relation between
lighting levels and the glare perception had been developed through controlled experiments in
laboratories where lighting circumstances had been evaluated against questionnaires answered by
occupants.
2.2
glare metrics
Throughout the years a lot of research had been made in order to create some glare indices that
could be used to evaluate glare. With all other sorts of scales it was important to mention that also

to it. The development of glare scales were all based on researches done on human subjects. A range
of daylight glare index indices such as (DGI), CIE glare index (CGI) and uniﬁed glare rating (UGR)
[Salvatore Carlucci, 2015] had been created to describe discomfort glare, but in the present the most

2.3
investigations of fabric screens
One method for controlling glare was by the use of a fabric screen, which compared to a venetian
blind system were less adaptable to the change in direction of incoming sunlight. While the venetian
blinds in the same ways as fabric screens could be raised or lowered to cover a certain percentage of
a window the venetian blinds also offers the possibility of angular control to optimise the redirection
of direct sunlight. Interior roller shades also refereed to as a fabric screen was available to control
heat gain from the sun, visual connection to the outside and daylight level. Studies have shown the
energy saving potential of shading control using fabric screens [Shen and Tzempelikos, 2014], and
further it had been found that the fabric screens had the potential to functions well in multi sec-
tional dynamic facade concepts [Chan and Tzempelikos, 2014], were it was seen that a fabric screen
placed in the lower part of a window in a combination with a light shelf system controlling the
upper window area was found to result in a greater save in energy on lighting compared to other
solutions.
Besides from daylight savings other factors needed to be considered when selecting a fabric screens.
When a fabric screen was used, the amount of light transmitted into the building was reduced and
also the visibility to the exterior was decreased. In a study four different control strategies were
investigated in order to get an overview of the total building performance as a function of different
shading control strategies. By comparing different control strategies was was found that they per-
formed very differently in terms of daylight levels, glare perception and energy consumption, and
therefore it was important to not only control for reduction of glare but also dependent on daylight
and thermal comfort [Tzempelikos and Shen, 2013].
When controlling a fabric it was shown that a better correlation between controlling for vertical
illuminance was found compared to horizontal illuminance [Konstantzos et al., 2015] but also com-
pared to radiation [Tzempelikos and Shen, 2013]. Due to the less adaptable fabric screens it was
thereby important to apply the right values of OF and Tv with a proper control strategy for utilising

daylight and control glare. Due to the many parameters that deﬁnes a fabric screen studies were
carried out to deﬁne how factors such as opening factor OF and visible transmittance Tv of them
inﬂuenced the glare perception [Konstantzos and Tzempelikos, 2016]. It were found that the values
of OF and Tv for controlling annual glare were inﬂuenced by parameters such as room orientation,
geographic position, occupant position and LT of the window [Chan and Protzman, 2014]. To com-
pletely avoid glare problems at all times with fabric screens, a very low openness factors less than
2% or fully diffuse materials was found in a study [Konstantzos et al., 2015].
The Tv was seen to be related to the total transmission of light trough the fabric screen while the
OF refers to amount of direct light transmitted. These two factors had been found to depend on the
incident angle of sunlight which were why a study presented models for calculating the off normal
solar optical properties of the roller blinds, which could be used in building simulation software
[Kotey and Collins M, 2009]. Simulating fabric screens had be found to be most properly done by
use of a Bidirectional scattering distribution function (BSDF), which takes into account the scattering
of both direct and diffuse light at different incident angels. It had though been found that smaller
uncertainties in the distribution of light could still occur when simulating the same Tv and OF pro-
vided from fabric screen manufactures modelled as a BSDF function [Chan and Protzman, 2014].
However there was still no method for selecting properties of fabric screens that could assist consult-
ing engineers in their decisions of a optimal shading solutions for reduce glare problems and at the
same time utilise daylight and save energy. One study investigated two threshold value one for the
vertical illuminance (1000 lux) caused by direct sunlight and one for the total vertical illuminance
(2670 lux) to propose optimal values for both OF and Tv to secure either 0% annual or 5% annual
discomfort [Chan and Protzman, 2014], which showed a great variance in OF and Tv dependent on
room orientation, LT of window and occupant position.
Full scale experiments had also investigated how people when sitting in front of a window experi-
enced glare when exposed to daylight light, when using different solar shading fabrics. One study
showed an increased dissatisfaction with the optical environment when the opening factor and light
transmittance of screens where increased and resulted in new rough estimations of threshold values
for vertical illuminance (870 lux) caused by direct sunlight and one for the total vertical illuminance
(2800 lux)[Konstantzos and Tzempelikos, 2016]
The biggest issue about assessing discomfort glare seems to be caused by the sun being visible in
the ﬁeld of view or not and or if the vertical illuminance on the eye is caused by direct of diffuse
light. One of the most important factors were thereby found in the literature to think about how the
optical properties OF and Tv of a fabric screens control direct and diffuse light that reaches the eye
of person.

Radiance and Daysim
Daysim was a validated Radiance based day lighting analysis software that models the annual
amount of daylight in and around buildings. Daysim could be used to model dynamic facades
systems and may further be used to model complex electric lighting systems and controls including
manual light switches, occupancy sensors and controlled dimming. Daysim divides the celestial
hemisphere and afterwards it calculates daylight coefﬁcients that were used to determine the interior
illuminance level.
Radiance was a program that was created for analysis and visualisation of lighting. The program
used input ﬁles to specify the scene geometry, materials, luminaries, time, date and sky conditions.
Calculated values include spectral radiance, radiance and glare indices. Radiance used the method
of backward ray-tracing as calculating method. This method was performed as the light rays being
traced back to the light source from the point of measurement or view to the light source.
In
Radiance a total number of 25 different materials were available, were the most common used
material was plastic, which were also the most used mateial within this thesis. Plastic had the
following equation for the total reluctance of the material as seen in equation 7. [RAD, 2018]
ρ = (0.265R + 0.670G + 0.065B) × (1 −S) + S
(7)
where R was the reﬂectance for red light, G was the reﬂectance for green light, B was the reﬂectance
for blue light and S was the specularity of the material. The reason for green light having a higher
weighting was that the human eye was more sensitive to green light than to both red and blue. For
plastic materials values for specularity above 0.1 and roughness of more than 0.2 were uncommon
and look unrealistic.
Beside from plastic a basic glass material within Radiance was used to model the windows inves-
tigated. It shall though be noticed that the glass material within radiance was optimised to model
the behaviour of single layer un coated glass. The fabric screens investigated were found to be most
properly modelled as a BSDF material using a XML ﬁle since the characteristic curve of a fabric
screen differed from ect. a window.

4.1
daylight
Daylight was deﬁned as the total amount of direct and diffuse sunlight, where the indirect sunlight
could be caused by either the diffuse sky radiation or daylight that has been reﬂected by objects
on the earth. Daylight was transferred into a building through transparent windows, and with a
fraction dependent on the light transmittance of the window. Direct light from a patch of the sky
that was visible at a point in the room was determined as, the sky component (SC). Light that
has been reﬂected from an exterior surface and then reaching a point in a room was known as
the externally reﬂected component (ERC). Light transmitted through a window that reaches a point
only after reﬂection from an internal surface was known as the internally reﬂected component (IRC).
A visual explanation of the measured illuminance in a speciﬁc point in a room caused by the sun
could be seen in ﬁgure 5
Figure 5: Daylight deﬁnition
The sum of these three components resulted in the illuminance level measured in lux. The ERC was
inﬂuenced on the placement of exterior buildings and objects and furthermore their reﬂectances
were also very important.
IRC was inﬂuenced by the interior geometry of the room but most
important the surface properties of the materials. The SC was the one that deﬁned the distribution

Screens
A screen which could also be referred to as a roller shade was investigated which was surface
which was a window covering that had the purpose of shading for unwanted sunlight within a
room. Screens cane in various of colours and OF which affects the way they distributed light and
heat. Light coloured screens were often more transparent than dark coloured screens if they had
the same OF. The OF referred to how tight the weave was, which directly reﬂected the amount of
light that was passing through the screen.
A low OF meant that the screen was more tightly and would thereby be less visibility through
it, which meant it had a better daylight control. On the other hand a high OF features a looser
weave and therefore it gave a worse daylight control caused by the higher amount of direct sunlight
transmitted. Mermet was one of many fabric production companies that produced screens for both
interior and exterior use and had a series of screens with different OF. A Visual illustration of their
white coloured fabric series with different OF and Tv were shown in ﬁgure 14 [MER, 2018].

D I S C U S S I O N
This chapter will discuss the results obtained in chapter 9, chapter 10, chapter 11, chapter 12 and in
the mean time some main conclusions will be made.
13.1
orientation study
Since the occupied time was chosen to be 8-17 for all the 365 days it was seen that north oriented
room experienced glare in very limited period of time for these directions. If the occupied time
of the building instead was from 7-18 then glare would have become a greater issue for northern
orientations. Even though the same models were simulated for different orientation the results
showed that northern oriented windows experience less duration of glare compared to southern
oriented windows.
13.2
evaluation of the parametric study of a basic room.
From the total of 7 different facade expressions analysed with a total of 4 chosen occupant positions
and views it was experienced that the duration of glare was highly inﬂuenced by the parameters
analysed even though only minor differences were obtained when comparing the daylight metrics.
While the duration of glare was seen to be lowest for the positions of 1m(L) and 1m(R) compared
too the two other positions 1m(S) and 2m(S), it where mainly caused by the sun being less visible
in the ﬁeld of view and also the reduction in vertical eye illuminance. The position 1m (R) resulted
in least duration of glare which were caused by a more northern view compared to 1m (L) with less
hours of sun sun being visible in the ﬁeld of view.
The duration of sun visible in the ﬁeld of view were seen to be directly connected with the duration
of glare for all the scenarios. It was though experienced that the lowering the LT of a window
combined with an adjusted GR according to BR18 was seen to decrease the duration of glare for
all orientations for the positions 1m (S) and 2m(S) but was seen to be most efﬁcient for southern
orientations S and SW. For the positions of 1m (L) and 1m (R) the increased windows with a lower
LT were though seen to result in a higher duration of glare hours for some of the scenarios caused
by a higher duration of sun visible in the ﬁeld of view.
Beside from the LT also the window layout was more difﬁcult to conclude anything on since even
though a wide window layout combined with a low LT were seen to result in least duration of
glare for all orientations with the positions 1m (S) and 2m (S) the wide window layout combined
with a high LT were seen to result in the highest duration of glare hours except for the north/west
orientation.
Compared with the daylight metrics it was seen that even with the same scene but for different
orientation the metrics resulted in very different values. A higher window layout allowed daylight
to penetrate deeper into the room resulting in a higher DFmean for the high window layouts but
also causes more higher sun incident angels to increase the glare duration within southern oriented
rooms. The UDI<100lux−2000lux resulted in almost the same values except for the north/west situation
80
==PAGE:100==
with a wide window which had signiﬁcant lower values.
The southern orientation had the most daylight hours with the sun visible in the ﬁeld of view, which
were also to be seen from the DA300lux[50%] which were much higher for the totally south orientation
compared to the other orientations. Because this metric considered the occupied hours instead of
the daylight hours within BR18 it could be discussed if it was a proper metric to use to evaluate the
scenarios that were created using the GR rule from BR18.
The MD74 scenarios for window solutions including the MicroShade-D were seen to result in values
less than 50% for all orientation except for the south. The glass in the MicroShade-D solution had
a high LT=74% and together with the micro lammellas it were seen to control glare and daylight
very differently. While the MicroShade resulted in the lowest duration of glare for 1m (S) and 2m
(S) compared to the other window solutions it was seen that the MD74 performed best for a totally
south oriented window and a south west orientation.
For the west orientation the MD74 solution were seen to result in the least glare hours with the 1m
(S) position while it experienced the most hours with a 1m (L) position. The 1m (R) position was
not evaluated for this scenario so it was unknown if it was only the 1m (L) position which would
result in more glare hours than the other window layouts. But overall the effect of the MicroShade
seemed to be biggest on south oriented windows just like increasing the glass area and applying a
lower LT.
From the proposed values of exterior Ev of 10.000 lux and 20.000 lux it was experienced that most
of the glare values of DGP> 0.40 were experienced above these values. The exception was DGP
values caused by sunlight of low incident angle. For those scenarios with more hours with the sun
being visible in the ﬁeld of view with a low altitude were also seen to result in more hours with
DGP values when Ev of 10.000 lux and 20.000 lux.
The used DGP formula were seen to be strong dependent on the vertical illuminance, but since
the scenarios analysed where carried out close to a window with direct sun hours the simpliﬁed
calculation method of DGP was not suitable. It was though obtained that a good relation between
the DGPs and eDGPs method were found for the 1m (S) position, but the DGP of 0.40 was though
seen to be caused at around 4000 lux rather than 3474 lux. For the 2m (S) more hours of DGP>0.40
were though seen to be caused by a lower Ev at eye level than 4000 lux. For the 1m (L) and 1m (R)
position even more of DGP>0.40 were caused by lower illuminance caused by direct sunlight.
While the parametric study showed some tendencies about how the orientation of the room inﬂu-
enced the duration of glare with the same investigated parameters it was difﬁcult to propose a
special window layout and or LT for the orientations, but MicroShade-D were seen to perform good
for the S and SW oriented rooms for all occupant positions. Lowering the LT with an adjusted
window area was seen to perform better for the positions 1m (S) and 2m (S) but for 1m (L) and 1m
(R) the position of the occupant should be considered to evaluate if the performance was also better
for these view directions.
13.3
angular investigation of fabric screens
While the total glare perception was seen to be a result of both the vertical eye illuminance being
above a certain level and or the experience of low sun incident angels on the eye, the investigation
of the fabric screens showed how the values of OF and Tv were limited to control glare at different
incident angels for both a clear sky with sun and a intermediate clear sky with sun.
The view directions in these simulations were carried out as static view that was assumed critical
placed close to a window. The boundary values found for OF and Tv values were thought limited
to the way the simulations were carried out. First of all only three positions close to the window
were investigated to cover assumed critical scenarios and furthermore did it only consider a certain
81
==PAGE:101==
distance to the window. How the boundaries of OF and Tv was dependent on the distance to the
screen were thereby not covered by this study.
The fabric screens were modelled as BSDF functions, but from the comparison with the semi em-
pirical formula smaller deviations were found for the numbers of τbb and τbt and therefore values
of OF and Tv simulated could have differed from how real products would have performed. Also
the values of OF and Tv obtained from the XML ﬁle using the BSDF viewer covered more speciﬁc
values for OF and Tv than were normal available from a fabric company such as Mermet.
While it was not possible to run annual simulations using including the BSDF material within DIVA
simulating with the epw. weather ﬁle the clear and intermediate sky was thought to be used to
investigate and evaluate how the fabric screens performed under critical sky components using dif-
ferent incident angles and illuminance levels as the inputs. Based on the glare class A and class B
the two sky components were though thought to represent some critical scenarios what would be
experienced within 5% of the occupied hours with the clear sky with sun and or as one of the worst
scenarios within the 95% of the occupied hours for the intermediate clear sky with sun component.
While the choice of OF and Tv of the fabric screens were seen to be very dependent on the view
position the most important factor was still seen to be the incident angle. While higher incident
angels caused fabrics with higher OF to control glare it could be suggested that the distribution
of incident angle on a window was considered when deciding the boundary of the OF, since this
parameter where found to be more inﬂuenced on the incident angle than the the Tv.
The distribution of sun incident angles within certain levels could though only be used as a rough in-
dicator on which incident angels a projected fabric screen would experience. While this study were
limited to only cover models without external obstructions it was still unknown how they would
affect the performance of the fabric screens. Some of the very low light incident angels provided by
the sun might be blocked from exterior obstructions and in general external obstructions should be
seen as a very complex factor that varies and could have had a inﬂuence on the performance of OF
and Tv.
13.4
investigation of a modern classroom
A proposal of some some window scenarios were investigated on a modern classroom, which
showed that the total glare duration of 47% for the original proposed windows could be reduced to
9% if a lower LT of 46% were chosen with increased glass area placed in the facade opposite to the
view direction of the students but in front of the teacher.
Even though the DGP were calculated using the eDGPs method the evaluation did only consider
reﬂected glare from the modelled whiteboard behind the teacher.
The two proposed scenarios 4 and 5, were reduced the duration of glare the most were also seen to
reduce the amount of hours with DGP>0.40 when the exterior vertical illuminance level were below
10.000 lux an 20.000 lux.
For the scenarios with window area placed within one window facade it was easier to determine
that the glare was caused by that window but for the three other scenarios with windows in two
facades it was more difﬁcult to deﬁne what window that caused glare especially during the middle
of the day with direct sunlight through both window. The teacher was seen as the position that
could experience glare problems caused by both windows.
While the glare perception was evaluated as a percentage of hours with DGP<0.40 and DGP<0.35
for the hours of 8-17 the study was limited to the fact that only one view direction and the occupant
being present all hours within the deﬁned period was analysed. While as other indoor parameters
the user will adapt at some point the evaluation using the eDGPs method should be seen as a tool
82
==PAGE:102==
that could evaluate the overall glare perception within a room and show some tendencies when
evaluating different solutions rather than indicate the actual duration of discomfort glare.
Since laptops could be a more common things that the students was using in the school it could
be interesting to evaluate models were a reﬂective screen were placed in front of the occupants in
future studies. One important thing that needs to be discussed was though what standard views,
positions and objects should be modelled within a glare evaluation study in general. While the
glare analysis using the eDGPs takes some time to simulate it was important that the number of
positions, view directions evaluated were kept a minimum as well as the complexity of the scene if
a glare evaluation should be suitable to use in larger scaled projects.
13.5
investigation of a modern meeting room
The investigation of the modern meeting roomed showed that even though the room had a very
high GR a reduction of the LT of the window from 74% to 46%, without adjusting the GR resulted
in lowering the amount of hours were glare control was needed with 6% according to the proposed
design class B.
For the LT=74% occupant 6 was seen to have the highest duration of glare while for the LT=46% it
was seen to be occupant 3. Because the amount of time that glare should be controlled for a room
depends on all the current positions a improvement of one critical spot showed that this was equal
to the total improvement.
From a estimation of a critical incident angle perceived by occupant 3 two screens based on the
results from the fabric screen investigations were chosen based on the assumption that the critical
incident angle would not experience more than 5% of the occupied hours. The investigation of the
critical time periods showed that the proposed screens were able to control glare with DGP values
below 0.40 for both the investigated scenarios. It was though seen that for occupant 1 making a 180o
turn resulted in 0.40 < DGP, and therefore it was showed how important it was to consider the view
direction and position of and occupant when proposing a fabric screen based on the boundaries for
OF and Tv found in the fabric study.
A investigation of illuminance level on the work plane for the critical time periods with the fabric
screen fully drawn showed that the LT=46% with the SE17 screen resulted in less percentage of the
work plane with less than 300 lux which where 13% for the time 16.30 compared to the LT=46%
with the SA43 screen, which were 34.4%.
Beside from showing a reduction in the total glare duration a more even distribution of illuminance
was also found when simulating with a lower LT and the proposed screen. Assuming the Tv of
the screen being equal to the LT of a window showed that the duration of glare was reduced while
UDI was improved and the sDA was only lowered by 19% for the LT=74% scenario and 14% for
the LT=46% scenario. It was thereby important to consider if a lower LT could be used if a high GR
was projected. Also the reduction in LT showed that the amount of hours with DGP>0.40 could be
reduced from 5.1% to 2.1% considering a control of 20.000 lux on the exterior facade. While 95% of
the DGP appear above this level for both scenarios it could be a proposed glare control strategy for
the meeting room.
13.6
perspectivation
Glare was seen experienced as a very complex phenomena that was very dependent on the total
vertical eye illuminance Ev, incident angle of the sun and occupant position and therefore the out-
come of the parametric study were seen to show some tendencies between daylight design and glare
rather than propose good solutions.
83
==PAGE:103==
The ﬁndings of this thesis shows tendencies about how glare duration could be reduced by lowering
the LT of a window for some modern case studies and also the MicroShade-D solution were seen to
be able to reduce glare problems in southern oriented rooms.
The investigation of boundaries for fabric screens showed how important it was to consider critical
sky types and different incident angels since fabric screens with high OF 10% were not seen to be
able to reduce the DGP to below the proposed threshold in class A and class B at low incident
angels with the two critical sky component.
While the fabric screens with a high OF might secure a better vision to the exterior they were also
seem to be limited to control glare dependent on the sky type and incident angle of direct sunlight.
When using the fabric screen within a project it was thereby found very important to consider that
the fabric screen with a high OF was less suitable for controlling glare at low sun incident angels
with the sun visible in the ﬁeld of view.
Things that needs to be considered when using the transparent screens was therefor at ﬁrst how
transparent or how high OF was actual needed. While a more transparent solar shade was allowed
to be used in more than 30% of the occupied hours it should be evaluated if a fabric screen with
a higher OF but a worse glare control could be used within a project or that a lower OF should
be chosen for better daylight control which also means less vision to the exterior when the screen
was in use. Some of the important thing to consider when using a fabric screen with a high OF is
summarised in chapter 14
Thought the outcome of this thesis shows some tendencies between daylight design and glare and
how a fabric screen performs under critical conditions, uncovered parts were how the fabric screens
performed on a annual level compared to, cooling consumption and total energy consumption of
a building. Further research was thought needed to show how transparent screens with high OF
performs in relation to the total performance of the building.

final comments
While these design strategies were based on choosing a high OF value as possible for utilising vi-

presented proposed boundaries for OF which were based on a sample of 102 fabric screens and
thereby some combination may not have been tested. It is important that the matrices are based on
simulation and that differences in modelling and real performance could occur.
It was important that these strategies were seen as guidelines limited to the results obtained within
the thesis which did mainly consider a simple scene and furthermore discomfort glare were anal-
ysed by direct sun and only a limited amount of reﬂected sunlight from exterior surfaces.
The design strategies presented were limited to glare alone, which was why attention should be
taken to both daylight, cooling and energy consumption when considering the fabric screen as the
shading method.

C O N C L U S I O N
The investigation of a range of parameters inﬂuence on glare showed the complexity of creating
design guidelines to avoid glare problems and still consider daylight availability. Nevertheless the
outcome of this thesis showed some tendencies about how a window layout, LT of a window and
the position of an occupant inﬂuence glare. The duration of glare was seen to be correlated to both
the time when the sun was being visible in the ﬁeld of view, the vertical eye illuminance level and
the incident angle of direct sunlight.
The investigation of a sample of 102 fabric screens modelled as a BSDF material showed that choice
of OF and Tv value that were able to control glare was highly dependent on the occupant view
direction, sky type and the incident angle caused by the sun position. Rooms that was different
oriented were seen to experience very different amount of hours within a certain sun incident angel
radius and also the range of vertical eye illuminance where seen to differ for each orientation caused
by the sun having very different positions on the sky.
Based on the parametric investigation three matrices were created proposing boundaries for OF and
Tv values for controlling glare for assumed critical scenarios. To apply a suggested fabric screen
from one of the matrices one were seen to consider several thing such as LT of the window, view
orientation and window orientation.
A comparison of ﬁve window layouts proposed from results obtained in the basic parameter study,
showed that a lower LT with adjusted GR or the MicroShade-D could signiﬁcant reduce the duration
of glare and also if windows were placed in a proper facade orientation. The investigation were
though limited to only cover 5 critical positions and limited objects within the scene.
By testing one of the on a modern meeting room it was found that a proposals from one of the
matrices resulted in DGP ≤0.40 on a critical day with a clear sky with sun SC for the a modern
meeting room. A simpliﬁed method was used to show how the proposed Tv value modelled as a LT
value reduced the duration of glare to be below 95% of the occupied when applying shading control
whenever DGP > 0.40. Beside from this the exterior vertical illuminance level of 10.000-20.000 lux
were also found to be a possible value to control glare after, since it would result in only around 5%
of occupied time with discomfort glare.
Final conclusions on the performance of fabric screen were not made due to the uncertainties about
annual thermal performance and more precise annual daylight performance using a BSDF materials.
Future work was thereby seen to make dynamic simulations with shading controls such as fabric
screens were the ranges of OF and Tv from the three proposed matrices were tested and evaluated
on how they perform on a annual base in terms of reducing glare combined with and evaluation of
daylight and energy saved on cooling and artiﬁcial lighting.
